tall

(tɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of more than average height2. a. (postpositive) having a specified height ⇒ a woman five feet tallb. (in combination) ⇒ a twenty-foot-tall partition3. informal exaggerated or incredible ⇒ a tall story4. informal difficult to accomplish ⇒ a tall order5. an archaic word for excellent
Derived forms
ˈtallness noun
Word origin
C14 (in the sense: big, comely, valiant); related to Old English getæl prompt, Old High German gizal quick, Gothic untals foolish

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is tall has a greater height than is normal or average. ⇒ Being tall can make you feel incredibly self-confident. ⇒ She was a young woman, fairly tall and fairly slim. ⇒ The windows overlooked a lawn of tall waving grass.2. graded adjective & adjective [as ADJ as]You use tall to ask or talk about the height of someone or something. ⇒ How tall are you? ⇒ I'm only 5ft tall, and I look younger than my age. ⇒ I am already as tall as she is. ⇒ Tony, my oldest, is already taller than me, and he's only eleven. → high3. →  a tall order4. →  to walk tall
