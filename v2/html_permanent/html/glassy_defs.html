glassy

(ˈɡlɑːsɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: glassier or glassiest1. resembling glass, esp in smoothness, slipperiness, or transparency2. void of expression, life, or warmth ⇒ a glassy stare

Powered by COBUILD
(glɑːsi , glæsi) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as glassy, you mean that it is very smooth and shiny, like glass. [written] ⇒ The water was glassy. ⇒ ...glassy green pebbles.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe someone's eyes or expression as glassy, you mean that they are showing no feeling, emotion, or awareness. [mainly written] vacant ⇒ He was very still, his eyes glassy. ⇒ Henry gave Paul a glassy-eyed stare.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
