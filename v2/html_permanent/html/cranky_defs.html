cranky

(kræŋki )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe ideas or ways of behaving as cranky, you disapprove of them because you think they are strange. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ Vegetarianism has shed its cranky image. ⇒ The Front has often been dismissed as a cranky fringe group.2. graded adjective & adjectiveCranky means bad-tempered. [US, informal] ⇒ It was a long trek, and Jack and I both started to get cranky after about ten minutes.
