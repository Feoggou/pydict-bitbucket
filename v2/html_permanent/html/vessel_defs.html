vessel

(ˈvɛsəl )
Definitions
noun1. any object used as a container, esp for a liquid2. a passenger or freight-carrying ship, boat, etc3. an aircraft, esp an airship4. anatomy a tubular structure that transports such body fluids as blood and lymph5. botany a tubular element of xylem tissue consisting of a row of cells in which the connecting cell walls have broken down6. rare a person regarded as an agent or vehicle for some purpose or quality ⇒ she was the vessel of the Lord
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Old French vaissel, from Late Latin vascellum urn, from Latin vās vessel
