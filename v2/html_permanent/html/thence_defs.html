thence

(ðɛns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. from that place2. Also: thenceforth (ˈðɛnsˈfɔːθ ), thenceforward (ˈðɛnsˈfɔːwəd ) or obsolete thenceforwards from that time or event; thereafter3. therefore

Word Origin

C13 thannes, from thanne, from Old English thanon; related to Gothic thanana, Old Norse thanan

thence

Powered by COBUILD
(ðens )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [oft ADV before v]Thence means from a particular place, especially when you are giving directions about how to get somewhere. [formal, old-fashioned] ⇒ I ran straight up to Columbia County, then turned East, came down the Harlem Valley and thence home. ⇒ The mosaics were sent to Munich, and thence to Geneva.2. adverb [oft ADV before v]Thence is used to say that something changes from one state or condition to another. [formal, old-fashioned] ⇒ ...the conversion of sunlight into heat and thence into electricity.
