solatium

(səʊˈleɪʃɪəm )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tia (-ʃɪə)law, mainly US and Scottish compensation awarded to a party for injury to the feelings as distinct from physical suffering and pecuniary loss

Word Origin

C19: from Latin: see solace
