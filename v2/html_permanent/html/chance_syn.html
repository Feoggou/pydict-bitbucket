chance

noun

  1. probability, odds, possibility, prospect, liability, likelihood • This partnership has a good chance of success. OPPOSITES:  certainty, impossibility, surety, improbability, unlikelihood
  2. opportunity, opening, occasion, time, scope, window • All eligible people will get a chance to vote.
  3. accident, fortune, luck, fate, casualty, destiny, coincidence, misfortune, providence, fortuity • I met him quite by chance. OPPOSITES:  design, intention
  4. risk, speculation, gamble, hazard • I certainly think it's worth taking a chance.

adjective

  1. accidental, random, casual, incidental, unforeseen, unintentional, fortuitous, inadvertent, serendipitous, unforeseeable, unlooked-for • He describes their chance meeting as intense. OPPOSITES: planned, expected, designed, arranged, deliberate, foreseen, intentional

verb

  1. happen • A man I chanced to meet proved to be a most unusual character.
  2. happen, occur, fall out, come about, befall, come to pass, betide
  3. risk, try, stake, venture, gamble, hazard, wager • No sniper would chance a shot from amongst that crowd.
related adjective:
fortuitous
