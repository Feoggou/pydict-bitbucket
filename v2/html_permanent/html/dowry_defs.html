dowry

(ˈdaʊərɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ries1. the money or property brought by a woman to her husband at marriage2. (esp formerly) a gift made by a man to his bride or her parents3. Christianity a sum of money required on entering certain orders of nuns4. a natural talent or gift5. obsolete a widow's dower
Word origin
C14: from Anglo-French douarie, from Medieval Latin dōtārium; see dower
