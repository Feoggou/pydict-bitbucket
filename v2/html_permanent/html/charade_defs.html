(ʃərɑːd , US -reɪd )
Word forms: charades
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]If you describe someone's actions as a charade, you mean that their actions are so obviously false that they do not convince anyone. [disapproval] ⇒ I wondered why he had gone through the elaborate charade. [+ of] ⇒ The U.N. at the moment is still trying to maintain the charade of neutrality.2. uncount nounCharades is a game for teams of players in which one team acts a word or phrase, syllable by syllable, until other players guess the whole word or phrase. ⇒ She and her three brothers played charades.
