ulterior

(ʌlˈtɪərɪə )
Definitions
adjective1. lying beneath or beyond what is revealed, evident, or supposed ⇒ ulterior motives2. succeeding, subsequent, or later3. lying beyond a certain line or point
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ulteriorly (ulˈteriorly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: further, from ulter beyond

ulterior for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌltɪəriəʳ )
Definitions
adjective [ADJ n]If you say that someone has an ulterior motive for doing something, you believe that they have a hidden reason for doing it. [+ for] ⇒ Sheila had an ulterior motive for trying to help Stan. ⇒ Posing for the camera pleased him more than anything else. No doubt with the ulterior object of catching votes.
