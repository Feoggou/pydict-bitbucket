mindless

(ˈmaɪndlɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. stupid or careless2. requiring little or no intellectual effort ⇒ a mindless task

(maɪndləs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a violent action as mindless, you mean that it is done without thought and will achieve nothing. [mainly British, disapproval] ⇒ ...a plot that mixes blackmail, extortion and mindless violence.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person or group as mindless, you mean that they are stupid or do not think about what they are doing. [disapproval] ⇒ She wasn't at all the mindless little wife so many people perceived her to be.mindlessly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I was annoyed with myself for having so mindlessly lost thirty dollars.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an activity as mindless, you mean that it is extremely dull. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the mindless repetitiveness of some tasks.mindlessly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I spent many hours mindlessly banging a tennis ball against the wall.
