orderly

(ˈɔːdəlɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. in order, properly arranged, or tidy2. obeying or appreciating method, system, and arrangement3. harmonious or peaceful4. military of or relating to orders ⇒ an orderly bookadverb5. rare according to custom or rulenoun plural -lies6. medicine a male hospital attendant7. military a junior rank detailed to carry orders or perform minor tasks for a more senior officer

orderly for learners of English

(ɔːʳdəʳli )
Word forms: orderlies
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is done in an orderly fashion or manner, it is done in a well-organized and controlled way. ⇒ The organizers guided them in orderly fashion out of the building. ⇒ Despite the violence that preceded the elections, reports say that polling was orderly and peaceful.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is orderly is neat or arranged in a neat way. ⇒ It's a beautiful, clean and orderly city. ⇒ Their vehicles were parked in orderly rows.orderliness uncount noun ⇒ A balance is achieved in the painting between orderliness and unpredictability.3. countable nounAn orderly is a person who works in a hospital and does jobs that do not require special medical training.
