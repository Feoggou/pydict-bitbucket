modicum

(ˈmɒdɪkəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna small amount or portion

Word Origin

C15: from Latin: a little way, from modicus moderate

(mɒdɪkəm )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
quantifierA modicum of something, especially something that is good or desirable, is a reasonable but not large amount of it. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ I'd like to think I've had a modicum of success. [+ of] ⇒ ...a modicum of privacy.
Example sentences containing 'modicum'
He wasn't winning popularity contests yet, but he had earned a modicum of respect on campus. Barbara Delinsky SUDDENLY
