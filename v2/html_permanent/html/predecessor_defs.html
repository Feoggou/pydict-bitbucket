predecessor

(ˈpriːdɪˌsɛsə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who precedes another, as in an office2. something that precedes something else3. an ancestor; forefather

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Late Latin praedēcessor, from prae before + dēcēdere to go away, from dē away + cēdere to go

(priːdɪsesəʳ , US pred- )
Word forms: predecessors
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu poss N]Your predecessor is the person who had your job before you. ⇒ He maintained that he learned everything he knew from his predecessor Kenneth Sisam.2. countable noun [usu with poss]The predecessor of an object or machine is the object or machine that came before it in a sequence or process of development. ⇒ The car is some 40mm shorter than its predecessor.
