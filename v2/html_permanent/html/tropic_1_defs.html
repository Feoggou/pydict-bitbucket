tropic

(ˈtrɒpɪk )
Definitions
noun1. (sometimes capital) either of the parallel lines of latitude at about 231⁄2°N (tropic of Cancer) and 231⁄2°S (tropic of Capricorn) of the equator2. See the tropics3. astronomy either of the two parallel circles on the celestial sphere having the same latitudes and names as the corresponding lines on the earthadjective4. a less common word for tropical
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin tropicus belonging to a turn, from Greek tropikos, from tropos a turn; from the ancient belief that the sun turned back at the solstices
