turbine

(ˈtɜːbɪn , -baɪn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounany of various types of machine in which the kinetic energy of a moving fluid is converted into mechanical energy by causing a bladed rotor to rotate. The moving fluid may be water, steam, air, or combustion products of a fuel. See also reaction turbine, impulse turbine, gas turbine

Word Origin

C19: from French, from Latin turbō whirlwind, from turbāre to throw into confusion
