hopeless

(ˈhəʊplɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. having or offering no hope2. impossible to analyse or solve3. unable to learn, function, etc4. informal without skill or ability

hopeless

Powered by COBUILD
(hoʊpləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you feel hopeless, you feel very unhappy because there seems to be no possibility of a better situation or success. ⇒ He had not heard her cry before in this uncontrolled, hopeless way. ⇒ The economic crisis makes jobs almost impossible to find and even able pupils feel hopeless about job prospects.hopelessly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ I looked around hopelessly. ⇒ ...a young woman hopelessly in love with a handsome hero.hopelessness uncount noun [+ about] ⇒ She had a feeling of hopelessness about the future.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something thing that is hopeless is certain to fail or be unsuccessful. ⇒ I don't believe your situation is as hopeless as you think. If you love each other, you'll work it out. ⇒ A doctor is there to treat and to cure, not to dismiss anyone as a hopeless case.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is hopeless at something, they are very bad at it. [informal] [+ at] ⇒ I'd be hopeless at working for somebody else. ⇒ I was fine at sports, but pretty hopeless academically.4. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use hopeless to emphasize how bad or inadequate something or someone is. [emphasis] ⇒ I don't drive and the buses are quite hopeless. ⇒ Argentina's economic policies were a hopeless mess.hopelessly graded adverb & adverb [oft ADV after v] ⇒ The story is hopelessly confusing. ⇒ Harry was hopelessly lost. ⇒ By October 1990, when arrested, he was hopelessly in debt.
