republic

(rɪˈpʌblɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a form of government in which the people or their elected representatives possess the supreme power2. a political or national unit possessing such a form of government3. a constitutional form in which the head of state is an elected or nominated president4. any community or group that resembles a political republic in that its members or elements exhibit a general equality, shared interests, etc ⇒ the republic of letters
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from French république, from Latin rēspublica literally: the public thing, from rēs thing + publica public
