chaff1

(tʃɑːf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the mass of husks, etc, separated from the seeds during threshing2. finely cut straw and hay used to feed cattle3. something of little worth; rubbish (esp in the phrase separate the wheat from the chaff)4. the dry membranous bracts enclosing the flowers of certain composite plants5. thin strips of metallic foil released into the earth's atmosphere to confuse radar signals and prevent detection
Derived forms
chaffy (ˈchaffy) adjective
Word origin
Old English ceaf; related to Old High German keva husk

Powered by COBUILD
(tʃɑːf , tʃæf)
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounChaff is the outer part of grain such as wheat. It is removed before the grain is used as food.2. →  to separate the wheat from the chaff
