dimity

(ˈdɪmɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tiesa. a light strong cotton fabric with woven stripes or squaresb. (as modifier) ⇒ a dimity bonnet
Word origin
C15: from Medieval Latin dimitum, from Greek dimiton, from di-1 + mitos thread of the warp
Example sentences containing 'dimity'
Sensibly dressed Gwen, with her long-wearing nightclothes, flannel for winter, dimity for summer. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LIONFancy shook the bolt of dimity to unfold its pretty pink flounces to the floor of the Tabor General Store. Spellman, Cathy Cash PAINT THE WIND
