pallid

(ˈpælɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. lacking colour or brightness; wan ⇒ a pallid complexion2. lacking vigour; vapid ⇒ a pallid performance

Derived Forms

pallidly (ˈpallidly) adverbpallidness (ˈpallidness) or pallidity (palˈlidity) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin pallidus, from pallēre to be pale1

pallid

Powered by COBUILD
(pælɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is pallid is pale in an unattractive or unnatural way. ⇒ ...helpless grief on pallid faces. ⇒ ...pallid grey vapour.2. graded adjective & adjectiveYou can describe something such as a performance or book as pallid if it is weak or not at all exciting. ⇒ ...a pallid account of the future of transport. ⇒ ...a pallid unsuccessful romance.
