continuum

(kənˈtɪnjʊəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tinua (-ˈtɪnjʊə) or -tinuumsa continuous series or whole, no part of which is perceptibly different from the adjacent parts
Word origin
C17: from Latin, neuter of continuus continuous

continuum

Powered by COBUILD
(kəntɪnjuəm )
Word forms: continua (kəntɪnjuə ), continuums
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]A continuum is a set of things on a scale, which have a particular characteristic to different degrees. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ These various complaints are part of a continuum of ill-health. ⇒ It is at one end of the cost continuum.2. countable noun [usu sing]A continuum is a continuous series of closely connected events. [formal] ⇒ The medical professional bodies reply that development from fertilisation onwards is a continuum.
Example sentences containing 'continuum'
`It would seem that the orchestra exists on a continuum of screaming matches and angry silences. Goldsborough, Robert MURDER IN E MINORIt was traveling visibly through the larger continuum of the atmosphere.
