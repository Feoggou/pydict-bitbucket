druid

(ˈdruːɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (sometimes capital)1. a member of an ancient order of priests in Gaul, Britain, and Ireland in the pre-Christian era2. a member of any of several modern movements attempting to revive druidism
Derived forms
druidess (ˈdruːɪdɪs ) feminine noundruidic (druˈidic) or druidical (druˈidical) adjectivedruidry (ˈdruidry) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin druides, of Gaulish origin; compare Old Irish druid wizards
