fecund

(ˈfiːkənd , ˈfɛk-)
Definitions
adjective1. greatly productive; fertile2. intellectually productive; prolific
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin fēcundus; related to Latin fētus offspring

fecund

Powered by COBUILD
(fiːkənd , fek- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveLand or soil that is fecund is able to support the growth of a large number of strong healthy plants. [formal] ⇒ The pampas are still among the most fecund lands in the world. ⇒ Large animals are less fecund than small ones.fecundity (fekʌndɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ ...an island famous for the profusion and fecundity of its bird life.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as fecund, you approve of it because it produces a lot of good or useful things. [formal, approval] ⇒ It has now become clear how extraordinarily fecund a decade was the 1890s.fecundity uncount noun ⇒ ...the extraordinary fecundity and vitality of the city.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
