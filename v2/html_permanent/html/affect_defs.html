affect1

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (əˈfɛkt ) (transitive)1. to act upon or influence, esp in an adverse way ⇒ damp affected the sparking plugs2. to move or disturb emotionally or mentally ⇒ her death affected him greatly3. (of pain, disease, etc) to attacknoun (ˈæfɛkt , əˈfɛkt )4. psychology the emotion associated with an idea or set of ideas. See also affection
Word origin
C17: from Latin affectus, past participle of afficere to act upon, from ad- to + facere to do

affect2

(əˈfɛkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (mainly tr)1. to put on an appearance or show of; make a pretence of ⇒ to affect ignorance2. to imitate or assume, esp pretentiously ⇒ to affect an accent3. to have or use by preference ⇒ she always affects funereal clothing4. to adopt the character, manner, etc, of ⇒ he was always affecting the politician5. (of plants or animals) to live or grow in ⇒ penguins affect an arctic climate6. to incline naturally or habitually towards ⇒ falling drops of liquid affect roundness
Word origin
C15: from Latin affectāre to strive after, pretend to have; related to afficere to affect1

affect

Powered by COBUILD
(əfekt )
Word forms: affects , affecting , affected
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something affects a person or thing, it influences them or causes them to change in some way. [V n] ⇒ Nicotine adversely affects the functioning of the heart and arteries. [V n] ⇒ More than seven million people have been affected by drought. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the worst-affected areas of Somalia.2. verbIf a disease affects someone, it causes them to become ill. [V n] ⇒ Arthritis is a crippling disease which affects people all over the world.3. verbIf something or someone affects you, they make you feel a strong emotion, especially sadness or pity. [V n] ⇒ The divorce affected Jim deeply. [V n] ⇒ Gregor loved his sister, and her loss clearly still affects him.4. verbIf you affect a particular characteristic or way of behaving, you pretend that it is genuine, or natural for you. [literary] [V n] ⇒ He listened to them, affecting an amused interest. [V n] ⇒ Ms. Redgrave affects a heavy Italian accent. [Also V to-inf]
