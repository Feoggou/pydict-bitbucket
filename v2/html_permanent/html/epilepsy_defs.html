epilepsy

(ˈɛpɪˌlɛpsɪ )
Definitions
nouna disorder of the central nervous system characterized by periodic loss of consciousness with or without convulsions. In some cases it is due to brain damage but in others the cause is unknown. See also grand mal, petit mal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin epilēpsia, from Greek, from epilambanein to attack, seize, from lambanein to take
