defame

(dɪˈfeɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to attack the good name or reputation of; slander; libel2. archaic to indict or accuse
Derived forms
defamer (deˈfamer) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French defamer, from Latin dēfāmāre, from diffāmāre to spread by unfavourable report, from fāma fame
