burgess

(ˈbɜːdʒɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in England)a. a citizen or freeman of a boroughb. any inhabitant of a borough2. English history a Member of Parliament from a borough, corporate town, or university3. a member of the colonial assembly of Maryland or Virginia
Word origin
C13: from Old French burgeis, from borc town, from Late Latin burgus, of Germanic origin; see borough

Burgess

(ˈbɜːdʒɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1.  Anthony, real name John Burgess Wilson. 1917–93, English novelist and critic: his novels include A Clockwork Orange (1962), Tremor of Intent (1966), Earthly Powers (1980), and Any Old Iron (1989)2.  Guy. 1911–63, British spy, who fled to the Soviet Union (with Donald Maclean) in 1951
