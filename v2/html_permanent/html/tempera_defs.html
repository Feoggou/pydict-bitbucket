tempera

(ˈtɛmpərə )
Definitions
noun1. a painting medium for powdered pigments, consisting usually of egg yolk and water2. a. any emulsion used as a painting medium, with casein, glue, wax, etc, as a baseb. the paint made from mixing this with pigment3. the technique of painting with tempera
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Italian phrase pingere a tempera painting in tempera, from temperare to mingle; see temper
