foresee

(fɔːˈsiː )
Definitions
verb -sees, -seeing, -saw or -seen(tr; may take a clause as object) to see or know beforehand ⇒ he did not foresee that
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

foreseeable (foreˈseeable) adjectiveforeseer (foreˈseer) noun

foresee

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳsiː ) for learners of English
Word forms: foresees , foreseeing , foresaw , foreseen
Definitions
verbIf you foresee something, you expect and believe that it will happen. [V n] ⇒ He did not foresee any problems. [V n] ⇒ ...a dangerous situation which could have been foreseen. [V that] ⇒ He could never have foreseen that one day his books would sell in millions. [Also V wh]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
