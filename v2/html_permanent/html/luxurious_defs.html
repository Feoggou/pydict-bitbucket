luxurious

(lʌɡˈzjʊərɪəs )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by luxury2. enjoying or devoted to luxury3. an archaic word for lecherous▶ USAGE Luxurious is sometimes wrongly used where luxuriant is meant: he had a luxuriant (not luxurious) moustache; the walls were covered with a luxuriant growth of wisteria
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

luxuriously (luxˈuriously) adverbluxuriousness (luxˈuriousness) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin luxuriōsus excessive

luxurious

Powered by COBUILD
(lʌgʒʊəriəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as luxurious, you mean that it is very comfortable and expensive. ⇒ Our honeymoon was two days in Las Vegas at a luxurious hotel called Le Mirage. ⇒ She had come to enjoy Roberto's luxurious life-style.luxuriously graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The dining-room is luxuriously furnished and carpeted.2. graded adjective & adjectiveLuxurious means feeling or expressing great pleasure and comfort. ⇒ Amy tilted her wine in her glass with a luxurious sigh.luxuriously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ Liz laughed, stretching luxuriously.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
