endemic

(ɛnˈdɛmɪk )
Definitions
adjective also: endemial (ɛnˈdɛmɪəl ), endemical1. present within a localized area or peculiar to persons in such an areanoun2. an endemic disease or plant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

endemically (enˈdemically) adverbendemism (ˈendemism) or endemicity (ˌendeˈmicity) noun

Word Origin

C18: from New Latin endēmicus, from Greek endēmos native, from en-2 + dēmos the people

endemic

Powered by COBUILD
(endemɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf a disease or illness is endemic in a place, it is frequently found among the people who live there. [technical] ⇒ Polio was then endemic among children my age.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that a condition or problem is endemic, you mean that it is very common and strong, and cannot be dealt with easily. [written] ⇒ Street crime is virtually endemic in large cities. ⇒ ...powerful radicals with an endemic hatred and fear of the West.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
