opiate

Definitions
noun (ˈəʊpɪɪt )1. any of various narcotic drugs, such as morphine and heroin, that act on opioid receptors2. any other narcotic or sedative drug3. something that soothes, deadens, or induces sleepadjective (ˈəʊpɪɪt )4. containing or consisting of opium5. inducing relaxation; soporificverb (ˈəʊpɪˌeɪt ) (transitive) rare6. to treat with an opiate7. to dull or deaden
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin opiātus; from Latin opium poppy juice, opium
