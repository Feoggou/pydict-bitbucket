sordid

(ˈsɔːdɪd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. dirty, foul, or squalid2. degraded; vile; base ⇒ a sordid affair3. selfish and grasping ⇒ sordid avarice

Derived Forms

sordidly (ˈsordidly) adverbsordidness (ˈsordidness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin sordidus, from sordēre to be dirty

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as sordid, you mean that it is immoral or dishonest. [disapproval] ⇒ He sat with his head buried in his hands as his sordid double life was revealed. ⇒ I don't want to hear the sordid details of your relationship with Sandra. ⇒ She listened to Kate's explanation of the sordid affair.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a place as sordid, you mean that it is dirty, unpleasant, or depressing. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the attic windows of their sordid little rooms.
