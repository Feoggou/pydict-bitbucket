escrow

(ˈɛskrəʊ , ɛˈskrəʊ ) law
Definitions
noun1. money, goods, or a written document, such as a contract bond, delivered to a third party and held by him pending fulfilment of some condition2. the state or condition of being an escrow (esp in the phrase in escrow)verb (transitive)3. to place (money, a document, etc) in escrow
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old French escroe, of Germanic origin; see screed, shred, scroll

escrow

Powered by COBUILD
(eskroʊ ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount noun [oft N n]Escrow is money or property which is given to someone, but which is kept by another person until the first person has done a particular thing or met particular requirements. [mainly US, law] ⇒ They had $96,000 in their escrow account. ⇒ His stake has been held in escrow since the start of the year.

(ɛskroʊ) in Finance
Definitions
noun(Finance: General) Escrow is money held by an independent third party to facilitate a financial transaction between two other parties. ⇒ The remainder of the money will be held in escrow until a judge determines who is entitled to the proceeds. ⇒ A purchaser will deposit a fund into the escrow account and the money won't be released to the seller till a guaranteed delivery is made. ⇒ Escrow is money held by an independent third party to facilitate a financial transaction between two other parties.
