run into

Definitions
verb (preposition; mainly intr)1. (also tr) to collide with or cause to collide with ⇒ her car ran into a tree2. to encounter unexpectedly3. (also tr) to be beset by or cause to be beset by ⇒ the project ran into financial difficulties4. to extend to; be of the order of ⇒ debts running into thousands

run into

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you run into problems or difficulties, you unexpectedly begin to experience them. meet with [V P n] ⇒ They agreed to sell last year after they ran into financial problems. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ But the government's plans have run into strong opposition from civil rights campaigners.2. phrasal verbIf you run into someone, you meet them unexpectedly. [V P n] ⇒ He ran into Krettner in the corridor a few minutes later.3. phrasal verbIf a vehicle runs into something, it accidentally hits it. crash into, hit [V P n] ⇒ The driver failed to negotiate a bend and ran into a tree.4. phrasal verbYou use run into when indicating that the cost or amount of something is very great. [V P amount] ⇒ He said companies should face punitive civil penalties running into millions of pounds. →  run
