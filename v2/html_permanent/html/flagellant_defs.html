flagellant

(ˈflædʒɪlənt , fləˈdʒɛlənt ) or flagellator (ˈflædʒɪˌleɪtə )
Definitions
noun1. a person who whips himself or others either as part of a religious penance or for sexual gratification2. (often capital) (in medieval Europe) a member of a religious sect who whipped themselves in public
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flagellantism (ˈflagellantˌism) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin flagellāre to whip, from flagellum
