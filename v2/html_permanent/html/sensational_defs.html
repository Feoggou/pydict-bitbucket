sensational

(sɛnˈseɪʃənəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. causing or intended to cause intense feelings, esp of curiosity, horror, etc ⇒ sensational disclosures in the press2. informal extremely good ⇒ a sensational skater3. of or relating to the faculty of sensation4. philosophy of or relating to sensationalism

Derived Forms

sensationally (senˈsationally) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA sensational result, event, or situation is so remarkable that it causes great excitement and interest. ⇒ The world champions suffered a sensational defeat.sensationally graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ The rape trial was sensationally halted yesterday.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can describe stories or reports as sensational if you disapprove of them because they present facts in a way that is intended to cause feelings of shock, anger, or excitement. [disapproval] ⇒ ...sensational tabloid newspaper reports.3. adjectiveYou can describe something as sensational when you think that it is extremely good. ⇒ Her voice is sensational. ⇒ Experts agreed that this was a truly sensational performance.sensationally adverb ⇒ ...sensationally good food.
