codicil

(ˈkɒdɪsɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. law a supplement modifying a will or revoking some provision of it2. an additional provision; appendix
Derived forms
codicillary (ˌkɒdɪˈsɪlərɪ ) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin cōdicillus, literally: a little book, diminutive of codex
