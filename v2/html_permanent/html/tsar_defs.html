tsar

or czar (zɑː , tsɑː )
Definitions
noun1. (until 1917) the emperor of Russia2. a tyrant; autocrat3. informal a public official charged with responsibility for dealing with a certain problem or issue ⇒ a drugs tsar4. informal a person in authority; leader5. (formerly) any of several S Slavonic rulers, such as any of the princes of Serbia in the 14th century
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tsardom (ˈtsardom) or czardom (ˈczardom) noun

Word Origin

from Russian tsar, via Gothic kaisar from Latin Caesar
