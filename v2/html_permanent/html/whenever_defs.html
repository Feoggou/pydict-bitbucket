whenever

(wɛnˈɛvə )
Definitions
conjunction1. (subordinating) at every or any time that; when ⇒ I laugh whenever I see thatadverb also: when ever2. no matter when ⇒ it'll be here, whenever you decide to come for it3. informal at an unknown or unspecified time ⇒ I'll take it if it comes today, tomorrow, or whenever4. an intensive form of when, used in questions ⇒ whenever did he escape?

whenever for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwenevəʳ )
Definitions
1. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use whenever to refer to any time or every time that something happens or is true. ⇒ She always called at the vicarage whenever she was in the area. ⇒ You can have my cottage whenever you like. ⇒ I recommend that you avoid processed foods whenever possible.2. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use whenever to refer to a time that you do not know or are not sure about. ⇒ He married Miss Vancouver in 1963, or whenever it was.
