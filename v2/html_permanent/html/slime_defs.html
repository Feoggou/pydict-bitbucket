slime

(slaɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. soft thin runny mud or filth2. any moist viscous fluid, esp when noxious or unpleasant3. a mucous substance produced by various organisms, such as fish, slugs, and fungiverb (transitive)4. to cover with slime5. to remove slime from (fish) before canning

Word Origin

Old English slīm; related to Old Norse slīm, Old High German slīmen to smooth, Russian slimák snail, Latin līmax snail
