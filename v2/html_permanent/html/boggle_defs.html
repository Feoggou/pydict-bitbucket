boggle

(ˈbɒɡəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; often foll by at)1. to be surprised, confused, or alarmed (esp in the phrase the mind boggles)2. to hesitate or be evasive when confronted with a problem3. (transitive) to baffle; bewilder; puzzle
Word origin
C16: probably variant of bogle1

boggle

Powered by COBUILD
(bɒgəl )
Word forms: boggles , boggling , boggled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbIf you say that the mind boggles at something or that something boggles the mind, you mean that it is so strange or amazing that it is difficult to imagine or understand. [V + at] ⇒ The mind boggles at the possibilities that could be in store for us. [V] ⇒ The good grace with which they face the latest privations makes the mind boggle. [V n] ⇒ The management group's decision still boggled his mind.2. → see also mind-boggling
