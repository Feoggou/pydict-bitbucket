cellulose

(ˈsɛljʊˌləʊz , -ˌləʊs)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna polysaccharide consisting of long unbranched chains of linked glucose units: the main constituent of plant cell walls and used in making paper, rayon, and film
Derived forms
ˌcellulosic (ˌcelluˈlosic) adjective, noun
Word origin
C18: from French cellule cell (see cellule) + -ose2
