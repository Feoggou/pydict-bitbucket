mantis

(ˈmæntɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tises or -tes (-tiːz)any carnivorous typically green insect of the family Mantidae, of warm and tropical regions, having a long body and large eyes and resting with the first pair of legs raised as if in prayer: order Dictyoptera. Also called: praying mantis See also cockroach

Word Origin

C17: New Latin, from Greek: prophet, alluding to its praying posture
