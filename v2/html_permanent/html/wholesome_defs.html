wholesome

(ˈhəʊlsəm )
Definitions
adjective1. conducive to health or physical wellbeing2. conducive to moral wellbeing3. characteristic or suggestive of health or wellbeing, esp in appearance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wholesomely (ˈwholesomely) adverbwholesomeness (ˈwholesomeness) noun

Word Origin

C12: from whole (healthy) + -some1; related to German heilsam healing

wholesome for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hoʊlsəm )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as wholesome, you approve of it because you think it is likely to have a positive influence on people's behaviour or mental state, especially because it does not involve anything sexually immoral. [approval] OPPOSITE: unwholesome ⇒ ...good, wholesome fun. ⇒ ...a very decent and wholesome bunch of lads.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe food as wholesome, you approve of it because you think it is good for your health. [approval] ⇒ ...fresh, wholesome ingredients. ⇒ The food is filling and wholesome.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
