emission

(ɪˈmɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of emitting or sending forth2. energy, in the form of heat, light, radio waves, etc, emitted from a source3. a substance, fluid, etc, that is emitted; discharge4. a measure of the number of electrons emitted by a cathode or electron gun ⇒ at 1000°C the emission is 3 mA. See also secondary emission, thermionic emission5. physiology any bodily discharge, esp an involuntary release of semen during sleep6. an issue, as of currency
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

emissive (eˈmissive) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ēmissiō, from ēmittere to send forth, emit
