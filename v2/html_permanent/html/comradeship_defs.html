comradeship

Powered by COBUILD
(kɒmreɪdʃɪp , US -ræd- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounComradeship is friendship between a number of people who are doing the same work or who share the same difficulties or dangers. [+ of] ⇒ ...the comradeship of his fellow soldiers.
Example sentences containing 'comradeship'
Experience had taught him that, when in a hole, what is needed is a sense of comradeship. Salley Vickers MR GOLIGHTLY'S HOLIDAYThere was comfort in comradeship , the young man's company was agreeable and another pint would hardly detain him for long. Salley Vickers MR GOLIGHTLY'S HOLIDAYThe comradeship which springs up between travellers everywhere enveloped them. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFE
