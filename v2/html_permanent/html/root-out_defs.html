root out

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you root out a person, you find them and force them from the place they are in, usually in order to punish them. flush out [V P n] ⇒ The generals have to root out traitors. [V n P] ⇒ It shouldn't take too long to root him out.2. phrasal verbIf you root out a problem or an unpleasant situation, you find out who or what is the cause of it and put an end to it. [V P n] ⇒ There would be a major drive to root out corruption. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Any sort of wrong-doing had to be rooted out. [Also V n P] →  root
