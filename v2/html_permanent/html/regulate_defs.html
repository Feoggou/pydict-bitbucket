regulate

(ˈrɛɡjʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to adjust (the amount of heat, sound, etc, of something) as required; control2. to adjust (an instrument or appliance) so that it operates correctly3. to bring into conformity with a rule, principle, or usage
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

regulative (ˈregulative) or regulatory (ˈregulatory) adjectiveregulatively (ˈregulatively) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin rēgulāre to control, from Latin rēgula a ruler
