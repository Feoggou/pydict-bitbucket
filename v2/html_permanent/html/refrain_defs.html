refrain1

(rɪˈfreɪn )
Definitions
verb(intransitive; usually foll by from) to abstain (from action); forbear
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

refrainer (reˈfrainer) nounrefrainment (reˈfrainment) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin refrēnāre to check with a bridle, from re- + frēnum a bridle

refrain2

(rɪˈfreɪn )
Definitions
noun1. a regularly recurring melody, such as the chorus of a song2. a much repeated saying or idea
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French, ultimately from Latin refringere to break into pieces

refrain

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪfreɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: refrains , refraining , refrained
Definitions
1. verbIf you refrain from doing something, you deliberately do not do it. [V + from] ⇒ Mrs Hardie refrained from making any comment. [V from -ing/n] ⇒ He appealed to all factions to refrain from violence.2. countable nounA refrain is a short, simple part of a song, which is repeated many times. ⇒ ...a refrain from an old song.3. countable nounA refrain is a comment or saying that people often repeat. ⇒ Rosa's constant refrain is that she doesn't have a life.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
