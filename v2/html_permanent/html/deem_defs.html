deem

(diːm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to judge or consider ⇒ I do not deem him worthy of this honour
Word origin
Old English dēman; related to Old High German tuomen to judge, Gothic domjan; see doom

Powered by COBUILD
(diːm )
Word forms: deems , deeming , deemed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something is deemed to have a particular quality or to do a particular thing, it is considered to have that quality or do that thing. [formal] [be V-ed adj/n] ⇒ French and German were deemed essential. [V n adj/n] ⇒ He says he would support the use of force if the U.N. deemed it necessary. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ I was deemed to be a competent shorthand typist. [Also V n to-inf]
