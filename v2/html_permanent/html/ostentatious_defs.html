ostentatious for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒstenteɪʃəs )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as ostentatious, you disapprove of it because it is expensive and is intended to impress people. [formal, disapproval] showy ⇒ ...his house, which, however elaborate, is less ostentatious than the preserves of other Dallas tycoons. ⇒ ...an ostentatious wedding reception.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as ostentatious, you disapprove of them because they want to impress people with their wealth or importance. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ Obviously he had plenty of money and was generous in its use without being ostentatious. ⇒ She's got a lovely way with language without ever sounding ostentatious.ostentatiously graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Her servants were similarly, if less ostentatiously attired.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can describe an action or behaviour as ostentatious when it is done in an exaggerated way to attract people's attention. ⇒ His wife was fairly quiet but she is not an ostentatious person anyway.ostentatiously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ Harry stopped under a street lamp and ostentatiously began inspecting the contents of his bag.
