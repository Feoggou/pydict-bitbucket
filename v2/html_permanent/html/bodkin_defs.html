bodkin

(ˈbɒdkɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a blunt large-eyed needle used esp for drawing tape through openwork2. archaic a dagger3. printing a pointed steel tool used for extracting characters when correcting metal type4. archaic a long ornamental hairpin
Word origin
C14: probably of Celtic origin; compare Gaelic biodag dagger
Example sentences containing 'bodkin'
In that case the Vicar was a bodkin and his wife a bouncy ball. Secombe, Fred GOODBYE CURATEIt was a hunting arrow, narrow-headed, not an armor-punching bodkin. Garth Nix SABRIEL
