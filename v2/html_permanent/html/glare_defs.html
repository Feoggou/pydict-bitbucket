glare1

(ɡlɛə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to stare angrily; glower2. (transitive) to express by glowering3. (intransitive) (of light, colour, etc) to be very bright and intense4. (intransitive) to be dazzlingly ornamented or garishnoun5. an angry stare6. a dazzling light or brilliance7. garish ornamentation or appearance; gaudiness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

glareless (ˈglareless) adjectiveglary (ˈglary) adjective

Word Origin

C13: probably from Middle Low German, Middle Dutch glaren to gleam; probably related to Old English glæren glassy; see glass

glare

Powered by COBUILD
(gleəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: glares , glaring , glared
Definitions
1. verbIf you glare at someone, you look at them with an angry expression on your face. [V + at] ⇒ The old woman glared at him. [V] ⇒ Jacob glared and muttered something. [V-ing] ⇒ ...glaring eyes.2. countable nounA glare is an angry, hard, and unfriendly look. ⇒ His glasses magnified his irritable glare.3. verbIf the sun or a light glares, it shines with a very bright light which is difficult to look at. [V] ⇒ The sunlight glared. [V-ing] ⇒ ...glaring searchlight beams.4. uncount nounGlare is very bright light that is difficult to look at. ⇒ ...the glare of a car's headlights. ⇒ Special-purpose glasses reduce glare.5. singular nounIf someone is in the glare of publicity or public attention, they are constantly being watched and talked about by a lot of people. ⇒ Norma is said to dislike the glare of publicity. ⇒ She attacked police in the full glare of TV cameras.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
