mutter

(ˈmʌtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to utter (something) in a low and indistinct tone2. (intransitive) to grumble or complain3. (intransitive) to make a low continuous murmuring soundnoun4. a muttered sound or complaint

Derived Forms

mutterer (ˈmutterer) nounmuttering (ˈmuttering) noun, adjectivemutteringly (ˈmutteringly) adverb

Word Origin

C14 moteren; related to Norwegian (dialect) mutra, Old High German mutilōn; compare Old English mōtian to speak

mutter

Powered by COBUILD
(mʌtəʳ )
Word forms: mutters , muttering , muttered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you mutter, you speak very quietly so that you cannot easily be heard, often because you are complaining about something. [V with quote] ⇒ 'God knows what's happening in that madman's mind,' she muttered. [V + about] ⇒ She can hear the old woman muttering about consideration. [V + to] ⇒ He sat there shaking his head, muttering to himself. [V] ⇒ She was staring into the fire muttering.Mutter is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ They make no more than a mutter of protest.muttering Word forms: mutterings variable noun ⇒ He heard muttering from the front of the crowd. ⇒ He will lead the party into the election, in spite of the mutterings about his leadership.
Example sentences containing 'mutter'
They dropped me off at my gate, and I managed to mutter thank you. Kate Cann READY?Gollum often paused, sniffing the air, and then he would mutter to himself and urge them to greater speed. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSThe only sound to break the silence of the night was the soft mutter of my engine. Val McDermid DEAD BEAT
