scold

(skəʊld )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to find fault with or reprimand (a person) harshly; chide2. (intransitive) to use harsh or abusive languagenoun3. a person, esp a woman, who constantly finds fault
Derived forms
scoldable (ˈscoldable) adjectivescolder (ˈscolder) nounscolding (ˈscolding) nounscoldingly (ˈscoldingly) adverb
Word origin
C13: from Old Norse skald

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you scold someone, you speak angrily to them because they have done something wrong. [formal] [V n] ⇒ If he finds out, he'll scold me. [V n + for] ⇒ Later she scolded her daughter for having talked to her father like that. [V with quote] ⇒ 'You should be at school,' he scolded. [Also V]
