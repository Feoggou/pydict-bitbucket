tootle

Powered by COBUILD
(tuːtəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: tootles , tootling , tootled
Definitions
1. verbIf you tootle somewhere, you travel or go there without rushing or without any particular aim. [mainly British, informal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ I'm sure Ted is tootling down the motorway at this very moment.2. verbIf you tootle a tune on an instrument, you play it quietly, without concentrating or taking it seriously. [informal] [V n] ⇒ McCann tootled a tune on the piano. [Also V]
