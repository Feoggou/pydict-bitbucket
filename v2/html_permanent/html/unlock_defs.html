unlock

(ʌnˈlɒk )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to unfasten (a lock, door, etc)2. (transitive) to open, release, or let loose3. (transitive) to disclose or provide the key to ⇒ unlock a puzzle4. (intransitive) to become unlocked
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unlockable (unˈlockable) adjective

unlock for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnlɒk )
Word forms: unlocks , unlocking , unlocked
Definitions
1. verbIf you unlock something such as a door, a room, or a container that has a lock, you open it using a key. [V n] ⇒ He unlocked the car and threw the coat on to the back seat. [V n] ⇒ She unlocked the case and carefully lifted out the vase.2. verbIf you unlock the potential or the secrets of something or someone, you release them. [V n] ⇒ Education and training is the key that will unlock our nation's potential. [V n] ⇒ ...the man who dedicated his life to unlocking the secrets of the universe.
