5. Baker Day (British). A former name for the Inset day. A day when school teachers in England, Wales and Northern Ireland carry out administration duties or undertake training. Pupils are not required to attend school on these days. It is humorously named after Kenneth Baker, Secretary of State for Education, who introduced the concept in 1988.
