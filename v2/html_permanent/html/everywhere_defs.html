Definitions
1. indefinite adverb & adverb [n ADV] [ADV after v, be ADV, oft from ADV]You use everywhere to refer to a whole area or to all the places in a particular area. ⇒ Working people everywhere object to paying taxes. ⇒ We went everywhere together. ⇒ Dust is everywhere. ⇒ Tap water is drinkable everywhere in the Algarve. ⇒ People come here from everywhere to see these lights.2. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV after v]You use everywhere to refer to all the places that someone goes to. ⇒ Bradley is still accustomed to travelling everywhere in style. ⇒ Everywhere he went he was introduced as the current United States Open Champion.3. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV after v] [be ADV]You use everywhere to emphasize that you are talking about a large number of places, or all possible places. [emphasis] ⇒ I saw her picture everywhere. ⇒ I looked everywhere. I couldn't find him.4. indefinite adverb & adverb [be ADV, ADV after v]If you say that someone or something is everywhere, you mean that they are present in a place in very large numbers. ⇒ There were cartons of cigarettes everywhere. ⇒ Clothes were everywhere; hanging out of drawers, strewn across the floor, even spilling from the wastepaper basket.
