RMS

Powered by COBUILD
(ɑr ɛm ɛs) or root-mean-square in Electrical Engineering
Definitions
abbreviation(Electrical engineering: Circuits, Electrical power, Computing and control) The RMS value of a periodic waveform is the same as the effective value of the waveform, and is obtained by taking the square root of the mean of the squared waveform. ⇒ Anytime you hear about a voltage value for your house wiring or somewhere on the power grid, you can safely assume the value is in RMS form. ⇒ The RMS voltage is proportional to the temperature of the resistor and how resistive it is. ⇒ The RMS value of a periodic waveform is the same as the effective value of the waveform, and is obtained by taking the square root of the mean of the squared waveform.
