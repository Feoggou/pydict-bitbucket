fall in

Definitions
verb (intr, adverb)1. to collapse; no longer act as a support2. to adopt a military formation, esp as a soldier taking his place in a line3. (of a lease) to expire4. (of land) to come into the owner's possession on the expiry of the lease5. (often foll by with)a. to meet and joinb. to agree with or support a person, suggestion, etc6. Australian and New Zealand to make a mistake or come to grief7. New Zealand to become pregnantsentence substitute8. the order to adopt a military formation

fall in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf a roof or ceiling falls in, it collapses and falls to the ground. [V P] ⇒ Part of my bedroom ceiling has fallen in.2. phrasal verbIf you fall in behind or beside someone who is walking along, you start walking behind them or beside them. [V P behind/beside n] ⇒ Prentice saw Goss fall in behind the informer. →  fall
