bayonet

(ˈbeɪənɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a blade that can be attached to the muzzle of a rifle for stabbing in close combat2. a type of fastening in which a cylindrical member is inserted into a socket against spring pressure and turned so that pins on its side engage in slots in the socketverb -nets, -neting, -neted, -nets, -netting or -netted3. (transitive) to stab or kill with a bayonet
Word origin
C17: from French baïonnette, from Bayonne where it originated
