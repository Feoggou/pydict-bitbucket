intrigue

Definitions
verb (ɪnˈtriːɡ ) -trigues, -triguing or -trigued1. (transitive) to make interested or curious ⇒ I'm intrigued by this case, Watson2. (intransitive) to make secret plots or employ underhand methods; conspire3. (intransitive; often foll by with) to carry on a clandestine love affairnoun (ɪnˈtriːɡ , ˈɪntriːɡ )4. the act or an instance of secret plotting, etc5. a clandestine love affair6. the quality of arousing interest or curiosity; beguilement
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intriguer (inˈtriguer) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French intriguer, from Italian intrigare, from Latin intrīcāre; see intricate

Definitions
pronunciation note:   The noun is pronounced (ɪntriːg ). The verb is pronounced (ɪntriːg ).1. variable nounIntrigue is the making of secret plans to harm or deceive people. ⇒ ...political intrigue. ⇒ ...a powerful story of intrigue, passion and betrayal. ⇒ ...the plots and intrigues in the novel.2. verbIf something, especially something strange, intrigues you, it interests you and you want to know more about it. [V n] ⇒ The novelty of the situation intrigued him.
