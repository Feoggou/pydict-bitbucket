frantic

(ˈfræntɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. distracted with fear, pain, joy, etc2. marked by or showing frenzy ⇒ frantic efforts3. archaic insane
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frantically (ˈfrantically) or franticly (ˈfranticly) adverbfranticness (ˈfranticness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French frenetique, from Latin phrenēticus mad, frenetic

(fræntɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are frantic, you are behaving in a wild and uncontrolled way because you are frightened or worried. frenzied ⇒ A bird had been locked in and was by now quite frantic.frantically (fræntɪkli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ She clutched frantically at Emily's arm.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf an activity is frantic, things are done quickly and in an energetic but disorganized way, because there is very little time. ⇒ A busy night in the restaurant can be frantic in the kitchen.frantically graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ We have been frantically trying to save her life.
