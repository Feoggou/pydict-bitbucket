outdo

(ˌaʊtˈduː )
Definitions
verb -does, -doing, -did or -done(transitive) to surpass or exceed in performance or execution

outdo for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtduː )
Word forms: outdoes , outdoing , outdid , outdone
Definitions
1. verbIf you outdo someone, you are a lot more successful than they are at a particular activity. [V n] ⇒ It was important for me to outdo them, to feel better than they were. [V n] ⇒ Both sides have tried to outdo each other to show how tough they can be.2. →  not to be outdone
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
