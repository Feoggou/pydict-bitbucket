prestige

(prɛˈstiːʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. high status or reputation achieved through success, influence, wealth, etc; renown2. a. the power to influence or impress; glamourb. (modifier) ⇒ a prestige car

Word Origin

C17: via French from Latin praestigiae feats of juggling, tricks; apparently related to Latin praestringere to bind tightly, blindfold, from prae before + stringere to draw tight, bind

prestige

Powered by COBUILD
(prestiːʒ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounIf a person, a country, or an organization has prestige, they are admired and respected because of the position they hold or the things they have achieved. ⇒ ...efforts to build up the prestige of the United Nations. ⇒ It was his responsibility for foreign affairs that gained him international prestige. ⇒ ...high prestige jobs.2. adjective [ADJ n]Prestige is used to describe products, places, or activities which people admire because they are associated with being rich or having a high social position. ⇒ ...such prestige cars as Cadillac, Mercedes, Porsche and Jaguar.
