Definitions

empower

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmpaʊəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: empowers , empowering , empowered
Definitions
1. verbIf someone is empowered to do something, they have the authority or power to do it. [formal] [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ The army is now empowered to operate on a shoot-to-kill basis. [V n to-inf] ⇒ His position does not empower him to cite our views without consultation. [Also V n]2. verbTo empower someone means to give them the means to achieve something, for example to become stronger or more successful. [V n] ⇒ Empowering the underprivileged lies in assuring them that education holds the real source of power. [V n] ⇒ What I'm trying to do is to empower people, to give them ways to help them get well.
