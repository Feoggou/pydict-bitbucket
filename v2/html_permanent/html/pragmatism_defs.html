pragmatism

(ˈpræɡməˌtɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. action or policy dictated by consideration of the immediate practical consequences rather than by theory or dogma2. philosophya. the doctrine that the content of a concept consists only in its practical applicabilityb. the doctrine that truth consists not in correspondence with the facts but in successful coherence with experience. See also instrumentalism

Derived Forms

pragmatist (ˈpragmatist) noun, adjectiveˌpragmatistic (ˌpragmaˈtistic) adjective

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounPragmatism means thinking of or dealing with problems in a practical way, rather than by using theory or abstract principles. [formal] ⇒ She had a reputation for clear thinking and pragmatism.pragmatist Word forms: pragmatists countable noun ⇒ He is a political pragmatist, not an idealist.
