succumb

(səˈkʌm )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; often foll by to)1. to give way in face of the overwhelming force (of) or desire (for)2. to be fatally overwhelmed (by disease, old age, etc); die (of)

Derived Forms

succumber (sucˈcumber) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin succumbere to be overcome, from sub- + -cumbere from cubāre to lie down

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you succumb to temptation or pressure, you do something that you want to do, or that other people want you to do, although you feel it might be wrong. [formal] [V + to] ⇒ Don't succumb to the temptation to have just one cigarette. [V + to] ⇒ The Minister said his country would never succumb to pressure.2. verbIf you succumb to an illness, you become affected by it or die from it. [formal] [V to n] ⇒ A few years later, Katya succumbed to cancer in London. [V to n] ⇒ I was determined not to succumb to the virus.
Example sentences containing 'succumb'
Luckily, Sharon didn't succumb to one of her odd reveries this time. Laura Lippman EVERY SECRET THING
