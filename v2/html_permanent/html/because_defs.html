because

(bɪˈkɒz , -ˈkəz)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
conjunction1. (subordinating) on account of the fact that; on account of being; since ⇒ because it's so cold we'll go home2. See because ofSee note at reason
Word origin
C14 bi cause, from bi by + cause

(bɪkɒz , US bɪkɔːz )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use because when stating the reason for something. ⇒ He is called Mitch, because his name is Mitchell. ⇒ Because it is an area of outstanding natural beauty, you can't build on it. ⇒ Women are doing the job well. This is partly because women are increasingly moving into a man's world. ⇒ 'Why didn't you tell me, Archie?'—'Because you might have casually mentioned it to somebody else.'2. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use because when stating the explanation for a statement you have just made. ⇒ Maybe they just didn't want to ask too many questions, because they rented us a room without even asking to see our papers. ⇒ The President has played a shrewd diplomatic game because from the outset he called for direct talks with the United States. ⇒ I had a sense of déjà vu because I could recognise everything in London.3. →  because of4. →  just because
