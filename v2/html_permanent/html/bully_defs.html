bully1

(ˈbʊlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -lies1. a person who hurts, persecutes, or intimidates weaker people2. archaic a hired ruffian3. obsolete a procurer; pimp4. obsolete a fine fellow or friend5. obsolete a sweetheart; darlingverb -lies, -lying or -lied6. (when tr, often foll by into) to hurt, intimidate, or persecute (a weaker or smaller person), esp to make him or her do somethingadjective7. dashing; jolly ⇒ my bully boy8. informal very good; fineexclamation9. Also: bully for you informal well done! bravo!
Word origin
C16 (in the sense: sweetheart, hence fine fellow, hence swaggering coward): probably from Middle Dutch boele lover, from Middle High German buole, perhaps childish variant of bruoder brother

bully

Powered by COBUILD
(bʊli )
Word forms: bullies , bullying , bullied
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA bully is someone who uses their strength or power to hurt or frighten other people. ⇒ I fell victim to the office bully. ⇒ He's a coward and a bully who confuses physical strength with manhood.2. verbIf someone bullies you, they use their strength or power to hurt or frighten you. [V n] ⇒ I wasn't going to let him bully me. [V n] ⇒ I asked her if she was bullied by the other children.bullying uncount noun ⇒ ...schoolchildren who were victims of bullying.3. verbIf someone bullies you into something, they make you do it by using force or threats. [V n + into] ⇒ We think an attempt to bully them into submission would be counterproductive. [V n + into] ⇒ She used to bully me into doing my schoolwork. [be V-ed] ⇒ The government says it will not be bullied by the press. [Also V n]
