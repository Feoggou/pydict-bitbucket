shorten

(ˈʃɔːtən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make or become short or shorter2. (transitive) nautical to reduce the area of (sail)3. (transitive) to make (pastry, bread, etc) short, by adding butter or another fat4. gambling to cause (the odds) to lessen or (of odds) to become less
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbIf you shorten an event or the length of time that something lasts, or if it shortens, it does not last as long as it would otherwise do or as it used to do. [V n] ⇒ Smoking can shorten your life. [V n] ⇒ The trading day is shortened in observance of the Labor Day holiday. [V] ⇒ When the days shorten in winter some people suffer depression.2. ergative verb & verbIf you shorten an object or if it shortens, it becomes smaller in length. [V n] ⇒ Her father paid £1,000 for an operation to shorten her nose. [V] ⇒ As they shorten, cells become more prone to disease and death.3. verbIf you shorten a name or other word, you change it by removing some of the letters. [V n] ⇒ Originally called Lili, she eventually shortened her name to Lee.4. → to shorten the odds
