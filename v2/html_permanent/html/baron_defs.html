baron

(ˈbærən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a member of a specific rank of nobility, esp the lowest rank in the British Isles2. (in Europe from the Middle Ages) originally any tenant-in-chief of a king or other overlord, who held land from his superior by honourable service; a land-holding nobleman3. a powerful businessman or financier ⇒ a press baron4. English law (formerly) the title held by judges of the Court of Exchequer5. short for baron of beef
Word origin
C12: from Old French, of Germanic origin; compare Old High German baro freeman, Old Norse berjask to fight
