apostrophe1

(əˈpɒstrəfɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe punctuation mark ' used to indicate the omission of a letter or number, such as he's for he has or he is, also used in English to form the possessive, as in John's father and twenty pounds' worth
Word origin
C17: from Late Latin, from Greek apostrophos mark of elision, from apostrephein to turn away

apostrophe

Powered by COBUILD
(əpɒstrəfi )
Word forms: apostrophes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounAn apostrophe is the mark ' when it is written to indicate that one or more letters have been left out of a word, as in 'isn't' and 'we'll'. It is also added to nouns to form possessives, as in 'Mike's car'.
