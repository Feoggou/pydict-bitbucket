was

(wɒz , unstressed wəz )
Definitions
verb1. the past tense (indicative mood) of be12. not standard a form of the subjunctive mood used in place of were, esp in conditional sentences ⇒ if the film was to be with you, would you be able to process it?
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English wæs, from wesan to be; related to Old Frisian, Old High German was, Old Norse var
