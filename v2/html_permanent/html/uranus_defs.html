Uranus1

(jʊˈreɪnəs , ˈjʊrənəs )
Definitions
nounGreek mythology the personification of the sky, who, as a god, ruled the universe and fathered the Titans and Cyclopes on his wife and mother Gaea (earth). He was overthrown by his son Cronus
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Uranus2

(jʊˈreɪnəs , ˈjʊrənəs )
Definitions
nounone of the giant planets, the seventh planet from the sun, sometimes visible to the naked eye. It has 27 satellites, a ring system, and an axis of rotation almost lying in the plane of the orbit. Mean distance from sun: 2870 million km; period of revolution around sun: 84 years; period of axial rotation: 17.23 hours; diameter and mass: 4 and 14.5 times that of earth respectively
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Latin Ūranus, from Greek Ouranos heaven
