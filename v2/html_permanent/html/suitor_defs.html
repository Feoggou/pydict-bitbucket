suitor

(ˈsuːtə , ˈsjuːt-)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a man who courts a woman; wooer2. law a person who brings a suit in a court of law; plaintiff3. rare a person who makes a request or appeal for anything

Word Origin

C13: from Anglo-Norman suter, from Latin secūtor follower, from sequī to follow

Word forms: suitors
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA woman's suitor is a man who wants to marry her. [old-fashioned] ⇒ My mother had a suitor who adored her.2. countable nounA suitor is a company or organization that wants to buy another company. [business] ⇒ The company was making little progress in trying to find a suitor. ⇒ Whatever is offered by the bank is unlikely to be improved on by any rival suitor.
