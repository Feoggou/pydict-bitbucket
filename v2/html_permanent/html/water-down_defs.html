water down

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. to dilute or weaken with water2. to modify or adulterate, esp so as to omit anything harsh, unpleasant, or offensive ⇒ to water down the truth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

watered-down (ˌwatered-ˈdown) adjective

water down for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you water down a substance, for example food or drink, you add water to it to make it weaker. [V P n] ⇒ You can water down a glass of wine and make it last twice as long. [V n P] ⇒ I bought a water-based paint, then decided to water it down even more.2. verbIf something such as a proposal, speech, or statement is watered down, it is made much weaker and less forceful, or less likely to make people angry. [be V-ed P] ⇒ Proposed legislation affecting bird-keepers has been watered down.3. → See also watered-down →  water
