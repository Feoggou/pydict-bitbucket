termagant

(ˈtɜːməɡənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna. a shrewish woman; scoldb. (as modifier) ⇒ a termagant woman

Derived Forms

termagancy (ˈtermagancy) nountermagantly (ˈtermagantly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from earlier Tervagaunt, from Old French Tervagan, from Italian Trivigante; after an arrogant character in medieval mystery plays who was supposed to be a Muslim deity
Example sentences containing 'termagant'
Take this redhead, for instance, this termagant , this copper-headed Amazon, this female dragon in angel's clothing. Maclean, Alistair THE LONELY SEAHe remembered her well, all smiling grace and amiable goodness, obedient and mild... and somehow she'd become this termagant. Christina Dodd THE RUNAWAY PRINCESS
