predominate

(prɪˈdɒmɪˌneɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive; often foll by over) to have power, influence, or control2. (intransitive) to prevail or preponderate3. (transitive) rare to dominate or have control overadjective (prɪˈdɒmɪnɪt )4. another word for predominant

Derived Forms

predominately (preˈdominately) adverbpreˌdomination (preˌdomiˈnation) nounpredomiˌnator (preˈdomiˌnator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin praedominārī, from Latin prae before + dominārī to bear rule, domineer

Word forms: predominates , predominating , predominated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf one type of person or thing predominates in a group, there is more of that type of person or thing in the group than of any other. [formal] [V] ⇒ In older age groups women predominate because men tend to die younger. [V] ⇒ All nationalities were represented–but the English predominated.2. verbWhen a feature or quality predominates, it is the most important or noticeable one in a situation. [formal] [V] ⇒ He wants to create a society where Islamic principles predominate.
