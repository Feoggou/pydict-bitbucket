entangled

(ɪnˈtæŋɡəld)
Definitions
adjective1. caught in or as if in a tangle ⇒ Dolphins became entangled in the nets., ⇒ He became entangled in some ropes and was strangled., ⇒ a whale that became entangled in crab nets2. involved in an undesirable situation from which it is difficult to escape ⇒ entangled in crime, ⇒ Our country became increasingly entangled in the complicated civil war in the Congo., ⇒ This case was bound to get entangled in international politics.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

entangled

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntæŋgəld ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is entangled in something such as a rope, wire, or net, it is caught in it very firmly. [+ in/with] ⇒ ...a whale that became entangled in crab nets.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you become entangled in problems or difficulties, you become involved in problems or difficulties from which it is hard to escape. [+ in/with] ⇒ This case was bound to get entangled in international politics.
