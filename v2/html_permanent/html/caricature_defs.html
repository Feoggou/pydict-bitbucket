caricature

(ˈkærɪkəˌtjʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a pictorial, written, or acted representation of a person, which exaggerates his or her characteristic traits for comic effect2. a ludicrously inadequate or inaccurate imitation ⇒ he is a caricature of a statesmanverb3. (transitive) to represent in caricature or produce a caricature of
Derived forms
caricaˌtural (ˈcaricaˌtural) adjectivecaricaˌturist (ˈcaricaˌturist) noun
Word origin
C18: from Italian caricatura a distortion, exaggeration, from caricare to load, exaggerate; see cargo

Word forms: caricatures , caricaturing , caricatured
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA caricature of someone is a drawing or description of them that exaggerates their appearance or behaviour in a humorous or critical way. [+ of] ⇒ The poster showed a caricature of Hitler with a devil's horns and tail. ⇒ The Spanish Steps were crowded with the sellers of grotesque caricatures.2. verbIf you caricature someone, you draw or describe them in an exaggerated way in order to be humorous or critical. [be V-ed] ⇒ Her political career has been caricatured in headlines. [be V-ed + as] ⇒ He was caricatured as a turnip. [Also V n as n]3. countable nounIf you describe something as a caricature of an event or situation, you mean that it is a very exaggerated account of it. [disapproval] [+ of] ⇒ Hall is angry at what he sees as a caricature of the training offered to modern-day social workers.
