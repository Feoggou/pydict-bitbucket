gout

(ɡaʊt )
Definitions
noun1. a metabolic disease characterized by painful inflammation of certain joints, esp of the big toe and foot, caused by deposits of sodium urate in them2. archaic a drop or splash, esp of blood
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gouty (ˈgouty) adjectivegoutily (ˈgoutily) adverbgoutiness (ˈgoutiness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French goute gout (thought to result from drops of humours), from Latin gutta a drop
