separation

(ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of separating or state of being separated2. the place or line where a separation is made3. a gap that separates4. family law the cessation of cohabitation of a married couple, either by mutual agreement or under a decree of a court. Compare judicial separation, divorce5. a. the act of jettisoning a burnt-out stage of a multistage rocketb. the instant at which such a stage is jettisoned

(sepəreɪʃən )
Word forms: separations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounThe separation of two or more things or groups is the fact that they are separate or become separate, and are not linked. ⇒ He believes in the separation of the races. [+ between] ⇒ ...a 'Christian republic' in which there was a clear separation between church and state.2. variable nounDuring a separation, people who usually live together are not together. ⇒ She wondered if Harry had been unfaithful to her during this long separation. [+ from] ⇒ All children will tend to suffer from separation from their parents and siblings.3. variable nounIf a couple who are married or living together have a separation, they decide to live apart. ⇒ They agreed to a trial separation. ⇒ ...loss of a loved one through death, separation or divorce.
