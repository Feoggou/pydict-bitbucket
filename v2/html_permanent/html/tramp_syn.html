Synonyms of tramp

tramp

verb

  1. trudge, march, stamp, stump, toil, plod, traipse (informal), walk heavily
    • They put on their coats and tramped through the fallen snow.
  2. hike, walk, trek, roam, march, range, ramble, slog, rove, go walkabout (Australian), yomp, footslog
    • He spent a month tramping in the hills around Balmoral.
  3. trample, stamp, crush, tread, stomp (informal), walk over

noun

  1. vagrant, bum (informal), derelict, drifter, down-and-out, hobo (mainly US), vagabond, bag lady (mainly US), dosser (British, slang), derro (Australian, slang)
    • an old tramp who slept rough in our neighbourhood
  2. tread, stamp, footstep, footfall
    • the slow, heavy tramp of feet on the staircase
  3. hike, march, trek, ramble, slog
    • He had just come from a day-long tramp on some wild moor.
  4. (slang mainly US & Canadian) slut, tart, slag (British, slang), slapper (British, slang), scrubber (British & & Australian, slang), trollop, sloven, slattern, hornbag (Australian, slang)
    • Look at her. She's a tramp, getting undressed with the shades open.
