palaver

(pəˈlɑːvə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. tedious or time-consuming business, esp when of a formal nature ⇒ all the palaver of filling in forms2. loud and confused talk and activity; hubbub3. (often used humorously) a conference4. rare talk intended to flatter or persuade5. West Africaa. an argumentb. trouble arising from an argumentverb6. (intransitive) (often used humorously) to have a conference7. (intransitive) to talk loudly and confusedly8. (transitive) to flatter or cajole

Word Origin

C18: from Portuguese palavra talk, from Latin parabola parable
