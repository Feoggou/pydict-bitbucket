misread

(ˌmɪsˈriːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -reads, -reading or -read (-ˈrɛd) (transitive)1. to read incorrectly2. to misinterpret

Word forms: misreads , misreading
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The form misread is used in the present tense, and is the past tense and past participle, when it is pronounced (mɪsred ).1. verbIf you misread a situation or someone's behaviour, you do not understand it properly. [V n] ⇒ The government largely misread the mood of the electorate. [V n] ⇒ Mothers may also misread signals and think the baby is crying because he is hungry.misreading Word forms: misreadings countable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...a misreading of opinion in France.2. verbIf you misread something that has been written or printed, you look at it and think that it says something that it does not say. [V n] ⇒ His chauffeur misread his route and took a wrong turning.
