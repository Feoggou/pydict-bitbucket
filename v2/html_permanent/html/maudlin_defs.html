maudlin

(ˈmɔːdlɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivefoolishly tearful or sentimental, as when drunk

Derived Forms

maudlinism (ˈmaudlinism) nounmaudlinly (ˈmaudlinly) adverbmaudlinness (ˈmaudlinness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Middle English Maudelen Mary Magdalene, typically portrayed as a tearful penitent

maudlin

Powered by COBUILD
(mɔːdlɪn )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as maudlin, you mean that they are being sad and sentimental in a foolish way, perhaps because of drinking alcohol. ⇒ Jimmy turned maudlin after three drinks. ⇒ ...maudlin self-pity.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a song, book, or film as maudlin, you are criticizing it for being very sentimental. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the most maudlin song of all time. ⇒ ...a hugely entertaining (if over-long and maudlin) movie.

Just promise me you won't get drunk and maudlin thinking about all of this.
