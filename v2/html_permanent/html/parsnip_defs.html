parsnip

(ˈpɑːsnɪp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a strong-scented umbelliferous plant, Pastinaca sativa, cultivated for its long whitish root2. the root of this plant, eaten as a vegetable3. any of several similar plants, esp the cow parsnip

Word Origin

C14: from Old French pasnaie, from Latin pastināca, from pastināre to dig, from pastinum two-pronged tool for digging; also influenced by Middle English nepe turnip
