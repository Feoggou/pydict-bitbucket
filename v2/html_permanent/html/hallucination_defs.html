hallucination

(həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən )
Definitions
nounthe alleged perception of an object when no object is present, occurring under hypnosis, in some mental disorders, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hallucinational (halˌluciˈnational), hallucinative (halˈlucinative) or hallucinatory (halˈlucinatory) adjective

hallucination

Powered by COBUILD
(həluːsɪneɪʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: hallucinations
Definitions
1. variable nounA hallucination is the experience of seeing something that is not really there because you are ill or have taken a drug. ⇒ The drug induces hallucinations at high doses. ⇒ Hallucination is common in patients who have suffered damage to the brain.2. countable nounA hallucination is something that is not real that someone sees because they are ill or have taken a drug. ⇒ Perhaps the footprint was a hallucination.
