blond

(blɒnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of men's hair) of a light colour; fair2. (of a person, people or a race) having fair hair, a light complexion, and, typically, blue or grey eyes3. (of soft furnishings, wood, etc) light in colournoun4. a person, esp a man, having light-coloured hair and skin▶ USAGE Although blond and blonde correspond to masculine and feminine forms in French, this distinction is not consistently made in English. Blonde is the commoner form both as a noun and an adjective, and is more frequently used to refer to women than men. The less common variant blond occurs usually as an adjective, occasionally as a noun, and is the preferred form when referring to men with fair hair
Derived forms
blondness (ˈblondness) noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French blond, probably of Germanic origin; related to Late Latin blundus yellow, Italian biondo, Spanish blondo
