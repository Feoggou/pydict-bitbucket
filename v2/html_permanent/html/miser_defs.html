miser1

(ˈmaɪzə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who hoards money or possessions, often living miserably2. selfish person

Word Origin

C16: from Latin: wretched

Powered by COBUILD
(maɪzəʳ )
Word forms: misers
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounIf you say that someone is a miser, you disapprove of them because they seem to hate spending money, and to spend as little as possible. [disapproval] ⇒ I'm married to a miser.
