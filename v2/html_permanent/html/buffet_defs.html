buffet1

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (ˈbʊfeɪ ) a counter where light refreshments are served2. (ˈbʊfeɪ )a. a meal at which guests help themselves from a number of dishes and often eat standing upb. (as modifier) ⇒ a buffet lunch3. (ˈbʌfɪt , ˈbʊfeɪ ) a piece of furniture used from medieval times to the 18th century for displaying plates, etc, and typically comprising one or more cupboards and some open shelves4. (ˈbʌfɪ ) Scottish and Northern England dialect a kind of low stool, pouffe, or hassock
Word origin
C18: from French, of unknown origin

buffet2

(ˈbʌfɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fets, -feting or -feted1. (transitive) to knock against or about; batter ⇒ the wind buffeted the boat2. (transitive) to hit, esp with the fist; cuff3. to force (one's way), as through a crowd4. (intransitive) to struggle; battlenoun5. a blow, esp with a fist or hand6. aerodynamic excitation of an aircraft structure by separated flows
Derived forms
buffeter (ˈbuffeter) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French buffeter, from buffet a light blow, from buffe, of imitative origin

buffet

Powered by COBUILD
Word forms: buffets, buffeting , buffeted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   Pronounced (bʌfeɪ , US bʊfeɪ ) for meanings [sense 1] to [sense 3], and (bʌfɪt ) for meanings [sense 4] and [sense 5].1. countable noun [oft N n]A buffet is a meal of cold food that is displayed on a long table at a party or public occasion. Guests usually serve themselves from the table. ⇒ ...a buffet lunch. ⇒ A cold buffet had been laid out in the dining-room.2. countable noun [oft n N]A buffet is a café, usually in a hotel or station. ⇒ We sat in the station buffet sipping tea.3. countable noun [usu sing]On a train, the buffet or the buffet car is the carriage or car where meals and snacks are sold. [British]regional note:   in AM, use dining car4. verbIf something is buffeted by strong winds or by stormy seas, it is repeatedly struck or blown around by them. [be V-ed] ⇒ Their plane had been severely buffeted by storms. [V n] ⇒ Storms swept the country, closing roads, buffeting ferries and killing as many as 30 people.buffeting Word forms: buffetings countable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the buffetings of the winds.5. verbIf an economy or government is buffeted by difficult or unpleasant situations, it experiences many of them. [be V-ed] ⇒ The whole of Africa had been buffeted by social and political upheavals.
