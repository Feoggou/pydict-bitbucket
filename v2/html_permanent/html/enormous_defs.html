enormous

(ɪˈnɔːməs )
Definitions
adjective1. unusually large in size, extent, or degree; immense; vast2. archaic extremely wicked; heinous
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

enormously (eˈnormously) adverbenormousness (eˈnormousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ēnormis, from ē- out of, away from + norma rule, pattern

enormous

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnɔːʳməs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is enormous is extremely large in size or amount. ⇒ The main bedroom is enormous. ⇒ There is, of course, an enormous amount to see.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use enormous to emphasize the great degree or extent of something. [emphasis] ⇒ It was an enormous disappointment. ⇒ ...his enormous capacity for brutality.enormously adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ This book was enormously influential. ⇒ The new database will help horse breeders enormously.
