dodge

verb

  1. duck, dart, swerve, sidestep, shoot, shift, turn aside, body-swerve (Scottish) • We dodged behind a pillar.
  2. evade, avoid, escape, get away from, elude, body-swerve (Scottish), slip through the net of • Thieves dodged the security system in the shop.
  3. avoid, hedge, parry, get out of, shun, evade, sidestep, circumvent, shirk, fend off • He has repeatedly dodged the question.

noun

  1. trick, scheme, ploy, trap, device, fraud, con (slang), manoeuvre, deception, scam (slang), gimmick, hoax, wheeze (British, slang), deceit, ruse, artifice, subterfuge, canard, feint, stratagem, contrivance, machination, fastie (Australian, slang) • It was probably just a dodge to stop you going away.
