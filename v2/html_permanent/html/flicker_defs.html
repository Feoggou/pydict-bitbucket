flicker1

(ˈflɪkə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to shine with an unsteady or intermittent light ⇒ a candle flickers2. (intransitive) to move quickly to and fro; quiver, flutter, or vibrate3. (transitive) to cause to flickernoun4. an unsteady or brief light or flame5. a swift quivering or fluttering movement6. a visual sensation, often seen in a television image, produced by periodic fluctuations in the brightness of light at a frequency below that covered by the persistence of vision7. See the flickers
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flickeringly (ˈflickeringly) adverbflickery (ˈflickery) adjective

Word Origin

Old English flicorian; related to Dutch flikkeren, Old Norse flökra to flutter

flicker2

(ˈflɪkə )
Definitions
nounany North American woodpecker of the genus Colaptes, esp C. auratus (yellow-shafted flicker), which has a yellow undersurface to the wings and tail
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: perhaps imitative of the bird's call

flicker

Powered by COBUILD
(flɪkəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: flickers , flickering , flickered
Definitions
1. verbIf a light or flame flickers, it shines unsteadily. [V] ⇒ Fluorescent lights flickered, and then the room was brilliantly, blindingly bright. [V] ⇒ A television flickered in the corner.Flicker is also a noun. ⇒ Looking through the cabin window I saw the flicker of flames.2. countable noun [usu sing]If you experience a flicker of emotion, you feel that emotion only for a very short time, and not very strongly. glimmer [+ of] ⇒ He felt a flicker of regret. [+ of] ⇒ He looked at me, a flicker of amusement in his cold eyes.3. verbIf an expression flickers across your face, it appears very briefly. [written] [V across/over n] ⇒ A smile flickered across Vincent's grey features. [V across/over n] ⇒ ...a shadow of disquiet flickering over his face.4. verbIf someone's eyes flicker towards something, they look at it quickly. [written] [V prep/adv] ⇒ Dirk's eyes flickered towards the pistol. [V prep/adv] ⇒ His dark eyes flickered over her face.5. verbIf something flickers, it makes very slight, quick movements. [V] ⇒ In a moment her eyelids flickered, then opened. [V adj] ⇒ A few moments later Mrs Tenney's eyelids flickered open.
