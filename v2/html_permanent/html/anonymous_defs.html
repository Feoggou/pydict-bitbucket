anonymous

(əˈnɒnɪməs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. from or by a person, author, etc, whose name is unknown or withheld ⇒ an anonymous letter2. having no known name3. lacking individual characteristics; unexceptional4. (often capital) denoting an organization which provides help to applicants who remain anonymous ⇒ Alcoholics Anonymous
Derived forms
anonymity (ˌænəˈnɪmɪtɪ ) nounaˈnonymously adverbaˈnonymousness noun
Word origin
C17: via Late Latin from Greek anōnumos, from an- + onoma name

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf you remain anonymous when you do something, you do not let people know that you were the person who did it. OPPOSITE: known, named ⇒ You can remain anonymous if you wish. ⇒ An anonymous benefactor stepped in to provide the prize money. ⇒ ...anonymous phone calls.anonymity (ænɒnɪmɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ Both mother and daughter, who have requested anonymity, are doing fine.anonymously adverb ⇒ The latest photographs were sent anonymously to the magazine's Paris headquarters.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is anonymous does not reveal who you are. ⇒ Of course, that would have to be by anonymous vote.anonymity uncount noun ⇒ He claims many more people would support him in the anonymity of a voting booth.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a place as anonymous, you dislike it because it has no unusual or interesting features and seems unwelcoming. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the most anonymous part of north-west Washington. ⇒ It's nice to stay in a home rather than in an anonymous holiday villa.anonymity uncount noun ⇒ ...the anonymity of the rented room.
