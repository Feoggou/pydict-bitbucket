kosher

(ˈkəʊʃə )
Definitions
adjective1. Judaism conforming to religious law; fit for use: esp, (of food) prepared in accordance with the dietary laws. See also kasher, kashruth2. informala. genuine or authenticb. legitimate or proper
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Yiddish, from Hebrew kāshēr right, proper

kosher for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(koʊʃəʳ )
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething, especially food, that is kosher is approved of or allowed by the laws of Judaism. ⇒ ...a kosher butcher.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is kosher is generally approved of or considered to be correct. [informal] ⇒ I guessed something wasn't quite kosher. ⇒ Acting was not a kosher trade for an upper-class girl.
