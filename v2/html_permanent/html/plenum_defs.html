plenum

(ˈpliːnəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -nums or -na (-nə)1. an enclosure containing gas at a higher pressure than the surrounding environment2. a fully attended meeting or assembly, esp of a legislative body3. (esp in the philosophy of the Stoics) space regarded as filled with matter. Compare vacuum (sense 1)4. the condition or quality of being full

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: space filled by matter, from plēnus full
