fledgling

or fledgeling (ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ )
Definitions
noun1. a young bird that has just fledged2. a. a young and inexperienced or untried person, organization or systemb. (as modifier) ⇒ a fledgling government
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

fledgling

Powered by COBUILD
(fledʒlɪŋ ) for learners of English
Word forms: fledglings
Definitions
1. countable nounA fledgling is a young bird that has its feathers and is learning to fly.2. adjective [ADJ n]You use fledgling to describe a person, organization, or system that is new or without experience. ⇒ ...the sound practical advice he gave to fledgling writers. ⇒ ...Russia's fledgling democracy.
