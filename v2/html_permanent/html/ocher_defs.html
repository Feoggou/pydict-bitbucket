ocher in American English

(ˈoʊkər )
Definitions
noun1.  an earthy clay colored by iron oxide, usually yellow or reddish brown: used as a pigment in paints2.  the color of ocher; esp., dark yellow
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

ocherous (ˈocherous) adjective

Word Origin

ME ocra < L ochra < Gr ōchra < ōchros, pale, pale-yellow
