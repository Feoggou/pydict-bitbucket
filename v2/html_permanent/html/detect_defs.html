detect

(dɪˈtɛkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to perceive or notice ⇒ to detect a note of sarcasm2. to discover the existence or presence of (esp something likely to elude observation) ⇒ to detect alcohol in the blood3. to extract information from (an electromagnetic wave)4. obsolete to reveal or expose (a crime, criminal, etc)
Derived forms
detectable (deˈtectable) or detectible (deˈtectible) adjectivedetecter (deˈtecter) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin dētectus uncovered, from dētegere to uncover, from de- + tegere to cover

Word forms: detects , detecting , detected
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo detect something means to find it or discover that it is present somewhere by using equipment or making an investigation. [V n] ⇒ ...a sensitive piece of equipment used to detect radiation. [V n] ⇒ Most skin cancers can be cured if detected and treated early. [V wh] ⇒ ...a device which can detect who is more at risk of a heart attack.2. verbIf you detect something, you notice it or sense it, even though it is not very obvious. [V n] ⇒ Arnold could detect a certain sadness in the old man's face.
