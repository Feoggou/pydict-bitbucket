confound

(kənˈfaʊnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to astound or perplex; bewilder2. to mix up; confuse3. to treat mistakenly as similar to or identical with (one or more other things)4. (kɒnˈfaʊnd) to curse or damn (usually as an expletive in the phrase confound it!)5. to contradict or refute (an argument, etc)6. to rout or defeat (an enemy)7. obsolete to waste
Derived forms
confoundable (conˈfoundable) adjectiveconfounder (conˈfounder) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French confondre, from Latin confundere to mingle, pour together, from fundere to pour

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone or something confounds you, they make you feel surprised or confused, often by showing you that your opinions or expectations of them were wrong. [V n] ⇒ He momentarily confounded his critics by his cool handling of the hostage crisis. [V n] ⇒ The choice of Governor may confound us all.
