shackles

(ˈʃækəlz)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. two metal rings joined by a chain which are fastened around someone's wrists or ankles in order to prevent them from moving or escaping ⇒ They rattled their shackles frantically., ⇒ He unbolted the shackles on Billy's hands.2. restrictions to freedom ⇒ unfettered from the shackles of a job3. See to throw off the shackles of sth
