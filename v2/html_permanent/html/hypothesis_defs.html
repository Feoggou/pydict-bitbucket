hypothesis

(haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs )
Definitions
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. a suggested explanation for a group of facts or phenomena, either accepted as a basis for further verification (working hypothesis) or accepted as likely to be true. Compare theory (sense 5)2. an assumption used in an argument without its being endorsed; a supposition3. an unproved theory; a conjecture
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hypothesist (hyˈpothesist) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Greek, from hupotithenai to propose, suppose, literally: put under; see hypo-, thesis

Comment by ALRifai on 24 Jul 2016
hypothesis (hypothseses) n. (haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs). 1. an idea which is suggested as a possible explanation for a particular situation or condition, but which has not yet been proved to be correct. 2. a tentative insight into the natural world; a concept that is not yet verified but that if true would explain certain facts or phenomena; a proposal intended to explain certain facts or observations; a message expressing an opinion based on incomplete evidence; 3. a proposition made as a basis for reasoning. 4. an idea about why something happens but that does not have very much evidence to support it; assumption; theory, possibility, supposition. اِحْتِمال؛ إفْتِراض؛ تَقْدِير؛ حِسْبان؛ ظَنّيّة؛ فَرْضِيّة: رأي علمي لم يثبت بعد؛ نظرية. الفرضية: هي طرح أو تفسير مقترح لظاهرة أو عبارة عن أطروحة مقترحة منطقية تقدم علاقة ارتباط بين ظواهر متعددة، ويفترض المنهج العلمي دوماً أن تكون الفرضيات قابلة للفحص كي تعتبر فرضية علمية.
