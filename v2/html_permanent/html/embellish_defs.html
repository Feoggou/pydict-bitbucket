embellish

(ɪmˈbɛlɪʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to improve or beautify by adding detail or ornament; adorn2. to make (a story) more interesting by adding detail3. to provide (a melody, part, etc) with ornaments. See ornament (sense 5)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

embellisher (emˈbellisher) nounembellishment (emˈbellishment) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French embelir, from bel beautiful, from Latin bellus

embellish

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmbelɪʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: embellishes , embellishing , embellished
Definitions
1. verbIf something is embellished with decorative features or patterns, it has those features or patterns on it and they make it look more attractive. [be V-ed + with] ⇒ The stern was embellished with carvings in red and blue. [V n] ⇒ Ivy leaves embellish the front of the dresser. [V n] ⇒ Embellish basic covers and curtains with borders, ties and fringing.2. verbIf you embellish a story, you make it more interesting by adding details which may be untrue. [V n] ⇒ I launched into the parable, embellishing the story with invented dialogue and extra details. [V-ed] ⇒ Irving popularized the story in a dramatic and embellished account.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
