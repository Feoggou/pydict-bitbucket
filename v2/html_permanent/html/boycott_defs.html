boycott

(ˈbɔɪkɒt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to refuse to have dealings with (a person, organization, etc) or refuse to buy (a product) as a protest or means of coercion ⇒ to boycott foreign producenoun2. an instance or the use of boycotting
Word origin
C19: after Captain C. C. Boycott (1832–97), Irish land agent for the Earl of Erne, County Mayo, Ireland, who was a victim of such practices for refusing to reduce rents

verbIf a country, group, or person boycotts a country, organization, or activity, they refuse to be involved with it in any way because they disapprove of it.
