Teuton

(ˈtjuːtən )
Definitions
noun1. a member of an ancient Germanic people from Jutland who migrated to S Gaul in the 2nd century bc: annihilated by a Roman army in 102 bc2. a member of any people speaking a Germanic language, esp a Germanadjective3. Teutonic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Latin Teutonī the Teutons, of Germanic origin
