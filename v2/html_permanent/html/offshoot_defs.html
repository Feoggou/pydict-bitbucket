offshoot

(ˈɒfˌʃuːt )
Definitions
noun1. a shoot or branch growing from the main stem of a plant2. something that develops or derives from a principal source or origin
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

offshoot for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒfʃuːt , US ɔːf- )
Word forms: offshoots
Definitions
countable noun [usu with poss]If one thing is an offshoot of another, it has developed from that other thing. ⇒ Psychology began as a purely academic offshoot of natural philosophy. ⇒ Firstdirect, Midland Bank's telephone banking offshoot, has cut its Visa card rate to APR 22.2%.
