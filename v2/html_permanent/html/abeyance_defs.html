abeyance

(əˈbeɪəns ) or abeyancy (əˈbeɪənsɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (usually preceded by in or into) a state of being suspended or put aside temporarily2. (usually preceded by in) law an indeterminate state of ownership, as when the person entitled to an estate has not been ascertained
Derived forms
aˈbeyant adjective
Word origin
C16-17: from Anglo-French, from Old French abeance expectation, literally a gaping after, a reaching towards
