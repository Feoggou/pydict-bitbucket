cleanse

(klɛnz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to remove dirt, filth, etc, from2. to remove guilt from3. to remove a group of people from (an area) by means of ethnic cleansing
Derived forms
cleansable (ˈcleansable) adjective
Word origin
Old English clǣnsian; related to Middle Low German klēnsen; see clean

Word forms: cleanses , cleansing , cleansed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo cleanse a place, person, or organization of something dirty, unpleasant, or evil means to make them free from it. [V pron-refl + of] ⇒ Straight after your last cigarette your body will begin to cleanse itself of tobacco toxins. [V n of n] ⇒ It urged the party to cleanse its own ranks of those found guilty of human rights violations. [V n] ⇒ Confession cleanses the soul.2. verbIf you cleanse your skin or a wound, you clean it. [V n] ⇒ Catherine demonstrated the proper way to cleanse the face. [V-ing] ⇒ ...cleansing lotions.3. verbIf armed forces cleanse an area, they use violent methods to force certain groups of people to leave it and go to live elsewhere. You can also say that people are cleansed from an area. [disapproval] [V n] ⇒ ...a sustained and systematic effort to ethnically cleanse the entire province. [be V-ed from] ⇒ Many are refugees of three or four or even five other places where they've been cleansed from.4. → see also ethnic cleansing
