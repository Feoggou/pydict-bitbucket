suss

(sʌs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive) slang1. (often foll by out) to attempt to work out (a situation, person's character, etc), esp using one's intuition2. Also: sus to become aware of; suspect (esp in the phrase suss it)noun3. sharpness of mind; social astuteness▷ See also sus laws

Word Origin

C20: shortened from suspect

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you suss a person or situation, you realize or work out what their real character or nature is. [British, informal] [V n] ⇒ I think I've sussed the reason for it. [V that] ⇒ The women began to suss that there was no reason why they should be impressed by him. [get n V-ed] ⇒ We're getting the problem sussed. [Also V wh]Suss out means the same as suss. [V P n] ⇒ They're sussing out the area to see how strong the police presence is. [V n P] ⇒ He susses his colleagues out and he knows who he can trust. [V P wh] ⇒ I'd had the training to suss out what he was up to. [Also V that]
