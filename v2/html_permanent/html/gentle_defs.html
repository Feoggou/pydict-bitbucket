gentle

(ˈdʒɛntəl )
Definitions
adjective1. having a mild or kindly nature or character2. soft or temperate; mild; moderate ⇒ a gentle scolding3. gradual ⇒ a gentle slope4. easily controlled; tame ⇒ a gentle horse5. archaic of good breeding; noble ⇒ gentle blood6. archaic gallant; chivalrousverb (transitive)7. to tame or subdue (a horse)8. to appease or mollify9. obsolete to ennoble or dignifynoun10. a maggot, esp when used as bait in fishing11. archaic a person who is of good breeding
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gently (ˈgently) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old French gentil noble, from Latin gentīlis belonging to the same family; see gens

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is gentle is kind, mild, and calm. ⇒ My son was a quiet and gentle man. ⇒ Michael's voice was gentle and consoling.gently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ She smiled gently at him. ⇒ 'I'm sorry to disturb you,' Webb said gently.gentleness uncount noun ⇒ ...the gentleness with which she treated her pregnant mother.2. graded adjective & adjectiveGentle actions or movements are performed in a calm and controlled manner, with little force. ⇒ ...a gentle game of tennis. ⇒ His movements were gentle and deliberate.gently graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Patrick took her gently by the arm and led her to a chair.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe the weather, especially the wind, as gentle, you mean it is pleasant and calm and not harsh or violent. ⇒ The blustery winds of spring had dropped to a gentle breeze.gently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Light airs blew gently out of the south-east.4. graded adjective & adjectiveA gentle slope or curve is not steep or severe. ⇒ ...gentle, rolling meadows. ⇒ There were two passes over 13,000 feet but the slopes were gentle.gently graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV adj] ⇒ ...gently rolling hills. ⇒ Green meadows sloped gently up from the road.5. graded adjective & adjectiveA gentle heat is a fairly low heat. ⇒ Cook for 30 minutes over a gentle heat.gently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Add the onion and cook gently.
