privileged

(ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. enjoying or granted as a privilege or privileges2. lawa. not actionable as a libel or slanderb. (of a communication, document, etc) that a witness cannot be compelled to divulge3. nautical (of a vessel) having the right of way

privileged

Powered by COBUILD
(prɪvɪlɪdʒd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is privileged has an advantage or opportunity that most other people do not have, often because of their wealth or high social class. ⇒ They were, by and large, a very wealthy, privileged elite. ⇒ ...I felt very privileged to work at the university.The privileged are people who are privileged. ⇒ They are only interested in preserving the power of the privileged. ⇒ Family problems are found in every class, he said, but were more common among the less privileged.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Privileged information is known by only a small group of people, who are not legally required to give it to anyone else. ⇒ The data is privileged information, not to be shared with the general public. ⇒ Mr Nixon argued the tapes were privileged.
