archaic

(ɑːˈkeɪɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. belonging to or characteristic of a much earlier period; ancient2. out of date; antiquated ⇒ an archaic prison system3. (of an idiom, vocabulary, etc) characteristic of an earlier period of a language and not in ordinary use
Derived forms
archaically (arˈchaically) adverb
Word origin
C19: from French archaïque, from Greek arkhaïkos, from arkhaios ancient, from arkhē beginning, from arkhein to begin
