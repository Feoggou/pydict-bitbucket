trow

(trəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbarchaic to think, believe, or trust

Word Origin

Old English treow; related to Old Frisian triūwe, Old Saxon treuwa, Old High German triuwa; see troth, true
Example sentences containing 'trow'
And `tis well met I trow , that a strong hand rules best and a conquered heathen works best when oft reminded that he is conquered. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
