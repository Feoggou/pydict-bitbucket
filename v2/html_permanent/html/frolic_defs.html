frolic

(ˈfrɒlɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a light-hearted entertainment or occasion2. light-hearted activity; gaiety; merrimentverb -ics, -icking or -icked3. (intransitive) to caper about; act or behave playfullyadjective4. archaic or literary full of merriment or fun
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frolicker (ˈfrolicker) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Dutch vrolijk, from Middle Dutch vro happy, glad; related to Old High German frō happy

Definitions
verbWhen people or animals frolic, they play or move in a lively, happy way. [V] ⇒ Tourists sunbathe and frolic in the ocean. [V] ⇒ ...lambs frolicking in the fields.Frolic is also a noun. ⇒ Their relationship is never short on fun and frolic.
