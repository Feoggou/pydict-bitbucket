addition

(əˈdɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act, process, or result of adding2. a person or thing that is added or acquired3. a mathematical operation in which the sum of two numbers or quantities is calculated. Usually indicated by the symbol +4. mainly US and Canadian a part added to a building or piece of land; annexe5. obsolete a title following a person's name6. See in addition7. See in addition to
Word origin
C15: from Latin additiōn-, from addere to add

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. →  in addition2. countable nounAn addition to something is a thing which is added to it. [+ to] ⇒ This is a fine book; a worthy addition to the Cambridge Encyclopedia series. ⇒ This plywood addition helps to strengthen the structure.3. uncount nounThe addition of something is the fact that it is added to something else. [+ of] ⇒ It was completely refurbished in 1987, with the addition of a picnic site.4. uncount nounAddition is the process of calculating the total of two or more numbers. ⇒ ...simple addition and subtraction problems.
