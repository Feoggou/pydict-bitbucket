animus

(ˈænɪməs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. intense dislike; hatred; animosity2. motive, intention, or purpose3. (in Jungian psychology) the masculine principle present in the female unconscious. See also anima
Word origin
C19: from Latin: mind, spirit

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf a person has an animus against someone, they have a strong feeling of dislike for them, even when there is no good reason for it. [formal] ⇒ Your animus towards him suggests that you are the wrong man for the job. ⇒ ...Mr Milner's animus towards Mr Stevens.
