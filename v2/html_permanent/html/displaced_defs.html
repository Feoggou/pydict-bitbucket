displaced

(dɪsˈpleɪst)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a worker) removed from office or employment ⇒ displaced workers from the old steel mills2. (of a resident of an area) forced to moved away from that area ⇒ About 25,000 displaced residents returned home over the weekend.
Example sentences containing 'displaced'
Older people who had lived through the war talked about displaced persons. Various LOOKING FOR THE SPARKAnna's soft voice displaced the histrionic rant of the little corporal which had kept pace in his mind with the roar of the car. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEBut as the day went on and the world with it, defiance and gaiety displaced panic. Brandon, Ruth LEFT, RIGHT AND CENTRE
