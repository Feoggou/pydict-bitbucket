bloc

(blɒk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna group of people or countries combined by a common interest or aim ⇒ the Soviet bloc
Word origin
C20: from French: block

bloc

Powered by COBUILD
(blɒk )
Word forms: blocs
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA bloc is a group of countries which have similar political aims and interests and that act together over some issues. ⇒ ...the former Soviet bloc. ⇒ ...the world's largest trading bloc.2. → see also en bloc
Example sentences containing 'bloc'
It was as if a section of the Blackpool illuminations had been transferred en bloc to the middle of the Channel. Maclean, Alistair THE LONELY SEAThe Eastern bloc desperately needed Western goods but had no hard currency to pay for them, even if they were available legitimately. Dobbs, Michael WALL GAMESMy guess would be seaports where there are ships berthed from any Eastern bloc country -- and all airports. Forbes, Colin COVER STORYJesse Chapman worked for an agency in Vienna that helped refugees from the Eastern bloc find places to live in the West. Carroll, Jonathan FROM THE TEETH OF ANGELS
