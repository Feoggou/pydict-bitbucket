substructure

(ˈsʌbˌstrʌktʃə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a structure, pattern, etc, that forms the basis of anything2. a structure forming a foundation or framework for a building or other construction

Derived Forms

substructural (subˈstructural) adjective
Example sentences containing 'substructure'
The face was grey, soft and slack, falling away from its substructure. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSHis parents ' small circle of reliance on each other's companionship had been the substructure of his entire life. Jacquelyn Mitchard A THEORY OF RELATIVITY
