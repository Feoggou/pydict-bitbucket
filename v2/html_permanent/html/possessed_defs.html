possessed

(pəˈzɛst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (foll by of) owning or having2. (usually postpositive) under the influence of a powerful force, such as a spirit or strong emotion3. a less common word for self-possessed

possessed

Powered by COBUILD
(pəzest )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone is described as being possessed by an evil spirit, it is believed that their mind and body are controlled by an evil spirit. [+ by] ⇒ She even claimed the couple's daughter was possessed by the devil. ⇒ He behaved like someone possessed.2. adjective [v-link ADJ of n]If someone or something is possessed of a particular quality, ability, or feature, they have that quality, ability, or feature. If someone is possessed of a particular feeling or belief, they have that feeling or belief. [formal] ⇒ He is possessed of the most brilliant talents. ⇒ She was possessed of a terrifying sensation that the life was being squeezed slowly out of her.3. → see also possess
