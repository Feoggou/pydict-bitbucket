foreboding

(fɔːˈbəʊdɪŋ )
Definitions
noun1. a feeling of impending evil, disaster, etc2. an omen or portentadjective3. presaging something
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

forebodingly (foreˈbodingly) adverbforebodingness (foreˈbodingness) noun

foreboding

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳboʊdɪŋ ) for learners of English
Word forms: forebodings
Definitions
1. variable nounForeboding is a strong feeling that something terrible is going to happen. ⇒ His triumph was overshadowed by an uneasy sense of foreboding.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe something as foreboding, you mean that it makes you feel that something terrible is going to happen. ⇒ Prisons like Strangeways, built more than 100 years ago, were intended to look grim and foreboding places.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
