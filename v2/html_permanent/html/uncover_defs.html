uncover

(ʌnˈkʌvə )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to remove the cover, cap, top, etc, from2. (transitive) to reveal or disclose ⇒ to uncover a plot3. to take off (one's head covering), esp as a mark of respect

uncover for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnkʌvəʳ )
Word forms: uncovers , uncovering , uncovered
Definitions
1. verbIf you uncover something, especially something that has been kept secret, you discover or find out about it. [V n] ⇒ Auditors said they had uncovered evidence of fraud. [V n] ⇒ A specific plot to kill him was uncovered in the past couple of weeks.2. verbWhen people who are digging somewhere uncover something, they find a thing or a place that has been under the ground for a long time. [V n] ⇒ Archaeologists have uncovered an 11,700-year-old hunting camp in Alaska.3. verbTo uncover something means to remove something that is covering it. [V n] ⇒ When the seedlings sprout, uncover the tray.
