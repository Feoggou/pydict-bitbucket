conspicuous

(kənˈspɪkjʊəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. clearly visible; obvious or showy2. attracting attention because of a striking quality or feature ⇒ conspicuous stupidity
Derived forms
conspicuously (conˈspicuously) adverbconspicuousness (conˈspicuousness) or conspicuity (ˌkɒnspɪˈkjuːɪtɪ) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin conspicuus, from conspicere to perceive; see conspectus

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is conspicuous, people can see or notice them very easily. OPPOSITE: inconspicuous ⇒ The most conspicuous way in which the old politics is changing is in the growing use of referendums. ⇒ You may feel tearful in situations where you feel conspicuous.conspicuously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Britain continues to follow U.S. policy in this and other areas where American policies have most conspicuously failed. ⇒ Johnston's name was conspicuously absent from the list.2. →  conspicuous by one's absence
