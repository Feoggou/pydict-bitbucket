vigilance

(ˈvɪdʒɪləns )
Definitions
noun1. the fact, quality, or condition of being vigilant2. the abnormal state or condition of being unable to sleep
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'vigilance'
In the dimness of its dreary day few things even in that land of vigilance could have espied them, save from close at hand. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSDonald Sinclair -- once a journalist and now a politician -- had assessed, as James McGregor had, the vigilance of the media. Kippax, Frank THE SCARIn his eternal vigilance his hand holds a sword, ever at the ready, to show the magnitude of the danger. Terry Goodkind NAKED EMPIRE
