punctuate

(ˈpʌŋktjʊˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (mainly tr)1. (also intr) to insert punctuation marks into (a written text)2. to interrupt or insert at frequent intervals ⇒ a meeting punctuated by heckling3. to give emphasis to

Derived Forms

punctuˌator (ˈpunctuˌator) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin punctuāre to prick, from Latin punctum a prick, from pungere to puncture
