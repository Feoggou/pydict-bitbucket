Definitions
pronunciation note:   The auxiliary verb is pronounced (həd , STRONG hæd ). For the main verb, and for the meanings [sense 2] to [sense 5], the pronunciation is (hæd ).1. Had is the past tense and past participle of have1.2. auxiliary verbHad is sometimes used instead of 'if' to begin a clause which refers to a situation that might have happened but did not. For example, the clause 'had he been elected' means the same as 'if he had been elected'. [AUX n -ed] ⇒ Had he succeeded, he would have acquired a monopoly. [AUX n -ed] ⇒ Had I known what the problem was, we could have addressed it.3. →  to be had4. →  to have had it5. →  to have had it
