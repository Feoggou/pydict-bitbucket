prompter

(ˈprɒmptə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person offstage who reminds the actors of forgotten lines or cues2. a person, thing, etc, that prompts
Example sentences containing 'prompter'
He had the knife in his hand, and it was an effective prompter , I can tell you. Fraser, Anthea PRETTY MAIDS ALL IN A ROWShe looked down at Mafalda's feet, exactly where the prompter would have been in the theatre. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSMore generally, and unusually for a German paterfamilias, he acted in conversation as the prompter of others. NULL DARE CALL IT TREASON
