subdued

(səbˈdjuːd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. cowed, passive, or shy2. gentle or quiet ⇒ a subdued whisper3. (of colours, etc) not harsh or bright ⇒ subdued lighting

Derived Forms

subduedly (subˈduedly) adverbsubduedness (subˈduedness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is subdued is very quiet, often because they are sad or worried about something. ⇒ He faced the press, initially, in a somewhat subdued mood. ⇒ The audience are strangely subdued, clapping politely after each song.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSubdued sounds are not very loud. ⇒ The conversation around them was resumed, but in subdued tones.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSubdued lights or colours are not very bright. ⇒ The lighting was subdued.
