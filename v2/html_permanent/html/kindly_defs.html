kindly

(ˈkaɪndlɪ )
Definitions
adjective -lier or -liest1. having a sympathetic or warm-hearted nature2. motivated by warm and sympathetic feelings ⇒ a kindly act3. pleasant, mild, or agreeable ⇒ a kindly climate4. archaic natural; normaladverb5. in a considerate or humane way6. with tolerance or forbearance ⇒ he kindly forgave my rudeness7. cordially; pleasantly ⇒ he greeted us kindly8. please (often used to express impatience or formality) ⇒ will you kindly behave yourself!9. archaic in accordance with nature; appropriately10. See not take kindly to

kindliness (ˈkindliness) noun

kindly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(kaɪndli )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A kindly person is kind, caring, and sympathetic. ⇒ He was a stern critic but an extremely kindly man.kindliness uncount noun ⇒ His kindliness and warmth made him particularly effective with staff welfare.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV before v]If someone kindly does something for you, they act in a thoughtful and helpful way. ⇒ He kindly carried our picnic in a rucksack. ⇒ He had very kindly asked me to the cocktail party that evening.3. adverb [ADV before v]If someone asks you to kindly do something, they are asking you in a way which shows that they have authority over you, or that they are angry with you. [formal] ⇒ Will you kindly obey the instructions I am about to give?4. → See also kind15. →  look kindly on/look kindly upon6. →  not take kindly to
