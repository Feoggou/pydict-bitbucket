unreasonable

(ʌnˈriːznəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. immoderate; excessive ⇒ unreasonable demands2. refusing to listen to reason3. lacking reason or judgment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unreasonableness (unˈreasonableness) noununreasonably (unˈreasonably) adverb

unreasonable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnriːzənəbəl )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is being unreasonable, you mean that they are behaving in a way that is not fair or sensible. [+ in] ⇒ The strikers were being unreasonable in their demands, having rejected the deal two weeks ago. ⇒ It was her unreasonable behaviour with a Texan playboy which broke up her marriage. ⇒ It's unreasonable to expect your child to behave in a caring way if you behave selfishly.unreasonably (ʌnriːzənəbli ) adverb ⇒ We unreasonably expect near perfect behaviour from our children.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn unreasonable decision, action, price, or amount seems unfair and difficult to justify. ⇒ ...unreasonable increases in the price of petrol. ⇒ One in four consumers now say water prices are very unreasonable.unreasonably adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ The banks' charges are unreasonably high.
