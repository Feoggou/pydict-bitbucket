swagger1

(ˈswæɡə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to walk or behave in an arrogant manner2. (intransitive; often foll by about) to brag loudly3. (transitive) rare to force, influence, etc, by blusteringnoun4. arrogant gait, conduct, or manneradjective5. British informal, rare elegantly fashionable

Derived Forms

swaggerer (ˈswaggerer) nounswaggering (ˈswaggering) adjectiveswaggeringly (ˈswaggeringly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: probably from swag

swagger

Powered by COBUILD
(swægəʳ )
Word forms: swaggers , swaggering , swaggered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you swagger, you walk in a very proud, confident way, holding your body upright and swinging your hips. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A broad shouldered man wearing a dinner jacket swaggered confidently up to the bar. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The burly brute swaggered forward, towering over me, and shouted. [V-ing] ⇒ John Steed was an arrogant, swaggering young man. [Also V]Swagger is also a noun. ⇒ He walked with something of a swagger.
