distemper1

(dɪsˈtɛmpə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of various infectious diseases of animals, esp canine distemper, a highly contagious viral disease of dogs, characterized initially by high fever and a discharge from the nose and eyes. See also hard pad, strangles2. archaica. a disease or disorderb. disturbancec. discontentverb3. (transitive) archaic to disturb
Word origin
C14: from Late Latin distemperāre to derange the health of, from Latin dis-1 + temperāre to mix in correct proportions

distemper2

(dɪsˈtɛmpə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a technique of painting in which the pigments are mixed with water, glue, size, etc, used for poster, mural, and scene painting2. the paint used in this technique or any of various water-based paints, including, in Britain, whitewashverb3. (transitive) to mix (pigments) with water and size4. to paint (something) with distemper
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin distemperāre to soak, from Latin dis-1 + temperāre to mingle
