Illyrian

(ɪˈlɪərɪən )
Definitions
noun1. a member of the group of related Indo-European peoples who occupied Illyria from the late third millennium to the early first millennium bc2. the extinct and almost unrecorded language of these peoples: of uncertain relationship within the Indo-European family, but thought by some to be the ancestor of modern Albanianadjective3. of, characteristic of, or relating to Illyria, its people, or their language
