tip1

(tɪp )
Definitions
noun1. the extreme end of something, esp a narrow or pointed end2. the top or summit3. a small piece forming an extremity or end ⇒ a metal tip on a caneverbWord forms: tips, tipping or tipped (transitive)4. to adorn or mark the tip of5. to cause to form a tip
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tipless (ˈtipless) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old Norse typpa; related to Middle Low German, Middle Dutch tip

tip2

(tɪp )
Definitions
verbWord forms: tips, tipping or tipped1. to tilt or cause to tilt2. (usually foll by over or up) to tilt or cause to tilt, so as to overturn or fall3. British to dump (rubbish, etc)4. See tip one's hatnoun5. the act of tipping or the state of being tipped6. British a dump for refuse, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tippable (ˈtippable) adjective

Word Origin

C14: of uncertain origin; related to top1, topple

tip3

(tɪp )
Definitions
noun1. a payment given for services in excess of the standard charge; gratuity2. a helpful hint, warning, or other piece of information3. a piece of inside information, esp in betting or investingverbWord forms: tips, tipping or tipped4. to give a tip to (a person)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: perhaps from tip4

tip4

(tɪp )
Definitions
verbWord forms: tips, tipping or tipped (transitive)1. to hit or strike lightly2. to hit (a ball) indirectly so that it glances off the bat in cricketnoun3. a light blow4. a glancing hit in cricket

tip

Powered by COBUILD
(tɪp ) for learners of English
Word forms: tips , tipping , tipped
Definitions
1. countable nounThe tip of something long and narrow is the end of it. [+ of] ⇒ The sleeves covered his hands to the tips of his fingers. [+ of] ⇒ She poked and shifted things with the tip of her walking stick. ⇒ Hurricane Andrew has passed over the southern tip of Florida.2. ergative verb & verbIf you tip an object or part of your body or if it tips, it moves into a sloping position with one end or side higher than the other. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ He leaned away from her, and she had to tip her head back to see him. [V] ⇒ A young boy is standing on a stool, reaching for a cookie jar, and the stool is about to tip. [V-ed] ⇒ The north pole is slightly tipped towards the sun.3. verbIf you tip something somewhere, you pour it there. [V n prep] ⇒ Tip the vegetables into a bowl. [V n prep] ⇒ She took out the plate, stared blankly at the dried-up food on it, and tipped it into the bin. [V n with adv] ⇒ Tip away the salt and wipe the pan.4. verbTo tip rubbish means to get rid of it by leaving it somewhere. [British] [V n] ⇒ ...the costs of tipping rubbish in landfills. [V] ⇒ How do you stop people tipping? [V-ed] ⇒ We live in a street off Soho Road and there's rubbish tipped everywhere.regional note:   in AM, use dump5. countable nounA tip is a place where rubbish is left. [British] ⇒ Officers had found a large bread knife on the rubbish tip. ⇒ I took a load of rubbish and grass cuttings to the tip.regional note:   in AM, use garbage dump6. countable nounIf you describe a place as a tip, you mean it is very untidy. [British, informal] pigsty ⇒ The flat is an absolute tip.7. verbIf you tip someone such as a waiter in a restaurant, you give them some money in order to thank them for their services. [V n] ⇒ Do you really think it's customary to tip the waiters? [V n amount] ⇒ She tipped the barmen 10 dollars and bought drinks all round. [Also V]tipping uncount noun ⇒ A 10 percent service charge is added in lieu of tipping.8. countable nounIf you give a tip to someone such as a waiter in a restaurant, you give them some money to thank them for their services. ⇒ I gave the barber a tip. ⇒ The Head Porter was keeping all the tips.9. countable nounA tip is a useful piece of advice. [+ on] ⇒ It shows how to prepare a CV, and gives tips on applying for jobs. ⇒ ...tips for busy managers. ⇒ A good tip is to buy the most expensive lens you can afford. [Also + for]10. verb [usu passive]If a person is tipped to do something or is tipped for success at something, experts or journalists believe that they will do that thing or achieve that success. [British] [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ He is tipped to be the country's next foreign minister. [be V-ed + for] ⇒ He was widely tipped for success.11. countable nounSomeone's tip for a race or competition is their advice on its likely result, especially to someone who wants to bet money on the result. ⇒ I've a tip for the races. [+ for] ⇒ United are still my tip for the Title.12. →  the tip of the iceberg13. →  tip the scales/balance14. →  on the tip of your tongue► →  tip off► →  tip over► →  tip up
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
