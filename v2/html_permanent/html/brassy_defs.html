brassy

(ˈbrɑːsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: brassier or brassiest1. insolent; brazen2. flashy; showy3. (of sound) harsh, strident, or resembling the sound of a brass instrument4. like brass, esp in colour5. decorated with or made of brass6. a variant spelling of brassie
Derived forms
brassily (ˈbrassily) adverbbrassiness (ˈbrassiness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveBrassy music is bold, harsh, and loud. ⇒ Musicians blast their brassy jazz from street corners.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a woman's appearance or her behaviour as brassy, you think that she does not have good taste, and that she dresses or behaves in a way that is too loud or vulgar. [disapproval] ⇒ ...Alec and his brassy blonde wife. ⇒ Those ladies were brassy and busty, with pudgy fingers and painted eyes.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is brassy has a yellow metallic colour and sometimes looks cheap. ⇒ A woman with big brassy ear-rings.
