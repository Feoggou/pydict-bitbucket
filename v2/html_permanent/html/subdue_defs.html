subdue

(səbˈdjuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -dues, -duing or -dued (transitive)1. to establish ascendancy over by force2. to overcome and bring under control, as by intimidation or persuasion3. to hold in check or repress (feelings, emotions, etc)4. to render less intense or less conspicuous

Derived Forms

subduable (subˈduable) adjectivesubduably (subˈduably) adverbsubdual (subˈdual) noun

Word Origin

C14 sobdue, from Old French soduire to mislead, from Latin subdūcere to remove; English sense influenced by Latin subdere to subject
