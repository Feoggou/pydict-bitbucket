supple

(ˈsʌpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. bending easily without damage2. capable of or showing easy or graceful movement; lithe3. mentally flexible; responding readily4. disposed to agree, sometimes to the point of servilityverb5. rare to make or become supple

Derived Forms

suppleness (ˈsuppleness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French souple, from Latin supplex bowed

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA supple object or material bends or changes shape easily without cracking or breaking. ⇒ The leather is supple and sturdy enough to last for years. ⇒ Traditional cheesemaking skills give our brie its soft, supple, creamy texture.suppleness uncount noun ⇒ This luxurious talcum lotion restores softness and suppleness to dehydrated skin.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA supple person can move and bend their body very easily. ⇒ Paul was incredibly supple and strong. ⇒ Try these simple exercises to keep your feet supple.suppleness uncount noun ⇒ Exercise in pregnancy can build up your strength and suppleness.3. graded adjectiveIf you describe something such as a flavour or a sound as supple, you like it because it is smooth and rich. [literary] ⇒ The wine has a medium-bodied, supple flavour. ⇒ ...the haunting didgeridoo, the teasing keyboards and the supple bassline.
