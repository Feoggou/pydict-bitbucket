dialect

(ˈdaɪəˌlɛkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna. a form of a language spoken in a particular geographical area or by members of a particular social class or occupational group, distinguished by its vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciationb. a form of a language that is considered inferior ⇒ the farmer spoke dialect and was despised by the merchantsc. (as modifier) ⇒ a dialect word
Derived forms
ˌdialectal (ˌdiaˈlectal) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin dialectus, from Greek dialektos speech, dialect, discourse, from dialegesthai to converse, from legein to talk, speak
