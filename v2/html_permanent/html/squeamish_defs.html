squeamish

(ˈskwiːmɪʃ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. easily sickened or nauseated, as by the sight of blood2. easily shocked; fastidious or prudish3. easily frightened ⇒ squeamish about spiders

Derived Forms

squeamishly (ˈsqueamishly) adverbsqueamishness (ˈsqueamishness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Anglo-French escoymous, of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are squeamish, you are easily upset by unpleasant sights or situations. ⇒ I'm terribly squeamish. I can't bear gory films. ⇒ I am not squeamish about blood.squeamishness uncount noun ⇒ When you've got over your squeamishness, there will be no stopping you.
