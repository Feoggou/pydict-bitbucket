suffering

(ˈsʌfərɪŋ , ˈsʌfrɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the pain, misery, or loss experienced by a person who suffers2. the state or an instance of enduring pain, etc

Derived Forms

sufferingly (ˈsufferingly) adverb

Powered by COBUILD
(sʌfərɪŋ )
Word forms: sufferings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounSuffering is serious pain which someone feels in their body or their mind. ⇒ They began to recover slowly from their nightmare of pain and suffering. ⇒ It has caused terrible suffering to animals. ⇒ His many novels have portrayed the sufferings of his race.2. → see also long-suffering
Example sentences containing 'suffering'
She had stayed there for three months and they had told her she was suffering from paranoid schizophrenia, had got her on medication. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERThere would be many more out in the camp suffering the effects of Tarja's abortive rescue attempt. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPHe had a taste for power and a profound disregard for suffering. Val McDermid THE LAST TEMPTATIONThe State of Arizona had extracted a price of six short years from Andrew Carlisle in exchange for Gina Antone's suffering. J.A. Jance KISS OF THE BEES
