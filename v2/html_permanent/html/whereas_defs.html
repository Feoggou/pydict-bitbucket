whereas

(wɛərˈæz )
Definitions
conjunction1. (coordinating) but on the other hand ⇒ I like to go swimming whereas Sheila likes to sailsentence connector2. (in formal documents to begin sentences) it being the case that; since
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

whereas for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hweəræz )
Definitions
subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use whereas to introduce a comment which contrasts with what is said in the main clause. ⇒ Pensions are linked to inflation, whereas they should be linked to the cost of living. ⇒ Whereas the population of working age increased by 1 million between 1981 and 1986, today it is barely growing.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
