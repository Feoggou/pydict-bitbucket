debauch

(dɪˈbɔːtʃ ) or debosh (dɪˈbɒʃ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when tr, usually passive) to lead into a life of depraved self-indulgence2. (transitive) to seduce (a woman)noun3. an instance or period of extreme dissipation
Derived forms
debauchedly (dɪˈbɔːtʃɪdlɪ ) adverbdebauchedness (deˈbauchedness) noundebaucher (deˈbaucher) noundebauchery (deˈbauchery) or debauchment (deˈbauchment) noun
Word origin
C16: from Old French desbaucher to corrupt, literally: to shape (timber) roughly, from bauch beam, of Germanic origin
