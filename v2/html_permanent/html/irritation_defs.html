irritation

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪrɪteɪʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: irritations
Definitions
1. uncount nounIrritation is a feeling of annoyance, especially when something is happening that you cannot easily stop or control. ⇒ For the first time Leonard felt irritation at her methods. ⇒ He tried not to let his irritation show as he blinked in the glare of the television lights.2. countable nounAn irritation is something that keeps annoying you. ⇒ Don't allow a minor irritation in the workplace to mar your ambitions. ⇒ He describes the tourists as an irritation.3. variable nounIrritation in a part of your body is a feeling of slight pain and discomfort there. [+ to] ⇒ These oils may cause irritation to sensitive skins. ⇒ This is an irritation and inflammation of the edge of the eyelid.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
