cuff1

(kʌf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the part of a sleeve nearest the hand, sometimes turned back and decorative2. the part of a gauntlet or glove that extends past the wrist3. US, Canadian and Australian the turned-up fold at the bottom of some trouser legs. Also called (in Britain and certain other countries): turn-up4. See off the cuff
Word origin
C14 cuffe glove, of obscure origin

cuff

Powered by COBUILD
(kʌf )
Word forms: cuffs , cuffing , cuffed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]The cuffs of a shirt or dress are the parts at the ends of the sleeves, which are thicker than the rest of the sleeve. ⇒ ...a pale blue shirt with white collar and cuffs.2. countable noun [usu pl]The cuffs on a pair of pants or trousers are the parts at the ends of the legs, which are folded up. [US] [+ of] ⇒ ...the cuffs of his jeans.regional note:   in BRIT, use turn-up3. verbIf you cuff someone, you hit them quickly and lightly with your hand, usually on their head or their ear. [V n] ⇒ Brodie cuffed him on the side of the head.Cuff is also a noun. ⇒ He gave the dog a cuff.4. verbIf the police cuff someone, they put handcuffs on them. [informal] [V n] ⇒ She hoped they wouldn't cuff her hands behind her back.5. →  off the cuff
