inconclusive

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkənkluːsɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf research or evidence is inconclusive, it has not proved anything. ⇒ Research has so far proved inconclusive. ⇒ I find the evidence inconclusive.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a contest or conflict is inconclusive, it is not clear who has won or who is winning. ⇒ The past two elections were inconclusive. ⇒ ...eight years of bloody and inconclusive war.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
