invulnerable

(ɪnˈvʌlnərəbəl , -ˈvʌlnrəbəl)
Definitions
adjective1. incapable of being wounded, hurt, damaged, etc, either physically or emotionally2. incapable of being damaged or captured ⇒ an invulnerable fortress
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

invulnerability (inˌvulneraˈbility) or invulnerableness (inˈvulnerableness) nouninvulnerably (inˈvulnerably) adverb

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is invulnerable, they cannot be harmed or damaged. ⇒ Many daughters assume that their mothers are invulnerable. ⇒ ...a system that would make the U.S. invulnerable to nuclear attack. [Also + to]invulnerability (ɪnvʌlnərəbɪlɪti ) uncount noun [+ to] ⇒ They have a sense of invulnerability to disease.
