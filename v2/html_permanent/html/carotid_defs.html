carotid

(kəˈrɒtɪd ) or carotid artery (kəˈrɒtɪd ˈɑːtərɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. either one of the two principal arteries that supply blood to the head and neckadjective2. of or relating to either of these arteries
Derived forms
carotidal (caˈrotidal) adjective
Word origin
C17: from French, from Greek karōtides, from karoun to stupefy; so named by Galen, because pressure on them produced unconsciousness
