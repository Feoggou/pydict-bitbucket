goose1

(ɡuːs )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural geese (ɡiːs )1. any of various web-footed long-necked birds of the family Anatidae: order Anseriformes. They are typically larger and less aquatic than ducks and are gregarious and migratory. See also brent goose, barnacle goose, greylag, snow goose ▶ Related adjective: anserine2. the female of such a bird, as opposed to the male (gander)3. informal a silly person4. Word forms: plural gooses a pressing iron with a long curving handle, used esp by tailors5. the flesh of the goose, used as food6. See all his geese are swans7. See cook someone's goose8. See kill the goose that lays the golden eggs
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English gōs; related to Old Norse gās, Old High German gans, Old Irish gēiss swan, Greek khēn, Sanskrit hainsas

goose2 - slang

(ɡuːs )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to prod (a person) playfully in the behindnounWord forms: plural gooses2. a playful prod in the behind
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from goose1, probably from a comparison with the jabbing of a goose's bill
