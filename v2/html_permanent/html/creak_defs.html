creak

(kriːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make or cause to make a harsh squeaking sound2. (intransitive) to make such sounds while moving ⇒ the old car creaked alongnoun3. a harsh squeaking sound
Derived forms
creaky (ˈcreaky) adjectivecreakily (ˈcreakily) adverbcreakiness (ˈcreakiness) nouncreakingly (ˈcreakingly) adverb
Word origin
C14: variant of croak, of imitative origin

creak

Powered by COBUILD
(kriːk )
Word forms: creaks , creaking , creaked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something creaks, it makes a short, high-pitched sound when it moves. [V] ⇒ The bed-springs creaked. [V] ⇒ The steps creaked beneath his feet. [V adj] ⇒ The door creaked open. [V-ing] ⇒ ...the creaking stairs.Creak is also a noun. ⇒ The door was pulled open with a creak.
Example sentences containing 'creak'
I heard the front door creak open, the footsteps of the two coppers in the foyer. Adam, Paul A NASTY DOSE OF DEATHIt was a much slighter sound -- the very faint creak of timbers. Philip Marsden THE MAIN CAGESThis time the creak of the wooden stair was followed by the slap of a leather slipper on the tiled floor of the hall. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSThey were trying to be quiet, but he could hear the creak of the treads. Melinda Metz HAUNTED
