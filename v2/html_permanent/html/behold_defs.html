behold

(bɪˈhəʊld )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -holds, -holding or -held (often used in the imperative to draw attention to something) archaic or literaryto look (at); observe
Derived forms
beholder (beˈholder) noun
Word origin
Old English bihealdan; related to Old High German bihaltan, Dutch behouden; see be-, hold

Word forms: beholds , beholding , beheld
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you behold someone or something, you see them. [literary] [V n] ⇒ She looked into his eyes and beheld madness. [V] ⇒ He was a joy to behold.2. conventionPeople used to say or write 'Behold' to draw people's attention to something. [humorous, literary, old-fashioned] ⇒ Fear Not. Behold The Saviour. ⇒ Behold a series of thrilling photographs of Felix.3. → lo and behold
