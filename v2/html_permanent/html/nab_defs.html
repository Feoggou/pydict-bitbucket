nab

(næb )
Definitions
verbWord forms: nabs, nabbing or nabbed (transitive) informal1. to arrest2. to catch (someone) in wrongdoing3. to seize suddenly; snatch
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: perhaps of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish nappe, Swedish nappa to snatch. See kidnap

nab for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(næb )
Word forms: nabs , nabbing , nabbed
Definitions
verbIf people in authority such as the police nab someone who they think has done something wrong, they catch them or arrest them. [informal] [V n] ⇒ He killed 12 people before the authorities finally nabbed him. [get V-ed] ⇒ Soon he was back in the armed robbery business. Again, he got nabbed.
