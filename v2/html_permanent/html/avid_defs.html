avid

(ˈævɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. very keen; enthusiastic ⇒ an avid reader2. (postpositive; often foll by for or of) eager (for); desirous (of); greedy (for) ⇒ avid for revenge
Derived forms
avidly (ˈavidly) adverb
Word origin
C18: from Latin avidus, from avēre to long for

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use avid to describe someone who is very enthusiastic about something that they do. ⇒ He misses not having enough books because he's an avid reader. ⇒ ...an avid collector of art and history.avidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Thank you for a most entertaining magazine, which I read avidly each month.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that someone is avid for something, you mean that they are very eager to get it. [+ for] ⇒ He was intensely eager, indeed avid, for wealth.avidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Western suppliers too are competing avidly for business abroad.
