hers

Powered by COBUILD
(hɜːʳz ) for learners of English
Definitions
language note:   Hers is a third person possessive pronoun.1. possessive pronoun & pronounYou use hers to indicate that something belongs or relates to a woman, girl, or female animal. ⇒ His hand as it shook hers was warm and firm. ⇒ He'd never seen eyes as green as hers. ⇒ Professor Camm was a great friend of hers. ⇒ Hers was the suggestion I acted upon.2. possessive pronoun & pronounIn written English, hers is sometimes used to refer to a person without saying whether that person is a man or a woman. Some people dislike this use and prefer to use 'his or hers' or 'theirs'. ⇒ The author can report other people's results which more or less agree with hers.3. possessive pronoun & pronounHers is sometimes used to refer to a country or nation. [formal, or written]4. possessive pronounPeople sometimes use hers to refer to a car, machine, or ship. [informal]
