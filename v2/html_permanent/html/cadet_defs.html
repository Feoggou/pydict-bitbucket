cadet

(kəˈdɛt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a young person undergoing preliminary training, usually before full entry to the uniformed services, police, etc, esp for officer status2. a school pupil receiving elementary military training in a school corps3. (in England and in France before 1789) a gentleman, usually a younger son, who entered the army to prepare for a commission4. a younger son or brother5. See cadet branch6. (in New Zealand) a person learning sheep farming on a sheep station
Derived forms
cadetship (caˈdetship) noun
Word origin
C17: from French, from dialect (Gascon) capdet captain, ultimately from Latin caput head
