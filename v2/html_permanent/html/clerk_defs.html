clerk

(klɑːk , US English and Canadian English klɜːrk)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a worker, esp in an office, who keeps records, files, etc2. See clerk to the justices3. an employee of a court, legislature, board, corporation, etc, who keeps records and accounts, etc ⇒ a town clerk4. Also called: clerk of the House (in the UK) a senior official of the House of Commons5. Also called: clerk in holy orders a cleric6. US and Canadian short for salesclerk7. Also called: desk clerk US and Canadian a hotel receptionist8. archaic a scholarverb9. (intransitive) to serve as a clerk
Derived forms
clerkdom (ˈclerkdom) nounclerkish (ˈclerkish) adjectiveclerkship (ˈclerkship) noun
Word origin
Old English clerc, from Church Latin clēricus, from Greek klērikos cleric, relating to the heritage (alluding to the Biblical Levites, whose inheritance was the Lord), from klēros heritage

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA clerk is a person who works in an office, bank, or law court and whose job is to look after the records or accounts. ⇒ She was offered a job as an accounts clerk with a travel firm.2. countable nounIn a hotel, office, or hospital, a clerk is the person whose job is to answer the telephone and deal with people when they arrive. [mainly US] ⇒ ...a hotel clerk.3. countable nounA clerk is someone who works in a store. [US] 4. verbTo clerk means to work as a clerk. [mainly US] [V] ⇒ Gene clerked at the auction. [V for n] ⇒ He clerked for the chief justice of the Supreme Court.
