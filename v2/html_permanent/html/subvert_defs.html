subvert

(səbˈvɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to bring about the complete downfall or ruin of (something existing or established by a system of law, etc)2. to undermine the moral principles of (a person, etc); corrupt

Derived Forms

subverter (subˈverter) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin subvertere to overturn, from sub- from below + vertere to turn
