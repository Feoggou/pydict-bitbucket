swoon

(swuːn )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. a literary word for faint2. to become ecstaticnoun3. an instance of fainting

Derived Forms

swooning (ˈswooning) adjectiveswooningly (ˈswooningly) adverb

Word Origin

Old English geswōgen insensible, past participle of swōgan (unattested except in compounds) to suffocate

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you swoon, you are strongly affected by your feelings for someone you love or admire very much. [V + over] ⇒ Virtually every woman in the '20s swooned over Valentino. [V] ⇒ The ladies shriek and swoon at his every word. [Also V adv]
