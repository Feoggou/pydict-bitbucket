longitude

(ˈlɒndʒɪˌtjuːd , ˈlɒŋɡ-)
Definitions
noun1. distance in degrees east or west of the prime meridian at 0° measured by the angle between the plane of the prime meridian and that of the meridian through the point in question, or by the corresponding time difference. See latitude (sense 1)2. astronomy short for celestial longitude
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin longitūdō length, from longus long1
