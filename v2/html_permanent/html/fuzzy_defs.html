fuzzy

(ˈfʌzɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: fuzzier or fuzziest1. of, resembling, or covered with fuzz2. indistinct; unclear or distorted3. not clearly thought out or expressed4. (of the hair) tightly curled or very wavy5. mathematics of or relating to a form of set theory in which set membership depends on a likelihood function ⇒ fuzzy set, ⇒ fuzzy logic6. (of a computer program or system) designed to operate according to the principles of fuzzy logic, so as to be able to deal with data which is imprecise or has uncertain boundaries
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

fuzzily (ˈfuzzily) adverbfuzziness (ˈfuzziness) noun

fuzzy

Powered by COBUILD
(fʌzi ) for learners of English
Word forms: fuzzier , fuzziest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveFuzzy hair sticks up in a soft, curly mass. frizzy ⇒ He had fuzzy black hair and bright black eyes.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is fuzzy, it has a covering that feels soft and like fur. ⇒ ...fuzzy material.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA fuzzy picture, image, or sound is unclear and hard to see or hear. ⇒ A couple of fuzzy pictures have been published. ⇒ ...fuzzy bass lines.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you or your thoughts are fuzzy, you are confused and cannot think clearly. ⇒ He had little patience for fuzzy ideas.5. graded adjective & adjectiveYou describe something as fuzzy when it is vague and not clearly defined. ⇒ The border between science fact and science fiction gets a bit fuzzy.6. adjective [ADJ n]Fuzzy logic is a type of computer logic that is supposed to imitate the way that humans think, for example by adapting to changing circumstances rather than always following the same procedure. ⇒ ...domestic appliances that also use fuzzy logic to mimic the way a person would do the job manually. ⇒ ...research on fuzzy systems.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
