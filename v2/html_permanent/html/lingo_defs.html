lingo

(ˈlɪŋɡəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -goesinformal any foreign or unfamiliar language, jargon, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: perhaps from lingua franca; compare Portuguese lingoa tongue

lingo

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪŋgoʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: lingos
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]People sometimes refer to a foreign language, especially one that they do not speak or understand, as a lingo. [informal] ⇒ I don't speak the lingo.2. uncount noun [oft a N]A lingo is a range of words or a style of language which is used in a particular situation or by a particular group of people. [informal] ⇒ In record-business lingo, that means he wanted to buy the rights to the song and market it. ⇒ ...an author who writes in a lurid lingo, freely punctuated with crude expletives.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
