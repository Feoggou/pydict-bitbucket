rubric

(ˈruːbrɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a title, heading, or initial letter in a book, manuscript, or section of a legal code, esp one printed or painted in red ink or in some similarly distinguishing manner2. a set of rules of conduct or procedure3. a set of directions for the conduct of Christian church services, often printed in red in a prayer book or missal4. instructions to a candidate at the head of the examination paper5. an obsolete name for red ochreadjective6. written, printed, or marked in red
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rubrical (ˈrubrical) adjectiverubrically (ˈrubrically) adverb

Word Origin

C15 rubrike red ochre, red lettering, from Latin rubrīca (terra) red (earth), ruddle, from ruber red
