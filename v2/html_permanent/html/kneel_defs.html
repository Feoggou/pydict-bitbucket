kneel

(niːl )
Definitions
verbWord forms: kneels, kneeling, knelt or kneeled1. (intransitive) to rest, fall, or support oneself on one's kneesnoun2. the act or position of kneeling
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

kneeler (ˈkneeler) noun

Word Origin

Old English cnēowlian; see knee

kneel for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(niːl )
Word forms: kneels , kneeling , kneeled , knelt
Definitions
language note:   The forms kneeled and knelt can both be used for the past tense and past participle.verbWhen you kneel, you bend your legs so that your knees are touching the ground. [V prep/adv] ⇒ She knelt by the bed and prayed. [V] ⇒ Other people were kneeling, but she just sat. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a kneeling position.Kneel down means the same as kneel. [V P] ⇒ She kneeled down beside him.
