hero

(ˈhɪərəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -roes1. a man distinguished by exceptional courage, nobility, fortitude, etc2. a man who is idealized for possessing superior qualities in any field3. classical mythology a being of extraordinary strength and courage, often the offspring of a mortal and a god, who is celebrated for his exploits4. the principal male character in a novel, play, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin hērōs, from Greek

hero

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪəroʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: heroes
Definitions
1. countable nounThe hero of a book, play, film, or story is the main male character, who usually has good qualities. [+ of] ⇒ The hero of Doctor Zhivago dies in 1929. ⇒ ...the author's decision to make his hero a photographer.2. countable nounA hero is someone, especially a man, who has done something brave, new, or good, and who is therefore greatly admired by a lot of people. ⇒ He called Mr Mandela a hero who had inspired millions. ⇒ ...the goalscoring hero of the British hockey team. ⇒ They think you're some sort of hero.3. countable noun [usu sing, with poss]If you describe someone as your hero, you mean that you admire them a great deal, usually because of a particular quality or skill that they have. ⇒ My boyhood hero was Bobby Charlton. ⇒ No matter, he remained the hero of the crowds.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
