oratory1

(ˈɒrətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun1. the art of public speaking2. rhetorical skill or style
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

oratorical (ˌoraˈtorical) adjectiveoratorically (ˌoraˈtorically) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin (ars) ōrātōria (the art of) public speaking

oratory2

(ˈɒrətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun plural -riesa small room or secluded place, set apart for private prayer
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Anglo-Norman, from Church Latin ōrātōrium place of prayer, from ōrāre to plead, pray

Oratory

(ˈɒrətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun Roman Catholic Church1. Also called: Congregation of the Oratory the religious society of secular priests (Oratorians) living in a community founded by St Philip Neri2. any church belonging to this society ⇒ the Brompton Oratory
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
