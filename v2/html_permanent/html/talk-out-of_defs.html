talk out of

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you talk someone out of doing something they want or intend to do, you persuade them not to do it. [V n P P -ing/n] ⇒ My mother tried to talk me out of getting a divorce. [V n P P -ing/n] ⇒ People tried to talk him out of it, but he insisted.2. phrasal verbIf you talk yourself out of a particular situation or state, you get yourself out of it by talking. [V pron-refl P P n] ⇒ I tried to talk myself out of a fight. →  talk
