scud

(skʌd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: scuds, scudding or scudded1. (intransitive) (esp of clouds) to move along swiftly and smoothly2. (intransitive) nautical to run before a gale3. (transitive) Scottish to hit; slapnoun4. the act of scudding5. meteorologya. a formation of low fractostratus clouds driven by a strong wind beneath rain-bearing cloudsb. a sudden shower or gust of wind6. Scottish a slap

Word Origin

C16: probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian skudda to thrust, Swedish skudda to shake

Scud

(skʌd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
nouninformal a Soviet-made surface-to-surface missile, originally designed to carry nuclear warheads and with a range of 300 km; later modified to achieve greater range: used by Iraq in the Iran-Iraq War and in the Gulf Wars
