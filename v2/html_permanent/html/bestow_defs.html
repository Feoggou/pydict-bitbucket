bestow

(bɪˈstəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to present (a gift) or confer (an award or honour)2. archaic to apply (energy, resources, etc)3. archaic to house (a person) or store (goods)
Derived forms
bestowal (beˈstowal) or bestowment (beˈstowment) nounbestower (beˈstower) noun

verbTo bestow something on someone means to give or present it to them. [formal] [V n + on/upon] ⇒ The Queen has bestowed a knighthood on him. [Also V on/upon n n]
