inducement

(ɪnˈdjuːsmənt )
Definitions
noun1. the act of inducing2. a means of inducing; persuasion; incentive3. law (in pleading) the introductory part that leads up to and explains the matter in dispute

inducement

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndjuːsmənt , US -duːs- ) for learners of English
Word forms: inducements
Definitions
countable noun [oft N to-inf]If someone is offered an inducement to do something, they are given or promised gifts or benefits in order to persuade them to do it. ⇒ They offer every inducement to foreign businesses to invest in their states. ⇒ Various inducements are offered to encourage employees to wear safety clothing.
