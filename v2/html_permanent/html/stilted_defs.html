stilted

(ˈstɪltɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of speech, writing, etc) formal, pompous, or bombastic2. not flowing continuously or naturally ⇒ stilted conversation3. architecture (of an arch) having vertical piers between the impost and the springing

Derived Forms

stiltedly (ˈstiltedly) adverbstiltedness (ˈstiltedness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(stɪltɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone speaks in a stilted way, they speak in a formal or unnatural way, for example because they are not relaxed. ⇒ We made polite, stilted conversation. ⇒ His delivery was stilted and occasionally stumbling.
