muster

(ˈmʌstə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to call together (numbers of men) for duty, inspection, etc, or (of men) to assemble in this way2. USa. See muster inb. See muster out3. (transitive) Australian and New Zealand to round up (livestock)4. (transitive; sometimes foll by up) to summon or gather ⇒ to muster one's arguments, ⇒ to muster up couragenoun5. an assembly of military personnel for duty, inspection, etc6. a collection, assembly, or gathering7. Australian and New Zealand the rounding up of livestock8. a flock of peacocks9. See pass muster

Word Origin

C14: from old French moustrer, from Latin monstrāre to show, from monstrum portent, omen

muster

Powered by COBUILD
(mʌstəʳ )
Word forms: musters , mustering , mustered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you muster something such as support, strength, or energy, you gather as much of it as you can in order to do something. [V n] ⇒ He travelled around West Africa trying to muster support for his movement. [V n] ⇒ Mustering all her strength, Nancy pulled hard on both oars.2. ergative verb & verbWhen soldiers muster or are mustered, they gather together in one place in order to take part in a military action. [V] ⇒ The men mustered before their clan chiefs. [V n] ⇒ The general had mustered his troops north of the Hindu Kush.3. →  pass muster
