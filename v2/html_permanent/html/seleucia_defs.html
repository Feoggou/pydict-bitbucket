Seleucia

(sɪˈluːʃɪə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an ancient city in Mesopotamia, on the River Tigris: founded by Seleucus Nicator in 312 bc; became the chief city of the Seleucid empire; sacked by the Romans around 162 ad2. an ancient city in SE Asia Minor, on the River Calycadnus (modern Goksu Nehri): captured by the Turks in the 13th century; site of present-day Silifke (Turkey). Official name: Seleucia Tracheotis (ˌtrækɪˈəʊtɪs ), Seleucia Trachea (trəˈkɪə )3. an ancient port in Syria, on the River Orontes: the port of Antioch, of military importance during the wars between the Ptolemies and Seleucids; largely destroyed by earthquake in 526; site of present-day Samanda? (Turkey). Official name: Seleucia Pieria (paɪˈiːrɪə )
