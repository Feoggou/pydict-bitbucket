jovial

(ˈdʒəʊvɪəl )
Definitions
adjectivehaving or expressing convivial humour; jolly
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

joviality (ˌjoviˈality) or jovialness (ˈjovialness) nounjovially (ˈjovially) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin joviālis of (the planet) Jupiter, considered by astrologers to foster good humour

[written] ⇒ Father Whittaker appeared to be in a jovial mood. ⇒ Grandma was plump and jovial.joviality (dʒoʊviælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ ...his old expansive joviality.jovially graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'No problem,' he said jovially.
