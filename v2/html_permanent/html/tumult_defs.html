tumult

(ˈtjuːmʌlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a loud confused noise, as of a crowd; commotion2. violent agitation or disturbance3. great emotional or mental agitation

Word Origin

C15: from Latin tumultus, from tumēre to swell up

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. singular nounA tumult is a state of great confusion or excitement. [+ of] ⇒ A tumult of feelings inside her fought for supremacy. ⇒ ...the recent tumult in global financial markets.2. singular nounA tumult is a lot of noise made by a crowd of people. [+ of] ⇒ Round one ends, to a tumult of whistles, screams and shouts.
