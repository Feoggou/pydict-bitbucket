oppress

(əˈprɛs )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to subjugate by cruelty, force, etc2. to afflict or torment3. to lie heavy on (the mind, imagination, etc)4. an obsolete word for overwhelm
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

oppressingly (opˈpressingly) adverboppressor (opˈpressor) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Medieval Latin oppressāre, from Latin opprimere, from ob- against + premere to press

oppress for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(əpres )
Word forms: oppresses , oppressing , oppressed
Definitions
1. verbTo oppress people means to treat them cruelly, or to prevent them from having the same opportunities, freedom, and benefits as others. [be V-ed] ⇒ These people often are oppressed by the governments of the countries they find themselves in. [V n] ⇒ We are not normal like everybody else. If we were they wouldn't be oppressing us. [Also V, V n + with]2. verbIf something oppresses you, it makes you feel depressed, anxious, and uncomfortable. [literary] [V n] ⇒ The place oppressed Aubrey even before his eyes adjusted to the dark. [V n] ⇒ It was not just the weather which oppressed her.
