dreary

(ˈdrɪərɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: drearier or dreariest1. sad or dull; dismal2. wearying; boring3. archaic miserable
Derived forms
drearily (ˈdrearily) adverbdreariness (ˈdreariness) noun
Word origin
Old English drēorig gory; related to Old High German trūreg sad

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as dreary, you mean that it is dull and depressing. ⇒ ...a dreary little town in the Midwest. ⇒ They live such dreary lives.drearily graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ ...a drearily familiar scenario.
