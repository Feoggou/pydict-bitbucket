infantry

(ˈɪnfəntrɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -triesa. soldiers or units of soldiers who fight on foot with small armsb. (as modifier) ⇒ an infantry unit Abbreviation: Inf, inf
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Italian infanteria, from infante boy, foot soldier; see infant

infantry

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfəntri ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount noun [with sing or pl verb]Infantry are soldiers who fight on foot rather than in tanks or on horses. ⇒ The infantry were advancing to attack the ridge. ⇒ The enemy infantry was hiding. ⇒ ...an infantry division. ⇒ ...regiments of infantry.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'infantry'
He dodged outside and stared up, while half a regiment of infantry marched past behind him. Diana Wynne Jones THE MAGICIANS OF CAPRONA
