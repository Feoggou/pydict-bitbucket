diarrhoea

or especially US diarrhea (ˌdaɪəˈrɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounfrequent and copious discharge of abnormally liquid faeces
Derived forms
ˌdiarrhoeal (ˌdiarˈrhoeal), ˌdiarrhoeic (ˌdiarˈrhoeic) or especially US ˌdiarrheal (ˌdiarˈrheal) or ˌdiarrheic (ˌdiarˈrheic) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin, from Greek diarrhoia, from diarrhein to flow through, from dia- + rhein to flow
