fiscal

(ˈfɪskəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of or relating to government finances, esp tax revenues2. of or involving financial mattersnoun3. a. (in some countries) a public prosecutorb. Scottish short for procurator fiscal4. a postage or other stamp signifying payment of a tax
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fiscally (ˈfiscally) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin fiscālis concerning the state treasury, from fiscus public money; see fisc

Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Fiscal is used to describe something that relates to government money or public money, especially taxes. ⇒ ...in 1987, when the government tightened fiscal policy.fiscally adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV after v] ⇒ The scheme would be fiscally dangerous. ⇒ Many members are determined to prove that they are fiscally responsible.2. → See also procurator fiscal
