chuckle

(ˈtʃʌkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to laugh softly or to oneself2. (of animals, esp hens) to make a clucking soundnoun3. a partly suppressed laugh
Derived forms
chuckler (ˈchuckler) nounchucklingly (ˈchucklingly) adverb
Word origin
C16: probably from chuck3

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen you chuckle, you laugh quietly. [V] ⇒ The banker chuckled and said, 'Of course not.'. [V + at/over] ⇒ He chuckled at her forthrightness. [Also V with quote]Chuckle is also a noun. ⇒ He gave a little chuckle.
