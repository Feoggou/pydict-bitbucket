rejoice

(rɪˈdʒɔɪs )
Definitions
verb1. (when tr, takes a clause as object or an infinitive; when intr, often foll by in) to feel or express great joy or happiness2. (transitive) archaic to cause to feel joy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rejoicer (reˈjoicer) nounrejoicing (reˈjoicing) or rare rejoicement (reˈjoicement) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French resjoir, from re- + joir to be glad, from Latin gaudēre to rejoice

rejoice

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪdʒɔɪs ) for learners of English
Word forms: rejoices , rejoicing , rejoiced
Definitions
1. verbIf you rejoice, you are very pleased about something and you show it in your behaviour. [V + in/at] ⇒ Garbo plays the Queen, rejoicing in the love she has found with Antonio. [V in/at n] ⇒ A Foreign Ministry statement said that the French government rejoiced at the happy outcome to events. [V that] ⇒ Party activists in New Hampshire rejoiced that the presidential campaign had finally started. [Also V]rejoicing uncount noun [+ at] ⇒ There was general rejoicing at the news.2. →  rejoice in the name of
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
