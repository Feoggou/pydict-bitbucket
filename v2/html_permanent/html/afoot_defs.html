afoot

(əˈfʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective, adverb (postpositive)1. in circulation or operation; astir ⇒ mischief was afoot2. on or by foot

afoot

Powered by COBUILD
(əfʊt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that a plan or scheme is afoot, it is already happening or being planned, but you do not know much about it. ⇒ Everybody knew that something awful was afoot.
Example sentences containing 'afoot'
Everyone seemed to be afoot , making - she discovered - for the harbour and its old Venetian fortress. Curzon, Clare THE QUEST FOR KImportant things are afoot , things that require your presence.
