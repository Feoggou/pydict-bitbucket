bail out

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (intransitive) to make an emergency parachute jump from an aircraft2. (transitive) informal to help (a person, organization, etc) out of a predicament ⇒ the government bailed the company out3. (intransitive) informal to escape from a predicamentnoun bailout (ˈbeɪlaʊt)4. an act of bailing out, usually by the government, of a failing institution or business

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you bail someone out, you help them out of a difficult situation, often by giving them money. [V n P + of] ⇒ They will discuss how to bail the economy out of its slump. [VP n (not pron)] ⇒ He desperately needed cash to bail out the ailing restaurant. [VP n (not pron)] ⇒ The airline had losses of $35m and was bailed out by Qantas. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verbIf you bail someone out, you pay bail on their behalf. [V n P] ⇒ He has been jailed eight times. Each time, friends bailed him out. [Also V P n]3. phrasal verbIf a pilot bails out of an aircraft that is crashing, he or she jumps from it, using a parachute to land safely. [VP + of] ⇒ Reid was forced to bail out of the crippled aircraft. [VP] ⇒ The pilot bailed out safely.4. → see bail [sense 5] →  bail
