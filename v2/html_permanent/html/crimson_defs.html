crimson

(ˈkrɪmzən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a deep or vivid red colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ a crimson roseverb2. to make or become crimson3. (intransitive) to blush
Derived forms
crimsonness (ˈcrimsonness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old Spanish cremesin, from Arabic qirmizi red of the kermes, from qirmiz kermes

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. colourSomething that is crimson is deep red in colour. ⇒ ...a mass of crimson flowers. ⇒ ...the lurid, crimson glow of the blast furnaces.2. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If a person goes crimson, their face becomes red because they are angry or embarrassed. ⇒ I used to refuse invitations to parties because I knew I'd go crimson every time someone talked to me.
