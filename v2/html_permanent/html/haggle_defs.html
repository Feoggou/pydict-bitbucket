haggle

(ˈhæɡəl )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive; often foll by over) to bargain or wrangle (over a price, terms of an agreement, etc); barter2. (transitive) rare to hack
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

haggler (ˈhaggler) noun

Word Origin

C16: of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse haggva to hew

haggle

Powered by COBUILD
(hægəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: haggles , haggling , haggled
Definitions
verbIf you haggle, you argue about something before reaching an agreement, especially about the cost of something that you are buying. [V + with] ⇒ Ella taught her how to haggle with used furniture dealers. [V] ⇒ Meanwhile, as the politicians haggle, the violence worsens. [V] ⇒ Of course he'll still haggle over the price.haggling uncount noun ⇒ After months of haggling, they recovered only three-quarters of what they had lent.Haggle is also a noun. ⇒ She laughed again, enjoying the haggle.
