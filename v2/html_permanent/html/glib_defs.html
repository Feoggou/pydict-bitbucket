glib

(ɡlɪb )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: glibber or glibbestfluent and easy, often in an insincere or deceptive way

Derived Forms

glibly (ˈglibly) adverbglibness (ˈglibness) noun

Word Origin

glib

Powered by COBUILD
(glɪb ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe what someone says as glib, you disapprove of it because it implies that something is simple or easy, or that there are no problems involved, when this is not the case. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the glib talk of 'past misery'. ⇒ Mr. Lewis takes an insufferably glib attitude toward it all.glibly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ We talk glibly of equality of opportunity.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
