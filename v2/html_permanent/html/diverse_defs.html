diverse

(daɪˈvɜːs , ˈdaɪvɜːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having variety; assorted2. distinct in kind
Derived forms
diversely (diˈversely) adverbdiverseness (diˈverseness) noun
Word origin
C13: from Latin dīversus; see divers

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a group or range of things is diverse, it is made up of a wide variety of things. ⇒ ...shops selling a diverse range of gifts. ⇒ Society is now much more diverse than ever before.2. graded adjective & adjectiveDiverse people or things are very different from each other. ⇒ Jones has a much more diverse and perhaps younger audience.
