reside

(rɪˈzaɪd )
Definitions
verb (intransitive) formal1. to live permanently or for a considerable time (in a place); have one's home (in) ⇒ he now resides in London2. (of things, qualities, etc) to be inherently present (in); be vested (in) ⇒ political power resides in military strength
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

resider (reˈsider) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin residēre to sit back, from re- + sedēre to sit

(rɪzaɪd ) for learners of English
Word forms: resides , residing , resided
Definitions
1. verbIf someone resides somewhere, they live there or are staying there. [formal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ Margaret resides with her invalid mother in a London suburb. [V prep/adv] ⇒ All single full-time students must reside in university residence halls.2. verb [no cont]If a quality resides in something, the thing has that quality. [formal] [V + in] ⇒ Happiness does not reside in strength or money. [V in n] ⇒ The military is based on the principle that the ultimate authority resides in the armed forces.
