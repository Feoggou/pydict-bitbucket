annuity

(əˈnjuːɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. a fixed sum payable at specified intervals, esp annually, over a period, such as the recipient's life, or in perpetuity, in return for a premium paid either in instalments or in a single payment2. the right to receive or the duty to pay such a sum
Word origin
C15: from French annuité, from Medieval Latin annuitās, from Latin annuus annual
