rudimentary

(ˌruːdɪˈmɛntərɪ ) or less commonly rudimental
Definitions
adjective1. basic; fundamental; not elaborated or perfected2. incompletely developed; vestigial ⇒ rudimentary leaves
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rudimentarily (ˌrudiˈmentarily) or rare rudimentally (ˌrudiˈmentally) adverb

rudimentary

Powered by COBUILD
(ruːdɪmentri ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveRudimentary things are very basic or simple and are therefore unsatisfactory. [formal] ⇒ ...a kind of rudimentary kitchen. ⇒ They are deprived of the ability to exercise the most rudimentary workers' rights.2. graded adjective & adjectiveRudimentary knowledge includes only the simplest and most basic facts. [formal] ⇒ He had only a rudimentary knowledge of French. ⇒ ...a rudimentary grasp of economics.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
