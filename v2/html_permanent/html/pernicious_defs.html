pernicious

(pəˈnɪʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. wicked or malicious ⇒ pernicious lies2. causing grave harm; deadly

Derived Forms

perniciously (perˈniciously) adverbperniciousness (perˈniciousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin perniciōsus, from perniciēs ruin, from per- (intensive) + nex death

pernicious

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as pernicious, you mean that it is very harmful. [formal] ⇒ I did what I could, but her mother's influence was pernicious. ⇒ There is a pernicious culture of excellence: everything has to be not merely good but the best.
Example sentences containing 'pernicious'
The site was no more than a hundred and fifty yards ahead of him, and now with every step the Iad's pernicious influence grew. Clive Barker EVERVILLEIf the boy is being stuffed full of pernicious rubbish, someone has to present a more intelligent view. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCE`The British Government would never be guilty of such a pernicious act. Maclean, Alistair SAN ANDREASSo far as he could recall, these were the first inhabitants of the Stoienzar he had encountered who did not seem inherently pernicious. Robert Silverberg LORD PRESTIMION
