disadvantage

(ˌdɪsədˈvɑːntɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an unfavourable circumstance, state of affairs, thing, person, etc2. injury, loss, or detriment3. an unfavourable condition or situation (esp in the phrase at a disadvantage)verb4. (transitive) to put at a disadvantage; handicap

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA disadvantage is a factor which makes someone or something less useful, acceptable, or successful than other people or things. [+ of] ⇒ His two main rivals suffer the disadvantage of having been long-term political exiles. [+ of] ⇒ ...the advantages and disadvantages of allowing priests to marry.2. →  at a disadvantage3. →  to sb's disadvantage
