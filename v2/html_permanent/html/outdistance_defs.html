(ˌaʊtˈdɪstəns )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to leave far behind

outdistance for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtdɪstəns )
Word forms: outdistances , outdistancing , outdistanced
Definitions
1. verbIf you outdistance someone, you are a lot better and more successful than they are at a particular activity over a period of time. [V n] ⇒ It didn't matter that Ingrid had outdistanced them as a movie star.2. verbIf you outdistance your opponents in a contest of some kind, you beat them easily. [V n] ⇒ ...a millionaire businessman who easily outdistanced his major rivals for the nomination.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
