scrap1

(skræp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small piece of something larger; fragment2. an extract from something written3. a. waste material or used articles, esp metal, often collected and reprocessedb. (as modifier) ⇒ scrap iron4. (plural) pieces of discarded foodverbWord forms: scraps, scrapping or scrapped (transitive)5. to make into scrap6. to discard as useless

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse skrap; see scrape

scrap

Powered by COBUILD
(skræp )
Word forms: scraps , scrapping , scrapped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA scrap of something is a very small piece or amount of it. [+ of] ⇒ A crumpled scrap of paper was found in her handbag. ⇒ ...a fire fuelled by scraps of wood. [+ of] ⇒ They need every scrap of information they can get.2. plural nounScraps are pieces of unwanted food which are thrown away or given to animals. leftovers ⇒ ...the scraps from the Sunday dinner table.3. verbIf you scrap something, you get rid of it or cancel it. [informal, journalism] [V n] ⇒ President Hussein called on all countries in the Middle East to scrap nuclear or chemical weapons. [V n] ⇒ It had been thought that passport controls would be scrapped.4. adjective [ADJ n]Scrap metal or paper is no longer wanted for its original purpose, but may have some other use. ⇒ There's always tons of scrap paper in Dad's office.5. uncount nounScrap is metal from old or damaged machinery or cars. ⇒ Thousands of tanks, artillery pieces and armored vehicles will be cut up for scrap.6. countable nounYou can refer to a fight or a quarrel as a scrap, especially if it is not very serious. [informal] fight ⇒ Billy Bonds has never been one to avoid a scrap.
