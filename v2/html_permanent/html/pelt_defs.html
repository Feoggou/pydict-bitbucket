pelt1

(pɛlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to throw (missiles) at (a person)2. (transitive) to hurl (insults) at (a person)3. (intr; foll by along, over, etc) to move rapidly; hurry4. (intransitive; often foll by down) to rain heavilynoun5. a blow6. speed (esp in the phrase at full pelt)

Derived Forms

pelter (ˈpelter) noun

Word Origin

C15: of uncertain origin, perhaps from pellet

pelt2

(pɛlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the skin of a fur-bearing animal, such as a mink, esp when it has been removed from the carcass2. the hide of an animal, stripped of hair and ready for tanning

Word Origin

C15: perhaps back formation from peltry

pelt

Powered by COBUILD
(pelt )
Word forms: pelts , pelting , pelted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]The pelt of an animal is its skin, which can be used to make clothing or rugs. ⇒ ...a bed covered with beaver pelts. ⇒ ...rapidly diminishing suppliers of furs and pelts.2. verbIf you pelt someone with things, you throw things at them. [V n + with] ⇒ Some of the younger men began to pelt one another with snowballs. [V n with n] ⇒ Crowds started to pelt police cars with stones.3. verb [usu cont]If the rain is pelting down, or if it is pelting with rain, it is raining very hard. [informal] pouring [V adv] ⇒ The rain now was pelting down. [V with n] ⇒ It's pelting with rain. [V-ing] ⇒ We drove through pelting rain.4. verbIf you pelt somewhere, you run there very fast. [informal] [V prep] ⇒ Without thinking, she pelted down the stairs in her nightgown.5. →  full pelt/at full pelt
