sundry

(ˈsʌndrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
determiner1. several or various; miscellaneouspronoun2. See all and sundrynoun plural -dries3. (plural) miscellaneous unspecified items4. also called: extra Australian cricket a run not scored from the bat, such as a wide, no-ball, bye, or leg bye

Word Origin

Old English syndrig separate; related to Old High German suntarīg; see sunder, -y1

sundry

Powered by COBUILD
(sʌndri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]If someone refers to sundry people or things, they are referring to several people or things that are all different from each other. [formal] ⇒ Scientists, business people, and sundry others gathered on Monday for the official opening. ⇒ She could ring for food and drink, laundry and sundry services.2. →  all and sundry
Example sentences containing 'sundry'
More trolleys arrived and the group converged, selected, sampled and chatted to all and sundry. Low, Ona TO HIS JUST DESSERTSIt is only to be expected: we added one of our more specialized drugs to sundry items in your refrigerator. Pritchard, John NIGHT SISTERSSatisfied by Evelyn's reaction, she proceeded to point out sundry other striking features. Brent-Dyer, Elinor CHALLENGE FOR THE CHALET SCHOOL"You refer, I presume, to the precipitation of sundry objects on Ramses," said Emerson, buttering another piece of toast. Elizabeth Peters LORD OF THE SILENT
