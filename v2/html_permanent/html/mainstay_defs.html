Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. nautical the forestay that braces the mainmast2. a chief support

mainstay

Powered by COBUILD
(meɪnsteɪ )
Word forms: mainstays
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounIf you describe something as the mainstay of a particular thing, you mean that it is the most basic part of it. ⇒ Fish and rice were the mainstays of the country's diet. ⇒ This principle of collective bargaining has been a mainstay in labor relations in this country.
Example sentences containing 'mainstay'
Not just the village, though that's our mainstay , especially at midday. Penn, John WIDOW'S ENDJust as she'd been since the day we met, Florence was the mainstay of my life. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFUntil the advent of synthetic dyes, woad was cultivated in great plantations that were for a time a mainstay in some colonial economies. Tony Juniper SPIX'S MACAW: THE RACE TO SAVE THE WORLD'S RAREST BIRDIn this case, Cesare reasoned, the truth would deny the very quality that was the mainstay of the papacy. Mario Puzo THE FAMILY
