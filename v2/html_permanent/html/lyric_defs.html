lyric

(ˈlɪrɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. (of poetry)a. expressing the writer's personal feelings and thoughtsb. having the form and manner of a song2. of or relating to such poetry3. (of music) having songlike qualities4. (of a singing voice) having a light quality and tone5. intended for singing, esp (in classical Greece) to the accompaniment of the lyrenoun6. a short poem of songlike quality7. (plural) the words of a popular song
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lyrically (ˈlyrically) adverblyricalness (ˈlyricalness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin lyricus, from Greek lurikos, from lura lyre

lyric

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪrɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: lyrics
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]The lyrics of a song are its words. [+ by] ⇒ ...Kurt Weill's Broadway opera with lyrics by Langston Hughes.2. adjective [ADJ n]Lyric poetry is written in a simple and direct style, and usually expresses personal emotions such as love. ⇒ ...Lawrence's splendid short stories and lyric poetry.
