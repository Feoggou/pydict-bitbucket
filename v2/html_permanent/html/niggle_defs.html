niggle

(ˈnɪɡəl )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to find fault continually2. (intransitive) to be preoccupied with details; fuss3. (transitive) to irritate; worrynoun4. a slight or trivial objection or complaint5. a slight feeling as of misgiving, uncertainty, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

niggler (ˈniggler) nounniggly (ˈniggly) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Scandinavian; related to Norwegian nigla. Compare niggard

niggle for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪgəl )
Word forms: niggles , niggling , niggled
Definitions
1. verbIf something niggles you, it causes you to worry slightly over a long period of time. [mainly British] [V n] ⇒ I realise now that the things which used to niggle and annoy me just don't really matter. [V + at] ⇒ It's been niggling at my mind ever since I met Neville in Nice. [V away] ⇒ The puzzle niggled away in Arnold's mind. [Also V]Niggle is also a noun. ⇒ So why is there a little niggle at the back of my mind?2. verbIf someone niggles you, they annoy you by continually criticizing you for what you think are small or unimportant things. [mainly British] [V n] ⇒ I don't react anymore when opponents try to niggle me. [V + at] ⇒ You tend to niggle at your partner, and get hurt when he doesn't hug you. [Also V, V n that]Niggle is also a noun. ⇒ The life we have built together is more important than any minor niggle either of us might have.
