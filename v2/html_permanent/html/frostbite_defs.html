frostbite

(ˈfrɒstˌbaɪt )
Definitions
noun1. destruction of tissues, esp those of the fingers, ears, toes, and nose, by freezing, characterized by tingling, blister formation, and gangrene2. New Zealand a type of small sailing dinghy

frostbite

Powered by COBUILD
(frɒstbaɪt , US frɔːst- ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounFrostbite is a condition in which parts of your body, such as your fingers or toes, become seriously damaged as a result of being very cold. ⇒ The survivors suffered from frostbite.
