consummate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈkɒnsəˌmeɪt ) (transitive)1. to bring to completion or perfection; fulfil2. to complete (a marriage) legally by sexual intercourseadjective (kənˈsʌmɪt , ˈkɒnsəmɪt )3. accomplished or supremely skilled ⇒ a consummate artist4. (prenominal) (intensifier) ⇒ a consummate fool
Derived forms
consummately (conˈsummately) adverbˌconsummation (ˌconsumˈmation) nounconsumˌmative (ˈconsumˌmative) or consummatory (kənˈsʌmətərɪ ) adjectiveconsumˌmator (ˈconsumˌmator) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin consummāre to complete, from summus highest, utmost

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The adjective is pronounced (kɒnsʌmət ). The verb is pronounced (kɒnsəmeɪt ).1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use consummate to describe someone who is extremely skilful. [formal] ⇒ He acted the part with consummate skill. ⇒ Those familiar with Sanders call him a consummate politician.consummately graded adverb ⇒ The film is a well made, atmospheric, consummately acted piece.2. verbIf two people consummate a marriage or relationship, they make it complete by having sex. [formal] [V n] ⇒ They consummated their passion only after many hesitations and delays.consummation (kɒnsəmeɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...the morning after the consummation of their marriage.3. verbTo consummate an agreement means to complete it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ There have been several close calls, but no one has been able to consummate a deal.
