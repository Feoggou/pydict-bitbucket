none1

(nʌn )
Definitions
pronoun1. not any of a particular class ⇒ none of my letters has arrived2. no-one; nobody ⇒ there was none to tell the tale3. no part (of a whole); not any (of) ⇒ none of it looks edible4. See none other5. See none the6. See none too▶ USAGE None is a singular pronoun and should be used with a singular form of a verb: none of the students has (not have) a car
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English nān, literally: not one

none for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nʌn )
Definitions
1. quantifierNone of something means not even a small amount of it. None of a group of people or things means not even one of them. [+ of] ⇒ She did none of the maintenance on the vehicle itself. [+ of] ⇒ None of us knew how to treat her.None is also a pronoun. ⇒ I turned to bookshops and libraries seeking information and found none. ⇒ No one could imagine a great woman painter. None had existed yet. ⇒ Only two cars produced by Austin-Morris could reach 100 mph and none could pass the 10-second acceleration test.2. →  have none of3. →  none but4. →  none too5. →  none the6. → none of your business7. → none other than8. → second to none
