disinclination

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪsɪnklɪneɪʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular noun [usu N to-inf]A disinclination to do something is a feeling that you do not want to do it. [formal] ⇒ They are showing a marked disinclination to pursue these opportunities.
Example sentences containing 'disinclination'
Her disinclination for movement reinforced a mummified appearance. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEOne of his East End Jewish tendencies was an extreme disinclination to part with money, and Markham knew it. Loraine, Philip LOADED QUESTIONSEven so, Sabriel had a strong disinclination to enter the realm of the dead until she absolutely had to. Garth Nix SABRIEL
