imposture

(ɪmˈpɒstʃə )
Definitions
nounthe act or an instance of deceiving others, esp by assuming a false identity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impostrous (ɪmˈpɒstrəs ), impostorous (ɪmˈpɒstərəs ) or imposturous (imˈposturous) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from French, from Late Latin impostūra, from Latin impōnere; see impose

No, instead the two of them went to Paris where they could try out the imposture on the De Chantals first. Harcourt, Palma DOUBLE DECEITOh,'tis imposture all: And as no chemic yet the elixir got, Edward Docx THE CALLIGRAPHER"I was going to say no, but then I remembered my foolish imposture and said yes and he gave me an envelope.
