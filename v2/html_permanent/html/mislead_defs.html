mislead

(mɪsˈliːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -leads, -leading or -led (transitive)1. to give false or misleading information to2. to lead or guide in the wrong direction

Derived Forms

Word forms: misleads , misleading , misled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that someone has misled you, you mean that they have made you believe something which is not true, either by telling you a lie or by giving you a wrong idea or impression. [V n] ⇒ Jack was furious with his London doctors for having misled him. [V n] ⇒ Ministers must not knowingly mislead Parliament and the public.
Example sentences containing 'mislead'
