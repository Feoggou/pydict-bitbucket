Saturday

(ˈsætədɪ , -deɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe seventh and last day of the week: the Jewish Sabbath

Word Origin

Old English sæternes dæg, translation of Latin Sāturnī diēs day of Saturn; compare Middle Dutch saterdach, Dutch zaterdag

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounSaturday is the day after Friday and before Sunday. ⇒ She had a call from him on Saturday morning at the studio. ⇒ They had a 3-1 win against Liverpool last Saturday. ⇒ The overnight train runs only on Saturdays. ⇒ It was Saturday evening and I was getting ready to go out.
Example sentences containing 'Saturday'
Then on Saturday morning, Tamara abruptly announced that she had decided to leave, ostensibly to avoid the rush of Sunday traffic. Laurie R. King FOLLY
