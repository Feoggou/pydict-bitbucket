AD

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
abbreviation for1. (indicating years numbered from the supposed year of the birth of Christ) anno Domini ⇒ 70 ad. Compare BC2. military active duty3. military air defence4. Dame of the Order of Australia▶ USAGE In strict usage, ad is only employed with specific years: he died in 1621 ad, but he died in the 17th century (and not the 17th century ad). Formerly the practice was to write ad preceding the date (ad 1621), and it is also strictly correct to omit in when ad is used, since this is already contained in the meaning of the Latin anno Domini (in the year of Our Lord), but this is no longer general practice. bc is used with both specific dates and indications of the period: Heraclitus was born about 540 bc; the battle took place in the 4th century bc
Word origin
(sense 4) Latin: in the year of the Lord

AD

Powered by COBUILD
(eɪ diː )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
You use AD in dates to indicate the number of years or centuries that have passed since the year in which Jesus Christ is believed to have been born. Compare BC. ⇒ The cathedral was destroyed by the Great Fire of 1136 AD. ⇒ The original castle was probably built about AD 860. ⇒ The Roman Empire was divided in the fourth century AD.
