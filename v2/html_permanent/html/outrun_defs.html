outrun

(ˌaʊtˈrʌn )
Definitions
verb -runs, -running, -ran or -run (transitive)1. to run faster, farther, or better than2. to escape from by or as if by running3. to go beyond; exceed

outrun for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtrʌn )
Word forms: outruns , outrunning , outran
Definitions
language note:   The form outrun is used in the present tense and is also the past participle of the verb.1. verbIf you outrun someone, you run faster than they do, and therefore are able to escape from them or to arrive somewhere before they do. [V n] ⇒ There are not many players who can outrun me.2. verbIf one thing outruns another thing, the first thing develops faster than the second thing. [V n] ⇒ Spending could outrun the capacity of businesses to produce the goods.
