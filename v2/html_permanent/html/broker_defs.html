broker

(ˈbrəʊkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an agent who, acting on behalf of a principal, buys or sells goods, securities, etc, in return for a commission ⇒ insurance broker2. (formerly) short for stockbroker3. a dealer in second-hand goodsverb4. to act as a broker (in)
Word origin
C14: from Anglo-French brocour broacher (of casks, hence, one who sells, agent), from Old Northern French broquier to tap a cask, from broque tap of a cask; see broach1
