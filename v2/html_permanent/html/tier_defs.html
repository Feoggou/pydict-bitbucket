tier1

(tɪə )
Definitions
noun1. one of a set of rows placed one above and behind the other, such as theatre seats2. a. a layer or levelb. (in combination) ⇒ a three-tier cake3. a rank, order, or rowverb4. to be or arrange in tiers
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old French tire rank, of Germanic origin; compare Old English tīr embellishment

tier

Powered by COBUILD
(tɪəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: tiers
Definitions
1. countable nounA tier is a row or layer of something that has other layers above or below it. [+ of] ⇒ ...the auditorium with the tiers of seats around and above it.Tier is also a combining form. ⇒ ...a three-tier wedding cake.2. countable nounA tier is a level in an organization or system. [+ of] ⇒ Islanders have campaigned for the abolition of one of the three tiers of municipal power on the island.Tier is also a combining form. ⇒ ...the possibility of a two-tier system of universities.
