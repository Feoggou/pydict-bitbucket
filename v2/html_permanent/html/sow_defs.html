sow1

(səʊ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: sows, sowing, sowed, sown or sowed1. to scatter or place (seed, a crop, etc) in or on (a piece of ground, field, etc) so that it may grow ⇒ to sow wheat, ⇒ to sow a strip of land2. (transitive) to implant or introduce ⇒ to sow a doubt in someone's mind

Derived Forms

sowable (ˈsowable) adjectivesower (ˈsower) noun

Word Origin

Old English sāwan; related to Old Norse sā, Old High German sāen, Old Slavonic seja, Latin serere to sow

sow2

(saʊ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a female adult pig2. the female of certain other animals, such as the mink3. metallurgya. the channels for leading molten metal to the moulds in casting pig ironb. iron that has solidified in these channels

Word Origin

Old English sugu; related to Old Norse sӯr, Old High German sū, Latin sūs, Norwegian sugga, Dutch zeug: see swine
