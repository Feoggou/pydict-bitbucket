wealth

(wɛlθ )
Definitions
noun1. a large amount of money and valuable material possessions2. the state of being rich3. a great profusion ⇒ a wealth of gifts4. economics all goods and services with monetary, exchangeable, or productive value
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wealthless (ˈwealthless) adjective

Word Origin

C13 welthe, from weal2; related to well1

wealth for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(welθ )
Definitions
1. uncount nounWealth is the possession of a large amount of money, property, or other valuable things. You can also refer to a particular person's money or property as their wealth. ⇒ Economic reform has brought relative wealth to peasant farmers. ⇒ His own wealth grew.2. singular nounIf you say that someone or something has a wealth of good qualities or things, you are emphasizing that they have a very large number or amount of them. [formal, emphasis] [+ of] ⇒ ...such a wealth of creative expertise. ⇒ The city boasts a wealth of beautiful churches.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
