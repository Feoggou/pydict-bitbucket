visor

or vizor (ˈvaɪzə )
Definitions
noun1. a transparent flap on a helmet that can be pulled down to protect the face2. a piece of armour fixed or hinged to the helmet to protect the face and with slits for the eyes3. another name for peak (sense 6)4. a small movable screen used as protection against glare from the sun, esp one attached above the windscreen of a motor vehicle5. archaic or literary a mask or any other means of disguise or concealmentverb6. (transitive) to cover, provide, or protect with a visor; shield
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

visored (ˈvisored) or vizored (ˈvizored) adjectivevisorless (ˈvisorless) or vizorless (ˈvizorless) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Anglo-French viser, from Old French visiere, from vis face; see visage

Definitions
1. countable nounA visor is a movable part of a helmet which can be pulled down to protect a person's eyes or face. ⇒ He pulled on a battered old crash helmet with a scratched visor.2. countable noun [usu n N]A visor is a piece of plastic or other material fixed above the windscreen inside a car, which can be turned down to protect the driver's eyes from bright sunshine. ⇒ I put down the sun visor to shade my eyes from the light.
