dote

or now rarely doat (dəʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (foll by on or upon) to love to an excessive or foolish degree2. to be foolish or weak-minded, esp as a result of old age
Derived forms
doter (ˈdoter) or now rarely doater (ˈdoater) noun
Word origin
C13: related to Middle Dutch doten to be silly, Norwegian dudra to shake

dote

Powered by COBUILD
(doʊt )
Word forms: dotes , doting , doted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that someone dotes on a person or a thing, you mean that they love or care about them very much and ignore any faults they may have. [V + on/upon] ⇒ He dotes on his nine-year-old son.
Example sentences containing 'dote'
Hablet was quite certain he would find it much easier to dote on his daughter from a distance. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPThe teller of those tales, Old Zelim, was not the only man to dote on Samarkand, a city he had never seen. Clive Barker GALILEEAs for Dumuzzi, he seemed to dote on my father thereafter; as though that night they'd become brothers. Clive Barker GALILEEEven good old loyal Lindsay Snow had rounded on him once and said, angrily, "You dote on every word she says! Jacquelyn Mitchard A THEORY OF RELATIVITY
