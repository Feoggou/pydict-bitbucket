tyke

or tike (taɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a dog, esp a mongrel2. informal a small or cheeky child: used esp in affectionate reproof3. British dialect a rough ill-mannered person4. Also called: Yorkshire tyke British slang, often offensive a person from Yorkshire5. Australian slang, offensive a Roman Catholic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse tīk bitch

Comment by Bohdan0 on 19 Feb 2015
Why should people exclusively from Yorkshire have to be associated with the term? Of course there's the stereotype reiterated in 'All Creatures Great and Small', but tykes can be found all over Britain & Ireland, while almost certainly the overwhelming majority of Yorkshire people are smashing, great people. So, dear, kind Collins, wonderful propagators of real British English, for Scots, Welsh and Irish as well as the rest, please drop it. Otherwise, at the very least, why not define the correlation (probably invented by English 'gentlemen' of the South who while visiting deservingly got an earful) as being anachronistic.
