Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV after v] [be ADV]You use anywhere in statements with negative meaning to indicate that a place does not exist. ⇒ I haven't got anywhere to live. ⇒ There had never been such a beautiful woman anywhere in the world.2. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV after v] [be ADV, from ADV]You use anywhere in questions and conditional clauses to ask or talk about a place without saying exactly where you mean. ⇒ Did you try to get help from anywhere? ⇒ If she wanted to go anywhere at all she had to wait for her father to drive her.3. indefinite adverb & adverbYou use anywhere before words that indicate the kind of place you are talking about. ⇒ In America most leisure-time activities are about a million times better than anywhere else. ⇒ He'll meet you anywhere you want. ⇒ Let us know if you come across anywhere that has something special to offer.4. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV after v, be ADV]You use anywhere to refer to a place when you are emphasizing that it could be any of a large number of places. [emphasis] ⇒ Rachel would have known Julia Stone anywhere. ⇒ ...jokes that are so funny they always work anywhere.5. indefinite adverb & adverbWhen you do not want to be exact, you use anywhere to refer to a particular range of things. [+ from] ⇒ His shoes cost anywhere from $200 up. [+ from] ⇒ My visits lasted anywhere from three weeks to two months. [Also + between/to]6. indefinite adverb & adverb [ADV adj/adv]You use anywhere in expressions such as anywhere near and anywhere close to to emphasize a statement that you are making. [emphasis] ⇒ There weren't anywhere near enough empty boxes. ⇒ The only one who's anywhere close to the truth is my mother.7. →  not be getting anywhere/not be going anywhere
