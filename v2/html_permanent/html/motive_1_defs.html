motive

(ˈməʊtɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the reason for a certain course of action, whether conscious or unconscious2. a variant of motif (sense 2)adjective3. of or causing motion or action ⇒ a motive force4. of or acting as a motive; motivatingverb (transitive)5. to motivate

Derived Forms

motiveless (ˈmotiveless) adjectivemotivelessly (ˈmotivelessly) adverbmotivelessness (ˈmotivelessness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French motif, from Late Latin mōtīvus (adj) moving, from Latin mōtus, past participle of movēre to move
