ferule1

(ˈfɛruːl , -rəl)
Definitions
noun1. a flat piece of wood, such as a ruler, used in some schools to cane children on the handverb2. (transitive) rare to punish with a ferule
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ferula giant fennel, whip, rod; the stalk of the plant was used for punishment
