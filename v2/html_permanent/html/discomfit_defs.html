discomfit

(dɪsˈkʌmfɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to make uneasy, confused, or embarrassed2. to frustrate the plans or purpose of3. archaic to defeat in battle
Derived forms
discomfiter (disˈcomfiter) noundiscomfiture (disˈcomfiture) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French desconfire to destroy, from des- (indicating reversal) + confire to make, from Latin conficere to produce; see confect
