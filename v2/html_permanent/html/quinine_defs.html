quinine

(kwɪˈniːn , US English ˈkwaɪnaɪn)
Definitions
nouna bitter crystalline alkaloid extracted from cinchona bark, the salts of which are used as a tonic, antipyretic, analgesic, etc, and in malaria therapy. Formula: C20H24N2O2
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Spanish quina cinchona bark, from Quechua kina bark
