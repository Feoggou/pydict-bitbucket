sequent

(ˈsiːkwənt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. following in order or succession2. following as a result; consequentnoun3. something that follows; consequence4. logic a formal representation of an argument. The inference of A from A & B is written A & B ⊢ A. The sequent ⊢ A represents the derivation of A from no assumptions and thus indicates that A is a theorem

Derived Forms

sequently (ˈsequently) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin sequēns, from sequī to follow
