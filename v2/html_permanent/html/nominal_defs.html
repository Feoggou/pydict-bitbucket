nominal

(ˈnɒmɪnəl )
Definitions
adjective1. in name only; theoretical ⇒ the nominal leader2. minimal in comparison with real worth or what is expected; token ⇒ a nominal fee3. of, relating to, constituting, bearing, or giving a name4. grammar of or relating to a noun or noun phrasenoun5. grammar a nominal element; a noun, noun phrase, or syntactically similar structure6. bell-ringing the harmonic an octave above the strike tone of a bell
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nominally (ˈnominally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin nōminālis of a name, from nōmen name

nominal for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒmɪnəl )
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use nominal to indicate that someone or something is supposed to have a particular identity or status, but in reality does not have it. ⇒ As he was still not allowed to run a company, his wife became its nominal head. ⇒ I was brought up a nominal Christian.nominally adverb [oft ADV before v] ⇒ The Sultan was still nominally the Chief of Staff. ⇒ ...South Africa's nominally independent homeland of Transkei. ⇒ Nominally she is the king's prisoner.2. adjective [ADJ n]A nominal price or sum of money is very small in comparison with the real cost or value of the thing that is being bought or sold. ⇒ I am prepared to sell my shares at a nominal price. ⇒ All the ferries carry bicycles free or for a nominal charge.3. adjective [ADJ n]In economics, the nominal value, rate, or level of something is the one expressed in terms of current prices or figures, without taking into account general changes in prices that take place over time. ⇒ Inflation would be lower and so nominal rates would be rather more attractive in real terms. ⇒ In 1990 personal incomes grew a nominal 6.8 per cent.
