blemish

(ˈblɛmɪʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a defect; flaw; stainverb2. (transitive) to flaw the perfection of; spoil; tarnish
Word origin
C14: from Old French blemir to make pale, probably of Germanic origin

blemish

Powered by COBUILD
(blemɪʃ )
Word forms: blemishes , blemishing , blemished
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA blemish is a small mark on something that spoils its appearance. ⇒ Every piece is closely scrutinised, and if there is the slightest blemish on it, it is rejected.2. countable nounA blemish on something is a small fault in it. [+ on] ⇒ This is the one blemish on an otherwise resounding success.3. verbIf something blemishes someone's character or reputation, it spoils it or makes it seem less good than it was in the past. [V n] ⇒ He wasn't about to blemish that pristine record.
Example sentences containing 'blemish'
His character appeared to be without blemish but then she missed a lot owing to the restrictions her position imposed upon her. Fraser, Christine Marion NOBLE BEGINNNINGSMaybe she sees the bloodshed that will blemish the religion to bear Christ's name. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEHis secretary was beautiful and stony; matching scarlet lipstick and nails, every dermatological blemish concealed. Mark Burnell CHAMELEONHer toenails were perfect, unpainted, carefully but not, I judged, professionally manicured, skin without blemish. Greg Bear VITALS
