underpin

(ˌʌndəˈpɪn )
Definitions
verb -pins, -pinning or -pinned (transitive)1. to support from beneath, esp by a prop, while avoiding damaging or weakening the superstructure ⇒ to underpin a wall2. to give corroboration, strength, or support to

Definitions
verbIf one thing underpins another, it helps the other thing to continue or succeed by supporting and strengthening it. [V n] ⇒ ...mystical themes that underpin all religions. [V n] ⇒ ...the beliefs underpinning contemporary art. [V n] ⇒ ...a style of life extensively underpinned by public money.underpinning Word forms: underpinnings variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the economic underpinning of ancient Mexican society.
