brilliance

(ˈbrɪljəns ) or brilliancy
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. great brightness; radiance2. excellence or distinction in physical or mental ability; exceptional talent3. splendour; magnificence ⇒ the brilliance of the royal court4. physics a former term for luminance
Example sentences containing 'brilliance'
The beam from the car was assaulting the tower of the parish church with an unnatural brilliance. Hilton, John Buxton PASSION IN THE PEAKThrough the burning sand, and the dry, oxygen-rich air, there sparkled the hard brilliance of hot, white sunlight. Asimov, Isaac THE COMPLETE STORIES VOLUME 2Emotional intelligence was the key and Nick, for all his brilliance as a businessman, had none. Cathy Kelly JUST BETWEEN USIt was there in his voice, in the brilliance of his eyes, in the springy way he moved. Elizabeth Lowell AMBERBEACH
