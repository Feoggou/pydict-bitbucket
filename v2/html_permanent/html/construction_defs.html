construction

(kənˈstrʌkʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the process or act of constructing or manner in which a thing is constructed2. the thing constructed; a structure3. a. the business or work of building dwellings, offices, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ a construction site4. an interpretation or explanation of a law, text, action, etc ⇒ they put a sympathetic construction on her behaviour5. grammar a group of words that together make up one of the constituents into which a sentence may be analysed; a phrase or clause6. geometry a drawing of a line, angle, or figure satisfying certain conditions, used in solving a problem or proving a theorem7. an abstract work of art in three dimensions or relief. See also constructivism (sense 1)
Derived forms
constructional (conˈstructional) adjectiveconstructionally (conˈstructionally) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounConstruction is the building of things such as houses, factories, roads, and bridges. ⇒ He'd already started construction on a hunting lodge. ⇒ ...the only nuclear power station under construction in Britain. ⇒ ...the downturn in the construction industry. ⇒ Quincy wants a job in construction.2. uncount nounThe construction of something such as a vehicle or machine is the making of it. [+ of] ⇒ ...companies who have long experience in the construction of those types of equipment. ⇒ With the exception of teak, this is the finest wood for boat construction.3. uncount noun [with poss]The construction of something such as a system is the creation of it. [+ of] ⇒ ...the construction of a just system of criminal justice.4. countable nounYou can refer to an object that has been built or made as a construction. ⇒ The British pavilion is an impressive steel and glass construction the size of Westminster Abbey.5. uncount nounYou use construction to refer to the structure of something and the way it has been built or made. ⇒ The Shakers believed that furniture should be plain, simple, useful, practical and of sound construction. ⇒ The chairs were light in construction yet extremely strong.6. countable noun [usu sing]The construction that you put on what someone says or does is your interpretation of what it means. interpretation ⇒ The denial was limited to rejecting the construction put on his remarks. ⇒ He put the wrong construction on what he saw.7. countable nounA grammatical construction is a particular arrangement of words in a sentence, clause, or phrase. ⇒ Avoid complex verbal constructions.
