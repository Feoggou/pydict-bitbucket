thwart

(θwɔːt )
Definitions
verb1. to oppose successfully or prevent; frustrate ⇒ they thwarted the plan2. obsolete to be or move acrossnoun3. nautical a seat lying across a boat and occupied by an oarsmanadjective4. passing or being situated across5. archaic perverse or stubbornpreposition, adverb6. obsolete across
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

thwartedly (ˈthwartedly) adverbthwarter (ˈthwarter) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse thvert, from thverr transverse; related to Old English thweorh crooked, Old High German twerh transverse

Definitions
verbIf you thwart someone or thwart their plans, you prevent them from doing or getting what they want. [V n] ⇒ The accounting firm deliberately destroyed documents to thwart government investigators. [V n] ⇒ Her ambition to become an artist was thwarted by failing eyesight.
