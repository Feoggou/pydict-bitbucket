parched

(pɑːtʃt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. dried up as a result of being deprived of water ⇒ Rainfall in this parched landscape can seem so miraculous that some people call it God water., ⇒ The clouds gathered and showers poured down upon the parched earth., ⇒ a hill of parched brown grass2. unpleasantly dry ⇒ He licked his lips to moisten his parched mouth., ⇒ She touched her wet fingertips to her parched lips., ⇒ If your skin is parched and flaky, moisturize it with balms and creams.3. informal very thirsty ⇒ I'm parched!

parched

(pɑːʳtʃt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something, especially the ground or a plant, is parched, it is very dry, because there has been no rain. ⇒ The clouds gathered and showers poured down upon the parched earth. ⇒ ...a hill of parched brown grass.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf your mouth, throat, or lips are parched, they are unpleasantly dry.3. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that you are parched, you mean that you are very thirsty. [informal]
