woeful

(ˈwəʊfəl ) or woesome (ˈwəʊsəm)
Definitions
adjective1. expressing or characterized by sorrow2. bringing or causing woe3. pitiful; miserable ⇒ a woeful standard of work
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

woefully (ˈwoefully) or wofully (ˈwəʊfʊlɪ) adverbwoefulness (ˈwoefulness) or wofulness (ˈwəʊfʊlnɪs) noun

woeful for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(woʊfʊl )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is woeful, they are very sad. ⇒ ...a woeful ballad.woefully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He said woefully: 'I love my country, but it does not give a damn about me.'2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use woeful to emphasize that something is very bad or undesirable. [emphasis] ⇒ Such attitudes are the product of woeful ignorance. ⇒ ...the woeful state of the economy.woefully graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV before v] ⇒ Public expenditure on the arts is woefully inadequate. ⇒ Most of the ships were woefully short of ammunition.
