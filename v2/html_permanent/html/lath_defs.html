lath

(lɑːθ )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural laths (lɑːðz , lɑːθs )1. one of several thin narrow strips of wood used to provide a supporting framework for plaster, tiles, etc2. expanded sheet metal, wire mesh, etc, used to provide backing for plaster or rendering3. any thin strip of woodverb4. (transitive) to attach laths to (a ceiling, roof, floor, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lathlike (ˈlathˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

Old English lætt; related to Dutch lat, Old High German latta
