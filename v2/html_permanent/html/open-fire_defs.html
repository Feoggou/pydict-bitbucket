Thanks for your comment. However, there are thousands of examples of the phrase 'open fire' where 'open' is used as a verb. So you can say someone 'opens fire', 'opened fire', or 'is opening fire'. In the military command, 'open' is the imperative form of the verb; this is parallel to the use of the verb 'cease' in the phrase 'cease fire'.

Comment by doc on 21 Jun 2015
"Open fire" is a military command like "cease fire". Open fire does not mean to begin firing, it means that you are free to choose targets at your discretion and opportunity. You cannot, for example, say: "They opened fire." Open in this application does not mean begin, it describes the conditions under which you may fire your weapon. It is similar in kind to "Set your weapons on stun."
