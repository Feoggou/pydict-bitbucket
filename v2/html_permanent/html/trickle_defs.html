trickle

(ˈtrɪkəl )
Definitions
verb1. to run or cause to run in thin or slow streams ⇒ she trickled the sand through her fingers2. (intransitive) to move, go, or pass gradually ⇒ the crowd trickled awaynoun3. a thin, irregular, or slow flow of something4. the act of trickling
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

trickling (ˈtrickling) adjectivetricklingly (ˈtricklingly) adverbtrickly (ˈtrickly) adjective

Word Origin

C14: perhaps of imitative origin

trickle

Powered by COBUILD
(trɪkəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: trickles , trickling , trickled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbWhen a liquid trickles, or when you trickle it, it flows slowly in very small amounts. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A tear trickled down the old man's cheek. [V n] ⇒ Trickle water gently over the back of your baby's head. [V-ing] ⇒ The trickling stream glistened in the sunlight. [Also V]Trickle is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ There was not so much as a trickle of water.2. verbWhen people or things trickle in a particular direction, they move there slowly in small groups or amounts, rather than all together. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Some donations are already trickling in.Trickle is also a noun. ⇒ The flood of cars has now slowed to a trickle. ⇒ A trickle of refugees began to flee the country.
