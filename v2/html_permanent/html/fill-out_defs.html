fill out

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. to make or become fuller, thicker, or rounder ⇒ her figure has filled out since her marriage2. to make more substantial ⇒ the writers were asked to fill their stories out3. (transitive) to complete (a form, application, etc)

Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you fill out a form or other document requesting information, you write information in the spaces on it. [mainly US] [V P n] ⇒ Fill out the application carefully, and keep copies of it. [Also V n P]regional note:   in BRIT, usually use fill in2. phrasal verbIf a fairly thin person fills out, they become fatter. [V P] ⇒ A girl may fill out before she reaches her full height. →  fill
