induce

(ɪnˈdjuːs )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. (often foll by an infinitive) to persuade or use influence on2. to cause or bring about3. medicine to initiate or hasten (labour), as by administering a drug to stimulate uterine contractions4. logic obsolete to assert or establish (a general proposition, hypothesis, etc) by induction5. to produce (an electromotive force or electrical current) by induction6. to transmit (magnetism) by induction
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inducer (inˈducer) nouninducible (inˈducible) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin indūcere to lead in, from dūcere to lead

(ɪndjuːs , US -duːs- ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. verbTo induce a state or condition means to cause it. [V n] ⇒ Doctors said surgery could induce a heart attack. [V-ed] ⇒ ...an economic crisis induced by high oil prices.2. verbIf you induce someone to do something, you persuade or influence them to do it. [V n to-inf] ⇒ I would do anything to induce them to stay. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ More than 4,000 teachers were induced to take early retirement.3. verbIf a doctor or nurse induces labour or birth, they cause a pregnant woman to start giving birth by using drugs or other medical means. [medicine] [V n] ⇒ He might decide that it is best to induce labour.induction singular noun ⇒ ...if there are obvious medical reasons for induction.
