sleet

(sliːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. partly melted falling snow or hail or (esp US) partly frozen rain2. mainly US the thin coat of ice that forms when sleet or rain freezes on cold surfacesverb3. (intransitive) to fall as sleet
Derived forms
sleety (ˈsleety) adjective
Word origin
C13: from Germanic; compare Middle Low German slōten hail, Middle High German slōze, German Schlossen hailstones
