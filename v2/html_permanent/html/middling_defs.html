middling

(ˈmɪdlɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. mediocre in quality, size, etc; neither good nor bad, esp in health (often in the phrase fair to middling)adverb2. informal moderately ⇒ middling well

Derived Forms

middlingly (ˈmiddlingly) adverb

Word Origin

C15 (northern English and Scottish): from mid1 + -ling2

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a quality such as the size of something as middling, you mean that it is average. ⇒ The Beatles enjoyed only middling success until 1963. ⇒ ...a man of middling height.
Example sentences containing 'middling'
I feel guilty about that: I snapped and got away, and my life turned out - well - middling to all right. Barnard, Robert POLITICAL SUICIDEThe woman sounded young, middling young, lower middle-class trying not to be. Gee, Maggie LOST CHILDRENThere was no middling gentry in this part of the country, and no local tradition of going out to service. O'Brian, Patrick TESTIMONIESWhere we went to law school, middling grades leave you scrambling to find any kind of work. William Lashner FATAL FLAW
