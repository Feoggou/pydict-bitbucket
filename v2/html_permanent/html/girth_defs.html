girth

(ɡɜːθ )
Definitions
noun1. the distance around something; circumference2. size or bulk ⇒ a man of great girth3. a band around a horse's belly to keep the saddle in positionverb4. (usually foll by up) to fasten a girth on (a horse)5. (transitive) to encircle or surround
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse gjörth belt; related to Gothic gairda girdle1; see gird1

girth

Powered by COBUILD
(gɜːʳθ ) for learners of English
Word forms: girths
Definitions
1. variable noun [oft poss N]The girth of an object, for example a person's or an animal's body, is its width or thickness, considered as the measurement around its circumference. [formal] ⇒ A girl he knew had upset him by commenting on his increasing girth.2. countable nounA girth is a leather strap which is fastened firmly around the middle of a horse to keep the saddle or load in the right place.
