lavatory

(ˈlævətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun plural -ries1. Also called: toilet, water closet, WCa. a sanitary installation for receiving and disposing of urine and faeces, consisting of a bowl fitted with a water-flushing device and connected to a drainb. a room containing such an installation2. the washing place in a convent or monastic establishment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin lavātōrium, from Latin lavāre to wash
