reef1

(riːf )
Definitions
noun1. a ridge of rock, sand, coral, etc, the top of which lies close to the surface of the sea2. a ridge- or mound-like structure built by sedentary calcareous organisms (esp corals) and consisting mainly of their remains3. a vein of ore, esp one of gold-bearing quartz
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Middle Dutch ref, from Old Norse rif rib1, reef2

reef2 - nautical

(riːf )
Definitions
noun1. the part gathered in when sail area is reduced, as in a high windverb2. to reduce the area of (sail) by taking in a reef3. (transitive) to shorten or bring inboard (a spar)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Middle Dutch rif; related to Old Norse rif reef, rib1, German reffen to reef; see reef1
