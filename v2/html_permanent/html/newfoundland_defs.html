Newfoundland

(ˈnjuːfəndlənd , -fənlənd, -ˌlænd, njuːˈfaʊndlənd )
Definitions
noun1. an island of E Canada, separated from the mainland by the Strait of Belle Isle: with the Coast of Labrador, forms the province of Newfoundland and Labrador; consists of a rugged plateau with the Long Range Mountains in the west. Area: 110 681 sq km (42 734 sq miles)2. the former name for Newfoundland and Labrador3. a very large heavy breed of dog similar to a Saint Bernard with a flat coarse usually black coat
