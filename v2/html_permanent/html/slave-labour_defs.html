slave labour

British or slave labor US (sleɪv ˈleɪbə)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the work done by slaves, or the slaves themselves ⇒ The children were used as slave labour in gold mines in the jungle., ⇒ The pyramids were mostly built by slave labour.2. hard work for which you receive very little remuneration ⇒ He's been forced into slave labour at burger bars to earn a bit of cash., ⇒ It's just slave labour.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use slave labor1. uncount nounSlave labour refers to slaves or to work done by slaves. ⇒ The children were used as slave labour in gold mines in the jungle. ⇒ The report alleges that thousands of people have been forced into slave labour by the army.2. uncount nounIf people work very hard for long hours for very little money, you can refer to it as slave labour. [disapproval] ⇒ He's been forced into slave labour at burger bars to earn a bit of cash.
