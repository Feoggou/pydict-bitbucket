fluent

(ˈfluːənt )
Definitions
adjective1. able to speak or write a specified foreign language with facility2. spoken or written with facility ⇒ his French is fluent3. easy and graceful in motion or shape4. flowing or able to flow freely
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fluently (ˈfluently) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin: flowing, from fluere to flow

fluent

Powered by COBUILD
(fluːənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is fluent in a particular language can speak the language easily and correctly. You can also say that someone speaks fluent French, Chinese, or some other language. [+ in] ⇒ She studied eight foreign languages but is fluent in only six of them. ⇒ He speaks fluent Russian.fluency uncount noun ⇒ To work as a translator, you need fluency in at least one foreign language.fluently graded adverb & adverb ⇒ He spoke three languages fluently.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf your speech, reading, or writing is fluent, you speak, read, or write easily, smoothly, and clearly with no mistakes. ⇒ He had emerged from being a hesitant and unsure candidate into a fluent debater.fluency uncount noun ⇒ His son was praised for speeches of remarkable fluency.fluently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Alex didn't read fluently till he was nearly seven.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
