aloud

(əˈlaʊd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb, adjective (postpositive)1. in a normal voice; not in a whisper2. in a spoken voice; not silently3. archaic in a loud voice

aloud

Powered by COBUILD
(əlaʊd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [ADV after v]When you say something, read, or laugh aloud, you speak or laugh so that other people can hear you. out loud ⇒ When we were children, our father read aloud to us. ⇒ 'You fool,' he said aloud.2. →  think aloud
Example sentences containing 'aloud'
As my body got used to the cold I laughed aloud and turned and waved triumphantly at the shore. Kate Cann GO!Adrina had to bite her lip to prevent herself laughing aloud. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPFrodo stirred, feeling that he must break the silence: he longed to shout out aloud. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSEmerson is one of the few who has the courage to state his sentiments aloud. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY
