mostly

(ˈməʊstlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. almost entirely; chiefly2. on many or most occasions; usually

mostly

Powered by COBUILD
(moʊstli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverbYou use mostly to indicate that a statement is generally true, for example true about the majority of a group of things or people, true most of the time, or true in most respects. ⇒ I am working with mostly highly motivated people. ⇒ Cars are mostly metal. ⇒ ...men and women, mostly in their 30s. ⇒ Her own twelve pictures sold fairly well, mostly to friends and family. ⇒ They have mostly invested their money in expensive real estate.
