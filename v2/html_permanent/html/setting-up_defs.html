setting-up

(ˌsɛtɪŋˈʌp)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the establishment or creation of something ⇒ The resulting debate led eventually to the setting-up of the British Horseracing Board (BHB).2. the act of doing or organizing something without the knowledge of someone; doing a trick, stunt, or prank
Example sentences containing 'setting-up'
Like many of his colleagues he was increasingly disturbed by certain developments, in particular the setting-up of the Workers ' Militia. Forbes, Bryan A SONG AT TWILIGHTThe setting-up of the Militia had provoked fierce criticism from many quarters. Forbes, Bryan A SONG AT TWILIGHT
