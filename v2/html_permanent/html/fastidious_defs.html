fastidious

(fæˈstɪdɪəs )
Definitions
adjective1. very critical; hard to please2. excessively particular about details3. exceedingly delicate; easily disgusted
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fastidiously (fasˈtidiously) adverbfastidiousness (fasˈtidiousness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fastīdiōsus scornful, from fastīdium loathing, from fastus pride + taedium weariness

fastidious

Powered by COBUILD
(fæstɪdiəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is fastidious, you mean that they pay great attention to detail because they like everything to be very neat, accurate, and in good order. ⇒ ...her fastidious attention to historical detail. [+ about] ⇒ He was fastidious about his appearance.fastidiously graded adverb ⇒ He fastidiously copied every word of his notes on to clean paper.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is fastidious, you mean that they are concerned about keeping clean to an extent that many people consider to be excessive. ⇒ Be particularly fastidious about washing your hands before touching food.fastidiously graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Ernestine kept her daughters fastidiously clean.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
