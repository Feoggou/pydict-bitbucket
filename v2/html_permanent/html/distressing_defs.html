distressing

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪstresɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is distressing, it upsets you or worries you. ⇒ It is very distressing to see your baby attached to tubes and monitors. ⇒ Tranquillizers help alleviate the distressing symptoms of anxiety.distressingly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ ...a distressingly large bloodstain.
Example sentences containing 'distressing'
Besides the dogs there were other, perhaps more distressing signs of hurried exits. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOWThe telephone directories were more promising and at the same time more distressing. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEHad to admit it --- quite the most horrible, the most distressing walk he could ever remember. Nicola Barker BEHINDLINGS
