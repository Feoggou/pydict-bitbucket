Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fers, -ferring or -ferred(transitive) to delay or cause to be delayed until a future time; postpone
Derived forms
deferrable (deˈferrable) or deferable (deˈferable) adjectivedeferrer (deˈferrer) noun

defer2

(dɪˈfɜː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fers, -ferring or -ferred(intransitive; foll by to) to yield (to) or comply (with) the wishes or judgments of another ⇒ I defer to your superior knowledge
Word origin
C15: from Latin dēferre, literally: to bear down, from de- + ferre to bear

defer

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪfɜːʳ )
Word forms: defers , deferring , deferred
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you defer an event or action, you arrange for it to happen at a later date, rather than immediately or at the previously planned time. [V n/v-ing] ⇒ Customers often defer payment for as long as possible. [V n/-ing] ⇒ I'm not going to defer decisions just because they are not immediately politically popular.2. verbIf you defer to someone, you accept their opinion or do what they want you to do, even when you do not agree with it yourself, because you respect them or their authority. [V + to] ⇒ Doctors are encouraged to defer to experts.
