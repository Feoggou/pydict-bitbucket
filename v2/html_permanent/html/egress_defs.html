egress

Definitions
noun (ˈiːɡrɛs )1. Also called: egression the act of going or coming out; emergence2. a way out, such as a path; exit3. the right or permission to go out or depart4. astronomy another name for emersion (sense 2)verb (ɪˈɡrɛs ) (intransitive)5. to go forth; issue
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ēgredī to come forth, depart, from gradī to move, step

He had to move now, ingress and egress before his nerves frayed and the last adrenalin dribbled away.
