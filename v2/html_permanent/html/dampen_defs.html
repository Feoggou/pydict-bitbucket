Definitions

verb1. to make or become damp2. (transitive) to stifle; deaden

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo dampen something such as someone's enthusiasm or excitement means to make it less lively or intense. [V n] ⇒ Nothing seems to dampen his perpetual enthusiasm. [V n] ⇒ I hate to dampen your spirits but aren't you overlooking a couple of minor points.To dampen something down means the same as to dampen it. [V P n] ⇒ Although unemployment rose last month, this is unlikely to dampen down wage demands. [V n P] ⇒ The economy overheated and the Government used to interest rates to dampen it down.2. verbIf you dampen something, you make it slightly wet. [V n] ⇒ She took the time to dampen a washcloth and do her face.
