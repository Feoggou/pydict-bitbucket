unacceptable

(ˌʌnəkˈsɛptəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. not satisfactory; inadequate ⇒ the standard was wholly unacceptable2. intolerable ⇒ hitting children is unacceptable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unacceptable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnəkseptəbəl )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as unacceptable, you strongly disapprove of it or object to it and feel that it should not be allowed to continue. [+ for] ⇒ It is totally unacceptable for children to swear. ⇒ Joanna left her husband because of his unacceptable behaviour.unacceptably (ʌnəkseptəbli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV after v] ⇒ The reform program has brought unacceptably high unemployment and falling wages.
