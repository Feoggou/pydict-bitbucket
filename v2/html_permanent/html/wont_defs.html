wont

(wəʊnt )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive) accustomed (to doing something) ⇒ he was wont to come earlynoun2. a manner or action habitually employed by or associated with someone (often in the phrases as is my wont, as is his wont, etc)verb3. (when tr, usually passive) to become or cause to become accustomed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English gewunod, past participle of wunian to be accustomed to; related to Old High German wunēn (German wohnen), Old Norse una to be satisfied; see wean1, wish, winsome
