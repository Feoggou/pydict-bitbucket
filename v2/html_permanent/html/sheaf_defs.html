sheaf

(ʃiːf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural sheaves (ʃiːvz )1. a bundle of reaped but unthreshed corn tied with one or two bonds2. a bundle of objects tied together3. the arrows contained in a quiververb4. (transitive) to bind or tie into a sheaf

Word Origin

Old English sceaf, related to Old High German skoub sheaf, Old Norse skauf tail, Gothic skuft tuft of hair

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA sheaf of papers is a number of them held or fastened together. [+ of] ⇒ He took out a sheaf of papers and leafed through them.2. countable nounA sheaf of corn or wheat is a number of corn or wheat plants that have been cut down and tied together.
