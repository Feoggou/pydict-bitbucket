bulletin

(ˈbʊlɪtɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an official statement on a matter of public interest, such as the illness of a public figure2. a broadcast summary of the news3. a periodical publication of an association, etcverb4. (transitive) to make known by bulletin
Word origin
C17: from French, from Italian bullettino, from bulletta, diminutive of bulla papal edict, bull3

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA bulletin is a short news report on the radio or television. ⇒ ...the early morning news bulletin.2. countable nounA bulletin is a short official announcement made publicly to inform people about an important matter. ⇒ At 3.30 p.m. a bulletin was released announcing that the president was out of immediate danger.3. countable nounA bulletin is a regular newspaper or leaflet that is produced by an organization or group such as a school or church.
