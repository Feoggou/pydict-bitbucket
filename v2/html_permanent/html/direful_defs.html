direful

(ˈdaɪəfʊl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveliterary dire; awful; very bad ⇒ heedless of the direful consequences that might flow from such an act, ⇒ direful and pestilential diseases
Example sentences containing 'direful'
Egbert had put him to bed nicely oiled just a little while before George brought the direful news from the tool shed. Macleod, Charlotte THE RECYCLED CITIZEN
