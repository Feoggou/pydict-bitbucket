ruling

(ˈruːlɪŋ )
Definitions
noun1. a decision of someone in authority, such as a judge2. one or more parallel ruled linesadjective3. controlling or exercising authority ⇒ the ruling classes4. prevalent or predominant

ruling

Powered by COBUILD
(ruːlɪŋ ) for learners of English
Word forms: rulings
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]The ruling group of people in a country or organization is the group that controls its affairs. ⇒ ...the Mexican voters' growing dissatisfaction with the ruling party. ⇒ ...the domination of the ruling class. ⇒ ...the sport's ruling body, the International Cricket Council.2. countable noun [oft N that]A ruling is an official decision made by a judge or court. ⇒ Goodwin tried to have the court ruling overturned. ⇒ She appealed against a High Court ruling that she should be forcibly fed to save her life.3. adjective [ADJ n]Someone's ruling passion or emotion is the feeling they have most strongly, which influences their actions. ⇒ Their ruling passion is that of carnal love.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
