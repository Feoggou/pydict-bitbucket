Word Origin

horrendous

Powered by COBUILD
(hərendəs , US hɔːr- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is horrendous is very unpleasant or shocking. ⇒ He described it as the most horrendous experience of his life. ⇒ The violence used was horrendous.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSome people use horrendous to describe something that is so big or great that they find it extremely unpleasant. [informal] ⇒ Nor do their debts looks so horrendous when related to the value of their fixed assets. ⇒ ...the usually horrendous traffic jams.horrendously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed, oft ADV after v] ⇒ Many outings can now be horrendously expensive for parents with a young family.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
