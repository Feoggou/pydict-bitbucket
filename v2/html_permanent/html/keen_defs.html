keen1

(kiːn )
Definitions
adjective1. eager or enthusiastic2. (postpositive; foll by on) fond (of); devoted (to) ⇒ keen on a girl, ⇒ keen on golf3. intellectually acute ⇒ a keen wit4. (of sight, smell, hearing, etc) capable of recognizing fine distinctions5. having a sharp cutting edge or point6. extremely cold and penetrating ⇒ a keen wind7. intense or strong ⇒ a keen desire8. mainly British extremely low so as to be competitive ⇒ keen prices9. slang, mainly US and Canadian very good

Word Origin

keen for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(kiːn )
Word forms: keener , keenest , keens, keening, keened
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ that, ADJ to-inf]If you are keen on doing something, you very much want to do it. If you are keen that something should happen, you very much want it to happen. [mainly British] [+ on] ⇒ You're not keen on going, are you? ⇒ Both companies were keen on a merger. ⇒ I'm very keen that the European Union should be as open as possible to trade from Russia. ⇒ She's still keen to keep in touch. [+ for] ⇒ I am not keen for her to have a bicycle.keenness uncount noun [oft N to-inf] ⇒ ...Doyle's keenness to please. [+ for] ⇒ ...a keenness for the idea of a co-ordinated approach to development.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are keen on something, you like it a lot and are very enthusiastic about it. [mainly British] [+ on] ⇒ I got quite keen on the idea. ⇒ I wasn't too keen on physics and chemistry.keenness uncount noun [+ for] ⇒ ...his keenness for the arts.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]You use keen to indicate that someone has a lot of enthusiasm for a particular activity and spends a lot of time doing it. ⇒ She was a keen amateur photographer. [Also + on]4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as keen, you mean that they have an enthusiastic nature and are interested in everything that they do. ⇒ He's a very keen student and works very hard. ⇒ You're all very keen.keenness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the keenness of the students.5. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A keen interest or emotion is one that is very intense. ⇒ He had retained a keen interest in the progress of the work. ⇒ ...his keen sense of loyalty.keenly graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ She remained keenly interested in international affairs. ⇒ This is a keenly awaited project.6. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you are a keen supporter of a cause, movement, or idea, you support it enthusiastically. ⇒ He's been a keen supporter of the Labour Party all his life. ⇒ He is a keen advocate of park-and-ride schemes.7. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you say that someone has a keen mind, you mean that they are very clever and aware of what is happening around them. ⇒ They described him as a man of keen intellect. ⇒ Mr Walsh has a keen appreciation of the priorities of the electorate. ⇒ I can see you have a keen sense of humour.keenly graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ They're keenly aware that whatever they decide will set a precedent.8. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you have a keen eye or ear, you are able to notice things that are difficult to detect. ⇒ ...an amateur artist with a keen eye for detail. ⇒ Brand's keen ear caught the trace of an accent.keenly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Charles listened keenly. ⇒ ...his keenly observed portrait.9. graded adjective [v-link ADJ on n]If you are keen on someone, you find them sexually attractive and want to get to know them better. [British, informal] ⇒ Mick has always been very keen on Carla.10. graded adjective & adjectiveA keen fight or competition is one in which the competitors are all trying very hard to win, and it is not easy to predict who will win. ⇒ There is expected to be a keen fight in the local elections.keenly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The contest should be very keenly fought.11. graded adjective & adjectiveKeen prices are low and competitive. [mainly British] ⇒ The company negotiates very keen prices with their suppliers.keenly graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed] ⇒ The shops also offer a keenly priced curtain-making service.12. verbIf someone keens, they cry out or make sounds to express their sorrow at someone's death. [literary] wail [V] ⇒ He tossed back his head and keened. [V-ing] ⇒ Someone was making a low, keening noise.13. →  mad keen
