shooting

(ˈʃuːtɪŋ)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the activity of shooting; shots ⇒ to hear shooting in the night, ⇒ They heard shooting.2. an occasion when someone is injured or killed by being shot with a gun ⇒ a series of shootings, ⇒ A drug-related gang war led to a series of shootings in the city., ⇒ There was almost a riot after the shootings., ⇒ Two more bodies were found nearby after the shooting.3. a. the sport of shooting game ⇒ to go shooting, ⇒ Grouse shooting begins in August.b. (as modifier) ⇒ the shooting season4. the act of making cinema films ⇒ Ingrid was busy learning her lines for the next day's shooting., ⇒ The shooting of The Maltese Falcon proceeded without a hitch., ⇒ Her next project was cancelled after eight weeks of shooting.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary

[British] ⇒ Grouse shooting begins in August.regional note:   in AM, use hunting3. uncount nounThe shooting of a film is the act of filming it. ⇒ Ingrid was busy learning her lines for the next day's shooting. ⇒ The shooting of The Maltese Falcon proceeded without a hitch.
