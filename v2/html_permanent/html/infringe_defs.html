infringe

(ɪnˈfrɪndʒ )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to violate or break (a law, an agreement, etc)2. (intr; foll by on or upon) to encroach or trespass
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

infringement (inˈfringement) nouninfringer (inˈfringer) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin infringere to break off, from frangere to break

infringe

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfrɪndʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: infringes , infringing , infringed
Definitions
1. verbIf someone infringes a law or a rule, they break it or do something which disobeys it. [V n] ⇒ The film exploited his image and infringed his copyright. [V n] ⇒ The jury ruled that he had infringed no rules.2. verbIf something infringes people's rights, it interferes with these rights and does not allow people the freedom they are entitled to. [V n] ⇒ They rob us, they infringe our rights, they kill us. [V + on] ⇒ It's starting to infringe on our personal liberties.
