Comment by CopernicusScienc on 12 Feb 2014
I would like to call into question the listing of "in the order of" as an alternative to "of the order of". None of the examples given here include the phrase, nor is it listed in other reputable dictionaries (e.g. Oxford, Cambridge, and Merriam-Webster). Furthermore, "in the order" is a discrete term with a very different meaning, while "on the order of" is the North American variant of "of the order of". Given the latter point, I cannot help but wonder whether this listing is not a typo of "in" in place of "on". Any definitive feedback would be much appreciated.
