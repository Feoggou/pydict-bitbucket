Machiavellian

or Machiavelian (ˌmækɪəˈvɛlɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (sometimes not capital)1. of or relating to the alleged political principles of Machiavelli; cunning, amoral, and opportunistnoun2. a cunning, amoral, and opportunist person, esp a politician

Derived Forms

ˌMachiavellianism (ˌMachiaˈvellianism) or ˌMachiavellism (ˌMachiaˈvellism) nounˌMachiavellist (ˌMachiaˈvellist) adjective, noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as Machiavellian, you are critical of them because they often make clever and secret plans to achieve their aims and are not honest with people. [disapproval] ⇒ ...Machiavellian republicans plotting to destabilise the throne. ⇒ ...the Machiavellian and devious way decisions were made. ⇒ A Machiavellian plot was suspected.
