telling

(ˈtɛlɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having a marked effect or impact ⇒ a telling blow2. revealing ⇒ a telling smile

Derived Forms

tellingly (ˈtellingly) adverb

telling

(telɪŋ )
Word forms: tellings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounThe telling of a story or of something that has happened is the reporting of it to other people. [+ of] ⇒ Herbert sat quietly through the telling of this saga. ⇒ These stories grow in the telling.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is telling, it shows the true nature of a person or situation. ⇒ It was her expression that was the most telling. ⇒ How a man shaves may be a telling clue to his age.tellingly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Most tellingly, perhaps, chimpanzees do not draw as much information from the world around them as we do.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A telling argument or criticism is a very effective one. ⇒ He spoke reasonably, carefully, and with telling effect. ⇒ The most telling condemnation of the system was that it failed to fulfil its function.4. →  there's no telling
