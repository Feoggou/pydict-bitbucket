digress

(daɪˈɡrɛs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to depart from the main subject in speech or writing2. to wander from one's path or main direction
Derived forms
digresser (diˈgresser) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin dīgressus turned aside, from dīgredī, from dis- apart + gradī to go

Word forms: digresses , digressing , digressed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you digress, you move away from the subject you are talking or writing about and talk or write about something different for a while. [V] ⇒ I've digressed a little to explain the situation so far, so let me now recap. [V + from] ⇒ She digressed from her prepared speech to pay tribute to the President.digression (daɪgreʃən )Word forms: digressions variable noun ⇒ The text is dotted with digressions.
