preponderate

(prɪˈpɒndəˌreɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (often foll by over) to be more powerful, important, numerous, etc (than)2. to be of greater weight than something else

Derived Forms

preponderately (preˈponderately) adverbpreponderˌating (preˈponderˌating) adjectivepreˌponderation (preˌponderˈation) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin praeponderāre to be of greater weight, from pondus weight

Similar Words

predominate, rule, dominate, prevail, outnumber, hold sway, reign supreme
