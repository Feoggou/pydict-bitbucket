strum

(strʌm )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: strums, strumming or strummed1. to sound (the strings of a guitar, banjo, etc) with a downward or upward sweep of the thumb or of a plectrum2. to play (chords, a tune, etc) in this way

Derived Forms

strummer (ˈstrummer) noun

Word Origin

C18: probably of imitative origin; see thrum1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you strum a stringed instrument such as a guitar, you play it by moving your fingers backwards and forwards across the strings. [V n] ⇒ In the corner, one youth sat alone, softly strumming a guitar. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Vaska strummed away on his guitar.Strum is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ A little while later, I heard the strum of my father's guitar as he began to sing.

She pulled her gaze away from the empty doorway, tucked the guitar under her arm and began to strum. Lawson, Jonell ROSES ARE FOR THE RICHThe only sounds were the murmur of the generator and the strum of a guitar from a back room. Innes, Hammond HIGH STAND
