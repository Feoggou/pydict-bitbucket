plentiful

(ˈplɛntɪfʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. ample; abundant2. having or yielding an abundance ⇒ a plentiful year

Derived Forms

plentifully (ˈplentifully) adverbplentifulness (ˈplentifulness) noun

plentiful

Powered by COBUILD
(plentɪfʊl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Things that are plentiful exist in such large amounts or numbers that there is enough for people's wants or needs. ⇒ Fish are plentiful in the lake. ⇒ ...a plentiful supply of vegetables and salads and fruits.plentifully graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Nettle grows plentifully on any rich waste ground.
Example sentences containing 'plentiful'
It was wonderful up in the hills - almost as wonderful as down at home with Ranisa, and game was plentiful. NULL DARE CALL IT TREASONWe had a good bit of rain sometime back, and the grass is unusually plentiful for this season. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSThe fine red wine was plentiful , and helped to loosen their tongues. Mario Puzo THE FAMILY
