sparse

(spɑːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivescattered or scanty; not dense

Derived Forms

sparsely (ˈsparsely) adverbsparseness (ˈsparseness) or sparsity (ˈsparsity) noun

Word Origin

C18: from Latin sparsus, from spargere to scatter

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is sparse is small in number or amount and spread out over an area. ⇒ Many slopes are rock fields with sparse vegetation. ⇒ He was a tubby little man in his fifties, with sparse hair. ⇒ Traffic was sparse on the highway.sparsely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV -ed] ⇒ ...the sparsely populated interior region, where there are few roads.
