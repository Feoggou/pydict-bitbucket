spectre

or US specter (ˈspɛktə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a ghost; phantom; apparition2. a mental image of something unpleasant or menacing ⇒ the spectre of redundancy

Word Origin

C17: from Latin spectrum, from specere to look at

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use specter1. countable nounIf you refer to the spectre of something unpleasant, you are referring to something that you are frightened might occur. ⇒ Failure to arrive at a consensus over the issue raised the spectre of legal action. ⇒ Like many others, Handford was relieved to see the spectre of 15% interest rates evaporate by the end of the day.2. countable nounA spectre is a ghost.
