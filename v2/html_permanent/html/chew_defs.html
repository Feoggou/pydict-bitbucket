chew

(tʃuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to work the jaws and teeth in order to grind (food); masticate2. to bite repeatedly ⇒ she chewed her nails anxiously3. (intransitive) to use chewing tobacco4. See chew the fatnoun5. the act of chewing6. something that is chewed ⇒ a chew of tobacco
Derived forms
chewable (ˈchewable) adjectivechewer (ˈchewer) noun
Word origin
Old English ceowan; related to Old High German kiuwan, Dutch kauwen, Latin gingīva a gum

Powered by COBUILD
(tʃuː )
Word forms: chews , chewing , chewed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen you chew food, you use your teeth to break it up in your mouth so that it becomes easier to swallow. [V n] ⇒ Be certain to eat slowly and chew your food extremely well. [V + at/on] ⇒ Daniel leaned back on the sofa, still chewing on his apple. [V] ⇒ ...the sound of his mother chewing and swallowing.2. verbIf you chew gum or tobacco, you keep biting it and moving it around your mouth to taste the flavour of it. You do not swallow it. [V n] ⇒ One girl was chewing gum. [V n] ⇒ He chews tobacco constantly.3. verbIf you chew your lips or your fingernails, you keep biting them because you are nervous. [V n] ⇒ He chewed his lower lip nervously.4. verbIf a person or animal chews an object, they bite it with their teeth. [V n] ⇒ They pause and chew their pencils. [V prep] ⇒ One owner left his pet under the stairs where the animal chewed through electric cables.5. countable nounA chew is a sweet that you have to chew very hard before it becomes soft. [British] ⇒ ...a selection of penny chews.6. →  to bite off more than one can chew7. →  chew the fat8. → to chew the cud► →  chew out► →  chew over► →  chew up
