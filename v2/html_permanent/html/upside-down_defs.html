upside down

Definitions
adjective1. (usually postpositive; upside-down when prenominal) turned over completely; inverted2. (upside-down when prenominal) informal confused; muddled; topsy-turvy ⇒ an upside-down worldadverb3. in an inverted fashion4. in a chaotic or crazy manner
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

upside-downness (ˌupside-ˈdownness) noun

Word Origin

C16: variant, by folk etymology, of earlier upsodown

upside down for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌpsaɪd daʊn ) also upside-down
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v, n ADV]If something has been moved upside down, it has been turned round so that the part that is usually lowest is above the part that is usually highest. ⇒ The painting was hung upside down. ⇒ Salter held the bag by the corners and shook it upside down.Upside down is also an adjective. ⇒ His eyes were open and everything he saw was upside down. ⇒ Tony had an upside-down map of Britain on his wall.2. → to turn something upside down
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
