whim

(wɪm )
Definitions
noun1. a sudden, passing, and often fanciful idea; impulsive or irrational thought2. a horse-drawn winch formerly used in mining to lift ore or water
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from whim-wham

whim for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪm )
Word forms: whims
Definitions
variable noun [oft on/at N]A whim is a wish to do or have something which seems to have no serious reason or purpose behind it, and often occurs suddenly. ⇒ We decided, more or less on a whim, to sail to Morocco. [+ of] ⇒ The premium can increase at the whim of the insurers. ⇒ Lately, the president has been sacking and picking new ministers at whim.
