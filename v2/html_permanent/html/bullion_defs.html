bullion

(ˈbʊljən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. gold or silver in mass2. gold or silver in the form of bars and ingots, suitable for further processing3. Also called: bullion fringe a thick gold or silver wire or fringed cord used as a trimming, as on military uniforms
Word origin
C14 (in the sense: melted gold or silver): from Anglo-French: mint, probably from Old French bouillir to boil, from Latin bullīre
