naphtha

(ˈnæfθə , ˈnæp-)
Definitions
noun1. a distillation product from coal tar boiling in the approximate range 80–170°C and containing aromatic hydrocarbons2. a distillation product from petroleum boiling in the approximate range 100–200°C and containing aliphatic hydrocarbons: used as a solvent and in petrol3. an obsolete name for petroleum
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek, of Iranian origin; related to Persian neft naphtha

noun(Extractive engineering: Refinery processes, Product) Naphtha is a colorless, volatile distillate of petroleum, usually an intermediate product between gasoline and benzine. ⇒ Naphtha is a light, colorless to straw-colored liquid which distills off from petroleum between 158 to 194°F. ⇒ Naphtha is a liquid intermediate between the light gases in the crude oil and the heavier liquid kerosene. ⇒ Naphtha is a colorless, volatile distillate of petroleum, usually an intermediate product between gasoline and benzine.
