pen1

(pɛn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an implement for writing or drawing using ink, formerly consisting of a sharpened and split quill, and now of a metal nib attached to a holder. See also ballpoint, fountain pen2. the writing end of such an implement; nib3. style of writing4. See the pen5. the long horny internal shell of a squidverbWord forms: pens, penning or penned6. (transitive) to write or compose

Word Origin

Old English pinne, from Late Latin penna (quill) pen, from Latin: feather

Word Origin

pen

Powered by COBUILD
(pen )
Word forms: pens , penning , penned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA pen is a long thin object which you use to write in ink.2. → see also fountain pen3. → ballpoint4. → felt-tip5. verbIf someone pens a letter, article, or book, they write it. [formal] [V n to n] ⇒ I really intended to pen this letter to you early this morning. [V n + to] ⇒ She penned a short memo to his private secretary. [Also V n n]6. countable nounA pen is also a small area with a fence round it in which farm animals are kept for a short time. ⇒ ...a holding pen for sheep. ⇒ He wasn't sure exactly how a fox could have got into the sheep's pen.7. → see also playpen8. verb [usu passive]If people or animals are penned somewhere or are penned up, they are forced to remain in a very small area. [be V-ed] ⇒ ...to drive the cattle back to the house so they could be milked and penned for the night. [be V-ed] ⇒ The goats are penned in and fodder has to be cut and carried each day. [V-ed up] ⇒ I don't have to stay in my room penned up like a prisoner.9. countable noun [usu sing, the N]People sometimes say the pen to refer to a prison. [US, informal] penitentiary10. →  put pen to paper
