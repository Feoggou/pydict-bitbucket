spook

(spuːk ) informal

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a ghost or a person suggestive of this2. US and Canadian a spy3. South Africa slang any pale or colourless alcoholic spirit ⇒ spook and dieselverb (transitive) US and Canadian4. to frighten ⇒ to spook horses, ⇒ to spook a person5. (of a ghost) to haunt

Derived Forms

spookish (ˈspookish) adjective

Word Origin

C19: Dutch spook, from Middle Low German spōk ghost

1. countable nounA spook is a ghost. [informal]2. countable nounA spook is a spy. [US, informal] ⇒ ...as a U.S. intelligence spook said yesterday.3. verbIf people are spooked, something has scared them or made them nervous. [mainly US] [V n] ⇒ But was it the wind that spooked her? [be V-ed] ⇒ Investors were spooked by slowing economies.spooked graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ] ⇒ He was so spooked that he, too, began to believe that he heard strange clicks and noises on their telephones.
