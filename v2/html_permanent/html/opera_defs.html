opera1

(ˈɒpərə , ˈɒprə )
Definitions
noun1. an extended dramatic work in which music constitutes a dominating feature, either consisting of separate recitatives, arias, and choruses, or having a continuous musical structure2. the branch of music or drama represented by such works3. the score, libretto, etc, of an opera4. a theatre where opera is performed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: via Italian from Latin: work, a work, plural of opus work
