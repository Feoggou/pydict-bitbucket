consummation

(ˌkɑnsəˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun1.  a consummating or being consummated; completion; fulfillment2.  an end; conclusion; outcome
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.
Word origin
ME consummacioun < OFr consumation < L consummatio
Example sentences containing 'consummation'
He found the latter consummation as difficult to envisage as any, but one never knew, something might turn up. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARThe prospect of being included in possible violence, combined with the politics of destruction, was a consummation devoutly to be wished. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAME`The woman is left with a memory of perfect love and consummation...' `Yeah,' said Charlie, with no encouragement. Robert Wilson INSTRUMENTS OF DARKNESSLinc kept moving slowly, wanting it to last forever, to spend his life held within the shimmering instant of Holly's consummation. Elizabeth Lowell DESERT RAIN
