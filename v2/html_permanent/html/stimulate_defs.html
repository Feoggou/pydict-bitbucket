stimulate

(ˈstɪmjʊˌleɪt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (tr; usually passive) to fill (a person) with ideas or enthusiasm ⇒ he was stimulated by the challenge2. (transitive) physiology to excite (a nerve, organ, etc) with a stimulus3. to encourage (something) to start or progress further ⇒ a cut in interest rates should help stimulate economic recovery

Derived Forms

stimulable (ˈstimulable) adjectiveˌstimulation (ˌstimuˈlation) nounstimulative (ˈstimulative) or stimulatory (ˈstimulatory) adjective, nounstimuˌlator (ˈstimuˌlator) or stimuˌlater (ˈstimuˌlater) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin stimulāre; see stimulant

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo stimulate something means to encourage it to begin or develop further. [V n] ⇒ America's priority is rightly to stimulate its economy. [V n] ⇒ The Russian health service has stimulated public interest in home cures.stimulation (stɪmjʊleɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...an economy in need of stimulation.2. verb [usu passive]If you are stimulated by something, it makes you feel full of ideas and enthusiasm. [be V-ed] ⇒ Bill was stimulated by the challenge. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ I was stimulated to examine my deepest thoughts.stimulating graded adjective & adjective ⇒ It is a complex yet stimulating book. ⇒ The atmosphere was always stimulating.stimulation uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Many enjoy the mental stimulation of a challenging job.3. verbIf something stimulates a part of a person's body, it causes it to move or start working. [V n] ⇒ Exercise stimulates the digestive and excretory systems. [V n] ⇒ The production of melanin in the skin is stimulated by exposure to the sun. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ The body is stimulated to build up resistance.stimulating graded adjective & adjective ⇒ ...the stimulating effect of adrenaline.stimulation uncount noun ⇒ ...physical stimulation. [+ of] ⇒ ...the chemical stimulation of drugs.
