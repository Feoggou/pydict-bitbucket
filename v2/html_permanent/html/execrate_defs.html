execrate

(ˈɛksɪˌkreɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to loathe; detest; abhor2. (transitive) to profess great abhorrence for; denounce; deplore3. to curse (a person or thing); damn
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

execration (ˌexeˈcration) nounexecrative (ˈexeˌcrative) or execratory (ˈexeˌcratory) adjectiveexecratively (ˈexeˌcratively) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin exsecrārī to curse, from ex-1 + -secrārī from sacer sacred

Similar Words

loathe, hate, condemn, slam (slang), denounce, despise, deplore, detest, revile, vilify, abhor, excoriate, abominate
curse, damn, imprecate, anathematize
