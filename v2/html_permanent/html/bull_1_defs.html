bull1

(bʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any male bovine animal, esp one that is sexually mature ▶ Related adjective: taurine2. the uncastrated adult male of any breed of domestic cattle3. the male of various other animals including the elephant and whale4. a very large, strong, or aggressive person5. stock exchangea. a speculator who buys in anticipation of rising prices in order to make a profit on resaleb. (as modifier) ⇒ a bull market. Compare bear1 (sense 5)6. mainly British short for bull's-eye (sense 1), bull's-eye (sense 2)7. slang short for bullshit8. short for bulldog, bull terrier9. See a bull in a china shop10. See shoot the bull11. See take the bull by the hornsadjective12. male; masculine ⇒ a bull elephant13. large; strongverb14. (transitive) to raise or attempt to raise the price or prices of (a stock market or a security) by speculative buying15. (intransitive) (of a cow) to be on heat16. (intransitive) US slang to talk lightly or foolishly
Word origin
Old English bula, from Old Norse boli; related to Middle Low German bulle, Middle Dutch bolle
