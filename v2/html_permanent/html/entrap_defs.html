entrap

(ɪnˈtræp )
Definitions
verb -traps, -trapping or -trapped (transitive)1. to catch or snare in or as if in a trap2. to lure or trick into danger, difficulty, or embarrassment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

(ɪntræp ) for learners of English
Word forms: entraps , entrapping , entrapped
Definitions
verbIf you entrap someone, you trick or deceive them and make them believe or do something wrong. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The police have been given extra powers to entrap drug traffickers. [V n] ⇒ He overturned the conviction, saying the defendant was entrapped. [V n + into] ⇒ He claimed the government had entrapped him into doing something that he would not have done otherwise.
