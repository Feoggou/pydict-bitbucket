throw up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb, mainly tr)1. to give up; abandon, relinquish2. to build or construct hastily3. to reveal; produce ⇒ every generation throws up its own leaders4. (also intr) informal to vomit

throw up

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbWhen someone throws up, they vomit. [informal] [V P] ⇒ She said she had thrown up after reading reports of the trial.2. phrasal verbIf something throws up dust, stones, or water, when it moves or hits the ground, it causes them to rise up into the air. [V P n] ⇒ If it had hit the Earth, it would have made a crater 100 miles across and thrown up an immense cloud of dust. [Also V n P]3. phrasal verbIf you say that a building or structure is thrown up, you mean that it is built or made very quickly, usually so that it is not of very good quality. [be V-ed P] ⇒ ...living in the slums where wood and scrap metal dwellings are thrown up in any available space. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Youths threw up barricades on the streets. [Also V n P]4. phrasal verbTo throw up a particular person or thing means to produce them or cause them to become noticeable. [mainly British] [V P n] ⇒ The political struggle threw up a strong leader. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ These studies have already thrown up some interesting results. [Also V n P] →  throw
