font1

(fɒnt )
Definitions
noun1. a. a large bowl for baptismal water, usually mounted on a pedestalb. a receptacle for holy water2. the reservoir for oil in an oil lamp3. archaic or poetic a fountain or well
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fontal (ˈfontal) adjective

Word Origin

Old English, from Church Latin fons, from Latin: fountain

font2

(fɒnt )
Definitions
nounprinting a complete set of type of one style and size. Also called: fount
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old French fonte a founding, casting, from Vulgar Latin funditus (unattested) a casting, from Latin fundere to melt; see found3
