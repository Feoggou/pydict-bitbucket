eye1

(aɪ )
Definitions
noun1. the organ of sight of animals, containing light-sensitive cells associated with nerve fibres, so that light entering the eye is converted to nervous impulses that reach the brain. In man and other vertebrates the iris controls the amount of light entering the eye and the lens focuses the light onto the retina ▶ Related adjectives: ocular, oculate, ophthalmic, optic2. (often plural) the ability to see; sense of vision ⇒ weak eyes3. the visible external part of an eye, often including the area around it ⇒ heavy-lidded eyes, ⇒ piercing eyes4. a look, glance, expression, or gaze ⇒ a stern eye5. a sexually inviting or provocative look (esp in the phrases give (someone) the (glad) eye, make eyes at)6. attention or observation (often in the phrases catch someone's eye, keep an eye on, cast an eye over)7. ability to recognize, judge, or appreciate ⇒ an eye for antiques8. (often plural) opinion, judgment, point of view, or authority ⇒ in the eyes of the law9. a structure or marking having the appearance of an eye, such as the bud on a twig or potato tuber or a spot on a butterfly wing10. a small loop or hole, as at one end of a needle11. a small area of low pressure and calm in the centre of a tornado or cyclone12. See photocell13. informal See private eye14. See all eyes15. See my eye16. See an eye for an eye17. See cut one's eye after someone18. See eyes out19. See get one's eye in20. See half an eye21. See have eyes for22. See in one's mind's eye23. See in the public eye24. See keep an eye open25. See keep one's eyes peeled26. See look someone in the eye27. See make eyes28. See more than meets the eye29. See pick the eyes out30. See see eye to eye31. See set eyes on32. See the eye of the wind33. See turn a blind eye to34. See up to one's eyes35. See with a … eye36. See with an eye to37. See with one's eyes open38. See with one's eyes shutverbWord forms: eyes, eyeing, eying or eyed (transitive)39. to look at carefully or warily40. Also: eye up to look at in a manner indicating sexual interest; ogle

(aɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: eyes , eyeing , eying , eyed
Definitions
1. countable noun [oft poss N in pl]Your eyes are the parts of your body with which you see. ⇒ I opened my eyes and looked. ⇒ Maria's eyes filled with tears. ⇒ ...a tall, thin white-haired lady with piercing dark brown eyes. ⇒ He is now blind in one eye.2. verbIf you eye someone or something in a particular way, you look at them carefully in that way. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ Sally eyed Claire with interest. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ We eyed each other thoughtfully. [V n] ⇒ Martin eyed the bottle at Marianne's elbow.3. countable noun [usu sing]You use eye when you are talking about a person's ability to judge things or about the way in which they are considering or dealing with things. [+for] ⇒ William was a man of discernment, with an eye for quality. ⇒ Their chief negotiator turned his critical eye on the United States. ⇒ It did not take his practised eye long to notice that he was not the only one who was hanging about. ⇒ He first learnt to fish under the watchful eye of his grandmother.4. countable noun [adj N]An electric eye or infrared eye is a device which can recognize the presence of people or objects by detecting the light or heat coming from them. ⇒ An infra-red eye is said to detect the movement of any animal within an angle of 110 degrees at up to 10 metres.5. singular noun [usu with poss]People sometimes talk about the eye of the camera when they are talking about something being filmed or photographed, or the way something appears in a photograph or film. ⇒ I was again using the cold, unflinching eye of the camera to probe a sick society.6. countable nounAn eye on a potato is one of the dark spots from which new stems grow.7. countable nounAn eye is a small metal loop which a hook fits into, as a fastening on a piece of clothing.8. countable nounThe eye of a needle is the small hole at one end which the thread passes through.9. singular nounThe eye of a storm, tornado, or hurricane is the centre of it. [+ of] ⇒ The eye of the hurricane hit Florida just south of Miami.10. → See also black eye, private eye, shut-eye11. →  before/in front of/under your eyes12. →  cast/run your eye13. →  catch someone's eye14. → See also eye-catching15. →  catch someone's eye16. →  to clap eyes on someone17. →  to make eye contact18. →  to close your eyes to something19. →  to cry your eyes out20. →  an eye for an eye21. →  as far as the eye can see22. →  have an eye for something23. →  in/to someone's eyes24. →  to keep your eyes open25. →  to keep your eyes peeled26. →  keep your eye on something27. →  make eyes at someone28. →  there's more to this than meets the eye29. →  to meet someone's eyes30. →  all eyes are on something31. →  have your eye on someone32. →  have your eye on something33. →  with your eyes open34. →  to open your eyes35. →  to see eye to eye36. →  the eye of the storm37. →  take your eyes off something38. →  through someone's eyes39. →  up to your eyes40. → the apple of your eye41. → to turn a blind eye42. → to feast your eyes43. → to look someone in the eye44. → in your mind's eye45. → the naked eye46. → to pull the wool over someone's eyes► →  eye up
