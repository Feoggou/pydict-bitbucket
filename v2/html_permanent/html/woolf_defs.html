Woolf

(wʊlf )
Definitions
noun1.  Leonard Sidney. 1880–1969, English publisher and political writer2.  his wife, Virginia. 1882–1941, English novelist and critic. Her novels, which include Mrs Dalloway (1925), To the Lighthouse (1927), The Waves (1931), and Between the Acts (1941), employ such techniques as the interior monologue and stream of consciousness
