plinth

(plɪnθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: socle the rectangular slab or block that forms the lowest part of the base of a column, statue, pedestal, or pier2. Also called: plinth course the lowest part of the wall of a building that appears above ground level, esp one that is formed of a course of stone or brick3. a flat block on either side of a doorframe, where the architrave meets the skirting4. a flat base on which a structure or piece of equipment is placed

Word Origin

C17: from Latin plinthus, from Greek plinthos brick, shaped stone
