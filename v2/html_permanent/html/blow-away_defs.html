blow away

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb) slang, mainly US1. to kill (someone) by shooting2. to defeat decisively

blow away

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you say that you are blown away by something, or if it blows you away, you mean that you are very impressed by it. [informal] [be V-ed P] ⇒ I was blown away by the tone and the quality of the story. [V n P] ⇒ She just totally blew me away with her singing.2. phrasal verbIf someone blows another person away, they kill them by shooting them. [informal] [V n P] ⇒ He'd like to get hold of a gun and blow them all away. →  blow
