(staɪfəlɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveStifling heat is so intense that it makes you feel uncomfortable. You can also use stifling to describe a place that is extremely hot. suffocating ⇒ The stifling heat of the little room was beginning to make me nauseous.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a situation is stifling, it makes you feel uncomfortable because you cannot do what you want. suffocating ⇒ Life at home with her parents and two sisters was stifling. ⇒ ...a stifling bureaucracy.3. → see also stifle
Example sentences containing 'stifling'
The iciness of the first winter, followed by the stifling heat of summer. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LIONThey were sick of religiosity in all its forms, just as they were sick of the Order's omnipresent and stifling holism. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODRafael was standing at the window, obediently stifling his tears. Clive Barker SACRAMENTThe sleepy doorman let them out with barely a glance, too busy stifling a yawn.
