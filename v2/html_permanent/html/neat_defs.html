neat1

(niːt )
Definitions
adjective1. clean, tidy, and orderly2. liking or insisting on order and cleanliness; fastidious3. smoothly or competently done; efficient ⇒ a neat job4. pat or slick ⇒ his excuse was suspiciously neat5. (of alcoholic drinks) without added water, lemonade, etc; undiluted6. a less common word for net2 ⇒ neat profits7. slang, mainly US and Canadian good; pleasing; admirable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

neatly (ˈneatly) adverbneatness (ˈneatness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Old French net, from Latin nitidus clean, shining, from nitēre to shine; related to Middle Irish niam beauty, brightness, Old Persian naiba- beautiful

neat for learners of English

(niːt )
Word forms: neater , neatest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A neat place, thing, or person is tidy and smart, and has everything in the correct place. ⇒ So they left her in the neat little house, alone with her memories. ⇒ She undressed and put her wet clothes in a neat pile in the corner. ⇒ ...a girl in a neat grey flannel suit. ⇒ Everything was neat and tidy and gleamingly clean.neatly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ He folded his paper neatly and sipped his coffee. ⇒ At the door was a neatly dressed, dignified man.neatness uncount noun ⇒ The grounds were a perfect balance between neatness and natural wildness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is neat keeps their home or possessions tidy, with everything in the correct place. ⇒ 'That's not like Alf,' he said, 'leaving papers muddled like that. He's always so neat.'neatly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I followed her into that room which her mother had maintained so neatly. ⇒ He had maybe a thousand tapes, all neatly labelled and catalogued.neatness uncount noun ⇒ ...a paragon of neatness, efficiency and reliability.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A neat object, part of the body, or shape is quite small and has a smooth outline. ⇒ ...a faded woman with neat features. ⇒ ...neat handwriting.neatly graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed] ⇒ She was a small woman, slender and neatly made.4. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A neat movement or action is done accurately and skilfully, with no unnecessary movements. ⇒ A neat move between Black and Keane left Nigel Clough in the clear, but his shot skimmed wide of the far post.neatly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He watched her peel and dissect a pear neatly, no mess, no sticky fingers. ⇒ ...watching a solitary car backing out neatly.5. graded adjective & adjectiveA neat way of organizing, achieving, explaining, or expressing something is clever and convenient. ⇒ It had been such a neat, clever plan. ⇒ Neat solutions are not easily found to these issues. ⇒ 'Make the punishment fit the crime.' How neat and tidy it sounded.neatly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Real people do not fit neatly into these categories. ⇒ The theory that personality is determined by our biology neatly lets everyone off the hook.neatness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ He knew full well he had been outflanked, and he appreciated the neatness of it.6. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is neat, you mean that it is very good. [US, informal, approval] ⇒ 'Oh, those new apartments are really neat,' the girl babbled on. ⇒ It'll be neat to have a father and son playing on the same team. ⇒ He thought Mick was a really neat guy.7. adjective [ADJ n]When someone drinks strong alcohol neat, they do not add a weaker liquid such as water to it. [mainly British] ⇒ He poured himself a brandy and swallowed it neat. ⇒ He took a mouthful of neat whisky, and coughed.regional note:   in AM, use straight
