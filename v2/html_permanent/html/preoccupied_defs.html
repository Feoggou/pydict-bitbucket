(priːˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. engrossed or absorbed in something, esp one's own thoughts2. already or previously occupied3. biology (of a taxonomic name) already used to designate a genus, species, etc

preoccupied

(priɒkjʊpaɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are preoccupied, you are thinking a lot about something or someone, and so you hardly notice other things. [+ with/by] ⇒ Tom Banbury was preoccupied with the missing Shepherd child and did not want to devote time to the new murder. ⇒ She looked very preoccupied.
Example sentences containing 'preoccupied'
For weeks she had been preoccupied , distracted, dates no longer meaningful.
