vigour

or US vigor (ˈvɪɡə )
Definitions
noun1. exuberant and resilient strength of body or mind; vitality2. substantial effective energy or force ⇒ the vigour of the tempest3. forcefulness; intensity ⇒ the vigour of her complaints4. the capacity for survival or strong healthy growth in a plant or animal ⇒ hybrid vigour5. the most active period or stage of life, manhood, etc; prime6. mainly US legal force or effectiveness; validity (esp in the phrase in vigour)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French vigeur, from Latin vigor activity, from vigēre to be lively
