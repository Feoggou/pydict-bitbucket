pro bono

Latin (prəʊ ˈbəʊnəʊ) law
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. US of or relating to free legal work on cases in which a defendant is unable to pay for his own defence ⇒ The law firm has taken Kimberlin's case on a pro bono basis., ⇒ They spend between 5% and 10% of their time on pro bono cases.adverb2. US free of charge, for a defendant who is unable to pay for his own defence ⇒ The firm has undertaken to work pro bono (free of charge) even if it recovers substantial compensation.
