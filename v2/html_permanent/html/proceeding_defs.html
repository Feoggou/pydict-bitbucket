proceeding

(prəˈsiːdɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an act or course of action2. a. the institution of a legal actionb. any step taken in a legal action3. (plural) the minutes of the meetings of a club, society, etc4. (plural) legal action; litigation5. (plural) the events of an occasion, meeting, etc

Word forms: proceedings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Legal proceedings are legal action taken against someone. [formal] [+ against] ⇒ ...criminal proceedings against the former prime minister. ⇒ The Council had brought proceedings to stop the store from trading on Sundays.2. countable noun [usu pl]The proceedings are an organized series of events that take place in a particular place. [formal] ⇒ The proceedings of the enquiry will take place in private. ⇒ He viewed the proceedings with doubt and alarm.3. plural nounYou can refer to a written record of the discussions at a meeting or conference as the proceedings. minutes ⇒ The Department of Transport is to publish the conference proceedings.
