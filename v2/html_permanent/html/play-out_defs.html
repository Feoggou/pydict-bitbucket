play out

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) to finish ⇒ let's play the game out if we aren't too late2. (tr; often passive) informal to use up or exhaust3. (transitive) to release gradually ⇒ he played the rope out4. (intransitive) to happen or turn out ⇒ Let's wait and see how things play out

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verb [usu passive]If a dramatic event is played out, it gradually takes place. [be V-ed P] ⇒ Her union reforms were played out against a background of rising unemployment. [be V-ed P] ⇒ The film has eerie parallels with the drama being played out in real life. [Also V P n] →  play
