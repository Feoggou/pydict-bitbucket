low-level

(ˌləʊˈlɛvəl)
Definitions
adjective1. not high in the sky ⇒ They saw a government war plane make a series of low-level bombing raids.2. low in intensity or degree ⇒ If the Attorney General found evidence of low-level crimes (say, minor financial misdoing), he could threaten prosecution., ⇒ a renewed attempt to choose a site for a low-level radioactive waste dump3. at a relatively low level in a hierarchy ⇒ a thorough background check for a low-level employee, ⇒ burglary, shoplifting, low-level drug selling, and other nongang activities
