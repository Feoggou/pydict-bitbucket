garlic

(ˈɡɑːlɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a hardy widely cultivated Asian alliaceous plant, Allium sativum, having a stem bearing whitish flowers and bulbils2. a. the bulb of this plant, made up of small segments (cloves) that have a strong odour and pungent taste and are used in cookingb. (as modifier) ⇒ a garlic taste3. any of various other plants of the genus Allium
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English gārlēac, from gār spear + lēac leek
