harbor

(ˈhɑrbər ) in American English
Definitions
noun1.  a place of refuge, safety, etc.; retreat; shelter2.  a protected inlet, or branch of a sea, lake, etc., where ships can anchor, esp. one with port facilities verb transitive3.  to serve as, or provide, a place of protection to; shelter or house; conceal or hide4.  to be the dwelling place or habitat of5.  to hold in the mind; cling to ⇒ to harbor a grudge verb intransitive6.  to take shelter, as in a harbor7.  to live or exist
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

harborer (ˈharborer) noun harborless (ˈharborless) adjective

Word Origin

ME herberwe < OE herebeorg (& ON herbergi), lit., army shelter (< here, army + beorg, a shelter), akin to OHG heriberga: see harbinger

Powered by COBUILD
(hɑrbər ) for learners of American English
Word forms: harbors, harboring, harbored
Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, use harbour1. countable noun & "noun, in names"A harbor is an area of the sea at the coast which is partly enclosed by land or strong walls, so that boats can be left there safely. ⇒ She led us to a room with a balcony overlooking the harbor.2. transitive verbIf you harbor an emotion, thought, or secret, you have it in your mind over a long period of time. ⇒ He might have been murdered by a former client or someone harboring a grudge.3. transitive verbIf a person or country harbors someone who is wanted by the police, they let them stay in their house or country and offer them protection. ⇒ Accusations of harboring suspects were raised against the former Hungarian leadership.
