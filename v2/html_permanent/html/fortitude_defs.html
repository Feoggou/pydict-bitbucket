fortitude

(ˈfɔːtɪˌtjuːd )
Definitions
nounstrength and firmness of mind; resolute endurance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fortitudinous (ˌfortiˈtudinous) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fortitūdō courage

fortitude

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳtɪtjuːd , US -tuːd ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounIf you say that someone has shown fortitude, you admire them for being brave, calm, and uncomplaining when they have experienced something unpleasant or painful. [formal, approval] ⇒ He suffered a long series of illnesses with tremendous dignity and fortitude.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

If you have any doubts as to my fortitude , I will prove you wrong. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY
