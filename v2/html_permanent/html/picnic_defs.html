picnic

(ˈpɪknɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a trip or excursion to the country, seaside, etc, on which people bring food to be eaten in the open air2. a. any informal meal eaten outsideb. (as modifier) ⇒ a picnic lunch3. informal, mainly Australian a troublesome situation or experience4. See no picnicverb -nics, -nicking or -nicked5. (intransitive) to eat a picnic

Derived Forms

picnicker (ˈpicnicker) noun

Word Origin

C18: from French piquenique, of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounWhen people have a picnic, they eat a meal out of doors, usually in a field or a forest, or at the beach. ⇒ We're going on a picnic tomorrow. ⇒ We'll take a picnic lunch.2. verbWhen people picnic somewhere, they have a picnic. [V] ⇒ Afterwards, we picnicked on the riverbank. [V-ing] ⇒ ...such a perfect day for picnicking.picnicker Word forms: picnickers countable noun ⇒ ...fires started by careless picnickers.3. →  be no picnic
