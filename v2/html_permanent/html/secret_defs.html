secret

(ˈsiːkrɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. kept hidden or separate from the knowledge of others ▶ Related adjective: cryptic2. known only to initiates ⇒ a secret password3. hidden from general view or use ⇒ a secret garden4. able or tending to keep things private or to oneself5. operating without the knowledge of outsiders ⇒ a secret society6. outside the normal range of knowledgenoun7. something kept or to be kept hidden8. something unrevealed; mystery9. an underlying explanation, reason, etc, that is not apparent ⇒ the secret of success10. a method, plan, etc, known only to initiates11. liturgy a variable prayer, part of the Mass, said by the celebrant after the offertory and before the preface12. See in the secret

Derived Forms

secretly (ˈsecretly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin sēcrētus concealed, from sēcernere to sift; see secern

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n] [v-link ADJ]If something is secret, it is known about by only a small number of people, and is not told or shown to anyone else. ⇒ Soldiers have been training at a secret location. ⇒ The police have been trying to keep the documents secret.secretly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He wore a hidden microphone to secretly tape-record conversations. ⇒ ...secretly organised events.2. → see also top secret3. countable nounA secret is a fact that is known by only a small number of people, and is not told to anyone else. ⇒ I think he enjoyed keeping our love a secret. ⇒ I didn't want anyone to know about it, it was my secret.4. singular nounIf you say that a particular way of doing things is the secret of achieving something, you mean that it is the best or only way to achieve it. [+ of] ⇒ The secret of success is honesty and fair dealing. ⇒ I learned something about writing. The secret is to say less than you need.5. countable noun [usu pl, oft with poss]Something's secrets are the things about it which have never been fully explained. [+ of] ⇒ We have an opportunity now to really unlock the secrets of the universe. ⇒ The past is riddled with deep dark secrets.6. →  in secret7. →  to keep a secret8. →  make no secret
