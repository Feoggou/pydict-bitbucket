frenzy

(ˈfrɛnzɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -zies1. violent mental derangement2. wild excitement or agitation; distraction3. a bout of wild or agitated activity ⇒ a frenzy of preparationsverb -zies, -zying or -zied4. (transitive) to make frantic; drive into a frenzy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French frenesie, from Late Latin phrēnēsis madness, delirium, from Late Greek, ultimately from Greek phrēn mind; compare frenetic
