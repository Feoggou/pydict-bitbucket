reprint

Definitions
noun (ˈriːˌprɪnt )1. a reproduction in print of any matter already published; offprint2. a reissue of a printed work using the same type, plates, etc, as the originalverb (riːˈprɪnt )3. (transitive) to print again

reprinter (reˈprinter) noun

reprint

Powered by COBUILD

Word forms: reprints, reprinting , reprinted
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (riːprɪnt ). The noun is pronounced (riːprɪnt ).1. verb [usu passive]If a book is reprinted, further copies of it are printed when all the other ones have been sold. [be V-ed] ⇒ It remained an exceptionally rare book until it was reprinted in 1918.2. countable nounA reprint is a process in which new copies of a book or article are printed because all the other ones have been sold. ⇒ Demand picked up and a reprint was required last November.3. countable nounA reprint is a new copy of a book or article, printed because all the other ones have been sold or because minor changes have been made to the original. [+ of] ⇒ ...a reprint of a 1962 novel.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
