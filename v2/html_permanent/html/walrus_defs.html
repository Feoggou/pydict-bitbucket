walrus

(ˈwɔːlrəs , ˈwɒl-)
Definitions
noun plural -ruses or -rusa pinniped mammal, Odobenus rosmarus, of northern seas, having a tough thick skin, upper canine teeth enlarged as tusks, and coarse whiskers and feeding mainly on shellfish: family Odobenidae
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: probably from Dutch, from Scandinavian; compare Old Norse hrosshvalr (literally: horse whale) and Old English horschwæl; see horse, whale
