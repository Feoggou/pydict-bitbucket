faction1

(ˈfækʃən )
Definitions
noun1. a group of people forming a minority within a larger body, esp a dissentious group2. strife or dissension within a group
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

factional (ˈfactional) adjectivefactionalism (ˈfactionalˌism) nounfactionalist (ˈfactionalist) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin factiō a making, from facere to make, do

faction

Powered by COBUILD
(fækʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: factions
Definitions
1. countable nounA faction is an organized group of people within a larger group, which opposes some of the ideas of the larger group and fights for its own ideas. ⇒ A peace agreement will be signed by the leaders of the country's warring factions.2. uncount nounFaction is also used to describe argument and disagreement within a group of people. ⇒ Faction and self-interest appear to be the norm.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
