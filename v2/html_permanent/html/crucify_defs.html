crucify

(ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to put to death by crucifixion2. slang to defeat, ridicule, etc, totally ⇒ the critics crucified his performance3. to treat very cruelly; torment4. to subdue (passion, lust, etc); mortify
Derived forms
cruciˌfier (ˈcruciˌfier) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French crucifier, from Late Latin crucifīgere to crucify, to fasten to a cross, from Latin crux cross + fīgere to fasten

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verb [usu passive]If someone is crucified, they are killed by being tied or nailed to a cross and left to die. [be V-ed] ⇒ ...the day that Christ was crucified.2. verbTo crucify someone means to criticize or punish them severely. [informal] [V n] ⇒ She'll crucify me if she finds you still here. [V n] ⇒ She was crucified by the critics for her performance.
