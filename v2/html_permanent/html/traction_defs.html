traction

(ˈtrækʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of drawing or pulling, esp by motive power2. the state of being drawn or pulled3. medicine the application of a steady pull on a part during healing of a fractured or dislocated bone, using a system of weights and pulleys or splints4. the adhesive friction between a wheel and a surface, as between a driving wheel of a motor vehicle and the road
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tractional (ˈtractional) adjectivetractive (ˈtræktɪv ) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin tractiō, from Latin tractus dragged; see tractile

Definitions
1. uncount noun [oft in N]Traction is a form of medical treatment, in which weights and pulleys are used to gently pull or stretch an injured part of the body for a period of time. You say that a person who is having this treatment is in traction. ⇒ Is there an alternative to traction for a broken leg? ⇒ Isabelle's legs were in traction for about two and a half weeks.2. uncount nounTraction is a particular form of power that makes a vehicle move.3. uncount nounTraction is the grip that something has on the ground, especially the wheels of a vehicle.
