(lubrɪkeɪʃən) in Mechanical Engineering
Definitions
noun(Mechanical engineering: Fluid engineering) Lubrication involves adding a substance between solid surfaces that are moving against each other in order to reduce friction and wear. ⇒ It is important to use a good-quality grease for lubrication of the bearings. ⇒ The basic purpose of lubrication is to minimize friction and wear. ⇒ Lubrication involves adding a substance between solid surfaces that are moving against each other in order to reduce friction and wear.
