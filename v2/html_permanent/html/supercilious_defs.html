supercilious

(ˌsuːpəˈsɪlɪəs )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjectivedisplaying arrogant pride, scorn, or indifference

Derived Forms

ˌsuperciliously (ˌsuperˈciliously) adverbˌsuperciliousness (ˌsuperˈciliousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin superciliōsus, from supercilium eyebrow; see superciliary

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is supercilious, you disapprove of them because they behave in a way that shows they think they are better than other people. [disapproval] ⇒ His manner is supercilious and arrogant. ⇒ Her eyebrows were arched in supercilious surprise.
