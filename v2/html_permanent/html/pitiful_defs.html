Definitions

adjective1. arousing or deserving pity2. arousing or deserving contempt3. archaic full of pity or compassion

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is pitiful is so sad, weak, or small that you feel pity for them. ⇒ He sounded both pitiful and eager to get what he wanted. ⇒ It was the most pitiful sight I had ever seen.pitifully graded adverb & adverb ⇒ His legs were pitifully thin compared to the rest of his bulk.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as pitiful, you mean that it is completely inadequate. [disapproval] ⇒ The choice is pitiful and the quality of some of the products is very low. ⇒ The farmers pay pitiful wages, often in the form of food and clothes.pitifully graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ Clearly that constitutes a pitifully inadequate return on my investment.3. graded adjectiveIf you describe something as pitiful, you mean that it does not deserve respect or consideration. ⇒ This argument seems to show a pitiful lack of confidence in the capabilities of our juries.
