resolute

(ˈrɛzəˌluːt )
Definitions
adjective1. firm in purpose or belief; steadfast2. characterized by resolution; determined ⇒ a resolute answer
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

resolutely (ˈresoˌlutely) adverbresoluteness (ˈresoˌluteness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin resolutus, from resolvere to resolve

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as resolute, you approve of them because they are very determined not to change their mind or not to give up a course of action. [formal] ⇒ Voters perceive him as a decisive and resolute international leader. ⇒ He described the situation as very dangerous and called for resolute action.resolutely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ He resolutely refused to speak English unless forced to. ⇒ The United States remains resolutely opposed to this.
