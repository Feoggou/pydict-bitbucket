enduring

(ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. permanent; lasting2. having forbearance; long-suffering
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

enduringly (enˈduringly) adverbenduringness (enˈduringness) noun
Example sentences containing 'enduring'
The process produced a smoother and more enduring finish than the lighter, less painful, quicker drying synthetic waxes now available. Weldon, Fay SPLITTINGAmy tells me the student association was livid because they wanted a sports stadium, but art is less expensive and more enduring. Smith, Evelyn E MISS MELVILLE REGRETSBut the watchman remembered where he had sent the package entrusted to him by the enduring grandmother of Sophia Sinclair. Heller, Keith MAN'S LOVING FAMILYDeep inside herself, she had always known that she was a woman born for enduring things. Elizabeth Lowell BEAUTIFUL DREAMER
