administer

(ədˈmɪnɪstə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (mainly tr)1. (also intr) to direct or control (the affairs of a business, government, etc)2. to put into execution; dispense ⇒ administer justice3. (when intr, foll by to) to give or apply (medicine, assistance, etc) as a remedy or relief4. to apply formally; perform ⇒ to administer extreme unction5. to supervise or impose the taking of (an oath, etc)6. to manage or distribute (an estate, property, etc)
Word origin
C14: amynistre, via Old French from Latin administrare, from ad- to + ministrāre to minister

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone administers something such as a country, the law, or a test, they take responsibility for organizing and supervising it. [V n] ⇒ The plan calls for the U.N. to administer the country until elections can be held. [V n] ⇒ We hope that they're going to administer justice impartially. [V n] ⇒ Next summer's exams would be straightforward to administer and mark. [Also V n to n]2. verbIf a doctor or a nurse administers a drug, they give it to a patient. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Paramedics are trained to administer certain drugs. [Also V n + to]3. verbIf someone administers a punch or a kick, they punch or kick someone. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He is shown in the tape of the beating as administering most of the blows.
