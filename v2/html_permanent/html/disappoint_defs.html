disappoint

(ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to fail to meet the expectations, hopes, desires, or standards of; let down2. to prevent the fulfilment of (a plan, intention, etc); frustrate; thwart
Word origin
C15 (originally meaning: to remove from office): from Old French desapointier; see dis-1, appoint

verbIf things or people disappoint you, they are not as good as you had hoped, or do not do what you hoped they would do. [V n] ⇒ She knew that she would disappoint him. [V n] ⇒ A spokesman for UNHCR said he was surprised and disappointed by the decision.
