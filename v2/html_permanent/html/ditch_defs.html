ditch

(dɪtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a narrow channel dug in the earth, usually used for drainage, irrigation, or as a boundary marker2. any small, natural waterway3. Irish a bank made of earth excavated from and placed alongside a drain or stream4. informal either of the gutters at the side of a tenpin bowling lane5. See last ditchverb6. to make a ditch or ditches in (a piece of ground)7. (intransitive) to edge (something) with a ditch8. informal to crash or be crashed, esp deliberately, as to avoid more unpleasant circumstances ⇒ he had to ditch the car9. (transitive) slang to abandon or discard ⇒ to ditch a girlfriend10. informal to land (an aircraft) on water in an emergency11. (transitive) US slang to evade ⇒ to ditch the police
Derived forms
ditcher (ˈditcher) nounditchless (ˈditchless) adjective
Word origin
Old English dīc; related to Old Saxon dīk, Old Norse dīki, Middle High German tīch dyke, pond, Latin fīgere to stick, see dyke1

ditch

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪtʃ )
Word forms: ditches , ditching , ditched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA ditch is a long narrow channel cut into the ground at the side of a road or field.2. verbIf you ditch something that you have or are responsible for, you abandon it or get rid of it, because you no longer want it. [informal] [V n] ⇒ I decided to ditch the sofa bed. [V n] ⇒ Unpopular policies such as unilateral disarmament were ditched.3. verbIf someone ditches someone, they end a relationship with that person. [informal] [V n] ⇒ I can't bring myself to ditch him and start again.4. ergative verb & verbIf a pilot ditches an aircraft or if it ditches, the pilot makes an emergency landing. crash-land [V n] ⇒ One American pilot was forced to ditch his jet in the Gulf. [V] ⇒ A survivor was knocked unconscious when the helicopter ditched.5. → see also last-ditch
