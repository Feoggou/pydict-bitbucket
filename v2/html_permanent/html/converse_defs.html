converse1

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (kənˈvɜːs ) (intransitive; often foll by with)1. to engage in conversation (with)2. to commune spiritually (with)3. obsoletea. to associate; consortb. to have sexual intercoursenoun (ˈkɒnvɜːs )4. conversation (often in the phrase hold converse with)5. obsoletea. fellowship or acquaintanceb. sexual intercourse
Derived forms
converser (conˈverser) noun
Word origin
C16: from Old French converser, from Latin conversārī to keep company with, from conversāre to turn constantly, from vertere to turn

converse2

(ˈkɒnvɜːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (prenominal) reversed; opposite; contrarynoun2. something that is opposite or contrary3. logica. a categorical proposition obtained from another by the transposition of subject and predicate, as no bad man is bald from no bald man is badb. a proposition so derived, possibly by weakening a universal proposition to the corresponding particular, as some socialists are rich from all rich men are socialists4. logic, mathematics a relation that holds between two relata only when a given relation holds between them in reverse order: thus father of is the converse of son of
Word origin
C16: from Latin conversus turned around; see converse1

Word forms: converses, conversing , conversed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (kənvɜːʳs ). The noun is pronounced (kɒnvɜːʳs ).1. verbIf you converse with someone, you talk to them. You can also say that two people converse. [formal] [V + with] ⇒ Luke sat directly behind the pilot and conversed with him. [V] ⇒ They were conversing in German, their only common language.2. singular nounThe converse of a statement is its opposite or reverse. [formal] ⇒ What you do for a living is critical to where you settle and how you live - and the converse is also true.
