unable

(ʌnˈeɪbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; foll by to) lacking the necessary power, ability, or authority (to do something); not able2. archaic incompetent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌneɪbəl )
Definitions
adjectiveIf you are unable to do something, it is impossible for you to do it, for example because you do not have the necessary skill or knowledge, or because you do not have enough time or money. ⇒ The military may feel unable to hand over power to a civilian President next year. ⇒ Unable any longer to keep from breaking in she said, 'I simply cannot believe you're serious.'
