placate

(pləˈkeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to pacify or appease

Derived Forms

placation (plaˈcation) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin plācāre; see placable

Powered by COBUILD
(pləkeɪt , US pleɪkeɪt )
Word forms: placates , placating , placated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you placate someone, you do or say something to make them stop feeling angry. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He smiled, trying to placate me. [V-ing] ⇒ 'I didn't mean to upset you,' Agnew said in a placating voice. [Also V]
Example sentences containing 'placate'
I followed more slowly, wondering how to placate Jett without giving too much away.
