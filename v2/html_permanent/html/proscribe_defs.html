proscribe

(prəʊˈskraɪb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to condemn or prohibit2. to outlaw; banish; exile3. (in ancient Rome) to outlaw (a citizen) by posting his name in public

Derived Forms

proscriber (proˈscriber) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōscrībere to put up a written public notice, from prō- in public + scrībere to write
