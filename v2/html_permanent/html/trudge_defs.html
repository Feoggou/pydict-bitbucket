trudge

(trʌdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to walk or plod heavily or wearily2. (transitive) to pass through or over by trudgingnoun3. a long tiring walk

Derived Forms

trudger (ˈtrudger) noun

Word Origin

C16: of obscure origin

(trʌdʒ )
Word forms: trudges , trudging , trudged
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you trudge somewhere, you walk there slowly and with heavy steps, especially because you are tired or unhappy. [V prep/adv] ⇒ We had to trudge up the track back to the station.Trudge is also a noun. ⇒ We were reluctant to start the long trudge home.
Example sentences containing 'trudge'
I shook my head as I watched her go and then resumed my trudge down the hill. Bringle, Mary DEATH OF AN UNKNOWN MANBlindly, he turned away and began to trudge towards the mill. Garth Nix LIRAEL: DAUGHTER OF THE CLAYR"They trudge across the room and through Ivy's door, and Ivy follows them in. Alex Shakar THE SAVAGE GIRL
