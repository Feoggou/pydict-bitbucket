bullet

(ˈbʊlɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a small metallic missile enclosed in a cartridge, used as the projectile of a gun, rifle, etcb. the entire cartridge2. something resembling a bullet, esp in shape or effect3. stock exchange a fixed interest security with a single maturity date4. businessa. the final repayment of a loan that repays the whole of the sum borrowed, as interim payments have been for interest onlyb. (as modifier) ⇒ a bullet loan5. British slang dismissal, sometimes without notice (esp in the phrases get or give the bullet)6. printing See centred dot7. See bite the bullet
Derived forms
bullet-ˌlike (ˈbullet-ˌlike) adjective
Word origin
C16: from French boulette, diminutive of boule ball; see bowl2
