laurel

(ˈlɒrəl )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: bay, true laurel any lauraceous tree of the genus Laurus, such as the bay tree (see bay4) and L. canariensis, of the Canary Islands and Azores2. any lauraceous plant3. short for cherry laurel, mountain laurel4. See spurge laurel5. See spotted laurel6. (plural) a wreath of true laurel, worn on the head as an emblem of victory or honour in classical times7. (plural) honour, distinction, or fame8. See look to one's laurels9. See rest on one's laurelsverb -rels, -relling, -relled or US -rels, -reling or -reled10. (transitive) to crown with laurels
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13 lorer, from Old French lorier laurel tree, ultimately from Latin laurus
