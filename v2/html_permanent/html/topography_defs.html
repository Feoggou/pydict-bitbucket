topography

(təˈpɒɡrəfɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -phies1. the study or detailed description of the surface features of a region2. the detailed mapping of the configuration of a region3. the land forms or surface configuration of a region4. the surveying of a region's surface features5. the study or description of the configuration of any object

Derived Forms

topographer (toˈpographer) nountopographic (ˌtɒpəˈɡræfɪk ) or ˌtopographical (ˌtopoˈgraphical) adjectiveˌtopographically (ˌtopoˈgraphically) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounTopography is the study and description of the physical features of an area, for example its hills, valleys, or rivers, or the representation of these features on maps.2. countable noun [usu sing, with poss]The topography of a particular area is its physical shape, including its hills, valleys, and rivers. [+ of] ⇒ The topography of the river's basin has changed significantly since the floods.
