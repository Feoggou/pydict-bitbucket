contravene

(ˌkɒntrəˈviːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to come into conflict with or infringe (rules, laws, etc)2. to dispute or contradict (a statement, proposition, etc)
Derived forms
ˌcontravener (ˌcontraˈvener) nouncontravention (ˌkɒntrəˈvɛnʃən ) noun
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin contrāvenīre, from Latin contra- + venīre to come

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbTo contravene a law or rule means to do something that is forbidden by the law or rule. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The Board has banned the film on the grounds that it contravenes criminal libel laws. [V n] ⇒ He said the article did not contravene the industry's code of conduct.contravention (kɒntrəvenʃən )Word forms: contraventions variable noun [oft in N of n] [+ of] ⇒ The government has lent millions of pounds to debt-ridden banks in contravention of local banking laws.
