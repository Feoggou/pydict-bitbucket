Areopagus

(ˌærɪˈɒpəɡəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the hill to the northwest of the Acropolis in Athensb. (in ancient Athens) the judicial council whose members (Areopagites) met on this hill2. literary any high court
Word origin
via Latin from Greek Areiopagus, contracted from Areios pagos, hill of Ares
