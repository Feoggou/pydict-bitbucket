modulate

(ˈmɒdjʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to change the tone, pitch, or volume of2. (transitive) to adjust or regulate the degree of3. musica. to subject to or undergo modulation in musicb. (often foll by to) to make or become in tune (with a pitch, key, etc)4. (transitive) physics, electronics to cause to vary by a process of modulation

Derived Forms

modulability (ˌmɒdjʊləˈbɪlɪtɪ ) nounmodulative (ˈmodulative) or modulatory (ˈmodulatory) adjectivemoduˌlator (ˈmoduˌlator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin modulātus in due measure, melodious, from modulārī to regulate, from modus measure
