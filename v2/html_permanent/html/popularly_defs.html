popularly

(ˈpɒpjʊləlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. by the public as a whole; generally or widely2. usually; commonly ⇒ his full name is Robert, but he is popularly known as Bob3. in a popular manner

popularly

Powered by COBUILD
(pɒpjʊləʳli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adverb & adverbIf something or someone is popularly known as something, most people call them that, although it is not their official name or title. ⇒ ...the Mesozoic era, more popularly known as the age of dinosaurs. ⇒ ...an infection popularly called mad cow disease. ⇒ ...the United States Immigration Commission, known popularly as the Dillingham Commission.2. adverb [ADV -ed]If something is popularly believed or supposed to be the case, most people believe or suppose it to be the case, although it may not be true. ⇒ Schizophrenia is not a 'split mind' as is popularly believed. ⇒ She possessed the vibrant personality that is so often popularly associated with Spanish women.3. adverb [ADV -ed]A popularly elected leader or government has been elected by a majority of the people in a country. ⇒ Walesa was Poland's first popularly elected President.
