oscillate

(ˈɒsɪˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to move or swing from side to side regularly2. (intransitive) to waver between opinions, courses of action, etc3. physics to undergo or produce or cause to undergo or produce oscillation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Latin oscillāre to swing, from oscillum a swing

oscillate for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒsɪleɪt )
Word forms: oscillates , oscillating , oscillated
Definitions
1. verbIf an object oscillates, it moves repeatedly from one position to another and back again, or keeps getting bigger and smaller. [formal] [V] ⇒ I checked to see if the needle indicating volume was oscillating.oscillation (ɒsɪleɪʃən )Word forms: oscillations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ Some oscillation of the fuselage had been noticed on early flights.2. verb [no passive]If the level or value of something oscillates between one amount and another, it keeps going up and down between the two amounts. [formal] [V between pl-amount] ⇒ The share price has oscillated between a five-year high of 157.25p in 1997 and this year's low of 91.5p. [V] ⇒ Oil markets oscillated on the day's reports from Geneva. [V-ing] ⇒ ...an oscillating signal of microwave frequency.oscillation Word forms: oscillations variable noun [+ in] ⇒ There have always been slight oscillations in world temperature.3. verb [no passive]If you oscillate between two moods, attitudes, or types of behaviour, you keep changing from one to the other and back again. [formal] [V + between/and] ⇒ The president of the Republic oscillated between a certain audacity and a prudent realism.oscillation uncount noun [+ between/and] ⇒ ...that perpetual oscillation between despair and distracted joy.
