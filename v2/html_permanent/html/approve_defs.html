approve1

(əˈpruːv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when intr, often foll by of) to consider fair, good, or right; commend (a person or thing)2. (transitive) to authorize or sanction3. (transitive) obsolete to demonstrate or prove by trial
Derived forms
apˈprovingly adverb
Word origin
C14: from Old French aprover, from Latin approbāre to approve, from probāre to test, prove

approve2

Definitions

verb(transitive) law to improve or increase the value of (waste or common land), as by enclosure
Word origin

approve

Powered by COBUILD
(əpruːv )
Word forms: approves , approving , approved
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you approve of an action, event, or suggestion, you like it or are pleased about it. [V + of] ⇒ Not everyone approves of the festival. [V of n] ⇒ I approved of the proposal.2. verbIf you approve of someone or something, you like and admire them. [V + of] ⇒ You've never approved of Henry, have you? [V of n] ⇒ I didn't approve of his manner.3. verbIf someone in a position of authority approves a plan or idea, they formally agree to it and say that it can happen. [V n] ⇒ The Russian Parliament has approved a program of radical economic reforms. [V n] ⇒ MPs approved the Bill by a majority of 97.4. verbIf a product or person is approved by an official organization, they are declared to be of a good enough standard to be used or employed. authorize [be V-ed] ⇒ We have three suppliers in all who are approved by the Organic Farm Food Association. [Also V n]5. → see also approved, approving
