rowdy

(ˈraʊdɪ )
Definitions
adjective -dier or -diest1. tending to create noisy disturbances; rough, loud, or disorderly ⇒ a rowdy gang of football supportersnoun plural -dies2. a person who behaves in a rough disorderly fashion
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rowdily (ˈrowdily) adverbrowdiness (ˈrowdiness) noun

Word Origin

C19: originally US slang, perhaps related to row3

(raʊdi ) for learners of English
Word forms: rowdier , rowdiest , rowdies
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveWhen people are rowdy, they are noisy, rough, and likely to cause trouble. ⇒ He has complained to the police about rowdy neighbours. ⇒ There were rowdy scenes inside parliament during the debate.rowdiness uncount noun ⇒ ...adolescent behaviour like vandalism and rowdiness.2. plural nounIf you describe people as rowdies, you mean that they are noisy, rough, and likely to cause trouble. [informal] ⇒ ...a bar in New York where the owner kept a baseball bat to deal with rowdies.
