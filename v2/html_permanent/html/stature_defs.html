stature

(ˈstætʃə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the height of something, esp a person or animal when standing2. the degree of development of a person ⇒ the stature of a champion3. intellectual or moral greatness ⇒ a man of stature

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin statūra, from stāre to stand

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [usu with poss] [in N]Someone's stature is their height. ⇒ It's more than his physical stature that makes him remarkable. ⇒ Mother was of very small stature, barely five feet tall. ⇒ She was a little short in stature.2. uncount noun [usu with poss] [oft in N]The stature of a person is the importance and reputation that they have. ⇒ Who can deny his stature as the world's greatest cellist? ⇒ This club has grown in stature over the last 20 years.
