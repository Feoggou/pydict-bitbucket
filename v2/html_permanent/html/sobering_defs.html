(soʊbərɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You say that something is a sobering thought or has a sobering effect when a situation seems serious and makes you become serious and thoughtful. ⇒ Statistics paint a sobering picture–unemployment, tight credit, lower home values, sluggish job growth. ⇒ The events of October 1987 had a sobering effect on managers of large funds.
Example sentences containing 'sobering'
Ma flashed me a look of approval for sobering him up so quickly. Andrews, Virginia WEB OF DREAMSIt was all famous stuff, nearly all of it, anyway, and it was sobering to stroll round and see so much impressive sculpture. Malcolm, John THE GWEN JOHN SCULPTUREShe put her hand on my chest to stop me falling and I could feel its sobering power as her touch worked its magic. James Birrell THE MANANA MAN
