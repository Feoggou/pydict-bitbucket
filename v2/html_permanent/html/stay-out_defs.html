stay out

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (intransitive) to remain away from home ⇒ the cat stayed out all night2. (transitive) to remain beyond the end of ⇒ to stay out a welcome3. (transitive) to remain throughout ⇒ to stay the night out

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you stay out at night, you remain away from home, especially when you are expected to be there. stop out [V P] ⇒ That was the first time Elliot stayed out all night. [V P] ⇒ I met some friends and stayed out until eleven or twelve.2. phrasal verbIf workers who are striking stay out, they remain on strike. [mainly British] [V P] ⇒ The electricians at the power stations went on strike and stayed out nearly five days. →  stay
