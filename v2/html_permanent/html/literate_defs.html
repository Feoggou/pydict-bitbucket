literate

(ˈlɪtərɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. able to read and write2. educated; learned3. used to words rather than numbers as a means of expression. Compare numeratenoun4. a literate person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

literately (ˈliterately) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin litterātus learned. See letter

literate

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪtərət ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomeone who is literate is able to read and write. ⇒ Over one-quarter of the adult population are not fully literate.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as literate, you mean that they are intelligent and well-educated, especially about literature and the arts. [approval] ⇒ Scientists should be literate and articulate as well as able to handle figures. ⇒ His lyrics are highly literate. He even quotes Voltaire.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu adv ADJ]If you describe someone as literate in a particular subject, especially one that many people do not know anything about, you mean that they have a good knowledge and understanding of that subject. ⇒ Head teachers need to be financially literate. ⇒ We want to have more scientifically literate people running our television stations.4. → See also computer-literate
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
