virtuous

(ˈvɜːtʃʊəs )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by or possessing virtue or moral excellence; righteous; upright2. (of women) chaste or virginal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

virtuously (ˈvirtuously) adverbvirtuousness (ˈvirtuousness) noun

virtuous

Powered by COBUILD
(vɜːʳtʃuəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA virtuous person behaves in a moral and correct way. ⇒ Louis was shown as an intelligent, courageous and virtuous family man.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as virtuous, you mean that they have done what they ought to do and feel very pleased with themselves, perhaps too pleased. ⇒ I cleaned the flat, which left me feeling virtuous.virtuously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'I've already done that,' said Ronnie virtuously.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
