ambrosia

(æmˈbrəʊzɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. classical mythology the food of the gods, said to bestow immortality. Compare nectar (sense 2)2. anything particularly delightful to taste or smell3. another name for beebread4. any of various herbaceous plants constituting the genus Ambrosia, mostly native to America but widely naturalized: family Asteraceae (composites). The genus includes the ragweeds
Derived forms
amˈbrosial or amˈbrosian adjectiveamˈbrosially adverb
Word origin
C16: via Latin from Greek: immortality, from ambrotos, from a-1 + brotos mortal
