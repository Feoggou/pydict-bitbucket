restrain

(rɪˈstreɪn )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to hold (someone) back from some action, esp by force2. to deprive (someone) of liberty, as by imprisonment3. to limit or restrict
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

restrainable (reˈstrainable) adjective

Word Origin

C14 restreyne, from Old French restreindre, from Latin rēstringere to draw back tightly, from re- + stringere to draw, bind; see strain1

restrain

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪstreɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: restrains , restraining , restrained
Definitions
1. verbIf you restrain someone, you stop them from doing what they intended or wanted to do, usually by using your physical strength. [V n] ⇒ Wally gripped my arm, partly to restrain me and partly to reassure me. [V n] ⇒ One onlooker had to be restrained by police.2. verbIf you restrain an emotion or you restrain yourself from doing something, you prevent yourself from showing that emotion or doing what you wanted or intended to do. [V n] ⇒ She was unable to restrain her desperate anger. [V n] ⇒ Unable to restrain herself, she rose and went to the phone. [V n + from] ⇒ Gladys wanted to ask, 'Aren't you angry with him?' But she restrained herself from doing so.3. verbTo restrain something that is growing or increasing means to prevent it from getting too large. [V n] ⇒ The radical 500-day plan was very clear on how it intended to try to restrain inflation. [V n] ⇒ In the 1970s, the government tried to restrain corruption.
