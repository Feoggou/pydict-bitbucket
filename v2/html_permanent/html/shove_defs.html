shove

(ʃʌv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to give a thrust or push to (a person or thing)2. (transitive) to give a violent push to; jostle3. (intransitive) to push one's way roughly4. (transitive) informal to put (something) somewhere, esp hurriedly or carelessly ⇒ shove it in the binnoun5. the act or an instance of shoving

Derived Forms

shover (ˈshover) noun

Word Origin

Old English scūfan; related to Old Norse skūfa to push, Gothic afskiuban to push away, Old High German skioban to shove

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you shove someone or something, you push them with a quick, violent movement. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He shoved her out of the way. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He was then shoved face down on the pavement. [V n] ⇒ He's the one who shoved me. [V] ⇒ She shoved as hard as she could.Shove is also a noun. ⇒ She gave Gracie a shove towards the house.2. verbIf you shove something somewhere, you push it there quickly and carelessly. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ We shoved a copy of the newsletter beneath their door. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He shoved a cloth in my hand.3. →  if push comes to shove
