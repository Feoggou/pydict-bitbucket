croft

(krɒft )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun British1. a small enclosed plot of land, adjoining a house, worked by the occupier and his family, esp in Scotland2. Lancashire dialect a patch of wasteland, formerly one used for bleaching fabric in the sun
Word origin
Old English croft; related to Middle Dutch krocht hill, field, Old English creopan to creep
