twitter

(ˈtwɪtə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) (esp of a bird) to utter a succession of chirping sounds2. (intransitive) to talk or move rapidly and tremulously3. (intransitive) to giggle ⇒ her schoolmates twittered behind their desks4. (transitive) to utter in a chirping waynoun5. a twittering sound, esp of a bird6. the act of twittering7. a state of nervous excitement (esp in the phrase in a twitter)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

twitterer (ˈtwitterer) nountwittery (ˈtwittery) adjective

Word Origin

C14: of imitative origin

Twitter

(ˈtwɪtə )
Definitions
noun1. trademark a website where people can post short messages about their current activitiesverb2. (intr; sometimes not capital) to write a short message on the Twitter website

twitter

Powered by COBUILD
(twɪtəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: twitters , twittering , twittered
Definitions
1. verbWhen birds twitter, they make a lot of short high-pitched sounds. [V] ⇒ There were birds twittering in the eucalyptus trees. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a tree filled with twittering birds.Twitter is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ Naomi would waken to the twitter of birds.2. verbIf you say that someone is twittering about something, you mean that they are speaking about silly or unimportant things, usually rather fast or in a high-pitched voice. [V + about] ⇒ ...debutantes twittering excitedly about Christian Dior dresses. [V with quote] ⇒ She laughs, blushes and twitters: 'Oh, doesn't Giles have just the most charming sense of humour?' [Also V]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
