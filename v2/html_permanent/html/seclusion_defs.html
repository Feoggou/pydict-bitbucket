seclusion

(sɪˈkluːʒən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of secluding or the state of being secluded2. a secluded place

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin sēclūsiō; see seclude

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf you are living in seclusion, you are in a quiet place away from other people. ⇒ She lived in seclusion with her husband on their farm in Panama. [+ of] ⇒ They love the seclusion of their garden.
