strike up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (of a band, orchestra, etc) to begin to play or sing2. (transitive) to bring about; cause to begin ⇒ to strike up a friendship3. (transitive) to emboss (patterns, etc) on (metal)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbWhen you strike up a conversation or friendship with someone, you begin one. [written] [V P n] ⇒ I trailed her into Penney's and struck up a conversation. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ James struck up a friendship with a small boy who owned a pony on the island.2. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbWhen musicians strike up a piece of music, or when music strikes up, the music begins. [V P n] ⇒ And then the orchestra struck up the National Anthem. [V P] ⇒ Music struck up in one of the big old buildings along the seafront. [V P] ⇒ The band struck up, and riders paraded round the ring. →  strike
