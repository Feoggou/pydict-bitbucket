cantus

(ˈkæntəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tus1. a medieval form of church singing; chant2. Also called: canto the highest part in a piece of choral music3. (in 15th- or 16th-century music) a piece of choral music, usually secular, in polyphonic style
Word origin
Latin: song, from canere to sing
