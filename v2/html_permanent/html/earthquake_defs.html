earthquake

(ˈɜːθˌkweɪk )
Definitions
nouna sudden release of energy in the earth's crust or upper mantle, usually caused by movement along a fault plane or by volcanic activity and resulting in the generation of seismic waves which can be destructive ▶ Related adjective: seismic

noun(Insurance: Residence insurance) An earthquake is a sudden shaking of the earth's surface that often causes a lot of damage. ⇒ The policy includes coverage for damage by shock and by fire occasioned by or happening through an earthquake. ⇒ Most of the devastation caused by earthquakes is a direct result of the ground shaking violently. ⇒ An earthquake is a sudden shaking of the earth's surface that often causes a lot of damage. ▶  earthquake coverage
