inward

(ˈɪnwəd )
Definitions
adjective1. going or directed towards the middle of or into something2. situated within; inside3. of, relating to, or existing in the mind or spirit ⇒ inward meditation4. of one's own country or a specific country ⇒ inward investmentadverb5. a variant of inwards (sense 1)noun6. the inward part; inside

inward

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnwəʳd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Your inward thoughts or feelings are the ones that you do not express or show to other people. ⇒ I sighed with inward relief. ⇒ ...a glow of inward satisfaction.inwardly adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Chantal smiled inwardly. ⇒ Sara, while remaining outwardly amiable toward all concerned, was inwardly furious.2. adjective [ADJ n]An inward movement is one towards the inside or centre of something. ⇒ ...a sharp, inward breath like a gasp. ⇒ The athlete takes off from one leg from an inward twist.3. → See also inwards
