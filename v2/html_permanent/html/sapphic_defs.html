Sapphic

(ˈsæfɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. prosody denoting a metre associated with Sappho, consisting generally of a trochaic pentameter line with a dactyl in the third foot2. of or relating to Sappho or her poetry3. lesbiannoun4. prosody a verse, line, or stanza written in the Sapphic form
Example sentences containing 'Sapphic'
That masculine slouch, however, endeared her to the large clandestine horde of Sapphic ladies who followed her every move. Elliott, Sumner Locke FAIRYLAND
