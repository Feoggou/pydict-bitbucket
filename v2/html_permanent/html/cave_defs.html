cave1

(keɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an underground hollow with access from the ground surface or from the sea, often found in limestone areas and on rocky coastlines2. British history a secession or a group seceding from a political party on some issue. See Adullamite3. (modifier) living in cavesverb4. (transitive) to hollow out
Word origin
C13: from Old French, from Latin cava, plural of cavum cavity, from cavus hollow
