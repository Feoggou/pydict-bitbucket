strength

(strɛŋθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state or quality of being physically or mentally strong2. the ability to withstand or exert great force, stress, or pressure3. something that is regarded as being beneficial or a source of power ⇒ their chief strength is technology4. potency, as of a drink, drug, etc5. power to convince; cogency ⇒ the strength of an argument6. degree of intensity or concentration of colour, light, sound, flavour, etc7. the full or part of the full complement as specified ⇒ at full strength, ⇒ below strength8. finance firmness of or a rising tendency in prices, esp security prices9. archaic or poetic a stronghold or fortress10. Australian and New Zealand informal the general idea, the main purpose ⇒ to get the strength of something11. See from strength to strength12. See in strength13. See on the strength of
Word origin
Old English strengthu; related to Old High German strengida; see strong

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounYour strength is the physical energy that you have, which gives you the ability to perform various actions, such as lifting or moving things. [+ of] ⇒ She has always been encouraged to swim to build up the strength of her muscles. ⇒ He threw it forward with all his strength. ⇒ You don't need strength to take part in this sport. ⇒ He leant against the wall, fighting for strength to continue.2. uncount nounSomeone's strength in a difficult situation is their confidence or courage. ⇒ Something gave me the strength to overcome the difficulty. ⇒ His strength is an inspiration to me in my life. ⇒ I think she showed great strength of character in turning down what must have been a very lucrative offer. [+ of] ⇒ You need strength of mind to stand up for yourself.3. uncount nounThe strength of an object or material is its ability to be treated roughly, or to carry heavy weights, without being damaged or destroyed. [+ of] ⇒ He checked the strength of the cables. ⇒ ...the properties of a material, such as strength or electrical conductivity.4. uncount nounThe strength of a person, organization, or country is the power or influence that they have. ⇒ America values its economic leadership, and the political and military strength that goes with it. ⇒ The Alliance in its first show of strength drew a hundred thousand-strong crowd to a rally. ⇒ They have their own independence movement which is gathering strength.5. uncount nounIf you refer to the strength of a feeling, opinion, or belief, you are talking about how deeply it is felt or believed by people, or how much they are influenced by it. [+ of] ⇒ He was surprised at the strength of his own feeling. ⇒ ...the Civil War and the strength of feeling it had engendered among the Spanish people. [+ of] ⇒ What makes a mayor successful in Los Angeles is the strength of his public support.6. variable nounSomeone's strengths are the qualities and abilities that they have which are an advantage to them, or which make them successful. ⇒ Take into account your own strengths and weaknesses. ⇒ Vision and ambition are his great strengths. ⇒ Tact was never Mr Moore's strength. [+ of] ⇒ Organisation is the strength of any good army. ⇒ The book's strength lay in its depiction of present-day Tokyo.7. uncount nounIf you refer to the strength of a currency, economy, or industry, you mean that its value or success is steady or increasing. [+ of] ⇒ ...the long-term competitive strength of the American economy. ⇒ The drop was caused partly by the pound's strength against the dollar.8. uncount nounThe strength of a group of people is the total number of people in it. [+ of] ⇒ ...elite forces, comprising about one-tenth of the strength of the army.9. uncount nounThe strength of a wind, current, or other force is its power or speed. ⇒ Its oscillation depends on the strength of the gravitational field. ⇒ A tropical storm is gaining strength in the eastern Atlantic.10. uncount nounThe strength of a drink, chemical, or drug is the amount of the particular substance in it that gives it its particular effect. ⇒ It is very alcoholic, sometimes near the strength of port. [+ of] ⇒ Each capsule contains between 30 and 100 pellets of morphine sulphate according to the strength of dose required. ⇒ What's the scale that's used to compare the strength of acids and alkalis?11. uncount nounYou can talk about the strength of a flavour, smell, colour, sound, or light to describe how intense or easily noticed it is. [+ of] ⇒ The wine has lots of strength of flavour.12. →  go from strength to strength13. →  full strength14. →  in strength15. →  on the strength of16. →  under/below strength
