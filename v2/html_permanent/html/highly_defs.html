highly

(ˈhaɪlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. (intensifier) ⇒ highly pleased, ⇒ highly disappointed2. with great approbation or favour ⇒ we spoke highly of it3. in a high position ⇒ placed highly in class4. at or for a high price or cost

highly

Powered by COBUILD
(haɪli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV adj]Highly is used before some adjectives to mean 'very'. ⇒ Mr Singh was a highly successful salesman. ⇒ It seems highly unlikely that she ever existed. ⇒ ...the highly controversial nuclear energy programme.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed]You use highly to indicate that someone has an important position in an organization or set of people. ⇒ ...a highly placed government advisor. ⇒ ...highly ranked soccer teams.3. graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed]If someone is highly paid, they receive a large salary. ⇒ He was the most highly paid member of staff.4. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV -ed]If you think highly of something or someone, you think they are very good indeed. ⇒ Daphne and Michael thought highly of the school. ⇒ ...one of the most highly regarded chefs in the French capital.
