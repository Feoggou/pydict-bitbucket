luncheon

(ˈlʌntʃən )
Definitions
nouna lunch, esp a formal one
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: probably variant of nuncheon, from Middle English noneschench, from none noon + schench drink

luncheon

Powered by COBUILD
(lʌntʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: luncheons
Definitions
1. countable nounA luncheon is a formal lunch, for example to celebrate an important event or to raise money for charity. [+ for] ⇒ Earlier this month, a luncheon for former U.N. staff was held in Vienna.2. variable nounLuncheon is the meal that you eat in the middle of the day. It is usually called lunch. [mainly British, formal, or old-fashioned] ⇒ Promptly at one, luncheon was served. ⇒ I have a luncheon engagement.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
