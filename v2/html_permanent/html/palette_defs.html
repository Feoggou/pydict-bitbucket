palette

(ˈpælɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also: pallet a flat piece of wood, plastic, etc, used by artists as a surface on which to mix their paints2. the range of colours characteristic of a particular artist, painting, or school of painting ⇒ a restricted palette3. the available range of colours or patterns that can be displayed by a computer on a visual display unit4. either of the plates of metal attached by a strap to the cuirass in a suit of armour to protect the armpitsAvoid confusion with palate, pallet1, pallet2

Word Origin

C17: from French, diminutive of pale shovel, from Latin pala spade

1. countable nounA palette is a flat piece of wood or plastic on which an artist mixes paints.2. countable noun [usu sing]You can refer to the range of colours that are used by a particular artist or group of artists as their palette. ⇒ David Fincher paints from a palette consisting almost exclusively of grey and mud brown.
