symbolic

(sɪmˈbɒlɪk ) or symbolical

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to a symbol or symbols2. serving as a symbol3. characterized by the use of symbols or symbolism

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf you describe an event, action, or procedure as symbolic, you mean that it represents an important change, although it has little practical effect. ⇒ A lot of Latin-American officials are stressing the symbolic importance of the trip. ⇒ The move today was largely symbolic.symbolically (sɪmbɒlɪkli ) adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ It was a simple enough gesture, but symbolically important.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is symbolic of a person or thing is regarded or used as a symbol of them. [+ of] ⇒ Yellow clothes are worn as symbolic of spring. ⇒ The change from long to short hair is symbolic of the woman's need for change in her whole life.symbolically adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Each circle symbolically represents the whole of humanity.3. adjective [ADJ n]Symbolic is used to describe things involving or relating to symbols. ⇒ ...symbolic representations of landscape.
