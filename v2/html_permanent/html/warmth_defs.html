warmth

(wɔːmθ )
Definitions
noun1. the state, quality, or sensation of being warm2. intensity of emotion ⇒ he denied the accusation with some warmth3. affection or cordiality
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

warmth for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɔːʳmθ )
Definitions
1. uncount nounThe warmth of something is the heat that it has or produces. [+ of] ⇒ She went further into the room, drawn by the warmth of the fire. ⇒ June had brought with it the first of the summer warmth.2. uncount nounThe warmth of something such as a garment or blanket is the protection that it gives you against the cold. ⇒ The blanket will provide additional warmth and comfort in bed.3. uncount nounSomeone who has warmth is friendly and enthusiastic in their behaviour towards other people. ⇒ He greeted us both with warmth and affection.
