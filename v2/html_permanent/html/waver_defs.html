waver

(ˈweɪvə )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to be irresolute; hesitate between two possibilities2. to become unsteady3. to fluctuate or vary4. to move back and forth or one way and another5. (of light) to flicker or flashnoun6. the act or an instance of wavering
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

waverer (ˈwaverer) nounwavering (ˈwavering) adjectivewaveringly (ˈwaveringly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse vafra to flicker; related to German wabern to move about

waver for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weɪvəʳ )
Word forms: wavers , wavering , wavered
Definitions
1. verbIf you waver, you cannot decide about something or you consider changing your mind about something. [V] ⇒ Some military commanders wavered over whether to support the coup. [V] ⇒ Coleman has never wavered in his claim that he is innocent. [V] ⇒ Today on the streets of Montreal, opinion seems still to waver. [V-ing] ⇒ He told wavering colleagues the country must back the Government's stance.2. verbIf something wavers, it shakes with very slight movements or changes. [V] ⇒ The shadows of the dancers wavered continually. [V] ⇒ This time his voice wavered badly. [V-ing] ⇒ She draws another wavering breath.
