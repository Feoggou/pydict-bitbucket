chime in

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, adverb) informal1. to join in or interrupt a conversation, esp repeatedly and unwelcomely2. to voice agreement

chime in

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verbIf you chime in, you say something just after someone else has spoken. [V P with quote] ⇒ 'Why?' Pete asked impatiently.—'Yes, why?' Bob chimed in. 'It seems like a good idea to me.' [V P + with] ⇒ At this, some of the others chime in with memories of prewar deprivations. [Also V P] →  chime
