cage

(keɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. an enclosure, usually made with bars or wire, for keeping birds, monkeys, mice, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ cage bird2. a thing or place that confines or imprisons3. something resembling a cage in function or structure ⇒ the rib cage4. the enclosed platform of a lift, esp as used in a mine5. engineering a skeleton ring device that ensures that the correct amount of space is maintained between the individual rollers or balls in a rolling bearing6. informal the basket used in basketball7. informal the goal in ice hockey8. US a steel framework on which guns are supported9. See rattle someone's cageverb10. (transitive) to confine in or as in a cage
Word origin
C13: from Old French, from Latin cavea enclosure, from cavus hollow
