recoup

(rɪˈkuːp )
Definitions
verb1. to regain or make good (a financial or other loss)2. (transitive) to reimburse or compensate (someone), as for a loss3. law to keep back (something due), having rightful claim to do so; withhold; deduct
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recoupable (reˈcoupable) adjectiverecoupment (reˈcoupment) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French recouper to cut back, from re- + couper to cut, from coper to behead; see coup1
