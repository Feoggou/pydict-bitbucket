facetious

(fəˈsiːʃəs )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by levity of attitude and love of joking ⇒ a facetious person2. jocular or amusing, esp at inappropriate times ⇒ facetious remarks
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

facetiously (faˈcetiously) adverbfacetiousness (faˈcetiousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Old French facetieux, from facetie witty saying; see facetiae

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is being facetious, you are criticizing them because they are making humorous remarks or saying things that they do not mean in a situation where they ought to be serious. [disapproval] ⇒ The woman eyed him coldly. 'Don't be facetious,' she said.facetiously graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Al facetiously described himself as the Last Angry Man.
