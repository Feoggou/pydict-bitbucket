presumptuous

(prɪˈzʌmptjʊəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. characterized by presumption or tending to presume; bold; forward2. an obsolete word for presumptive

Derived Forms

presumptuously (preˈsumptuously) adverbpresumptuousness (preˈsumptuousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you describe someone or their behaviour as presumptuous, you disapprove of them because they are doing something that they have no right or authority to do. [disapproval] ⇒ It would be presumptuous to judge what the outcome will be.
Example sentences containing 'presumptuous'
Part of me was demanding how dare he be so presumptuous , while the rest of me was carolling a joyful, fiercely aroused song of victory. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFThe officer glanced drily at this presumptuous hand and then looked away, as if he were being offered something he did not wish to eat. Harvey, John COUP D'ETATBlair said carefully, `Perhaps we're all being a bit presumptuous in this.
