guest

(ɡɛst )
Definitions
noun1. a person who is entertained, taken out to eat, etc, and paid for by another2. a. a person who receives hospitality at the home of another ⇒ a weekend guestb. (as modifier) ⇒ the guest room3. a. a person who receives the hospitality of a government, establishment, or organizationb. (as modifier) ⇒ a guest speaker4. a. an actor, contestant, entertainer, etc, taking part as a visitor in a programme in which there are also regular participantsb. (as modifier) ⇒ a guest appearance5. a patron of a hotel, boarding house, restaurant, etc6. zoology a nontechnical name for inquiline7. See be my guestverb8. (intransitive) (in theatre and broadcasting) to be a guest ⇒ to guest on a show
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English giest guest, stranger, enemy; related to Old Norse gestr, Gothic gasts, Old High German gast, Old Slavonic gostǐ, Latin hostis enemy

guest

Powered by COBUILD
(gest ) for learners of English
Word forms: guests , guesting, guested
Definitions
1. countable nounA guest is someone who is visiting you or is at an event because you have invited them. ⇒ She was a guest at the wedding. ⇒ Their guests sipped drinks on the veranda.2. countable nounA guest is someone who visits a place or organization or appears on a radio or television show because they have been invited to do so. ⇒ ...a frequent chat show guest. ⇒ Dr Gerald Jeffers is the guest speaker. ⇒ They met when she made a guest appearance in the hit TV show Minder.3. countable nounA guest is someone who is staying in a hotel. ⇒ I was the only hotel guest. ⇒ Hotels operate a collection service for their guests from the airports. → client4. verbTo guest means to take part in a performance or a match as a guest, rather than as a regular member. [mainly British] [V prep] ⇒ He guested for one or two League sides. [V prep] ⇒ The band have recently guested on records by Ringo Starr and Tears For Fears.5. →  be my guest
