Anglican

(ˈæŋɡlɪkən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. denoting or relating to the Anglican communionnoun2. a member of the Church of England or one of the Churches in full communion with it
Word origin
C17: from Medieval Latin Anglicānus, from Anglicus English, from Latin Anglī the Angles

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Anglican means belonging or relating to the Church of England, or to the churches related to it. ⇒ ...the Anglican Church. ⇒ ...an Anglican priest. ⇒ ...the Anglican community.2. countable nounAn Anglican is a Christian who is a member of the Church of England, or of one of the churches related to it. ⇒ Both my parents were devout Anglicans.
