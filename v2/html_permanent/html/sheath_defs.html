sheath

(ʃiːθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural sheaths (ʃiːðz )1. a case or covering for the blade of a knife, sword, etc2. any similar close-fitting case3. biology an enclosing or protective structure, such as a leaf base encasing the stem of a plant4. the protective covering on an electric cable5. a figure-hugging dress with a narrow tapering skirt6. another name for condomverb7. (transitive) another word for sheathe

Word Origin

Old English scēath; related to Old Norse skeithir, Old High German sceida a dividing; compare Old English scādan to divide
