prediction

(prɪˈdɪkʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of predicting2. something predicted; a forecast, prophecy, etc

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounIf you make a prediction about something, you say what you think will happen. ⇒ He was unwilling to make a prediction about which books would sell in the coming year. ⇒ Predictions that the recession will be short are small comfort to those already affected. ⇒ Weather prediction has never been a perfect science.
Example sentences containing 'prediction'
Valentino's vengeful prediction proved entirely accurate: in the next few weeks the haunting of Coldheart Canyon began. Clive Barker COLDHEART CANYONThere was a late-night news bulletin and a brief prediction of what tomorrow's weather was going to be like, then nothing. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFBut he noted with satisfaction that his prediction was correct. Kippax, Frank THE SCARAnd then they pull aside the curtain, and I forget my prediction.
