abjure

(əbˈdʒʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to renounce or retract, esp formally, solemnly, or under oath2. to abstain from or reject
Derived forms
ˌabjuration (ˌabjuˈration) nounabjurer (abˈjurer) noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French abjurer or Latin abjurāre to deny on oath

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you abjure something such as a belief or way of life, you state publicly that you will give it up or that you reject it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He abjured the Protestant faith and became King in 1594. [V n] ⇒ ...a formal statement abjuring military action.
