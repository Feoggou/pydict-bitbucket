jurisdiction

(ˌdʒʊərɪsˈdɪkʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the right or power to administer justice and to apply laws2. the exercise or extent of such right or power3. power or authority in general
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jurisdictional (ˌjurisˈdictional) adjectivejurisdictionally (ˌjurisˈdictionally) adverbjurisdictive (ˌjurisˈdictive) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Latin jūrisdictiō administration of justice; see jus, diction

jurisdiction for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒʊərɪsdɪkʃən )
Word forms: jurisdictions
Definitions
1. uncount nounJurisdiction is the power that a court of law or an official has to carry out legal judgments or to enforce laws. [formal] [+ over] ⇒ The British police have no jurisdiction over foreign bank accounts.2. countable nounA jurisdiction is a state or other area in which a particular court and system of laws has authority. [law]
