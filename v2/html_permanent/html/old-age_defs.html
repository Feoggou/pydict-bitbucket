old age

(əʊld eɪdʒ)
Definitions
noun1. the period when someone is old and perhaps near the end of their life ⇒ We are not saving enough for our old age., ⇒ If you keep fit, you can enjoy life well into old age., ⇒ They worry about how they will support themselves in their old age., ⇒ In old age the two men wrote each other wistful letters.2. the quality or state of being old and near the end of life ⇒ We tend to consider old age as a social problem., ⇒ He is unable to travel much because of old age., ⇒ He died of old age., ⇒ She died of extreme old age., ⇒ at the ripe old age of 59, ⇒ Elephants can live to the ripe old age of 70.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. uncount noun [oft poss N]Your old age is the period of years towards the end of your life. ⇒ They worry about how they will support themselves in their old age. ⇒ In old age the two men wrote each other wistful letters.2. uncount nounOld age is the quality or state of being old and near the end of one's life. OPPOSITE: youth ⇒ In Britain today we tend to consider old age as a social problem. ⇒ He is unable to travel much because of old age.
