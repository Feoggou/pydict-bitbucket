earl

(ɜːl )
Definitions
noun1. (in the British Isles) a nobleman ranking below a marquess and above a viscount. Female equivalent: countess2. (in Anglo-Saxon England) a royal governor of any of the large divisions of the kingdom, such as Wessex
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English eorl; related to Old Norse jarl chieftain, Old Saxon erl man
