swig

(swɪɡ ) informal
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large swallow or deep drink, esp from a bottleverbWord forms: swigs, swigging or swigged2. to drink (some liquid) deeply, esp from a bottle

Derived Forms

swigger (ˈswigger) noun

Word Origin

C16: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you swig a drink, you drink it from a bottle or cup quickly and in large amounts. [V n with down/back] ⇒ I swigged down two white wines. [V n] ⇒ He was still hanging around, swigging the Coke out of the can.Swig is also a noun. ⇒ Brian took a swig of his beer. ⇒ McGuire took a long swig from his bottle of bitter lemon.
