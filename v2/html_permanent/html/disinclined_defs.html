disinclined

(ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪnd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveunwilling, reluctant, or averse ⇒ He felt disinclined for work., ⇒ He was disinclined to talk about himself, especially to his students., ⇒ They are disinclined to use violence because it is against their Buddhist faith.

disinclined

(dɪsɪnklaɪnd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [v-link ADJ, usu ADJ to-inf]If you are disinclined to do something, you do not want to do it. [formal] ⇒ He was disinclined to talk about himself, especially to his students. ⇒ They are disinclined to use violence because it is against their Buddhist faith.
