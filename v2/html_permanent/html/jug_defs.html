jug

(dʒʌɡ )
Definitions
noun1. a vessel for holding or pouring liquids, usually having a handle and a spout or lip. US equivalent: pitcher2. Australian and New Zealand such a vessel used as a kettle ⇒ an electric jug3. US a large vessel with a narrow mouth4. Also called: jugful the amount of liquid held by a jug5. British informal a glass of alcoholic drink, esp beer6. a slang word for jailverbWord forms: jugs, jugging or jugged7. to stew or boil (meat, esp hare) in an earthenware container8. (transitive) slang to put in jail
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: probably from Jug, nickname from girl's name Joan
