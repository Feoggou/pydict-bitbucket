(ˈɪfɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: iffier or iffiestinformal uncertain or subject to contingency ⇒ this scheme sounds a bit iffy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: from if + -y1

iffy

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪfi ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is iffy, you mean that it is not very good in some way. [informal] ⇒ If your next record's a bit iffy, you're forgotten. ⇒ He was from an iffy neighborhood.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is iffy, it is uncertain. [informal] ⇒ His political future has looked iffy for most of this year.
