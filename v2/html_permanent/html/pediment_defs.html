pediment

(ˈpɛdɪmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a low-pitched gable, esp one that is triangular, as used in classical architecture2. a gently sloping rock surface, formed through denudation under arid conditions

Derived Forms

ˌpedimental (ˌpediˈmental) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from obsolete periment, perhaps workman's corruption of pyramid
