midnight

(ˈmɪdˌnaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the middle of the night; 12 o'clock at nightb. (as modifier) ⇒ the midnight hour2. See burn the midnight oil

Derived Forms

midˌnightly (ˈmidˌnightly) adjective, adverb

midnight

Powered by COBUILD
(mɪdnaɪt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounMidnight is twelve o'clock in the middle of the night. ⇒ It was well after midnight by the time Anne returned to her apartment. ⇒ The entrance gates were locked at midnight.2. adjective [ADJ n]Midnight is used to describe something which happens or appears at midnight or in the middle of the night. ⇒ It is totally out of the question to postpone the midnight deadline. ⇒ Well we did have a midnight feast, me and my sister.3. →  to burn the midnight oil
