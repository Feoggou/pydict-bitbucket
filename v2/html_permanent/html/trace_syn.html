Synonyms of trace

trace

noun

  1. bit, drop, touch, shadow, suggestion, hint, dash, suspicion, tinge, trifle, whiff, jot, tincture, iota
    • Wash them in cold water to remove all traces of sand.
  2. remnant, remains, sign, record, mark, evidence, indication, token, relic, vestige, footprint, electronic footprint
    • The church has traces of fifteenth-century frescoes.
  3. track, trail, footstep, path, slot, footprint, spoor, footmark, electronic footprint
    • He disappeared mysteriously without a trace.

verb

  1. search for, follow, seek out, track, determine, pursue, unearth, ascertain, hunt down
    • I first went there to trace my roots.
  2. find, track (down), discover, trail, detect, unearth, hunt down, ferret out, locate
    • Police are anxious to trace a man seen leaving the house.
  3. outline, chart, sketch, draw, map out, depict, mark out, delineate
    • I traced the course of the river on the map.
  4. copy, map, draft, outline, sketch, reproduce, draw over
    • She learnt to draw by tracing pictures from story books.
