distort

(dɪˈstɔːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. (often passive) to twist or pull out of shape; make bent or misshapen; contort; deform2. to alter or misrepresent (facts, motives, etc)3. electronics to reproduce or amplify (a signal) inaccurately, changing the shape of the waveform
Derived forms
distorted (disˈtorted) adjectivedistortedly (disˈtortedly) adverbdistortedness (disˈtortedness) noundistorter (disˈtorter) noundistortive (disˈtortive) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin distortus misshapen, from distorquēre to turn different ways, from dis-1 + torquēre to twist

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you distort a statement, fact, or idea, you report or represent it in an untrue way. [V n] ⇒ The media distorts reality; categorises people as all good or all bad. [V n] ⇒ The minister has said his remarks at the weekend have been distorted.distorted graded adjective & adjective ⇒ These figures give a distorted view of the significance for the local economy.2. ergative verb & verbIf something you can see or hear is distorted or distorts, its appearance or sound is changed so that it seems unclear. [V n] ⇒ A painter may exaggerate or distort shapes and forms. [V n] ⇒ His size was persistently distorted by the cartoonists. [V] ⇒ This caused the sound to distort.distorted graded adjective & adjective ⇒ Sound was becoming more and more distorted through the use of hearing aids.
