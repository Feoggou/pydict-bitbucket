vindictive

(vɪnˈdɪktɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. disposed to seek vengeance2. characterized by spite or rancour3. English law (of damages) in excess of the compensation due to the plaintiff and imposed in punishment of the defendant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vindictively (vinˈdictively) adverbvindictiveness (vinˈdictiveness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin vindicta revenge, from vindicāre to vindicate

[disapproval] spiteful ⇒ How can you be so vindictive? ⇒ ...a vindictive woman desperate for revenge against the man who loved and left her.vindictiveness uncount noun ⇒ ...a dishonest person who is operating completely out of vindictiveness.
