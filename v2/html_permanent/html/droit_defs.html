droit

(drɔɪt , French drwa)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural droits (drɔɪts , French drwa)a legal or moral right or claim; due
Word origin
C15: from French: legal right, from Medieval Latin dīrēctum law, from Latin: a straight line; see direct
Example sentences containing 'droit'
Edward was supposed to exercise a kind of droit de seigneur over the junior members of the cast. Butler, Gwendoline COFFIN ON THE WATER
