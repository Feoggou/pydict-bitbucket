Marches

(ˈmɑːtʃɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun the Marches1. the border area between England and Wales or Scotland, both characterized by continual feuding (13th–16th centuries)2. a region of central Italy. Capital: Ancona. Pop: 1 484 601 (2003 est). Area: 9692 sq km (3780 sq miles). Italian name: Le Marche (le ˈmarke)3. any of various other border regions

Similar Words
