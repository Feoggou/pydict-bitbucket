mirror

(ˈmɪrə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a surface, such as polished metal or glass coated with a metal film, that reflects light without diffusion and produces an image of an object placed in front of it2. such a reflecting surface mounted in a frame3. any reflecting surface4. a thing that reflects or depicts something else ⇒ the press is a mirror of public opinionverb5. (transitive) to reflect, represent, or depict faithfully ⇒ he mirrors his teacher's ideals

Derived Forms

mirror-ˌlike (ˈmirror-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French from mirer to look at, from Latin mīrārī to wonder at

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA mirror is a flat piece of glass which reflects light, so that when you look at it you can see yourself reflected in it. ⇒ He absent-mindedly looked at himself in the mirror. ⇒ He checked his mirror and saw that a dark coloured van was immediately behind him.mirrored adjective ⇒ ...a mirrored ceiling.2. verbIf something mirrors something else, it has similar features to it, and therefore seems like a copy or representation of it. [V n] ⇒ The book inevitably mirrors my own interests and experiences. [V n] ⇒ His own shock was mirrored on her face.3. verbIf you see something reflected in water, you can say that the water mirrors it. [literary] [V n] ⇒ ...the sudden glitter where a newly-flooded field mirrors the sky. [V-ed] ⇒ The ship would lie there mirrored in a perfectly unmoving glossy sea.
