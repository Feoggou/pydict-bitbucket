integrate

Definitions
verb (ˈɪntɪˌɡreɪt )1. to make or be made into a whole; incorporate or be incorporated2. (transitive) to designate (a school, park, etc) for use by all races or groups; desegregate3. to amalgamate or mix (a racial or religious group) with an existing community4. mathematics to perform an integration on (a quantity, expression, etc)adjective (ˈɪntɪɡrɪt )5. made up of parts; integrated
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

integrable (ˈɪntəɡrəbəl ) adjectiveintegrability (ˌintegraˈbility) nounintegrative (ˈinteˌgrative) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin integrāre; see integer

(ɪntɪgreɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: integrates , integrating , integrated
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf someone integrates into a social group, or is integrated into it, they behave in such a way that they become part of the group or are accepted into it. [V + into/with] ⇒ He didn't integrate successfully into the Italian way of life. [V n + into/with] ⇒ Integrating the kids with the community, finding them a role, is essential. [V n] ⇒ The way Swedes integrate immigrants is, she feels, 100% more advanced. [V] ⇒ If they want to integrate, that's fine with me.integrated graded adjective & adjective ⇒ He thinks we are living in a fully integrated, supportive society.integration (ɪntɪgreɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the integration of people with disabilities into mainstream society.2. ergative verb & verbWhen races integrate or when schools and organizations are integrated, people who are black or belong to ethnic minorities can join white people in their schools and organizations. [US] [V] ⇒ Schools came to us because they wanted to integrate. [V n] ⇒ Encouraging teacher transfer would not, by itself, integrate the teaching corps.integrated adjective [ADJ n] ⇒ ...a black honor student in Chicago's integrated Lincoln Park High School.integration uncount noun ⇒ Lots of people in Chicago don't see that racial border. They see progress towards integration.3. ergative reciprocal verb & verbIf you integrate one thing with another, or one thing integrates with another, the two things become closely linked or form part of a whole idea or system. You can also say that two things integrate. [V n + with] ⇒ Integrating the pound with other European currencies could cause difficulties. [V + with] ⇒ Ann wanted the conservatory to integrate with the kitchen. [V n + into] ⇒ Little attempt was made to integrate the parts into a coherent whole. [V n] ⇒ Talks will now begin about integrating the activities of both companies. [Also V]integrated graded adjective & adjective ⇒ There is, he said, a lack of an integrated national transport policy. ⇒ ...a more integrated approach to land uses in a rural environment.integration uncount noun [oft adj N] ⇒ With Germany, France has been the prime mover behind closer European integration.
