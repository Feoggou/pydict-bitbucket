subjugate

(ˈsʌbdʒʊˌɡeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to bring into subjection2. to make subservient or submissive
Derived forms
subjugable (ˈsʌbdʒəɡəbəl ) adjectiveˌsubjugation (ˌsubjuˈgation) nounsubjuˌgator (ˈsubjuˌgator) noun
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin subjugāre to subdue, from Latin sub- + jugum yoke

(sʌbdʒʊgeɪt )
Word forms: subjugates , subjugating , subjugated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone subjugates a group of people, they take complete control of them, especially by defeating them in a war. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Their costly and futile attempt to subjugate the Afghans lasted just 10 years.subjugation (sʌbdʒʊgeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the brutal subjugation of native tribes.2. verb [usu passive]If your wishes or desires are subjugated to something, they are treated as less important than that thing. [formal] [be V-ed + to] ⇒ After having been subjugated to ambition, your maternal instincts are at last starting to assert themselves. [Also be V-ed]
