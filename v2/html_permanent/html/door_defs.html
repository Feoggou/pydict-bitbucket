door

(dɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a hinged or sliding panel for closing the entrance to a room, cupboard, etcb. (in combination) ⇒ doorbell, ⇒ doorknob2. a doorway or entrance to a room or building3. See early doors4. See lay at someone's door5. See out of doors6. See show someone the door▷ See also next-door
Word origin
Old English duru; related to Old Frisian dure, Old Norse dyrr, Old High German turi, Latin forēs, Greek thura

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA door is a piece of wood, glass, or metal, which is moved to open and close the entrance to a building, room, cupboard, or vehicle. ⇒ I knocked at the front door, but there was no answer. ⇒ The policeman opened the door and looked in. ⇒ I heard a door slamming.2. countable nounA door is the space in a wall when a door is open. ⇒ She looked through the door of the kitchen. Her daughter was at the stove.3. countable nounThe door is the entrance to a large building such as a shop, hotel, or theatre. entrance ⇒ He entered Harrods by the main door. ⇒ The queues at the door wound around the building.4. plural nounDoors is used in expressions such as a few doors down or three doors up to refer to a place that is a particular number of buildings away from where you are. [informal] ⇒ Mrs Cade's house was only a few doors down from her daughter's apartment.5. → see also next door6. →  answer the door7. →  by/through the back door8. →  to close the door on something9. →  behind closed doors10. →  from door to door/door to door11. →  from door to door/door to door12. →  foot in the door13. →  to shut the door in someone's face14. →  to lay something at someone's door15. →  open the door to something16. →  out of doors17. →  see someone to the door18. →  to show someone the door19. → at death's door
