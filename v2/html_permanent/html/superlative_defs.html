superlative

(suːˈpɜːlətɪv )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of outstanding quality, degree, etc; supreme2. grammar denoting the form of an adjective or adverb that expresses the highest or a very high degree of quality. In English the superlative degree is usually marked by the suffix -est or the word most, as in loudest or most loudly. Compare positive (sense 10), comparative (sense 3)3. (of language or style) excessive; exaggeratednoun4. a thing that excels all others or is of the highest quality5. grammar the superlative form of an adjective6. the highest degree; peak

Derived Forms

superlatively (suˈperlatively) adverbsuperlativeness (suˈperlativeness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French superlatif, via Late Latin from Latin superlātus extravagant, from superferre to carry beyond, from super- + ferre to bear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf you describe something as superlative, you mean that it is extremely good. ⇒ Some superlative wines are made in this region. ⇒ The Regent hotel has a superlative view of Hong Kong island.superlatively adverb ⇒ The Philharmonia played this staggeringly difficult music superlatively well.2. countable noun [usu pl]If someone uses superlatives to describe something, they use adjectives and expressions which indicate that it is extremely good. ⇒ ...a spectacle which has critics world-wide reaching for superlatives.3. adjective [ADJ n]In grammar, the superlative form of an adjective or adverb is the form that indicates that something has more of a quality than anything else in a group. For example, 'biggest' is the superlative form of 'big'. Compare comparative.Superlative is also a noun. ⇒ ...his tendency towards superlatives and exaggeration.
