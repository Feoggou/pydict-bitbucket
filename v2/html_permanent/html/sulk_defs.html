sulk

(sʌlk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to be silent and resentful because of a wrong done to one, esp in order to gain sympathy; brood sullenly ⇒ the child sulked in a corner after being slappednoun2. (often plural) a state or mood of feeling resentful or sullen ⇒ he's in a sulk because he lost the game, ⇒ he's got the sulks3. Also: sulker a person who sulks

Word Origin

C18: perhaps a back formation from sulky1

verbIf you sulk, you are silent and bad-tempered for a while because you are annoyed about something. [V] ⇒ He turned his back and sulked.Sulk is also a noun. ⇒ He went off in a sulk. ⇒ Now she must be tired of my sulks.
