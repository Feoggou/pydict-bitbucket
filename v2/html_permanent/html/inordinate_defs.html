inordinate

(ɪnˈɔːdɪnɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. exceeding normal limits; immoderate2. unrestrained, as in behaviour or emotion; intemperate3. irregular or disordered
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inordinacy (inˈordinacy) or inordinateness (inˈordinateness) nouninordinately (inˈordinately) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin inordinātus disordered, from in-1 + ordināre to put in order

Definitions
adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as inordinate, you are emphasizing that it is unusually or excessively great in amount or degree. [formal, emphasis] ⇒ They spend an inordinate amount of time talking. ⇒ ...their inordinate number of pets.inordinately adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ He is inordinately proud of his wife's achievements.
