rekindle

(riːˈkɪndəl )
Definitions
verb1. to arouse or cause to be aroused again ⇒ rekindle the romance in your relationship2. to set alight or start to burn again

rekindle

Powered by COBUILD
(riːkɪndəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: rekindles , rekindling , rekindled
Definitions
1. verbIf something rekindles an interest, feeling, or thought that you used to have, it makes you think about it or feel it again. [V n] ⇒ Ben Brantley's article on Sir Ian McKellen rekindled many memories. [V n] ⇒ Her interest was rekindled when she saw herbs in everyday medicinal use there.2. verbIf something rekindles an unpleasant situation, it makes the unpleasant situation happen again. [V n] ⇒ There are fears that the series could rekindle animosity between the two countries.
