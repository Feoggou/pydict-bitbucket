organize

or organise (ˈɔːɡəˌnaɪz )
Definitions
verb1. to form (parts or elements of something) into a structured whole; coordinate2. (transitive) to arrange methodically or in order3. (transitive) to provide with an organic structure4. (transitive) to enlist (the workers) of (a factory, concern, or industry) in a trade union5. (intransitive) to join or form an organization or trade union6. (transitive) informal to put (oneself) in an alert and responsible frame of mind
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin organizare, from Latin organum organ

[V n] ⇒ ...the way in which the Army is organised.4. verbIf you organize yourself, you plan your work and activities in an ordered, efficient way.
