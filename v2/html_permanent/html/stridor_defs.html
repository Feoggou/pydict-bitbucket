stridor

(ˈstraɪdɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. pathology a high-pitched whistling sound made during respiration, caused by obstruction of the air passages2. mainly literary a harsh or shrill sound
Word origin
C17: from Latin; see strident
Example sentences containing 'stridor'
Anaphylactic shock, Dr Albright had said, resulting in respiratory stridor. Lawson, Jonell ROSES ARE FOR THE RICH
