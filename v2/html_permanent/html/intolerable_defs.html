intolerable

(ɪnˈtɒlərəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. more than can be tolerated or endured; insufferable2. informal extremely irritating or annoying
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intolerability (inˌtoleraˈbility) or intolerableness (inˈtolerableness) nounintolerably (inˈtolerably) adverb

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as intolerable, you mean that it is so bad or extreme that no one can bear it or tolerate it. ⇒ They felt this would put intolerable pressure on them. ⇒ Human rights abuses by any party are intolerable.intolerably (ɪntɒlərəbli ) graded adverb & adverb ⇒ ...intolerably cramped conditions. ⇒ Her leg ached intolerably.
