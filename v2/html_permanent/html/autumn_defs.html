autumn

(ˈɔːtəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (sometimes capital)a. Also called (esp US): fall the season of the year between summer and winter, astronomically from the September equinox to the December solstice in the N hemisphere and from the March equinox to the June solstice in the S hemisphereb. (as modifier) ⇒ autumn leaves2. a period of late maturity, esp one followed by a decline
Word origin
C14: from Latin autumnus, perhaps of Etruscan origin

variable nounAutumn is the season between summer and winter when the weather becomes cooler and the leaves fall off the trees. [British] ⇒ We are always plagued by wasps in autumn. ⇒ A final vote will take place next autumn. ⇒ ...in the autumn of 2000. ⇒ Her hair was the colour of autumn leaves.regional note:   in AM, usually use fall
