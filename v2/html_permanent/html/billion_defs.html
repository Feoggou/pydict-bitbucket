billion

(ˈbɪljən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -lions or -lion1. one thousand million: it is written as 1 000 000 000 or 1092. (formerly, in Britain) one million million: it is written as 1 000 000 000 000 or 10123. (often plural) any exceptionally large numberdeterminer4. (preceded by a or a cardinal number)a. amounting to a billion ⇒ it seems like a billion years agob. (as pronoun) ⇒ we have a billion here
Derived forms
billionth (ˈbillionth) adjective, noun
Word origin
C17: from French, from bi-1 + -llion as in million

Word forms: billions
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The plural form is billion after a number, or after a word or expression referring to a number, such as 'several' or 'a few'.1. numberA billion is a thousand million. ⇒ ...3 billion dollars. ⇒ This year, almost a billion birds will be processed in the region.2. quantifierIf you talk about billions of people or things, you mean that there is a very large number of them but you do not know or do not want to say exactly how many. [+ of] ⇒ Biological systems have been doing this for billions of years. [+ of] ⇒ He urged U.S. executives to invest billions of dollars in his country.You can also use billions as a pronoun. ⇒ He thought that it must be worth billions.
