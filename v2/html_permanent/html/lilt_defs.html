lilt

(lɪlt )
Definitions
noun1. (in music) a jaunty rhythm2. a buoyant motionverb (intransitive)3. (of a melody) to have a lilt4. to move in a buoyant manner
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lilting (ˈlilting) adjective

Word Origin

C14 lulten, origin obscure

(lɪlt ) for learners of English
Definitions
singular nounIf someone's voice has a lilt in it, the pitch of their voice rises and falls in a pleasant way, as if they were singing. ⇒ Her voice is child-like, with a West Country lilt.
