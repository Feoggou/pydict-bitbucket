bull's-eye

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the small central disc of a target, usually the highest valued area2. a shot hitting this3. informal something that exactly achieves its aim4. a small circular or oval window or opening5. a thick disc of glass set into a ship's deck, etc, to admit light6. the glass boss at the centre of a sheet of blown glass7. a. a small thick plano-convex lens used as a condenserb. a lamp or lantern containing such a lens8. a peppermint-flavoured, usually striped, boiled sweet9. nautical a circular or oval wooden block with a groove around it for the strop of a shroud and a hole at its centre for a line. Compare deadeye10. meteorology the eye or centre of a cyclone

Word forms: bull's-eyes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe bull's-eye is the small circular area at the centre of a target. ⇒ Five of his bullets had hit the bull's-eye.2. countable nounIn shooting or the game of darts, a bull's-eye is a shot or throw of a dart that hits the bull's-eye.3. countable nounIf something that you do or say hits the bull's eye, it has exactly the effect that you intended it to have. [informal]
