sufficient reason

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun philosophy1. the principle that nothing happens by pure chance, but that an explanation must always be available2. the view that such an explanation is a reason for God to have chosen one alternative rather than another
Example sentences containing 'sufficient reason'
She slapped Mrs Flodge's face for good and sufficient reason. MacLeod, Charlotte SOMETHING IN THE WATERUnless you convince me that you have good and sufficient reason for such conduct, I shall have you thrown out.
