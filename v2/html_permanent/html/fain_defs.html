fain

(feɪn )
Definitions
adverb1. (usually with would) archaic willingly; gladly ⇒ she would fain be deadadjective2. obsoletea. willing or eagerb. compelled
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English fægen; related to Old Norse fegiun happy, Old High German gifehan to be glad, Gothic fahehs joy; see fawn2
Example sentences containing 'fain'
They would fain be so holy as to draw themselves up into the eternal Godhead and pass by the eternal holy humanity of our Lord Jesus Christ. Newbery, Linda RIDDLE ME THIS
