mountain

(ˈmaʊntɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a natural upward projection of the earth's surface, higher and steeper than a hill and often having a rocky summitb. (as modifier) ⇒ mountain people, ⇒ mountain sceneryc. (in combination) ⇒ a mountaintop2. a huge heap or mass ⇒ a mountain of papers3. anything of great quantity or size4. a surplus of a commodity, esp in the European Union ⇒ the butter mountain5. See a mountain to climb6. See make a mountain out of a molehill

Word Origin

C13: from Old French montaigne, from Vulgar Latin montānea (unattested) mountainous, from Latin montānus, from mons mountain

(maʊntɪn , US -tən )
Word forms: mountains
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA mountain is a very high area of land with steep sides. ⇒ Ben Nevis, in Scotland, is Britain's highest mountain. ⇒ ...a lovely little mountain village.2. quantifierIf you talk about a mountain of something, or mountains of something, you are emphasizing that there is a large amount of it. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ They are faced with a mountain of bureaucracy. ⇒ They have mountains of coffee to sell.3. →  a mountain to climb4. → to make a mountain out of a molehill
