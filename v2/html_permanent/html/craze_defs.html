craze

(kreɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a short-lived current fashion2. a wild or exaggerated enthusiasm ⇒ a craze for chestnuts3. mental disturbance; insanityverb4. to make or become mad5. ceramics, metallurgy to develop or cause to develop a fine network of cracks6. (transitive) British archaic or dialect to break7. (transitive) archaic to weaken
Word origin
C14 (in the sense: to break, shatter): probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish krasa to shatter, ultimately of imitative origin
