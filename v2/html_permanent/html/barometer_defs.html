barometer

(bəˈrɒmɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an instrument for measuring atmospheric pressure, usually to determine altitude or weather changes2. anything that shows change or impending change ⇒ the barometer of social change
Derived forms
barometric (ˌbærəˈmɛtrɪk ) or ˌbarometrical (ˌbaroˈmetrical) adjectiveˌbarometrically (ˌbaroˈmetrically) adverbbarometry (baˈrometry) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA barometer is an instrument that measures air pressure and shows when the weather is changing.2. countable nounIf something is a barometer of a particular situation, it indicates how things are changing or how things are likely to develop. [+ of] ⇒ In past presidential elections, Missouri has been a barometer of the rest of the country. ⇒ Our sleep pattern is a barometer to our psychological well-being.
