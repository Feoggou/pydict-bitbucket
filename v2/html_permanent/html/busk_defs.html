busk1

(bʌsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a strip of whalebone, wood, steel, etc, inserted into the front of a corset to stiffen it2. archaic or dialect the corset itself
Word origin
C16: from Old French busc, probably from Old Italian busco splinter, stick, of Germanic origin

busk2

(bʌsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intransitive) British to make money by singing, dancing, acting, etc, in public places, as in front of theatre queues
Derived forms
busker (ˈbusker) nounbusking (ˈbusking) noun
Word origin
C20: perhaps from Spanish buscar to look for

busk3

(bʌsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive) Scottish1. to make ready; prepare2. to dress or adorn
Word origin
C14: from Old Norse būask, from būa to make ready, dwell; see bower1
