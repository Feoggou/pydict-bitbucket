mordant

(ˈmɔːdənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. sarcastic or caustic2. having the properties of a mordant3. pungentnoun4. a substance used before the application of a dye, possessing the ability to fix colours in textiles, leather, etc. See also lake2 (sense 1)5. an acid or other corrosive fluid used to etch lines on a printing plateverb6. (transitive) to treat (a fabric, yarn, etc) with a mordant

Derived Forms

mordancy (ˈmordancy) nounmordantly (ˈmordantly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Old French: biting, from mordre to bite, from Latin mordēre
