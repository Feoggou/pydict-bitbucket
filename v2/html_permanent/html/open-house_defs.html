open house

Definitions
noun1. US and Canadian an occasion on which an institution, such as a school, is open for inspection by the public. Also called (in Britain and certain other countries): open day, at-home2. See keep open house3. US and New Zealand a house available for inspection by prospective buyers

open house for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. uncount nounIf you say that someone keeps open house, you mean that they welcome friends or visitors to their house whenever they arrive and allow them to stay for as long as they want to. ⇒ Father Illtyd kept open house and the boys would congregate in his study during their recreation time, playing cards or games.2. variable noun [oft N n]An open house is a day on which members of the public are encouraged to visit a particular institution or place to see what it is like. [US] ⇒ ...at a Saturday open house earlier this month. [+ at] ⇒ A week later, Sara and I attended open house at Ted's school. ⇒ The farm is open to the public only during two open-house weekends.regional note:   in BRIT, use open day
