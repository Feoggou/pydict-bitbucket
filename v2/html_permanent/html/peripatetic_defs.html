peripatetic

(ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk ) or peripatetical (ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪkəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. itinerant2. British employed in two or more educational establishments and travelling from one to another ⇒ a peripatetic football coachnoun3. a peripatetic person

Derived Forms

ˌperipatetically (ˌperipaˈtetically) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin peripatēticus, from Greek peripatētikos, from peripatein to pace to and fro

Peripatetic

(ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to the teachings of Aristotle, who used to teach philosophy while walking about the Lyceum in ancient Athensnoun2. a student of Aristotelianism

(perɪpətetɪk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If someone has a peripatetic life or career, they travel around a lot, living or working in places for short periods of time. [formal] ⇒ Her father was in the army and the family led a peripatetic existence. ⇒ ...a hectic, peripatetic life full of people and parties.
