forlorn

(fəˈlɔːn )
Definitions
adjective1. miserable, wretched, or cheerless; desolate2. deserted; forsaken3. (postpositive; foll by of) destitute; bereft ⇒ forlorn of hope4. desperate ⇒ the last forlorn attempt
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

forlornly (forˈlornly) adverbforlornness (forˈlornness) noun

Word Origin

Old English forloren lost, from forlēosan to lose; related to Old Saxon farliosan, Gothic fraliusan, Greek luein to release

forlorn

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳlɔːʳn ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n, v-link ADJ, ADJ after v]If someone is forlorn, they feel alone and unhappy. [literary] ⇒ One of the demonstrators, a young woman, sat forlorn on the pavement. ⇒ He looked a forlorn figure as he limped off after 26 minutes.forlornly graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ A Dutch newspaper photographed the president waiting forlornly in the rain.2. graded adjectiveIf a place is forlorn, it is deserted and not cared for, or has little in it. [literary] desolate ⇒ They headed inland on a forlorn road that was rutted and pocked. ⇒ The once glorious palaces stood empty and forlorn.forlornly graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ It is stranded somewhat forlornly in the middle of the plain.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A forlorn hope or attempt is one that you think has no chance of success. ⇒ Peasants have left the land in the forlorn hope of finding a better life in cities. ⇒ ...a forlorn effort to keep from losing my mind.forlornly graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ His father forlornly hoped someone might have seen them.
