complacent

(kəmˈpleɪsənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. pleased or satisfied, esp extremely self-satisfied2. an obsolete word for complaisant
Derived forms
complacently (comˈplacently) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin complacēns very pleasing, from complacēre to be most agreeable to, from com- (intensive) + placēre to please

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveA complacent person is very pleased with themselves or feels that they do not need to do anything about a situation, even though the situation may be uncertain or dangerous. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ We cannot afford to be complacent about our health. ⇒ ...the Chancellor's complacent attitude towards the far-right's activities.complacently graded adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ He sat back, smiling complacently at his own cleverness.
