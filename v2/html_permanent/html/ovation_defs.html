ovation

(əʊˈveɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. an enthusiastic reception, esp one of prolonged applause ⇒ a standing ovation2. a victory procession less glorious than a triumph awarded to a Roman general
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ovational (oˈvational) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ovātiō rejoicing, from ovāre to exult
