diabolic

(ˌdaɪəˈbɒlɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of, relating to, or proceeding from the devil; satanic2. befitting a devil; extremely cruel or wicked; fiendish3. very difficult or unpleasant
Word origin
C14: from Late Latin diabolicus, from Greek diabolikos, from diabolos devil

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]Diabolic is used to describe things that people think are caused by or belong to the Devil. [formal] ⇒ ...the diabolic forces which lurk in all violence.2. adjectiveIf you describe something as diabolic, you are emphasizing that it is very bad, extreme, or unpleasant. [mainly US, emphasis] ⇒ Pitt's smile returned, and it was hideously diabolic.
