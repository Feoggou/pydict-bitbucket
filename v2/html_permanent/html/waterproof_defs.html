waterproof

(ˈwɔːtəˌpruːf )
Definitions
adjective1. not penetrable by water. Compare water-repellent, water-resistantnoun2. mainly British a waterproof garment, esp a raincoatverb (transitive)3. to make (a fabric, item of clothing, etc) waterproof
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

waterproof for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɔːtəʳpruːf )
Word forms: waterproofs , waterproofing , waterproofed
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething which is waterproof does not let water pass through it. ⇒ Take waterproof clothing–Orkney weather is unpredictable. ⇒ Designed to be completely waterproof, the lights are manufactured from heavy duty plastic.2. countable noun [usu pl]Waterproofs are items of clothing which do not let water in. [mainly British] ⇒ For staying dry you'll want nice lightweight waterproofs to wear over your leathers.3. verb [usu passive]If something is waterproofed, it is treated so that water cannot pass through it or damage it. [be V-ed] ⇒ The whole boat has been totally waterproofed. [V-ed] ⇒ Waterproofed fabric pants are more expensive than plastic pants.
