demonstration

(ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of demonstrating2. proof or evidence leading to proof3. an explanation, display, illustration, or experiment showing how something works4. a manifestation of grievances, support, or protest by public rallies, parades, etc5. a manifestation of emotion6. a show of military force or preparedness7. mathematics a logical presentation of the assumptions and equations used in solving a problem or proving a theorem
Derived forms
ˌdemonstrational (ˌdemonˈstrational) adjectiveˌdemonstrationist (ˌdemonˈstrationist) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA demonstration is a march or gathering which people take part in to show their opposition to something or their support for something. ⇒ Riot police broke up a demonstration by students. ⇒ ...mass demonstrations.2. countable nounA demonstration of something is a talk by someone who shows you how to do it or how it works. ⇒ ...a cookery demonstration. ⇒ ...demonstrations of new products.3. countable nounA demonstration of a fact or situation is a clear proof of it. [+ of] ⇒ This is a clear demonstration of how technology has changed. ⇒ This was a very practical demonstration of why the Army trained people to be disciplined.4. countable nounA demonstration of a quality or feeling is an expression of it. [+ of] ⇒ There's been no public demonstration of opposition to the President. ⇒ ...physical demonstrations of affection.
