proboscis

(prəʊˈbɒsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cises or -cides (-sɪˌdiːz)1. a long flexible prehensile trunk or snout, as of an elephant2. the elongated mouthparts of certain insects, adapted for piercing or sucking food3. any similar part or organ4. informal, facetious a person's nose, esp if large

Word Origin

C17: via Latin from Greek proboskis trunk of an elephant, from boskein to feed
Example sentences containing 'proboscis'
The Reverend Arthur Bowen's Roman proboscis was the one fascinating feature of a chinless face. Secombe, Fred GOODBYE CURATE
