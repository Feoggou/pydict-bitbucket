hem1

(hɛm )
Definitions
noun1. an edge to a piece of cloth, made by folding the raw edge under and stitching it down2. short for hemlineverbWord forms: hems, hemming or hemmed (transitive)3. to provide with a hem4. (usually foll by in, around, or about) to enclose or confine
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hemm; related to Old Frisian hemme enclosed land

hem2

(hɛm )
Definitions
noun, exclamation1. a representation of the sound of clearing the throat, used to gain attention, express hesitation, etcverbWord forms: hems, hemming or hemmed2. (intransitive) to utter this sound3. See hem and haw
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

hem

Powered by COBUILD
(hem ) for learners of English
Word forms: hems , hemming , hemmed
Definitions
1. countable nounA hem on something such as a piece of clothing is an edge that is folded over and stitched down to prevent threads coming loose. The hem of a skirt or dress is the bottom edge.2. verbIf you hem something, you form a hem along its edge. [V n] ⇒ Turn under and hem the outer edges. [V n] ⇒ Each dress is hemmed and scrupulously checked for imperfections.3. → hem and haw► →  hem in
