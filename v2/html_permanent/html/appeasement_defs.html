appeasement

(əˈpiːzmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the policy of acceding to the demands of a potentially hostile nation in the hope of maintaining peace2. the act of appeasing

uncount nounAppeasement means giving people what they want to prevent them from harming you or being angry with you. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ He denied there is a policy of appeasement. ⇒ They have already been accused of appeasement by more militant organisations.
