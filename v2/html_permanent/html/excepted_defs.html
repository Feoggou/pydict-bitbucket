excepted

(ɪkˈsɛptɪd)
Definitions
prepositionformal used after mentioning a person or thing to show that they are not included in the statement ⇒ Jeremy excepted, the men seemed friendly enough., ⇒ present company excepted

excepted

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪkseptɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
adverb [n ADV]You use excepted after you have mentioned a person or thing to show that you do not include them in the statement you are making. [formal] ⇒ Jeremy excepted, the men seemed personable. ⇒ You normally receive 4 treatments each day, Sundays excepted.
