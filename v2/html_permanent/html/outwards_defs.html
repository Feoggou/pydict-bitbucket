outwards for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtwəʳdz ) also outward
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v]If something moves or faces outwards, it moves or faces away from the place you are in or the place you are talking about. ⇒ The top door opened outwards. ⇒ The idea is to spread social democracy and economics outwards from Europe.2. adverb [ADV after v]If you say that a person or a group of people, such as a government, looks outwards, you mean that they turn their attention to another group that they are interested in or would like greater involvement with. ⇒ Other poor countries looked outward, strengthening their ties to the economic superpowers. ⇒ His belief is that the future of the Community lies in looking outwards.
