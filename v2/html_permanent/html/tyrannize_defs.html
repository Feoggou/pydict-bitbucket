tyrannize

or tyrannise (ˈtɪrəˌnaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(when intr, often foll by over) to rule or exercise power (over) in a cruel or oppressive manner

Derived Forms

tyranˌnizer (ˈtyranˌnizer) or tyranˌniser (ˈtyranˌniser) noun

tyrannize

(tɪrənaɪz )
Word forms: tyrannizes , tyrannizing , tyrannized
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use tyranniseverbIf you say that one person tyrannizes another, you mean that the first person uses their power over the second person in order to treat them very cruelly and unfairly. [V n] ⇒ ...fathers who tyrannize their families. [V + over] ⇒ Armed groups use their power to tyrannise over civilians. [Also V]
Example sentences containing 'tyrannize'
