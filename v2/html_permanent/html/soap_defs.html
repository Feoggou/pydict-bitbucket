soap

(səʊp )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a cleaning or emulsifying agent made by reacting animal or vegetable fats or oils with potassium or sodium hydroxide. Soaps often contain colouring matter and perfume and act by emulsifying grease and lowering the surface tension of water, so that it more readily penetrates open materials such as textiles. See also detergent ▶ Related adjective: saponaceous2. any metallic salt of a fatty acid, such as palmitic or stearic acid. See also metallic soap3. slang flattery or persuasive talk (esp in the phrase soft soap)4. informal short for soap opera5. US and Canadian slang money, esp for bribery6. See no soapverb7. (transitive) to apply soap to8. (transitive; often foll by up) slanga. to flatter or talk persuasively tob. US and Canadian to bribe

Word Origin
