jot

(dʒɒt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: jots, jotting or jotted1. (transitive; usually foll by down) to write a brief note ofnoun2. (used with a negative) a little bit (in phrases such as not to care (or give) a jot)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin jota, from Greek iōta, of Semitic origin; see iota

jot for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɒt )
Word forms: jots , jotting , jotted
Definitions
1. verbIf you jot something short such as an address somewhere, you write it down so that you will remember it. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ Could you just jot his name on there.Jot down means the same as jot. [V P n] ⇒ Keep a pad handy to jot down queries as they occur. [V n P] ⇒ Listen carefully to the instructions and jot them down.2. quantifierIf you say that there is not a jot or not one jot of something, you are emphasizing that there is not even a very small amount of it. [old-fashioned, emphasis] [+ of] ⇒ There is not a jot of evidence to say it does them any good. [+ of] ⇒ It makes not one jot of difference.3. →  a jot/one jot
