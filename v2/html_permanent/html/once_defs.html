once

(wʌns )
Definitions
adverb1. one time; on one occasion or in one case2. at some past time; formerly ⇒ I could speak French once3. by one step or degree (of relationship) ⇒ a cousin once removed4. (in conditional clauses, negatives, etc) ever; at all ⇒ if you once forget it5. multiplied by one6. See once and away7. See once and for all8. See once in a while9. See once or twice10. See once upon a timeconjunction11. (subordinating) as soon as; if ever or whenever ⇒ once you begin, you'll enjoy itnoun12. one occasion or case ⇒ you may do it, this once13. See all at once14. See at once15. See for once

Word Origin

C12 ones, ānes, adverbial genitive of on, ān one

once for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wʌns )
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV with v]If something happens once, it happens one time only. ⇒ I met Wilma once, briefly. ⇒ Since that evening I haven't once slept through the night. ⇒ Mary had only been to Manchester once before.Once is also a pronoun. ⇒ 'Have they been to visit you yet?'—'Just the once, yeah.'. ⇒ Listen to us, if only this once.2. adverbYou use once with 'a' and words like 'day', 'week', and 'month' to indicate that something happens regularly, one time in each day, week, or month. ⇒ Lung cells die and are replaced about once a week. ⇒ We arranged a special social event once a year to which we invited our major customers.3. adverb [ADV every n, ADV every num n]You use once with 'every' and words like 'day', 'week', and 'year' to indicate that something happens a specified number of times and on a regular basis. ⇒ The patient was seen for follow-up visits once every three months. ⇒ My daughter comes to visit me once every fortnight.4. adverb [ADV with v] [ADV with be]If something was once true, it was true at some time in the past, but is no longer true. ⇒ The culture minister once ran a theatre. ⇒ I lived there once myself, before I got married. ⇒ The house where she lives was once the village post office. ⇒ My memory isn't as good as it once was. ⇒ ...an undulating park, once lovely but now ruined by new buildings.5. adverb [ADV with v]If someone once did something, they did it at some time in the past. ⇒ I once went camping at Lake Darling with a friend. ⇒ We once walked across London at two in the morning. ⇒ Diana had taken that path once.6. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionIf something happens once another thing has happened, it happens immediately afterwards. ⇒ The decision had taken about 10 seconds once he'd read a market research study. ⇒ Once customers come to rely on these systems they almost never take their business elsewhere. ⇒ Once inside her apartment she felt an urge to brush her teeth.7. →  all at once8. →  at once9. →  at once/all at once10. →  for once11. →  once again12. →  once and for all13. →  once in a while14. →  once or twice15. →  once upon a time16. → once in a blue moon
