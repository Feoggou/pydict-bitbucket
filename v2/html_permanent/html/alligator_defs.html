alligator

(ˈælɪˌɡeɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large crocodilian, Alligator mississipiensis, of the southern US, having powerful jaws and sharp teeth and differing from the crocodiles in having a shorter and broader snout: family Alligatoridae (alligators and caymans)2. a similar but smaller species, A. sinensis, occurring in China near the Yangtse River3. any crocodilian belonging to the family Alligatoridae4. any of various tools or machines having adjustable toothed jaws, used for gripping, crushing, or compacting
Word origin
C17: from Spanish el lagarto the lizard, from Latin lacerta
