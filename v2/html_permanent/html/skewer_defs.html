skewer

(ˈskjʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a long pin for holding meat in position while being cooked, etc2. a similar pin having some other function3. chess a tactical manoeuvre in which an attacked man is made to move and expose another man to captureverb4. (transitive) to drive a skewer through or fasten with a skewer

Word Origin

C17: probably from dialect skiver

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA skewer is a long metal pin which is used to hold pieces of food together during cooking.2. verbIf you skewer something, you push a long, thin, pointed object through it. [V n prep] ⇒ He skewered his victim through the neck. [V-ed] ⇒ ...skewered beef with vegetables.
