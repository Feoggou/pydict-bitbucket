(bəʊd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to be an omen of (good or ill, esp of ill); portend; presage2. (transitive) archaic to predict; foretell
Derived forms
boding (ˈboding) noun, adjectivebodement (ˈbodement) noun
Word origin
Old English bodian; related to Old Norse botha to proclaim, Old Frisian bodia to invite

bode

Powered by COBUILD
(boʊd )
Word forms: bodes , boding , boded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something bodes ill, it makes you think that something bad will happen in the future. If something bodes well, it makes you think that something good will happen. [formal] augur [V adv + for] ⇒ She says the way the bill was passed bodes ill for democracy. [V adv] ⇒ Grace had dried her eyes. That boded well.
Example sentences containing 'bode'
While introducing them Laura thought humorously that something in the air did not bode well for the girl who had invited Kurt to lunch. Mosco, Maisie OUT OF THE ASHESThey call him Beria out in that glass monstrosity they call the Centre, and that doesn't bode well! Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENIf she does not insist upon it now, it does not bode well for me to have any real power when I come to the throne. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANA broken Charter Stone did not bode well, but there would be some magic there and the path into Death would be easier to tread. Garth Nix SABRIEL
