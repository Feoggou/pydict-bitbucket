foreclose

(fɔːˈkləʊz )
Definitions
verb1. law to deprive (a mortgagor, etc) of the right to redeem (a mortgage or pledge)2. (transitive) to shut out; bar3. (transitive) to prevent or hinder4. (transitive) to answer or settle (an obligation, promise, etc) in advance5. (transitive) to make an exclusive claim to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

foreclosable (foreˈclosable) adjectiveforeclosure (fɔːˈkləʊʒə ) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French forclore, from for- out + clore to close, from Latin claudere

Definitions
verbIf the person or organization that lent someone money forecloses, they take possession of a property that was bought with the borrowed money, for example because regular repayments have not been made. [business] [V + on] ⇒ The bank foreclosed on the mortgage for his previous home. [Also V]
