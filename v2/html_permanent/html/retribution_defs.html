retribution

(ˌrɛtrɪˈbjuːʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of punishing or taking vengeance for wrongdoing, sin, or injury2. punishment or vengeance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

retributive (rɪˈtrɪbjʊtɪv ) or rare retributory (reˈtributory) adjectiveretributively (reˈtributively) adverb

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Church Latin retribūtiō, from Latin retribuere to repay, from re- + tribuere to pay; see tribute
