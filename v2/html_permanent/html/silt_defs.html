silt

(sɪlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fine deposit of mud, clay, etc, esp one in a river or lakeverb2. (usually foll by up) to fill or become filled with silt; choke

Derived Forms

siltation (silˈtation) nounsilty (ˈsilty) adjective

Word Origin

C15: of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian, Danish sylt salt marsh; related to Old High German sulza salt marsh; see salt
