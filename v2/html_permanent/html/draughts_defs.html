draughts

(drɑːfts )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun(functioning as singular) a game for two players using a draughtboard and 12 draughtsmen each. The object is to jump over and capture the opponent's pieces. US and Canadian name: checkers
Word origin
C14: plural of draught (in obsolete sense: a chess move)

He'd like to show the boy how to play a game like draughts. Seymour, Gerald FIELD OF BLOODShe trembled a little and took in occasional long draughts of air, as though she were swimming.
