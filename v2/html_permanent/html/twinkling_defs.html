twinkling

(ˈtwɪŋklɪŋ ) or twink (twɪŋk )
Definitions
nouna very short time; instant; moment. Also called: twinkling of an eye
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'twinkling'
Dusk was falling and the lights were twinkling on in the scattered houses on the island. Lewis, Roy A TROUT IN THE MILKDarkness came down quickly, as they plodded slowly downhill and up again, until at last they saw lights twinkling some distance ahead. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSThe pool was illuminated: its bright turquoise water twinkling. Clive Barker COLDHEART CANYONA large wire-framed model of a reindeer covered in twinkling lights stood in the front yard. Neil Gaiman AMERICAN GODS
