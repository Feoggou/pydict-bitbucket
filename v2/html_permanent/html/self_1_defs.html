self

(sɛlf )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural selves (sɛlvz )1. the distinct individuality or identity of a person or thing2. a person's usual or typical bodily make-up or personal characteristics ⇒ she looked her old self again3. See good self4. one's own welfare or interests ⇒ he only thinks of self5. an individual's consciousness of his own identity or being6. See the self7. a bird, animal, etc, that is a single colour throughout, esp a self-coloured pigeonpronoun8. not standard myself, yourself, etc ⇒ seats for self and wifeadjective9. of the same colour or material ⇒ a dress with a self belt. See also self-coloured10. obsolete the same

Word Origin

Old English seolf; related to Old Norse sjālfr, Gothic silba, Old High German selb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu adj N]Your self is your basic personality or nature, especially considered in terms of what you are really like as a person. ⇒ You're looking more like your usual self. ⇒ She was back to her old self again.2. countable noun [usu adj N]A person's self is the essential part of their nature which makes them different from everyone and everything else. ⇒ I want to explore and get in touch with my inner self. ⇒ The face is the true self visible to others. ⇒ ...our subconscious selves.
