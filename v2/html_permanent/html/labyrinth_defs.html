labyrinth

(ˈlæbərɪnθ )
Definitions
noun1. a mazelike network of tunnels, chambers, or paths, either natural or man-made. Compare maze (sense 1)2. any complex or confusing system of streets, passages, etc3. a complex or intricate situation4. a. any system of interconnecting cavities, esp those comprising the internal earb. another name for internal ear5. electronics an enclosure behind a high-performance loudspeaker, consisting of a series of air chambers designed to absorb unwanted sound waves
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek laburinthos, of obscure origin

labyrinth

Powered by COBUILD
(læbɪrɪnθ ) for learners of English
Word forms: labyrinths
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you describe a place as a labyrinth, you mean that it is made up of a complicated series of paths or passages, through which it is difficult to find your way. [literary] [+ of] ⇒ ...the labyrinth of corridors.2. countable nounIf you describe a situation, process, or area of knowledge as a labyrinth, you mean that it is very complicated. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ ...a labyrinth of conflicting political and sociological interpretations.
