nuisance

(ˈnjuːsəns )
Definitions
noun1. a. a person or thing that causes annoyance or botherb. (as modifier) ⇒ nuisance calls2. law something unauthorized that is obnoxious or injurious to the community at large (public nuisance) or to an individual, esp in relation to his or her ownership or occupation of property (private nuisance)3. See nuisance value
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: via Old French from nuire to injure, from Latin nocēre

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]If you say that someone or something is a nuisance, you mean that they annoy you or cause you a lot of problems. ⇒ He could be a bit of a nuisance when he was drunk. ⇒ Sorry to be a nuisance.→  make a nuisance of oneself
