inexperience

(ˌɪnɪkˈspɪərɪəns )
Definitions
nounlack of experience or of the knowledge and understanding derived from experience

Derived Forms

inexperience

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnɪkspɪəriəns ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounIf you refer to someone's inexperience, you mean that they have little knowledge or experience of a particular situation or activity. [+ of] ⇒ Critics attacked the youth and inexperience of his staff.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'inexperience'
It was probably also a symptom of inexperience , a matter of trying a touch too hard. Richard Francis PROSPECT HILLThey didn't recognize him but, judging by his inexperience in the art of pursuit, they guessed that he was pretty new to the job. MacNeill, Alastair CODE BREAKERThe blame for those aberrations must lie with my then-friend Tinmeer, by whom, in my inexperience , I regrettably let myself be directed. David Cavanagh MUSIC FOR BOYSThe accounts of Fancher's murder seemed incomplete to Tess, but she chalked that up to the inexperience of the local reporter. Laura Lippman THE LAST PLACE
