beach

(biːtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an extensive area of sand or shingle sloping down to a sea or lake, esp the area between the high- and low-water marks on a seacoast ▶ Related adjective: littoralverb2. to run or haul (a boat) onto a beach
Word origin
C16: perhaps related to Old English bæce river, beck2

Word forms: beaches , beaching , beached
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA beach is an area of sand or stones beside the sea. ⇒ ...a beautiful sandy beach. ⇒ I just want to lie on the beach in the sun.2. ergative verb & verbIf something such as a boat beaches, or if it is beached, it is pulled or forced out of the water and onto land. [V n] ⇒ We beached the canoe, running it right up the bank. [V] ⇒ The boat beached on a mud flat. [V pron-refl] ⇒ Experts are unable to explain why the whales beached themselves.
