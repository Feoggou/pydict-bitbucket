glitter

(ˈɡlɪtə )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (of a hard, wet, or polished surface) to reflect light in bright flashes2. (of light) to be reflected in bright flashes3. (usually foll by with) to be decorated or enhanced by the glamour (of) ⇒ the show glitters with famous actorsnoun4. sparkle or brilliance5. show and glamour6. tiny pieces of shiny decorative material used for ornamentation, as on the skin7. Also called: silver thaw Canadian ice formed from freezing rain
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

glittery (ˈglittery) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse glitra; related to Old High German glīzan to shine

glitter

Powered by COBUILD
(glɪtəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: glitters , glittering , glittered
Definitions
1. verbIf something glitters, light comes from or is reflected off different parts of it. [V] ⇒ The bay glittered in the sunshine. [V prep] ⇒ The Palace glittered with lights.2. verbIf someone's eyes glitter, they are bright and express a particular emotion such as excitement or interest. [written] gleam [V] ⇒ His eyes glittered with a tense amusement.3. uncount nounGlitter consists of tiny shining pieces of metal. It is glued to things for decoration. ⇒ Decorate the tunic with sequins or glitter.4. uncount nounYou can use glitter to refer to superficial attractiveness or to the excitement connected with something. glamour ⇒ She was blinded by the glitter and the glamour of her own life.
