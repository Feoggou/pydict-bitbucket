inevitable

(ɪnˈɛvɪtəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. unavoidable2. sure to happen; certainnoun3. See the inevitable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inevitability (inˌevitaˈbility) or inevitableness (inˈevitableness) nouninevitably (inˈevitably) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin inēvītābilis, from in-1 + ēvītābilis, from ēvītāre to shun, from vītāre to avoid

Definitions
adjectiveIf something is inevitable, it is certain to happen and cannot be prevented or avoided. ⇒ If the case succeeds, it is inevitable that other trials will follow. ⇒ The defeat had inevitable consequences for British policy.The inevitable is something which is inevitable. ⇒ 'It's just delaying the inevitable,' he said.
