strike off

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to remove or erase from (a list, record, etc) by or as if by a stroke of the pen2. (adverb) to cut off or separate by or as if by a blow ⇒ she was struck off from the inheritance

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verb [usu passive]If someone such as a doctor or lawyer is struck off, their name is removed from the official register and they are not allowed to do medical or legal work any more. [British] [be V-ed P] ⇒ ...a company lawyer who had been struck off for dishonest practices. [be V-ed P] ⇒ He could be struck off the medical register. →  strike
