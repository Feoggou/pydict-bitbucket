bacillus

(bəˈsɪləs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cilli (-ˈsɪlaɪ)1. any rod-shaped bacterium, such as a clostridium bacterium. Compare coccus (sense 2), spirillum (sense 1)2. any of various rodlike spore-producing bacteria constituting the family Bacillaceae, esp of the genus Bacillus
Word origin
C19: from Latin: a small staff, from baculum walking stick
