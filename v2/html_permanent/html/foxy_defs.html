foxy

(ˈfɒksɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: foxier or foxiest1. of or resembling a fox, esp in craftiness2. smelling strongly like a fox3. of a reddish-brown colour4. (of paper, wood, etc) spotted, esp by mildew5. (of wine) having the flavour of fox grapes6. (of oats) having a musty smell as a result of getting wet, fermenting, and drying out7. slang sexy; sexually attractive
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

foxily (ˈfoxily) adverbfoxiness (ˈfoxiness) noun

foxy

Powered by COBUILD
(fɒksi ) for learners of English
Word forms: foxier , foxiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as foxy, you mean that they are deceitful in a clever, secretive way. ⇒ He had wary, foxy eyes.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a man calls a woman foxy, he means that she is physically attractive. [mainly US, informal] ⇒ ...a foxy blonde in a turtleneck sweater.
