let on

Definitions
verb (adverb; when tr, takes a clause as object) informal1. to allow (something, such as a secret) to be known; reveal ⇒ he never let on that he was married2. (transitive) to cause or encourage to be believed; pretend

Definitions
phrasal verbIf you do not let on that something is true, you do not tell anyone that it is true, and you keep it a secret. [informal] [V P that/wh] ⇒ She never let on that anything was wrong. [V P + to] ⇒ I didn't let on to the staff what my conversation was. [V P] ⇒ He knows the culprit but is not letting on. →  let
