gravitation

(ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the force of attraction that bodies exert on one another as a result of their mass2. any process or result caused by this interaction, such as the fall of a body to the surface of the earth▷ See also Newton's law of gravitation
