conjure

(ˈkʌndʒə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to practise conjuring or be a conjuror2. (intransitive) to call upon supposed supernatural forces by spells and incantations3. (kənˈdʒʊə) (transitive) to appeal earnestly or strongly to ⇒ I conjure you to help me4. See a name to conjure with
Word origin
C13: from Old French conjurer to plot, from Latin conjūrāre to swear together, form a conspiracy, from jūrāre to swear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you conjure something out of nothing, you make it appear as if by magic. [V n + from/out of] ⇒ Thirteen years ago she found herself having to conjure a career from thin air. [V n] ⇒ They managed to conjure a victory.Conjure up means the same as conjure. [V P n] ⇒ Every day a different chef will be conjuring up delicious dishes in the restaurant. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ He conjured up a smile and reached out to squeeze her hand. [Also V n P]2. →  a name to conjure with► →  conjure up
