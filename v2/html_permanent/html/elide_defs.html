elide

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪlaɪd ) for learners of English
Word forms: elides , eliding , elided
Definitions
1. verbIf you elide something, especially a distinction, you leave it out or ignore it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ These habits of thinking elide the difference between what is common and what is normal.2. verbIn linguistics, if you elide a word, you do not pronounce or write it fully. [technical] [V n] ⇒ He complained about BBC announcers eliding their words.
