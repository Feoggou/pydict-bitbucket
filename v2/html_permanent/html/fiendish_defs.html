fiendish

(ˈfiːndɪʃ )
Definitions
adjective1. of or like a fiend2. diabolically wicked or cruel3. informal extremely difficult or unpleasant ⇒ a fiendish problem
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fiendishly (ˈfiendishly) adverbfiendishness (ˈfiendishness) noun

fiendish

Powered by COBUILD
(fiːndɪʃ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A fiendish plan, action, or device is very clever or imaginative. [informal] ⇒ ...a fiendish plot.fiendishly adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ This figure is reached by a fiendishly clever equation.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]A fiendish problem or task is very difficult and challenging. [informal] ⇒ A rather neat option allows you to design your own fiendish puzzle. ⇒ ...the fiendish difficulty of the questions.fiendishly adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ America's trade laws are fiendishly complex.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A fiendish person enjoys being cruel. ⇒ This was a fiendish act of wickedness.
