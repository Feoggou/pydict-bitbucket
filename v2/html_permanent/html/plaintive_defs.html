(ˈpleɪntɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveexpressing melancholy; mournful

Derived Forms

plaintively (ˈplaintively) adverbplaintiveness (ˈplaintiveness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French plaintif grieving, from plainte plaint

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveA plaintive sound or voice sounds sad. [literary] ⇒ They lay on the firm sands, listening to the plaintive cry of the seagulls. ⇒ Her voice was small and plaintive.plaintively graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'Why don't we do something?' Davis asked plaintively.
