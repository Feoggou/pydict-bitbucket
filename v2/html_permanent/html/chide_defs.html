chide

(tʃaɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: chides, chiding, chided, chid, chided, chid or chidden1. to rebuke or scold2. (transitive) to goad into action
Derived forms
chider (ˈchider) nounchidingly (ˈchidingly) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you chide someone, you speak to them angrily because they have done something wicked or foolish. [old-fashioned] [V n + for/about] ⇒ Cross chided himself for worrying. [V n] ⇒ He gently chided the two women.
Example sentences containing 'chide'
Anthony loved the excuse to chide , to mock, to exercise, in appearance, a little affectionate tutelage. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WAROnly the morning before he'd heard her chide Jo-Beth for being unpunctual; there was nothing informal about her working hours.
