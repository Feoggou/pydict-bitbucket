fitted

(ˈfɪtɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. designed for excellent fit ⇒ a fitted suit2. (of a carpet) cut, sewn, or otherwise adapted to cover a floor completely3. a. (of furniture) built to fit a particular space ⇒ a fitted cupboardb. (of a room) equipped with fitted furniture ⇒ a fitted kitchen4. (of sheets) having ends that are elasticated and shaped to fit tightly over a mattress5. having accessory parts

fitted

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪtɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A fitted piece of clothing is designed so that it is the same size and shape as your body rather than being loose. tailored ⇒ ...baggy trousers with fitted jackets.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A fitted piece of furniture, for example a cupboard, is designed to fill a particular space and is fixed in place. ⇒ I've re-carpeted our bedroom and added fitted wardrobes.3. adjective [ADJ n]A fitted carpet is cut to the same shape as a room so that it covers the floor completely. ⇒ ...fitted carpets, central heating and double glazing.4. adjective [ADJ n]A fitted sheet has the corners sewn so that they fit over the corners of the mattress and do not have to be folded.5. graded adjective [v-link ADJ to-inf]A person who is fitted to do something or fitted for something has the right qualities for it. suited ⇒ Helen was ill fitted to fulfil her daughter's ideal of a gentle mother-figure.6. adjective [v-link ADJ with n, ADJ n]If a room is fitted with objects, those objects are in the room and are normally fixed in place. ⇒ Bedrooms are fitted with alarm pull cords to alert the manager in an emergency.
