buoy

(bɔɪ , US English ˈbuːɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a distinctively shaped and coloured float, anchored to the bottom, for designating moorings, navigable channels, or obstructions in a body of water. See also life buoyverb2. (transitive; usually foll by up) to prevent from sinking ⇒ the belt buoyed him up3. (transitive; usually foll by up) to raise the spirits of; hearten4. (transitive) nautical to mark (a channel or obstruction) with a buoy or buoys5. (intransitive) to rise to the surface
Word origin
C13: probably of Germanic origin; compare Middle Dutch boeie, boeye; see beacon

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA buoy is a floating object that is used to show ships and boats where they can go and to warn them of danger.2. verbIf someone in a difficult situation is buoyed by something, it makes them feel more cheerful and optimistic. [be V-ed + by] ⇒ In May they danced in the streets, buoyed by their victory. [V n] ⇒ German domestic consumption buoyed the German economy.Buoy up means the same as buoy. [be V-ed P] ⇒ They are buoyed up by a sense of hope. [Also V n P]
