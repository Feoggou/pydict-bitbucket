arum

(ˈɛərəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any plant of the aroid genus Arum, of Europe and the Mediterranean region, having arrow-shaped leaves and a typically white spathe. See also cuckoopint2. See arum lily
Word origin
C16: from Latin, a variant of aros wake-robin, from Greek aron
Example sentences containing 'arum'
Now it was May and great white arum lilies stood around the altar, the painting of Christ risen. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LION
