mist

(mɪst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a thin fog resulting from condensation in the air near the earth's surface2. meteorology such an atmospheric condition with a horizontal visibility of 1–2 kilometres3. a fine spray of any liquid, such as that produced by an aerosol container4. chemistry a colloidal suspension of a liquid in a gas5. condensed water vapour on a surface that blurs the surface6. something that causes haziness or lack of clarity, such as a film of tearsverb7. to cover or be covered with or as if with mist

Word Origin

Old English; related to Middle Dutch, Swedish mist, Greek omikhlē fog

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounMist consists of a large number of tiny drops of water in the air, which make it difficult to see very far. ⇒ Thick mist made flying impossible. ⇒ A bluish mist hung in the air. ⇒ Mists and fog swirled about the road.2. ergative verb & verbIf a piece of glass mists or is misted, it becomes covered with tiny drops of moisture, so that you cannot see through it easily. [V] ⇒ The windows misted, blurring the stark streetlight. [V n] ⇒ The temperature in the car was misting the window.Mist over and mist up mean the same as mist. [V P] ⇒ The front windshield was misting over. [V-ed P] ⇒ She stood in front of the misted-up mirror.3. verbIf someone's eyes mist, they cannot see easily because there are tears in their eyes. [V] ⇒ Her eyes misted with tears.Mist over means the same as mist. [V P] ⇒ His eyes misted over and he started to shake.► →  mist over► →  mist up
