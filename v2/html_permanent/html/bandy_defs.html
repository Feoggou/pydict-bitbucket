bandy

(ˈbændɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -dier or -diest1. Also: bandy-legged having legs curved outwards at the knees2. (of legs) curved outwards at the knees3. See knock someone bandyverb -dies, -dying or -died (transitive)4. to exchange (words) in a heated or hostile manner5. to give and receive (blows)6. (often foll by about) to circulate (a name, rumour, etc)7. to throw or strike to and fro; toss aboutnoun plural -dies8. an early form of hockey, often played on ice9. a stick, curved at one end, used in the game of bandy10. an old form of tennis
Word origin
C16: probably from Old French bander to hit the ball back and forth at tennis
