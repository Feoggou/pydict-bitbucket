crabbed

(ˈkræbɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. surly; irritable; perverse2. (esp of handwriting) cramped and hard to decipher
Derived forms
crabbedly (ˈcrabbedly) adverbcrabbedness (ˈcrabbedness) noun
Word origin
C13: probably from crab1 (from its wayward gait), influenced by crab(apple) (from its tartness)
Example sentences containing 'crabbed'
Handsome Adam sat next to Old Adam and whispered in his old, crabbed ear. Reeves, Robert DOUBTING THOMASThen I had to bring a branch of candles near it before I could make out the crabbed and faded handwriting. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANShe lived on in the mountains in her crabbed grey cottage, clamped like a barnacle to the earth. Gee, Maggie LOST CHILDRENNot the prophecy as everyone believed it to be, but as it really was, written in Santa Leopolda's own crabbed , spidery handwriting. Christina Dodd THE RUNAWAY PRINCESS
