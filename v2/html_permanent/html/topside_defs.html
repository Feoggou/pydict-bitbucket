topside

(ˈtɒpˌsaɪd )
Definitions
noun1. the uppermost side of anything2. British and New Zealand a lean cut of beef from the thigh containing no bone3. (often plural)a. the part of a ship's sides above the waterlineb. the parts of a ship above decks

topside

Powered by COBUILD
(tɒpsaɪd ) for learners of English
Word forms: topsides
Definitions
1. uncount nounTopside is a joint of beef that is cut from the upper part of the cow's leg. It is usually cooked by roasting or stewing. [British]regional note:   in AM, use top round2. adverb [ADV after v]On a ship, if you go topside, you go up onto the top deck. [technical] ⇒ He left the control station and went topside.3. countable noun [usu pl]The topside or topsides of a ship or boat are the top deck or the parts which you can see above the water. [technical]
