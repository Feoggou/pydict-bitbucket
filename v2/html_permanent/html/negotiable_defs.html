negotiable

(nɪˈɡəʊʃəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. able to be negotiated2. (of a bill of exchange, promissory note, etc) legally transferable in title from one party to another
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

negotiable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪgoʊʃəbəl )
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething that is negotiable can be changed or agreed when people discuss it. ⇒ He warned that his economic programme for the country was not negotiable. ⇒ The Manor is for sale at a negotiable price.2. adjectiveContracts or assets that are negotiable can be transferred to another person in exchange for money. ⇒ The bonds may no longer be negotiable. ⇒ ...negotiable bearer bonds.3. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]An area of land that is negotiable is easy to travel across. ⇒ Parts of the road had been washed away by streams, but it was negotiable.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

negotiable in Accounting

Definitions
adjective(Accounting: Commerce) A negotiable asset is able to be transferred legally from one owner to another. ⇒ Negotiable paper is a document that can be traded for value by its holder independent of the parties that created it. ⇒ When a negotiable paper is traded, often people will obtain it improperly and sell it to innocent buyers. ⇒ A negotiable asset is able to be transferred legally from one owner to another.
