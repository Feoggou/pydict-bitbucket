successful

(səkˈsɛsfʊl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having succeeded in one's endeavours2. marked by a favourable outcome3. having obtained fame, wealth, etc

Derived Forms

successfully (sucˈcessfully) adverbsuccessfulness (sucˈcessfulness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is successful achieves what it was intended to achieve. Someone who is successful achieves what they intended to achieve. ⇒ How successful will this new treatment be? ⇒ I am looking forward to a long and successful partnership with him. [+ in] ⇒ She has been comparatively successful in maintaining her privacy.successfully adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The doctors have successfully concluded preliminary tests.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is successful is popular or makes a lot of money. ⇒ ...the hugely successful movie that brought Robert Redford an Oscar for his directing. ⇒ One of the keys to successful business is careful planning.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is successful achieves a high position in what they do, for example in business or politics. [+ in] ⇒ Women do not necessarily have to imitate men to be successful in business. ⇒ She is a successful lawyer.
