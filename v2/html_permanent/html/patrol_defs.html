patrol

(pəˈtrəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the action of going through or around a town, neighbourhood, etc, at regular intervals for purposes of security or observation2. a person or group that carries out such an action3. a military detachment with the mission of security, gathering information, or combat with enemy forces4. a division of a troop of Scouts or Guidesverb -trols, -trolling or -trolled5. to engage in a patrol of (a place)

Derived Forms

patroller (paˈtroller) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French patrouiller, from patouiller to flounder in mud, from patte paw

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen soldiers, police, or guards patrol an area or building, they move around it in order to make sure that there is no trouble there. [V n] ⇒ Prison officers continued to patrol the grounds within the jail.Patrol is also a noun. ⇒ He failed to return from a patrol.2. →  on patrol3. countable nounA patrol is a group of soldiers or vehicles that are patrolling an area. ⇒ Guerrillas attacked a patrol with hand grenades. ⇒ ...a border patrol operating near the Burmese frontier.
