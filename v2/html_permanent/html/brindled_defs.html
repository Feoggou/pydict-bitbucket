brindled

(ˈbrɪndəld )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivebrown or grey streaked or patched with a darker colour ⇒ a brindled dog
Word origin
C17: changed from C15 brended, literally: branded, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse bröndottr; see brand
Example sentences containing 'brindled'
Lirael always said hello to the visiting dogs, even when they were huge brindled wolfhounds with studded collars. Garth Nix LIRAEL: DAUGHTER OF THE CLAYRHe's about fifty and he wears huge spectacles with thick tortoiseshell frames to them, like a pair of brindled goggles. Malcolm, John WHISTLER IN THE DARKThe person indicated was the one Jarvis had thought of as ` brindled " the night before. Lyall, Francis A DEATH IN TIMEThe elegance of his appearance was only slightly marred by the great brindled feline perched on his knee. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY
