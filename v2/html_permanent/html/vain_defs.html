vain

(veɪn )
Definitions
adjective1. inordinately proud of one's appearance, possessions, or achievements2. given to ostentatious display, esp of one's beauty3. worthless4. senseless or futilenoun5. See in vain6. See take someone's name in vain
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vainly (ˈvainly) adverbvainness (ˈvainness) noun

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin vānus

vain

Powered by COBUILD
(veɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: vainer , vainest
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]A vain attempt or action is one that fails to achieve what was intended. ⇒ The drafting committee worked through the night in a vain attempt to finish on schedule. ⇒ I was singing in a vain effort to cheer him up.vainly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He hunted vainly through his pockets for a piece of paper.2. adjective [ADJ n]If you describe a hope that something will happen as a vain hope, you mean that there is no chance of it happening. ⇒ He married his fourth wife, Susan, in the vain hope that she would improve his health.vainly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He then set out for Virginia for what he vainly hoped would be a peaceful retirement.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as vain, you are critical of their extreme pride in their own beauty, intelligence, or other good qualities. [disapproval] conceited ⇒ I think he is shallow, vain and untrustworthy.4. →  in vain5. →  in vain
