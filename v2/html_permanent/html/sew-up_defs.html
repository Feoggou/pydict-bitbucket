sew up

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to fasten or mend completely by sewing2. US to acquire sole use or control of3. informal to complete or negotiate successfully ⇒ to sew up a deal

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you sew up pieces of cloth or tears in cloth or skin, you join them together using a needle and thread. [V P n] ⇒ Next day, Miss Stone decided to sew up the rip. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ This material was then put into cotton bags which were weighed and then sewn up. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verbIf someone sews up something such as a business deal, an election, or a game, they make sure that they will get the result they want. [informal] [V n P] ⇒ If they didn't move fast, Johnson could sew this deal up within days. [V-ed P] ⇒ The Italians think they've got it all sewn up. [Also V P n] →  sew
