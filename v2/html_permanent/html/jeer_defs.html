jeer

(dʒɪə )
Definitions
verb1. (often foll by at) to laugh or scoff (at a person or thing); mocknoun2. a remark or cry of derision; gibe; taunt
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jeerer (ˈjeerer) nounjeering (ˈjeering) adjective, nounjeeringly (ˈjeeringly) adverb

Word Origin

jeer for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɪəʳ )
Word forms: jeers , jeering , jeered
Definitions
1. verbTo jeer at someone means to say or shout rude and insulting things to them to show that you do not like or respect them. [V + at] ⇒ Marchers jeered at white passers-by, but there was no violence, nor any arrests. [be V-ed] ⇒ His motorcade was jeered by angry residents. [V n] ⇒ Demonstrators have jeered the mayor as he arrived for a week long visit. [V] ⇒ I didn't come here today to jeer: I want to give advice. [V-ing] ⇒ ...mobs of jeering bystanders. [Also V with quote]jeering uncount noun [+ from] ⇒ There was constant jeering and interruption from the floor.2. countable noun [usu pl]Jeers are rude and insulting things that people shout to show they do not like or respect someone. ⇒ He stared sullenly into space, pretending not to hear the jeers. [+ of] ⇒ ...the heckling and jeers of his audience.
