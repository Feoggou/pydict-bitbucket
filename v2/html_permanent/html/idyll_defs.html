idyll

or sometimes US idyl (ˈɪdɪl )
Definitions
noun1. a poem or prose work describing an idealized rural life, pastoral scenes, etc2. any simple narrative or descriptive piece in poetry or prose3. a charming or picturesque scene or event4. a piece of music with a calm or pastoral character
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin īdyllium, from Greek eidullion, from eidos shape, (literary) form
