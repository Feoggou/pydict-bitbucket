ill-suited

(ˌɪlˈsjuːtɪd, ˌɪlˈsuːtɪd)
Definitions
adjective1. See ill-suited for 2. not very compatible ⇒ However the couple were ill-suited., ⇒ They were ill-suited and their relationship was destined to failure.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'ill-suited'
It was too cold to shed the warmth of her cloak, and underneath she wore a Fardohnyan costume ill-suited to the bitter cold. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPhe piped in the reedy androgynous voice so ill-suited to his fearsome looks. Butterworth, Michael THE FIVE MILLION DOLLAR PRINCEYet her clothes weren't ill-suited : a gown of shimmering lavender intercut with a lace the color of whipped eggs. Gregory Maguire CONFESSIONS OF AN UGLY STEPSISTER
