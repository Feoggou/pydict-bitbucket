her

(hɜː , unstressed hə , ə )
Definitions
pronoun (objective)1. refers to a female person or animal ⇒ he loves her, ⇒ they sold her a bag, ⇒ something odd about her, ⇒ lucky her!2. refers to things personified as feminine or traditionally to ships and nations3. mainly US a dialect word for herself ⇒ she needs to get her a better jobdeterminer4. of, belonging to, or associated with her ⇒ her silly ideas, ⇒ her hair, ⇒ her smoking annoys meSee note at me1
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hire, genitive and dative of hēo she, feminine of hēo he1; related to Old High German ira, Gothic izōs, Middle Dutch hare

her

Powered by COBUILD
(həʳ , STRONG hɜːʳ ) for learners of English
Definitions
Her is a third person singular pronoun. Her is used as the object of a verb or a preposition. Her is also a possessive determiner.1. singular pronoun & pronounYou use her to refer to a woman, girl, or female animal. ⇒ I went in the room and told her I had something to say to her. ⇒ Catherine could not give her the advice she most needed. ⇒ I really thought I'd lost her. Everybody kept asking me, 'Have you found your cat?'Her is also a possessive determiner. ⇒ Liz travelled round the world for a year with her boyfriend James. ⇒ We admire her courage, compassion and dedication. ⇒ ...a black dog, her hair erect along the centre of her back.2. singular pronoun & pronounIn written English, her is sometimes used to refer to a person without saying whether that person is a man or a woman. Some people dislike this use and prefer to use 'him or her' or 'them'. ⇒ Talk to your baby, play games, and show her how much you enjoy her company.Her is also a possessive determiner. ⇒ The non-drinking, non smoking model should do nothing to risk her reputation.3. singular pronoun & pronounHer is sometimes used to refer to a country or nation. [formal, or written]Her is also a possessive determiner. ⇒ Our reporter looks at reactions to Britain's apparently deep-rooted distrust of her E.U. partner.4. singular pronoun [v PRON, prep PRON]People sometimes use her to refer to a car, machine, or ship. ⇒ Kemp got out of his car. 'Just fill her up, thanks.'Her is also a possessive determiner. ⇒ This dramatic photograph was taken from Carpathia's deck by one of her passengers.
