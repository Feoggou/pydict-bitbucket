poor

(pʊə , pɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. a. lacking financial or other means of subsistence; needyb. (as collective noun; preceded by the) ⇒ the poor2. characterized by or indicating poverty ⇒ the country had a poor economy3. deficient in amount; scanty or inadequate ⇒ a poor salary4. (when postpositive, usually foll by in) badly supplied (with resources, materials, etc) ⇒ a region poor in wild flowers5. lacking in quality; inferior6. giving no pleasure; disappointing or disagreeable ⇒ a poor play7. (prenominal) deserving of pity; unlucky ⇒ poor John is ill again8. See poor man's something

Derived Forms

poorness (ˈpoorness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French povre, from Latin pauper; see pauper, poverty

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is poor has very little money and few possessions. ⇒ The reason our schools cannot afford better teachers is because people here are poor. ⇒ He was one of thirteen children from a poor family.The poor are people who are poor. ⇒ Even the poor have their pride.2. graded adjective & adjectiveThe people in a poor country or area have very little money and few possessions. ⇒ Many countries in the Third World are as poor as they have ever been. ⇒ ...a settlement house for children in a poor neighborhood.3. adjective [ADJ n]You use poor to express your sympathy for someone. [feelings] ⇒ I feel sorry for that poor child. ⇒ Poor chap–he was killed in an air crash. ⇒ Poor Gordon!4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as poor, you mean that it is of a low quality or standard or that it is in bad condition. ⇒ The flat was in a poor state of repair. ⇒ The gap between the best and poorest childcare provision in the European Union has widened. ⇒ The wine was poor.poorly graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ Some are living in poorly built dormitories, even in tents. ⇒ They were dressed and fed poorly.5. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an amount, rate, or number as poor, you mean that it is less than expected or less than is considered reasonable. ⇒ ...poor wages and working conditions.poorly graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ During the first week, the evening meetings were poorly attended. ⇒ For one of the top ten releases in rock history, the record sold poorly.6. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use poor to describe someone who is not very skilful in a particular activity. ⇒ He was a poor actor. [+ at] ⇒ Hospitals are poor at collecting information.poorly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ That is the fact of Hungarian football–they can play very well or very poorly.7. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is poor in a particular quality or substance, it contains very little of the quality or substance. low in ⇒ Fats and sugar are very rich in energy but poor in vitamins, minerals and dietary fibre. ⇒ ...soil that is poor in zinc.
