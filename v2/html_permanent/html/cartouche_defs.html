cartouche

or cartouch (kɑːˈtuːʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a carved or cast ornamental tablet or panel in the form of a scroll, sometimes having an inscription2. an oblong figure enclosing characters expressing royal or divine names in Egyptian hieroglyphics3. the paper case holding combustible materials in certain fireworks4. rare a cartridge or a box for cartridges
Word origin
C17: from French: scroll, cartridge, from Italian cartoccio, from carta paper; see card1
Example sentences containing 'cartouche'
I was about to suggest that the golden ring bearing the royal cartouche may be significant. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY
