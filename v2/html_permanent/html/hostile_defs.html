hostile

(ˈhɒstaɪl )
Definitions
adjective1. antagonistic; opposed2. of or relating to an enemy3. unfriendlynoun4. a hostile person; enemy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hostilely (ˈhostilely) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin hostīlis, from hostis enemy

hostile

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒstaɪl , US -təl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are hostile to another person or an idea, you disagree with them or disapprove of them, often showing this in your behaviour. [+ to] ⇒ Many people felt he would be hostile to the idea of foreign intervention. [+ to/towards] ⇒ The West has gradually relaxed its hostile attitude to this influential state. ⇒ The Governor faced hostile crowds when he visited the town yesterday.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is hostile is unfriendly and aggressive. ⇒ Drinking may make a person feel relaxed and happy, or it may make her hostile, violent, or depressed. ⇒ The prisoner eyed him in hostile silence.3. graded adjective & adjectiveHostile situations and conditions make it difficult for you to achieve something. ⇒ ...some of the most hostile climatic conditions in the world. ⇒ If this round of talks fails, the world's trading environment is likely to become increasingly hostile.4. adjectiveA hostile takeover bid is one that is opposed by the company that is being bid for. [business] ⇒ Kingfisher launched a hostile bid for Dixons.5. adjective [ADJ n]In a war, you use hostile to describe your enemy's forces, organizations, weapons, land, and activities. ⇒ The city is encircled by a hostile army. ⇒ They were in hostile territory. ⇒ ...hostile aircraft.
