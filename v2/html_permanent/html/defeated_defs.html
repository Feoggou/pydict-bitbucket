defeated

(dɪˈfiːtɪd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having suffered defeat; beaten ⇒ Marshal Foch had forced the defeated Germans to agree to punitive terms., ⇒ the defeated and now dying Disraeli, ⇒ the defeated candidate, ⇒ the defeated England team2. showing signs of discouragement ⇒ a defeated look on his face
Example sentences containing 'defeated'
He slipped forward in his bent, defeated posture, testing the feeling in his fingertips, the weight of the two men against him. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENThe sky was clearing, and in the growing light the three of them looked sodden, dirty, and defeated. Amanda Hemingway THE GREENSTONE GRAIL: THE SANGREAL TRILOGY ONEHe had arrived shattered and defeated , but now of the two of them he was looking the more confident. St. James, Ian FINAL RESORT
