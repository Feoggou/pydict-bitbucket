tenet

(ˈtɛnɪt , ˈtiːnɪt )
Definitions
nouna belief, opinion, or dogma
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin, literally: he (it) holds, from tenēre to hold

tenet

Powered by COBUILD
(tenɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: tenets
Definitions
countable nounThe tenets of a theory or belief are the main principles on which it is based. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Non-violence and patience are the central tenets of their faith. ⇒ The judge's ruling was based on the simple commonsense tenet that no man is above the law.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
