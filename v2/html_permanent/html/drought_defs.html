drought

(draʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a prolonged period of scanty rainfall2. a prolonged shortage3. an archaic or dialect word for thirst. Archaic and Scot form: drouth
Derived forms
droughty (ˈdroughty) adjective
Word origin
Old English drūgoth; related to Dutch droogte; see dry
