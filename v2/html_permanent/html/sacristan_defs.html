sacristan

(ˈsækrɪstən ) or sacrist (ˈsækrɪst , ˈseɪ-)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who has charge of the contents of a church, esp the sacred vessels, vestments, etc2. a less common word for sexton (sense 1)

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin sacristānus, from sacrista, from Latin sacer holy
Example sentences containing 'sacristan'
There was a sacristan kindling branches of candles on the high altar; a deacon, he judged unerringly. Colleen McCullough THE THORN BIRDS
