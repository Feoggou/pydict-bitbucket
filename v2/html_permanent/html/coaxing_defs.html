coaxing

(ˈkəʊksɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of persuading by tenderness, flattery, pleading, etc ⇒ It took a lot of convincing to get the lady to continue the walk, but the coaxing was worth it: the temple was magnificent.adjective2. serving to persuade or manipulate
Example sentences containing 'coaxing'
The whine in her voice gave him hope; the plea seemed languorous, a bit coaxing. McCorquodale, Robin DANSVILLEXaphista spoke to her often, coaxing one minute, taunting the next. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEP
