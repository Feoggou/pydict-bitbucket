Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (ˈprɛsɪdənt )1. law a judicial decision that serves as an authority for deciding a later case2. an example or instance used to justify later similar occurrencesadjective (prɪˈsiːdənt , ˈprɛsɪdənt )3. preceding

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounIf there is a precedent for an action or event, it has happened before, and this can be regarded as an argument for doing it again. [formal] [+ for] ⇒ The trial could set an important precedent for dealing with large numbers of similar cases. ⇒ There are plenty of precedents in Hollywood for letting people out of contracts.
