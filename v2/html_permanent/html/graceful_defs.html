graceful

Powered by COBUILD
(greɪsfʊl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is graceful moves in a smooth and controlled way which is attractive to watch. OPPOSITE: graceless ⇒ His movements were so graceful they seemed effortless. ⇒ ...graceful ballerinas.gracefully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ She stepped gracefully onto the stage.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is graceful is attractive because it has a pleasing shape or style. OPPOSITE: graceless ⇒ A graceful medieval cathedral. ⇒ His handwriting, from earliest young manhood, was flowing and graceful.gracefully adverb [ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ She loved the gracefully high ceiling, with its white-painted cornice.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a person's behaviour is graceful, it is polite, kind, and pleasant, especially in a difficult situation. ⇒ Aubrey could think of no graceful way to escape Corbet's company. ⇒ He was charming, cheerful, and graceful under pressure.gracefully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ We managed to decline gracefully.
