frozen

(ˈfrəʊzən )
Definitions
verb1. the past participle of freezeadjective2. turned into or covered with ice3. obstructed or blocked by ice4. killed, injured, or stiffened by extreme cold5. (of a region or climate) icy or snowy6. (of food) preserved by a freezing process7. a. (of prices, wages, etc) arbitrarily pegged at a certain levelb. (of business assets) not convertible into cash, as by government direction or business conditions8. frigid, unfeeling, or disdainful in manner9. motionless or unyielding ⇒ he was frozen with horror

frozen

Powered by COBUILD
(froʊzən ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. Frozen is the past participle of freeze.2. adjectiveIf the ground is frozen it has become very hard because the weather is very cold. ⇒ It was bitterly cold now and the ground was frozen hard. ⇒ ...the frozen bleakness of the Far North.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Frozen food has been preserved by being kept at a very low temperature. ⇒ Frozen fish is a very healthy convenience food. ⇒ ...frozen desserts like ice cream.4. adjectiveIf you say that you are frozen, or a part of your body is frozen, you are emphasizing that you feel very cold. [emphasis] ⇒ He put one hand up to his frozen face. ⇒ I'm frozen to the bone out here.→  frozen stiff5. adjective [oft ADJ in/with n]If you describe someone as frozen, you mean that their body is fixed in a particular position, for example because they are very worried or afraid. ⇒ One boy, aged about 11, looks frozen with fright. ⇒ Katherine was frozen in horror.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
