foolery

(ˈfuːlərɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -eries1. foolish behaviour2. an instance of this, esp a prank or trick
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'foolery'
But underlying all this foolery -- "Gerhardt used the word deliberately. NULL DARE CALL IT TREASONAnd the people of these times are something less easily hoodwinked, I think, by such stark foolery. Heller, Keith MAN'S LOVING FAMILYLeaping from the Hun, he reached up to snatch his wife from the saddle, angry at her foolery and the fear she caused him. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
