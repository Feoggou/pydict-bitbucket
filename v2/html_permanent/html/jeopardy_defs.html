jeopardy

(ˈdʒɛpədɪ )
Definitions
noun (usually preceded by in)1. danger of injury, loss, death, etc; risk; peril; hazard ⇒ his health was in jeopardy2. law danger of being convicted and punished for a criminal offence. See also double jeopardy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French jeu parti, literally: divided game, hence uncertain issue, from jeu game, from Latin jocus joke, game + partir to divide, from Latin partīrī
