idiocy

(ˈɪdɪəsɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -cies1. old-fashioned, offensive severe learning difficulties2. foolishness or senselessness; stupidity3. a foolish act or remark
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

idiocy

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪdiəsi ) for learners of English
Word forms: idiocies
Definitions
variable nounIf you refer to something as idiocy, you mean that you think it is very stupid. [+ of] ⇒ ...the idiocy of continuing government subsidies for environmentally damaging activities. ⇒ ...his gentle, ironic analysis of the idiocies of the previous regime.
