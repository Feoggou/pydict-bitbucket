necessarily

(ˈnɛsɪsərɪlɪ , ˌnɛsɪˈsɛrɪlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. as an inevitable or natural consequence ⇒ girls do not necessarily like dolls2. as a certainty ⇒ he won't necessarily come
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

necessarily for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nesɪserɪli , -srɪli)
Definitions
1. adverb [with neg] [ADV before v]If you say that something is not necessarily the case, you mean that it may not be the case or is not always the case. [vagueness] ⇒ Anger is not necessarily the most useful or acceptable reaction to such events. ⇒ Speed and safety are not necessarily incompatible. ⇒ A higher fee does not necessarily mean a better course.→  not necessarily2. adverb [ADV before v]If you say that something necessarily happens or is the case, you mean that it has to happen or be the case and cannot be any different. ⇒ The most desirable properties necessarily command astonishingly high prices. ⇒ Tourism is an industry that has a necessarily close connection with governments.
