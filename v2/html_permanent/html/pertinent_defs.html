pertinent

(ˈpɜːtɪnənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiverelating to the matter at hand; relevant

Derived Forms

pertinence (ˈpertinence) or pertinency (ˈpertinency) nounpertinently (ˈpertinently) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin pertinēns, from pertinēre to pertain

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is pertinent is relevant to a particular subject. [formal] ⇒ She had asked some pertinent questions. ⇒ Pertinent information will be forwarded to the appropriate party. [+ to] ⇒ ...knowledge and skills pertinent to classroom teaching.pertinently graded adverb [ADV with v, ADV with cl] ⇒ 'If we pay players, how far do we go?' Gresson asked pertinently. ⇒ Where had they learned all this, or, more pertinently, why had they remembered it?pertinence uncount noun ⇒ I do not see the pertinence of most of this material.
