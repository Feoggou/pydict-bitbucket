esplanade

(ˌɛspləˈneɪd , -ˈnɑːd)
Definitions
noun1. a long open level stretch of ground for walking along, esp beside the seashore. Compare promenade (sense 1)2. an open area in front of a fortified place, in which attackers are exposed to the defenders' fire
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from French, from Old Italian spianata, from spianare to make level, from Latin explānāre; see explain
