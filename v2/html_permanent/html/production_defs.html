production

(prəˈdʌkʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of producing2. anything that is produced; product3. the amount produced or the rate at which it is produced4. economics the creation or manufacture for sale of goods and services with exchange value5. any work created as a result of literary or artistic effort6. the organization and presentation of a film, play, opera, etc7. British the artistic direction of a play8. a. the supervision of the arrangement, recording, and mixing of a recordb. the overall sound quality or character of a recording ⇒ the material is very strong but the production is poor9. (modifier) manufactured by a mass-production process ⇒ a production model of a car10. See make a production out of

Derived Forms

productional (proˈductional) adjective

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft into N]Production is the process of manufacturing or growing something in large quantities. ⇒ That model won't go into production before late 1990. [+ of] ⇒ ...tax incentives to encourage domestic production of oil.2. uncount nounProduction is the amount of goods manufactured or grown by a company or country. ⇒ We needed to increase the volume of production. ⇒ It expected to maintain production of cars at the same level as last year.3. uncount nounThe production of something is its creation as the result of a natural process. [+ of] ⇒ These proteins stimulate the production of blood cells.4. uncount nounProduction is the process of organizing and preparing a play, film, programme, or CD, in order to present it to the public. ⇒ During the film's production, the director wanted to shoot a riot scene but the filming was blocked. ⇒ She is head of the production company.5. countable nounA production is a play, opera, or other show that is performed in a theatre. ⇒ For this production she has learnt the role in Spanish. [+ of] ⇒ ...a critically acclaimed production of Othello.6. →  on production of something/on the production of something
