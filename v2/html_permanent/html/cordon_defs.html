cordon

(ˈkɔːdən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a chain of police, soldiers, ships, etc, stationed around an area2. a ribbon worn as insignia of honour or rank3. a cord or ribbon worn as an ornament or fastening4. Also called: stringcourse, belt course, table architecture an ornamental projecting band or continuous moulding along a wall5. horticulture a form of fruit tree consisting of a single stem bearing fruiting spurs, produced by cutting back all lateral branchesverb6. (transitive; often foll by off) to put or form a cordon (around); close (off)
Word origin
C16: from Old French, literally: a little cord, from corde string, cord
