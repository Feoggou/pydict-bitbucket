parallelism

(ˈpærəlɛˌlɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state of being parallel2. grammar the repetition of a syntactic construction in successive sentences for rhetorical effect3. philosophy the dualistic doctrine that mental and physical processes are regularly correlated but are not causally connected, so that, for example, pain always accompanies, but is not caused by, a pin-prick. Compare interactionism, occasionalism

Derived Forms

paralˌlelist (ˈparalˌlelist) noun, adjective
