lake1

(leɪk )
Definitions
noun1. an expanse of water entirely surrounded by land and unconnected to the sea except by rivers or streams ▶ Related adjective: lacustrine2. anything resembling this3. a surplus of a liquid commodity ⇒ a wine lake
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: lac, via Old French from Latin lacus basin

lake2

(leɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a bright pigment used in textile dyeing and printing inks, produced by the combination of an organic colouring matter with an inorganic compound, usually a metallic salt, oxide, or hydroxide. See also mordant2. a red dye obtained by combining a metallic compound with cochineal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: variant of lac1

Lake

Definitions
a lake on the border between the US and Canada, in N Vermont and S Quebec. Length: about 43 km (27 miles). Width: up to 6 km (4 miles)▷ See Memphremagog
