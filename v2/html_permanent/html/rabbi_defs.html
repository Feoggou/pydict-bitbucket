rabbi

(ˈræbaɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -bis1. (in Orthodox Judaism) a man qualified in accordance with traditional religious law to expound, teach, and rule in accordance with this law2. the religious leader of a congregation; the minister of a synagogue3. See the Rabbis▷ See also Rav
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Hebrew, from rabh master + -ī my
