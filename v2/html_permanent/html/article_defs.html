article

(ˈɑːtɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. one of a class of objects; item ⇒ an article of clothing2. an unspecified or previously named thing, esp a small object ⇒ he put the article on the table3. a distinct part of a subject or action4. a written composition on a subject, often being one of several found in a magazine, newspaper, etc5. grammar a kind of determiner, occurring in many languages including English, that lacks independent meaning but may serve to indicate the specificity of reference of the noun phrase with which it occurs. See also definite article, indefinite article6. a clause or section in a written document such as a treaty, contract, statute, etc7. See in articles8. (often capital) Christianity See article of faith, Thirty-nine Articles9. archaic a topic or subjectverb (transitive)10. archaic to accuse
Word origin
C13: from Old French, from Latin articulus small joint, from artus joint

Word forms: articles
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn article is a piece of writing that is published in a newspaper or magazine. ⇒ ...a newspaper article. ⇒ ...a travel article. ⇒ According to an article in The Economist the drug could have side effects. ⇒ ...Canning's article about the Buxton Festival.2. countable nounYou can refer to objects as articles of some kind. [+ of] ⇒ ...articles of clothing. ⇒ He had stripped the house of all articles of value. ⇒ ...household articles.3. →  the genuine article4. countable nounAn article of a formal agreement or document is a section of it which deals with a particular point. ⇒ The country appears to be violating several articles of the convention. ⇒ ...Article 50 of the U.N. charter.5. plural nounSomeone who is in articles is being trained as a lawyer or accountant by a firm with whom they have a written agreement. [British] ⇒ In 1986, 44 per cent of those admitted to articles were women.6. countable nounIn grammar, an article is a kind of determiner. In English, 'a' and 'an' are called the indefinite article, and 'the' is called the definite article.
