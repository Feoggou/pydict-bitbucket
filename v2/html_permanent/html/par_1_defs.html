par

(pɑː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an accepted level or standard, such as an average (esp in the phrase up to par)2. a state of equality (esp in the phrase on a par with)3. finance the established value of the unit of one national currency in terms of the unit of another where both are based on the same metal standard4. businessa. See par valueb. the condition of equality between the current market value of a share, bond, etc, and its face value (the nominal par). This equality is indicated by at par, while above (or below) par indicates that the market value is above (or below) face value5. golf an estimated standard score for a hole or course that a good player should make ⇒ par for the course was 726. See below par7. See par for the courseadjective8. average or normal9. (usually prenominal) business of or relating to par ⇒ par value

Word Origin

C17: from Latin pār equal, on a level; see peer1
