numen

(ˈnjuːmɛn )
Definitions
noun plural -mina (-mɪnə)1. (esp in ancient Roman religion) a deity or spirit presiding over a thing or place2. a guiding principle, force, or spirit
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: a nod (indicating a command), divine power; compare nuere to nod
Example sentences containing 'numen'
Taped mere physical work, however, for hours, strange, a numen coming off him and off the mask he was working on, hard to describe. Michael Gruber TROPIC OF NIGHT
