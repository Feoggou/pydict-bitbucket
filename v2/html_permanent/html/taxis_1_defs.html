taxis

(ˈtæksɪs )
Definitions
noun1. the movement of a cell or organism in a particular direction in response to an external stimulus2. surgery the repositioning of a displaced organ or part by manual manipulation only
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: via New Latin from Greek: arrangement, from tassein to place in order
