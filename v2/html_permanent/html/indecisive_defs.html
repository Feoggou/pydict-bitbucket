indecisive

(ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. (of a person) vacillating; irresolute2. not decisive or conclusive
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

indecision (ˌindeˈcision) or indecisiveness (ˌindeˈcisiveness) nounindecisively (ˌindeˈcisively) adverb

indecisive

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndɪsaɪsɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is indecisive, you mean that they find it very difficult to make decisions. ⇒ Michael was indecisive about how to decorate the room. ⇒ He was criticised as a weak and indecisive leader.indecisiveness uncount noun [oft with poss] ⇒ The mayor was criticized by radical reformers for his indecisiveness.2. adjectiveAn indecisive result in a contest or election is one which is not clear or definite. ⇒ An indecisive result would force a second round of voting. ⇒ The outcome of the battle was indecisive.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
