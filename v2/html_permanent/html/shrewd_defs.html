shrewd

(ʃruːd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. astute and penetrating, often with regard to business2. artful and crafty ⇒ a shrewd politician3. obsoletea. piercing ⇒ a shrewd windb. spiteful

Derived Forms

shrewdly (ˈshrewdly) adverbshrewdness (ˈshrewdness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from shrew (obsolete vb) to curse, from shrew

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveA shrewd person is able to understand and judge a situation quickly and to use this understanding to their own advantage. ⇒ She's a shrewd businesswoman. ⇒ His grey eyes were shrewd but kindly. ⇒ It should prove a shrewd investment.shrewdly graded adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ She looked at him shrewdly. ⇒ ...a shrewdly worded statement.shrewdness uncount noun ⇒ His natural shrewdness tells him what is needed to succeed.
