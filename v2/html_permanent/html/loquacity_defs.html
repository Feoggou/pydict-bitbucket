loquacity

(loʊˈkwæsəti ) in American English
Definitions
noun talkativeness, esp. when excessive
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

L loquacitas < loquax: see loquacious
Example sentences containing 'loquacity'
I stared in stupefaction at Rudy, as the implications of his sudden loquacity sunk in. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSERamses opened his mouth to reply; Emerson, who knew his son's tendency toward unnecessary loquacity as well as I did, replied for him. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY

Similar Words

talkativeness, chattering, babbling, gabbling, volubility, effusiveness, chattiness, garrulity, gassiness (informal)
