clout

(klaʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. informal a blow with the hand or a hard object2. power or influence, esp in politics3. archerya. the target used in long-distance shootingb. the centre of this targetc. a shot that hits the centre4. Also called: clout nail a short flat-headed nail used esp for attaching sheet metal to wood5. British dialecta. a piece of cloth ⇒ a dish cloutb. a garmentc. a patchverb (transitive)6. informal to give a hard blow to, esp with the hand7. to patch with a piece of cloth or leather
Derived forms
clouter (ˈclouter) noun
Word origin
Old English clūt piece of metal or cloth, clūtian to patch (C14: to strike with the hand); related to Dutch kluit a lump, and to clod

Powered by COBUILD
(klaʊt )
Word forms: clouts , clouting , clouted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you clout someone, you hit them. [informal] [V n] ⇒ Rachel clouted him. [V n + on] ⇒ The officer clouted her on the head.Clout is also a noun. ⇒ I was half tempted to give one of them a clout myself.2. uncount nounA person or institution that has clout has influence and power. [informal] ⇒ Mr Sutherland may have the clout needed to push the two trading giants into a deal. ⇒ The two firms wield enormous clout in financial markets.
