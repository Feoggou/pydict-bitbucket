repertory

(ˈrɛpətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun plural -ries1. the entire stock of things available in a field or of a kind; repertoire2. a building or place where a stock of things is kept; repository3. short for repertory company
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

repertorial (ˌreperˈtorial) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin repertōrium storehouse, from Latin reperīre to obtain, from re- + parere to bring forth

repertory

Powered by COBUILD
(repəʳtri , US -tɔːri ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount noun [usu N n]A repertory company is a group of actors and actresses who perform a small number of plays for just a few weeks at a time. They work in a repertory theatre. ⇒ ...a well-known repertory company in Boston. ⇒ He was in repertory in Dundee.2. singular noun [usu poss N]A performer's repertory is all the plays or pieces of music that he or she has learned and can perform. ⇒ Her repertory was vast and to her it seemed that each song told some part of her life.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
