reflux

(ˈriːflʌks )
Definitions
verb1. chemistry to boil or be boiled in a vessel attached to a condenser, so that the vapour condenses and flows back into the vesselnoun2. chemistrya. an act of refluxingb. (as modifier) ⇒ a reflux condenser3. the act or an instance of flowing back; ebb
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin refluxus, from Latin refluere to flow back
