propound

(prəˈpaʊnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to suggest or put forward for consideration2. English lawa. to produce (a will or similar instrument) to the proper court or authority in order for its validity to be establishedb. (of an executor) to bring (an action to obtain probate) in solemn form

Derived Forms

propounder (proˈpounder) noun

Word Origin

C16 propone, from Latin prōpōnere to set forth, from pro-1 + pōnere to place
