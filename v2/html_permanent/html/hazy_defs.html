hazy

(ˈheɪzɪ )
Definitions
adjective -zier or -ziest1. characterized by reduced visibility; misty2. indistinct; vague
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hazily (ˈhazily) adverbhaziness (ˈhaziness) noun

hazy

Powered by COBUILD
(heɪzi ) for learners of English
Word forms: hazier , haziest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveHazy weather conditions are those in which things are difficult to see, because of light mist, hot air, or dust. ⇒ The air was thin and crisp, filled with hazy sunshine and frost. ⇒ The floodlights were hazy behind the slanting rain.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are hazy about ideas or details, or if they are hazy, you are uncertain or confused about them. ⇒ I'm a bit hazy about that. ⇒ I have only a hazy memory of what he was really like. ⇒ She had only a hazy idea of Britain's prison problems. ⇒ Many details remain hazy.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf things seem hazy, you cannot see things clearly, for example because you are feeling ill. ⇒ She sipped the wine. Everything was hazy now, except for Nick's face. ⇒ My vision has grown so hazy. ⇒ It's as if I'm living in a hazy dream world.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
