pendulous

(ˈpɛndjʊləs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivehanging downwards, esp so as to swing from side to side

Derived Forms

pendulously (ˈpendulously) adverbpendulousness (ˈpendulousness) or pendulosity (ˌpɛndjʊˈlɒsɪtɪ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin pendulus, from pendēre to hang down

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveSomething that is pendulous hangs downwards and moves loosely, usually in an unattractive way. [literary] ⇒ ...a stout, gloomy man with a pendulous lower lip. ⇒ ...pendulous cheeks.
