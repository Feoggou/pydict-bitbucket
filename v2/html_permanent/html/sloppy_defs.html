sloppy

(ˈslɒpɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -pier or -piest1. (esp of ground conditions, etc) wet; slushy2. informal careless; untidy3. informal mawkishly sentimental4. (of food or drink) watery and unappetizing5. splashed with slops6. (of clothes) loose; baggy
Derived forms
sloppily (ˈsloppily) adverbsloppiness (ˈsloppiness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's work or activities as sloppy, you mean they have been done in a careless and lazy way. [disapproval] ⇒ He has little patience for sloppy work from colleagues. ⇒ His language is disjointed and sloppy.sloppily (slɒpɪli ) graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ They lost because they played sloppily.sloppiness uncount noun ⇒ Miss Furniss could not abide sloppiness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as sloppy, you mean that they are sentimental and romantic. ⇒ It's ideal for people who like a sloppy movie. ⇒ ...some sloppy love-story.
