café

(ˈkæfeɪ , ˈkæfɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small or inexpensive restaurant or coffee bar, serving light meals and refreshments2. South Africa a corner shop or grocer
Word origin
C19: from French: coffee

café

Powered by COBUILD
(kæfeɪ , US kæfeɪ ) also cafe
Word forms: cafés
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA café is a place where you can buy drinks, simple meals, and snacks, but, in Britain, not usually alcoholic drinks.2. countable noun [n N]A street café or a pavement café is a café which has tables and chairs on the pavement outside it where people can eat and drink. ⇒ ...an Italian street café. ⇒ ...sidewalk cafés and boutiques.usage note:   In Britain, a café serves tea, coffee, soft drinks, and light meals, but not usually alcoholic drinks. If you want an alcoholic drink, you can go to a pub. In American English, a pub is more usually called a bar. Many pubs serve food, especially at lunchtime, but for a larger or more special meal, you might go to a restaurant.
