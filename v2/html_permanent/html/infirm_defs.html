infirm

(ɪnˈfɜːm )
Definitions
adjective1. a. weak in health or body, esp from old ageb. (as collective noun; preceded by the) ⇒ the infirm2. lacking moral certainty; indecisive or irresolute3. not stable, sound, or secure ⇒ an infirm structure, ⇒ an infirm claim4. law (of a law, custom, etc) lacking legal force; invalid
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

infirmly (inˈfirmly) adverbinfirmness (inˈfirmness) noun
