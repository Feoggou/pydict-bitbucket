cantilever

(ˈkæntɪˌliːvə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a beam, girder, or structural framework that is fixed at one end and is free at the otherb. (as modifier) ⇒ a cantilever wing2. a wing or tailplane of an aircraft that has no external bracing or support3. a part of a beam or a structure projecting outwards beyond its supportverb4. (transitive) to construct (a building member, beam, etc) so that it is fixed at one end only5. (intransitive) to project like a cantilever
Word origin
C17: perhaps from cant2 + lever
