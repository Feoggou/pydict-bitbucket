video

(ˈvɪdɪˌəʊ )
Definitions
adjective1. relating to or employed in the transmission or reception of a televised image2. of, concerned with, or operating at video frequenciesnoun plural -os3. the visual elements of a television broadcast4. a film recorded on a video cassette5. short for video cassette, video cassette recorder6. US an informal name for televisionverbWord forms: videos, videoing or videoed7. to record (a television programme, etc) on a video cassette recorder▷ Compare audio
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: from Latin vidēre to see, on the model of audio

video

Powered by COBUILD
(vɪdioʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: videos , videoing , videoed
Definitions
1. countable nounA video is a film or television programme recorded on tape for people to watch on a television set. ⇒ We were watching videos with her. ⇒ ...the makers of films and videos.2. uncount noun [oft on N]Video is the system of recording films and events on tape so that people can watch them on a television set. ⇒ She has watched the race on video. ⇒ ...manufacturers of audio and video equipment. ⇒ ...lessons which use video and film.3. countable nounA video is a machine that you can use to record television programmes and play videotapes on a television set. [mainly British] ⇒ He'd set the video for 8.00.regional note:   in AM, usually use VCR4. verbIf you video a television programme or event, you record it on tape using a video recorder or video camera, so that you can watch it later. [mainly British] [V n] ⇒ She had been videoing the highlights of the tournament. [V n] ⇒ The club specialises in videoing its student golfers to correct their faults.regional note:   in AM, usually use tape, videotape5. uncount nounVideo is a system by which you can see television images or films on your computer, rather than on a television set.
