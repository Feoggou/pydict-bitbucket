christen

(ˈkrɪsən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to give a Christian name to in baptism as a sign of incorporation into a Christian Church2. another word for baptize3. to give a name to (anything), esp with some ceremony4. informal to use for the first time
Derived forms
christener (ˈchristener) noun
Word origin
Old English cristnian, from Crīst Christ

christen

Powered by COBUILD
(krɪsən )
Word forms: christens , christening , christened
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verb [usu passive]When a baby is christened, he or she is given a name during the Christian ceremony of baptism. Compare baptize. [be V-ed] ⇒ She was born in March and christened in June. [be V-ed] ⇒ She was christened Susan.2. verbYou say that you christen a person, place, or object a particular name if you choose a name for them and start calling them by that name. [informal] [be V-ed] ⇒ The pair were christened 'The Women in Black' after they both wore black dresses at a party. [V n n] ⇒ We had a tiny little room which was christened Hades.3. verbYou say that you christen something new when you use it for the first time, especially if you do something special to mark the occasion. [informal] [V n] ⇒ To christen the new hall, a number of great orchestras have been invited to play.
