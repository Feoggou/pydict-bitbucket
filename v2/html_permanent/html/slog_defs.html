slog

(slɒɡ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: slogs, slogging or slogged1. to hit with heavy blows, as in boxing2. (intransitive) to work hard; toil3. (intr; foll by down, up, along, etc) to move with difficulty; plod4. cricket to score freely by taking large swipes at the ballnoun5. a tiring hike or walk6. long exhausting work7. a heavy blow or swipe

Derived Forms

slogger (ˈslogger) noun

Word Origin

C19: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you slog through something, you work hard and steadily through it. [informal] [V prep] ⇒ They secure their degrees by slogging through an intensive 11-month course. [V + way through] ⇒ She has slogged her way through ballet classes since the age of six. [V] ⇒ While slogging at work, have you neglected your marriage?Slog away means the same as slog. [V P] ⇒ Edward slogged away, always learning.2. singular nounIf you describe a task as a slog, you mean that it is tiring and requires a lot of effort. [informal] ⇒ I eventually got financial backing, but it was a slog. ⇒ There is little to show for the two years of hard slog.3. verbIf you slog somewhere, you make a long and tiring journey there. [informal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ The men had to slog up a steep muddy incline. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Why should Melissa have to slog around the supermarket on her own?4. singular nounA slog is a long tiring journey. [informal] ⇒ ...a slog through heather and bracken to the top of a hill.5. →  slog it out► →  slog away
