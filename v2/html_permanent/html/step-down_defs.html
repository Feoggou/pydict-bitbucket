step down

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) to reduce gradually2. (intransitive) informal to resign or abdicate (from a position)3. (intransitive) informal to assume an inferior or less senior positionadjective step-down (prenominal)4. (of a transformer) reducing a high voltage applied to the primary winding to a lower voltage on the secondary winding. Compare step up5. decreasing or falling by stagesnoun step-down6. informal a decrease in quantity or size

step-down

(ˈstɛpˌdaʊn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a transformer) reducing a high voltage applied to the primary winding to a lower voltage on the secondary winding ⇒ The step-down transformers are buried underground.2. informal decreasing or falling by stages ⇒ lack of effective step-down care for those returning to the community, ⇒ step-down services to help people recover when they are leaving hospitalnoun3. informal a decrease in quantity, size, etc ⇒ a 22 per cent step-down in engine revs, ⇒ The move reflects a step-down in India-Pakistan tensions over Kashmir.
