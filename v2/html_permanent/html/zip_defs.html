zip

(zɪp )
Definitions
noun1. a. Also called: zip fastener a fastening device operating by means of two parallel rows of metal or plastic teeth on either side of a closure that are interlocked by a sliding tab. US and Canadian term: zipperb. (modifier) having or equipped with such a device ⇒ a zip bag2. a short sharp whizzing sound, as of a passing bullet3. informal energy; vigour; vitality4. US slang nothing5. sport, US and Canadian slang nilverbWord forms: zips, zipping or zipped6. (transitive; often foll by up) to fasten (clothing, a bag, etc) with a zip7. (intransitive) to move with a zip ⇒ the bullet zipped past8. (intr; often foll by along, through, etc) to hurry; rush ⇒ they zipped through town9. (transitive) computing to compress (a file) in order to reduce the amount of memory required to store it or to make sending it electronically quicker

Word Origin

C19: of imitative origin

zip

Powered by COBUILD
(zɪp ) for learners of English
Word forms: zips , zipping , zipped
Definitions
1. countable nounA zip or zip fastener is a device used to open and close parts of clothes and bags. It consists of two rows of metal or plastic teeth which separate or fasten together as you pull a small tag along them. [mainly British] [+ of] ⇒ He pulled the zip of his leather jacket down slightly.regional note:   in AM, usually use zipper2. verbWhen you zip something, you fasten it using a zip. [V n] ⇒ She zipped her jeans. [V n] ⇒ I slowly zipped and locked the heavy black nylon bags.3. verbTo zip a computer file means to compress it so that it needs less space for storage on disk and can be transmitted more quickly. [computing]Zip up means the same as zip. [V P] ⇒ These files have been zipped up to take up less disk space. [Also V P n]4. verbIf you say that something or someone zips somewhere, you mean that they move there very quickly. [informal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ My craft zipped across the bay. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Max zips back and forth across the living room.5. uncount nounIf you say that someone or something has zip, you mean that they show a lot of energy and enthusiasm. [informal] verve ⇒ Share options will play a bigger role in future, putting zip into the performance of middle managers. ⇒ Tommy Tune gives the choreography his usual class and zip.► →  zip up
