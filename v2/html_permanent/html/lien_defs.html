lien

(ˈliːən , liːn )
Definitions
nounlaw a right to retain possession of another's property pending discharge of a debt
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: via Old French from Latin ligāmen bond, from ligāre to bind

lien

Powered by COBUILD
(lin) in Finance
Word forms: (regular plural) liens
Definitions
noun(Finance: Mortgage) A lien is a legal claim on a piece of property that must be paid off before the property can be sold. ⇒ If a court decides that a person must repay a debt, a lien may be placed against that person's property. ⇒ The seller may obtain clear title by paying the contractor and removing the lien. ⇒ A lien is a legal claim on a piece of property that must be paid off before the property can be sold.
