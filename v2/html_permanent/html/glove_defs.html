glove

(ɡlʌv )
Definitions
noun1. (often plural) a shaped covering for the hand with individual sheaths for the fingers and thumb, made of leather, fabric, etc. See also gauntlet1 (sense 2)2. any of various large protective hand covers worn in sports, such as a boxing glove3. See hand in glove4. See handle with kid gloves5. See with the gloves offverb6. (tr; usually passive) to cover or provide with or as if with gloves
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gloved adjectivegloveless (ˈgloveless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English glōfe; related to Old Norse glōfi

Definitions
1. countable nounGloves are pieces of clothing which cover your hands and wrists and have individual sections for each finger. You wear gloves to keep your hands warm or dry or to protect them. ⇒ He stuck his gloves in his pocket. ⇒ ...a pair of white cotton gloves.2. →  to fit like a glove3. → See also kid gloves4. → hand in glove
