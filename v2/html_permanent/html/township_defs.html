township

(ˈtaʊnʃɪp )
Definitions
noun1. a small town2. (in the Scottish Highlands and islands) a small crofting community3. (in the US and Canada) a territorial area, esp a subdivision of a county: often organized as a unit of local government4. (formerly, in South Africa) a planned urban settlement of Black Africans or Coloured people. Compare location (sense 4)5. English historya. any of the local districts of a large parish, each division containing a village or small townb. the particular manor or parish itself as a territorial divisionc. the inhabitants of a township collectively
