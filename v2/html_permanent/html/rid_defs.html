rid

(rɪd )
Definitions
verbWord forms: rids, ridding, rid or ridded (transitive)1. (foll by of) to relieve or deliver from something disagreeable or undesirable; make free (of) ⇒ to rid a house of mice2. See get rid of
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ridder (ˈridder) noun

Word Origin

C13 (meaning: to clear land): from Old Norse rythja; related to Old High German riutan to clear land

rid

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪd ) for learners of English
Word forms: rids , ridding
Definitions
language note:   The form rid is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle of the verb.1. →  get rid of sth2. →  get rid of sb3. verbIf you rid a place or person of something undesirable or unwanted, you succeed in removing it completely from that place or person. [V n + of] ⇒ The proposals are an attempt to rid the country of political corruption. [V n of n] ⇒ The new vaccine may rid the world of one of its most terrifying diseases.4. verbIf you rid yourself of something you do not want, you take action so that you no longer have it or are no longer affected by it. [V pron-refl + of] ⇒ Why couldn't he ever rid himself of those thoughts, those worries? [V pron-refl of n] ⇒ ...the country's efforts to rid itself of poverty and hunger.5. adjectiveIf you are rid of someone or something that you did not want or that caused problems for you, they are no longer with you or causing problems for you. [+ of] ⇒ The family had sought a way to be rid of her and the problems she had caused them.6. →  be well rid of sb
