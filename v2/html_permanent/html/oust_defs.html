oust

(aʊst )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to force out of a position or place; supplant or expel2. property law to deprive (a person) of the possession of land
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Anglo-Norman ouster, from Latin obstāre to withstand, from ob- against + stāre to stand
