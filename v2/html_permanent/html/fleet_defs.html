fleet1

(fliːt )
Definitions
noun1. a number of warships organized as a tactical unit2. all the warships of a nation3. a number of aircraft, ships, buses, etc, operating together or under the same ownership
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flēot ship, flowing water, from flēotan to float

fleet2

(fliːt )
Definitions
adjective1. rapid in movement; swift2. poetic fleeting; transientverb3. (intransitive) to move rapidly4. (intransitive) archaic to fade away smoothly; glide5. (transitive) nauticala. to change the position of (a hawser)b. to pass (a messenger or lead) to a hawser from a winch for hauling inc. to spread apart (the blocks of a tackle)6. (intransitive) obsolete to float or swim7. (transitive) obsolete to cause (time) to pass rapidly
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fleetly (ˈfleetly) adverbfleetness (ˈfleetness) noun

Word Origin

probably Old English flēotan to float, glide rapidly; related to Old High German fliozzan to flow, Latin pluere to rain

fleet3

(fliːt )
Definitions
nounmainly Southeast England a small coastal inlet; creek
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flēot flowing water; see fleet1

Fleet

(fliːt )
Definitions
noun the Fleet1. a stream that formerly ran into the Thames between Ludgate Hill and Fleet Street and is now a covered sewer2. Also called: Fleet Prison (formerly) a London prison, esp used for holding debtors
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

fleet

Powered by COBUILD
(fliːt ) for learners of English
Word forms: fleets
Definitions
1. countable nounA fleet is a group of ships organized to do something together, for example to fight battles or to catch fish. ⇒ The damage inflicted upon the British fleet was devastating. ⇒ ...restaurants supplied by local fishing fleets.2. countable nounA fleet of vehicles is a group of them, especially when they all belong to a particular organization or business, or when they are all going somewhere together. [+ of] ⇒ With its own fleet of trucks, the company delivers most orders overnight. ⇒ A fleet of ambulances took the injured to hospital.
