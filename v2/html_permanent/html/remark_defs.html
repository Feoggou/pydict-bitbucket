remark

(rɪˈmɑːk )
Definitions
verb1. (when intr, often foll by on or upon; when tr, may take a clause as object) to pass a casual comment (about); reflect in informal speech or writing2. (tr; may take a clause as object) to perceive; observe; noticenoun3. a brief casually expressed thought or opinion; observation4. notice, comment, or observation ⇒ the event passed without remark5. engraving a variant spelling of remarque
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

remarker (reˈmarker) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Old French remarquer to observe, from re- + marquer to note, mark1

remark

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪmɑːʳk ) for learners of English
Word forms: remarks , remarking , remarked
Definitions
1. verbIf you remark that something is the case, you say that it is the case. [V that] ⇒ I remarked that I would go shopping that afternoon. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Some people have more money than sense,' Winston had remarked. [V + on/upon] ⇒ On several occasions she had remarked on the boy's improvement. [V on/upon n/wh] ⇒ Everyone has remarked on what a lovely lady she is.2. countable nounIf you make a remark about something, you say something about it. [+ about] ⇒ She has made outspoken remarks about the legalisation of cannabis in Britain. ⇒ Geoff Cooke's recent remark that no one is indispensable will certainly not have escaped him.
