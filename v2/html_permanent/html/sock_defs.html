sock1

(sɒk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a cloth covering for the foot, reaching to between the ankle and knee and worn inside a shoe2. an insole put in a shoe, as to make it fit better3. a light shoe worn by actors in ancient Greek and Roman comedy, sometimes taken to allude to comic drama in general (as in the phrase sock and buskin). See buskin4. another name for windsock5. See pull one's socks up6. See put a sock in itverb7. (transitive) to provide with socks8. See socked in

Word Origin

Old English socc a light shoe, from Latin soccus, from Greek sukkhos

sock2 - slang

(sɒk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (usually tr) to hit with force2. See sock it tonoun3. a forceful blow

Word Origin
