complacency

(kəmˈpleɪsənsɪ ) or complacence
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cencies or -cences1. a feeling of satisfaction, esp extreme self-satisfaction; smugness2. an obsolete word for complaisance

complacency

(kəmpleɪsənsi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounComplacency is being complacent about a situation. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ ...a worrying level of complacency about the risks of infection from AIDS. [+ on] ⇒ She warned that there was no room for complacency on inflation.
Example sentences containing 'complacency'
She explored his mouth slowly with the tips of her fingers, while she watched him with an enigmatic complacency. McCorquodale, Robin DANSVILLEHe thumped the steering wheel with his fist, cursing himself for his earlier complacency. Christina Jones TICKLED PINKHe hoped she'd interpreted it as a shrug of impotence at the impossibility of making amends, rather than as a shrug of complacency. Richard Francis PROSPECT HILL
