(dɪstræktɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is distracting, you mean that it makes it difficult for you to concentrate properly on what you are doing. off-putting ⇒ It's distracting to have someone watching me while I work.
Example sentences containing 'distracting'
She knew they were distracting her from dealing with the problem of her mother. Various LOOKING FOR THE SPARKIt's a bad habit, distracting , apt to make you preoccupied with correlations that don't exist... Malcolm, John WHISTLER IN THE DARKFrom her rough capture she still hurt, a constant, distracting gnaw. Kerr, Katharine A TIME OF WAR
