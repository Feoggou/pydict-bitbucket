headline

(ˈhɛdˌlaɪn )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: head, headinga. a phrase at the top of a newspaper or magazine article indicating the subject of the article, usually in larger and heavier typeb. a line at the top of a page indicating the title, page number, etc2. (usually plural) the main points of a television or radio news broadcast, read out before the full broadcast and summarized at the end3. See hit the headlinesverb4. (transitive) to furnish (a story or page) with a headline5. to have top billing (in)

headline

Powered by COBUILD
(hedlaɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: headlines , headlining , headlined
Definitions
1. countable nounA headline is the title of a newspaper story, printed in large letters at the top of the story, especially on the front page. ⇒ The Daily Mail has the headline 'The Voice of Conscience'. ⇒ I'm sick of reading headlines involving the Kennedys in sex scandals.2. plural nounThe headlines are the main points of the news which are read on radio or television. ⇒ I'm Claudia Polley with the news headlines.3. verb [usu passive]If a newspaper or magazine article is headlined a particular thing, that is the headline that introduces it. [be V-ed quote] ⇒ The article was headlined 'Tell us the truth'. [Also V n quote]4. verbIf someone headlines a show, they are the main performer in it. [V n] ⇒ Sir Cliff Richard headlined the event.5. →  to hit the headlines
