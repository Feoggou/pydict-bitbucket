duly

(ˈdjuːlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. in a proper or fitting manner2. at the proper time; punctually
Word origin
C14: see due, -ly2

duly

(djuːli , US duː- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [ADV before v]If you say that something duly happened or was done, you mean that it was expected to happen or was requested, and it did happen or it was done. ⇒ It was the beginning of the end and Watson duly went on to win his fourth Open Golf Championship. ⇒ Westcott appealed to Waite for an apology, which he duly received.2. adverb [ADV before v]If something is duly done, it is done in the correct way. [formal] ⇒ ...the duly elected president of the country.
