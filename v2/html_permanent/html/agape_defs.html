agape

(əˈɡeɪp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (postpositive)1. (esp of the mouth) wide open2. very surprised, expectant, or eager, esp as indicated by a wide open mouth
Word origin
C17: a-2 + gape

Agape

(ˈæɡəpɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun Christianity1. Christian love, esp as contrasted with erotic love; charity2. a communal meal in the early Church taken in commemoration of the Last Supper; love feast
Word origin
C17: Greek agapē love

agape

Powered by COBUILD
(əgeɪp )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [v-link ADJ]If you describe someone as having their mouth agape, their mouth is open very wide, often because they are very surprised by something. [written] ⇒ She stood looking at Carmen with her mouth agape.

Stan was looking round in wonder, his eyes wide and his mouth agape. James Birrell THE MANANA MANHer whole body felt agape , melting in an ecstasy of abandonment. Stewart, Michael COMPULSIONAimed right at Trish, standing there in the kitchen doorway, agape at the carnage: so pale she looked about to faint.
