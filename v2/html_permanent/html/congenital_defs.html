congenital

(kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. denoting or relating to any nonhereditary condition, esp an abnormal condition, existing at birth ⇒ congenital blindness2. informal complete, as if from birth ⇒ a congenital idiot
Derived forms
congenitally (conˈgenitally) adverbcongenitalness (conˈgenitalness) noun
Word origin
C18: from Latin congenitus born together with, from genitus born, from gignere to bear, beget

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A congenital disease or medical condition is one that a person has had from birth, but is not inherited. [medicine] ⇒ When John was 17, he died of congenital heart disease.congenitally adverb [ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ ...congenitally deaf patients.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A congenital characteristic or feature in a person is so strong that you cannot imagine it ever changing, although there may seem to be no reason for it. ⇒ He was a congenital liar and usually in debt.congenitally adverb ⇒ I admit to being congenitally lazy.
