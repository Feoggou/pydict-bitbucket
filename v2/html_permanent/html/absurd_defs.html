absurd

(əbˈsɜːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. at variance with reason; manifestly false2. ludicrous; ridiculousnoun3. See the absurd▷ See also theatre of the absurd
Derived forms
abˈsurdity or abˈsurdness nounabˈsurdly adverb
Word origin
C16: via French from Latin absurdus dissonant, senseless, from ab-1 (intensive) + surdus dull-sounding, indistinct

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is absurd, you are criticizing it because you think that it is ridiculous or that it does not make sense. [disapproval] ⇒ It is absurd to be discussing compulsory redundancy policies for teachers. ⇒ I've known clients of mine go to absurd lengths, just to avoid paying me a few pounds. ⇒ That's absurd.The absurd is something that is absurd. [formal] ⇒ Parkinson had a sharp eye for the absurd.absurdly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Prices were still absurdly low, in his opinion. ⇒ Absurdly, it makes shoppers overpay farmers to grow too much food.absurdity (æbsɜːʳdɪti )Word forms: absurdities variable noun [+ of] ⇒ I find myself growing increasingly angry at the absurdity of the situation. ⇒ ...the absurdities of fashionable childcare methods.
