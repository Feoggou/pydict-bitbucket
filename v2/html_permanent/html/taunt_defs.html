taunt1

(tɔːnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to provoke or deride with mockery, contempt, or criticism2. to tease; tantalizenoun3. a jeering remark4. archaic the object of mockery

Derived Forms

taunter (ˈtaunter) nountaunting (ˈtaunting) adjectivetauntingly (ˈtauntingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from French phrase tant pour tant like for like, rejoinder

taunt

Powered by COBUILD
(tɔːnt )
Word forms: taunts , taunting , taunted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone taunts you, they say unkind or insulting things to you, especially about your weaknesses or failures. [V n] ⇒ A gang taunted a disabled man. [V n + about] ⇒ Other youths taunted him about his clothes.Taunt is also a noun. ⇒ For years they suffered racist taunts.
