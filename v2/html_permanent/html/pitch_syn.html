Synonyms of pitch 1

pitch1

noun

  1. sports field, ground, stadium, arena, park, field of play
    • a cricket pitch
  2. tone, sound, key, frequency, timbre, modulation
    • He raised his voice to a higher pitch.
  3. level, point, degree, summit, extent, height, intensity, high point
    • Tensions have reached such a pitch in the area that the army have been called in.
  4. talk, line, patter, spiel (informal)
    • He was impressed with her hard sales pitch.
  5. throw, cast, delivery, toss, hurl, lob
    • On the second pitch, Hernandez threw a ball to the backstop.
  6. slope, angle, dip, incline, tilt, gradient, cant, steepness
    • the 45-degree pitch of the roof

verb

  1. throw, launch, cast, toss, hurl, fling, chuck (informal), sling, lob (informal), bung (British, slang), heave
    • Simon pitched the empty bottle into the lake.
  2. aim, direct, design for, mean for, level
    • I think this book is pitched at too high a level for you.
  3. fall, drop, plunge, dive, stagger, tumble, topple, plummet, fall headlong, (take a) nosedive
    • He pitched head-first over the low wall.
  4. set up, place, station, locate, raise, plant, settle, fix, put up, erect
    • He had pitched his tent in the yard.
  5. toss (about), roll, plunge, flounder, lurch, wallow, welter, make heavy weather
    • The ship was pitching and rolling as if in mid-ocean.
  • See make a pitch for something
  • See pitch in
  • See pitch into something
  • See pitch on

noun

  1. tar, asphalt, bitumen
    • The timbers of the houses were painted with pitch.
