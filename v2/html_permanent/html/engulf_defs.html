engulf

or ingulf (ɪnˈɡʌlf )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to immerse, plunge, bury, or swallow up2. (often passive) to overwhelm ⇒ engulfed by debts
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

engulfment (enˈgulfment) noun

engulf

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪngʌlf ) for learners of English
Word forms: engulfs , engulfing , engulfed
Definitions
1. verbIf one thing engulfs another, it completely covers or hides it, often in a sudden and unexpected way. [V n] ⇒ A seven-year-old boy was found dead after a landslide engulfed a block of flats. [be V-ed] ⇒ The flat is engulfed in flames.2. verbIf a feeling or emotion engulfs you, you are strongly affected by it. [V n] ⇒ ...the pain that engulfed him. [V n] ⇒ He looked around his dark, cluttered office and was engulfed by a feeling of emptiness.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
