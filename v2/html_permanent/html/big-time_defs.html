Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use big time to refer to the highest level of an activity or sport where you can achieve the greatest amount of success or importance. If you describe a person as big time, you mean they are successful and important. [informal] ⇒ He took a long time to settle in to big-time football. ⇒ ...a big-time investment banker.2. singular nounIf someone hits the big time, they become famous or successful in a particular area of activity. [informal] ⇒ Matsuoka is the first player from Japan to really hit the tennis big time. ⇒ He hit the big time with films such as Ghost and Dirty Dancing.3. adverb [ADV after v]You can use big time if you want to emphasize the importance or extent of something that has happened. [US, informal, emphasis] ⇒ Mike Edwards has tasted success big time. ⇒ They screwed things up big time. ⇒ America lost big-time.
