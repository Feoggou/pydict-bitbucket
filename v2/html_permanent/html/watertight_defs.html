watertight

(ˈwɔːtəˌtaɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. not permitting the passage of water either in or out ⇒ a watertight boat2. without loopholes ⇒ a watertight argument3. kept separate from other subjects or influences ⇒ different disciplines are often thought of in watertight compartments
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

watertightness (ˈwaterˌtightness) noun

watertight for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɔːtəʳtaɪt ) also water-tight
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething that is watertight does not allow water to pass through it, for example because it is tightly sealed. ⇒ The flask is completely watertight, even when laid on its side. ⇒ The batteries are safely enclosed in a watertight compartment.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA watertight case, argument, or agreement is one that has been so carefully put together that nobody will be able to find a fault in it. ⇒ The police had a watertight case. They even got his fingerprints from that glass cabinet. ⇒ ...a legally watertight agreement.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
