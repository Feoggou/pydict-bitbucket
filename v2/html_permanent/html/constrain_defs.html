constrain

(kənˈstreɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to compel or force, esp by persuasion, circumstances, etc; oblige2. to restrain by or as if by force; confine
Derived forms
constrainer (conˈstrainer) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French constreindre, from Latin constringere to bind together, from stringere to bind

(kənstreɪn )
Word forms: constrains , constraining , constrained
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo constrain someone or something means to limit their development or force them to behave in a particular way. [formal] [be V-ed] ⇒ Women are too often constrained by family commitments and by low expectations. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ How can we produce top-class engineers when universities are constrained to offer salaries that can only attract mediocre staff? [V n] ⇒ It's the capacity of those roads which is going to constrain the amount of travel by car that can take place.constrained graded adjective ⇒ These will be very constrained budgets designed to get the deficit down.2. →  feel constrained
