attached

(əˈtætʃt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (foll by to) fond (of); full of regard (for) ⇒ he was very attached to the old lady2. married, engaged, or associated in an exclusive sexual relationship ⇒ it's no good dancing with her, she's already attached

attached

Powered by COBUILD
(ətætʃt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are attached to someone or something, you like them very much. [+ to] ⇒ She is very attached to her family and friends.2. adjectiveIf someone is attached to an organization or group of people, they are working with them, often only for a short time. [+ to] ⇒ Ford was attached to the battalion's first line of transport.3. adjectiveIf one organization or institution is attached to a larger organization, it is part of that organization and is controlled and run by it. [+ to] ⇒ At one time the schools were mainly attached to the church. ⇒ ...a think-tank, attached to the Academy of Sciences.
