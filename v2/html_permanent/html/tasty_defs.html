tasty

(ˈteɪstɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: tastier or tastiest1. having a pleasant flavour2. British informal attractive: used chiefly by men when talking of women3. British informal skilful or impressive ⇒ she was a bit tasty with a cutlass4. New Zealand (of cheddar cheese) having a strong flavour

Derived Forms

tastily (ˈtastily) adverbtastiness (ˈtastiness) noun

(teɪsti )
Word forms: tastier , tastiest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that food, especially savoury food, is tasty, you mean that it has a fairly strong and pleasant flavour which makes it good to eat. ⇒ Try this tasty dish for supper with a crispy salad. ⇒ I thought the food was very tasty.2. graded adjectiveIf someone describes a person, especially a woman, as tasty, they think they are sexually desirable. [British, informal]
