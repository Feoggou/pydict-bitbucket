expense

(ɪkˈspɛns )
Definitions
noun1. a particular payment of money; expenditure2. money needed for individual purchases; cost; charge3. (plural) incidental money spent in the performance of a job, commission, etc, usually reimbursed by an employer or allowable against tax4. something requiring money for its purchase or upkeep ⇒ the car was more of an expense than he had expected5. See at the expense ofverb6. (transitive) US and Canadian to treat as an expense for book-keeping or tax purposes
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin expēnsa, from Latin expēnsus weighed out; see expend
