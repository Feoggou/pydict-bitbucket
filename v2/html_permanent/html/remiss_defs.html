remiss

(rɪˈmɪs )
Definitions
adjective (postpositive)1. lacking in care or attention to duty; negligent2. lacking in energy; dilatory
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

remissly (reˈmissly) adverbremissness (reˈmissness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin remissus from remittere to release, from re- + mittere to send

Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone is remiss, they are careless about doing things which ought to be done. [formal] negligent ⇒ I would be remiss if I did not do something about it. [Also + in]
