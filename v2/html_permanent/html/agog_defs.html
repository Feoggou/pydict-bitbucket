agog

(əˈɡɒɡ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(postpositive) highly impatient, eager, or curious
Word origin
C15: perhaps from Old French en gogues in merriments, origin unknown

agog

Powered by COBUILD
(əgɒg )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are agog, you are excited about something, and eager to know more about it. ⇒ The city was agog with rumours last night that the two had been executed.
Example sentences containing 'agog'
After the barren hinterlands of Hertfordshire, I looked on, agog. Alex George LOVE YOU MADLYPine had his arm round Molly and they sat agog as I explained how she came to end up at St Pete's. James Birrell THE MANANA MANOh well, I thought, here we go, this should have Sir Richard and Jeremy all agog once again.
