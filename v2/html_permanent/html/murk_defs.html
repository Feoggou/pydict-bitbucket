murk1

or mirk (mɜːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. gloomy darknessadjective2. an archaic variant of murky

Word Origin

C13: probably from Old Norse myrkr darkness; compare Old English mirce dark

murk2

(mɜːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive) slang1. to murder (a person)2. to defeat (a team) convincingly

Word Origin

C20: of unknown origin

murk

Powered by COBUILD
(mɜːʳk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular nounThe murk is darkness, dark water, or thick mist that is very difficult to see through. ⇒ All of a sudden a tall old man in a black cloak loomed out of the murk.
Example sentences containing 'murk'
The rainfall had mellowed to a drizzle, the murk was melting away; he could even see lights glimmering in the valley. Clive Barker SACRAMENTTwo black, piggy little eyes gleamed out from the murk inside. Barnard, Robert THE DISPOSAL OF THE LIVINGThe shape of the latter really unnerved me: like an iron mantis crouching in the murk. Pritchard, John NIGHT SISTERSThey rode in silence and were doubly alert, for they knew they could be easily surprised from the murk that surrounded them. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
