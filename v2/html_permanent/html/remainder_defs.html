remainder

(rɪˈmeɪndə )
Definitions
noun1. a part or portion that is left, as after use, subtraction, expenditure, the passage of time, etc ⇒ the remainder of the milk, ⇒ the remainder of the day2. mathematicsa. the amount left over when one quantity cannot be exactly divided by another ⇒ for 10 ÷ 3, the remainder is 1b. another name for difference (sense 7b)3. property law a future interest in property; an interest in a particular estate that will pass to one at some future date, as on the death of the current possessor4. a number of copies of a book left unsold when demand slows or ceases, which are sold at a reduced price by the publisherverb5. (transitive) to sell (copies of a book) as a remainder
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Anglo-French, from Old French remaindre (infinitive used as noun), variant of remanoir; see remain

remainder

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪmeɪndəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: remainders, remaindering, remaindered
Definitions
1. quantifierThe remainder of a group are the things or people that still remain after the other things or people have gone or have been dealt with. [+ of] ⇒ He gulped down the remainder of his coffee. ⇒ I spent the remainder of the day feeling terrible.Remainder is also a pronoun. ⇒ Only 5.9 per cent of the area is now covered in trees. Most of the remainder is farmland. ⇒ Of the 59 committee members, 40 were against legalisation, 13 were in favour and the remainder were undecided.2. singular noun [the N]In arithmetic, the remainder is the amount that remains when one amount cannot be exactly divided by another. For example, if you divide 22 by 7, the answer is 3 and the remainder is 1.3. verb [usu passive]If a book is remaindered, it is sold at a reduced price because it has not been selling very well and will not be published again. [be V-ed] ⇒ It failed to sell and was soon remaindered.4. countable nounA remainder is a book that has been remaindered.
