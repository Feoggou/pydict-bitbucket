rill

(rɪl )
Definitions
noun1. a brook or stream; rivulet2. a small channel or gulley, such as one formed during soil erosion3. Also: rille one of many winding cracks on the moon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Low German rille; related to Dutch ril
Example sentences containing 'rill'
He returned to the bank, close to where the rill from the spring trickled out into the River. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSSoon a rill of nightingalelike notes rose into the quiet afternoon. Elizabeth Lowell AMBERBEACH
