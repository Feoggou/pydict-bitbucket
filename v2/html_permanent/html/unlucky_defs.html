unlucky

(ʌnˈlʌkɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by misfortune or failure ⇒ an unlucky person, ⇒ an unlucky chance2. ill-omened; inauspicious ⇒ an unlucky date3. regrettable; disappointing4. British dialect causing trouble; mischievous
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unluckily (unˈluckily) adverbunluckiness (unˈluckiness) noun

unlucky for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnlʌki )
Word forms: unluckier , unluckiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ to-inf]If someone is unlucky, they have bad luck. ⇒ Owen was unlucky not to score on two occasions. ⇒ Others were unlucky victims of falling debris.2. graded adjective & adjectiveYou can use unlucky to describe unpleasant things which happen to someone, especially when you feel that the person does not deserve them. ⇒ ...Argentina's unlucky defeat by Ireland.3. graded adjective & adjectiveUnlucky is used to describe something that is thought to cause bad luck. ⇒ Some people think it is unlucky to look at a new moon through glass.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
