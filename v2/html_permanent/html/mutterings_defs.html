mutterings

(ˈmʌtərɪŋz)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural nouncriticisms, complaints, or scepticism about someone's ability to do something ⇒ He will lead the party into the election, in spite of the mutterings about his leadership.
Example sentences containing 'mutterings'
It was unavoidable that there were mutterings that I had had him murdered in vengeance for old wrongs. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANRoote ended up in hospital with his wrists slashed and there were mutterings about police harassment. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEAD
