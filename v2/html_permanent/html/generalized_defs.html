generalized

or generalised (ˈdʒɛnrəˌlaɪzd)
Definitions
adjective1. general; non-specific ⇒ a generalized discussion about admirable singers, ⇒ generalized feelings of inadequacy2. medicine affecting the whole body ⇒ She experienced an increase in generalized aches and pains.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

generalized

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒenrəlaɪzd ) for learners of English
Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, also use generalised1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Generalized means involving many different things, rather than one or two specific things. ⇒ ...a generalised discussion about admirable singers. ⇒ ...generalised feelings of inadequacy.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use generalized to describe medical conditions or problems which affect the whole of someone's body, or the whole of a part of their body. [medicine] OPPOSITE: localized ⇒ She experienced an increase in generalized aches and pains. ⇒ ...generalised muscle disorders.
