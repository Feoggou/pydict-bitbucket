adroit

(əˈdrɔɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. skilful or dexterous2. quick in thought or reaction
Derived forms
adroitly (aˈdroitly) adverbadroitness (aˈdroitness) noun
Word origin
C17: from French à droit according to right, rightly

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is adroit is quick and skilful in their thoughts, behaviour, or actions. ⇒ She is a remarkably adroit and determined politician.adroitly graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He drove adroitly.adroitness uncount noun ⇒ He governed with an adroitness that gained him the nickname 'the old fox'.
