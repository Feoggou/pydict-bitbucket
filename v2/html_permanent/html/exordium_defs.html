exordium

(ɛkˈsɔːdɪəm )
Definitions
noun plural -diums or -dia (-dɪə)an introductory part or beginning, esp of an oration or discourse
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exordial (exˈordial) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin, from exōrdīrī to begin, from ōrdīrī to begin
Example sentences containing 'exordium'
He'd explained his dilemma without any exordium of confidentiality. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEAD

Similar Words

introduction, opening, beginning, prelude, preface, preamble, foreword, prologue, opening remarks, proem, prolegomenon
