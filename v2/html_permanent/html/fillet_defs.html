fillet

(ˈfɪlɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a. Also called: fillet steak a strip of boneless meat, esp the undercut of a sirloin of beefb. the boned side of a fishc. the white meat of breast and wing of a chicken2. a narrow strip of any material3. a thin strip of ribbon, lace, etc, worn in the hair or around the neck4. a narrow flat moulding, esp one between other mouldings5. a narrow band between two adjacent flutings on the shaft of a column6. Also called: fillet weld a narrow strip of welded metal of approximately triangular cross-section used to join steel members at right angles7. heraldry a horizontal division of a shield, one quarter of the depth of the chief8. Also called: listel, list the top member of a cornice9. anatomy a band of sensory nerve fibres in the brain connected to the thalamus. Technical name: lemniscus10. a. a narrow decorative line, impressed on the cover of a bookb. a wheel tool used to impress such lines11. another name for fairingverb -lets, -leting or -leted (transitive)12. to cut or prepare (meat or fish) as a fillet13. to cut fillets from (meat or fish)14. anatomy to surgically remove a bone from (part of the body) so that only soft tissue remains15. to bind or decorate with or as if with a fillet
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French filet, from fil thread, from Latin fīlum

fillet

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪlɪt , US fɪleɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: fillets , filleting , filleted
Definitions
1. variable noun [oft N n]Fillet is a strip of meat, especially beef, that has no bones in it. ⇒ ...fillet of beef with shallots. ⇒ ...chicken breast fillets. ⇒ ...fillet steak.2. countable nounA fillet of fish is the side of a fish with the bones removed. ⇒ ...anchovy fillets. ⇒ I ordered a fine fillet of salmon.3. verbWhen you fillet fish or meat, you prepare it by taking the bones out. [V n] ⇒ Don't be afraid to ask your fishmonger to fillet flat fish.
