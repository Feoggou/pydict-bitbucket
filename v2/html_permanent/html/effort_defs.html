effort

(ˈɛfət )
Definitions
noun1. physical or mental exertion, usually considerable when unqualified ⇒ the rock was moved with effort2. a determined attempt ⇒ our effort to save him failed3. achievement; creation ⇒ a great literary effort4. physics an applied force acting against inertia
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Old French esfort, from esforcier to force, ultimately from Latin fortis strong; see force1

effort

Powered by COBUILD
(efəʳt ) for learners of English
Word forms: efforts
Definitions
1. variable noun [oft N to-inf]If you make an effort to do something, you try very hard to do it. ⇒ He made no effort to hide his disappointment. ⇒ Finding a cure requires considerable time and effort. ⇒ ...his efforts to reform Italian research. [+ of] ⇒ Despite the efforts of the United Nations, the problem of drug traffic continues to grow. ⇒ But a concerted effort has begun to improve the quality of the urban air.2. uncount noun [usu with N, oft a N]If you say that someone did something with effort or with an effort, you mean it was difficult for them to do. [written] ⇒ She took a deep breath and sat up slowly and with great effort. ⇒ With an effort she contained her irritation.3. countable nounAn effort is a particular series of activities that is organized by a group of people in order to achieve something. ⇒ ...a famine relief effort in Angola.4. singular nounIf you say that something is an effort, you mean that an unusual amount of physical or mental energy is needed to do it. ⇒ Even carrying the camcorder while hiking in the forest was an effort.5. →  make the effort6. →  an effort of will7. →  worth the effort
