archetypal

(ˌɑːkɪˈtaɪpəl ) or archetypical (ˌɑːkɪˈtɪpɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. perfect or typical as a specimen of something2. being an original model or pattern or a prototype3. psychoanalysis of or relating to Jungian archetypes4. constantly recurring as a symbol or motif in literature, painting, etc
Derived forms
ˌarcheˈtypally or ˌarcheˈtypically adverb

Definitions

graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Someone or something that is archetypal has all the most important characteristics of a particular kind of person or thing and is a perfect example of it. [formal] ⇒ Cricket is the archetypal English game.
