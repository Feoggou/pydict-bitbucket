sup1

(sʌp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: sups, supping or supped1. (intransitive) archaic to have supper2. (transitive) obsolete to provide with supper

Word Origin

C13: from Old French soper; see sup2

sup2

(sʌp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: sups, supping or supped1. to partake of (liquid) by swallowing a little at a time2. Scottish and Northern England dialect to drinknoun3. a sip

Word Origin

Old English sūpan; related to Old High German sūfan, German saufen; see also sup1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you sup something, you drink it, especially by taking small amounts. [literary, or old-fashioned] [V n] ⇒ We supped mulled wine.2. verbIf you sup, you eat dinner in the evening. [literary, or old-fashioned] [V] ⇒ He had been invited to sup with a colleague and his wife.
