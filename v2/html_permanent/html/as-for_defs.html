as for

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseYou use as for and as to at the beginning of a sentence in order to introduce a slightly different subject that is still connected to the previous one. ⇒ I feel that there's a lot of pressure put on policemen. And as for putting guns in their hands, I don't think that's a very good idea at all. →  as
