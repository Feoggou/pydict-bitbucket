persuaded

(pəˈsweɪdɪd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveforced by the power of argument to accept something; convinced ⇒ He is not persuaded of the need for electoral reform., ⇒ I am not persuaded of the validity of the argument., ⇒ I remain persuaded that the decisions we made last year were broadly right.
Example sentences containing 'persuaded'
She persuaded her proprietor to put up a small financial prize for the winning entry, which would also be published in the paper. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEADHe understood now why the wretched diplomat could be persuaded to perjure himself. Harcourt, Palma DOUBLE DECEITIn fact, most laboratory animals could be persuaded to do almost anything for the promised reward of a simple glimpse out of the window. Stewart, Michael COMPULSION
