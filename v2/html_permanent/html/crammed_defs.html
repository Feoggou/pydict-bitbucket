crammed

(kræmd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. bursting or overflowing ⇒ Her bag was crammed with books., ⇒ The house is crammed with furniture and works of art., ⇒ Living in a divided city crammed with foreign soldiers is not a matter we joke about.2. crowded ⇒ Thousands of refugees were crammed into the church buildings.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If a place is crammed with things or people, it is full of them, so that there is hardly room for anything or anyone else. [+ with/full of] ⇒ The house is crammed with priceless furniture and works of art. ⇒ Living in a divided city crammed with foreign soldiers is not a matter we joke about.2. adjectiveIf people or things are crammed into a place or vehicle, it is full of them. [+ into] ⇒ Between two and three thousand refugees were crammed into the church buildings.
