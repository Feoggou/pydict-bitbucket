gaberdine

(ˈgæbərˌdin ; ˌ gæbərˈdin ) in American English
Definitions
noun1.  a loose coat of coarse cloth, worn in the Middle Ages and later associated with Jews2.  Chiefly British gabardine
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

earlier gawbardyne < OFr gaverdine, kind of cloak < ? MHG walvart, pilgrimage < wallen, to wander about (< IE *wē-, var. of base awē, to blow, flutter) + vart, a trip < varen, fare
