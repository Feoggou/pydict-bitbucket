unforgiving

(ˌʌnfəˈɡɪvɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. not willing to forgive; unmerciful2. (of a machine, system, etc) allowing little or no opportunity for mistakes to be corrected3. harsh and unremitting ⇒ an unforgiving and desolate landscape
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unforgiving for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnfəʳgɪvɪŋ )
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf you describe someone as unforgiving, you mean that they are unwilling to forgive other people. [formal] ⇒ He was an unforgiving man who never forgot a slight. [+ of] ⇒ He finds human foibles endearing, but is unforgiving of pretension.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a situation or activity as unforgiving, you mean that it causes a lot of people to experience great difficulty or failure, even people who deserve to succeed. ⇒ Business is a competitive activity. It is very fierce and very unforgiving.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
