pioneer

(ˌpaɪəˈnɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a colonist, explorer, or settler of a new land, region, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ a pioneer wagon2. an innovator or developer of something new3. military a member of an infantry group that digs entrenchments, makes roads, etc4. ecology the first species of plant or animal to colonize an area of bare groundverb5. to be a pioneer (in or of)6. (transitive) to initiate, prepare, or open up ⇒ to pioneer a medical programme

Word Origin

C16: from Old French paonier infantryman, from paon pawn2; see also peon1

Pioneer

(ˌpaɪəˈnɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a total abstainer from alcoholic drink, esp a member of the Pioneer Total Abstinence Association, a society devoted to abstention2. any of a series of US spacecraft that studied the solar system, esp Pioneer 10, which made the first flyby of Jupiter (1973), and Pioneer 11, which made the first flyby of Saturn (1979)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [N n]Someone who is referred to as a pioneer in a particular area of activity is one of the first people to be involved in it and develop it. [+ of/in] ⇒ ...one of the leading pioneers of British photo journalism. ⇒ What they lacked in speed those pioneer pilots made up for by flying only a few feet above the ground.2. verbSomeone who pioneers a new activity, invention, or process is one of the first people to do it. [V n] ⇒ ...Professor Alec Jeffreys, who invented and pioneered DNA tests. [V n] ⇒ The campaigns are part of American-style innovations being pioneered by the new universities. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the folk-tale writing style pioneered by Gabriel Garcia Marquez. [Also V]3. countable nounPioneers are people who leave their own country or the place where they were living, and go and live in a place that has not been lived in before. ⇒ ...abandoned settlements of early European pioneers.
