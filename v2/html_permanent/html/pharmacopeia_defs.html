pharmacopeia

(ˌfɑrməkoʊˈpiə ) or ˌpharmacoˈpoeia (ˌfɑrməkoʊˈpiə )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun1.  an authoritative book containing a list and description of drugs and medicines together with the standards established under law for their production, dispensation, use, etc.2.  Obsolete a stock of drugs
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

ˌpharmacoˈpeial adjective or ˌpharmacoˈpoeial

Word Origin

ModL < Gr pharmakopoiïa < pharmakon, drug + poiein, to make: see poet1
