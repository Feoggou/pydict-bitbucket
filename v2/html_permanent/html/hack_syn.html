Synonyms of hack 1

hack1

verb

  1. (sometimes with away) cut, chop, slash, mutilate, mangle, mangulate (Australian, slang), gash, hew, lacerate
    • He desperately hacked through the undergrowth.
    • Some were hacked to death with machetes.
  2. (informal) stand, take, bear, handle, cope with, stomach, endure, tolerate, abide, put up with (informal)
    • I can't hack all the violence
  3. (informal) cough, bark, wheeze, rasp
    • The patients splutter and hack.
  4. [a computer, phone, etc] manipulate, exploit, attack, hijack, access, spoof, bluejack, pharm, bluesnarf, phish, phreak, spear-phish

noun

  1. (informal) cough, bark, wheeze, rasp
    • smoker's hack
  2. cut, chop, slash, notch, gash
  • See be hacked off
  • See hack it
  • See hack something off

hack2

noun

  1. reporter, writer, correspondent, journalist, scribbler, contributor, hackette (derogatory), literary hack, penny-a-liner, Grub Street writer
    • tabloid hacks, always eager to find victims
  2. yes-man, lackey, toady, flunky, brown-noser (taboo & slang)
    • Party hacks from the old days still hold influential jobs.
  3. hired horse, nag, jade, crock, poor old tired horse

adjective

  1. unoriginal, pedestrian, mediocre, poor, tired, stereotyped, banal, undistinguished, uninspired
    • ill-paid lectureships and hack writing
