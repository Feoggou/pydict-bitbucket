padlock

(ˈpædˌlɒk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a detachable lock having a hinged or sliding shackle, which can be used to secure a door, lid, etc, by passing the shackle through rings or staplesverb2. (transitive) to fasten with or as if with a padlock

Word Origin

C15 pad, of obscure origin

Word forms: padlocks , padlocking , padlocked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA padlock is a lock which is used for fastening two things together. It consists of a block of metal with a U-shaped bar attached to it. One end of the bar is released by turning a key in the lock. ⇒ They had put a padlock on the door of his flat.2. verbIf you padlock something, you lock it or fasten it to something else using a padlock. [V n] ⇒ Eddie parked his cycle against a lamp post and padlocked it. [V n] ⇒ An old mailbox has been padlocked shut. [Also V n + to]
