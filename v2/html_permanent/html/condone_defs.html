condone

(kənˈdəʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to overlook or forgive (an offence)2. law (esp of a spouse) to pardon or overlook (an offence, usually adultery)
Derived forms
condonable (conˈdonable) adjectivecondonation (ˌkɒndəʊˈneɪʃən ) nouncondoner (conˈdoner) noun
Word origin
C19: from Latin condōnāre to remit a debt, from com- (intensive) + dōnāre to donate

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone condones behaviour that is morally wrong, they accept it and allow it to happen. [V n] ⇒ I have never encouraged nor condoned violence. [V n] ⇒ I couldn't condone what she was doing.
