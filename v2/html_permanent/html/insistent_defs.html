insistent

(ɪnˈsɪstənt )
Definitions
adjective1. making continual and persistent demands2. demanding notice or attention; compelling ⇒ the insistent cry of a bird
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

insistence (inˈsistence) or insistency (inˈsistency) nouninsistently (inˈsistently) adverb

insistent

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnsɪstənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ that]Someone who is insistent keeps insisting that a particular thing should be done or is the case. ⇒ Stalin was insistent that the war would be won and lost in the machine shops. ⇒ He is most insistent on this point. ⇒ 'When can I see him?' Her tone was insistent now. [Also + on]insistently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'What is it?' his wife asked again, gently but insistently.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn insistent noise or rhythm keeps going on for a long time and holds your attention. ⇒ ...the insistent rhythms of the Caribbean and Latin America.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
