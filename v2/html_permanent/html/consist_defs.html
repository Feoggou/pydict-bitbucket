consist

(kənˈsɪst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (foll by of) to be composed (of); be formed (of) ⇒ syrup consists of sugar and water2. (foll by in or of) to have its existence (in); lie (in); be expressed (by) ⇒ his religion consists only in going to church3. to be compatible or consistent; accord
Word origin
C16: from Latin consistere to halt, stand firm, from sistere to stand, cause to stand; related to stāre to stand

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbSomething that consists of particular things or people is formed from them. be made up of [V + of] ⇒ Breakfast consisted of porridge served with butter. [V of n/-ing] ⇒ Her crew consisted of children from Devon and Cornwall.2. verbSomething that consists in something else has that thing as its main or only part. [V + in] ⇒ His work as a consultant consisted in advising foreign companies on the siting of new factories. [V in n/-ing] ⇒ ...Baudelaire's idea that genius consists in the ability to summon up childhood.
