witter

(ˈwɪtə ) informal
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive; often foll by on) to chatter or babble pointlessly or at unnecessary lengthnoun2. pointless chat; chatter
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: from dialect; compare twitter

witter for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪtəʳ )
Word forms: witters , wittering , wittered
Definitions
verbIf you say that someone is wittering about something, you mean that they are talking a lot about things that you think are silly and boring. [British, informal, disapproval] [V + about] ⇒ They just sat there wittering about what lectures they had tomorrow. [Also V]Witter on means the same as witter. [V P + about] ⇒ They started wittering on about their last trip to Provence. [Also V P]
