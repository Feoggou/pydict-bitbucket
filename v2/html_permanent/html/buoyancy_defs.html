buoyancy

(ˈbɔɪənsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the ability to float in a liquid or to rise in a fluid2. the property of a fluid to exert an upward force (upthrust) on a body that is wholly or partly submerged in it3. the ability to recover quickly after setbacks; resilience4. cheerfulness

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounBuoyancy is the ability that something has to float on a liquid or in the air. ⇒ Air can be pumped into the diving suit to increase buoyancy.2. uncount nounBuoyancy is a person's ability to remain cheerful, even in sad or unpleasant situations. ⇒ I'll have to take stock, go carefully and regain my buoyancy.3. uncount nounBuoyancy is a feeling of cheerfulness. ⇒ ...a mood of buoyancy and optimism.4. uncount nounThere is economic buoyancy when the economy is growing. ⇒ The likelihood is that the slump will be followed by a period of buoyancy.
