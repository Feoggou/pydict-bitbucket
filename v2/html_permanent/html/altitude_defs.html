altitude

(ˈæltɪˌtjuːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the vertical height of an object above some chosen level, esp above sea level; elevation2. geometry the perpendicular distance from the vertex to the base of a geometrical figure or solid3. Also called: elevation astronomy, nautical the angular distance of a celestial body from the horizon measured along the vertical circle passing through the body. Compare azimuth (sense 1)4. surveying the angle of elevation of a point above the horizontal plane of the observer5. (often plural) a high place or region
Derived forms
ˌaltitudinal (ˌaltiˈtudinal) adjective
Word origin
C14: from Latin altitūdō, from altus high, deep
