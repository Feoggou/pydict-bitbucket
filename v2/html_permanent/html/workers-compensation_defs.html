workers' compensation in American English

Definitions
1.  a government-sponsored insurance system, funded by contributions from employers, for compensating employees for injury or occupational disease suffered in connection with their employment2.  compensation given under such a system

Definitions
noun(Insurance: Life insurance) Workers' compensation is insurance in the U.S. that replaces wages and pays for medical treatment for employees who are injured while they are working. ⇒ To protect injured employees, state laws set out requirements for the assumption of workers' compensation programs. ⇒ Workers' compensation provides benefits for injury, disability, or death as a result of an occupational hazard. ⇒ Workers' compensation is insurance in the U.S. that replaces wages and pays for medical treatment for employees who are injured while they are working.
