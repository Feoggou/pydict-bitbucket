pittance

(ˈpɪtəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna small amount or portion, esp a meagre allowance of money

Word Origin

C16: from Old French pietance ration, ultimately from Latin pietās duty

pittance

(pɪtəns )
Word forms: pittances
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu sing]If you say that you receive a pittance, you are emphasizing that you get only a very small amount of money, probably not as much as you think you deserve. [emphasis] ⇒ Her secretaries work tirelessly for a pittance.
