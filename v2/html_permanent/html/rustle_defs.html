rustle1

(ˈrʌsəl )
Definitions
verb1. to make or cause to make a low crisp whispering or rubbing sound, as of dry leaves or paper2. to move with such a soundnoun3. such a sound or sounds
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rustling (ˈrustling) adjective, nounrustlingly (ˈrustlingly) adverb

Word Origin

Old English hrūxlian; related to Gothic hrukjan to crow2, Old Norse hraukr raven, crow1

rustle2

(ˈrʌsəl )
Definitions
verb1. mainly US and Canadian to steal (cattle, horses, etc)2. US and Canadian informal to move swiftly and energetically
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: probably special use of rustle1 (in the sense: to move with quiet sound)

rustle

Powered by COBUILD
(rʌsəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: rustles , rustling , rustled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbWhen something thin and dry rustles or when you rustle it, it makes soft sounds as it moves. [V] ⇒ The leaves rustled in the wind. [V n] ⇒ She rustled her papers impatiently. [V prep] ⇒ A snake rustled through the dry grass.Rustle is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ She sat perfectly still, without even a rustle of her frilled petticoats.rustling Word forms: rustlings variable noun ⇒ ...a rustling sound coming from beneath one of the seats. ⇒ ...the rustlings of women's dresses.2. → See also rustling► →  rustle up
