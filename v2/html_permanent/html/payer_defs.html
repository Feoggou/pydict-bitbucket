payer

(ˈpeɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who pays2. the person named in a commercial paper as responsible for its payment on redemption

Word forms: payers
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft n N]You can refer to someone as a payer if they pay a particular kind of bill or fee. For example, a mortgage payer is someone who pays a mortgage. ⇒ Lower interest rates pleased millions of mortgage payers.2. → see also ratepayer, taxpayer3. countable noun [adj N]A good payer pays you quickly or pays you a lot of money. A bad payer takes a long time to pay you, or does not pay you very much. ⇒ I have always been a good payer and have never gone into debt. ⇒ The firm was owed around £100,000 by late payers.
