chink1

(tʃɪŋk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small narrow opening, such as a fissure or crack2. See chink in one's armourverb3. (transitive) mainly US and Canadian to fill up or make cracks in
Derived forms
chinky (ˈchinky) adjective
Word origin
C16: perhaps variant of earlier chine, from Old English cine crack; related to Middle Dutch kene, Danish kin

chink

Powered by COBUILD
(tʃɪŋk )
Word forms: chinks , chinking, chinked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA chink in a surface is a very narrow crack or opening in it. [+ in] ⇒ ...a chink in the wall. [+ in] ⇒ He peered through a chink in the curtains.2. countable nounA chink of light is a small patch of light that shines through a small opening in something. [+ of] ⇒ I noticed a chink of light at the end of the corridor.3. ergative verbWhen objects chink, or you chink them, they touch each other, making a light ringing sound. clink [V] ⇒ ...cutlery chinking in the silence. [V n] ⇒ They poured out the rest of the wine and she chinked his mug.4. →  a chink in someone's armour
Example sentences containing 'chink'
The downstairs curtains were drawn but a wedge of light shone through a chink at the top. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEEvery chink in the post-war curtain spread across Germany had once again been closed. Dobbs, Michael WALL GAMESThe crack by which he had laid himself had closed together, so that not a chink could be seen. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSSomehow he couldn't fence with her today; it was as if she had found the chink in his armor, crept inside with her spider's poison. Colleen McCullough THE THORN BIRDS
