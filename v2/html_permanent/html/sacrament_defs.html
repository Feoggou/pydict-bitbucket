sacrament

(ˈsækrəmənt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an outward sign combined with a prescribed form of words and regarded as conferring some specific grace upon those who receive it. The Protestant sacraments are baptism and the Lord's Supper. In the Roman Catholic and Eastern Churches they are baptism, penance, confirmation, the Eucharist, holy orders, matrimony, and the anointing of the sick (formerly extreme unction)2. (often capital) the Eucharist3. the consecrated elements of the Eucharist, esp the bread4. something regarded as possessing a sacred or mysterious significance5. a symbol; pledge

Word Origin

C12: from Church Latin sacrāmentum vow, from Latin sacrāre to consecrate
