Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveIf you say that something is so-so, you mean that it is average in quality, rather than being very good or very bad. [informal] ⇒ Their lunch was only so-so. ⇒ Sommers performed a flute solo a few weeks ago that got so-so reviews.So-so is also an adverb. ⇒ 'How's it going?'—'So-so.'
