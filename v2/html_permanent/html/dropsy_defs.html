dropsy

(ˈdrɒpsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. pathology a condition characterized by an accumulation of watery fluid in the tissues or in a body cavity2. slang a tip or bribe
Derived forms
dropsical (ˈdrɒpsɪkəl ) or dropsied (ˈdropsied) adjectivedropsically (ˈdropsically) adverb
Word origin
C13: shortened from ydropesie, from Latin hydrōpisis, from Greek hudrōps, from hudōr water
