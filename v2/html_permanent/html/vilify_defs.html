vilify

(ˈvɪlɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to revile with abusive or defamatory language; malign ⇒ he has been vilified in the tabloid press2. rare to make vile; debase; degrade
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vilification (ˌvɪlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ) nounvilifier (ˈviliˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin vīlificāre, from Latin vīlis worthless + facere to make

(vɪlɪfaɪ ) for learners of English

Definitions
verbIf you are vilified by someone, they say or write very unpleasant things about you, so that people will have a low opinion of you. [formal] [be V-ed + for] ⇒ The agency has been vilified by some doctors for being unnecessarily slow to approve life-saving drugs. [be V-ed] ⇒ He was vilified, hounded, and forced into exile by the FBI. [Also V n, V n + as]vilification (vɪlɪfɪkeɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ Clare did not deserve the vilification she had been subjected to.
