impersonal

(ɪmˈpɜːsənəl )
Definitions
adjective1. without reference to any individual person; objective ⇒ an impersonal assessment2. devoid of human warmth or sympathy; cold ⇒ an impersonal manner3. not having human characteristics ⇒ an impersonal God4. grammar (of a verb) having no logical subject. Usually in English the pronoun it is used in such cases as a grammatical subject, as for example in It is raining5. grammar (of a pronoun) not denoting a person

impersonal

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmpɜːʳsənəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a place, organization, or activity as impersonal, you mean that it is not very friendly and makes you feel unimportant because it involves or is used by a large number of people. [disapproval] ⇒ Before then many children were cared for in large impersonal orphanages. ⇒ The health service has been criticized for being too impersonal.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as impersonal, you mean that they do not show any emotion about the person they are dealing with. dispassionate ⇒ We must be as impersonal as a surgeon with his knife. ⇒ I gave Coe an impersonal stare.impersonally graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The doctor treated Ted gently but impersonally.3. graded adjective & adjectiveAn impersonal room or statistic does not give any information about the character of the person to whom it belongs or relates. ⇒ The rest of the room was neat and impersonal. ⇒ History reduces the carnage to impersonal numbers.
