pantomime

(ˈpæntəˌmaɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in Britain)a. a kind of play performed at Christmas time characterized by farce, music, lavish sets, stock roles, and topical jokes. Sometimes shortened to: pantob. (as modifier) ⇒ a pantomime horse2. a theatrical entertainment in which words are replaced by gestures and bodily actions3. action without words as a means of expression4. (in ancient Rome) an actor in a dumb show5. informal, mainly British a confused or farcical situationverb6. another word for mime (sense 5)

Derived Forms

pantomimic (ˌpæntəˈmɪmɪk ) or pantomimical (ˌpæntəˈmɪmɪkəl) adjectiveˌpantomimically (ˌpantoˈmimically) adverbpantomimist (ˈpæntəˌmaɪmɪst ) noun

Word Origin

C17: via Latin from Greek pantomīmos; see panto-, mime

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA pantomime is a funny musical play for children. Pantomimes are usually based on fairy stories and are performed at Christmas. [British]2. uncount nounPantomime is the form of entertainment which involves producing a pantomime. [British] ⇒ What she does very well is pantomime. ⇒ He is currently starring in pantomime in Weston-super-Mare.3. uncount nounPantomime is acting something out without speaking. mime ⇒ Chaplin feared that the art of pantomime was under threat.4. singular nounIf you say that a situation or a person's behaviour is a pantomime, you mean that it is silly or exaggerated and that there is something false about it. [mainly British] [+ of] ⇒ They were made welcome with the usual pantomime of exaggerated smiles and gestures. ⇒ The rights of every American to good government have been damaged by the pantomime on Capitol Hill.
