weakness

(ˈwiːknɪs )
Definitions
noun1. the state or quality of being weak2. a deficiency or failing, as in a person's character3. a self-indulgent fondness or liking ⇒ a weakness for chocolates
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

weakness for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wiːknəs )
Word forms: weaknesses
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]If you have a weakness for something, you like it very much, although this is perhaps surprising or undesirable. [+ for] ⇒ Stephen himself had a weakness for cats. ⇒ His one weakness, apart from aeroplanes, is ice cream.2. → See also weak
