sanity

(ˈsænɪtɪ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state of being sane2. good sense or soundness of judgment

Word Origin

C15: from Latin sānitās health, from sānus healthy

sanity

Powered by COBUILD
(sænɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounA person's sanity is their ability to think and behave normally and reasonably. ⇒ He and his wife finally had to move from their apartment just to preserve their sanity.2. uncount nounIf there is sanity in a situation or activity, there is a purpose and a regular pattern, rather than confusion and worry. ⇒ Rafsanjani has been considering various ways of introducing some sanity into the currency market.
Example sentences containing 'sanity'
Think about work, never think about home, that way sanity lies. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPEven one more day would be too long for her to keep her sanity. Olivia Goldsmith INSIDERS
