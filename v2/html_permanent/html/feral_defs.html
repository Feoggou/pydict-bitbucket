feral1

(ˈfɪərəl , ˈfɛr-)
Definitions
adjective1. Also: ferine (of animals and plants) existing in a wild or uncultivated state, esp after being domestic or cultivated2. Also: ferine savage; brutal3. Australian derogatory, slang (of a person) tending to be interested in environmental issues and having a rugged, unkempt appearancenoun4. Australian derogatory, slang a person who displays such tendencies and appearance5. Australian slang disgusting6. Australian slang excellent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin ferālis, from Latin fera a wild beast, from ferus savage

feral

Powered by COBUILD
(ferəl , fɪər- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Feral animals are wild animals that are not owned or controlled by anyone, especially ones that belong to species which are normally owned and kept by people. [formal] ⇒ ...feral cats.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something or someone as feral, you mean that they seem wild, fierce, and uncontrolled. [literary] savage ⇒ ...the prowling, feral guitars underpinned by metronomic drum beats.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
