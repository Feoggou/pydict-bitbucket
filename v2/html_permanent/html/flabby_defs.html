flabby

(ˈflæbɪ )
Definitions
adjective -bier or -biest1. lacking firmness; loose or yielding ⇒ flabby muscles2. having flabby flesh, esp through being overweight3. lacking vitality; weak; ineffectual
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flabbily (ˈflabbily) adverbflabbiness (ˈflabbiness) noun

Word Origin

C17: alteration of flappy, from flap + -y1; compare Dutch flabbe drooping lip

flabby

Powered by COBUILD
(flæbi ) for learners of English
Word forms: flabbier , flabbiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveFlabby people are rather fat, with loose flesh over their bodies. [disapproval] ⇒ This exercise is brilliant for getting rid of flabby tums. ⇒ My bulging thighs and flabby stomach made me depressed.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as flabby, you are criticizing it for being disorganized or wasteful. [disapproval] ⇒ You hear talk about American business being flabby.
