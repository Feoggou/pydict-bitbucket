paralysis

(pəˈrælɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. pathologya. impairment or loss of voluntary muscle function or of sensation (sensory paralysis) in a part or area of the body, usually caused by a lesion or disorder of the muscles or the nerves supplying themb. a disease characterized by such impairment or loss; palsy2. cessation or impairment of activity ⇒ paralysis of industry by strikes

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek paralusis; see para-1, -lysis

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounParalysis is the loss of the ability to move and feel in all or part of your body. [+ of] ⇒ ...paralysis of the leg.2. uncount nounParalysis is the state of being unable to act or function properly. [+ of] ⇒ The paralysis of the leadership leaves the army without its supreme command. ⇒ ...a kind of mental paralysis.
