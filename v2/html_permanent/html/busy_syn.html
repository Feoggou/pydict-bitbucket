busy

adjective

  1. active, efficient, brisk, hard-pressed, tireless, diligent, industrious, hardworking, assiduous, rushed off your feet, BY (text messaging) • He's a very busy man. OPPOSITES:  relaxed, lazy, idle, inactive, indolent, lackadaisical, slothful, shiftless
  2. occupied with, working, engaged in, on duty, employed in, preoccupied with, absorbed in, immersed in, hard at work, engrossed in, in harness, on active service, hard at work on, BY (text messaging) • Life is what happens to you while you're busy making other plans. OPPOSITES:  off duty, unoccupied
  3. hectic, full, active, tiring, exacting, energetic, strenuous, on the go (informal), BY (text messaging) • I'd had a busy day and was rather tired.
  4. crowded, full, packed, crushed, mobbed, cramped, bustling, swarming, overflowing, thronged, teeming, congested, populous, jam-packed, BY (text messaging) • The ward was busy and Amy hardly had time to talk.
  • See busy yourself
