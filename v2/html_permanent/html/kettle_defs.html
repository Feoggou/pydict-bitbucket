kettle

(ˈkɛtəl )
Definitions
noun1. a metal or plastic container with a handle and spout for boiling water2. any of various metal containers for heating liquids, cooking fish, etc3. a large metal vessel designed to withstand high temperatures, used in various industrial processes such as refining and brewing4. British informal an enclosed space formed by a police cordon in order to contain people involved in a public demonstration 5. short for kettle holeverb6. (transitive) British informal (of a police force) to contain (people involved in a public demonstration) in an enclosed space
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse ketill; related to Old English cietel kettle, Old High German kezzil; all ultimately from Latin catillus a little pot, from catīnus pot

kettle for learners of English

(ketəl )
Word forms: kettles
Definitions
1. countable nounA kettle is a covered container that you use for boiling water. It has a handle, and a spout for the water to come out of. [mainly British] ⇒ I'll put the kettle on and make us some tea.A kettle of water is the amount of water contained in a kettle. [+ of] ⇒ Pour a kettle of boiling water over the onions.regional note:   in AM, use teakettle2. countable nounA kettle is a metal pot for boiling or cooking things in. [mainly US] ⇒ Put the meat into a small kettle.regional note:   in BRIT, use pan3. →  a different kettle of fish
