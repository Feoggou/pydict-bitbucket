immature

(ˌɪməˈtjʊə , -ˈtʃʊə)
Definitions
adjective1. not fully grown or developed2. deficient in maturity; lacking wisdom, insight, emotional stability, etc3. geography a less common term for youthful (sense 4)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

immaturity (ˌimmaˈturity) or immatureness (ˌimmaˈtureness) nounimmaturely (ˌimmaˈturely) adverb

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmətjʊəʳ , US -tʊr ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething or someone that is immature is not yet completely grown or fully developed. ⇒ The birds were in immature plumage. ⇒ She is emotionally immature.immaturity (ɪmətjʊərɪti , US -tʊr-) uncount noun ⇒ In spite of some immaturity in the figure drawing and painting, it showed real imagination.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as immature, you are being critical of them because they do not behave in a sensible or responsible way. [disapproval] ⇒ ...grossly immature drivers who flout the rules of the road. ⇒ She's just being childish and immature.immaturity uncount noun ⇒ ...his immaturity and lack of social skills.
