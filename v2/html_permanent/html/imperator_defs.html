imperator

(ˌɪmpəˈrɑːtɔː )
Definitions
noun1. a. (in imperial Rome) a title of the emperorb. (in republican Rome) a temporary title of honour bestowed upon a victorious general2. a less common word for emperor
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

imperatorial (ɪmˌpɛrəˈtɔːrɪəl ) adjectiveimperatorially (imˌperaˈtorially) adverbimperatorship (ˌimpeˈratorˌship) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin: commander, from imperāre to command
