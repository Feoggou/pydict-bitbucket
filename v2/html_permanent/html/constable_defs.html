constable

(ˈkʌnstəbəl , ˌkɒn-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in Britain, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, etc) a police officer of the lowest rank2. any of various officers of the peace, esp one who arrests offenders, serves writs, etc3. the keeper or governor of a royal castle or fortress4. (in medieval Europe) the chief military officer and functionary of a royal household, esp in France and England5. an officer of a hundred in medieval England, originally responsible for raising the military levy but later assigned other administrative duties
Derived forms
constableˌship (ˈconstableˌship) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French, from Late Latin comes stabulī officer in charge of the stable, from Latin comes comrade + stabulum dwelling, stable; see also count2
