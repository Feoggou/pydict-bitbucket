mimic

(ˈmɪmɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -ics, -icking or -icked (transitive)1. to imitate (a person, a manner, etc), esp for satirical effect; ape ⇒ known mainly for his ability to mimic other singers2. to take on the appearance of; resemble closely ⇒ certain flies mimic wasps3. to copy closely or in a servile mannernoun4. a person or an animal, such as a parrot, that is clever at mimicking5. an animal that displays mimicryadjective6. of, relating to, or using mimicry; imitative7. simulated, make-believe, or mock

Derived Forms

mimicker (ˈmimicker) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin mīmicus, from Greek mimikos, from mimos mime

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you mimic the actions or voice of a person or animal, you imitate them, usually in a way that is meant to be amusing or entertaining. [V n] ⇒ He could mimic anybody. [V n] ⇒ He mimicked her upper-class accent.2. verbIf someone or something mimics another person or thing, they try to be like them. [V n] ⇒ Don't try to mimic anybody. You have to be yourself if you are going to do your best. [V n] ⇒ The computer doesn't mimic human thought; it reaches the same ends by different means.3. countable nounA mimic is a person who is able to mimic people or animals. impressionist
