omnibus

(ˈɒmnɪˌbʌs , -bəs)
Definitions
noun plural -buses1. a less common word for bus (sense 1)2. Also called: omnibus volume a collection of works by one author or several works on a similar topic, reprinted in one volume3. Also called: omnibus edition a television or radio programme consisting of two or more programmes broadcast earlier in the weekadjective4. (prenominal) of, dealing with, or providing for many different things or cases
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Latin, literally: for all, from omnis all

omnibus for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒmnɪbʌs )
Word forms: omnibuses
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu N n]An omnibus edition of a radio or television programme contains two or more similar programmes that were originally broadcast separately. [British] ⇒ I enjoy the omnibus edition of Eastenders on Sunday.2. countable nounAn omnibus is a book which contains a large collection of stories or articles, often by a particular person or about a particular subject. ⇒ ...a new omnibus edition of three Ruth Rendell chillers.3. countable nounAn omnibus is a bus. [old-fashioned]
