(kənsɪdərɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. prepositionYou use considering to indicate that you are thinking about a particular fact when making a judgment or giving an opinion. ⇒ He must be hoping, but considering the situation in June he may hoping for too much too soon. ⇒ The former hostage is in remarkably good shape considering his ordeal.2. →  considering that3. adverbWhen you are giving an opinion or making a judgment, you can use considering to suggest that you have thought about all the circumstances, and often that something has succeeded in spite of these circumstances. [spoken] all things considered ⇒ I think you're pretty safe, considering.
