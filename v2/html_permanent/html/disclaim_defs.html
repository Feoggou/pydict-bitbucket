disclaim

(dɪsˈkleɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to deny or renounce (any claim, connection, etc)2. (transitive) to deny the validity or authority of3. law to renounce or repudiate (a legal claim or right)
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you disclaim knowledge of something or disclaim responsibility for something, you say that you did not know about it or are not responsible for it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ She disclaims any knowledge of her husband's business. [V n] ⇒ The government has disclaimed responsibility for the deaths of six people.
