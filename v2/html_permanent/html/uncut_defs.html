uncut

(ʌnˈkʌt )
Definitions
adjective1. (of a book) not having the edges of its pages trimmed or slit2. (of a gemstone) not cut and faceted3. not abridged or shortened
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

uncut for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnkʌt )
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething that is uncut has not been cut. ⇒ ...a patch of uncut grass. ⇒ Trees were to be left uncut, roads unpaved.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]An uncut book, play, or film has not had parts removed. ⇒ We saw the uncut version of 'Caligula' when we were in Europe.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Uncut diamonds and other precious stones have not been cut into a regular shape.
