serf

(sɜːf )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun(esp in medieval Europe) an unfree person, esp one bound to the land. If his lord sold the land, the serf was passed on to the new landlord

Derived Forms

serfdom (ˈserfdom) or serfhood (ˈserfhood) nounserfˌlike (ˈserfˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French, from Latin servus a slave; see serve
