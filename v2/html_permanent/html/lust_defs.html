lust

(lʌst )
Definitions
noun1. a strong desire for sexual gratification2. a strong desire or driveverb3. (intr; often foll by after or for) to have a lust (for)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English; related to Old High German lust desire, Old Norse losti sexual desire, Latin lascīvus playful, wanton, lustful. Compare listless

(lʌst ) for learners of English
Word forms: lusts, lusting, lusted
Definitions
1. uncount nounLust is a feeling of strong sexual desire for someone. ⇒ His relationship with Angie was the first which combined lust with friendship. [+ for] ⇒ His lust for her grew until it was overpowering.2. uncount nounA lust for something is a very strong and eager desire to have it. [+ for] ⇒ It was Fred's lust for glitz and glamour that was driving them apart.► →  lust after
