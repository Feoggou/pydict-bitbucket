oleander

(ˌəʊlɪˈændə )
Definitions
nouna poisonous evergreen Mediterranean apocynaceous shrub or tree, Nerium oleander, with fragrant white, pink, or purple flowers. Also called: rosebay
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin, variant of arodandrum, perhaps from Latin rhododendron

variable nounAn oleander is an evergreen tree or shrub that has white, pink, or purple flowers. Oleanders grow in Mediterranean countries and in some parts of Asia and Australia.
