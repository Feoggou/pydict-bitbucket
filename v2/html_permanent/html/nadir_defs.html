nadir

(ˈneɪdɪə , ˈnæ-)
Definitions
noun1. the point on the celestial sphere directly below an observer and diametrically opposite the zenith2. the lowest or deepest point; depths ⇒ the nadir of despair
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, from Arabic nazīr as-samt, literally: opposite the zenith

nadir for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(neɪdɪəʳ , US -dər )
Definitions
1. singular noun [usu with poss]The nadir of something such as someone's career or the history of an organization is its worst time. [literary] [+ of] ⇒ 1945 to 1946 was the nadir of Truman's presidency.2. singular nounIn astronomy, the nadir is the point at which the sun or moon is directly below you, on the other side of the earth. Compare zenith. [technical]
