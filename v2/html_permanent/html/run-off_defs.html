run off

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. (intransitive) to depart in haste2. (transitive) to produce quickly, as copies on a duplicating machine3. to drain (liquid) or (of liquid) to be drained4. (transitive) to decide (a race) by a runoff5. (transitive) to get rid of (weight, etc) by running6. (intransitive) (of a flow of liquid) to begin to dry up; cease to run7. See run off withnoun runoff, run-off8. a. an extra race to decide the winner after a tieb. a contest or election held after a previous one has failed to produce a clear victory for any one person9. that portion of rainfall that runs into streams as surface water rather than being absorbed into ground water or evaporating10. the overflow of a liquid from a container11. New Zealand grazing land for store cattle

run-off

(ˈrʌnɒf)
Definitions
noun1. politics an extra vote which is held in order to decide the winner of an election, because no-one has yet clearly won ⇒ next month's presidential runoff election, ⇒ a run-off between two candidates, ⇒ There will be a run-off between these two candidates on December 9th.2. sport an extra contest which is held in order to decide the winner of a competition, because no-one has yet clearly won ⇒ He claimed the title in a run-off., ⇒ He claimed the title after beating the American Sean Moran in a run-off at Bradford.3. meteorology rainwater that forms a stream rather than being absorbed by the ground ⇒ storm run-off, ⇒ The sewers collected sewage and storm run-off and discharged it, untreated, into the harbour.. See also agricultural run-off
