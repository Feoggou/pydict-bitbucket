senses

(ˈsɛnsɪz)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. the physical abilities of sight, smell, hearing, touch, and taste ⇒ She stared at him again, unable to believe the evidence of her senses.2. sound practical judgment or intelligence ⇒ Have you taken leave of your senses?3. See come to one's senses4. See come to one's senses/bring someone to their senses5. See have taken leave of one's senses
Example sentences containing 'senses'
Her senses sharpened, pupils expanding, nostrils keen to the scent of aniseed. Mark Burnell CHAMELEONNot only could I not see, it dulled all my other senses , too. Fraser, Anthea PRETTY MAIDS ALL IN A ROWAt this point, fortunately, Masters was brought back to his senses by the banging of a gavel. Kippax, Frank THE SCAR
