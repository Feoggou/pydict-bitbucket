conspiracy

(kənˈspɪrəsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cies1. a secret plan or agreement to carry out an illegal or harmful act, esp with political motivation; plot2. the act of making such plans in secret
Derived forms
conspirator (conˈspirator) nounconspiratorial (kənˌspɪrəˈtɔːrɪəl ) or conspiratory (conˈspiratory) adjectiveconˌspiratorially (conˌspiraˈtorially) adverb

Word forms: conspiracies
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [oft N to-inf]Conspiracy is the secret planning by a group of people to do something illegal. ⇒ Seven men, all from Bristol, admitted conspiracy to commit arson. ⇒ He believes there probably was a conspiracy to kill President Kennedy in 1963.2. countable noun [oft N to-inf]A conspiracy is an agreement between a group of people which other people think is wrong or is likely to be harmful. ⇒ It's all part of a conspiracy to dispense with the town centre all together and move everything out to Meadowhall. ⇒ There was no evidence to link the brigade to any conspiracy against Mr Bush.3. →  conspiracy of silence
