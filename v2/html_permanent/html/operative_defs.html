operative

(ˈɒpərətɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. in force, effect, or operation2. exerting force or influence3. producing a desired effect; significant ⇒ the operative word4. of or relating to a surgical procedurenoun5. a worker, esp one with a special skill6. US a private detective

operative for learners of English

(ɒpərətɪv )
Word forms: operatives
Definitions
1. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]A system or service that is operative is working or having an effect. [formal] in operation ⇒ The commercial telephone service was no longer operative. ⇒ The Youth Training Scheme was operative by the end of 1983.2. countable nounAn operative is a worker, especially one who does work with their hands. [formal] ⇒ In an automated car plant there is not a human operative to be seen.3. countable nounAn operative is someone who works for a government agency such as the intelligence service. [mainly US] ⇒ Naturally the CIA wants to protect its operatives.4. →  the operative word
