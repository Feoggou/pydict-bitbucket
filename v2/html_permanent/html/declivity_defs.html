declivity

(dɪˈklɪvɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tiesa downward slope, esp of the ground. Compare acclivity
Derived forms
declivitous (deˈclivitous) adjective
Word origin
C17: from Latin dēclīvitās, from de- + clīvus a slope, hill
Example sentences containing 'declivity'
There was not room for the two of them in the slight declivity left by the moving of the flagstone. Butterworth, Michael THE FIVE MILLION DOLLAR PRINCEFor a long time she stood on the edge staring down into the declivity. Erskine, Barbara MIDNIGHT IS A LONELY PLACEThe spring was at the bottom of a narrow declivity at a place where the sandstone met a harder formation of limestone. Hillerman, Tony THE JOE LEAPHORN MYSTERIES

Similar Words

slope, incline, descent, slant, brae (Scottish), declination
