sire

(saɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a male parent, esp of a horse or other domestic animal2. a respectful term of address, now used only in addressing a male monarch3. obsolete a man of high rankverb4. (transitive) (esp of a domestic animal) to father; beget
Word origin
C13: from Old French, from Latin senior an elder, from senex an old man

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen a male animal, especially a horse, sires a young animal, he makes a female pregnant so that she gives birth to it. [technical] [V n] ⇒ Comet also sired the champion foal out of Spinway Harvest.2. verbIf a man sires a child, he makes a woman pregnant and she gives birth to a child. [old-fashioned] father [V n] ⇒ Hunt married three women and sired 15 children.3. countable nounYour sire is your father. [old-fashioned]
