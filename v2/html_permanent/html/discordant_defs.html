discordant

(dɪsˈkɔːdənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. at variance; disagreeing2. harsh in sound; inharmonious
Derived forms
discordantly (disˈcordantly) adverb

discordant

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪskɔːʳdənt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is discordant is strange or unpleasant because it does not fit in with other things. incongruous ⇒ His agenda is discordant with ours.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA discordant sound or musical effect is unpleasant to hear.
Example sentences containing 'discordant'
As he stood there brooding, the radio came on, loudly playing a discordant love-song. Gee, Maggie LOST CHILDRENLooking over my shoulder, I saw the Elvis mirror above the cabinet, a discordant note in the otherwise classical decor in the room.
