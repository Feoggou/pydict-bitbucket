windfall

(ˈwɪndˌfɔːl )
Definitions
noun1. a piece of unexpected good fortune, esp financial gain2. something blown down by the wind, esp a piece of fruit3. mainly US and Canadian a plot of land covered with trees blown down by the wind
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

windfall for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪndfɔːl )
Word forms: windfalls
Definitions
1. countable nounA windfall is a sum of money that you receive unexpectedly or by luck, for example if you win a lottery.2. countable nounA windfall is a fruit, especially an apple, that has fallen from a tree.
