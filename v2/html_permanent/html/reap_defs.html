reap

(riːp )
Definitions
verb1. to cut or harvest (a crop), esp corn, from (a field or tract of land)2. (transitive) to gain or get (something) as a reward for or result of some action or enterprise
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reapable (ˈreapable) adjective

Word Origin

Old English riopan; related to Norwegian ripa to scratch, Middle Low German repen to card, ripple (flax)

[V n] ⇒ We are not in this to reap immense financial rewards.2. verbTo reap crops means to cut them down and gather them. [V n] ⇒ The painting depicted a group of peasants reaping a harvest of fruits and vegetables.
