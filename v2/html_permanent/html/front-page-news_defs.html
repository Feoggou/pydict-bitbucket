front-page news

(ˈfrʌntˌpeɪdʒ njuːz)
Definitions
nouna story printed on the first page of a newspaper ⇒ It was front page news here for weeks.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'front-page news'
Both her divorces made front-page news and launched her as an overnight celebrity. Pacter, Trudi YELLOW BIRDNo good purpose would be served if the unfortunate death of Miss Oates was blown up into front-page news for the Tory gutter press. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEThat afternoon Mahoney returned from a three-day flight, and it was front-page news in all the papers: The Rhodesian War is over!
