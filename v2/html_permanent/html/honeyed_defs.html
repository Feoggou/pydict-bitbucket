honeyed

or honied (ˈhʌnɪd )
Definitions
adjective poetic1. flattering or soothing2. made sweet or agreeable ⇒ honeyed words3. of, full of, or resembling honey
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

honeyedly (ˈhoneyedly) or honiedly (ˈhoniedly) adverb

honeyed

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌnid ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You can describe someone's voice or words as honeyed when they are very pleasant to listen to, especially if you want to suggest that they are insincere. ⇒ His gentle manner and honeyed tones reassured Andrew. ⇒ They could not understand how anyone could believe her honeyed words.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can describe something as honeyed when it tastes or smells of honey, or is the pale yellowish colour of honey. [literary] ⇒ I could smell the honeyed ripeness of melons and peaches. ⇒ ...a warm, honeyed light.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
