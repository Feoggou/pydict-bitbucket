despicable

(dɪˈspɪkəbəl , ˈdɛspɪk-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveworthy of being despised; contemptible; mean
Derived forms
deˌspicability (deˌspicaˈbility) or despicableness (deˈspicableness) noundespicably (deˈspicably) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin dēspicābilis, from dēspicārī to disdain; compare despise

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that a person or action is despicable, you are emphasizing that they are extremely nasty, cruel, or evil. [emphasis] contemptible, vile ⇒ The Minister said the bombing was a despicable crime.
