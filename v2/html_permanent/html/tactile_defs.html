tactile

(ˈtæktaɪl )
Definitions
adjective1. of, relating to, affecting, or having a sense of touch ⇒ a tactile organ, ⇒ tactile stimuli2. rare capable of being touched; tangible
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tactility (tækˈtɪlɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin tactilis, from tangere to touch

tactile

Powered by COBUILD
(tæktaɪl , US -təl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as tactile, you mean that they tend to touch other people a lot when talking to them. ⇒ The children are very tactile, with warm, loving natures.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething such as fabric which is tactile is pleasant or interesting to touch. ⇒ Tweed is timeless, tactile and tough.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Tactile experiences or sensations are received or felt by touch. [formal] ⇒ Babies who sleep with their parents receive much more tactile stimulation than babies who sleep in a cot. ⇒ Heat, cold, tactile and other sensations contribute to flavour.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
