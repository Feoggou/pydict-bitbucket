bishop

(ˈbɪʃəp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in the Roman Catholic, Anglican, and Greek Orthodox Churches) a clergyman having spiritual and administrative powers over a diocese or province of the Church. See also suffragan ▶ Related adjective: episcopal2. (in some Protestant Churches) a spiritual overseer of a local church or a number of churches3. a chesspiece, capable of moving diagonally over any number of unoccupied squares of the same colour4. mulled wine, usually port, spiced with oranges, cloves, etc
Word origin
Old English biscop, from Late Latin epīscopus, from Greek episkopos, from epi- + skopos watcher
