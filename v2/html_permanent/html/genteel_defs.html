genteel

(dʒɛnˈtiːl )
Definitions
adjective1. affectedly proper or refined; excessively polite2. respectable, polite, and well-bred ⇒ a genteel old lady3. appropriate to polite or fashionable society ⇒ genteel behaviour
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

genteelly (genˈteelly) adverbgenteelness (genˈteelness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from French gentil well-born; see gentle

genteel

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒentiːl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA genteel person is respectable and well-mannered, and comes or seems to come from a high social class. ⇒ It was a place to which genteel families came in search of health and quiet. ⇒ ...two maiden ladies with genteel manners and voices.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A genteel place or area is quiet and traditional, but may also be old-fashioned and dull. ⇒ ...the genteel towns of Winchester and Chichester.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
