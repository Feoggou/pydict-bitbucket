abridge

(əˈbrɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to reduce the length of (a written work) by condensing or rewriting2. to curtail; diminish3. archaic to deprive of (privileges, rights, etc)
Derived forms
aˈbridgable or aˈbridgeable adjectiveaˈbridger noun
Word origin
C14: via Old French abregier from Late Latin abbreviāre to shorten
Example sentences containing 'abridge'
To abridge a little: I worried myself into a pretty state by the autumn. O'Brian, Patrick TESTIMONIESHow can someone who wants to adopt a child do so by trying to abridge the rights of another adopted child, this child's very own uncle? Jacquelyn Mitchard A THEORY OF RELATIVITY
