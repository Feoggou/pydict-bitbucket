bide

(baɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: bides, biding, bided, bode or bided1. (intransitive) archaic or dialect to continue in a certain place or state; stay2. (intransitive) archaic or dialect to live; dwell3. (transitive) archaic or dialect to tolerate; endure4. See bide a wee5. See bide by6. See bide one's time
Word origin
Old English bīdan; related to Old Norse bītha to wait, Gothic beidan, Old High German bītan
