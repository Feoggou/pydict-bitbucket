philosophize

or philosophise (fɪˈlɒsəˌfaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to make philosophical pronouncements and speculations2. (transitive) to explain philosophically

Derived Forms

phiˌlosophization (phiˌlosophiˈzation) or phiˌlosophisation (phiˌlosophiˈsation) nounphilosoˌphizer (phiˈlosoˌphizer) or philosoˌphiser (phiˈlosoˌphiser) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use philosophiseverbIf you say that someone is philosophizing, you mean that they are talking or thinking about important subjects, sometimes instead of doing something practical. [V] ⇒ He philosophized, he admitted, not because he was certain of establishing the truth, but because it gave him pleasure. [V + about/on] ⇒ ...a tendency to philosophize about racial harmony. [Also V with quote]philosophizing uncount noun ⇒ The General was anxious to cut short the philosophizing and get down to more urgent problems.
