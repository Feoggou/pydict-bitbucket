hasty

(ˈheɪstɪ )
Definitions
adjective -tier or -tiest1. rapid; swift; quick2. excessively or rashly quick3. short-tempered4. showing irritation or anger ⇒ hasty words
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

hasty

Powered by COBUILD
(heɪsti ) for learners of English
Word forms: hastier , hastiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A hasty movement, action, or statement is sudden, and often done in reaction to something that has just happened. ⇒ One company is giving its employees airplane tickets in the event they need to make a hasty escape.hastily (heɪstɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'It may be satisfying, but it's not fun.' 'No, I'm sure it's not,' said Virginia hastily. 'I didn't mean that.'2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A hasty event or action is one that is completed more quickly than normal. ⇒ After the hasty meal, the men had moved forward to take up their positions.hastily graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He said good night hastily, promising that he would phone Hans in the morning. ⇒ The survivors were recovering in hastily erected tents.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person or their behaviour as hasty, you mean that they are acting too quickly, without thinking carefully, for example because they are angry. [disapproval] ⇒ So let's not be hasty. After all, he can't run away. ⇒ A number of the United States' allies had urged him not to take a hasty decision.hastily graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I decided that nothing should be done hastily, that things had to be sorted out carefully.
