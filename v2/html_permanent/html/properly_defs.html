(prɒpəʳli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj]If something is done properly, it is done in a correct and satisfactory way. ⇒ You're too thin. You're not eating properly. ⇒ There needs to be a properly informed public debate.2. adverb [ADV after v]If someone behaves properly, they behave in a way that is considered acceptable and not rude. ⇒ He's a spoilt brat and it's about time he learnt to behave properly. ⇒ They will be concerned to do the right thing–to dress properly, for instance.

I stood by and watched as he pushed the man's knees into his chest so the body would fit properly on the tarpaulin.
