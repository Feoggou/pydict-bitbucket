actuate

(ˈæktʃʊˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to put into action or mechanical motion2. to motivate or incite into action ⇒ actuated by unworthy desires
Derived forms
ˌactuˈation nounˈactuˌator noun
Word origin
C16: from Medieval Latin actuātus, from actuāre to incite to action, from Latin āctus act
