wash up

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. mainly British to wash (dishes, cutlery, etc) after a meal2. (intransitive) US to wash one's face and handsnoun washup3. Australian the outcome of a process ⇒ in the washup, three candidates were elected4. anything washed up by the sea5. an act of washing up6. a place for washing up7. mining the washing of ore; an amount of gold mined by washing

wash up for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you wash up, you wash the plates, cups, cutlery, and pans which have been used for cooking and eating a meal. [British] [V P] ⇒ I ran some hot water and washed up. [V P n] ⇒ I bet you make breakfast and wash up their plates, too. [Also V n P]regional note:   in AM, use wash the dishes2. phrasal verbIf you wash up, you clean part of your body with soap and water, especially your hands and face. [US] [V P] ⇒ He headed to the bathroom to wash up.regional note:   in BRIT, use wash3. phrasal verb [usu passive]If something is washed up on a piece of land, it is carried by a river or sea and left there. ⇒ Thousands of herring and crab are washed up on the beaches during every storm. [V-ed P] ⇒ The fossils appear to be an early form of seaweed washed up on a beach.4. → See also washed up, washing-up →  wash
