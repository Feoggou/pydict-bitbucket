squeal

(skwiːl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a high shrill yelp, as of pain2. a screaming sound, as of tyres when a car brakes suddenlyverb3. to utter a squeal or with a squeal4. (intransitive) slang to confess information about another5. (intransitive) informal, mainly British to complain or protest loudly

Derived Forms

squealer (ˈsquealer) noun

Word Origin

C13 squelen, of imitative origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone or something squeals, they make a long, high-pitched sound. [V + with] ⇒ Jennifer squealed with delight and hugged me. [V] ⇒ The car's tires squealed again as it sped around the corner. [Also V with quote]Squeal is also a noun. ⇒ At that moment there was a squeal of brakes and the angry blowing of a car horn.
