cilia

(ˈsɪliə )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
plural nounWord forms: singular ˈcilium (ˈsɪliəm )1.  the eyelashes2.  Botany small hairlike processes extending from certain plant cells and forming a fringe, as on the edges of some leaves3.  Zoology short, hairlike outgrowths of certain cells, usually capable of rhythmic beating that can produce locomotion and feeding currents, as in protozoans or small worms, or the movement of fluids, as in the ducts of higher forms

Word origin
L, pl. of cilium, eyelid; akin to celare: see conceal; (sense 2) ModL
