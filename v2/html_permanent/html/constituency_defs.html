constituency

(kənˈstɪtjʊənsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cies1. the whole body of voters who elect one representative to a legislature or all the residents represented by one deputy2. a. a district that sends one representative to a legislatureb. (as modifier) ⇒ constituency organization

constituency

Powered by COBUILD
(kənstɪtʃuənsi )
Word forms: constituencies
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA constituency is an area for which someone is elected as the representative in a parliament or government.2. countable nounA particular constituency is a section of society that may give political support to a particular party or politician. ⇒ Mr Jackson had a natural constituency among American blacks. ⇒ In France, farmers are a powerful political constituency.
Example sentences containing 'constituency'
With the combined backing of the Anstruther-Wetherbys and the Cynsters, you should be assured of a sound constituency. Stephanie Laurens DEVIL'S BRIDE
