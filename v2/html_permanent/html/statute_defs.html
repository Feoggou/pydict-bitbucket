statute

(ˈstætjuːt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. an enactment of a legislative body expressed in a formal documentb. this document2. a permanent rule made by a body or institution for the government of its internal affairs

Word Origin

C13: from Old French estatut, from Late Latin statūtum, from Latin statuere to set up, decree, ultimately from stāre to stand

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounA statute is a rule or law which has been made by a government or other organization and formally written down. ⇒ The new statute covers the care for, bringing up and protection of children. ⇒ The independence of the judiciary in France is guaranteed by statute.
