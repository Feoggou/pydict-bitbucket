nameless

(ˈneɪmlɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. without a name; anonymous2. incapable of being named; indescribable ⇒ a nameless horror seized him3. too unpleasant or disturbing to be mentioned ⇒ nameless atrocities4. having no legal name; illegitimate ⇒ a nameless child
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

namelessly (ˈnamelessly) adverbnamelessness (ˈnamelessness) noun

nameless for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(neɪmləs )
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You describe people or things as nameless when you do not know their name or when they do not have a name. ⇒ They can have their cases rejected, without reasons being given, by nameless officials.2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that someone or something will remain nameless, you mean that you will not mention their name, often because you do not want to embarrass them. ⇒ A local friend who shall be nameless warned me that I was in for trouble soon.
