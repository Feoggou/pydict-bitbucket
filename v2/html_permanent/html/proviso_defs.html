proviso

(prəˈvaɪzəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -sos or -soes1. a clause in a document or contract that embodies a condition or stipulation2. a condition or stipulation

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin phrase prōvīsō quod it being provided that, from Latin prōvīsus provided

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [oft N that]A proviso is a condition in an agreement. You agree to do something if this condition is fulfilled. ⇒ I told Norman I would invest in his venture as long as he agreed to one proviso. ⇒ Okay, with the proviso that Jane agrees, I accept.
