conceited

(kənˈsiːtɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having a high or exaggerated opinion of oneself or one's accomplishments2. archaic fanciful3. obsolete witty or intelligent
Derived forms
conceitedly (conˈceitedly) adverbconceitedness (conˈceitedness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is conceited, you are showing your disapproval of the fact that they are far too proud of their abilities or achievements. [disapproval] ⇒ I thought him conceited and arrogant. ⇒ You conceited idiot.
