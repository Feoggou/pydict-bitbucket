Word Frequency

votive

(ˈvəʊtɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. offered, given, undertaken, performed, or dedicated in fulfilment of or in accordance with a vow2. Roman Catholic Church optional; not prescribed; having the nature of a voluntary offering ⇒ a votive Mass, ⇒ a votive candle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

votively (ˈvotively) adverbvotiveness (ˈvotiveness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin vōtīvus promised by a vow, from vōtum a vow

The lights of the car were like red votive candles followed by two pale, swift streamers. McCorquodale, Robin DANSVILLEAnnie, who was busily arranging white votive candles into a circle in the middle of the floor, laughed. Isobel Bird CIRCLE OF THREE: BOOK 6: RING OF LIGHT
