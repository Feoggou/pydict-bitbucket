tenacity

Powered by COBUILD
(tɪnæsɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf you have tenacity, you are very determined and do not give up easily. ⇒ Talent, hard work and sheer tenacity are all crucial to career success.
Example sentences containing 'tenacity'
He also thought that that was why Tyrrel's hands gripped the manuscript with such tenacity , relinquishing it only in his last moment. Alan Judd THE DEVIL'S OWN WORKOver the next days, Helena pursued these with the tenacity she brought to her investigative journalism. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEOr until the end of the world, I thought, as she had the same sort of tenacity as James ' housekeeper. Anita Anderson SOMEBODYAfter all, it had been Annie's tenacity that had first gotten Cooper to talk to her and Kate about her own interest in Wicca. Isobel Bird CIRCLE OF THREE: BOOK 7: BLUE MOON
