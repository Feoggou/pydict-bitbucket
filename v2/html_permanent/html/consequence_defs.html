consequence

(ˈkɒnsɪkwəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a result or effect of some previous occurrence2. an unpleasant result (esp in the phrase take the consequences)3. significance or importance ⇒ it's of no consequence, ⇒ a man of consequence4. logica. a conclusion reached by reasoningb. the conclusion of an argumentc. the relations between the conclusion and the premises of a valid argument5. the relation between an effect and its cause6. See in consequence

(kɒnsɪkwens )
Word forms: consequences
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe consequences of something are the results or effects of it. [+ of] ⇒ Her lawyer said she understood the consequences of her actions and was prepared to go to jail. [+ for] ⇒ An economic crisis may have tremendous consequences for our global security.2. →  in consequence, as a consequence3. →  of (...) consequence4. →  take the consequences/face the consequences
