antipathy

(ænˈtɪpəθɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -thies1. a feeling of intense aversion, dislike, or hostility2. the object of such a feeling
Word origin
C17: from Latin antipathia, from Greek antipatheia, from anti- + patheia feeling

antipathy

Powered by COBUILD
(æntɪpəθi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounAntipathy is a strong feeling of dislike or hostility towards someone or something. [formal] ⇒ She'd often spoken of her antipathy towards London.
Example sentences containing 'antipathy'
Given Ulbricht's antipathy to Brandt, Moscow doesn't think his contribution to any negotiations is going to be positive. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSThe genesis of my neurosis was one Frenchman in particular, but as the years have passed my antipathy has spread to the whole lot of them.
