(brɔːdli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adverb & adverbYou can use broadly to indicate that something is generally true. ⇒ The President broadly got what he wanted out of his meeting. ⇒ The idea that software is capable of any task is broadly true in theory.2. → see also broad

Paul's bewilderment had given way to amusement, and he was grinning broadly. Penn, John UNTO THE GRAVEThe door was opened by Fraulein Eisermann, smiling broadly.
