illogical

(ɪˈlɒdʒɪkəl )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by lack of logic; senseless or unreasonable2. disregarding logical principles
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

illogicality (ɪˌlɒdʒɪˈkælɪtɪ ) or illogicalness (ilˈlogicalness) nounillogically (ilˈlogically) adverb

illogical

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪlɒdʒɪkəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an action, feeling, or belief as illogical, you are critical of it because you think that it does not result from a logical and ordered way of thinking. [disapproval] ⇒ It is illogical to oppose the repatriation of economic migrants. ⇒ She may think your reaction is crazy or illogical, but she cannot deny what you are feeling. ⇒ ...his completely illogical arguments.illogically (ɪlɒdʒɪkli ) graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Illogically, I felt guilty.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
