solo

(ˈsəʊləʊ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -los1. plural -los or -li (-liː) a musical composition for one performer with or without accompaniment2. any of various card games in which each person plays on his or her own instead of in partnership with another, such as solo whist3. a flight in which an aircraft pilot is unaccompanied4. a. any performance, mountain climb, or other undertaking carried out by an individual without assistance from othersb. (as modifier) ⇒ a solo attemptadjective5. music unaccompanied ⇒ a sonata for cello soloadverb6. by oneself; alone ⇒ to fly soloverb7. (intransitive) to undertake a venture alone, esp to operate an aircraft alone or climb alone

Word Origin

C17: via Italian from Latin sōlus alone, sole1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use solo to indicate that someone does something alone rather than with other people. ⇒ He had just completed his final solo album. ⇒ ...Daniel Amokachi's spectacular solo goal. ⇒ ...Mick Taylor, who had long since quit the Stones for a solo career.Solo is also an adverb. ⇒ Charles Lindbergh became the very first person to fly solo across the Atlantic.2. countable nounA solo is a piece of music or a dance performed by one person. ⇒ The original version featured a guitar solo.
