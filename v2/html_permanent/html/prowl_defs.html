prowl

(praʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when intr, often foll by around or about) to move stealthily around (a place) as if in search of prey or plundernoun2. the act of prowling3. See on the prowl

Derived Forms

prowler (ˈprowler) noun

Word Origin

C14 prollen, of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf an animal or a person prowls around, they move around quietly, for example when they are hunting. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Policemen prowled around the building. [Also V, V n]2. →  on the prowl
Example sentences containing 'prowl'
He never got up enough nerve to meet me, but he had plenty of time to prowl the hills.
