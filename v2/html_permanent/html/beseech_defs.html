beseech

(bɪˈsiːtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -seeches, -seeching, -sought or -seeched(transitive) to ask (someone) earnestly (to do something or for something); beg
Derived forms
beseecher (beˈseecher) nounbeseeching (beˈseeching) adjectivebeseechingly (beˈseechingly) adverb
Word origin
C12: see be-, seek; related to Old Frisian besēka

Powered by COBUILD
(bɪsiːtʃ )
Word forms: beseeches , beseeching , beseeched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you beseech someone to do something, you ask them very eagerly and anxiously. [literary] [V n to-inf] ⇒ She beseeched him to cut his drinking and his smoking. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Please stay and read to me, mummy' he beseeched. [Also V n, V n + for]
