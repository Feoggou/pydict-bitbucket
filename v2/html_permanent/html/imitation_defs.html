imitation

(ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act, practice, or art of imitating; mimicry2. an instance or product of imitating, such as a copy of the manner of a person; impression3. a. a copy or reproduction of a genuine article; counterfeitb. (as modifier) ⇒ imitation jewellery4. (in contrapuntal or polyphonic music) the repetition of a phrase or figure in one part after its appearance in another, as in a fugue5. a literary composition that adapts the style of an older work to the writer's own purposes
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

imitation

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmɪteɪʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: imitations
Definitions
1. countable nounAn imitation of something is a copy of it. [+ of] ⇒ ...the most accurate imitation of Chinese architecture in Europe.2. uncount nounImitation means copying someone else's actions. [+ of] ⇒ They discussed important issues in imitation of their elders. ⇒ Molly learned her golf by imitation.3. adjective [ADJ n]Imitation things are not genuine but are made to look as if they are. ⇒ ...a complete set of Dickens bound in imitation leather.4. countable nounIf someone does an imitation of another person, they copy the way they speak or behave, sometimes in order to be funny. [+ of] ⇒ He gave his imitation of Queen Elizabeth's royal wave. ⇒ I could do a pretty good imitation of him.
