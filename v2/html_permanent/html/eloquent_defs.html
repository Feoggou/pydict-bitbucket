eloquent

(ˈɛləkwənt )
Definitions
adjective1. (of speech, writing, etc) characterized by fluency and persuasiveness2. visibly or vividly expressive, as of an emotion ⇒ an eloquent yawn
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

eloquently (ˈeloquently) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin ēloquēns, from ēloquī to speak out, from loquī to speak

eloquent

Powered by COBUILD
(eləkwənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSpeech or writing that is eloquent is well expressed and effective in persuading people. ⇒ I heard him make a very eloquent speech at that dinner.eloquence uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the eloquence of his prose.eloquently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Jan speaks eloquently about her art.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA person who is eloquent is good at speaking and able to persuade people. [approval] ⇒ He was eloquent about his love of books. ⇒ ...one particularly eloquent German critic.eloquence uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ I wish I'd had the eloquence of Helmut Schmidt.
