Synonyms of rot

rot

verb

  1. decay, break down, spoil, corrupt, deteriorate, taint, perish, degenerate, fester, decompose, corrode, moulder, go bad, putrefy
    • The grain will start rotting in the silos.
  2. crumble, decay, disintegrate, perish, decompose, become rotten
    • It is not true to say that this wood never rots.
  3. deteriorate, decline, languish, degenerate, wither away, waste away
    • I was left to rot nine years for a crime I didn't commit.

noun

  1. decay, disintegration, corrosion, decomposition, corruption, mould, blight, deterioration, canker, putrefaction, putrescence
    • Investigations revealed rot in the beams.
  2. (informal) nonsense, rubbish, drivel, twaddle, malarkey, balls (taboo & slang), bull (slang), shit (taboo & slang), pants (slang), crap (slang), garbage (mainly US), trash, bunk (informal), bullshit (taboo & slang), hot air (informal), tosh (slang, mainly British), pap, cobblers (British, taboo & slang), bilge (informal), tripe (informal), guff (slang), moonshine, claptrap (informal), hogwash, hokum (slang, mainly US & & Canadian), codswallop (British, slang), piffle (informal), poppycock (informal), balderdash, bosh (informal), eyewash (informal), stuff and nonsense, flapdoodle (slang), tommyrot, horsefeathers (US, slang), bunkum or buncombe (mainly US), bizzo (Australian, slang), bull's wool (Australian & & New Zealand, slang)
    • You do talk rot!

putrid
