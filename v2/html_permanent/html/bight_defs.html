bight

(baɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a wide indentation of a shoreline, or the body of water bounded by such a curve2. the slack middle part of an extended rope3. a curve or loop in a ropeverb4. (transitive) to fasten or bind with a bight
Word origin
Old English byht; see bow2
