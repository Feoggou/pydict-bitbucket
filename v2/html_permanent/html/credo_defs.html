Credo

(ˈkriːdəʊ , ˈkreɪ-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -dos1. the Apostles' Creed or the Nicene Creed2. a musical setting of the Creed
Word origin
C12: from Latin, literally: I believe; first word of the Apostles' and Nicene Creeds

countable nounA credo is a set of beliefs, principles, or opinions that strongly influence the way a person lives or works. [formal] ⇒ Lord Clarendon's liberal credo was one of the foundations of his political conduct.
