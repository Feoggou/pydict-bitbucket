odor in American English

(ˈoʊdər )
Definitions
noun1. a.  that characteristic of a substance which makes it perceptible to the sense of smellb.  a smell, whether pleasant or unpleasant; fragrance, stench, etc.2.  a pervasive atmosphere or quality ⇒ an odor of intolerance3.  Archaic a perfume or other sweet-smelling substance be in bad odor or be in ill odor to be in ill repute
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

OFr < L < IE base *od-, to smell, odor > Gr odmē, (var. of osmē), scent, Swed os, smell, suffocating gas

odor in the Oil and Gas Industry

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊdər) or odour
Word forms: (regular plural) odors
Definitions
noun(Extractive engineering: General) An odor is a smell. ⇒ Sulfur has an unpleasant odor resembling that of rotten eggs. ⇒ Because LPG has no natural odor, an agent with a distinctive smell is added so that it will be noticeable should a leak occur. ⇒ An odor is a smell.US UK EnglishThe spelling odor is used in American English and the spelling odour is usually used in British English.
