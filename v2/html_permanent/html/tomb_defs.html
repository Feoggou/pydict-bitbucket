tomb

(tuːm )
Definitions
noun1. a place, esp a vault beneath the ground, for the burial of a corpse2. a stone or other monument to the dead3. See the tomb4. anything serving as a burial place ⇒ the sea was his tombverb5. (transitive) rare to place in a tomb; entomb
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tomblike (ˈtombˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French tombe, from Late Latin tumba burial mound, from Greek tumbos; related to Latin tumēre to swell, Middle Irish tomm hill
