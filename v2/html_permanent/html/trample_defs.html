trample

(ˈtræmpəl )
Definitions
verb (when intr, usually foll by on, upon, or over)1. to stamp or walk roughly (on) ⇒ to trample the flowers2. to encroach (upon) so as to violate or hurt ⇒ to trample on someone's feelingsnoun3. the action or sound of trampling
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

trampler (ˈtrampler) noun

Word Origin

C14: frequentative of tramp; compare Middle High German trampeln

trample

Powered by COBUILD
(træmpəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: tramples , trampling , trampled
Definitions
1. verbTo trample on someone's rights or values or to trample them means to deliberately ignore them. [V + on] ⇒ They say loggers are destroying rain forests and trampling on the rights of natives. [V n] ⇒ Diplomats denounced the leaders for trampling their citizens' civil rights. [V n] ⇒ When a tribe encounters civilization, the first things to get trampled underfoot are the religious beliefs of the tribe.2. verb [usu passive]If someone is trampled, they are injured or killed by being stepped on by animals or by other people. [be V-ed] ⇒ Many people were trampled in the panic that followed. [V-ed] ⇒ Thousands of victims perished, trampled underfoot.3. verbIf someone tramples something or tramples on it, they step heavily and carelessly on it and damage it. [V n] ⇒ They don't want people trampling the grass, pitching tents or building fires. [V n] ⇒ ...half-ripe apples that were being trampled underfoot by the fighting men. [V + on] ⇒ Please don't trample on the azaleas. [V-ed] ⇒ There was a smell of trampled grass and earth.
