joust

(dʒaʊst ) history
Definitions
noun1. a combat between two mounted knights tilting against each other with lances. A tournament consisted of a series of such engagementsverb2. (intr; often foll by against or with) to encounter or engage in such a tournament ⇒ he jousted with five opponents
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jouster (ˈjouster) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French jouste, from jouster to fight on horseback, from Vulgar Latin juxtāre (unattested) to come together, from Latin juxtā close

joust for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒaʊst )
Word forms: jousts , jousting , jousted
Definitions
1. verbWhen two or more people or organizations joust, they compete to see who is better. [literary] [V] ⇒ ...lawyers joust in the courtroom. [V + with] ⇒ The oil company jousts with Esso for lead position in U.K. sales.Joust is also a noun. ⇒ There were notable jousts with the Secretary of Commerce.2. verbIn medieval times, when two knights on horseback jousted, they fought against each other using long spears called lances. [V] ⇒ Knights joust and frolic. [Also + V with]jousting uncount noun ⇒ ...medieval jousting tournaments.
