Comment by seanogmacgearailt on 14 Jan 2014
I'm very surprised that Collins has no note attached to this controversial term, something which the OED has. It's rarely used in Ireland except to make a pro-British political point. It’s not used in agreements between the governments of Ireland and the UK. The former does not recognise it, nor do most (all?) Irish mapmakers and academics as it is viewed, correctly, as a political term in origin and use. It has been dropped by National Geographic, Michelin etc. The earliest usage in English can only be traced to John Dee in a 1577 treatise claiming Ireland for the English crown (Source: OED). From its inception then it has been a political, not geographic, term.
