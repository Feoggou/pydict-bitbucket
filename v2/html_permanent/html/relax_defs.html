relax

(rɪˈlæks )
Definitions
verb1. to make (muscles, a grip, etc) less tense or rigid or (of muscles, a grip, etc) to become looser or less rigid2. (intransitive) to take rest or recreation, as from work or effort ⇒ on Sundays, she just relaxes, ⇒ she relaxes by playing golf3. to lessen the force of (effort, concentration, etc) or (of effort) to become diminished4. to make (rules or discipline) less rigid or strict or (of rules, etc) to diminish in severity5. (intransitive) (of a person) to become less formal; unbend
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

relaxable (reˈlaxable) adjectiverelaxed (reˈlaxed) adjectiverelaxedly (rɪˈlæksɪdlɪ ) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin relaxāre to loosen, from re- + laxāre to loosen, from laxus loose, lax

relax

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪlæks ) for learners of English
Word forms: relaxes , relaxing , relaxed
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf you relax or if something relaxes you, you feel more calm and less worried or tense. [V] ⇒ I ought to relax and stop worrying about it. [V] ⇒ For the first time since his arrival he relaxed slightly. [V n] ⇒ Do something that you know relaxes you.2. ergative verb & verbWhen a part of your body relaxes, or when you relax it, it becomes less stiff or firm. [V n] ⇒ Massage is used to relax muscles, relieve stress and improve the circulation. [V] ⇒ His face relaxes into a contented smile.3. verbIf you relax your grip or hold on something, you hold it less tightly than before. [V n] ⇒ He gradually relaxed his grip on the arms of the chair.4. ergative verb & verbIf you relax a rule or your control over something, or if it relaxes, it becomes less firm or strong. [V] ⇒ Rules governing student conduct relaxed somewhat in recent years. [V n] ⇒ How much can the President relax his grip over the nation? [V n] ⇒ Some analysts believe that the government soon will begin relaxing economic controls.5. → See also relaxed, relaxing
