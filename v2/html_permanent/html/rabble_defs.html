rabble1

(ˈræbəl )
Definitions
noun1. a disorderly crowd; mob2. See the rabble
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14 (in the sense: a pack of animals): of uncertain origin; perhaps related to Middle Dutch rabbelen to chatter, rattle

rabble2

(ˈræbəl )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: rabbler an iron tool or mechanical device for stirring, mixing, or skimming a molten charge in a roasting furnaceverb2. (transitive) to stir, mix, or skim (the molten charge) in a roasting furnace
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from French râble, from Latin rutābulum rake for a furnace, from ruere to rake, dig up

rabble

Powered by COBUILD
(ræbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. singular nounA rabble is a crowd of noisy people who seem likely to cause trouble. [+ of] ⇒ He seems to attract a rabble of supporters more loyal to the man than to the cause.2. singular noun [the N]People sometimes refer to ordinary people in general as the rabble to suggest that they are superior to them. [disapproval] ⇒ In 40 years, the Guards' Polo Club has changed, but it has managed to keep most of the rabble out.
