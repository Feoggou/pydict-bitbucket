ballast

(ˈbæləst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any dense heavy material, such as lead or iron pigs, used to stabilize a vessel, esp one that is not carrying cargo2. crushed rock, broken stone, etc, used for the foundation of a road or railway track3. coarse aggregate of sandy gravel, used in making concrete4. anything that provides stability or weight5. electronics a device for maintaining the current in a circuitverb (transitive)6. to give stability or weight to
Word origin
C16: probably from Low German; related to Old Danish, Old Swedish barlast, literally: bare load (without commercial value), from bar bare, mere + last load, burden

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounBallast is any substance that is used in ships or hot-air balloons to make them heavier and more stable. Ballast usually consists of water, sand, or iron.
