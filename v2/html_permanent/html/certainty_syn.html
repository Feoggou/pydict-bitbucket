certainty

noun

  1. confidence, trust, faith, conviction, assurance, certitude, sureness, positiveness, authoritativeness, indubitableness • I have said with absolute certainty that there will be no change of policy. OPPOSITES:  doubt, uncertainty, disbelief, scepticism, qualm, indecision, unsureness
  2. inevitability • There is too little certainty about the outcome yet. OPPOSITE:  uncertainty
  3. fact, truth, reality, sure thing (informal), surety, banker • A general election became a certainty three weeks ago.

QUOTATIONS:
 In this world nothing is certain but death and taxesBenjamin Franklin If a man will begin with certainties, he shall end in doubts; but if he will be content to begin with doubts, he shall end in certainties Francis Bacon – The Advancement of Learning
