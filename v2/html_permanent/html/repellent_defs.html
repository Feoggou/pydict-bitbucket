repellent

or repellant (rɪˈpɛlənt )
Definitions
adjective1. giving rise to disgust or aversion; distasteful or repulsive2. driving or forcing away or back; repellingnoun also: repellant3. something, esp a chemical substance, that repels ⇒ insect repellent4. a substance with which fabrics are treated to increase their resistance to water
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

repellence (reˈpellence), repellance (reˈpellance), repellency (reˈpellency) or repellancy (reˈpellancy) nounrepellently (reˈpellently) or repellantly (reˈpellantly) adverb

Definitions
language note:   The spelling repellant is also used for meaning [sense 2].1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you think that something is horrible and disgusting you can say that it is repellent. [formal] repugnant, repulsive ⇒ ...a very large, very repellent toad. ⇒ She still found the place repellent.2. variable noun [usu n N]Insect repellent is a product containing chemicals that you spray into the air or on your body in order to keep insects away. ⇒ ...mosquito repellent.
