skulk

(skʌlk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to move stealthily so as to avoid notice2. to lie in hiding; lurk3. to shirk duty or evade responsibilities; malingernoun4. a person who skulks5. obsolete a pack of foxes or other animals that creep about stealthily

Derived Forms

skulker (ˈskulker) noun

Word Origin

C13: of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian skulka to lurk, Swedish skolka, Danish skulke to shirk
