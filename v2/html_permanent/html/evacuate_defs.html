evacuate

(ɪˈvækjʊˌeɪt )
Definitions
verb (mainly tr)1. (also intr) to withdraw or cause to withdraw from (a place of danger) to a place of greater safety2. to make empty by removing the contents of3. (also intr) physiologya. to eliminate or excrete (faeces); defecateb. to discharge (any waste product) from (a part of the body)4. (transitive) to create a vacuum in (a bulb, flask, reaction vessel, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

evacuation (eˌvacuˈation) nounevacuative (eˈvacuative) adjectiveevacuator (eˈvacuˌator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ēvacuāre to void, from vacuus empty

evacuate

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪvækjueɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: evacuates , evacuating , evacuated
Definitions
1. verbTo evacuate someone means to send them to a place of safety, away from a dangerous building, town, or area. [V n] ⇒ They were planning to evacuate the seventy American officials still in the country. [be V-ed + from] ⇒ Since 1951, 18,000 people have been evacuated from the area.evacuation (ɪvækjueɪʃən )Word forms: evacuations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the evacuation of the sick and wounded. [+ of] ⇒ An evacuation of the city's four-million inhabitants is planned for later this week.2. verbIf people evacuate a place, they move out of it for a period of time, especially because it is dangerous. [V n] ⇒ The fire is threatening about sixty homes, and residents have evacuated the area. [V] ⇒ Officials ordered the residents to evacuate.evacuation Word forms: evacuations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the mass evacuation of the Bosnian town of Srebrenica. [+ from] ⇒ Burning sulfur from the wreck has forced evacuations from the area.
