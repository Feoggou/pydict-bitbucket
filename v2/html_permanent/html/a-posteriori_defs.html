a posteriori

(eɪ pɒsˌtɛrɪˈɔːraɪ , -rɪ, ɑː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective logic1. relating to or involving inductive reasoning from particular facts or effects to a general principle2. derived from or requiring evidence for its validation or support; empirical; open to revision3. statistics See posterior probability▷ Compare a priori, synthetic (sense 4)
Word origin
C18: from Latin, literally: from the latter (that is, from effect to cause)
