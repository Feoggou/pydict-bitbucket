unleash

(ʌnˈliːʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to release from or as if from a leash2. to free from restraint or control

unleash for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnliːʃ )
Word forms: unleashes , unleashing , unleashed
Definitions
verbIf you say that someone or something unleashes a powerful force, feeling, activity, or group, you mean that they suddenly start it or send it somewhere. [V n] ⇒ Then he unleashed his own, unstoppable, attack. [V n] ⇒ The officers were still reluctant to unleash their troops in pursuit of a defeated enemy. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the fury unleashed by the church schools' proposals.
