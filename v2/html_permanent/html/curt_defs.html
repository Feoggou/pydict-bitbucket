curt

(kɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. rudely blunt and brief; abrupt ⇒ a curt reply2. short or concise
Derived forms
curtly (ˈcurtly) adverbcurtness (ˈcurtness) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin curtus cut short, mutilated

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as curt, you mean that they speak or reply in a brief and rather rude way. ⇒ Her tone of voice was curt. ⇒ 'The matter is closed,' was the curt reply.curtly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'I'm leaving,' she said curtly.
