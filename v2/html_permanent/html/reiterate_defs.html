reiterate

(riːˈɪtəˌreɪt )
Definitions
verb(tr; may take a clause as object) to say or do again or repeatedly
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reiterant (reˈiterant) adjectivereiteration (reˌiterˈation) nounreiterative (reˈiterative) adjectivereiteratively (reˈiteratively) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin reiterāre to repeat, from re- + iterāre to do again, from iterum again

Powered by COBUILD
(riːɪtəreɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: reiterates , reiterating , reiterated
Definitions
verbIf you reiterate something, you say it again, usually in order to emphasize it. [formal, journalism] [V n] ⇒ He reiterated his opposition to the creation of a central bank. [V that] ⇒ I want to reiterate that our conventional weapons are superior. [Also V quote]reiteration (riːɪtəreɪʃən )Word forms: reiterations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ It was really a reiteration of the same old entrenched positions.
