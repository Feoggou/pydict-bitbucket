extinguish

(ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to put out or quench (a light, flames, etc)2. to remove or destroy entirely; annihilate3. archaic to eclipse or obscure by or as if by superior brilliance4. law to discharge (a debt)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

extinguishable (exˈtinguishable) adjectiveextinguisher (exˈtinguisher) nounextinguishment (exˈtinguishment) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin exstinguere, from stinguere to quench

[V n] ⇒ The lights are extinguished as soon as the news conference is over.2. verbIf something extinguishes a feeling or idea, it destroys it. [V n] ⇒ The message extinguished her hopes of Richard's return.
