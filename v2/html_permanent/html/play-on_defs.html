play on

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (adverb) to continue to play2. Also: play upon (preposition) to exploit or impose upon (the feelings or weakness of another) to one's own advantage3. (adverb) cricket to hit the ball into one's own wicket

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verbIf you play on someone's fears, weaknesses, or faults, you deliberately use them in order to persuade that person to do something, or to achieve what you want. [V P n] ⇒ ...an election campaign which plays on the population's fear of change. [V P n] ⇒ I felt guilty saying that, playing on her generosity. →  play
