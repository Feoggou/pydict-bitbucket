innocence

(ˈɪnəsəns )
Definitions
nounthe quality or state of being innocent. Archaic word: innocency (ˈɪnəsənsɪ )
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin innocentia harmlessness, from innocēns doing no harm, blameless, from in-1 + nocēns harming, from nocēre to hurt, harm; see noxious

innocence

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnəsəns ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounInnocence is the quality of having no experience or knowledge of the more complex or unpleasant aspects of life. [+ of] ⇒ ...the sweet innocence of youth. ⇒ Youngsters are losing their childhood innocence too quickly.2. uncount noun [oft poss N]If someone proves their innocence, they prove that they are not guilty of a crime. ⇒ He claims he has evidence which could prove his innocence.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
