self-pity

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounSelf-pity is a feeling of unhappiness that you have about yourself and your problems, especially when this is unnecessary or greatly exaggerated. [disapproval] ⇒ I was unable to shake off my self-pity. ⇒ Throughout, he showed no trace of self-pity.
Example sentences containing 'self-pity'
You either survive, a stronger person for it, or else you drown in self-pity. MacNeill, Alastair THE DEVIL'S DOORShe summoned up all her pity for him, to smother her self-pity. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENIt had taken Carlson's measured cruelty to shock Angel out of self-pity. Elizabeth Lowell A WOMAN WITHOUT LIES
