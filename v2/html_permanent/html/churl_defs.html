churl

(tʃɜːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a surly ill-bred person2. archaic a farm labourer3. a variant spelling of ceorl
Word origin
Old English ceorl; related to Old Norse karl, Middle Low German kerle, Greek gerōn old man
Example sentences containing 'churl'
There was a surge of muttering among the guardsmen, but most of it seemed to be agreement that the young man was more churl than Charl. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MAN
