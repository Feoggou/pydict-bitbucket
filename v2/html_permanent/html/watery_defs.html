watery

(ˈwɔːtərɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. relating to, consisting of, containing, or resembling water2. discharging or secreting water or a water-like fluid ⇒ a watery wound3. tearful; weepy4. insipid, thin, or weak
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

watery for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɔːtəri )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is watery is weak or pale. ⇒ A watery light began to show through the branches. ⇒ Martha managed to produce a dim, watery smile.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe food or drink as watery, you dislike it because it contains too much water, or has no flavour. [disapproval] ⇒ ...a plateful of watery soup. ⇒ ...watery beer.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is watery contains, resembles, or consists of water. ⇒ There was a watery discharge from her ear. ⇒ Emma's eyes went red and watery.
