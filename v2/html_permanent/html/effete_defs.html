effete

(ɪˈfiːt )
Definitions
adjective1. weak, ineffectual, or decadent as a result of overrefinement ⇒ an effete academic2. exhausted of vitality or strength; worn out; spent3. (of animals or plants) no longer capable of reproduction
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

effetely (efˈfetely) adverbeffeteness (efˈfeteness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin effētus having produced young, hence, exhausted by bearing, from fētus having brought forth; see fetus
