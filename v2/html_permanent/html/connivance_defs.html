connivance

or connivence (kəˈnaɪvəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act or fact of conniving2. law the tacit encouragement of or assent to another's wrongdoing, esp (formerly) of the petitioner in a divorce suit to the respondent's adultery. Also (obsolete): connivancy, connivency

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [oft with the N of n]Connivance is a willingness to allow or assist something to happen even though you know it is wrong. [disapproval] [+ of] ⇒ The deficit had grown with the connivance of the banks. ⇒ The goods were exported with official connivance.
