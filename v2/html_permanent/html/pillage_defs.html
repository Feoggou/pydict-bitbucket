pillage

(ˈpɪlɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to rob (a town, village, etc) of (booty or spoils), esp during a warnoun2. the act of pillaging3. something obtained by pillaging; booty

Derived Forms

pillager (ˈpillager) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from piller to despoil, probably from peille rag, from Latin pīleus felt cap

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf a group of people pillage a place, they steal property from it using violent methods. [V n] ⇒ Soldiers went on a rampage, pillaging stores and shooting. [V] ⇒ ...the boldness to pillage and rape.Pillage is also a noun. ⇒ There were no signs of violence or pillage.pillaging uncount noun ⇒ ...pillaging by people looking for something to eat.
