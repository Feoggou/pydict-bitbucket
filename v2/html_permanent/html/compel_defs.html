compel

(kəmˈpɛl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -pels, -pelling or -pelled (transitive)1. to cause (someone) by force (to be or do something)2. to obtain by force; exact ⇒ to compel obedience3. to overpower or subdue4. archaic to herd or drive together
Derived forms
compellable (comˈpellable) adjectivecompellably (comˈpellably) adverbcompeller (comˈpeller) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin compellere to drive together, from com- together + pellere to drive

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf a situation, a rule, or a person compels you to do something, they force you to do it. [V n to-inf] ⇒ ...the introduction of legislation to compel cyclists to wear a helmet. [V n to-inf] ⇒ Leonie's mother was compelled to take in washing to help support her family. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ Local housing authorities have been compelled by the housing crisis to make offers of sub-standard accommodation. [Also V n]2. →  feel compelled
