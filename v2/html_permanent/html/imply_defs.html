imply

(ɪmˈplaɪ )
Definitions
verb -plies, -plying or -plied (tr; may take a clause as object)1. to express or indicate by a hint; suggest ⇒ what are you implying by that remark?2. to suggest or involve as a necessary consequence3. logic to enable (a conclusion) to be inferred4. obsolete to entangle or enfoldSee note at infer
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French emplier, from Latin implicāre to involve; see implicate

imply

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmplaɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: implies , implying , implied
Definitions
1. verbIf you imply that something is the case, you say something which indicates that it is the case in an indirect way. [V that] ⇒ 'Are you implying that I have something to do with those attacks?' she asked coldly. [V-ed] ⇒ She felt undermined by the implied criticism. [Also V n]2. verbIf an event or situation implies that something is the case, it makes you think it likely that it is the case. [V that] ⇒ Exports in June rose 1.5%, implying that the economy was stronger than many investors had realized. [V n] ⇒ A 'frontier-free' Europe implies a greatly increased market for all economic operators.
