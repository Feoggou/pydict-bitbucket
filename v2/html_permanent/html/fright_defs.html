fright

(fraɪt )
Definitions
noun1. sudden intense fear or alarm2. a sudden alarming shock3. informal a horrifying, grotesque, or ludicrous person or thing ⇒ she looks a fright in that hat4. See take frightverb5. a poetic word for frighten
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English fryhto; related to Gothic faurhtei, Old Frisian fruchte, Old High German forhta

fright

Powered by COBUILD
(fraɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: frights
Definitions
1. uncount nounFright is a sudden feeling of fear, especially the fear that you feel when something unpleasant surprises you. ⇒ The steam pipes rattled suddenly, and Franklin uttered a shriek and jumped with fright. ⇒ The birds smashed into the top of their cages in fright. ⇒ To hide my fright I asked a question.2. countable noun [usu sing]A fright is an experience which makes you suddenly afraid. ⇒ The snake picked up its head and stuck out its tongue which gave everyone a fright. ⇒ The last time you had a real fright, you nearly crashed the car.3. →  take fright4. → the fright of your life
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
