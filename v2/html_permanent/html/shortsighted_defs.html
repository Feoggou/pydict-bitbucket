shortsighted

(ˈʃɔrtˌsaɪtɪd )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
adjective1.  nearsighted2.  having or showing a lack of foresight

shortsighted

Powered by COBUILD
(ʃɔrtsaɪtɪd ) also short-sighted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced American English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf someone is shortsighted about something, or if their ideas are shortsighted, they do not make proper or careful judgments about the future. ⇒ Environmentalists fear that this is a shortsighted approach to the problem of global warming.2. adjectiveIf you are short-sighted, you cannot see things properly when they are far away, because there is something wrong with your eyes. [mainly British]regional note:   in AM, usually use nearsighted
Example sentences containing 'shortsighted'
Catherine's other motive was understandable enough, if shortsighted : she wanted to get the fighting out of Italy. Newbery, Linda RIDDLE ME THIS
