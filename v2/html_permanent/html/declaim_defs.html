declaim

(dɪˈkleɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make (a speech, statement, etc) loudly and in a rhetorical manner2. to speak lines from (a play, poem, etc) with studied eloquence; recite3. (intransitive; foll by against) to protest (against) loudly and publicly
Derived forms
declaimer (deˈclaimer) or declaimant (deˈclaimant) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin dēclāmāre, from clāmāre to call out
