stock in trade

or stock-in-trade

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. goods in stock necessary for carrying on a business2. anything constantly used by a person as a part of his or her profession, occupation, or trade ⇒ friendliness is the salesman's stock in trade

stock-in-trade

or stock in trade (ˌstɒkɪnˈtreɪd)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. anything frequently used by someone as a part of a profession, occupation, trade or behaviour ⇒ Confession and repentance, the stock-in-trade of the revivalist preacher, do not constitute the infallible means of improving our behaviour., ⇒ His stock-in-trade is charm., ⇒ There are no reviews - the stock-in-trade of the specialist magazine., ⇒ Confidentiality was their stock-in-trade., ⇒ Patriotism is every politician's stock-in-trade., ⇒ Friendliness is the salesman's stock-in-trade., ⇒ Unhappy families are the writer's stock-in-trade., ⇒ Delicious potted shrimps and prawns were once the stock-in-trade of the harbourside cafe.2. goods in stock necessary for carrying on a business ⇒ One motorist, a rep who had been trapped by snowdrifts, maintained body temperature by wrapping himself in his stock-in-trade - ladies' stockings.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular noun [with poss]If you say that something is someone's stock-in-trade, you mean that it is a usual part of their behaviour or work. ⇒ Patriotism is every politician's stock-in-trade. ⇒ Delicious potted shrimps and prawns were once the stock-in-trade of the harbourside cafe.
