burp

(bɜːp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. informal a belchverb2. (intransitive) informal to belch3. (transitive) to cause (a baby) to burp to relieve flatulence after feeding
Word origin
C20: of imitative origin

burp

Powered by COBUILD
(bɜːʳp )
Word forms: burps , burping , burped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen someone burps, they make a noise because air from their stomach has been forced up through their throat. [V] ⇒ Charlie burped loudly.Burp is also a noun. ⇒ There followed a barely audible burp.
Example sentences containing 'burp'
The boiler came on with a burp and a quiet roaring, startling the misshapen shadow behind the torch.
