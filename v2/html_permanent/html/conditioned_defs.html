conditioned

(kənˈdɪʃənd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. psychology of or denoting a response that has been learned. Compare unconditioned2. (foll by to) accustomed; inured; prepared by training
Example sentences containing 'conditioned'
Andony nodded, too well conditioned to question his orders. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPThey lived in the walls, and had been conditioned away from any but the most vestigial fear of humans. Doris Lessing ON CATSShe smiled at him, but it was the smile of somebody conditioned to turn compassion on and off like a light. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEBecause we had been silent so long, the whole nation conditioned like zombies. Terrence Chen SONS OF HEAVEN
