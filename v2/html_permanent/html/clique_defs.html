clique

(kliːk , klɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna small exclusive group of friends or associates
Derived forms
cliquish (ˈcliquish) adjectivecliquishly (ˈcliquishly) adverbcliquishness (ˈcliquishness) noun
Word origin
C18: from French, perhaps from Old French: latch, from cliquer to click; suggestive of the necessity to exclude nonmembers

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounIf you describe a group of people as a clique, you mean that they spend a lot of time together and seem unfriendly towards people who are not in the group. [disapproval] ⇒ Anna Ford recently hit out at the male clique which she believes holds back women in television.
