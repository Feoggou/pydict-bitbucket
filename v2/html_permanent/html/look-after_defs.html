look after

Definitions
verb (intr, preposition)1. to take care of; be responsible for ⇒ she looked after the child while I was out2. to follow with the eyes ⇒ he looked after the girl thoughtfully

look after

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you look after someone or something, you do what is necessary to keep them healthy, safe, or in good condition. take care of [V P n] ⇒ I love looking after the children. [V P n] ⇒ People don't look after other people's property in the same way as they look after their own.2. phrasal verbIf you look after something, you are responsible for it and deal with it or make sure it is all right, especially because it is your job to do so. attend to [V P n] ⇒ ...the farm manager who looks after the day-to-day organization. [V P n] ⇒ We'll help you look after your finances. →  look
