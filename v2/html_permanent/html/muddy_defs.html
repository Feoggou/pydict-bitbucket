muddy

(ˈmʌdɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -dier or -diest1. covered or filled with mud2. not clear or bright ⇒ muddy colours3. cloudy ⇒ a muddy liquid4. (esp of thoughts) confused or vagueverb -dies, -dying or -died5. to become or cause to become muddy

Derived Forms

muddily (ˈmuddily) adverbmuddiness (ˈmuddiness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is muddy contains mud or is covered in mud. ⇒ ...a muddy track. ⇒ The ground was still very muddy. ⇒ ...his muddy boots.2. verbIf you muddy something, you cause it to be muddy. [V n] ⇒ The ground still smelled of rain and they muddied their shoes. [V-ed] ⇒ His new grey jacket was torn and muddied.3. graded adjective [ADJ n]Muddy is used to describe a colour which is dull and brownish. ⇒ The paper has turned a muddy colour.4. verbIf someone or something muddies a situation or issue, they cause it to seem less clear and less easy to understand. [V n] ⇒ It's difficult enough without muddying the issue with religion. [V n] ⇒ ...the mixed motives that muddied Mr Crane's efforts.muddied graded adjective & adjective ⇒ Overseas the legal issues are more muddied.→  muddy the waters
