outpost

(ˈaʊtˌpəʊst )
Definitions
noun1. militarya. a position stationed at a distance from the area occupied by a major formationb. the troops assigned to such a position2. an outlying settlement or position3. a limit or frontier

Definitions
countable nounAn outpost is a small group of buildings used for trading or military purposes, either in a distant part of your own country or in a foreign country. ⇒ ...a remote mountain outpost, linked to the outside world by the poorest of roads. [Also + of]
