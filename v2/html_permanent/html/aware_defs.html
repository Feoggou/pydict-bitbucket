aware

(əˈwɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (postpositive; foll by of) having knowledge; cognizant ⇒ aware of his error2. informed of current developments ⇒ politically aware
Derived forms
aˈwareness noun
Word origin
Old English gewær; related to Old Saxon, Old High German giwar Latin verērī to be fearful; see beware, wary

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ that]If you are aware of something, you know about it. [+ of] ⇒ Smokers are well aware of the dangers to their own health. [+ of] ⇒ He should have been aware of what his junior officers were doing. ⇒ He must have been aware that my parents' marriage was breaking up.awareness uncount noun [oft N that] [+ of/about] ⇒ The 1980s brought an awareness of green issues. ⇒ There has been an increasing awareness that many people are affected by crime.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ that]If you are aware of something, you realize that it is present or is happening because you hear it, see it, smell it, or feel it. [+ of] ⇒ She was acutely aware of the noise of the city. ⇒ Jane was suddenly aware that she was digging her nails into her thigh.3. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]Someone who is aware notices what is happening around them or happening in the place where they live. ⇒ They are politically very aware.awareness uncount noun ⇒ He introduced radio to the school to increase the children's awareness.
