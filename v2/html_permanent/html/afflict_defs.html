afflict

(əˈflɪkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to cause suffering or unhappiness to; distress greatly
Derived forms
afˈflictive adjective
Word origin
C14: from Latin afflictus, past participle of afflīgere to knock against, from flīgere to knock, to strike

Word forms: afflicts , afflicting , afflicted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you are afflicted by pain, illness, or disaster, it affects you badly and makes you suffer. [formal] [be V-ed + by/with] ⇒ Italy has been afflicted by political corruption for decades. [V n] ⇒ There are two main problems which afflict people with hearing impairments. [V-ed] ⇒ The afflicted person should keep off solid foods and drink plenty of fluids.The afflicted are people who are afflicted. ⇒ It is food that the afflicted want now.
