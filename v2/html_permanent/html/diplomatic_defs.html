diplomatic

(ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to diplomacy or diplomats2. skilled in negotiating, esp between states or people3. tactful in dealing with people4. of or relating to diplomatics
Derived forms
ˌdiplomatically (ˌdiploˈmatically) adverb
Word origin
C18: from French diplomatique concerning the documents of diplomacy, from New Latin diplōmaticus; see diploma

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Diplomatic means relating to diplomacy and diplomats. ⇒ ...before the two countries resume full diplomatic relations. ⇒ Efforts are being made to avert war and find a diplomatic solution. ⇒ These diplomatic skills led to her appointment as the President of the United Nations General Assembly.diplomatically (dɪpləmætɪkli ) adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ ...a growing sense of doubt that the conflict can be resolved diplomatically. ⇒ The President made it clear he did not want to see the country diplomatically isolated.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is diplomatic is able to be careful to say or do things without offending people. ⇒ She is very direct. I tend to be more diplomatic, I suppose.diplomatically graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'Their sound is very interesting,' he says, diplomatically.
