pretext

(ˈpriːtɛkst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fictitious reason given in order to conceal the real one2. a specious excuse; pretence

Word Origin

C16: from Latin praetextum disguise, from praetexere to weave in front, disguise; see texture

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA pretext is a reason which you pretend has caused you to do something. [+ for] ⇒ They wanted a pretext for subduing the region by force. ⇒ He excused himself on the pretext of a stomach upset. ⇒ They would now find some dubious pretext to restart the war.
Example sentences containing 'pretext'
Without looking at the Fool directly, I explained, `That was my pretext for visiting Chade's chambers this morning. Mark Mills AMAGANSETTHe was talking about the propaganda pretext for launching a war. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEThat will give me a convenient and uncontroversial pretext for visiting Zimroel.
