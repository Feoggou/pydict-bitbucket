critical

adjective

  1. crucial, decisive, momentous, deciding, pressing, serious, vital, psychological, urgent, all-important, pivotal, high-priority, now or never • The incident happened at a critical point in the campaign. OPPOSITE:  unimportant
  2. grave, serious, dangerous, acute, risky, hairy (slang), precarious, perilous • Ten of the injured are said to be in a critical condition. OPPOSITES:  safe, settled, secure
  3. disparaging, disapproving, scathing, derogatory, nit-picking (informal), censorious, cavilling, fault-finding, captious, carping, niggling, nit-picky (informal) • He has apologized for critical remarks he made about the referee. OPPOSITES:  approving, complimentary, appreciative, uncritical
  4. analytical, penetrating, discriminating, discerning, diagnostic, perceptive, judicious, accurate, precise • What is needed is a critical analysis of the evidence. OPPOSITE: undiscriminating
