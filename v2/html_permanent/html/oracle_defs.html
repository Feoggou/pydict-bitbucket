oracle

(ˈɒrəkəl )
Definitions
noun1. a prophecy, often obscure or allegorical, revealed through the medium of a priest or priestess at the shrine of a god2. a shrine at which an oracular god is consulted3. an agency through which a prophecy is transmitted4. any person or thing believed to indicate future action with infallible authority5. a statement believed to be infallible and authoritative6. Biblea. a message from Godb. the holy of holies in the Israelite temple
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin ōrāculum, from ōrāre to request
