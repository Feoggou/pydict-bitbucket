dismayed

(ˌdɪsˈmeɪd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. filled with depression or discouragement ⇒ He was dismayed at the cynicism of the youngsters., ⇒ I was dismayed at the poor reviews., ⇒ I was dismayed to find that the clothes did not fit.2. filled with apprehension or alarm ⇒ He was dismayed at the sight., ⇒ He was dismayed to find that his hands were shaking.
Example sentences containing 'dismayed'
Right now, I'm a bit dismayed at how tired I am just from the ride down here. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThe answer came in a lower tone, doubtful, a little dismayed. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENNothing dismayed Danlo more than this tendency toward worship. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GOD
