decant

(dɪˈkænt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to pour (a liquid, such as wine) from one container to another, esp without disturbing any sediment2. (transitive) to rehouse (people) while their homes are being rebuilt or refurbished
Word origin
C17: from Medieval Latin dēcanthāre, from canthus spout, rim; see canthus
