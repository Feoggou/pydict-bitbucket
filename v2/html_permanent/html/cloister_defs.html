cloister

(ˈklɔɪstə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a covered walk, usually around a quadrangle in a religious institution, having an open arcade or colonnade on the inside and a wall on the outside2. (sometimes plural) a place of religious seclusion, such as a monastery3. life in a monastery or conventverb4. (transitive) to confine or seclude in or as if in a monastery
Derived forms
cloister-ˌlike (ˈcloister-ˌlike) adjective
Word origin
C13: from Old French cloistre, from Medieval Latin claustrum monastic cell, from Latin: bolt, barrier, from claudere to close; influenced in form by Old French cloison partition
