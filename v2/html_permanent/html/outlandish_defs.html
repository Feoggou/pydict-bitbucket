outlandish

(aʊtˈlændɪʃ )
Definitions
adjective1. grotesquely unconventional in appearance, habits, etc2. archaic foreign
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

outlandishly (outˈlandishly) adverboutlandishness (outˈlandishness) noun

outlandish for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtlændɪʃ )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as outlandish, you disapprove of it because you think it is very unusual, strange, or unreasonable. [disapproval] ⇒ They appeared at parties in outlandish clothes. ⇒ This idea is not as outlandish as it sounds.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'outlandish'
His report had outlined six distinct, if somewhat outlandish , avenues of attack and the security measures necessary to counteract them. Terman, Douglas CORMORANTThey were a couple of miles south of Point Sal, overlooking an outlandish stretch of coast. Trenhalle, John A MEANS TO EVILTo spite his parents, to excuse his arrogance, to explain his outlandish behaviour.
