instill

(ɪnˈstɪl ) or inˈstil (ɪnˈstɪl ) in American English
Definitions
verb transitiveWord forms: inˈstilled or inˈstilling1.  to put in drop by drop2.  to put (an idea, principle, feeling, etc.) in or into little by little; impart gradually
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

instillation (ˌinstilˈlation) noun instiller (inˈstiller) noun instillment (inˈstillment) noun or inˈstilment

Word Origin

MFr instiller < L instillare < in-, in + stillare, to drop < stilla, a drop
