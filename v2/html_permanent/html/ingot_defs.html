ingot

(ˈɪŋɡət )
Definitions
noun1. a piece of cast metal obtained from a mould in a form suitable for storage, transporting, and further useverb2. (transitive) to shape (metal) into ingots
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: perhaps from in-2 + Old English goten, past participle of geotan to pour

Definitions
noun(Mechanical engineering: General) An ingot is an unfinished cast piece of metal. ⇒ Steel ingots are blocks of steel ready to be rolled or melted. ⇒ Forging involves using hard blows to form and shape metallic ingots into useful items. ⇒ An ingot is an unfinished cast piece of metal.Related wordsCompare ingot with billet, which is a length of metal that has been partly processed but is not in its final state.
