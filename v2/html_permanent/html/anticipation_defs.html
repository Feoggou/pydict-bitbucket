anticipation

(ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of anticipating; expectation, premonition, or foresight2. the act of taking or dealing with funds before they are legally available or due3. music an unstressed, usually short note introduced before a downbeat and harmonically related to the chord immediately following it. Compare suspension (sense 11)

(æntɪsɪpeɪʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAnticipation is a feeling of excitement about something pleasant or exciting that you know is going to happen. ⇒ There's been an atmosphere of anticipation around here for a few days now. ⇒ We await the next volume of this superb edition with keen anticipation.2. →  in anticipation of sth

The excitement, the anticipation aboard these ships was intense. Maclean, Alistair THE LONELY SEAThen he stumped forward on his sticks, his face already creased with anticipation of Aubrey's mood.
