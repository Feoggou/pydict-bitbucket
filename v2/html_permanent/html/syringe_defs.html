syringe

(ˈsɪrɪndʒ , sɪˈrɪndʒ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. medicine an instrument, such as a hypodermic syringe or a rubber ball with a slender nozzle, for use in withdrawing or injecting fluids, cleaning wounds, etc2. any similar device for injecting, spraying, or extracting liquids by means of pressure or suctionverb3. (transitive) to cleanse, inject, or spray with a syringe

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin, from Latin: syrinx
