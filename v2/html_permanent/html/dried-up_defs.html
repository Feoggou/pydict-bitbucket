dried-up

(ˌdraɪdˈʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. informal old and dull ⇒ her fears of becoming a dried-up old prune2. suffering from lack of water ⇒ dried-up grass3. empty of water ⇒ a dried-up river bed

dried-up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as dried-up, you are saying rudely that they are old and dull, and not worth paying attention to. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ ...her fears of becoming a dried-up old prune.2. → see also dry up
