monogamy

(mɒˈnɒɡəmɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state or practice of having only one husband or wife over a period of time. Compare bigamy, polygamy (sense 1), digamy2. zoology the practice of having only one mate

Derived Forms

monogamous (moˈnogamous) adjectivemonogamously (moˈnogamously) adverbmonogamousness (moˈnogamousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: via French from Late Latin monogamia, from Greek; see mono- + -gamy

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounMonogamy is used to refer to the state or custom of having a sexual relationship with only one partner. ⇒ People still opt for monogamy and marriage.2. uncount nounMonogamy is the state or custom of being married to only one person at a particular time. OPPOSITE: polygamy ⇒ In many non-Western societies, however, monogamy has never dominated.
