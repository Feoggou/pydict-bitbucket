prefigure

(priːˈfɪɡə ) or prefigurate (priːˈfɪɡjʊˌreɪt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to represent or suggest in advance2. to imagine or consider beforehand

Derived Forms

prefigurement (preˈfigurement) noun

prefigure

Powered by COBUILD
(priːfɪgəʳ , US -gjər )
Word forms: prefigures , prefiguring , prefigured
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf one thing prefigures another, it is a first indication which suggests or determines that the second thing will happen. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The wall through Berlin was finally ruptured, prefiguring the reunification of Germany.
