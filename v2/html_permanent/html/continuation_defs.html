continuation

(kənˌtɪnjʊˈeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a part or thing added, esp to a book or play, that serves to continue or extend; sequel2. a renewal of an interrupted action, process, etc; resumption3. the act or fact of continuing without interruption; prolongation4. another word for contango (sense 1), contango (sense 2)

Powered by COBUILD
(kəntɪnjueɪʃən )
Word forms: continuations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [usu with poss]The continuation of something is the fact that it continues, rather than stopping. [+ of] ⇒ It's the coalition forces who are to blame for the continuation of the war. ⇒ What we'll see in the future is, in fact, a continuation of that trend.2. countable noun [usu sing]Something that is a continuation of something else is closely connected with it or forms part of it. ⇒ ...since this chapter is a continuation of Chapter 8. [+ of] ⇒ It would just be a continuation of previous visits he has made to Israel.
