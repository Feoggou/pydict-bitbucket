supposition

(ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of supposing2. a fact, theory, etc, that is supposed

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft N that]A supposition is an idea or statement which someone believes or assumes to be true, although they may have no evidence for it. [formal] ⇒ There's a popular supposition that we're publicly funded but the bulk of our money comes from competitive contracts. ⇒ But as with many such suppositions in natural history, no one had ever tested it.2. uncount nounYou can describe someone's ideas or statements as supposition if you disapprove of the fact that they have no evidence to support them. [disapproval] ⇒ The report has been rejected by the authorities, who said much of it was based on supposition or inaccuracy.
