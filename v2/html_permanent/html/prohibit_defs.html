prohibit

(prəˈhɪbɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to forbid by law or other authority2. to hinder or prevent

Derived Forms

prohibiter (proˈhibiter) or prohibitor (proˈhibitor) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin prohibēre to prevent, from pro-1 + habēre to hold

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf a law or someone in authority prohibits something, they forbid it or make it illegal. [formal] [V n] ⇒ ...a law that prohibits tobacco advertising in newspapers and magazines. [V n] ⇒ Fishing is prohibited. [V n + from] ⇒ Federal law prohibits foreign airlines from owning more than 25% of any U.S. airline.prohibition uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the prohibition of women on air combat missions.
