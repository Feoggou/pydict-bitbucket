hecatomb

(ˈhɛkəˌtəʊm , -ˌtuːm)
Definitions
noun1. (in ancient Greece or Rome) any great public sacrifice and feast, originally one in which 100 oxen were sacrificed2. a great sacrifice
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin hecatombē, from Greek hekatombē, from hekaton hundred + bous ox
