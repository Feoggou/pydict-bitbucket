carapace

(ˈkærəˌpeɪs ) or carapax (ˈkærəˌpæks)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe thick hard shield, made of chitin or bone, that covers part of the body of crabs, lobsters, tortoises, etc
Word origin
C19: from French, from Spanish carapacho, of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA carapace is the protective shell on the back of some animals such as tortoises or crabs. [formal]2. countable nounYou can refer to an attitude that someone has in order to protect themselves as their carapace. [literary] ⇒ The arrogance became his protective carapace.
