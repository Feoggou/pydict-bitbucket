smart

adjective

  1. chic, trim, neat, fashionable, stylish, fine, elegant, trendy (British, informal), spruce, snappy, natty (informal), modish, well turned-out, schmick (Australian, informal) • I was dressed in a smart navy-blue suit. OPPOSITES:  scruffy, dowdy, unfashionable, dull, old-fashioned, sloppy, out-of-date, naff (British, slang), outmoded, passé, uncool, unhip (slang), fogeyish, untrendy (British, informal)
  2. clever, bright, intelligent, quick, sharp, keen, acute, shrewd, apt, ingenious, astute, canny, quick-witted • He thinks he's much smarter than Sarah. OPPOSITES:  thick, stupid, dull, slow, foolish, dense, dumb (informal), daft (informal), idiotic, moronic, unintelligent, dumb-ass (slang), dim-witted (informal)
  3. fashionable, stylish, chic, genteel, in vogue, voguish (informal) • smart dinner parties
  4. brisk, quick, lively, vigorous, spirited, cracking (informal), spanking, jaunty • We set off at a smart pace.
  5. stinging, hard, sharp, keen, painful, piercing, resounding
  6. impertinent, pointed, ready, witty, saucy, smart-alecky (informal), nimble-witted

verb

  1. sting, burn, tingle, pain, hurt, throb • My eyes smarted from the smoke.
  2. feel resentful, feel annoyed, feel offended, feel wounded, feel indignant • He is still smarting over criticism of his clumsy performance.

noun

  1. sting, pain, smarting, pang, soreness, burning sensation
