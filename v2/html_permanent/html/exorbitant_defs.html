exorbitant

(ɪɡˈzɔːbɪtənt )
Definitions
adjective(of prices, demands, etc) in excess of what is reasonable; excessive; extravagant; immoderate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exorbitance (exˈorbitance) or exorbitancy (exˈorbitancy) nounexorbitantly (exˈorbitantly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin exorbitāre to deviate, from Latin orbita track

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something such as a price or fee as exorbitant, you are emphasizing that it is much greater than it should be. [emphasis] ⇒ Exorbitant housing prices have created an acute shortage of affordable housing for the poor.exorbitantly adverb ⇒ ...exorbitantly high salaries.
