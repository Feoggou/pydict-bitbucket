misdemeanor

(ˌmɪsdəˈminər )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun1.  Rare the act of misbehaving2.  Law any minor offense, as the breaking of a municipal ordinance, for which statute provides a lesser punishment than for a felony: the penalty is usually a fine or imprisonment for a short time (usually less than one year) in a local jail, workhouse, etc.: Brit. sp. ˌmisdeˈmeanour

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced American English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, use misdemeanour1. countable nounA misdemeanor is an act that some people consider to be wrong or unacceptable. [formal] ⇒ Paul appeared before the faculty to account for his various misdemeanors.2. countable nounIn the United States and other countries where the legal system distinguishes between very serious crimes and less serious ones, a misdemeanor is a less serious crime. [law] ⇒ Under state law, it is a misdemeanor to possess a firearm on school premises.
