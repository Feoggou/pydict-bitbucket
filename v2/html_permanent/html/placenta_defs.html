placenta

(pləˈsɛntə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tas or -tae (-tiː)1. the vascular organ formed in the uterus during pregnancy, consisting of both maternal and embryonic tissues and providing oxygen and nutrients for the fetus and transfer of waste products from the fetal to the maternal blood circulation. See also afterbirth2. the corresponding organ or part in certain mammals3. botanya. the part of the ovary of flowering plants to which the ovules are attachedb. the mass of tissue in nonflowering plants that bears the sporangia or spores

Word Origin

C17: via Latin from Greek plakoeis flat cake, from plax flat
