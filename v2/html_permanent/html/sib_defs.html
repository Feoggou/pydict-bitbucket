sib

or sibb (sɪb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a blood relative2. a brother or sister; sibling3. kinsmen collectively; kindred4. any social unit that is bonded by kinship through one line of descent only

Word Origin

Old English sibb; related to Old Norse sifjar relatives, Old High German sippa kinship, Latin suus one's own; see gossip

SIB

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
abbreviation for (in Britain)(the former) Securities and Investments Board
Example sentences containing 'sib'
Often my younger sib was already there in the warm, soft, breathing dark. Ursula K. Le Guin THE BIRTHDAY OF THE WORLD
