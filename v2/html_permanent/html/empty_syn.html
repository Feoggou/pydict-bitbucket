Synonyms of empty

empty

adjective

  1. bare, clear, abandoned, deserted, vacant, free, void, desolate, destitute, uninhabited, unoccupied, waste, unfurnished, untenanted, without contents, MT (text messaging)
    • The room was bare and empty.
    OPPOSITES:  full, stuffed, packed, occupied, inhabited
  2. meaningless, cheap, hollow, vain, idle, trivial, ineffective, futile, insubstantial, insincere, MT (text messaging)
    • His father said he was going to beat him, but he knew it was an empty threat.
  3. worthless, meaningless, hollow, pointless, unsatisfactory, futile, unreal, senseless, frivolous, fruitless, aimless, inane, valueless, purposeless, otiose, bootless (old-fashioned), MT (text messaging)
    • My life was hectic but empty before I met him.
    OPPOSITES:  interesting, full, useful, busy, satisfying, occupied, valuable, fulfilled, worthwhile, meaningful, purposeful
  4. blank, absent, vacant, stony, deadpan, vacuous, impassive, expressionless, unintelligent, MT (text messaging)
    • She saw the empty look in his eyes as he left.
  5. (informal) hungry, unfilled, famished, starving (informal), unfed, MT (text messaging)
    • Never drink on an empty stomach.

verb

  1. clear, drain, gut, void, unload, pour out, unpack, unburden, remove the contents of, MT (text messaging)
    • I emptied the ashtray.
    OPPOSITES:  fill, stuff, pack, cram
  2. exhaust, consume the contents of, void, deplete, use up, MT (text messaging)
    • Cross emptied his glass with one swallow.
    OPPOSITES:  stock, replenish
  3. evacuate, clear, vacate, MT (text messaging)
    • a bore who could empty a room in two minutes just by talking about his therapy
