Definitions

adjectiveextended over a large expanse of space or time ⇒ Ian's family is very spread out., ⇒ The Kurds are spread out across five nations.

spread out

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf people, animals, or vehicles spread out, they move apart from each other. [V P] ⇒ Felix watched his men move like soldiers, spreading out into two teams.2. phrasal verbIf something such as a city or forest spreads out, it gets larger and gradually begins to covers a larger area. [V P] ⇒ Cities such as Tokyo are spreading out. [V P] ⇒ A crude oil slick quickly spreads out over water.3. → see spread [sense 1], spread [sense 2] →  spread
