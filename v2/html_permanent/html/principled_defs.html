principled

(ˈprɪnsɪpəld )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivea. having high moral principlesb. (in combination) ⇒ high-principled

principled

Powered by COBUILD
(prɪnsɪpəld )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as principled, you approve of them because they have strong moral principles. [approval] ⇒ She was a strong, principled woman. ⇒ You have to take a principled stand.
Example sentences containing 'principled'
Your father is loyal, principled and obedient to me, which is how it should be. Fay Weldon NOTHING TO WEAR AND NOWHERE TO HIDE: A COLLECTION OF SHORT STORIES"She's so principled , so sure of herself, so emphatic in her beliefs. Laura Lippman IN A STRANGE CITY
