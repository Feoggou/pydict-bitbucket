mammal

(ˈmæməl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounany animal of the Mammalia, a large class of warm-blooded vertebrates having mammary glands in the female, a thoracic diaphragm, and a four-chambered heart. The class includes the whales, carnivores, rodents, bats, primates, etc

Derived Forms

mammalian (mæˈmeɪlɪən ) adjective, nounmammal-ˌlike (ˈmammal-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C19: via New Latin from Latin mamma breast

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounMammals are animals such as humans, dogs, lions, and whales. In general, female mammals give birth to babies rather than laying eggs, and feed their young with milk.
