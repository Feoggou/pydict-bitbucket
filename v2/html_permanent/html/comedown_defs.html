comedown

(ˈkʌmˌdaʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a decline in position, status, or prosperity2. informal a disappointment3. slang a depressed or unexcited stateverb come down (intr, adverb)4. to come to a place regarded as lower5. to lose status, wealth, etc (esp in the phrase to come down in the world)6. to reach a decision ⇒ the report came down in favour of a pay increase7. (often foll by to) to be handed down or acquired by tradition or inheritance8. British to leave college or university9. (foll by with) to succumb (to illness or disease)10. (foll by on) to rebuke or criticize harshly11. (foll by to) to amount in essence (to) ⇒ it comes down to two choices12. slang to lose the effects of a drug and return to a normal or more normal state13. Australian informal (of a river) to flow in flood

Definitions

regional note:   in BRIT, also use come-downsingular nounIf you say that something is a comedown, you think that it is not as good as something else that you have just done or had. let-down ⇒ The prospect of relegation is a comedown for a club that finished second two seasons ago.
