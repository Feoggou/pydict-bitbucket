raucous

(ˈrɔːkəs )
Definitions
adjective(of voices, cries, etc) harshly or hoarsely loud
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

raucously (ˈraucously) adverbraucousness (ˈraucousness) or rare raucity (ˈrɔːsɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C18: from Latin raucus hoarse

Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A raucous sound is loud, harsh, and rather unpleasant. ⇒ They heard a bottle being smashed, then more raucous laughter. ⇒ ...the raucous cries of the sea-birds. ⇒ ...a raucous crowd of 25,000 delirious fans.raucously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ They laughed together raucously.
