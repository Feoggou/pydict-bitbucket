enmity

(ˈɛnmɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -tiesa feeling of hostility or ill will, as between enemies; antagonism
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Old French enemistié, from enemi enemy

enmity

Powered by COBUILD
(enmɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: enmities
Definitions
variable nounEnmity is a feeling of hatred towards someone that lasts for a long time. [+ between] ⇒ I think there is an historic enmity between them. ⇒ President Mitterrand arrived in Hanoi yesterday to bury old colonial and cold war enmities.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
