mobile

(ˈməʊbaɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having freedom of movement; movable2. changing quickly in expression ⇒ a mobile face3. sociology (of individuals or social groups) moving within and between classes, occupations, and localities ⇒ upwardly mobile4. (of military forces) able to move freely and quickly to any given area5. (postpositive) informal having transport available ⇒ are you mobile tonight?noun6. a. a sculpture suspended in midair with delicately balanced parts that are set in motion by air currentsb. (as modifier) ⇒ mobile sculpture. Compare stabile7. short for mobile phone

Word Origin

C15: via Old French from Latin mōbilis, from movēre to move

(moʊbaɪl , US -bəl )
Word forms: mobiles
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use mobile to describe something large that can be moved easily from place to place. ⇒ ...special mobile units where men can have their fingerprints taken and donate a specimen of blood. ⇒ ...the four hundred seat mobile theatre.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are mobile, you can move or travel easily from place to place, for example because you do not have a physical disability or because you have your own transport. ⇒ I'm still very mobile.mobility (moʊbɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ Two cars gave them the freedom and mobility to go their separate ways.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIn a mobile society, people move easily from one job, home, or social class to another. ⇒ We're a very mobile society, and people move after they get divorced. ⇒ ...young, mobile professionals.mobility uncount noun ⇒ Prior to the nineteenth century, there were almost no channels of social mobility.4. graded adjectiveIf someone has a mobile face, the expression on their face changes quickly as their feelings change. ⇒ Robyn had the more mobile, more expressive face.5. countable nounA mobile is a decoration which you hang from a ceiling. It usually consists of several small objects which move as the air around them moves.6. countable nounA mobile is the same as a mobile phone.7. → see also upwardly mobile
