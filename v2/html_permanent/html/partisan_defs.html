partisan1

or partizan (ˌpɑːtɪˈzæn , ˈpɑːtɪˌzæn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an adherent or devotee of a cause, party, etc2. a. a member of an armed resistance group within occupied territory, esp in Italy or the Balkans in World War IIb. (as modifier) ⇒ partisan forcesadjective3. of, relating to, or characteristic of a partisan4. relating to or excessively devoted to one party, faction, etc; one-sided ⇒ partisan control

Derived Forms

ˌpartisanship (ˌpartiˈsanship) or ˌpartizanship (ˌpartiˈzanship) noun

Word Origin

C16: via French, from Old Italian partigiano, from parte faction, from Latin pars part

(pɑːʳtɪzæn , US -zən )
Word forms: partisans
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Someone who is partisan strongly supports a particular person or cause, often without thinking carefully about the matter. ⇒ He is clearly too partisan to be a referee.A partisan is someone who is partisan. champion ⇒ At first the eager young poet was a partisan of the Revolution.2. countable nounPartisans are ordinary people, rather than soldiers, who join together to fight enemy soldiers who are occupying their country. ⇒ He was rescued by some Italian partisans. ⇒ ...serving in resistance and partisan activities.
