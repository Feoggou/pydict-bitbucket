minion

(ˈmɪnjən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a favourite or dependant, esp a servile or fawning one2. a servile agent ⇒ the minister's minions3. a size of printer's type, approximately equal to 7 pointadjective4. dainty, pretty, or elegant

Word Origin

C16: from French mignon, from Old French mignot, of Gaulish origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu pl, usu poss N]If you refer to someone's minions, you are referring to people who have to do what that person tells them to do, especially unimportant or boring tasks. [literary, disapproval] ⇒ She delegated the job to one of her minions.
