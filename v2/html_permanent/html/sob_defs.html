sob

(sɒb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: sobs, sobbing or sobbed1. (intransitive) to weep with convulsive gasps2. (transitive) to utter with sobs3. to cause (oneself) to be in a specified state by sobbing ⇒ to sob oneself to sleepnoun4. a convulsive gasp made in weeping

Derived Forms

sobber (ˈsobber) nounsobbing (ˈsobbing) noun, adjective

Word Origin

C12: probably from Low German; compare Dutch sabben to suck

s.o.b. - slang, mainly US and Canadian

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
abbreviation forson of a bitch

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen someone sobs, they cry in a noisy way, breathing in short breaths. [V] ⇒ She began to sob again, burying her face in the pillow. [V] ⇒ Her sister broke down, sobbing into her handkerchief.sobbing uncount noun ⇒ The room was silent except for her sobbing.2. verbIf you sob something, you say it while you are crying. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Everything's my fault,' she sobbed.3. countable nounA sob is one of the noises that you make when you are crying.
