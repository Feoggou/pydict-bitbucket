unwind

(ʌnˈwaɪnd )
Definitions
verb -winds, -winding or -wound1. to slacken, undo, or unravel or cause to slacken, undo, or unravel2. (transitive) to disentangle3. to make or become relaxed ⇒ he finds it hard to unwind after a busy day at work
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unwindable (unˈwindable) adjectiveunwinder (unˈwinder) noun

unwind for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnwaɪnd )
Word forms: unwinds , unwinding , unwound
Definitions
1. verbWhen you unwind, you relax after you have done something that makes you tense or tired. [V] ⇒ It helps them to unwind after a busy day at work. [V] ⇒ Singing is a nice way of unwinding.2. ergative verb & verbIf you unwind a length of something that is wrapped round something else or round itself, you loosen it and make it straight. You can also say that it unwinds. [V n] ⇒ One of them unwound a length of rope from around his waist. [V] ⇒ The thread unwound a little more.
