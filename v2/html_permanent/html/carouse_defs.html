carouse

(kəˈraʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to have a merry drinking spree; drink freelynoun2. another word for carousal
Derived forms
carouser (caˈrouser) nouncarousing (caˈrousing) noun
Word origin
C16: via French carrousser from German (trinken) gar aus (to drink) right out

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that people are carousing, you mean that they are behaving very noisily and drinking a lot of alcohol as they enjoy themselves. [V + with] ⇒ They told him to stay home with his wife instead of going out and carousing with friends.carousing uncount noun ⇒ The singing and carousing did not end until after midnight.
