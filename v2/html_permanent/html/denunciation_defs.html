denunciation

(dɪˌnʌnsɪˈeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. open condemnation; censure; denouncing2. law obsolete a charge or accusation of crime made by an individual before a public prosecutor or tribunal3. a formal announcement of the termination of a treaty4. archaic an announcement in the form of an impending threat or warning

denunciation

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪnʌnsieɪʃən )
Word forms: denunciations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounDenunciation of someone or something is severe public criticism of them. [+ of] ⇒ On September 24, he wrote a stinging denunciation of his critics. ⇒ He has been scathing in his denunciation of corrupt and incompetent politicians.2. variable nounDenunciation is the act of reporting someone who has broken a rule or law to the authorities. [+ of] ⇒ ...the denunciation of Jews to the Nazis during the Second World War.
