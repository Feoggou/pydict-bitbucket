oblivion

(əˈblɪvɪən )
Definitions
noun1. the condition of being forgotten or disregarded2. the state of being mentally withdrawn or blank3. law an intentional overlooking, esp of political offences; amnesty; pardon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin oblīviō forgetfulness, from oblīviscī to forget

oblivion for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(əblɪviən )
Definitions
1. uncount noun [usu into N]Oblivion is the state of not being aware of what is happening around you, for example because you are asleep or unconscious. unconsciousness ⇒ He just drank himself into oblivion. ⇒ Within the hour he had slipped once again into deep and dreamless oblivion.2. uncount noun [usu into N]Oblivion is the state of having been forgotten or of no longer being considered important. ⇒ It seems that the so-called new theory is likely to sink into oblivion.3. uncount nounIf you say that something is bombed or blasted into oblivion, you are emphasizing that it is completely destroyed. [emphasis] ⇒ An entire poor section of town was bombed into oblivion.
