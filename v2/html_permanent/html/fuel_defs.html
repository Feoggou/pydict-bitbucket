fuel

(fjʊəl )
Definitions
noun1. any substance burned as a source of heat or power, such as coal or petrol2. a. the material, containing a fissile substance, such as uranium-235, that produces energy in a nuclear reactorb. a substance that releases energy in a fusion reactor3. something that nourishes or builds up emotion, action, etcverbWord forms: fuels, fuelling, fuelledWord forms: US fuels, fueling or fueled4. to supply with or receive fuel
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fueller (ˈfueller) or US fueler (ˈfueler) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French feuaile, from feu fire, ultimately from Latin focus fireplace, hearth
