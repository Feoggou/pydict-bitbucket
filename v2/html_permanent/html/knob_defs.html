knob

(nɒb )
Definitions
noun1. a rounded projection from a surface, such as a lump on a tree trunk2. a handle of a door, drawer, etc, esp one that is rounded3. a round hill or knoll or morainic ridge4. British taboo a slang word for penis5. See and the same to you with knobs onverbWord forms: knobs, knobbing or knobbed6. (transitive) to supply or ornament with knobs7. (intransitive) to form into a knob; bulge8. British taboo to have sexual intercourse with (a person)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

knoblike (ˈknobˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Middle Low German knobbe knot in wood; see knop

knob for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒb )
Word forms: knobs
Definitions
1. countable nounA knob is a round handle on a door or drawer which you use in order to open or close it. ⇒ He turned the knob and pushed against the door.2. countable nounA knob is a rounded lump or ball on top of a post or stick. ⇒ A loose brass knob on the bedstead rattled.3. countable nounA knob is a round switch on a piece of machinery or equipment. ⇒ ...the volume knob.4. countable nounA knob of butter is a small amount of it. [mainly British] [+ of] ⇒ Top the steaming hot potatoes with a knob of butter.
