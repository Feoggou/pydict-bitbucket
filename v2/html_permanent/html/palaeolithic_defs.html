Palaeolithic

(ˌpælɪəʊˈlɪθɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the period of the emergence of primitive man and the manufacture of unpolished chipped stone tools, about 2.5 million to 3 million years ago until about 12 000 bc. See also Lower Palaeolithic, Middle Palaeolithic, Upper Palaeolithicadjective2. (sometimes not capital) of or relating to this period
Example sentences containing 'Palaeolithic'
Worn teeth with a discoloured crust up by the gums over a dark, hollow Palaeolithic mouth, maybe a stalactite coming down at the back there. Robert Wilson A DARKENING STAIN
