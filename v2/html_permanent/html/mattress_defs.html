mattress

(ˈmætrɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large flat pad with a strong cover, filled with straw, foam rubber, etc, and often incorporating coiled springs, used as a bed or as part of a bed2. Also called: Dutch mattress a woven mat of brushwood, poles, etc, used to protect an embankment, dyke, etc, from scour3. a concrete or steel raft or slab used as a foundation or footing. Sometimes shortened to: mat4. a network of reinforcing rods or expanded metal sheeting, used in reinforced concrete5. civil engineering another name for blinding (sense 3)

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Italian materasso, from Arabic almatrah place where something is thrown
