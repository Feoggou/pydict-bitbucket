slew

Powered by COBUILD
(sluː )
Word forms: slews , slewing , slewed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. Slew is the past tense of slay.2. ergative verb & verbIf a vehicle slews or is slewed across a road, it slides across it. [V adv/prep] ⇒ The bus slewed sideways. [V adv/prep] ⇒ A seven-ton lorry slewed across their path. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He slewed the car against the side of the building.3. countable noun [usu sing]A slew of things is a large number of them. [mainly US] [+ of] ⇒ There have been a whole slew of shooting incidents. ⇒ They dealt with a slew of other issues.
