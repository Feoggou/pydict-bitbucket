woe

(wəʊ )
Definitions
noun1. literary intense grief or misery2. (often plural) affliction or misfortune3. See woe betide someoneexclamation4. Also: woe is me archaic an exclamation of sorrow or distress
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English wā, wǣ; related to Old Saxon, Old High German wē, Old Norse vei, Gothic wai, Latin vae, Sanskrit uvē; see wail

woe for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(woʊ )
Word forms: woes
Definitions
1. uncount nounWoe is very great sadness. [literary] ⇒ He listened to my tale of woe. ⇒ All around women wailed their woe or screamed abuse.2. plural noun [usu with poss]You can refer to someone's problems as their woes. [written] ⇒ He did not tell his relatives and friends about his woes.3. → woe betide
