buttonhole

(ˈbʌtənˌhəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a slit in a garment, etc, through which a button is passed to fasten two surfaces together2. a flower or small bunch of flowers worn pinned to the lapel or in the buttonhole, esp at weddings, formal dances, etc. US name: boutonniereverb (transitive)3. to detain (a person) in conversation. Also called: buttonhold4. to make buttonholes in5. to sew with buttonhole stitch

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA buttonhole is a hole that you push a button through in order to fasten a shirt, coat, or other piece of clothing.2. countable nounA buttonhole is a flower that you wear on your coat or dress. [British]3. verbIf you buttonhole someone, you stop them and make them listen to you. [V n] ⇒ Several people buttonholed television reporters to explain to them their reasons for not voting.
