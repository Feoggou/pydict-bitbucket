incredulous

(ɪnˈkrɛdjʊləs )
Definitions
adjective(often foll by of) not prepared or willing to believe (something); unbelieving
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incredulously (inˈcredulously) adverbincredulousness (inˈcredulousness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkredʒʊləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is incredulous, they are unable to believe something because it is very surprising or shocking. ⇒ 'He made you do it?' Her voice was incredulous. ⇒ There was a brief, incredulous silence.incredulously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'You told Pete?' Rachel said incredulously. 'I can't believe it!'
