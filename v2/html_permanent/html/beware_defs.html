beware

(bɪˈwɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(usually used in the imperative or infinitive; often foll by of) to be cautious or wary (of); be on one's guard (against)
Word origin
C13 be war, from be (imperative) + war wary

beware

Powered by COBUILD
(bɪweəʳ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verb [only imper and inf]If you tell someone to beware of a person or thing, you are warning them that the person or thing may harm them or be dangerous. [V + of] ⇒ Beware of being too impatient with others. [V of n/-ing] ⇒ Motorists were warned to beware of slippery conditions. [V] ⇒ Beware, this recipe is not for slimmers.
Example sentences containing 'beware'
The anchorage is good there, but beware of their docks where ensorcelled ships may call down curses on your own vessels of honest wood.
