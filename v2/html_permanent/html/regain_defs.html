regain

Definitions
verb (rɪˈɡeɪn ) (transitive)1. to take or get back; recover2. to reach againnoun (ˈriːˌɡeɪn )3. the process of getting something back, esp lost weight ⇒ this regain was inevitable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪgeɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: regains , regaining , regained
Definitions
1. verbIf you regain something that you have lost, you get it back again. [V n] ⇒ Troops have regained control of the city. [V n] ⇒ It took him a while to regain his composure.2. verbIf you regain a place that you have left, you succeed in getting back there. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Davis went to regain his carriage.
