facsimile

(fækˈsɪmɪlɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a. an exact copy or reproductionb. (as modifier) ⇒ a facsimile publication2. an image produced by facsimile transmissionverb -les, -leing or -led3. (transitive) to make an exact copy of
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin fac simile! make something like it!, from facere to make + similis similar, like

Definitions
1. countable noun [N n]A facsimile of something is a copy or imitation of it. [formal] ⇒ ...a facsimile of his writing desk. ⇒ ...a facsimile edition of Beethoven's musical manuscripts.2. countable nounA facsimile is the same as a fax. [formal]
