dawdling

(ˈdɔːdəlɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. proceeding at a slow pace ⇒ The pace never rose above dawdling, and the whole thing took hours., ⇒ Dawdling shoppers have to be rounded up and ushered toward the exit.noun2. an act of going very slowly and wasting time ⇒ If it weren't for Lulu's dawdling we should have been back at the hotel an hour ago!, ⇒ Dawdling is a typical preschool behaviour.
Derived forms
dawdlingly (ˈdawdlingly) adverb
Example sentences containing 'dawdling'
But you must go dangling after him, dawdling and talking, and riding round twice as far as you needed. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSHe was hurrying along the pavement, quickening when we could make a yard or two, dawdling in each hiatus. Gash, Jonathan THE TARTAN RINGERS
