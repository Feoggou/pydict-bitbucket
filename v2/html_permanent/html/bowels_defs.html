bowels

(ˈbaʊəlz)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. innards; entrails2. literary the deep or innermost part (esp in the phrase the bowels of the earth) ⇒ deep in the bowels of the earth, ⇒ Lyn went off into the dark bowels of the building.3. archaic the emotions, esp of pity or sympathy
Example sentences containing 'bowels'
Then, with a chill of understanding which turned my bowels to ice, I realized that I was not smelling cigarette smoke. Matthews, Lew A CONVICTION OF GUILTIt was like extracting his hand from the bowels of something dead or dying. Clive Barker EVERVILLEIn fact nobody truly understood what was happening in the bowels of the house. Clive Barker COLDHEART CANYON
