chivalry

(ˈʃɪvəlrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ries1. the combination of qualities expected of an ideal knight, esp courage, honour, justice, and a readiness to help the weak2. courteous behaviour, esp towards women3. the medieval system and principles of knighthood4. knights, noblemen, etc, collectively
Word origin
C13: from Old French chevalerie, from chevalier

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounChivalry is polite, kind, and unselfish behaviour, especially by men towards women. ⇒ Marie seemed to revel in his old-fashioned chivalry.2. uncount nounIn the Middle Ages, chivalry was the set of rules and way of behaving which knights were expected to follow. ⇒ ...the age of chivalry.
