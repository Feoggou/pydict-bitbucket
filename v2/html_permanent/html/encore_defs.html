encore

(ˈɒŋkɔː )
Definitions
exclamation1. again; once more: used by an audience to demand an extra or repeated performancenoun2. an extra or repeated performance given in response to enthusiastic demandverb3. (transitive) to demand an extra or repeated performance of (a work, piece of music, etc) by (a performer)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from French: still, again, perhaps from Latin in hanc hōram until this hour

encore

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒŋkɔːʳ , -kɔːʳ) for learners of English
Word forms: encores , encoring, encored
Definitions
1. countable nounAn encore is a short extra performance at the end of a longer one, which an entertainer gives because the audience asks for it. ⇒ Lang's final encore last night was 'Barefoot'.2. verbIf an entertainer encores, they perform an encore. [informal] [V] ⇒ They encore with a superlative version of The Who's 'The Kids Are Alright'.3. convention'Encore' is the word shouted by classical concert audiences when they want a performer to perform an encore.
