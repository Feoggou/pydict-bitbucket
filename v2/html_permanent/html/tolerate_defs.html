tolerate

(ˈtɒləˌreɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to treat with indulgence, liberality, or forbearance2. to permit3. to be able to bear; put up with4. medicine to have tolerance for (a drug, poison, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tolerative (ˈtolerative) adjectivetolerator (ˈtolerˌator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin tolerāre sustain; related to thole2

Definitions
1. verbIf you tolerate a situation or person, you accept them although you do not particularly like them. [V n] ⇒ She can no longer tolerate the position that she's in. [V n] ⇒ The cousins tolerated each other, but did not really get on well together.toleration (tɒləreɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...his views on religious toleration, education, and politics.2. verbIf you can tolerate something unpleasant or painful, you are able to bear it. [V n] ⇒ The ability to tolerate pain varies from person to person.
