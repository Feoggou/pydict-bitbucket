prophylactic

(ˌprɒfɪˈlæktɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. protecting from or preventing disease2. protective or preventivenoun3. a prophylactic drug or device, esp a condom

Word Origin

C16: via French from Greek prophulaktikos, from prophulassein to guard by taking advance measures, from pro-2 + phulax a guard

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Prophylactic means concerned with preventing disease. [medicine] ⇒ Vaccination and other prophylactic measures can be carried out.2. countable nounA prophylactic is a substance or device used for preventing disease. [medicine] ⇒ The region began to use quinine successfully as a prophylactic.3. countable nounA prophylactic is a condom. [formal] ⇒ The prospect of teachers passing out prophylactics to their pupils has infuriated some parents.
