glean

(ɡliːn )
Definitions
verb1. to gather (something) slowly and carefully in small pieces ⇒ to glean information from the newspapers2. to gather (the useful remnants of a crop) from the field after harvesting
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gleanable (ˈgleanable) adjectivegleaner (ˈgleaner) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French glener, from Late Latin glennāre, probably of Celtic origin

Definitions
verbIf you glean something such as information or knowledge, you learn or collect it slowly and patiently, and perhaps indirectly. [V n + from] ⇒ At present we're gleaning information from all sources. [be V-ed + from] ⇒ 10,000 pages of evidence were gleaned from hundreds and hundreds of interviews.
