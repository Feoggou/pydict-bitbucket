restless

(ˈrɛstlɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. unable to stay still or quiet2. ceaselessly active or moving ⇒ the restless wind3. worried; anxious; uneasy4. not restful; without repose ⇒ a restless night
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

restless

Powered by COBUILD
(restləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are restless, you are bored, impatient, or dissatisfied, and you want to do something else. ⇒ By 1982, she was restless and needed a new impetus for her talent. ⇒ ...a major new initiative to placate the country's restless intellectuals.restlessness uncount noun ⇒ From the audience came increasing sounds of restlessness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is restless, they keep moving around because they find it difficult to keep still. ⇒ My father seemed very restless and excited.restlessness uncount noun ⇒ Karen complained of hyperactivity and restlessness.restlessly adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ He paced up and down restlessly, trying to put his thoughts in order.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you have a restless night, you do not sleep properly and when you wake up you feel tired and uncomfortable. ⇒ The shocking revelations of the 700-page report had caused him several restless nights. ⇒ Hurt had spent a restless few hours on the plane from Paris.
