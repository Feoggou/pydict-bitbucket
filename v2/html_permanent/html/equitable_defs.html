equitable

(ˈɛkwɪtəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. impartial or reasonable; fair; just ⇒ an equitable decision2. law relating to or valid in equity, as distinct from common law or statute law3. law (formerly) recognized in a court of equity only, as claims, rights, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

equitableness (ˈequitableness) nounequitably (ˈequitably) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from French équitable, from équité equity
