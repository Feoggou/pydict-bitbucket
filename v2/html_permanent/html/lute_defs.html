lute1

(luːt )
Definitions
nounan ancient plucked stringed instrument, consisting of a long fingerboard with frets and gut strings, and a body shaped like a sliced pear
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French lut, via Old Provençal from Arabic al 'ūd, literally: the wood

lute2

(luːt )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: luting a mixture of cement and clay used to seal the joints between pipes, etc2. dentistry a thin layer of cement used to fix a crown or inlay in place on a toothverb3. (transitive) to seal (a joint or surface) with lute
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French ultimately from Latin lutum clay
