insure

(ɪnˈʃʊə , -ˈʃɔː)
Definitions
verb1. (often foll by against) to guarantee or protect (against risk, loss, etc) ⇒ we insured against disappointment by making an early reservation2. (often foll by against) to issue (a person) with an insurance policy or take out an insurance policy (on) ⇒ his house was heavily insured against fire, ⇒ after all his car accidents the company refuses to insure him again3. another word (esp US) for ensure (sense 1), ensure (sense 2)

(ɪnʃʊəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: insures , insuring , insured
Definitions
1. verbIf you insure yourself or your property, you pay money to an insurance company so that, if you become ill or if your property is damaged or stolen, the company will pay you a sum of money. [V n] ⇒ For protection against unforeseen emergencies, you insure your house, your furnishings and your car. [V + against/for] ⇒ Think carefully before you insure against accident, sickness and redundancy. [V n + against] ⇒ We automatically insure your belongings against fire and theft. [Also V n + for]2. verbIf you insure yourself against something unpleasant that might happen in the future, you do something to protect yourself in case it happens, or to prevent it happening. [V pron-refl + against] ⇒ He insured himself against failure by treating only people he was sure he could cure. [V + against] ⇒ All the electronics in the world cannot insure against accidents, though.3. → See also ensure
