clownish

(klaʊnɪʃ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person's appearance or behaviour as clownish, you mean that they look or behave rather like a clown, and often that they appear rather foolish. ⇒ He had a clownish sense of humour. ⇒ ...their clownish stupidity.

A small, busy-looking woman with a mop of red curls that made her look slightly clownish , she smiled at Malone. Cleary, Jon MURDER SONGOn either side of him the grotesque, clownish faces; set in the brilliant mosaic walls assumed a more menacing, protective aspect.
