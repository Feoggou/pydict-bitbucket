Tudor

(ˈtjuːdə )
Definitions
noun1. an English royal house descended from a Welsh squire, Owen Tudor (died 1461), and ruling from 1485 to 1603. Monarchs of the Tudor line were Henry VII, Henry VIII, Edward VI, Mary I, and Elizabeth Iadjective2. denoting a style of architecture of the late perpendicular period and characterized by half-timbered houses
