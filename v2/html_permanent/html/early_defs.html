early

(ˈɜːlɪ )
Definitions
adjective -lier or -liest1. before the expected or usual time2. occurring in or characteristic of the first part of a period or sequence3. occurring in or characteristic of a period far back in time4. occurring in the near future5. See at the earliest6. See early days7. See early doorsadverbWord forms: -lier8. before the expected or usual time9. near the first part of a period or sequence ⇒ I was talking to him earlier
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

earliness (ˈearliness) noun

Word Origin

Old English ǣrlīce, from ǣr ere + -līce -ly2; related to Old Norse arliga

early

Powered by COBUILD
(ɜːʳli ) for learners of English
Word forms: earlier , earliest
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v]Early means before the usual time that a particular event or activity happens. ⇒ I knew I had to get up early. ⇒ Why do we have to go to bed so early?Early is also an adjective. ⇒ I decided that I was going to take early retirement. ⇒ I planned an early night.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Early means near the beginning of a day, week, year, or other period of time. ⇒ ...in the 1970s and the early 1980s. ⇒ ...a few weeks in early summer. ⇒ She was in her early teens. ⇒ ...the early hours of Saturday morning.Early is also an adverb. ⇒ We'll hope to see you some time early next week. ⇒ ...early in the season.3. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v]Early means before the time that was arranged or expected. ⇒ She arrived early to secure a place at the front. ⇒ The first snow came a month earlier than usual.Early is also an adjective. ⇒ I'm always early.4. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Early means near the beginning of a period in history, or in the history of something such as the world, a society, or an activity. ⇒ ...the early stages of pregnancy. ⇒ ...Fassbinder's early films. ⇒ ...the early days of the occupation. ⇒ It's too early to declare his efforts a success.5. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Early means near the beginning of something such as a piece of work or a process. ⇒ ...the book's early chapters.Early is also an adverb. ⇒ ...an incident which occurred much earlier in the game.6. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Early refers to plants which flower or crop before or at the beginning of the main season. ⇒ ...these early cabbages and cauliflowers.Early is also an adverb. ⇒ ...early flowering shrubs.7. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Early reports or indications of something are the first reports or indications about it. [formal] ⇒ The early indications look encouraging. ⇒ Earlier reports that troops opened fire are now being denied.8. →  as early as9. →  it's early days
