wick1

(wɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a cord or band of loosely twisted or woven fibres, as in a candle, cigarette lighter, etc, that supplies fuel to a flame by capillary action2. See get on someone's wick

Derived Forms

wicking (ˈwicking) noun

Word Origin

Old English weoce; related to Old High German wioh, Middle Dutch wēke (Dutch wiek)
