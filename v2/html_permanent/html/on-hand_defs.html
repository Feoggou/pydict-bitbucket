on hand for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
phraseIf someone or something is on hand, they are near and able to be used if they are needed. ⇒ The Bridal Department will have experts on hand to give you all the help and advice you need. ⇒ There was simply no cash on hand to meet the cost of food. →  hand

on hand in Retail

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒn hænd)
Definitions
phrase(Retail: Merchandising) Goods on hand are physically present, for example in a stock room or on display. ⇒ Last week there were only 5 units, the last we had in stock, but I'll check if we have more stock on hand now. ⇒ Your chosen supplier should ideally have the merchandise already on hand in their warehouse and ready to send out as needed. ⇒ Goods on hand are physically present, for example in a stock room or on display.
