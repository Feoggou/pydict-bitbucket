forth

(fɔːθ )
Definitions
adverb1. forward in place, time, order, or degree2. out, as from concealment, seclusion, or inaction3. away, as from a place or country4. and so on; et ceterapreposition5. archaic out of; away from
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English; related to Middle High German vort; see for, further

Forth

(fɔːθ )
Definitions
noun1. See Firth of Forth2. a river in S Scotland, flowing generally east to the Firth of Forth. Length: about 104 km (65 miles)

forth

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳθ ) for learners of English
Definitions
In addition to the uses shown below, forth is also used in the phrasal verbs 'put forth' and 'set forth'.1. adverb [ADV after v]When someone goes forth from a place, they leave it. [literary] ⇒ Go forth into the desert. ⇒ I came forth to take the air.2. adverb [ADV after v]If one thing brings forth another, the first thing produces the second. [literary] ⇒ Nature herself brings forth new forms of life. ⇒ My reflections brought forth no conclusion.3. adverb [ADV after v]When someone or something is brought forth, they are brought to a place or moved into a position where people can see them. [literary] ⇒ Pilate ordered Jesus to be brought forth. ⇒ He brought forth a small gold amulet from beneath his robe.4. → back and forth5. → to hold forth
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
