advertise

or sometimes US advertize (ˈædvəˌtaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to present or praise (goods, a service, etc) to the public, esp in order to encourage sales2. to make (something, such as a vacancy, article for sale, etc) publicly known, as to possible applicants, buyers, etc ⇒ to advertise a job3. (intransitive; foll by for) to make a public request (for), esp in a newspaper, etc ⇒ she advertised for a cook4. obsolete to warn; caution
Derived forms
ˈadverˌtiser or sometimes US ˈadverˌtizer noun
Word origin
C15: from a lengthened stem of Old French avertir, ultimately from Latin advertere to turn one's attention to. See adverse

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you advertise something such as a product, an event, or a job, you tell people about it in newspapers, on television, or on posters in order to encourage them to buy the product, go to the event, or apply for the job. [V n] ⇒ The players can advertise baked beans, but not rugby boots. [V n] ⇒ In 1991, the house was advertised for sale at $49,000. [V] ⇒ Religious groups are currently not allowed to advertise on television.2. verbIf you advertise for someone to do something for you, for example to work for you or share your accommodation, you announce it in a newspaper, on television, or on a notice board. [V + for] ⇒ We advertised for staff in a local newspaper. [V for n] ⇒ I shall advertise for someone to go with me.3. verbIf someone or something advertises a particular quality, they show it in their appearance or behaviour. [literary] emphasize [V n] ⇒ His hard sinewy body advertised his ruthlessness of purpose.4. verbIf you do not advertise the fact that something is the case, you try not to let other people know about it. [V n] ⇒ There is no need to advertise the fact that you are a single woman. ⇒ I didn't want to advertise the fact that he hadn't driven me to the airport.5. → see also advertising
