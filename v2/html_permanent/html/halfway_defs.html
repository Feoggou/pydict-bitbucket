halfway

(ˌhɑːfˈweɪ )
Definitions
adverb, adjective1. at or to half the distance; at or to the middle2. in or of an incomplete manner or nature3. See meet halfway

halfway

Powered by COBUILD
(hɑːfweɪ , US hæf- ) also half-way for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [oft ADV after v]Halfway means in the middle of a place or between two points, at an equal distance from each of them. ⇒ Half-way across the car-park, he noticed she was walking with her eyes closed. ⇒ He was halfway up the ladder. ⇒ She was halfway down when she heard the noise.2. adverbHalfway means in the middle of a period of time or of an event. ⇒ By then, it was October and we were more than halfway through our tour.Halfway is also an adjective. ⇒ Welsh international Matthew Postle was third fastest at the halfway point.3. →  to meet someone halfway4. adverb [ADV adj]Halfway means reasonably. [informal] ⇒ You need hard currency to get anything halfway decent. ⇒ All I had to do was be halfway cool.
