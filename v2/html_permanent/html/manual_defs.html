manual

(ˈmænjʊəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to a hand or hands2. operated or done by hand ⇒ manual controls3. physical, as opposed to mental or mechanical ⇒ manual labour4. by human labour rather than automatic or computer-aided means5. of, relating to, or resembling a manualnoun6. a book, esp of instructions or information ⇒ a car manual7. music one of the keyboards played by hand on an organ8. military the prescribed drill with small arms

Derived Forms

manually (ˈmanually) adverb

Word Origin

C15: via Old French from Latin manuālis, from manus hand

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Manual work is work in which you use your hands or your physical strength rather than your mind. ⇒ ...skilled manual workers. ⇒ They have no reservations about taking factory or manual jobs.2. adjective [ADJ n]Manual is used to talk about movements which are made by someone's hands. [formal] ⇒ ...toys designed to help develop manual dexterity.3. adjective [ADJ n]Manual means operated by hand, rather than by electricity or a motor. ⇒ There is a manual pump to get rid of the water.manually adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The device is manually operated, using a simple handle.4. countable nounA manual is a book which tells you how to do something or how a piece of machinery works. ⇒ ...the instruction manual.
