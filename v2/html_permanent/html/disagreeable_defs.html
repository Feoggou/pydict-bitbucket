disagreeable

(ˌdɪsəˈɡriːəbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. not likable, esp bad-tempered, offensive, or disobliging ⇒ disagreeable remarks2. not to one's liking; unpleasant ⇒ a disagreeable task
Derived forms
ˌdisagreeableness (ˌdisaˈgreeableness) or ˌdisaˌgreeability (ˌdisaˌgreeaˈbility) nounˌdisagreeably (ˌdisaˈgreeably) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is disagreeable is rather unpleasant. ⇒ ...a disagreeable odour. ⇒ ...a comfort kit designed to make flying an altogether less disagreeable experience.disagreeably (dɪsəgriːəbli ) adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV with v] ⇒ The taste is bitter and disagreeably pungent.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Someone who is disagreeable is unfriendly or unhelpful. ⇒ He's a shallow, disagreeable man.
