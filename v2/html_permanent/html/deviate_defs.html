deviate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈdiːvɪˌeɪt )1. (usually intr) to differ or diverge or cause to differ or diverge, as in belief or thought2. (usually intr) to turn aside or cause to turn aside; diverge or cause to diverge3. (intransitive) psychology to depart from an accepted standard or conventionnoun, adjective (ˈdiːvɪɪt )4. another word for deviant
Derived forms
deviˌator (ˈdeviˌator) noundeviatory (ˈdeviatory) adjective
Word origin
C17: from Late Latin dēviāre to turn aside from the direct road, from de- + via road

deviate

Powered by COBUILD
(diːvieɪt )
Word forms: deviates , deviating , deviated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbTo deviate from something means to start doing something different or not planned, especially in a way that causes problems for others. [V + from] ⇒ They stopped you as soon as you deviated from the script. [V from n] ⇒ He planned his schedule far in advance, and he didn't deviate from it. [V from n] ⇒ From very early on he believed a military revolution was necessary and he has never deviated from that ideological path. [Also V]
