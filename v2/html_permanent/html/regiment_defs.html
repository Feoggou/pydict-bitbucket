regiment

Definitions
noun (ˈrɛdʒɪmənt )1. a military formation varying in size from a battalion to a number of battalions2. a large number in regular or organized groups ⇒ regiments of beer bottlesverb (ˈrɛdʒɪˌmɛnt ) (transitive)3. to force discipline or order on, esp in a domineering manner4. to organize into a regiment or regiments5. to form into organized groups6. to assign to a regiment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

regimental (ˌregiˈmental) adjectiveregimentally (ˌregiˈmentally) adverbregimentation (ˌregimenˈtation) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Late Latin regimentum government, from Latin regere to rule
