spatter

(ˈspætə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to scatter or splash (a substance, esp a liquid) or (of a substance) to splash (something) in scattered drops ⇒ to spatter mud on the car, ⇒ mud spattered in her face2. (transitive) to sprinkle, cover, or spot (with a liquid)3. (transitive) to slander or defame4. (intransitive) to shower or rain down ⇒ bullets spattered around themnoun5. the sound of something spattering6. something spattered, such as a spot or splash7. the act or an instance of spattering

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin; related to Low German, Dutch spatten to spout, Frisian spatteren to splash

(spætəʳ )
Word forms: spatters , spattering , spattered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
ergative verb & verbIf a liquid spatters a surface or you spatter a liquid over a surface, drops of the liquid fall on an area of the surface. [V prep] ⇒ He stared at the rain spattering on the glass. [V n prep] ⇒ Gently turn the fish, being careful not to spatter any hot butter on yourself. [V n] ⇒ Blood spattered the dark concrete. [V n with n] ⇒ I always spatter my blouse with gravy when I eat. [V-ed] ⇒ Her dress was spattered with mud.
