supplicant

(ˈsʌplɪkənt ) or suppliant
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who supplicatesadjective2. entreating humbly; supplicating

Word Origin

C16: from Latin supplicāns beseeching; see supple

supplicant

Powered by COBUILD
(sʌplɪkənt )
Word forms: supplicants
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA supplicant is a person who prays to God or respectfully asks an important person to help them or to give them something that they want very much. [formal] ⇒ He flung himself down in the flat submissive posture of a mere supplicant.
