capitulate

(kəˈpɪtjʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intransitive) to surrender, esp under agreed conditions
Derived forms
capituˌlator (caˈpituˌlator) noun
Word origin
C16 (meaning: to arrange under heads, draw up in order; hence, to make terms of surrender): from Medieval Latin capitulare to draw up under heads, from capitulum chapter

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you capitulate, you stop resisting and do what someone else wants you to do. [V] ⇒ The club eventually capitulated and now grants equal rights to women. [V + to] ⇒ In less than two hours Cohen capitulated to virtually every demand.capitulation (kəpɪtʃʊleɪʃən ) uncount noun [also a N] submission ⇒ Lord Luce's capitulation to pressure from the Duke of Edinburgh has stored up huge problems for the future.
