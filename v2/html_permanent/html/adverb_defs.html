adverb

(ˈædˌvɜːb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna. a word or group of words that serves to modify a whole sentence, a verb, another adverb, or an adjective; for example, probably, easily, very, and happily respectively in the sentence They could probably easily envy the very happily married coupleb. (as modifier) ⇒ an adverb marker Abbreviation: adv
Word origin
C15–C16: from Latin adverbium adverb, literally: added word, a translation of Greek epirrhēma a word spoken afterwards
