nickname

(ˈnɪkˌneɪm )
Definitions
noun1. a familiar, pet, or derisory name given to a person, animal, or place ⇒ his nickname was Lefty because he was left-handed2. a shortened or familiar form of a person's name ⇒ Joe is a nickname for Josephverb3. (transitive) to call by a nickname; give a nickname to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15 a nekename, mistaken division of an ekename an additional name, from eke addition + name

Definitions
1. countable nounA nickname is an informal name for someone or something. ⇒ Red got his nickname for his red hair.2. verbIf you nickname someone or something, you give them an informal name. [V n n] ⇒ When he got older I nicknamed him Little Alf. [be V-ed n] ⇒ Which newspaper was once nicknamed The Thunderer?
