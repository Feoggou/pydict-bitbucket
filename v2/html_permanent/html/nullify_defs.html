nullify

(ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to render legally void or of no effect2. to render ineffective or useless; cancel out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nullification (ˌnullifiˈcation) nounnullifier (ˈnulliˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin nullificāre to despise, from Latin nullus of no account + facere to make

Powered by COBUILD
(nʌlɪfaɪ )
Word forms: nullifies , nullifying , nullified
Definitions
1. verbTo nullify a legal decision or procedure means to declare that it is not legally valid. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He used his broad executive powers to nullify decisions by local governments. [be V-ed] ⇒ It is worth remembering that previous wills are nullified automatically upon marriage.2. verbTo nullify something means to make it have no effect. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He may be able to nullify that disadvantage by offering a wider variety of produce. [V n] ⇒ This, of course, would nullify the effect of the move and merely accelerate inflation.
