vigorous

(ˈvɪɡərəs )
Definitions
adjective1. endowed with bodily or mental strength or vitality; robust2. displaying, involving, characterized by, or performed with vigour ⇒ vigorous growth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

vigorous

Powered by COBUILD
(vɪgərəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveVigorous physical activities involve using a lot of energy, usually to do short and repeated actions. ⇒ Very vigorous exercise can increase the risk of heart attacks. ⇒ African dance is vigorous, but full of subtlety.vigorously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ He shook his head vigorously. ⇒ She shivered and rubbed her arms vigorously.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A vigorous person does things with great energy and enthusiasm. A vigorous campaign or activity is done with great energy and enthusiasm. ⇒ Sir Robert was a strong and vigorous politician. ⇒ ...the most vigorous critics of the government. ⇒ They will take vigorous action to recover the debts. ⇒ ...a vigorous campaign against GM food.vigorously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The police vigorously denied that excessive force had been used.3. graded adjectiveA vigorous person is strong and healthy and full of energy. ⇒ He was a vigorous, handsome young man.
