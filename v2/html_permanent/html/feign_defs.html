feign

(feɪn )
Definitions
verb1. to put on a show of (a quality or emotion); pretend ⇒ to feign innocence2. (transitive) to make up; invent ⇒ to feign an excuse3. (transitive) to copy; imitate ⇒ to feign someone's laugh
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

feigner (ˈfeigner) nounfeigningly (ˈfeigningly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old French feindre to pretend, from Latin fingere to form, shape, invent

Powered by COBUILD
(feɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: feigns , feigning , feigned
Definitions
verbIf someone feigns a particular feeling, attitude, or physical condition, they try to make other people think that they have it or are experiencing it, although this is not true. [formal] [V n] ⇒ One morning, I didn't want to go to school, and decided to feign illness. [V-ed] ⇒ 'Giles phoned this morning,' Mirella said with feigned indifference. [Also V to-inf]
