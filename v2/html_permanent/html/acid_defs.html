acid

(ˈæsɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any substance that dissociates in water to yield a sour corrosive solution containing hydrogen ions, having a pH of less than 7, and turning litmus red. See also Lewis acid2. a sour-tasting substance3. a slang name for LSDadjective4. chemistrya. of, derived from, or containing acid ⇒ an acid radicalb. being or having the properties of an acid ⇒ sodium bicarbonate is an acid salt5. sharp or sour in taste6. cutting, sharp, or hurtful in speech, manner, etc; vitriolic; caustic7. (of rain, snow, etc) containing pollutant acids in solution8. (of igneous rocks) having a silica content of more than 60% of the total and containing at least one tenth quartz9. metallurgy of or made by a process in which the furnace or converter is lined with an acid material ⇒ acid steel
Derived forms
ˈacidly adverbˈacidness nounˈacidy adjective
Word origin
C17: (first used by Francis Bacon): from French acide or Latin acidus, from acēre to be sour or sharp

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounAn acid is a chemical substance, usually a liquid, which contains hydrogen and can react with other substances to form salts. Some acids burn or dissolve other substances that they come into contact with. OPPOSITE: alkali ⇒ ...citric acid. ⇒ Acids in the stomach destroy the virus.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn acid substance contains acid. ⇒ These shrubs must have an acid, lime-free soil.acidity (æsɪdɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the acidity of rainwater.3. graded adjectiveAn acid fruit or drink has a sour or sharp taste. acidic ⇒ These wines may taste rather hard and somewhat acid.acidity uncount noun ⇒ ...a finely balanced wine with ripe acidity and soft fruit flavours.4. graded adjectiveAn acid remark, or acid humour, is very unkind or critical. ⇒ This comedy of contemporary manners is told with compassion and acid humour.acidly graded adverb [usu ADV with v, also ADV adj] ⇒ 'You don't know how to be a mother and you never did,' she said acidly.5. uncount nounThe drug LSD is sometimes referred to as acid. [informal]6. → see also amino acid, hydrochloric acid, nitric acid, nucleic acid, sulphuric acid
