constancy

(ˈkɒnstənsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the quality of having a resolute mind, purpose, or affection; steadfastness2. freedom from change or variation; stability3. psychology the perceptual phenomenon in which attributes of an object appear to remain the same in a variety of different presentations, e.g., a given object looks roughly the same size regardless of its distance from the observer4. ecology the frequency of occurrence of a particular species in sample plots from a plant community

(kɒnstənsi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounConstancy is the quality of staying the same even though other things change. ⇒ Climate reflects a basic struggle between constancy and change. ⇒ We live in a world without constancy.2. uncount nounConstancy is the quality of being faithful and loyal to a particular person or belief. [approval] ⇒ ...those who have proved their constancy in love.
