pseudonym

(ˈsjuːdəˌnɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna fictitious name adopted, esp by an author

Derived Forms

ˌpseudonymity (ˌpseudoˈnymity) noun

Word Origin

C19: via French from Greek pseudōnumon

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA pseudonym is a name which someone, usually a writer, uses instead of his or her real name. [+ of/for] ⇒ Both plays were published under the pseudonym of Philip Dayre.

The rules of entry required that, in the interests of impartial judging, entrants used a pseudonym under their story title.
