touchy

Powered by COBUILD
(tʌtʃi ) for learners of English
Word forms: touchier , touchiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as touchy, you mean that they are easily upset, offended, or irritated. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ She is very touchy about her past. ⇒ Don't be so touchy.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you say that something is a touchy subject, you mean that it is a subject that needs to be dealt with carefully and in a sensitive way, because it might upset or offend people. ⇒ ...the touchy question of political reform.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
