doze

(dəʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to sleep lightly or intermittently2. (often foll by off) to fall into a light sleepnoun3. a short sleep
Derived forms
dozer (ˈdozer) noun
Word origin
C17: probably from Old Norse dūs lull; related to Danish döse to drowse, Swedish dialect dusa slumber

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen you doze, you sleep lightly or for a short period, especially during the daytime. [V] ⇒ For a while she dozed fitfully.Doze is also a noun. ⇒ After lunch I had a doze.► →  doze off
