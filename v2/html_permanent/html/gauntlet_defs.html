gauntlet1

(ˈɡɔːntlɪt ) or gantlet
Definitions
noun1. a medieval armoured leather glove2. a heavy glove with a long cuff3. See take up the gauntlet4. See throw down the gauntlet
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Old French gantelet, diminutive of gant glove, of Germanic origin

gauntlet2

(ˈɡɔːntlɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a punishment in which the victim is forced to run between two rows of men who strike at him as he passes: formerly a military punishment2. See run the gauntlet3. a testing ordeal; trial4. a variant spelling of gantlet1 (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: changed (through influence of gauntlet1) from earlier gantlope; see gantlet1
