dominate

(ˈdɒmɪˌneɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to control, rule, or govern (someone or something)2. to tower above (surroundings, etc); overlook3. (tr; usually passive) to predominate in (something or someone)
Derived forms
domiˌnating (ˈdomiˌnating) adjectivedomiˌnatingly (ˈdomiˌnatingly) adverbdominative (ˈdominative) adjectivedomiˌnator (ˈdomiˌnator) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin dominārī to be lord over, from dominus lord

(dɒmɪneɪt )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo dominate a situation means to be the most powerful or important person or thing in it. [V n] ⇒ The book is expected to dominate the best-seller lists. [V n] ⇒ ...countries where life is dominated by war. [V] ⇒ No single factor appears to dominate.domination (dɒmɪneɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the domination of the market by a small number of organizations.2. verbIf one country or person dominates another, they have power over them. [V n] ⇒ He denied that his country wants to dominate Europe. [V n] ⇒ Women are no longer dominated by the men in their relationships. [V] ⇒ The countries of Eastern Europe immediately started to dominate.domination uncount noun ⇒ They had five centuries of domination by the Romans.3. verbIf a building, mountain, or other object dominates an area, it is so large or impressive that you cannot avoid seeing it. [V n] ⇒ It's one of the biggest buildings in this area, and it really dominates this whole place. [V n] ⇒ ...its skyline dominated by the central mosque.
