whereabouts

(ˈwɛərəˌbaʊts )
Definitions
adverb1. Also: whereabout at what approximate location or place; where ⇒ whereabouts are you?2. obsolete about or concerning whichnoun3. (functioning as singular or plural) the place, esp the approximate place, where a person or thing is

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
pronunciation note:   Pronounced (hweərəbaʊts ) for meaning [sense 1], and (hweərəbaʊts ) for meanings [sense 2] and [sense 3].1. singular noun [with sing or pl verb, with poss]If you refer to the whereabouts of a particular person or thing, you mean the place where that person or thing may be found. [+ of] ⇒ The police are anxious to hear from anyone who may know the whereabouts of the firearms. ⇒ Once he knew his father's name, finding his whereabouts proved surprisingly easy.2. adverbYou use whereabouts in questions when you are asking precisely where something is. [+ in] ⇒ Whereabouts in Liverpool are you from? ⇒ Whereabouts are you living? ⇒ 'I actually live near Chester.'—'Whereabouts?'3. subordinating conjunction & conjunctionYou use whereabouts after certain words, especially verbs and adjectives, to introduce a clause in which you mention precisely where something is situated or happens. ⇒ I live in a village near to Germaine Greer and know whereabouts she lives.
