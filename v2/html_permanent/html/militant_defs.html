militant

(ˈmɪlɪtənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. aggressive or vigorous, esp in the support of a cause ⇒ a militant protest2. warring; engaged in warfarenoun3. a militant person

Derived Forms

militancy (ˈmilitancy) or rare militantness (ˈmilitantness) nounmilitantly (ˈmilitantly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin mīlitāre to be a soldier, from mīles soldier

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveYou use militant to describe people who believe in something very strongly and are active in trying to bring about political or social change, often in extreme ways that other people find unacceptable. ⇒ Militant mineworkers in the Ukraine have voted for a one-day stoppage next month. ⇒ ...one of the most active militant groups.Militant is also a noun. ⇒ The militants might still find some new excuse to call a strike.militancy uncount noun ⇒ ...the rise of trade union militancy.militantly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ Their army is militantly nationalist.
