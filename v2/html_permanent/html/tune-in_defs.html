tune in

Definitions
verb (adverb; often foll by to)1. to adjust (a radio or television) to receive (a station or programme)2. slang to make or become more aware, knowledgeable, etc (about)

tune in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you tune in to a particular television or radio station or programme, you watch or listen to it. [V P + to] ⇒ More than six million youngsters tune in to Blockbusters every day. [V P] ⇒ The idea that people plan their radio listening is nonsense; most tune in impulsively.2. phrasal verbIf you tune in to something such as your own or other people's feelings, you become aware of them. [V P + to] ⇒ You can start now to tune in to your own physical, social and spiritual needs.3. → See also tuned in →  tune
