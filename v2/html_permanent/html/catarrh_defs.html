catarrh

(kəˈtɑː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. inflammation of a mucous membrane with increased production of mucus, esp affecting the nose and throat in the common cold2. the mucus so formed
Derived forms
catarrhal (caˈtarrhal) or catarrhous (caˈtarrhous) adjective
Word origin
C16: via French from Late Latin catarrhus, from Greek katarrous, from katarrhein to flow down, from kata- down + rhein to flow

Definitions

uncount nounCatarrh is a medical condition in which a lot of mucus is produced in your nose and throat. You may get catarrh when you have a cold.
