disarm

(dɪsˈɑːm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to remove defensive or offensive capability from (a country, army, etc)2. (transitive) to deprive of weapons3. (transitive) to remove the triggering device of (a bomb, shell, etc)4. (transitive) to win the confidence or affection of5. (intransitive) (of a nation, etc) to decrease the size and capability of one's armed forces6. (intransitive) to lay down weapons
Derived forms
disarmer (disˈarmer) noun

Word forms: disarms , disarming , disarmed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo disarm a person or group means to take away all their weapons. [V n] ⇒ We will agree to disarming troops and leaving their weapons at military positions.2. verbIf a country or group disarms, it gives up the use of weapons, especially nuclear weapons. [V] ⇒ There has also been a suggestion that the forces in Lebanon should disarm. [V pron-refl] ⇒ We're not ready to disarm ourselves in order to make it easier for them to kill us.3. verbIf a person or their behaviour disarms you, they cause you to feel less angry, hostile, or critical towards them. [V n] ⇒ His unease disarmed her. [V n] ⇒ She did her best to disarm her critics.
