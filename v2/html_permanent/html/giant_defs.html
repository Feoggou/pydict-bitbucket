giant

(ˈdʒaɪənt )
Definitions
noun1. a mythical figure of superhuman size and strength, esp in folklore or fairy tales. Also (feminine): giantess (ˈdʒaɪəntɪs)2. a person or thing of exceptional size, reputation, etc ⇒ a giant in nuclear physics3. Greek mythology any of the large and powerful offspring of Uranus (sky) and Gaea (earth) who rebelled against the Olympian gods but were defeated in battle4. pathology a person suffering from gigantism5. astronomy See giant star6. mining another word for monitor (sense 8)adjective7. remarkably or supernaturally large8. architecture another word for colossal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

giant-like (ˈgiant-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French geant, from Vulgar Latin gagās (unattested), from Latin gigās, gigant-, from Greek

giant

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒaɪənt ) for learners of English
Word forms: giants
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Something that is described as giant is much larger or more important than most others of its kind. ⇒ ...Italy's giant car maker, Fiat. ⇒ ...a giant oak table. ⇒ ...a giant step towards unification with the introduction of monetary union.2. countable noun [usu n N]Giant is often used to refer to any large, successful business organization or country. [journalism] ⇒ ...Japanese electronics giant Sony. ⇒ ...one of Germany's industrial giants, Daimler-Benz.3. countable nounA giant is an imaginary person who is very big and strong, especially one mentioned in old stories. ⇒ ...a Nordic saga of giants.4. countable noun [usu a N of n]You can refer to someone, especially a man, as a giant, if they seem important or powerful or if they are big and strong. ⇒ He has enormous charisma. He is a giant of a man. ⇒ The biggest man in the patrol, a giant of a man, lifted Mattie on to his shoulders.5. countable nounYou can refer to someone such as a famous musician or writer as a giant, if they are regarded as one of the most important or successful people in their field. ⇒ ...the giant of opera, Luciano Pavarotti. ⇒ He was without question one of the giants of Japanese literature.
