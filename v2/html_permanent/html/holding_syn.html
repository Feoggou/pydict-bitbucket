Synonyms of holding

holding (often plural)

noun

  1. property, securities, investments, resources, estate, assets, possessions, stocks and shares, land interests
    • His wealth comes from holdings in oil, gas, land and property.
  2. property, resources, assets, possessions

hold

verb

  1. carry, keep, grip, grasp, cling to, clasp, HD (text messaging)
    • Hold the baby while I load the car.
  2. support, take, bear, shoulder, sustain, prop, brace, HD (text messaging)
    • Hold the weight with a straight arm above your head.
    OPPOSITES:  break, give way, loosen, come undone
  3. embrace, grasp, clutch, hug, squeeze, cradle, clasp, enfold, HD (text messaging)
    • If only he would hold her close to him.
  4. restrain, constrain, check, bind, curb, hamper, hinder, HD (text messaging)
    • He was held in an arm lock.
    OPPOSITES:  free, release, let go, let loose
  5. detain, arrest, confine, imprison, impound, pound, hold in custody, put in jail, HD (text messaging)
    • the return of two seamen held on spying charges
    OPPOSITES:  release, free
  6. accommodate, take, contain, seat, comprise, have a capacity for, HD (text messaging)
    • The small bottles don't seem to hold much.
  7. consider, think, believe, view, judge, regard, maintain, assume, reckon, esteem, deem, presume, entertain the idea, HD (text messaging)
    • She holds that it is not admissible to ordain women.
    OPPOSITES:  deny, reject, put down, refute, disavow, disclaim
  8. occupy, have, fill, maintain, retain, possess, hold down (informal), HD (text messaging)
    • She has never held a ministerial post.
  9. possess, have, own, bear, retain, be in possession of, HD (text messaging)
    • Applicants should normally hold an Honours degree.
    OPPOSITES:  give, offer, give up, hand over, give away, turn over, bestow
  10. conduct, convene, have, call, run, celebrate, carry on, assemble, preside over, officiate at, solemnize, HD (text messaging)
    • They held frequent consultations concerning technical problems.
    OPPOSITES:  cancel, postpone, call off
  11. (sometimes with up) continue, last, remain, stay, wear, resist, endure, persist, persevere, HD (text messaging)
    • Our luck couldn't hold for ever.
  12. apply, exist, be the case, stand up, operate, be in force, remain true, hold good, remain valid, HD (text messaging)
    • Today, most people think that argument no longer holds.
  13. keep, catch, maintain, capture, absorb, engross, HD (text messaging)
    • Didn't it hold your attention?

noun

  1. grip, grasp, clutch, clasp, HD (text messaging)
    • He released his hold on the camera.
  2. foothold, footing, purchase, leverage, vantage, anchorage, HD (text messaging)
    • The idea didn't really get a hold in this country.
  3. control, authority, influence, pull (informal), sway, dominance, clout (informal), mastery, dominion, ascendancy, mana (New Zealand), HD (text messaging)
    • It's always useful to have a hold over people.
