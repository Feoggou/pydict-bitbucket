rearrange

(ˌriːəˈreɪndʒ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to put (something) into a new order ⇒ to rearrange the lighting2. to put (something) back in its original order after it has been displaced3. to fix a new date or time for (something postponed) ⇒ to rearrange a match

rearrange

Powered by COBUILD
(riːəreɪndʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: rearranges , rearranging , rearranged
Definitions
1. verbIf you rearrange things, you change the way in which they are organized or ordered. [V n] ⇒ When she returned, she found Malcolm had rearranged all her furniture. [V n] ⇒ A waiter was rapidly rearranging tables for the big group.2. verbIf you rearrange a meeting or an appointment, you arrange for it to take place at a different time to that originally intended. [V n] ⇒ You may cancel or rearrange the appointment. [V n] ⇒ The meeting on 31 October is rearranged for 30 January.
