honk

(hɒŋk )
Definitions
noun1. a representation of the sound made by a goose2. any sound resembling this, esp a motor horn3. British and Australian slang a bad smellverb4. to make or cause (something) to make such a sound5. (intransitive) British a slang word for vomit6. British and Australian slang to have a bad smell

honk

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒŋk ) for learners of English
Word forms: honks , honking , honked
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf you honk the horn of a vehicle or if the horn honks, you make the horn produce a short loud sound. [V n] ⇒ Drivers honked their horns in solidarity with the peace marchers. [V] ⇒ Horns honk. An angry motorist shouts. [V] ⇒ ...people yelling and honking at you to get out of the cab.Honk is also a noun. ⇒ She pulled to the right with a honk.2. verbIf a bird, person, or musical instrument honks, they make a short, loud, harsh noise. [V] ⇒ If you're going to sneeze and honk all night, we'll never get any sleep. [V n] ⇒ A lone mother Canada goose honked a warning to stay away from her nest. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a honking saxophone playing gospel music.Honk is also a noun. ⇒ The honk of geese can be heard.
