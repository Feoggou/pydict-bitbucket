guzzle

Powered by COBUILD
(gʌzəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: guzzles , guzzling , guzzled
Definitions
1. verbIf you guzzle something, you drink it or eat it quickly and greedily. [informal] [V n] ⇒ Melissa had guzzled gin and tonics like they were lemonade. [Also V]2. verbIf you say that a vehicle guzzles fuel, you mean that it uses a lot of it in a way that is wasteful and unnecessary. [V n] ⇒ The plane was deafeningly noisy, guzzled fuel, and left a trail of smoke.-guzzling combining form [ADJ n] ⇒ The boom of the 1980s led to a taste for petrol-guzzling cars. ⇒ ...big energy-guzzling houses.3. → See also gas guzzler
