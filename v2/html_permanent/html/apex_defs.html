apex

(ˈeɪpɛks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural apexes or apices (ˈæpɪˌsiːz , ˈeɪ-)1. the highest point; vertex2. the pointed end or tip of something3. a pinnacle or high point, as of a career, etc4. Also called: solar apex astronomy the point on the celestial sphere, lying in the constellation Hercules, towards which the sun appears to move at a velocity of 20 kilometres per second relative to the nearest stars
Word origin
C17: from Latin: point

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular noun [usu N n]An Apex or an Apex ticket is a ticket for a journey by air or rail which costs less than the standard ticket, but which you have to book a specified period in advance. ⇒ The Apex fare is £195 return.

apex

Powered by COBUILD
(eɪpeks )
Word forms: apexes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. singular nounThe apex of an organization or system is the highest and most important position in it. ⇒ At the apex of the party was its central committee.2. countable noun [usu sing]The apex of something is its pointed top or end. ⇒ ...the apex of the pyramid.
