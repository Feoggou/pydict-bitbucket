bury

(ˈbɛrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: buries, burying or buried (transitive)1. to place (a corpse) in a grave, usually with funeral rites; inter2. to place in the earth and cover with soil3. to lose through death4. to cover from sight; hide5. to embed; sink ⇒ to bury a nail in plaster6. to occupy (oneself) with deep concentration; engross ⇒ to be buried in a book7. to dismiss from the mind; abandon ⇒ to bury old hatreds8. See bury the hatchet9. See bury one's head in the sand
Word origin
Old English byrgan to bury, hide; related to Old Norse bjarga to save, preserve, Old English beorgan to defend

(beri )
Word forms: buries , burying , buried
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo bury something means to put it into a hole in the ground and cover it up with earth. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ They make the charcoal by burying wood in the ground and then slowly burning it. [V n] ⇒ ...squirrels who bury nuts and seeds. [V-ed] ⇒ ...buried treasure.2. verbTo bury a dead person means to put their body into a grave and cover it with earth. [V n] ⇒ ...soldiers who helped to bury the dead in large communal graves. [V n adj] ⇒ I was horrified that people would think I was dead and bury me alive. [V-ed] ⇒ More than 9,000 men lie buried here.3. verbIf someone says they have buried one of their relatives, they mean that one of their relatives has died. [V n] ⇒ He had buried his wife some two years before he retired.4. verbIf you bury something under a large quantity of things, you put it there, often in order to hide it. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ She buried it under some leaves. [be V-ed prep/adv] ⇒ I was looking for my handbag, which was buried under a pile of old newspapers.5. verbIf something buries a place or person, it falls on top of them so that it completely covers them and often harms them in some way. [V n] ⇒ Latest reports say that mud slides buried entire villages. [V n] ⇒ Their house was buried by a landslide. [V-ed] ⇒ He was buried under the debris for several hours.6. verbIf you bury your head or face in something, you press your head or face against it, often because you are unhappy. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ She buried her face in the pillows. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He held her closely, burying his head against her shoulder.7. verbIf something buries itself somewhere, or if you bury it there, it is pushed very deeply in there. [V pron-refl prep/adv] ⇒ The missile buried itself deep in the grassy hillside. [V-ed] ⇒ He stood on the sidewalk with his hands buried in the pockets of his dark overcoat. [Also V n prep/adv]8. verbIf you bury a feeling, you try not to show it. If you bury a memory, you try to forget it. [written] suppress [V n] ⇒ When we feel anger, we bury the emotion and feel guilty instead. [V n] ⇒ It is time to bury our past misunderstandings. [V-ed] ⇒ ...deeply-buried memories.9. verbIf you bury yourself in a place or in an activity such as your work, you spend all your time in that place or doing that activity, usually because you want to forget about things. [V pron-refl in n] ⇒ His reaction was to withdraw, to bury himself in work. [V pron-refl in n] ⇒ ...the popular image of writers burying themselves in the country in order to write.10. verbIf you bury your head in something such as a book or newspaper, or bury yourself in it, you look at it closely and concentrate very hard on it. [V n in n] ⇒ My father buried his head in his newspaper. [V pron-refl in n] ⇒ He buried himself in his detective story again.11. → to bury the hatchet
