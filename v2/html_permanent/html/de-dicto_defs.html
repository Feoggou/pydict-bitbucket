de dicto

Latin (ˈdeɪˈdɪktəʊ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivelogic, philosophy relating to the expression of a belief, possibility, etc, rather than to the individuals mentioned, as in the number of the planets is the number of satellites of the sun, the truth of which is independent of what number that is. Compare de re. See also Electra paradox
Word origin
literally: about the saying
