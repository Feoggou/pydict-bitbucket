rug

(rʌɡ )
Definitions
noun1. a floor covering, smaller than a carpet and made of thick wool or of other material, such as an animal skin2. mainly British a blanket, esp one used as a wrap or lap robe for travellers3. slang a wig4. See pull the rug out from under
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ruglike (ˈrugˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Scandinavian; compare Norwegian rugga, Swedish rugg coarse hair. See rag1

rug

Powered by COBUILD
(rʌg ) for learners of English
Word forms: rugs
Definitions
1. countable nounA rug is a piece of thick material that you put on a floor. It is like a carpet but covers a smaller area. ⇒ A Persian rug covered the hardwood floors.2. countable nounA rug is a small blanket which you use to cover your shoulders or your knees to keep them warm. [mainly British] ⇒ The old lady was seated in her chair at the window, a rug over her knees. ⇒ ...a travel rug.3. →  pull the rug from under someone/pull the rug from under someone's feet4. → to sweep something under the rug
