presage

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (ˈprɛsɪdʒ )1. an intimation or warning of something about to happen; portent; omen2. a sense of what is about to happen; foreboding3. archaic a forecast or predictionverb (ˈprɛsɪdʒ , prɪˈseɪdʒ )4. (transitive) to have a presentiment of5. (transitive) to give a forewarning of; portend6. (intransitive) to make a prediction

Derived Forms

presageful (preˈsageful) adjectivepresagefully (preˈsagefully) adverbpresager (preˈsager) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin praesāgium presentiment, from praesāgīre to perceive beforehand, from sāgīre to perceive acutely
