aisle

(aɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a passageway separating seating areas in a theatre, church, etc; gangway2. a lateral division in a church flanking the nave or chancel3. See rolling in the aisles
Derived forms
aisled adjectiveˈaisleless adjective
Word origin
C14 ele (later aile, aisle, through confusion with isle (island)), via Old French from Latin āla wing

(aɪl )
Word forms: aisles
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn aisle is a long narrow gap that people can walk along between rows of seats in a public building such as a church or between rows of shelves in a supermarket. ⇒ He started down the centre aisle. ⇒ ...the frozen food aisle.2. singular nounThe aisle is used in expressions such as walking down the aisle to refer to the activity of getting married. ⇒ He was in no hurry to walk down the aisle.
