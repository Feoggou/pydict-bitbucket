outmoded

(ˌaʊtˈməʊdɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. no longer fashionable or widely accepted2. no longer practical or usable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

outmodedly (ˌoutˈmodedly) adverboutmodedness (ˌoutˈmodedness) noun

outmoded for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtmoʊdɪd )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as outmoded, you mean that you think it is old-fashioned and no longer useful or relevant to modern life. ⇒ Romania badly needs aid to modernise its outmoded industries. ⇒ What hope is there, if people in positions of power continue to promote outmoded attitudes? ⇒ The political system has become thoroughly outmoded.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
