admiral

(ˈædmərəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the supreme commander of a fleet or navy2. Also called: admiral of the fleet, fleet admiral a naval officer of the highest rank, equivalent to general of the army or field marshal3. a senior naval officer entitled to fly his own flag. See also rear admiral, vice admiral4. mainly British the master of a fishing fleet5. any of various nymphalid butterflies, esp the red admiral or white admiral
Derived forms
ˈadmiralˌship noun
Word origin
C13: amyral, from Old French amiral emir, and from Medieval Latin admīrālis (the spelling with d probably influenced by admīrābilis admirable); both from Arabic amīr emir, commander, esp in the phrase amīr-al commander of, as in amīr-al-bahr commander of the sea
