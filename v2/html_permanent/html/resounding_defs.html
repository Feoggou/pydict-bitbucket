resounding

(rɪˈzaʊndɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. clear and emphatic; unmistakable ⇒ a resounding vote of confidence2. full of or characterized by resonance; reverberating ⇒ a resounding slap
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

resoundingly (reˈsoundingly) adverb

resounding

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪzaʊndɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A resounding sound is loud and clear. ⇒ There was a resounding slap as Andrew struck him violently across the face. ⇒ She got a resounding round of applause. ⇒ The answer, Segal says, was a resounding yes!resoundingly adverb ⇒ Leatherdale was hit resoundingly on the side of the head.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]You can refer to a very great success as a resounding success. [emphasis] ⇒ The good weather helped to make the occasion a resounding success.resoundingly graded adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ They resoundingly support government programs for the poor. ⇒ International trading remains resoundingly successful.
