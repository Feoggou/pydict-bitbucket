in case

Definitions
(adverb)a. in order to allow for eventualitiesb. (as conjunction) in order to allow for the possibility that ⇒ take your coat in case it rainsc. US if▷ See case

in case

Definitions
phraseYou use in case in expressions like 'in case you didn't know' or 'in case you've forgotten' when you are telling someone in a rather irritated way something that you think is either obvious or none of their business. [feelings] ⇒ She's nervous about something, in case you didn't notice. ⇒ 'I'm waiting for Mary Ann,' she said, 'in case you're wondering.' →  case
