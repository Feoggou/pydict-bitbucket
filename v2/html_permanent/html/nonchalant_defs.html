nonchalant

(ˈnɒnʃələnt )
Definitions
adjectivecasually unconcerned or indifferent; uninvolved
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nonchalance (ˈnonchalance) nounnonchalantly (ˈnonchalantly) adverb

Word Origin

C18: from French, from nonchaloir to lack warmth, from non- + chaloir, from Latin calēre to be warm

nonchalant for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒnʃələnt , US -lɑːnt )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as nonchalant, you mean that they appear not to worry or care about things and that they seem very calm. [+ about] ⇒ Clark's mother is nonchalant about her role in her son's latest work. ⇒ Denis tried to look nonchalant and uninterested. ⇒ It merely underlines our rather more nonchalant attitude to life.nonchalance (nɒnʃələns , US -lɑːns) uncount noun ⇒ Affecting nonchalance, I handed her two hundred dollar bills.nonchalantly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'Does Will intend to return with us?' Joanna asked as nonchalantly as she could.
