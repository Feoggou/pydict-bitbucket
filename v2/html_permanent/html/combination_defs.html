combination

(ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of combining or state of being combined2. a union of separate parts, qualities, etc3. an alliance of people or parties; group having a common purpose4. a. the set of numbers that opens a combination lockb. the mechanism of this type of lock5. British a motorcycle with a sidecar attached6. mathematicsa. an arrangement of the numbers, terms, etc, of a set into specified groups without regard to order in the group ⇒ the combinations of a, b, and c, taken two at a time, are ab, bc, acb. a group formed in this way. The number of combinations of n objects taken r at a time is n!/[(n – r)!r!]. Symbol: nCr. Compare permutation (sense 1)7. the chemical reaction of two or more compounds, usually to form one other compound8. chess a tactical manoeuvre involving a sequence of moves and more than one piece
Derived forms
ˌcombinational (ˌcombiˈnational) adjective
