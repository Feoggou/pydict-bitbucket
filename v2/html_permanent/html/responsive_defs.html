responsive

(rɪˈspɒnsɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. reacting or replying quickly or favourably, as to a suggestion, initiative, etc2. (of an organism) reacting to a stimulus
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

responsive

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪspɒnsɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA responsive person is quick to react to people or events and to show emotions such as pleasure and affection. ⇒ Harriet was an easy, responsive little girl. ⇒ This is a responsive class with plenty of ideas. ⇒ Three months before birth babies are already responsive to sound.responsiveness uncount noun ⇒ This condition decreases sexual desire and responsiveness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is responsive, they react quickly and favourably. [+ to] ⇒ With an election coming soon, your MP should be very responsive to your request.responsiveness uncount noun [+ to] ⇒ Such responsiveness to public pressure is extraordinary.3. adjective [ADJ n]A responsive action is made as a reaction to something that has just been said or done. ⇒ At hearing his name spoken, the dog gave a responsive wag of his tail.
