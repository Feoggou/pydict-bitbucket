ceremony

(ˈsɛrɪmənɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -nies1. a formal act or ritual, often set by custom or tradition, performed in observation of an event or anniversary ⇒ a ceremony commemorating Shakespeare's birth2. a religious rite or series of rites3. a courteous gesture or act ⇒ the ceremony of toasting the Queen4. ceremonial observances or gestures collectively ⇒ the ceremony of a monarchy5. See stand on ceremony6. See without ceremony
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin cēremōnia, from Latin caerimōnia what is sacred, a religious rite

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA ceremony is a formal event such as a wedding. ⇒ ...his grandmother's funeral, a private ceremony attended only by the family. ⇒ Today's award ceremony took place at the British Embassy in Tokyo.2. uncount noun [usu with N]Ceremony consists of the special things that are said and done on very formal occasions. ⇒ The Republic was proclaimed in public with great ceremony. ⇒ ...the pomp and ceremony of the Pope's visit.3. uncount noun [without N]If you do something without ceremony, you do it quickly and in a casual way. ⇒ 'Is Hilton here?' she asked without ceremony.4. → see also master of ceremonies
