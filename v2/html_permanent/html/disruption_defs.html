disruption

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪsrʌpʃən )
Word forms: disruptions
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounWhen there is disruption of an event, system, or process, it is prevented from continuing or operating in a normal way. ⇒ The strike is expected to cause delays and disruption to flights from Britain. ⇒ The rail strike is causing major disruptions at the country's ports.
Example sentences containing 'disruption'
The road ahead was now blocked solid by other Indians in dhotis, all welcoming any disruption to a dreary life of drudgery. Leasor, James TANK OF SERPENTSSuch tactics led to further disruption , victimization, war, and madness. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEThey knew how to open locks without damaging them, how to search a place with the minimum of disruption. Adam, Paul A NASTY DOSE OF DEATH
