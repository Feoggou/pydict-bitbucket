pine1

(paɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any evergreen resinous coniferous tree of the genus Pinus, of the N hemisphere, with long needle-shaped leaves and brown cones: family Pinaceae. See also longleaf pine, nut pine, pitch pine, Scots pine2. any other tree or shrub of the family Pinaceae3. the wood of any of these trees4. any of various similar but unrelated plants, such as ground pine and screw pine

Word Origin

Old English pīn, from Latin pīnus pine

pine2

(paɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intr; often foll by for or an infinitive) to feel great longing or desire; yearn2. (intransitive; often foll by away) to become ill, feeble, or thin through worry, longing, etc3. (transitive) archaic to mourn or grieve for

Word Origin

Old English pīnian to torture, from pīn pain, from Medieval Latin pēna, from Latin poena pain

pine

Powered by COBUILD
(paɪn )
Word forms: pines , pining , pined
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA pine tree or a pine is a tall tree which has very thin, sharp leaves and a fresh smell. Pine trees have leaves all year round. ⇒ ...high mountains covered in pine trees.Pine is the wood of this tree. ⇒ ...a big pine table.2. verbIf you pine for someone who has died or gone away, you want them to be with you very much and feel sad because they are not there. [V + for] ⇒ She'd be sitting at home pining for her lost husband. [V] ⇒ Make sure your pet won't pine while you're away.3. verbIf you pine for something, you want it very much, especially when it is unlikely that you will be able to have it. yearn for [V + for] ⇒ I pine for the countryside. [V for n] ⇒ ...the democracy they have pined for since 1939.
