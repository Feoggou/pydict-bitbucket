impudence

(ˈɪmpjʊdəns ) or impudency
Definitions
noun1. the quality of being impudent2. an impudent act or statement
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin impudēns shameless, from im- (not) + pudēns modest; see pudency
Example sentences containing 'impudence'
Margot's very blue eyes flashed at this piece of impudence and she flushed. Brent-Dyer, Elinor CHALLENGE FOR THE CHALET SCHOOLNor could she move anything, for that would be impudence , a claim to refinement that she, a simple maid, could never possess. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSWhat it was all in all, it seemed to Helen, was a per formance - an exercise in impudence stretched to the breaking-point. Delman, David DEATH OF A NYMPHAnd of more importance in your case - restrain your impudence. Christina Dodd MY FAVORITE BRIDE
