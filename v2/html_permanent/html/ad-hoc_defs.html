ad hoc

(æd ˈhɒk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective, adverbfor a particular purpose only; lacking generality or justification ⇒ an ad hoc decision, ⇒ an ad hoc committee
Word origin
Latin, literally: to this

ad hoc

Powered by COBUILD
(æd hɒk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]An ad hoc activity or organization is done or formed only because a situation has made it necessary and is not planned in advance. ⇒ The Council meets on an ad hoc basis to discuss problems. ⇒ ...ad hoc committees to examine specific problems.
Example sentences containing 'ad hoc'
The house was even more of a shambles than it had been before, the remains of several ad hoc meals spread around. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOW"She'd finished her ad hoc meal, and went back into the bedroom to set the plate down. Clive Barker COLDHEART CANYONAll the stacked tables and chairs made a terrific ad hoc playground. Patrick Ness THE CRASH OF HENNINGTON
