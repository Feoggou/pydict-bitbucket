countenance

(ˈkaʊntɪnəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the face, esp when considered as expressing a person's character or mood ⇒ a pleasant countenance2. support or encouragement; sanction3. composure; self-control (esp in the phrases keep or lose one's countenance; out of countenance)verb (transitive)4. to support or encourage; sanction5. to tolerate; endure
Derived forms
countenancer (ˈcountenancer) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French contenance mien, behaviour, from Latin continentia restraint, control; see contain

Word forms: countenances , countenancing , countenanced
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone will not countenance something, they do not agree with it and will not allow it to happen. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Jake would not countenance Janis's marrying while still a student. [V n] ⇒ ...the military men who refused to countenance the overthrow of the president.2. countable nounSomeone's countenance is their face. [literary] ⇒ He met each inquiry with an impassive countenance.
Example sentences containing 'countenance'
But the sight of Benvenuto's evil lopsided countenance staring at her out of Tonino's arms left her with almost nothing to say. Diana Wynne Jones THE MAGICIANS OF CAPRONA
