rob

(rɒb )
Definitions
verbWord forms: robs, robbing or robbed1. (transitive) to take something from (someone) illegally, as by force or threat of violence2. to plunder (a house, shop, etc)3. (transitive) to deprive unjustly ⇒ to be robbed of an opportunity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

robber (ˈrobber) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French rober, of Germanic origin; compare Old High German roubōn to rob

(rɒb ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. verbIf someone is robbed, they have money or property stolen from them. [be V-ed + of] ⇒ Mrs Yacoub was robbed of her £3,000 designer watch at her West London home. [V n] ⇒ Police said Stefanovski had robbed a man just hours earlier. [Also V]2. verbIf someone is robbed of something that they deserve, have, or need, it is taken away from them. [be V-ed + of] ⇒ When Miles Davis died, jazz was robbed of its most distinctive voice. [V n + of] ⇒ I can't forgive Lewis for robbing me of an Olympic gold.
