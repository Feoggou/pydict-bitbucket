bounds

(baʊndz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. (sometimes singular) a limit; boundary (esp in the phrase know no bounds)2. something that restrains or confines, esp the standards of a society ⇒ within the bounds of modesty3. See beat the bounds▷ See also out of bounds
Example sentences containing 'bounds'
Within these bounds , the novice is free to structure his own education. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODStrange exotic places, just out of bounds of austerity holidays abroad. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LION
