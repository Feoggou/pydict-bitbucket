outrage

(ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ )
Definitions
noun1. a wantonly vicious or cruel act2. a gross violation of decency, morality, honour, etc3. profound indignation, anger, or hurt, caused by such an actverb (transitive)4. to cause profound indignation, anger, or resentment in5. to offend grossly (feelings, decency, human dignity, etc)6. to commit an act of wanton viciousness, cruelty, or indecency on7. a euphemistic word for rape1
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13 (meaning: excess): via French from outré beyond, from Latin ultrā

Word forms: outrages, outraging , outraged
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (aʊtreɪdʒ ). The noun is pronounced (aʊtreɪdʒ ).1. verbIf you are outraged by something, it makes you extremely shocked and angry. [be V-ed] ⇒ Many people have been outraged by some of the things that have been said. [V n] ⇒ Reports of torture and mass executions in Serbia's detention camps have outraged the world's religious leaders.outraged graded adjective & adjective [+ at/about] ⇒ He is truly outraged about what's happened to him. ⇒ Some outraged readers said the story was extremely offensive and distressing.2. uncount nounOutrage is an intense feeling of anger and shock. [+ from] ⇒ The decision provoked outrage from women and human rights groups. ⇒ The Treaty has failed to arouse genuine public outrage.3. countable nounYou can refer to an act or event which you find very shocking as an outrage. ⇒ The latest outrage was to have been a co-ordinated gun and bomb attack on the station. ⇒ Tom, this is an outrage!
