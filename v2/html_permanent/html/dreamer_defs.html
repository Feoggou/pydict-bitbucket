dreamer

(ˈdriːmə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who dreams habitually2. a person who lives in or escapes to a world of fantasy or illusion; escapist3. archaic a prophet; visionary

dreamer

Powered by COBUILD
(driːməʳ )
Word forms: dreamers
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounIf you describe someone as a dreamer, you mean that they spend a lot of time thinking about and planning for things that they would like to happen but which are improbable or impractical. ⇒ Far from being a dreamer, she's a level-headed pragmatist.
Example sentences containing 'dreamer'
His awareness of time being lost was that of a dreamer rather than a participant. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENIt ached beneath the skin and weakened the muscles the way a monstrous dream can stay with the dreamer throughout the day. Heller, Keith MAN'S LOVING FAMILYShe was a dreamer by instinct, by nature, by inclination; a de facto dreamer. Nicola Barker BEHINDLINGSSlender and graceful, she moved like a dreamer through the stream of students. S.M. Stirling T2©: RISING STORM
