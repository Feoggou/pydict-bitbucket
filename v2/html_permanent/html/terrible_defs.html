terrible

(ˈtɛrəbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. very serious or extreme ⇒ a terrible cough2. informal of poor quality; unpleasant or bad ⇒ a terrible meal, ⇒ a terrible play3. causing terror4. causing awe ⇒ the terrible nature of God

Derived Forms

terribleness (ˈterribleness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin terribilis, from terrēre to terrify

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA terrible experience or situation is very serious or very unpleasant. ⇒ Tens of thousands more suffered terrible injuries in the world's worst industrial disaster. ⇒ I often have the most terrible nightmares. ⇒ Prison life, he told me, was terrible.terribly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ My son has suffered terribly. He has lost his best friend.2. graded adjective [feel/look ADJ]If you feel terrible, you feel extremely ill or unhappy. If you tell someone that they look terrible, you mean that they look as if they are extremely ill or unhappy. awful ⇒ He did feel terrible at the time but seems to be fine now. ⇒ Are you all right? You look terrible. Are you sick?3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is terrible, it is very bad or of very poor quality. ⇒ She admits her French is terrible.4. adjective [ADJ n]You use terrible to emphasize the great extent or degree of something. [emphasis] ⇒ I was a terrible fool, you know. I remember that now. ⇒ Her death is a terrible waste.terribly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ I'm terribly sorry to bother you at this hour. ⇒ I suffered terribly when she died.
