research

(rɪˈsɜːtʃ , ˈriːsɜːtʃ )
Definitions
noun1. systematic investigation to establish facts or principles or to collect information on a subjectverb2. to carry out investigations into (a subject, problem, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

researchable (reˈsearchable) adjectiveresearcher (reˈsearcher) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Old French recercher to seek, search again, from re- + cercher to search

(rɪsɜːʳtʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: researches , researching , researched
Definitions
1. uncount nounResearch is work that involves studying something and trying to discover facts about it. ⇒ 65 percent of the 1987 budget went for nuclear weapons research and production. ⇒ ...cancer research. ⇒ ...his researches into which kinds of flowers bees get their best honey from.2. verbIf you research something, you try to discover facts about it. [V n] ⇒ She spent two years in South Florida researching and filming her documentary. [V] ⇒ So far we haven't been able to find anything, but we're still researching.researcher Word forms: researchers countable noun ⇒ He chose to join the company as a market researcher.
