duration

(djʊˈreɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe length of time that something lasts or continues
Derived forms
durational (duˈrational) adjective
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin dūrātiō, from Latin dūrāre to last

duration

Powered by COBUILD
(djʊreɪʃən , US dʊr- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe duration of an event or state is the time during which it happens or exists. ⇒ He was given the task of protecting her for the duration of the trial. ⇒ Courses are of two years' duration.2. →  for the duration
Example sentences containing 'duration'
The manufacturers have agreed to amalgamate their brand names for the duration in the interest of the Allied war effort. Penelope Fitzgerald HUMAN VOICESThis was a competition not only of accuracy but of duration. James Birrell THE MANANA MANMam had volunteered him for the Miller Street Home Guard but he only lasted a week or two and then he'd disappeared for the duration. Laurie Graham MR STARLIGHTThe beasts complained and moaned, and moved behind the tents, where they would take shelter for the duration. C.J. Cherryh HAMMERFALL
