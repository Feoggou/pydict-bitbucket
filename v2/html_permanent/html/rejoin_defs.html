rejoin1

(riːˈdʒɔɪn )
Definitions
verb1. to come again into company with (someone or something)2. (transitive) to put or join together again; reunite
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

rejoin2

(rɪˈdʒɔɪn )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to say (something) in reply; answer, reply, or retort2. law to answer (a claimant's reply)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Old French rejoign-, stem of rejoindre; see re-, join

rejoin

Powered by COBUILD
(riːdʒɔɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: rejoins , rejoining , rejoined
Definitions
pronunciation note:   Pronounced (riːdʒɔɪn) for meanings [sense 1], [sense 2], and [sense 3], and (rɪdʒɔɪn) for meaning [sense 4].1. verbIf you rejoin a group, club, or organization, you become a member of it again after not being a member for a period of time. [V n] ⇒ The Prime Minister of Fiji has said Fiji is in no hurry to rejoin the Commonwealth. [V n] ⇒ He rejoined Sadler's Wells Royal Ballet as Assistant Administrator in 1988. [Also V]2. verbIf you rejoin someone, you go back to them after a short time away from them. [V n] ⇒ Mimi and her family went off to Tunisia to rejoin her father.3. verbIf you rejoin a route, you go back to it after travelling along a different route for a time. [V n] ⇒ At Dorset Wharf go left to rejoin the river. [V n] ⇒ In the morning I rejoined Highway 127 south.4. verb [no cont]To rejoin means to answer quickly what someone has said, usually in a clever or critical manner. [formal, written] retort [V with quote] ⇒ 'I dare say they do,' rejoined his wife drily. 'And who's to say who's right?' [Also V that]
