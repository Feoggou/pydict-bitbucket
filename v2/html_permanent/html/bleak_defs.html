bleak1

(bliːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. exposed and barren; desolate2. cold and raw3. offering little hope or excitement; dismal ⇒ a bleak future
Derived forms
bleakly (ˈbleakly) adverbbleakness (ˈbleakness) noun
Word origin
Old English blāc bright, pale; related to Old Norse bleikr white, Old High German bleih pale

bleak

Powered by COBUILD
(bliːk )
Word forms: bleaker , bleakest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a situation is bleak, it is bad, and seems unlikely to improve. ⇒ The immediate outlook remains bleak. ⇒ Many predicted a bleak future.bleakness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ The continued bleakness of the American job market was blamed.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a place as bleak, you mean that it looks cold, empty, and unattractive. ⇒ The island's pretty bleak. ⇒ ...bleak inner-city streets.3. graded adjective & adjectiveWhen the weather is bleak, it is cold, dull, and unpleasant. ⇒ The weather can be quite bleak on the coast.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone looks or sounds bleak, they look or sound depressed, as if they have no hope or energy. ⇒ His face was bleak. ⇒ Alberg gave him a bleak stare.bleakly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'There is nothing left,' she says bleakly.
