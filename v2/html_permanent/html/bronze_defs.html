bronze

(brɒnz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. any hard water-resistant alloy consisting of copper and smaller proportions of tin and sometimes zinc and leadb. any similar copper alloy containing other elements in place of tin, such as aluminium bronze, beryllium bronze, etc. See also phosphor bronze, gunmetal. Compare brass (sense 1)2. a yellowish-brown colour or pigment3. a statue, medal, or other object made of bronze4. short for bronze medaladjective5. made of or resembling bronze6. of a yellowish-brown colour ⇒ a bronze skinverb7. (esp of the skin) to make or become brown; tan8. (transitive) to give the appearance of bronze to
Derived forms
bronzy (ˈbronzy) adjective
Word origin
C18: from French, from Italian bronzo, perhaps ultimately from Latin Brundisium Brindisi, famed for its bronze
