sic

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
You write sic in brackets after a word or expression when you want to indicate to the reader that although the word looks odd or wrong, you intended to write it like that or the original writer wrote it like that. ⇒ The latest school jobs page advertises a 'wide range (sic) of 6th-form courses.'
Example sentences containing 'sic'
The period of mourning was clearly over with; sic transit Jasper Flodge. MacLeod, Charlotte SOMETHING IN THE WATER"If you say `Just like a woman,'" Shelley warned, "I'm going to sic Squeeze on you. Elizabeth Lowell WHERE THE HEART IS
