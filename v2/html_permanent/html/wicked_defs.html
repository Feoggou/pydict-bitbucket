wicked

(ˈwɪkɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. a. morally bad in principle or practiceb. (as collective noun; preceded by the) ⇒ the wicked2. mischievous or roguish, esp in a playful way ⇒ a wicked grin3. causing injury or harm4. troublesome, unpleasant, or offensive5. slang very good
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wickedly (ˈwickedly) adverbwickedness (ˈwickedness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from dialect wick, from Old English wicca sorcerer, wicce witch1

wicked for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪkɪd )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use wicked to describe someone or something that is very bad and deliberately harmful to people. ⇒ She described the shooting as a wicked attack. ⇒ She flew at me, shouting how wicked and evil I was.wickedness uncount noun ⇒ ...moral arguments about the wickedness of nuclear weapons.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as wicked, you mean that they are rather naughty, but in a way that you find attractive or enjoyable. ⇒ She had a wicked sense of humour. ⇒ I adore white chocolate, as it has such a sweet taste and I always feel very wicked when eating it.wickedly adverb [usu ADV adj, also ADV after v] ⇒ This collection of letters is affectionate, candid and wickedly funny. ⇒ Michael grinned wickedly.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
