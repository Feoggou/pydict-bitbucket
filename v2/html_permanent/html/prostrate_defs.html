prostrate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (ˈprɒstreɪt )1. lying with the face downwards, as in submission2. exhausted physically or emotionally3. helpless or defenceless4. (of a plant) growing closely along the groundverb (prɒˈstreɪt ) (transitive)5. to bow or cast (oneself) down, as in submission6. to lay or throw down flat, as on the ground7. to make helpless or defenceless8. to make exhausted

Derived Forms

prostration (prosˈtration) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin prōsternere to throw to the ground, from prō- before + sternere to lay low

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (prɒstreɪt , US prɑːstreɪt ). The adjective is pronounced (prɒstreɪt ).1. verbIf you prostrate yourself, you lie down flat on the ground, on your front, usually to show respect for God or a person in authority. [V pron-refl] ⇒ They prostrated themselves before their king.2. adjective [ADJ after v]If you are lying prostrate, you are lying flat on the ground, on your front. ⇒ Percy was lying prostrate, his arms outstretched and his eyes closed.3. adjectiveIf someone is prostrate, they are so distressed or affected by a very bad experience that they are unable to do anything at all. [formal] ⇒ Immediately after my father's death my stepmother was prostrate. ⇒ I was prostrate with grief. ⇒ ...his country's prostrate economy.
