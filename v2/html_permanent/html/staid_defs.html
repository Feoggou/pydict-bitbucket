staid

(steɪd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of a settled, sedate, and steady character2. rare permanent

Derived Forms

staidly (ˈstaidly) adverbstaidness (ˈstaidness) noun

Word Origin

C16: obsolete past participle of stay1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is staid, you mean that they are serious, dull, and rather old-fashioned. ⇒ ...a staid seaside resort.
Example sentences containing 'staid'
This determination sent the staid academicians into a panic.
