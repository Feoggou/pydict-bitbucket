beforehand

(bɪˈfɔːˌhænd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective, adverb (postpositive)early; in advance; in anticipation ⇒ she came an hour beforehand

beforehand

Powered by COBUILD
(bɪfɔːʳhænd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [usu ADV after v]If you do something beforehand, you do it earlier than a particular event. ⇒ How could she tell beforehand that I was going to go out? ⇒ Saunas can be hazardous if misused. Avoid a big meal beforehand.
Example sentences containing 'beforehand'
He came regularly to visit his grandson, but never without telling Ginny beforehand. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENHe can't have known it beforehand , or he wouldn't have bothered to search our house at all. Ferrars, Elizabeth UNREASONABLE DOUBTOnce the mere possibility of such an encounter would have been enough to keep Susan awake for nights beforehand. Smith, Evelyn E MISS MELVILLE REGRETSThese were explicitly love affairs, so designated beforehand. Joyce Carol Oates FAITHLESS: TALES OF TRANSGRESSION
