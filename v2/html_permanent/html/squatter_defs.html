squatter

(ˈskwɒtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who occupies property or land to which he or she has no legal title2. (in Australia)a. (formerly) a person who occupied a tract of land, esp pastoral land, as tenant of the Crownb. a farmer of sheep or cattle on a large scale3. (in New Zealand) a 19th-century settler who took up large acreage on a Crown lease

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA squatter is someone who lives in an unused building without having a legal right to do so and without paying any rent or any property tax.2. countable nounA squatter is someone who occupies unused land, either to farm it or to build a house on it, without having a legal right to do so. ⇒ When they returned to Tuol Sakor, they found all the best land taken by squatters.
