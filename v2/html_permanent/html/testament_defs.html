testament

(ˈtɛstəmənt )
Definitions
noun1. law a will setting out the disposition of personal property (esp in the phrase last will and testament)2. a proof, attestation, or tribute ⇒ his success was a testament to his skills3. a. a covenant instituted between God and man, esp the covenant of Moses or that instituted by Christb. a copy of either the Old or the New Testament, or of the complete Bible
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

testamental (ˌtestaˈmental) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin: a will, from testārī to bear witness, from testis a witness

Testament

(ˈtɛstəmənt )
Definitions
noun1. either of the two main parts of the Bible; the Old Testament or the New Testament2. the New Testament as distinct from the Old
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
