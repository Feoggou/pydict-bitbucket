(French ɑ̃ mas)
Definitions
adverbin a group, body, or mass; as a whole; all together

Word Origin

en masse

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒn mæs ) for learners of English
Definitions
adverb [ADV after v, n ADV]If a group of people do something en masse, they do it all together and at the same time. ⇒ The people marched en masse. ⇒ ...the arrival en masse of the Latin American delegates.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'en masse'
Marie, she said, certainly resented her family's descending on her en masse , and Frank was moody and difficult. Howker, Janni ISAAC CAMPION
