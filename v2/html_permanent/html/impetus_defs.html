impetus

(ˈɪmpɪtəs )
Definitions
noun plural -tuses1. an impelling movement or force; incentive or impulse; stimulus2. physics the force that sets a body in motion or that tends to resist changes in a body's motion
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: attack, from impetere to assail, from im- (in) + petere to make for, seek out
