vinegar

(ˈvɪnɪɡə )
Definitions
noun1. a sour-tasting liquid consisting of impure dilute acetic acid, made by oxidation of the ethyl alcohol in beer, wine, or cider. It is used as a condiment or preservative2. sourness or peevishness of temper, countenance, speech, etc3. pharmacology a medicinal solution in dilute acetic acid4. US and Canadian informal vitalityverb5. (transitive) to apply vinegar to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vinegarish (ˈvinegarish) adjectivevinegar-like (ˈvinegar-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French vinaigre, from vin wine + aigre sour, from Latin acer sharp
