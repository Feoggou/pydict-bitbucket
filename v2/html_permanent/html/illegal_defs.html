illegal

(ɪˈliːɡəl )
Definitions
adjective1. forbidden by law; unlawful; illicit2. unauthorized or prohibited by a code of official or accepted rulesnoun3. a person who has entered or attempted to enter a country illegally
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

illegally (ilˈlegally) adverbillegality (ˌilleˈgality) noun

Definitions
1. adjectiveIf something is illegal, the law says that it is not allowed. unlawful ⇒ It is illegal to intercept radio messages. ⇒ Birth control was illegal there until 1978. ⇒ He has been charged with membership of an illegal organisation. ⇒ ...illegal drugs. ⇒ ...an illegal action.illegally adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ They were yesterday convicted of illegally using a handgun. ⇒ The previous government had acted illegally.illegality (ɪlɪgælɪti )Word forms: illegalities variable noun ⇒ There is no evidence of illegality.2. adjective [ADJ n]Illegal immigrants or workers have travelled into a country or are working without official permission.Illegal immigrants or workers are sometimes referred to as illegals. ⇒ ...a clothing factory where many illegals worked.
