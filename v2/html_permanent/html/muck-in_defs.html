muck in

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verbIf someone mucks in, they join in with an activity or help other people with a job and do not consider themselves to be too important to do it. [mainly British, informal] [V P] ⇒ Course residents are expected to muck in and be prepared to share rooms. [V P + with] ⇒ She mucked in with the chores and did her own washing and ironing. [V P with n] ⇒ Ian was never afraid to take his coat off and muck in with everybody else. [Also V P together] →  muck
