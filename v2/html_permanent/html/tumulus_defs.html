tumulus

(ˈtumjələs ; ˈtjumjələs ) in American English
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural ˈtumuˌli (ˈtumjuˌlaɪ ; ˈtjumjuˌlaɪ ) or ˈtumuluses an artificial mound; esp., an ancient burial mound; barrow
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

L, mound, hillock, akin to tumere: see tumor

tumulus

(ˈtjuːmjʊləs )
Definitions
noun plural -li (-liː)archaeology (no longer in technical usage) another word for barrow2
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: a hillock, from tumēre to swell up
