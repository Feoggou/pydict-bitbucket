antithesis

(ænˈtɪθɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. the exact opposite2. contrast or opposition3. rhetoric the juxtaposition of contrasting ideas, phrases, or words so as to produce an effect of balance, such as my words fly up, my thoughts remain below4. philosophy the second stage in the Hegelian dialectic contradicting the thesis before resolution by the synthesis
Word origin
C15: via Latin from Greek: a setting against, from anti- + tithenai to place

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe antithesis of something is its exact opposite. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ The little black dress is the antithesis of fussy dressing.2. countable nounIf there is an antithesis between two things, there is a contrast between them. [formal] ⇒ ...the antithesis between instinct and reason.
