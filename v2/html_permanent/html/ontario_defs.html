Ontario

(ɒnˈtɛərɪəʊ )
Definitions
noun1. a province of central Canada: lies mostly on the Canadian Shield and contains the fertile plain of the lower Great Lakes and the St Lawrence River, one of the world's leading industrial areas; the second largest and the most populous province. Capital: Toronto. Pop: 12 851 821 (2011 est). Area: 891 198 sq km (344 092 sq miles). Abbreviation: Ont., ON2. See Lake Ontario
