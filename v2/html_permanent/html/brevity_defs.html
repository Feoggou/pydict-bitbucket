brevity

(ˈbrɛvɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. conciseness of expression; lack of verbosity2. a short duration; brief time
Word origin
C16: from Latin brevitās shortness, from brevis brief

brevity

Powered by COBUILD
(brevɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounThe brevity of something is the fact that it is short or lasts for only a short time. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ The bonus of this homely soup is the brevity of its cooking time. ⇒ The brevity of the letter concerned me.
Example sentences containing 'brevity'
But the very brevity of my reply alerted him that there was much I had not said. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThen, the brevity of her response seeming to strike her, she added, `But there was no reason for him to get in touch. Fraser, Anthea THE GOSPEL MAKERSThere was only one figure he recognized, and no conversational threat to judge by his brevity at their earlier meeting. Curzon, Clare THE QUEST FOR KThen Count Considat spoke again, no more ably than before, but at least with greater brevity. Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
