compose

verb

  1. put together, make up, constitute, comprise, make, build, form, fashion, construct, compound • They agreed to form a council composed of leaders of the rival factions. OPPOSITES:  destroy, dismantle, demolish, obliterate, bulldoze, raze
  2. create, write, produce, imagine, frame, invent, devise, contrive, indite • He started at once to compose a reply to her letter.
  3. arrange, make up, construct, put together, order, organize • The drawing is beautifully composed.
  • See compose yourself

'compose' in Other Languages

    to be composed of sth The things that something is composed of are its parts or members. The separate things that compose something are the parts or members that form it.The force would be composed of troops from NATO countries.
