mouthpiece

(ˈmaʊθˌpiːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the part of a wind instrument into which the player blows2. the part of a telephone receiver into which a person speaks3. the part of a container forming its mouth4. a person who acts as a spokesman, as for an organization5. a publication, esp a periodical, expressing the official views of an organization6. boxing another name for gumshield

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe mouthpiece of a telephone is the part that you speak into. ⇒ He shouted into the mouthpiece.2. countable nounThe mouthpiece of a musical instrument or other device is the part that you put into your mouth. ⇒ He showed him how to blow into the ivory mouthpiece. ⇒ ...clamping the mouthpiece of my snorkel tightly between my teeth.3. countable noun [usu with poss]The mouthpiece of an organization or person is someone who informs other people of the opinions and policies of that organization or person. ⇒ Their mouthpiece is the vice-president. ⇒ He might be considered little more than a convenient mouthpiece for the Prime Minister's own economic views.
