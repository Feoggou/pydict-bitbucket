spin out

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to extend or protract (a story, etc) by including superfluous detail; prolong2. to spend or pass (time)3. to contrive to cause (money, etc) to last as long as possiblenoun spinout4. a spinning skid in a car that causes it to run off the road

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you spin something out, you make it last longer than it normally would. [V n P] ⇒ My wife's solicitor was anxious to spin things out for as long as possible. [V P n] ⇒ The Government will try to spin out the conference into next autumn.2. → see also spin off →  spin
