debased

(dɪˈbeɪst)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. rendered less valuable or admirable ⇒ the debased standards of today's media, ⇒ one of the most overused and debased words in the language2. (of coins) having cheaper metal added
Example sentences containing 'debased'
Only because the vocabulary relating to it is so archaic and debased that one ends up sounding like either a fundamentalist or a lunatic. Howatch, Susan ABSOLUTE TRUTHS`Thugs," said Kemp, his head swimming only just above swirling soupy water, `now there's a word that's been debased. Meek, M R D IN REMEMBRANCE OF ROSE
