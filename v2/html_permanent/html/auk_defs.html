auk

(ɔːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of various diving birds of the family Alcidae of northern oceans having a heavy body, short tail, narrow wings, and a black-and-white plumage: order Charadriiformes. See also great auk, razorbill2. See little auk
Word origin
C17: from Old Norse ālka; related to Swedish alka, Danish alke
