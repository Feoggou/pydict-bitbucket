pronoun

(ˈprəʊˌnaʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounone of a class of words that serves to replace a noun phrase that has already been or is about to be mentioned in the sentence or context. Abbreviation: pron

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōnōmen, from pro-1 + nōmen noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA pronoun is a word that you use to refer to someone or something when you do not need to use a noun, often because the person or thing has been mentioned earlier. Examples are 'it', 'she', 'something', and 'myself'.2. → see also indefinite pronoun, personal pronoun, reflexive pronoun, relative pronoun
