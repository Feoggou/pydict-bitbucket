tobacco

(təˈbækəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -cos or -coes1. any of numerous solanaceous plants of the genus Nicotiana, having mildly narcotic properties, tapering hairy leaves, and tubular or funnel-shaped fragrant flowers. The species N. tabacum is cultivated as the chief source of commercial tobacco2. the leaves of certain of these plants dried and prepared for snuff, chewing, or smoking
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tobaccoless (toˈbaccoless) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Spanish tabaco, perhaps from Taino: leaves rolled for smoking, assumed by the Spaniards to be the name of the plant
