freight

(freɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a. commercial transport that is slower and cheaper than expressb. the price charged for such transportc. goods transported by this meansd. (as modifier) ⇒ freight transport2. mainly British a ship's cargo or part of itverb (transitive)3. to load with goods for transport4. mainly US and Canadian to convey commercially as or by freight5. to load or burden; charge
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

freightless (ˈfreightless) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Middle Dutch vrecht; related to French fret, Spanish flete, Portuguese frete

freight

Powered by COBUILD
(freɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: freights , freighting , freighted
Definitions
1. uncount nounFreight is the movement of goods by lorries, trains, ships, or aeroplanes. ⇒ France derives 16% of revenue from air freight.2. uncount nounFreight is goods that are transported by lorries, trains, ships, or aeroplanes. ⇒ ...26 tons of freight. ⇒ 90% of managers wanted to see more freight carried by rail.3. verb [usu passive]When goods are freighted, they are transported in large quantities over a long distance. [be V-ed adv/prep] ⇒ From these ports the grain is freighted down to Addis Ababa.
