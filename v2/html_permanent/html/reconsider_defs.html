reconsider

(ˌriːkənˈsɪdə )
Definitions
verb1. to consider (something) again, with a view to changing one's policy or course of action2. (in a legislative assembly or similar body) to consider again (a bill or other matter) that has already been voted upon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
verbIf you reconsider a decision or opinion, you think about it and try to decide whether it should be changed. [V n] ⇒ We want you to reconsider your decision to resign from the board. [V n] ⇒ This has forced the United States to seriously reconsider its position. [V] ⇒ If at the end of two years you still feel the same, we will reconsider.reconsideration (riːkənsɪdəreɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ The report urges reconsideration of the decision.
