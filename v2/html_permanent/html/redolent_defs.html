redolent

(ˈrɛdələnt )
Definitions
adjective1. having a pleasant smell; fragrant2. (postpositive; foll by of or with) having the odour or smell (of); scented (with) ⇒ a room redolent of country flowers3. (postpositive; foll by of or with) reminiscent or suggestive (of) ⇒ a picture redolent of the 18th century
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

redolence (ˈredolence) or rare redolency (ˈredolency) nounredolently (ˈredolently) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin redolens smelling (of), from redolēre to give off an odour, from red- re + olēre to smell

redolent

(redələnt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If something is redolent of something else, it has features that make you think of that other thing. [literary] [+ of] ⇒ ...percussion instruments, redolent of Far Eastern cultures. ⇒ ...a sad tale, redolent with regret.2. graded adjective [v-link ADJ, usu ADJ of n]If something is redolent of something else, it smells strongly of that other thing. [literary] ⇒ ...the air redolent of cinnamon and apple. ⇒ ...redolent with the scent of rosemary.
