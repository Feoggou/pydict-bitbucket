Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo safeguard something or someone means to protect them from being harmed, lost, or badly treated. [formal] [V n] ⇒ They will press for international action to safeguard the ozone layer. [V n] ⇒ The interests of minorities will have to be safeguarded under a new constitution. [V n + from] ⇒ They are taking precautionary measures to safeguard their forces from the effects of chemical weapons.2. countable nounA safeguard is a law, rule, or measure intended to prevent someone or something from being harmed. [+ against] ⇒ Many people took second jobs as a safeguard against unemployment. ⇒ A system like ours lacks adequate safeguards for civil liberties.
