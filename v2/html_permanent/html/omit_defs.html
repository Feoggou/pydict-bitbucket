omit

(əʊˈmɪt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: omits, omitting or omitted (transitive)1. to neglect to do or include2. to fail (to do something)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

omissible (əʊˈmɪsɪbəl ) adjectiveomitter (oˈmitter) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin omittere, from ob- away + mittere to send

omit for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊmɪt )
Word forms: omits , omitting , omitted
Definitions
1. verbIf you omit something, you do not include it in an activity or piece of work, deliberately or accidentally. [V n] ⇒ Omit the salt in this recipe. [V n + from] ⇒ Our apologies to David Pannick for omitting his name from last week's article.2. verbIf you omit to do something, you do not do it. [formal] [V to-inf] ⇒ His new girlfriend had omitted to tell him she was married.
