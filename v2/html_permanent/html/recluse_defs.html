recluse

(rɪˈkluːs )
Definitions
noun1. a person who lives in seclusion2. a person who lives in solitude to devote himself to prayer and religious meditation; a hermit, anchorite, or anchoressadjective3. solitary; retiring
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reclusion (rɪˈkluːʒən ) nounreclusive (reˈclusive) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French reclus, from Late Latin reclūdere to shut away, from Latin re- + claudere to close
