step up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb) informal1. (transitive) to increase or raise by stages; accelerate2. (intransitive) to make progress or effect an advancement; be promoted3. See step up to the plateadjective step-up (prenominal)4. (of a transformer) increasing a low voltage applied to the primary winding to a higher voltage on the secondary winding. Compare step down (sense 4)5. informal involving a rise by stagesnoun step-up6. informal an increment in quantity, size, etc

step-up

(ˈstɛpˌʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a transformer) increasing a low voltage applied to the primary winding to a higher voltage on the secondary winding ⇒ In a step-up transformer, the primary coil has fewer turns than the secondary.2. informal involving a rise by stages ⇒ High dose corticosteroids are used in the step-up phase of treatment.noun3. informal an increment in quantity, size, etc ⇒ the gradual step-up of dosage
