exhale

(ɛksˈheɪl , ɪɡˈzeɪl )
Definitions
verb1. to expel (breath, tobacco smoke, etc) from the lungs; breathe out2. to give off (air, vapour, fumes, etc) or (of air, vapour, etc) to be given off; emanate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exhalable (exˈhalable) adjectiveexhalation (ˌexhaˈlation) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin exhālāre to breathe out, from hālāre to breathe
