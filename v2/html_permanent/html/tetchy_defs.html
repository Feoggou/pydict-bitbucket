tetchy

(ˈtɛtʃɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: tetchier or tetchiestbeing or inclined to be cross, irritable, or touchy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tetchily (ˈtetchily) adverbtetchiness (ˈtetchiness) noun

Word Origin

C16: probably from obsolete tetch defect, from Old French tache spot, of Germanic origin

tetchy

Powered by COBUILD
(tetʃi ) for learners of English
Word forms: tetchier , tetchiest
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is tetchy, you mean they are bad-tempered and likely to get angry suddenly without an obvious reason. [mainly British, informal] ⇒ You always get tetchy when you're hungry. ⇒ He was in a particularly tetchy mood yesterday.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
