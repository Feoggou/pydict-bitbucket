occiput

(ˈɒksɪˌpʌt , -pət)
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural occiputs or occipita (ɒkˈsɪpɪtə )the back part of the head or skull
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin, from ob- at the back of + caput head
Example sentences containing 'occiput'
His occiput is fractured but only surgery can tell us how serious it is. Maclean, Alistair SAN ANDREASHe had a bald brown face and scalp, no hair left between cheeks and occiput , though he wasn't past his middle forties. Ursula K. Le Guin THE DISPOSSESSED
