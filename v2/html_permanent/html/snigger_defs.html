snigger

(ˈsnɪɡə ) or US and Canadian snicker (ˈsnɪkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a sly or disrespectful laugh, esp one partly stifledverb (intransitive)2. to utter such a laugh

Word Origin

C18: variant of snicker

snigger

Powered by COBUILD
(snɪgəʳ )
Word forms: sniggers , sniggering , sniggered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone sniggers, they laugh quietly in a disrespectful way, for example at something rude or unkind. snicker [V] ⇒ Suddenly, three schoolkids sitting near me started sniggering. [V] ⇒ How can I forget, with people sniggering behind my back? [V at n] ⇒ The tourists snigger at the locals' outdated ways and dress. [V with quote] ⇒ 'We know what that means,' Robert sniggered. [Also V about n]Snigger is also a noun. ⇒ ...trying to suppress a snigger.
