caper1

(ˈkeɪpə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a playful skip or leap2. a high-spirited escapade3. See cut a caper4. slang a crime, esp an organized robbery5. Australian informal a job or occupation6. Australian informal a person's behaviourverb7. (intransitive) to leap or dance about in a light-hearted manner
Derived forms
caperer (ˈcaperer) nouncaperingly (ˈcaperingly) adverb
Word origin
C16: probably from capriole

caper2

(ˈkeɪpə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a spiny trailing Mediterranean capparidaceous shrub, Capparis spinosa, with edible flower buds2. any of various similar plants or their edible parts. See also bean caper
Word origin
C15: from earlier capers, capres (assumed to be plural), from Latin capparis, from Greek kapparis

caper

Powered by COBUILD
(keɪpəʳ )
Word forms: capers , capering , capered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Capers are the small green buds of caper plants. They are usually sold preserved in vinegar.2. verbIf you caper about, you run and jump around because you are happy or excited. cavort [V adv/prep] ⇒ They were capering about, shouting and laughing. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Painted musicians capered behind gorgeous banners.3. countable noun [usu supp N]A dishonest or illegal activity can be referred to as a caper. [informal] ⇒ She served six months in prison for the helicopter caper.4. countable noun [usu supp N]Activities or behaviour that are not at all serious can be referred to as capers. [informal] ⇒ Jack would have nothing to do with such capers. ⇒ What a mess we were in at the end of this caper–hair, eyes, cheeks and neck covered in treacle.
