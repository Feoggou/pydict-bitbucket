preposterous

(prɪˈpɒstərəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivecontrary to nature, reason, or sense; absurd; ridiculous

Derived Forms

preposterously (preˈposterously) adverbpreposterousness (preˈposterousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin praeposterus reversed, from prae in front, before + posterus following

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as preposterous, you mean that it is extremely unreasonable and foolish. [disapproval] ⇒ The whole idea was preposterous. ⇒ ...their preposterous claim that they had unearthed a plot.preposterously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/adv] ⇒ Some prices are preposterously high.
