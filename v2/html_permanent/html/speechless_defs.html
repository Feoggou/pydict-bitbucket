speechless

(ˈspiːtʃlɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. not able to speak2. temporarily deprived of speech3. not expressed or able to be expressed in words ⇒ speechless fear

Derived Forms

speechlessly (ˈspeechlessly) adverbspeechlessness (ˈspeechlessness) noun

speechless

Powered by COBUILD
(spiːtʃləs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are speechless, you are temporarily unable to speak, usually because something has shocked you. [+ with] ⇒ Alex was almost speechless with rage and despair.
Example sentences containing 'speechless'
His disdain for our old life, spoken so plainly, left me speechless. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANAnd I was standing there, speechless with rage, when the phone rang. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEThen he sat mute, speechless with ecstasy, gazing into the sky. Shah, Idries KARA KUSHStanbridge was speechless for the first time in their memories. James W. Huston BALANCE OF POWER
