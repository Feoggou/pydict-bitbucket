decaying

(dɪˈkeɪɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. rotting as a result of bacterial, fungal, or chemical action; decomposing2. declining gradually in health, prosperity, excellence, etc
Example sentences containing 'decaying'
The slightly decaying lilies brought by Father Harpur from his church could not mask the odour of life's fluids curdling. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSEach one struck a Dead Hand, blowing a fist-sized hole straight through decaying flesh. Garth Nix LIRAEL: DAUGHTER OF THE CLAYRThe flat was crowded and chaotic, an enclave of students and hangers-on, in a decaying Victorian villa. Skelton, Alison Scott AN OLDER WOMAN
