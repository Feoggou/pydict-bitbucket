coast

(kəʊst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the line or zone where the land meets the sea or some other large expanse of waterb. (in combination) ⇒ coastland ▶ Related adjective: littoral2. British the seaside3. USa. a slope down which a sledge may slideb. the act or an instance of sliding down a slope4. obsolete borderland or frontier5. See the coast is clearverb6. to move or cause to move by momentum or force of gravity7. (intransitive) to proceed without great effort ⇒ to coast to victory8. to sail along (a coast)
Derived forms
coastal (ˈcoastal) adjectivecoastally (ˈcoastally) adverb
Word origin
C13: from Old French coste coast, slope, from Latin costa side, rib

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft adj N]The coast is an area of land that is next to the sea. ⇒ Campsites are usually situated along the coast, close to beaches. [+ of] ⇒ ...the west coast of Scotland.2. verbIf a vehicle coasts somewhere, it continues to move there with the motor switched off, or without being pushed or pedalled. freewheel [V prep/adv] ⇒ I switched off the engine and coasted round the corner. [V prep/adv] ⇒ They picked up momentum, then slipped into neutral and coasted quietly down the slope. [Also V]3. verbIf a person or a team is coasting, they are doing something easily, especially winning a competition. cruise [V to n] ⇒ Ivan Lendl coasted to a 6-3, 6-2, 6-3 victory over Roger Rasheed. [V] ⇒ The company was coasting on the enormous success of its early products. [Also V adv]4. verbIf you say that someone is coasting, you are emphasizing that they are not putting enough effort into what they are doing. [emphasis] [V] ⇒ There was a time when Charles was coasting at school and I should have told him to buckle down. [Also V prep]Coast along means the same as coast. [V P] ⇒ Matthew had no drive. He coasted along on his good looks.5. →  the coast is clear
