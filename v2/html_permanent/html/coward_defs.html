coward

(ˈkaʊəd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna person who shrinks from or avoids danger, pain, or difficulty
Word origin
C13: from Old French cuard, from coue tail, from Latin cauda; perhaps suggestive of a frightened animal with its tail between its legs

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounIf you call someone a coward, you disapprove of them because they are easily frightened and avoid dangerous or difficult situations. [disapproval] ⇒ She accused her husband of being a coward.
