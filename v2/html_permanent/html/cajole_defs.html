cajole

(kəˈdʒəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbto persuade (someone) by flattery or pleasing talk to do what one wants; wheedle; coax
Derived forms
cajolement (caˈjolement) nouncajoler (caˈjoler) nouncajolery (caˈjolery) nouncajolingly (caˈjolingly) adverb
Word origin
C17: from French cajoler to coax, of uncertain origin

cajole

Powered by COBUILD
(kədʒoʊl )
Word forms: cajoles , cajoling , cajoled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you cajole someone into doing something, you get them to do it after persuading them for some time. [V n + into] ⇒ It was he who had cajoled Garland into doing the film. [V n to-inf] ⇒ He cajoled Mr Dobson to stand for mayor. [Also V n, V]
Example sentences containing 'cajole'
He had no need to flatter or cajole ; he had no philosophy to debate, or sermon to deliver. Clive Barker THE HELLBOUND HEARTHe was a meek boy, so constrained by the edicts of his religion that Adrina would never be able to cajole him into anything. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPI had to intrigue and cajole in order to win a better chance of dying.
