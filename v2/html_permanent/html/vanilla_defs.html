vanilla

(vəˈnɪlə )
Definitions
noun1. any tropical climbing orchid of the genus Vanilla, esp V. plonifolia, having spikes of large fragrant greenish-yellow flowers and long fleshy pods containing the seeds (beans)2. the pod or bean of certain of these plants, used to flavour food, etc3. a flavouring extract prepared from vanilla beans and used in cookingadjective4. flavoured with or as if with vanilla ⇒ vanilla ice cream5. slang ordinary or conventional ⇒ a vanilla kind of guy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from New Latin, from Spanish vainilla pod, from vaina a sheath, from Latin vāgīna sheath

vanilla

(vənɪlə ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount noun [usu N n]Vanilla is a flavouring used in ice cream and other sweet food. ⇒ I added a dollop of vanilla ice-cream to the pie. ⇒ ...vanilla essence.2. adjectiveIf you describe a person or thing as vanilla, you mean that they are ordinary, with no special or extra features. ⇒ ...just plain vanilla couples like me and Tony. ⇒ The tensions of the Nixon presidency were replaced by the plain vanilla administration of a friendly, middle-aged, middle-class man from the Middle West.
