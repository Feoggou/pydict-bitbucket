supplicate

(ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make a humble request to (someone); plead2. (transitive) to ask for or seek humbly

Derived Forms

suppliˌcatory (ˈsuppliˌcatory) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin supplicāre to beg on one's knees; see supple
Example sentences containing 'supplicate'
Aylward of Evesham, a weaver, together with other members of that town, visited the shrine to supplicate before the Martyr's tomb. Grace, C.L A SHRINE OF MURDERS
