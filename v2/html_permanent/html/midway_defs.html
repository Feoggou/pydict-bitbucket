midway

(ˈmɪdˌweɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective, adverb1. in or at the middle of the distance; halfwaynoun2. US and Canadian a place in a fair, carnival, etc, where sideshows are located3. obsolete a middle place, way, etc

midway

Powered by COBUILD
(mɪdweɪ ) also mid-way
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverbIf something is midway between two places, it is between them and the same distance from each of them. ⇒ The studio is midway between his aunt's old home and his cottage.Midway is also an adjective. ⇒ ...the midway point between Gloucester, Hereford and Worcester.2. adverb [ADV after v]If something happens midway through a period of time, it happens during the middle part of it. [+ through] ⇒ He crashed midway through the race. ⇒ He returned midway through the afternoon.Midway is also an adjective. ⇒ They were denied an obvious penalty before the midway point of the first half.
