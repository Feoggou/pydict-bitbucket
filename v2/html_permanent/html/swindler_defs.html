swindler

(ˈswɪndlər )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun a person who swindles; cheat
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

Ger schwindler < schwindeln, to be dizzy, defraud, cheat < OHG swintilon, freq. of swintan, to disappear, wither, prob. < IE base *(s)wendh-, to disappear > OSlav uvędati, to wither

You asked me to keep you informed about that swindler who has disappeared," Karma reported. Forbes, Colin COVER STORYIf this man Blake is only a common swindler , I have lost a fortune.
