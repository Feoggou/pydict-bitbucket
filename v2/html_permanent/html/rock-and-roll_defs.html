rock and roll

or rock'n'roll
Definitions
noun1. a. a type of pop music originating in the 1950s as a blend of rhythm and blues and country and western. It is generally based upon the twelve-bar blues, the first and third beats in each bar being heavily accentedb. (as modifier) ⇒ the rock-and-roll era2. dancing performed to such music, with exaggerated body movements stressing the beatverb3. (intransitive) to perform this dance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

rock and roller or rock'n'roller noun

rock-and-roll

, rock-'n'-roll or rock'n'roll (ˌrɒkənˈrəʊl)
Definitions
modifier1. of, playing, or relating to rock and roll music ⇒ rock-and-roll group/number/record/album, ⇒ his rock-and-roll lifestyle, ⇒ a rock'n'roll legend, ⇒ the greatest rock 'n' roll band in the worldverb (intransitive)2. dancing to perform this dance
