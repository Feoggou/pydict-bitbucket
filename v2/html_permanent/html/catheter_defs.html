catheter

(ˈkæθɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounmedicine a long slender flexible tube for inserting into a natural bodily cavity or passage for introducing or withdrawing fluid, such as urine or blood
Word origin
C17: from Late Latin, from Greek kathetēr, from kathienai to send down, insert
