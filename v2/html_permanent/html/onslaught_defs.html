onslaught

(ˈɒnˌslɔːt )
Definitions
nouna violent attack
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Middle Dutch aenslag, from aan on + slag a blow, related to slay

onslaught for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒnslɔːt )
Word forms: onslaughts
Definitions
1. countable nounAn onslaught on someone or something is a very violent, forceful attack against them. ⇒ The attackers launched another vicious onslaught on their victim. [+ against] ⇒ The rebels responded to a military onslaught against them by launching a major assault on an army camp. ⇒ ...a media onslaught. [Also + by]2. countable nounIf you refer to an onslaught of something, you mean that there is a large amount of it, often so that it is very difficult to deal with. ⇒ The onslaught of orders should keep aircraft manufacturers busy for some time. [+ of] ⇒ ...the constant onslaught of ads on American TV.
