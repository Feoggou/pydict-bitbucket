kerb

or US and Canadian curb (kɜːb )
Definitions
noun1. a line of stone or concrete forming an edge between a pavement and a roadway, so that the pavement is some 15 cm above the level of the roadverb2. (transitive) to provide with or enclose with a kerb
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Old French courbe bent, from Latin curvus; see curve
