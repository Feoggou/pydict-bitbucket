thrum1

(θrʌm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: thrums, thrumming or thrummed1. to strum rhythmically but without expression on (a musical instrument)2. (intransitive) to drum incessantly ⇒ rain thrummed on the roof3. to repeat (something) monotonouslynoun4. a repetitive strumming or recitation

Derived Forms

thrummer (ˈthrummer) noun

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

thrum2 - textiles

(θrʌm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. any of the unwoven ends of warp thread remaining on the loom when the web has been removedb. such ends of thread collectively2. a fringe or tassel of short unwoven threadsverbWord forms: thrums, thrumming or thrummed3. (transitive) to trim with thrums

Word Origin

C14: from Old English; related to Old High German drum remnant, Dutch dreum
