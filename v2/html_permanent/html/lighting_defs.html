lighting

(ˈlaɪtɪŋ )
Definitions
noun1. the act or quality of illumination or ignition2. the apparatus for supplying artificial light effects to a stage, film, or television set3. the distribution of light on an object or figure, as in painting, photography, etc

[+ of] ⇒ ...the bright fluorescent lighting of the laboratory. ⇒ The whole room is bathed in soft lighting. ⇒ ...street lighting.2. uncount nounThe lighting in a film or play is the use of different electric lights to give a particular effect. ⇒ Peter Mumford's lighting and David Freeman's direction make a crucial contribution to the success of the staging.
