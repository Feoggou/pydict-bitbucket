hawker1

(ˈhɔːkə )
Definitions
nouna person who travels from place to place selling goods
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: probably from Middle Low German hōker, from hōken to peddle; see huckster

hawker2

(ˈhɔːkə )
Definitions
nouna person who hunts with hawks, falcons, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hafecere; see hawk1, -er1

hawker

Powered by COBUILD
(hɔːkəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hawkers
Definitions
countable nounYou can use hawker to refer to a person who tries to sell things by calling at people's homes or standing in the street, especially when you do not approve of this activity. [disapproval] ⇒ ...as soon as she saw that it was a visitor and not a hawker or tramp at her door.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'hawker'
I found a street hawker outside with a spare lad and asked him to take me to Quarshie's. Robert Wilson BLOOD IS DIRT

Similar Words

pedlar, tout, vendor, travelling salesman, crier, huckster, barrow boy (British), door-to-door salesman, cheap-jack (informal), colporteur
