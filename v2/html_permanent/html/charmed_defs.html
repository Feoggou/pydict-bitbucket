charmed

(tʃɑːmd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. delighted or fascinated ⇒ a charmed audience2. seemingly protected by a magic spell ⇒ he bears a charmed life3. physics possessing charm ⇒ a charmed quark

charmed

Powered by COBUILD
(tʃɑːʳmd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]A charmed place, time, or situation is one that is very beautiful or pleasant, and seems slightly separate from the real world or real life. [written] ⇒ ...the charmed atmosphere of Oxford in the late Twenties.2. →  lead/have/live/bear a charmed life
Example sentences containing 'charmed'
It needed no imagination to see how charmed his father must have been with a girl of twenty-three. Sara MacDonald SEA MUSICWe should be charmed , wined, welcomed, even here, in the stinking belly of the ship!
