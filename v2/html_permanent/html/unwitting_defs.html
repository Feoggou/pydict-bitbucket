unwitting

(ʌnˈwɪtɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective (usually prenominal)1. not knowing or conscious2. not intentional; inadvertent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unwittingly (unˈwittingly) adverbunwittingness (unˈwittingness) noun

Word Origin

Old English unwitende, from un-1 + witting, present participle of witan to know; related to Old High German wizzan to know, Old Norse vita

unwitting for learners of English

Definitions
adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a person or their actions as unwitting, you mean that the person does something or is involved in something without realizing it. ⇒ We're unwitting victims of the system. ⇒ It had been an unwitting blunder on Blair's part.unwittingly adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ He was unwittingly caught up in the confrontation.
