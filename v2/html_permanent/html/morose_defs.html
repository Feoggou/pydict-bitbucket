morose

(məˈrəʊs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveill-tempered or gloomy

Derived Forms

morosely (moˈrosely) adverbmoroseness (moˈroseness) or morosity (moˈrosity) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin mōrōsus peevish, capricious, from mōs custom, will, caprice

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is morose is miserable, bad-tempered, and not willing to talk very much to other people. ⇒ She was morose, pale, and reticent.morosely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ One elderly man sat morosely at the bar.
