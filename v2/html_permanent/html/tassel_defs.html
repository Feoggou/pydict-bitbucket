tassel

(ˈtæsəl )
Definitions
noun1. a tuft of loose threads secured by a knot or ornamental knob, used to decorate soft furnishings, clothes, etc2. anything resembling this tuft, esp the tuft of stamens at the tip of a maize inflorescenceverb -sels, -selling, -selled or US -sels, -seling or -seled3. (transitive) to adorn with a tassel or tassels4. (intransitive) (of maize) to produce stamens in a tuft5. (transitive) to remove the tassels from
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tasselly (ˈtasselly) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, from Vulgar Latin tassellus (unattested), changed from Latin taxillus a small die, from tālus gaming die
