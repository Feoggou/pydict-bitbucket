scythe

(saɪð )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a manual implement for cutting grass, etc, having a long handle held with both hands and a curved sharpened blade that moves in a plane parallel to the groundverb2. (transitive) to cut (grass, etc) with a scythe

Derived Forms

scytheˌlike (ˈscytheˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

Old English sigthe; related to Old Norse sigthr, Old High German segansa

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA scythe is a tool with a long curved blade at right angles to a long handle. It is used to cut long grass or grain.2. verbIf you scythe grass or grain, you cut it with a scythe. [V n] ⇒ Two men were attempting to scythe the long grass.
