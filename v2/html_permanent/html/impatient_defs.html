impatient

(ɪmˈpeɪʃənt )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking patience; easily irritated at delay, opposition, etc2. exhibiting lack of patience ⇒ an impatient retort3. (postpositive; foll by of) intolerant (of) or indignant (at) ⇒ impatient of indecision4. (postpositive; often foll by for) restlessly eager (for something or to do something)

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are impatient, you are annoyed because you have to wait too long for something. ⇒ He is impatient as the first hour passes and then another. [+ at] ⇒ The big clubs are becoming increasingly impatient at the rate of progress.impatiently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ People have been waiting impatiently for a chance to improve the situation.impatience (ɪmpeɪʃəns ) uncount noun [+ with] ⇒ There is considerable impatience with the slow pace of political change. ⇒ Impatience is growing after three days in which nothing has been achieved.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are impatient, you are easily irritated by things. [+ with] ⇒ Beware of being too impatient with others. ⇒ He threw it aside with an impatient gesture and another oath and walked off.impatiently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'Come on, David,' Harry said impatiently.impatience uncount noun ⇒ There was a hint of impatience in his tone.3. adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf]If you are impatient to do something or impatient for something to happen, you are eager to do it or for it to happen and do not want to wait. ⇒ He was impatient to get home. ⇒ They are impatient for jobs and security. [Also + for]impatience uncount noun [N to-inf] ⇒ She showed impatience to continue the climb. [Also + for]
