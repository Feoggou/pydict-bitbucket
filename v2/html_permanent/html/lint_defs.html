lint

(lɪnt )
Definitions
noun1. an absorbent cotton or linen fabric with the nap raised on one side, used to dress wounds, etc2. shreds of fibre, yarn, etc3. mainly US staple fibre for making cotton yarn
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

linty (ˈlinty) adjective

Word Origin

C14: probably from Latin linteus made of linen, from līnum flax
