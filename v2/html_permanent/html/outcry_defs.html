outcry

Definitions
noun (ˈaʊtˌkraɪ ) plural -cries1. a widespread or vehement protest2. clamour; uproar3. business a method of trading in which dealers shout out bids and offers at a prearranged meeting ⇒ sale by open outcryverb (ˌaʊtˈkraɪ ) -cries, -crying or -cried4. (transitive) to cry louder or make more noise than (someone or something)

outcry for learners of English

(aʊtkraɪ )
Word forms: outcries
Definitions
variable nounAn outcry is a reaction of strong disapproval and anger shown by the public or media about a recent event. ⇒ The killing caused an international outcry. ⇒ There was public outcry from those opposed to abortion.
