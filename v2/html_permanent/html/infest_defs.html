infest

(ɪnˈfɛst )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to inhabit or overrun in dangerously or unpleasantly large numbers2. (of parasites such as lice) to invade and live on or in (a host)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

infestation (ˌinfesˈtation) nouninfester (inˈfester) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin infestāre to molest, from infestus hostile

1. verbWhen creatures such as insects or rats infest plants or a place, they are present in large numbers and cause damage. [V n] ⇒ ...pests like aphids which infest cereal crops.infested adjective [+ with] ⇒ The prison is infested with rats. ⇒ In less than seven days the infested plants had recovered.-infested combining form ⇒ ...the rat-infested slums where the plague flourished.infestation (ɪnfesteɪʃən )Word forms: infestations variable noun [oft n N] ⇒ The premises were treated for cockroach infestation. ⇒ Parts of California are suffering from an infestation of oriental fruit flies. [Also + of]2. verbIf you say that people or things you disapprove of or regard as dangerous are infesting a place, you mean that there are large numbers of them in that place. [disapproval] [V n] ⇒ Crime and drugs are infesting the inner cities.infested adjective [+ with] ⇒ The road further south was infested with bandits.-infested combining form ⇒ ...the shark-infested waters of the Great Barrier Reef.
