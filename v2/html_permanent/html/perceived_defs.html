perceived

(pəˈsiːvd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveobserved ⇒ There are a lot of perceived problems as opposed to actual problems., ⇒ How would they tackle perceived problems of staff morale?, ⇒ the debate as to how to cope with the perceived threat of the tyranny of the majority, ⇒ the perceived threat of nuclear weapons, ⇒ the president's perceived failure to deal with these problems
Example sentences containing 'perceived'
He would have wakened me as soon as he perceived I was hearing an intruder. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANBut Caroline would care too much about how she was perceived to skimp on that.
