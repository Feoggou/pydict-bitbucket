bromine

(ˈbrəʊmiːn , -mɪn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna pungent dark red volatile liquid element of the halogen series that occurs in natural brine and is used in the production of chemicals, esp ethylene dibromide. Symbol: Br; atomic no: 35; atomic wt: 79.904; valency: 1, 3, 5, or 7; relative density 3.12; density (gas): 7.59 kg/m3; melting pt: –7.2°C; boiling pt: 58.78°C
Word origin
C19: from French brome bromine, from Greek brōmos bad smell + -ine2, of uncertain origin
