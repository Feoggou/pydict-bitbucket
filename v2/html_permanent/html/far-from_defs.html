far from

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
phraseIf you say that something is far from a particular thing or far from being the case, you are emphasizing that it is not that particular thing or not at all the case, especially when people expect or assume that it is. [emphasis] ⇒ It was obvious that much of what they recorded was far from the truth. ⇒ Far from being relaxed, we both felt so uncomfortable we hardly spoke. ⇒ It is still far from clear exactly what the Thais intend to do. →  far
