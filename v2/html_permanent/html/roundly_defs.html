roundly

(ˈraʊndlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. frankly, bluntly, or thoroughly ⇒ to be roundly criticized2. in a round manner or so as to be round
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

roundly

Powered by COBUILD
(raʊndli ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adverb & adverbIf you are roundly condemned or criticized, you are condemned or criticized forcefully or by many people. If you are roundly defeated, you are defeated completely. ⇒ Political leaders have roundly condemned the shooting. ⇒ Liz Davies took the issue to the party but was roundly defeated.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
