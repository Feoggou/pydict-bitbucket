pathos

(ˈpeɪθɒs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the quality or power, esp in literature or speech, of arousing feelings of pity, sorrow, etc2. a feeling of sympathy or pity ⇒ a stab of pathos

Word Origin

C17: from Greek: suffering; related to penthos sorrow

pathos

(peɪθɒs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounPathos is a quality in a situation, film, or play that makes people feel sadness and pity. [+ of] ⇒ ...the pathos of man's isolation. ⇒ With touching pathos he described the pangs of hunger.
Example sentences containing 'pathos'
Although she found the pathos of this last remark irritating, Vi was dissatisfied with herself. Penelope Fitzgerald HUMAN VOICESshe asked, matter of fact, wringing the pathos out of her voice.
