paltry

(ˈpɔːltrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -trier or -triest1. insignificant; meagre2. worthless or petty

Derived Forms

paltrily (ˈpaltrily) adverbpaltriness (ˈpaltriness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Low Germanic palter, paltrig ragged

paltry

Powered by COBUILD
(pɔːltri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A paltry amount of money or of something else is one that you consider to be very small. ⇒ ...a paltry fine of £150. ⇒ They suffered an electoral catastrophe, winning a paltry 3 seats.2. graded adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use paltry to describe something or someone that you consider to be small or unimportant. ⇒ The parents had little interest in paltry domestic concerns.
