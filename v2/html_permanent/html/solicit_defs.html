solicit

(səˈlɪsɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -its, -iting or -ited1. (when intr, foll by for) to make a request, application, or entreaty to (a person for business, support, etc)2. to accost (a person) with an offer of sexual relations in return for money3. to provoke or incite (a person) to do something wrong or illegal
Derived forms
soˌlicitation (soˌliciˈtation) noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French solliciter to disturb, from Latin sollicitāre to harass, from sollicitus agitated, from sollus whole + citus, from ciēre to excite
