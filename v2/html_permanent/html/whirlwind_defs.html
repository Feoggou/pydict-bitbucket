whirlwind

(ˈwɜːlˌwɪnd )
Definitions
noun1. a column of air whirling around and towards a more or less vertical axis of low pressure, which moves along the land or ocean surface2. a. a motion or course resembling this, esp in rapidityb. (as modifier) ⇒ a whirlwind romance3. an impetuously active person

whirlwind for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɜːʳlwɪnd )
Word forms: whirlwinds
Definitions
1. countable nounA whirlwind is a tall column of air which spins round and round very fast and moves across the land or sea.2. countable noun [usu sing]You can describe a situation in which a lot of things happen very quickly and are very difficult for someone to control as a whirlwind. [+ of] ⇒ I had been running around southern England in a whirlwind of activity. ⇒ He had been swept aside in the whirlwind of reform and anarchy.3. adjective [ADJ n]A whirlwind event or action happens or is done much more quickly than normal. ⇒ He got married after a whirlwind romance. ⇒ ... a whirlwind tour of France.
