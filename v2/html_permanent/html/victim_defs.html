victim

(ˈvɪktɪm )
Definitions
noun1. a person or thing that suffers harm, death, etc, from another or from some adverse act, circumstance, etc ⇒ victims of tyranny2. a person who is tricked or swindled; dupe3. a living person or animal sacrificed in a religious rite▶ USAGE Using the word victim or victims in relation to chronic illness or disability is often considered demeaning and disempowering. Alternative phrases such as who experiences, who has been diagnosed with, or simply with and then the name of the disability or illness, can be used instead
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin victima

(vɪktɪm ) for learners of English
Word forms: victims
Definitions
1. countable nounA victim is someone who has been hurt or killed. ⇒ Not all the victims survived. [+ of] ⇒ Statistically our chances of being the victims of violent crime are remote.2. countable nounA victim is someone who has suffered as a result of someone else's actions or beliefs, or as a result of unpleasant circumstances. [+ of] ⇒ He was a victim of racial prejudice. ⇒ He described himself and Altman as victims rather than participants in the scandal. ⇒ Infectious diseases are spreading among many of the flood victims.3. →  fall victim to
