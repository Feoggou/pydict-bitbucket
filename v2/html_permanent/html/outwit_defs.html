outwit

(ˌaʊtˈwɪt )
Definitions
verb -wits, -witting or -witted (transitive)1. to get the better of by cunning or ingenuity2. archaic to be of greater intelligence than
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

outwit for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtwɪt )
Word forms: outwits , outwitting , outwitted
Definitions
verbIf you outwit someone, you use your intelligence or a clever trick to defeat them or to gain an advantage over them. outsmart [V n] ⇒ To win the presidency he had first to outwit his rivals within the Socialist Party. [V n] ⇒ Seeing how cleverly he had been outwitted he made no complaint.
