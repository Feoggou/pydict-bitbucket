seraph

(ˈsɛrəf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -aphs, -aphim (-əfɪm) or -aphin (-əfɪn)1. theology a member of the highest order of angels in the celestial hierarchies, often depicted as the winged head of a child2. Old Testament one of the fiery six-winged beings attendant upon Jehovah in Isaiah's vision (Isaiah 6)

Word Origin

C17: back formation from plural seraphim, via Late Latin from Hebrew
