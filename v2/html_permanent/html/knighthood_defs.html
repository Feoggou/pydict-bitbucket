knighthood

(ˈnaɪthʊd )
Definitions
noun1. the order, dignity, or rank of a knight2. the qualities of a knight; knightliness3. knights collectively
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

knighthood for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(naɪthʊd )
Word forms: knighthoods
Definitions
countable nounA knighthood is a title that is given to a man by a British king or queen for his achievements or his service to his country. A man who has been given a knighthood can put 'Sir' in front of his name instead of 'Mr'.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
