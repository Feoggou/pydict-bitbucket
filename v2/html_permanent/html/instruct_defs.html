instruct

(ɪnˈstrʌkt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to direct to do something; order2. to teach (someone) how to do (something)3. to furnish with information; apprise4. law, mainly Britisha. (esp of a client to his solicitor or a solicitor to a barrister) to give relevant facts or information tob. to authorize (a barrister or solicitor) to conduct a case on a person's behalf ⇒ to instruct counsel
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

instructible (inˈstructible) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin instruere to construct, set in order, equip, teach, from struere to build

(ɪnstrʌkt ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. verbIf you instruct someone to do something, you formally tell them to do it. [formal] [V n to-inf] ⇒ The family has instructed solicitors to sue Thomson for compensation. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Go and have a word with her, Ken,' Webb instructed. [V n that] ⇒ I want you to instruct them that they've got three months to get the details sorted out. [Also V n with quote]2. verbSomeone who instructs people in a subject or skill teaches it to them. [V n + in/on] ⇒ He instructed family members in nursing techniques. [Also V]
