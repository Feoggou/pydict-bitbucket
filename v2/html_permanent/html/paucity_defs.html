paucity

(ˈpɔːsɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. smallness of quantity; insufficiency; dearth2. smallness of number; fewness

Word Origin

C15: from Latin paucitās scarcity, from paucus few

paucity

Powered by COBUILD
(pɔːsɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular nounIf you say that there is a paucity of something, you mean that there is not enough of it. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Even the film's impressive finale can't hide the first hour's paucity of imagination. [+ of] ⇒ ...the paucity of good British women sprinters.
Example sentences containing 'paucity'
There had been no paucity of witnesses to Tesla's relocation of a part of Coney Eye. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOWThat small word was hardly adequate to express such epic paucity of spirit, such a monumental failure of the heart. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEMarvel at the paucity of her contribution to life's great discussion! Edward Docx THE CALLIGRAPHERMade sense; judging by the paucity of gear in the kitchen, Nelson hadn't apprenticed at Spago. Jack Kerley THE HUNDREDTH MAN
