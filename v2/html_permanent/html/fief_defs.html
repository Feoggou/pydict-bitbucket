fief

or feoff (fiːf )
Definitions
noun(in feudal Europe) the property or fee granted to a vassal for his maintenance by his lord in return for service
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Old French fie, of Germanic origin; compare Old English fēo cattle, money, Latin pecus cattle, pecūnia money, Greek pokos fleece
