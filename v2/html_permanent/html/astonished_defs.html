astonished

(əˈstɒnɪʃt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivefilled with amazement; greatly surprised ⇒ An astonished eye witness said, 'It almost ended in tragedy.', ⇒ 'You never knew?' she asked in an astonished voice., ⇒ Her mother looked astonished., ⇒ I was astonished by the amount he could eat., ⇒ I was astonished at his stupidity., ⇒ They were astonished to see the driver was a six-year-old boy., ⇒ She was astonished to hear him speak English.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [ADJ to-inf, ADJ that]If you are astonished by something, you are very surprised about it. ⇒ I was astonished by his stupidity. ⇒ They were astonished to find the driver was a six-year-old boy.
Example sentences containing 'astonished'
She wasn't in the least astonished , a lady well used to being asked for aid. Curzon, Clare THE QUEST FOR KFrances, who expected Painter to say something scathing, was astonished to see him beam at this extravagance.
