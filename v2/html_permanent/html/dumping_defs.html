dumping

(ˈdʌmpɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the disposal (of waste) in the sea or on land ⇒ We need to stop the dumping of rubbish in our canals., ⇒ efforts to end the dumping of waste into waterways, ⇒ German law forbids the dumping of hazardous waste on German soil., ⇒ The dumping of waste into local rivers has discoloured the water., ⇒ proposals by Scandinavian countries to ban the dumping of toxic waste at sea2. economics the offering for sale of large quantities (of goods) on foreign markets at low prices in order to maintain a high price in the home market and obtain a share of the foreign markets ⇒ There is nothing we can do about dumping - selling goods at below production cost., ⇒ They have called for an investigation to determine whether dumping of products is taking place.3. the hasty selling (of stocks and shares) in the belief that prices will fall or continue falling ⇒ the wholesale dumping of shares on stock markets around the world

COBUILD Key Words for Chemical Engineering
noun(Chemical Engineering: Distillation) Dumping is a fault in distillation, caused by the low speed of a gas, which means that valve trays (= trays with covered holes) do not operate very well. ⇒ This design uses a packing on the tray to limit fluid oscillations and prevent dumping at low vapor velocities. ⇒ If the vapor velocity is too low, vapor does not flow through all the holes in the tray, and liquid leaks through, which is known as dumping. ⇒ Dumping is a fault in distillation, caused by the low speed of a gas, which means that valve trays do not operate very well.
