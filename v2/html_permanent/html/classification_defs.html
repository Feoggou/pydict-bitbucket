classification

(ˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. systematic placement in categories2. one of the divisions in a system of classifying3. biologya. the placing of animals and plants in a series of increasingly specialized groups because of similarities in structure, origin, molecular composition, etc, that indicate a common relationship. The major groups are domain or superkingdom, kingdom, phylum (in animals) or division (in plants), class, order, family, genus, and speciesb. the study of the principles and practice of this process; taxonomy4. government the designation of an item of information as being secret and not available to people outside a restricted group
Derived forms
ˌclassificational (ˌclassifiˈcational) adjectiveˌclassificatory (ˌclassifiˈcatory) adjective
Word origin
C18: from French; see class, -ify, -ation
