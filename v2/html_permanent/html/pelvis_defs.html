pelvis

(ˈpɛlvɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -vises or -ves (-viːz)1. the large funnel-shaped structure at the lower end of the trunk of most vertebrates: in man it is formed by the hipbones and sacrum2. the bones that form this structure3. any anatomical cavity or structure shaped like a funnel or cup4. short for renal pelvis

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: basin, laver
