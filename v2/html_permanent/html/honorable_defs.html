honorable

(ˈɑnərəbəl ) in American English
Definitions
adjective1.  worthy of being honored; specif.,a.  [H-] of, or having a position of, high rank or worth, used as a title of courtesy for certain officials and for the children of certain British peers ⇒ our Representative, the Honorable Jane Smithb.  noble; illustriousc.  of good reputation; respectable2.  having or showing a sense of right and wrong; characterized by honesty and integrity; upright3.  bringing honor to the owner or doer4.  doing honor; accompanied with marks of respect ⇒ an honorable burial
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

honorability (ˌhonoraˈbility) noun or ˈhonorableness honorably (ˈhonorably) adverb

Word Origin

ME honourable < OFr honorable < L honorabilis < honor
