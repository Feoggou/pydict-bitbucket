amused

(əˈmjuːzd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. believing something to have a humorous quality ⇒ He was not amused., ⇒ Sara was not amused by Franklin's teasing., ⇒ We were amused to see how assiduously the animal groomed its fur., ⇒ She was smiling enigmatically as if amused by some private joke., ⇒ We were amused at the antics of the chimps., ⇒ He was most amused by the story.2. pleasantly occupied ⇒ Having pictures to colour will keep children amused for hours., ⇒ Archie kept us amused with his stories.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf]If you are amused by something, it makes you want to laugh or smile. [+ by/at] ⇒ Sara was not amused by Franklin's teasing. ⇒ We were amused to see how assiduously the animal groomed its fur.2. →  keep sb amused
