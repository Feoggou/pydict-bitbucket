laughable

(ˈlɑːfəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. producing scorn; ludicrous ⇒ he offered me a laughable sum for the picture2. arousing laughter
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

laughableness (ˈlaughableness) nounlaughably (ˈlaughably) adverb

laughable

Powered by COBUILD
(lɑːfəbəl , læf-) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you say that something such as an idea or suggestion is laughable, you mean that it is so stupid as to be funny and not worth serious consideration. ⇒ The idea that TV shows like 'Dallas' or 'Dynasty' represent typical American life is laughable. ⇒ He has denied suggestions that he hates all English people, claiming the allegations are 'laughable'.laughably adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ To an outsider, the issues that we fight about would seem almost laughably petty.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
