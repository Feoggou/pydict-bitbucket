sleepy

(ˈsliːpɪ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: sleepier or sleepiest1. inclined to or needing sleep; drowsy2. characterized by or exhibiting drowsiness, sluggishness, etc3. conducive to sleep; soporific4. without activity or bustle ⇒ a sleepy town

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are sleepy, you are very tired and are almost asleep. ⇒ I was beginning to feel amazingly sleepy. ⇒ She was still tired and sleepy when he woke her.sleepily graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Joanna sat up, blinking sleepily.sleepiness uncount noun ⇒ He tried to fight the sleepiness that overwhelmed him.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A sleepy place is quiet and does not have much activity or excitement. OPPOSITE: bustling, lively ⇒ Valence is a sleepy little town just south of Lyon.
