bone

(bəʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of the various structures that make up the skeleton in most vertebrates2. the porous rigid tissue of which these parts are made, consisting of a matrix of collagen and inorganic salts, esp calcium phosphate, interspersed with canals and small holes ▶ Related adjectives: osseous, osteal3. something consisting of bone or a bonelike substance4. (plural) the human skeleton or body ⇒ they laid his bones to rest, ⇒ come and rest your bones5. a thin strip of whalebone, light metal, plastic, etc, used to stiffen corsets and brassieres6. (plural) the essentials (esp in the phrase the bare bones) ⇒ to explain the bones of a situation7. (plural) dice8. (plural) an informal nickname for a doctor9. See close to the bone10. See feel in one's bones11. See have a bone to pick12. See make no bones about13. See point the boneverb (mainly tr)14. to remove the bones from (meat for cooking, etc)15. to stiffen (a corset, etc) by inserting bones16. to fertilize with bone meal17. taboo, slang to have sexual intercourse with18. British a slang word for steal▷ See also bone up
Derived forms
boneless (ˈboneless) adjective
Word origin
Old English bān; related to Old Norse béin, Old Frisian bēn, Old High German bein

Definitions

[V n] ⇒ Make sure that you do not pierce the skin when boning the chicken thighs.
