cloaca

(kləʊˈeɪkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cae (-kiː)1. a cavity in the pelvic region of most vertebrates, except higher mammals, and certain invertebrates, into which the alimentary canal and the genital and urinary ducts open2. a sewer
Derived forms
cloacal (cloˈacal), cloacaline (cloˈacaline) or ˌcloacinal (ˌcloaˈcinal) adjective
Word origin
C18: from Latin: sewer; related to Greek kluzein to wash out
