Jacobite

(ˈdʒækəˌbaɪt )
Definitions
noun1. British history an adherent of James II after his overthrow in 1688, or of his descendants in their attempts to regain the throne2. a member of the Monophysite Church of Syria, which became a schismatic church in 451 ad
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

Jacobitic (ˌdʒækəˈbɪtɪk ) adjectiveJacobitism (ˈJacoˌbitism) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin Jacōbus James + -ite1
