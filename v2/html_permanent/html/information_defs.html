information

(ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. knowledge acquired through experience or study2. knowledge of specific and timely events or situations; news3. the act of informing or the condition of being informed4. a. an office, agency, etc, providing informationb. (as modifier) ⇒ information service5. a. a charge or complaint made before justices of the peace, usually on oath, to institute summary criminal proceedingsb. a complaint filed on behalf of the Crown, usually by the attorney general6. computinga. the meaning given to data by the way in which it is interpretedb. another word for data (sense 2)7. See too much information
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. uncount nounInformation about someone or something consists of facts about them. [+ about] ⇒ Pat refused to give her any information about Sarah. [+ on] ⇒ Each centre would provide information on technology and training. ⇒ For further information contact the number below. ⇒ ...an important piece of information. ⇒ The information was passed on to another government department.2. uncount nounInformation consists of the facts and figures that are stored and used by a computer program. [computing] ⇒ Pictures are scanned into a form of digital information that computers can recognize.3. uncount nounInformation is a service which you can telephone to find out someone's telephone number. [US]regional note:   in BRIT, use directory enquiries
