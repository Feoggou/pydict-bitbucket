casuist

(ˈkæzjʊɪst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person, esp a theologian, who attempts to resolve moral dilemmas by the application of general rules and the careful distinction of special cases2. a person who is oversubtle in his or her analysis of fine distinctions; sophist
Derived forms
ˌcasuistic (ˌcasuˈistic) or ˌcasuistical (ˌcasuˈistical) adjectiveˌcasuistically (ˌcasuˈistically) adverb
Word origin
C17: from French casuiste, from Spanish casuista, from Latin cāsus case1
