gratify

(ˈɡrætɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to satisfy or please2. to yield to or indulge (a desire, whim, etc)3. obsolete to reward
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gratifier (ˈgratiˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin grātificārī to do a favour to, from grātus grateful + facere to make

(grætɪfaɪ ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. verbIf you are gratified by something, it gives you pleasure or satisfaction. [formal] [be V-ed] ⇒ Mr. Dambar was gratified by his response. [Also V n]gratified graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ to-inf, ADJ that] ⇒ He was gratified to hear that his idea had been confirmed. ⇒ They were gratified that America kept its promise.gratifying graded adjective & adjective ⇒ We took a chance and we've won. It's very gratifying. ⇒ ...a gratifying development.gratification (grætɪfɪkeɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ He is waiting for them to recognise him and eventually they do, much to his gratification.2. verbIf you gratify your own or another person's desire, you do what is necessary to please yourself or them. [formal] [V n] ⇒ We gratified our friend's curiosity. [V n] ⇒ Every whim will be gratified.gratification uncount noun ⇒ ...sexual gratification.
