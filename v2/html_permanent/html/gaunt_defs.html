gaunt

(ɡɔːnt )
Definitions
adjective1. bony and emaciated in appearance2. (of places) bleak or desolate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gauntly (ˈgauntly) adverbgauntness (ˈgauntness) noun

Word Origin

C15: perhaps of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect gand tall lean person

gaunt

Powered by COBUILD
(gɔːnt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone looks gaunt, they look very thin, usually because they have been very ill or worried. ⇒ Looking gaunt and tired, he denied there was anything to worry about.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you describe a building as gaunt, you mean it is very plain and unattractive. [literary] ⇒ Above on the hillside was a large, gaunt, grey house.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
