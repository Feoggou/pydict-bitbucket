apt

(æpt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. suitable for the circumstance or purpose; appropriate2. (postpositive; foll by an infinitive) having a tendency (to behave as specified)3. having the ability to learn and understand easily; clever (esp in the phrase an apt pupil)
Derived forms
ˈaptly adverbˈaptness noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin aptus fitting, suitable, from apere to fasten

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveAn apt remark, description, or choice is especially suitable. apposite, appropriate ⇒ The words of this report are as apt today as in 1929. ⇒ ...an apt description of the situation.aptly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ ...the beach in the aptly named town of Oceanside.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is apt to do something, they often do it and so it is likely that they will do it again. ⇒ She was apt to raise her voice and wave her hands about. ⇒ This type of weather is apt to be more common in winter.
