listen

(ˈlɪsən )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to concentrate on hearing something2. to take heed; pay attention ⇒ I told you many times but you wouldn't listen
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

listener (ˈlistener) noun

Word Origin

Old English hlysnan; related to Old High German lūstrēn

listen

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪsən ) for learners of English
Word forms: listens , listening , listened
Definitions
1. verbIf you listen to someone who is talking or to a sound, you give your attention to them or it. [V + to] ⇒ He spent his time listening to the radio. [V] ⇒ Sonia was not listening.listener Word forms: listeners countable noun ⇒ One or two listeners had fallen asleep while the President was speaking.2. verbIf you listen for a sound, you keep alert and are ready to hear it if it occurs. [V + for] ⇒ We listen for footsteps approaching. [V] ⇒ They're both asleep upstairs, but you don't mind listening just in case of trouble, do you?Listen out means the same as listen. [British] [V P + for] ⇒ I didn't really listen out for the lyrics. [Also V P]3. verbIf you listen to someone, you do what they advise you to do, or you believe them. [V + to] ⇒ Anne, you need to listen to me this time. [V] ⇒ When I asked him to stop, he would not listen.4. conventionYou say listen when you want someone to pay attention to you because you are going to say something important. ⇒ Listen, I finish at one.5. conventionYou say listen up when you want someone to listen to what you are going to say. [US, spoken] ⇒ Okay, listen up, guys. We've got to talk a little about how you look.6. →  listen here► →  listen in
