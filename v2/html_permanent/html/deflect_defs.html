Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you deflect something such as criticism or attention, you act in a way that prevents it from being directed towards you or affecting you. [V n] ⇒ Cage changed his name to deflect accusations of nepotism. [V n + from] ⇒ It's a maneuver to deflect the attention of the people from what is really happening.2. verbTo deflect someone from a course of action means to make them decide not to continue with it by putting pressure on them or by offering them something desirable. [V n + from] ⇒ The war did not deflect him from the path he had long ago taken. [V n] ⇒ Never let a little problem deflect you.3. verbIf you deflect something that is moving, you make it go in a slightly different direction, for example by hitting or blocking it. [V n prep] ⇒ He stuck out his boot and deflected the shot over the bar seconds before the final whistle. [V n] ⇒ My forearm deflected most of the first punch.
