Thebes

(θiːbz )
Definitions
noun1. (in ancient Greece) the chief city of Boeotia, destroyed by Alexander the Great (336 bc)2. (in ancient Egypt) a city on the Nile: at various times capital of Upper Egypt or of the entire country
