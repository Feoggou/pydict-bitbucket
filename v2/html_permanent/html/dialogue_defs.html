dialogue

or often US dialog (ˈdaɪəˌlɒɡ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. conversation between two or more people2. an exchange of opinions on a particular subject; discussion3. the lines spoken by characters in drama or fiction4. a particular passage of conversation in a literary or dramatic work5. a literary composition in the form of a dialogue6. a political discussion between representatives of two nations or groupsverb rare7. (transitive) to put into the form of a dialogue8. (intransitive) to take part in a dialogue; converse
Derived forms
dialogic (ˌdaɪəˈlɒdʒɪk ) adjectivediaˌloguer (ˈdiaˌloguer) or diaˌloger (ˈdiaˌloger) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French dialoge, from Latin dialogus, from Greek dialogos, from dialegesthai to converse; see dialect
