opossum

(əˈpɒsəm )
Definitions
noun plural -sums or -sum1. any thick-furred marsupial, esp Didelphis marsupialis (common opossum), of the family Didelphidae of S North, Central, and South America, having an elongated snout and a hairless prehensile tail. Sometimes (informal) shortened to: possum2. Also called (Austral and NZ): possum any of various similar animals, esp the phalanger, Trichosurus vulpecula, of the New Zealand bush
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Algonquian aposoum; related to Delaware apässum, literally: white beast
