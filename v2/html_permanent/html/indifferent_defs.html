indifferent

(ɪnˈdɪfrənt , -fərənt)
Definitions
adjective1. (often foll by to) showing no care or concern; uninterested ⇒ he was indifferent to my pleas2. unimportant; immaterial3. a. of only average or moderate size, extent, quality, etcb. not at all good; poor4. showing or having no preferences; impartial5. biologya. (of cells or tissues) not differentiated or specializedb. (of a species) not found in any particular community
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

indifferently (inˈdifferently) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin indifferēns making no distinction

indifferent

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndɪfərənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you accuse someone of being indifferent to something, you mean that they have a complete lack of interest in it. [+ to] ⇒ People have become indifferent to the suffering of others. ⇒ ...the row over the police's indifferent attitude to the killings.indifferently graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ 'Not that it matters,' said Tench indifferently.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something or someone as indifferent, you mean that their standard or quality is not very good, and often quite bad. ⇒ She had starred in several very indifferent movies. ⇒ Much of the food we eat is of very poor or indifferent quality.indifferently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...an eight-year-old girl who reads tolerably and writes indifferently.
