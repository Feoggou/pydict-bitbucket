freely

Powered by COBUILD
(friːli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV adj]Freely means many times or in large quantities. ⇒ We have referred freely to his ideas. ⇒ George was spending very freely. ⇒ ...the United States, where consumer goods are freely available.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v]If you can talk freely, you can talk without needing to be careful about what you say. ⇒ She wondered whether he had someone to whom he could talk freely.3. graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v]If someone gives or does something freely, they give or do it willingly, without being ordered or forced to do it. willingly ⇒ Danny shared his knowledge freely with anyone interested. ⇒ Williams freely admits he lives for racing.4. graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v]If something or someone moves freely, they move easily and smoothly, without any obstacles or resistance. ⇒ The clay court was slippery and he was unable to move freely. ⇒ You must allow the clubhead to swing freely.5. → See also free
