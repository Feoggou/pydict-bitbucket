Synonyms of pressure

pressure

noun

  1. force, crushing, squeezing, compressing, weight, compression, heaviness
    • The pressure of his fingers had relaxed.
  2. power, influence, force, obligation, constraint, sway, compulsion, coercion
    • He may be putting pressure on her to agree.
  3. stress, demands, difficulty, strain, press, heat, load, burden, distress, hurry, urgency, hassle (informal), uphill (South Africa), adversity, affliction, exigency
    • The pressures of modern life are great.

verb

  1. force, influence, persuade, compel, intimidate, drive, badger, coerce, bulldoze, brainwash, dragoon, pressurize, breathe down someone's neck, browbeat, press-gang, prevail on, twist someone's arm (informal), turn on the heat (informal), put the screws on (slang)
    • He claimed the police pressured him to change his testimony.

QUOTATION:
If you can't stand the heat, get out of the kitchenHarry S Truman
