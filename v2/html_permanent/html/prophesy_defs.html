prophesy

(ˈprɒfɪˌsaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -sies, -sying or -sied1. to reveal or foretell (something, esp a future event) by or as if by divine inspiration2. (intransitive) archaic to give instruction in religious subjects

Derived Forms

propheˌsiable (ˈpropheˌsiable) adjectivepropheˌsier (ˈpropheˌsier) noun

Word Origin

C14 prophecien, from prophecy

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you prophesy that something will happen, you say that you strongly believe that it will happen. [V that] ⇒ He prophesied that within five years his opponent would either be dead or in prison. [V n] ⇒ She prophesied a bad ending for the expedition. [Also V]
