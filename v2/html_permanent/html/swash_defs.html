swash

(swɒʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) (esp of water or things in water) to wash or move with noisy splashing2. (transitive) to dash (a liquid, esp water) against or upon3. (intransitive) archaic to swagger or blusternoun4. Also called: send the dashing movement or sound of water, such as that of waves on a beach. Compare backwash5. any other swashing movement or sound6. a sandbar washed by the waves7. Also called: swash channel a channel of moving water cutting through or running behind a sandbank8. archaica. swagger or blusterb. a swashbuckler

Word Origin

C16: probably of imitative origin
