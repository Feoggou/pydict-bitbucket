antelope

(ˈæntɪˌləʊp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -lopes or -lope1. any bovid mammal of the subfamily Antilopinae, of Africa and Asia. They are typically graceful, having long legs and horns, and include the gazelles, springbok, impala, gerenuk, blackbuck, and dik-diks2. any of various similar bovids of Africa and Asia3. See American antelope
Word origin
C15: from Old French antelop, from Medieval Latin antalopus, from Late Greek antholops a legendary beast

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounAn antelope is an animal like a deer, with long legs and horns, that lives in Africa or Asia. Antelopes are graceful and can run fast. There are many different types of antelope.
