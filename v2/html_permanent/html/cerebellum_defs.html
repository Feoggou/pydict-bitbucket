cerebellum

(ˌsɛrɪˈbɛləm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -lums or -la (-lə)one of the major divisions of the vertebrate brain, situated in man above the medulla oblongata and beneath the cerebrum, whose function is coordination of voluntary movements and maintenance of bodily equilibrium
Derived forms
ˌcerebellar (ˌcereˈbellar) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin, diminutive of cerebrum

Walking unblocks the thinking processes - shakes up the cerebellum , they tell me. Moore, Margaret FORESTS OF THE NIGHTInjury or organic disease in the cerebellum may produce such symptoms as a staggering walk, palsy, slurred speech, chronic malaise. Joyce Carol Oates MIDDLE AGE: A ROMANCE
