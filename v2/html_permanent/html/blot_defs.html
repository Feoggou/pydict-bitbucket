blot1

(blɒt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a stain or spot of ink, paint, dirt, etc2. something that spoils or detracts from the beauty or worth of something3. a blemish or stain on one's character or reputationverbWord forms: blots, blotting or blotted4. (of ink, dye, etc) to form spots or blobs on (a material) or (of a person) to cause such spots or blobs to form on (a material)5. See blot one's copybook6. (intransitive) to stain or become stained or spotted7. (transitive) to cause a blemish in or on; disgrace8. to soak up (excess ink, etc) by using blotting paper or some other absorbent material9. (of blotting paper or some other absorbent material) to absorb (excess ink, etc)10. (transitive; often foll by out)a. to darken or hide completely; obscure; obliterateb. to destroy; annihilate
Word origin
C14: probably of Germanic origin; compare Middle Dutch bluyster blister

blot

Powered by COBUILD
(blɒt )
Word forms: blots , blotting , blotted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf something is a blot on a person's or thing's reputation, it spoils their reputation. black mark [+ on] ⇒ ...a blot on the reputation of the architectural profession. [+ on] ⇒ This drugs scandal is another blot on the Olympics.2. countable nounA blot is a drop of liquid that has fallen on to a surface and has dried. ⇒ ...an ink blot.3. verbIf you blot a surface, you remove liquid from it by pressing a piece of soft paper or cloth onto it. [V n] ⇒ Before applying make-up, blot the face with a tissue to remove any excess oils. [Also V n adj]4. →  blot on the landscape► →  blot out
