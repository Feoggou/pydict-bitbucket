patter1

(ˈpætə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to walk or move with quick soft steps2. to strike with or make a quick succession of light tapping sounds3. (transitive) rare to cause to patternoun4. a quick succession of light tapping sounds, as of feet ⇒ the patter of mice

Word Origin

C17: from pat1

patter2

(ˈpætə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the glib rapid speech of comedians, salesmen, etc2. quick idle talk; chatter3. the jargon of a particular group; lingoverb4. (intransitive) to speak glibly and rapidly5. to repeat (prayers) in a mechanical or perfunctory manner

Word Origin

C14: from Latin pater in Pater Noster Our Father

patter

Powered by COBUILD
(pætəʳ )
Word forms: patters , pattering , pattered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something patters on a surface, it hits it quickly several times, making quiet, tapping sounds. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Rain pattered gently outside, dripping on to the roof from the pines.2. singular nounA patter is a series of quick, quiet, tapping sounds. [+ of] ⇒ ...the patter of the driving rain on the roof.3. singular noun [usu poss N]Someone's patter is a series of things that they say quickly and easily, usually in order to entertain people or to persuade them to buy or do something. spiel ⇒ There's no doubt women found him charming. It must have been his patter because he's certainly not at all good-looking. ⇒ Fran began her automatic patter about how Jon had been unavoidably detained.
