irreversible

(ˌɪrɪˈvɜːsəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. not able to be reversed ⇒ the irreversible flow of time2. not able to be revoked or repealed; irrevocable3. chemistry, physics capable of changing or producing a change in one direction only ⇒ an irreversible reaction4. thermodynamics (of a change, process, etc) occurring through a number of intermediate states that are not all in thermodynamic equilibrium
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

(ɪrɪvɜrsɪbəl) in Mechanical Engineering
Definitions
adjective(Mechanical engineering: Energy, thermodynamics and heat transfer) An irreversible thermodynamic process cannot happen in the reverse direction without changing the surroundings. ⇒ The best-known example of an irreversible process is the flow of heat, which can only take place from a warmer area to a cooler one. ⇒ Many thermodynamic processes are irreversible. ⇒ An irreversible thermodynamic process cannot happen in the reverse direction without changing the surroundings.
