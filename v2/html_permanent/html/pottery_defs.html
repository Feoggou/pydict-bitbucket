pottery

(ˈpɒtərɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -teries1. articles, vessels, etc, made from earthenware and dried and baked in a kiln2. a place where such articles are made3. the craft or business of making such articlesfictile

Word Origin

C15: from Old French poterie, from potier potter, from pot pot1

Word forms: potteries
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounYou can use pottery to refer to pots, dishes, and other objects which are made from clay and then baked in an oven until they are hard.2. uncount nounYou can use pottery to refer to the hard clay that some pots, dishes, and other objects are made of. ⇒ Some bowls were made of pottery and wood.3. uncount nounPottery is the craft or activity of making objects out of clay.4. countable nounA pottery is a factory or other place where pottery is made.
