progeny

(ˈprɒdʒɪnɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -nies1. the immediate descendant or descendants of a person, animal, etc2. a result or outcome

Word Origin

C13: from Latin prōgeniēs lineage; see progenitor

progeny

Powered by COBUILD
(prɒdʒəni )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. plural noun [usu with poss]You can refer to a person's children or to an animal's young as their progeny. [formal] ⇒ Davis was never loquacious on the subject of his progeny.2. plural noun [usu with poss]The progeny of a particular thing are the things that develop from it. offspring ⇒ Among its many progeny, the 1944 Education Act gave birth to the modern youth service.
