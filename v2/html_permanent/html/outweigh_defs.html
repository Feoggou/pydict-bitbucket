outweigh

(ˌaʊtˈweɪ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to prevail over; overcome ⇒ his desire outweighed his discretion2. to be more important or significant than3. to be heavier than
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

outweigh for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtweɪ )
Word forms: outweighs , outweighing , outweighed
Definitions
verbIf one thing outweighs another, the first thing is of greater importance, benefit, or significance than the second thing. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The medical benefits of x-rays far outweigh the risk of having them. [V n] ⇒ The advantages of this deal largely outweigh the disadvantages.
