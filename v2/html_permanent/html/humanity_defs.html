humanity

(hjuːˈmænɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the human race2. the quality of being human3. kindness or mercy4. See the humanities5. the study of Ancient Greek and Roman language, literature, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

humanity

Powered by COBUILD
(hjuːmænɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: humanities
Definitions
1. uncount nounAll the people in the world can be referred to as humanity. ⇒ They face charges of committing crimes against humanity. ⇒ ...a young lawyer full of illusions and love of humanity.2. uncount noun [with poss]A person's humanity is their state of being a human being, rather than an animal or an object. [formal] ⇒ ...a man who's almost lost his humanity in his bitter hatred of his rivals.3. uncount nounHumanity is the quality of being kind, thoughtful, and sympathetic towards others. ⇒ Her speech showed great maturity and humanity.4. plural nounThe humanities are the subjects such as history, philosophy, and literature which are concerned with human ideas and behaviour. arts ⇒ ...students majoring in the humanities.
