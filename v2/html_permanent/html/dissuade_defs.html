dissuade

(dɪˈsweɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. (often foll by from) to deter (someone) by persuasion from a course of action, policy, etc2. to advise against (an action, etc)
Derived forms
dissuadable (disˈsuadable) adjectivedissuader (disˈsuader) noundissuasion (disˈsuasion) noundissuasive (disˈsuasive) or dissuasory (disˈsuasory) adjectivedissuasively (disˈsuasively) adverbdissuasiveness (disˈsuasiveness) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin dissuādēre, from dis-1 + suādēre to persuade

Word forms: dissuades , dissuading , dissuaded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you dissuade someone from doing or believing something, you persuade them not to do or believe it. [formal] [V n + from] ⇒ Doctors had tried to dissuade patients from smoking. [V n from -ing/n] ⇒ She steadfastly maintained that her grandsons were innocent, and nothing could dissuade her from that belief. [V n] ⇒ He considered emigrating, but his family managed to dissuade him.
