dimple

(ˈdɪmpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small natural dent or crease in the flesh, esp on the cheeks or chin2. any slight depression in a surface3. a bubble or dent in glassverb4. to make or become dimpled5. (intransitive) to produce dimples by smiling
Derived forms
dimply (ˈdimply) adjective
Word origin
C13 dympull; compare Old English dyppan to dip, German Tümpel pool
