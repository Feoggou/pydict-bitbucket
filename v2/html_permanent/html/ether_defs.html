ether

(ˈiːθə )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: diethyl ether, ethyl ether, ethoxyethane a colourless volatile highly flammable liquid with a characteristic sweetish odour, made by the reaction of sulphuric acid with ethanol: used as a solvent and anaesthetic. Formula: C2H5OC2H52. any of a class of organic compounds with the general formula ROR′ where R and R′ are alkyl groups, as in diethyl ether C2H5OC2H53. See the ether4. Greek mythology the upper regions of the atmosphere; clear sky or heaven5. a rare word for air
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

etheric (iːˈθɛrɪk ) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin aether, from Greek aithēr, from aithein to burn
