fleece

(fliːs )
Definitions
noun1. the coat of wool that covers the body of a sheep or similar animal and consists of a mass of crinkly hairs2. the wool removed from a single sheep3. something resembling a fleece in texture or warmth4. sheepskin or a fabric with soft pile, used as a lining for coats, etc5. a warm polyester fabric with a brushed nap, used for outdoor garments6. a jacket or top made from such a fabricverb (transitive)7. to defraud or charge exorbitantly; swindle8. another term for shear (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flēos; related to Middle High German vlius, Dutch vlies fleece, Latin plūma feather, down

fleece

Powered by COBUILD
(fliːs ) for learners of English
Word forms: fleeces , fleecing , fleeced
Definitions
1. countable nounA sheep's fleece is the coat of wool that covers it.2. countable nounA fleece is the wool that is cut off one sheep in a single piece.3. verbIf you fleece someone, you get a lot of money from them by tricking them or charging them too much. [informal] [V n + out of] ⇒ She claims he fleeced her out of thousands of pounds. [Also V n]4. variable nounFleece is a soft warm artificial fabric. A fleece is also a jacket or other garment made from this fabric.
