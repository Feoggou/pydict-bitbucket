sue

(sjuː , suː )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: sues, suing or sued1. to institute legal proceedings (against)2. to make suppliant requests of (someone for something)3. archaic to pay court (to)

Derived Forms

suer (ˈsuer) noun

Word Origin

C13: via Anglo-Norman from Old French sivre, from Latin sequī to follow

Sue - French

( sy)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun Eugène (øʒɛn). original name Marie-Joseph Sue. 1804–57, French novelist, whose works, notably Les mystères de Paris (1842–43) and Le juif errant (1844–45), were among the first to reflect the impact of the industrial revolution on France

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you sue someone, you start a legal case against them, usually in order to claim money from them because they have harmed you in some way. [V n + for] ⇒ Mr Warren sued him for libel over the remarks. [V n for n] ⇒ The company could be sued for damages. [V] ⇒ One former patient has already indicated his intention to sue. [Also V n]
