parameter

(pəˈræmɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. one of a number of auxiliary variables in terms of which all the variables in an implicit functional relationship can be explicitly expressed. See parametric equations2. a variable whose behaviour is not being considered and which may for present purposes be regarded as a constant, as y in the partial derivative ∂f(x,y)/∂x3. statistics a characteristic of the distribution of a population, such as its mean, as distinct from that of a sample. Compare statistic4. informal any constant or limiting factor ⇒ a designer must work within the parameters of budget and practicality

Derived Forms

parametric (ˌpærəˈmɛtrɪk ) or ˌparametrical (ˌparaˈmetrical) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from New Latin; see para-1, -meter
