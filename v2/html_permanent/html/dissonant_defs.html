dissonant

(ˈdɪsənənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. discordant; cacophonous2. incongruous or discrepant3. music characterized by dissonance
Derived forms
dissonantly (ˈdissonantly) adverb
Word origin
C15: from Latin dissonāre to be discordant, from dis-1 + sonāre to sound
Example sentences containing 'dissonant'
When Ellel spoke of Hunagor and Werra dying, her voice pealed like dissonant bells, an enjoyment Qualary perceived but did not question. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSThe wheels of the clown's cooler hiccuped across sidewalk cracks, a dissonant night song. Gregg Andrew Hurwitz DO NO HARM
