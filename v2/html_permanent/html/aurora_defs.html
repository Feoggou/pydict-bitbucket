aurora

(ɔːˈrɔːrə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ras or -rae (-riː)1. an atmospheric phenomenon consisting of bands, curtains, or streamers of light, usually green, red, or yellow, that move across the sky in polar regions. It is caused by collisions between air molecules and charged particles from the sun that are trapped in the earth's magnetic field2. poetic the dawn
Derived forms
auˈroral adjectiveauˈrorally adverb
Word origin
C14: from Latin: dawn; see east
