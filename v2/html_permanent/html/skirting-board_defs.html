Comment by lothrian on 10 Nov 2014
I was always brought up to call this 'wainskirting'. I didn't think anything of my choice of word until I was at work one day, painting the town council chamber and my boss laughed at me: 'When were you born ? Victorian times ?!' Slightly embarrassed, I stuck to 'skirting board' ever since. I have often wondered though, where exactly did I get this word from ? Had I somehow made it up as a kid ? There is 'wainscot', which is something different from 'skirting', in that 'wainscot' would appear to be high wooden panelling in a room. Browsing the web just now, I came across a question asked on a site back in 2003 regarding the use of the word 'wayne skirting' (quote-unquote). So there ARE others like me out there possessing this lingustic quirk! If you stumble on this comment and it puts your mind at ease, I'm very pleased: Carry on using 'wainskirting', because it seems to be a correct alternative.
