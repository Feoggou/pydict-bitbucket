secretion

(sɪˈkriːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a substance that is released from a cell, esp a glandular cell, and is synthesized in the cell2. the process involved in producing and releasing such a substance from the cell
Derived forms
secretionary (seˈcretionary) adjective
Word origin
C17: from Medieval Latin sēcrētiō, from Latin: a separation; see secern

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounSecretion is the process by which certain liquid substances are produced by parts of plants or from the bodies of people or animals. [+ of] ⇒ ...the secretion of adrenaline. ⇒ ...insulin secretion.2. plural nounSecretions are liquid substances produced by parts of plants or bodies. ⇒ ...gastric secretions.
