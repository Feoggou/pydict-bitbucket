quorum

(ˈkwɔːrəm )
Definitions
nouna minimum number of members in an assembly, society, board of directors, etc, required to be present before any valid business can be transacted ⇒ the quorum is forty, ⇒ we don't have a quorum
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin, literally: of whom, occurring in Latin commissions in the formula quorum vos … duos (etc) volumus of whom we wish that you be … two

Definitions
singular nounA quorum is the minimum number of people that a committee needs in order to carry out its business officially. When a meeting has a quorum, there are at least that number of people present. ⇒ ...enough deputies to make a quorum.
