impeachment

(ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt )
Definitions
noun1. rare (in England) committal by the House of Commons, esp of a minister of the Crown, for trial by the House of Lords. The last instance occurred in 18052. (in the US) a proceeding brought against a federal government official3. an accusation or charge4. obsolete discredit; reproach
