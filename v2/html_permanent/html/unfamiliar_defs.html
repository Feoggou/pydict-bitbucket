unfamiliar

(ˌʌnfəˈmɪljə )
Definitions
adjective1. not known or experienced; strange2. (postpositive; foll by with) not familiar
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unfamiliar for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnfəmɪlɪəʳ )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is unfamiliar to you, you know nothing or very little about it, because you have not seen or experienced it before. [+ to] ⇒ She grew many wonderful plants that were unfamiliar to me. ⇒ I was alone in an unfamiliar city.unfamiliarity (ʌnfəmɪliærɪti ) uncount noun [oft with poss] [+ to] ⇒ ...problems which arise from the newness of the approach and its unfamiliarity to prisoners.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ with n]If you are unfamiliar with something, it is unfamiliar to you. OPPOSITE: familiar with [+ with] ⇒ She speaks no Japanese and is unfamiliar with Japanese culture.unfamiliarity uncount noun [+ with] ⇒ ...her unfamiliarity with the politics of the region.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
