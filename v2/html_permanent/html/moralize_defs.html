moralize

or moralise (ˈmɒrəˌlaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to make moral pronouncements2. (transitive) to interpret or explain in a moral sense3. (transitive) to improve the morals of

Derived Forms

ˌmoralization (ˌmoraliˈzation) or ˌmoralisation (ˌmoraliˈsation) nounmoralˌizer (ˈmoralˌizer) or moralˌiser (ˈmoralˌiser) noun

regional note:   in BRIT, also use moraliseverbIf you say that someone is moralizing, you are critical of them for telling people what they think is right or wrong, especially when they have not been asked their opinion.
