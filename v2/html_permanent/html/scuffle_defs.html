scuffle1

(ˈskʌfəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to fight in a disorderly manner2. to move by shuffling3. to move in a hurried or confused mannernoun4. a disorderly struggle5. the sound made by scuffling or shuffling

Word Origin

C16: from Scandinavian; compare Swedish skuff, skuffa to push

scuffle

Powered by COBUILD
(skʌfəl )
Word forms: scuffles , scuffling , scuffled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA scuffle is a short, disorganized fight or struggle. ⇒ Violent scuffles broke out between rival groups demonstrating for and against independence.2. verbIf people scuffle, they fight for a short time in a disorganized way. [V + with] ⇒ Police scuffled with some of the protesters. [V] ⇒ He and Hannah had been scuffling in the yard outside his house.
