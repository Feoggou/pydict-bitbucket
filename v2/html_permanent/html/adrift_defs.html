adrift

(əˈdrɪft )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective, adverb (postpositive)1. floating without steering or mooring; drifting2. without purpose; aimless3. informal off course or amiss ⇒ the project went adrift

(ədrɪft )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [v-link ADJ]If a boat is adrift, it is floating on the water and is not tied to anything or controlled by anyone. ⇒ They were spotted after three hours adrift in a dinghy.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone is adrift, they feel alone with no clear idea of what they should do. ⇒ Amy had the growing sense that she was adrift and isolated.3. adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ after v]If something comes adrift, it is no longer attached to an object that it should be part of. [British] [+ from] ⇒ Three insulating panels had come adrift from the base of the vehicle.4. graded adjective [v-link ADJ, usu ADJ of n]In sporting competitions, if a team or a player is adrift of their rivals, they are behind them, usually by a specified number of points or by a specified distance. [British] ⇒ Aberdeen are nine points adrift of Rangers at the top of the Scottish League.5. graded adjective [v-link ADJ]If something has gone adrift, it is no longer happening in the way that was intended. awry ⇒ We have seen this as an attempt to place the blame for a policy that has gone adrift.
