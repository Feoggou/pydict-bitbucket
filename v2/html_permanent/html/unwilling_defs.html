unwilling

(ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. unfavourably inclined; reluctant2. performed, given, or said with reluctance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unwillingly (unˈwillingly) adverbunwillingness (unˈwillingness) noun

unwilling for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnwɪlɪŋ )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link, usu ADJ to-inf]If you are unwilling to do something, you do not want to do it and will not agree to do it. ⇒ Initially the government was unwilling to accept the defeat. ⇒ For months I had been either unwilling or unable to go through with it.unwillingness uncount noun [oft N to-inf] OPPOSITE: willingness ⇒ ...their unwillingness to accept responsibility for mistakes. ⇒ ...the unwillingness of banks to grant loans.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use unwilling to describe someone who does not really want to do the thing they are doing. ⇒ A youthful teacher, he finds himself an unwilling participant in school politics. ⇒ She was certainly an unwilling victim of circumstances.unwillingly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ My beard had started to grow, and I had unwillingly complied with the order to shave it off. ⇒ Unwillingly, she moved aside.
