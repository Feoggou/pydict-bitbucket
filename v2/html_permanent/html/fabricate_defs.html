fabricate

(ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to make, build, or construct2. to devise, invent, or concoct (a story, lie, etc)3. to fake or forge
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fabrication (ˌfabriˈcation) nounfabricative (ˈfabricative) adjectivefabricator (ˈfabriˌcator) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fabricāre to build, make, from fabrica workshop; see fabric

[V-ed] ⇒ Eleven key officials were hanged on fabricated charges.fabrication (fæbrɪkeɪʃən )Word forms: fabrications variable noun ⇒ She described the interview with her in an Italian magazine as a 'complete fabrication'. ⇒ This story is total fabrication.2. verbIf something is fabricated from different materials or substances, it is made out of those materials or substances.
