centrifugal

(sɛnˈtrɪfjʊɡəl , ˈsɛntrɪˌfjuːɡəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. acting, moving, or tending to move away from a centre. Compare centripetal2. of, concerned with, or operated by centrifugal force ⇒ centrifugal pump3. botany (esp of certain inflorescences) developing outwards from a centre4. physiology another word for efferentnoun5. any device that uses centrifugal force for its action6. the rotating perforated drum in a centrifuge
Derived forms
centrifugally (cenˈtrifugally) adverb
Word origin
C18: from New Latin centrifugus, from centri- + Latin fugere to flee

`The moon revolves around the earth and the centrifugal force counteracts the force of gravity.
