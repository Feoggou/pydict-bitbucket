slow-moving

(ˌsləʊˈmuːvɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. moving without much speed; moving very slowly ⇒ a slow-moving lorry, ⇒ Do you get frustrated by slow-moving traffic?, ⇒ a slow-moving warm front, ⇒ a wide, slow-moving river2. taking a long time to progress ⇒ a long, slow-moving supermarket queue3. developing at a slow pace ⇒ a slow-moving French drama
Example sentences containing 'slow-moving'
He tried to think of a way out of the cycle of pain, but his thoughts were sluggish, as opaque and slow-moving as glacier ice. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODBy now I'd run into a spot of slow-moving traffic, so that I was barely doing more than 5mph when I drew alongside the oncoming vehicle. Magnus Mills THE SCHEME FOR FULL EMPLOYMENTHe had other people in mind, dark, concerned, slow-moving , some driving cattle before them. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFETony tracked the car with ease, as the traffic was so congested, the horses, carts, and cars making a slow-moving mess. Lisa Scottoline THE VENDETTA DEFENCE
