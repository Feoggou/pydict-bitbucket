tone down

Definitions
verb(adverb) to moderate or become moderated in tone ⇒ to tone down an argument, ⇒ to tone down a bright colour

tone down

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you tone down something that you have written or said, you make it less forceful, severe, or offensive. [V P n] ⇒ The fiery right-wing leader toned down his militant statements after the meeting. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ The forecasts have since had to be toned down, as the economy has exhibited unmistakable signs of slowing. [V n P] ⇒ We have had to ask the agency and their client to tone their ads down.2. phrasal verbIf you tone down a colour or a flavour, you make it less bright or strong. [V P n] ⇒ When Ken Hom wrote his first book for the BBC he was asked to tone down the spices and garlic in his recipes. [Also V n P] →  tone
