rigid

(ˈrɪdʒɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. not bending; physically inflexible or stiff ⇒ a rigid piece of plastic2. unbending; rigorously strict; severe ⇒ rigid rulesadverb3. completely or excessively ⇒ the lecture bored him rigid
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rigidly (ˈrigidly) adverbrigidity (riˈgidity) or rigidness (ˈrigidness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin rigidus, from rigēre to be stiff

rigid

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪdʒɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveLaws, rules, or systems that are rigid cannot be changed or varied, and are therefore considered to be rather severe. [disapproval] ⇒ Several colleges in our study have rigid rules about student conduct. ⇒ Hospital routines for nurses are very rigid.rigidity (rɪdʒɪdɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the rigidity of government policy.rigidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The caste system was rigidly enforced.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you disapprove of someone because you think they are not willing to change their way of thinking or behaving, you can describe them as rigid. [disapproval] ⇒ She was a fairly rigid person who had strong religious views. ⇒ My father is very rigid in his thinking.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA rigid substance or object is stiff and does not bend, stretch, or twist easily. ⇒ ...rigid plastic containers. ⇒ These plates are fairly rigid.rigidity uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the strength and rigidity of glass.4. graded adjective [usu v-link ADJ, oft ADJ with n]If someone goes rigid, their body becomes very straight and stiff, usually as a result of shock or fear. ⇒ I went rigid with shock. ⇒ Andrew went rigid when he saw a dog, any dog, anywhere.rigidly graded adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ She stood rigidly and stared into the room.
