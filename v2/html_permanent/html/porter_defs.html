porter1

(ˈpɔːtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person employed to carry luggage, parcels, supplies, etc, esp at a railway station or hotel2. (in hospitals) a person employed to move patients from place to place3. US and Canadian a railway employee who waits on passengers, esp in a sleeper4. East Africa a manual labourer

Word Origin

C14: from Old French portour, from Late Latin portātōr, from Latin portāre to carry

porter2

(ˈpɔːtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. mainly British a person in charge of a gate or door; doorman or gatekeeper2. a person employed by a university or college as a caretaker and doorkeeper who also answers enquiries3. a person in charge of the maintenance of a building, esp a block of flats4. Also called: ostiary Roman Catholic Church a person ordained to what was formerly the lowest in rank of the minor orders

Word Origin

C13: from Old French portier, from Late Latin portārius doorkeeper, from Latin porta door
