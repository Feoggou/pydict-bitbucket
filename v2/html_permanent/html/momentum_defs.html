momentum

(məʊˈmɛntəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ta (-tə) or -tums1. physics the product of a body's mass and its velocity. Symbol: p See also angular momentum2. the impetus of a body resulting from its motion3. driving power or strength

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: movement; see moment

momentum

Powered by COBUILD
(moʊmentəm )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounIf a process or movement gains momentum, it keeps developing or happening more quickly and keeps becoming less likely to stop. ⇒ This campaign is really gaining momentum. ⇒ They are each anxious to maintain the momentum of the search for a solution.2. uncount nounIn physics, momentum is the mass of a moving object multiplied by its speed in a particular direction. [technical]
