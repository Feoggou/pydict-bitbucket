tabernacle

(ˈtæbəˌnækəl )
Definitions
noun1. (often capital) Old Testamenta. the portable sanctuary in the form of a tent in which the ancient Israelites carried the Ark of the Covenant (Exodus 25–27)b. the Jewish Temple regarded as the shrine of the divine presence2. Judaism an English word for sukkah3. a meeting place for worship used by Mormons or Nonconformists4. a small ornamented cupboard or box used for the reserved sacrament of the Eucharist5. the human body regarded as the temporary dwelling of the soul6. mainly Roman Catholic Church a canopied niche or recess forming the shrine of a statue7. nautical a strong framework for holding the foot of a mast stepped on deck, allowing it to be swung down horizontally to pass under low bridges, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tabernacular (ˌtaberˈnacular) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Latin tabernāculum a tent, from taberna a hut; see tavern
