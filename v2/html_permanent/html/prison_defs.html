prison

(ˈprɪzən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a public building used to house convicted criminals and accused persons remanded in custody and awaiting trial. See also jail, penitentiary, reformatory2. any place of confinement or seeming confinement

Word Origin

C12: from Old French prisun, from Latin prēnsiō a capturing, from prehendere to lay hold of

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable noun [oft in names]A prison is a building where criminals are kept as punishment or where people accused of a crime are kept before their trial. ⇒ The prison's inmates are being kept in their cells. ⇒ He was sentenced to life in prison. ⇒ They released Mr Mandela from prison in 1990. ⇒ ...the gas chamber at San Quentin Prison.
