philology

(fɪˈlɒlədʒɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. comparative and historical linguistics2. the scientific analysis of written records and literary texts3. (no longer in scholarly use) the study of literature in general

Derived Forms

philological (ˌfɪləˈlɒdʒɪkəl ) adjectiveˌphilologically (ˌphiloˈlogically) or ˌphilologic (ˌphiloˈlogic) adverbphilologist (phiˈlologist), philologian (ˌfɪləˈləʊdʒən) or rare philologer (phiˈlologer) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin philologia, from Greek: love of language
