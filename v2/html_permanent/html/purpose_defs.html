purpose

(ˈpɜːpəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the reason for which anything is done, created, or exists2. a fixed design, outcome, or idea that is the object of an action or other effort3. fixed intention in doing something; determination ⇒ a man of purpose4. practical advantage or use ⇒ to work to good purpose5. that which is relevant or under consideration (esp in the phrase to or from the purpose)6. archaic purport7. See on purposeverb (transitive)8. to intend or determine to do (something)

Word Origin

C13: from Old French porpos, from porposer to plan, from Latin prōpōnere to propose

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe purpose of something is the reason for which it is made or done. [+ of] ⇒ The purpose of the occasion was to raise money for medical supplies. ⇒ Various insurance schemes already exist for this purpose. ⇒ ...the use of nuclear energy for military purposes. ⇒ He was asked about casualties, but said it would serve no purpose to count bodies. ⇒ Most of them are destroyed because they've served their purpose.2. countable noun [with poss]Your purpose is the thing that you want to achieve. ⇒ They might well be prepared to do you harm in order to achieve their purpose. ⇒ His purpose was to make a profit by improving the company's performance.3. uncount nounPurpose is the feeling of having a definite aim and of being determined to achieve it. ⇒ The teachers are enthusiastic and have a sense of purpose.4. → see also cross-purposes5. →  for all practical purposes/to all intents and purposes6. →  on purpose
