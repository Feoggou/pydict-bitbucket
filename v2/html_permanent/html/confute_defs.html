confute

(kənˈfjuːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to prove (a person or thing) wrong, invalid, or mistaken; disprove2. obsolete to put an end to
Derived forms
confutable (conˈfutable) adjectiveconfutation (ˌkɒnfjʊˈteɪʃən ) nounconfutative (conˈfutative) adjectiveconfuter (conˈfuter) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin confūtāre to check, silence

Similar Words

disprove, overthrow, set aside, refute, invalidate, rebut, prove false, blow out of the water (slang), controvert, oppugn
