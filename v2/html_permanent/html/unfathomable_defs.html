unfathomable

(ʌnˈfæðəməbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. incapable of being fathomed; immeasurable2. incapable of being understood

unfathomable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnfæðəməbəl )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as unfathomable, you mean that it cannot be understood or explained, usually because it is very strange or complicated. ⇒ For some unfathomable reason, there are no stairs where there should be. ⇒ How odd life was, how unfathomable, how profoundly unjust.2. adjectiveIf you use unfathomable to describe a person or the expression on their face, you mean that you cannot tell what they are thinking or what they intend to do. [literary] ⇒ ...a strange, unfathomable and unpredictable individual. ⇒ ...the dark eyes that right now seemed opaque and unfathomable.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
