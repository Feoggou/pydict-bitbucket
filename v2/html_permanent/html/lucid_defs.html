lucid

(ˈluːsɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. readily understood; clear2. shining or glowing3. psychiatry of or relating to a period of normality between periods of insane or irresponsible behaviour
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lucidity (luˈcidity) or lucidness (ˈlucidness) nounlucidly (ˈlucidly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin lūcidus full of light, from lūx light

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveLucid writing or speech is clear and easy to understand. ⇒ ...a lucid account of the history of mankind. ⇒ His prose as always lucid and compelling.lucidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Both of them had the ability to present complex matters lucidly.lucidity (luːsɪdɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ His writings were marked by an extraordinary lucidity and elegance of style.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is lucid, they are thinking clearly again after a period of illness or confusion. [formal] clear-headed ⇒ He wasn't very lucid, he didn't quite know where he was.lucidity uncount noun ⇒ The pain had lessened in the night, but so had his lucidity.
