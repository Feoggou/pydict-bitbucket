come over

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (adverb) (of a person or his or her words) to communicate the intended meaning or impression ⇒ he came over very well2. (adverb) to change allegiances ⇒ some people came over to our side in the war3. informal to undergo or feel a particular sensation ⇒ I came over funnynoun comeover4. (in the Isle of Man) a person who has come over from the mainland of Britain to settle

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verb [no passive]If a feeling or desire, especially a strange or surprising one, comes over you, it affects you strongly. [V P n] ⇒ As I entered the corridor which led to my room that eerie feeling came over me. [V P n] ⇒ I'm sorry, I don't know what came over me.2. link phrasal verb & phrasal verbIf someone comes over all dizzy or shy, for example, they suddenly start feeling or acting in that way. [V P adj] ⇒ When Connie pours her troubles out to him, Joe comes over all sensitive. [V P adj] ⇒ Now you are coming over all puritanical about nothing.3. phrasal verbIf someone or what they are saying comes over in a particular way, they make that impression on people who meet them or are listening to them. [V P + as] ⇒ You come over as a capable and amusing companion. [V P adv] ⇒ He came over well–perhaps a little pompous, but nevertheless honest and straightforward. →  come
