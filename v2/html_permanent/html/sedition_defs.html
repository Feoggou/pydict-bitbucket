sedition

(sɪˈdɪʃən )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. speech or behaviour directed against the peace of a state2. an offence that tends to undermine the authority of a state3. an incitement to public disorder4. archaic revolt

Derived Forms

seditionary (seˈditionary) noun, adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin sēditiō discord, from sēd- apart + itiō a going, from īre to go
