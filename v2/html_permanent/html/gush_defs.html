gush

(ɡʌʃ )
Definitions
verb1. to pour out or cause to pour out suddenly and profusely, usually with a rushing sound2. to act or utter in an overeffusive, affected, or sentimental mannernoun3. a sudden copious flow or emission, esp of liquid4. something that flows out or is emitted5. an extravagant and insincere expression of admiration, sentiment, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gushing (ˈgushing) adjectivegushingly (ˈgushingly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: probably of imitative origin; compare Old Norse gjósa, Icelandic gusa

gush

Powered by COBUILD
(gʌʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: gushes , gushing , gushed
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbWhen liquid gushes out of something, or when something gushes a liquid, the liquid flows out very quickly and in large quantities. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Piping-hot water gushed out. [V n] ⇒ A supertanker continues to gush oil off the coast of Spain.2. singular nounA gush of liquid is a sudden, rapid flow of liquid, or a quantity of it that suddenly flows out. [+ of] ⇒ I heard a gush of water.3. verbIf someone gushes, they express their admiration or pleasure in an exaggerated way. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Oh, it was brilliant,' he gushes. [V prep] ⇒ He gushed about his love for his wife.gushing graded adjective & adjective ⇒ He delivered a gushing speech.4. singular noun [N of n]You can use gush in expressions such as a gush of enthusiasm to refer to a sudden intense feeling or an expression of a feeling. ⇒ She felt a gush of pure affection for her mother. ⇒ Their initial gush of sympathy gradually dried up.
