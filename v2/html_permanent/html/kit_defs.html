kit1

(kɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a set of tools, supplies, construction materials, etc, for use together or for a purpose ⇒ a first-aid kit, ⇒ a model aircraft kit2. the case or container for such a set3. a. a set of pieces of equipment ready to be assembledb. (as modifier) ⇒ kit furniture4. a. clothing and other personal effects, esp those of a traveller or soldier ⇒ safari kit, ⇒ battle kitb. informal clothing in general (esp in the phrase get one's kit off)5. New Zealand a flax basket6. See the whole kit
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

kit for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(kɪt )
Word forms: kits , kitting , kitted
Definitions
1. countable noun [oft n N]A kit is a group of items that are kept together, often in the same container, because they are all used for similar purposes. ⇒ ...a well-stocked first aid kit. ⇒ The kit consisted of about twenty cosmetic items and a lady's shaver.2. uncount nounKit is special clothing and equipment that you use when you take part in a particular activity, especially a sport. [mainly British] ⇒ I forgot my gym kit.3. countable nounA kit is a set of parts that can be put together in order to make something. ⇒ Her popular pot holder is also available in do-it-yourself kits.4. →  get your kit off/keep your kit on► →  kit out
