Carmelite

(ˈkɑːməˌlaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun Roman Catholic Church1. a member of an order of mendicant friars founded about 1154; White Friar2. a member of a corresponding order of nuns founded in 1452, noted for its austere rule3. (modifier) of or relating to the Carmelite friars or nuns
Word origin
C14: from French; named after Mount Carmel, where the order was founded
