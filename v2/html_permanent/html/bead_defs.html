bead

(biːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small usually spherical piece of glass, wood, plastic, etc, with a hole through it by means of which it may be strung with others to form a necklace, etc2. a small drop of moisture ⇒ a bead of sweat3. a small bubble in or on a liquid4. a small metallic knob acting as the sight of a firearm5. See draw a bead on6. Also called: astragal architecture, carpentry a small convex moulding having a semicircular cross section7. chemistry a small solid globule made by fusing a powdered sample with borax or a similar flux on a platinum wire. The colour of the globule serves as a test for the presence of certain metals (bead test)8. metallurgy a deposit of welding metal on the surface of a metal workpiece, often used to examine the structure of the weld zone9. Roman Catholic Church one of the beads of a rosary10. See count one's beadsverb11. (transitive) to decorate with beads12. to form into beads or drops
Derived forms
beaded (ˈbeaded) adjective
Word origin
Old English bed prayer; related to Old High German gibet prayer

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Beads are small pieces of coloured glass, wood, or plastic with a hole through the middle. Beads are often put together on a piece of string or wire to make jewellery. ⇒ ...a string of beads. ⇒ ...a multicoloured bead necklace.2. countable nounA bead of liquid or moisture is a small drop of it. [+ of] ⇒ ...beads of blood. [+ of] ⇒ He wiped away the beads of sweat on his forehead.
