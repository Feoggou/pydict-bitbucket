nebula

(ˈnɛbjʊlə )
Definitions
noun plural -lae (-ˌliː) or -las1. astronomy a diffuse cloud of particles and gases (mainly hydrogen) that is visible either as a hazy patch of light (either an emission or a reflection nebula) or an irregular dark region against a brighter background (dark nebula). Compare planetary nebula2. pathologya. opacity of the corneab. cloudiness of the urine3. any substance for use in an atomizer spray
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nebular (ˈnebular) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: mist, cloud; related to Greek nephétē cloud, Old High German nebul cloud, Old Norse njól night
