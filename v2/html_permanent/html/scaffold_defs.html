scaffold

(ˈskæfəld , -fəʊld)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a temporary metal or wooden framework that is used to support workmen and materials during the erection, repair, etc, of a building or other construction2. a raised wooden platform on which plays are performed, tobacco, etc, is dried, or (esp formerly) criminals are executedverb (transitive)3. to provide with a scaffold4. to support by means of a scaffold

Derived Forms

scaffolder (ˈscaffolder) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French eschaffaut, from Vulgar Latin catafalicum (unattested); see catafalque
