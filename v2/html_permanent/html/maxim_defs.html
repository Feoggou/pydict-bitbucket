maxim

(ˈmæksɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna brief expression of a general truth, principle, or rule of conduct

Word Origin

C15: via French from Medieval Latin, from maxima, in the phrase maxima prōpositio basic axiom (literally: greatest proposition); see maximum

maxim

(mæksɪm )
Word forms: maxims
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA maxim is a rule for good or sensible behaviour, especially one in the form of a saying. ⇒ I believe in the maxim 'if it ain't broke, don't fix it'.
Example sentences containing 'maxim'
Possession is nine-tenths of the law, a maxim which presumably also applies here in Germany. Martin, Joy THE IMAGE OF LAURAI felt like Serena as I mentally groped for some comforting maxim. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSESometimes she even found herself believing Andrew McClintock's maxim that things seldom happened, they were made to happen. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMEN
