canal

(kəˈnæl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an artificial waterway constructed for navigation, irrigation, water power, etc2. any of various tubular passages or ducts ⇒ the alimentary canal3. any of various elongated intercellular spaces in plants4. astronomy any of the indistinct surface features of Mars originally thought to be a network of channels but not seen on close-range photographs. They are caused by an optical illusion in which faint geological features appear to have a geometric structureverb -nals, -nalling, -nalled or US -nals, -naling or -naled (transitive)5. to dig a canal through6. to provide with a canal or canals
Word origin
C15 (in the sense: pipe, tube): from Latin canālis channel, water pipe, from canna reed, cane1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA canal is a long, narrow stretch of water that has been made for boats to travel along or to bring water to a particular area. ⇒ ...the Grand Union Canal. ⇒ ...Venetian canals and bridges.2. countable nounA canal is a narrow tube inside your body for carrying food, air, or other substances. ⇒ ...delaying the food's progress through the alimentary canal.
