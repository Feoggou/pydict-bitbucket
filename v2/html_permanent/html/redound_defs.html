redound

(rɪˈdaʊnd )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive; foll by to) to have an advantageous or disadvantageous effect (on) ⇒ brave deeds redound to your credit2. (intransitive; foll by on or upon) to recoil or rebound3. (intransitive) archaic to arise; accrue ⇒ wealth redounding from wise investment4. (transitive) archaic to reflect; bring ⇒ his actions redound dishonour upon him
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French redonder, from Latin redundāre to stream over, from red- re- + undāre to rise in waves, from unda a wave

Definitions
verbIf an action or situation redounds to your benefit or advantage, it gives people a good impression of you or brings you something that can improve your situation. [V + to] ⇒ The success in the Middle East redounds to his benefit. [V to n] ⇒ My skill in such matters might redound to my advantage.
