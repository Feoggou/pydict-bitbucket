elicit

(ɪˈlɪsɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to give rise to; evoke ⇒ to elicit a sharp retort2. to bring to light ⇒ to elicit the truth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

elicitable (eˈlicitable) adjectiveelicitation (eˌliciˈtation) nounelicitor (eˈlicitor) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ēlicere to lure forth, from licere to entice

elicit

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪlɪsɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: elicits , eliciting , elicited
Definitions
1. verbIf you elicit a response or a reaction, you do or say something which makes other people respond or react. [V n] ⇒ Mr Norris said he was hopeful that his request would elicit a positive response. [V n] ⇒ The crime elicited an outcry against illicit drugs.2. verbIf you elicit a piece of information, you get it by asking the right questions. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Phone calls elicited no further information.
