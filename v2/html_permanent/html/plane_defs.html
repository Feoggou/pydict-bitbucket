plane1

(pleɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. mathematics a flat surface in which a straight line joining any two of its points lies entirely on that surface2. a flat or level surface3. a level of existence, performance, attainment, etc4. a. short for aeroplaneb. a wing or supporting surface of an aircraft or hydroplaneadjective5. level or flat6. mathematics (of a curve, figure, etc) lying entirely in one planeverb (intransitive)7. to fly without moving wings or using engines; glide8. (of a boat) to rise partly and skim over the water when moving at a certain speed9. to travel by aeroplane

Derived Forms

planeness (ˈplaneness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin plānum level surface

plane2

(pleɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a tool with an adjustable sharpened steel blade set obliquely in a wooden or iron body, for levelling or smoothing timber surfaces, cutting mouldings or grooves, etc2. a flat tool, usually metal, for smoothing the surface of clay or plaster in a mouldverb (transitive)3. to level, smooth, or cut (timber, wooden articles, etc) using a plane or similar tool4. (often foll by off) to remove using a plane

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Late Latin plāna plane, from plānāre to level

plane

Powered by COBUILD
(pleɪn )
Word forms: planes , planing , planed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA plane is a vehicle with wings and one or more engines, which can fly through the air. ⇒ He had plenty of time to catch his plane. ⇒ Her mother was killed in a plane crash. ⇒ ...fighter planes.2. countable nounA plane is a flat, level surface which may be sloping at a particular angle. ⇒ ...a building with angled planes.3. singular nounIf a number of points are in the same plane, one line or one flat surface could pass through them all. ⇒ All the planets orbit the Sun in roughly the same plane, round its equator.4. countable noun [adj N]If you say that something is on a higher plane, you mean that it is more spiritual or less concerned with ordinary things. [literary] level ⇒ ...life on a higher plane of existence. ⇒ We felt we were living life on several different planes.5. countable nounA plane is a tool that has a flat bottom with a sharp blade in it. You move the plane over a piece of wood in order to remove thin pieces of its surface.6. verbIf you plane a piece of wood, you make it smaller or smoother by using a plane. [V n] ⇒ She watches him plane the surface of a walnut board. [V n adj] ⇒ Again I planed the surface flush.Plane down means the same as plane. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ The piece was reduced in size by planing down the four corners. [Also V n P]7. verbIf something such as a boat planes across water, it moves quickly across the water, just touching the surface. skim [V across n] ⇒ All four of the boats planed across the Solent with the greatest of ease. [Also V]8. countable nounA plane or a plane tree is a large tree with broad leaves which often grows in towns.► →  plane down
