asylum

(əˈsaɪləm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a safe or inviolable place of refuge, esp as formerly offered by the Christian Church to criminals, outlaws, etc; sanctuary (often in the phrase give asylum to)2. shelter; refuge3. international law refuge afforded to a person whose extradition is sought by a foreign government ⇒ political asylum4. obsolete an institution for the shelter, treatment, or confinement of individuals, esp a mental hospital (formerly termed lunatic asylum)
Word origin
C15: via Latin from Greek asulon refuge, from asulos that may not be seized, from a-1 + sulon right of seizure

Definitions
