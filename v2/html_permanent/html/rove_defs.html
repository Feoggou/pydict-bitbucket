rove1

(rəʊv )
Definitions
verb1. to wander about (a place) with no fixed direction; roam2. (intransitive) (of the eyes) to look around; wander3. See have a roving eye4. (intransitive) Australian Rules football to play as a rovernoun5. the act of roving
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15 roven (in archery) to shoot at a target chosen at random (C16: to wander, stray), from Scandinavian; compare Icelandic rāfa to wander

rove2

rove

Powered by COBUILD
(roʊv ) for learners of English
Word forms: roves , roving , roved
Definitions
1. verbIf someone roves about an area or roves an area, they wander around it. [literary] [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...roving about the town in the dead of night and seeing something peculiar. [V n] ⇒ She became a photographer, roving the world with her camera in her hand.2. verbIf you say that someone's eyes rove round a place, you mean that they are looking around to see what is interesting. [literary] wander [V prep] ⇒ Houston's eyes roved restlessly about the room. [V] ⇒ His eyes roved to see how many of the group appreciated his heavy humour.3. → See also roving
