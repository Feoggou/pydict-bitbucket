augur

(ˈɔːɡə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: auspex (in ancient Rome) a religious official who observed and interpreted omens and signs to help guide the making of public decisions2. any prophet or soothsayerverb3. to predict (some future event), as from signs or omens4. (tr; may take a clause as object) to be an omen (of); presage5. (intransitive) to foreshadow future events to be as specified; bode ⇒ this augurs well for us
Derived forms
augural (ˈɔːɡjʊrəl ) adjectiveˈaugurship noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin: a diviner, perhaps from augēre to increase
