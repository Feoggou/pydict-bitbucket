unaccountable

(ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. allowing of no explanation; inexplicable2. puzzling; extraordinary ⇒ an unaccountable fear of hamburgers3. not accountable or answerable to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unaccountableness (ˌunacˈcountableness) or unaccountability (ˌunacˌcountaˈbility) noununaccountably (ˌunacˈcountably) adverb

unaccountable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnəkaʊntəbəl )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is unaccountable does not seem to have any sensible explanation. ⇒ For some unaccountable reason, it struck me as extremely funny. ⇒ He had an unaccountable change of mind.unaccountably (ʌnəkaʊntəbli ) graded adverb & adverb ⇒ And then, unaccountably, she giggled. ⇒ Leonard felt unaccountably happy.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person or organization as unaccountable, you are critical of them because they are not responsible to anyone for their actions, or do not feel they have to explain their actions to anyone. [disapproval] ⇒ Economic policy in Europe should not be run by an unaccountable committee of governors of central banks. ⇒ ...an arrogant, unaccountable police chief.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
