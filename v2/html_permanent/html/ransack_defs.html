ransack

(ˈrænsæk )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to search through every part of (a house, box, etc); examine thoroughly2. to plunder; pillage
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ransacker (ˈransacker) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse rann house + saka to search, seek

(rænsæk ) for learners of English
Word forms: ransacks , ransacking , ransacked
Definitions
verbIf people ransack a building, they damage things in it or make it very untidy, often because they are looking for something in a quick and careless way. [V n] ⇒ Demonstrators ransacked and burned the house where he was staying. [V n] ⇒ He returned from hospital to find thieves had ransacked his home. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the wrecked schools and churches, the ransacked embassies and homes.ransacking singular noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the ransacking of the opposition party's offices.
