meteor

(ˈmiːtɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a very small meteoroid that has entered the earth's atmosphere. Such objects have speeds approaching 70 kilometres per second2. Also called: shooting star, falling star the bright streak of light appearing in the sky due to the incandescence of such a body heated by friction at its surface

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin meteōrum, from Greek meteōron something aloft, from meteōros lofty, from meta- (intensifier) + aeirein to raise
