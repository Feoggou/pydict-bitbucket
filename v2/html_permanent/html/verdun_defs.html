Verdun

(French vɛrdœ̃, English ˈvɛədʌn)
Definitions
noun1. a fortified town in NE France, on the Meuse: scene of the longest and most severe battle (1916) of World War I, in which the French repelled a powerful German offensive. Pop: 19 624 (1999). Ancient name: Verodunum (ˌvɛrəˈdjuːnəm )2. See Treaty of Verdun
