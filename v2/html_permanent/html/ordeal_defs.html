ordeal

(ɔːˈdiːl )
Definitions
noun1. a severe or trying experience2. history a method of trial in which the guilt or innocence of an accused person was determined by subjecting him or her to physical danger, esp by fire or water. The outcome was regarded as an indication of divine judgment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English ordāl, ordēl; related to Old Frisian ordēl, Old High German urteili (German Urteil) verdict. See deal1, dole1

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing, oft with poss]If you describe an experience or situation as an ordeal, you think it is difficult and unpleasant. ⇒ ...the painful ordeal of the last eight months. ⇒ She described her agonising ordeal.
