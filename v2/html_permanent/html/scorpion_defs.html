scorpion

(ˈskɔːpɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any arachnid of the order Scorpionida, of warm dry regions, having a segmented body with a long tail terminating in a venomous sting2. See false scorpion3. any of various other similar arachnids, such as the whip scorpion, or other arthropods, such as the water scorpion4. Old Testament a barbed scourge (I Kings 12:11)5. history a war engine for hurling stones; ballista

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin scorpiō, from Greek skorpios, of obscure origin
