exigent

(ˈɛksɪdʒənt )
Definitions
adjective1. urgent; pressing2. exacting; demanding
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exigently (ˈexigently) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin exigere to drive out, weigh out, from agere to drive, compel
Example sentences containing 'exigent'
He didn't debate; he collected briskly the Captain's salute, and strode off to attend to his exigent duties. Harvey, John COUP D'ETATHe rushed up here and searched the house without a warrant because he had established exigent circumstances. Phillip Margolin WILD JUSTICE
