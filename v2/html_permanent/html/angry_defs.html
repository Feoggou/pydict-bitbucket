angry

(ˈæŋɡrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -grier or -griest1. feeling or expressing annoyance, animosity, or resentment; enraged2. suggestive of anger ⇒ angry clouds3. severely inflamed ⇒ an angry sore▶ USAGE It was formerly considered incorrect to talk about being angry at a person, but this use is now acceptable
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]When you are angry, you feel strong dislike or impatience about something. [+ at] ⇒ She had been very angry at the person who stole her new bike. [+ with] ⇒ Are you angry with me for some reason? [+ about] ⇒ I was angry about the rumours. ⇒ He's angry that people have called him a racist. ⇒ An angry mob gathered outside the courthouse.angrily (æŋgrɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Officials reacted angrily to those charges. ⇒ Health workers are angrily demanding higher pay.2. graded adjectiveAn angry wound or rash is red and painful. ⇒ He was badly concussed, the glass leaving two angry cuts across his forehead.3. graded adjectiveIf you describe the sky or sea as angry, you mean that it is dark and stormy. [literary] threatening ⇒ Under the angry red sky he ran, into the thickening darkness.
