tout

(taʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to solicit (business, customers, etc) or hawk (merchandise), esp in a brazen way2. (intransitive)a. to spy on racehorses being trained in order to obtain information for betting purposesb. to sell, or attempt to sell, such information or to take bets, esp in public places3. (transitive) informal to recommend flatteringly or excessivelynoun4. a. a person who spies on racehorses so as to obtain betting information to sellb. a person who sells information obtained by such spying5. a person who solicits business in a brazen way6. Also called: ticket tout a person who sells tickets unofficially for a heavily booked sporting event, concert, etc, at greatly inflated prices7. Northern Ireland a police informer

Derived Forms

touter (ˈtouter) noun

Word Origin

C14 (in the sense: to peer, look out): related to Old English tӯtan to peep out

(taʊt )
Word forms: touts , touting , touted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone touts something, they try to sell it or convince people that it is good. [disapproval] [V n] ⇒ It has the trappings of an election campaign in the United States, with slick television ads touting the candidates. [V n] ⇒ ...a popular advertising industry practice of using performers to tout products. [be V-ed as n/adj/-ing] ⇒ He was being touted as the most interesting thing in pop. [be V-ed as n/adj/-ing] ⇒ The product is touted as being completely natural. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a couple of highly touted novels.2. verbIf someone touts for business or custom, they try to obtain it. [mainly British] [V + for] ⇒ He visited Thailand and Singapore to tout for investment. [V for n] ⇒ Minicabs are not allowed to tout for hire on the streets.3. verbIf someone touts tickets, they sell them outside a sports ground or theatre, usually for more than their original value. [British] [V n] ⇒ ...a man who made his money touting tickets. [V n] ⇒ The queue stretches several hundred yards and tickets are touted for a tenner.regional note:   in AM, use scalp4. countable nounA tout is someone who sells things such as tickets unofficially, usually at prices which are higher than the official ones. [British]regional note:   in AM, use scalper
