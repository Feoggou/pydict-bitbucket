tunic

(ˈtjuːnɪk )
Definitions
noun1. any of various hip-length or knee-length garments, such as the loose sleeveless garb worn in ancient Greece or Rome, the jacket of some soldiers, or a woman's hip-length garment, worn with a skirt or trousers2. anatomy, botany, zoology a covering, lining, or enveloping membrane of an organ or part. See also tunica3. mainly Roman Catholic Church another word for tunicle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English tunice (unattested except in the accusative case), from Latin tunica
