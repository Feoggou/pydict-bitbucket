bare1

(bɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. unclothed; exposed: used esp of a part of the body2. without the natural, conventional, or usual covering or clothing ⇒ a bare tree3. lacking appropriate furnishings, etc ⇒ a bare room4. unembellished; simple ⇒ the bare facts5. (prenomial) just sufficient; mere ⇒ he earned the bare minimum6. See with one's bare handsverb7. (transitive) to make bare; uncover; reveal
Derived forms
bareness (ˈbareness) noun
Word origin
Old English bær; compare Old Norse berr, Old High German bar naked, Old Slavonic bosǔ barefoot

bare

Powered by COBUILD
(beəʳ )
Word forms: barer , barest , bares , baring , bared
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf a part of your body is bare, it is not covered by any clothing. ⇒ She was wearing only a thin robe over a flimsy nightdress, and her feet were bare. ⇒ She had bare arms and a bare neck.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A bare surface is not covered or decorated with anything. ⇒ They would have liked bare wooden floors throughout the house.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a tree or a branch is bare, it has no leaves on it. ⇒ ...an old, twisted tree, its bark shaggy, many of its limbs brittle and bare.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a room, cupboard, or shelf is bare, it is empty. ⇒ His fridge was bare apart from three very withered tomatoes. ⇒ He led me through to a bare, draughty interviewing room.5. graded adjective & adjectiveAn area of ground that is bare has no plants growing on it. ⇒ That's probably the most bare, bleak, barren and inhospitable island I've ever seen.6. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone gives you the bare facts or the barest details of something, they tell you only the most basic and important things. ⇒ Newspaper reporters were given nothing but the bare facts by the Superintendent in charge of the investigation.7. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you talk about the bare minimum or the bare essentials, you mean the very least that is necessary. ⇒ The army would try to hold the western desert with a bare minimum of forces. ⇒ These are the bare essentials you'll need to dress your baby during the first few months.8. adjectiveBare is used in front of an amount to emphasize how small it is. [emphasis] ⇒ Sales are growing for premium wines, but at a bare 2 percent a year.9. verbIf you bare something, you uncover it and show it. [written] [V n] ⇒ Walsh bared his teeth in a grin. [V n] ⇒ He bared his muscular, hairy chest for a women's magazine.10. → bare bones11. →  with one's bare hands12. →  to lay something bare13. →  lay sth bare14. →  to bare one's soul
