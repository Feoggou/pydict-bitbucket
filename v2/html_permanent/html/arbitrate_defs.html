arbitrate

(ˈɑːbɪˌtreɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to settle or decide (a dispute); achieve a settlement between parties2. to submit to or settle by arbitration
Derived forms
ˈarbitrable adjectiveˈarbiˌtrator nounˈarbiˌtratrix feminine noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin arbitrāri to give judgment; see arbiter

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen someone in authority arbitrates between two people or groups who are in dispute, they consider all the facts and make an official decision about who is right. adjudicate [V + between] ⇒ He arbitrates between investors and members of the association. [V] ⇒ The tribunal had been set up to arbitrate in the dispute.arbitrator (ɑːʳbɪtreɪtəʳ )Word forms: arbitrators countable noun ⇒ He served as an arbitrator in a series of commercial disputes in India.
