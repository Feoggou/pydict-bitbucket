cataclysm

(ˈkætəˌklɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a violent upheaval, esp of a political, military, or social nature2. a disastrous flood; deluge3. geology another name for catastrophe (sense 4)
Derived forms
ˌcataclysmic (ˌcataˈclysmic) or ˌcataclysmal (ˌcataˈclysmal) adjectiveˌcataclysmically (ˌcataˈclysmically) adverb
Word origin
C17: via French from Latin, from Greek kataklusmos deluge, from katakluzein to flood, from kluzein to wash
