squander

(ˈskwɒndə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to spend wastefully or extravagantly; dissipate2. an obsolete word for scatternoun3. rare extravagance or dissipation

Derived Forms

squanderer (ˈsquanderer) noun

Word Origin

C16: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you squander money, resources, or opportunities, you waste them. [V n + on] ⇒ Hooker didn't squander his money on flashy cars or other vices. [V n] ⇒ He had squandered his chances to win.
