literary

(ˈlɪtərərɪ , ˈlɪtrərɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. of, relating to, concerned with, or characteristic of literature or scholarly writing ⇒ a literary discussion, ⇒ a literary style2. versed in or knowledgeable about literature ⇒ a literary man3. (of a word) formal; not colloquial
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

literarily (ˈliterarily) adverbliterariness (ˈliterariness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin litterārius concerning reading and writing. See letter

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪtərəri , US -reri ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Literary means concerned with or connected with the writing, study, or appreciation of literature. ⇒ Her literary criticism focuses on the way great literature suggests ideas. ⇒ She's the literary editor of the 'Sunday Review'. ⇒ ...a literary masterpiece.2. graded adjective & adjectiveLiterary words and expressions are often unusual in some way and are used to create a special effect in a piece of writing such as a poem, speech, or novel.
