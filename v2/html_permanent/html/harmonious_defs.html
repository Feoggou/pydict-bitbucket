harmonious

(hɑːˈməʊnɪəs )
Definitions
adjective1. (esp of colours or sounds) fitting together well2. having agreement or consensus3. tuneful, consonant, or melodious
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

harmoniously (harˈmoniously) adverbharmoniousness (harˈmoniousness) noun

harmonious

Powered by COBUILD
(hɑːʳmoʊniəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA harmonious relationship, agreement, or discussion is friendly and peaceful. ⇒ Their harmonious relationship resulted in part from their similar goals. ⇒ It is a harmonious community where pupils are happy and industrious.harmoniously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ To live together harmoniously as men and women is an achievement.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is harmonious has parts which go well together and which are in proportion to each other. ⇒ The architecture is harmonious and no building is over five or six floors high. ⇒ ...a harmonious balance of mind, body, and spirit.harmoniously graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV after v] ⇒ ...a pure, harmoniously proportioned face. ⇒ ...stone paths that blend harmoniously with the scenery.3. graded adjective & adjectiveMusical notes that are harmonious produce a pleasant sound when played together. ⇒ ...the mysterious skill involved in producing harmonious sounds.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
