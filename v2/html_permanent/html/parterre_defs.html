parterre

(pɑːˈtɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a formally patterned flower garden2. British and Irish the pit in a theatre3. US another name for parquet circle

Word Origin

C17: from French, from par along + terre ground
Example sentences containing 'parterre'
Around the central fountain a parterre was laid out in patterns outlined by box hedges and filled with herbs. Melville, Anne THE HARDIE INHERITANCEShe walked on a few steps, and appraised the cool precision of the orderly parterre with its mixed beds of lavender, rosemary and sage. Skelton, Alison Scott AN OLDER WOMANThey reached a spot where a mulberry tree spread its branches over the parterre ; he drew her into their shade. Stephanie Laurens ON A WILD NIGHT
