wayward

(ˈweɪwəd )
Definitions
adjective1. wanting to have one's own way regardless of the wishes or good of others2. capricious, erratic, or unpredictable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

waywardly (ˈwaywardly) adverbwaywardness (ˈwaywardness) noun

Word Origin

C14: changed from awayward turned or turning away

wayward for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weɪwəʳd )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a person or their behaviour as wayward, you mean that they behave in a selfish, bad, or unpredictable way, and are difficult to control. unruly, unmanageable ⇒ ...wayward children with a history of severe emotional problems.waywardness uncount noun [oft with poss] ⇒ ...the curiosity, caprice and waywardness of children.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

RUSHING TO PARADISEThey will not sink, lade them as you will; but they are wayward if mishandled. J.R.R.
