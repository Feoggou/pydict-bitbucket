Allies

(ˈælaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. (in World War I) the powers of the Triple Entente (France, Russia, and Britain) together with the nations allied with them2. (in World War II) the countries that fought against the Axis. The main Allied powers were Britain and the Commonwealth countries, the US, the Soviet Union, France, China, and Poland. See also Axis
