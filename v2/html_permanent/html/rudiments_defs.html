rudiments

(ˈruːdɪmənts)
Definitions
plural noun1. the first principles or elementary stages of a subject ⇒ Salek had begun to teach Paolo the rudiments of Arabic., ⇒ She learned the rudiments of brick-laying as she went along.2. partially developed versions of something ⇒ We had the rudiments of a platform.

rudiments

Powered by COBUILD
(ruːdɪmənts ) for learners of English
Definitions
plural nounWhen you learn the rudiments of something, you learn the simplest or most essential things about it. [+ of] ⇒ She helped to build a house, learning the rudiments of brick-laying as she went along.
