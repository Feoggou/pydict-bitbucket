partiality

(ˌpɑːʃɪˈælɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. favourable prejudice or bias2. (usually foll by for) liking or fondness3. the state or condition of being partial
Example sentences containing 'partiality'
Elspeth made allowances for the older woman's partiality. West, Charles STAGE FRIGHTNowhere did she allow for the fact that `her "country might need more than a few Band-Aids as a result of God's partiality. Kallen, Lucille C B GREENFIELD - A LITTLE MADNESSAs she resettled her glasses, she considered Cal's well-known partiality for very young women. Susan Elizabeth Phillips NOBODY'S BABY BUT MINE
