illumination

(ɪˌluːmɪˈneɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of illuminating or the state of being illuminated2. a source of light3. (often plural) mainly British a light or lights, esp coloured lights, used as decoration in streets, parks, etc4. spiritual or intellectual enlightenment; insight or understanding5. the act of making understood; clarification6. decoration in colours, gold, or silver used on some manuscripts or printed works7. physics another name (not in technical usage) for illuminance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

illuminational (ilˌlumiˈnational) adjective

(ɪluːmɪneɪʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: illuminations
Definitions
1. uncount nounIllumination is the lighting that a place has. [formal] ⇒ The only illumination came from a small window high in the opposite wall.2. plural nounIlluminations are coloured lights which are put up in towns, especially at Christmas, in order to make them look attractive, especially at night. [mainly British] ⇒ ...the famous Blackpool illuminations.3. uncount nounYou can use illumination to describe an increased understanding of something, especially something of a religious or spiritual nature. [formal] enlightenment ⇒ ...a sense of illumination. ⇒ No further illumination can be had from this theory.
