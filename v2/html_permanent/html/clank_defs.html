clank

(klæŋk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an abrupt harsh metallic soundverb2. to make or cause to make such a sound3. (intransitive) to move or operate making such a sound
Derived forms
clankingly (ˈclankingly) adverb
Word origin
C17: of imitative origin

Word forms: clanks , clanking , clanked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen large metal objects clank, they make a noise because they are hitting together or hitting against something hard. [V] ⇒ A pan rattled and clanked. [V prep] ⇒ 'Here we are now,' Beth said, as the train clanked into a tiny station. [V-ing] ⇒ ...the clanking noise of the ferry.Clank is also a noun. ⇒ ...the clank and rattle of human activity.
