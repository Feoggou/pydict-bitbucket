incapable

(ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. (when postpositive, often foll by of) not capable (of); lacking the ability (to)2. powerless or helpless, as through injury or intoxication3. (postpositive; foll by of) not susceptible (to); not admitting (of) ⇒ a problem incapable of solution
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incapability (inˌcapaˈbility) or incapableness (inˈcapableness) nounincapably (inˈcapably) adverb

incapable

(ɪnkeɪpəbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomeone who is incapable of doing something is unable to do it. [+ of] ⇒ She seemed incapable of taking decisions. ⇒ He was a man incapable of violence.2. adjectiveAn incapable person is weak or stupid. ⇒ He lost his job for allegedly being incapable.
