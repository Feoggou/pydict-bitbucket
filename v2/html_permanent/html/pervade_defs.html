pervade

(pɜːˈveɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to spread through or throughout, esp subtly or gradually; permeate

Derived Forms

pervader (perˈvader) nounpervasion (pɜːˈveɪʒən ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin pervādere, from per- through + vādere to go

Powered by COBUILD

Word forms: pervades , pervading , pervaded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something pervades a place or thing, it is a noticeable feature throughout it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The smell of sawdust and glue pervaded the factory. [V n] ⇒ ...the corruption that pervades every stratum of the country. [V-ing] ⇒ Throughout the book there is a pervading sense of menace.
Example sentences containing 'pervade'
An odour, a sickly, cloying odour that seemed to pervade the room. Lewis, Roy A TROUT IN THE MILKSince she moved in her dark, sly personality seemed to pervade the whole house, filling it with new shadows and subtle fears. Amanda Hemingway THE GREENSTONE GRAIL: THE SANGREAL TRILOGY ONEIt's odd how certain themes, once started, seem to pervade life for a bit. Malcolm, John THE GWEN JOHN SCULPTUREThe musky smell of the sea dragons, preceding the dragons themselves by a little while, must have begun to pervade the water.
