slit

(slɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: slits, slitting or slit (transitive)1. to make a straight long incision in; split open2. to cut into strips lengthwise3. to severnoun4. a long narrow cut5. a long narrow opening

Derived Forms

slitter (ˈslitter) noun

Word Origin

Old English slītan to slice; related to Old Norse slita, Old High German slīzen

slit

Powered by COBUILD
(slɪt )
Word forms: slits , slitting
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The form slit is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle.1. verbIf you slit something, you make a long narrow cut in it. [V n] ⇒ They say somebody slit her throat. [V n with open] ⇒ He began to slit open each envelope. [V-ed + to/from] ⇒ She was wearing a white dress slit to the thigh.2. countable nounA slit is a long narrow cut. [+ in] ⇒ Make a slit in the stem about half an inch long.3. countable nounA slit is a long narrow opening in something. [+ in] ⇒ She watched them through a slit in the curtains.
Example sentences containing 'slit'
The knife slit in the meat of my hip felt like someone had sewn bees in there. Jack Kerley THE HUNDREDTH MAN
