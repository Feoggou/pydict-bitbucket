betwixt

(bɪˈtwɪkst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
preposition, adverb1. archaic another word for between2. See betwixt and between
Word origin
Old English betwix; related to Old High German zwiski two each

betwixt

(biˈtwɪkst ; bɪˈtwɪkst )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
preposition, adverb betweenarchaic except in the phrase betwixt and between, in an intermediate position; neither altogether one nor altogether the other
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.
Word origin
ME bitwix < OE betwix < be, by + a form related to twegen, twain; -t is unhistoric
