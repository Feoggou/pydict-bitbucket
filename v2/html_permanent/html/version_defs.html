version

(ˈvɜːʃən , -ʒən)
Definitions
noun1. an account of a matter from a certain point of view, as contrasted with others ⇒ his version of the accident is different from the policeman's2. a translation, esp of the Bible, from one language into another3. a variant form of something; type4. an adaptation, as of a book or play into a film5. medicine manual turning of a fetus to correct an irregular position within the uterus6. pathology an abnormal displacement of the uterus characterized by a tilting forwards (anteversion), backwards (retroversion), or to either side (lateroversion)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

versional (ˈversional) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin versiō a turning, from Latin vertere to turn

Word forms: versions
Definitions
1. countable nounA version of something is a particular form of it in which some details are different from earlier or later forms. [+ of] ⇒ ...an updated version of his book. [+ of] ⇒ Ludo is a version of an ancient Indian racing game. ⇒ The second-hand version is a poor copy of the original.2. countable noun [oft poss N]Someone's version of an event is their own description of it, especially when it is different to other people's. [+ of] ⇒ Yesterday afternoon the White House put out a new version of events. ⇒ She and her friends wanted to go public with their version of the incident. ⇒ There have been widely differing versions in the newspapers about the prison siege.
