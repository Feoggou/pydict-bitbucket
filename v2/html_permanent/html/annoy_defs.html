annoy

(əˈnɔɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to irritate or displease2. to harass with repeated attacks
Derived forms
anˈnoyer noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French anoier, from Late Latin inodiāre to make hateful, from Latin in odiō (esse) (to be) hated, from odium hatred

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone or something annoys you, it makes you fairly angry and impatient. [V n] ⇒ Try making a note of the things which annoy you. [V n that] ⇒ It annoyed me that I didn't have time to do more reading. [V n to-inf] ⇒ It just annoyed me to hear him going on. [Also V]2. → see also annoyed, annoying
