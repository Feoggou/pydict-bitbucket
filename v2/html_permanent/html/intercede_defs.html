intercede

(ˌɪntəˈsiːd )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (often foll by in) to come between parties or act as mediator or advocate ⇒ to intercede in the strike2. Roman history (of a tribune or other magistrate) to interpose a veto
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

interceder (ˌinterˈceder) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin intercēdere to intervene, from inter- + cēdere to move

verbIf you intercede with someone, you try to persuade them to forgive someone or end their disagreement with them. [formal] [V + with] ⇒ They asked my father to intercede with the king on their behalf. [V for n] ⇒ He had occasionally tried to intercede for me. [V] ⇒ It has also asked Britain and the United States to intercede.
