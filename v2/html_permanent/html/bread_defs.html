bread

(brɛd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a food made from a dough of flour or meal mixed with water or milk, usually raised with yeast or baking powder and then baked2. necessary food; nourishment ⇒ give us our daily bread3. a slang word for money4. Christianity a small loaf, piece of bread, or wafer of unleavened bread used in the Eucharist5. See bread and circuses6. See break bread7. See cast one's bread upon the waters8. See know which side one's bread is buttered9. See take the bread out of someone's mouthverb10. (transitive) to cover with breadcrumbs before cooking ⇒ breaded veal
Word origin
Old English brēad; related to Old Norse braud, Old Frisian brād, Old High German brōt

bread

Powered by COBUILD
(bred )
Word forms: breads , breading , breaded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounBread is a very common food made from flour, water, and yeast. ⇒ ...a loaf of bread. ⇒ ...bread and butter. ⇒ There is more fibre in wholemeal bread than in white bread.2. → to know what side your bread is buttered on3. uncount noun [usu with poss]If you earn your bread doing a particular job or activity, you earn your money doing it. [informal] ⇒ There's not a living soul in Colorado who doesn't depend for his bread on silver.4. verb [usu passive]If food such as fish or meat is breaded, it is covered in tiny pieces of dry bread called breadcrumbs. It can then be fried or grilled. [be V-ed] ⇒ It is important that food be breaded just minutes before frying.breaded adjective ⇒ ...breaded fish.
