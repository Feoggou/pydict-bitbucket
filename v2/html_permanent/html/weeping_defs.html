noun(Chemical Engineering: Distillation) Weeping happens in distillation when a descending liquid reaches a particular limit and then misses the contact zone, which makes the process less effective. ⇒ Weeping is indicated by a sharp pressure drop in the column and reduced separation efficiency. ⇒ If pressure increases too quickly, vapor rates through the trays decrease, which can cause weeping and dumping. ⇒ Weeping happens in distillation when a descending liquid reaches a particular limit and then misses the contact zone, which makes the process less effective.
