mortgage

(ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an agreement under which a person borrows money to buy property, esp a house, and the lender may take possession of the property if the borrower fails to repay the money2. the deed effecting such an agreement3. the loan obtained under such an agreement ⇒ a mortgage of £148 0004. a regular payment of money borrowed under such an agreement ⇒ a mortgage of £447 per monthverb (transitive)5. to pledge (a house or other property) as security for the repayment of a loanadjective6. of or relating to a mortgage ⇒ a mortgage payment

Derived Forms

mortgageable (ˈmortgageable) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, literally: dead pledge, from mort dead + gage security, gage1
