thrilled

(θrɪld)
Definitions
adjective1. extremely pleased ⇒ I was thrilled., ⇒ Sue and John were especially thrilled with this award., ⇒ I'm really thrilled that the public have taken to the song., ⇒ I was thrilled to be sitting next to such a distinguished author., ⇒ I was so thrilled to get a good report from him., ⇒ He's absolutely thrilled to bits.2. See thrilled to bits

thrilled

Powered by COBUILD
(θrɪld ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. →  thrilled to bits2. phraseIf you say that someone is thrilled to bits, you are emphasizing the fact that they are extremely pleased about something. You can also say thrilled to pieces, especially in American English. [emphasis] ⇒ I'm thrilled to bits to have won the cash. ⇒ He's thrilled to bits at the news. ⇒ He just thoroughly enjoyed reading it with me and was thrilled to bits that it was his very own story. [Also + at/with]3. → See also thrill
