curious

(ˈkjʊərɪəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. eager to learn; inquisitive2. overinquisitive; prying3. interesting because of oddness or novelty; strange; unexpected4. rare (of workmanship, etc) highly detailed, intricate, or subtle5. obsolete fastidious or hard to please
Derived forms
curiously (ˈcuriously) adverbcuriousness (ˈcuriousness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin cūriōsus taking pains over something, from cūra care

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are curious about something, you are interested in it and want to know more about it. [+ about] ⇒ Steve was intensely curious about the world I came from. ⇒ Children are naturally curious. ⇒ ...a group of curious villagers.curiously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ The woman in the shop had looked at them curiously. ⇒ 'Is that how you got that scar on your face?' Bess asked curiously.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as curious, you mean that it is unusual or difficult to understand. ⇒ There is a curious thing about her writings in this period. ⇒ The pageant promises to be a curious mixture of the ancient and modern. ⇒ The naval high command's response to these developments is rather curious.curiously graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ Harry was curiously silent through all this. ⇒ Curiously, the struggle to survive has greatly improved her health.
