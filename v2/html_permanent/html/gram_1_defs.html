gram1

(ɡræm )
Definitions
nouna metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram. It is equivalent to 15.432 grains or 0.002 205 pounds. Symbol: g
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from French gramme, from Late Latin gramma, from Greek: small weight, from graphein to write

gram

Definitions
noun(Electrical engineering: General) A gram is a unit of weight that is a one thousandth part of a kilogram. ⇒ A weight of 5 grams is used as the controlling weight in a gravity-controlled instrument. ⇒ The metric system unit of heat is called a calorie, which is the energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water 1 degree Celsius. ⇒ A gram is a unit of weight that is a one thousandth part of a kilogram.
