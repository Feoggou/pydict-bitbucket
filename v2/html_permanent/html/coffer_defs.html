coffer

(ˈkɒfə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a chest, esp for storing valuables2. (usually plural) a store of money3. Also called: caisson, lacuna an ornamental sunken panel in a ceiling, dome, etc4. a watertight box or chamber5. a. short for cofferdamb. a recessed panel in a concrete, metal, or timber soffitverb (transitive)6. to store, as in a coffer7. to decorate (a ceiling, dome, etc) with coffers
Word origin
C13: from Old French coffre, from Latin cophinus basket, from Greek kophinos

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA coffer is a large strong chest used for storing valuable objects such as money or gold and silver. [old-fashioned]2. plural noun [n N]The coffers of an organization consist of the money that it has to spend, imagined as being collected together in one place. [+ of] ⇒ The proceeds from the lottery go towards sports and recreation, as well as swelling the coffers of the government. ⇒ Large public and private companies have long been contributors to Tory party coffers.
