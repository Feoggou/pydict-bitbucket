Synonyms of rear 1

rear1

noun

  1. back part, back, back end, back part, rear part, stern (nautical)
    • He settled back in the rear of the taxi.
    OPPOSITES:  front, nose, bow, stem, vanguard, forward end
  2. back, end, tail, rearguard, tail end, back end
    • Musicians played at the front and rear of the procession.
  3. (informal) behind, backside (informal), rump, rear end, seat, tail (informal), butt (US & & Canadian, informal), bum (British, slang), ass (US & & Canadian, taboo & slang), buns (US, slang), arse (taboo & slang), buttocks, posterior, derrière (euphemistic), tush (US, slang), fundament, jacksy (British, slang)
    • She blames her rounded rear on her love of butter.

modifier

  1. back, aft, hind, hindmost, after (nautical), last, following, trailing
    • the rear end of a tractor
    OPPOSITES:  leading, front, forward, foremost

rear2

verb

  1. bring up, raise, educate, care for, train, nurse, foster, nurture
    • I was reared in east Texas.
  2. breed, keep, raise, tend
    • She spends a lot of time rearing animals.
  3. raise up, lift, hold up, elevate, hoist, set upright
    • The horse reared and threw off its rider.
  4. (often with up or over) rise, tower, soar, loom
    • The exhibition hall reared above me behind a high fence.
  5. build, construct, put up, erect, fabricate
