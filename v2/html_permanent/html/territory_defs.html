territory

(ˈtɛrɪtərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
noun plural -ries1. any tract of land; district2. the geographical domain under the jurisdiction of a political unit, esp of a sovereign state3. the district for which an agent, etc, is responsible ⇒ a salesman's territory4. an area inhabited and defended by an individual animal or a breeding group of animals5. an area of knowledge ⇒ science isn't my territory6. (in football, hockey, etc) the area defended by a team7. (often capital) a region of a country, esp of a federal state, that enjoys less autonomy and a lower status than most constituent parts of the state8. (often capital) a protectorate or other dependency of a country
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin territōrium land surrounding a town, from terra land

territory

Powered by COBUILD
(terətri , US -tɔːri ) for learners of English
Word forms: territories
Definitions
1. variable nounTerritory is land which is controlled by a particular country or ruler. ⇒ The government denies that any of its territory is under rebel control. ⇒ ...Russian territory.2. countable nounA territory is a country or region that is controlled by another country. ⇒ They just want to return to their families in the occupied territories. ⇒ He toured some of the disputed territories now under U.N. control.3. uncount nounYou can use territory to refer to an area of knowledge or experience. ⇒ Following the futuristic The Handmaid's Tale, Margaret Atwood's seventh novel, Cat's Eye, returns to more familiar territory. ⇒ Reading from a tedious technical brief for hours on end, he would stray into difficult territory.4. → virgin territory5. variable nounAn animal's territory is an area which it regards as its own and which it defends when other animals try to enter it. ⇒ The territory of a cat only remains fixed for as long as the cat dominates the area.6. uncount noun [usu adj N]Territory is land with a particular character. ⇒ ...mountainous territory. ⇒ ...a vast and uninhabited territory.7. →  come with the territory
