pendant

(ˈpɛndənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. an ornament that hangs from a piece of jewelleryb. a necklace with such an ornament2. a hanging light, esp a chandelier3. a carved ornament that is suspended from a ceiling or roof4. something that matches or complements something else5. Also called: pennant nautical a length of wire or rope secured at one end to a mast or spar and having a block or other fitting at the lower endadjective6. a variant spelling of pendent

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, from pendre to hang, from Latin pendēre to hang down; related to Latin pendere to hang, pondus weight, Greek span to pull
