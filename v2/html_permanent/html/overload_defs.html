overload

Definitions
verb (ˌəʊvəˈləʊd )1. (transitive) to put too large a load on or innoun (ˈəʊvəˌləʊd )2. an excessive load

overload for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Word forms: overloads, overloading , overloaded
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (oʊvəʳloʊd ). The noun is pronounced (oʊvəʳloʊd ).1. verbIf you overload something such as a vehicle, you put more things or people into it than it was designed to carry. [V n] ⇒ Don't overload the boat or it will sink. [V n] ⇒ Large meals overload the digestive system.overloaded graded adjective & adjective ⇒ Some trains were so overloaded that their suspension collapsed.2. verbTo overload someone with work, problems, or information means to give them more work, problems, or information than they can cope with. [V n + with] ⇒ ...an effective method that will not overload staff with yet more paperwork.Overload is also a noun. ⇒ 57 per cent complained of work overload. ⇒ The greatest danger is that we simply create information overload for our executives.overloaded graded adjective & adjective ⇒ The bar waiter was already overloaded with orders.3. verbIf you overload an electrical system, you cause too much electricity to flow through it, and so damage it. [V n] ⇒ Never overload an electrical socket.

overload in Electrical Engineering

(oʊvərloʊd)
Definitions
noun(Electrical engineering: Electrical power, Power consumption) Overload is a situation in which extra power is taken from an electrical supply, and the increased current causes the cables to heat up, which may end in an electrical fault. ⇒ In the case of overload, the corresponding switch on the breaker panel trips, cutting off only that part of the home or business. ⇒ This series provides protection of AC or DC loads against damage from overload currents. ⇒ Overload is a situation in which extra power is taken from an electrical supply, and the increased current causes the cables to heat up, which may end in an electrical fault.
