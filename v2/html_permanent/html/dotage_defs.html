dotage

(ˈdəʊtɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. feebleness of mind, esp as a result of old age2. foolish infatuation
Word origin
C14: from dote + -age

dotage

(doʊtɪdʒ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [usu poss N]If someone is in their dotage, they are very old and becoming weak. ⇒ Even in his dotage, the Professor still sits on the committee. ⇒ ...spending his dotage in a riverside cottage.
Example sentences containing 'dotage'
