almoner

(ˈɑːmənə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. British obsolete a trained hospital social worker responsible for the welfare of patients2. (formerly) a person who distributes alms or charity on behalf of a household or institution
Word origin
C13: from Old French almosnier, from almosne alms, from Vulgar Latin alemosina (unattested), from Late Latin eleēmosyna; see alms
