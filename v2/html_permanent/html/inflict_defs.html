inflict

(ɪnˈflɪkt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. (often foll by on or upon) to impose (something unwelcome, such as pain, oneself, etc)2. rare to cause to suffer; afflict (with)3. to deal out (blows, lashes, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inflictable (inˈflictable) adjectiveinflicter (inˈflicter) or inflictor (inˈflictor) nouninfliction (inˈfliction) nouninflictive (inˈflictive) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin inflīgere to strike (something) against, dash against, from flīgere to strike
