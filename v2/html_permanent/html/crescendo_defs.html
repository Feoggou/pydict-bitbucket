crescendo

(krɪˈʃɛndəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -dos or -di (-dɪ)1. musica. a gradual increase in loudness or the musical direction or symbol indicating this. Abbreviation: cresc. Symbol: (written over the music affected) ≺b. (as modifier) ⇒ a crescendo passage2. a gradual increase in loudness or intensity ⇒ the rising crescendo of a song3. a peak of noise or intensity ⇒ the cheers reached a crescendoverb -does, -doing or -doed4. (intransitive) to increase in loudness or forceadverb5. with a crescendo
Word origin
C18: from Italian, literally: increasing, from crescere to grow, from Latin

crescendo

Powered by COBUILD
(krɪʃendoʊ )
Word forms: crescendos
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]A crescendo is a noise that gets louder and louder. Some people also use crescendo to refer to the point when a noise is at its loudest. ⇒ She spoke in a crescendo: 'You are a bad girl! You are a wicked girl! You are evil!'. ⇒ The crescendo of noise was continuous. ⇒ The applause rose to a crescendo and cameras clicked.2. countable noun [usu sing]People sometimes describe an increase in the intensity of something, or its most intense point, as a crescendo. [journalism] [+ of] ⇒ There was a crescendo of parliamentary and press criticism. ⇒ And now the story reaches a crescendo.3. countable noun [usu sing]In music, a crescendo is a section of a piece of music in which the music gradually gets louder and louder.
