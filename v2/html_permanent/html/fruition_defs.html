fruition

(fruːˈɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the attainment or realization of something worked for or desired; fulfilment2. enjoyment of this3. the act or condition of bearing fruit
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin fruitiō enjoyment, from Latin fruī to enjoy

fruition

Powered by COBUILD
(fruɪʃən ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount noun [usu to N]If something comes to fruition, it starts to succeed and produce the results that were intended or hoped for. [formal] ⇒ These plans take time to come to fruition. ⇒ His hopes for a new political party have little chance of reaching fruition.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'fruition'
The psychological profiles they had studied, the charts they had projected, the whole scenario, all coming to fruition. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENThe first of my reasons for coming didn't look as if it would find fruition : we hadn't exchanged a word since leaving the department. Pritchard, John NIGHT SISTERSThere was still much that had to be arranged before any of the plans he had set in motion could continue on to fruition. Raymond E. Feist SHARDS OF A BROKEN CROWN
