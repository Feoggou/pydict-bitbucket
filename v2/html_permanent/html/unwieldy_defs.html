unwieldy

(ʌnˈwiːldɪ ) or unwieldly
Definitions
adjective1. too heavy, large, or awkwardly shaped to be easily handled2. ungainly; clumsy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unwieldily (unˈwieldily) or unwieldlily (unˈwieldlily) adverbunwieldiness (unˈwieldiness) or unwieldliness (unˈwieldliness) noun

unwieldy for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnwiːldi )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an object as unwieldy, you mean that it is difficult to move or carry because it is so big or heavy. ⇒ They came panting up to his door with their unwieldy baggage.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a system as unwieldy, you mean that it does not work very well as a result of it being too large or badly organized. ⇒ His firm must contend with the unwieldy Russian bureaucracy. ⇒ ...an unwieldy legal system.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
