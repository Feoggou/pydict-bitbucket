round off

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. (often foll by with) to bring to a satisfactory conclusion; complete, esp agreeably ⇒ we rounded off the evening with a brandy2. to make round or less jagged

round off

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
phrasal verbIf you round off an activity with something, you end the activity by doing something that provides a clear or satisfactory conclusion to it. [V P n] ⇒ The Italian way is to round off a meal with an ice-cream. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ ...a dazzling firework display which rounded off a lovely day. [V n P] ⇒ This rounded the afternoon off perfectly. [V P + by] ⇒ He rounds off by proposing a toast to the attendants. →  round
