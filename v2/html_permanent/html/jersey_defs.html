Jersey

(ˈdʒɜːzɪ )
Definitions
noun1. an island in the English Channel, the largest of the Channel Islands: forms, with two other islands, the bailiwick of Jersey; colonized from Normandy in the 11th century and still officially French-speaking; noted for finance, market gardening, dairy farming, and tourism. Capital: St Helier. Pop: 95 732 (2013 est). Area: 116 sq km (45 sq miles)2. a breed of dairy cattle producing milk with a high butterfat content, originating from the island of Jersey

Definitions
noun1. a knitted garment covering the upper part of the body2. a. a machine-knitted slightly elastic cloth of wool, silk, nylon, etc, used for clothingb. (as modifier) ⇒ a jersey suit3. a football shirt

Word Origin

C16: from Jersey, from the woollen sweaters traditionally worn by the fishermen
