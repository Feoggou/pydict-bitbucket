assault

(əˈsɔːlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a violent attack, either physical or verbal2. law an intentional or reckless act that causes another person to expect to be subjected to immediate and unlawful violence. Compare battery (sense 4), assault and battery3. a. the culmination of a military attack, in which fighting takes place at close quartersb. (as modifier) ⇒ assault troops4. rape or attempted rapeverb (transitive)5. to make an assault upon6. to rape or attempt to rape
Derived forms
asˈsaulter nounasˈsaultive adjective
Word origin
C13: from Old French asaut, from Vulgar Latin assaltus (unattested), from assalīre (unattested) to leap upon; see assail

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn assault by an army is a strong attack made on an area held by the enemy. [+ on/upon/against] ⇒ The rebels are poised for a new assault on the government garrisons. ⇒ Most U.S. soldiers welcomed the ground assault when the order was finally given.2. adjective [ADJ n]Assault weapons such as rifles are intended for soldiers to use in battle rather than for purposes such as hunting. ⇒ ...a high-powered assault rifle.3. variable nounAn assault on a person is a physical attack on them. [+ on/upon] ⇒ The attack is one of a series of savage sexual assaults on women in the university area. ⇒ At the police station, I was charged with assault.4. verbTo assault someone means to physically attack them. [V n] ⇒ The gang assaulted him with iron bars. [be V-ed] ⇒ She may have been sexually assaulted by her killer.5. countable nounAn assault on someone's beliefs is a strong criticism of them. [+ on/upon/against] ⇒ He leveled a verbal assault against his Democratic opponents.
