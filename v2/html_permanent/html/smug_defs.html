smug

(smʌɡ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: smugger or smuggest1. excessively self-satisfied or complacent2. archaic trim or neat

Derived Forms

smugly (ˈsmugly) adverbsmugness (ˈsmugness) noun

Word Origin

C16: of Germanic origin; compare Low German smuck neat

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is smug, you are criticizing the fact they seem very pleased with how good, clever, or lucky they are. [disapproval] ⇒ Thomas and his wife looked at each other in smug satisfaction.smugly graded adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ The Major smiled smugly and sat down.smugness uncount noun ⇒ He had the smugness that many lawyers show. ⇒ ...a trace of smugness in his voice.
