despond

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (dɪˈspɒnd )1. (intransitive) to lose heart or hope; become disheartened; despairnoun (ˈdɛspɒnd , dɪˈspɒnd )2. archaic lack of hope; despondency
Derived forms
despondingly (deˈspondingly) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin dēspondēre to promise, make over to, yield, lose heart, from de- + spondēre to promise
Example sentences containing 'despond'
How did this afternoon become so wonderful, she asked herself, when a couple of hours ago I was in the slough of despond ? Richard Francis PROSPECT HILL
