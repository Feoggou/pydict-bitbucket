imitate

(ˈɪmɪˌteɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to try to follow the manner, style, character, etc, of or take as a model ⇒ many writers imitated the language of Shakespeare2. to pretend to be or to impersonate, esp for humour; mimic3. to make a copy or reproduction of; duplicate; counterfeit4. to make or be like; resemble or simulate ⇒ her achievements in politics imitated her earlier successes in business
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

imitable (ˈimitable) adjectiveimitability (ˌimitaˈbility) or imitableness (ˈimitableness) nounimitator (ˈimiˌtator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin imitārī; see image
