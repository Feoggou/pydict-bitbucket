mild

(maɪld )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a taste, sensation, etc) not powerful or strong; bland ⇒ a mild curry2. gentle or temperate in character, climate, behaviour, etc3. not extreme; moderate ⇒ a mild rebuke4. feeble; unassertivenoun5. British draught beer, of darker colour than bitter and flavoured with fewer hops

Derived Forms

mildly (ˈmildly) adverbmildness (ˈmildness) noun

Word Origin

Old English milde; compare Old Saxon mildi, Old Norse mildr

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Mild is used to describe something such as a feeling, attitude, or illness that is not very strong or severe. ⇒ Teddy turned to Mona with a look of mild confusion. ⇒ Anna put up a mild protest. ⇒ If you have only mild symptoms, try an over-the-counter treatment.mildly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/adv, oft ADV after v] ⇒ I'm only mildly surprised. ⇒ Josephine must have had the disease very mildly as she showed no symptoms.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A mild person is gentle and does not get angry easily. ⇒ He is a mild man, who is reasonable almost to the point of blandness.mildly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ 'I'm not meddling,' Kenworthy said mildly, 'I'm just curious.'3. graded adjective & adjectiveMild weather is pleasant because it is neither extremely hot nor extremely cold. ⇒ The area is famous for its very mild winter climate. → hot4. graded adjective & adjectiveYou describe food as mild when it does not taste or smell strong, sharp, or bitter, especially when you like it because of this. ⇒ This cheese has a soft, mild flavour. ⇒ ...a mild curry powder.5. graded adjectiveMild soap or washing-up liquid feels pleasant on your skin and does not contain any substances that might make your skin sore. OPPOSITE: harsh ⇒ Wash your face thoroughly with a mild soap and warm water.6. uncount nounMild is a clear, dark-coloured beer. [British]7. → see also mildly
