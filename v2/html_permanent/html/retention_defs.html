retention

(rɪˈtɛnʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of retaining or state of being retained2. the capacity to hold or retain liquid3. the capacity to remember4. pathology the abnormal holding within the body of urine, faeces, etc, that are normally excreted5. business a sum of money owed to a contractor but not paid for an agreed period as a safeguard against any faults found in the work carried out6. (plural) accounting profits earned by a company but not distributed as dividends; retained earnings
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin retentiō, from retinēre to retain
