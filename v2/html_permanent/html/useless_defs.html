useless

(ˈjuːslɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. having no practical use or advantage2. informal ineffectual, weak, or stupid ⇒ he's useless at history
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

uselessly (ˈuselessly) adverbuselessness (ˈuselessness) noun

useless for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(juːsləs )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If something is useless, you cannot use it. ⇒ He realised that their money was useless in this country. ⇒ Computers would be useless without software writers.uselessly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ His right arm hung rather uselessly.uselessness uncount noun ⇒ The car had rusted almost to the point of uselessness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is useless, it does not achieve anything helpful or good. ⇒ She knew it was useless to protest. ⇒ ...a useless punishment which fails to stop drug trafficking.uselessly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Uselessly, he checked the same pockets he'd checked before.uselessness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the uselessness of their research.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is useless, you mean that they are no good at all. [informal] ⇒ Their education system is useless. [+ at] ⇒ He was useless at any game with a ball.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone feels useless, they feel bad because they are unable to help someone or achieve anything. ⇒ She sits at home all day, watching TV and feeling useless.uselessness uncount noun ⇒ ...the sense of uselessness and the boredom of empty days.
