Definitions
noun(Mechanical engineering: Manufacturing and assembly) A hydraulic press is a press that uses liquid pressure to make a small force applied to a small piston produce a large force on a larger piston. ⇒ A hydraulic press produces a great deal of force from the application of a small amount of force to the small piston. ⇒ A hydraulic press applies pressure of about 2500 psi between the second pressure-bonding plate and the pressure-bonding die. ⇒ A hydraulic press is a press that uses liquid pressure to make a small force applied to a small piston produce a large force on a larger piston. ▶  hydraulic, press
