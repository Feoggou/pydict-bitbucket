grievous

(ˈɡriːvəs )
Definitions
adjective1. very severe or painful ⇒ a grievous injury2. very serious; heinous ⇒ a grievous sin3. showing or marked by grief ⇒ a grievous cry4. causing great pain or suffering ⇒ a grievous attack

grievous

Powered by COBUILD
(griːvəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something such as a loss as grievous, you mean that it is extremely serious or worrying in its effects. ⇒ Their loss would be a grievous blow to our engineering industries. ⇒ Mr Morris said the victims had suffered from a very grievous mistake.grievously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Birds, sea-life and the coastline all suffered grievously.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A grievous injury to your body is one that causes you great pain and suffering. ⇒ He survived in spite of suffering grievous injuries.grievously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Nelson Piquet, three times world champion, was grievously injured.
