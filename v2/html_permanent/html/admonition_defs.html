admonition

Powered by COBUILD
(ædmənɪʃən )
Word forms: admonitions
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounAn admonition is a warning or criticism about someone's behaviour. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ She ignored the admonitions of her mother.
Example sentences containing 'admonition'
Brenner left him, with an admonition to hurry, and himself went back to the Audi. Harcourt, Palma A MATTER OF CONSCIENCEHe had therefore not needed the vicar's admonition to contact the Thames Valley Police HQ at Kidlington. Penn, John WIDOW'S ENDLooming over them, the fat man's whole bowing, perspiring presence was an admonition to them not to worry. Ferrars, Elizabeth UNREASONABLE DOUBTWith that probably useless admonition , he resumed his perambulation about the lawns. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
