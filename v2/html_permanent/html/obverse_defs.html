obverse

(ˈɒbvɜːs )
Definitions
adjective1. facing or turned towards the observer2. forming or serving as a counterpart3. (of certain plant leaves) narrower at the base than at the topnoun4. a counterpart or complement5. the side of a coin that bears the main design or device. Compare reverse (sense 15)6. logic a categorial proposition derived from another by replacing the original predicate by its negation and changing the proposition from affirmative to negative or vice versa, as no sum is correct from every sum is incorrect
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obversely (obˈversely) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin obversus turned towards, past participle of obvertere, from ob- to + vertere to turn
