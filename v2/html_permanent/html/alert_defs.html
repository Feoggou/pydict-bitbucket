alert

(əˈlɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (usually postpositive)1. vigilantly attentive ⇒ alert to the problems2. brisk, nimble, or livelynoun3. an alarm or warning, esp a siren warning of an air raid4. the period during which such a warning remains in effect5. See on the alertverb (transitive)6. to warn or signal (troops, police, etc) to prepare for action7. to warn of danger, an attack, etc
Derived forms
aˈlertly adverbaˈlertness noun
Word origin
C17: from Italian all'erta on the watch, from erta lookout post, from ergere to build up, from Latin ērigere; see erect

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are alert, you are paying full attention to things around you and are able to deal with anything that might happen. ⇒ We all have to stay alert. ⇒ He had been spotted by an alert neighbour.alertness uncount noun ⇒ The drug improved mental alertness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are alert to something, you are fully aware of it. [+ to] ⇒ The bank is alert to the danger.3. countable nounAn alert is a situation in which people prepare themselves for something dangerous that might happen soon. ⇒ Due to a security alert, this train will not be stopping at Oxford Circus.4. verbIf you alert someone to a situation, especially a dangerous or unpleasant situation, you tell them about it. [V n + to] ⇒ He wanted to alert people to the activities of the group. [V n] ⇒ I was hoping he'd alert the police.5. → see also red alert6. →  on alert7. →  on the alert
