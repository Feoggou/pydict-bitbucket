bomb

(bɒm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a hollow projectile containing an explosive, incendiary, or other destructive substance, esp one carried by aircraftb. (as modifier) ⇒ bomb disposal, ⇒ a bomb bayc. (in combination) ⇒ a bombload, ⇒ bombproof2. any container filled with explosive ⇒ a car bomb, ⇒ a letter bomb3. See the bomb4. a round or pear-shaped mass of volcanic rock, solidified from molten lava that has been thrown into the air5. medicine a container for radioactive material, applied therapeutically to any part of the body ⇒ a cobalt bomb6. British slang a large sum of money (esp in the phrase make a bomb)7. US and Canadian slang a disastrous failure ⇒ the new play was a total bomb8. Australian and New Zealand slang an old or dilapidated motorcar9. American football a very long high pass10. (in rugby union) another term for up-and-under11. See like a bombverb12. to attack with or as if with a bomb or bombs; drop bombs (on)13. (intr; often foll by off, along, etc) informal to move or drive very quickly14. (intransitive) slang to fail disastrously; be a flop ⇒ the new play bombed. See also bomb out
Word origin
C17: from French bombe, from Italian bomba, probably from Latin bombus a booming sound, from Greek bombos, of imitative origin; compare Old Norse bumba drum
