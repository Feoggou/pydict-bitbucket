wavelength

(ˈweɪvˌlɛŋθ )
Definitions
noun1. the distance, measured in the direction of propagation, between two points of the same phase in consecutive cycles of a wave. Symbol: λ2. the wavelength of the carrier wave used by a particular broadcasting station3. See on someone's wavelength

wavelength for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weɪvleŋθ )
Word forms: wavelengths
Definitions
1. countable nounA wavelength is the distance between a part of a wave of energy such as light or sound and the next similar part. [+ of] ⇒ Sunlight consists of different wavelengths of radiation. ⇒ Blue light has a shorter wavelength than red.2. countable nounA wavelength is the size of radio wave which a particular radio station uses to broadcast its programmes. [+ of] ⇒ She found the wavelength of their broadcasts, and left the radio tuned to their station.3. →  on the same wavelength
