immodest

(ɪˈmɒdɪst )
Definitions
adjective1. indecent, esp with regard to sexual propriety; improper2. bold, impudent, or shameless
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

immodestly (imˈmodestly) adverbimmodesty (imˈmodesty) noun

immodest

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmɒdɪst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone's behaviour as immodest, you mean that it shocks or embarrasses you because you think that it is rude.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you say that someone is immodest, you disapprove of the way in which they often say how good, important, or clever they are. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ He could become ungraciously immodest about his own capacities.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
