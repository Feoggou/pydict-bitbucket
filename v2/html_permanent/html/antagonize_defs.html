antagonize

or antagonise (ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to make hostile; annoy or irritate2. to act in opposition to or counteract
Derived forms
anˈtagoˌnizable or anˈtagoˌnisable adjectiveanˌtagoniˈzation or anˌtagoniˈsation noun
Word origin
C17: from Greek antagōnizesthai, from anti- + agōnizesthai to strive, from agōn contest
