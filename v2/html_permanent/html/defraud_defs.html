defraud

(dɪˈfrɔːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to take away or withhold money, rights, property, etc, from (a person) by fraud; cheat; swindle
Derived forms
defraudation (ˌdiːfrɔːˈdeɪʃən ) or defraudment (deˈfraudment) noundefrauder (deˈfrauder) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone defrauds you, they take something away from you or stop you from getting what belongs to you by means of tricks and lies. [V n] ⇒ He pleaded guilty to charges of conspiracy to defraud the government. [V n + of/out of] ⇒ ...allegations that he defrauded taxpayers of thousands of dollars.
