prolix

(ˈprəʊlɪks , prəʊˈlɪks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a speech, book, etc) so long as to be boring; verbose2. indulging in prolix speech or writing; long-winded

Derived Forms

prolixity (proˈlixity) or rare prolixness (proˈlixness) nounprolixly (proˈlixly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin prōlixus stretched out widely, from pro-1 + līquī to flow
Example sentences containing 'prolix'
It's called couple therapy," he explained helpfully, prolix with drink. Gee, Maggie LOST CHILDREN

Similar Words
