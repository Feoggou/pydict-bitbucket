intermission

(ˌɪntəˈmɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. an interval, as between parts of a film2. a period between events or activities; pause3. the act of intermitting or the state of being intermitted
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intermissive (ˌinterˈmissive) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin intermissiō, from intermittere to leave off, intermit

Definitions
countable nounAn intermission is a short break between two parts of a film, concert, or show. [+ of] ⇒ ...during the intermission of the musical 'Steppin' Out'.In American English, you can also use intermission to refer to a short break between two parts of a game, or say that something happens at, after, or during intermission. ⇒ Fraser did not perform until after intermission.
