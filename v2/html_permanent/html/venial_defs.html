venial

(ˈviːnɪəl )
Definitions
adjectiveeasily excused or forgiven ⇒ a venial error
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

veniality (ˌviːnɪˈælɪtɪ) or venialness (ˈviːnɪəlnəs) nounvenially (ˈvenially) adverb

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Late Latin veniālis, from Latin venia forgiveness; related to Latin venus love
Example sentences containing 'venial'
Father Gerard explained there were other sins than the venial kind. Salley Vickers INSTANCES OF THE NUMBER 3

Similar Words

forgivable, minor, slight, trivial, insignificant, allowable, excusable, pardonable
