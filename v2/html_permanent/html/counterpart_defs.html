counterpart

(ˈkaʊntəˌpɑːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person or thing identical to or closely resembling another2. one of two parts that complement or correspond to each other3. a person acting opposite another in a play4. a duplicate, esp of a legal document; copy

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu poss N]Someone's or something's counterpart is another person or thing that has a similar function or position in a different place. ⇒ The Foreign Secretary telephoned his Italian counterpart to protest. ⇒ The Finnish organization was very different from that of its counterparts in the rest of the Nordic region.
