scamper

(ˈskæmpə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to run about playfully2. (often foll by through) to hurry quickly through (a place, task, book, etc)noun3. the act of scampering

Derived Forms

scamperer (ˈscamperer) noun

Word Origin

C17: probably from scamp (vb); see scamp1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen people or small animals scamper somewhere, they move there quickly with small, light steps. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Children scampered off the yellow school bus and into the playground. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The flash sent the foxes scampering away.
Example sentences containing 'scamper'
She knew no coquetry until she saw grey cat's: did not pose, flirt, roll, scamper , or show off. Doris Lessing ON CATSThe smaller man crumpled but managed to scamper across the slick canvas to avoid a clumsy kick to the head. Mark Burnell CHAMELEONAs soon as it saw her, it tried to retreat, but its scamper was too fast.
