entrenchment

or intrenchment (ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt )
Definitions
noun1. the act of entrenching or state of being entrenched2. a position protected by trenches3. one of a series of deep trenches constructed as a shelter from gunfire

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntrentʃmənt ) for learners of English
Word forms: entrenchments
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]Entrenchments are a series of long deep holes called trenches which are dug for defence by soldiers in war.2. uncount nounEntrenchment means the firm establishment of a system or your own position in a situation. [+ of] ⇒ ...the entrenchment of democratic norms.
