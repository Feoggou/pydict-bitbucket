rural

(ˈrʊərəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of, relating to, or characteristic of the country or country life2. living in or accustomed to the country3. of, relating to, or associated with farming▷ Compare urban
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ruralism (ˈruralism) nounruralist (ˈruralist) nounrurality (ruˈrality) nounrurally (ˈrurally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: via Old French from Latin rūrālis, from rūs the country

rural

Powered by COBUILD
(rʊərəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Rural places are far away from large towns or cities. ⇒ These plants have a tendency to grow in the more rural areas. ⇒ ...the closure of rural schools.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Rural means having features which are typical of areas that are far away from large towns or cities. ⇒ ...the old rural way of life. ⇒ He spoke with a heavy rural accent.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
