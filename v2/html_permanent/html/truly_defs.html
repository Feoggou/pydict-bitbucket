truly

Powered by COBUILD
(truːli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV before v]You use truly to emphasize that something has all the features or qualities of a particular thing, or is the case to the fullest possible extent. [emphasis] ⇒ ...a truly democratic system. ⇒ Not all doctors truly understand the reproductive cycle. ⇒ Spain was truly a European nation.2. adverb [ADV adj]You can use truly in order to emphasize your description of something. [emphasis] ⇒ ...a truly splendid man. ⇒ They were truly appalling.3. adverb [ADV adj, ADV before v]You use truly to emphasize that feelings are genuine and sincere. [emphasis] ⇒ Believe me, Susan, I am truly sorry. ⇒ He truly loved his children.4. adverb [ADV with cl]You can use truly in order to emphasize that what you are saying is true. [emphasis] honestly ⇒ I truly never minded caring for Rusty. ⇒ I do not expect a war between my country and yours. Truly I do not.5. → well and truly6. →  Yours truly7. →  yours truly
