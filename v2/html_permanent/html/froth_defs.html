froth

(frɒθ )
Definitions
noun1. a mass of small bubbles of air or a gas in a liquid, produced by fermentation, detergent, etc2. a mixture of saliva and air bubbles formed at the lips in certain diseases, such as rabies3. trivial ideas, talk, or entertainmentverb4. to produce or cause to produce froth5. (transitive) to give out in the form of froth6. (transitive) to cover with froth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frothy (ˈfrothy) adjectivefrothily (ˈfrothily) adverbfrothiness (ˈfrothiness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse frotha or frauth; related to Old English āfrēothan to foam, Sanskrit prothati he snorts

froth

Powered by COBUILD
(frɒθ , US frɔːθ ) for learners of English
Word forms: froths , frothing , frothed
Definitions
1. uncount nounFroth is a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid. ⇒ ...the froth of bubbles on the top of a glass of beer. ⇒ The froth is blown away.2. verbIf a liquid froths, small bubbles appear on its surface. [V prep] ⇒ The sea froths over my feet. [V] ⇒ Add a little of the warmed milk and allow to froth a little.3. verbIf you say that someone is frothing, or that they are frothing at the mouth, you are emphasizing that they are very angry or excited about something. [written, emphasis] foam [V with quote] ⇒ 'No! No! Never!' he froths. [V prep] ⇒ This story has many ingredients which make any news editor froth at the mouth with excitement. [Also V]4. uncount nounIf you refer to an activity or object as froth, you disapprove of it because it appears exciting or attractive, but has very little real value or importance. [disapproval] ⇒ No substance at all, just froth. ⇒ Falling in love the first time is all froth and fantasy.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
