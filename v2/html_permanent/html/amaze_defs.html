amaze

(əˈmeɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to fill with incredulity or surprise; astonish2. an obsolete word for bewildernoun3. an archaic word for amazement
Word origin
Old English āmasian

verbIf something amazes you, it surprises you very much. [V n] ⇒ He amazed us by his knowledge of Welsh history. [V] ⇒ The Riverside Restaurant promises a variety of food that never ceases to amaze! [Also it V n that/wh]amazed graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ] astonished [+ by] ⇒ He said most of the cast was amazed by the play's success. ⇒ I was amazed to learn she was still writing her stories. ⇒ She is amazed that people still risk travelling without insurance.
