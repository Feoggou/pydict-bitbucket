pumice

(ˈpʌmɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: pumice stone a light porous acid volcanic rock having the composition of rhyolite, used for scouring and, in powdered form, as an abrasive and for polishingverb2. (transitive) to rub or polish with pumice

Derived Forms

pumiceous (pjuːˈmɪʃəs ) adjective

Word Origin

C15 pomys, from Old French pomis, from Latin pūmex

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounPumice is a kind of grey stone from a volcano and is very light in weight. It can be rubbed over surfaces, especially your skin, that you want to clean or make smoother.
