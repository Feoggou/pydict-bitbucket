Stygian

(ˈstɪdʒɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to the river Styx2. mainly literarya. dark, gloomy, or hellishb. completely inviolable, as a vow sworn by the river Styx

Word Origin

C16: from Latin Stygius, from Greek Stugios, from Stux Styx; related to stugein to hate
Example sentences containing 'Stygian'
Each detonation illuminated the short terrace for an instant then the houses were plunged into a Stygian dark. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEAnd so we plunged down the Stygian path, the policeman taking one of his men with him. Barnard, Robert DEATH IN PURPLE PROSESomeday when time travel is discovered again, the name of Lancelot Stebbins will rest in Stygian darkness, unrecognized. Asimov, Isaac THE COMPLETE STORIES VOLUME 2He estimated that only twenty minutes of light remained in his last lamp before the Stygian gloom returned for good. Cussler, Clive INCA GOLD

Similar Words
