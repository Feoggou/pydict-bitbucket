cutting edge

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The spelling cutting-edge is used for meaning [sense 3].1. singular noun [usu at/on the N of n]If you are at the cutting edge of a particular field of activity, you are involved in its most important or most exciting developments. [+ of] ⇒ This shipyard is at the cutting edge of world shipbuilding technology.2. singular nounIf someone or something gives you a cutting edge, they give you an advantage over your competitors. ⇒ If Pearce had been fit, we would have won. We missed the cutting edge he would have given us.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Cutting-edge techniques or equipment are the most advanced that there are in a particular field. ⇒ What we are planning is cutting-edge technology never seen in Australia before. ⇒ Some of what is happening at Packard is cutting-edge medicine.
