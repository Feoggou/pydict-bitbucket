telescope

(ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp )
Definitions
noun1. an optical instrument for making distant objects appear larger and brighter by use of a combination of lenses (refracting telescope) or lenses and curved mirrors (reflecting telescope). See also terrestrial telescope, astronomical telescope, Cassegrain telescope, Galilean telescope, Newtonian telescope2. any instrument, such as a radio telescope, for collecting, focusing, and detecting electromagnetic radiation from spaceverb3. to crush together or be crushed together, as in a collision ⇒ the front of the car was telescoped by the impact4. to fit together like a set of cylinders that slide into one another, thus allowing extension and shortening5. to make or become smaller or shorter ⇒ the novel was telescoped into a short play
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Italian telescopio or New Latin telescopium, literally: far-seeing instrument; see tele-, -scope
