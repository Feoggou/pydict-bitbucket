furtive

(ˈfɜːtɪv )
Definitions
adjectivecharacterized by stealth; sly and secretive
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

furtively (ˈfurtively) adverbfurtiveness (ˈfurtiveness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin furtīvus stolen, clandestine, from furtum a theft, from fūr a thief; related to Greek phōr thief

furtive

Powered by COBUILD
(fɜːʳtɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as furtive, you disapprove of them behaving as if they want to keep something secret or hidden. [disapproval] ⇒ With a furtive glance over her shoulder, she unlocked the door and entered the house.furtively graded adverb [usu ADV with v, also ADV adj] ⇒ He walked towards the summerhouse, at first furtively, then with more confidence.
