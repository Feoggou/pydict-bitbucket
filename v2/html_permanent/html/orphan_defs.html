orphan

(ˈɔːfən )
Definitions
noun1. a. a child, one or (more commonly) both of whose parents are deadb. (as modifier) ⇒ an orphan child2. printing the first line of a paragraph separated from the rest of the paragraph by occurring at the foot of a pageverb3. (transitive) to deprive of one or both parents
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin orphanus, from Greek orphanos; compare Latin orbus bereaved

orphan for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɔːʳfən )
Word forms: orphans , orphaned
Definitions
1. countable nounAn orphan is a child whose parents are dead. ⇒ I'm an orphan and pretty much grew up on my own. ⇒ ...a young orphan girl brought up by peasants.2. passive verb [no cont]If a child is orphaned, their parents die, or their remaining parent dies. [be V-ed] ⇒ Jones was orphaned at the age of ten, and taken in by next-door neighbours. [be V-ed] ⇒ By the end of the decade, some ten million children are expected to have been orphaned by the disease. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a fifteen-year-old boy left orphaned by the recent disaster.
