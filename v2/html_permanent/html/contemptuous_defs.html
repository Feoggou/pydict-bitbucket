(kənˈtɛmptjʊəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(when predicative, often foll by of) showing or feeling contempt; disdainful
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are contemptuous of someone or something, you do not like or respect them at all. ⇒ He was contemptuous of private farmers. [+ of] ⇒ He's openly contemptuous of all the major political parties. ⇒ She gave a contemptuous little laugh.contemptuously graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'A deal!' she said contemptuously, 'I hate all deals.'
