coupling

(ˈkʌplɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a mechanical device that connects two things2. a device for connecting railway cars or trucks together3. the part of the body of a horse, dog, or other quadruped that lies between the forequarters and the hindquarters4. electronics the act or process of linking two or more circuits so that power can be transferred between them usually by mutual induction, as in a transformer, or by means of a capacitor or inductor common to both circuits. See also direct coupling5. physics an interaction between different properties of a system, such as a group of atoms or nuclei, or between two or more systems6. genetics the occurrence of two specified nonallelic genes from the same parent on the same chromosome
