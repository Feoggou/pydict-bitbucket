prominent

(ˈprɒmɪnənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. jutting or projecting outwards2. standing out from its surroundings; noticeable3. widely known; eminent

Derived Forms

prominently (ˈprominently) adverbprominentness (ˈprominentness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōminēre to jut out, from pro-1 + ēminēre to project

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is prominent is important. ⇒ ...a prominent member of the Law Society. ⇒ ...the children of very prominent or successful parents.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is prominent is very noticeable or is an important part of something else. ⇒ Here the window plays a prominent part in the design. ⇒ ...Romania's most prominent independent newspaper.prominently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Trade will figure prominently in the second day of talks in Washington. ⇒ Entries will be prominently displayed in the exhibition hall.
