yourselves

(ˈjɔːsɛlvz, jʊəˈsɛlvz)
Definitions
pronoun1. the reflexive form of plural you ⇒ Treat yourselves to a glass of wine., ⇒ Look after yourselves!, ⇒ You've given yourselves a difficult task., ⇒ You should get yourselves something more challenging to do., ⇒ Did you enjoy yourselves?, ⇒ Buy yourselves something nice., ⇒ Try not to talk about yourselves so much., ⇒ Be honest with yourselves.2. used to emphasize that the people one is speaking to did something alone, rather than anyone else ⇒ Did you do it yourselves?, ⇒ Did you make it yourselves?3. used as an intensifier to emphasize that one means 'you' ⇒ You and Annie could have a bit of a rest yourselves., ⇒ Do you yourselves know why she came?4. used instead of 'you' for emphasis or in order to be more polite when 'you' is the object of a verb or preposition ⇒ I wouldn't want to cause such important people as yourselves any bother., ⇒ How are yourselves?5. See by yourselves

  • British English: yourselves /jɔːˈsɛlvz/ PRONOUN
    A speaker or writer uses yourselves to refer to the people that they are talking or writing to. Yourselves is used when the object of a verb or preposition refers to the same people as the subject of the verb.Treat yourselves to an ice cream.
