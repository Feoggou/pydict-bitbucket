molt

(moʊlt )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
verb intransitive1.  to cast off or shed the exoskeleton, hair, outer skin, horns, or feathers at certain intervals, prior to replacement of the castoff parts by a new growthsaid of reptiles, birds, insects, etc. verb transitive2.  to replace by molting noun3.  the act or process of molting4.  the parts so shed

ˈmolter noun

Word Origin

ME mouten (with unhistoric -l- after fault, in which the letter was orig. silent) < OE (be)mutian, to exchange < L mutare, to change: see mutate
