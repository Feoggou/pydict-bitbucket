excruciating

(ɪkˈskruːʃɪˌeɪtɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. unbearably painful; agonizing2. intense; extreme ⇒ he took excruciating pains to do it well3. informal irritating; trying4. humorous very bad ⇒ an excruciating pun

excruciating

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪkskruːʃieɪtɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as excruciating, you are emphasizing that it is extremely painful, either physically or emotionally. [emphasis] ⇒ I was in excruciating pain and one leg wouldn't move. ⇒ Her search for love has often caused her excruciating misery and loneliness.excruciatingly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV after v] ⇒ He found the transition to boarding school excruciatingly painful. ⇒ The ball hit him excruciatingly in the most sensitive part of his anatomy.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as excruciating, you mean that it is very unpleasant to experience, for example because it is very boring or embarrassing. ⇒ Meanwhile, the boredom is excruciating. ⇒ There was a moment of excruciating silence.excruciatingly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV with v] ⇒ The dialogue is excruciatingly embarrassing. ⇒ ...the one where the children's chorus goes on excruciatingly about 'Grocer Jack'.
