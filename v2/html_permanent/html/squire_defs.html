squire

(skwaɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a country gentleman in England, esp the main landowner in a rural community2. feudal history a young man of noble birth, who attended upon a knight3. rare a man who courts or escorts a woman4. informal, mainly British a term of address used by one man to another, esp, unless ironic, to a member of a higher social class5. Australian an immature snapper. See snapper (sense 2)verb6. (transitive) (of a man) to escort (a woman)

Word Origin

C13: from Old French esquier; see esquire
