unsympathetic

(ˌʌnsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. not characterized by, feeling, or showing sympathy2. (when postpositive, often foll by to or towards) not showing agreement (with) or favour (towards)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unsympathetic for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnsɪmpəθetɪk )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is unsympathetic, they are not kind or helpful to a person in difficulties. ⇒ Her husband was unsympathetic and she felt she had no one to turn to. ⇒ ...an unsympathetic doctor.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn unsympathetic person is unpleasant and difficult to like. ⇒ ...a very unsympathetic main character. ⇒ He's unsympathetic, but charismatic and complex.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are unsympathetic to a particular idea or aim, you are not willing to support it. [+ to] ⇒ I'm highly unsympathetic to what you are trying to achieve.
