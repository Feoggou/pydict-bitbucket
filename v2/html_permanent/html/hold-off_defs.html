hold off

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) to keep apart or at a distance2. (intransitive; often foll by from) to refrain (from doing something) ⇒ he held off buying the house until prices fell slightly

hold off

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you hold off doing something, you delay doing it or delay making a decision about it. [V P v-ing] ⇒ The hospital staff held off taking Rosenbaum in for an X-ray. [V P] ⇒ They have threatened military action but held off until now.2. phrasal verbIf you hold off a challenge in a race or competition, you do not allow someone to pass you. [V P n] ⇒ Between 1987 and 1990, Steffi Graf largely held off Navratilova's challenge for the crown. [Also V n P] →  hold
