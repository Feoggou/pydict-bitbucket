dower

(ˈdaʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the life interest in a part of her husband's estate allotted to a widow by law2. an archaic word for dowry (sense 1)3. a natural gift or talentverb4. (transitive) to endow
Derived forms
dowerless (ˈdowerless) adjective
Word origin
C14: from Old French douaire, from Medieval Latin dōtārium, from Latin dōs gift
