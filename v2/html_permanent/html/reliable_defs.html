Powered by COBUILD
(rɪlaɪəbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectivePeople or things that are reliable can be trusted to work well or to behave in the way that you want them to. ⇒ She was efficient and reliable. ⇒ Japanese cars are so reliable.reliably (rɪlaɪəbli ) graded adverb & adverb ⇒ It's been working reliably for years.reliability (rɪlaɪəbɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ He's not at all worried about his car's reliability.2. graded adjective & adjectiveInformation that is reliable or that is from a reliable source is very likely to be correct. ⇒ There is no reliable information about civilian casualties. ⇒ It's very difficult to give a reliable estimate. ⇒ We have reliable sources.reliably graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Sonia, we are reliably informed, loves her family very much.reliability uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Both questioned the reliability of recent opinion polls.
