lashing

Powered by COBUILD
(læʃɪŋ ) for learners of English
Word forms: lashings
Definitions
1. quantifierLashings of something means a large quantity or amount of it. [mainly British, informal] [+ of] ⇒ Serve by cutting the scones in half and spreading with jam and lashings of clotted cream. ⇒ ...lashings of slapstick comedy.2. countable noun [usu pl]Lashings are ropes or cables that are used to tie one thing to another. ⇒ We made a tour of the yacht, checking lashings and emergency gear.3. countable nounIf you refer to someone's comments as a lashing, you mean that they are very critical and angry. ⇒ He never grew used to the lashings he got from the critics.4. countable nounA lashing is a punishment in which a person is hit with a whip.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
