sinecure

(ˈsaɪnɪˌkjʊə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a paid office or post involving minimal duties2. a Church benefice to which no spiritual or pastoral charge is attached

Derived Forms

sineˌcurism (ˈsineˌcurism) nounsineˌcurist (ˈsineˌcurist) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin phrase (beneficium) sine cūrā (benefice) without cure (of souls), from Latin sine without + cūra cure, care
