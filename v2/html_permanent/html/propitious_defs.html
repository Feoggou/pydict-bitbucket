propitious

(prəˈpɪʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. favourable; auguring well2. gracious or favourably inclined

Derived Forms

propitiously (proˈpitiously) adverbpropitiousness (proˈpitiousness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin propitius well disposed, from prope close to

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is propitious, it is likely to lead to success. [formal] ⇒ They should wait for the most propitious moment between now and the next election. ⇒ The omens for the game are still not propitious.
