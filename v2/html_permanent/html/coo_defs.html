coo

(kuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: coos, cooing or cooed1. (intransitive) (of doves, pigeons, etc) to make a characteristic soft throaty call2. (transitive) to speak in a soft murmur3. (intransitive) to murmur lovingly (esp in the phrase bill and coo)noun4. the sound of cooingexclamation5. British slang an exclamation of surprise, awe, etc

(kuː )
Word forms: coos , cooing , cooed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen a dove or pigeon coos, it makes the soft sounds that doves and pigeons typically make. [V] ⇒ Pigeons fluttered in and out, cooing gently.2. verbWhen someone coos, they speak in a very soft, quiet voice which is intended to sound attractive. [V + at/over] ⇒ She paused to coo at the baby. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Isn't this marvellous?' she cooed.
