citizenship

(ˈsɪtɪzənˌʃɪp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the condition or status of a citizen, with its rights and duties2. a person's conduct as a citizen ⇒ an award for good citizenship

citizenship

(sɪtɪzənʃɪp )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft adj N]If you have citizenship of a country, you are legally accepted as belonging to it. ⇒ After 15 years in the U.S.A., he has finally decided to apply for American citizenship. ⇒ Only people who can trace their family history in Kuwait back before 1920 are entitled to citizenship.2. uncount nounCitizenship is the fact of belonging to a community because you live in it, and the duties and responsibilities that this brings. ⇒ Their German peers had a more developed sense of citizenship.
