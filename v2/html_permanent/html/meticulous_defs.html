meticulous

(mɪˈtɪkjʊləs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivevery precise about details, even trivial ones; painstaking

Derived Forms

meticulously (meˈticulously) adverbmeticulousness (meˈticulousness) or meticu'losity noun

Word Origin

C16 (meaning: timid): from Latin meticulōsus fearful, from metus fear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as meticulous, you mean that they do things very carefully and with great attention to detail. ⇒ He was so meticulous about everything. ⇒ The painting had been executed with meticulous attention to detail.meticulously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ The flat had been meticulously cleaned.
