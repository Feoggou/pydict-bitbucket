state of affairs

(steɪt əv əˈfɛəz)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna situation; present circumstances or condition ⇒ This state of affairs cannot continue for too long, if parliament is to recover., ⇒ The nation had a chance to move towards a more democratic, and modern, state of affairs., ⇒ We could not allow that state of affairs to continue.

state of affairs

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular nounIf you refer to a particular state of affairs, you mean the general situation and circumstances connected with someone or something. ⇒ This state of affairs cannot continue for too long, if parliament is to recover. ⇒ The nation had a chance to move towards a more democratic, and modern, state of affairs.
Example sentences containing 'state of affairs'
Official histories tend to gloss over this state of affairs or, more commonly, ignore it altogether, an understandable myopia. Maclean, Alistair SAN ANDREASIn the harbour, it had been comparatively warm and sheltered, but a very different state of affairs existed beyond the headland. Maclean, Alistair THE LONELY SEAThat this abysmal state of affairs should have resulted in the death of my first-born son was more than I could bear. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERS
