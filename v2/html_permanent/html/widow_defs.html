widow

(ˈwɪdəʊ )
Definitions
noun1. a woman who has survived her spouse, esp one who has not remarried2. (usually with a modifier) informal a woman whose spouse is often away from home indulging in a sport, etc ⇒ a golf widow3. printing a short line at the end of a paragraph, esp one that occurs as the top line of a page or column. Compare orphan (sense 3)4. (in some card games) an additional hand or set of cards exposed on the tableverb (tr; usually passive)5. to cause to become a widow or a widower6. to deprive of something valued or desirable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

widowhood (ˈwidowhood) noun

Word Origin

Old English widuwe; related to German Witwe, Latin vidua (feminine of viduus deprived), Sanskrit vidhavā
