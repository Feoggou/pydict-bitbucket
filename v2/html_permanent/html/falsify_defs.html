falsify

(ˈfɔːlsɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to make (a report, evidence, accounts, etc) false or inaccurate by alteration, esp in order to deceive2. to prove false; disprove
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

falsifiable (ˈfalsiˌfiable) adjectivefalsification (ˌfɔːlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ) nounfalsifier (ˈfalsiˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French falsifier, from Late Latin falsificāre, from Latin falsus false + facere to make
