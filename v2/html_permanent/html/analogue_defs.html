analogue

or sometimes US analog (ˈænəˌlɒɡ )analogon (əˈnæləɡɒn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a physical object or quantity, such as a pointer on a dial or a voltage, used to measure or represent another quantityb. (as modifier) ⇒ analogue watch, ⇒ analogue recording2. something analogous to something else3. biology an analogous part or organ4. chemistrya. an organic chemical compound related to another by substitution of hydrogen atoms with alkyl groups ⇒ toluene is an analogue of benzeneb. an organic compound that is similar in structure to another organic compound ⇒ thiols are sulphur analogues of alcohols5. informal a person who is afraid of using new technological devices. Compare digital native, digital immigrantSee note at analog

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The spelling analog is used in American English, and also in British English for meaning [sense 2].1. countable nounIf one thing is an analogue of another, it is similar in some way. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ No model can ever be a perfect analogue of nature itself.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Analogue technology involves measuring, storing, or recording an infinitely variable amount of information by using physical quantities such as voltage. ⇒ The analogue signals from the video tape are converted into digital code.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]An analogue watch or clock shows what it is measuring with a pointer on a dial rather than with a number display. Compare digital.
