lost

(lɒst )
Definitions
adjective1. unable to be found or recovered2. unable to find one's way or ascertain one's whereabouts3. confused, bewildered, or helpless ⇒ he is lost in discussions of theory4. (sometimes foll by on) not utilized, noticed, or taken advantage of (by) ⇒ rational arguments are lost on her5. no longer possessed or existing because of defeat, misfortune, or the passage of time ⇒ a lost art6. destroyed physically ⇒ the lost platoon7. (foll by to) no longer available or open (to)8. (foll by to) insensible or impervious (to a sense of shame, justice, etc)9. (foll by in) engrossed (in) ⇒ he was lost in his book10. morally fallen ⇒ a lost woman11. damned ⇒ a lost soul12. See get lost

lost

Powered by COBUILD
(lɒst , US lɔːst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. Lost is the past tense and past participle of lose.2. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are lost or if you get lost, you do not know where you are or are unable to find your way. ⇒ Barely had I set foot in the street when I realised I was lost. ⇒ I took a wrong turn and we got lost in the mountains.3. adjectiveIf something is lost, or gets lost, you cannot find it, for example because you have forgotten where you put it. mislaid ⇒ ...a lost book. ⇒ My paper got lost. ⇒ He was scrabbling for his pen, which had got lost somewhere under the sheets of paper.4. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you feel lost, you feel very uncomfortable because you are in an unfamiliar situation. ⇒ Of the funeral he remembered only the cold, the waiting, and feeling very lost. ⇒ I feel lost and lonely in a strange town alone.5. adjectiveIf you describe a person or group of people as lost, you think that they do not have a clear idea of what they want to do or achieve. ⇒ They are a lost generation in search of an identity.6. adjectiveIf you describe something as lost, you mean that you no longer have it or it no longer exists. ⇒ ...their lost homeland. ⇒ ...a lost job or promotion. ⇒ The sense of community is lost. ⇒ The riots will also mean lost income for Los Angeles County.7. adjective [ADJ n]You use lost to refer to a period or state of affairs that existed in the past and no longer exists. ⇒ He seemed to pine for his lost youth. ⇒ They are links to a lost age. ⇒ ...the relics of a lost civilisation.8. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If something is lost, it is not used properly and is considered wasted. wasted ⇒ Fox is not bitter about the lost opportunity to compete in the Games. ⇒ The advantage is lost.9. →  get lost10. →  be lost on someone11. →  be lost in thought12. →  be lost without
