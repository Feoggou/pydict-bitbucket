liqueur

(lɪˈkjʊə , French likœr)
Definitions
noun1. a. any of several highly flavoured sweetened spirits such as kirsch or cointreau, intended to be drunk after a mealb. (as modifier) ⇒ liqueur glass2. a small hollow chocolate sweet containing liqueur
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from French; see liquor

(lɪkjʊəʳ , US -kɜːr ) for learners of English
Word forms: liqueurs
Definitions
1. variable nounA liqueur is a strong alcoholic drink with a sweet taste. You drink it after a meal. ⇒ ...liqueurs such as Grand Marnier and Kirsch. ⇒ ...small glasses of liqueur.A liqueur is a glass of liqueur. [+ with] ⇒ 'What about a liqueur with your coffee?' suggested the waitress.2. countable nounLiqueurs are a type of chocolate. They contain a sweet substance that has the flavour of an alcoholic liqueur. ⇒ ...two boxes of liqueurs.
