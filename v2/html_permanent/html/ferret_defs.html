ferret1

(ˈfɛrɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a domesticated albino variety of the polecat Mustela putorius, bred for hunting rats, rabbits, etc2. an assiduous searcher3. See black-footed ferretverb -rets, -reting or -reted4. to hunt (rabbits, rats, etc) with ferrets5. (transitive; usually foll by out) to drive from hiding ⇒ to ferret out snipers6. (transitive; usually foll by out) to find by persistent investigation7. (intransitive) to search around
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ferreter (ˈferreter) nounferrety (ˈferrety) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French furet, from Latin fur thief
