poignant

(ˈpɔɪnjənt , -nənt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. sharply distressing or painful to the feelings2. to the point; cutting or piercing ⇒ poignant wit3. keen or pertinent in mental appeal ⇒ a poignant subject4. pungent in smell

Derived Forms

poignancy (ˈpoignancy) or poignance (ˈpoignance) nounpoignantly (ˈpoignantly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, from Latin pungens pricking, from pungere to sting, pierce, grieve
