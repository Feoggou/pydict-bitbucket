drunk

(drʌŋk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. intoxicated with alcohol to the extent of losing control over normal physical and mental functions2. overwhelmed by strong influence or emotion ⇒ drunk with joynoun3. a person who is drunk or drinks habitually to excess4. informal a drinking bout
Word origin
Old English druncen, past participle of drincan to drink; see drink

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is drunk has drunk so much alcohol that they cannot speak clearly or behave sensibly. ⇒ Stewart could not remember exactly why he had done it because he was so drunk. ⇒ I got drunk and had to be carried home. ⇒ He was arrested on suspicion of drunk driving.2. countable nounA drunk is someone who is drunk or frequently gets drunk. drunkard ⇒ A drunk lay in the alley.3. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are drunk with a strong emotion or an experience, you are in a state of great excitement because of it. [+ with] ⇒ They are currently drunk with success. ⇒ I felt drunk with the excitement of life.4. Drunk is the past participle of drink.
