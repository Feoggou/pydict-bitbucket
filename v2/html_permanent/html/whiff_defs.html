whiff1

(wɪf )
Definitions
noun1. a passing odour2. a brief gentle gust of air3. a single inhalation or exhalation from the mouth or noseverb4. to come, convey, or go in whiffs; puff or waft5. to take in or breathe out (tobacco smoke, air, etc)6. (transitive) to sniff or smell7. (intransitive) British slang to have an unpleasant smell; stink
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

whiffer (ˈwhiffer) noun

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

whiff for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪf )
Word forms: whiffs
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]If there is a whiff of a particular smell, you smell it only slightly or only for a brief period of time, for example as you walk past someone or something. [+ of] ⇒ He caught a whiff of her perfume.2. countable noun [usu sing]A whiff of something bad or harmful is a slight sign of it. [+ of] ⇒ Not a whiff of scandal has ever tainted his private life. ⇒ The TV show had the whiff of hypocrisy and pomposity.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
