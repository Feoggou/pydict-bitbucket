fare

(fɛə )
Definitions
noun1. the sum charged or paid for conveyance in a bus, train, aeroplane, etc2. a paying passenger, esp when carried by taxi3. a range of food and drink; dietverb (intransitive)4. to get on (as specified); manage ⇒ he fared well5. (with it as a subject) to turn out or happen as specified ⇒ it fared badly with him6. archaic to eat ⇒ we fared sumptuously7. (often foll by forth) archaic to go or travel
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

farer (ˈfarer) noun

Word Origin

Old English faran; related to Old Norse fara to travel, Old High German faran to go, Greek poros ford

fare

Powered by COBUILD
(feəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: fares , faring , fared
Definitions
1. countable nounA fare is the money that you pay for a journey that you make, for example, in a bus, train, or taxi. ⇒ He could barely afford the railway fare. ⇒ ...taxi fares.2. uncount nounThe fare at a restaurant or café is the type of food that is served there. [written] ⇒ The fare has much improved since Hugh has taken charge of the kitchen. ⇒ ...traditional Portuguese fare in a traditional setting.3. verbIf you say that someone or something fares well or badly, you are referring to the degree of success they achieve in a particular situation or activity. [V adv] ⇒ It is unlikely that the marine industry will fare any better in September. [V adv] ⇒ Some later expeditions fared better, though they were no better equipped.
