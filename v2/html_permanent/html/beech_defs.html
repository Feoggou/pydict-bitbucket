beech

(biːtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any N temperate tree of the genus Fagus, esp F. sylvatica of Europe, having smooth greyish bark: family Fagaceae2. any tree of the related genus Nothofagus, of temperate Australasia and South America3. the hard wood of any of these trees, used in making furniture, etc4. See copper beech
Derived forms
beechen (ˈbeechen) or beechy (ˈbeechy) adjective
Word origin
Old English bēce; related to Old Norse bók, Old High German buohha, Middle Dutch boeke, Latin fāgus beech, Greek phēgos edible oak
