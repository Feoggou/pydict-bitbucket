terrific

(təˈrɪfɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. very great or intense ⇒ a terrific noise2. informal very good; excellent ⇒ a terrific singer3. very frightening
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

terrifically (terˈrifically) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin terrificus, from terrēre to frighten; see -fic

terrific

Powered by COBUILD
(tərɪfɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something or someone as terrific, you are very pleased with them or very impressed by them. [informal] ⇒ What a terrific idea! ⇒ Everybody there was having a terrific time. ⇒ You look terrific, Ann. You really do.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Terrific means very great in amount, degree, or intensity. [emphasis] ⇒ He did a terrific amount of fundraising. ⇒ All of a sudden there was a terrific bang and a flash of smoke.terrifically (tərɪfɪkli ) adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ She really is terrifically pretty. ⇒ ...the only child of terrifically repressed parents.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
