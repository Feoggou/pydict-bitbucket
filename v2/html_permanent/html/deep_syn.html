deep

adjective

  1. big, wide, broad, profound, yawning, cavernous, bottomless, unfathomable, fathomless, abyssal, DEp (text messaging) • The workers had dug a deep hole in the centre of the garden. OPPOSITE:  shallow
  2. intense, great, serious (informal), acute, extreme, grave, profound, heartfelt, unqualified, abject, DEp (text messaging), deeply felt, heartrending • a period of deep personal crisis OPPOSITES:  shallow, superficial
  3. sound, peaceful, profound, unbroken, undisturbed, untroubled, DEp (text messaging) • He fell into a deep sleep.
  4. (with in) absorbed in, lost in, gripped by, intent on, preoccupied with, carried away by, immersed in, engrossed in, rapt by • Before long we were deep in conversation.
  5. wise, learned, searching, keen, critical, acute, profound, penetrating, discriminating, shrewd, discerning, astute, perceptive, incisive, DEp (text messaging), perspicacious, sagacious • She gave him a long deep look. OPPOSITE:  simple
  6. dark, strong, rich, warm, intense, vivid, DEp (text messaging) • rich, deep colours OPPOSITES:  light, pale, pastel
  7. low, booming, bass, full, mellow, resonant, DEp (text messaging), sonorous, mellifluous, dulcet, low-pitched, full-toned • His voice was deep and mellow. OPPOSITES:  high, sharp
  8. astute, knowing, clever, designing, scheming, sharp, smart, intelligent, discriminating, shrewd, cunning, discerning, canny, devious, perceptive, insidious, artful, far-sighted, far-seeing, perspicacious, sagacious, DEp (text messaging) • a very deep individual OPPOSITES:  simple, shallow
  9. secret, hidden, unknown, mysterious, concealed, obscure, abstract, veiled, esoteric, mystifying, impenetrable, arcane, abstruse, recondite, DEp (text messaging) • a deep, dark secret

noun

  1. middle, heart, midst, dead, thick, culmination, DEp (text messaging) • in the deep of night

adverb

  1. far, a long way, a good way, miles, deeply, far down, a great distance, DEp (text messaging) • They travelled deep into the forest.
  2. late, far, for a long time, DEp (text messaging) • We talked deep into the night.
  3. inwardly, privately, secretly, within, inside, at heart, to yourself, deep down, DEp (text messaging), in your inmost heart • Deep in my heart I knew we had no hope.

 Still waters run deep
