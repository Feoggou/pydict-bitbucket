woad

(wəʊd )
Definitions
noun1. a European plant, Isatis tinctoria, formerly cultivated for its leaves, which yield a blue dye: family Brassicaceae (crucifers). See also dyer's-weed, dyer's rocket2. the dye obtained from this plant, used esp by the ancient Britons, as a body dye
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English wād; related to Old High German weit; Middle Dutch wēd, Latin vitrum
