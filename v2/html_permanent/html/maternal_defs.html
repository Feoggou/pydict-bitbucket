maternal

(məˈtɜːnəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of, relating to, derived from, or characteristic of a mother2. related through the mother's side of the family ⇒ his maternal uncle

Derived Forms

maternalism (maˈternalism) nounmaˌternalistic (maˌternalˈistic) adjectivematernally (maˈternally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin māternālis, from Latin māternus, from māter mother

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Maternal is used to describe feelings or actions which are typical of those of a kind mother towards her child. ⇒ She had little maternal instinct. ⇒ Her feelings towards him were almost maternal.2. adjective [ADJ n]Maternal is used to describe things that relate to the mother of a baby. ⇒ Maternal smoking can damage the unborn child.3. adjective [ADJ n]A maternal relative is one who is related through a person's mother rather than their father. ⇒ Her maternal grandfather was Mayor of Karachi.
