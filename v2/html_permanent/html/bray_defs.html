bray1

(breɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) (of a donkey) to utter its characteristic loud harsh sound; heehaw2. (intransitive) to make a similar sound, as in laughing ⇒ he brayed at the joke3. (transitive) to utter with a loud harsh soundnoun4. the loud harsh sound uttered by a donkey5. a similar loud cry or uproar ⇒ a bray of protest
Derived forms
brayer (ˈbrayer) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French braire, probably of Celtic origin

bray

Powered by COBUILD
(breɪ )
Word forms: brays , braying , brayed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen a donkey brays, it makes a loud harsh sound. [V] ⇒ The donkey brayed and tried to bolt.2. verbIf someone brays, they make a loud harsh sound or talk in a loud harsh way. [written] [V prep] ⇒ Neil brayed with angry laughter. [V with quote] ⇒ Her voice was shockingly loud. 'Put the chair down,' she brayed. [V-ing] ⇒ William had a loud, braying voice.Bray is also a noun. ⇒ She cut him off with a wild bray of laughter.
