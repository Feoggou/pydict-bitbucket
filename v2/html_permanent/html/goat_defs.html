goat

(ɡəʊt )
Definitions
noun1. any sure-footed agile bovid mammal of the genus Capra, naturally inhabiting rough stony ground in Europe, Asia, and N Africa, typically having a brown-grey colouring and a beard. Domesticated varieties (C. hircus) are reared for milk, meat, and wool ▶ Related adjectives: caprine, hircine2. short for Rocky Mountain goat3. informal a lecherous man4. a bad or inferior member of any group (esp in the phrase separate the sheep from the goats)5. short for scapegoat6. See act the goat7. See get someone's goat
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

goatlike (ˈgoatˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

Old English gāt; related to Old Norse geit, Old High German geiz, Latin haedus kid
