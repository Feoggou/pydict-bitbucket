trivial

(ˈtrɪvɪəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of little importance; petty or frivolous ⇒ trivial complaints2. ordinary or commonplace; trite ⇒ trivial conversation3. mathematics (of the solutions of a set of homogeneous equations) having zero values for all the variables4. biology denoting the specific name of an organism in binomial nomenclature5. biology, chemistry denoting the popular name of an organism or substance, as opposed to the scientific one6. of or relating to the trivium
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

trivially (ˈtrivially) adverbtrivialness (ˈtrivialness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin triviālis belonging to the public streets, common, from trivium crossroads, junction of three roads, from tri- + via road
