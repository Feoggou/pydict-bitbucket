tantalize

or tantalise (ˈtæntəˌlaɪz )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to tease or make frustrated, as by tormenting with the sight of something greatly desired but inaccessible
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tantalization (ˌtantaliˈzation) or tantalisation (ˌtantaliˈsation) nountantalizer (ˈtantaˌlizer) or tantaliser (ˈtantaˌliser) nountantalizing (ˈtantaˌlizing) or tantalising (ˈtantaˌlising) adjectivetantalizingly (ˈtantaˌlizingly) or tantalisingly (ˈtantaˌlisingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from the punishment of Tantalus

[V n with n] ⇒ The boy would come into the room and tantalize the dog with his feed. [V n] ⇒ ...the dreams of democracy that have so tantalized them. [Also V]tantalizing graded adjective & adjective ⇒ A tantalising aroma of roast beef fills the air.tantalizingly graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj] [ADV with v] ⇒ She went away disappointed after getting tantalisingly close to breaking the record. ⇒ A political settlement remains tantalisingly out of reach.
