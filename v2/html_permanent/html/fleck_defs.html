fleck

(flɛk )
Definitions
noun1. a small marking or streak; speckle2. a small particle; speck ⇒ a fleck of dustverb3. Also: flecker (transitive) to mark or cover with flecks; speckle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: probably from Old Norse flekkr stain, spot; related to Old High German flec spot, plot of land

fleck

Powered by COBUILD
(flek ) for learners of English
Word forms: flecks
Definitions
countable noun [usu pl]Flecks are small marks on a surface, or objects that look like small marks. ⇒ He went to the men's room to wash flecks of blood from his shirt. ⇒ His hair is dark grey with flecks of ginger.
