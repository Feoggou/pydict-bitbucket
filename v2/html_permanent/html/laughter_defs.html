laughter

(ˈlɑːftə )
Definitions
noun1. the action of or noise produced by laughing2. the experience or manifestation of mirth, amusement, scorn, or joy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hleahtor; related to Old Norse hlātr

laughter

Powered by COBUILD
(lɑːftəʳ , læf-) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounLaughter is the sound of people laughing, for example because they are amused or happy. ⇒ Their laughter filled the corridor. ⇒ He delivered the line perfectly, and everybody roared with laughter. ⇒ ...hysterical laughter.2. uncount nounLaughter is the fact of laughing, or the feeling of fun and amusement that you have when you are laughing. ⇒ Pantomime is about bringing laughter to thousands. ⇒ My interests: eating out, fun nights in, music and laughter.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
