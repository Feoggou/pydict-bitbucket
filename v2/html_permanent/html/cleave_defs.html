cleave1

(kliːv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: cleaves, cleaving, cleft, cleaved, clove, cleft, cleaved or cloven1. to split or cause to split, esp along a natural weakness2. (transitive) to make by or as if by cutting ⇒ to cleave a path3. (when intr, foll by through) to penetrate or traverse
Derived forms
cleavable (ˈcleavable) adjectiveˌcleavability (ˌcleavaˈbility) or cleavableness (ˈcleavableness) noun
Word origin
Old English clēofan; related to Old Norse kljūfa, Old High German klioban, Latin glūbere to peel

cleave

Powered by COBUILD
(kliːv )
Word forms: cleaves , cleaving
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The past tense can be either cleaved or clove; the past participle can be cleaved, cloven, or cleft for meaning [sense 1], and is cleaved for meaning [sense 2].1. verbTo cleave something means to split or divide it into two separate parts, often violently. [literary] [V n] ⇒ They just cleave the stone along the cracks.2. verbIf someone cleaves to something or to someone else, they begin or continue to have strong feelings of loyalty towards them. [formal] [V + to] ⇒ She has cleaved to these principles all her life. [V to n] ⇒ He still clove to this ideal.
