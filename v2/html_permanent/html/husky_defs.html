husky1

(ˈhʌskɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: huskier or huskiest1. (of a voice, an utterance, etc) slightly hoarse or rasping2. of, like, or containing husks3. informal big, strong, and well-built
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

huskily (ˈhuskily) adverbhuskiness (ˈhuskiness) noun

Word Origin

C19: probably from husk, from the toughness of a corn husk

husky2

(ˈhʌskɪ )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural huskies1. a breed of Arctic sled dog with a thick dense coat, pricked ears, and a curled tail2. Canadian slanga. a member of the Inuit peopleb. the Inuit language
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: probably based on Eskimo

husky

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌski ) for learners of English
Word forms: huskier , huskiest , huskies
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone's voice is husky, it is low and rather rough, often in an attractive way. ⇒ His voice was husky with grief. ⇒ ...Dietrich's deep, husky voice.huskily graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ 'Ready?' I asked huskily.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a man as husky, you think that he is tall, strong, and attractive. [informal] ⇒ ...a very husky young man, built like a football player.3. countable nounA husky is a strong, furry dog, which is used to pull sledges across snow.
