trump1

(trʌmp )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: trump carda. any card from the suit chosen as trumpsb. this suit itself; trumps2. Also called: trump card a decisive or advantageous move, resource, action, etc3. informal a fine or reliable personverb4. to play a trump card on (a suit, or a particular card of a suit, that is not trumps)5. (transitive) to outdo or surpass
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

trump2 - archaic or literary

(trʌmp )
Definitions
noun1. a trumpet or the sound produced by one2. See the last trumpverb3. (intransitive) to produce a sound upon or as if upon the trumpet4. (transitive) to proclaim or announce with or as if with a fanfare5. (intransitive) British slang to expel intestinal gas through the anus
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

trump

Powered by COBUILD
(trʌmp ) for learners of English
Word forms: trumps , trumping , trumped
Definitions
1. uncount noun [with sing or pl verb]In a game of cards, trumps is the suit which is chosen to have the highest value in one particular game. ⇒ Hearts are trumps. ⇒ ...the ace of trumps.2. countable nounIn a game of cards, a trump is a playing card which belongs to the suit which has been chosen as trumps. ⇒ He played a trump.3. verbIf you trump what someone has said or done, you beat it by saying or doing something else that seems better. [V n] ⇒ The Socialists tried to trump this with their slogan. [V n] ⇒ The Hong Kong and Shanghai Bank has trumped Lloyds by raising its offer.4. →  sb's trump card5. →  to come up trumps
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
