civet

(ˈsɪvɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any catlike viverrine mammal of the genus Viverra and related genera, of Africa and S Asia, typically having blotched or spotted fur and secreting a powerfully smelling fluid from anal glands2. the yellowish fatty secretion of such an animal, used as a fixative in the manufacture of perfumes3. the fur of such an animal4. short for palm civet
Word origin
C16: from Old French civette, from Italian zibetto, from Arabic zabād civet perfume
