catharsis

(kəˈθɑːsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses1. (in Aristotelian literary criticism) the purging or purification of the emotions through the evocation of pity and fear, as in tragedy2. psychoanalysis the bringing of repressed ideas or experiences into consciousness, thus relieving tensions. See also abreaction3. purgation, esp of the bowels
Word origin
C19: New Latin, from Greek katharsis, from kathairein to purge, purify
