amiss

(əˈmɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. in an incorrect, inappropriate, or defective manner2. See take something amissadjective3. (postpositive) wrong, incorrect, or faulty
Word origin
C13 a mis, from mis wrong; see miss1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that something is amiss, you mean there is something wrong. ⇒ Their instincts warned them something was amiss. [+ in] ⇒ Something is radically amiss in our health care system.2. →  not go amiss/not come amiss
