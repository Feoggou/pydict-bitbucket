detract

(dɪˈtrækt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when intr, usually foll by from) to take away a part (of); diminish ⇒ her anger detracts from her beauty2. (transitive) to distract or divert3. (transitive) obsolete to belittle or disparage▶ USAGE Detract is sometimes wrongly used where distract is meant: a noise distracted (not detracted) my attention
Derived forms
detractingly (deˈtractingly) adverbdetractive (deˈtractive) or detractory (deˈtractory) adjectivedetractively (deˈtractively) adverbdetractor (deˈtractor) noundetractress (deˈtractress) feminine noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin dētractus drawn away, from dētrahere to pull away, disparage, from de- + trahere to drag

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf one thing detracts from another, it makes it seem less good or impressive. [V + from] ⇒ The publicity could detract from our election campaign. [Also V n + from]
