sit back

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intr, adverb) to relax, as when action should be taken ⇒ many people just sit back and ignore the problems of today

phrasal verbIf you sit back while something is happening, you relax and do not become involved in it. [informal] [V P] ⇒ They didn't have to do anything except sit back and enjoy life. [V P to-inf] ⇒ American firms handed over technologies to their partners and then sat back to enjoy the cash flow. →  sit
