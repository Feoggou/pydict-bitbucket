implacable

(ɪmˈplækəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. incapable of being placated or pacified; unappeasable2. inflexible; intractable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

implacability (imˌplacaˈbility) or implacableness (imˈplacableness) nounimplacably (imˈplacably) adverb

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is implacable, you mean that they have very strong feelings of hostility or disapproval which nobody can change. ⇒ ...the threat of invasion by a ruthless and implacable enemy. ⇒ The move has won the implacable opposition of many economists.implacably graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV -ed/adj, oft ADV after v] ⇒ His union was implacably opposed to the privatization of the company.
