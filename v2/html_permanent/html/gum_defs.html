gum1

(ɡʌm )
Definitions
noun1. any of various sticky substances that exude from certain plants, hardening on exposure to air and dissolving or forming viscous masses in water2. any of various products, such as adhesives, that are made from such exudates3. any sticky substance used as an adhesive; mucilage; glue4. New Zealand short for kauri gum5. See chewing gum, bubble gum, gumtree6. mainly British a gumdropverbWord forms: gums, gumming or gummed7. to cover or become covered, clogged, or stiffened with or as if with gum8. (transitive) to stick together or in place with gum9. (intransitive) to emit or form gum
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gumless (ˈgumless) adjectivegumlike (ˈgumˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French gomme, from Latin gummi, from Greek kommi, from Egyptian kemai

Powered by COBUILD
(gʌm ) for learners of English
Word forms: gums , gumming , gummed
Definitions
1. variable nounGum is a substance, usually tasting of mint, which you chew for a long time but do not swallow.2. → See also bubble gum, chewing gum3. countable noun [usu pl]Your gums are the areas of firm, pink flesh inside your mouth, which your teeth grow out of. ⇒ The toothbrush gently removes plaque without damaging the gums. ⇒ ...gum disease.4. variable nounGum is a type of glue that is used to stick two pieces of paper together. [mainly British] ⇒ He was holding up a pound note that had been torn in half and stuck together with gum.gummed adjective [usu ADJ n] ⇒ ...gummed labels.5. adjectiveIf two things are gummed together, they are stuck together. [British] ⇒ It is a mild infection in which a baby's eyelashes can become gummed together.6. mass nounGum is a sticky substance which comes from the eucalyptus tree or from various other trees and shrubs.► →  gum up
