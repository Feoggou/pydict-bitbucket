salutation

(ˌsæljʊˈteɪʃən )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an act, phrase, gesture, etc, that serves as a greeting2. a form of words used as an opening to a speech or letter, such as Dear Sir or Ladies and Gentlemen3. the act of saluting

Word Origin

C14: from Latin salūtātiō, from salūtāre to greet; see salute

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft in/of N]Salutation or a salutation is a greeting to someone. [formal] ⇒ Jackson nodded a salutation. ⇒ The old man moved away, raising his hand in salutation.2. countable nounThe salutation of a letter is the phrase that is used at the beginning of it, such as 'Dear Sir' or 'Dear Mr Rodd'. [formal]
