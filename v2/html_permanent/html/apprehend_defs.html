apprehend

(ˌæprɪˈhɛnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to arrest and escort into custody; seize2. to perceive or grasp mentally; understand3. (transitive) to await with fear or anxiety; dread
Word origin
C14: from Latin apprehendere to lay hold of

apprehend

Powered by COBUILD
(æprɪhend )
Word forms: apprehends , apprehending , apprehended
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf the police apprehend someone, they catch them and arrest them. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Police have not apprehended her killer.2. verbIf you apprehend something, you understand it fully. [literary] grasp [V n] ⇒ Only now can I begin to apprehend the power of these forces.
