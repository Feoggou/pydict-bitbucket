fertile

(ˈfɜːtaɪl )
Definitions
adjective1. capable of producing offspring2. a. (of land) having nutrients capable of sustaining an abundant growth of plantsb. (of farm animals) capable of breeding stock3. biologya. capable of undergoing growth and development ⇒ fertile seeds, ⇒ fertile eggsb. (of plants) capable of producing gametes, spores, seeds, or fruits4. producing many offspring; prolific5. highly productive; rich; abundant ⇒ a fertile brain6. physics (of a substance) able to be transformed into fissile or fissionable material, esp in a nuclear reactor7. conducive to productiveness ⇒ fertile rain
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fertilely (ˈfertilely) adverbfertileness (ˈfertileness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fertilis, from ferre to bear
