elocution

(ˌɛləˈkjuːʃən )
Definitions
nounthe art of public speaking, esp of voice production, delivery, and gesture
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

elocutionary (ˌeloˈcutionary) or elocutory (ˌɛləˈkjuːtərɪ) adjectiveelocutionist (ˌeloˈcutionist) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin ēlocūtiō a speaking out, from ēloquī, from loquī to speak

Definitions
uncount nounElocution lessons are lessons in which someone is taught to speak clearly and in an accent that is considered to be standard and acceptable. ⇒ When I was 11 my mother sent me to elocution lessons.
