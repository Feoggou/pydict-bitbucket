curtail

(kɜːˈteɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to cut short; abridge
Derived forms
curtailer (curˈtailer) nouncurtailment (curˈtailment) noun
Word origin
C16: changed (through influence of tail1) from obsolete curtal to dock; see curtal

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you curtail something, you reduce or limit it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ NATO plans to curtail the number of troops being sent to the region. [V n] ⇒ I told Louie that old age would curtail her activities in time. [V n] ⇒ His powers will be severely curtailed.
