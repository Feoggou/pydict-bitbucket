propitiate

(prəˈpɪʃɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to appease or make well disposed; conciliate

Derived Forms

propitiable (proˈpitiable) adjectiveproˌpitiation (proˌpitiˈation) nounproˌpitiatious (proˌpitiˈatious) adjectivepropitiative (proˈpitiative) adjectivepropitiˌator (proˈpitiˌator) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin propitiāre to appease, from propitius gracious

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you propitiate someone, you stop them being angry or impatient by doing something to please them. [formal] [V n] ⇒ I've never gone out of my way to propitiate people. [V n] ⇒ These ancient ceremonies propitiate the spirits of the waters.
