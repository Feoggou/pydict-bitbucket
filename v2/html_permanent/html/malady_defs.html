malady

(ˈmælədɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -dies1. any disease or illness2. any unhealthy, morbid, or desperate condition ⇒ a malady of the spirit

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, from Vulgar Latin male habitus (unattested) in poor condition, from Latin male badly + habitus, from habēre to have

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA malady is an illness or disease. [old-fashioned] ⇒ He was stricken at twenty-one with a crippling malady.2. countable nounIn written English, people sometimes use maladies to refer to serious problems in a society or situation. ⇒ When apartheid is over the maladies will linger on. ⇒ ...the maladies of love.
