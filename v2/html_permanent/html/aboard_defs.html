aboard

(əˈbɔːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb, adjective, preposition (postpositive)1. on, in, onto, or into (a ship, train, aircraft, etc)2. nautical alongside (a vessel)3. See all aboard!

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
prepositionIf you are aboard a ship or plane, you are on it or in it. ⇒ She invited 750 people aboard the luxury yacht, the Savarona. ⇒ They said goodbye to him as he got aboard the train at Union Station.Aboard is also an adverb. ⇒ It had taken two hours to load all the people aboard. ⇒ The United States has a naval task force offshore, with two thousand marines aboard.
