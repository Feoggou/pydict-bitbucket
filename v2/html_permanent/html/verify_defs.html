verify

(ˈvɛrɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to prove to be true; confirm; substantiate2. to check or determine the correctness or truth of by investigation, reference, etc3. law to add a verification to (a pleading); substantiate or confirm (an oath)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

verifiable (ˈveriˌfiable) adjectiveverifiableness (ˈveriˌfiableness) nounverifiably (ˈveriˌfiably) adverbverifier (ˈveriˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French verifier, from Medieval Latin vērificāre, from Latin vērus true + facere to make

(verɪfaɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: verifies , verifying , verified
Definitions
1. verbIf you verify something, you check that it is true by careful examination or investigation. [V n] ⇒ I verified the source from which I had that information. [V that] ⇒ A clerk simply verifies that the payment and invoice amount match.verification (verɪfɪkeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ All charges against her are dropped pending the verification of her story. ⇒ ...the agency's verification procedures.2. verb [no cont]If you verify something, you state or confirm that it is true. [V n] ⇒ The government has not verified any of those reports. [V that] ⇒ I can verify that it takes about thirty seconds.
