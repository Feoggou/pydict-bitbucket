eddy

(ˈɛdɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -dies1. a movement in a stream of air, water, or other fluid in which the current doubles back on itself causing a miniature whirlwind or whirlpool2. a deviation from or disturbance in the main trend of thought, life, etc, esp one that is relatively unimportantverb -dies, -dying or -died3. to move or cause to move against the main current
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse itha; related to Old English ed- again, back, Old High German it-

[literary] [V] ⇒ The dust whirled and eddied in the sunlight. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The crowds were eddying into the road.
