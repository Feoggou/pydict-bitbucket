procure

(prəˈkjʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to obtain or acquire; secure2. to obtain (women or girls) to act as prostitutes

Derived Forms

procurable (proˈcurable) adjectiveprocurance (proˈcurance) or procural (proˈcural) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Latin prōcūrāre to look after, from pro-1 + cūrāre to care for

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you procure something, especially something that is difficult to get, you obtain it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ It remained very difficult to procure food, fuel and other daily necessities.2. verbIf someone procures a prostitute, they introduce the prostitute to a client. [V n] ⇒ He procured girls of 16 and 17 to be mistresses for his influential friends.
