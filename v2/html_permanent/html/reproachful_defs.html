reproachful

(rɪˈprəʊtʃfʊl )
Definitions
adjective1. full of or expressing reproach2. archaic deserving of reproach; disgraceful
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reproachfully (reˈproachfully) adverbreproachfulness (reˈproachfulness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪproʊtʃfʊl ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveReproachful expressions or remarks show that you are disappointed, upset, or angry because someone has done something wrong. ⇒ She gave Isabelle a reproachful look. ⇒ He turned to Alex and his tone was reproachful.reproachfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ Luke's mother stopped smiling and looked reproachfully at him.
