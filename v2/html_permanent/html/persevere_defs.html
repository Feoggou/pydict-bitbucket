persevere

(ˌpɜːsɪˈvɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intransitive; often foll by in) to show perseverance

Derived Forms

ˌpersevering (ˌperseˈvering) adjectiveˌperseveringly (ˌperseˈveringly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French perseverer, from Latin persevērāre, from perseverus very strict; see severe

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you persevere with something, you keep trying to do it and do not give up, even though it is difficult. [V] ⇒ ...his ability to persevere despite obstacles and setbacks. [V + with] ⇒ ...a school with a reputation for persevering with difficult and disruptive children. [V prep] ⇒ She persevered in her idea despite obvious objections raised by friends.persevering graded adjective & adjective ⇒ He is a persevering, approachable family man.
