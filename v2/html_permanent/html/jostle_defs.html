jostle

(ˈdʒɒsəl )
Definitions
verb1. to bump or push (someone) roughly2. to come or bring into contact3. to force (one's way) by pushingnoun4. the act of jostling5. a rough bump or push

jostlement (ˈjostlement) nounjostler (ˈjostler) noun

Word Origin

jostle for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɒsəl )
Word forms: jostles , jostling , jostled
Definitions
1. verbIf people jostle you, they bump against you or push you in a way that annoys you, usually because you are in a crowd and they are trying to get past you. [V n] ⇒ You get 2,000 people jostling each other and bumping into furniture. [V prep/adv] ⇒ We spent an hour jostling with the crowds as we did our shopping. [V to-inf] ⇒ She was cheered and clapped by tourists who jostled to see her. [Also V n prep/adv]2. verbIf people or things are jostling for something such as attention or a reward, they are competing with other people or things in order to get it. [V + for] ⇒ ...the contenders who have been jostling for the top job. [V for n] ⇒ There is a wide spread of stories jostling for coverage on today's front pages.
