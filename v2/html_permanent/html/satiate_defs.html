satiate

(ˈseɪʃɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to fill or supply beyond capacity or desire, often arousing weariness2. to supply to satisfaction or capacity

Derived Forms

ˌsatiation (ˌsatiˈation) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin satiāre to satisfy, from satis enough

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something such as food or pleasure satiates you, you have all that you need or all that you want of it, often so much that you become tired of it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The dinner was enough to satiate the gourmets. [V n] ⇒ The Edinburgh International Festival offers enough choice to satiate most appetites.satiated adjective ⇒ She finished the meal and sat back with a satiated sigh.
