recusant

(ˈrɛkjʊzənt )
Definitions
noun1. (in 16th to 18th century England) a Roman Catholic who did not attend the services of the Church of England, as was required by law2. any person who refuses to submit to authorityadjective3. (formerly, of Catholics) refusing to attend services of the Church of England4. refusing to submit to authority
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recusance (ˈrecusance) or recusancy (ˈrecusancy) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin recūsāns refusing, from recūsāre from re- + causārī to dispute, from causa a cause
