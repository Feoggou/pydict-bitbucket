unify

(ˈjuːnɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fiedto make or become one; unite
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unifiable (ˈuniˌfiable) adjectiveunifier (ˈuniˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin ūnificāre, from Latin ūnus one + facere to make

Powered by COBUILD
(juːnɪfaɪ )
Word forms: unifies , unifying , unified
Definitions
ergative reciprocal verb & verbIf someone unifies different things or parts, or if the things or parts unify, they are brought together to form one thing. [V n] ⇒ A flexible retirement age is being considered by Ministers to unify men's and women's pension rights. [V n] ⇒ He said he would seek to unify the Conservative Party and win the next general election. [V] ⇒ The plan has been for the rival armies to demobilise, to unify, and then to hold elections to decide who rules. [V n with n] ⇒ Both sides say they want to re-unify their country, which has been divided since the end of the Second World War. [V + with] ⇒ ...the benefits of unifying with the West.unified graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n] ⇒ ...a unified German state. ⇒ ...a unified system of taxation.
