execrable

(ˈɛksɪkrəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. deserving to be execrated; abhorrent2. of very poor quality ⇒ an execrable meal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

execrableness (ˈexecrableness) nounexecrably (ˈexecrably) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin exsecrābilis, from exsecrārī to execrate

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as execrable, you mean that it is very bad or unpleasant. [formal] ⇒ Accusing us of being disloyal to cover his own sorry behavior is truly execrable. ⇒ ...an execrable meal.
