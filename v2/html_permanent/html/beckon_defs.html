beckon

(ˈbɛkən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to summon with a gesture of the hand or head2. to entice or lurenoun3. a summoning gesture
Derived forms
beckoner (ˈbeckoner) nounbeckoning (ˈbeckoning) adjective, noun
Word origin
Old English bīecnan, from bēacen sign; related to Old Saxon bōknian; see beacon

Powered by COBUILD
(bekən )
Word forms: beckons , beckoning , beckoned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you beckon to someone, you signal to them to come to you. [V + to] ⇒ He beckoned to the waiter. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ I beckoned her over. [V n to-inf] ⇒ Hughes beckoned him to sit down on a sofa. [Also V]2. verbIf something beckons, it is so attractive to someone that they feel they must become involved in it. [V] ⇒ All the attractions of the peninsula beckon. [V n] ⇒ The bright lights of Hollywood beckon many. [Also V + to]3. verbIf something beckons for someone, it is very likely to happen to them. [V + for] ⇒ The big time beckons for him. [V] ⇒ Old age beckons.
