protect

(prəˈtɛkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to defend from trouble, harm, attack, etc2. economics to assist (domestic industries) by the imposition of protective tariffs on imports3. business to provide funds in advance to guarantee payment of (a note, draft, etc)

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōtegere to cover before, from pro-1 + tegere to cover

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo protect someone or something means to prevent them from being harmed or damaged. [V n + from/against] ⇒ So, what can women do to protect themselves from heart disease? [V n from/against n] ⇒ A long thin wool coat and a purple headscarf protected her against the wind. [V n] ⇒ The government is committed to protecting the interests of tenants.2. verbIf an insurance policy protects you against an event such as death, injury, fire, or theft, the insurance company will give you or your family money if that event happens. [V n + against] ⇒ Many manufacturers have policies to protect themselves against blackmailers. [Also V + against]
