solecism

(ˈsɒlɪˌsɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the nonstandard use of a grammatical constructionb. any mistake, incongruity, or absurdity2. a violation of good manners

Derived Forms

solecist (ˈsolecist) nounˌsolecistic (ˌsoleˈcistic) or ˌsolecistical (ˌsoleˈcistical) adjectiveˌsolecistically (ˌsoleˈcistically) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin soloecismus, from Greek soloikismos, from soloikos speaking incorrectly, from Soloi an Athenian colony of Cilicia where the inhabitants spoke a corrupt form of Greek
Example sentences containing 'solecism'
Miss Rondel didn't commit the solecism of laughing at her own quip, but there was gladness in her voice. MacLeod, Charlotte SOMETHING IN THE WATERSomewhat warily---was he committing some witchy solecism ? Stephanie Laurens SCANDAL'S BRIDE
