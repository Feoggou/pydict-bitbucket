bring forward

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to present or introduce (a subject) for discussion2. accounting to transfer (a figure representing the sum of the figures on a page or in a column) to the top of the next page or column3. to move to an earlier time or date ⇒ the kickoff has been brought forward to 2 p.m.

bring forward

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you bring forward a meeting or event, you arrange for it to take place at an earlier date or time than had been planned. [V P n] ⇒ He had to bring forward an 11 o'clock meeting so that he could get to the funeral on time. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ The election date had to be brought forward by two months. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verbIf you bring forward an argument or proposal, you state it so that people can consider it. [V P n] ⇒ The Government will bring forward several proposals for legislation.
