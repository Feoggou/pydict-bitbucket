atmosphere

(ˈætməsˌfɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the gaseous envelope surrounding the earth or any other celestial body. See also troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, ionosphere2. the air or climate in a particular place ⇒ the atmosphere was thick with smoke3. a general pervasive feeling or mood ⇒ an atmosphere of elation4. the prevailing tone or mood of a novel, symphony, painting, or other work of art5. a special mood or character associated with a place6. any local gaseous environment or medium ⇒ an inert atmosphere7. a unit of pressure; the pressure that will support a column of mercury 760 mm high at 0°C at sea level. 1 atmosphere is equivalent to 101 325 newtons per square metre or 14.72 pounds per square inch. Abbreviation: at, atm
Derived forms
ˌatmosˈpheric or ˌatmosˈpherical adjectiveˌatmosˈpherically adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]A planet's atmosphere is the layer of air or other gases around it. ⇒ ...dangerous levels of pollution in the Earth's atmosphere. ⇒ The Partial Test-Ban Treaty bans nuclear testing in the atmosphere.2. countable noun [usu sing]The atmosphere of a place is the air that you breathe there. [+ of] ⇒ These gases pollute the atmosphere of towns and cities.3. singular nounThe atmosphere of a place is the general impression that you get of it. ⇒ Pale wooden floors and plenty of natural light add to the relaxed atmosphere. [+ of] ⇒ There's still an atmosphere of great hostility and tension in the city.4. uncount nounIf a place or an event has atmosphere, it is interesting. ⇒ The old harbour is still full of atmosphere and well worth visiting.
