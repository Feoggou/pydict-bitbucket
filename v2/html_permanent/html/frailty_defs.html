frailty

(ˈfreɪltɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. physical or moral weakness2. (often plural) a fault symptomatic of moral weakness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

frailty

Powered by COBUILD
(freɪlti ) for learners of English
Word forms: frailties
Definitions
1. variable nounIf you refer to the frailties or frailty of people, you are referring to their weaknesses. [+ of] ⇒ ...the frailties of human nature. ⇒ ...a triumph of will over human frailty.2. uncount nounFrailty is the condition of having poor health. ⇒ She died after a long period of increasing frailty.
