detection

(dɪˈtɛkʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of discovering or the fact of being discovered ⇒ detection of crime2. the act or process of extracting information, esp at audio or video frequencies, from an electromagnetic wave. See also demodulation

detection

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪtekʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounDetection is the act of noticing or sensing something. [+ of] ⇒ ...the early detection of breast cancer.2. uncount nounDetection is the discovery of something which is supposed to be hidden. ⇒ They are cheating but are sophisticated enough to avoid detection. [Also + of]3. uncount nounDetection is the work of investigating a crime in order to find out what has happened and who committed it. ⇒ The detection rate for motor vehicle theft that year was just 11.7 per cent. ⇒ The most important deterrent for most criminals is the likelihood of detection and arrest.
