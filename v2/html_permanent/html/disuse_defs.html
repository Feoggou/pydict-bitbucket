disuse

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪsjuːs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [oft into N]If something falls into disuse, people stop using it. If something becomes worse as a result of disuse, it becomes worse because no one uses it. ⇒ ...a church which has fallen into disuse. ⇒ The wheel had long since rusted from years of disuse.
Example sentences containing 'disuse'
I drew in a deep breath and then stretched and heard my joints crackle from disuse. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThe hoisting beam still jutted out from the roof but its wheel had long since rusted from years of disuse. MacNeill, Alistair ALASTAIR MCLEAN'S 'NIGHT WATCH'Graffiti covered it at ground level, protecting the building with an air of disuse like a fence made of spray paint. Greg Bear VITALS
