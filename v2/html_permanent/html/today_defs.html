today

(təˈdeɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. this day, as distinct from yesterday or tomorrow2. the present age ⇒ children of todayadverb3. during or on this day4. nowadays

Word Origin

Old English tō dæge, literally: on this day, from to + dæge, dative of dæg day

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverbYou use today to refer to the day on which you are speaking or writing. ⇒ How are you feeling today? ⇒ I wanted him to come with us today, but he couldn't.Today is also a noun. ⇒ Today is Friday, September 14th. ⇒ The Prime Minister remains the main story in today's newspapers.2. adverb [n ADV]You can refer to the present period of history as today. ⇒ The United States is in a serious recession today. ⇒ He thinks pop music today is as exciting as it's ever been.Today is also a noun. ⇒ In today's America, health care is one of the very biggest businesses. ⇒ ...the Africa of today.
