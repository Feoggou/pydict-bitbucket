grill1

(ɡrɪl )
Definitions
verb1. to cook (meat, fish, etc) by direct heat, as under a grill or over a hot fire, or (of meat, fish, etc) to be cooked in this way. Usual US and Canadian word: broil2. (tr; usually passive) to torment with or as if with extreme heat ⇒ the travellers were grilled by the scorching sun3. (transitive) informal to subject to insistent or prolonged questioningnoun4. a device with parallel bars of thin metal on which meat, fish, etc, may be cooked by a fire; gridiron5. a device on a cooker that radiates heat downwards for grilling meat, fish, etc6. food cooked by grilling7. See grillroom

Powered by COBUILD
(grɪl ) for learners of English
Word forms: grills , grilling , grilled
Definitions
1. countable nounA grill is a part of a stove which produces strong heat to cook food that has been placed underneath it. [British] ⇒ Place the omelette under a gentle grill until the top is set.regional note:   in AM, use broiler2. countable nounA grill is a flat frame of metal bars on which food can be cooked over a fire.3. ergative verb & verbWhen you grill food, or when it grills, you cook it using very strong heat directly above or below it. [British] [V n] ⇒ Grill the meat for 20 minutes each side. [V adv] ⇒ Apart from peppers and aubergines, many other vegetables grill well. [V-ed] ⇒ ...grilled chicken.regional note:   in AM, use broil → cookgrilling uncount noun ⇒ The breast can be cut into portions for grilling.4. verbIf you grill someone about something, you ask them a lot of questions for a long period of time. [informal] [V n + about/on] ⇒ Grill your travel agent about the facilities for families with children. [V n] ⇒ The police grilled him for hours.grilling Word forms: grillings countable noun ⇒ They gave him a grilling about the implications of a united Europe.5. countable nounA grill is a restaurant that serves grilled food.
