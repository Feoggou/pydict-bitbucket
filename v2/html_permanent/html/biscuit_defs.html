biscuit

(ˈbɪskɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. British a small flat dry sweet or plain cake of many varieties, baked from a dough. US and Canadian word: cookie2. US and Canadian a kind of small roll similar to a muffin3. a. a pale brown or yellowish-grey colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ biscuit gloves4. Also called: bisque earthenware or porcelain that has been fired but not glazed5. See take the biscuit
Word origin
C14: from Old French, from (pain) bescuit twice-cooked (bread), from bes bis + cuire to cook, from Latin coquere
