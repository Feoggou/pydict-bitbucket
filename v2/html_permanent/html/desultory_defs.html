desultory

(ˈdɛsəltərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. passing or jumping from one thing to another, esp in a fitful way; unmethodical; disconnected2. occurring in a random or incidental way; haphazard ⇒ a desultory thought
Derived forms
desultorily (ˈdesultorily) adverbdesultoriness (ˈdesultoriness) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin dēsultōrius, relating to one who vaults or jumps, hence superficial, from dēsilīre to jump down, from de- + salīre to jump
