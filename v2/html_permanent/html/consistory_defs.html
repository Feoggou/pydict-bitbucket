consistory

(kənˈsɪstərɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ries1. Church of Englanda. the court of a diocese (other than Canterbury) administering ecclesiastical lawb. the area in a church where the consistory meets2. Roman Catholic Church an assembly of the cardinals and the pope3. (in certain Reformed Churches) the governing body of a local congregation or church4. archaic a council or assembly
Derived forms
consistorial (ˌkɒnsɪˈstɔːrɪəl ) or ˌconsistorian (ˌconsisˈtorian) adjective
Word origin
C14: from Old French consistorie, from Medieval Latin consistōrium ecclesiastical tribunal, ultimately from Latin consistere to stand still
Example sentences containing 'consistory'
We will meet with the consistory of cardinals in a few days, and you will give them back the red hat. Mario Puzo THE FAMILY
