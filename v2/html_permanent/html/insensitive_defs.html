insensitive

(ɪnˈsɛnsɪtɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking sensitivity; unfeeling2. lacking physical sensation3. (postpositive; foll by to) not sensitive (to) or affected (by) ⇒ insensitive to radiation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

insensitively (inˈsensitively) adverbinsensitiveness (inˈsensitiveness) or insensitivity (inˌsensiˈtivity) noun

insensitive

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnsensɪtɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as insensitive, you are criticizing them for being unaware of or unsympathetic to other people's feelings. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ I feel my husband is very insensitive about my problem.insensitivity (ɪnsensɪtɪvɪti ) uncount noun [+ towards] ⇒ I was ashamed at my insensitivity towards her.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is insensitive to a situation or to a need does not think or care about it. [+ to] ⇒ Women's and Latino organizations that say he is insensitive to civil rights.insensitivity uncount noun [+ to] ⇒ ...insensitivity to the environmental consequences.3. adjectiveSomeone who is insensitive to a physical sensation is unable to feel it. [+ to] ⇒ He had become insensitive to cold.
