rimmed

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪmd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf something is rimmed with a substance or colour, it has that substance or colour around its border. ⇒ The plates and glassware were rimmed with gold.2. → See also rim, -rimmed
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

His fingers, holding it, had long ragged nails which were rimmed with black filth. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFThe lip wobbled, eyes rimmed with tears of uncertainty as his faith in her fought with the evidence before him. Dobbs, Michael THE TOUCH OF INNOCENTSThe waiters, mostly Asian, wore gold epaulettes that made them look like Ruritanian generals; the plate and glassware were rimmed with gold.
