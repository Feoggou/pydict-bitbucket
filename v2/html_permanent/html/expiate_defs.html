expiate

(ˈɛkspɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to atone for or redress (sin or wrongdoing); make amends for
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

expiator (ˈexpiˌator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin expiāre, from pius dutiful; see pious

expiate

Powered by COBUILD
(ekspieɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: expiates , expiating , expiated
Definitions
verbIf you expiate guilty feelings or bad behaviour, you do something to indicate that you are sorry for what you have done. [formal] [V n] ⇒ It seemed that Alice was expiating her father's sins with her charity work. [Also V n + for]expiation (ekspieɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...an often painful process of evaluation and expiation.
