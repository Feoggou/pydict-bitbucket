symbolize

or symbolise (ˈsɪmbəˌlaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to serve as or be a symbol of2. (transitive; usually foll by by) to represent by a symbol or symbols3. (intransitive) to use symbols4. (transitive) to treat or regard as symbolic or figurative

Derived Forms

ˌsymbolization (ˌsymboliˈzation) or ˌsymbolisation (ˌsymboliˈsation) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use symboliseverbIf one thing symbolizes another, it is used or regarded as a symbol of it. [V n] ⇒ The fall of the Berlin Wall symbolised the end of the Cold War between East and West. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the post-war world order symbolised by the United Nations.
Example sentences containing 'symbolize'
These flames were meant to symbolize the life of all artificial organisms, and the street should have been glorious with light. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GOD
