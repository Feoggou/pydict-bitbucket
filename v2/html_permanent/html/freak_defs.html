freak1

(friːk )
Definitions
noun1. a person, animal, or plant that is abnormal or deformed; monstrosity2. a. an object, event, etc, that is abnormal or extremely unusualb. (as modifier) ⇒ a freak storm3. a personal whim or caprice4. informal a person who acts or dresses in a markedly unconventional or strange way5. informal a person who is obsessed with something specified ⇒ a jazz freakverb6. See freak out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

freak

Powered by COBUILD
(friːk ) for learners of English
Word forms: freaks , freaking , freaked
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]A freak event or action is one that is a very unusual or extreme example of its type. chance ⇒ Weir broke his leg in a freak accident playing golf. ⇒ The ferry was hit by a freak wave off the North Wales coast.2. countable noun [n N]If you describe someone as a particular kind of freak, you are emphasizing that they are very enthusiastic about a thing or activity, and often seem to think about nothing else. [informal] ⇒ Oat bran became the darling of health freaks last year. ⇒ ...computer freaks.3. → See also control freak4. countable nounPeople are sometimes referred to as freaks when their behaviour or attitude is very different from that of the majority of people. [disapproval] ⇒ However, outside of these institutions the black thinking woman is looked upon as a freak. ⇒ Not so long ago, transsexuals were regarded as freaks.5. countable nounIf you refer to someone as a freak, you mean that they are physically abnormal in some way. This use could cause offence. [disapproval]6. ergative verbIf someone freaks, or if something freaks them, they suddenly feel extremely surprised, upset, angry, or confused. [informal] [V] ⇒ I saw five cop cars pull into the driveway. And I literally freaked. [get V-ed] ⇒ I think they got freaked by women laughing at them. [Also V n]► →  freak out
