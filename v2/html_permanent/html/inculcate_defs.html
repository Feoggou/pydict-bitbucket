inculcate

(ˈɪnkʌlˌkeɪt , ɪnˈkʌlkeɪt )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to instil by forceful or insistent repetition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inculcation (ˌinculˈcation) nouninculcator (ˈinculˌcator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin inculcāre to tread upon, ram down, from in-2 + calcāre to trample, from calx heel

inculcate

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkʌlkeɪt , US ɪnkʌl- ) for learners of English
Word forms: inculcates , inculcating , inculcated
Definitions
verbIf you inculcate an idea or opinion in someone's mind, you teach it to them by repeating it until it is fixed in their mind. [formal] [V n + in] ⇒ We have tried to inculcate a feeling of citizenship in youngsters. [V n + with] ⇒ The aim is to inculcate business people with an appreciation of different cultures. [V n] ⇒ Great care was taken to inculcate the values of nationhood and family.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
