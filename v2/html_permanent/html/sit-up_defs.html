sit up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to raise (oneself or another) from a recumbent to an upright or alert sitting posture2. (intransitive) to remain out of bed and awake, esp until a late hour3. (intransitive) informal to become suddenly interested or alert ⇒ devaluation of the dollar made the money market sit upnoun sit-up, situp4. a physical exercise in which the body is brought into a sitting position from one lying on the back. Also: trunk curl

sit up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you sit up, you move into a sitting position when you have been leaning back or lying down. [V P] ⇒ Her head spins dizzily as soon as she sits up.2. phrasal verbIf you sit someone up, you move them into a sitting position when they have been leaning back or lying down. [V n P] ⇒ She sat him up and made him comfortable. [Also V P n (not pron)]3. phrasal verbIf you sit up, you do not go to bed although it is very late. [V P] ⇒ We sat up drinking and talking. [V P] ⇒ I didn't feel like sitting up all night.4. phrasal verbIf something makes you sit up, it makes you suddenly pay attention to what is happening. [V P] ⇒ A defeat like that makes you sit up and think.5. → see also sit-up →  sit
