inhibition

(ˌɪnɪˈbɪʃən , ˌɪnhɪ-)
Definitions
noun1. the act of inhibiting or the condition of being inhibited2. psychologya. a mental state or condition in which the varieties of expression and behaviour of an individual become restrictedb. the weakening of a learned response usually as a result of extinction or because of the presence of a distracting stimulusc. (in psychoanalytical theory) the unconscious restraining of an impulse. See also repression3. the process of stopping or retarding a chemical reaction4. physiology the suppression of the function or action of an organ or part, as by stimulation of its nerve supply5. Church of England an episcopal order suspending an incumbent
