nones

(nəʊnz )
Definitions
noun (functioning as singular or plural)1. (in the Roman calendar) the ninth day before the ides of each month: the seventh day of March, May, July, and October, and the fifth of each other month. See also calends2. mainly Roman Catholic Church the fifth of the seven canonical hours of the divine office, originally fixed at the ninth hour of the day, about 3 pm
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English nōn, from Latin nōna hora ninth hour, from nōnus ninth
