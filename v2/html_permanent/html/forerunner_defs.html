forerunner

(ˈfɔːˌrʌnə )
Definitions
noun1. a person or thing that precedes another; precursor2. a person or thing coming in advance to herald the arrival of someone or something; harbinger3. an indication beforehand of something to follow; omen; portent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

forerunner

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳrʌnəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: forerunners
Definitions
countable nounIf you describe a person or thing as the forerunner of someone or something similar, you mean they existed before them and either influenced their development or were a sign of what was going to happen. [+ of] ⇒ ...a machine which, in some respects, was the forerunner of the modern helicopter. ⇒ Some respiratory symptoms can be the forerunners of asthma.
