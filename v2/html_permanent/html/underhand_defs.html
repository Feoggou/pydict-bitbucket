underhand for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌndəʳhænd ) or underhanded
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If an action is underhand or if it is done in an underhand way, it is done secretly and dishonestly. [disapproval] ⇒ ...underhand financial deals. ⇒ ...a list of the underhanded ways in which their influence operates in the United States. ⇒ Mr Livingstone accused the government of being underhand.2. adjective [ADJ n]You use underhand or underhanded to describe actions, such as throwing a ball, in which you do not raise your arm above your shoulder. [US] ⇒ ...an underhanded pitch.Underhand is also an adverb. ⇒ In softball, pitches are tossed underhand.regional note:   in BRIT, use underarm
