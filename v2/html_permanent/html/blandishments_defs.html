(ˈblændɪʃmənts )
Definitions

blandishments

Powered by COBUILD
(blændɪʃmənts )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
plural noun [oft with poss]Blandishments are pleasant things that someone says to another person in order to persuade them to do something. [formal] ⇒ At first Lewis resisted their blandishments.
Example sentences containing 'blandishments'
It shouted its absurd blandishments in thick capitals half an inch high. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEAs it was, it occurred to him for perhaps a few seconds, until the biscuit took hold, and his taste-buds succumbed to its blandishments. Amanda Hemingway THE GREENSTONE GRAIL: THE SANGREAL TRILOGY ONEIt appalled him to see these phenomena and sickened him that he himself was prone to their blandishments. Clive Barker SACRAMENT
