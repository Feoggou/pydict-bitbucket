bicker

(ˈbɪkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to argue over petty matters; squabble2. poetica. (esp of a stream) to run quicklyb. to flicker; glitternoun3. a petty squabble
Derived forms
bickerer (ˈbickerer) nounbickering (ˈbickering) noun, adjective
Word origin
C13: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen people bicker, they argue or quarrel about unimportant things. [V + over/about] ⇒ I went into medicine to care for patients, not to waste time bickering over budgets. [pl-n V] ⇒ The two women bickered constantly. [V + over/about] ⇒ ...as states bicker over territory. [V + with] ⇒ He is still bickering with the control tower over admissible approach routes.bickering uncount noun ⇒ The election will end months of political bickering.
