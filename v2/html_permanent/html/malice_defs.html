malice

(ˈmælɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the desire to do harm or mischief2. evil intent3. law the state of mind with which an act is committed and from which the intent to do wrong may be inferred

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin malitia, from malus evil

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounMalice is behaviour that is intended to harm people or their reputations, or cause them embarrassment and upset. ⇒ There was a strong current of malice in many of his portraits. ⇒ There was no malice on his part.
