inadequate

(ɪnˈædɪkwɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. not adequate; insufficient2. not capable or competent; lacking
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

inadequate

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnædɪkwət ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is inadequate, there is not enough of it or it is not good enough. ⇒ Supplies of food and medicines are inadequate. ⇒ The problem goes far beyond inadequate staffing.inadequately graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The projects were inadequately funded.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If someone feels inadequate, they feel that they do not have the qualities and abilities necessary to do something or to cope with life in general. ⇒ I still feel inadequate, useless and mixed up. ⇒ Mary Ann felt painfully inadequate in the crisis.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
