virtuoso

(ˌvɜːtjʊˈəʊzəʊ , -səʊ)
Definitions
noun plural -sos or -si (-siː)1. a consummate master of musical technique and artistry2. a person who has a masterly or dazzling skill or technique in any field of activity3. a connoisseur, dilettante, or collector of art objects4. obsolete a scholar or savant5. (modifier) showing masterly skill or brilliance ⇒ a virtuoso performance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

virtuosic (ˌvɜːtjʊˈɒsɪk ) adjectivevirtuosity (ˌvirtuˈosity) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Italian: skilled, from Late Latin virtuōsus good, virtuous; see virtue

virtuoso

Powered by COBUILD
(vɜːʳtʃuoʊzoʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: virtuosos , virtuosi (vɜːʳtʃuoʊzi )
Definitions
1. countable nounA virtuoso is someone who is extremely good at something, especially at playing a musical instrument. ⇒ He was gaining a reputation as a remarkable virtuoso.2. adjective [ADJ n]A virtuoso performance or display shows great skill. ⇒ England's football fans were hoping for a virtuoso performance against Cameroon.
