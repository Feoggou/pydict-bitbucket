calibre

or US caliber (ˈkælɪbə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the diameter of a cylindrical body, esp the internal diameter of a tube or the bore of a firearm2. the diameter of a shell or bullet3. ability; distinction ⇒ a musician of high calibre4. personal character ⇒ a man of high calibre
Derived forms
calibred (ˈcalibred) or US calibered (ˈcalibered) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Old French, from Italian calibro, from Arabic qālib shoemaker's last, mould

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use caliber1. uncount noun [usu adj N]The calibre of a person is the quality or standard of their ability or intelligence, especially when this is high. [+ of] ⇒ I was impressed by the high calibre of the researchers and analysts. ⇒ It became apparent that we could never get the calibre of people we wanted.2. uncount nounThe calibre of something is its quality, especially when it is good. ⇒ The quality of his character and the caliber of his accomplishments produced a lasting influence. [+ of] ⇒ The calibre of teaching was very high.3. countable noun [adj N]The calibre of a gun is the width of the inside of its barrel. [technical] ⇒ ...a .22 calibre rifle. ⇒ ...a small-calibre pistol.
