leek

(liːk )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: scallion an alliaceous plant, Allium porrum, with a slender white bulb, cylindrical stem, and broad flat overlapping leaves: used in cooking2. any of several related species, such as A. ampeloprasum (wild leek)3. a leek, or a representation of one, as a national emblem of Wales
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English lēac; related to Old Norse laukr, Old High German louh
