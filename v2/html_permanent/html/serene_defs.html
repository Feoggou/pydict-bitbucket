serene

(sɪˈriːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. peaceful or tranquil; calm2. clear or bright ⇒ a serene sky3. (often capital) honoured: used as part of certain royal titles ⇒ His Serene Highness

Derived Forms

serenely (seˈrenely) adverbsereneness (seˈreneness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin serēnus

graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is serene is calm and quiet. ⇒ She looked as calm and serene as she always did. ⇒ He didn't speak much, he just smiled with that serene smile of his. ⇒ ...the beautiful, serene park.serenely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ We sailed serenely down the river. ⇒ She carried on serenely sipping her gin and tonic. ⇒ ...serenely beautiful.serenity (sɪrenɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ I had a wonderful feeling of peace and serenity when I saw my husband.
