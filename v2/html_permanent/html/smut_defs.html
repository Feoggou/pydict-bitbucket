smut

(smʌt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small dark smudge or stain, esp one caused by soot2. a speck of soot or dirt3. something obscene or indecent4. a. any of various fungal diseases of flowering plants, esp cereals, in which black sooty masses of spores cover the affected partsb. any parasitic basidiomycetous fungus of the order Ustilaginales that causes such a disease5. angling a midge or other insect relished by troutverbWord forms: smuts, smutting or smutted6. to mark or become marked or smudged, as with soot7. to affect (grain) or (of grain) to be affected with smut8. (transitive) to remove smut from (grain)9. (transitive) to make obscene10. (intransitive) to emit soot or smut11. (intransitive) angling (of trout) to feed voraciously on smuts
Derived forms
smutty (ˈsmutty) adjectivesmuttily (ˈsmuttily) adverbsmuttiness (ˈsmuttiness) noun
Word origin
Old English smitte; related to Middle High German smitze; associated with smudge, smutch
