certify

(ˈsɜːtɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied1. to confirm or attest (to), usually in writing ⇒ the letter certified her age2. (transitive) to endorse or guarantee (that certain required standards have been met)3. to give reliable information or assurances ⇒ he certified that it was Walter's handwriting4. (transitive) to declare legally insane5. (transitive) US and Canadian (of a bank) to state in writing on (a cheque) that payment is guaranteed
Derived forms
certiˌfier (ˈcertiˌfier) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French certifier, from Medieval Latin certificāre to make certain, from Latin certus certain + facere to make

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone in an official position certifies something, they officially state that it is true. [V that] ⇒ ...if the president certified that the project would receive at least $650m from overseas sources. [V n] ⇒ The National Election Council is supposed to certify the results of the election. [be V-ed as adj] ⇒ It has been certified as genuine. [be V-ed adj] ⇒ Mrs Simpson was certified dead. [Also V n adj, V n as adj]certification (səʳtɪfɪkeɪʃən )Word forms: certifications variable noun ⇒ An employer can demand written certification that the relative is really ill.2. verb [usu passive]If someone is certified as a particular kind of worker, they are given a certificate stating that they have successfully completed a course of training in their profession. [get V-ed + as] ⇒ They wanted to get certified as divers. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a certified accountant. [V-ed] ⇒ All three doctors are certified as addictions specialists.certification uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Pupils would be offered on-the-job training leading to the certification of their skill in a particular field.
