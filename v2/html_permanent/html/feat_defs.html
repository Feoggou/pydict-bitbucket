feat1

(fiːt )
Definitions
nouna remarkable, skilful, or daring action; exploit; achievement ⇒ feats of strength
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Anglo-French fait, from Latin factum deed; see fact

feat2

Definitions

Word Origin

feat

Powered by COBUILD
(fiːt ) for learners of English
Word forms: feats
Definitions
countable nounIf you refer to an action, or the result of an action, as a feat, you admire it because it is an impressive and difficult achievement. [approval] accomplishment ⇒ A racing car is an extraordinary feat of engineering.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
