vote of confidence

(vəʊt əv ˈkɒnfɪdəns)
Definitions
noun1. parliament a vote of confidence is a vote in which members of a group are asked to indicate that they still support the person or group in power, usually the government ⇒ The Indian Prime Minister, V P Singh, lost a vote of confidence in the Indian parliament.2. a vote of confidence is something that you say or do which shows that you approve of or support a person or a group ⇒ The ten-year deal is a vote of confidence in coal-fired power stations.
