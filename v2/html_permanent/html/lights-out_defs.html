lights out

or lights-out (ˌlaɪtsˈaʊt)
Definitions
noun1. the time when those resident at an institution, such as soldiers in barracks or children at a boarding school, are expected to retire to bed2. a fanfare or other signal indicating or signifying this

lights-out

(ˌlaɪtsˈaʊt)
Definitions
noun1. the time when those resident at an institution, such as soldiers in barracks or children at a boarding school, are expected to retire to bed ⇒ Lights out at 9 o'clock., ⇒ Lights out!2. a fanfare or other signal indicating or signifying this
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
