cynosure

(ˈsɪnəˌzjʊə , -ʃʊə)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person or thing that attracts notice, esp because of its brilliance or beauty2. something that serves as a guide
Derived forms
ˌcynosural (ˌcynoˈsural) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin Cynosūra the constellation of Ursa Minor, from Greek Kunosoura, from cyno- + oura tail
Example sentences containing 'cynosure'
`The cynosure ," said his assistant complacently, spreading the word out as if it were caviare. Barnard, Robert POLITICAL SUICIDEAmid the lighter, pastel colors, she was a dramatic highlight; as intended, she was the cynosure of all eyes. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
