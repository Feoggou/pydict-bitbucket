Cádiz - Spanish

(kəˈdɪz , ˈkaðiθ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna port in SW Spain, on a narrow peninsula that forms the Bay of Cádiz at the E end of the Gulf of Cádiz, founded about 1100 bc as a Phoenician trading colony; centre of trade with America from the 16th to 18th centuries. Pop: 134 989 (2003 est)
