delusive

(dɪˈlusɪv )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
adjective1.  tending to delude; misleading2.  of or like a delusion; unreal
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.
Derived forms
deˈlusively adverb deˈlusiveness noun
Word origin
L delusus (see delusion) + -ive
Example sentences containing 'delusive'
She sat there rocking to and fro, in the delusive belief that she was facing the worst. Ruell, Patrick THE ONLY GAMEAnd why did he always yearn to embrace those delusive fantasies? Wright, Richard THE OUTSIDER
