zing

(zɪŋ )
Definitions
noun informal1. a short high-pitched buzzing sound, as of a bullet or vibrating string2. vitality; zest3. US informal a witty remarkverb4. (intransitive) to make or move with or as if with a high-pitched buzzing sound5. (transitive) US informal to make a witty remark at the expense of (someone)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: of imitative origin

Definitions
uncount noun [oft a N]If you refer to the zing in someone or something, you mean the quality that makes them lively or interesting. [informal] ⇒ He just lacked that extra zing. ⇒ There's nothing like fresh basil to put a zing into a tomato sauce.
