ruby

(ˈruːbɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -bies1. a deep red transparent precious variety of corundum: occurs naturally in Myanmar and Sri Lanka but is also synthesized. It is used as a gemstone, in lasers, and for bearings and rollers in watchmaking. Formula: Al2O32. a. the deep-red colour of a rubyb. (as adjective) ⇒ ruby lips3. a. something resembling, made of, or containing a rubyb. (as modifier) ⇒ ruby necklace4. (modifier) denoting a fortieth anniversary ⇒ our ruby wedding5. (formerly) a size of printer's type approximately equal to 51⁄2 point
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ruby-like (ˈruby-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French rubi, from Latin rubeus reddish, from ruber red
