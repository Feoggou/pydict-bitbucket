limpid

(ˈlɪmpɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. clear or transparent2. (esp of writings, style, etc) free from obscurity3. calm; peaceful
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

limpidity (limˈpidity) or limpidness (ˈlimpidness) nounlimpidly (ˈlimpidly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from French limpide, from Latin limpidus clear

limpid

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪmpɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is limpid, you mean that it is very clear and transparent. [literary] ⇒ ...limpid blue eyes. ⇒ ...limpid rock-pools.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe speech, writing, or music as limpid, you like it because it is clear, simple and flowing. [literary, approval] ⇒ He thought the speech a model of its kind, limpid and unaffected.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
