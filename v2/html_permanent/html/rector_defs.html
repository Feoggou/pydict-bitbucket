rector

(ˈrɛktə )
Definitions
noun1. Church of England a clergyman in charge of a parish in which, as its incumbent, he would formerly have been entitled to the whole of the tithes. Compare vicar2. Roman Catholic Church a cleric in charge of a college, religious house, or congregation3. Episcopal Church, Scottish Episcopal Church a clergyman in charge of a parish4. mainly British the head of certain schools or colleges5. (in Scotland) a high-ranking official in a university: now a public figure elected for three years by the students
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rectorate (ˈrectorate) nounrectorial (rɛkˈtɔːrɪəl ) adjectiverectorship (ˈrectorship) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin: director, ruler, from regere to rule
