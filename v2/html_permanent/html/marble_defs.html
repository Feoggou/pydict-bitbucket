marble

(ˈmɑːbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a hard crystalline metamorphic rock resulting from the recrystallization of a limestone: takes a high polish and is used for building and sculptureb. (as modifier) ⇒ a marble bust ▶ Related adjective: marmoreal2. a block or work of art of marble3. a small round glass or stone ball used in playing marbles4. See make one's marble good5. See pass in one's marbleverb6. (transitive) to mottle with variegated streaks in imitation of marbleadjective7. cold, hard, or unresponsive8. white like some kinds of marble

Derived Forms

marbled (ˈmarbled) adjectivemarbler (ˈmarbler) nounmarbly (ˈmarbly) adjective

Word Origin

C12: via Old French from Latin marmor, from Greek marmaros, related to Greek marmairein to gleam

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft N n]Marble is a type of very hard rock which feels cold when you touch it and which shines when it is cut and polished. Statues and parts of buildings are sometimes made of marble. ⇒ The house has a superb staircase made from oak and marble. ⇒ The entrance-hall was paved with black and white marble tiles.2. countable noun [usu pl]Marbles are sculptures made of marble. ⇒ ...marbles and bronzes from the Golden Age of Athens.3. uncount nounMarbles is a children's game played with small balls, usually made of coloured glass. You roll a ball along the ground and try to hit an opponent's ball with it. ⇒ On the far side of the street, two boys were playing marbles.4. countable nounA marble is one of the small balls used in the game of marbles.5. →  lose one's marbles
