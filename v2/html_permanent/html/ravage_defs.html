ravage

(ˈrævɪdʒ )
Definitions
verb1. to cause extensive damage tonoun2. (often plural) destructive action ⇒ the ravages of time
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ravagement (ˈravagement) nounravager (ˈravager) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French, from Old French ravir to snatch away, ravish

ravage

Powered by COBUILD
(rævɪdʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: ravages , ravaging , ravaged
Definitions
verb [usu passive]A town, country, or economy that has been ravaged is one that has been damaged so much that it is almost completely destroyed. [be V-ed] ⇒ For two decades the country has been ravaged by civil war and foreign intervention. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the ravaged streets of Sarajevo. [V-ed] ⇒ ...Nicaragua's ravaged economy.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
