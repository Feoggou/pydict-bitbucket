Word forms: disbands , disbanding , disbanded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
ergative verb & verbIf someone disbands a group of people, or if the group disbands, it stops operating as a single unit. [be V-ed] ⇒ All the armed groups will be disbanded. [V] ⇒ The rebels were to have fully disbanded by June the tenth. [Also V n]
Example sentences containing 'disband'
It's too, too dangerous, and therefore we've decided to disband the kalla ceremony. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODThe two women were simply aiding and abetting each other to disband the Seraglio.
