Christmas

(ˈkrɪsməs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the annual commemoration by Christians of the birth of Jesus Christ on Dec 25b. Also called: Christmas Day Dec 25, observed as a day of secular celebrations when gifts and greetings are exchangedc. (as modifier) ⇒ Christmas celebrations2. Also called: Christmas Day (in England, Wales, and Ireland) Dec 25, one of the four quarter days. Compare Lady Day, Midsummer's Day, Michaelmas3. Also called (archaic or literary): Christmastide the season of Christmas extending from Dec 24 (Christmas Eve) to Jan 6 (the festival of the Epiphany or Twelfth Night)
Word origin
Old English Crīstes mæsse Mass of Christ

Christmas is celebrated on the 25th of December. ⇒ The day after Christmas is generally a busy one for retailers. ⇒ Merry Christmas, Mom.2. variable noun [oft N n]Christmas is the period of several days around and including Christmas Day. ⇒ During the Christmas holidays there's a tremendous amount of traffic between the Northeast and Florida. ⇒ He'll be in the hospital over Christmas, so we'll be spending our Christmas Day there.
