Visigoth

(ˈvɪzɪˌɡɒθ )
Definitions
nouna member of the western group of the Goths, who were driven into the Balkans in the late 4th century ad. Moving on, they sacked Rome (410) and established a kingdom in present-day Spain and S France that lasted until 711
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

Visigothic (ˌVisiˈgothic) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin Visigothī (pl), of Germanic origin, visi- perhaps meaning: west
