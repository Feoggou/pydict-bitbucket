semblance

(ˈsɛmbləns )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. outward appearance, esp without any inner substance or reality2. a resemblance or copy

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, from sembler to seem, from Latin simulāre to imitate, from similis like

semblance

Powered by COBUILD
(sembləns )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf there is a semblance of a particular condition or quality, it appears to exist, even though this may be a false impression. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ At least a semblance of normality has been restored to parts of the country. ⇒ They had nursed Peter back to some semblance of health.
Example sentences containing 'semblance'
"I'm going to call the vicar," she said, tugging her mouth into a wan semblance of a smile. Clive Barker SACRAMENTPlacing a physical distance between herself and Johannes had given Bettina a semblance of control. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEThere were tailors who would create a fair semblance of anything, from a worker's civilian clothes to a German uniform. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARMy banks" books are in some semblance of restoration; there is order out of chaos. Lustbader, Eric FLOATING CITY
