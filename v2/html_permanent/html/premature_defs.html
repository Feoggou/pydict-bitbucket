premature

(ˌprɛməˈtjʊə , ˈprɛməˌtjʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. occurring or existing before the normal or expected time2. impulsive or hasty ⇒ a premature judgment3. (of an infant) weighing less than 2500 g (51⁄2 lbs) and usually born before the end of the full period of gestation

Derived Forms

ˌprematurely (ˌpremaˈturely) adverbˌprematureness (ˌpremaˈtureness) or ˌprematurity (ˌpremaˈturity) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin praemātūrus, very early, from prae in advance + mātūrus ripe

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is premature happens earlier than usual or earlier than people expect. ⇒ Accidents are still the number one cause of premature death for Americans. ⇒ His career was brought to a premature end by a succession of knee injuries. ⇒ ...a twenty-four-year-old man who suffered from premature baldness.prematurely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ The war had prematurely aged him. ⇒ The heavy rain driving against the windows made the room prematurely dark.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]You can say that something is premature when it happens too early and is therefore inappropriate. ⇒ It now seems their optimism was premature. ⇒ I think it's premature for restaurants to come out with that advice.prematurely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ Holmgren is careful not to celebrate prematurely.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA premature baby is one that was born before the date when it was expected to be born. ⇒ Even very young premature babies respond to their mother's presence. ⇒ My daughter Emma was born two and a half months premature.prematurely graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ Danny was born prematurely, weighing only 3lb 3oz.
