split-off

(ˈsplɪtˌɒf)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounbusiness a separation (from the main group) ⇒ the recently announced partial split-off of its chemicals and polymers businesses, ⇒ It will sell the rest through a split-off next year., ⇒ after its split-off from media giant Viacom

phrasal verbIf people split off from a group, they stop being part of the group and become separated from it. [V P] ⇒ Somehow, Quentin split off from his comrades. [V P n] ⇒ ...the Youth Wing which split off the National Liberal party earlier this year. [V P] ⇒ A key member of the San Diego Yacht Club team is splitting off to form his own team. →  split
