dandelion

(ˈdændɪˌlaɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a plant, Taraxacum officinale, native to Europe and Asia and naturalized as a weed in North America, having yellow rayed flowers and deeply notched basal leaves, which are used for salad or wine: family Asteraceae (composites)2. any of several similar related plants
Word origin
C15: from Old French dent de lion, literally: tooth of a lion, referring to its leaves

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA dandelion is a wild plant which has yellow flowers with lots of thin petals. When the petals of each flower drop off, a fluffy white ball of seeds grows.
