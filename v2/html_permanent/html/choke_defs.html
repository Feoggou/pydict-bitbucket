choke

(tʃəʊk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to hinder or stop the breathing of (a person or animal), esp by constricting the windpipe or by asphyxiation2. (intransitive) to have trouble or fail in breathing, swallowing, or speaking3. (transitive) to block or clog up (a passage, pipe, street, etc)4. (transitive) to retard the growth or action of ⇒ the weeds are choking my plants5. (transitive) to suppress (emotion) ⇒ she choked her anger6. (intransitive) slang to die7. (transitive) to enrich the petrol-air mixture by reducing the air supply to (a carburettor, petrol engine, etc)8. (intransitive) (esp in sport) to be seized with tension and fail to perform wellnoun9. the act or sound of choking10. a device in the carburettor of a petrol engine that enriches the petrol-air mixture by reducing the air supply11. any constriction or mechanism for reducing the flow of a fluid in a pipe, tube, etc12. Also called: choke coil electronics an inductor having a relatively high impedance, used to prevent the passage of high frequencies or to smooth the output of a rectifier13. the inedible centre of the head of an artichoke
Derived forms
chokeable (ˈchokeable) adjective
Word origin
Old English ācēocian, of Germanic origin; related to cheek
