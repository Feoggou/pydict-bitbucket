agile

(ˈædʒaɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. quick in movement; nimble2. mentally quick or acute
Derived forms
ˈagilely adverbagility (əˈdʒɪlɪtɪ ) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin agilis, from agere to do, act

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is agile can move quickly and easily. ⇒ At 20 years old he was not as agile as he is now.agility (ədʒɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ She blinked in surprise at his agility.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you have an agile mind, you think quickly and intelligently. ⇒ She was quick-witted and had an extraordinarily agile mind.agility uncount noun ⇒ His intellect and mental agility have never been in doubt.
