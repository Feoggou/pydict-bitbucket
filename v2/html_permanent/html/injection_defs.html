injection

(ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən )
Definitions
noun1. fluid injected into the body, esp for medicinal purposes2. something injected3. the act of injecting4. a. the act or process of introducing fluid under pressure, such as fuel into the combustion chamber of an engineb. (as modifier) ⇒ injection moulding5. mathematics a function or mapping for which f(x) = f(y) only if x = y. See also surjection, bijection

(ɪndʒekʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: injections
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you have an injection, a doctor or nurse puts a medicine into your body using a device with a needle called a syringe. ⇒ They gave me an injection to help me sleep. ⇒ It has to be given by injection, usually twice daily.2. countable nounAn injection of money or resources into an organization is the act of providing it with more money or resources, to help it become more efficient or profitable. [business] [+ of] ⇒ An injection of cash is needed to fund some of these projects. ⇒ The company is hoping to obtain a £250 million cash injection from the government.

(ɪndʒɛkʃən) in the Oil and Gas Industry
Definitions
noun(Extractive engineering: Field development, Drilling) Injection is the process of inserting water or gas into a reservoir to pressurize it and displace hydrocarbons. ⇒ Enhanced oil recovery involves the injection of water, steam, gases, or chemicals into underground reservoirs to cause oil to flow toward producing wells. ⇒ The purpose of injection is to maintain overall and hydrostatic reservoir pressure and force the oil toward the production wells. ⇒ Injection is the process of inserting water or gas into a reservoir to pressurize it and displace hydrocarbons.
