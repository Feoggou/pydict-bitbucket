oracular

(ɒˈrækjʊlə )
Definitions
adjective1. of or relating to an oracle ⇒ Apollo had his oracular shrine at Delphi2. wise and prophetic ⇒ an oracular political thriller3. mysterious or ambiguous
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

oracularly (oˈracularly) adverb
Example sentences containing 'oracular'
But with all my oracular powers, I do not yet know the truth about her. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSThen the dog, making some oracular choice, licked the man's hand and moved away from the thorn. Salley Vickers MR GOLIGHTLY'S HOLIDAY* * * ---He is in Shabikant, kneeling before the two oracular trees, the ancient Trees of the Sun and the Moon. Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
