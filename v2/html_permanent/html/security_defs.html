security

(sɪˈkjʊərɪtɪ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the state of being secure2. assured freedom from poverty or want ⇒ he needs the security of a permanent job3. a person or thing that secures, guarantees, etc4. precautions taken to ensure against theft, espionage, etc ⇒ the security in the government offices was not very good5. (often plural)a. a certificate of creditorship or property carrying the right to receive interest or dividend, such as shares or bondsb. the financial asset represented by such a certificate6. the specific asset that a creditor can claim title to in the event of default on an obligation7. something given or pledged to secure the fulfilment of a promise or obligation8. a person who undertakes to fulfil another person's obligation9. the protection of data to ensure that only authorized personnel have access to computer files10. archaic carelessness or overconfidence

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft N n]Security refers to all the measures that are taken to protect a place, or to ensure that only people with permission enter it or leave it. ⇒ They are now under a great deal of pressure to tighten their airport security. ⇒ Strict security measures are in force in the capital. ⇒ ...a top security jail.2. uncount nounA feeling of security is a feeling of being safe and free from worry. [+ of] ⇒ He loves the security of a happy home life. ⇒ If an alarm gives you that feeling of security, then it's worth carrying.→  a false sense of security3. uncount nounIf something is security for a loan, you promise to give that thing to the person who lends you money, if you fail to pay the money back. [business] ⇒ The central bank will provide special loans, and the banks will pledge the land as security.4. plural nounSecurities are stocks, shares, bonds, or other certificates that you buy in order to earn regular interest from them or to sell them later for a profit. [business] ⇒ National banks can package their own mortgages and underwrite them as securities. ⇒ ...U.S. government securities and bonds.5. → see also social security
