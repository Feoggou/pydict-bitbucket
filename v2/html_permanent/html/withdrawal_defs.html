withdrawal

(wɪðˈdrɔːəl )
Definitions
noun1. an act or process of withdrawing; retreat, removal, or detachment2. the period a drug addict goes through following abrupt termination in the use of narcotics, usually characterized by physical and mental symptoms (withdrawal symptoms)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

withdrawal for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪðdrɔːəl )
Word forms: withdrawals
Definitions
1. variable nounThe withdrawal of something is the act or process of removing it, or ending it. [formal] ⇒ ...withdrawal of friendship. [+ of] ⇒ If you experience any unusual symptoms after withdrawal of the treatment then contact your doctor. ⇒ ...allied troop withdrawal from the north of the country.2. uncount nounSomeone's withdrawal from an activity or an organization is their decision to stop taking part in it. [+ from] ⇒ ...his withdrawal from government in 1946.3. countable nounA withdrawal is an amount of money that you take from your bank account.4. singular noun [N of n]The withdrawal of a remark or statement that you have made is the act of saying that you want people to ignore it. retraction ⇒ The charity says it wants a withdrawal of the comments.5. uncount nounWithdrawal is the period during which someone feels ill after they have stopped taking a drug which they were addicted to. [+ from] ⇒ Withdrawal from heroin is actually like a severe attack of gastric flu.6. uncount nounWithdrawal is behaviour in which someone prefers to be alone and does not want to talk to other people. ⇒ ...an inability to cope with emotional problems except by retreating into withdrawal.
