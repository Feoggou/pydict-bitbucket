Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf a place or person is steeped in a quality or characteristic, they are surrounded by it or deeply influenced by it. [+ in] ⇒ The castle is steeped in history and legend. ⇒ He said they were unrealistic and steeped in the past.
Example sentences containing 'steeped'
A beginning steeped in both privilege and prospects which had led to an end as sudden and complete as the progress had been glittering. Dobbs, Michael THE TOUCH OF INNOCENTS`Isn't the law steeped in the rights of people who want to discover their origins, their true parents?
