unlike

(ʌnˈlaɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. not alike; dissimilar or unequal; different2. archaic unlikelypreposition3. not like; not typical of ⇒ unlike his father, he lacks intelligence
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unlike

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnlaɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. prepositionIf one thing is unlike another thing, the two things have different qualities or characteristics from each other. dissimilar to ⇒ This was a foreign country, so unlike San Jose. ⇒ She was unlike him in every way except for her coal black eyes.2. prepositionYou can use unlike to contrast two people, things, or situations, and show how they are different. in contrast to ⇒ Unlike aerobics, walking entails no expensive fees for classes or clubs.3. prepositionIf you describe something that a particular person has done as being unlike them, you mean that you are surprised by it because it is not typical of their character or normal behaviour. ⇒ It was so unlike him to say something like that, with such intensity, that I was astonished. ⇒ 'We'll all be arrested!' Thomas yelled, which was most unlike him.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
