ugly

(ˈʌɡlɪ )
Definitions
adjective -lier or -liest1. of unpleasant or unsightly appearance2. repulsive, objectionable, or displeasing in any way ⇒ war is ugly3. ominous or menacing ⇒ an ugly situation4. bad-tempered, angry, or sullen ⇒ an ugly mood
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

uglily (ˈuglily) adverbugliness (ˈugliness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse uggligr dreadful, from ugga fear

ugly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌgli )
Word forms: uglier , ugliest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is ugly, you mean that they are very unattractive and unpleasant to look at. ⇒ ...an ugly little hat. ⇒ She makes me feel dowdy and ugly.ugliness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the raw ugliness of his native city.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you refer to an event or situation as ugly, you mean that it is very unpleasant, usually because it involves violent or aggressive behaviour. ⇒ There have been some ugly scenes. ⇒ The confrontation turned ugly. ⇒ ...an ugly publicity stunt.ugliness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the subtlety and ugliness of sexual harassment.3. → to rear its ugly head
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
