prose

(prəʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. spoken or written language as in ordinary usage, distinguished from poetry by its lack of a marked metrical structure2. a passage set for translation into a foreign language3. commonplace or dull discourse, expression, etc4. Roman Catholic Church a hymn recited or sung after the gradual at Mass5. (modifier) written in prose6. (modifier) matter-of-factverb7. to write or say (something) in prose8. (intransitive) to speak or write in a tedious style

Derived Forms

proseˌlike (ˈproseˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin phrase prōsa ōrātiō straightforward speech, from prorsus prosaic, from prōvertere to turn forwards, from pro-1 + vertere to turn
