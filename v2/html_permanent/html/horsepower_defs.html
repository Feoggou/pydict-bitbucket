horsepower

(ˈhɔːsˌpaʊə )
Definitions
noun1. an fps unit of power, equal to 550 foot-pounds per second (equivalent to 745.7 watts)2. a US standard unit of power, equal to 746 watts

horsepower

Powered by COBUILD
(hɔrspaʊər) in Automotive Engineering
Definitions
noun(Automotive engineering: Design and performance) Horsepower is a unit of power used for measuring how powerful an engine is. ⇒ This vehicle has a 300-horsepower engine. ⇒ The term horsepower describes how much power an engine can produce, and is directly related to how much speed a car can achieve. ⇒ The 3-liter engine was meant to give 300 horsepower at 7500 rpm.
