pop-up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of an appliance) characterized by or having a mechanism that pops up ⇒ a pop-up toaster2. (of a book) having pages that rise when opened to simulate a three-dimensional form3. computing (of a menu on a computer screen, etc) suddenly appearing when an option is selected4. (of a shop, restaurant, venue, or other small business) intentionally opening and closing for business within a very short span of time ⇒ a pop-up shopverb pop up5. (intr, adverb) to appear suddenly from belownoun6. computing something that appears over or above the open window on a computer screen

pop-up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]A pop-up book, usually a children's book, has pictures that stand up when you open the pages.2. adjective [ADJ n]A pop-up toaster has a mechanism that pushes slices of bread up when they are toasted.3. adjective [ADJ n]On a computer screen, a pop-up menu or advertisement is a small window containing a menu or advertisement that appears on the screen when you perform a particular operation. [computing] ⇒ ...a program for stopping pop-up ads.4. 5. adjective [ADJ n]A pop-up shop, restaurant or gallery opens only for a short time and is intended to be temporary. ⇒ There's a pop-up gallery where the café used to be.
