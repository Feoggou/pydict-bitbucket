wallow

(ˈwɒləʊ )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (esp of certain animals) to roll about in mud, water, etc, for pleasure2. to move about with difficulty3. to indulge oneself in possessions, emotion, etc ⇒ to wallow in self-pity4. (of smoke, waves, etc) to billownoun5. the act or an instance of wallowing6. a muddy place or depression where animals wallow
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wallower (ˈwallower) noun

Word Origin

Old English wealwian to roll (in mud); related to Latin volvere to turn, Greek oulos curly, Russian valun round pebble

wallow for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɒloʊ )
Word forms: wallows , wallowing , wallowed
Definitions
1. verbIf you say that someone is wallowing in an unpleasant situation, you are criticizing them for being deliberately unhappy. [disapproval] [V + in] ⇒ His tired mind continued to wallow in self-pity. [V in n] ⇒ I wanted only to wallow in my own grief.2. verbIf a person or animal wallows in water or mud, they lie or roll about in it slowly for pleasure. [V + in] ⇒ Never have I had such a good excuse for wallowing in deep warm baths. [V in n] ⇒ Dogs love splashing in mud and hippos wallow in it.
