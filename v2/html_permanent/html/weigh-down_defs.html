weigh down

Definitions
verb(adverb) to press (a person) down by or as if by weight ⇒ his troubles weighed him down

weigh down for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf something that you are wearing or carrying weighs you down, it stops you moving easily by making you heavier. [V n P] ⇒ He took off his shoes. If they had to swim, he didn't want anything weighing him down. [V P n] ⇒ These nests increase in size each year, and can eventually weigh down the branch. [V-ed P] ⇒ ...soldiers weighed down by their heavy packs.2. phrasal verbIf you are weighed down by something, it makes you extremely worried or causes you great problems. [be V-ed by/with n] ⇒ The merchant bank is being weighed down by a £1.3 billion book of bad debts. [V n P] ⇒ ...the depression that weighed him down each morning.weighed down graded adjective [v-link ADJ] ⇒ I was too weighed down by guilt to eat the sweet. →  weigh
