majesty

(ˈmædʒɪstɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. great dignity of bearing; loftiness; grandeur2. supreme power or authority3. an archaic word for royalty

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, from Latin mājestās; related to Latin major, comparative of magnus great

Majesty

(ˈmædʒɪstɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties(preceded by Your, His, Her, or Their) a title used to address or refer to a sovereign or the wife or widow of a sovereign

(mædʒɪsti )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounYou use majesty in expressions such as Your Majesty or Her Majesty when you are addressing or referring to a King or Queen. [politeness] highness ⇒ I quite agree, Your Majesty. ⇒ His Majesty requests your presence in the royal chambers. ⇒ Their Majesties celebrated our arrival by giving us each a little silver spoon.2. uncount nounMajesty is the quality of being beautiful, dignified, and impressive. ⇒ ...the majesty of the mainland mountains.
