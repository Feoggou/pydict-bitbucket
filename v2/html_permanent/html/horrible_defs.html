horrible

(ˈhɒrəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. causing horror; dreadful2. disagreeable; unpleasant3. informal cruel or unkind
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

horribleness (ˈhorribleness) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin horribilis, from horrēre to tremble

(hɒrɪbəl , US hɔːr- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something or someone as horrible, you do not like them at all. [informal] ⇒ The record sounds horrible. ⇒ ...a horrible small boy.horribly (hɒrɪbli , US hɔːr-) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ When trouble comes they behave selfishly and horribly.2. graded adjective & adjectiveYou can call something horrible when it causes you to feel great shock, fear, and disgust. ⇒ Still the horrible shrieking came out of his mouth.horribly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ A two-year-old boy was horribly murdered.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Horrible is used to emphasize how bad something is. [emphasis] ⇒ That seems like a horrible mess that will drag on for years. ⇒ Unless you respect other people's religions, horrible mistakes and conflict will occur.horribly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Our plans have gone horribly wrong. ⇒ You got horribly drunk.
