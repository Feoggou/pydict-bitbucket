cicada

(sɪˈkɑːdə ) or cicala
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -das, -dae (-diː), -las or -le (-leɪ)any large broad insect of the homopterous family Cicadidae, most common in warm regions. Cicadas have membranous wings and the males produce a high-pitched drone by vibration of a pair of drumlike abdominal organs
Word origin
C19: from Latin
