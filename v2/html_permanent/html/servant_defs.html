servant

(ˈsɜːvənt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person employed to work for another, esp one who performs household duties2. See public servant

Derived Forms

servant-ˌlike (ˈservant-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: via Old French, from servant serving, from servir to serve

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA servant is someone who is employed to work at another person's home, for example as a cleaner or a gardener.2. countable nounYou can use servant to refer to someone or something that provides a service for people or can be used by them. ⇒ Like any other public servants, police must respond to public demand. ⇒ The question is whether technology is going to be our servant or our master.3. → see also civil servant
