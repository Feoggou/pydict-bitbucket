barley1

(ˈbɑːlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of various erect annual temperate grasses of the genus Hordeum, esp H. vulgare, that have short leaves and dense bristly flower spikes and are widely cultivated for grain and forage2. the grain of any of these grasses, used in making beer and whisky and for soups, puddings, etc. See also pearl barley
Word origin
Old English bærlīc (adj); related to bere barley, Old Norse barr barley, Gothic barizeins of barley, Latin farīna flour
