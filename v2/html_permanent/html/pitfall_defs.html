pitfall

(ˈpɪtˌfɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an unsuspected difficulty or danger2. a trap in the form of a concealed pit, designed to catch men or wild animals

Word Origin

Old English pytt pit1 + fealle trap

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu pl]The pitfalls involved in a particular activity or situation are the things that may go wrong or may cause problems. [+ of] ⇒ The pitfalls of working abroad are numerous. ⇒ He also points out that forward planning can help avoid stressful pitfalls.
Example sentences containing 'pitfall'
This, of course, is the pitfall of cults and religions dominated by a guru, sage, or messiah. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODHe had not thought to warn his son of love's one pitfall : true love empowers a woman and imperils a man. Mario Puzo THE FAMILY
