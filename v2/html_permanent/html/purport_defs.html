purport

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (pɜːˈpɔːt ) (transitive)1. to claim (to be a certain thing, etc) by manner or appearance, esp falsely2. (esp of speech or writing) to signify or implynoun (ˈpɜːpɔːt )3. meaning; significance4. purpose; object; intention

Word Origin

C15: from Anglo-French: contents, from Old French porporter to convey, from por- forth + porter to carry, from Latin portāre

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that someone or something purports to do or be a particular thing, you mean that they claim to do or be that thing, although you may not always believe that claim. [formal] [V to-inf] ⇒ ...a book that purports to tell the whole truth.
