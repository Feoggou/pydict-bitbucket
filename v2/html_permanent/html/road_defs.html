road

(rəʊd )
Definitions
noun1. a. an open way, usually surfaced with asphalt or concrete, providing passage from one place to anotherb. (as modifier) ⇒ road traffic, ⇒ a road map, ⇒ a road signc. (in combination) ⇒ the roadside2. a. a streetb. (capital when part of a name) ⇒ London Road3. a. US short for railroadb. British one of the tracks of a railway4. a way, path, or course ⇒ the road to fame5. Also called: roadstead (often plural) nautical a partly sheltered anchorage6. a drift or tunnel in a mine, esp a level one7. See hit the road8. See on the road9. See take the road10. See one for the road
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

roadless (ˈroadless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English rād; related to rīdan to ride, and to Old Saxon rēda, Old Norse reith

road

Powered by COBUILD
(roʊd ) for learners of English
Word forms: roads
Definitions
1. countable noun [oft in names, oft by N]A road is a long piece of hard ground which is built between two places so that people can drive or ride easily from one place to the other. ⇒ There was very little traffic on the roads. ⇒ We just go straight up the Bristol Road. ⇒ He was coming down the road the same time as the girl was turning into the lane. ⇒ Buses carry 30 per cent of those travelling by road. ⇒ ...road accidents.2. countable noun [usu sing]The road to a particular result is the means of achieving it or the process of achieving it. [+ to] ⇒ We are bound to see some ups and downs along the road to recovery.3. →  to hit the road4. →  on the road5. →  on the road6. → the end of the road
