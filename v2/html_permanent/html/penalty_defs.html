penalty

(ˈpɛnəltɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. a legal or official punishment, such as a term of imprisonment2. some other form of punishment, such as a fine or forfeit for not fulfilling a contract3. loss, suffering, or other unfortunate result of one's own action, error, etc4. sport, games a handicap awarded against a player or team for illegal play, such as a free shot at goal by the opposing team, loss of points, etc

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin poenālitās penalty; see penal

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]A penalty is a punishment that someone is given for doing something which is against a law or rule. ⇒ One of those arrested could face the death penalty. ⇒ The maximum penalty is up to 7 years' imprisonment or an unlimited fine.2. countable nounIn sports such as football, rugby, and hockey, a penalty is an opportunity to score a goal, which is given to the attacking team if the defending team breaks a rule near their own goal. ⇒ Referee Michael Reed had no hesitation in awarding a penalty. ⇒ Jonathan Davies scored a penalty goal.3. countable nounThe penalty that you pay for something you have done is something unpleasant that you experience as a result. [+ for] ⇒ Why should I pay the penalty for somebody else's mistake? ⇒ It's a penalty of us being girls–sons have an easy time.
