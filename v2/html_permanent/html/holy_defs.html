holy

(ˈhəʊlɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: holier or holiest1. of, relating to, or associated with God or a deity; sacred2. endowed or invested with extreme purity or sublimity3. devout, godly, or virtuous4. See holier-than-thou5. See holy terrornoun plural -lies6. See the holy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hālig, hǣlig; related to Old Saxon hēlag, Gothic hailags, German heilig; see hallow

holy

Powered by COBUILD
(hoʊli ) for learners of English
Word forms: holier , holiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as holy, you mean that it is considered to be special because it is connected with God or a particular religion. ⇒ To them, as to all Christians, this is a holy place. ⇒ ...Yom Kippur, the holiest day in the Jewish calendar.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA holy person is a religious leader or someone who leads a religious life. ⇒ The Indians think of him as a holy man, a combination of doctor and priest.3. adjective [ADJ n]Holy is used in exclamations such as 'Holy cow!' and 'Holy smoke!' to express an emotion such as surprise or panic. [feelings]4. → See also holier-than-thou
