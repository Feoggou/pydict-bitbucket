short cut

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a route that is shorter than the usual one2. a means of saving time or effortverb shortcut -cuts, -cutting or -cut3. (intransitive) to use a short cut

Word Origin

C16: from cut (in the sense: a direct route)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA short cut is a quicker way of getting somewhere than the usual route. ⇒ I tried to take a short cut and got lost.2. countable nounA short cut is a method of achieving something more quickly or more easily than if you use the usual methods. [+ to] ⇒ Fame can be a shortcut to love and money. ⇒ There is no short cut from dictatorship to democracy.3. countable nounOn a computer, a shortcut is an icon on the desktop that allows you to go immediately to a program, document and so on. [computing] ⇒ ...ways to move or copy icons or create shortcuts in Windows.4. countable nounOn a computer, a shortcut is a keystroke or a combination of keystrokes that allows you to give commands without using the mouse. [computing] ⇒ ...a handy keyboard shortcut that takes you to the top of the screen.
