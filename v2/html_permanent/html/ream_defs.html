ream1

(riːm )
Definitions
noun1. a number of sheets of paper, formerly 480 sheets (short ream), now 500 sheets (long ream) or 516 sheets (printer's ream or perfect ream). One ream is equal to 20 quires2. (often plural) informal a large quantity, esp of written matter ⇒ he wrote reams
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French raime, from Spanish rezma, from Arabic rizmah bale

ream2

(riːm )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to enlarge (a hole) by use of a reamer2. US to extract (juice) from (a citrus fruit) using a reamer
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: perhaps from C14 remen to open up, from Old English rӯman to widen
