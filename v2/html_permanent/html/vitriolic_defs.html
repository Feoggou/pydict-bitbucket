vitriolic

(ˌvɪtrɪˈɒlɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. (of a substance, esp a strong acid) highly corrosive2. severely bitter or caustic; virulent ⇒ vitriolic criticism
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

vitriolic

Powered by COBUILD
(vɪtriɒlɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone's language or behaviour as vitriolic, you disapprove of it because it is full of bitterness and hate, and so causes a lot of distress and pain. [disapproval] ⇒ There was a vicious and vitriolic attack on him in one of the Sunday newspapers two weeks ago.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
