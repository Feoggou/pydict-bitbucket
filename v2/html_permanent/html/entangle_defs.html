entangle

(ɪnˈtæŋɡəl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to catch or involve in or as if in a tangle; ensnare or enmesh2. to make tangled or twisted; snarl3. to make complicated; confuse4. to involve in difficulties; entrap

entangler (enˈtangler) noun

entangle

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntæŋgəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: entangles , entangling , entangled
Definitions
1. verbIf one thing entangles itself with another, the two things become caught together very tightly. [V n + with/in] ⇒ The blade of the oar had entangled itself with something in the water. [Also V n]2. verbIf something entangles you in problems or difficulties, it causes you to become involved in problems or difficulties from which it is hard to escape. [V n] ⇒ Bureaucracy can entangle ventures for months. [V n + in/with] ⇒ His tactics were to entangle the opposition in a web of parliamentary procedure.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
