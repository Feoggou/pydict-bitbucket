trundle

(ˈtrʌndəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to move heavily on or as if on wheels ⇒ the bus trundled by2. (transitive) archaic to rotate or spinnoun3. the act or an instance of trundling4. a small wheel or roller5. a. the pinion of a lanternb. any of the bars in a lantern pinion6. a small truck with low wheels

Word Origin

Old English tryndel; related to Middle High German trendel disc

(trʌndəl )
Word forms: trundles , trundling , trundled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf a vehicle trundles somewhere, it moves there slowly, often with difficulty or an irregular movement. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The truck was trundling along the escarpment of the Zambesi valley. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A few horse-drawn carts still trundle through the dilapidated mining villages. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The train eventually trundled in at 7.54.2. verbIf you trundle something somewhere, especially a small, heavy object with wheels, you move or roll it along slowly. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ The old man lifted the barrow and trundled it away. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ They trundled his mowers outside and dumped them.3. verbIf you say that someone is trundling somewhere, you mean that they are walking slowly, often in a tired way or with heavy steps. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Girls trundle in carrying heavy book bags.
