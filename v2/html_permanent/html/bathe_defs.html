bathe

(beɪð )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to swim or paddle in a body of open water or a river, esp for pleasure2. (transitive) to apply liquid to (skin, a wound, etc) in order to cleanse or soothe3. to immerse or be immersed in a liquid ⇒ to bathe machine parts in oil4. mainly US and Canadian to wash in a bath5. (tr; often passive) to suffuse ⇒ her face was bathed with radiance6. (transitive) (of water, the sea, etc) to lap; wash ⇒ waves bathed the shorenoun7. British a swim or paddle in a body of open water or a river
Derived forms
bather (ˈbather) noun
Word origin
Old English bathian; related to Old Norse batha, Old High German badōn

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you bathe in a sea, river, or lake, you swim, play, or wash yourself in it. Birds and animals can also bathe. [mainly British, formal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ The police have warned the city's inhabitants not to bathe in the polluted river. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...small ponds for the birds to bathe in. [Also V]Bathe is also a noun. ⇒ Fifty soldiers were taking an early morning bathe in a nearby lake.bathing uncount noun ⇒ Nude bathing is not allowed. ⇒ ...Britain's 440 designated bathing beaches.2. verbWhen you bathe, you have a bath. [US, also British, formal] [V] ⇒ At least 60% of us now bathe or shower once a day.3. verbIf you bathe someone, especially a child, you wash them in a bath. [US, also British, formal] [V n] ⇒ Back home, Shirley plays with, feeds and bathes the baby.4. verbIf you bathe a part of your body or a wound, you wash it gently or soak it in a liquid. [V n] ⇒ Bathe the infected area in a salt solution. [V n] ⇒ She paused long enough to bathe her blistered feet.5. verbIf a place is bathed in light, it is covered with light, especially a gentle, pleasant light. [be V-ed + in] ⇒ The arena was bathed in warm sunshine. [V-ed] ⇒ I was led to a small room bathed in soft red light. [V n + in] ⇒ The lamp behind him seems to bathe him in warmth. [Also V n]6. → see also sunbathe
