exult

(ɪɡˈzʌlt )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to be joyful or jubilant, esp because of triumph or success; rejoice2. (often foll by over) to triumph (over); show or take delight in the defeat or discomfiture (of)See note at exalt
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exultation (ˌɛɡzʌlˈteɪʃən ) nounexultingly (exˈultingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin exsultāre to jump or leap for joy, from saltāre to leap
