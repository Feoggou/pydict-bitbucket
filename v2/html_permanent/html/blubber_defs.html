blubber

(ˈblʌbə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to sob without restraint2. to utter while sobbing3. (transitive) to make (the face) wet and swollen or disfigured by cryingnoun4. a thick insulating layer of fatty tissue below the skin of aquatic mammals such as the whale: used by man as a source of oil5. informal excessive and flabby body fat6. the act or an instance of weeping without restraint7. Australian an informal name for jellyfishadjective8. (often in combination) swollen or fleshy ⇒ blubber-faced, ⇒ blubber-lips
Derived forms
blubberer (ˈblubberer) noun
Word origin
C12: perhaps from Low German blubbern to bubble, of imitative origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounBlubber is the fat of whales, seals, and similar sea animals. ⇒ The baby whale develops a thick layer of blubber to protect it from the cold sea.2. verbIf someone blubbers, they cry noisily and in an unattractive way. [informal] [V] ⇒ She started to blubber like a child.
