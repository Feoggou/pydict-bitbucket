malaria

(məˈlɛərɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounan infectious disease characterized by recurring attacks of chills and fever, caused by the bite of an anopheles mosquito infected with any of four protozoans of the genus Plasmodium (P. vivax, P. falciparum, P. malariae, or P. ovale)

Derived Forms

malarial (maˈlarial), malarian (maˈlarian) or malarious (maˈlarious) adjective

Word Origin

C18: from Italian mala aria bad air, from the belief that the disease was caused by the unwholesome air in swampy districts
