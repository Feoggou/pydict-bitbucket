inset

Definitions
verb (ɪnˈsɛt ) -sets, -setting or -set1. (transitive) to set or place in or within; insertnoun (ˈɪnˌsɛt )2. something inserted3. printinga. a small map or diagram set within the borders of a larger oneb. another name for insert (sense 4)4. a piece of fabric inserted into a garment, as to shape it or for decoration5. a flowing in, as of the tide

Definitions
1. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Something that is inset with a decoration or piece of material has the decoration or material set inside it. [+ with] ⇒ ...a gold pendant, inset with a diamond. ⇒ ...the glass bricks inset high along the corridor's opposite wall.2. countable nounAn inset is a small picture, diagram, or map that is inside a larger one. ⇒ I frequently paint between 10 and 20 insets for my murals.

6. Inset Day (British). A day when school teachers in England, Wales and Northern Ireland carry out administration duties or undertake training. Pupils are not required to attend school on these days. It was formerly referred to as a Baker Day, named after Kenneth Baker, Secretary of State for Education, who introduced the concept in 1988.
