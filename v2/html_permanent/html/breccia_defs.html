breccia

(ˈbrɛtʃɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna rock consisting of angular fragments embedded in a finer matrix, formed by erosion, impact, volcanic activity, etc
Derived forms
brecciˌated (ˈbrecciˌated) adjective
Word origin
C18: from Italian, from Old High German brecha a fragment; see breach
Example sentences containing 'breccia'
Is it all this broken-up breccia or is there, maybe, a big old finger of basalt sticking up that we could scramble right on down? Tony Hillerman THE FALLEN MAN
