rampant

(ˈræmpənt )
Definitions
adjective1. unrestrained or violent in behaviour, desire, opinions, etc2. growing or developing unchecked3. (postpositive) heraldry (of a beast) standing on the hind legs, the right foreleg raised above the left4. (of an arch) having one abutment higher than the other
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rampancy (ˈrampancy) nounrampantly (ˈrampantly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French ramper to crawl, rear; see ramp

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something bad, such as a crime or disease, as rampant, you mean that it is very common and is increasing in an uncontrolled way. ⇒ Inflation is rampant and industry in decline. ⇒ ...the rampant corruption of the administration.
