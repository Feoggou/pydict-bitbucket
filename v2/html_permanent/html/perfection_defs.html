perfection

(pəˈfɛkʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of perfecting or the state or quality of being perfect2. the highest degree of a quality, etc ⇒ the perfection of faithfulness3. an embodiment of perfection

Word Origin

C13: from Latin perfectiō a completing, from perficere to finish

perfection

Powered by COBUILD
(pəʳfekʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounPerfection is the quality of being as good as it is possible for something of a particular kind to be. ⇒ His quest for perfection is relentless. ⇒ Physical perfection in a human being is exceedingly rare.2. uncount nounIf you say that something is perfection, you mean that you think it is as good as it could possibly be. ⇒ The house and garden were perfection.3. uncount nounThe perfection of something such as a skill, system, or product involves making it as good as it could possibly be. [+ of] ⇒ Madame Clicquot is credited with the perfection of this technique.4. →  to perfection
