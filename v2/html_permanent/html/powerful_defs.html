(ˈpaʊəfʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having great power, force, potency, or effect2. extremely effective or efficient in action ⇒ a powerful drug, ⇒ a powerful lens3. dialect large or great ⇒ a powerful amount of troubleadverb4. dialect extremely; very ⇒ he ran powerful fast

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA powerful person or organization is able to control or influence people and events. ⇒ You're a powerful man–people will listen to you. ⇒ ...Russia and India, two large, powerful countries. ⇒ ...Hong Kong's powerful business community.2. → see also all-powerful3. graded adjective & adjectiveYou say that someone's body is powerful when it is physically strong. ⇒ Hans flexed his powerful muscles. ⇒ It's such a big powerful dog.powerfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He is described as a strong, powerfully-built man of 60. ⇒ You can contract your muscles more powerfully by linking up your breathing to the exercise.4. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A powerful machine or substance is effective because it is very strong. ⇒ The more powerful the car the more difficult it is to handle. ⇒ ...powerful computer systems. ⇒ Alcohol is also a powerful and fast-acting drug.powerfully adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ Crack is a much cheaper, smokable form of cocaine which is powerfully addictive.5. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A powerful smell is very strong. ⇒ There was a powerful smell of stale beer. ⇒ ...tiny creamy flowers with a powerful scent.powerfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ The railway station smelt powerfully of cats and drains.6. graded adjective & adjectiveA powerful voice is loud and can be heard from a long way away. ⇒ At that moment Mrs. Jones's powerful voice interrupted them, announcing a visitor.7. graded adjective & adjectiveYou describe a piece of writing, speech, or work of art as powerful when it has a strong effect on people's feelings or beliefs. ⇒ ...Bleasdale's powerful 11-part drama about a corrupt city leader. ⇒ ...one of the world's most powerful and moving operas, Verdi's 'Otello'. ⇒ ...a powerful new style of dance-theatre.powerfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ It's a play–painful, funny and powerfully acted.
