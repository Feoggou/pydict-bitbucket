oligarchy

(ˈɒlɪˌɡɑːkɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -chies1. government by a small group of people2. a state or organization so governed3. a small body of individuals ruling such a state4. mainly US a small clique of private citizens who exert a strong influence on government
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

oligarchic (ˌoliˈgarchic), oligarchical (ˌoliˈgarchical) or oligarchal (ˌoliˈgarchal) adjectiveoligarchically (ˌoliˈgarchically) adverb

Word Origin

C16: via Medieval Latin from Greek oligarkhia, from olígos few + -archy

Definitions
1. countable nounAn oligarchy is a small group of people who control and run a particular country or organization. You can also refer to a country which is governed in this way as an oligarchy. ⇒ Athens was suffering under the rule of an oligarchy that had no concern for the people's welfare.2. uncount nounOligarchy is a situation in which a country or organization is run by an oligarchy. ⇒ ...a protest against imperialism and oligarchy in the region.
