non sequitur

(ˈnɒn ˈsɛkwɪtə )
Definitions
noun1. a statement having little or no relevance to what preceded it2. logic a conclusion that does not follow from the premises
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Latin, literally: it does not follow

non sequitur for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒn sekwɪtəʳ )
Word forms: non sequiturs
Definitions
variable nounA non sequitur is a statement, remark, or conclusion that does not follow naturally or logically from what has just been said. [formal] ⇒ Had she missed something important, or was this just a non sequitur?
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
