criticize

or criticise (ˈkrɪtɪˌsaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to judge (something) with disapproval; censure2. to evaluate or analyse (something)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use criticiseverbIf you criticize someone or something, you express your disapproval of them by saying what you think is wrong with them. [V n] ⇒ His mother had rarely criticized him or any of her children. [V n + for] ⇒ The minister criticised the police for failing to come up with any leads. [V n for n/-ing] ⇒ The regime has been harshly criticized for serious human rights violations.
