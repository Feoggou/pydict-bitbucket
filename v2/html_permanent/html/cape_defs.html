cape1

(keɪp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a sleeveless garment like a cloak but usually shorter2. a strip of material attached to a coat or other garment so as to fall freely, usually from the shoulders
Word origin
C16: from French, from Provençal capa, from Late Latin cappa; see cap

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA cape is a large piece of land that sticks out into the sea from the coast. ⇒ In 1978, Naomi James became the first woman to sail solo around the world via Cape Horn.2. countable nounA cape is a short cloak. ⇒ ...a woollen cape.
