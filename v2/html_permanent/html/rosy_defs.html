rosy

(ˈrəʊzɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: rosier or rosiest1. of the colour rose or pink2. having a healthy pink complexion ⇒ rosy cheeks3. optimistic, esp excessively so ⇒ a rosy view of social improvements4. full of health, happiness, or joy ⇒ rosy slumbers5. resembling, consisting of, or abounding in roses

rosy

Powered by COBUILD
(roʊzi ) for learners of English
Word forms: rosier , rosiest
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf you say that someone has a rosy face, you mean that they have pink cheeks and look very healthy. ⇒ Bethan's round, rosy face seemed hardly to have aged at all. ⇒ She had bright, rosy cheeks.2. adjectiveSomething that is rosy is reddish-pink in colour. ⇒ ...the rosy brick buildings.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that a situation looks rosy or that the picture looks rosy, you mean that the situation seems likely to be good or successful. ⇒ Little over a year ago, things looked very rosy for the Social Democrats. ⇒ The job prospects for those graduating in engineering are far less rosy now than they used to be. ⇒ Is the picture really so rosy?
