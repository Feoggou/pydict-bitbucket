Cameroon

(ˌkæməˈruːn , ˈkæməˌruːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a republic in West Africa, on the Gulf of Guinea: became a German colony in 1884; divided in 1919 into the Cameroons (administered by Britain) and Cameroun (administered by France); Cameroun and the S part of the Cameroons formed a republic in 1961 (the N part joined Nigeria); became a member of the Commonwealth in 1995. Official languages: French and English. Religions: Christian, Muslim, and animist. Currency: franc. Capital: Yaoundé. Pop: 20 549 221 (2013 est). Area: 475 500 sq km (183 591 sq miles). French name: Cameroun. German name: Kamerun2. an active volcano in W Cameroon: the highest peak on the West African coast. Height: 4070 m (13 352 ft)
