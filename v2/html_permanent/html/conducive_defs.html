Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If one thing is conducive to another thing, it makes the other thing likely to happen. ⇒ Make your bedroom as conducive to sleep as possible. [+ to] ⇒ Sometimes the home environment just isn't conducive to reading.
Example sentences containing 'conducive'
The room was like its owner: correct, tasteful, and slightly conducive to illness at ease. Delman, David DEATH OF A NYMPHNo, we've got a tutorial arranged, and the atmosphere in here is hardly conducive to rational dialogue. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEADThis was comfortable on a windy day but it was not conducive to meeting eligible men. Martin, Joy THE IMAGE OF LAURAOf course it was a narrow view; he'd never found a broad perspective conducive to his sense of right and wrong. Armstrong, Campbell MAMBO
