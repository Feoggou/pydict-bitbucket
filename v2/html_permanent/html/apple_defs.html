apple

(ˈæpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a rosaceous tree, Malus sieversii, native to Central Asia but widely cultivated in temperate regions in many varieties, having pink or white fragrant flowers and firm rounded edible fruits. See also crab apple2. the fruit of this tree, having red, yellow, or green skin and crisp whitish flesh3. the wood of this tree4. any of several unrelated trees that have fruits similar to the apple, such as the custard apple, sugar apple, and May apple. See also love apple, oak apple, thorn apple5. See apple of one's eye6. See bad apple
Word origin
Old English æppel; related to Old Saxon appel, Old Norse apall, Old High German apful
