cancellation

or US cancelation (ˌkænsɪˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the fact or an instance of cancelling2. something that has been cancelled, such as a theatre ticket, esp when it is available for another person to take ⇒ we have a cancellation in the stalls3. the marks or perforation made by cancelling
Example sentences containing 'cancellation'
Only a cancellation could have got us this suite at such short notice at this time of year. Mosco, Maisie OUT OF THE ASHESMelstead had argued against cancellation , but was an outsider. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENOr perhaps you erase or fade a heavy cancellation - what we call a `killer blob '. Tapply, William G THE DUTCH BLUE ERRORBig seas have caused the cancellation of all fixed-wing flying. Patrick Robinson NIMITZ CLASS
