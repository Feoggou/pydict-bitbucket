unnatural

(ʌnˈnætʃərəl , -ˈnætʃrəl)
Definitions
adjective1. contrary to nature; abnormal2. not in accordance with accepted standards of behaviour or right and wrong ⇒ unnatural love3. uncanny; supernatural ⇒ unnatural phenomena4. affected or forced ⇒ an unnatural manner5. inhuman or monstrous; wicked ⇒ an unnatural crime6. obsolete illegitimate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unnatural for learners of English

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as unnatural, you mean that it is strange and often frightening, because it is different from what you normally expect. ⇒ The aircraft rose with unnatural speed on take-off. ⇒ The altered landscape looks unnatural and weird.unnaturally graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ The house was unnaturally silent. ⇒ ...unnaturally cold conditions.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Behaviour that is unnatural seems artificial and not normal or genuine. ⇒ She gave him a bright, determined smile which seemed unnatural.unnaturally graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Try to avoid shouting or speaking unnaturally.
