lay on

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. to provide or supply ⇒ to lay on entertainment2. British to install ⇒ to lay on electricity3. See lay it on

lay on

Powered by COBUILD

Definitions
phrasal verbIf you lay on something such as food, entertainment, or a service, you provide or supply it, especially in a generous or grand way. [mainly British] [V P n] ⇒ They laid on a superb evening. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Every facility was laid on to ease their homecoming. [Also V n P] →  lay
