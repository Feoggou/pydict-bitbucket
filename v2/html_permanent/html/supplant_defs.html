supplant

(səˈplɑːnt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to take the place of, often by trickery or force ⇒ he easily supplanted his rival

Derived Forms

supplantation (ˌsʌplɑːnˈteɪʃən ) nounsupplanter (supˈplanter) noun

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin supplantāre to trip up, from sub- from below + planta sole of the foot

verbIf a person or thing is supplanted, another person or thing takes their place. [formal] [be V-ed] ⇒ He may be supplanted by a younger man. [V n] ⇒ By the 1930s the wristwatch had almost completely supplanted the pocket watch.
