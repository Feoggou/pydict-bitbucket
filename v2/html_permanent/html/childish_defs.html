childish

(ˈtʃaɪldɪʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. in the manner of, belonging to, or suitable to a child2. foolish or petty; puerile ⇒ childish fears
Derived forms
childishly (ˈchildishly) adverbchildishness (ˈchildishness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Childish means relating to or typical of a child. ⇒ ...childish enthusiasm.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone, especially an adult, as childish, you disapprove of them because they behave in an immature way. [disapproval] ⇒ ...Penny's selfish and childish behaviour. ⇒ Don't be so childish.childishly graded adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ He hit back angrily, saying such remarks were childishly simplistic. ⇒ He knew that he had behaved childishly.childishness uncount noun ⇒ ...regressing into childishness.
