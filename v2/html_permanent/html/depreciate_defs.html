depreciate

(dɪˈpriːʃɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to reduce or decline in value or price2. (transitive) to lessen the value of by derision, criticism, etc; disparageAvoid confusion with deprecate
Derived forms
depreciˌatingly (deˈpreciˌatingly) adverbdepreciˌator (deˈpreciˌator) noundepreciatory (dɪˈpriːʃɪətərɪ , -trɪ) or depreciative (deˈpreciative) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin dēpretiāre to lower the price of, from Latin de- + pretium price
