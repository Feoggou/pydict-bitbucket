sunken

(ˈsʌŋkən )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. a past participle of sinkadjective2. unhealthily hollow ⇒ sunken cheeks3. situated at a lower level than the surrounding or usual one4. situated under water; submerged5. depressed; low ⇒ sunken spirits

(sʌŋkən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]Sunken ships have sunk to the bottom of a sea, ocean, or lake. ⇒ The sunken sailing-boat was a glimmer of white on the bottom. ⇒ Try diving for sunken treasure.2. adjective [ADJ n]Sunken gardens, roads, or other features are below the level of their surrounding area. ⇒ Steps lead down to the sunken garden. ⇒ The room was dominated by a sunken bath.3. adjectiveSunken eyes, cheeks, or other parts of the body curve inwards and make you look thin and unwell. ⇒ Her eyes were sunken and black-ringed. ⇒ ...an elderly man with sunken cheeks.
