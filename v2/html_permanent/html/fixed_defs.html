fixed

(fɪkst )
Definitions
adjective1. attached or placed so as to be immovable2. not subject to change; stable ⇒ fixed prices3. steadily directed ⇒ a fixed expression4. established as to relative position ⇒ a fixed point5. not fluctuating; always at the same time ⇒ a fixed holiday6. (of ideas, notions, etc) firmly maintained7. (of an element) held in chemical combination ⇒ fixed nitrogen8. (of a substance) nonvolatile9. arranged10. astrology of, relating to, or belonging to the group consisting of the four signs of the zodiac Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, and Aquarius, which are associated with stability. Compare cardinal (sense 9), mutable (sense 2)11. informal equipped or provided for, as with money, possessions, etc12. informal illegally arranged ⇒ a fixed trial

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪkst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use fixed to describe something which stays the same and does not or cannot vary. ⇒ They issue a fixed number of shares that trade publicly. ⇒ ...a world without fixed laws. ⇒ Tickets will be printed with fixed entry times. ⇒ Many restaurants offer fixed-price menus.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you say that someone has fixed ideas or opinions, you mean that they do not often change their ideas and opinions, although perhaps they should. ⇒ ...people who have fixed ideas about things.3. adjectiveIf someone has a fixed smile on their face, they are smiling even though they do not feel happy or pleased. ⇒ I had to go through the rest of the evening with a fixed smile on my face.4. →  no fixed abode5. → See also fix
