Nicaragua

(ˌnɪkəˈræɡjʊə , -ɡwə, Spanish nikaˈraɣwa)
Definitions
noun1. a republic in Central America, on the Caribbean and the Pacific: colonized by the Spanish from the 1520s; gained independence in 1821 and was annexed by Mexico, becoming a republic in 1838. Official language: Spanish. Religion: Roman Catholic majority. Currency: córdoba. Capital: Managua. Pop: 5 788 531 (2013 est). Area: 131 812 sq km (50 893 sq miles)2. See Lake Nicaragua
