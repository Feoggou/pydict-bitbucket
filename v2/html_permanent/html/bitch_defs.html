bitch

(bɪtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a female dog or other female canine animal, such as a wolf2. offensive, slang a malicious, spiteful, or coarse woman3. slang a complaint4. slang a difficult situation or problem5. slang a person who acts as a subordinate or slave to another personverb informal6. (intransitive) to complain; grumble7. to behave (towards) in a spiteful or malicious manner8. (transitive; often foll by up) to botch; bungle
Word origin
Old English bicce

bitch

Powered by COBUILD
(bɪtʃ )
Word forms: bitches , bitching , bitched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf someone calls a woman a bitch, they are saying in a very rude way that they think she behaves in a very unpleasant way. [informal, offensive, disapproval]2. → see also son of a bitch3. singular nounIf you describe a situation as a bitch, you mean that it is very unpleasant or difficult to deal with. [informal, rude]4. verb [oft cont]If you say that someone is bitching about something, you mean that you disapprove of the fact that they are complaining about it in an unpleasant way. [informal, disapproval] [V + about] ⇒ They're forever bitching about everybody else. [Also V]5. countable nounA bitch is a female dog.
