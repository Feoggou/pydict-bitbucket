definitive

(dɪˈfɪnɪtɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. serving to decide or settle finally; conclusive2. most reliable, complete, or authoritative ⇒ the definitive reading of a text3. serving to define or outline4. zoology fully developed; complete ⇒ the definitive form of a parasite5. a. (of postage stamps) permanently on saleb. (as noun) a definitive postage stampnoun6. grammar a word indicating specificity of reference, such as the definite article or a demonstrative adjective or pronoun
Derived forms
definitively (deˈfinitively) adverbdefinitiveness (deˈfinitiveness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is definitive provides a firm conclusion that cannot be questioned. ⇒ No one has come up with a definitive answer as to why this should be so. ⇒ There is no definitive test as yet for the condition.definitively adverb ⇒ The Constitution did not definitively rule out divorce.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A definitive book or performance is thought to be the best of its kind that has ever been done or that will ever be done. ⇒ His 'An Orkney Tapestry' is still the definitive book on the islands.
