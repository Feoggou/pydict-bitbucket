Definitions
noun(Accounting: Commerce) A lessee is a person who is paying to lease an asset such as a car or building. ⇒ The lessee must make a series of fixed payments and, if they fail to do so, the lessor can repossess the asset. ⇒ In a net lease, the lessee agrees to maintain the asset, insure it, and pay any property taxes. ⇒ A lessee is a person who is paying to lease an asset such as a car or building.Word builder-eeThe suffix -ee is sometimes used to change a verb into a noun meaning 'the person the thing is done to'.
