soot

(sʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. finely divided carbon deposited from flames during the incomplete combustion of organic substances such as coalverb2. (transitive) to cover with soot

Word Origin

Old English sōt; related to Old Norse, Middle Low German sōt, Lithuanian sódis, Old Slavonic sažda, Old Irish sūide
