drama

(ˈdrɑːmə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a work to be performed by actors on stage, radio, or television; play2. the genre of literature represented by works intended for the stage3. the art of the writing and production of plays4. a situation or sequence of events that is highly emotional, tragic, or turbulent
Word origin
C17: from Late Latin: a play, from Greek: something performed, from drān to do

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA drama is a serious play for the theatre, television, or radio. ⇒ He acted in radio dramas.2. uncount nounYou use drama to refer to plays in general or to work that is connected with plays and the theatre, such as acting or producing. ⇒ He knew nothing of Greek drama. ⇒ She met him when she was at drama school.3. variable nounYou can refer to a real situation which is exciting or distressing as drama. ⇒ There was none of the drama and relief of a hostage release. ⇒ For all its drama, the event was not unexpected.
