cleft

(klɛft )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. the past tense and a past participle of cleave1noun2. a fissure or crevice3. an indentation or split in something, such as the chin, palate, etcadjective4. split; divided5. (of leaves) having one or more incisions reaching nearly to the midrib
Word origin
Old English geclyft (n); related to Old High German kluft tongs, German Kluft gap, fissure; see cleave1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA cleft in a rock or in the ground is a narrow opening in it. [+ in] ⇒ ...a narrow cleft in the rocks too small for humans to enter.2. countable nounA cleft in someone's chin is a line down the middle of it.3. adjective [ADJ n]If someone has a cleft chin, they have a cleft in their chin.4. →  in a cleft stick5. Cleft is a past participle of cleave.
