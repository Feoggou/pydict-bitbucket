twinge

(twɪndʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a sudden brief darting or stabbing pain2. a sharp emotional pang ⇒ a twinge of guiltverb3. to have or cause to have a twinge4. (transitive) obsolete to pinch; tweak

Word Origin

Old English twengan to pinch; related to Old High German zwengen

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA twinge is a sudden sharp feeling or emotion, usually an unpleasant one. ⇒ I would have twinges of guilt occasionally. [+ of] ⇒ For a moment, Arnold felt a twinge of sympathy for Mr Wilson.2. countable nounA twinge is a sudden sharp pain. [+ in] ⇒ He felt a slight twinge in his damaged hamstring. ⇒ ...the occasional twinge of indigestion.
