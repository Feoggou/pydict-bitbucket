(ɡəʊʃ )
Definitions
adjectivelacking ease of manner; tactless
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gauchely (ˈgauchely) adverbgaucheness (ˈgaucheness) noun

Word Origin

C18: French: awkward, left, from Old French gauchir to swerve, ultimately of Germanic origin; related to Old High German wankōn to stagger

(goʊʃ ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as gauche, you mean that they are awkward and uncomfortable in the company of other people. ⇒ We're all a bit gauche when we're young. ⇒ She was a rather gauche, provincial creature.
