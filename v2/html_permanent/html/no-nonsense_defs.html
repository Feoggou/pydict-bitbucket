no-nonsense

(ˌnəʊˈnɒnsəns )
Definitions
adjectivesensible, practical, straightforward; without nonsense of any kind ⇒ a businesslike no-nonsense approach, ⇒ a severe no-nonsense look
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

no-nonsense for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as a no-nonsense person, you approve of the fact that they are efficient, direct, and quite tough. [approval] ⇒ She saw herself as a direct, no-nonsense modern woman.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as a no-nonsense thing, you approve of the fact that it is plain and does not have unnecessary parts. [approval] ⇒ You'll need no-nonsense boots for the jungle.
