bombast

(ˈbɒmbæst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. pompous and grandiloquent language2. obsolete material used for padding
Derived forms
bombastic (bomˈbastic) adjectivebombastically (bomˈbastically) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Old French bombace, from Medieval Latin bombāx cotton; see bombacaceous

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounBombast is trying to impress people by saying things that sound impressive but have little meaning. [disapproval] ⇒ There was no bombast or conceit in his speech.
