yawl1

(jɔːl )
Definitions
noun1. a two-masted sailing vessel, rigged fore-and-aft, with a large mainmast and a small mizzenmast stepped aft of the rudderpost. Compare ketch, sloop2. a ship's small boat, usually rowed by four or six oars
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Dutch jol or Middle Low German jolle, of unknown origin

yawl2

Definitions
