adulterate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (əˈdʌltəˌreɪt )1. (transitive) to debase by adding inferior material ⇒ to adulterate milk with wateradjective (əˈdʌltərɪt , -ˌreɪt)2. adulterated; debased or impure3. a less common word for adulterous
Derived forms
aˌdulteration (aˌdulterˈation) nounadulterˌator (aˈdulterˌator) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin adulterāre to corrupt, commit adultery, probably from alter another, hence to approach another, commit adultery

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verb [usu passive]If something such as food or drink is adulterated, someone has made its quality worse by adding water or cheaper products to it. [be V-ed] ⇒ The food had been adulterated to increase its weight. [V-ed] ⇒ There is a regulation against adulterated cosmetics.adulteration (ədʌltəreɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the adulteration of tobacco.
