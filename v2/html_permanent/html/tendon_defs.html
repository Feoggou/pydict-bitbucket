tendon

(ˈtɛndən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna cord or band of white inelastic collagenous tissue that attaches a muscle to a bone or some other part; sinew

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin tendō, from Latin tendere to stretch; related to Greek tenōn sinew

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA tendon is a strong cord in a person's or animal's body which joins a muscle to a bone.2. → see also Achilles tendon
