scourge

(skɜːdʒ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who harasses, punishes, or causes destruction2. a means of inflicting punishment or suffering3. a whip used for inflicting punishment or tortureverb (transitive)4. to whip; flog5. to punish severely

Derived Forms

scourger (ˈscourger) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Anglo-French escorge, from Old French escorgier (unattested) to lash, from es- ex-1 + Latin corrigia whip

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA scourge is something that causes a lot of trouble or suffering to a group of people. [+ of] ⇒ ...the best chance in 20 years to end the scourge of terrorism. ⇒ Drugs are a scourge that is devastating our society.2. verbIf something scourges a place or group of people, it causes great pain and suffering to people. [V n] ⇒ Economic anarchy scourged the post-war world.
