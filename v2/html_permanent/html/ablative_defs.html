ablative

(ˈæblətɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. grammar (in certain inflected languages such as Latin) denoting a case of nouns, pronouns, and adjectives indicating the agent in passive sentences or the instrument, manner, or place of the action described by the verb2. taking away or removing ⇒ ablative surgery3. able to disintegrate or be worn away at a very high temperature ⇒ a thick layer of ablative materialnoun4. grammara. the ablative caseb. a word or speech element in the ablative case
