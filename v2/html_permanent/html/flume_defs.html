flume

(fluːm )
Definitions
noun1. a ravine through which a stream flows2. a narrow artificial channel made for providing water for power, floating logs, etc3. a slide in the form of a long and winding tube with a stream of water running through it that descends into a purpose-built poolverb4. (transitive) to transport (logs) in a flume
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C12: from Old French flum, ultimately from Latin flūmen stream, from fluere to flow
Example sentences containing 'flume'
For a moment they could see it clinging to the flume like a woodpecker clinging to a tree trunk. Kerr, Katharine A TIME OF WAR
