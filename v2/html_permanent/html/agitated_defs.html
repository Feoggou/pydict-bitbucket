(ædʒɪteɪtɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is agitated, they are very worried or upset, and show this in their behaviour, movements, or voice. ⇒ Susan seemed agitated about something. ⇒ The man in the house was in a very excited and agitated state.
Example sentences containing 'agitated'
As the potential implications of Piercey's words forced their way into his consciousness Dayton became agitated. Harcourt, Palma A MATTER OF CONSCIENCEHe knew his position was weakening with every wring of Michelle's agitated hands. Dobbs, Michael WALL GAMESHe paced up and down, swinging his viewfinder in agitated fashion. Forbes, Bryan A SONG AT TWILIGHTTo Willi's surprise, a small knot of clearly agitated people were gathered at the steps leading into the Monterey. Collins, Larry BLACK EAGLES
