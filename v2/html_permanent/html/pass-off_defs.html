pass off

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to be or cause to be accepted or circulated in a false character or identity ⇒ he passed the fake diamonds off as real2. (intransitive) to come to a gradual end; disappear ⇒ eventually the pain passed off3. to emit (a substance) as a gas or vapour, or (of a substance) to be emitted in this way4. (intransitive) to take place ⇒ the meeting passed off without disturbance5. (transitive) to set aside or disregard ⇒ I managed to pass off his insult
