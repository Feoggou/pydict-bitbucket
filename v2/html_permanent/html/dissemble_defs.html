dissemble

(dɪˈsɛmbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to conceal (one's real motives, emotions, etc) by pretence2. (transitive) to pretend; simulate3. obsolete to ignore
Derived forms
dissemblance (disˈsemblance) noundissembler (disˈsembler) noundissembling (disˈsembling) noun, adjectivedissemblingly (disˈsemblingly) adverb
Word origin
C15: from earlier dissimulen, from Latin dissimulāre; probably influenced by obsolete semble to resemble
