detachment

(dɪˈtætʃmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. indifference to other people or to one's surroundings; aloofness2. freedom from self-interest or bias; disinterest3. the act of disengaging or separating something4. the condition of being disengaged or separated; disconnection5. militarya. the separation of a small unit from its main body, esp of ships or troopsb. the unit so detached6. Canadian a branch office of a police force7. logic the rule whereby the consequent of a true conditional statement, given the truth of its antecedent, may be asserted on its own. See also modus ponens

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounDetachment is the feeling that you have of not being personally involved in something or of having no emotional interest in it. ⇒ ...a doctor's professional detachment. ⇒ Ridley viewed his work with a cynical detachment.2. countable nounA detachment is a group of soldiers who are sent away from the main group to do a special job. ⇒ ...a detachment of marines.
