disgrace

(dɪsˈɡreɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a condition of shame, loss of reputation, or dishonour2. a shameful person, thing, or state of affairs3. exclusion from confidence or trust ⇒ he is in disgrace with his fatherverb (transitive)4. to bring shame upon; be a discredit to5. to treat or cause to be treated with disfavour
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft in N]If you say that someone is in disgrace, you are emphasizing that other people disapprove of them and do not respect them because of something that they have done. [emphasis] dishonour ⇒ His vice president also had to resign in disgrace. ⇒ She has brought disgrace upon womankind.2. singular nounIf you say that something is a disgrace, you are emphasizing that it is very bad or wrong, and that you find it completely unacceptable. [emphasis] ⇒ The way the sales were handled was a complete disgrace. ⇒ The national airline is a disgrace.3. singular nounYou say that someone is a disgrace to someone else when you want to emphasize that their behaviour causes the other person to feel ashamed. [emphasis] [+ to] ⇒ Republican leaders called him a disgrace to the party. ⇒ What went on was a scandal. It was a disgrace to Britain.4. verbIf you say that someone disgraces someone else, you are emphasizing that their behaviour causes the other person to feel ashamed. [emphasis] [V n] ⇒ I have disgraced my family's name. [V pron-refl] ⇒ I've disgraced myself by the actions I've taken.
