accompanying

(əˈkʌmpənɪɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. provided at the same time as something else as an extra or addition; supplementary ⇒ The legislation and accompanying 'guidelines' are worded in such a way that many are denied help., ⇒ Perhaps the accompanying illustration will explain the mystery., ⇒ a ten-part television series with an accompanying book2. occurring at the same time; associated ⇒ young men facing the prospect of initiation ceremonies and accompanying adult responsibilities, ⇒ his college fees and accompanying living expenses
Example sentences containing 'accompanying'
Mildred Sauvage-Brown was also in the council chamber, accompanying Sarah Ellis. Lewis, Roy A TROUT IN THE MILKFrau Anna Langenbach will be accompanying a police officer from this place. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARI wasn't going to let a bunch of chicken satay merchants stop me accompanying my wife on her big night. Tony Parsons MAN AND WIFE
