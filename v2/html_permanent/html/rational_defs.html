rational

(ˈræʃənəl )
Definitions
adjective1. using reason or logic in thinking out a problem2. in accordance with the principles of logic or reason; reasonable3. of sound mind; sane ⇒ the patient seemed quite rational4. endowed with the capacity to reason; capable of logical thought ⇒ man is a rational being5. mathematics expressible as a ratio of two integers or polynomials ⇒ a rational number; a rational functionnoun6. mathematics a rational number
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rationally (ˈrationally) adverbrationalness (ˈrationalness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin ratiōnālis, from ratiō reason

rational

Powered by COBUILD
(ræʃənəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Rational decisions and thoughts are based on reason rather than on emotion. ⇒ He's asking you to look at both sides of the case and come to a rational decision. ⇒ Mary was able to short-circuit her stress response by keeping her thoughts calm and rational.rationally graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ It can be very hard to think rationally when you're feeling so vulnerable and alone.rationality (ræʃənælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ We live in an era of rationality.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA rational person is someone who is sensible and is able to make decisions based on intelligent thinking rather than on emotion. ⇒ Did he come across as a sane rational person? ⇒ Rachel looked calmer and more rational now.
