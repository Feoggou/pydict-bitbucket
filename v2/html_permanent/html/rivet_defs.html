rivet

(ˈrɪvɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a short metal pin for fastening two or more pieces together, having a head at one end, the other end being hammered flat after being passed through holes in the piecesverb -ets, -eting or -eted (transitive)2. to join by riveting3. to hammer in order to form into a head4. (often passive) to cause to be fixed or held firmly, as in fascinated attention, horror, etc ⇒ to be riveted to the spot
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

riveter (ˈriveter) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, from river to fasten, fix, of unknown origin
