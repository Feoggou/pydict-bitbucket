hoe

(həʊ )
Definitions
noun1. any of several kinds of long-handled hand implement equipped with a light blade and used to till the soil, eradicate weeds, etcverbWord forms: hoes, hoeing or hoed2. to dig, scrape, weed, or till (surface soil) with or as if with a hoe
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hoer (ˈhoer) nounhoelike (ˈhoeˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Old French houe from Germanic: compare Old High German houwā, houwan to hew, German Haue hoe

hoe

Powered by COBUILD
(hoʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hoes , hoeing , hoed
Definitions
1. countable nounA hoe is a gardening tool with a long handle and a small square blade, which you use to remove small weeds and break up the surface of the soil.2. verbIf you hoe a field or crop, you use a hoe on the weeds or soil there. [V n] ⇒ I have to feed the chickens and hoe the potatoes. [V] ⇒ Today he was hoeing in the vineyard.3. →  a hard row to hoe
