calumny

(ˈkæləmnɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -nies1. the malicious utterance of false charges or misrepresentation; slander; defamation2. such a false charge or misrepresentation
Word origin
C15: from Latin calumnia deception, slander

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounCalumny or a calumny is an untrue statement made about someone in order to reduce other people's respect and admiration for them. [formal] ⇒ He was the victim of calumny.
