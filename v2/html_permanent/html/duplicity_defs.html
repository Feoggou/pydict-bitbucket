duplicity

(djuːˈplɪsɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tiesdeception; double-dealing
Derived forms
duplicitous (duˈplicitous) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Old French duplicite, from Late Latin duplicitās a being double, from Latin duplex

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf you accuse someone of duplicity, you mean that they are deceitful. [formal] ⇒ Malcolm believed he was guilty of duplicity in his private dealings.
