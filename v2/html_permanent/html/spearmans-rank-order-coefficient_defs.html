Spearman's rank-order coefficient

(ˈspɪəmənz )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
nouna statistic measuring the extent to which two sets of discrete data place the distinct items in the same order, given by rS = 1 – 6Σd2/n(n2 – 1), where Σd2 is the sum of the squares of the differences of ranks between the two orderings and n is the number of items in each. Also called: Spearman's rank-order correlation coefficient

Word Origin

named after Charles E. Spearman (1863–1945), English mathematician and statistician
