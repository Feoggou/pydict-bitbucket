surmount

(sɜːˈmaʊnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to prevail over; overcome ⇒ to surmount tremendous difficulties2. to ascend and cross to the opposite side of3. to lie on top of or rise above4. to put something on top of or above5. obsolete to surpass or exceed

Derived Forms

surmountable (surˈmountable) adjectivesurmountableness (surˈmountableness) nounsurmounter (surˈmounter) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French surmonter, from sur-1 + monter to mount1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you surmount a problem or difficulty, you deal successfully with it. [V n] ⇒ I realized I had to surmount the language barrier.2. verb [usu passive]If something is surmounted by a particular thing, that thing is on top of it. [formal] [be V-ed] ⇒ The island is surmounted by a huge black castle.
