noisome

(ˈnɔɪsəm )
Definitions
adjective1. (esp of smells) offensive2. harmful or noxious
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

noisomely (ˈnoisomely) adverbnoisomeness (ˈnoisomeness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from obsolete noy, variant of annoy + -some1

noisome for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɔɪsəm )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something or someone as noisome, you mean that you find them extremely unpleasant. [literary] ⇒ Noisome vapours arise from the mud left in the docks. ⇒ His noisome reputation for corruption had already begun to spread.
