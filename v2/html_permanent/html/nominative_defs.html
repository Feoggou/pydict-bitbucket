nominative

(ˈnɒmɪnətɪv , ˈnɒmnə-)
Definitions
adjective1. grammar denoting a case of nouns and pronouns in inflected languages that is used esp to identify the subject of a finite verb. See also subjective (sense 6)2. appointed rather than elected to a position, office, etc3. bearing the name of a personnoun4. grammara. the nominative caseb. a word or speech element in the nominative case
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nominatival (ˌnɒmɪnəˈtaɪvəl , ˌnɒmnə-) adjectivenominatively (ˈnominatively) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin nōminātīvus belonging to naming, from nōmen name
