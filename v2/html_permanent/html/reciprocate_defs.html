reciprocate

(rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪt )
Definitions
verb1. to give or feel in return2. to move or cause to move backwards and forwards3. (intransitive) to be correspondent or equivalent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reciprocation (reˌciproˈcation) nounreciprocative (reˈciprocative) or reciprocatory (reˈciproˌcatory) adjectivereciprocator (reˈciproˌcator) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin reciprocāre, from reciprocus reciprocal

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪsɪprəkeɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: reciprocates , reciprocating , reciprocated
Definitions
verbIf your feelings or actions towards someone are reciprocated, the other person feels or behaves in the same way towards you as you have felt or behaved towards them. [V n] ⇒ ...he reciprocated Mr Prescott's good wishes. [V n] ⇒ Their attraction to each other as friends is reciprocated. [V + by] ⇒ He needs these people to fulfill his ambitions and reciprocates by bringing out the best in each of them.reciprocation (rɪsɪprəkeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ There was no reciprocation of affection.
