lozenge

(ˈlɒzɪndʒ )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: pastille, troche medicine a medicated tablet held in the mouth until it has dissolved2. geometry another name for rhombus3. heraldry a diamond-shaped charge
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French losange, of Gaulish origin; compare Vulgar Latin lausa flat stone

lozenge

Powered by COBUILD
(lɒzɪndʒ) in the Pharmaceutical Industry
Word forms: (regular plural) lozenges
Definitions
noun(Pharmaceutical: Dosage forms) A lozenge is a medicated tablet held in the mouth until it has dissolved. ⇒ Place the lozenge on your tongue and let it dissolve. ⇒ Sucking a lozenge as it dissolves causes more saliva to be produced in the mouth. ⇒ A lozenge is a medicated tablet held in the mouth until it has dissolved.
