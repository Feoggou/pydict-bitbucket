stipple

(ˈstɪpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to draw, engrave, or paint using dots or flecks2. to apply paint, powder, etc, to (something) with many light dabs3. to give (wet paint, cement, etc) a granular effectnoun also: stippling4. the technique of stippling or a picture produced by or using stippling

Derived Forms

stippler (ˈstippler) noun

Word Origin

C18: from Dutch stippelen, from stippen to prick, from stip point
Example sentences containing 'stipple'
That's when he saw the blood, a rusty stipple on the front of her tunic and down the hip of her pants. Chuck Logan ABSOLUTE ZERO
