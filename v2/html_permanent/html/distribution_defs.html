distribution

(ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of distributing or the state or manner of being distributed2. a thing or portion distributed3. arrangement or location4. business the process of physically satisfying the demand for goods and services5. economics the division of the total income of a community among its members, esp between labour incomes (wages and salaries) and property incomes (rents, interest, and dividends)6. statistics the set of possible values of a random variable, or points in a sample space, considered in terms of new theoretical or observed frequency ⇒ a normal distribution7. law the apportioning of the estate of a deceased intestate among the persons entitled to share in it8. law the lawful division of the assets of a bankrupt among his or her creditors9. financea. the division of part of a company's profit as a dividend to its shareholdersb. the amount paid by dividend in a particular distribution10. engineering the way in which the fuel-air mixture is supplied to each cylinder of a multicylinder internal-combustion engine
Derived forms
ˌdistributional (ˌdistriˈbutional) adjective

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe distribution of things involves giving or delivering them to a number of people or places. [+ of] ⇒ ...the council which controls the distribution of foreign aid. ⇒ He admitted there had been distribution problems. ⇒ ...emergency food distribution.2. variable nounThe distribution of something is how much of it there is in each place or at each time, or how much of it each person has. [+ of] ⇒ ...a more equitable distribution of wealth. ⇒ One of the side effects may be to change the geographical distribution of parasitic diseases such as malaria.3. uncount nounFilm distribution involves deciding which cinemas a particular film is shown in. ⇒ ...a film-distribution company.
