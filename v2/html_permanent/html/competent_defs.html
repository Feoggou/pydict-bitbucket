competent

(ˈkɒmpɪtənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having sufficient skill, knowledge, etc; capable2. suitable or sufficient for the purpose ⇒ a competent answer3. law (of a witness) having legal capacity; qualified to testify, etc4. (postpositive; foll by to) belonging as a right; appropriate
Derived forms
competently (ˈcompetently) adverbcompetentness (ˈcompetentness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin competēns, from competere to be competent; see compete

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is competent is efficient and effective. ⇒ He was a loyal, distinguished and very competent civil servant. ⇒ ...a competent performance.competently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ The government performed competently in the face of multiple challenges.2. graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ to-inf]If you are competent to do something, you have the skills, abilities, or experience necessary to do it well. ⇒ Most adults do not feel competent to deal with a medical emergency involving a child.
