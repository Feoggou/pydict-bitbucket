ill-prepared

(ˌɪlprɪˈpɛəd)
Definitions
adjectivenot having made all the necessary preparations for something ⇒ The ill-prepared troops were left to face certain defeat., ⇒ The climbers had been ill-prepared., ⇒ At that time the country was still woefully ill-prepared for war., ⇒ a policy that has left thousands of pupils ill-prepared for life beyond the classroom, ⇒ But the country is ill-prepared for the transition., ⇒ The government was ill-prepared for the problems it now faces., ⇒ This leaves Britain ill-prepared for the economic challenges ahead., ⇒ The department was ill-prepared to meet this challenge., ⇒ We are ill-prepared to cope with famine., ⇒ We are ill-prepared to deal with a sudden crisis.

ill-prepared

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf]If you are ill-prepared for something, you have not made the correct preparations for it, for example because you are not expecting it to happen. [+ for] ⇒ The government was ill-prepared for the problems it now faces. ⇒ It was ill-prepared to meet this challenge.
