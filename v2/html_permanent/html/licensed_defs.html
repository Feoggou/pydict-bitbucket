licensed

(ˈlaɪsənst)
Definitions
adjective1. British able to serve alcoholic drinks by virtue of having a licence for this ⇒ licensed premises, ⇒ He won't stay at a hotel unless it's licensed., ⇒ The restaurant is licensed, offering draught beers, wines, spirits and soft drinks.2. supplied with a proper or necessary licence ⇒ The car is licensed and insured., ⇒ While searching the house they discovered an unlicensed shotgun and a licensed rifle., ⇒ a licensed doctor

licensed

Powered by COBUILD
(laɪsənst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [oft ADJ to-inf]If you are licensed to do something, you have official permission from the government or from the authorities to do it. ⇒ There were about 250 people on board, about 100 more than the ferry was licensed to carry. ⇒ ...a licensed doctor.2. adjectiveIf something that you own or use is licensed, you have official permission to own it or use it. ⇒ While searching the house they discovered an unlicensed shotgun and a licensed rifle.3. adjectiveIf a place such as a restaurant or hotel is licensed, it has been given a licence to sell alcoholic drinks. [British] ⇒ ...licensed premises. ⇒ The restaurant is licensed, offering draught beers, wines, spirits and soft drinks.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
