instruction

(ɪnˈstrʌkʃən )
Definitions
noun1. a direction; order2. the process or act of imparting knowledge; teaching; education3. computing a part of a program consisting of a coded command to the computer to perform a specified function
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

instructional (inˈstructional) adjective

instruction

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnstrʌkʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: instructions
Definitions
1. countable nounAn instruction is something that someone tells you to do. ⇒ Two lawyers were told not to leave the building but no reason for this instruction was given.2. uncount nounIf someone gives you instruction in a subject or skill, they teach it to you. [formal] [+ in] ⇒ Each candidate is given instruction in safety. ⇒ All schoolchildren must now receive some religious instruction.3. plural nounInstructions are clear and detailed information on how to do something. directions ⇒ Always read the instructions before you start taking the medicine.
