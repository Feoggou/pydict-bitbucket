missile

(ˈmɪsaɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any object or weapon that is thrown at a target or shot from an engine, gun, etc2. a. a rocket-propelled weapon that flies either in a fixed trajectory (ballistic missile) or in a trajectory that can be controlled during flight (guided missile)b. (as modifier) ⇒ a missile carrier

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: missilis, from mittere to send

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA missile is a tube-shaped weapon that travels long distances through the air and explodes when it reaches its target. ⇒ The authorities offered to stop firing missiles if the rebels agreed to stop attacking civilian targets. ⇒ ...nuclear missiles.2. countable nounAnything that is thrown as a weapon can be called a missile. ⇒ The football supporters began throwing missiles, one of which hit the referee.3. → see also cruise missile, guided missile
