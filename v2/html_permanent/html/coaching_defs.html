coaching

(ˈkəʊˌtʃɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. sport the act of training a person or team of people in a particular sport ⇒ He has been doing a fair bit of coaching in Melbourne this season., ⇒ The pressure of coaching got to him and he temporarily walked away from the game.2. the act of training staff in business or office practice3. the act of giving a person special teaching in a particular subject, esp in order to prepare him or her for an examination
Example sentences containing 'coaching'
So she took private pupils for individual coaching during the three academic terms, and spent a month each summer in Switzerland. Melville, Anne THE HARDIE INHERITANCEThe Staro Arms was a former medieval coaching inn which nowadays conjured up images of a favourite slipper: shabby but comfortable.
