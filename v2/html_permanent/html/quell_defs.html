quell

(kwɛl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to suppress or beat down (rebellion, disorder, etc); subdue2. to overcome or allay ⇒ to quell pain, ⇒ to quell grief
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

queller (ˈqueller) noun

Word Origin

Old English cwellan to kill; related to Old Saxon quellian, Old High German quellen, Old Norse kvelja to torment

Definitions
1. verbTo quell opposition or violent behaviour means to stop it. [V n] ⇒ Troops eventually quelled the unrest.2. verbIf you quell an unpleasant feeling such as fear or anger, you stop yourself or other people from having that feeling. [V n] ⇒ The Information Minister is trying to quell fears of a looming oil crisis.
