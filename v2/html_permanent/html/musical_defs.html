musical

(ˈmjuːzɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of, relating to, or used in music ⇒ a musical instrument2. harmonious; melodious ⇒ musical laughter3. talented in or fond of music4. involving or set to music ⇒ a musical eveningnoun5. short for musical comedy

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]You use musical to indicate that something is connected with playing or studying music. ⇒ We have a wealth of musical talent in this region. ⇒ Stan Getz's musical career spanned five decades. ⇒ London's musical life might become as exciting as Berlin's.musically (mjuːzɪkli ) adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ Musically there is a lot to enjoy. ⇒ ...trying to communicate verbally what he can only communicate musically.2. countable nounA musical is a play or film that uses singing and dancing in the story. ⇒ ...London's smash hit musical Miss Saigon.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is musical has a natural ability and interest in music. ⇒ I came from a musical family.musicality (mjuːzɪkælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ ...a people of extraordinary musicality.4. graded adjective & adjectiveSounds that are musical are light and pleasant to hear. ⇒ He had a soft, almost musical voice.musically graded adverb ⇒ The voice was as musically soft as ever.
