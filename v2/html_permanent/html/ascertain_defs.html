ascertain

(ˌæsəˈteɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to determine or discover definitely2. archaic to make certain
Derived forms
ˌascerˈtainable adjectiveˌascerˈtainably adverbˌascerˈtainment noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French acertener to make certain

Word forms: ascertains , ascertaining , ascertained
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you ascertain the truth about something, you find out what it is, especially by making a deliberate effort to do so. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Through doing this, the teacher will be able to ascertain the extent to which the child understands what he is reading. [V that] ⇒ Once they had ascertained that he was not a spy, they agreed to release him. [V wh] ⇒ Take time to ascertain what services your bank is providing, and at what cost.
