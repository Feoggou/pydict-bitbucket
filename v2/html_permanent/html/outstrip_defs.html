outstrip

(ˌaʊtˈstrɪp )
Definitions
verb -strips, -stripping or -stripped (transitive)1. to surpass in a sphere of activity, competition, etc2. to be or grow greater than3. to go faster than and leave behind

Definitions
verbIf one thing outstrips another, the first thing becomes larger in amount, or more successful or important, than the second thing. surpass [V n] ⇒ In the mid-eighteenth century the production of food far outstripped the rise in population. [V n] ⇒ In 1989 and 1990 demand outstripped supply, and prices went up by more than a third.
