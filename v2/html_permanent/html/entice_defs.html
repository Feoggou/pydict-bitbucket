entice

(ɪnˈtaɪs )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to attract or draw towards oneself by exciting hope or desire; tempt; allure
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

enticement (enˈticement) nounenticer (enˈticer) nounenticing (enˈticing) adjectiveenticingly (enˈticingly) adverbenticingness (enˈticingness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French enticier, from Vulgar Latin intitiāre (unattested) to incite, from Latin titiō firebrand

entice

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntaɪs ) for learners of English
Word forms: entices , enticing , enticed
Definitions
verbTo entice someone to go somewhere or to do something means to try to persuade them to go to that place or to do that thing. [V n prep] ⇒ Retailers have tried almost everything to entice shoppers through their doors. [V n with adv] ⇒ Child murderers in the past have very often carried photographs of young children to entice their victims away. [V n to-inf] ⇒ They'll entice doctors to move from the cities by paying them better salaries. [Also V n]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
