loam

(ləʊm )
Definitions
noun1. rich soil consisting of a mixture of sand, clay, and decaying organic material2. a paste of clay and sand used for making moulds in a foundry, plastering walls, etcverb3. (transitive) to cover, treat, or fill with loam
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

loamy (ˈloamy) adjectiveloaminess (ˈloaminess) noun

Word Origin

Old English lām; related to Old Swedish lēmo clay, Old High German leimo
