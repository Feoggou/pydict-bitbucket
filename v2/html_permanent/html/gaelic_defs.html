Gaelic

(ˈɡeɪlɪk , ˈɡæl-)
Definitions
noun1. any of the closely related languages of the Celts in Ireland, Scotland, or (formerly) the Isle of Man. Compare Goidelicadjective2. of, denoting, or relating to the Celtic people of Ireland, Scotland, or the Isle of Man or their language or customs

Gaelic

Powered by COBUILD
(geɪlɪk , gælɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounGaelic is a language spoken by people in parts of Scotland and Ireland. ⇒ We weren't allowed to speak Gaelic at school.Gaelic is also an adjective. ⇒ ...the Gaelic language.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Gaelic means coming from or relating to Scotland and Ireland, especially the parts where Gaelic is spoken. ⇒ ...an evening of Gaelic music and drama.
