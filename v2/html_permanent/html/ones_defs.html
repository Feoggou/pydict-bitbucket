one's

(wʌnz)
Definitions
adjective1. formal a third-person singular possessive corresponding to one ⇒ The end of one's useful professional life is hard to accept., ⇒ It seems to me a fatal illusion to expect one's children simply to reproduce one's own views., ⇒ a feeling of responsibility for the welfare of others in one's community, ⇒ One has cut one's finger., ⇒ One must wash one's hair.. See onecontraction of2. one is ⇒ when one's running a business, ⇒ if one's feeling a bit tired, ⇒ One's not going to hurt you.3. one has ⇒ when one's got something to do, ⇒ every positive experience one's had, ⇒ When one's gone, the other partner is going to be totally lost., ⇒ One's been here for a long time.

one's for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wʌnz )
Definitions
1. possessive determiner & determinerSpeakers and writers use one's to indicate that something belongs or relates to people in general, or to themselves in particular. [formal] ⇒ ...a feeling of responsibility for the welfare of others in one's community. ⇒ It seems to me a fatal illusion to expect one's children simply to reproduce one's own views.2. One's can be used as a spoken form of 'one is' or 'one has', especially when 'has' is an auxiliary verb. ⇒ No one's going to hurt you. ⇒ One's got to consider all the possibilities.3. → See one
