turquoise

(ˈtɜːkwɔɪz , -kwɑːz)
Definitions
noun1. a greenish-blue fine-grained secondary mineral consisting of hydrated copper aluminium phosphate. It occurs in igneous rocks rich in aluminium and is used as a gemstone. Formula: CuAl6(PO4)4(OH)8.4H2O2. a. the colour of turquoiseb. (as adjective) ⇒ a turquoise dress
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French turqueise Turkish (stone)
