deprecate

(ˈdɛprɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to express disapproval of; protest against2. to depreciate (a person, someone's character, etc); belittle3. archaic to try to ward off by prayerAvoid confusion with depreciate
Derived forms
depreˌcating (ˈdepreˌcating) adjectivedepreˌcatingly (ˈdepreˌcatingly) adverbˌdeprecation (ˌdepreˈcation) noundeprecative (ˈdeprecative) adjectivedeprecatively (ˈdeprecatively) adverbdepreˌcator (ˈdepreˌcator) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin dēprecārī to avert, ward off by entreaty, from de- + precārī to pray
