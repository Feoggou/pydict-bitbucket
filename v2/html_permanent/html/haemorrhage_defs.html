haemorrhage

or US hemorrhage (ˈhɛmərɪdʒ )
Definitions
noun1. profuse bleeding from ruptured blood vessels2. a steady or severe loss or depletion of resources, staff, etcverb3. (intransitive) to bleed profusely4. (transitive) to undergo a steady or severe loss or depletion of (resources, staff, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

haemorrhagic or US hemorrhagic (ˌhɛməˈrædʒɪk ) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin haemorrhagia; see haemo-, -rrhagia

haemorrhage

Powered by COBUILD
(hemərɪdʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: haemorrhages , haemorrhaging , haemorrhaged
Definitions
regional note:   in AM, use hemorrhage1. variable nounA haemorrhage is serious bleeding inside a person's body. ⇒ Shortly after his admission into hospital he had a massive brain haemorrhage and died. ⇒ These drugs will not be used if hemorrhage is the cause of the stroke.2. verbIf someone is haemorrhaging, there is serious bleeding inside their body. [V] ⇒ I haemorrhaged badly after the birth of all three of my sons. [V] ⇒ If this is left untreated, one can actually haemorrhage to death.haemorrhaging uncount noun ⇒ A post mortem showed he died from shock and haemorrhaging.3. singular nounA haemorrhage of people or resources is a rapid loss of them from a group or place, seriously weakening its position. [+ of] ⇒ He said the move would definitely stem the haemorrhage of talent and enterprise from the colony.4. ergative verb & verbTo haemorrhage people or resources means to lose them rapidly and become weak. You can also say that people or resources haemorrhage from a place or organization. [V n] ⇒ Venice is haemorrhaging the very resource which could save it: its own people. [V + from] ⇒ The figures showed that cash was haemorrhaging from the conglomerate.
