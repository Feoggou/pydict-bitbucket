tether

(ˈtɛðə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a restricting rope, chain, etc, by which an animal is tied to a particular spot2. the range of one's endurance, etc3. See at the end of one's tetherverb4. (transitive) to tie or limit with or as if with a tether

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse tjothr; related to Middle Dutch tūder tether, Old High German zeotar pole of a wagon

tether

Powered by COBUILD
(teðəʳ )
Word forms: tethers , tethering , tethered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. →  the end of your tether2. countable nounA tether is a rope or chain which is used to tie an animal to a post or fence so that it can only move around within a small area.3. verbIf you tether an animal or object to something, you attach it there with a rope or chain so that it cannot move very far. [V n + to] ⇒ The officer dismounted, tethering his horse to a tree. [Also V n]
