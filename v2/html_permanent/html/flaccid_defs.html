flaccid

(ˈflæksɪd , ˈflæs-)
Definitions
adjectivelacking firmness; soft and limp; flabby
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flaccidity (flacˈcidity) or flaccidness (ˈflaccidness) nounflaccidly (ˈflaccidly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin flaccidus, from flaccus

flaccid

Powered by COBUILD
(flæsɪd , flæksɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveYou use flaccid to describe a part of someone's body when it is unpleasantly soft and not hard or firm. ⇒ I picked up her wrist. It was limp and flaccid.
