rosin

(ˈrɒzɪn )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: colophony a translucent brittle amber substance produced in the distillation of crude turpentine oleoresin and used esp in making varnishes, printing inks, and sealing waxes and for treating the bows of stringed instruments2. (not in technical usage) another name for resin (sense 1)verb3. (transitive) to treat or coat with rosin

Derived Forms

rosiny (ˈrosiny) adjective

Word Origin

C14: variant of resin
