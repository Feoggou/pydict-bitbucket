similar

(ˈsɪmɪlə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. showing resemblance in qualities, characteristics, or appearance; alike but not identical2. geometry (of two or more figures) having corresponding angles equal and all corresponding sides in the same ratio. Compare congruent (sense 2)3. mathematics (of two classes) equinumerous▶ USAGE As should not be used after similar: Wilson held a similar position to Jones (not a similar position as Jones); the system is similar to the one in France (not similar as the one in France)

Derived Forms

similarity (ˌsɪmɪˈlærɪtɪ ) nounsimilarly (ˈsimilarly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Old French similaire, from Latin similis
