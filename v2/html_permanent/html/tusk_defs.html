tusk

(tʌsk )
Definitions
noun1. a pointed elongated usually paired tooth in the elephant, walrus, and certain other mammals that is often used for fighting2. the canine tooth of certain animals, esp horses3. a sharp pointed projection4. Also called: tusk tenon building a tenon shaped with an additional oblique shoulder to make a stronger jointverb5. to stab, tear, or gore with the tusks
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tusked adjectivetusklike (ˈtuskˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

Old English tūsc; related to Old Frisian tosk; see tooth
