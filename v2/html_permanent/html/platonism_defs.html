Platonism

(ˈpleɪtəˌnɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the teachings of Plato and his followers, esp the philosophical theory that the meanings of general words are real existing abstract entities (Forms) and that particular objects have properties in common by virtue of their relationship with these Forms. Compare nominalism, conceptualism, intuitionism2. the realist doctrine that mathematical entities have real existence and that mathematical truth is independent of human thought3. See Neo-Platonism
