whip up

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. to excite; arouse ⇒ to whip up a mob, ⇒ to whip up discontent2. informal to prepare quickly ⇒ to whip up a meal

whip up for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf someone whips up an emotion, especially a dangerous one such as hatred, or if they whip people up into an emotional state, they deliberately cause and encourage people to feel that emotion. [V P n] ⇒ He accused politicians of whipping up anti-foreign sentiments in order to win right-wing votes. [V P n (not pron) into n] ⇒ Joe McCarthy whipped up Americans into a frenzy of anti-Communist activity in the Fifties. [Also V n P + into]2. phrasal verbIf a force such as the wind whips up dust or water, it makes it rise up. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ In 1346 a hurricane whipped up the sea to destroy the town. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ ...clouds of smoke and sand whipped up by a strong wind. [Also V n P]3. phrasal verbIf you whip up something, especially a meal, you make it quickly. [informal] [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ I used to entertain at home quite a lot, and I can still whip up a fairly decent dinner party. [Also V n P] →  whip
