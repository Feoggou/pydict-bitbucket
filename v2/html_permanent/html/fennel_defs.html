fennel

(ˈfɛnəl )
Definitions
noun1. a strong-smelling yellow-flowered umbelliferous plant, Foeniculum vulgare, whose seeds and feathery leaves are used to season and flavour food. See also finocchio2. another name for mayweed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English fenol, from Latin faeniculum fennel, diminutive of faenum hay
