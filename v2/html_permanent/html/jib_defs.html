jib1

(dʒɪb )
Definitions
noun1. nautical any triangular sail set forward of the foremast of a vessel2. See cut of someone's jib3. obsoletea. the lower lip, usually when it protrudes forwards in a grimaceb. the face or nose
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: of unknown origin

jib2

(dʒɪb )
Definitions
verbWord forms: jibs, jibbing or jibbed (intransitive) mainly British1. (often foll by at) to be reluctant (to); hold back (from); balk (at)2. (of an animal) to stop short and refuse to go forwards ⇒ the horse jibbed at the jump3. nautical variant of gybe
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jibber (ˈjibber) noun

Word Origin

C19: of unknown origin

jib3

(dʒɪb )
Definitions
nounthe projecting arm of a crane or the boom of a derrick, esp one that is pivoted to enable it to be raised or lowered
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: probably based on gibbet

jib4

(dʒɪb )
Definitions
noun(often plural) South Wales dialect a contortion of the face; a face ⇒ stop making jibs
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

special use of jib1 (in the sense: lower lip, face)

Definitions
