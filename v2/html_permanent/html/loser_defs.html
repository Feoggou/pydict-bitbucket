loser

(ˈluːzə )
Definitions
noun1. a person or thing that loses2. a person or thing that seems destined to be taken advantage of, fail, etc ⇒ a born loser3. bridge a card that will not take a trick
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

loser

Powered by COBUILD
(luːzəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: losers
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]The losers of a game, contest, or struggle are the people who are defeated or beaten. [+ of] ⇒ ...the Dallas Cowboys and Buffalo Bills, the winners and losers of this year's Super Bowl.→  good loser/bad loser2. countable nounIf you refer to someone as a loser, you have a low opinion of them because you think they are always unsuccessful. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ They've only been trained to compete with other men, so a successful woman can make them feel like a real loser.3. countable noun [usu pl]People who are losers as the result of an action or event, are in a worse situation because of it or do not benefit from it. ⇒ Some of Britain's top business leaders of the 1980s became the country's greatest losers in the recession.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
