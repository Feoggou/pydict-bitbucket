Word Frequency

exiguous

(ɪɡˈzɪɡjʊəs , ɪkˈsɪɡ-)
Definitions
adjectivescanty or slender; meagre ⇒ an exiguous income
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exiguity (ˌɛksɪˈɡjuːɪtɪ ) or exiguousness (exˈiguousness) nounexiguously (exˈiguously) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin exiguus, from exigere to weigh out; see exigent
Example sentences containing 'exiguous'
If Lillian's dessert plate was festooned with candied violets, mine would have an exiguous broken petal or two. Katherine Mosb THE SEASON OF LILLIAN DAWES

Similar Words

meagre, spare, bare, slender, negligible, sparse, paltry, skimpy, scanty
