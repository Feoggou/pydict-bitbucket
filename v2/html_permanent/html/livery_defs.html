livery1

(ˈlɪvərɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -eries1. the identifying uniform, badge, etc, of a member of a guild or one of the servants of a feudal lord2. a uniform worn by some menservants and chauffeurs3. an individual or group that wears such a uniform4. distinctive dress or outward appearance5. a. the stabling, keeping, or hiring out of horses for moneyb. (as modifier) ⇒ a livery horse6. See at livery7. legal history an ancient method of conveying freehold land
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Anglo-French from Old French livrée allocation, from livrer to hand over, from Latin līberāre to set free
