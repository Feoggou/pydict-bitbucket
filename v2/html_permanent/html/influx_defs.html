influx

(ˈɪnˌflʌks )
Definitions
noun1. the arrival or entry of many people or things2. the act of flowing in; inflow3. the mouth of a stream or river
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin influxus, from influere; see influence

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]An influx of people or things into a place is their arrival there in large numbers. [+ of] ⇒ ...problems caused by the influx of refugees. ⇒ ...the influx of American popcorn into the British market.
