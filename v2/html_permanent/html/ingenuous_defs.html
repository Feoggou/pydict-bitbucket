ingenuous

(ɪnˈdʒɛnjʊəs )
Definitions
adjective1. naive, artless, or innocent2. candid; frank; straightforward
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ingenuously (inˈgenuously) adverbingenuousness (inˈgenuousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ingenuus freeborn, worthy of a freeman, virtuous, from in-2 + -genuus, from gignere to beget

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndʒenjuəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as ingenuous, you mean that they are innocent, trusting, and honest. [formal] ⇒ He seemed too ingenuous for a reporter. ⇒ With ingenuous sincerity, he captivated his audience.ingenuously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Somewhat ingenuously, he explains how the crime may be accomplished. ⇒ He smiled, eyes ingenuously wide.
