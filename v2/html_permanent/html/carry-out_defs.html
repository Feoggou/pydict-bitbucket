carry out

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to perform or cause to be implemented ⇒ I wish he could afford to carry out his plan2. to bring to completion; accomplishnoun carry-out mainly Scottish3. alcohol bought at a pub or off-licence for consumption elsewhere4. a. hot cooked food bought at a shop or restaurant for consumption elsewhereb. a shop or restaurant that sells such food ⇒ we'll get something from the Chinese carry-outc. (as modifier) ⇒ a carry-out shop

carry-out

(ˈkærɪaʊt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun Scottish1. hot cooked food bought at a shop or restaurant for consumption elsewhere ⇒ We bought a carry-out from the restaurant.2. a shop or restaurant that sells such food ⇒ We'll get something from the Chinese carry-out.3. alcohol bought at a pub or off-licence for consumption elsewhere ⇒ a coming of age when young people buy their first carry-out and get drunkmodifier4. Scottish of, relating to, or selling hot cooked food for consumption outside the place of purchase ⇒ She ordered a chicken kebab from a local carry-out shop., ⇒ The restaurant sells carry-out meals.

phrasal verbIf you carry out a threat, task, or instruction, you do it or act according to it. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ The Social Democrats could still carry out their threat to leave the government. [be V-ed P + by] ⇒ Police say they believe the attacks were carried out by nationalists. [V n P] ⇒ Commitments have been made with very little intention of carrying them out. →  carry
