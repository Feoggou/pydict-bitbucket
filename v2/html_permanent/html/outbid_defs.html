outbid

(ˌaʊtˈbɪd )
Definitions
verb -bids, -bidding, -bid, -bidden or -bid(transitive) to bid higher than; outdo in bidding

outbid for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtbɪd )
Word forms: outbids , outbidding
Definitions
language note:   The form outbid is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle.verbIf you outbid someone, you offer more money than they do for something that you both want to buy. [V n] ⇒ The Museum has antagonised rivals by outbidding them for the world's greatest art treasures. [V n] ⇒ Last year, Cray failed to take over the software company, when it was outbid by Electronic Data Systems.
