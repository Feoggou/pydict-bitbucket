attuned

(əˈtjuːnd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. able to perceive; sensitive (to) ⇒ He seemed unusually attuned to people's feelings., ⇒ I have become attuned to Carlisle's industrial past.2. accustomed (to) ⇒ Their ears were still attuned to the sounds of the London suburb.

attuned

Powered by COBUILD
(ətjuːnd , US ətuːnd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf you are attuned to something, you can understand and appreciate it. ⇒ I have become attuned to Carlisle's industrial past. ⇒ He seemed unusually attuned to people's feelings.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf your ears are attuned to a sound, you can hear it and recognize it quickly. [+ to] ⇒ Their ears were still attuned to the sounds of the London suburb.
Example sentences containing 'attuned'
They were more attuned to the subtleties of human behaviour. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERBut then, as he drove the man back to Marseille, he became attuned to something else. Loraine, Philip LAST SHOTHe himself is not aware how illuminating this difference will be to one mind closely attuned to his own. Butler, Gwendoline COFFIN ON THE WATER
