rack up

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. to accumulate (points)2. Also: rack down to adjust the vertical alignment of (the picture from a film projector or telecine machine) so that the upper or lower edges of the frame do not show

Definitions
phrasal verb [no passive]If a business racks up profits, losses, or sales, it makes a lot of them. If a sportsman, sportswoman, or team racks up wins, they win a lot of matches or races. [V P n] ⇒ Lower rates mean that firms are more likely to rack up profits in the coming months. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ India while not racking up such an impressive score beat Japan 3-0. →  rack
