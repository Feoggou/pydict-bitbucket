pacify

(ˈpæsɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to calm the anger or agitation of; mollify2. to restore to peace or order, esp by the threat or use of force

Derived Forms

paciˌfiable (ˈpaciˌfiable) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French pacifier; see pacific

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you pacify someone who is angry, upset, or not pleased, you succeed in making them calm or pleased. [V n] ⇒ Is this a serious step, or is this just something to pacify the critics? [V n] ⇒ She shrieked again, refusing to be pacified.2. verbIf the army or the police pacify a group of people, they use force to overcome their resistance or protests. [V n] ⇒ Government forces have found it difficult to pacify the rebels. [V n] ⇒ They were eventually pacified by officers of the local police.pacification (pæsɪfɪkeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the pacification of the country.
