inveterate

(ɪnˈvɛtərɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. long established, esp so as to be deep-rooted or ingrained ⇒ an inveterate feeling of hostility2. (prenominal) settled or confirmed in a habit or practice, esp a bad one; hardened ⇒ an inveterate smoker3. obsolete full of hatred; hostile
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inveteracy (inˈveteracy) or inveterateness (inˈveterateness) nouninveterately (inˈveterately) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin inveterātus of long standing, from inveterāre to make old, from in-2 + vetus old
