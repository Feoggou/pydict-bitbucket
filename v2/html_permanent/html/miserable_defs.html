miserable

(ˈmɪzərəbəl , ˈmɪzrə-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. unhappy or depressed; wretched2. causing misery, discomfort, etc ⇒ a miserable life3. contemptible ⇒ a miserable villain4. sordid or squalid ⇒ miserable living conditions5. Scottish, Australian and New Zealand mean; stingy6. (pejorative intensifier) ⇒ you miserable wretch

Derived Forms

miserableness (ˈmiserableness) nounmiserably (ˈmiserably) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Old French, from Latin miserābilis worthy of pity, from miserārī to pity, from miser wretched

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are miserable, you are very unhappy. ⇒ I took a series of badly paid secretarial jobs which made me really miserable. ⇒ She went to bed, miserable and depressed.miserably (mɪzərəbli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV after v] ⇒ He looked miserably down at his plate.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a place or situation as miserable, you mean that it makes you feel unhappy or depressed. ⇒ There was nothing at all in this miserable place to distract him.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe the weather as miserable, you mean that it makes you feel depressed, because it is raining or dull. ⇒ It was a grey, wet, miserable day. ⇒ It was very cold, damp and miserable.4. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you describe someone as miserable, you mean that you do not like them because they are bad-tempered or unfriendly. ⇒ He always was a miserable man. He never spoke to me nor anybody else.5. adjectiveYou can describe a quantity or quality as miserable when you think that it is much smaller or worse than it ought to be. [emphasis] paltry, measly ⇒ Our speed over the ground was a miserable 2.2 knots. ⇒ It has so far accepted a miserable 1,100 refugees from the former Yugoslavia.miserably adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ ...the miserably inadequate supply of books now provided for schools.6. adjective [ADJ n]A miserable failure is a very great one. [emphasis] ⇒ The film was a miserable commercial failure both in Italy and in the United States.miserably adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Some manage it. Some fail miserably.
