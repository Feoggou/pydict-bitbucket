forestall

(fɔːˈstɔːl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to delay, stop, or guard against beforehand2. to anticipate3. a. to prevent or hinder sales at (a market, etc) by buying up merchandise in advance, etcb. to buy up (merchandise) for profitable resale. Compare corner (sense 21)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

forestaller (foreˈstaller) nounforestalment (foreˈstalment) or especially US forestallment (foreˈstallment) noun

Word Origin

C14 forestallen to waylay, from Old English foresteall an ambush, from fore- in front of + steall place
