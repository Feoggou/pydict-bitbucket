inept

(ɪnˈɛpt )
Definitions
adjective1. awkward, clumsy, or incompetent2. not suitable, appropriate, or fitting; out of place
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ineptitude (inˈeptiˌtude) nounineptly (inˈeptly) adverbineptness (inˈeptness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ineptus, from in-1 + aptus fitting, suitable

inept

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnept ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is inept, you are criticizing them because they do something with a complete lack of skill. [disapproval] ⇒ He was inept and lacked the intelligence to govern. ⇒ You are completely inept at writing. ⇒ ...his inept handling of the army.
