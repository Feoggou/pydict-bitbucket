denial

(dɪˈnaɪəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a refusal to agree or comply with a statement; contradiction2. the rejection of the truth of a proposition, doctrine, etc ⇒ a denial of God's existence3. a negative reply; rejection of a request4. a refusal to acknowledge; renunciation; disavowal ⇒ a denial of one's leader5. a psychological process by which painful truths are not admitted into an individual's consciousness. See also defence mechanism6. abstinence; self-denial

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA denial of something is a statement that it is not true, does not exist, or did not happen. ⇒ Despite official denials, the rumours still persist. [+ of] ⇒ Denial of the Mafia's existence is nothing new.2. uncount nounThe denial of something to someone is the act of refusing to let them have it. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ ...the denial of visas to international relief workers. ⇒ This does not justify the denial of constitutional protection.3. uncount noun [oft in N]In psychology, denial is when a person cannot or will not accept an unpleasant truth. ⇒ With major life traumas, like losing a loved one, for instance, the mind's first reaction is denial. ⇒ ...an addict who is in denial about his addiction.
