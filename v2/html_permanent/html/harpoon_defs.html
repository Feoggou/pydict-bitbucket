harpoon

(hɑːˈpuːn )
Definitions
noun1. a. a barbed missile attached to a long cord and hurled or fired from a gun when hunting whales, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ a harpoon gunverb2. (transitive) to spear with or as if with a harpoon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

harpooner (harˈpooner) or harpooneer (ˌharpoonˈeer) nounharpoon-like (harˈpoon-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C17: probably from Dutch harpoen, from Old French harpon clasp, from harper to seize, perhaps of Scandinavian origin

Definitions
1. countable nounA harpoon is a long pointed weapon with a long rope attached to it, which is fired or thrown by people hunting whales or large sea fish.2. verbTo harpoon a whale or large fish means to hit it with a harpoon. [V n] ⇒ Norwegian whalers said yesterday they had harpooned a female minke whale.
