temperate

(ˈtɛmpərɪt , ˈtɛmprɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. having a climate intermediate between tropical and polar; moderate or mild in temperature2. mild in quality or character; exhibiting temperance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

temperately (ˈtemperately) adverbtemperateness (ˈtemperateness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin temperātus

temperate

Powered by COBUILD
(tempərɪt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Temperate is used to describe a climate or a place which is never extremely hot or extremely cold. ⇒ The Nile Valley keeps a temperate climate throughout the year.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a person's behaviour is temperate, it is calm and reasonable, so that they do not get angry or lose their temper easily. [formal] ⇒ His final report to the President was far more temperate and balanced than the earlier memorandum.
