symmetry

(ˈsɪmɪtrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tries1. similarity, correspondence, or balance among systems or parts of a system2. mathematics an exact correspondence in position or form about a given point, line, or plane. See symmetrical (sense 2)3. beauty or harmony of form based on a proportionate arrangement of parts4. physics the independence of a property with respect to direction; isotropy

Word Origin

C16: from Latin symmetria, from Greek summetria proportion, from syn- + metron measure

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounSomething that has symmetry is symmetrical in shape, design, or structure. ⇒ ...the incredible beauty and symmetry of a snowflake. ⇒ I loved the house because it had perfect symmetry. ⇒ Their own lives already seemed to possess the symmetries of narrative art.2. uncount nounSymmetry in a relationship or agreement is the fact of both sides giving and receiving an equal amount. ⇒ The superpowers pledged to maintain symmetry in their arms shipments.3. variable nounYou can refer to symmetry between countries, institutions, or situations if you think that there is a close similarity between them. ⇒ He said that Britain would not rejoin the ERM 'until there is much greater symmetry between our economy and other European economies.'
