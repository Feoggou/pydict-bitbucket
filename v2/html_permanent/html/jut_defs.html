jut

(dʒʌt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: juts, jutting or jutted1. (intransitive; often foll by out) to stick out or overhang beyond the surface or main part; protrude or projectnoun2. something that juts out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jutting (ˈjutting) adjective

Word Origin

C16: variant of jet1

jut for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒʌt )
Word forms: juts , jutting , jutted
Definitions
1. verbIf something juts out, it sticks out above or beyond a surface. [V adv/prep] ⇒ The northern end of the island juts out like a long, thin finger into the sea. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Tombstones jutted out of the ground in broken clusters. [V-ing] ⇒ He had clear blue eyes and a jutting chin.2. ergative verb & verbIf you jut a part of your body, especially your chin, or if it juts, you push it forward in an aggressive or determined way. [V adv/prep] ⇒ His jaw jutted stubbornly forward; he would not be denied. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ Gwen jutted her chin forward and did not answer the teacher. [V] ⇒ Ken's jaw jutted with determination. [Also V n]
