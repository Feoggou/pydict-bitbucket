hothouse

(ˈhɒtˌhaʊs )
Definitions
noun1. a. a greenhouse in which the temperature is maintained at a fixed level above that of the surroundingsb. (as modifier) ⇒ a hothouse plant2. a. an environment that encourages rapid developmentb. (as modifier) ⇒ a hot-house atmosphere3. an environment where there is great pressure ⇒ showjumping is a tough, hothouse world4. (modifier) informal, often pejorative sensitive or delicate ⇒ a hothouse temperament
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

hothouse

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒthaʊs ) for learners of English
Word forms: hothouses
Definitions
1. countable nounA hothouse is a heated building, usually made of glass, in which plants and flowers can be grown.2. countable noun [oft N n]You can refer to a situation or place as a hothouse when there is intense activity, especially intellectual or emotional activity. ⇒ The vast crowds make Rome a frantic hothouse at times. ⇒ ...the reputation of the College as a hothouse of novel ideas.
