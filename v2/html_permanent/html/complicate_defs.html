complicate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈkɒmplɪˌkeɪt )1. to make or become complexadjective (ˈkɒmplɪkɪt )2. biology folded on itself ⇒ a complicate leaf3. a less common word for complicated
Word origin
C17: from Latin complicāre to fold together, from plicāre to fold

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbTo complicate something means to make it more difficult to understand or deal with. [V n] ⇒ What complicates the issue is the burden of history. [V n] ⇒ The day's events, he said, would only complicate the task of the peacekeeping forces. [V n] ⇒ To complicate matters further, everybody's vitamin requirements vary. [V n] ⇒ Bad weather continues to complicate efforts to deal with oil spilling from the tanker.
