acclamation

(ˌækləˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an enthusiastic reception or exhibition of welcome, approval, etc2. an expression of approval by a meeting or gathering through shouts or applause3. Canadian an instance of electing or being elected without opposition ⇒ there were two acclamations in the 1985 election4. See by acclamation
Derived forms
acclamatory (əˈklæmətərɪ , -trɪ) adjective

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAcclamation is a noisy or enthusiastic expression of approval for someone or something. [formal] ⇒ The news was greeted with considerable popular acclamation.2. uncount nounIf someone is chosen or elected by acclamation, they are elected without a written vote. [formal] ⇒ At first it looked like I was going to win by acclamation.
