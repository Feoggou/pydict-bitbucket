rye1

(raɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a tall hardy widely cultivated annual grass, Secale cereale, having soft bluish-green leaves, bristly flower spikes, and light brown grain. See also wild rye2. the grain of this grass, used in making flour and whiskey, and as a livestock food3. Also called: rye whiskey whiskey distilled from rye. US whiskey must by law contain not less than 51 per cent rye4. US short for ryebread
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English ryge; related to Old Norse rugr, Old French rogga, Old Saxon roggo

Definitions
1. uncount noun [oft N n]Rye is a cereal grown in cold countries. Its grains can be used to make flour, bread, or other foods. ⇒ One of the first crops that I grew when we came here was rye. ⇒ ...100g wholemeal or rye flour.2. uncount noun [usu on N]Rye is bread made from rye. [US] ⇒ I was eating ham and Swiss cheese on rye.
