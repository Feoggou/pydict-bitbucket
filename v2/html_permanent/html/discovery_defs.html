discovery

(dɪˈskʌvərɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -eries1. the act, process, or an instance of discovering2. a person, place, or thing that has been discovered3. law the compulsory disclosure by a party to an action of relevant documents in his or her possession

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [oft N that]If someone makes a discovery, they become aware of something that they did not know about before. ⇒ I felt I'd made an incredible discovery. ⇒ ...the discovery that both his wife and son are HIV positive.2. variable nounIf someone makes a discovery, they are the first person to find or become aware of a place, substance, or scientific fact that no one knew about before. ⇒ In that year, two momentous discoveries were made. ⇒ ...the discovery of the ozone hole over the South Pole. ⇒ The fascination of discovery has never left him.3. variable nounIf someone makes a discovery, they recognize that an actor, musician, or other performer who is not well-known has talent. ⇒ His job is the discovery and promotion of new artists.4. variable nounWhen the discovery of people or objects happens, someone finds them, either by accident or as a result of looking for them. [+ of] ⇒ ...the discovery and destruction by soldiers of millions of marijuana plants.
