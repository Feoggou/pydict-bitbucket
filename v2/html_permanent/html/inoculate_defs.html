inoculate

(ɪˈnɒkjʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb1. to introduce (the causative agent of a disease) into the body of (a person or animal), in order to induce immunity2. (transitive) to introduce (microorganisms, esp bacteria) into (a culture medium)3. (transitive) to cause to be influenced or imbued, as with ideas or opinions
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inoculation (inˌocuˈlation) nouninoculative (inˈoculative) adjectiveinoculator (inˈocuˌlator) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin inoculāre to implant, from in-2 + oculus eye, bud
