passenger

(ˈpæsɪndʒə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a person travelling in a car, train, boat, etc, not driven by him or herb. (as modifier) ⇒ a passenger seat2. mainly British a member of a group or team who is a burden on the others through not participating fully in the work

Word Origin

C14: from Old French passager passing, from passage1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA passenger in a vehicle such as a bus, boat, or plane is a person who is travelling in it, but who is not driving it or working on it. [+ in] ⇒ Mr Fullemann was a passenger in the car when it crashed. ⇒ ...a flight from Milan with more than forty passengers on board. → client2. adjective [ADJ n]Passenger is used to describe something that is designed for passengers, rather than for drivers or goods. ⇒ I sat in the passenger seat. ⇒ ...a passenger train.
