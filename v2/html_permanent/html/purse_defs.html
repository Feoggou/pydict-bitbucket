purse

(pɜːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small bag or pouch, often made of soft leather, for carrying money, esp coins2. US and Canadian a woman's handbag3. anything resembling a small bag or pouch in form or function4. wealth; funds5. a sum of money that is offered, esp as a prizeverb6. (transitive) to contract (the mouth, lips, etc) into a small rounded shape

Word Origin

Old English purs, probably from Late Latin bursa bag, ultimately from Greek: leather

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA purse is a very small bag that people, especially women, keep their money in. [British]regional note:   in AM, use change purse2. countable nounA purse is a small bag that women carry. [US] ⇒ She looked at me and then reached in her purse for cigarettes.regional note:   in BRIT, use bag, handbag3. singular nounPurse is used to refer to the total amount of money that a country, family, or group has. ⇒ The money could simply go into the public purse, helping to lower taxes.4. verbIf you purse your lips, you move them into a small, rounded shape, usually because you disapprove of something or when you are thinking. [V n] ⇒ She pursed her lips in disapproval.
