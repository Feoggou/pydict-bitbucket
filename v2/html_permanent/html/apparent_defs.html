apparent

(əˈpærənt , əˈpɛər-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. readily seen or understood; evident; obvious2. (usually prenominal) seeming, as opposed to real ⇒ his apparent innocence belied his complicity in the crime3. physics as observed but ignoring such factors as the motion of the observer, changes in the environment, etc. Compare true (sense 9)
Derived forms
apˈparentness noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin appārēns, from appārēre to appear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]An apparent situation, quality, or feeling seems to exist, although you cannot be certain that it does exist. ⇒ I was a bit depressed by our apparent lack of progress. ⇒ There is at last an apparent end to the destructive price war.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If something is apparent to you, it is clear and obvious to you. ⇒ It has been apparent that in other areas standards have held up well. ⇒ The presence of a star is already apparent in the early film.3. →  for no apparent reason
