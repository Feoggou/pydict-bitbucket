transcendent

(trænˈsɛndənt )
Definitions
adjective1. exceeding or surpassing in degree or excellence2. a. (in the philosophy of Kant) beyond or before experience; a priorib. (of a concept) falling outside a given set of categoriesc. beyond consciousness or direct apprehension3. theology (of God) having continuous existence outside the created world. Compare immanent (sense 2)4. free from the limitations inherent in matternoun5. philosophy a transcendent thing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

transcendence (tranˈscendence) or transcendency (tranˈscendency) nountranscendently (tranˈscendently) adverbtranscendentness (tranˈscendentness) noun
