allay

(əˈleɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to relieve (pain, grief, etc) or be relieved2. (transitive) to reduce (fear, anger, etc)
Word origin
Old English ālecgan to put down, from lecgan to lay1

(əleɪ )
Word forms: allays , allaying , allayed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you allay someone's fears or doubts, you stop them feeling afraid or doubtful. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He did what he could to allay his wife's fears.
