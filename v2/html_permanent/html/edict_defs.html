edict

(ˈiːdɪkt )
Definitions
noun1. a decree, order, or ordinance issued by a sovereign, state, or any other holder of authority2. any formal or authoritative command, proclamation, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

edictal (eˈdictal) adjectiveedictally (eˈdictally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin ēdictum, from ēdīcere to declare
