mitigating

(ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(of a case, factor, or circumstance) lessening the impact of something such as a crime or mistake ⇒ a wide variety of mitigating measures to improve the living conditions of the people, ⇒ There are various mitigating factors.

mitigating

(mɪtɪgeɪtɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [ADJ n]Mitigating circumstances or factors make a bad action easier to understand and excuse, and may result in the person responsible being punished less severely. [law, formal] ⇒ The judge found that in her case there were mitigating circumstances. ⇒ There are various mitigating factors.
