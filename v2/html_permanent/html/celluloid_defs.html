celluloid

(ˈsɛljʊˌlɔɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a flammable thermoplastic material consisting of cellulose nitrate mixed with a plasticizer, usually camphor: used in sheets, rods, and tubes for making a wide range of articles2. a. a cellulose derivative used for coating filmb. one of the transparent sheets on which the constituent drawings of an animated film are preparedc. a transparent sheet used as an overlay in artworkd. cinema film
