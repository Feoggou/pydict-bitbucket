fender

(ˈfɛndə )
Definitions
noun1. a low metal frame which confines falling coals to the hearth2. mainly US a metal frame fitted to the front of locomotives to absorb shock, clear the track, etc3. a cushion-like device, such as a car tyre hung over the side of a vessel to reduce damage resulting from accidental contact or collision4. US and Canadian the part of a car body that surrounds the wheels. Also called (in Britain and certain other countries): wing
