crouch

(kraʊtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to bend low with the limbs pulled up close together, esp (of an animal) in readiness to pounce2. (intransitive) to cringe, as in humility or fear3. (transitive) to bend (parts of the body), as in humility or fearnoun4. the act of stooping or bending
Word origin
C14: perhaps from Old French crochir to become bent like a hook, from croche hook

Word forms: crouches , crouching , crouched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you are crouching, your legs are bent under you so that you are close to the ground and leaning forward slightly. [V prep/adv] ⇒ We were crouching in the bushes. [V prep/adv] ⇒ I crouched on the ground. [V-ed] ⇒ The man was crouched behind the Mercedes.Crouch is also a noun. ⇒ They walked in a crouch, each bent over close to the ground.Crouch down means the same as crouch. [V P] ⇒ He crouched down and reached under the mattress. [V P prep/adv] ⇒ He crouched down beside him.2. verbIf you crouch over something, you bend over it so that you are very near to it. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Meantime, here I crouch over a cup of tea in my unheated study. [V-ed] ⇒ ...sitting crouched over the steering wheel.
