fluke1

(fluːk )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: flue a flat bladelike projection at the end of the arm of an anchor2. either of the two lobes of the tail of a whale or related animal3. Also called: flue the barb or barbed head of a harpoon, arrow, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: perhaps a special use of fluke3 (in the sense: a flounder)

fluke2

(fluːk )
Definitions
noun1. an accidental stroke of luck2. any chance happeningverb3. (transitive) to gain, make, or hit by a fluke
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: of unknown origin

fluke3

(fluːk )
Definitions
noun1. any parasitic flatworm, such as the blood fluke and liver fluke, of the classes Monogenea and Digenea (formerly united in a single class Trematoda)2. another name for flounder2 (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flōc; related to Old Norse flōki flounder, Old Saxon flaka sole, Old High German flah smooth

fluke

Powered by COBUILD
(fluːk ) for learners of English
Word forms: flukes
Definitions
countable noun [usu sing, oft by N]If you say that something good is a fluke, you mean that it happened accidentally rather than by being planned or arranged. [informal] ⇒ The discovery was something of a fluke. ⇒ By sheer fluke, one of the shipowner's employees was in the city. [Also + of]
