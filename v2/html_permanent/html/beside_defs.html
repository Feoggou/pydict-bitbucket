beside

(bɪˈsaɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
preposition1. next to; at, by, or to the side of2. as compared with3. away from; wide of ⇒ beside the point4. archaic besides5. See beside oneselfadverb6. at, by, to, or along the side of something or someone
Word origin
Old English be sīdan; see by, side

(bɪsaɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. prepositionSomething that is beside something else is at the side of it or next to it. next to, by ⇒ On the table beside an empty plate was a pile of books. ⇒ I moved from behind my desk to sit beside her.2. → see also besides3. →  beside oneself4. → beside the point
