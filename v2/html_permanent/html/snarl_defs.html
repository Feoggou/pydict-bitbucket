snarl1

(snɑːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) (of an animal) to growl viciously, baring the teeth2. to speak or express (something) viciously or angrilynoun3. a vicious growl, utterance, or facial expression4. the act of snarling

Derived Forms

snarling (ˈsnarling) adjectivesnarlingly (ˈsnarlingly) adverbsnarly (ˈsnarly) adjective

Word Origin

C16: of Germanic origin; compare Middle Low German snarren, Middle Dutch snarren to drone

snarl2

(snɑːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a tangled mass of thread, hair, etc2. a complicated or confused state or situation3. a knot in woodverb4. (often foll by up) to be, become, or make tangled or complicated5. (transitive; often foll by up) to confuse mentally6. (transitive) to flute or emboss (metal) by hammering on a tool held against the under surface

Derived Forms

snarler (ˈsnarler) nounsnarly (ˈsnarly) adjective

Word Origin

C14: of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Swedish snarel noose, Old Norse snara snare1

snarl

Powered by COBUILD
(snɑːʳl )
Word forms: snarls , snarling , snarled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen an animal snarls, it makes a fierce, rough sound in its throat while showing its teeth. [V] ⇒ He raced ahead up into the bush, barking and snarling. [V + at] ⇒ The dogs snarled at the intruders.Snarl is also a noun. ⇒ With a snarl, the second dog made a dive for his heel.2. verbIf you snarl something, you say it in a fierce, angry way. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Let go of me,' he snarled. [V + at] ⇒ I vaguely remember snarling at someone who stepped on my foot. [V n] ⇒ 'Aubrey.' Hyde seemed almost to snarl the name.Snarl is also a noun. ⇒ His eyes flashed, and his lips were drawn back in a furious snarl.3. countable nounA snarl is a disorganized mass of things. [+ of] ⇒ She was tangled in a snarl of logs and branches. ⇒ A radio-link automatically advises it of traffic snarls and plots a detour.► →  snarl up
