wig

(wɪɡ )
Definitions
noun1. an artificial head of hair, either human or synthetic, worn to disguise baldness, as part of a theatrical or ceremonial dress, as a disguise, or for adornmentverbWord forms: wigs, wigging or wigged (transitive)2. obsolete to furnish with a wig3. British slang to berate severely
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wigged adjectivewigless (ˈwigless) adjectivewiglike (ˈwigˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C17: shortened from periwig
