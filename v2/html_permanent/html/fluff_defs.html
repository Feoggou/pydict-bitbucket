fluff

(flʌf )
Definitions
noun1. soft light particles, such as the down or nap of cotton or wool2. any light downy substance3. an object, matter, etc, of little importance; trifle4. informal a mistake, esp in speaking or reading lines or performing music5. informal a young woman (esp in the phrase a bit of fluff)verb6. to make or become soft and puffy by shaking or patting; puff up7. informal to make a mistake in performing (an action, dramatic speech, music, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: perhaps from flue2

fluff

Powered by COBUILD
(flʌf ) for learners of English
Word forms: fluffs, fluffing, fluffed
Definitions
1. uncount noun [oft n of N]Fluff consists of soft threads or fibres in the form of small, light balls or lumps. For example, you can refer to the fur of a small animal as fluff. ⇒ ...the nestbox which contained two chicks: just small grey balls of fluff. ⇒ She noticed some bits of fluff on the sleeve of her sweater.2. verbIf you fluff something that you are trying to do, you are unsuccessful or you do it badly. [informal] [V n] ⇒ She fluffed her interview at Oxford.3. verbIf you fluff things such as cushions or feathers, you get a lot of air into them, for example by shaking or brushing them, in order to make them seem larger and lighter. [V n] ⇒ She stood up and fluffed her hair, wiggling her fingers through it and then throwing it back.Fluff up means the same as fluff. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Take the pan off the heat and cover for 5 minutes to fluff up the rice. [Also V n P]
