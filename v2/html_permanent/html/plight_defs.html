plight1

(plaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna condition of extreme hardship, danger, etc

Word Origin

C14 plit, from Old French pleit fold, plait; probably influenced by Old English pliht peril, plight2

plight2

(plaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to give or pledge (one's word) ⇒ he plighted his word to attempt it2. to promise formally or pledge (allegiance, support, etc) ⇒ to plight aid3. See plight one's trothnoun4. archaic or dialect a solemn promise, esp of engagement; pledge

Derived Forms

plighter (ˈplighter) noun

Word Origin

Old English pliht peril; related to Old High German, German Pflicht duty

plight

Powered by COBUILD
(plaɪt )
Word forms: plights
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu sing]If you refer to someone's plight, you mean that they are in a difficult or distressing situation that is full of problems. [+ of] ⇒ ...the worsening plight of Third World countries plagued by debts.
