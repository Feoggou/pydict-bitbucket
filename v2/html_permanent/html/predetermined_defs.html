predetermined

(ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːmɪnd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. determined beforehand ⇒ The Prince's destiny was predetermined from the moment of his birth., ⇒ The capsules can be made to release the pesticides at a predetermined time.2. influenced or inclined towards an opinion beforehand; biased

predetermined

(priːdɪtɜːʳmɪnd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveIf you say that something is predetermined, you mean that its form or nature was decided by previous events or by people rather than by chance. ⇒ The Prince's destiny was predetermined from the moment of his birth. ⇒ The capsules can be made to release the pesticides at a predetermined time.
