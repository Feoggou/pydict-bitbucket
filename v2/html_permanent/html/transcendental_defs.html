transcendental

(ˌtrænsɛnˈdɛntəl )
Definitions
adjective1. transcendent, superior, or surpassing2. (in the philosophy of Kant)a. (of a judgment or logical deduction) being both synthetic and a priorib. of or relating to knowledge of the presuppositions of thought3. philosophy beyond our experience of phenomena, although not beyond potential knowledge4. theology surpassing the natural plane of reality or knowledge; supernatural or mystical
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

transcendentality (ˌtranscendenˈtality) nountranscendentally (ˌtranscenˈdentally) adverb

Definitions
adjective [usu ADJ n]Transcendental refers to things that lie beyond the practical experience of ordinary people, and cannot be discovered or understood by ordinary reasoning. ⇒ ...the transcendental nature of God.
