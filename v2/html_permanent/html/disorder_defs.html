disorder

(dɪsˈɔːdə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a lack of order; disarray; confusion2. a disturbance of public order or peace3. an upset of health; ailment4. a deviation from the normal system or orderverb (transitive)5. to upset the order of; disarrange; muddle6. to disturb the health or mind of

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA disorder is a problem or illness which affects someone's mind or body. ⇒ ...a rare nerve disorder that can cause paralysis of the arms. ⇒ ...a psychiatrist who specialises in eating disorders. ⇒ He appeared to be suffering from a severe mental disorder and had served a term in prison.2. uncount noun [oft in N]Disorder is a state of being untidy, badly prepared, or badly organized. ⇒ The emergency room was in disorder. ⇒ Inside all was disorder: drawers fallen out, shoes and boots scattered.3. variable nounDisorder is violence or rioting in public. ⇒ He called on the authorities to stop public disorder. ⇒ There are other forms of civil disorder–most notably, football hooliganism.
