analogous

(əˈnæləɡəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. similar or corresponding in some respect2. biology (of organs and parts) having the same function but different evolutionary origin ⇒ the paddle of a whale and the fin of a fish are analogous. Compare homologous (sense 4)3. linguistics formed by analogy ⇒ an analogous plural▶ USAGE The use of with after analogous should be avoided: swimming has no event that is analogous to (not with) the 100 metres in athletics
Derived forms
aˈnalogously adverbaˈnalogousness noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin analogus, from Greek analogos proportionate, from ana- + logos speech, ratio
