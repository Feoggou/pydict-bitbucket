sentimental

(ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. tending to indulge the emotions excessively2. making a direct appeal to the emotions, esp to romantic feelings3. relating to or characterized by sentiment

Derived Forms

ˌsentimentally (ˌsentiˈmentally) adverb

sentimental

Powered by COBUILD
(sentɪmentəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is sentimental feels or shows pity or love, sometimes to an extent that is considered exaggerated and foolish. [+ about] ⇒ I'm trying not to be sentimental about the past. ⇒ It's a very sentimental play.sentimentally graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ Childhood had less freedom and joy than we sentimentally attribute to it.sentimentality (sentɪmentælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ In this book there is no sentimentality.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Sentimental means relating to or involving feelings such as pity or love, especially for things in the past. ⇒ Our paintings and photographs are of sentimental value only. ⇒ Perhaps he has returned for sentimental reasons.
