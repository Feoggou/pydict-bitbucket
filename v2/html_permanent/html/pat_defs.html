pat1

(pæt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: pats, patting or patted1. to hit (something) lightly with the palm of the hand or some other flat surface ⇒ to pat a ball2. to slap (a person or animal) gently, esp on the back, as an expression of affection, congratulation, etc3. (transitive) to shape, smooth, etc, with a flat instrument or the palm4. (intransitive) to walk or run with light footsteps5. See pat someone on the backnoun6. a light blow with something flat7. a gentle slap8. a small mass of something ⇒ a pat of butter9. the sound made by a light stroke or light footsteps10. See pat on the back

Word Origin

C14: perhaps imitative

pat

Powered by COBUILD
(pæt )
Word forms: pats , patting , patted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you pat something or someone, you tap them lightly, usually with your hand held flat. [V n + on] ⇒ 'Don't you worry about any of this,' she said patting me on the knee. [V n] ⇒ The landlady patted her hair nervously. [V n adj] ⇒ Wash the lettuce and pat it dry.Pat is also a noun. [+ on] ⇒ He gave her an encouraging pat on the shoulder.2. countable nounA pat of butter or something else that is soft is a small lump of it.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that an answer or explanation is pat, you disapprove of it because it is too simple and sounds as if it has been prepared in advance. [disapproval] ⇒ There's no pat answer to that. ⇒ Despite the film's merits I felt it was too pat.4. →  a pat on the back/pat sb on the back5. →  have sth down pat/have sth off pat6. →  stand pat
