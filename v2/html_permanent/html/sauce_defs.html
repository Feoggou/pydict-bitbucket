sauce

(sɔːs )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any liquid or semiliquid preparation eaten with food to enhance its flavour2. anything that adds piquancy3. US and Canadian stewed fruit4. US dialect vegetables eaten with meat5. informal impudent language or behaviourverb (transitive)6. to prepare (food) with sauce7. to add zest to8. to make agreeable or less severe9. informal to be saucy to

Derived Forms

sauceless (ˈsauceless) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin salsus salted, from salīre to sprinkle with salt, from sal salt
