aloft

(əˈlɒft )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb, adjective (postpositive)1. in or into a high or higher place; up above2. nautical in or into the rigging of a vessel
Word origin
C12: from Old Norse ā lopt in the air; see lift1, loft

Definitions

adverb [ADV after v, be ADV]Something that is aloft is in the air or off the ground. [literary] ⇒ He held the trophy proudly aloft. ⇒ Four of the nine starting balloons were still aloft the next day.
