tempo

(ˈtɛmpəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -pos or -pi (-piː)1. the speed at which a piece or passage of music is meant to be played, usually indicated by a musical direction (tempo marking) or metronome marking2. rate or pace
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Italian, from Latin tempus time

[+ of] ⇒ ...owing to the slow tempo of change in an overwhelmingly rural country. ⇒ Both teams played with a lot of quality, pace and tempo.2. variable nounThe tempo of a piece of music is the speed at which it is played. ⇒ In a new recording, the Boston Philharmonic tried the original tempo. ⇒ Elgar supplied his works with precise indications of tempo.
