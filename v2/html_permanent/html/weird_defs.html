weird

(wɪəd )
Definitions
adjective1. suggestive of or relating to the supernatural; eerie2. strange or bizarre3. archaic of or relating to fate or the Fatesnoun4. archaic, mainly Scottisha. fate or destinyb. one of the Fates5. See dree one's weirdverb6. (transitive) Scottish to destine or ordain by fate; predict
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

weirdly (ˈweirdly) adverbweirdness (ˈweirdness) noun

Word Origin

Old English (ge)wyrd destiny; related to weorthan to become, Old Norse urthr bane, Old Saxon wurd; see worth2
