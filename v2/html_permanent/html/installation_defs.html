installation

(ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of installing or the state of being installed2. a large device, system, or piece of equipment that has been installed3. a military establishment usually serving in a support role4. an art exhibit often involving video or moving parts where the relation of the parts to the whole is important to the interpretation of the piece

Definitions
noun(Electrical engineering: Electrical power) An electrical installation is a combination of electrical equipment installed from a common electrical supply to fulfill a particular purpose. ⇒ Induction generators are often used in wind turbines and some micro hydro installations due to their ability to produce useful power at varying rotor speeds. ⇒ All single-phase loads in an electrical installation with a three-phase supply should be evenly and reasonably distributed among the phases. ⇒ An electrical installation is a combination of electrical equipment installed from a common electrical supply to fulfill a particular purpose.
