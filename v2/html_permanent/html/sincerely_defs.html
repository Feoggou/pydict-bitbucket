Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj]If you say or feel something sincerely, you really mean or feel it, and are not pretending. ⇒ 'Congratulations,' he said sincerely. ⇒ ...sincerely held religious beliefs. ⇒ 'I sincerely hope we shall meet again', he said. ⇒ He sincerely believed he was acting in both women's best interests.2. conventionIn Britain, people write 'Yours sincerely' before their signature at the end of a formal letter when they have addressed it to someone by name. In the United States, people usually write 'Sincerely yours' or 'Sincerely' instead. ⇒ Yours sincerely, James Brown.
