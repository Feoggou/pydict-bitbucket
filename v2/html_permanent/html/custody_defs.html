custody

(ˈkʌstədɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -dies1. the act of keeping safe or guarding, esp the right of guardianship of a minor2. the state of being held by the police; arrest (esp in the phrases in custody, take into custody)
Derived forms
custodial (kʌˈstəʊdɪəl ) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Latin custōdia, from custōs guard, defender

Powered by COBUILD
(kʌstədi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounCustody is the legal right to keep and look after a child, especially the right given to a child's mother or father when they get divorced. [+ of] ⇒ I'm going to go to court to get custody of the children. ⇒ Child custody is normally granted to the mother. ⇒ ...a bitter custody battle.2. →  in custody3. uncount nounIf someone is being held in a particular type of custody, they are being kept in a place that is similar to a prison. ⇒ The youngster got nine months' youth custody. ⇒ Barrett was taken into protective custody.
