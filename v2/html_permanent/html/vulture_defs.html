vulture

(ˈvʌltʃə )
Definitions
noun1. any of various very large diurnal birds of prey of the genera Neophron, Gyps, Gypaetus, etc, of Africa, Asia, and warm parts of Europe, typically having broad wings and soaring flight and feeding on carrion: family Accipitridae (hawks). See also griffon1 (sense 2), lammergeier2. any similar bird of the family Cathartidae of North, Central, and South America. See also condor, turkey buzzard3. a person or thing that preys greedily and ruthlessly on others, esp the helpless
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vulture-like (ˈvulture-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French voltour, from Latin vultur; perhaps related to Latin vellere to pluck, tear
