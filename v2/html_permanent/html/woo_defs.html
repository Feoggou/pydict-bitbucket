woo

(wuː )
Definitions
verbWord forms: woos, wooing or wooed1. to seek the affection, favour, or love of (a woman) with a view to marriage2. (transitive) to seek after zealously or hopefully ⇒ to woo fame3. (transitive) to bring upon oneself (good or evil results) by one's own action4. (transitive) to beg or importune (someone)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wooer (ˈwooer) nounwooing (ˈwooing) noun

Word Origin

Old English wōgian, of obscure origin

woo for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wuː )
Word forms: woos , wooing , wooed
Definitions
1. verbIf you woo people, you try to encourage them to help you, support you, or vote for you, for example by promising them things which they would like. [V n] ⇒ They wooed customers by offering low interest rates. [V n with adv] ⇒ They are trying to woo back electoral support.wooing uncount noun [oft poss N] [+ of] ⇒ This election has been marked so far by the candidates' wooing of each other's traditional political bases.2. verbIf a man woos a woman, he spends time with her and tries to persuade her to marry him. [old-fashioned] [V n] ⇒ The penniless author successfully wooed and married Fanny.wooing uncount noun [oft poss N] [+ of] ⇒ ...the hero's wooing of his beautiful cousin Roxanne.
