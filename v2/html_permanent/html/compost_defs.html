compost

(ˈkɒmpɒst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a mixture of organic residues such as decomposed vegetation, manure, etc, used as a fertilizer2. a mixture, normally of plant remains, peat, charcoal, etc, in which plants are grown, esp in pots3. rare a compound or mixtureverb (transitive)4. to make (vegetable matter) into compost5. to fertilize with compost
Word origin
C14: from Old French compost, from Latin compositus put together; see composite

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounCompost is a mixture of decayed plants and vegetable waste which is added to the soil to help plants grow. ⇒ ...a small compost heap.2. variable nounCompost is specially treated soil that you buy and use to grow seeds and plants in pots.3. verbTo compost things such as unwanted bits of plants means to make them into compost. [V n] ⇒ Cut down and compost spent cucumbers, tomatoes and other crops. [V n] ⇒ All garden waste should be composted and returned to the garden.composting uncount noun ⇒ Composting is the ideal way of getting rid of vegetable, garden and organic waste.
