epitome

(ɪˈpɪtəmɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a typical example of a characteristic or class; embodiment; personification ⇒ he is the epitome of sloth2. a summary of a written work; abstract
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

epitomical (ˌɛpɪˈtɒmɪkəl ) or epitomic (ˌepiˈtomic) adjective

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek epitomē, from epitemnein to abridge, from epi- + temnein to cut

epitome

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪpɪtəmi ) for learners of English
Definitions
singular nounIf you say that a person or thing is the epitome of something, you are emphasizing that they are the best possible example of a particular type of person or thing. [formal, emphasis] [+ of] ⇒ Maureen was the epitome of sophistication. ⇒ The Dorchester Hotel is the epitome of luxury in the heart of London.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
