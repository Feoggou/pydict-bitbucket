stuffy

(ˈstʌfɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: stuffier or stuffiest1. lacking fresh air2. excessively dull, staid, or conventional3. (of the nasal passages) blocked with mucus

Derived Forms

stuffily (ˈstuffily) adverbstuffiness (ˈstuffiness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(stʌfi )
Word forms: stuffier , stuffiest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveStuffy people or institutions are formal and old-fashioned. [disapproval] ⇒ Why were grown-ups always so stuffy and slow to recognize good ideas? ⇒ ...a firm of lawyers in Lincoln's Inn, immensely stuffy and respectable. ⇒ ...stuffy attitudes.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf it is stuffy in a place, it is unpleasantly warm and there is not enough fresh air. ⇒ It was hot and stuffy in the classroom.3. graded adjective [usu ADJ n]If you have a stuffy nose, your nose is blocked, usually because you have a cold. bunged up ⇒ Aromatic capsules are great for easing the discomfort of a stuffy nose.stuffiness uncount noun ⇒ Peppermint leaves are believed to relieve tiredness and nasal stuffiness.
