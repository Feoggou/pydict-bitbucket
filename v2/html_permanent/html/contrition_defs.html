contrition

(kənˈtrɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. deeply felt remorse; penitence2. Christianity detestation of past sins and a resolve to make amends, either from love of God (perfect contrition) or from hope of heaven (imperfect contrition)
Example sentences containing 'contrition'
Louise was weeping quietly now, reading the passages of contrition for the damage inflicted on her and her children. Moore, Margaret FORESTS OF THE NIGHTNothing could save him, for there was no contrition in his heart. Penman, Sharon HERE BE DRAGONSShe had replaced the receiver before I could launch into my overwrought hymn of contrition. Alex George LOVE YOU MADLYHe went up the steps and entered the downstairs hallway; he paused, full of contrition at the thought of Eva. Wright, Richard THE OUTSIDER
