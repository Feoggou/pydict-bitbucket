four hundred

in American English
Definitions
Us [also F- H-] the exclusive social set of a particular place, preceded by the
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

popularized by C. J. Allen, New York Sun society reporter, from a remark by Ward McAllister: “There are only 400 people in New York that one really knows”; prob. from limited capacity of Mrs. J. J. Astor's ballroom
