errand

(ˈɛrənd )
Definitions
noun1. a short trip undertaken to perform a necessary task or commission (esp in the phrase run errands)2. the purpose or object of such a trip
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English ǣrende; related to ār messenger, Old Norse erendi message, Old High German ārunti, Swedish ärende

errand

Powered by COBUILD
(erənd ) for learners of English
Word forms: errands
Definitions
1. countable nounAn errand is a short trip that you make in order to do a job for someone, for example when you go to a shop to buy something for them. ⇒ She went off on some errand. ⇒ She had a more urgent errand.2. →  to run an errand
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
