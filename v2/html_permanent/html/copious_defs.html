copious

(ˈkəʊpɪəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. abundant; extensive in quantity2. having or providing an abundant supply3. full of words, ideas, etc; profuse
Derived forms
copiously (ˈcopiously) adverbcopiousness (ˈcopiousness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin cōpiōsus well supplied, from cōpia abundance, from ops wealth

graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A copious amount of something is a large amount of it. ⇒ I went out for a meal last night and drank copious amounts of red wine. ⇒ He attended his lectures and took copious notes.copiously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV -ed] ⇒ The victims were bleeding copiously.
