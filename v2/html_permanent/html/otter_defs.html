otter

(ˈɒtə )
Definitions
noun plural -ters or -ter1. any freshwater carnivorous musteline mammal of the subfamily Lutrinae, esp Lutra lutra (Eurasian otter), typically having smooth fur, a streamlined body, and webbed feet2. the fur of any of these animals3. Also called: otter board a type of fishing tackle consisting of a weighted board to which hooked and baited lines are attachedverb4. to fish using an otter
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English otor; related to Old Norse otr, Old High German ottar, Greek hudra, Sanskrit udra
