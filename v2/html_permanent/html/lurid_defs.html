lurid

(ˈlʊərɪd , ˈljʊərɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. vivid in shocking detail; sensational2. horrible in savagery or violence3. pallid in colour; wan4. glowing with an unnatural glare
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

luridly (ˈluridly) adverbluridness (ˈluridness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin lūridus pale yellow; probably related to lūtum a yellow vegetable dye

lurid

Powered by COBUILD
(ljʊərɪd , US lʊrɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you say that something is lurid, you are critical of it because it involves a lot of violence, sex, or shocking detail. [disapproval] ⇒ ...lurid accounts of Claire's sexual exploits. ⇒ Some reports have contained lurid accounts of deaths and mutilations.luridly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ His cousin was soon cursing luridly.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as lurid, you do not like it because it is very brightly coloured. [disapproval] ⇒ She took care to paint her toe nails a lurid red or orange.luridly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ It had a high ceiling and a luridly coloured square of carpet on the floor.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
