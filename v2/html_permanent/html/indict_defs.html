indict

(ɪnˈdaɪt )
Definitions
verb(transitive) criminal law to charge (a person) with crime, esp formally in writing; accuseSee note at indite
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

indictee (ˌindictˈee) nounindicter (inˈdicter) or indictor (inˈdictor) noun

Word Origin

C14: alteration of enditen to indite

indict

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndaɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: indicts , indicting , indicted
Definitions
verb [usu passive]If someone is indicted for a crime, they are officially charged with it. [mainly US, law] [be V-ed + on] ⇒ He was later indicted on corruption charges. [be V-ed + for] ⇒ She has been indicted for possessing cocaine. [be V-ed] ⇒ The police said he'd been formally indicted on Saturday. [V-ed] ⇒ Attorneys for the indicted officers tried to delay the trial.
