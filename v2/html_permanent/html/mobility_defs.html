mobility

(məʊˈbɪlɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the ability to move physically ⇒ a knee operation has restricted his mobility, ⇒ mobility is part of physical education2. sociology (of individuals or social groups) movement within or between classes and occupations. See also vertical mobility, horizontal mobility3. time that a resident of a secure unit is allowed to spend outside the unit, as preparation for an eventual return to society
Example sentences containing 'mobility'
If there were guys around waiting to put the boots to me, mobility would be more valuable than warmth. Wood, Ted SNOWJOBShe had hired a car because this gave her so much more mobility. Forbes, Colin COVER STORYTwo cars gave them the freedom and mobility to go their separate ways on the Island when they felt like it. Wright, Eric A BODY SURROUNDED BY WATER
