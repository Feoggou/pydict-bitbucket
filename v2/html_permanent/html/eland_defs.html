eland

(ˈiːlənd )
Definitions
noun1. a large spiral-horned antelope, Taurotragus oryx, inhabiting bushland in eastern and southern Africa. It has a dewlap and a hump on the shoulders and is light brown with vertical white stripes2. See giant eland
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: via Afrikaans from Dutch eland elk; related to Old Slavonic jeleni stag, Greek ellos fawn
