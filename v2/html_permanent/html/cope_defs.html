cope1

(kəʊp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive; foll by with) to contend (against)2. (intransitive) to deal successfully with or handle a situation; manage ⇒ she coped well with the problem3. (transitive) archaica. to deal withb. to meet in battle
Word origin
C14: from Old French coper to strike, cut, from coup blow; see coup1

cope

Powered by COBUILD
(koʊp )
Word forms: copes , coping , coped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you cope with a problem or task, you deal with it successfully. [V + with] ⇒ It was amazing how my mother coped with bringing up three children on less than three pounds a week. [V] ⇒ The problems were an annoyance, but we managed to cope.2. verbIf you have to cope with an unpleasant situation, you have to accept it or bear it. [V with n/-ing] ⇒ Never before has the industry had to cope with war and recession at the same time. [V + with] ⇒ She has had to cope with losing all her previous status and money.3. verbIf a machine or a system can cope with something, it is large enough or complex enough to deal with it satisfactorily. [V + with] ⇒ New blades have been designed to cope with the effects of dead insects. [V with n] ⇒ The country's prisons are filled with drug-takers, and cannot cope with the numbers. [V] ⇒ The speed of economic change has been so great that the tax-collecting system has been unable to cope.4. countable nounA cope is a long sleeveless piece of clothing worn by some Christian priests on special occasions.
