sensible

(ˈsɛnsɪbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having or showing good sense or judgment ⇒ a sensible decision2. (of clothing) serviceable; practical ⇒ sensible shoes3. having the capacity for sensation; sensitive4. capable of being apprehended by the senses5. perceptible to the mind6. (sometimes foll by of) having perception; aware ⇒ sensible of your kindness7. readily perceived; considerable ⇒ a sensible differencenoun8. Also called: sensible note a less common term for leading note

Derived Forms

sensibleness (ˈsensibleness) nounsensibly (ˈsensibly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, from Late Latin sēnsibilis, from Latin sentīre to sense

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSensible actions or decisions are good because they are based on reasons rather than emotions. ⇒ It might be sensible to get a solicitor. ⇒ The sensible thing is to leave them alone. ⇒ ...sensible advice.sensibly (sensɪbli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He sensibly decided to lie low for a while. ⇒ They have very sensibly adjusted their diet.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSensible people behave in a sensible way. ⇒ She was a sensible girl and did not panic. [+ about] ⇒ Oh come on, let's be sensible about this. ⇒ I'm trying to persuade you to be more sensible.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Sensible shoes or clothes are practical and strong rather than fashionable and attractive. ⇒ Wear loose clothing and sensible footwear.sensibly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV -ed] ⇒ They were not sensibly dressed.
