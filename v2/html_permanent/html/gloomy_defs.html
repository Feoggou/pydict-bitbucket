gloomy

(ˈɡluːmɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: gloomier or gloomiest1. dark or dismal2. causing depression, dejection, or gloom ⇒ gloomy news3. despairing; sad
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gloomily (ˈgloomily) adverbgloominess (ˈgloominess) noun

gloomy

Powered by COBUILD
(gluːmi ) for learners of English
Word forms: gloomier , gloomiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a place is gloomy, it is almost dark so that you cannot see very well. ⇒ Inside it's gloomy after all that sunshine. ⇒ ...this huge gloomy church.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf people are gloomy, they are unhappy and have no hope. ⇒ Miller is gloomy about the fate of the serious playwright in America.gloomily graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He tells me gloomily that he has been called up for army service.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a situation is gloomy, it does not give you much hope of success or happiness. ⇒ ...a gloomy picture of an economy sliding into recession. ⇒ Officials say the outlook for next year is gloomy.
