factitious

(fækˈtɪʃəs )
Definitions
adjective1. artificial rather than natural ⇒ factitious demands created by the mass media2. not genuine; sham ⇒ factitious enthusiasm
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

factitiously (facˈtitiously) adverbfactitiousness (facˈtitiousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin factīcius, from facere to make, do

Similar Words

artificial, affected, engineered, made-up, assumed, manufactured, put-on, false, fake, mock, synthetic, imitation, simulated, sham, unnatural, pseudo (informal), unreal, fabricated, counterfeited, spurious, insincere, pinchbeck, phoney or phony (informal)
