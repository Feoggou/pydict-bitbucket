byword

(ˈbaɪˌwɜːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person, place, or thing regarded as a perfect or proverbial example of something ⇒ their name is a byword for good service2. an object of scorn or derision3. a common saying; proverb
Word origin
Old English bīwyrde; see by, word; compare Old High German pīwurti, from Latin prōverbium proverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounSomeone or something that is a byword for a particular quality is well known for having that quality. ⇒ ...a region that had become a byword for violence and degeneracy. [+ for] ⇒ ...the Rolls-Royce brand name, a byword for quality.2. countable nounA byword is a word or phrase which people often use. [+ of] ⇒ Loyalty and support became the bywords of the day.
