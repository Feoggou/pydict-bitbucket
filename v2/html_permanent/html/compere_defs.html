compere

or compère (ˈkɒmpɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
Britishnoun1. a master of ceremonies who introduces cabaret, television acts, etcverb2. to act as a compere (for)
Word origin
C20: from French, literally: godfather; see compeer, compadre

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA compere is the person who introduces the people taking part in a radio or television show or a live show. [British] regional note:   in AM, use emcee2. verbThe person who comperes a show introduces the people who take part in it. [British] [V n] ⇒ Sarita Sagharwal compered the programme. [V] ⇒ They asked Paul to compere.regional note:   in AM, use emcee
