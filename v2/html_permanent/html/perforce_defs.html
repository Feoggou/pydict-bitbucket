Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV with v]Perforce is used to indicate that something happens or is the case because it cannot be prevented or avoided. [old-fashioned] ⇒ The war in 1939 perforce ushered in an era of more grime and drabness.
Example sentences containing 'perforce'
This scent breathed in, Rebecca is brought back, perforce , to a memory. Sean Thomas THE CHEEK PERFORATION DANCEShe perforce sat in the chair opposite him, clasping her hands in her lap. Fraser, Anthea THE GOSPEL MAKERS`My colleague and I are from the police," he said perforce , speaking slowly and distinctly. Fraser, Anthea DEATH SPEAKS SOFTLYTo reach the doors giving onto the gardens they had, perforce , to travel the length of the room. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
