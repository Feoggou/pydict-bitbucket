reconstitute

(riːˈkɒnstɪˌtjuːt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to restore (food, etc) to its former or natural state or a semblance of it, as by the addition of water to a concentrate ⇒ reconstituted lemon juice2. to reconstruct; form again
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Powered by COBUILD
(riːkɒnstɪtjuːt , US -tuːt ) for learners of English
Word forms: reconstitutes , reconstituting , reconstituted
Definitions
1. verb [usu passive]If an organization or state is reconstituted, it is formed again in a different way. [be V-ed] ⇒ Slowly Jewish communities were reconstituted and Jewish life began anew. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the reconstituted Communist Party, now called the Party of Democratic Socialism.reconstitution (riːkɒnstɪtjuːʃən , US -tuːʃ- ) uncount noun [oft N of n] ⇒ The report recommended suspension and reconstitution of the district party under redrafted rules.2. verbTo reconstitute dried food means to add water to it so that it can be eaten. [V n] ⇒ To reconstitute dried tomatoes, simmer in plain water until they are tender. [V-ed] ⇒ Try eating reconstituted dried prunes, figs or apricots.
