re-examine

(ˌriːɪɡˈzæmɪn )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to examine again2. law to examine (one's own witness) again upon matters arising out of his cross-examination
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
regional note:   in AM, also use reexamineverbIf a person or group of people re-examines their ideas, beliefs, or attitudes, they think about them carefully because they are no longer sure if they are correct. [V n] ⇒ We may reexamine our goals or our methods for attaining our goals. [V n] ⇒ Her husband and children will also have to re-examine their expectations.re-examination Word forms: re-examinations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ It was time for a re-examination of the situation. ⇒ Energy policy is badly in need of radical re-examination.
