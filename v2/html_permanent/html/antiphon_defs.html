antiphon

(ˈæntɪfən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a short passage, usually from the Bible, recited or sung as a response after certain parts of a liturgical service2. a psalm, hymn, etc, chanted or sung in alternate parts3. any response or answer
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin antiphōna sung responses, from Late Greek, plural of antiphōnon (something) responsive, from antiphōnos, from anti- + phōnē sound
