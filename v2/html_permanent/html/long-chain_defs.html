long-chain

Powered by COBUILD
(lɔŋ tʃeɪn) in the Oil and Gas Industry
Definitions
adjective(Extractive engineering: Refinery processes, Product) Long-chain describes a molecule with a long chain of atoms.
COLLOCATIONS: 
~ hydrocarbons~ molecules ⇒ In cracking, long-chain hydrocarbons are broken down into shorter ones. ⇒ At the refinery, long-chain hydrocarbons are isolated from the crude oil by fractional distillation, and they are then broken into a mixture of shorter-chain hydrocarbons by cracking. ⇒ Long-chain describes a molecule with a long chain of atoms.
