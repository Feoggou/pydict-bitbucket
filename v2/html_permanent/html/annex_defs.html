annex

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (æˈnɛks ) (transitive)1. to join or add, esp to something larger; attach2. to add (territory) by conquest or occupation3. to add or append as a condition, warranty, etc4. to appropriate without permissionnoun (ˈænɛks )5. a variant spelling (esp US) of annexe
Derived forms
anˈnexable adjective
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin annexāre, from Latin annectere to attach to, from nectere to join
