enclosure

or inclosure (ɪnˈkləʊʒə )
Definitions
noun1. the act of enclosing or state of being enclosed2. a region or area enclosed by or as if by a fence3. a. the act of appropriating land, esp common land, by putting a hedge or other barrier around itb. history such acts as were carried out at various periods in England, esp between the 12th and 14th centuries and finally in the 18th and 19th centuries4. a fence, wall, etc, that serves to enclose5. something, esp a supporting document, enclosed within an envelope or wrapper, esp together with a letter6. British a section of a sports ground, racecourse, etc, allotted to certain spectators
