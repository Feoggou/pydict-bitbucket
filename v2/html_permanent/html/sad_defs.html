sad

(sæd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: sadder or saddest1. feeling sorrow; unhappy2. causing, suggestive, or expressive of such feelings ⇒ a sad story3. unfortunate; unsatisfactory; shabby; deplorable ⇒ her clothes were in a sad state4. British informal ludicrously contemptible; pathetic ⇒ he's a sad, boring little wimp5. (of pastry, cakes, etc) not having risen fully; heavy6. (of a colour) lacking brightness; dull or dark7. archaic serious; graveverb8. New Zealand to express sadness or displeasure strongly

Derived Forms

sadly (ˈsadly) adverbsadness (ˈsadness) noun

Word Origin

Old English sæd weary; related to Old Norse sathr, Gothic saths, Latin satur, satis enough

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ that/to-inf]If you are sad, you feel unhappy, usually because something has happened that you do not like. ⇒ The relationship had been important to me and its loss left me feeling sad and empty. ⇒ I'm sad that Julie's marriage is on the verge of splitting up. ⇒ I'd grown fond of our little house and felt sad to leave it. [+ about] ⇒ I'm sad about my toys getting burned in the fire.sadly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ ...a gallant man who will be sadly missed by all his comrades. ⇒ Judy said sadly, 'He has abandoned me.'sadness uncount noun ⇒ It is with a mixture of sadness and joy that I say farewell.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Sad stories and sad news make you feel sad. ⇒ ...a desperately humorous, impossibly sad novel. ⇒ I received the sad news that he had been killed in a motor-cycle accident.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA sad event or situation is unfortunate or undesirable. ⇒ It's a sad truth that children are the biggest victims of passive smoking.sadly adverb [usu ADV adj] ⇒ Sadly, bamboo plants die after flowering. ⇒ Things are sadly different in Britain.4. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as sad, you do not have any respect for them and think their behaviour or ideas are ridiculous. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ ...sad old bikers and youngsters who think that Jim Morrison is God.5. →  sad to say
