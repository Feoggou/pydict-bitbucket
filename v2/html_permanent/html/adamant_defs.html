adamant

(ˈædəmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. unshakable in purpose, determination, or opinion; unyielding2. a less common word for adamantine (sense 1)noun3. any extremely hard or apparently unbreakable substance4. a legendary stone said to be impenetrable, often identified with the diamond or loadstone
Derived forms
adamantly (ˈadamantly) adverb
Word origin
Old English: from Latin adamant-, stem of adamas, from Greek; literal meaning perhaps: unconquerable, from a-1 + daman to tame, conquer
