coin

(kɔɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a metal disc or piece used as money2. metal currency, as opposed to securities, paper currency, etc ▶ Related adjective: nummary3. architecture a variant spelling of quoin4. See pay someone back in his or her own coin5. See the other side of the coinverb6. (transitive) to make or stamp (coins)7. (transitive) to make into a coin8. (transitive) to fabricate or invent (words, etc)9. (transitive) informal to make (money) rapidly (esp in the phrase coin it in)10. See to coin a phrase
Derived forms
coinable (ˈcoinable) adjectivecoiner (ˈcoiner) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French: stamping die, from Latin cuneus wedge

Word forms: coins , coining , coined
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA coin is a small piece of metal which is used as money. ⇒ ...50 pence coins. ⇒ ...Frederick's gold coin collection.2. verbIf you coin a word or a phrase, you are the first person to say it. [V n] ⇒ Jaron Lanier coined the term 'virtual reality' and pioneered its early development. [V n] ⇒ The word 'lunatic' was coined to describe people who went mad at the full moon.3. verb [usu cont]If you say that someone is coining it or is coining money, you are emphasizing that they are making a lot of money very quickly, often without really earning it. [informal, emphasis] [V it] ⇒ Many private colleges are coining it. [V n] ⇒ One wine shop is coining money selling Wembley-label champagne.Coining in means the same as coining. [V it P] ⇒ She's coining it in with a $10 million contract with Revlon.4. →  to coin a phrase5. →  the other side of the coin6. →  two sides of the same coin
