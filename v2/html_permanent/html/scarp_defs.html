scarp

(skɑːp )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a steep slope, esp one formed by erosion or faulting; escarpment. See also cuesta2. fortifications the side of a ditch cut nearest to and immediately below a rampartverb3. (tr; often passive) to wear or cut so as to form a steep slope

Word Origin

C16: from Italian scarpa

He pulled his sled across the shoreline, up across the frozen sands and dune scarp. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODThey hit one scarp particularly hard and Rex was convinced they'd blown a tire, but the truck rattled on, undeterred. Gregg Andrew Hurwitz MINUTES TO BURN
