replete

(rɪˈpliːt )
Definitions
adjective (usually postpositive)1. (often foll by with) copiously supplied (with); abounding (in)2. having one's appetite completely or excessively satisfied by food and drink; stuffed; gorged; satiated
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

repletely (reˈpletely) adverbrepleteness (reˈpleteness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin replētus, from replēre to refill, from re- + plēre to fill

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ with n]To be replete with something means to be full of it. [formal] ⇒ The Harbor was replete with boats. ⇒ History is replete with examples of populations out of control.2. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are replete, you are pleasantly full of food and drink. [formal] ⇒ Replete, guests can then retire to the modern conservatory for coffee.
