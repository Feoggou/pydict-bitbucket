incapacity

(ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. lack of power, strength, or capacity; inability2. lawa. legal disqualification or ineligibilityb. a circumstance causing this

incapacity

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkəpæsɪti ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount noun [oft with poss, oft N to-inf]The incapacity of a person, society, or system to do something is their inability to do it. [formal] ⇒ ...Europe's incapacity to take collective action. ⇒ Patients with no mental incapacity can refuse treatment.
