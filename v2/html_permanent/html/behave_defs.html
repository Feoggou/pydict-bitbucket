behave

(bɪˈheɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to act or function in a specified or usual way2. to conduct (oneself) in a specified way ⇒ he behaved badly towards her3. to conduct (oneself) properly or as desired ⇒ the child behaved himself all day
Word origin
C15: see be-, have

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbThe way that you behave is the way that you do and say things, and the things that you do and say. [V prep/adv] ⇒ I couldn't believe these people were behaving in this way. [V prep/adv] ⇒ He'd behaved badly.2. verbIf you behave or behave yourself, you act in the way that people think is correct and proper. be good [V] ⇒ You have to behave. [V pron-refl] ⇒ They were expected to behave themselves.3. verbIn science, the way that something behaves is the things that it does. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Under certain conditions, electrons can behave like waves rather than particles.
