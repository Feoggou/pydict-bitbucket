burdened

(ˈbɜːdənd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. literary oppressed ⇒ Nicaragua was burdened with a foreign debt of $11 billion., ⇒ burdened by guilt and regret, ⇒ We humans are the only animals who are burdened with the knowledge that we're going to die.2. physically encumbered ⇒ She arrived burdened with bags and baskets., ⇒ Burdened with packages, she hired a taxi., ⇒ burdened with heavy camera gear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are burdened with something, it causes you a lot of worry or hard work. [+ with] ⇒ Nicaragua was burdened with a foreign debt of $11 billion. [+ by] ⇒ They may be burdened by guilt and regret.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as burdened with a heavy load, you are emphasizing that it is very heavy and that they are holding it or carrying it with difficulty. [emphasis] weighed down ⇒ ...a stocky man who did everything at the run, even when burdened with heavy camera gear. [+ by] ⇒ Anna and Rosemary arrived burdened by bags and food baskets. [Also + with]
