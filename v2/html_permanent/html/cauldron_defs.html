cauldron

or caldron (ˈkɔːldrən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna large pot used for boiling, esp one with handles
Word origin
C13: from earlier cauderon, from Anglo-French, from Latin caldārium hot bath, from calidus warm

Word forms: cauldrons
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA cauldron is a very large, round metal pot used for cooking over a fire. In stories and fairy tales, a cauldron is used by witches for their spells.2. countable noun [usu sing, oft N of n]If you describe a situation as a cauldron, you mean that it is unstable or dangerous. [journalism] ⇒ A thin veneer of law and order barely keeps the seething, bubbling cauldron of chaos and anarchy in check.
