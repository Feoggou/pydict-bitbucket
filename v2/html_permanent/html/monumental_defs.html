monumental

(ˌmɒnjʊˈmɛntəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. like a monument, esp in large size, endurance, or importance ⇒ a monumental work of art2. of, relating to, or being a monument3. informal (intensifier) ⇒ monumental stupidity

Derived Forms

ˌmonumentality (ˌmonumenˈtality) nounˌmonumentally (ˌmonuˈmentally) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use monumental to emphasize the size or extent of something. [emphasis] ⇒ It had been a monumental blunder to give him the assignment. ⇒ ...a series of monumental disappointments.monumentally graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed, oft ADV after v] ⇒ Suddenly it was monumentally successful. ⇒ ...the most monumentally hideous night of my life.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a book or musical work as monumental, you are emphasizing that it is very large and impressive, and is likely to be important for a long time. [emphasis] ⇒ ...his monumental work on Chinese astronomy.3. adjective [ADJ n]A monumental building or sculpture is very large and impressive. ⇒ ...monumental sculpture.
