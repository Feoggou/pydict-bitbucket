negation

(nɪˈɡeɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the opposite or absence of something2. a negative thing or condition3. the act or an instance of negating4. logica. the operator that forms one sentence from another and corresponds to the English notb. a sentence so formed. It is usually written –p, ~p, p̄ or ⇁p, where p is the given sentence, and is false when the given sentence is true, and true when it is false

negation for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪgeɪʃən )
Definitions
1. singular nounThe negation of something is its complete opposite or something which destroys it or makes it lose its effect. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Unintelligible legislation is the negation of the rule of law and of parliamentary democracy.2. uncount nounNegation is disagreement, refusal, or denial. [formal] ⇒ The editor grimaced, gesturing in negation. ⇒ Isabella shook her head, but in bewilderment, not negation.
