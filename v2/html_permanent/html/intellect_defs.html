intellect

(ˈɪntɪˌlɛkt )
Definitions
noun1. the capacity for understanding, thinking, and reasoning, as distinct from feeling or wishing2. a mind or intelligence, esp a brilliant one ⇒ his intellect is wasted on that job3. informal a person possessing a brilliant mind; brain4. those possessing the greatest mental power ⇒ the intellect of a nation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intellective (ˌintelˈlective) adjectiveintellectively (ˌintelˈlectively) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin intellectus comprehension, intellect, from intellegere to understand; see intelligence

intellect

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntɪlekt ) for learners of English
Word forms: intellects
Definitions
1. variable nounIntellect is the ability to understand or deal with ideas and information. ⇒ Do the emotions develop in parallel with the intellect? ⇒ The intellect is not the most important thing in life.2. variable noun [oft poss N]Intellect is the quality of being very intelligent or clever. ⇒ Her intellect is famed far and wide. ⇒ The members of the committee described Gates as a man of keen intellect.3. countable nounIf you describe someone as an intellect, you mean that they are very intelligent. ⇒ My boss isn't a great intellect.
