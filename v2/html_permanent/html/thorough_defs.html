thorough

(ˈθʌrə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. carried out completely and carefully ⇒ a thorough search2. (prenominal) utter ⇒ a thorough bore3. painstakingly careful ⇒ my work is thorough

Derived Forms

thoroughly (ˈthoroughly) adverbthoroughness (ˈthoroughness) noun

Word Origin

Old English thurh; related to Old Frisian thruch, Old Saxon thuru, Old High German duruh; see through

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A thorough action or activity is one that is done very carefully and in a detailed way so that nothing is forgotten. ⇒ We are making a thorough investigation. ⇒ This very thorough survey goes back to 1784. ⇒ How thorough is the assessment?thoroughly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Food that is being offered hot must be reheated thoroughly. ⇒ ...a thoroughly researched and illuminating biography.thoroughness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ The thoroughness of the evaluation process we went through was impressive.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Someone who is thorough is always very careful in their work, so that nothing is forgotten. ⇒ Martin would be a good judge, I thought. He was calm and thorough. ⇒ The men were expert, thorough and careful.thoroughness uncount noun ⇒ His thoroughness and attention to detail is legendary.3. adjectiveThorough is used to emphasize the great degree or extent of something. [emphasis] ⇒ I was a thorough little academic snob. ⇒ We regard the band as a thorough shambles.thoroughly graded adverb & adverb [ADV before v, ADV adj] ⇒ I thoroughly enjoy your programme. ⇒ We returned home thoroughly contented.
