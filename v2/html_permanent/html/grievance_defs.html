grievance

(ˈɡriːvəns )
Definitions
noun1. a real or imaginary wrong causing resentment and regarded as grounds for complaint2. a feeling of resentment or injustice at having been unfairly treated3. obsolete affliction or hardship
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15 grevance, from Old French, from grever to grieve1

Definitions
variable nounIf you have a grievance about something that has happened or been done, you believe that it was unfair. ⇒ They had a legitimate grievance. ⇒ The main grievance of the drivers is the imposition of higher fees for driving licences. ⇒ ...a deep sense of grievance.
