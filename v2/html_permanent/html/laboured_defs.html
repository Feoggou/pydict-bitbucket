laboured

or US labored (ˈleɪbəd )
Definitions
adjective1. (of breathing) performed with difficulty2. showing effort; contrived; lacking grace or fluency

laboured

Powered by COBUILD
(leɪbəʳd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone's breathing is laboured, it is slow and seems to take a lot of effort. ⇒ From his slow walk and laboured breathing, Ginny realized he was far from well.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something such as someone's writing or speech is laboured, they have put too much effort into it so it seems awkward and unnatural. ⇒ The prose of his official communications was so laboured, pompous and verbose. ⇒ Daniel's few encounters with Gold had been characterized by a laboured politeness.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
