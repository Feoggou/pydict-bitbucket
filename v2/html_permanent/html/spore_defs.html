spore

(spɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a reproductive body, produced by bacteria, fungi, various plants, and some protozoans, that develops into a new individual. A sexual spore is formed after the fusion of gametes and an asexual spore is the result of asexual reproduction2. a germ cell, seed, dormant bacterium, or similar bodyverb3. (intransitive) to produce, carry, or release spores

Word Origin

C19: from New Latin spora, from Greek: a sowing; related to Greek speirein to sow
