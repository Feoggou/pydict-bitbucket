strangle

(ˈstræŋɡəl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to kill by compressing the windpipe; throttle2. (transitive) to prevent or inhibit the growth or development of ⇒ to strangle originality3. (transitive) to suppress (an utterance) by or as if by swallowing suddenly ⇒ to strangle a cry

Word Origin

C13: via Old French, ultimately from Greek strangalē a halter

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo strangle someone means to kill them by squeezing their throat tightly so that they cannot breathe. [V n] ⇒ He tried to strangle a border policeman and steal his gun. [V n] ⇒ He was almost strangled by his parachute harness straps.strangler (stræŋgləʳ )Word forms: stranglers countable noun ⇒ ...a vigilante group looking for a strangler who's terrorising the town.2. verbTo strangle something means to prevent it from succeeding or developing. [V n] ⇒ The country's economic plight is strangling its scientific institutions. [V n] ⇒ His creative drive has been strangled by his sense of political guilt.
