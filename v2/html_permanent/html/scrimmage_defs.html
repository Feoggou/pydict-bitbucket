scrimmage

(ˈskrɪmɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a rough or disorderly struggle2. American football the clash of opposing linemen at every downverb3. (intransitive) to engage in a scrimmage4. (transitive) to put (the ball) into a scrimmage
Derived forms
scrimmager (ˈscrimmager) noun
Word origin
C15: from earlier scrimish, variant of skirmish
Example sentences containing 'scrimmage'
Shoulders hunched, it gave a dead-eyed stare over the line of scrimmage which was the chain across the opening of the port gates. Robert Wilson A DARKENING STAINA woman in her forties, just ahead of them in the genteel scrimmage for wine, turned her head and said, `Oh, I must agree with you. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFE"On the first play from scrimmage , Oregon's star running back ran sixty-five yards and Oregon was only down by thirteen. Phillip Margolin TIES THAT BIND
