acquit

(əˈkwɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -quits, -quitting or -quitted (transitive)1. (foll by of)a. to free or release (from a charge of crime)b. to pronounce not guilty2. (foll by of) to free or relieve (from an obligation, duty, responsibility, etc)3. to repay or settle (something, such as a debt or obligation)4. to perform (one's part); conduct (oneself)
Derived forms
acquitter (acˈquitter) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French aquiter, from quiter to release, free from, quit

Word forms: acquits , acquitting , acquitted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verb [usu passive]If someone is acquitted of a crime in a court of law, they are formally declared not to have committed the crime. [be V-ed + of] ⇒ Mr Ling was acquitted of disorderly behaviour by magistrates.2. verbIf you acquit yourself well or admirably in a particular situation, other people feel that you have behaved well or admirably. [formal] [V pron-refl adv] ⇒ Most officers and men acquitted themselves well throughout the action.
