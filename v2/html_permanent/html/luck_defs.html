luck

(lʌk )
Definitions
noun1. events that are beyond control and seem subject to chance; fortune2. success or good fortune3. something considered to bring good luck4. See down on one's luck5. See no such luck6. See try one's luck
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Middle Dutch luc; related to Middle High German gelücke, late Old Norse lukka, lykka

luck

Powered by COBUILD
(lʌk ) for learners of English
Word forms: lucks, lucking, lucked
Definitions
1. uncount nounLuck or good luck is success or good things that happen to you, that do not come from your own abilities or efforts. [+ to-inf] ⇒ I knew I needed a bit of luck to win. [+ with] ⇒ The Sri Lankans have been having no luck with the weather. ⇒ The goal, when it came, owed more to good luck than good planning.2. uncount nounBad luck is lack of success or bad things that happen to you, that have not been caused by yourself or other people. ⇒ I had a lot of bad luck during the first half of this season. ⇒ Randall's illness was only bad luck.3. → See also hard luck4. →  any luck5. →  bad luck6. →  bring someone luck7. →  down on one's luck8. →  the luck of the draw9. →  good luck10. →  be in luck11. →  be just sb's luck12. →  be out of luck13. →  no such luck14. →  to push your luck15. →  luck was on sb's side16. →  to try your luck17. →  with any luck18. → pot luck► →  luck out
