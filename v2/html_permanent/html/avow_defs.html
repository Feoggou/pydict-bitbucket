avow

(əˈvaʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to state or affirm2. to admit openly3. law rare to justify or maintain (some action taken)
Derived forms
aˈvowable adjectiveaˈvowal nounavowed (əˈvaʊd ) adjectiveavowedly (əˈvaʊɪdlɪ ) adverbaˈvower noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French avouer to confess, from Latin advocāre to appeal to, call upon; see avouch, advocate
