verily

(ˈvɛrɪlɪ )
Definitions
adverb(sentence modifier) archaic in truth; truly ⇒ verily, thou art a man of God
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from very + -ly2

verily

Powered by COBUILD
(verɪli ) for learners of English
Definitions
adverbVerily is an old-fashioned or religious word meaning 'truly'. It is used to emphasize a statement or opinion. [emphasis] ⇒ Verily I say unto you, that one of you shall betray me.
