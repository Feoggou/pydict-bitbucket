emollient

(ɪˈmɒlɪənt )
Definitions
adjective1. softening or soothing, esp to the skin2. helping to avoid confrontation; calmingnoun3. any preparation or substance that has a softening or soothing effect, esp when applied to the skin
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

emollience (eˈmollience) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ēmollīre to soften, from mollis soft

Definitions

[formal]2. adjective [ADJ n]An emollient cream makes your skin softer or reduces pain. [formal]3. graded adjectiveAn emollient person or emollient speech tries to make things calm and reduce conflict.
