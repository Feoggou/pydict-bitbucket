screwed up

(skruːd ʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective informal1. very confused or worried, or having psychological problems ⇒ How many screwed-up adults are there now whose parents stayed together for the children's sake?, ⇒ Both of us are screwed up, neither of us able to live in the real world., ⇒ He was really screwed up with his emotional problems., ⇒ She's really screwed up about whether to finish with him or not.. See also screw-up2. ruined or spoilt; in a mess or not working ⇒ Neither of them was willing to risk making an arrangement in case it got screwed up., ⇒ The telephone was screwed up and this guy offered to drop us off in Dover on his way home., ⇒ It's all screwed up, Angela. It's all a mess.
