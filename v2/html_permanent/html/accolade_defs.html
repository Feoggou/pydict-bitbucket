accolade

(ˈækəˌleɪd , ˌækəˈleɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. strong praise or approval; acclaim2. an award or honour3. the ceremonial gesture used to confer knighthood, originally an embrace, now a touch on the shoulder with a sword4. a rare word for brace (sense 7)5. architecture a curved ornamental moulding, esp one having the shape of an ogee arch
Word origin
C17: via French and Italian from Vulgar Latin accollāre (unattested) to hug; related to Latin collum neck

countable nounIf someone is given an accolade, something is done or said about them which shows how much people admire them. [formal] ⇒ The Nobel prize has become the ultimate accolade in the sciences. ⇒ Ari Hart recently received the accolade of being represented in the Museum of Modern Art.
