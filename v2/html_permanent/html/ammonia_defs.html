ammonia

(əˈməʊnɪə , -njə)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a colourless pungent highly soluble gas mainly used in the manufacture of fertilizers, nitric acid, and other nitrogenous compounds, and as a refrigerant and solvent. Formula: NH32. a solution of ammonia in water, containing the compound ammonium hydroxide
Word origin
C18: from New Latin, from Latin (sal) ammōniacus (sal) ammoniac1
