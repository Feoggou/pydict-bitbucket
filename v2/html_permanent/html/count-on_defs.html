Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you count on something or count upon it, you expect it to happen and include it in your plans. rely on [V P n/v-ing] ⇒ The government thought it could count on the support of the trades unions. [V P n/-ing] ⇒ I'll be back. You can count on it. [V P n/-ing] ⇒ He is counting on winning seats and perhaps a share in the new government.2. phrasal verbIf you count on someone or count upon them, you rely on them to support you or help you. rely on [V P n] ⇒ Don't count on Lillian. [V P n to-inf] ⇒ I can always count on you to cheer me up. [V P n to-inf] ⇒ Diana seemed a strong young girl who could be counted upon to produce an heir. →  count
