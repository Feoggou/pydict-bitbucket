grandly

Powered by COBUILD
(grændli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj]You say that someone speaks or behaves grandly when they are trying to impress other people. [disapproval] ⇒ He grandly declared that 'international politics is a struggle for power'.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV before v]You use grandly in expressions such as 'grandly named' or 'grandly called' to say that the name of a place or thing makes it sound much more impressive than it really is. [mainly British] ⇒ The grandly named European Cricketer Cup is based at Worksop College. ⇒ Lucille's home was very grandly called a chateau, though in truth it was nothing more than a large moated farm.
