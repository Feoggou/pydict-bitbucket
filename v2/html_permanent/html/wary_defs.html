wary

(ˈwɛərɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: warier or wariest1. watchful, cautious, or alert2. characterized by caution or watchfulness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

warily (ˈwarily) adverbwariness (ˈwariness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from ware2 + -y1

wary for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weəri )
Word forms: warier , wariest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are wary of something or someone, you are cautious because you do not know much about them and you believe they may be dangerous or cause problems. [+ of] ⇒ People did not teach their children to be wary of strangers. [+ about] ⇒ They were very wary about giving him a contract.warily (weərɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ She studied me warily, as if I might turn violent.2. →  a wary eye
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
