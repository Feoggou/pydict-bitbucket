happen

(ˈhæpən )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) (of an event in time) to come about or take place; occur2. (intransitive; foll by to) (of some unforeseen circumstance or event, esp death), to fall to the lot (of); be a source of good or bad fortune (to) ⇒ if anything happens to me, it'll be your fault3. (transitive) to chance (to be or do something) ⇒ I happen to know him4. (tr; takes a clause as object) to be the case, esp if by chance, that ⇒ it happens that I know himadverb, sentence substitute5. Northern England dialecta. another word for perhapsb. (as sentence modifier) ⇒ happen I'll see thee tomorrowSee note at occur
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: see hap1, -en1

happen

Powered by COBUILD
(hæpən ) for learners of English
Word forms: happens , happening , happened
Definitions
1. verbSomething that happens occurs or is done without being planned. [V] ⇒ We cannot say for sure what will happen. [V] ⇒ The accident happened close to Martha's Vineyard.2. verbIf something happens, it occurs as a result of a situation or course of action. [V] ⇒ She wondered what would happen if her parents found her. [V] ⇒ He trotted to the truck and switched on the ignition. Nothing happened.3. verbWhen something, especially something unpleasant, happens to you, it takes place and affects you. [V + to] ⇒ If we had been spotted at that point, I don't know what would have happened to us. [V to n] ⇒ It's the best thing that ever happened to me.4. verbIf you happen to do something, you do it by chance. If it happens that something is the case, it occurs by chance. [V to-inf] ⇒ We happened to discover we had a friend in common. [V to-inf] ⇒ I looked in the nearest paper, which happened to be the Daily Mail. [V that] ⇒ If it happens that I'm wanted badly somewhere, my mother will take the call and phone through to me here.5. →  as it happens
