finite

(ˈfaɪnaɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. bounded in magnitude or spatial or temporal extent ⇒ a finite difference2. mathematics, logic having a number of elements that is a natural number; able to be counted using the natural numbers less than some natural number. Compare denumerable, infinite (sense 4)3. a. limited or restricted in nature ⇒ human existence is finiteb. (as noun) ⇒ the finite4. denoting any form or occurrence of a verb inflected for grammatical features such as person, number, and tense
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

finitely (ˈfinitely) adverbfiniteness (ˈfiniteness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fīnītus limited, from fīnīre to limit, end

finite

Powered by COBUILD
(faɪnaɪt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomething that is finite has a definite fixed size or extent. [formal] ⇒ ...a finite set of elements. ⇒ Only a finite number of situations can arise. ⇒ The fossil fuels (coal and oil) are finite resources.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A finite clause is a clause based on a verb group which indicates tense, such as 'went', 'is waiting', or 'will be found', rather than on an infinitive or a participle. Compare non-finite.
