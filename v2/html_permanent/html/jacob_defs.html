Jacob

(ˈdʒeɪkəb )
Definitions
noun1. Old Testament the son of Isaac, twin brother of Esau, and father of the twelve patriarchs of Israel2. Also called: Jacob sheep any of an ancient breed of sheep having a fleece with dark brown patches and two or four horns
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

sense 2 in allusion to Genesis 30:40
