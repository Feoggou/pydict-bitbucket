(ˈskeɪðɪŋ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. harshly critical; scornful ⇒ a scathing remark2. damaging; painful

Derived Forms

scathingly (ˈscathingly) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is being scathing about something, you mean that they are being very critical of it. [+ about] ⇒ His report was scathing about Loyalist and Republican terror groups. ⇒ He then launched a scathing attack on previous leaders.scathingly graded adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ 'Oh, they want to be excused,' the other girl said scathingly.
