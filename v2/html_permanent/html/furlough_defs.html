furlough

(ˈfɜːləʊ )
Definitions
noun1. leave of absence from military duty2. US a temporary laying-off of employees, usually because there is insufficient work to occupy themverb (transitive)3. to grant a furlough to4. US to lay off (staff) temporarily
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Dutch verlof, from ver- for- + lof leave, permission; related to Swedish förlof

furlough

Powered by COBUILD
(fɜːʳloʊ ) for learners of English
Word forms: furloughs , furloughing , furloughed
Definitions
1. variable nounIf workers are given furlough, they are told to stay away from work for a certain period because there is not enough for them to do. [US] ⇒ This could mean a massive furlough of government workers.2. verbIf people who work for a particular organization are furloughed, they are given a furlough. [US] [be V-ed] ⇒ We regret to inform you that you are being furloughed indefinitely. [V n] ⇒ The factories have begun furloughing hundreds of workers.3. variable nounWhen soldiers are given furlough, they are given official permission to leave the area where they are based or are fighting, for a certain period. [US] ⇒ I was at home on furlough.regional note:   in BRIT, use leave
