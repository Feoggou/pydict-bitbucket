moan

(məʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a low prolonged mournful sound expressive of suffering or pleading2. any similar mournful sound, esp that made by the wind3. a grumble or complaintverb4. to utter (words) in a low mournful manner5. (intransitive) to make a sound like a moan6. (usually intr) to grumble or complain (esp in the phrase moan and groan)

Derived Forms

moaner (ˈmoaner) nounmoanful (ˈmoanful) adjectivemoaning (ˈmoaning) noun, adjectivemoaningly (ˈmoaningly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: related to Old English mǣnan to grieve over

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you moan, you make a low sound, usually because you are unhappy or in pain. [V] ⇒ Tony moaned in his sleep and then turned over on his side. [V with quote] ⇒ 'My head, my head,' he moaned. 'I can't see.'Moan is also a noun. ⇒ Suddenly she gave a low, choking moan and began to tremble violently. ⇒ ...her moan of sorrow.2. verbTo moan means to complain or speak in a way which shows that you are very unhappy. [disapproval] [V] ⇒ I used to moan if I didn't get at least six hours' sleep at night. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...moaning about the weather. [V prep/adv] ⇒ They moan on a lot about money. [V with quote] ⇒ Meg moans, 'I hated it!' [V that] ⇒ The gardener was moaning that he had another garden to do later that morning.3. countable nounA moan is a complaint. [informal] ⇒ They have been listening to people's gripes, moans and praise.4. →  have a moan5. countable nounA moan is a low noise. [literary] [+ of] ⇒ ...the occasional moan of the wind round the corners of the house. ⇒ ...the moan of distant traffic.
