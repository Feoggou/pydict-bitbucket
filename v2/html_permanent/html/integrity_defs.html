integrity

(ɪnˈtɛɡrɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun1. adherence to moral principles; honesty2. the quality of being unimpaired; soundness3. unity; wholeness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin integritās; see integer

integrity

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntegrɪti ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounIf you have integrity, you are honest and firm in your moral principles. ⇒ I have always regarded him as a man of integrity. ⇒ He was praised for his fairness and high integrity.2. uncount noun [with poss]The integrity of something such as a group of people or a text is its state of being a united whole. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Separatist movements are a threat to the integrity of the nation.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
