posting

Powered by COBUILD
(poʊstɪŋ )
Word forms: postings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf you get a posting to a different town or country, your employers send you to work there, usually for several years. [mainly British] [+ to] ⇒ He was rewarded with a posting to New York. ⇒ Relevant work experience is required for overseas postings.2. → see also post1regional note:   in AM, usually use assignment3. countable nounIf a member of an armed force gets a posting to a particular place, they are sent to live and work there for a period. [+ to] ⇒ ...awaiting his posting to a field ambulance corps in early 1941. ⇒ ...a prestigious military posting.4. countable nounA posting is a message that is placed on the Internet, for example on a bulletin board or website, for everyone to read. [computing] ⇒ Postings on the Internet can be accessed from anywhere in the world.
