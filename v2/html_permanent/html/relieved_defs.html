relieved

(rɪˈliːvd )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; often foll by at, about, etc) experiencing relief, esp from worry or anxiety2. mechanical engineering having part of the surface cut away to avoid friction or wear

relieved

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪliːvd ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, oft ADJ to-inf/that]If you are relieved, you feel happy because something unpleasant has not happened or is no longer happening. ⇒ We are all relieved to be back home. ⇒ I am very relieved that it is over. ⇒ He was relieved at the result.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

relieved

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪlivd) in Mechanical Engineering
Definitions
adjective(Mechanical engineering: Manufacturing and assembly) A relieved component has part of its surface cut away to avoid friction or wear. ⇒ The rear part of the stock is relieved so that the bolt will not hit it during functioning. ⇒ Broad pieces of wood are frequently given a relieved back, a hidden groove or grooves that reduce the effective thickness of the piece and thus the tendency of the piece to cup. ⇒ A relieved component has part of its surface cut away to avoid friction or wear.
