blare

(blɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to sound loudly and harshly2. to proclaim loudly and sensationallynoun3. a loud and usually harsh or grating noise
Word origin
C14: from Middle Dutch bleren; of imitative origin

blare

Powered by COBUILD
(bleəʳ )
Word forms: blares , blaring , blared
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
ergative verb & verbIf something such as a siren or radio blares or if you blare it, it makes a loud, unpleasant noise. [V] ⇒ The fire engines were just pulling up, sirens blaring. [V] ⇒ Music blared from the flat behind me. [V n] ⇒ I blared my horn.Blare is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ ...the blare of a radio through a thin wall.Blare out means the same as blare. [V P] ⇒ Music blares out from every cafe. [V P n] ⇒ ...giant loudspeakers which blare out patriotic music and the speeches of their leader. [Also V n P]
