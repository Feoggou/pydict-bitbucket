ridicule

(ˈrɪdɪˌkjuːl )
Definitions
noun1. language or behaviour intended to humiliate or mock; derisionverb2. (transitive) to make fun of, mock, or deride
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ridiculer (ˈridiˌculer) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French, from Latin rīdiculus, from rīdēre to laugh

ridicule

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪdɪkjuːl ) for learners of English
Word forms: ridicules , ridiculing , ridiculed
Definitions
1. verbIf you ridicule someone or ridicule their ideas or beliefs, you make fun of them in an unkind way. [V n] ⇒ I admired her all the more for allowing them to ridicule her and never striking back. [V n] ⇒ I don't think his faith should be ridiculed.2. uncount nounIf someone or something is an object of ridicule or is held up to ridicule, someone makes fun of them in an unkind way. ⇒ As a heavy child, she became the object of ridicule from classmates. ⇒ The process of judicial selection was held up to ridicule. ⇒ Davis was subjected to public ridicule.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
