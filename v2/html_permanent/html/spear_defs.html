spear1

(spɪə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a weapon consisting of a long shaft with a sharp pointed end of metal, stone, or wood that may be thrown or thrust2. a similar implement used to catch fish3. another name for spearmanverb4. to pierce (something) with or as if with a spear

Derived Forms

spearer (ˈspearer) noun

Word Origin

Old English spere; related to Old Norse spjör spears, Greek sparos gilthead

spear2

(spɪə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
nouna shoot, slender stalk, or blade, as of grass, asparagus, or broccoli

Word Origin

C16: probably variant of spire1, influenced by spear1

spear

Powered by COBUILD
(spɪəʳ )
Word forms: spears , spearing , speared
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA spear is a weapon consisting of a long pole with a sharp metal point attached to the end.2. verbIf you spear something, you push or throw a pointed object into it. [V n] ⇒ Spear a piece of fish with a carving fork and dip it in the batter. [be V-ed to n] ⇒ A police officer was speared to death.3. countable nounAsparagus or broccoli spears are individual stalks of asparagus or broccoli.
