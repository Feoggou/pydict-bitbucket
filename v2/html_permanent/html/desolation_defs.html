desolation

(ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of desolating or the state of being desolated; ruin or devastation2. solitary misery; wretchedness3. a desolate region; barren waste

(desəleɪʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounDesolation is a feeling of great unhappiness and hopelessness. ⇒ Kozelek expresses his sense of desolation absolutely without self-pity.2. uncount nounIf you refer to desolation in a place, you mean that it is empty and frightening, for example because it has been destroyed by a violent force or army. [disapproval] ⇒ We looked out upon a scene of desolation and ruin. ⇒ She says the army left desolation and death through the whole of northern Morazan.
