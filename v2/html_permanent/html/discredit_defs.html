discredit

(dɪsˈkrɛdɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to damage the reputation of2. to cause to be disbelieved or distrusted3. to reject as untrue or of questionable accuracynoun4. a person, thing, or state of affairs that causes disgrace5. damage to a reputation6. lack of belief or confidence

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo discredit someone or something means to cause them to lose people's respect or trust. [V n] ⇒ ...a secret unit within the company that had been set up to discredit its major rival. [V n] ⇒ He says his accusers are trying to discredit government foreign-aid policies. [V n] ⇒ He said such methods would discredit the party worldwide.discredited graded adjective & adjective ⇒ The previous government is, by now, thoroughly discredited. ⇒ ...the old, discredited regimes.2. verbTo discredit an idea or evidence means to make it appear false or not certain. [V n] ⇒ They realized there would be difficulties in discrediting the evidence.
