carnival

(ˈkɑːnɪvəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a festive occasion or period marked by merrymaking, processions, etc: esp in some Roman Catholic countries, the period just before Lentb. (as modifier) ⇒ a carnival atmosphere2. a travelling fair having merry-go-rounds, etc3. a show or display arranged as an amusement4. Australian a sports meeting
Word origin
C16: from Italian carnevale, from Old Italian carnelevare a removing of meat (referring to the Lenten fast)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA carnival is a public festival during which people play music and sometimes dance in the streets.2. countable nounA carnival is a travelling show which is held in a park or field and at which there are machines to ride on, entertainments, and games. [US]regional note:   in BRIT, use funfair3. countable noun [N of n]A carnival of something such as colours or sounds is a bright or exciting mixture of them. [literary] ⇒ The avenues lined with jacaranda trees burst into a carnival of purple.
