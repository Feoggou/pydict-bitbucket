acquiesce

(ˌækwɪˈɛs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intr; often foll by in or to) to comply (with); assent (to) without protest▶ USAGE The use of to after acquiesce was formerly regarded as incorrect, but is now acceptable
Derived forms
ˌacquiescence (ˌacquiˈescence) nounˌacquiescent (ˌacquiˈescent) adjectiveˌacquiescently (ˌacquiˈescently) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin acquiēscere to remain at rest, agree without protest, from ad- at + quiēscere to rest, from quiēs quiet

Word forms: acquiesces , acquiescing , acquiesced
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you acquiesce in something, you agree to do what someone wants or to accept what they do. [formal] [V + in/to] ⇒ Steve seemed to acquiesce in the decision. [V in/to n] ⇒ He has gradually acquiesced to the demands of the opposition. [V] ⇒ When her mother suggested that she stay, Alice willingly acquiesced.
