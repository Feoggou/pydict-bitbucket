lemon

(ˈlɛmən )
Definitions
noun1. a small Asian evergreen tree, Citrus limon, widely cultivated in warm and tropical regions, having pale green glossy leaves and edible fruits ▶ Related adjectives: citric, citrine, citrous2. a. the yellow oval fruit of this tree, having juicy acidic flesh rich in vitamin Cb. (as modifier) ⇒ a lemon jelly3. Also called: lemon yellowa. a greenish-yellow or strong yellow colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ lemon wallpaper4. a distinctive tart flavour made from or in imitation of the lemon5. slang a person or thing considered to be useless or defective
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lemonish (ˈlemonish) adjectivelemon-like (ˈlemon-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin lemōn-, from Arabic laymūn

lemon

Powered by COBUILD
(lemən ) for learners of English
Word forms: lemons
Definitions
1. variable nounA lemon is a bright yellow fruit with very sour juice. Lemons grow on trees in warm countries. ⇒ ...a slice of lemon. ⇒ ...oranges, lemons and other citrus fruits. ⇒ ...lemon juice.2. uncount nounLemon is a drink that tastes of lemons.3. colourLemon is the same as lemon yellow.4. countable nounIf you think that something is a failure, or not as good or as useful as it should be, you can say that it is a lemon. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ He took a little test drive and agreed the car was a lemon.5. countable nounIf you think that someone looks foolish because they are shy or slow to take action, you can say that they are like a lemon. [British, informal, disapproval] ⇒ I just stood there like a lemon.
