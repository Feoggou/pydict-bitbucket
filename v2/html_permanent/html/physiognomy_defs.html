physiognomy

(ˌfɪzɪˈɒnəmɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person's features or characteristic expression considered as an indication of personality2. the art or practice of judging character from facial features3. the outward appearance of something, esp the physical characteristics of a geographical region

Derived Forms

physiognomic (ˌfɪzɪəˈnɒmɪk ) or ˌphysiognomical (ˌphysiogˈnomical) adjectiveˌphysiognomically (ˌphysiogˈnomically) adverbˌphysiognomist (ˌphysiˈognomist) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French phisonomie, via Medieval Latin, from Late Greek phusiognōmia, erroneous for Greek phusiognōmonia, from phusis nature + gnōmōn judge
