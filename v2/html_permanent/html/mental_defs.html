mental1

(ˈmɛntəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or involving the mind or an intellectual process2. occurring only in the mind ⇒ mental calculations3. affected by mental illness ⇒ a mental patient. Preferred form: psychiatric4. concerned with care for persons with mental illness ⇒ a mental hospital. Preferred form: psychiatric5. slang insane

Derived Forms

mentally (ˈmentally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin mentālis, from Latin mēns mind

mental

Powered by COBUILD
(mentəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]Mental means relating to the process of thinking. ⇒ ...the mental development of children. ⇒ ...intensive mental effort.mentally adverb [ADV adj/adv] [ADV with v] ⇒ I think you are mentally tired. ⇒ I had never felt more mentally alive. ⇒ Physically I might not have been overseas but mentally and spiritually I was with them.2. adjective [ADJ n]Mental means relating to the state or the health of a person's mind. ⇒ The mental state that had created her psychosis was no longer present. ⇒ ...mental health problems.mentally adverb [usu ADV with cl/group, oft ADV after v] ⇒ ...an inmate who is mentally disturbed. ⇒ ...the needs of the mentally ill.3. adjective [ADJ n]A mental act is one that involves only thinking and not physical action. ⇒ Practise mental arithmetic when you go out shopping. ⇒ Graham made a quick mental calculation. ⇒ She made a mental note not to sit anywhere near him.mentally adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ This technique will help people mentally organize information.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is mental, you mean that you think they are mad. [British, informal, disapproval] ⇒ I just said to him 'you must be mental'.5. →  to make a mental note
