lumpen

(ˈlʌmpən )
Definitions
adjectiveinformal stupid or unthinking
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

from German Lump vagabond, influenced in meaning by Lumpen rag, as in lumpenproletariat

lumpen

Powered by COBUILD
(lʌmpən ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A lumpen object is large, heavy, and lumpy. [mainly British, literary] ⇒ She was kneading a lumpen mass of dough. ⇒ Lumpen shapes began to appear out of the shadows.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe people as lumpen, you think they are dull and clumsy. [mainly British, literary, disapproval] ⇒ The people seemed lumpen and boring.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
