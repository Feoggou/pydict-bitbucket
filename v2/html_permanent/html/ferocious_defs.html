ferocious

(fəˈrəʊʃəs )
Definitions
adjectivesavagely fierce or cruel ⇒ a ferocious tiger, ⇒ a ferocious argument
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ferociously (feˈrociously) adverbferocity (fəˈrɒsɪtɪ ) or ferociousness (feˈrociousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ferox fierce, untamable, warlike

ferocious

Powered by COBUILD
(fəroʊʃəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA ferocious animal, person, or action is very fierce and violent. ⇒ ...a ferocious guard-dog. ⇒ The police had had to deal with some of the most ferocious violence ever seen on the streets of London.ferociously graded adverb ⇒ She kicked out ferociously.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA ferocious war, argument, or other form of conflict involves a great deal of anger, bitterness, and determination. ⇒ Fighting has been ferocious. ⇒ A ferocious battle to select a new parliamentary candidate is in progress.ferociously graded adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ These days he is ferociously competitive.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe actions or feelings as ferocious, you mean that they are intense and determined. ⇒ Lindbergh was startled at the ferocious depth of anti-British feeling.ferociously graded adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ He set himself ferociously tough standards; and he was ardently devoted to ballet.
