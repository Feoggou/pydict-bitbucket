scull

(skʌl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a single oar moved from side to side over the stern of a boat to propel it2. one of a pair of short-handled oars, both of which are pulled by one oarsman, esp in a racing shell3. a racing shell propelled by an oarsman or oarsmen pulling two oars4. (plural) a race between racing shells, each propelled by one, two, or four oarsmen pulling two oars5. an act, instance, period, or distance of scullingverb6. to propel (a boat) with a scull

Derived Forms

sculler (ˈsculler) noun

Word Origin

C14: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Sculls are small oars which are held by one person and used to move a boat through water.2. countable nounA scull is a small light racing boat which is rowed with two sculls.3. verbTo scull a boat means to row it using sculls. [V n] ⇒ An old woman sculling a coracle came alongside. [Also V]
