pollen

(ˈpɒlən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna fine powdery substance produced by the anthers of seed-bearing plants, consisting of numerous fine grains containing the male gametes

Derived Forms

pollinic (pəˈlɪnɪk ) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin: powder; compare Greek palē pollen

(pɒlən )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounPollen is a fine powder produced by flowers. It fertilizes other flowers of the same species so that they produce seeds.
