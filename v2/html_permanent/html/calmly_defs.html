calmly

Powered by COBUILD
(kɑːmli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [ADV with v]You can use calmly to emphasize that someone is behaving in a very controlled or ordinary way in a frightening or unusual situation. [written, emphasis] ⇒ The gunmen calmly walked away and escaped in a waiting car.2. → see also calm
Example sentences containing 'calmly'
Maybe I'll get in touch with him over the holidays, I thought, and we can meet and talk calmly. Kate Cann SEXHe said calmly ,'What you appear to be saying is that you've been checking up on my past record quite illegally. Jack Higgins THE KEYS OF HELLShe spoke in this artificial manner, slowly, calmly , I could tell she'd rehearsed it. Hugo Wilcken THE EXECUTION
