tarry

(ˈtærɪ )
Definitions
verb -ries, -rying or -ried1. (intransitive) to delay in coming or going; linger2. (intransitive) to remain temporarily or briefly3. (intransitive) to wait or stay4. (transitive) archaic or poetic to awaitnoun plural -ries5. rare a stay
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tarrier (ˈtarrier) noun

Word Origin

C14 tarien, of uncertain origin

Word forms: tarries , tarrying , tarried
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (tæri ). The adjective is pronounced (tɑːri ).1. verbIf you tarry somewhere, you stay there longer than you meant to and delay leaving. [old-fashioned] [V] ⇒ Two old boys tarried on the street corner discussing cattle.2. adjectiveIf you describe something as tarry, you mean that it has a lot of tar in it or is like tar. ⇒ I smelled tarry melted asphalt. ⇒ ...cups of tarry coffee.
