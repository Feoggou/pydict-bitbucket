strophe

(ˈstrəʊfɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun prosody1. (in ancient Greek drama)a. the first of two movements made by a chorus during the performance of a choral odeb. the first part of a choral ode sung during this movement2. (in classical verse) the first division of the threefold structure of a Pindaric ode3. the first of two metrical systems used alternately within a poem▷ See also antistrophe, epode

Word Origin

C17: from Greek: a verse, literally: a turning, from strephein to twist
