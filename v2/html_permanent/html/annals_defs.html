annals

(ˈænəlz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. yearly records of events, generally in chronological order2. history or records of history in general3. regular reports of the work of a society, learned body, etc
Derived forms
annalist (ˈannalist) nounˌannalistic (ˌannalˈistic) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin (librī) annālēs yearly (books), from annus year

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. plural noun [usu in the N of n]If something is in the annals of a nation or field of activity, it is recorded as part of its history. ⇒ He has become a legend in the annals of military history.2. plural noun [with supp, usu the N of n]Annals is used in the titles of journals of a particular field of academic research. ⇒ It was published in the 'Annals of Internal Medicine'.
