each way

Definitions
adjective, adverbhorse racing, mainly British (of a bet) made on the same runner or contestant to win or come second or third in a race. Also: both ways. US term: across-the-board

each way

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v]If you bet money each way on the result of a horse race or a dog race, you will win some money if the animal you bet on comes first, second, third, or sometimes fourth. [British] ⇒ In the last race I put £20 each way on two outsiders.Each way is also an adjective ⇒ ...a $10,000 each way bet on Minnehoma at 33-1.2. adjective [ADJ n]If you say that something is a good each way bet, you mean that you think it is a good thing to support or invest in because it is unlikely to fail. [British] ⇒ Large overseas-based trusts are an excellent each way bet. ⇒ ...a good each way investment.
