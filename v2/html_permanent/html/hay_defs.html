hay1

(heɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a. grass, clover, etc, cut and dried as fodderb. (in combination) ⇒ a hayfield, ⇒ a hayloft2. See hit the hay3. See make hay of4. See make hay while the sun shines5. See roll in the hayverb6. to cut, dry, and store (grass, clover, etc) as fodder7. (transitive) to feed with hay
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hieg; related to Old Norse hey, Gothic hawi, Old Frisian hē, Old High German houwi; see hew

hay2

or hey (heɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a circular figure in country dancing2. a former country dance in which the dancers wove in and out of a circle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: of uncertain origin

Hay

(heɪ )
Definitions
noun Will. 1888–1949, British music-hall comedian, who later starred in films, such as Oh, Mr Porter! (1937)
