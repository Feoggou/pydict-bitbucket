wattle1

(ˈwɒtəl )
Definitions
noun1. a frame of rods or stakes interwoven with twigs, branches, etc, esp when used to make fences2. the material used in such a construction3. a loose fold of skin, often brightly coloured, hanging from the neck or throat of certain birds, lizards, etc4. any of various chiefly Australian acacia trees having spikes of small brightly coloured flowers and flexible branches, which were used by early settlers for making fences. See also golden wattle5. a southern African caesalpinaceous tree, Peltophorum africanum, with yellow flowersverb (transitive)6. to construct from wattle7. to bind or frame with wattle8. to weave or twist (branches, twigs, etc) into a frameadjective9. made of, formed by, or covered with wattle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
