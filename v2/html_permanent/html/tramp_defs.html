tramp

(træmp )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to walk long and far; hike2. to walk heavily or firmly across or through (a place); march or trudge3. (intransitive) to wander about as a vagabond or tramp4. (transitive) to make (a journey) or traverse (a place) on foot, esp laboriously or wearily ⇒ to tramp the streets in search of work5. (transitive) to tread or trample6. (intransitive) New Zealand to walk for sport or recreation, esp in the bushnoun7. a person who travels about on foot, usually with no permanent home, living by begging or doing casual work8. a long hard walk; hike9. a heavy or rhythmic step or tread10. the sound of heavy treading11. Also called: tramp steamer a merchant ship that does not run between ports on a regular schedule but carries cargo wherever the shippers desire12. slang, mainly US and Canadian a prostitute or promiscuous girl or woman13. an iron plate on the sole of a boot
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tramping (ˈtramping) nountrampish (ˈtrampish) adjective

Word Origin

C14: probably from Middle Low German trampen; compare Gothic ana-trimpan to press heavily upon, German trampen to hitchhike

tramp

Powered by COBUILD
(træmp ) for learners of English
Word forms: tramps , tramping , tramped
Definitions
1. countable nounA tramp is a person who has no home or job, and very little money. Tramps go from place to place, and get food or money by asking people or by doing casual work. down-and-out, hobo2. verbIf you tramp somewhere, you walk there slowly and with regular, heavy steps, for a long time. [V prep/adv] ⇒ They put on their coats and tramped through the falling snow. [V n] ⇒ She spent all day yesterday tramping the streets, gathering evidence.3. uncount nounThe tramp of people is the sound of their heavy, regular walking. [+ of] ⇒ He heard the slow, heavy tramp of feet on the stairs. ⇒ ...the tramp of heavy boots.4. countable nounIf someone refers to a woman as a tramp, they are insulting her, because they think that she is immoral in her sexual behaviour. [mainly US, offensive, disapproval] slut
