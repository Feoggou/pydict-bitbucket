maul

(mɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to handle clumsily; paw2. to batter or laceratenoun3. a heavy two-handed hammer suitable for driving piles, wedges, etc4. rugby a loose scrum that forms around a player who is holding the ball and on his feet

Derived Forms

mauler (ˈmauler) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French mail, from Latin malleus hammer. See mallet

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you are mauled by an animal, you are violently attacked by it and badly injured. [be V-ed + by] ⇒ He had been mauled by a bear. [V n] ⇒ The dog went berserk and mauled one of the girls.2. verb [usu passive]If someone is mauled, they are attacked aggressively, and often harmed in some way. [be V-ed] ⇒ The troops were severely mauled and lost a quarter of their strength before evacuating the island. [be V-ed] ⇒ The cable-TV and health-care industries are both being mauled by government.
