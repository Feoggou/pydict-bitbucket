canteen

(kænˈtiːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a restaurant attached to a factory, school, etc, providing meals for large numbers of people2. a. a small shop that provides a limited range of items, such as toilet requisites, to a military unitb. a recreation centre for military personnel3. a soldier's eating and drinking utensils4. a temporary or mobile stand at which food is provided5. a. a box in which a set of cutlery is laid outb. the cutlery itself6. a flask or canister for carrying water or other liquids, as used by soldiers or travellers
Word origin
C18: from French cantine, from Italian cantina wine cellar, from canto corner, from Latin canthus iron hoop encircling chariot wheel; see cant2

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA canteen is a place in a factory, shop, or college where meals are served to the people who work or study there. ⇒ Rennie had eaten his tea in the canteen. ⇒ ...a school canteen. ⇒ ...canteen food.2. countable nounA canteen is a small plastic bottle for carrying water and other drinks. Canteens are used by soldiers. water bottle [+ of] ⇒ ...a full canteen of water.3. countable nounA canteen of cutlery is a set of knives, forks, and spoons in a specially designed box.
