emit

(ɪˈmɪt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: emits, emitting or emitted (transitive)1. to give or send forth; discharge ⇒ the pipe emitted a stream of water2. to give voice to; utter ⇒ she emitted a shrill scream3. physics to give off (radiation or particles)4. to put (currency) into circulation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ēmittere to send out, from mittere to send

emit

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: emits , emitting , emitted
Definitions
1. verbIf something emits heat, light, gas, or a smell, it produces it and sends it out by means of a physical or chemical process. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The new device emits a powerful circular column of light. [V n] ⇒ ...the amount of carbon dioxide emitted.2. verbTo emit a sound or noise means to produce it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Polly blinked and emitted a long, low whistle.
