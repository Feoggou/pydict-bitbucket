standstill

(ˈstændˌstɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna complete cessation of movement; stop; halt ⇒ the car came to a standstill

standstill

Powered by COBUILD
(stændstɪl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
singular noun [usu to/at N]If movement or activity comes to or is brought to a standstill, it stops completely. ⇒ Abruptly the group ahead of us came to a standstill. ⇒ Production is more or less at a standstill.
Example sentences containing 'standstill'
He lifted his arm in warning and the men bumped to a standstill behind him. Thomas, Rosie THE WHITE DOVEOn this occasion the legs came to a complete standstill , leaving Ryle in no doubt. Harcourt, Palma DOUBLE DECEITStumbling to a standstill he stared round, his chest heaving, feeling the ice in the hail rasp against the delicate linings of his nostrils. Erskine, Barbara MIDNIGHT IS A LONELY PLACEHe'd assumed they were at a temporary standstill with their investigations, that they'd need to consider the question of where next. Stephanie Laurens ALL ABOUT LOVE
