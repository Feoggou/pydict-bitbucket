salvo1

(ˈsælvəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -vos or -voes1. a discharge of fire from weapons in unison, esp on a ceremonial occasion2. concentrated fire from many weapons, as in a naval battle3. an outburst, as of applause
Word origin
C17: from Italian salva, from Old French salve, from Latin salvē! greetings! from salvēre to be in good health, from salvus safe

salvo2

(ˈsælvəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -vos rare1. an excuse or evasion2. an expedient to save a reputation or soothe hurt feelings3. (in legal documents) a saving clause; reservation
Word origin
C17: from such Medieval Latin phrases as salvō iūre the right of keeping safe, from Latin salvus safe

Powered by COBUILD
(sælvoʊ )
Word forms: salvoes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA salvo is the firing of several guns or missiles at the same time in a battle or ceremony. [+ of] ⇒ They were to fire a salvo of blanks, after the national anthem.2. countable nounA salvo of angry words is a lot of them spoken or written at about the same time. [+ of] ⇒ His testimony, however, was only one in a salvo of new attacks.
