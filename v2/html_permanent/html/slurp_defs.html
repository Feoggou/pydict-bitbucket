slurp

(slɜːp ) informal

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to eat or drink (something) noisilynoun2. a sound produced in this way

Word Origin

C17: from Middle Dutch slorpen to sip; related to German schlürfen

slurp

Powered by COBUILD
(slɜːʳp )
Word forms: slurps , slurping , slurped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you slurp a liquid, you drink it noisily. [V n + from/off] ⇒ He blew on his soup before slurping it off the spoon. [V adv n] ⇒ He slurped down a cup of sweet, black coffee. [Also V n, V]2. countable nounA slurp is a noise that you make with your mouth when you drink noisily, or a mouthful of liquid that you drink noisily. ⇒ He takes a slurp from a cup of black coffee.
