coupon

(ˈkuːpɒn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a detachable part of a ticket or advertisement entitling the holder to a discount, free gift, etcb. a detachable slip usable as a commercial order formc. a voucher given away with certain goods, a certain number of which are exchangeable for goods offered by the manufacturers2. one of a number of detachable certificates attached to a bond, esp a bearer bond, the surrender of which entitles the bearer to receive interest payments3. one of several detachable cards used for making hire-purchase payments4. a ticket issued to facilitate rationing5. British a detachable entry form for any of certain competitions, esp football pools
Word origin
C19: from French, from Old French colpon piece cut off, from colper to cut, variant of couper; see cope1
