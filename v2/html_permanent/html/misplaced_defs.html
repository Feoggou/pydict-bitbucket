misplaced

(ˌmɪsˈpleɪst)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of trust, loyalty, etc) bestowed inappropriately ⇒ Lenders rely on the misplaced loyalty of existing borrowers to make their profit., ⇒ A telling sign of misplaced priorities is the concentration on health, not environmental issues.2. inappropriate to the occasion or mood ⇒ I think your concern is misplaced. Ackroyd is no threat to anyone.3. (of a letter or an accent in writing) put in the wrong place ⇒ Half a point would be taken off for a misplaced accent.

graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a feeling or action as misplaced, you are critical of it because you think it is inappropriate, or directed towards the wrong thing or person.
