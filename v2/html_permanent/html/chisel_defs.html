chisel

(ˈtʃɪzəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a hand tool for working wood, consisting of a flat steel blade with a cutting edge attached to a handle of wood, plastic, etc. It is either struck with a mallet or used by handb. a similar tool without a handle for working stone or metalverb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled2. to carve (wood, stone, metal, etc) or form (an engraving, statue, etc) with or as with a chisel3. slang to cheat or obtain by cheating
Word origin
C14: via Old French, from Vulgar Latin cīsellus (unattested), from Latin caesus cut, from caedere to cut

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use chiseling, chiseled1. countable nounA chisel is a tool that has a long metal blade with a sharp edge at the end. It is used for cutting and shaping wood and stone. ⇒ ...a hammer and chisel.2. verbIf you chisel wood or stone, you cut and shape it using a chisel. [V n] ⇒ They sit and chisel the stone to size.
