accumulation

(əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act or process of collecting together or becoming collected2. something that has been collected, gathered, heaped, etc3. financea. the continuous growth of capital by retention of interest or earningsb. (in computing the yield on a bond purchased at a discount) the amount that is added to each yield to bring the cost of the bond into equality with its par value over its life. Compare amortization (sense 2)4. the taking of a first and an advanced university degree simultaneously

(əkjuːmjʊleɪʃən )
Word forms: accumulations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn accumulation of something is a large number of things which have been collected together or acquired over a period of time. [+ of] ⇒ ...an accumulation of experience and knowledge. ⇒ ...accumulations of dirt.2. uncount nounAccumulation is the collecting together of things over a period of time. [+ of] ⇒ ...the accumulation of capital and the distribution of income. ⇒ The rate of accumulation decreases with time.

accumulation n. (əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən). Accumulation may refer to: 1- in finance: Accumulation function, a mathematical function defined in terms of the ratio future value to present value. Capital accumulation, the gathering of objects of value. 2- in science and engineering: Glacier ice accumulation, an element in the glacier mass balance formula. Tree accumulation, in computer science the process of accumulating data placed in tree nodes according to their tree structure.
