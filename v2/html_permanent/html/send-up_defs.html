send up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. slang to send to prison2. British informal to make fun of, esp by doing an imitation or parody of ⇒ he sent up the teacher marvellouslynoun send-up3. British informal a parody or imitation

send-up

(ˈsɛndʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounBritish informal a piece of writing or acting in which someone or something is imitated in an amusing way that makes them appear foolish ⇒ He played his classic send-up of sixties rock, 'Get Crazy'.

send up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you send someone or something up, you imitate them in an amusing way that makes them appear foolish. [British, informal] [V n P] ⇒ You sense he's sending himself up as well as everything else. [V P n] ⇒ ...a spoof that sends up the macho world of fighter pilots.2. → see also send-up3. phrasal verb [usu passive]If someone who is on trial is sent up, they are found guilty and sent to prison. [US] [be V-ed P] ⇒ If I'm going to be sent up for killing one guy, then I might as well kill three more.regional note:   in BRIT, use send down →  send
