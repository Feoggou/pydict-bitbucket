gulf

(ɡʌlf )
Definitions
noun1. a large deep bay2. a deep chasm3. something that divides or separates, such as a lack of understanding4. something that engulfs, such as a whirlpoolverb5. (transitive) to swallow up; engulf
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gulflike (ˈgulfˌlike) adjectivegulfy (ˈgulfy) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French golfe, from Italian golfo, from Greek kolpos

(gʌlf ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. countable nounA gulf is an important or significant difference between two people, things, or groups. [+ between] ⇒ Within society, there is a growing gulf between rich and poor. [+ between] ⇒ ...the gulf between rural and urban life.2. countable nounA gulf is a large area of sea which extends a long way into the surrounding land. ⇒ Hurricane Andrew was last night heading into the Gulf of Mexico.
