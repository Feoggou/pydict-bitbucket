fairly

(ˈfɛəlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. (not used with a negative) moderately2. as deserved; justly3. (not used with a negative) positively; absolutely ⇒ the hall fairly rang with applause4. archaic clearly5. obsolete courteously

fairly

Powered by COBUILD
(feəʳli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV adj/adv]Fairly means to quite a large degree. For example, if you say that something is fairly old, you mean that it is old but not very old. ⇒ Both ships are fairly new. ⇒ We did fairly well but only fairly well.2. adverb [ADV adj/adv]You use fairly instead of 'very' to add emphasis to an adjective or adverb without making it sound too forceful. [vagueness] ⇒ Were you always fairly bright at school? ⇒ You've got to be fairly single-minded about it. ⇒ I'll have no income and no home and will need a job fairly badly.3. adverb [ADV before v]Fairly is used to emphasize that something happens to a very great degree or extent. [emphasis] positively ⇒ He fairly flew across the room. ⇒ For him, the place is fairly boiling with humanity.4. graded adverb [ADV before v]You use fairly to suggest that a statement is probably or possibly true, and therefore deserves to be accepted or considered. justifiably ⇒ It can no doubt be fairly argued that Sir John, whose pay is linked to performance, is entitled to every penny. ⇒ After I had read the book I could fairly claim to be an expert.5. → See also fairusage note:   Fairly, pretty, quite, and rather can all be used to modify adjectives and adverbs, but are all less strong than very. Rather and pretty are the strongest of these words and are the closest to very. Pretty, in this sense, is informal. Therefore, if you said to someone, 'Your work is pretty good' or 'Your work is rather good', they would be more likely to be pleased than if you said 'Your work is quite good' or 'Your work is fairly good'. However, rather is commonly used with words indicating negative qualities. I was feeling rather sad. It is the only one of these words than can be used with comparatives, and with too. Global warming could be rather worse than we think it will be... He was becoming rather too friendly with my ex-boyfriend. Quite is slightly stronger than fairly, and may suggest that something has more of a quality than expected. Nobody here's ever heard of it but it is actually quite common.
