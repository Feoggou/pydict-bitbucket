obscene

(əbˈsiːn )
Definitions
adjective1. offensive or outrageous to accepted standards of decency or modesty2. law (of publications) having a tendency to deprave or corrupt3. disgusting; repellent ⇒ an obscene massacre
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obscenely (obˈscenely) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin obscēnus inauspicious, perhaps related to caenum filth

obscene for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒbsiːn )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as obscene, you mean it offends you because it relates to sex or violence in a way that you think is unpleasant and shocking. ⇒ I'm not prudish but I think these photographs are obscene. ⇒ He continued to use obscene language and also to make threats.2. adjectiveIn legal contexts, books, pictures, or films which are judged obscene are illegal because they deal with sex or violence in a way that is considered offensive to the general public. ⇒ A city magistrate ruled that the novel was obscene and copies should be destroyed. ⇒ The bill leaves it up to the courts to decide what is obscene. ⇒ ...the Obscene Publications Act.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as obscene, you disapprove of it very strongly and consider it to be offensive or immoral. [disapproval] ⇒ It was obscene to spend millions producing unwanted food. ⇒ His salary was obscene for three 40-minute shows a week.
