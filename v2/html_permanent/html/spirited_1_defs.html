spirited

(ˈspɪrɪtɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. displaying animation, vigour, or liveliness2. (in combination) characterized by mood, temper, or disposition as specified ⇒ high-spirited, ⇒ public-spirited

Derived Forms

spiritedly (ˈspiritedly) adverbspiritedness (ˈspiritedness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A spirited action shows great energy and courage. ⇒ This television program provoked a spirited debate in the United Kingdom.spiritedly graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ She had talked spiritedly about her adventures.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A spirited person is very active, lively, and confident. ⇒ He was by nature a spirited little boy.
