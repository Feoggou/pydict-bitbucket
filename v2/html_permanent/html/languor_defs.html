languor

(ˈlæŋɡə )
Definitions
noun1. physical or mental laziness or weariness2. a feeling of dreaminess and relaxation3. oppressive silence or stillness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14 langour, via Old French from Latin languor, from languēre to languish; the modern spelling is directly from Latin

Definitions
uncount nounLanguor is a pleasant feeling of being relaxed and not having any energy or interest in anything. [literary] ⇒ She, in her languor, had not troubled to eat much.
