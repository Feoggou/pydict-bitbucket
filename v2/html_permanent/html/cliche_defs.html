cliché

(ˈkliːʃeɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a word or expression that has lost much of its force through overexposure, as for example the phrase ⇒ it's got to get worse before it gets better2. an idea, action, or habit that has become trite from overuse3. printing, mainly British a stereotype or electrotype plate
Derived forms
clichéd (ˈclichéd) or cliché'd (ˈcliché'd) adjective
Word origin
C19: from French, from clicher to stereotype; imitative of the sound made by the matrix when it is dropped into molten metal

cliché

Powered by COBUILD
(kliːʃeɪ , US kliːʃeɪ )
Word forms: clichés
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use clichecountable nounA cliché is an idea or phrase which has been used so much that it is no longer interesting or effective or no longer has much meaning. [disapproval] [+ about] ⇒ I've learned that the cliche about life not being fair is true. ⇒ It has become a cliche to describe Asia-Pacific as the world's most dynamic economic area.
