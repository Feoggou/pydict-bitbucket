meson

(ˈmiːzɒn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounany of a group of elementary particles, such as a pion or kaon, that usually has a rest mass between those of an electron and a proton, and an integral spin. They are responsible for the force between nucleons in the atomic nucleus. Former name: mesotron See also muon

Derived Forms

mesonic (meˈsonic) or mesic (ˈmesic) adjective

Word Origin

C20: from meso- + -on
