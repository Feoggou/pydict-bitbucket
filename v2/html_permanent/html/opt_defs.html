opt

(ɒpt )
Definitions
verb(when intr, foll by for) to show preference (for) or choose (to do something)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from French opter, from Latin optāre to choose

opt for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒpt )
Word forms: opts , opting , opted
Definitions
verbIf you opt for something, or opt to do something, you choose it or decide to do it in preference to anything else. [V + for] ⇒ Depending on your circumstances you may wish to opt for one method or the other. [V to-inf] ⇒ Our students can also opt to stay in residence.► →  opt in► →  opt out
