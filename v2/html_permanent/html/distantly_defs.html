Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adverb & adverb [also ADV -ed]Distantly means very far away. [literary] ⇒ They were too distantly seated for any conversation. ⇒ Distantly, to her right, she could make out the town of Chiffa.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v]If you are distantly aware of something or if you distantly remember it, you are aware of it or remember it, but not very strongly. ⇒ She became distantly aware that the light had grown brighter. ⇒ They distantly remember that the islands were the only part of Britain occupied during the war.3. graded adverb [ADV after v]If you do or say something distantly, you do it without showing much emotion or involvement, for example because you are thinking about something else or because you do not care. ⇒ 'Do you like this colour, Victor?' she asked. 'It is fine,' said Vincent distantly.4. → see also distant
