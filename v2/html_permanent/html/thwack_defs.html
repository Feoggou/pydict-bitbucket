thwack

(θwæk )
Definitions
verb1. to beat, hit, or flog, esp with something flatnoun2. a. a blow with something flatb. the sound made by itexclamation3. an exclamation imitative of this sound
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

thwacker (ˈthwacker) noun

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

countable nounA thwack is a sound made when two solid objects hit each other hard. [+ of] ⇒ I listened to the thwack of the metal balls. ⇒ Then the woodcutter let his axe fly–Thwack! Everyone heard it.
