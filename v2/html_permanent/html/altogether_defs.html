Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [ADV after v]You use altogether to emphasize that something has stopped, been done, or finished completely. [emphasis] ⇒ When Artie stopped calling altogether, Julie found a new man. ⇒ His tour may have to be cancelled altogether. ⇒ Clinical test results are certainly encouraging - 10 per cent of wrinkles disappear altogether.2. adverb [ADV adj/adv]You use altogether in front of an adjective or adverb to emphasize a quality that someone or something has. [emphasis] ⇒ The choice of language is altogether different. ⇒ Today's celebrations have been altogether more sedate. ⇒ This wine has an altogether stronger, more pronounced flavour than their other white wines.3. adverb [with neg]You use altogether to modify a negative statement and make it less forceful. ⇒ We were not altogether sure that the comet would miss the Earth. ⇒ The fashion business, he claims, not altogether convincingly, is more real than the film business. ⇒ 'I'm not altogether a fool,' she said gruffly.4. adverbYou can use altogether to introduce a summary of what you have been saying. ⇒ Altogether, it was a delightful town garden, peaceful and secluded.5. adverbIf several amounts add up to a particular amount altogether, that amount is their total. in total ⇒ Britain has a dozen warships in the area, with a total of five thousand military personnel altogether. ⇒ Altogether seven inmates escaped by scaling a wall and climbing down scaffolding.
