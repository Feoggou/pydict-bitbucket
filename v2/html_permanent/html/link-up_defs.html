link up

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. reciprocal phrasal verb & phrasal verbIf you link up with someone, you join them for a particular purpose. [V P + with] ⇒ They linked up with a series of local anti-nuclear and anti-apartheid groups. [V P] ⇒ The Russian and American armies linked up for the first time on the banks of the river Elbe.2. phrasal verb [usu passive]If one thing is linked up to another, the two things are connected to each other. [be V-ed P + to] ⇒ The television screens of the next century will be linked up to an emerging world telecommunications grid. →  link

link-up

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Word forms: link-ups
Definitions
1. countable nounA link-up is a connection between two machines or communication systems. [+ with] ⇒ ...a live satellite link-up with Bonn. [+ with] ⇒ ...computer link-ups with banks in Spain, Portugal, and France. [Also + between]2. countable nounA link-up is a relationship or partnership between two organizations. [+ between] ⇒ ...new link-ups between school and commerce. ⇒ The U.S. airline has just announced a formal link-up with British Airways. [Also + with]
