knell

(nɛl )
Definitions
noun1. the sound of a bell rung to announce a death or a funeral2. something that precipitates or indicates death or destructionverb3. (intransitive) to ring a knell4. (transitive) to proclaim or announce by or as if by a tolling bell
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English cnyll; related to Middle High German knüllen to strike, Dutch knallen to bang
Example sentences containing 'knell'
For me, the death knell of my carefree youth, the herald of sombre responsibility, was when I started having to buy lavender toilet paper. Alex George LOVE YOU MADLYIt was a line from Macbeth: `Hear it not, Duncan, for it is a knell summoning thee to heaven or to hell. Collins, Larry BLACK EAGLES
