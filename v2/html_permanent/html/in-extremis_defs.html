in extremis

Latin (ɪn ɪkˈstriːmɪs)
Definitions
adverb1. in extremity; in dire straits2. at the point of death
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

literally: in the furthest reaches

in extremis

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪn ɪkstriːmɪs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. phraseIf someone or something is in extremis, they are in a very difficult situation and have to use extreme methods. [formal] ⇒ Yet now he was here, in extremis, to seek an alliance. ⇒ The use of antibiotics is permitted only in extremis.2. phraseYou can say that someone is in extremis when they are very ill and likely to die. [formal] ⇒ ...to treat patients who were in extremis.
