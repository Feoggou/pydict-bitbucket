make over

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to transfer the title or possession of (property, etc)2. to renovate or remodel ⇒ she made over the dress to fit her sisternoun makeover (ˈmeɪkˌəʊvə )3. a complete remodelling4. a series of alterations, including beauty treatments and new clothes, intended to make a noticeable improvement in a person's appearance

phrasal verbIf you make something over to someone, you legally transfer the ownership of it to them. [be V-ed P to n] ⇒ Hampton Court was made over to Henry VIII as a present. [V P to n n] ⇒ John had made over to him most of the land. [V n P to n] ⇒ They moved on to a larger farm and in time made it over to Francis. →  make
