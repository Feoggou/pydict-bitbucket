seniority

(ˌsiːnɪˈɒrɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the state of being senior2. precedence in rank, etc, due to senior status

seniority

Powered by COBUILD
(siːniɒrɪti , US -ɔːrɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounA person's seniority in an organization is the importance and power that they have compared with others, or the fact that they have worked there for a long time. ⇒ He has said he will fire editorial employees without regard to seniority.
Example sentences containing 'seniority'
Instead its purpose was to remind all present of his seniority , and therefore his authority, over the rest of us. Magnus Mills THE SCHEME FOR FULL EMPLOYMENTPeople of your seniority don't take off into the blue with any anonymity. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENElkins was a disbarred lawyer with some seniority in Joliet and he understood about such things. Hillerman, Tony TALKING GOD
