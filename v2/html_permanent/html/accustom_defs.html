accustom

(əˈkʌstəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive; usually foll by to) to make (oneself) familiar (with) or used (to), as by practice, habit, or experience
Word origin
C15: from Old French acostumer, from costume custom

(əkʌstəm )
Word forms: accustoms , accustoming , accustomed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you accustom yourself or another person to something, you make yourself or them become used to it. [formal] [V pron-refl + to] ⇒ The team has accustomed itself to the pace of first division rugby. [V n + to] ⇒ Shakespeare has accustomed us to a mixture of humor and tragedy in the same play.2. → see also accustomed
