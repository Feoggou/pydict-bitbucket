solidify

(səˈlɪdɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied1. to make or become solid or hard2. to make or become strong, united, determined, etc

Derived Forms

Powered by COBUILD
(səlɪdɪfaɪ )
Word forms: solidifies , solidifying , solidified
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbWhen a liquid solidifies or is solidified, it changes into a solid. [V] ⇒ The thicker lava would have taken two weeks to solidify. [V n] ⇒ The Energy Department plans to solidify the deadly waste in a high-tech billion-dollar factory. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a frying-pan full of solidified fat.2. ergative verb & verbIf something such as a position or opinion solidifies, or if something solidifies it, it becomes firmer and more definite and unlikely to change. [V] ⇒ Her attitudes solidified through privilege and habit. [V n] ⇒ ...his attempt to solidify his position as chairman. [V n] ⇒ Her behavior this week has solidified her support within the Department of Justice.
