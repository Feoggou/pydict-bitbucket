Synonyms of glance

glance

verb

  1. peek, look, view, check, clock (British, informal), gaze, glimpse, check out (informal), peep, take a dekko at (British, slang)
    • He glanced at his watch.
    OPPOSITES:  study, scrutinize, peruse
  2. gleam, reflect, flash, shine, glitter, shimmer, twinkle, glint, glimmer, glisten
    • A light glanced in the distance.
  3. (with over, through, etc.) scan, browse, dip into, leaf through, flip through, thumb through, skim through, riffle through, run over or through, surf (computing)
    • I picked up the book and glanced through it.

noun

  1. peek, look, glimpse, peep, squint, butcher's (British, slang), quick look, gander (informal), brief look, dekko (slang), shufti (British, slang), gink (New Zealand, slang)
    • She stole a quick glance at her watch.
    OPPOSITES:  examination, inspection, good look, perusal
  2. gleam, flash, reflection, sparkle, twinkle, glint, glimmer
  3. allusion, reference, passing mention
  • See at first glance
  • See glance off

usage note:   Care should be taken not to confuse glance and glimpse: he caught a glimpse (not glance) of her making her way through the crowd; he gave a quick glance (not glimpse) at his watch. A glance is a deliberate action, while a glimpse seems opportunistic.
