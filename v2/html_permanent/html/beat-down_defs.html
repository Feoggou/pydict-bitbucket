beat down

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) informal to force or persuade (a seller) to accept a lower price ⇒ I beat him down three pounds2. (intransitive) (of the sun) to shine intensely; be very hot

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbWhen the sun beats down, it is very hot and bright.2. phrasal verbWhen the rain beats down, it rains very hard. [V P] ⇒ Even in the winter with the rain beating down, it's nice and cosy in there.3. phrasal verbIf you beat down a person who is selling you something, you force them to accept a lower price for it than they wanted to get. [V n P] ⇒ A fair employer, when arranging for the pay of a carpenter, does not try to beat him down. [V P n] ⇒ Beat down the seller to the price that suits you. →  beat
