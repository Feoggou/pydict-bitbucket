migrate

(maɪˈɡreɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to go from one region, country, or place of abode to settle in another, esp in a foreign country2. (of birds, fishes, etc) to journey between different areas at specific times of the year

Derived Forms

migrator (miˈgrator) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin migrāre to change one's abode

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf people migrate, they move from one place to another, especially in order to find work or to live somewhere for a short time. [V prep/adv] ⇒ People migrate to cities like Jakarta in search of work. [V] ⇒ Farmers have learned that they have to migrate if they want to survive.migration (maɪgreɪʃən )Word forms: migrations variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the migration of Soviet Jews to Israel.2. verbWhen birds, fish, or animals migrate, they move at a particular season from one part of the world or from one part of a country to another, usually in order to breed or to find new feeding grounds. [V] ⇒ Most birds have to fly long distances to migrate. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...a dam system that kills the fish as they migrate from streams to the ocean.migration variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the migration of animals in the Serengeti.
