affront

(əˈfrʌnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a deliberate insultverb (transitive)2. to insult, esp openly3. to offend the pride or dignity of4. obsolete to confront defiantly
Word origin
C14: from Old French afronter to strike in the face, from Vulgar Latin affrontāre (unattested), from the Latin phrase ad frontem to the face

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something affronts you, you feel insulted and hurt because of it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ ...an incident which particularly affronted Kasparov.affronted graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ] ⇒ He pretended to be affronted, but inwardly he was pleased. ⇒ Reggie reacted with the same affronted horror Midge had felt.2. countable noun [usu sing]If something is an affront to you, it is an obvious insult to you. [+ to] ⇒ It's an affront to human dignity to keep someone alive like this. ⇒ She has taken my enquiry as a personal affront.
