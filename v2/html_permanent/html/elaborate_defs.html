elaborate

Definitions
adjective (ɪˈlæbərɪt )1. planned or executed with care and exactness; detailed2. marked by complexity, ornateness, or detailverb (ɪˈlæbəˌreɪt )3. (intr; usually foll by on or upon) to add information or detail (to an account); expand (upon)4. (transitive) to work out in detail; develop5. (transitive) to make more complicated or ornate6. (transitive) to produce by careful labour; create7. (transitive) physiology to change (food or simple substances) into more complex substances for use in the body

Word Origin

Definitions
pronunciation note:   The adjective is pronounced (ɪlæbərət ). The verb is pronounced (ɪlæbəreɪt ).1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use elaborate to describe something that is very complex because it has a lot of different parts. ⇒ ...an elaborate research project. ⇒ ...an elaborate ceremony that lasts for eight days.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Elaborate plans, systems, and procedures are complicated because they have been planned in very great detail, sometimes too much detail. ⇒ ...elaborate efforts at the highest level to conceal the problem. ⇒ ...an elaborate management training scheme for graduates.elaborately graded adverb & adverb ⇒ It was clearly an elaborately planned operation.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Elaborate clothing or material is made with a lot of detailed artistic designs. ⇒ He is known for his elaborate costumes.elaborately graded adverb & adverb ⇒ ...elaborately costumed dolls.4. verbIf you elaborate a plan or theory, you develop it by making it more complicated and more effective. [V n] ⇒ His task was to elaborate policies which would make a market economy compatible with a clean environment. [V n] ⇒ ...the plan elaborated by the five permanent members of the U.N. Security Council.elaboration (ɪlæbəreɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the elaboration of specific policies and mechanisms.5. verbIf you elaborate on something that has been said, you say more about it, or give more details. [V + on] ⇒ A spokesman declined to elaborate on a statement released late yesterday. [V] ⇒ Would you care to elaborate?
