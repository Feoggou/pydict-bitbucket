Definitions
adjective(Mechanical engineering: General) Incompressible fluids and solids will not change in volume if a pressure is applied to them. ⇒ Water is not incompressible - it reduces in volume by around 0.5 percent per 1,000 psi of pressure. ⇒ Solid matter is rigid, has a fixed shape, and is incompressible. ⇒ Incompressible fluids and solids will not change in volume if a pressure is applied to them.
