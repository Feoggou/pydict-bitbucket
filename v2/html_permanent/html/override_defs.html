override

(ˌəʊvəˈraɪd )
Definitions
verb -rides, -riding, -rode or -ridden (transitive)1. to set aside or disregard with superior authority or power2. to supersede or annul3. to dominate or vanquish by or as if by trampling down4. to take manual control of (a system that is usually under automatic control)5. to extend or pass over, esp to overlap6. to ride (a horse) too hard7. to ride over or acrossnoun8. a device or system that can override an automatic control

override for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
also over-ride
Word forms: overrides, overriding , overrode , overridden
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (oʊvəʳraɪd ). The noun is pronounced (oʊvəʳraɪd ).1. verbIf one thing in a situation overrides other things, it is more important than them. [V n] ⇒ The welfare of a child should always override the wishes of its parents. [V n] ⇒ Their work is frequently an obsession that overrides all other considerations.2. verbIf someone in authority overrides a person or their decisions, they cancel their decisions. [V n] ⇒ The president vetoed the bill, and the Senate failed by a single vote to override his veto. [V n] ⇒ I'm applying in advance for the authority to override him. [V n] ⇒ Big companies think they can sometimes override local opinion.3. countable nounAn override is an attempt to cancel someone's decisions by using your authority over them or by gaining more votes than them in an election or contest. [US] ⇒ The bill now goes to the House where an override vote is expected to fail. ⇒ An override of the veto appears unlikely.
