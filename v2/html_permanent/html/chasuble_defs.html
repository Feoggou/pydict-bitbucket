chasuble

(ˈtʃæzjʊbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounChristianity a long sleeveless outer vestment worn by a priest when celebrating Mass
Word origin
C13: from French, from Late Latin casubla garment with a hood, apparently from casula cloak, literally: little house, from Latin casa cottage
Example sentences containing 'chasuble'
Though the face may be hidden by visor or veil, yet those with skill to read will know how to interpret the blazon or the chasuble. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEAD
