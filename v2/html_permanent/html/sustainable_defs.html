sustainable

(səˈsteɪnəbəl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. capable of being sustained2. (of economic development, energy sources, etc) capable of being maintained at a steady level without exhausting natural resources or causing severe ecological damage ⇒ sustainable development3. (of economic growth) non-inflationary

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use sustainable to describe the use of natural resources when this use is kept at a steady level that is not likely to damage the environment. ⇒ ...the management, conservation and sustainable development of forests. ⇒ Try to buy wood that you know has come from a sustainable source.sustainability (səsteɪnəbɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ ...the growing concern about environmental sustainability.sustainably adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ It wants timber to come, where possible, from sustainably managed sources.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA sustainable plan, method, or system is designed to continue at the same rate or level of activity without any problems. OPPOSITE: unsustainable ⇒ The creation of an efficient and sustainable transport system is critical to the long-term future of London. ⇒ ...a sustainable recovery in consumer spending.sustainability uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...doubts about the sustainability of the current economic expansion.
