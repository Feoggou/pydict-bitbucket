Worcester

(ˈwʊstə )
Definitions
noun1. a cathedral city in W central England, the administrative centre of Worcestershire on the River Severn: scene of the battle (1651) in which Charles II was defeated by Cromwell. Pop: 94 029 (2001)2. an industrial city in the US, in central Massachusetts: Clark University (1887). Pop: 175 706 (2003 est)3. a town in S South Africa; centre of a fruit-growing region. Pop: 66 349 (2001)
