poised

(pɔɪzd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. self-possessed; dignified; exhibiting composure2. balanced and prepared for action ⇒ a skier poised at the top of the slope

poised

Powered by COBUILD
(pɔɪzd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf a part of your body is poised, it is completely still but ready to move at any moment. ⇒ He studied the keyboard carefully, one finger poised.2. adjective [v-link ADJ, usu ADJ to-inf]If someone is poised to do something, they are ready to take action at any moment. all set ⇒ Britain was poised to fly medical staff to the country at short notice. [+ for] ⇒ U.S. forces are poised for a massive air, land and sea assault.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are poised, you are calm, dignified, and self-controlled. ⇒ She was self-assured, poised, almost self-satisfied. ⇒ Rachel appeared poised and calm.
