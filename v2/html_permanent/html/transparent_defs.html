transparent

(trænsˈpærənt , -ˈpɛər-)
Definitions
adjective1. permitting the uninterrupted passage of light; clear ⇒ a window is transparent2. easy to see through, understand, or recognize; obvious3. (of a substance or object) permitting the free passage of electromagnetic radiation ⇒ a substance that is transparent to X-rays4. candid, open, or frank
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

transparently (transˈparently) adverbtransparentness (transˈparentness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin transpārēre to show through, from Latin trans- + pārēre to appear

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf an object or substance is transparent, you can see through it. ⇒ ...a sheet of transparent coloured plastic. ⇒ I looked at his thin face with its almost transparent skin.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a situation, system, or activity is transparent, it is easily understood or recognized. ⇒ We are now striving hard to establish a transparent parliamentary democracy. ⇒ The company has to make its accounts and operations as transparent as possible.transparently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ The system was clearly not functioning smoothly or transparently. ⇒ The book is remarkably accurate and transparently fair-minded.3. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use transparent to describe a statement or action that is obviously dishonest or wrong, and that you think will not deceive people. ⇒ ...a transparent effort by officials to blame foreigners. ⇒ He thought he could fool people with transparent deceptions.transparently graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ To force this agreement on the nation is transparently wrong. ⇒ Her answers were transparently untruthful.
