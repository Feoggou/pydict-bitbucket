inclined

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnklaɪnd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf, so ADJ]If you are inclined to behave in a particular way, you often behave in that way, or you want to do so. ⇒ Nobody felt inclined to argue with Smith. ⇒ He was inclined to self-pity. ⇒ If you are so inclined, you can watch TV.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that you are inclined to have a particular opinion, you mean that you hold this opinion but you are not expressing it strongly. [vagueness] ⇒ I am inclined to think that the ancient Greeks understood this better than we do. ⇒ I am inclined to agree with Alan.3. adjective [adv ADJ]Someone who is mathematically inclined or artistically inclined, for example, has a natural talent for mathematics or art. ⇒ Bratby's grandfather had been artistically inclined. ⇒ ...the needs of academically inclined pupils.4. → See also incline
