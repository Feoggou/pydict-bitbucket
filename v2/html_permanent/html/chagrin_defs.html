chagrin

(ˈʃæɡrɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a feeling of annoyance or mortificationverb (transitive)2. to embarrass and annoy; mortify
Derived forms
chagrined (ˈchagrined) or chagrinned (ˈchagrinned) adjective
Word origin
C17: from French chagrin, chagriner, of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [usu with poss]Chagrin is a feeling of disappointment, upset, or annoyance, perhaps because of your own failure. [formal, written] ⇒ One of the first things we did when we moved in, to the chagrin of the architect, was to replace the leaded windows.
