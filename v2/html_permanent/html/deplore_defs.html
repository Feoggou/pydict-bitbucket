deplore

(dɪˈplɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to express or feel sorrow about; lament; regret2. to express or feel strong disapproval of; censure
Derived forms
deplorer (deˈplorer) noundeploringly (deˈploringly) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Old French deplorer, from Latin dēplōrāre to weep bitterly, from plōrāre to weep, lament

Word forms: deplores , deploring , deplored
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that you deplore something, you think it is very wrong or immoral. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He's a judo black belt but he says he deplores violence. [V n] ⇒ He deplored the fact that the Foreign Secretary was driven into resignation. [V n] ⇒ I deplore what has happened.
Example sentences containing 'deplore'
The watermen joined in drinks at the Admiral's Head to deplore this sad dropping away from standards. Butler, Gwendoline COFFIN ON THE WATERMuch as I deplore the destruction of part of the wood, sometimes it's necessary to compromise. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERHe might deplore its end result, but Tony couldn't find it in his heart to condemn unequivocally the desire for vengeance that fuelled him. Val McDermid THE LAST TEMPTATIONI do not deplore your imagination, my dear, for indeed it is one of your more charming qualities. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY
