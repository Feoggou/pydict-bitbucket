candidate

(ˈkændɪˌdeɪt , -dɪt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person seeking or nominated for election to a position of authority or honour or selection for a job, promotion, etc2. a person taking an examination or test3. a person or thing regarded as suitable or likely for a particular fate or position ⇒ this wine is a candidate for his cellar
Derived forms
candidacy (ˈkændɪdəsɪ ), candidature (ˈkændɪdətʃə ) or candidateˌship (ˈcandidateˌship) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin candidātus clothed in white (because in ancient Rome a candidate wore a white toga), from candidus white

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA candidate is someone who is being considered for a position, for example someone who is running in an election or applying for a job. ⇒ The Democratic candidate is still leading in the polls. ⇒ He is a candidate for the office of Governor. ⇒ We all spoke to them and John emerged as the best candidate.2. countable nounA candidate is someone who is taking an examination. [British]3. countable nounA candidate is someone who is studying for a degree at a college. [US]4. countable nounA candidate is a person or thing that is regarded as being suitable for a particular purpose or as being likely to do or be a particular thing. ⇒ I think Birmingham City are prime candidates for relegation next season. [+ for] ⇒ Those who are overweight or indulge in high-salt diets are candidates for hypertension.
