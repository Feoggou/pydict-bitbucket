muse1

(mjuːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when intr, often foll by on or about) to reflect (about) or ponder (on), usually in silence2. (intransitive) to gaze thoughtfullynoun3. archaic a state of abstraction

Derived Forms

muser (ˈmuser) nounmuseful (ˈmuseful) adjectivemusefully (ˈmusefully) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French muser, perhaps from mus snout, from Medieval Latin mūsus

Muse

(mjuːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounGreek mythology any of nine sister goddesses, each of whom was regarded as the protectress of a different art or science. Daughters of Zeus and Mnemosyne, the nine are Calliope, Clio, Erato, Euterpe, Melpomene, Polyhymnia, Terpsichore, Thalia, and Urania

muse

Powered by COBUILD
(mjuːz )
Word forms: muses , musing , mused
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you muse on something, you think about it, usually saying or writing what you are thinking at the same time. [written] [V + on/about/over] ⇒ Many of the papers muse on the fate of the President. [V with quote] ⇒ 'As a whole,' she muses, 'the 'organized church' turns me off'. [V that] ⇒ He once mused that he would have voted Labour in 1964 had he been old enough.musing Word forms: musings countable noun ⇒ His musings were interrupted by Montagu who came and sat down next to him.2. countable nounA muse is a person, usually a woman, who gives someone, usually a man, a desire to create art, poetry, or music, and gives them ideas for it. [+ to] ⇒ Once she was a nude model and muse to French artist Henri Matisse. ⇒ ...the muses who fuel his inspiration.
