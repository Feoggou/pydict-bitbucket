intolerant

(ɪnˈtɒlərənt )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking respect for practices and beliefs other than one's own2. (postpositive; foll by of) not able or willing to tolerate or endure ⇒ intolerant of noise
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intolerance (inˈtolerance), intolerantness (inˈtolerantness) or intoleration (inˌtoleˈration) nounintolerantly (inˈtolerantly) adverb

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as intolerant, you mean that they do not accept behaviour and opinions that are different from their own. [disapproval] ⇒ He was intolerant of both suggestions and criticisms. ⇒ ...intolerant attitudes toward non-Catholics.
