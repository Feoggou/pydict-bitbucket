promulgate

(ˈprɒməlˌɡeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to put into effect (a law, decree, etc), esp by formal proclamation2. to announce or declare officially3. to make widespread

Derived Forms

ˌpromulgation (ˌpromulˈgation) nounpromulˌgator (ˈpromulˌgator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōmulgāre to bring to public knowledge; probably related to provulgāre to publicize, from pro-1 + vulgāre to make common, from vulgus the common people
