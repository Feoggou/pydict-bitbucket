quack1

(kwæk )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (of a duck) to utter a harsh guttural sound2. to make a noise like a ducknoun3. the harsh guttural sound made by a duck
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: of imitative origin; related to Dutch kwakken, German quacken

quack2

(kwæk )
Definitions
noun1. a. an unqualified person who claims medical knowledge or other skillsb. (as modifier) ⇒ a quack doctor2. British, Australian and New Zealand informal a doctor; physician or surgeonverb3. (intransitive) to act in the manner of a quack
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

quackish (ˈquackish) adjective

Word Origin

C17: short for quacksalver

quack

Powered by COBUILD
(kwæk ) for learners of English
Word forms: quacks , quacking , quacked
Definitions
1. countable noun [oft N n]If you call someone a quack or a quack doctor, you mean that they claim to be skilled in medicine but are not. [disapproval] ⇒ I went everywhere for treatment, tried all sorts of quacks.2. adjective [ADJ n]Quack remedies or quack cures are medical treatments that you think are unlikely to work because they are not scientific. [disapproval] ⇒ Why do intelligent people find quack remedies so appealing?3. verbWhen a duck quacks, it makes the noise that ducks typically make. [V] ⇒ There were ducks quacking on the lawn.Quack is also a noun. ⇒ Suddenly he heard a quack.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
