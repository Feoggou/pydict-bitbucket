poetry

(ˈpəʊɪtrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. literature in metrical form; verse2. the art or craft of writing verse3. poetic qualities, spirit, or feeling in anything4. anything resembling poetry in rhythm, beauty, etc

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin poētria, from Latin poēta poet

poetry

Powered by COBUILD
(poʊɪtri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounPoems, considered as a form of literature, are referred to as poetry. ⇒ ...Russian poetry. ⇒ Lawrence Durrell wrote a great deal of poetry. ⇒ Since when have you been interested in poetry?2. uncount nounYou can describe something very beautiful as poetry. ⇒ His music is purer poetry than a poem in words.
