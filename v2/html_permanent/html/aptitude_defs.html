aptitude

(ˈæptɪˌtjuːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. inherent or acquired ability2. ease in learning or understanding; intelligence3. the condition or quality of being apt
Word origin
C15: via Old French from Late Latin aptitūdō, from Latin aptus apt

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounSomeone's aptitude for a particular kind of work or activity is their ability to learn it quickly and to do it well. [+ for] ⇒ An aptitude for computing is beneficial for students taking this degree. ⇒ Some students have more aptitude for academic work than others.
