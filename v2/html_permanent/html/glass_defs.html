glass

(ɡlɑːs )
Definitions
noun1. a. a hard brittle transparent or translucent noncrystalline solid, consisting of metal silicates or similar compounds. It is made from a fused mixture of oxides, such as lime, silicon dioxide, etc, and is used for making windows, mirrors, bottles, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ a glass bottle ▶ Related adjectives: vitreous, vitric2. any compound that has solidified from a molten state into a noncrystalline form3. something made of glass, esp a drinking vessel, a barometer, or a mirror4. Also called: glassful the amount contained in a drinking glass5. glassware collectively6. See volcanic glass7. See fibreglassverb (transitive)8. to cover with, enclose in, or fit with glass9. informal to hit (someone) in the face with a glass or a bottle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
