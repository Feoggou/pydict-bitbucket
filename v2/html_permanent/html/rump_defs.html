rump

(rʌmp )
Definitions
noun1. the hindquarters of a mammal, not including the legs2. the rear part of a bird's back, nearest to the tail3. a person's buttocks4. Also called: rump steak a cut of beef from behind the loin and above the round5. an inferior remnant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rumpless (ˈrumpless) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Scandinavian; compare Danish rumpe, Icelandic rumpr, German Rumpf trunk of the body

(rʌmp ) for learners of English
Word forms: rumps
Definitions
1. singular nounThe rump of a group, organization, or country consists of the members who remain in it after the rest have left. [mainly British] [+ of] ⇒ The rump of the party does in fact still have considerable assets. ⇒ He finds himself heading a rump party largely out of tune with his own views.2. countable noun [usu poss N]An animal's rump is its rear end. ⇒ The cows' rumps were marked with a number.3. →  rump steak4. countable noun [usu sing]A person's rump is his or her bottom. ⇒ ...jeans stretching across her rump.
