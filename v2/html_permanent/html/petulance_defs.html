petulance

Powered by COBUILD
(petʃʊləns )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounPetulance is unreasonable, childish bad temper over something unimportant. ⇒ His petulance made her impatient.
Example sentences containing 'petulance'
Leo sprang to his feet with an expression of petulance at yet another interruption. Fraser, Anthea PRETTY MAIDS ALL IN A ROWWe are allowed our fits of melancholy, bitterness, envy, grief, even petulance. Fay Weldon THE PRESIDENT'S CHILDThe wail betokened petulance , not major grief, but what did Lady Rice know? Weldon, Fay SPLITTING
