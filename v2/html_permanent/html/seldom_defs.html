seldom

(ˈsɛldəm )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adverbnot often; rarely. Also (obsolete): seldomly

Word Origin

Old English seldon; related to Old Norse sjāldan, Old High German seltan

seldom

Powered by COBUILD
(seldəm )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
broad negative adverb & adverb [ADV before v]If something seldom happens, it happens only occasionally. ⇒ They seldom speak. ⇒ I've seldom felt so happy. ⇒ We were seldom at home.
Example sentences containing 'seldom'
His eyes could seldom meet the eyes of anyone else for long. Ferrars, Elizabeth MURDER MOVES INIn his professional life he seldom made mistakes - he saw to that. Martin, Joy THE IMAGE OF LAURAGraham knew he was taking a big risk but a hunch told him Drago was lying -- and his hunches were seldom wrong.
