meddle

(ˈmɛdəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (usually foll by with) to interfere officiously or annoyingly2. (usually foll by in) to involve oneself unwarrantedly ⇒ to meddle in someone's private affairs

Derived Forms

meddler (ˈmeddler) nounmeddling (ˈmeddling) adjectivemeddlingly (ˈmeddlingly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French medler, ultimately from Latin miscēre to mix

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that someone meddles in something, you are criticizing the fact that they try to influence or change it without being asked. [disapproval] [V + in/with] ⇒ Already some people are asking whether scientists have any right to meddle in such matters. [V] ⇒ If only you hadn't felt compelled to meddle. [V-ing] ⇒ ...the inept and meddling bureaucrats.meddler Word forms: meddlers countable noun ⇒ They view activists as little more than meddlers.
