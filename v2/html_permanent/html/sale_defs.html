sale

(seɪl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the exchange of goods, property, or services for an agreed sum of money or credit2. the amount sold3. the opportunity to sell; market ⇒ there was no sale for luxuries4. the rate of selling or being sold ⇒ a slow sale of synthetic fabrics5. a. an event at which goods are sold at reduced prices, usually to clear old stocksb. (as modifier) ⇒ sale bargains6. an auction

Word Origin

Old English sala, from Old Norse sala. See also sell

sale

Powered by COBUILD
(seɪl )
Word forms: sales
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. singular nounThe sale of goods is the act of selling them for money. [+ of] ⇒ Efforts were made to limit the sale of alcohol. [+ to] ⇒ ...a proposed arms sale to Saudi Arabia. ⇒ He had never intended living there after his wife's death and immediately set about trying to make a sale.2. plural nounThe sales of a product are the quantity of it that is sold. [+ of] ⇒ The newspaper has sales of 1.72 million. ⇒ ...the huge Christmas sales of computer games. ⇒ ...retail sales figures.3. plural nounThe part of a company that deals with sales deals with selling the company's products. ⇒ Until 1983 he worked in sales and marketing. ⇒ She was their Dusseldorf sales manager.4. countable nounA sale is an occasion when a shop sells things at less than their normal price. ⇒ ...a pair of jeans bought half-price in a sale. ⇒ Many stores have started their January sales a month early.5. countable nounA sale is an event when goods are sold to the person who offers the highest price. ⇒ The painting was bought by dealers at the Christie's sale.6. → see also car boot sale, jumble sale7. →  for sale8. →  on sale9. →  on sale10. →  up for sale
