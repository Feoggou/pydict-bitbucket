judicial

(dʒuːˈdɪʃəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of or relating to the administration of justice2. of or relating to judgment in a court of law or to a judge exercising this function3. inclined to pass judgment; discriminating4. allowed or enforced by a court of law ⇒ a decree of judicial separation5. having qualities appropriate to a judge6. giving or seeking judgment, esp determining or seeking determination of a contested issue
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

judicially (juˈdicially) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin jūdiciālis belonging to the law courts, from jūdicium judgment, from jūdex a judge
