turnover

Powered by COBUILD
(tɜːʳnoʊvəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: turnovers
Definitions
1. variable nounThe turnover of a company is the value of the goods or services sold during a particular period of time. [business] ⇒ Her annual turnover is around £45,000. [+ of] ⇒ The company had a turnover of £3.8 million.2. variable nounThe turnover of people in an organization or place is the rate at which people leave and are replaced. [business] ⇒ Short-term contracts increase staff turnover. ⇒ The industry has a high turnover of young people. [Also + of]

Definitions
noun(Accounting: Management) A company's turnover is the value of the goods or services that it sells during a particular period of time, usually a year. ⇒ The company had a turnover of $3.8 million. ⇒ They expect 25 percent of their annual turnover next year to come from exports. ⇒ A company's turnover is the value of the goods or services that it sells during a particular period of time, usually a year.
