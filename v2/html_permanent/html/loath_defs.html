loath

or loth (ləʊθ )
Definitions
adjective1. (usually foll by to) reluctant or unwilling2. See nothing loath
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

loathness (ˈloathness) or lothness (ˈlothness) noun

Word Origin

Old English lāth (in the sense: hostile); related to Old Norse leithr

loath

Powered by COBUILD
(loʊθ ) also loth for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are loath to do something, you do not want to do it. ⇒ She is loath to give up her hard-earned liberty. [+ to-inf] ⇒ The new finance minister seems loth to cut income tax.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
