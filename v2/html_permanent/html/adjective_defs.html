adjective

(ˈædʒɪktɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a word imputing a characteristic to a noun or pronounb. (as modifier) ⇒ an adjective phrase. Abbreviation: adjadjective2. additional or dependent3. (of law) relating to court practice and procedure, as opposed to the principles of law dealt with by the courts. Compare substantive (sense 7)
Derived forms
adjectival (ˌædʒɪkˈtaɪvəl ) adjective
Word origin
C14: from Late Latin adjectīvus attributive, from adjicere to throw to, add, from ad- to + jacere to throw; in grammatical sense, from the Latin phrase nōmen adjectīvum attributive noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounAn adjective is a word such as 'big', 'dead', or 'financial' that describes a person or thing, or gives extra information about them. Adjectives usually come before nouns or after link verbs.
