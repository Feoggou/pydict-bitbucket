shrivel

(ˈʃrɪvəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled1. to make or become shrunken and withered2. to lose or cause to lose vitality

Word Origin

C16: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish dialect skryvla wrinkle

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use shriveling, shriveledergative verb & verbWhen something shrivels or when something shrivels it, it becomes dryer and smaller, often with lines in its surface, as a result of losing the water it contains. [V] ⇒ The plant shrivels and dies. [V n] ⇒ ...dry weather that shrivelled this summer's crops.Shrivel up means the same as shrivel. [V P] ⇒ The leaves started to shrivel up.shrivelled graded adjective & adjective ⇒ ...a shriveled chestnut. ⇒ It looked old and shrivelled.
