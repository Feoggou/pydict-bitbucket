irradiate

(ɪˈreɪdɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) physics to subject to or treat with light or other electromagnetic radiation or with beams of particles2. (transitive) to expose (food) to electromagnetic radiation to kill bacteria and retard deterioration3. (transitive) to make clear or bright intellectually or spiritually; illumine4. a less common word for radiate (sense 1)5. (intransitive) obsolete to become radiant

[technical] [be V-ed] ⇒ He observed that leukaemia in children was more common if the fathers had been heavily irradiated. [V n] ⇒ ...the Chernobyl disaster, which irradiated large parts of Europe.irradiation (ɪreɪdieɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...the harmful effects of irradiation and pollution.2. verb [usu passive]If food is irradiated, it is treated with radiation to kill bacteria and make it last longer. [technical] [be V-ed] ⇒ It's safe to eat foods that have been irradiated to prolong their shelf life. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the risks and benefits of irradiated food.irradiation uncount noun [oft n N] ⇒ ...doubts about the safety of food irradiation.
