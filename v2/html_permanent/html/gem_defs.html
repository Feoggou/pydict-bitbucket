gem

(dʒɛm )
Definitions
noun1. a precious or semiprecious stone used in jewellery as a decoration; jewel2. a person or thing held to be a perfect example; treasure3. a size of printer's type, approximately equal to 4 point4. New Zealand a type of small sweet cakeverbWord forms: gems, gemming or gemmed5. (transitive) to set or ornament with gems
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gemlike (ˈgemˌlike) adjectivegemmy (ˈgemmy) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French gemme, from Latin gemma bud, precious stone

[informal] [+ of] ⇒ ...a gem of a hotel, Castel Clara. ⇒ Miss Famous, as she was called, was a gem.
