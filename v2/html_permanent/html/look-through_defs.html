look through

Definitions
verb1. (intr, preposition; or tr, adverb) to examine, esp cursorily ⇒ he looked through his notes before the lecture2. (intr, preposition) to ignore (a person) deliberately ⇒ whenever he meets his ex-girlfriend, she looks straight through him

look through

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you look through a group of things, you examine each one so that you can find or choose the one that you want. [V P n] ⇒ Peter starts looking through the mail as soon as the door shuts.2. phrasal verbIf you look through something that has been written or printed, you read it. [V P n] ⇒ He happened to be looking through the medical book 'Gray's Anatomy' at the time.3. phrasal verbIf you say that someone looks through another person, you mean that they look at that person without seeming to see them or recognize them, for example because they are angry with them or are thinking deeply about something else. [V P n] ⇒ As for doctors, when you go to see them they just look right through you. →  look
