newt

(njuːt )
Definitions
noun1. any of various small semiaquatic urodele amphibians, such as Triturus vulgaris (common newt) of Europe, having a long slender body and tail and short feeble legs2. mainly British any other urodele amphibian, including the salamanders
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from a newt, a mistaken division of an ewt; ewt, from Old English eveta eft1
