untidy

(ʌnˈtaɪdɪ )
Definitions
adjective -dier or -diest1. not neat; slovenlyverb -dies, -dying or -died2. (transitive) to make untidy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

untidily (unˈtidily) adverbuntidiness (unˈtidiness) noun

untidy for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌntaɪdi )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as untidy, you mean that it is not neat or well arranged. ⇒ The place quickly became untidy. ⇒ ...a thin man with untidy hair. ⇒ Clothes were thrown in the luggage in an untidy heap.untidily (ʌntaɪdɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ Her long hair tumbles untidily around her shoulders. ⇒ ...the desk piled untidily with books and half-finished homework.untidiness uncount noun ⇒ The dust and untidiness in her room no longer bothered her.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person as untidy, you mean that they do not care about whether things are neat and well arranged, for example in their house. ⇒ I'm untidy in most ways.
