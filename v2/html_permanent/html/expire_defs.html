expire

(ɪkˈspaɪə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to finish or run out; cease; come to an end2. to breathe out (air); exhale3. (intransitive) to die
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

expirer (exˈpirer) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French expirer, from Latin exspīrāre to breathe out, from spīrāre to breathe

(ɪkspaɪəʳ ) for learners of English

Definitions
1. verbWhen something such as a contract, deadline, or visa expires, it comes to an end or is no longer valid. [V] ⇒ He had lived illegally in the United States for five years after his visitor's visa expired.2. verbWhen someone expires, they die. [literary] [V] ⇒ He endured excruciating agonies before he finally expired.
