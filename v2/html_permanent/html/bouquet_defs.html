bouquet

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (bəʊˈkeɪ, buː-) a bunch of flowers, esp a large carefully arranged one2. Also called: nose (buːˈkeɪ ) the characteristic aroma or fragrance of a wine or liqueur3. a compliment or expression of praise
Word origin
C18: from French: thicket, from Old French bosc forest, wood, probably of Germanic origin; see bush1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA bouquet is a bunch of flowers which is attractively arranged. [+ of] ⇒ The woman carried a bouquet of dried violets.2. variable nounThe bouquet of something, especially wine, is the pleasant smell that it has. ⇒ ...a Sicilian wine with a bouquet of cloves.
