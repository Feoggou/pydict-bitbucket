daunt

(dɔːnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr; often passive)1. to intimidate2. to dishearten
Derived forms
daunter (ˈdaunter) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French danter, changed from donter to conquer, from Latin domitāre to tame

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something daunts you, it makes you feel slightly afraid or worried about dealing with it. [V n] ⇒ ...a gruelling journey that would have daunted a woman half her age. [V n] ⇒ I'm somewhat daunted by the size of the task.daunted graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ] ⇒ It is hard to pick up such a book and not to feel a little daunted.
