(bɪgətɪd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is bigoted has strong, unreasonable prejudices or opinions and will not change them, even when they are proved to be wrong. [disapproval] ⇒ He was bigoted and racist.
Example sentences containing 'bigoted'
Yes, these people were vulnerable and ignorant; yes, they were probably crass, most of them, and bigoted and belligerent. Clive Barker EVERVILLE`Are you suggesting that you have a Darwinian right to sound like a bigoted pig? Alex George LOVE YOU MADLY
