Synonyms of simple

simple

adjective

  1. uncomplicated, clear, plain, understandable, coherent, lucid, recognizable, unambiguous, comprehensible, intelligible, uninvolved
    • simple pictures and diagrams
    OPPOSITES:  involved, difficult, complex, complicated, advanced, sophisticated, elaborate, refined, intricate, convoluted, highly developed
  2. easy, straightforward, not difficult, light, elementary, manageable, effortless, painless, uncomplicated, undemanding, easy-peasy (slang)
    • The job itself had been simple enough.
  3. plain, natural, basic, classic, severe, Spartan, uncluttered, unadorned, unfussy, unembellished, bare-bones
    • She's shunned Armani for a simple blouse and jeans.
    OPPOSITES:  elaborate, intricate, ornate, fussy, contrived
  4. pure, mere, sheer, unalloyed
    • His refusal to talk was simple stubbornness.
  5. artless, innocent, naive, natural, frank, green, sincere, simplistic, unaffected, childlike, unpretentious, unsophisticated, ingenuous, guileless
    • He was as simple as a child.
    OPPOSITES:  worldly, smart, sophisticated, artful, worldly-wise
  6. unpretentious, modest, humble, homely, lowly, rustic, uncluttered, unfussy, unembellished
    • It was a simple home.
    OPPOSITES:  fancy, extravagant, flashy
  7. honest, direct, basic, frank, plain, naked, stark, sincere, bald, undeniable, unvarnished
    • The simple truth is that experts haven't a clue.
  8. feeble-minded (informal), slow, thick, stupid, silly, shallow, foolish, dense, dumb (informal), feeble, moronic, obtuse, brainless, credulous, half-witted, dumb-ass (slang)
    • He's no fool, though perhaps a bit simple in some ways.
    OPPOSITES:  knowing, quick, bright, sharp, smart, wise, clever, intelligent, astute, on the ball, quick-witted, quick on the uptake
