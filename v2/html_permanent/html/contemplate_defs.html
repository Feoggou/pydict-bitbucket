contemplate

(ˈkɒntɛmˌpleɪt , -təm-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (mainly tr)1. to think about intently and at length; consider calmly2. (intransitive) to think intently and at length, esp for spiritual reasons; meditate3. to look at thoughtfully; observe pensively4. to have in mind as a possibility ⇒ to contemplate changing jobs
Derived forms
contemˌplator (ˈcontemˌplator) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin contemplāre, from templum temple1

Word forms: contemplates , contemplating , contemplated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you contemplate an action, you think about whether to do it or not. [V n] ⇒ For a time he contemplated a career as an army medical doctor. [V v-ing] ⇒ She contemplates leaving for the sake of the kids.2. verbIf you contemplate an idea or subject, you think about it carefully for a long time. [V n] ⇒ As he lay in his hospital bed that night, he cried as he contemplated his future. [V n] ⇒ That makes it difficult to contemplate the idea that the present policy may not be sustainable.contemplation (kɒntəmpleɪʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ It is a place of quiet contemplation.3. verbIf you contemplate something or someone, you look at them for a long time. [V n] ⇒ He contemplated his hands, still frowning.contemplation uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ He was lost in the contemplation of the landscape for a while.
