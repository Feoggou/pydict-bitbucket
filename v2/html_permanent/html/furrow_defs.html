furrow

(ˈfʌrəʊ )
Definitions
noun1. a long narrow trench made in the ground by a plough or a trench resembling this2. any long deep groove, esp a deep wrinkle on the foreheadverb3. to develop or cause to develop furrows or wrinkles4. to make a furrow or furrows in (land)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

furrower (ˈfurrower) nounfurrowless (ˈfurrowless) adjectivefurrow-like (ˈfurrow-ˌlike) or furrowy (ˈfurrowy) adjective

Word Origin

Old English furh; related to Old Frisian furch, Old Norse for, Old High German furuh furrow, Latin porca ridge between furrows

furrow

Powered by COBUILD
(fʌroʊ , US fɜːr- ) for learners of English
Word forms: furrows , furrowing , furrowed
Definitions
1. countable nounA furrow is a long, thin line in the earth which a farmer makes in order to plant seeds or to allow water to flow along.2. countable nounA furrow is a deep, fairly wide line in the surface of something. ⇒ Dirt bike trails crisscrossed the grassy furrows.3. countable nounA furrow is a deep fold or line in the skin of someone's face. ⇒ ...the deep furrows that marked the corners of his mouth.4. ergative verb & verbIf someone furrows their brow or forehead or if it furrows, deep folds appear in it because the person is annoyed, unhappy, or confused. [written] [V n] ⇒ My bank manager furrowed his brow, fingered his calculator and finally pronounced 'Aha!'. [V] ⇒ Midge's forehead furrowed as she saw that several were drinking. [V-ed] ⇒ Fatigue and stress quickly result in a dull complexion and a furrowed brow.5. →  to plough a furrow
