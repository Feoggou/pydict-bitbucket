peregrine

(ˈpɛrɪɡrɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective archaic1. coming from abroad2. travelling or migratory; wandering

Word Origin

C14: from Latin peregrīnus foreign, from pereger being abroad, from per through + ager land (that is, beyond one's own land)

Within two days he had seen a peregrine falcon, a pair of seals and a group of basking sharks. Philip Marsden THE MAIN CAGESIt's way too big for a peregrine , which is sort of what it does look like.
