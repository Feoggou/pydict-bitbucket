grieve1

(ɡriːv )
Definitions
verb1. to feel or cause to feel great sorrow or distress, esp at the death of someone2. (transitive) obsolete to inflict injury, hardship, or sorrow on
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

griever (ˈgriever) noungrieving (ˈgrieving) noun, adjectivegrievingly (ˈgrievingly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old French grever, from Latin gravāre to burden, from gravis heavy

grieve

Powered by COBUILD
(griːv ) for learners of English
Word forms: grieves , grieving , grieved
Definitions
1. verbIf you grieve over something, especially someone's death, you feel very sad about it. [V prep] ⇒ He's grieving over his dead wife and son. [V] ⇒ I didn't have any time to grieve. [V-ing] ⇒ Margery's grieving family battled to come to terms with their loss.2. verbIf you are grieved by something, it makes you unhappy or upset. [be V-ed + by/at] ⇒ He was deeply grieved by the sufferings of the common people. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ I was grieved to hear of the suicide of James. [V n to-inf] ⇒ It grieved me to see the poor man in such distress. [Also V n]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
