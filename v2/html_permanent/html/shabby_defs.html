shabby

(ˈʃæbɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -bier or -biest1. threadbare or dilapidated in appearance2. wearing worn and dirty clothes; seedy3. mean, despicable, or unworthy ⇒ shabby treatment4. dirty or squalid

Derived Forms

shabbily (ˈshabbily) adverbshabbiness (ˈshabbiness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Old English sceabb scab + -y1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveShabby things or places look old and in bad condition. ⇒ His clothes were old and shabby. ⇒ He walked past her into a tiny, shabby room. ⇒ ...one of the shabbiest and poorest areas of London.shabbily (ʃæbɪli ) graded adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ ...a shabbily dressed young man.shabbiness uncount noun ⇒ ...the shabbiness of the building.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA person who is shabby is wearing old, worn clothes. ⇒ ...a shabby, tall man with dark eyes.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as shabby, you think they behave in an unfair or unacceptable way. [disapproval] ⇒ It was hard to say why the man deserved such shabby treatment. [+ of] ⇒ I knew it was shabby of me, but I couldn't help feeling slightly disappointed.shabbily graded adverb ⇒ I feel I behaved shabbily.
