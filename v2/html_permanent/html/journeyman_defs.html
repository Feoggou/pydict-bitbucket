journeyman

(ˈdʒɜːnɪmən )
Definitions
noun plural -men1. a craftsman, artisan, etc, who is qualified to work at his trade in the employment of another2. a competent workman3. (formerly) a worker hired on a daily wage
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from journey (in obsolete sense: a day's work) + man

journeyman for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɜːʳnimən )
Word forms: journeymen
Definitions
1. countable nounIn former times, a journeyman was a worker who had finished learning a trade and who was employed by someone rather than working on his or her own.2. countable noun [oft N n]If you refer to someone as a journeyman, you mean that they have the basic skill which their job requires, but are not very talented or original. [journalism] ⇒ Douglas was a 29-year-old journeyman fighter, erratic in his previous fights.
