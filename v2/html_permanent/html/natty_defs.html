natty

(ˈnætɪ )
Definitions
adjective -tier or -tiestinformal smart in appearance or dress; spruce; dapper ⇒ a natty outfit
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nattily (ˈnattily) adverbnattiness (ˈnattiness) noun

Word Origin

C18: perhaps from obsolete netty, from net neat1; compare Old French net trim

natty for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(næti )
Word forms: nattier , nattiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe clothes, especially men's clothes, as natty, you mean that they are smart and neat. [informal, approval] ⇒ ...a natty pin stripe suit. ⇒ Cliff was a natty dresser.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as natty, you think it is smart and cleverly designed. [informal, approval] ⇒ ...natty little houses.
