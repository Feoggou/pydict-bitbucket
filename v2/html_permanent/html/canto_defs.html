canto

(ˈkæntəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tos1. music another word for cantus (sense 2)2. a main division of a long poem
Word origin
C16: from Italian: song, from Latin cantus, from canere to sing
Example sentences containing 'canto'
This singing devil, this bugaboo of bel canto , could well be an embodiment of the dark side of his own character. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEAll of that was told in the third canto of the vast epic poem that was every schoolchild's bane, Aithin Furvain's The Book of Changes. Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
