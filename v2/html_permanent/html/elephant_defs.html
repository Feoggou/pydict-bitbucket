elephant

(ˈɛlɪfənt )
Definitions
noun plural -phants or -phant1. either of the two proboscidean mammals of the family Elephantidae. The African elephant (Loxodonta africana) is the larger species, with large flapping ears and a less humped back than the Indian elephant (Elephas maximus), of S and SE Asia2. mainly British a size of writing paper, 23 by 28 inches3. See elephant in the room
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

elephantoid (ˈelephanˌtoid) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Latin elephantus, from Greek elephas elephant, ivory, of uncertain origin
