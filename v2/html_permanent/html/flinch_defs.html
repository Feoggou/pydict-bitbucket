flinch1

(flɪntʃ )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to draw back suddenly, as from pain, shock, etc; wince ⇒ he flinched as the cold water struck him2. (often foll by from) to avoid contact (with); shy away ⇒ he never flinched from his dutynoun3. the act or an instance of drawing back4. a card game in which players build sequences
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flincher (ˈflincher) nounflinchingly (ˈflinchingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Old French flenchir; related to Middle High German lenken to bend, direct

flinch

Powered by COBUILD
(flɪntʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: flinches , flinching , flinched
Definitions
1. verb [usu neg]If you flinch, you make a small sudden movement, especially when something surprises you or hurts you. [V] ⇒ Murat had looked into the eyes of the firing squad without flinching. [V] ⇒ The sharp surface of the rock caught at her skin, making her flinch.2. verbIf you flinch from something unpleasant, you are unwilling to do it or think about it, or you avoid doing it. shy away [V] ⇒ The world community should not flinch in the face of this challenge. [V + from] ⇒ He has never flinched from harsh financial decisions.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
