anything

Powered by COBUILD
(enɪθɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anything in statements with negative meaning to indicate in a general way that nothing is present or that an action or event does not or cannot happen. ⇒ We can't do anything. ⇒ Dad sat, not saying anything. ⇒ She couldn't see or hear anything at all. ⇒ By the time I get home, I'm too tired to do anything active. ⇒ I couldn't manage anything without you.2. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anything in questions and conditional clauses to ask or talk about whether something is present or happening. ⇒ What happened, is anything wrong? ⇒ Did you find anything? ⇒ Is there anything you can do to help? ⇒ If there's anything I could do for him, I would.3. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou can use anything before words which indicate the kind of thing you are talking about. ⇒ More than anything else, he wanted to become a teacher. ⇒ Anything that's cheap this year will be even cheaper next year. ⇒ She collects anything that has charm.4. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anything to emphasize a possible thing, event, or situation, when you are saying that it could be any one of a very large number of things. [emphasis] ⇒ He is young, fresh, and ready for anything. ⇒ At that point, anything could happen. ⇒ He is convinced he just has to say 'please' and he can have anything.5. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anything in expressions such as anything near, anything close to and anything like to emphasize a statement that you are making. [emphasis] ⇒ Doctors have decided the only way he can live anything near a normal life is to give him an operation. ⇒ Only Cowans played anything close to his true form. ⇒ Plainer examples of the early period do not fetch anything like these sums.6. indefinite pronoun & pronounWhen you do not want to be exact, you use anything to talk about a particular range of things or quantities. ⇒ Factory farming has turned the cow into a milk machine, producing anything from 25 to 40 litres of milk per day. ⇒ Fights with his father lasted anything between fifteen minutes and an hour.7. →  as anything8. →  anything but9. →  would not do sth for anything/would not be sth for anything10. →  if anything11. →  or anything
