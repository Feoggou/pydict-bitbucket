Definitions

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use patronisinggraded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is patronizing, they speak or behave towards you in a way that seems friendly, but which shows that they think they are superior to you. [disapproval] ⇒ The tone of the interview was unnecessarily patronizing . ⇒ ...his patronising attitude to the homeless.patronizingly graded adverb condescending ⇒ Schneider patted the girl patronizingly on the cheek.
Example sentences containing 'patronizing'
His tone of voice, so patronizing , so self-righteous, filled her with rage. Stewart, Michael COMPULSIONTheir gaiety was infectious, and I mentally ticked myself off for my patronizing response to the club. Val McDermid DEAD BEATThe Harel women, all three generations of them, were very obviously still patronizing Miss Blanche. Pacter, Trudi YELLOW BIRD
