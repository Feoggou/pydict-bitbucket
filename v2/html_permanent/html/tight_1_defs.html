tight

(taɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. stretched or drawn so as not to be loose; taut ⇒ a tight cord2. fitting or covering in a close manner ⇒ a tight dress3. held, made, fixed, or closed firmly and securely ⇒ a tight knot4. a. of close and compact construction or organization, esp so as to be impervious to water, air, etcb. (in combination) ⇒ watertight, ⇒ airtight5. unyielding or stringent ⇒ to keep a tight hold on resources6. cramped or constricted ⇒ a tight fit7. mean or miserly8. difficult and problematic ⇒ a tight situation9. hardly profitable ⇒ a tight bargain10. economicsa. (of a commodity) difficult to obtain; in excess demandb. (of funds, money, etc) difficult and expensive to borrow because of high demand or restrictive monetary policyc. (of markets) characterized by excess demand or scarcity with prices tending to rise. Compare easy (sense 8)11. (of a match or game) very close or even12. (of a team or group, esp of a pop group) playing well together, in a disciplined coordinated way13. informal drunk14. informal (of a person) showing tension15. archaic or dialect neatadverb16. in a close, firm, or secure way ⇒ pull it tight17. See sit tight18. See sleep tight

Word Origin

C14: probably variant of thight, from Old Norse thēttr close; related to Middle High German dīhte thick
