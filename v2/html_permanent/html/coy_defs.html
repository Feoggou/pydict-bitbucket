coy

(kɔɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (usually of a woman) affectedly demure, esp in a playful or provocative manner2. shy; modest3. evasive, esp in an annoying way
Derived forms
coyish (ˈcoyish) adjectivecoyishly (ˈcoyishly) adverbcoyishness (ˈcoyishness) nouncoyly (ˈcoyly) adverbcoyness (ˈcoyness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French coi reserved, from Latin quiētus quiet

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA coy person is shy, or pretends to be shy, about love and sex. demure ⇒ She is modest without being coy. ⇒ I was sickened by the way Carol charmed all the men by turning coy.coyly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ She smiled coyly at Algie as he took her hand and raised it to his lips.coyness uncount noun ⇒ The public may once have liked her, but her coyness and flirting now interfere with her interviews.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If someone is being coy, they are unwilling to talk about something that they feel guilty or embarrassed about. ⇒ The hotel are understandably coy about the incident. [+ about] ⇒ Mr Alexander is not the slightest bit coy about his ambitions.coyly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The administration coyly refused to put a firm figure on the war's costs.coyness uncount noun reticence ⇒ ...their coyness about financial aid.
