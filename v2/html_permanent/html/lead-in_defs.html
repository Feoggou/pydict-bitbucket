lead-in

(ˈliːdˌɪn )
Definitions
noun1. a. an introduction to a subjectb. (as modifier) ⇒ a lead-in announcement2. the connection between a radio transmitter, receiver, etc, and the aerial or transmission line
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

lead-in

Powered by COBUILD
(liːd ɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: lead-ins
Definitions
countable nounA lead-in is something that is said or done as an introduction before the main subject or event, especially before a radio or television programme. ⇒ They were thrilled, finally, to have a decent lead-in for their 9 a.m. local programs.
