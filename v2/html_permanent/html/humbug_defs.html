humbug

(ˈhʌmˌbʌɡ )
Definitions
noun1. a person or thing that tricks or deceives2. nonsense; rubbish3. British a hard boiled sweet, usually flavoured with peppermint and often having a striped patternverb -bugs, -bugging or -bugged4. to cheat or deceive (someone)

humbug

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌmbʌg ) for learners of English
Word forms: humbugs
Definitions
1. uncount nounIf you describe someone's language or behaviour as humbug, you mean that it is dishonest or insincere. [disapproval] ⇒ There was all the usual humbug and obligatory compliments from ministers. ⇒ Britain's laws on homosexuality are hypocritical humbug.2. countable nounYou can refer to a person as a humbug when you think they are being dishonest, insincere, or pretending to be someone who they are not. [old-fashioned, disapproval] ⇒ What a revolting humbug the man was!3. countable nounA humbug is a hard, striped sweet that tastes of peppermint.
