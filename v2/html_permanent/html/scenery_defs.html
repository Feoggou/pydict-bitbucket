scenery

(ˈsiːnərɪ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -eries1. the natural features of a landscape2. theatre the painted backcloths, stage structures, etc, used to represent a location in a theatre or studio

Word Origin

C18: from Italian scenario

scenery

Powered by COBUILD
(siːnəri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe scenery in a country area is the land, water, or plants that you can see around you. ⇒ ...the island's spectacular scenery. ⇒ Sometimes they just drive slowly down the lane enjoying the scenery. → countryside2. uncount nounIn a theatre, the scenery consists of the structures and painted backgrounds that show where the action in the play takes place.3. →  a change of scenery
