coif

(kɔɪf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a close-fitting cap worn under a veil, worn in the Middle Ages by many women but now only by nuns2. any similar cap, such as a leather cap worn under a chain-mail hood3. (formerly in England) the white cap worn by a serjeant at law4. a base for the elaborate women's headdresses of the 16th century5. (kwɑːf ) a less common word for coiffure (sense 1)verbWord forms: coifs, coiffing or coiffed (transitive)6. to cover with or as if with a coif7. (kwɑːf ) to arrange (the hair)
Word origin
C14: from Old French coiffe, from Late Latin cofea helmet, cap, of obscure origin
Example sentences containing 'coif'
Framed by the coif , her small pretty face, lightly lined, stared at Maria. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LIONShe hung her head before speaking again and her dark hair fanned out around her shoulders like a nun's coif. Spellman, Cathy Cash PAINT THE WIND
