fraught

(frɔːt )
Definitions
adjective1. (usually postpositive; and foll by with) filled or charged; attended ⇒ a venture fraught with peril2. informal showing or producing tension or anxiety ⇒ she looks rather fraught, ⇒ a fraught situation3. archaic (usually postpositive; and foll by with) freightednoun4. an obsolete word for freight
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Middle Dutch vrachten, from vracht freight

fraught

Powered by COBUILD
(frɔːt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [v-link ADJ with n]If a situation or action is fraught with problems or risks, it is filled with them. ⇒ The earliest operations employing this technique were fraught with dangers.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that a situation or action is fraught, you mean that it is worrying or difficult. ⇒ It has been a somewhat fraught day.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
