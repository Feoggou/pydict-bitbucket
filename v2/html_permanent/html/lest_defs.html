lest

(lɛst )
Definitions
conjunction (subordinating; takes should or a subjunctive verb)1. so as to prevent any possibility that ⇒ he fled the country lest he be captured and imprisoned2. (after verbs or phrases expressing fear, worry, anxiety, etc) for fear that; in case ⇒ he was alarmed lest she should find out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English the lǣste, earlier thӯ lǣs the, literally: whereby less that

lest

Powered by COBUILD
(lest ) for learners of English
Definitions
subordinating conjunction & conjunctionIf you do something lest something unpleasant should happen, you do it to try to prevent the unpleasant thing from happening. [formal] ⇒ I was afraid to open the door lest he should follow me. ⇒ The president has gone along with the hardliners lest they be tempted to oust him. ⇒ And, lest we forget, Einstein wrote his most influential papers while working as a clerk.
