ventilate

(ˈvɛntɪˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to drive foul air out of (an enclosed area)2. to provide with a means of airing3. to expose (a question, grievance, etc) to public examination or discussion4. physiology to oxygenate (the blood) in the capillaries of the lungs5. to winnow (grain)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ventilable (ˈventilable) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin ventilāre to fan, from ventulus diminutive of ventus wind
