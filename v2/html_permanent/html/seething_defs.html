seething

(ˈsiːðɪŋ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. boiling or foaming as if boiling2. crowded and full of restless activity3. in a state of extreme agitation, esp through anger

Derived Forms

seethingly (ˈseethingly) adverb
Example sentences containing 'seething'
Half a cable distant across the angry seething stretch of water, a ship was dying. Lunnon-Wood, Mike LET NOT THE DEEPHer ears, sharper than they'd ever been, caught the seething of insect life. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOWBy the time they finally came for her, Adrina was seething. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEP
