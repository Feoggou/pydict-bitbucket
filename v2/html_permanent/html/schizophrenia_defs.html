schizophrenia

(ˌskɪtsəʊˈfriːnɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of a group of psychotic disorders characterized by progressive deterioration of the personality, withdrawal from reality, hallucinations, delusions, social apathy, emotional instability, etc. See catatonia, hebephrenia, paranoia2. informal behaviour that appears to be motivated by contradictory or conflicting principles

Word Origin

C20: from schizo- + Greek phrēn mind + -ia
