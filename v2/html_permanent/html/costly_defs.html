costly

(ˈkɒstlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -lier or -liest1. of great price or value; expensive2. entailing great loss or sacrifice ⇒ a costly victory3. splendid; lavish
Derived forms
costliness (ˈcostliness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(kɒstli , US kɔːst- )
Word forms: costlier , costliest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is costly, you mean that it costs a lot of money, often more than you would want to pay. ⇒ Having professionally-made curtains can be costly, so why not make your own?2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's action or mistake as costly, you mean that it results in a serious disadvantage for them, for example the loss of a large amount of money or the loss of their reputation. ⇒ Psychometric tests can save organizations from grim and costly mistakes. ⇒ This sort of scandal in international banking has been politically costly.
