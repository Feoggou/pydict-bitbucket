corporal1

(ˈkɔːpərəl , -prəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to the body; bodily2. an obsolete word for corporeal
Derived forms
ˌcorporality (ˌcorpoˈrality) nouncorporally (ˈcorporally) adverb
Word origin
C14: from Latin corporālis of the body, from corpus body

corporal2

(ˈkɔːpərəl , -prəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a noncommissioned officer junior to a sergeant in the army, air force, or marines2. (in the Royal Navy) a petty officer who assists the master-at-arms
Derived forms
corporalˌship (ˈcorporalˌship) noun
Word origin
C16: from Old French, via Italian, from Latin caput head; perhaps also influenced in Old French by corps body (of men)

corporal3

(ˈkɔːpərəl , -prəl) or corporale (ˌkɔːpəˈreɪlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna white linen cloth on which the bread and wine are placed during the Eucharist
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin corporāle pallium eucharistic altar cloth, from Latin corporālis belonging to the body, from corpus body (of Christ)
