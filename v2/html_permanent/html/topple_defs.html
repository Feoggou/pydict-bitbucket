topple

(ˈtɒpəl )
Definitions
verb1. to tip over or cause to tip over, esp from a height2. (intransitive) to lean precariously or totter3. (transitive) to overthrow; oust
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: frequentative of top1 (verb)

topple

Powered by COBUILD
(tɒpəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: topples , toppling , toppled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf someone or something topples somewhere or if you topple them, they become unsteady or unstable and fall over. [V adv/prep] ⇒ He just released his hold and toppled slowly backwards. [V n] ⇒ Winds and rain toppled trees and electricity lines. [Also V]Topple over means the same as topple. [V P] ⇒ The tree is so badly damaged they are worried it might topple over. [V P] ⇒ We lost our balance and toppled over on to a table.2. verbTo topple a government or leader, especially one that is not elected by the people, means to cause them to lose power. [journalism] [V n] ⇒ ...the revolution which toppled the regime.
