jolly

(ˈdʒɒlɪ )
Definitions
adjective -lier or -liest1. full of good humour; jovial2. having or provoking gaiety and merrymaking; festive3. greatly enjoyable; pleasingadverb4. British (intensifier) ⇒ you're jolly niceverb -lies, -lying or -lied (transitive) informal5. (often foll by up or along) to try to make or keep (someone) cheerful6. to make good-natured fun ofnoun7. informal, mainly British a festivity or celebration8. informal, mainly British a trip, esp one made for pleasure by a public official or committee at public expense9. British slang a Royal Marine
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jolliness (ˈjolliness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French jolif, probably from Old Norse jōl yule

jolly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɒli )
Word forms: jollier , jolliest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is jolly is happy and cheerful in their appearance or behaviour. ⇒ She was a jolly, kindhearted woman.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A jolly event is lively and enjoyable. ⇒ I was looking forward to a jolly party. ⇒ She had a very jolly time in Korea.3. adverb [ADV adj/adv]Jolly is sometimes used to emphasize an adjective or adverb. [British, informal, old-fashioned, emphasis] ⇒ She was jolly good at jigsaws. ⇒ It was jolly hard work, but I loved it.4. →  jolly well
