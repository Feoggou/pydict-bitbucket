roll in

Definitions
verb (mainly intr)1. (adverb) to arrive in abundance or in large numbers2. (adverb) informal to arrive at one's destination3. (preposition) informal to abound or luxuriate in (wealth, money, etc)4. (adverb; also tr) hockey to return (the ball) to play after it has crossed the touchline

roll in

Powered by COBUILD
or roll into for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verb [usu cont]If something such as money is rolling in, it is appearing or being received in large quantities. [informal] [V P] ⇒ Don't forget, I have always kept the money rolling in. [Also V P n]2. phrasal verbIf someone rolls into a place or rolls in, they arrive in a casual way and often late. [mainly British] [V P] ⇒ 'I've made you late.'—'No that's all right. I can roll in when I feel like it.' [V P n] ⇒ The brothers usually roll into their studio around midday.3. phrasal verbWhen you talk about tanks or troops rolling into a place, you mean that they move into that place in order to take control. [V P n] ⇒ More than 100 tanks rolled into eastern Croatia. [V P n] ⇒ A convoy of United Nations trucks rolled into Sarajevo today. [VP] ⇒ M-60 tanks roll in. →  roll
