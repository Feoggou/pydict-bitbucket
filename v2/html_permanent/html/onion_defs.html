onion

(ˈʌnjən )
Definitions
noun1. an alliaceous plant, Allium cepa, having greenish-white flowers: cultivated for its rounded edible bulb2. the bulb of this plant, consisting of concentric layers of white succulent leaf bases with a pungent odour and taste3. any of several related plants similar to A. cepa, such as A. fistulosum (Welsh onion)4. See know one's onions
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

oniony (ˈoniony) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Anglo-Norman from Old French oignon, from Latin unio onion, related to union
