quota

(ˈkwəʊtə )
Definitions
noun1. the proportional share or part of a whole that is due from, due to, or allocated to a person or group2. a prescribed number or quantity, as of items to be manufactured, imported, or exported, immigrants admitted to a country, or students admitted to a college
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin quota pars how big a share?, from quotus of what number

quota

Powered by COBUILD
(kwoʊtə ) for learners of English
Word forms: quotas
Definitions
1. countable nounA quota is the limited number or quantity of something which is officially allowed. [+ of] ⇒ The quota of four tickets per person had been reduced to two.2. countable noun [N n]A quota is a fixed maximum or minimum proportion of people from a particular group who are allowed to do something, such as come and live in a country or work for the government. ⇒ The bill would force employers to adopt a quota system when recruiting workers. [Also + of]3. countable nounSomeone's quota of something is their expected or deserved share of it. [+ of] ⇒ They have the usual quota of human weaknesses, no doubt.
