cesspool

(ˈsɛsˌpuːl ) or cesspit (ˈsɛsˌpɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: sink, sump a covered cistern, etc, for collecting and storing sewage or waste water2. a filthy or corrupt place ⇒ a cesspool of iniquity
Word origin
C17: changed (through influence of pool1) from earlier cesperalle, from Old French souspirail vent, air, from soupirer to sigh; see suspire
