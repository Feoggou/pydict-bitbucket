tabloid

(ˈtæblɔɪd )
Definitions
noun1. a newspaper with pages about 30 cm (12 inches) by 40 cm (16 inches), usually characterized by an emphasis on photographs and a concise and often sensational style. Compare broadsheet2. (modifier) designed to appeal to a mass audience or readership; sensationalist ⇒ the tabloid press, ⇒ tabloid television
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: from earlier Tabloid, a trademark for a medicine in tablet form
