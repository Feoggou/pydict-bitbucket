rampart

(ˈræmpɑːt )
Definitions
noun1. the surrounding embankment of a fort, often including any walls, parapets, walks, etc, that are built on the bank2. anything resembling a rampart in form or function, esp in being a defence or bulwark3. Canadian a steep rock wall in a river gorgeverb4. (transitive) to provide with a rampart; fortify
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old French, from remparer, from re- + emparer to take possession of, from Old Provençal antparar, from Latin ante before + parāre to prepare
