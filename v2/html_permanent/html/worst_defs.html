worst

(wɜːst )
Definitions
adjective1. the superlative of bad1adverb2. in the most extreme or bad manner or degree3. least well, suitably, or acceptably4. (in combination) in or to the smallest degree or extent; least ⇒ worst-lovednoun5. See the worst6. (often preceded by at) the most poor, unpleasant, or unskilled quality or condition ⇒ television is at its worst these days7. the greatest amount of damage or wickedness of which a person or group is capable ⇒ the invaders came and did their worst8. the weakest effort or poorest achievement that a person or group is capable of making ⇒ the applicant did his worst at the test because he did not want the job9. See the worst10. See if the worst comes to the worst11. See come off worstverb12. (transitive) to get the advantage over; defeat or beat
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English wierrest; related to Old Frisian wersta, Old Saxon, Old High German wirsisto, Old Norse verstr

worst for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɜːʳst )
Definitions
1. Worst is the superlative of bad.2. Worst is the superlative of badly.3. singular nounThe worst is the most unpleasant or unfavourable thing that could happen or does happen. ⇒ Though mine safety has much improved, miners' families still fear the worst. [+ of] ⇒ The country had come through the worst of the recession.4. Worst is used to form the superlative of compound adjectives beginning with 'bad' and 'badly'. For example, the superlative of 'badly-affected' is 'worst-affected'.5. →  worst of all6. →  at (the) worst7. →  at one's worst8. →  if the worst comes to the worst9. →  to do one's worst
