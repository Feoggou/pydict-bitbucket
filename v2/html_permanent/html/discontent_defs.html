discontent

(ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: discontentment lack of contentment, as with one's condition or lot in life2. a discontented personadjective3. dissatisfiedverb4. (transitive) to make dissatisfied
Derived forms
ˌdiscontented (ˌdisconˈtented) adjectiveˌdiscontentedly (ˌdisconˈtentedly) adverbˌdiscontentedness (ˌdisconˈtentedness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounDiscontent is the feeling that you have when you are not satisfied with your situation. ⇒ There are reports of widespread discontent in the capital.
Example sentences containing 'discontent'
Marriage, discontent with home, desire for pastures new, departure. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFThe fact that this meant only that no signal had been received reduced neither his concern nor his discontent. Ross, Melville LOHENGRINThere are straws in the wind, a growing discontent with the French.
