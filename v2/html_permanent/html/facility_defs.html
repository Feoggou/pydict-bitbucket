facility

(fəˈsɪlɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. ease of action or performance; freedom from difficulty2. ready skill or ease deriving from practice or familiarity3. (often plural) the means or equipment facilitating the performance of an action4. rare easy-going disposition5. military an organization or building offering supporting capability6. (usually plural) a euphemistic word for lavatory
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin facilitās, from facilis easy; see facile

facility

Powered by COBUILD
(fəsɪlɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: facilities
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]Facilities are buildings, pieces of equipment, or services that are provided for a particular purpose. ⇒ What recreational facilities are now available? ⇒ The problem lies in getting patients to a medical facility as soon as possible.2. countable noun [oft n N] [N to-inf]A facility is something such as an additional service provided by an organization or an extra feature on a machine which is useful but not essential. ⇒ It is very useful to have an overdraft facility. ⇒ One of the new models has the facility to reproduce speech as well as text.3. countable noun [usu sing, usu N for n, N to-inf]If you have a facility for something, for example learning a language, you find it easy to do. ⇒ He and Marcia shared a facility for languages. ⇒ Smell is a very basic sense but humans have lost much of the facility to use it properly.
