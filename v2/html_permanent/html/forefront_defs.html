forefront

(ˈfɔːˌfrʌnt )
Definitions
noun1. the extreme front2. the position of most prominence, responsibility, or action

forefront

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳfrʌnt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. singular noun [usu at/in/to N]If you are at the forefront of a campaign or other activity, you have a leading and influential position in it. [+ of/in] ⇒ They have been at the forefront of the campaign for political change.2. singular noun [usu at/in/to N of n]If something is at the forefront of people's minds or attention, they think about it a lot because it is particularly important to them. ⇒ The pension issue was not at the forefront of his mind in the spring of 1985.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
