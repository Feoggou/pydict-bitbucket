tingle

(ˈtɪŋɡəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (usually intr) to feel or cause to feel a prickling, itching, or stinging sensation of the flesh, as from a cold plunge or electric shocknoun2. a sensation of tingling

Derived Forms

tingler (ˈtingler) nountingling (ˈtingling) adjectivetinglingly (ˈtinglingly) adverbtingly (ˈtingly) adjective

Word Origin

C14: perhaps a variant of tinkle

tingle

Powered by COBUILD
(tɪŋgəl )
Word forms: tingles , tingling , tingled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen a part of your body tingles, you have a slight stinging feeling there. [V] ⇒ The backs of his thighs tingled. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a taste which is first sweet, then bitter, leaving a tingling sensation on the tongue.tingling uncount noun [+ in] ⇒ Its effects on the nervous system include weakness, paralysis, and tingling in the hands and feet.2. verbIf you tingle with a feeling such as excitement, you feel it very strongly. [V + with] ⇒ She tingled with excitement. [V] ⇒ When I look over and see Terry I tingle all over.Tingle is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ I felt a sudden tingle of excitement.
