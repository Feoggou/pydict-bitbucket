unsuited

(ʌnˈsuːtɪd , ʌnˈsjuː-)
Definitions
adjective1. not appropriate for a particular purpose ⇒ temperamentally unsuited to his role2. (of two people) not likely to have a successful relationship
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unsuited for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnsuːtɪd )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is unsuited to a particular job, situation, or place, they do not have the right qualities or characteristics for it. [+ to] ⇒ He's totally unsuited to the job. [+ to] ⇒ The snow cruiser proved hopelessly unsuited to Antarctic conditions.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf two people, especially a man and a woman, are unsuited to each other, they have different personalities or interests, and so are unlikely to have a successful relationship. [+ to] ⇒ By the end of that first year, I knew how totally unsuited we were to each other.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
