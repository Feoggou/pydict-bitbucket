unbind

(ʌnˈbaɪnd )
Definitions
verb -binds, -binding or -bound (transitive)1. to set free from restraining bonds or chains; release2. to unfasten or make loose (a bond, tie, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unbind for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnbaɪnd )
Word forms: unbinds , unbinding , unbound
Definitions
verbIf you unbind something or someone, you take off a piece of cloth, string, or rope that has been tied round them. [V n] ⇒ She unbound her hair and let it flow loose in the wind. [V-ed] ⇒ Many cultures still have fairly strict rules about women displaying unbound hair.
