make for

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, preposition)1. to head towards, esp in haste2. to prepare to attack3. to help to bring about ⇒ your cooperation will make for the success of our project

make for

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you make for a place, you move towards it. head for [V P n] ⇒ He rose from his seat and made for the door.2. phrasal verbIf something makes for another thing, it causes or helps to cause that thing to happen or exist. [informal] [V P n] ⇒ A happy parent makes for a happy child. →  make
