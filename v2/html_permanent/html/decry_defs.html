decry

(dɪˈkraɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -cries, -crying or -cried (transitive)1. to express open disapproval of; disparage2. to depreciate by proclamation ⇒ to decry obsolete coinage
Derived forms
decrial (deˈcrial) noundecrier (deˈcrier) noun
Word origin
C17: from Old French descrier, from des- dis-1 + crier to cry

verbIf someone decries an idea or action, they criticize it strongly. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He is impatient with those who decry the scheme. [V n + as] ⇒ People decried the campaign as a waste of money.
