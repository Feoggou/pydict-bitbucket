gurnard

(ˈɡɜːnəd ) or gurnet (ˈɡɜːnɪt )
Definitions
noun plural -nard, -nards, -net or -netsany European marine scorpaenoid fish of the family Triglidae, such as Trigla lucerna (tub or yellow gurnard), having a heavily armoured head and finger-like pectoral fins
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French gornard grunter, from grognier to grunt, from Latin grunnīre
