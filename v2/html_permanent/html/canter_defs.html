canter

(ˈkæntə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an easy three-beat gait of horses, etc, between a trot and a gallop in speed2. See at a canterverb3. to move or cause to move at a canter
Word origin
C18: short for Canterbury trot, the supposed pace at which pilgrims rode to Canterbury

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen a horse canters, it moves at a speed that is slower than a gallop but faster than a trot. [V + into, V prep/adv] ⇒ The competitors cantered into the arena to conclude the closing ceremony. [Also V]Canter is also a noun. ⇒ Carnac set off at a canter.
