rearguard

(ˈrɪəˌɡɑːd )
Definitions
noun1. a detachment detailed to protect the rear of a military formation, esp in retreat2. an entrenched or conservative element, as in a political party3. See rearguard action
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Old French rereguarde (modern French arrière-garde), from rer, from Latin retro back + guarde guard; compare vanguard
