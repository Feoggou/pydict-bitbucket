typify

(ˈtɪpɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (transitive)1. to be typical of; characterize2. to symbolize or represent completely, by or as if by a type

Derived Forms

ˌtypification (ˌtypifiˈcation) nountypiˌfier (ˈtypiˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin typus type + -ify

(tɪpɪfaɪ )
Word forms: typifies , typifying , typified
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something or someone typifies a situation or type of thing or person, they have all the usual characteristics of it and are a typical example of it. [V n] ⇒ These two buildings typify the rich extremes of Irish architecture. [V n] ⇒ ...a reaction against the achievements of science, as typified by the development of nuclear weapons. [V n] ⇒ ...a more intelligent and articulate breed of disc jockeys, typified by university graduate Simon Mayo.
