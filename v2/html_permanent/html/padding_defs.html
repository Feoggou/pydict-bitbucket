padding

(ˈpædɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any soft material used to pad clothes, furniture, etc2. superfluous material put into a speech or written work to pad it out; waffle3. inflated or false entries in a financial account, esp an expense account

padding

(pædɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounPadding is soft material which is put on something or inside it in order to make it less hard, to protect it, or to give it a different shape. ⇒ ...the foam rubber padding on the headphones. ⇒ Players must wear padding to protect them from injury. ⇒ ...the fat on our bodies, which acts as padding.2. uncount nounPadding is unnecessary words or information used to make a piece of writing or a speech longer. ⇒ Of the sonnet eleven of the lines are mere padding and say nothing. ⇒ ...the kind of subject that politicians put in their speeches for a bit of padding.
