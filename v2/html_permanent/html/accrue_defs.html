accrue

or accrew (əˈkruː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -crues, -cruing or -crued (intransitive)1. to increase by growth or addition, esp (of capital) to increase by periodic addition of interest2. (often foll by to) to fall naturally (to); come into the possession (of); result (for)3. law (of a right or demand) to become capable of being enforced
Word origin
C15: from Old French accreue growth, ultimately from Latin accrēscere to increase, from ad- to, in addition + crēscere to grow

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbIf money or interest accrues or if you accrue it, it gradually increases in amount over a period of time. [business] [V-ed] ⇒ I owed £5,000–part of this was accrued interest. [V] ⇒ If you do not pay within 28 days, interest will accrue. [V n] ⇒ Officials say the options will offer investors a longer time in which to accrue profits.2. ergative verb & verbIf things such as profits or benefits accrue to someone, they are added to over a period of time. [formal] [V] ⇒ ...the expectation that profits will accrue. [V + to] ⇒ ...a project from which considerable benefit will accrue to the community. [V-ed] ⇒ In many cases, the fee structure alone will exceed the tax benefits accrued. [Also V n, V to n]
