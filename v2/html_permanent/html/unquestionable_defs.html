unquestionable

(ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. indubitable or indisputable2. not admitting of exception or qualification ⇒ an unquestionable decision
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unquestionability (unˌquestionaˈbility) or unquestionableness (unˈquestionableness) noun

unquestionable for learners of English

(ʌnkwestʃənəbəl )
Definitions
adjectiveIf you describe something as unquestionable, you are emphasizing that it is so obviously true or real that nobody can doubt it. [emphasis] ⇒ He inspires affection and respect as a man of unquestionable integrity. ⇒ There is an unquestionable link between job losses and deteriorating services.unquestionably (ʌnkwestʃənəbli ) graded adverb & adverb ⇒ They have seen the change as unquestionably beneficial to the country. ⇒ He is unquestionably a star.
