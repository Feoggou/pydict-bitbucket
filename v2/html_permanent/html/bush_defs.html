bush1

(bʊʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a dense woody plant, smaller than a tree, with many branches arising from the lower part of the stem; shrub2. a dense cluster of such shrubs; thicket3. something resembling a bush, esp in density ⇒ a bush of hair4. See the bush5. Canadian an area of land on a farm on which timber is grown and cut. Also called: bush lot, woodlot6. a forested area; woodland7. See the bush8. a fox's tail; brush9. obsoletea. a bunch of ivy hung as a vintner's sign in front of a tavernb. any tavern sign10. See beat about the bushadjective11. Australian and New Zealand informal rough-and-ready12. West Africa informal ignorant or stupid, esp as considered typical of unwesternized rustic life13. US and Canadian informal unprofessional, unpolished, or second-rate14. See go bushverb15. (intransitive) to grow thick and bushy16. (transitive) to cover, decorate, support, etc, with bushes17. See bush it
Word origin
C13: of Germanic origin; compare Old Norse buski, Old High German busc, Middle Dutch bosch; related to Old French bosc wood, Italian bosco
