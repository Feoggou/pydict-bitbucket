sarcasm

(ˈsɑːkæzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. mocking, contemptuous, or ironic language intended to convey scorn or insult2. the use or tone of such language

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin sarcasmus, from Greek sarkasmos, from sarkazein to rend the flesh, from sarx flesh

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounSarcasm is speech or writing which actually means the opposite of what it seems to say. Sarcasm is usually intended to mock or insult someone. ⇒ 'What a pity,' Graham said with a hint of sarcasm. ⇒ His voice was heavy with sarcasm. ⇒ Fred ignored the sarcasm.
