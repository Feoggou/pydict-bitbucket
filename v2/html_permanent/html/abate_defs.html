abate

(əˈbeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make or become less in amount, intensity, degree, etc ⇒ the storm has abated2. (transitive) lawa. to remove, suppress, or terminate (a nuisance)b. to suspend or extinguish (a claim or action)c. to annul (a writ)3. (intransitive) law (of a writ, legal action, etc) to become null and void4. (transitive) to subtract or deduct, as part of a price
Word origin
C14: from Old French abatre to beat down, fell

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something bad or undesirable abates, it becomes much less strong or severe. [formal] die down [V] ⇒ The storms had abated by the time they rounded Cape Horn. [V] ⇒ ...a crime wave that shows no sign of abating.
