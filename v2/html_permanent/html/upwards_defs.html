upwards

(ˈʌpwədz ) or upward
Definitions
adverb1. from a lower to a higher place, level, condition, etc2. towards a higher level, standing, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

upwards for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌpwəʳdz ) also upward
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v, n ADV]If someone moves or looks upwards, they move or look up towards a higher place. ⇒ 'There,' said Jack, pointing upwards. ⇒ They climbed upward along the steep cliffs surrounding the village. ⇒ Hunter nodded again and gazed upwards in fear. ⇒ Lie face upwards with a cushion under your head.2. adverb [ADV after v]If an amount or rate moves upwards, it increases. ⇒ ...with prices soon heading upwards in high street stores. ⇒ Unemployment will continue upward for much of this year. ⇒ The share price is likely to leap upwards.3. →  upwards of sth
