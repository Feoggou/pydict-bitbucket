Definitions

readily

Powered by COBUILD
(redɪli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v]If you do something readily, you do it in a way which shows that you are very willing to do it. willingly ⇒ I asked her if she would allow me to interview her, and she readily agreed. ⇒ When I was invited to the party, I readily accepted.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v]You also use readily to say that something can be done or obtained quickly and easily. For example, if you say that something can be readily understood, you mean that people can understand it quickly and easily. ⇒ The components are readily available in hardware shops. ⇒ I don't readily make friends.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
