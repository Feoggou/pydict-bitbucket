eulogy

(ˈjuːlədʒɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -gies1. a formal speech or piece of writing praising a person or thing, esp a person who has recently died2. high praise or commendationAvoid confusion with elegy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin eulogia, from Greek: praise, from eu- + -logy; influenced by Latin ēlogium short saying, inscription
