eager1

(ˈiːɡə )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; often foll by to or for) impatiently desirous (of); anxious or avid (for) ⇒ he was eager to see her departure2. characterized by or feeling expectancy or great desire ⇒ an eager look3. archaic tart or biting; sharp
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

eagerly (ˈeagerly) adverbeagerness (ˈeagerness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French egre, from Latin acer sharp, keen

eager

Powered by COBUILD
(iːgəʳ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf]If you are eager to do or have something, you want to do or have it very much. ⇒ Robert was eager to talk about life in the Army. [+ for] ⇒ When my own son was five years old, I became eager for another baby. ⇒ The low prices still pull in crowds of eager buyers.eagerness uncount noun ⇒ ...an eagerness to learn.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you look or sound eager, you look or sound as if you expect something interesting or enjoyable to happen. ⇒ Arty sneered at the crowd of eager faces around him. ⇒ Her voice was girlish and eager.eagerly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ 'So what do you think will happen?' he asked eagerly.eagerness uncount noun ⇒ ...a woman speaking with breathless eagerness.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
