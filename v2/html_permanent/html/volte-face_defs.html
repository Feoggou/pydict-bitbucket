volte-face

(ˈvɒltˈfɑːs )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural volte-face1. a reversal, as in opinion or policy2. a change of position so as to look, lie, etc, in the opposite direction
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from French, from Italian volta-faccia, from volta a turn + faccia face

volte-face

Powered by COBUILD
(vɒlt fɑːs ) for learners of English
Word forms: volte-faces
Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]If you say that someone's behaviour is a volte-face, you mean that they have changed their opinion or decision completely, so that it is the opposite of what it was before. [formal] ⇒ The day's events were a remarkable volte-face.
