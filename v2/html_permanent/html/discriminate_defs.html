discriminate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪt )1. (intr; usually foll by in favour of or against) to single out a particular person, group, etc, for special favour or, esp, disfavour, often because of a characteristic such as race, colour, sex, intelligence, etc2. (when intr, foll by between or among) to recognize or understand the difference (between); distinguish ⇒ to discriminate right and wrong, ⇒ to discriminate between right and wrong3. (intransitive) to constitute or mark a difference4. (intransitive) to be discerning in matters of tasteadjective (dɪˈskrɪmɪnɪt )5. showing or marked by discrimination
Derived forms
discriminately (disˈcriminately) adverbdiscrimiˌnator (disˈcrimiˌnator) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin discrīmināre to divide, from discrīmen a separation, from discernere to discern

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you can discriminate between two things, you can recognize that they are different. [V + between] ⇒ He is incapable of discriminating between a good idea and a terrible one. [V between pl-n] ⇒ The device can discriminate between the cancerous and the normal cells.2. verbTo discriminate against a group of people or in favour of a group of people means to unfairly treat them worse or better than other groups. [V + against] ⇒ They believe the law discriminates against women. [V + in favour of] ⇒ ...legislation which would discriminate in favour of racial minorities. [V] ⇒ The Commission for Racial Equality teaches organisations not to discriminate.
