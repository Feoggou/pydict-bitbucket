come from

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, preposition)1. to be or have been a resident or native (of) ⇒ Ernst comes from Geneva2. to originate from or derive from ⇒ chocolate comes from the cacao tree, ⇒ the word filibuster comes from the Dutch word for pirate3. See where someone is coming from
Example sentences containing 'come from'
Then he turned and cantered back in the direction they had come from. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPNo. Odds were the complaint had come from the club itself, probably without Cordwell's knowledge. Mark Mills AMAGANSETT
