(ɪnkəmpætɪbɪlɪti) in the Pharmaceutical Industry
Definitions
noun(Pharmaceutical: Physiology) Incompatibility occurs where substances cannot act together or are antagonistic in their action. ⇒ An incompatibility occurs if one of the drugs used in a treatment becomes less effective when another drug is administered. ⇒ If drugs that have antagonistic actions are combined, there is a therapeutic incompatibility. ⇒ Incompatibility occurs where substances cannot act together or are antagonistic in their action.
