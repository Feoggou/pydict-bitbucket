verb

(vɜːb )
Definitions
noun1. (in traditional grammar) any of a large class of words in a language that serve to indicate the occurrence or performance of an action, the existence of a state or condition, etc. In English, such words as run, make, do, and the like are verbs2. (in modern descriptive linguistic analysis)a. a word or group of words that functions as the predicate of a sentence or introduces the predicateb. (as modifier) ⇒ a verb phrase
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

verbless (ˈverbless) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin verbum a word
