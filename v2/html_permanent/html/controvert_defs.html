controvert

(ˈkɒntrəˌvɜːt , ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to deny, refute, or oppose (some argument or opinion)2. to argue or wrangle about
Derived forms
controˌverter (ˈcontroˌverter) nounˌcontrovertible (ˌcontroˈvertible) adjectiveˌcontrovertibly (ˌcontroˈvertibly) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin contrōversus; see controversy
Example sentences containing 'controvert'
I forbid you to try to gain custody and I would controvert personally any such application. Low, Ona MURKY SHALLOWS
