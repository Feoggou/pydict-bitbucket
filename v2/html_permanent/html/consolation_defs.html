consolation

(ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of consoling or state of being consoled; solace2. a person or thing that is a source of comfort in a time of suffering, grief, disappointment, etc
Derived forms
consolatory (kənˈsɒlətərɪ , -trɪ) adjective
Example sentences containing 'consolation'
It was a bit of consolation for the fact that they'd taken away his glasses. Meek, M R D A WORM OF DOUBTThe illusion, he realized, was essential for solace and in the telling, he tasted some of its consolation himself. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEIt had been some consolation to see how little love was lost between them. Murray, Stephen DEATH AND TRANSFIGURATIONIt pleased him-a consolation prize given the limitations of their present engagement. Stephanie Laurens THE PERFECT LOVER
