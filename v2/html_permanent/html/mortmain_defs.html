mortmain

(ˈmɔːtˌmeɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounlaw the state or condition of lands, buildings, etc, held inalienably, as by an ecclesiastical or other corporation

Word Origin

C15: from Old French mortemain, from Medieval Latin mortua manus dead hand, inalienable ownership
