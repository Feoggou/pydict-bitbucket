alleviate

(əˈliːvɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to make (pain, sorrow, etc) easier to bear; lessen; relieveSee note at ameliorate
Derived forms
alˌleviˈation nounalˈleviative adjectivealˈleviˌator noun
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin alleviāre to mitigate, from Latin levis light

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you alleviate pain, suffering, or an unpleasant condition, you make it less intense or severe. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Nowadays, a great deal can be done to alleviate back pain. [V n] ⇒ ...the problem of alleviating mass poverty.alleviation (əliːvieɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Their energies were focussed on the alleviation of the refugees' misery.
