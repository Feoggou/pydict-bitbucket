tripod

(ˈtraɪpɒd )
Definitions
noun1. an adjustable and usually collapsible three-legged stand to which a camera, etc, can be attached to hold it steady2. a stand or table having three legs
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tripodal (ˈtrɪpədəl ) adjective

Word Origin

C17: via Latin from Greek tripod-, tripous three-footed, from tri- + pous foot
