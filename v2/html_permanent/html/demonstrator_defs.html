demonstrator

(ˈdɛmənˌstreɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who demonstrates equipment, machines, products, etc2. a person who takes part in a public demonstration3. a piece of merchandise, such as a car that one test-drives, used to display merits or performance to prospective buyers

demonstrator

Powered by COBUILD
(demənstreɪtəʳ )
Word forms: demonstrators
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Demonstrators are people who are marching or gathering somewhere to show their opposition to something or their support for something. ⇒ I saw the police using tear gas to try and break up a crowd of demonstrators.2. countable nounA demonstrator is a person who shows people how something works or how to do something.3. countable noun [oft N n]A demonstrator is an item for sale that customers can use and test so they can decide whether they want to buy it. ⇒ Saab dealers are currently giving away unaccompanied 24-hour test drives in posh demonstrator models.
Example sentences containing 'demonstrator'
Like a demonstrator in a department store, she cut a large square of cloth as she spoke. McKewan, Ian THE INNOCENT
