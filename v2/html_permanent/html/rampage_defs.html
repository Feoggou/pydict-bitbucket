rampage

Definitions
verb (ræmˈpeɪdʒ )1. (intransitive) to rush about in an angry, violent, or agitated fashionnoun (ˈræmpeɪdʒ , ræmˈpeɪdʒ )2. angry or destructive behaviour3. See on the rampage
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rampageous (ramˈpageous) adjectiverampageously (ramˈpageously) adverbrampageousness (ramˈpageousness) nounrampager (ˈrampager) noun

Word Origin

C18: from Scottish, of uncertain origin; perhaps based on ramp

Definitions
pronunciation note:   Pronounced (ræmpeɪdʒ ) for meaning [sense 1], and (ræmpeɪdʒ ) for meaning [sense 2].1. verbWhen people or animals rampage through a place, they rush about there in a wild or violent way, causing damage or destruction. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Hundreds of youths rampaged through the town, shop windows were smashed and cars overturned. [V-ing] ⇒ He used a sword to try to defend his shop from a rampaging mob.2. →  go on the rampage
