blindfolded

(ˈblaɪndfəʊldɪd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. wearing a blindfold ⇒ the blindfolded figure of justice, ⇒ They put you into a car blindfolded and drive you around and around., ⇒ You have to be able to dismantle the gun and put it together again while blindfolded.2. See able to do something blindfolded
Example sentences containing 'blindfolded'
R'shiel turned her horse and led the way, although Brak could have found his way blindfolded. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPNow we sit trussed and blindfolded in our offices, not daring to do a damned thing. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSYou'd think the whole thing was another hoax or it was a gangland office in a derelict warehouse and we'd come here blindfolded. Dexter Petley WHITE LIES
