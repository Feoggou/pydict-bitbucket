flounce1

(flaʊns )
Definitions
verb1. (intr; often foll by about, away, out, etc) to move or go with emphatic or impatient movementsnoun2. the act of flouncing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian flunsa to hurry, Swedish flunsa to splash

flounce2

(flaʊns )
Definitions
nounan ornamental gathered ruffle sewn to a garment by its top edge
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Old French fronce wrinkle, from froncir to wrinkle, of Germanic origin

flounce

Powered by COBUILD
(flaʊns ) for learners of English
Word forms: flounces , flouncing , flounced
Definitions
1. verbIf you flounce somewhere, you walk there quickly with exaggerated movements, in a way that shows you are annoyed or upset. [V adv/prep] ⇒ She flounced out of my room in a huff. [V] ⇒ She will flounce and argue when asked to leave the room.2. countable nounA flounce is a piece of cloth that has been sewn into folds and put around the edge of something, for example a skirt, dress, tablecloth, or curtain. ⇒ ...a gown with a flounce round the hem.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
