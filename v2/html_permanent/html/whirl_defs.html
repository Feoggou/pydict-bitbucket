whirl

(wɜːl )
Definitions
verb1. to spin, turn, or revolve or cause to spin, turn, or revolve2. (intransitive) to turn around or away rapidly3. (intransitive) to have a spinning sensation, as from dizziness, etc4. to move or drive or be moved or driven at high speednoun5. the act or an instance of whirling; swift rotation or a rapid whirling movement6. a condition of confusion or giddiness ⇒ her accident left me in a whirl7. a swift round, as of events, meetings, etc8. a tumult; stir9. informal a brief trip, dance, etc10. See give something a whirl
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

whirler (ˈwhirler) nounwhirling (ˈwhirling) adjectivewhirlingly (ˈwhirlingly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse hvirfla to turn about; related to Old High German wirbil whirlwind

whirl for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɜːʳl )
Word forms: whirls , whirling , whirled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf something or someone whirls around or if you whirl them around, they move around or turn around very quickly. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Not receiving an answer, she whirled round. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ He was whirling Anne around the floor. [V] ⇒ The smoke began to whirl and grew into a monstrous column. [Also V n]Whirl is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ ...the barely audible whirl of wheels.2. countable noun [usu sing]You can refer to a lot of intense activity as a whirl of activity. [+ of] ⇒ In half an hour's whirl of activity she does it all. ⇒ Your life is such a social whirl.3. →  in a whirl4. →  give it a whirl
