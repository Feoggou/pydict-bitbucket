vexed

(vɛkst )
Definitions
adjective1. annoyed, confused, or agitated2. much debated and discussed (esp in the phrase a vexed question)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vexedly (ˈvɛksɪdlɪ ) adverbvexedness (ˈvexedness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(vekst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A vexed problem or question is very difficult and causes people a lot of trouble. ⇒ Ministers have begun work on the vexed issue of economic union. ⇒ Later Mr Moi raised the vexed question of refugees.2. → See also vex

He'd always vexed himself with such imponderables; like trying to imagine infinity, or what it would feel like to touch the sun. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOWIt vexed him that casual bumping - the slightest of accidental contact - required immediate apology. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODFor a moment he worries this is too sharp, that she will be vexed , but she just laughs, grabs for his face, leans and kisses him. Sean Thomas THE CHEEK PERFORATION DANCE
