heretic

(ˈhɛrətɪk )
Definitions
noun1. mainly Roman Catholic Church a person who maintains beliefs contrary to the established teachings of the Church2. a person who holds unorthodox opinions in any field
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

heretical (hɪˈrɛtɪkəl ) adjectiveheretically (heˈretically) adverb

heretic

Powered by COBUILD
(herɪtɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: heretics
Definitions
1. countable nounA heretic is someone whose beliefs or actions are considered wrong by most people, because they disagree with beliefs that are generally accepted. ⇒ He was considered a heretic and was ridiculed and ostracized for his ideas.2. countable nounA heretic is a person who belongs to a particular religion, but whose beliefs or actions seriously disagree with the principles of that religion. ⇒ Thousands of heretics were burned at the stake.
