charlatan

(ˈʃɑːlətən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounsomeone who professes knowledge or expertise, esp in medicine, that he or she does not have; quack
Derived forms
charlatanˌism (ˈcharlatanˌism) or charlatanry (ˈcharlatanry) nounˌcharlatanistic (ˌcharlatanˈistic) adjective
Word origin
C17: from French, from Italian ciarlatano, from ciarlare to chatter

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounYou describe someone as a charlatan when they pretend to have skills or knowledge that they do not really possess. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ He was exposed as a charlatan.
