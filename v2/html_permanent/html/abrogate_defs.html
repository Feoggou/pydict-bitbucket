abrogate

(ˈæbrəʊˌɡeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to cancel or revoke formally or officially; repeal; annul
Derived forms
ˌabrogation (ˌabroˈgation) nounabroˌgator (ˈabroˌgator) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin abrogātus repealed, from ab-1 + rogāre to propose (a law)
