anorexia

(ˌænɒˈrɛksɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. loss of appetite2. Also called: anorexia nervosa (nɜːˈvəʊsə ) a disorder characterized by fear of becoming fat and refusal of food, leading to debility and even death
Derived forms
ˌanorectic (ˌanoˈrectic) or ˌanorexic (ˌanoˈrexic) adjective, noun
Word origin
C17: via New Latin from Greek, from an- + orexis appetite

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounAnorexia or anorexia nervosa is an illness in which a person has an overwhelming fear of becoming fat, and so refuses to eat enough and becomes thinner and thinner.
