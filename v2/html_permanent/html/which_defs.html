which

(wɪtʃ )
Definitions
determiner1. a. used with a noun in requesting that its referent be further specified, identified, or distinguished from the other members of a class ⇒ which house did you want to buy?b. (as pronoun) ⇒ which did you find?c. (used in indirect questions) ⇒ I wondered which apples were cheaper2. a. whatever of a class; whichever ⇒ bring which car you wantb. (as pronoun) ⇒ choose which of the cars suit you3. used in relative clauses with inanimate antecedents ⇒ the house, which is old, is in poor repair4. as; and that: used in relative clauses with verb phrases or sentences as their antecedents ⇒ he died of cancer, which is what I predicted5. See the whichSee note at that
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hwelc, hwilc; related to Old High German hwelīh (German welch), Old Norse hvelīkr, Gothic hvileiks, Latin quis, quid

which for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪtʃ )
Definitions
pronunciation note:   Usually pronounced (hwɪtʃ) for meanings [sense 2], [sense 3] and [sense 4].1. adverbYou use which in questions when there are two or more possible answers or alternatives. ⇒ Which do they want me to do, declare war or surrender? ⇒ Which are the ones you really like? ⇒ 'You go down that passageway over there.'—'Which one?'. ⇒ Which vitamin supplements are good value?2. determinerYou use which to refer to a choice between two or more possible answers or alternatives. ⇒ I wanted to know which school it was you went to. ⇒ I can't remember which teachers I had. ⇒ Scientists have long wondered which parts of the brain are involved in musical tasks.Which is also a conjunction. ⇒ In her panic she couldn't remember which was Mr Grainger's cabin. ⇒ There are so many diets on the market, how do you know which to choose?3. relative pronoun & pronounYou use which at the beginning of a relative clause when specifying the thing that you are talking about or when giving more information about it. ⇒ Soldiers opened fire on a car which failed to stop at an army checkpoint. ⇒ He's based in Banja Luka, which is the largest city in northern Bosnia. ⇒ Colic describes a whole variety of conditions in which a horse suffers abdominal pain. ⇒ I'm no longer allowed to smoke in any room which he currently occupies.4. relative pronoun & pronounYou use which to refer back to an idea or situation expressed in a previous sentence or sentences, especially when you want to give your opinion about it. ⇒ They ran out of drink. Which actually didn't bother me because I wasn't drinking. ⇒ Since we started in September we have raised fifty thousand pounds, which is pretty good going. ⇒ Visited Park West. Viewed a flat, no. 76. Which I like.Which is also a determiner. ⇒ The chances are you haven't fully decided what you want from your career at the moment, in which case you're definitely not cut out to be a boss yet!5. →  which sth is which6. → any which way7. → every which way
