pest

(pɛst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person or thing that annoys, esp by imposing itself when it is not wanted; nuisance2. a. any organism that damages crops, injures or irritates livestock or man, or reduces the fertility of landb. (as modifier) ⇒ pest control3. rare an epidemic disease or pestilence

Word Origin

C16: from Latin pestis plague, of obscure origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounPests are insects or small animals which damage crops or food supplies. ⇒ ...crops which are resistant to some of the major insect pests and diseases. ⇒ Each year ten percent of the crop is lost to a pest called corn rootworm. ⇒ ...new and innovative methods of pest control.2. countable nounYou can describe someone, especially a child, as a pest if they keep bothering you. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ He climbed on the table, pulled my hair, and was generally a pest.
