pigeon1

(ˈpɪdʒɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of numerous birds of the family Columbidae, having a heavy body, small head, short legs, and long pointed wings: order Columbiformes. See rock dove2. slang a victim or dupe

Word Origin

C14: from Old French pijon young dove, from Late Latin pīpiō young bird, from pīpīre to chirp

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA pigeon is a bird, usually grey in colour, which has a fat body. Pigeons often live in towns.2. → see also clay pigeon, homing pigeon3. → to put the cat among the pigeons
