lamed

(ˈlɑːmɪd , Hebrew ˈlamɛd)
Definitions
nounthe 12th letter in the Hebrew alphabet (ל), transliterated as l. Also: lamedh (ˈlamɛd)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

from Hebrew, literally: ox goad (from its shape)
Example sentences containing 'lamed'
Perhaps something in her mind had been lamed at that time, leaving it with an inability to take calamity seriously. Ferrars, Elizabeth MURDER MOVES INEither of them, if faced with a lamed horse, could easily have trekked home through the snow. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThe distances at Yaramera, even inside the house, were daunting to a lamed man. Ursula K. Le Guin THE BIRTHDAY OF THE WORLD
