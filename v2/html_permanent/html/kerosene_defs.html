kerosene

or kerosine (ˈkɛrəˌsiːn )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: paraffin a liquid mixture consisting mainly of alkane hydrocarbons with boiling points in the range 150°–300°C, used as an aircraft fuel, in domestic heaters, and as a solvent2. the general name for paraffin as a fuel for jet aircraft▶ USAGE The spelling kerosine is now the preferred form in technical and industrial usage
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Greek kēros wax + -ene

Definitions
noun(Extractive engineering: Refinery processes, Product) Kerosene is an oil distillate, usually used as a heating oil, as fuel in jet engines. ⇒ Kerosene is a refined middle-distillate petroleum product that is commonly used as a jet fuel, and in cooking and heating. ⇒ Kerosene is a petroleum fraction containing hydrocarbons that are slightly heavier than those found in gasoline and naphtha.
