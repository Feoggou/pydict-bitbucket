dissipate

(ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to exhaust or be exhausted by dispersion2. (transitive) to scatter or break up3. (intransitive) to indulge in the pursuit of pleasure
Derived forms
dissiˌpater (ˈdissiˌpater) or dissiˌpator (ˈdissiˌpator) noundissiˌpative (ˈdissiˌpative) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Latin dissipāre to disperse, from dis-1 + supāre to throw

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbWhen something dissipates or when you dissipate it, it becomes less or becomes less strong until it disappears or goes away completely. [formal] [V] ⇒ The tension in the room had dissipated. [V n] ⇒ He wound down the windows to dissipate the heat.dissipation uncount noun ⇒ ...heat dissipation.2. verbWhen someone dissipates money, time, or effort, they waste it in a foolish way. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He is dissipating his time and energy on too many different things. [V n] ⇒ Her father had dissipated her inheritance.dissipation uncount noun ⇒ ...the dissipation of my wealth.
