(əˈnjuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. over again; once more2. in a different way; afresh
Word origin
Old English of nīwe; see of, new

anew

(ənjuː , US ənuː )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV after v]If you do something anew, you do it again, often in a different way from before. [written] ⇒ She's ready to start anew. ⇒ He began his work anew.
Example sentences containing 'anew'
He had watched it built anew , divided into four pieces, then divided by a wall. Sara MacDonald SEA MUSICThe great circle of life and consciousness, continually shedding death like an old skin and being born anew. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODThis woman and her daughter should now get together and plan their lives anew. Emecheta, Buchi GWENDOLENPrestimion felt his judgment of her son confirmed anew in her.
