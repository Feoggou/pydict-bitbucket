Vulgate

(ˈvʌlɡeɪt , -ɡɪt)
Definitions
nouna. (from the 13th century onwards) the fourth-century version of the Bible produced by Jerome, partly by translating the original languages, and partly by revising the earlier Latin text based on the Greek versionsb. (as modifier) ⇒ the Vulgate version
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin Vulgāta, from Late Latin vulgāta editiō popular version (of the Bible), from Latin vulgāre to make common, from vulgus the common people
