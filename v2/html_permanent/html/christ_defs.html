Christ

(kraɪst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Jesus of Nazareth (Jesus Christ), regarded by Christians as fulfilling Old Testament prophecies of the Messiah2. the Messiah or anointed one of God as the subject of Old Testament prophecies3. an image or picture of Christexclamation4. taboo, slang an oath expressing annoyance, surprise, etc▷ See also Jesus
Derived forms
Christly (ˈChristly) adjective
Word origin
Old English Crīst, from Latin Chrīstus, from Greek khristos anointed one (from khriein to anoint), translating Hebrew māshīah Messiah

Christ

Powered by COBUILD
(kraɪst )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. proper nounChrist is one of the names of Jesus, whom Christians believe to be the son of God and whose teachings are the basis of Christianity. ⇒ ...the teachings of Christ.2. exclamationSome people say 'Christ!' when they are surprised, shocked, or annoyed, or in order to emphasize what they are saying. This use could cause offence. [informal, feelings] ⇒ He looked at her watch. 'Christ! We only have three minutes!'
Example sentences containing 'Christ'
On the wall over the stained mattress was a large poster of Jesus Christ , a halo behind His head. Karin Slaughter BLINDSIGHTED
