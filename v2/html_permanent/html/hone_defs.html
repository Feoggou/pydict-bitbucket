hone1

(həʊn )
Definitions
noun1. a fine whetstone, esp for sharpening razors2. a tool consisting of a number of fine abrasive slips held in a machine head, rotated and reciprocated to impart a smooth finish to cylinder bores, etcverb3. (transitive) to sharpen or polish with or as if with a hone▶ USAGE Hone is sometimes wrongly used where home is meant: this device makes it easier to home in on (not hone in on) the target
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hān stone; related to Old Norse hein

hone2

verb (intransitive) dialect1. (often foll by for or after) to yearn or pine2. to moan or grieve
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Old French hogner to growl, probably of Germanic origin; compare Old High German hōnen to revile

hone

Powered by COBUILD
(hoʊn ) for learners of English
Word forms: hones , honing , honed
Definitions
1. verbIf you hone something, for example a skill, technique, idea, or product, you carefully develop it over a long period of time so that it is exactly right for your purpose. [V n] ⇒ Leading companies spend time and money on honing the skills of senior managers. [V n] ⇒ His body is honed and kept in trim with constant exercise.2. verbIf you hone a blade, weapon, or tool, you sharpen it on a stone or with a special device. [technical] [V n] ⇒ ...four grinding wheels for honing fine edged tools and implements. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a thin, honed blade.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
