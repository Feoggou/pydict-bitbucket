upstairs

(ˈʌpˈstɛəz )
Definitions
adverb1. up the stairs; to or on an upper floor or level2. informal to or into a higher rank or office3. informal in the mind ⇒ a little weak upstairs4. See kick upstairsnoun (functioning as singular or plural)5. a. an upper floor or levelb. (as modifier) ⇒ an upstairs room6. British informal, old-fashioned the masters and mistresses of a household collectively, esp of a large house. Compare downstairs (sense 3)

upstairs for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌpsteəʳz )
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v]If you go upstairs in a building, you go up a staircase towards a higher floor. ⇒ He went upstairs and changed into fresh clothes. ⇒ I walked upstairs and unlocked my front door.2. adverb [be ADV, n ADV]If something or someone is upstairs in a building, they are on a floor that is higher than the ground floor. ⇒ The restaurant is upstairs and consists of a large, open room. ⇒ The boys are curled asleep in the small bedroom upstairs.3. adjective [ADJ n]An upstairs room or object is situated on a floor of a building that is higher than the ground floor. ⇒ Marsani moved into the upstairs apartment. ⇒ ...an upstairs balcony.4. singular nounThe upstairs of a building is the floor or floors that are higher than the ground floor. ⇒ Together we went through the upstairs. [+ of] ⇒ Frances invited them to occupy the upstairs of her home.
