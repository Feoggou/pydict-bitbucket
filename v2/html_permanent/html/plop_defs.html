plop

(plɒp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the characteristic sound made by an object dropping into water without a splashverbWord forms: plops, plopping or plopped2. to fall or cause to fall with the sound of a plop ⇒ the stone plopped into the waterexclamation3. an exclamation imitative of this sound ⇒ to go plop

Word Origin

C19: imitative of the sound

plop

Powered by COBUILD
(plɒp )
Word forms: plops , plopping , plopped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA plop is a soft, gentle sound, like the sound made by something dropping into water without disturbing the surface much. ⇒ Another drop of water fell with a soft plop.2. verbIf something plops somewhere, it drops there with a soft, gentle sound. [V prep] ⇒ The ice cream plopped to the ground.3. verbIf you plop something somewhere, you drop it there gently and without making a loud noise. [informal] [V n prep/adv] ⇒ Just plop the noodles over the center of the sauce. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ She picked up the coffee pot, then plopped it down and went into the living room.► →  plop down
