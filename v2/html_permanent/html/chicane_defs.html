chicane

(ʃɪˈkeɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a bridge or whist hand without trumps2. motor racing a short section of sharp narrow bends formed by barriers placed on a motor-racing circuit to provide an additional test of driving skill3. a less common word for chicaneryverb4. (transitive) to deceive or trick by chicanery5. (transitive) to quibble about; cavil over6. (intransitive) to use tricks or chicanery
Derived forms
chicaner (chiˈcaner) noun
Word origin
C17: from French chicaner to quibble, of obscure origin
