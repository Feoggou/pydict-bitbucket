Waterhouse

(ˈwɔːtəˌhaʊs )
Definitions
noun1.  Alfred. 1830–1905, British architect; a leader of the Gothic Revival. His buildings include Manchester Town Hall (1868) and the Natural History Museum, London (1881)2.  George Marsden. 1824–1906, New Zealand statesman, born in England: prime minister of New Zealand (1872–73)3.  Keith (Spencer). 1929–2009, British novelist, dramatist, and journalist: best known for the novel Billy Liar (1959) and his collaborations with the dramatist Willis Hall (1929–2005)
