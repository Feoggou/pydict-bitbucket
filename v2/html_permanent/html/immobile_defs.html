immobile

(ɪˈməʊbaɪl )
Definitions
adjective1. not moving; motionless2. not able to move or be moved; fixed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

immobility (ˌɪməʊˈbɪlɪtɪ ) noun

immobile

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmoʊbaɪl , US -bəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Someone or something that is immobile is completely still. ⇒ Joe remained as immobile as if he had been carved out of rock.immobility (ɪmoʊbɪlɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Hyde maintained the rigid immobility of his shoulders.2. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Someone or something that is immobile is unable to move or unable to be moved. ⇒ A riding accident left him immobile. ⇒ ...a very heavy or immobile object.immobility uncount noun ⇒ Again, the pain locked me into immobility.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
