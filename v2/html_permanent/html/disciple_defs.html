disciple

(dɪˈsaɪpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a follower of the doctrines of a teacher or a school of thought2. one of the personal followers of Christ (including his 12 apostles) during his earthly life
Derived forms
discipleˌship (disˈcipleˌship) noundiscipular (dɪˈsɪpjʊlə ) adjective
Word origin
Old English discipul, from Latin discipulus pupil, from discere to learn
