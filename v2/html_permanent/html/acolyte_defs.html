acolyte

(ˈækəˌlaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a follower or attendant2. Christianity an officer who attends or assists a priest
Word origin
C16: via Old French and Medieval Latin from Greek akolouthos a follower

(ækəlaɪt )
Word forms: acolytes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn acolyte is a follower or assistant of an important person. [formal] ⇒ Richard Brome, an acolyte of Ben Jonson's, wrote 'The Jovial Crew' in 1641. ⇒ To his acolytes, he is known simply as 'the Boss'.2. countable nounAn acolyte is someone who assists a priest in performing certain religious services. ⇒ When the barge reached the shrine, acolytes removed the pall.
