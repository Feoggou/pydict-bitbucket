jockey

(ˈdʒɒkɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a person who rides horses in races, esp as a profession or for hireverb2. a. (transitive) to ride (a horse) in a raceb. (intransitive) to ride as a jockey3. (intransitive; often foll by for) to try to obtain an advantage by manoeuvring, esp literally in a race or metaphorically, as in a struggle for power (esp in the phrase jockey for position)4. to trick or cheat (a person)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16 (in the sense: lad): from name Jock + -ey

[V + for] ⇒ The rival political parties are already jockeying for power. [V to-inf] ⇒ Already, both sides are jockeying to belittle the other side.→  jockey for position
