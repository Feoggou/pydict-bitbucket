fixation

(fɪkˈseɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of fixing or the state of being fixed2. a preoccupation or obsession3. psychologya. the act of fixatingb. (in psychoanalytical schools) a strong attachment of a person to another person or an object in early life4. chemistrya. the conversion of nitrogen in the air into a compound, esp a fertilizerb. the conversion of a free element into one of its compounds5. the reduction of a substance from a volatile or fluid form to a nonvolatile or solid form

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]If you accuse a person of having a fixation on something or someone, you mean they think about a particular subject or person to an extreme and excessive degree. ⇒ The country's fixation on the war may delay a serious examination of domestic needs.
