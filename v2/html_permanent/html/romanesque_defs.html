Romanesque

(ˌrəʊməˈnɛsk )
Definitions
adjective1. denoting, relating to, or having the style of architecture used in W and S Europe from the 9th to the 12th century, characterized by the rounded arch, the groin vault, massive-masonry wall construction, and a restrained use of mouldings. See also Norman1 (sense 6)2. denoting or relating to a corresponding style in painting, sculpture, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: Roman + -esque
