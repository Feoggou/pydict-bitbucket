recalcitrant

(rɪˈkælsɪtrənt )
Definitions
adjective1. not susceptible to control or authority; refractorynoun2. a recalcitrant person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recalcitrance (reˈcalcitrance) noun

Word Origin

C19: via French from Latin recalcitrāre, from re- + calcitrāre to kick, from calx heel

recalcitrant

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪkælsɪtrənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone or something as recalcitrant, you mean that they are unwilling to obey orders or are difficult to deal with. [formal] ⇒ The danger is that recalcitrant local authorities will reject their responsibilities. ⇒ He had a knack for coaxing even the most recalcitrant engine to life.recalcitrance (rɪkælsɪtrəns ) uncount noun ⇒ ...the government's recalcitrance over introducing even the smallest political reform.
