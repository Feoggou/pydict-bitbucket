outmanoeuvre

or US outmaneuver (ˌaʊtməˈnuːvə )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to secure a strategic advantage over by skilful manoeuvre

outmanoeuvre for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtmənuːvəʳ )
Word forms: outmanoeuvres , outmanoeuvring , outmanoeuvred
Definitions
regional note:   in AM, use outmaneuververbIf you outmanoeuvre someone, you gain an advantage over them in a particular situation by behaving in a clever and skilful way. [V n] ⇒ He has shown once again that he's able to outmanoeuvre the military. [V n] ⇒ Great Britain's hockey players were outmanoeuvred by Germany.
