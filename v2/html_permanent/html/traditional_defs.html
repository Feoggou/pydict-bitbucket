traditional

(trəˈdɪʃənəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of, relating to, or being a tradition2. of or relating to the style of jazz originating in New Orleans, characterized by collective improvisation by a front line of trumpet, trombone, and clarinet accompanied by various rhythm instruments
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Traditional customs, beliefs, or methods are ones that have existed for a long time without changing. ⇒ ...traditional teaching methods. ⇒ ...traditional Indian music.traditionally graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Married women have traditionally been treated as dependent on their husbands. ⇒ Hell is traditionally associated with suffering. ⇒ Traditionally, election campaigns start on Labor Day.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A traditional organization or person prefers older methods and ideas to modern ones. ⇒ We're still a traditional school in a lot of ways. ⇒ ...traditional parents, who believed in laying down the law for their children.traditionally graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed, ADV adj] ⇒ He is loathed by some of the more traditionally minded officers.
