ductile

(ˈdʌktaɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a metal, such as gold or copper) able to be drawn out into wire2. able to be moulded; pliant; plastic3. easily led or influenced; tractable
Derived forms
ductilely (ˈductilely) adverbductility (dʌkˈtɪlɪtɪ ) or ductileness (ˈductileness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French, from Latin ductilis, from dūcere to lead
