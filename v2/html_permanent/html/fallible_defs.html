fallible

(ˈfælɪbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. capable of being mistaken; erring2. liable to mislead
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fallibility (ˌfalliˈbility) or fallibleness (ˈfallibleness) nounfallibly (ˈfallibly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin fallibilis, from Latin fallere to deceive

fallible

Powered by COBUILD
(fælɪbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is fallible, you mean that they are not perfect and are likely to make mistakes or to fail in what they are doing. [formal] ⇒ They are only human and all too fallible. ⇒ The system has proved fallible time after time. ⇒ Human reason is a fallible guide.fallibility (fælɪbɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ Errors may have been made due to human fallibility. ⇒ The fallibility of science is one of the great betrayals of our times.
