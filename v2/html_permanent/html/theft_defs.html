theft

(θɛft )
Definitions
noun1. criminal law the dishonest taking of property belonging to another person with the intention of depriving the owner permanently of its possession2. rare something stolen
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

theftless (ˈtheftless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English thēofth; related to Old Norse thӯfth, Old Frisian thiūvethe, Middle Dutch düfte; see thief
