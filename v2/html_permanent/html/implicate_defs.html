implicate

(ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to show to be involved, esp in a crime2. to involve as a necessary inference; imply ⇒ his protest implicated censure by the authorities3. to affect intimately ⇒ this news implicates my decision4. rare to intertwine or entangle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

implicative (ɪmˈplɪkətɪv ) adjectiveimplicatively (imˈplicatively) adverbimplicativeness (imˈplicativeness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin implicāre to involve, from im- + plicāre to fold
