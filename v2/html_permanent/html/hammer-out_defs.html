hammer out

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. to shape or remove with or as if with a hammer2. to form or produce (an agreement, plan, etc) after much discussion or dispute

Definitions
phrasal verbIf people hammer out an agreement or treaty, they succeed in producing it after a long or difficult discussion. [V P n] ⇒ I think we can hammer out a solution. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ The new fixture package has been hammered out by the Premiership clubs in talks over the last ten days. [Also V n P] →  hammer
