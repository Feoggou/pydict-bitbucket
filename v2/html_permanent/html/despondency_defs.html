despondency

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪspɒndənsi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounDespondency is a strong feeling of unhappiness caused by difficulties which you feel you cannot overcome. ⇒ There's a mood of gloom and despondency in the country.
Example sentences containing 'despondency'
Arnold left the crumbling terrace of Willington Hall with a feeling of despondency. Lewis, Roy A TROUT IN THE MILKWhy spread gloom and despondency unnecessarily, especially when there's damn-all we can do about it. Maclean, Alistair SAN ANDREASIt was as though he had needed prodding from a trough of despondency in order to return her smile. Mosco, Maisie OUT OF THE ASHESSo many times, over the years, had Septach Melayn ' s well-timed frivolity pulled him back from the brink of despondency ! Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
