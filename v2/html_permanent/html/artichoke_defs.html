artichoke

(ˈɑːtɪˌtʃəʊk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: globe artichoke a thistle-like Eurasian plant, Cynara scolymus, cultivated for its large edible flower head containing many fleshy scalelike bracts: family Asteraceae (composites)2. the unopened flower head of this plant, which can be cooked and eaten3. See Jerusalem artichoke
Word origin
C16: from Italian articiocco, from Old Spanish alcarchofa, from Arabic al-kharshūf
