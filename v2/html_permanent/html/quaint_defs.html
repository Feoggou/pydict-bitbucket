quaint

(kweɪnt )
Definitions
adjective1. attractively unusual, esp in an old-fashioned style ⇒ a quaint village2. odd, peculiar, or inappropriate ⇒ a quaint sense of duty
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

quaintly (ˈquaintly) adverbquaintness (ˈquaintness) noun

Word Origin

C13 (in the sense: clever): from Old French cointe, from Latin cognitus known, from cognoscere to ascertain
