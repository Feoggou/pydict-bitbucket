brisk

(brɪsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. lively and quick; vigorous ⇒ a brisk walk, ⇒ trade was brisk2. invigorating or sharp ⇒ brisk weatherverb3. (often foll by up) to enliven; make or become brisk
Derived forms
briskly (ˈbriskly) adverbbriskness (ˈbriskness) noun
Word origin
C16: probably variant of brusque

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A brisk activity or action is done quickly and in an energetic way. ⇒ Taking a brisk walk can often induce a feeling of well-being. ⇒ The horse broke into a brisk trot.briskly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Eve walked briskly down the corridor to her son's room.briskness uncount noun ⇒ With determined briskness, Amy stood up and put their cups back on the tray.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf trade or business is brisk, things are being sold very quickly and a lot of money is being made. [business] ⇒ Vendors were doing a brisk trade in souvenirs. ⇒ Its sales had been brisk since July.briskly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ A trader said gold sold briskly on the local market.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf the weather is brisk, it is cold and fresh. ⇒ ...a typically brisk winter's day on the South Coast. ⇒ The breeze was cool, brisk and invigorating.4. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is brisk behaves in a busy, confident way which shows that they want to get things done quickly. ⇒ The Chief summoned me downstairs. He was brisk and businesslike. ⇒ She is noted for her brisk handling of business.briskly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'Anyhow,' she added briskly, 'it's none of my business.'briskness uncount noun ⇒ He felt her familiar briskness, and he knew that it was all over.
