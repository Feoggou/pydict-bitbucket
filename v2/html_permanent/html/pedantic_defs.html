pedantic

(pɪˈdæntɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveof, relating to, or characterized by pedantry. Also (obsolete): pedantical

Derived Forms

pedantically (peˈdantically) adverb

pedantic

(pɪdæntɪk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you think someone is pedantic, you mean that they are too concerned with unimportant details or traditional rules, especially in connection with academic subjects. [disapproval] ⇒ His lecture was so pedantic and uninteresting.
Example sentences containing 'pedantic'
His pedantic , almost fussy manner hid a brilliant brain which could absorb endless amounts of information. Various LOOKING FOR THE SPARKAnne says I'm becoming more and more pedantic ; one of the penalties of growing older. Ashford, Jeffrey A QUESTION OF PRINCIPLE`Well, it was only... The receptionist said Dr McIntosh would come -- "She heard her voice, pedantic , fussy. Haines, Pamela THE GOLDEN LION
