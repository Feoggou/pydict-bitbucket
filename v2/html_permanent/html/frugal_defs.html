frugal

(ˈfruːɡəl )
Definitions
adjective1. practising economy; living without waste; thrifty2. not costly; meagre
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frugality (fruˈgality) or frugalness (ˈfrugalness) nounfrugally (ˈfrugally) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin frūgālis, from frūgī useful, temperate, from frux fruit

frugal

Powered by COBUILD
(fruːgəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectivePeople who are frugal or who live frugal lives do not eat much or spend much money on themselves. thrifty ⇒ She lives a frugal life.frugality uncount noun ⇒ We must practise the strictest frugality and economy.frugally graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ We lived fairly frugally. ⇒ He frugally saved various bits of the machine in carefully marked boxes.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA frugal meal is small and not expensive. ⇒ The diet was frugal: cheese and water, rice and beans.
