petitioner

(pɪˈtɪʃənə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who presents a petition2. mainly British the plaintiff in a divorce suit

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA petitioner is a person who presents or signs a petition.2. countable nounA petitioner is a person who brings a legal case to a court of law. [law] ⇒ The judge awarded the costs of the case to the petitioners.3. countable nounA petitioner is someone who goes to a court of law to ask for a divorce. [British, law] ⇒ Both the petitioner and respondent provide for the upkeep of the children.
