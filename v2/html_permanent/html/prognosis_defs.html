prognosis

(prɒɡˈnəʊsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -noses (-ˈnəʊsiːz)1. medicinea. a prediction of the course or outcome of a disease or disorderb. the chances of recovery from a disease2. any forecast or prediction

Word Origin

C17: via Latin from Greek: knowledge beforehand

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA prognosis is an estimate of the future of someone or something, especially about whether a patient will recover from an illness. [formal] ⇒ If the cancer is caught early the prognosis is excellent. ⇒ ...a gloomy prognosis of the Scots' championship prospects.
Example sentences containing 'prognosis'
We talked on, quietly, almost whispering, as though we were in the sickroom of a patient whose prognosis was uncertain. Reeves, Robert DOUBTING THOMASHe had patted her hand, her cheek, her shoulder - all surrogates for her rump - but on being asked for a prognosis , he, too, wouldn't say. Delman, David DEATH OF A NYMPH"So you believe he has deeply ingrained psychopathology and his prognosis is bad," David said. Gregg Andrew Hurwitz DO NO HARM
