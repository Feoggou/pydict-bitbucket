reclamation

(ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the conversion of desert, marsh, or other waste land into land suitable for cultivation2. the recovery of useful substances from waste products3. the act of reclaiming or state of being reclaimed

Definitions
noun(Extractive engineering: General, Safety) Reclamation is the process of making sure that the land and sea do not suffer any permanent effects from oil and gas production. ⇒ Reclamation is the restoration of land to its original condition by regrading contours and replanting after the land has been mined or drilled. ⇒ Reclamation was initiated to stabilize disturbed surfaces on well pads, roads, and pipelines. ⇒ Reclamation is the process of making sure that the land and sea do not suffer any permanent effects from oil and gas production.
