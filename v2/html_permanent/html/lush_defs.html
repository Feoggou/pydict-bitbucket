lush1

(lʌʃ )
Definitions
adjective1. (of vegetation) abounding in lavish growth2. (esp of fruits) succulent and fleshy3. luxurious, elaborate, or opulent4. slang very attractive or pleasing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lushly (ˈlushly) adverblushness (ˈlushness) noun

Word Origin

C15: probably from Old French lasche lax, lazy, from Latin laxus loose; perhaps related to Old English lǣc, Old Norse lakr weak, German lasch loose

lush2 - slang

(lʌʃ )
Definitions
noun1. a heavy drinker, esp an alcoholic2. alcoholic drinkverb3. US and Canadian to drink (alcohol) to excess
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: origin unknown

lush

Powered by COBUILD
(lʌʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: lushes, lusher , lushest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveLush fields or gardens have a lot of very healthy grass or plants. ⇒ ...the lush green meadows bordering the river. ⇒ The beautifully landscaped gardens sprawl with lush vegetation.lushness uncount noun ⇒ ...a tropical lushness.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you describe a place or thing as lush, you mean that it is very luxurious. luxurious ⇒ ...a mirrored bathroom done in soft pink tiles with a lush, plush carpet. ⇒ The fabrics were lush.3. countable nounIf you describe someone as a lush, you mean that they drink too much alcohol. [informal]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
