ruminate

(ˈruːmɪˌneɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (of ruminants) to chew (the cud)2. (when intr, often foll by upon, on, etc) to meditate or ponder (upon)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rumination (ˌrumiˈnation) nounruminative (ˈruminative) adjectiveruminatively (ˈruminatively) adverbruminator (ˈrumiˌnator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin rūmināre to chew the cud, from rumen

ruminate

Powered by COBUILD
(ruːmɪneɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: ruminates , ruminating , ruminated
Definitions
1. verbIf you ruminate on something, you think about it very carefully. [formal] [V + on/about/over] ⇒ He ruminated on the terrible wastage that typified American life. [V on/about/over n] ⇒ Obsessional personalities commonly ruminate excessively about death. [Also V]2. verbWhen animals ruminate, they bring food back from their stomach into their mouth and chew it again. [technical] [V] ⇒ He wanted to have a look at the two oxen, both ruminating without raising their eyes.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
