animate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈænɪˌmeɪt ) (transitive)1. to give life to or cause to come alive2. to make lively; enliven3. to encourage or inspire4. to impart motion to; move to action or work5. to record on film or video tape so as to give movement to ⇒ an animated cartoonadjective (ˈænɪmɪt )6. being alive or having life7. gay, spirited, or lively
Word origin
C16: from Latin animāre to fill with breath, make alive, from anima breath, spirit

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The adjective is pronounced (ænɪmət ). The verb is pronounced (ænɪmeɪt ).1. adjectiveSomething that is animate has life, in contrast to things like stones and machines which do not. OPPOSITE: inanimate ⇒ ...all aspects of the material world, animate and inanimate.2. verbTo animate something means to make it lively or more cheerful. [V n] ⇒ There was precious little about the cricket to animate the crowd. [V n] ⇒ The girls watched, little teasing smiles animating their faces.
