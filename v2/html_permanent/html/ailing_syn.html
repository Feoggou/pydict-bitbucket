ailing

adjective

  1. weak, failing, poor, flawed, unstable, feeble, unsatisfactory, deficient, unsound • A rise in overseas sales is good news for the ailing economy.
  2. ill, suffering, poorly, diseased, sick, weak, crook (Australian & & New Zealand, informal), feeble, invalid, debilitated, sickly, unwell, infirm, off colour, under the weather (informal), indisposed • She stopped working to care for her ailing mother.

ail

verb

  1. (literary) trouble, worry, bother, distress, pain, upset, annoy, irritate, sicken, afflict, be the matter with • a debate on what ails the industry
  2. be ill, be sick, be unwell, feel unwell, be crook (Australian & & New Zealand, informal), be indisposed, be or feel off colour • He is said to be ailing at his home in the country.
