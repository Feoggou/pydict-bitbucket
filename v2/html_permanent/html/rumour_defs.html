rumour

or US rumor (ˈruːmə )
Definitions
noun1. a. information, often a mixture of truth and untruth, passed around verballyb. (in combination) ⇒ a rumour-monger2. gossip or hearsay3. archaic din or clamour4. obsolete fame or reputationverb5. (tr; usually passive) to pass around or circulate in the form of a rumour ⇒ it is rumoured that the Queen is coming6. literary to make or cause to make a murmuring noise
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin rūmor common talk; related to Old Norse rymja to roar, Sanskrit rāut he cries

regional note:   in AM, use rumorvariable noun [oft N that]A rumour is a story or piece of information that may or may not be true, but that people are talking about. ⇒ Simon denied rumours that he was planning to visit Bulgaria later this month. ⇒ There have been persistent rumours of quarrels within the movement. ⇒ There's a strange rumour going around at the moment about Peter.
