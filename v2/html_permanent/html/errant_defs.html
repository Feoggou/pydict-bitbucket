errant

(ˈɛrənt )
Definitions
adjective (often postpositive)1. archaic or literary wandering in search of adventure2. erring or straying from the right course or accepted standards
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

errantly (ˈerrantly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French: journeying, from Vulgar Latin iterāre (unattested), from Latin iter journey; influenced by Latin errāre to err

Definitions
adjective [ADJ n]Errant is used to describe someone whose actions are considered unacceptable or wrong by other people. For example, an errant husband is unfaithful to his partner. [formal] ⇒ Usually his cases involved errant husbands and wandering wives. ⇒ His errant son at Dartmouth ran up debts of £2250.
