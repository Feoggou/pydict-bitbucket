diffuse

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (dɪˈfjuːz )1. to spread or cause to spread in all directions2. to undergo or cause to undergo diffusion3. to scatter or cause to scatter; disseminate; disperseadjective (dɪˈfjuːs )4. spread out over a wide area5. lacking conciseness6. (esp of some creeping stems) spreading loosely over a large area7. characterized by or exhibiting diffusion ⇒ diffuse light, ⇒ diffuse reflection8. botany (of plant growth) occurring throughout a tissueAvoid confusion with defuse
Derived forms
diffusely (dɪˈfjuːslɪ ) adverbdiffuseness (difˈfuseness) noundiffusible (dɪˈfjuːzəbəl ) adjectivedifˌfusibility (difˌfusiˈbility) or diffusibleness (difˈfusibleness) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin diffūsus spread abroad, from diffundere to pour forth, from dis- away + fundere to pour

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (dɪfjuːz ). The adjective is pronounced (dɪfjuːs ).1. ergative verb & verbIf something such as knowledge or information is diffused, or if it diffuses somewhere, it is made known over a wide area or to a lot of people. [written] [be V-ed] ⇒ Over time, the technology is diffused and adopted by other countries. [V n] ⇒ ...an attempt to diffuse new ideas. [V prep] ⇒ As agriculture developed, agricultural ideas diffused across Europe.diffusion (dɪfjuːʒən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the development and diffusion of ideas.2. verbTo diffuse a feeling, especially an undesirable one, means to cause it to weaken and lose its power to affect people. [V n] ⇒ The arrival of letters from the Pope did nothing to diffuse the tension.3. verbIf something diffuses light, it causes the light to spread weakly in different directions. [V n] ⇒ Diffusing a light also reduces its power. [V n] ⇒ The sun slid behind trees, its last light diffused by wintry branches.4. ergative verb & verbTo diffuse or be diffused through something means to move and spread through it. [V prep] ⇒ It allows nicotine to diffuse slowly and steadily into the bloodstream. [V prep] ⇒ It created a glowing centre of warmth that quickly diffused through my limbs. [V n prep] ⇒ The moisture present in all foods absorbs the flavour of the smoke and eventually diffuses that flavour into its interior. [Also V, V n]diffusion uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ There are data on the rates of diffusion of molecules.5. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is diffuse is not directed towards one place or concentrated in one place but spread out over a large area. [written] ⇒ ...a diffuse community. ⇒ A cold, diffuse light filtered in through the skylight.6. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as diffuse, you mean that it is vague and difficult to understand or explain. ⇒ His writing is so diffuse and obscure that it is difficult to make out what it is he is trying to say.
