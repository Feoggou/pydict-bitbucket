clang

(klæŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make or cause to make a loud resounding noise, as metal when struck2. (intransitive) to move or operate making such a soundnoun3. a resounding metallic noise4. the harsh cry of certain birds
Word origin
C16: from Latin clangere

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen a large metal object clangs, it makes a loud noise. [V] ⇒ A little later the church bell clanged. [V] ⇒ The door clanged shut behind them.Clang is also a noun. ⇒ He pulled the gates to with a clang. ⇒ In the older part of the city, the clang and bang of the builders goes on all day.clanging uncount noun ⇒ ...the clanging of the cell doors.
