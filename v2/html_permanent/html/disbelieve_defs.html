disbelieve

(ˌdɪsbɪˈliːv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to reject as false or lying; refuse to accept as true or truthful2. (intransitive; usually foll by in) to have no faith (in) ⇒ disbelieve in God
Derived forms
ˌdisbeliever (ˌdisbeˈliever) nounˌdisbelieving (ˌdisbeˈlieving) adjectiveˌdisbelievingly (ˌdisbeˈlievingly) adverb

disbelieve

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪsbɪliːv )
Word forms: disbelieves , disbelieving , disbelieved
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you disbelieve someone or disbelieve something that they say, you do not believe that what they say is true. [V n] ⇒ There is no reason to disbelieve him. [V n] ⇒ He had never been able to disbelieve it completely. [Also V that]2. verbIf you disbelieve in something, you do not believe that it exists or that it works. [V in n] ⇒ Frank disbelieved in astrology.
