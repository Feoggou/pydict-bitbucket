adjacent

(əˈdʒeɪsənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. being near or close, esp having a common boundary; adjoining; contiguous2. mathematicsa. (of a pair of vertices in a graph) joined by a common edgeb. (of a pair of edges in a graph) meeting at a common vertexnoun3. geometry the side lying between a specified angle and a right angle in a right-angled triangle
Derived forms
adˈjacency nounadˈjacently adverb
Word origin
C15: from Latin adjacēre to lie next to, from ad- near + jacēre to lie

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveIf one thing is adjacent to another, the two things are next to each other. ⇒ He sat in an adjacent room and waited. ⇒ The schools were adjacent but there were separate doors. [+ to] ⇒ ...offices adjacent to the museum.
