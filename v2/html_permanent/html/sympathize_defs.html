sympathize

or sympathise (ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; often foll by with)1. to feel or express compassion or sympathy (for); commiserate ⇒ he sympathized with my troubles2. to share or understand the sentiments or ideas (of); be in sympathy (with)

Derived Forms

sympaˌthizer (ˈsympaˌthizer) or sympaˌthiser (ˈsympaˌthiser) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use sympathise1. verbIf you sympathize with someone who is in a bad situation, you show that you are sorry for them. [V + with] ⇒ I must tell you how much I sympathize with you for your loss, Professor. [V] ⇒ He would sympathize but he wouldn't understand.2. verbIf you sympathize with someone's feelings, you understand them and are not critical of them. [V + with] ⇒ Some Europeans sympathize with the Americans over the issue. [V + with] ⇒ He liked Max, and sympathized with his ambitions. [Also V]3. verbIf you sympathize with a proposal or action, you approve of it and are willing to support it. [V + with] ⇒ Most of the people living there sympathized with the guerrillas.
