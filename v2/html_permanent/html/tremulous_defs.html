tremulous

(ˈtrɛmjʊləs )
Definitions
adjective1. vibrating slightly; quavering; trembling ⇒ a tremulous voice2. showing or characterized by fear, anxiety, excitement, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tremulously (ˈtremulously) adverbtremulousness (ˈtremulousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin tremulus quivering, from tremere to shake

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone's voice, smile, or actions are tremulous, they are unsteady because the person is uncertain, afraid, or upset. [literary] ⇒ She fidgeted in her chair as she took a deep, tremulous breath.tremulously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'He was so good to me,' she said tremulously.
