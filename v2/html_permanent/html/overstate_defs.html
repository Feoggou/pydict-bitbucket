overstate for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊvəʳsteɪt )
Word forms: overstates , overstating , overstated
Definitions
verbIf you say that someone is overstating something, you mean they are describing it in a way that makes it seem more important or serious than it really is. OPPOSITE: understate [V n] ⇒ The authors no doubt overstated their case with a view to catching the public's attention. [V n] ⇒ The importance of health education cannot be overstated.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
