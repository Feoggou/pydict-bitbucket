put upon

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, preposition, usually passive)1. to presume on (a person's generosity, good nature, etc); take advantage of ⇒ he's always being put upon2. to impose hardship on; maltreat

put-upon

(ˈpʊtəpɒn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveinformal treated badly, esp by somebody who takes advantage of one's willingness to help them, or by being asked to do an excessive amount of work ⇒ Volunteers from all walks of life are feeling put upon., ⇒ The film centres on Bernard's put-upon wife, Maud., ⇒ I feel put-upon.
