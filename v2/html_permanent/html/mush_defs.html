mush1

(mʌʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a soft pulpy mass or consistency2. US a thick porridge made from corn meal3. informal cloying sentimentality4. radio interference in reception, esp a hissing noiseverb5. (transitive) to reduce (a substance) to a soft pulpy mass

Word Origin

C17: from obsolete moose porridge; probably related to mash; compare Old English mōs food

Powered by COBUILD
(mʌʃ )
Word forms: mushes , mushing , mushed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [oft a N]Mush is a thick, soft paste. ⇒ The brown mush in the fridge is some veg soup left over.2. uncount nounIf you describe something such as a film or book as mush, you mean that it is very sentimental. [disapproval] ⇒ Whenever famous actresses get together to make a 'woman's film' you can bet on an overload of sentimental mush.3. verbIf you mush something, you make it into a mush. [V-ed up] ⇒ ...mushed-up potato and cauliflower. [Also V n, V-ed]
