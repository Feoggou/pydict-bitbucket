underfoot

(ˌʌndəˈfʊt )
Definitions
adverb1. underneath the feet; on the ground2. in a position of subjugation or subservience3. in the way
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

underfoot for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌndəʳfʊt )
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v, n ADV]You describe something as being underfoot when you are standing or walking on it. ⇒ ...a room, high and square with carpet underfoot and tapestries on the walls. ⇒ It was still wet underfoot.2. adverb [ADV after v]If you trample or crush something underfoot, you spoil or destroy it by stepping on it. ⇒ Morgan dropped his cigarette and crushed it underfoot. ⇒ ...half-ripe apples that were being trampled underfoot.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
