quaver

(ˈkweɪvə )
Definitions
verb1. to say or sing (something) with a trembling voice2. (intransitive) (esp of the voice) to quiver, tremble, or shake3. (intransitive) rare to sing or play quavers or ornamental trillsnoun4. music a note having the time value of an eighth of a semibreve. Usual US and Canadian name: eighth note5. a tremulous sound or note
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

quaverer (ˈquaverer) nounquavering (ˈquavering) adjectivequaveringly (ˈquaveringly) adverbquavery (ˈquavery) adjective

Word Origin

C15 (in the sense: to vibrate, quiver1): from quaven to tremble, of Germanic origin; compare Low German quabbeln to tremble

quaver

Powered by COBUILD
(kweɪvəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: quavers , quavering , quavered
Definitions
1. verbIf someone's voice quavers, it sounds unsteady, usually because they are nervous or uncertain. [V] ⇒ Her voice quavered and she fell silent.Quaver is also a noun. [+ in] ⇒ There was a quaver in Beryl's voice.2. countable nounA quaver is a musical note that is half as long as a crotchet. [mainly British]regional note:   in AM, use eighth note
