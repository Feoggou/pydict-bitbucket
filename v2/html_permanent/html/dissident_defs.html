dissident

(ˈdɪsɪdənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. disagreeing; dissentingnoun2. a person who disagrees, esp one who disagrees with the government
Derived forms
dissidence (ˈdissidence) noundissidently (ˈdissidently) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Latin dissidēre to be remote from, from dis-1 + sedēre to sit

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounDissidents are people who disagree with and criticize their government, especially because it is undemocratic. ⇒ ...political dissidents. ⇒ ...former Soviet dissident Natan Scharansky.2. adjective [ADJ n]Dissident people disagree with or criticize their government or a powerful organization they belong to. ⇒ ...a dissident Russian novelist. ⇒ She was suspected of having links with a dissident group. ⇒ ...dissident shareholders.
