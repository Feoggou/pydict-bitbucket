allure

(əˈljʊə , əˈlʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to entice or tempt (someone) to a person or place or to a course of action; attractnoun2. attractiveness; appeal ⇒ the cottage's allure was its isolation
Derived forms
alˈlurement nounalˈlurer noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French alurer, from lure bait, lure

allure

Powered by COBUILD
(əljʊəʳ , US əlʊr )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounThe allure of something or someone is the pleasing or exciting quality that they have. ⇒ It's a game that has really lost its allure. ⇒ ...the captivating allure of Isabelle Adjani.
Example sentences containing 'allure'
She was a magical person; allured by her, he fought against the allure. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEA way of life taken for granted suddenly gained the allure of the unattainable. St. James, Ian FINAL RESORTHowever, I wouldn't have imagined such esoteric distractions would hold much allure for a young lady such as you. Stephanie Laurens ON A WILD NIGHT
