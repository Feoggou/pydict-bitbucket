abstract

adjective

  1. theoretical, general, complex, academic, intellectual, subtle, profound, philosophical, speculative, unrealistic, conceptual, indefinite, deep, separate, occult, hypothetical, generalized, impractical, arcane, notional, abstruse, recondite, theoretic, conjectural, unpractical, nonconcrete • starting with a few abstract principles OPPOSITES:  real, material, specific, actual, concrete, definite, factual
  2. symbolic, figurative, emblematic, impressionistic, non-representational • a modern abstract painting

noun

  1. summary, résumé, outline, extract, essence, summing-up, digest, epitome, rundown, condensation, compendium, synopsis, précis, recapitulation, review, abridgment • If you want to submit a paper, you must supply an abstract. OPPOSITES:  expansion, enlargement

verb

  1. extract, draw, pull, remove, separate, withdraw, isolate, pull out, take out, take away, detach, dissociate, pluck out • The author has abstracted poems from earlier books. OPPOSITES:  add, combine, inject
  2. summarize, outline, digest, shorten, condense, abbreviate, epitomize, abridge, précis, synopsize (US)
