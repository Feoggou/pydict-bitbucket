furnish

(ˈfɜːnɪʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to provide (a house, room, etc) with furniture, carpets, etc2. to equip with what is necessary; fit out3. to give; supply ⇒ the records furnished the information required
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

furnisher (ˈfurnisher) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French fournir, of Germanic origin; related to Old High German frummen to carry out

Definitions
1. verbIf you furnish a room or building, you put furniture and furnishings into it. [V n + with] ⇒ Many proprietors try to furnish their hotels with antiques. [Also V n]2. verbIf you furnish someone with something, you provide or supply it. [formal] [V n + with] ⇒ They'll be able to furnish you with the rest of the details.
