gallant

Definitions
adjective (ˈɡælənt )1. brave and high-spirited; courageous and honourable; dashing ⇒ a gallant warrior2. (ɡəˈlænt , ˈɡælənt ) (of a man) attentive to women; chivalrous3. imposing; dignified; stately ⇒ a gallant ship4. archaic showy in dressnoun (ˈɡælənt , ɡəˈlænt ) archaic5. a woman's lover or suitor6. a dashing or fashionable young man, esp one who pursues women7. a brave, high-spirited, or adventurous manverb (ɡəˈlænt , ˈɡælənt ) rare8. (when intr, usually foll by with) to court or flirt (with)9. (transitive) to attend or escort (a woman)

Word Origin

C15: from Old French galant, from galer to make merry, from gale enjoyment, pleasure, of Germanic origin; related to Old English wela weal2

(gælənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
pronunciation note:   Also pronounced (gəlænt ) for meaning [sense 3].1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is gallant, they behave bravely and honourably in a dangerous or difficult situation. [old-fashioned] ⇒ The gallant soldiers lost their lives so that peace might reign again.gallantly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The town responded gallantly to the War.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]A gallant effort or fight is one in which someone tried very hard to do something difficult, although in the end they failed. [written, approval] ⇒ He died at the age of 82, after a gallant fight against illness.gallantly graded adverb [ADV with v] valiantly ⇒ The Spaniard gallantly fought off 11 set points before Seles won 8-6.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a man is gallant, he is kind, polite, and considerate towards women. [old-fashioned] ⇒ Douglas was a complex man, thoughtful, gallant, and generous.gallantly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He gallantly kissed Marie's hand as we prepared to leave.
