dictation

(dɪkˈteɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of dictating material to be recorded or taken down in writing2. the material dictated3. authoritative commands or the act of giving them
Derived forms
dictational (dicˈtational) adjective

(dɪkteɪʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounDictation is the speaking or reading aloud of words for someone else to write down. ⇒ ...taking dictation from the dean of the Faculty. ⇒ He had had an arm amputated and relied on her to take down his books from dictation.2. uncount nounDictation is the giving of orders in a forceful and commanding way. ⇒ The Europeans, while keen for partnership with the U.S., would not accept dictation.
