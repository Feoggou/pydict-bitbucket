work up

Definitions
verb (tr, mainly adverb)1. to arouse the feelings of; excite2. to cause to grow or develop ⇒ to work up a hunger3. (also preposition) to move or cause to move gradually upwards4. to manipulate or mix into a specified object or shape5. to gain knowledge of or skill at (a subject)

work up for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you work yourself up, you make yourself feel very upset or angry about something. [V pron-refl P + into/to] ⇒ She worked herself up into a bit of a state. ⇒ Don't just lie there working yourself up, do something about it.2. → See also worked up3. phrasal verbIf you work up the enthusiasm or courage to do something, you succeed in making yourself feel it. summon up [V P n] ⇒ Your creative talents can also be put to good use, if you can work up the energy. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Malcolm worked up the nerve to ask Grandma Rose for some help.4. phrasal verbIf you work up a sweat or an appetite, you make yourself sweaty or hungry by doing exercise or hard work. [V P n] ⇒ You can really work up a sweat doing housework. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ It was around seven when I finished at the library. I wasn't hungry, but I'd worked up a thirst.5. phrasal verbIf you work up something such as a piece of writing, you spend time and effort preparing it. [V P n] ⇒ I sketched the layout of a prototype store and worked up a business plan. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ They asked me to work up some sample drawings and bring them down. →  work
