Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverb [ADV before v]If you say that something is presently happening, you mean that it is happening now. ⇒ She is presently developing a number of projects. ⇒ The island is presently uninhabited. ⇒ He is presently the medical director for the Fellowship of World Christians.2. adverbYou use presently to indicate that something happened quite a short time after the time or event that you have just mentioned. [written] ⇒ Presently, a young woman in a white coat came in. ⇒ 'You're not looking too well, Thomas,' he said presently.3. adverb [ADV after v]If you say that something will happen presently, you mean that it will happen quite soon. [formal] ⇒ 'Who's Agnes?'—'You'll be meeting her presently.'. ⇒ 'Just take it easy,' David said. 'You'll feel better presently.'
