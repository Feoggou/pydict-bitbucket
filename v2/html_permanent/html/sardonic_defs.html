sardonic

(sɑːˈdɒnɪk ) or sardonical (sɑːˈdɒnɪkəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivecharacterized by irony, mockery, or derision

Derived Forms

sardonically (sarˈdonically) adverbsardonicism (sarˈdonicism) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French sardonique, from Latin sardonius, from Greek sardonios derisive, literally: of Sardinia, alteration of Homeric sardanios scornful (laughter or smile)

sardonic

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone as sardonic, you mean their attitude to people or things is humorous but rather critical. ⇒ ...a big, sardonic man, who intimidated his students. ⇒ ...a sardonic sense of humour.sardonically (sɑːʳdɒnɪkli ) graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He grinned sardonically and bowed towards her.
