drench

(drɛntʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to make completely wet; soak2. to give liquid medicine to (an animal), esp by forcenoun3. the act or an instance of drenching4. a dose of liquid medicine given to an animal
Derived forms
drencher (ˈdrencher) noundrenching (ˈdrenching) noun, adjective
Word origin
Old English drencan to cause to drink; related to Old High German trenken

(drentʃ )
Word forms: drenches , drenching , drenched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbTo drench something or someone means to make them completely wet. [V n] ⇒ They turned fire hoses on the people and drenched them. [get V-ed] ⇒ They were getting drenched by icy water. [V-ed] ⇒ We were completely drenched and cold. [V-ing] ⇒ I reached Kilmarnock in the early morning in drenching rain.-drenched combining form ⇒ ...the rain-drenched streets of the capital.
