sibyl

(ˈsɪbɪl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in ancient Greece and Rome) any of a number of women believed to be oracles or prophetesses, one of the most famous being the sibyl of Cumae, who guided Aeneas through the underworld2. a witch, fortune-teller, or sorceress

Derived Forms

sibylline (ˈsɪbɪˌlaɪn , sɪˈbɪlaɪn ) or sibyllic or sibylic (sɪˈbɪlɪk ) adjective

Word Origin

C13: ultimately from Greek Sibulla, of obscure origin

Similar Words

prophetess, oracle, Cassandra, seer, Pythia, pythoness
