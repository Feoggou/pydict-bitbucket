circumflex

(ˈsɜːkəmˌflɛks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a mark (^) placed over a vowel to show that it is pronounced with rising and falling pitch, as in ancient Greek, as a long vowel rather than a short one, as in French, or with some other different qualityadjective2. (of certain nerves, arteries, or veins) bending or curving around
Derived forms
ˌcircumflexion (ˌcircumˈflexion) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin circumflexus, from circumflectere to bend around, from circum- + flectere to bend

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA circumflex or a circumflex accent is a symbol written over a vowel in French and other languages, usually to indicate that it should be pronounced longer than usual. It is used for example in the word 'rôle'.
