attest

(əˈtɛst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to affirm the correctness or truth of2. (when intr, usually foll by to) to witness (an act, event, etc) or bear witness to (an act, event, etc) as by signature or oath3. (transitive) to make evident; demonstrate ⇒ his life of luxury attests his wealth4. (transitive) to provide evidence for ⇒ the marks in the ground attested the presence of a fossil
Derived forms
atˈtestable adjectiveatˈtestant, atˈtester or especially law atˈtestor or atˈtestator nounattestation (ˌætɛˈsteɪʃən ) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin attestārī to prove, from testārī to bear witness, from testis a witness

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbTo attest something or attest to something means to say, show, or prove that it is true. [formal] [V + to] ⇒ Police records attest to his long history of violence. [V that] ⇒ I can personally attest that the cold and flu season is here. [V n] ⇒ His beautifully illustrated book well attested his love of the university. [Also V with quote]
