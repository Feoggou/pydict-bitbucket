moribund

(ˈmɒrɪˌbʌnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. near death2. stagnant; without force or vitality

Derived Forms

ˌmoribundity (ˌmoriˈbundity) nounmoriˌbundly (ˈmoriˌbundly) adverb

Word Origin

C18: from Latin, from morī to die

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as moribund, you mean that it is in a very bad condition. [formal] ⇒ ...the moribund economy. ⇒ ...the moribund housing market. ⇒ The British music scene is nostalgic, decrepit and moribund.
