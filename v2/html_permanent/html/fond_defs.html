fond1

(fɒnd )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; foll by of) predisposed (to); having a liking (for)2. loving; tender ⇒ a fond embrace3. indulgent; doting ⇒ a fond mother4. (of hopes, wishes, etc) cherished but unlikely to be realized ⇒ he had fond hopes of starting his own business5. archaic or dialecta. foolishb. credulous
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fondly (ˈfondly) adverbfondness (ˈfondness) noun

Word Origin

C14 fonned, from fonnen to be foolish, from fonne a fool

fond

Powered by COBUILD
(fɒnd ) for learners of English
Word forms: fonder , fondest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are fond of someone, you feel affection for them. [+ of] ⇒ I am very fond of Michael. [+ of] ⇒ She was especially fond of a little girl named Betsy.fondness uncount noun [+ for] ⇒ ...a great fondness for children.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]You use fond to describe people or their behaviour when they show affection. ⇒ ...a fond father. ⇒ He gave him a fond smile.fondly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ Liz saw their eyes meet fondly across the table.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are fond of something, you like it or you like doing it very much. [+ of] ⇒ He was fond of marmalade. [+ of] ⇒ She is fond of collecting rare carpets.fondness uncount noun [+ for] ⇒ I've always had a fondness for jewels. ⇒ ...his fondness for cooking.4. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you have fond memories of someone or something, you remember them with pleasure. ⇒ I have very fond memories of living in our village.fondly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ My dad took us there when I was about four and I remembered it fondly.5. adjective [ADJ n]You use fond to describe hopes, wishes, or beliefs which you think are foolish because they seem unlikely to be fulfilled. ⇒ My fond hope is that we will be ready by Christmastime.fondly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I fondly imagined that surgery meant a few stitches and an overnight stay in hospital.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
