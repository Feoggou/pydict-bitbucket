blatant

(ˈbleɪtənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. glaringly conspicuous or obvious ⇒ a blatant lie2. offensively noticeable ⇒ blatant disregard for a person's feelings3. offensively noisy
Derived forms
blatancy (ˈblatancy) nounblatantly (ˈblatantly) adverb
Word origin
C16: coined by Edmund Spenser; probably influenced by Latin blatīre to babble; compare Middle Low German pladderen
