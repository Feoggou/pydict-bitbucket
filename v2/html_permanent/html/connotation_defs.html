connotation

(ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an association or idea suggested by a word or phrase; implication2. the act or fact of connoting3. logic another word for intension (sense 1)
Derived forms
connotative (ˈkɒnəˌteɪtɪv , kəˈnəʊtə-) or connotive (conˈnotive) adjectiveconnoˌtatively (ˈconnoˌtatively) or connotively (conˈnotively) adverb

Word forms: connotations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounThe connotations of a particular word or name are the ideas or qualities which it makes you think of. ⇒ It's just one of those words that's got so many negative connotations. [+ of] ⇒ 'Urchin', with its connotation of mischievousness, may not be a particularly apt word.
Example sentences containing 'connotation'
I wondered if the man she'd mentioned had a romantic connotation. Anita Anderson SUMMER OF SECRETSAnd suddenly my confrontation with the guardsmen in the steams took on a new connotation. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThough Maelgwn clearly disapproved of his connotation , he simply focused his attention on the players. Traci Harding THE ANCIENT FUTURE: THE DARK AGE
