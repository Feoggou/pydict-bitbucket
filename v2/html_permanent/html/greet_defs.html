greet1

(ɡriːt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to meet or receive with expressions of gladness or welcome2. to send a message of friendship to3. to receive in a specified manner ⇒ her remarks were greeted by silence4. to become apparent to ⇒ the smell of bread greeted him
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English grētan; related to Old High German gruozzen to address

greet

Powered by COBUILD
(griːt ) for learners of English
Word forms: greets , greeting , greeted
Definitions
1. verbWhen you greet someone, you say 'Hello' or shake hands with them. [V n] ⇒ She liked to be home to greet Steve when he came in from school.2. verb [usu passive]If something is greeted in a particular way, people react to it in that way. [be V-ed + with/by] ⇒ The European Court's decision has been greeted with dismay by fishermen. [be V-ed adv] ⇒ It is unlikely that this suggestion will be greeted enthusiastically in the Baltic States.3. verbIf you are greeted by something, it is the first thing you notice in a particular place. [written] [be V-ed + by] ⇒ I was greeted by a shocking sight. [V n] ⇒ The savoury smell greeted them as they went through the door.
