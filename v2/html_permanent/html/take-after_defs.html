Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, preposition)1. to resemble in appearance, character, behaviour, etc2. to follow as an example

take after

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verb [no passive]If you take after a member of your family, you resemble them in your appearance, your behaviour, or your character. [V P n] ⇒ Ted's always been difficult, Mr Kemp–he takes after his dad. →  take
