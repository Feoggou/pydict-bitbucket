aunt

(ɑːnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (often capital, esp as a term of address)1. a sister of one's father or mother2. the wife of one's uncle3. a term of address used by children for any woman, esp for a friend of the parents4. See my aunt!
Word origin
C13: from Old French ante, from Latin amita a father's sister

Comment by acli on 30 May 2013
The listed Chinese translation is extremely misleading. The Chinese language has a different understanding of what an “aunt” is and distinguishes between more than four types of aunts, each described by a different word, and the word listed is not the usual one used in Mandarin (which is suggested by the use of simplified Chinese) and, as far as I know, not used for sense 3 in any dialect. Even if we restrict to sense 1 (and so only words that corresponds to the English understanding of “aunt”), depending on the dialect “aunt” still corresponds to two to four Chinese words that are NOT interchangeable, In other words, the listed translation is wrong 50% of the time at best, 75% of the time wrong at worst.
