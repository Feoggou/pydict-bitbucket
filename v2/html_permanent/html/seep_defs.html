seep

(siːp )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to pass gradually or leak through or as if through small openings; oozenoun2. a small spring or place where water, oil, etc, has oozed through the ground3. another word for seepage

Word Origin

Old English sīpian; related to Middle High German sīfen, Swedish dialect sipa

Word forms: seeps , seeping , seeped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something such as liquid or gas seeps somewhere, it flows slowly and in small amounts into a place where it should not go. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Radioactive water had seeped into underground reservoirs. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The gas is seeping out of the rocks. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Engineers said that plutonium could begin seeping from the corroded sub.Seep is also a noun. ⇒ ...an oil seep.2. verbIf something such as secret information or an unpleasant emotion seeps somewhere, it comes out gradually. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...the tide of racism which is sweeping Europe seeps into Britain. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...letting information seep out of the Treasury.
