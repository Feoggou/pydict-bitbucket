Carthusian

(kɑːˈθjuːzɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounRoman Catholic Churcha. a member of an austere monastic order founded by Saint Bruno in 1084 near Grenoble, Franceb. (as modifier) ⇒ a Carthusian monastery
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin Carthusianus, from Latin Carthusia Chartreuse, near Grenoble
