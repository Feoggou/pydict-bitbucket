grand jury

Definitions
nounlaw (esp in the US and, now rarely, in Canada) a jury of between 12 and 23 persons summoned to inquire into accusations of crime and ascertain whether the evidence is adequate to found an indictment. Abolished in Britain in 1948. Compare petit jury

Definitions
countable nounA grand jury is a jury, usually in the United States, which considers a criminal case in order to decide if someone should be tried in a court of law. ⇒ They have already given evidence before a grand jury in Washington.
