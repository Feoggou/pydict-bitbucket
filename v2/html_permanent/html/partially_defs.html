(pɑːʳʃəli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverbIf something happens or exists partially, it happens or exists to some extent, but not completely. ⇒ Lisa is deaf in one ear and partially blind.
Example sentences containing 'partially'
But they were also made with considerable magic, and were partially sentient as a result. Garth Nix LIRAEL: DAUGHTER OF THE CLAYRJean-Luc would have been the ideal fantasy figure to appeal to a steady visitor with an active brain and a partially dysfunctional body. MacLeod, Charlotte SOMETHING IN THE WATERI sat down over the other side of the park, on a bench partially hidden by a ragged bush. Hugo Wilcken THE EXECUTION
