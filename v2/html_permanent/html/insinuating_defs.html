insinuating

(ɪnˈsɪnjʊeɪtɪŋ)
Definitions
adjective1. suggesting by indirect allusion, hints, innuendo, etc ⇒ Marcus kept making insinuating remarks., ⇒ 'Yes.' My tone was insinuating. 'I heard she was a real friendly girl.'2. introducing subtly or deviously3. causing someone, esp oneself, to be accepted by gradual approaches or manoeuvres

insinuating

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnsɪnjueɪtɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
adjectiveIf you describe someone's words or voice as insinuating, you mean that they are saying in an indirect way that something bad is the case. [disapproval] ⇒ Marcus kept making insinuating remarks. ⇒ 'Yes.' My tone was insinuating. 'I heard she was a real friendly girl.'
