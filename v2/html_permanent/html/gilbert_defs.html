Gilbert

(ˈɡɪlbət )
Definitions
noun1.  Grove Karl. 1843–1918, US geologist who pioneered the study of river development and valley erosion2.  Sir Humphrey. ?1539–83, English navigator: founded the colony at St John's, Newfoundland (1583)3.  William. 1540–1603, English physician and physicist, noted for his study of terrestrial magnetism in De Magnete (1600)4.  Sir W(illiam) S(chwenck). 1836–1911, English dramatist, humorist, and librettist. He collaborated (1871–96) with Arthur Sullivan on the famous series of comic operettas, including The Pirates of Penzance (1879), Iolanthe (1882), and The Mikado (1885)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

gilbert

(ˈɡɪlbət )
Definitions
nouna unit of magnetomotive force; the magnetomotive force resulting from the passage of 4π abamperes through one turn of a coil. 1 gilbert is equivalent to 10⁄4π = 0.795 775 ampere-turn. Symbols: Gb, Gi
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: named after William Gilbert
