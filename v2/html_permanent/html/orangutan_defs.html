orangutan in American English

(ɔˈræŋʊˌtæn )
Definitions
noun a great ape (Pongo pygmaeus) with shaggy, reddish-brown hair, very long arms, small ears, and a hairless face: it is smaller than the gorilla and is found only in the swampy, coastal jungles of Borneo and Sumatra: also oˈrangouˌtang (oʊˈræŋuˌtæŋ ; ɔˈræŋuˌtæŋ ; əˈræŋuˌtæŋ ; oʊˈræŋəˌtæŋ ; ɔˈræŋəˌtæŋ ; əˈræŋəˌtæŋ )
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

Malay oraṅ utan, lit., man of the forest < oraṅ, man + utan, forest, term for savage tribes: first applied to the ape by Europeans
