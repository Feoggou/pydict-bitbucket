oppose

(əˈpəʊz )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to fight against, counter, or resist strongly2. (transitive) to be hostile or antagonistic to; be against3. (transitive) to place or set in opposition; contrast or counterbalance4. (transitive) to place opposite or facing5. (intransitive) to be or act in opposition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

opposer (opˈposer) nounopposing (opˈposing) adjectiveopposingly (opˈposingly) adverboppositive (əˈpɒzɪtɪv ) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin oppōnere, from ob- against + pōnere to place
