(blaɪndli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] [oft ADV adj]If you say that someone does something blindly, you mean that they do it without having enough information, or without thinking about it. [disapproval] ⇒ Don't just blindly follow what the banker says. ⇒ Without adequate information, many students choose a college almost blindly. ⇒ ...the cricket team and its blindly optimistic supporters.2. → see also blind

It was all about turning my feelings around, transforming them into a weapon with which I could blindly hit back.
