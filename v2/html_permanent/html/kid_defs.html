kid1

(kɪd )
Definitions
noun1. the young of a goat or of a related animal, such as an antelope2. soft smooth leather made from the hide of a kid3. informala. a young person; childb. (modifier) younger or being still a child ⇒ kid brother, ⇒ kid sister4. See our kidverbWord forms: kids, kidding or kidded5. (of a goat) to give birth to (young)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

kiddishness (ˈkiddishness) nounkidlike (ˈkidˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C12: of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse kith, Shetland Islands kidi lamb

kid2

(kɪd )
Definitions
verbWord forms: kids, kidding or kidded (sometimes foll by on or along) informal1. (transitive) to tease or deceive for fun2. (intransitive) to behave or speak deceptively for fun3. (transitive) to delude or fool (oneself) into believing (something) ⇒ don't kid yourself that no-one else knows
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

kiddingly (ˈkiddingly) adverb

Word Origin

kid for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(kɪd )
Word forms: kids , kidding , kidded
Definitions
1. countable nounYou can refer to a child as a kid. [informal] ⇒ They've got three kids. ⇒ All the kids in my class could read.2. countable noun & vocative nounYoung people who are no longer children are sometimes referred to as kids. [informal] ⇒ There were gangs of kids on motorbikes roaming around. ⇒ That's a lot for a kid of 22 to cope with.3. adjective [ADJ n]You can refer to your younger brother as your kid brother and your younger sister as your kid sister. [informal] ⇒ My kid sister woke up and started crying.4. countable nounA kid is a young goat.5. verb [usu cont]If you are kidding, you are saying something that is not really true, as a joke. [informal] [V] ⇒ I'm not kidding, Frank. There's a cow out there, just standing around. [V] ⇒ I'm just kidding. [V n] ⇒ Are you sure you're not kidding me?6. verbIf you kid someone, you tease them. [V n] ⇒ He liked to kid Ingrid a lot. [V n + about] ⇒ He used to kid me about being chubby.7. verbIf people kid themselves, they allow themselves to believe something that is not true because they wish that it was true. [V pron-refl] ⇒ We're kidding ourselves, Bill. We're not winning, we're not even doing well. [V pron-refl that] ⇒ I could kid myself that you did this for me, but it would be a lie.8. →  no kidding9. →  no kidding?10. →  sb has got to be kidding/sb must be kidding11. →  who is sb kidding/who is sb trying to kid?
