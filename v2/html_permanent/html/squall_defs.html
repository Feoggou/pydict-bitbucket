squall1

(skwɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a sudden strong wind or brief turbulent storm2. any sudden commotion or show of temperverb3. (intransitive) to blow in a squall

Derived Forms

squallish (ˈsquallish) adjectivesqually (ˈsqually) adjective

Word Origin

C18: perhaps a special use of squall2

squall2

(skwɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to cry noisily; yellnoun2. a shrill or noisy yell or howl

Derived Forms

squaller (ˈsqualler) noun

Word Origin

C17: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Icelandic skvala to shout; see squeal

squall

Powered by COBUILD
(skwɔːl )
Word forms: squalls , squalling , squalled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA squall is a sudden strong wind which often causes a brief, violent rain storm or snow storm. ⇒ The boat was hit by a squall north of the island.2. verbIf a person or animal squalls, they make a loud unpleasant noise like the noise made by a crying baby. [V] ⇒ There was an infant squalling in the back of the church. [V-ing] ⇒ ...squalling guitars.
