whiplash

(ˈwɪpˌlæʃ )
Definitions
noun1. a quick lash or stroke of a whip or like that of a whip2. medicine See whiplash injury

whiplash for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪplæʃ )
Definitions
uncount noun [oft N n]Whiplash is a neck injury caused by the head suddenly moving forwards and then back again, for example in a car accident. ⇒ His wife suffered whiplash and shock. ⇒ Four police officers were taken to hospital with whiplash injuries.
