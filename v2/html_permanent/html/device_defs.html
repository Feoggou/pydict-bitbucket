device

(dɪˈvaɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a machine or tool used for a specific task; contrivance2. euphemistic a bomb3. a plan or plot, esp a clever or evil one; scheme; trick4. any ornamental pattern or picture, as in embroidery5. a piece of computer hardware that is designed for a specific function6. a written, printed, or painted design or figure, used as a heraldic sign, emblem, trademark, etc7. a particular pattern of words, figures of speech, etc, used in literature to produce an effect on the reader8. archaic the act or process of planning or devising9. See leave someone to his or her own devices
Word origin
C13: from Old French devis purpose, contrivance and devise difference, intention, from deviser to divide, control; see devise

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA device is an object that has been invented for a particular purpose, for example for recording or measuring something. ⇒ ...an electronic device that protects your vehicle 24 hours a day. ⇒ ...a device that could measure minute quantities of matter. ⇒ We believe that an explosive device had been left inside a container.2. countable nounA device is a method of achieving something. [+ for] ⇒ They claim that military spending is used as a device for managing the economy. ⇒ ...the literary device of the metaphor.3. →  to leave someone to their own devices
