glee

(ɡliː )
Definitions
noun1. great merriment or delight, often caused by someone else's misfortune2. a type of song originating in 18th-century England, sung by three or more unaccompanied voices. Compare madrigal (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English gléo; related to Old Norse glӯ

glee

Powered by COBUILD
(gliː ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount noun [oft with N]Glee is a feeling of happiness and excitement, often caused by someone else's misfortune. ⇒ His victory was greeted with glee by his fellow American golfers. ⇒ There was much glee among journalists over the leaked letter.
