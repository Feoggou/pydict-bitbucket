crude

(kruːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. lacking taste, tact, or refinement; vulgar ⇒ a crude joke2. in a natural or unrefined state3. lacking care, knowledge, or skill ⇒ a crude sketch4. (prenominal) stark; blunt ⇒ the crude facts5. (of statistical data) unclassified or unanalysed6. archaic unripenoun7. short for crude oil
Derived forms
crudely (ˈcrudely) adverbcrudity (ˈcrudity) or crudeness (ˈcrudeness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin crūdus bloody, raw; related to Latin cruor blood

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA crude method or measurement is not exact or detailed, but may be useful or correct in a rough, general way. ⇒ Standard measurements of blood pressure are an important but crude way of assessing the risk of heart disease or strokes. ⇒ Birthplace data are only the crudest indicator of actual migration paths.crudely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ The donors can be split–a little crudely–into two groups. ⇒ Put crudely, the liberationists favour extending a number of rights to children.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an object that someone has made as crude, you mean that it has been made in a very simple way or from very simple parts. ⇒ ...crude wooden boxes.crudely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV -ed] ⇒ ...a crudely-carved wooden form. ⇒ In some places maps are scarce, and are often crudely produced.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as crude, you disapprove of them because they speak or behave in a rude, offensive, or unsophisticated way. [disapproval] ⇒ Nev! Must you be quite so crude? ⇒ ...crude language. ⇒ ...crude sexual jokes.crudely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ He hated it when she spoke so crudely. ⇒ To put it crudely, nobody really gives a toss about this any more.crudity (kruːdɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ He had not expected such crudity from so sophisticated a minister.crudeness uncount noun ⇒ Crudeness is on the rise in all fields of entertainment.4. adjective [ADJ n]Crude substances are in a natural or unrefined state, and have not yet been used in manufacturing processes. ⇒ ...8.5 million tonnes of crude steel.5. variable nounCrude is the same as crude oil.
