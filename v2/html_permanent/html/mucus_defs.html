mucus

(ˈmjuːkəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe slimy protective secretion of the mucous membranes, consisting mainly of mucinSee note at mucous

Word Origin

C17: from Latin: nasal secretions; compare mungere to blow the nose; related to Greek muxa mucus, muktēr nose

mucus

Powered by COBUILD
(mjuːkəs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounMucus is a thick liquid that is produced in some parts of your body, for example the inside of your nose.
