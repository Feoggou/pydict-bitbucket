pertain

(pəˈteɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; often foll by to)1. to have reference, relation, or relevance ⇒ issues pertaining to women2. to be appropriate ⇒ the product pertains to real user needs3. to belong (to) or be a part (of); be an adjunct, attribute, or accessory (of)

Word Origin

C14: from Latin pertinēre, from per- (intensive) + tenēre to hold

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf one thing pertains to another, it relates, belongs, or applies to it. [formal] [V to n] ⇒ The restrictions he imposed pertained to the type and height of buildings and the activities for which they could be used. [V to n] ⇒ I would much rather that you asked Mrs Zuckerman any questions pertaining to herself. [V + to] ⇒ ...matters pertaining to naval district defense. [Also V]
