honest

(ˈɒnɪst )
Definitions
adjective1. not given to lying, cheating, stealing, etc; trustworthy2. not false or misleading; genuine3. just or fair ⇒ honest wages4. characterized by sincerity and candour ⇒ an honest appraisal5. without pretensions or artificial traits ⇒ honest farmers6. archaic (of a woman) respectable7. See honest broker8. See honest Injun9. See honest to God10. See make an honest woman of
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

honestness (ˈhonestness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French honeste, from Latin honestus distinguished, from honōs honour

honest

Powered by COBUILD
(ɒnɪst ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as honest, you mean that they always tell the truth, and do not try to deceive people or break the law. ⇒ My dad was the most honest man I ever met. ⇒ I know she's honest and reliable.honestly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ She fought honestly for a just cause and for freedom.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are honest in a particular situation, you tell the complete truth or give your sincere opinion, even if this is not very pleasant. [+ about] ⇒ I was honest about what I was doing. [+ with] ⇒ He had been honest with her and she had tricked him! ⇒ What do you think of the school, in your honest opinion?honestly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ It came as a shock to hear an old friend speak so honestly about Ted.3. adverbYou say 'honest' before or after a statement to emphasize that you are telling the truth and that you want people to believe you. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ I'm not sure, honest.4. →  honest to God5. →  to be honest
