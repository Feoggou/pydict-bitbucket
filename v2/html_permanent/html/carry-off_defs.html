carry off

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to remove forcefully2. to win ⇒ he carried off all the prizes3. to manage or handle (a situation) successfully ⇒ he carried off the introductions well4. to cause to die ⇒ he was carried off by pneumonia

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you carry something off, you do it successfully. [V n P] ⇒ He's got the experience and the authority to carry it off. [Also V P n]2. phrasal verbIf you carry off a prize or a trophy, you win it. [V P n] ⇒ It carried off the Evening Standard drama award for best play. [Also V n P] →  carry
