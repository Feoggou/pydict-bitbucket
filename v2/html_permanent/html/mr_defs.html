Mr

(ˈmɪstə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural Messrs (ˈmɛsəz )1. a title used before a man's name or names or before some office that he holds ⇒ Mr Jones, ⇒ Mr President2. (in military contexts) a title used in addressing a warrant officer, officer cadet, or junior naval officer3. a title placed before the surname of a surgeon

Word Origin

C17: abbreviation of mister

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use Mr.1. title nounMr is used before a man's name when you are speaking or referring to him. ⇒ ...Mr Grant. ⇒ ...Mr Bob Price. ⇒ ...Mr and Mrs Daniels.2. countable noun [N n]Mr is sometimes used in front of words such as 'President' and 'Chairman' to address the man who holds the position mentioned. ⇒ Mr. President, you're aware of the system.3. → see also Messrs4. → Mr Right
