satin

(ˈsætɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fabric of silk, rayon, etc, closely woven to show much of the warp, giving a smooth glossy appearance2. (modifier) of or like satin in texture ⇒ a satin finish

Derived Forms

satin-ˌlike (ˈsatin-ˌlike) adjectivesatiny (ˈsatiny) adjective

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Arabic zaitūnī of Zaytūn, Arabic rendering of Chinese Tseutung (now Tsinkiang), port in southern China from which the cloth was probably first exported

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounSatin is a smooth, shiny kind of cloth, usually made from silk. ⇒ ...a peach satin ribbon. ⇒ ...stylish dresses in silks and satins.2. adjective [ADJ n]If something such as a paint, wax, or cosmetic gives something a satin finish, it reflects light to some extent but is not very shiny. ⇒ The final stage of waxing left it with a satin sheen.
