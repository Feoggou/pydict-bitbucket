kitchen

(ˈkɪtʃɪn )
Definitions
nouna. a room or part of a building equipped for preparing and cooking foodb. (as modifier) ⇒ a kitchen table
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English cycene, ultimately from Late Latin coquīna, from Latin coquere to cook; see kiln

kitchen in the Oil and Gas Industry

Powered by COBUILD
(kɪtʃən)
Word forms: (regular plural) kitchens
Definitions
noun(Extractive engineering: Petroleum geology) A kitchen is a subsurface area where oil and gas are generated because of pressure and temperature conditions. ⇒ An area of the subsurface where source rock has reached appropriate conditions of pressure and temperature to generate hydrocarbons is known as a kitchen. ⇒ Hydrocarbon accumulations can result from the interruption of the hydrocarbon migration path linking oil and gas kitchens with the earth's surface. ⇒ A kitchen is a subsurface area where oil and gas are generated because of pressure and temperature conditions.
