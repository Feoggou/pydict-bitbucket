time out

(taɪm aʊt) or time-out
Definitions
noun1. sport in basketball, American football, ice hockey, and some other sports, when a team calls a time out, they call a stop to the game for a few minutes in order to rest and discuss how they are going to play2. if you take time out from a job or activity, you have a break from it and do something different instead ⇒ Women are returning to the labour market after time out to raise young families., ⇒ He took time out from campaigning to accompany his mother to a dinner.

time-out

Definitions
noun1. sport an interruption in play during which players rest, discuss tactics, or make substitutions2. a break taken during working hours3. computing a condition occurring when the amount of time a computer has been instructed to wait for another device to perform a task has expired, usually indicated by an error messageverb time out4. (intransitive) (of a computer) to stop operating because of a time-out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
