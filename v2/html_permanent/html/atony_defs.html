atony

(ˈætənɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. pathology lack of normal tone or tension, as in muscles; abnormal relaxation of a muscle2. phonetics lack of stress or accent on a syllable or word
Word origin
C17: from Latin atonia, from Greek: tonelessness, from atonos slack, from a-1 + tonos tone
