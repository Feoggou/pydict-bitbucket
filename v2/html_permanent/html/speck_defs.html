speck

(spɛk )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a very small mark or spot2. a small or tiny piece of somethingverb3. (transitive) to mark with specks or spots

Word Origin

Old English specca; related to Middle Dutch spekelen to sprinkle

Word forms: specks
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA speck is a very small stain, mark, or shape. [+ of] ⇒ ...a speck of blood.2. countable nounA speck is a very small piece of a powdery substance. [+ of] ⇒ Billy leaned forward and brushed a speck of dust off his shoes.
