fanatic

(fəˈnætɪk )
Definitions
noun1. a person whose enthusiasm or zeal for something is extreme or beyond normal limits2. informal a person devoted to a particular hobby or pastime; fan ⇒ a jazz fanaticadjective3. a variant of fanatical
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin fānāticus belonging to a temple, hence, inspired by a god, frenzied, from fānum temple

fanatic

Powered by COBUILD
(fənætɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: fanatics
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you describe someone as a fanatic, you disapprove of them because you consider their behaviour or opinions to be very extreme, for example in the way they support particular religious or political ideas. [disapproval] ⇒ I am not a religious fanatic but I am a Christian.2. countable noun [usu n N]If you say that someone is a fanatic, you mean that they are very enthusiastic about a particular activity, sport, or way of life. ⇒ Both Rod and Phil are football fanatics.3. graded adjective & adjectiveFanatic means the same as fanatical.
