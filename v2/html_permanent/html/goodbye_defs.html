goodbye

(ˌɡʊdˈbaɪ )
Definitions
sentence substitute1. farewell: a conventional expression used at leave-taking or parting with people and at the loss or rejection of things or ideasnoun2. a leave-taking; parting ⇒ they prolonged their goodbyes for a few more minutes3. a farewell ⇒ they said goodbyes to each other
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: contraction of God be with ye

goodbye

Powered by COBUILD
(gʊdbaɪ ) also good-bye for learners of English
Word forms: goodbyes
Definitions
1. conventionYou say 'Goodbye' to someone when you or they are leaving, or at the end of a telephone conversation. [formulae]2. countable nounWhen you say your goodbyes, you say something such as 'Goodbye' when you leave. ⇒ He said his goodbyes knowing that a long time would pass before he would see his child again. ⇒ I said a hurried goodbye and walked home in the cold. ⇒ Perry and I exchanged goodbyes.3. →  say goodbye/wave goodbye4. →  say goodbye to something/wave goodbye to something5. → to kiss something goodbye
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
