legal

(ˈliːɡəl )
Definitions
adjective1. established by or founded upon law; lawful2. of or relating to law3. recognized, enforceable, or having a remedy at law rather than in equity4. relating to or characteristic of the profession of law
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

legally (ˈlegally) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin lēgālis, from lēx law

(liːgəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Legal is used to describe things that relate to the law. ⇒ He vowed to take legal action. ⇒ ...the British legal system. ⇒ I sought legal advice on this. ⇒ ...the legal profession.legally adverb [ADV with v] [ADV adj] ⇒ There are reasons to doubt that a second trial is morally, legally or politically justified. ⇒ It could be a bit problematic, legally speaking.2. adjectiveAn action or situation that is legal is allowed or required by law. ⇒ What I did was perfectly legal. ⇒ ...drivers who have more than the legal limit of alcohol.legally adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ The school is legally responsible for your child's safety. ⇒ A lorry driver can legally work eighty-two hours a week.
