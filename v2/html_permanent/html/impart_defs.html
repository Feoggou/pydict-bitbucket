impart

(ɪmˈpɑːt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to communicate (information); relate2. to give or bestow (something, esp an abstract quality) ⇒ to impart wisdom
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impartable (imˈpartable) adjectiveimpartation (ˌimparˈtation) or impartment (imˈpartment) nounimparter (imˈparter) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French impartir, from Latin impertīre, from im- (in) + partīre to share, from pars part

impart

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmpɑːʳt ) for learners of English
Word forms: imparts , imparting , imparted
Definitions
1. verbIf you impart information to people, you tell it to them. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The ability to impart knowledge and command respect is the essential qualification for teachers. [V n + to] ⇒ I am about to impart knowledge to you that you will never forget.2. verbTo impart a particular quality to something means to give it that quality. [formal] [V n + to] ⇒ She managed to impart great elegance to the unpretentious dress she was wearing. [V n] ⇒ His production of Harold Pinter's play fails to impart a sense of excitement or danger.
