protract

(prəˈtrækt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to lengthen or extend (a speech, etc); prolong in time2. (of a muscle) to draw, thrust, or extend (a part, etc) forwards3. to plot or draw using a protractor and scale

Derived Forms

protractive (proˈtractive) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōtrahere to prolong, from pro-1 + trahere to drag

Similar Words

extend, continue, prolong, keep going, stretch out, lengthen, draw out, spin out, drag on or out
