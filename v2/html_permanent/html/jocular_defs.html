jocular

(ˈdʒɒkjʊlə )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by joking and good humour2. meant lightly or humorously; facetious
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jocularity (ˌdʒɒkjʊˈlærɪtɪ ) nounjocularly (ˈjocularly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin joculāris, from joculus little joke

jocular for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɒkjʊləʳ )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone has a jocular manner, you mean that they are cheerful and often make jokes or try to make people laugh. [formal] ⇒ He was in a less jocular mood than usual. ⇒ The song was written in a light-hearted jocular way.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
