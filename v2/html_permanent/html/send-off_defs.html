send off

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbWhen you send off a letter or package, you send it somewhere by post. [V P n] ⇒ He sent off copies to various people for them to read and make comments. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verb [usu passive]If a football player is sent off, the referee makes them leave the field during a game, as a punishment for seriously breaking the rules. [be V-ed P] ⇒ The 30-year-old Scottish international was sent off for arguing with a linesman.3. → see also sending-off →  send

send-off

Word forms: send-offs
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu adj N]If a group of people give someone who is going away a send-off, they come together to say goodbye to them. [informal] ⇒ All the people in the buildings came to give me a rousing send-off.
Example sentences containing 'send off'
And I'd send off two or three flares if you really want a response. Laurie R. King FOLLY
Example sentences containing 'send off'
He had robbed them of a decent send-off , a prolonged farewell. Maupin, Armistead SIGNIFICANT OTHERS
