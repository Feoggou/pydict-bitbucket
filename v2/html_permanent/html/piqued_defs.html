piqued

(piːkt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveresentful or irritated; annoyed ⇒ She wrinkled her nose, piqued by his total lack of enthusiasm., ⇒ Granny was a little piqued because it had all been arranged without her knowledge., ⇒ a bit piqued, ⇒ slightly piqued

piqued

Powered by COBUILD
(piːkt )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If someone is piqued, they are offended or annoyed, often by something that is not very important. ⇒ Granny was astounded and a little piqued, I think, because it had all been arranged without her knowledge. [+ by] ⇒ She wrinkled her nose, piqued by his total lack of enthusiasm.
