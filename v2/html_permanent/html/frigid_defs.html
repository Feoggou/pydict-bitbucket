frigid

(ˈfrɪdʒɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. formal or stiff in behaviour or temperament; lacking in affection or warmth2. (esp of a woman)a. lacking sexual responsivenessb. averse to sexual intercourse or unable to achieve orgasm during intercourse3. characterized by physical coldness ⇒ a frigid zone
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frigidity (friˈgidity) or frigidness (ˈfrigidness) nounfrigidly (ˈfrigidly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin frigidus cold, from frīgēre to be cold, freeze; related to Latin frīgus frost

frigid

Powered by COBUILD
(frɪdʒɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveFrigid means extremely cold. [formal] ⇒ A snowstorm hit the West today, bringing with it frigid temperatures. ⇒ The water was too frigid to allow him to remain submerged for long.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If a woman is frigid, she finds it difficult to become sexually aroused. You can often use frigid to show disapproval. ⇒ My husband says I am frigid.frigidity (frɪdʒɪdɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ ...an inability to experience orgasm (often called frigidity).3. graded adjectiveIf you describe the atmosphere in a place or someone's behaviour as frigid, you mean that it is very formal and unfriendly. [disapproval] chilly ⇒ He presided at all councils of ministers, where the atmosphere could be frigid on occasions. ⇒ 'Well, dear,' her hostess would reply with a frigid smile.
