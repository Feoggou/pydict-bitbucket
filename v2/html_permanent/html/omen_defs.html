omen

(ˈəʊmən )
Definitions
noun1. a phenomenon or occurrence regarded as a sign of future happiness or disaster2. prophetic significanceverb3. (transitive) to portend
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin

omen for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊmen )
Word forms: omens
Definitions
countable nounIf you say that something is an omen, you think it indicates what is likely to happen in the future and whether it will be good or bad. portent ⇒ Her appearance at this moment is an omen of disaster. [+ for] ⇒ Could this at last be a good omen for peace?
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
