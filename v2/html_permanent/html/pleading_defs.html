pleading

(ˈpliːdɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun law1. the act of presenting a case in court, as by a lawyer on behalf of his client2. the art or science of preparing the formal written statements of the parties to a legal action. See also pleadings

(pliːdɪŋ )
Word forms: pleadings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A pleading expression or gesture shows someone that you want something very much. ⇒ ...his pleading eyes. ⇒ ...the pleading expression on her face. ⇒ Her voice was pleading.pleadingly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ He looked at me pleadingly.2. uncount nounPleading is asking someone for something you want very much, in an intense or emotional way. ⇒ He simply ignored Sid's pleading. ⇒ ...the pleadings of the poorer countries.3. → see also special pleading
