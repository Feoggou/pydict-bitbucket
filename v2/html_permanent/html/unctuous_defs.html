unctuous

(ˈʌŋktjʊəs )
Definitions
adjective1. slippery or greasy2. affecting an oily charm
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unctuosity (ˌʌŋktjʊˈɒsɪtɪ ) or unctuousness (ˈunctuousness) noununctuously (ˈunctuously) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin unctuōsus, from Latin unctum ointment, from ungere to anoint

unctuous for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌŋktʃuəs )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as unctuous, you are critical of them because they seem to be full of praise, kindness, or interest, but are obviously insincere. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ ...the kind of unctuous tone that I've heard often at diplomatic parties.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe food or drink as unctuous, you mean that it is creamy or oily. [formal] ⇒ Goose fat gives the most unctuous flavour but you can use chicken fat.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
