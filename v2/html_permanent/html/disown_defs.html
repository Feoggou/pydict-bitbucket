(dɪsoʊn )
Word forms: disowns , disowning , disowned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you disown someone or something, you say or show that you no longer want to have any connection with them or any responsibility for them. [V n] ⇒ The man who murdered the girl is no son of mine. I disown him. [V n] ⇒ Those comments were later disowned by an official army spokesman.
Example sentences containing 'disown'
My family - especially my distinguished brother - have chosen to disown me. Penn, John UNTO THE GRAVEIn a moment of doubt, he wondered if his father had decided to disown him. Pacter, Trudi YELLOW BIRDThis made it more difficult than it ought to have been to disown activities which in fact were completely outside his control. Melville, Anne THE HARDIE INHERITANCE"Well, if I have to act like someone else to get promoted, then I'm S-O-L, because I'm never going to disown who I am. Kimberla Lawson Roby A TASTE OF REALITY
