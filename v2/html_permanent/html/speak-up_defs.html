speak up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, adverb)1. to speak more loudly2. to state one's beliefs, objections, etc, bravely and firmly

speak up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you speak up, you say something, especially to defend a person or protest about something, rather than just saying nothing. [V P + for] ⇒ Uncle Herbert never argued, never spoke up for himself. [V P] ⇒ Don't be afraid of speaking up. [V P prep] ⇒ I am not suggesting that individuals never speak up about wrong-doing.2. phrasal verb [no cont]If you ask someone to speak up, you are asking them to speak more loudly. [V P] ⇒ I'm quite deaf–you'll have to speak up. →  speak
