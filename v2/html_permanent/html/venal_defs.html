venal

(ˈviːnəl )
Definitions
adjective1. easily bribed or corrupted; mercenary ⇒ a venal magistrate2. characterized by corruption ⇒ a venal civilization3. open to purchase, esp by bribery ⇒ a venal contract
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

venality (viːˈnælɪtɪ ) nounvenally (ˈvenally) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin vēnālis, from vēnum sale

Powered by COBUILD
(viːnəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as venal, you disapprove of them because they are prepared to do almost anything in return for money, even things which are dishonest or immoral. [disapproval] ⇒ Ian Trimmer is corrupt and thoroughly venal. ⇒ ...venal politicians.
