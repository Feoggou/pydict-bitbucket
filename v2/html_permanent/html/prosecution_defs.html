prosecution

(ˌprɒsɪˈkjuːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of prosecuting or the state of being prosecuted2. a. the institution and conduct of legal proceedings against a personb. the proceedings brought in the name of the Crown to put an accused on trial3. the lawyers acting for the Crown to put the case against a person. Compare defence (sense 6)4. the following up or carrying on of something begun, esp with a view to its accomplishment or completion

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounProsecution is the action of charging someone with a crime and putting them on trial. [+ of] ⇒ Yesterday the head of government called for the prosecution of those responsible for the deaths.2. singular nounThe lawyers who try to prove that a person on trial is guilty are called the prosecution. OPPOSITE: the defence ⇒ Colonel Pugh, for the prosecution, said that the offences occurred over a six-year period.
