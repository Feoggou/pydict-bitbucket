French1

(frɛntʃ )
Definitions
noun1. the official language of France: also an official language of Switzerland, Belgium, Canada, and certain other countries. It is the native language of approximately 70 million people; also used for diplomacy. Historically, French is an Indo-European language belonging to the Romance group. See also Old French, Anglo-French2. See the French3. See French vermouthadjective4. relating to, denoting, or characteristic of France, the French, or their language ▶ Related prefixes: Franco-, Gallo-5. (in Canada) of or relating to French Canadians
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

French

Powered by COBUILD
(frentʃ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveFrench means belonging or relating to France, or to its people, language, or culture. ⇒ ...the French parliament. ⇒ All the staff are French.2. plural nounThe French are the people of France. ⇒ Two-thirds of the French are in favour of limiting foreign imports into Europe.3. uncount nounFrench is the language spoken in France and in parts of some other countries, including Belgium, Canada, and Switzerland. ⇒ The villagers spoke French.
