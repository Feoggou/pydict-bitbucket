engaged

(ɪnˈɡeɪdʒd )
Definitions
adjective1. pledged to be married; betrothed2. employed, occupied, or busy3. architecture built against or attached to a wall or similar structure ⇒ an engaged column4. (of a telephone line) already in use
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

engagedly (ɪnˈɡeɪdʒɪdlɪ ) adverb

engaged

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪngeɪdʒd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveSomeone who is engaged in or engaged on a particular activity is doing that thing. [formal] [+ in] ⇒ They found the three engaged in target practice. [+ on] ⇒ ...the various projects he was engaged on.2. adjective [usu v-link ADJ]When two people are engaged, they have agreed to marry each other. ⇒ We got engaged on my eighteenth birthday. ⇒ He was engaged to Miss Julia Maria Boardman. ⇒ ...the engaged couple. [Also + to]3. adjective [v-link ADJ]If a telephone or a telephone line is engaged, it is already being used by someone else so that you are unable to speak to the person you are phoning. [British] ⇒ The line is engaged. ⇒ We tried to call you back but you were engaged.regional note:   in AM, use busy4. adjective [v-link ADJ]If a public toilet is engaged, it is already being used by someone else. [British]regional note:   in AM, usually use occupied
