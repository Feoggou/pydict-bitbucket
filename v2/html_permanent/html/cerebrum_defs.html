cerebrum

(ˈsɛrɪbrəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -brums or -bra (-brə)1. the anterior portion of the brain of vertebrates, consisting of two lateral hemispheres joined by a thick band of fibres: the dominant part of the brain in man, associated with intellectual function, emotion, and personality. See telencephalon2. the brain considered as a whole3. the main neural bundle or ganglion of certain invertebrates
Derived forms
cereˌbroid (ˈcereˌbroid) adjectivecerebric (ˈsɛrɪbrɪk ) adjective
Word origin
C17: from Latin: the brain
