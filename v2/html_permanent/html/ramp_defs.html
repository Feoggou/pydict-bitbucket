ramp

(ræmp )
Definitions
noun1. a sloping floor, path, etc, that joins two surfaces at different levels2. a movable stairway by which passengers enter and leave an aircraft3. the act of ramping4. British slang a swindle, esp one involving exorbitant prices5. another name for sleeping policemanverb6. (intr; often foll by about or around) (esp of animals) to rush around in a wild excited manner7. to act in a violent or threatening manner, as when angry (esp in the phrase ramp and rage)8. (transitive) finance to buy (a security) in the market with the object of raising its price and enhancing the image of the company behind it for financial gain
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18 (n): from C13 rampe, from Old French ramper to crawl or rear, probably of Germanic origin; compare Middle Low German ramp cramp
