credence

(ˈkriːdəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. acceptance or belief, esp with regard to the truth of the evidence of others ⇒ I cannot give credence to his account2. something supporting a claim to belief; recommendation; credential (esp in the phrase letters of credence)3. short for credence table
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin crēdentia trust, credit, from Latin crēdere to believe

(kriːdəns )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounIf something lends or gives credence to a theory or story, it makes it easier to believe. [formal] [+ to] ⇒ Good studies are needed to lend credence to the notion that genuine progress can be made in this important field.2. uncount nounIf you give credence to a theory or story, you believe it. [formal] [+ to] ⇒ You're surely not giving any credence to this story of Hythe's?
