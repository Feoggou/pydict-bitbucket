kickoff in American English

(ˈkɪkˌɔf )
Definitions
noun1.  Us, American Football a placekick that puts the ball into play at the beginning of each half or after a touchdown or field goal2.  Us a beginning, as of a campaign or drive

kickoff for learners of American English

Powered by COBUILD
(kɪkɔf )
Word forms: kickoffs
Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, use kick-off1. variable nounIn football or soccer, the kickoff is the time at which a particular game starts. ⇒ Hakan Sukur netted the goal just 10.8 seconds after the kickoff.2. countable nounIn football, a kickoff is the kick that begins a play, for example at the beginning of a half or after a touchdown or goal has been scored. [US] ⇒ Gunn fumbled away the opening kickoff for the second straight week.3. singular nounThe kickoff of an event or activity is its beginning. [informal] ⇒ Memorial Day weekend marks the kickoff of the summer vacation season.
