close up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to shut entirely2. (intransitive) to draw together ⇒ the ranks closed up3. (intransitive) (of wounds) to heal completely

close-up

(ˈkləʊsˌʌp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a photograph or film or television shot taken at close range2. a detailed or intimate view or examination ⇒ a close-up of modern society

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf someone closes up a building, they shut it completely and securely, often because they are going away. [V P n] ⇒ Just close up the shop. [V-ed P] ⇒ The summer house had been closed up all year.2. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbIf an opening, gap, or something hollow closes up, or if you close it up, it becomes closed or covered.
