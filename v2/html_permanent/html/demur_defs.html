demur

(dɪˈmɜː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -murs, -murring or -murred (intransitive)1. to raise objections or show reluctance; object2. law to raise an objection by entering a demurrer3. archaic to hesitate; delaynoun also: demurral (dɪˈmʌrəl )4. the act of demurring5. an objection raised6. archaic hesitation
Derived forms
demurrable (deˈmurrable) adjective
Word origin
C13: from Old French demorer, from Latin dēmorārī to loiter, linger, from morārī to delay, from mora a delay

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you demur, you say that you do not agree with something or will not do something that you have been asked to do. [formal] [V] ⇒ Hunt wanted to know, would I be prepared to take over the whole operation and supervise it? At first I demurred. [V] ⇒ The doctor demurred, but Piercey was insistent.2. →  without demur
