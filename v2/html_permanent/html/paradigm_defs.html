paradigm

(ˈpærəˌdaɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. grammar the set of all the inflected forms of a word or a systematic arrangement displaying these forms2. a pattern or model3. a typical or stereotypical example (esp in the phrase paradigm case)4. (in the philosophy of science) a very general conception of the nature of scientific endeavour within which a given enquiry is undertaken

Derived Forms

paradigmatic (ˌpærədɪɡˈmætɪk ) or paradigmatical (ˌpærədɪɡˈmætɪkəl) adjective

Word Origin

C15: via French and Latin from Greek paradeigma pattern, from paradeiknunai to compare, from para-1 + deiknunai to show

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA paradigm is a model for something which explains it or shows how it can be produced. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ ...a new paradigm of production.2. countable nounA paradigm is a clear and typical example of something. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ He had become the paradigm of the successful man.
