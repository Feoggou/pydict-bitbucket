ablution

(əˈbluːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the ritual washing of a priest's hands or of sacred vessels2. (often plural) the act of washing (esp in the phrase perform one's ablutions)3. (plural) military informal a washing place
Derived forms
abˈlutionary adjective
Word origin
C14: ultimately from Latin ablūere to wash away
Example sentences containing 'ablution'
At six o'clock he took towel and ablution bag into the bathroom, sleep like a blanket still muffling the ward. Sillitoe, Alan THE OPEN DOORThe hour of ablution had passed, and most of the pilgrims had departed. Leasor, James TANK OF SERPENTS
