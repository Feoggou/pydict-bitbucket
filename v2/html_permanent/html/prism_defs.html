prism

(ˈprɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a transparent polygonal solid, often having triangular ends and rectangular sides, for dispersing light into a spectrum or for reflecting and deviating light. They are used in spectroscopes, binoculars, periscopes, etc2. a form of crystal with faces parallel to the vertical axis3. mathematics a polyhedron having parallel, polygonal, and congruent bases and sides that are parallelograms

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin prisma, from Greek: something shaped by sawing, from prizein to saw

[literary] ⇒ Through the smoky prism of time, I could just barely make out my father as a young man.
