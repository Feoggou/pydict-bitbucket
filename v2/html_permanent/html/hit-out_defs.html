hit out

Definitions
verb (intr, adverb; often foll by at)1. to direct blows forcefully and vigorously2. to make a verbal attack (upon someone)

hit out

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you hit out at someone, you try to hit them, although you may miss them. [mainly British] [V P + at] ⇒ I used to hit out at my husband and throw things at him. [V P] ⇒ I had never punched anybody in my life but I hit out and gave him a black eye.2. phrasal verbIf you hit out at someone or something, you criticize them strongly because you do not agree with them. [journalism] [V P + at/against] ⇒ The President took the opportunity to hit out at what he sees as foreign interference. [V P] ⇒ Brazilian soccer boss Carlos Parreira hit out angrily last night after his side were barred from training at Wembley. →  hit
