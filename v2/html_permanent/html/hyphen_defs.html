hyphen

(ˈhaɪfən )
Definitions
noun1. the punctuation mark (-), used to separate the parts of some compound words, to link the words of a phrase, and between syllables of a word split between two consecutive lines of writing or printingverb2. (transitive) another word for hyphenate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin (meaning: the combining of two words), from Greek huphen (adv) together, from hypo- + heis one

Definitions
countable nounA hyphen is the punctuation sign used to join words together to make a compound, as in 'left-handed'. People also use a hyphen to show that the rest of a word is on the next line.
