privacy

(ˈpraɪvəsɪ , ˈprɪvəsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the condition of being private or withdrawn; seclusion2. the condition of being secret; secrecy3. philosophy the condition of being necessarily restricted to a single person

privacy

Powered by COBUILD
(prɪvəsi , US praɪ- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft poss N]If you have privacy, you are in a place or situation which allows you to do things without other people seeing you or disturbing you. ⇒ He saw the publication of this book as an embarrassing invasion of his privacy. ⇒ Thatched pavilions provide shady retreats for relaxing and reading in privacy. ⇒ ...a collection of over 60 designs to try on in the privacy of your own home.2. →  to invade someone's privacy
Example sentences containing 'privacy'
In the privacy of her bedroom, she had dismissed any lingering doubts. Mark Mills AMAGANSETTNow the last bastion of the human mystery could be breached - the privacy of human thought. Stewart, Michael COMPULSIONVic didn't follow her inside, stayed out on the porch, maybe out of respect for her privacy. James W. Hall OFF THE CHART
