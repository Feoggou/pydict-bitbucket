apart from

(əˈpɑːt frəm)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
preposition1. except for ⇒ The room was empty apart from one man., ⇒ She was the only British competitor apart from Richard Meade., ⇒ Apart from Patrick, the car was empty., ⇒ Apart from that, everything's fine.2. in addition to ⇒ There was always something to look forward to, apart from Rachel's visits., ⇒ Apart from his salary, he also has a private income.3. See quite apart from something

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseYou use apart from when you are making an exception to a general statement. except for ⇒ The room was empty apart from one man seated beside the fire. ⇒ She was the only British competitor apart from Richard Meade. →  apart
