interregnum

(ˌɪntəˈrɛɡnəm )
Definitions
noun plural -nums or -na (-nə)1. an interval between two reigns, governments, incumbencies, etc2. any period in which a state lacks a ruler, government, etc3. a period of absence of some control, authority, etc4. a gap in a continuity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

interregnal (ˌinterˈregnal) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Latin, from inter- + regnum reign
