cantharides

(kænˈθærɪˌdiːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural nounWord forms: singular cantharis (ˈkænθərɪs )a diuretic and urogenital stimulant or irritant prepared from the dried bodies of Spanish fly (family Meloidae, not Cantharidae), once thought to be an aphrodisiac. Also called: Spanish fly
Word origin
C15: from Latin, plural of cantharis, from Greek kantharis Spanish fly
