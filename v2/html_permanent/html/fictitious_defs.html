fictitious

(fɪkˈtɪʃəs )
Definitions
adjective1. not genuine or authentic; assumed; false ⇒ to give a fictitious address2. of, relating to, or characteristic of fiction; created by the imagination
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fictitiously (ficˈtitiously) adverbfictitiousness (ficˈtitiousness) noun

fictitious

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪktɪʃəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Fictitious is used to describe something that is false or does not exist, although some people claim that it is true or exists. non-existent ⇒ We're interested in the source of these fictitious rumours.2. adjectiveA fictitious character, thing, or event occurs in a story, play, or film but never really existed or happened. ⇒ The persons and events portrayed in this production are fictitious.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
