kick up for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you kick up a fuss about something, you make it very obvious that you are annoyed or dissatisfied. [V P n] ⇒ Those customers who have kicked up a fuss have received refunds. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ They kick up a rumpus and throw things at each other.2. phrasal verbIf you kick up dust or dirt, you create a cloud of dust or dirt as you move along a dusty road. [V P n] ⇒ She shuffled along, kicking up clouds of dust. →  kick
