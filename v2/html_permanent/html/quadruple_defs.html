quadruple

(ˈkwɒdrʊpəl , kwɒˈdruːpəl )
Definitions
verb1. to multiply by four or increase fourfoldadjective2. four times as much or as many; fourfold3. consisting of four partsnoun4. a quantity or number four times as great as another
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

quadruply (ˈquadruply) adverb

Word Origin

C16: via Old French from Latin quadruplus, from quadru- (see quadri-) + -plus -fold

quadruple

Powered by COBUILD
(kwɒdruːpəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: quadruples , quadrupling , quadrupled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf someone quadruples an amount or if it quadruples, it becomes four times bigger. [V n] ⇒ Norway has quadrupled its exports to the E.U.. [V] ⇒ The price has quadrupled in the last few years.2. predeterminerIf one amount is quadruple another amount, it is four times bigger. ⇒ They could sell their merchandise for quadruple the asking price.3. adjective [ADJ n]You use quadruple to indicate that something has four parts or happens four times. ⇒ ...a quadruple murder.
