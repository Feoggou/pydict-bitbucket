damned

(dæmd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. a. condemned to hellb. (as noun) ⇒ the damnedadverb, adjective slang2. (intensifier) ⇒ a damned good try, ⇒ a damned liar, ⇒ I should damned well think so!3. used to indicate amazement, disavowal, or refusal (in such phrases as I'll be damned and damned if I care)

damned

Powered by COBUILD
(dæmd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [ADJ n]Damned is used by some people to emphasize what they are saying, especially when they are angry or frustrated. [informal, rude, emphasis] ⇒ The damned meeting seemed endless. ⇒ They're a damned nuisance.Damned is also an adverb. ⇒ We are making a damned good profit, I tell you that.2. plural noun [the N]According to some religions, the damned are people whose punishment is to stay in hell for ever after they have died.3. →  be damned if4. →  I'll be damned
