with it

or with-it (wɪð ɪt)
Definitions
adjective informal1. fashionable; in style ⇒ Such would-be with-it phrases only get used in TV advertisements when they are no longer fashionable., ⇒ a with-it young man, ⇒ fashion magazines trying to be with it2. comprehending what is happening or being said ⇒ Tell her to get with it., ⇒ I'm not with it today., ⇒ Sorry, didn't mean to leave that info out. I'm not with it today., ⇒ She wasn't really with it. She hadn't taken in the practical consequences.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

with it for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
also with-it
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is with it, you mean that they are fashionable or know about new things, especially in culture. [informal, old-fashioned]2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone is not with it, they do not feel alert and therefore fail to understand things. [informal] ⇒ She wasn't really with it. She hadn't taken in the practical consequences.
