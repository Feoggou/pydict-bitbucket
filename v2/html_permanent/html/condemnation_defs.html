condemnation

Powered by COBUILD
(kɒndemneɪʃən )
Word forms: condemnations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounCondemnation is the act of saying that something or someone is very bad and unacceptable. [+ of] ⇒ There was widespread condemnation of Saturday's killings. [+ from] ⇒ The raids have drawn a strong condemnation from the United Nations Security Council.
Example sentences containing 'condemnation'
They had scanned the pamphlets in the rack: a potted life of Bernadette Soubirous, a condemnation of the Russian Revolution. Hilton, John Buxton DISPLACED PERSONThe use of tear gas and rubber bullets was a monstrous overreaction that calls for immediate condemnation. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEHis cold blue eyes were wide open and full of condemnation. S.M. Stirling T2: INFILTRATOR
