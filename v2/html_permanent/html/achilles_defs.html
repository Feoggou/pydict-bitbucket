Achilles

(əˈkɪliːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounGreek mythology Greek hero, the son of Peleus and the sea goddess Thetis: in the Iliad the foremost of the Greek warriors at the siege of Troy. While he was a baby his mother plunged him into the river Styx making his body invulnerable except for the heel by which she held him. After slaying Hector, he was killed by Paris who wounded him in the heel
Derived forms
Achillean (ˌækɪˈliːən ) adjective
