unanimous

(juːˈnænɪməs )
Definitions
adjective1. in complete or absolute agreement2. characterized by complete agreement ⇒ a unanimous decision
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unanimously (uˈnanimously) adverbunanimity (ˌjuːnəˈnɪmɪtɪ ) or unanimousness (uˈnanimousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin ūnanimus from ūnus one + animus mind

unanimous for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(juːnænɪməs )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ that]When a group of people are unanimous, they all agree about something or all vote for the same thing. [+ in] ⇒ Editors were unanimous in their condemnation of the proposals. ⇒ They were unanimous that Chortlesby Manor must be preserved.unanimously adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Today its executive committee voted unanimously to reject the proposals. ⇒ The board of ministers unanimously approved the project last week.2. adjectiveA unanimous vote, decision, or agreement is one in which all the people involved agree. ⇒ ...the unanimous vote for Hungarian membership. ⇒ Their decision was unanimous.
