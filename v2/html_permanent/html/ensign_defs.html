ensign

(ˈɛnsaɪn )
Definitions
noun1. (also ˈɛnsən) a flag flown by a ship, branch of the armed forces, etc, to indicate nationality, allegiance, etc. See also Red Ensign, White Ensign2. any flag, standard, or banner3. a standard-bearer4. a symbol, token, or emblem; sign5. (in the US Navy) a commissioned officer of the lowest rank6. (in the British infantry) a colours bearer7. (formerly in the British infantry) a commissioned officer of the lowest rank
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ensignship (ˈensignˌship) or ensigncy (ˈensigncy) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French enseigne, from Latin insignia
