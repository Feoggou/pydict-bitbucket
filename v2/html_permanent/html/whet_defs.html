whet

(wɛt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: whets, whetting or whetted (transitive)1. to sharpen, as by grinding or friction2. to increase or enhance (the appetite, desire, etc); stimulatenoun3. the act of whetting4. a person or thing that whets
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

whetter (ˈwhetter) noun

Word Origin

Old English hwettan; related to hvæt sharp, Old High German hwezzen, Old Norse hvetja, Gothic hvatjan
