Word forms: distrusts , distrusting , distrusted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you distrust someone or something, you think they are not honest, reliable, or safe. [V n] ⇒ I don't have any particular reason to distrust them.2. variable noun [oft a N]Distrust is the feeling of doubt that you have towards someone or something you distrust. [+ of] ⇒ What he saw there left him with a profound distrust of all political authority. ⇒ ...a decision that should help to dispel much of the atmosphere of distrust.
