excoriate

(ɪkˈskɔːrɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to strip (the skin) from (a person or animal); flay2. medicine to lose (a superficial area of skin), as by scratching, the application of chemicals, etc3. to denounce vehemently; censure severely
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

excoriation (exˌcoriˈation) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin excoriāre to strip, flay, from Latin corium skin, hide
