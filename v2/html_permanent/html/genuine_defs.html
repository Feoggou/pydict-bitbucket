genuine

(ˈdʒɛnjʊɪn )
Definitions
adjective1. not fake or counterfeit; original; real; authentic2. not pretending; frank; sincere3. being of authentic or original stock
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

genuinely (ˈgenuinely) adverbgenuineness (ˈgenuineness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin genuīnus inborn, hence (in Late Latin) authentic, from gignere to produce

genuine

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒenjuɪn ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Genuine is used to describe people and things that are exactly what they appear to be, and are not false or an imitation. ⇒ There was a risk of genuine refugees being returned to Vietnam. ⇒ ...genuine leather. ⇒ They're convinced the picture is genuine.2. graded adjective & adjectiveGenuine refers to things such as emotions that are real and not pretended. ⇒ There was genuine joy in this room. ⇒ If this offer is genuine I will gladly accept it.genuinely graded adverb & adverb ⇒ He was genuinely surprised.genuineness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ He needed at least three days to assess the genuineness of their intentions.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person as genuine, you approve of them because they are honest, truthful, and sincere in the way they live and in their relationships with other people. [approval] ⇒ She is very caring and very genuine.genuineness uncount noun ⇒ I have no doubt about their genuineness.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
