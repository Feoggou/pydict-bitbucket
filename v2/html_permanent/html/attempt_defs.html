attempt

(əˈtɛmpt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to make an effort (to do something) or to achieve (something); try2. to try to surmount (an obstacle)3. to try to climb ⇒ they will attempt the north wall of the Eiger4. archaic to attack5. archaic to temptnoun6. an endeavour to achieve something; effort7. a result of an attempt or endeavour8. an attack, esp with the intention to kill ⇒ an attempt on his life▶ USAGE Attempt should not be used in the passive when followed by an infinitive: attempts were made to find a solution (not a solution was attempted to be found)
Derived forms
atˈtemptable adjectiveatˈtempter noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French attempter, from Latin attemptāre to strive after, from tentāre to try

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you attempt to do something, especially something difficult, you try to do it. [V to-inf] ⇒ The only time that we attempted to do something like that was in the city of Philadelphia. [V n] ⇒ Before I could attempt a reply he added over his shoulder: 'Wait there.'2. countable noun [oft N to-inf]If you make an attempt to do something, you try to do it, often without success. ⇒ ...a deliberate attempt to destabilise the defence. [+ at] ⇒ It was one of his rare attempts at humour. ⇒ ...their involvement in a coup attempt in September.3. countable nounAn attempt on someone's life is an attempt to kill them. [+ on] ⇒ ...an attempt on the life of the former Iranian Prime Minister.4. countable nounIn British English, if a sportsman or sportswoman makes an attempt on a sporting record, they try to beat it. In American English you say that they make an attempt to break it. ⇒ Everything is almost ready for me to make another attempt on the record.
