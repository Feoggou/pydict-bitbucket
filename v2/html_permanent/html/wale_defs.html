wale1

(weɪl )
Definitions
noun1. the raised mark left on the skin after the stroke of a rod or whip2. a. the weave or texture of a fabric, such as the ribs in corduroyb. a vertical row of stitches in knitting. Compare course (sense 14)3. nauticala. a ridge of planking along the rail of a shipb. See gunwaleverb (transitive)4. to raise a wale or wales on by striking5. to weave with a wale
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English walu weal1; related to Old Norse vala knuckle, Dutch wäle
