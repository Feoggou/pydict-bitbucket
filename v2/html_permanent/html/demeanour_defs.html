demeanour

or US demeanor (dɪˈmiːnə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the way a person behaves towards others; conduct2. bearing, appearance, or mien
Word origin
C15: see demean2

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use demeanoruncount noun [usu poss N]Your demeanour is the way you behave, which gives people an impression of your character and feelings. [formal] ⇒ From his general demeanour I didn't get the impression that he was being ironical. ⇒ ...her calm and cheerful demeanour.

Under the ladylike demeanour Kemp sensed the iron of self-esteem. Meek, M R D IN REMEMBRANCE OF ROSEAt the moment, their demeanour was more that of jailers than of suitors.
