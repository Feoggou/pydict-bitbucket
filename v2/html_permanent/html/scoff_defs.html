scoff1

(skɒf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive; often foll by at) to speak contemptuously (about); express derision (for); mock2. (transitive) obsolete to regard with derisionnoun3. an expression of derision4. an object of derision

Derived Forms

scoffer (ˈscoffer) nounscoffing (ˈscoffing) adjectivescoffingly (ˈscoffingly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: probably from Scandinavian; compare Old Frisian skof mockery, Danish skof, skuf jest

scoff

Powered by COBUILD
(skɒf )
Word forms: scoffs , scoffing , scoffed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you scoff at something, you speak about it in a way that shows you think it is ridiculous or inadequate. [V + at] ⇒ At first I scoffed at the notion. [V] ⇒ You may scoff but I honestly feel I'm being cruel only to be kind. [V with quote] ⇒ 'You'll have to do better than that,' Joanna scoffed.2. verbIf you scoff food, you eat it quickly and greedily. [British, informal] gobble [V n] ⇒ The pancakes were so good that I scoffed the lot.
