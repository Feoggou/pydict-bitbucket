deflate

(dɪˈfleɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to collapse or cause to collapse through the release of gas2. (transitive) to take away the self-esteem or conceit from3. economics to cause deflation of (an economy, the money supply, etc)
Derived forms
deflator (deˈflator) noun
Word origin
C19: from de- + (in)flate

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪfleɪt )
Word forms: deflates , deflating , deflated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you deflate someone or something, you take away their confidence or make them seem less important. [V n] ⇒ Like any actor he can be self-centred but I think I've worked out how to deflate him. [V n] ⇒ Britain's other hopes of medals were deflated earlier in the day.deflated graded adjective & adjective ⇒ When she refused I felt deflated.2. ergative verb & verbWhen something such as a tyre or balloon deflates, or when you deflate it, all the air comes out of it. [V] ⇒ When it returns to shore, the life-jacket will deflate and revert to a harness. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a deflated dinghy. [V n] ⇒ We deflate the tyres to make it easier to cross the desert.
