delete

(dɪˈliːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to remove (something printed or written); erase; cancel; strike out
Word origin
C17: from Latin dēlēre to destroy, obliterate

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you delete something that has been written down or stored in a computer, you cross it out or remove it. [V n] ⇒ He also deleted files from the computer system. [V n] ⇒ The word 'exploded' had been deleted.deletion (dɪliːʃən )Word forms: deletions variable noun [+ of] ⇒ This involved the deletion of a great deal of irrelevant material. ⇒ David wanted to make several deletions and additions to the text.
