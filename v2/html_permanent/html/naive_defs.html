naive

, naïve (naɪˈiːv )naïf
Definitions
adjective1. a. having or expressing innocence and credulity; ingenuousb. (as collective noun; preceded by the) ⇒ only the naive believed him2. artless or unsophisticated3. lacking developed powers of analysis, reasoning, or criticism ⇒ a naive argument4. another word for primitive (sense 5)noun5. rare a person who is naive, esp in artistic style. See primitive (sense 10)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

naively (naˈively), naïvely (naˈïvely) or naïfly (naˈïfly) adverbnaiveness (naˈiveness), naïveness (naˈïveness) or naïfness (naˈïfness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from French, feminine of naïf, from Old French naif native, spontaneous, from Latin nātīvus native, from nasci to be born

naive for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(naɪiːv , US nɑː- ) also naïve
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [ADJ to-inf]If you describe someone as naive, you think they lack experience and so expect things to be easy or people to be honest or kind. ⇒ It's naive to think that teachers are always tolerant. ⇒ I must have been naive to think we would get my parents' blessing. ⇒ ...naive idealists. ⇒ Their view was that he had been politically naive.naively graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ ...naively applying Western solutions to Eastern problems. ⇒ I thought, naively, that this would be a nine-to-five job.naivety (naɪiːvɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ I was alarmed by his naivety and ignorance of international affairs.
