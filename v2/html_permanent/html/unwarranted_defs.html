unwarranted

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnwɒrəntɪd , US -wɔːr- ) for learners of English
Definitions
adjectiveIf you describe something as unwarranted, you are critical of it because there is no need or reason for it. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ Any attempt to discuss the issue of human rights was rejected as an unwarranted interference in the country's internal affairs. ⇒ He accused the police of using unwarranted brutality.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
