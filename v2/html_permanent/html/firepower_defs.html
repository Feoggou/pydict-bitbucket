firepower

or fire power (ˈfaɪəpaʊə)
Definitions
noun1. the amount of fire that can be delivered by a particular weapon or military force ⇒ The US also had superior firepower., ⇒ America has enough firepower in the area to mount sustained air strikes.2. the amount of attacking strength that can be brought to bear against an opponent in a contest ⇒ He promoted some younger MPs in the hope of giving his front bench team more firepower., ⇒ They used their commercial strength and legal firepower to overwhelm opponents., ⇒ The Australian team lacks the firepower of its predecessors.
