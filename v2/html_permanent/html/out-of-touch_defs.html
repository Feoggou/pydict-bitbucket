out of touch

(aʊt əv tʌtʃ)
Definitions
adjective1. no longer knowledgeable about or up to date with ⇒ If they really believe this then they must be completely out of touch., ⇒ Washington politicians are out of touch with the American people.2. not or no longer in contact with (someone) ⇒ James wasn't invited. We've been out of touch for years.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]Someone who is out of touch with a situation is not aware of recent changes in it. [+ with] ⇒ Washington politicians are out of touch with the American people. ⇒ If they really believe in this then they must be completely out of touch.2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are out of touch with someone, you have not been in contact with them recently and are not familiar with their present situation. in touch [+ for] ⇒ James wasn't invited. We've been out of touch for years. [Also + with]
