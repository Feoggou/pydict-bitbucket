beak1

(biːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the projecting jaws of a bird, covered with a horny sheath; bill2. any beaklike mouthpart in other animals, such as turtles3. slang a person's nose, esp one that is large, pointed, or hooked4. any projecting part, such as the pouring lip of a bucket5. architecture the upper surface of a cornice, which slopes out to throw off water6. chemistry the part of a still or retort through which vapour passes to the condenser7. nautical another word for ram (sense 5)
Derived forms
beaked (biːkt ) adjectivebeakless (ˈbeakless) adjectivebeakˌlike (ˈbeakˌlike) adjectivebeaky (ˈbeaky) adjective
Word origin
C13: from Old French bec, from Latin beccus, of Gaulish origin
