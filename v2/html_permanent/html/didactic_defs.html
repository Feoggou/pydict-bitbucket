didactic

(dɪˈdæktɪk ) or didactical (dɪˈdæktɪkəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. intended to instruct, esp excessively2. morally instructive; improving3. (of works of art or literature) containing a political or moral message to which aesthetic considerations are subordinated
Derived forms
didactically (diˈdactically) adverbdidacticism (diˈdacticism) noun
Word origin
C17: from Greek didaktikos skilled in teaching, from didaskein to teach

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is didactic is intended to teach people something, especially a moral lesson. [formal] ⇒ In totalitarian societies, art exists for didactic purposes.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is didactic tells people things rather than letting them find things out or discussing things. [formal] ⇒ He is more didactic in his approach to the learning process.
