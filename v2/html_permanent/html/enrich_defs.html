enrich

(ɪnˈrɪtʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to increase the wealth of2. to endow with fine or desirable qualities ⇒ to enrich one's experience by travelling3. to make more beautiful; adorn; decorate ⇒ a robe enriched with jewels4. to improve in quality, colour, flavour, etc5. to increase the food value of by adding nutrients ⇒ to enrich dog biscuits with calcium6. to make (soil) more productive, esp by adding fertilizer7. physics to increase the concentration or abundance of one component or isotope in (a solution or mixture); concentrate ⇒ to enrich a solution by evaporation, ⇒ enrich a nuclear fuel

enrich

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnrɪtʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: enriches , enriching , enriched
Definitions
1. verbTo enrich something means to improve its quality, usually by adding something to it. [V n] ⇒ An extended family enriches life in many ways. [V n] ⇒ It is important to enrich the soil prior to planting.-enriched combining form ⇒ ...nutrient-enriched water.2. verbTo enrich someone means to increase the amount of money that they have. [V n] ⇒ He will drain, rather than enrich, the country. [V n] ⇒ Those who know how to use the system can enrich themselves at the expense of the very people who work for those companies.3. verbTo enrich a nuclear fuel such as uranium means to increase the number of atoms of a particular kind in it, so that it can be used to produce more energy or a greater explosion. [technical] [V n] ⇒ It was actually used for enriching uranium to weapons-grade levels.
