discretionary

(dɪˈskrɛʃənərɪ , -ənrɪ) or discretional
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivehaving or using the ability to decide at one's own discretion ⇒ discretionary powers
Derived forms
discretionarily (disˈcretionarily) or discretionally (disˈcretionally) adverb

discretionary

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪskreʃənri , US -neri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]Discretionary things are not fixed by rules but are decided on by people in authority, who consider each individual case. ⇒ Magistrates were given wider discretionary powers. ⇒ Check whether you are entitled to a discretionary grant for your course.
Example sentences containing 'discretionary'
That's a discretionary procedure, up to the officer in charge, and I just happen to be in charge. Terman, Douglas CORMORANTCecilia's awakening as an activist had crowded out everything she deemed discretionary. Laura Lippman IN A STRANGE CITY
