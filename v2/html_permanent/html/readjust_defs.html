Word forms: readjusts , readjusting , readjusted
Definitions
1. verbWhen you readjust to a new situation, usually one you have been in before, you adapt to it. [V + to] ⇒ I can understand why astronauts find it difficult to readjust to life on Earth. [V] ⇒ They are bound to take time to readjust after a holiday.2. verbIf you readjust the level of something, your attitude to something, or the way you do something, you change it to make it more effective or appropriate. [V n] ⇒ The rebel army has readjusted its strategy. [V n] ⇒ In the end you have to readjust your expectations. [V n] ⇒ We are simply going to readjust her medication and see how things are.3. verbIf you readjust something such as a piece of clothing or a mechanical device, you correct or alter its position or setting. [V n] ⇒ Michael groaned and readjusted his shorts. [V n] ⇒ Readjust your watch. You are now on Moscow time.
