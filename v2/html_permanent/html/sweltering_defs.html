sweltering

(ˈswɛltərɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveoppressively hot and humid ⇒ a sweltering day. Also (archaic): sweltry

Derived Forms

swelteringly (ˈswelteringly) adverb

sweltering

Powered by COBUILD
(sweltərɪŋ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveIf you describe the weather as sweltering, you mean that it is extremely hot and makes you feel uncomfortable.
Example sentences containing 'sweltering'
He tugged a panama hat over his straggly blond hair, and shifted uncomfortably in the sweltering heat. MacNeill, Alastair THE DEVIL'S DOORThe vicar, once more sweltering in his Liberace jacket, clapped his hands.
