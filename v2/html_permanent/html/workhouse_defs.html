workhouse

(ˈwɜːkˌhaʊs )
Definitions
noun1. (formerly in England) an institution maintained at public expense where able-bodied paupers did unpaid work in return for food and accommodation2. (in the US) a prison for petty offenders serving short sentences at manual labour

Word forms: workhouses
Definitions
countable nounIn Britain, in the seventeenth to nineteenth centuries, a workhouse was a place where very poor people could live and do unpleasant jobs in return for food. People use the workhouse to refer to these places in general. ⇒ ...a struggling Shropshire family which lived in fear of the workhouse.
