probable

(ˈprɒbəbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. likely to be or to happen but not necessarily so2. most likely ⇒ the probable cause of the accidentnoun3. a person who is probably to be chosen for a team, event, etc

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin probābilis that may be proved, from probāre to prove

probable

Powered by COBUILD
(prɒbəbəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is probable, you mean that it is likely to be true or likely to happen. [vagueness] ⇒ It is probable that the medication will suppress the symptom without treating the condition. ⇒ She does not want to be taken to an optician with the probable result of having to wear glasses. ⇒ An airline official said a bomb was the incident's most probable cause.2. adjective [ADJ n]You can use probable to describe a role or function that someone or something is likely to have. ⇒ The Socialists united behind their probable presidential candidate, Michel Rocard.
