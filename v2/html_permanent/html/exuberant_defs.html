exuberant

(ɪɡˈzjuːbərənt )
Definitions
adjective1. abounding in vigour and high spirits; full of vitality2. lavish or effusive; excessively elaborate ⇒ exuberant compliments3. growing luxuriantly or in profusion
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exuberance (exˈuberance) nounexuberantly (exˈuberantly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Latin exūberāns, from ūberāre to be fruitful, from ūber fertile

exuberant

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪgzjuːbərənt , US -zuːb- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are exuberant, you are full of energy, excitement, and cheerfulness. effervescent, lively ⇒ ...an exuberant young girl who decided to become a screen actress.exuberantly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ They both laughed exuberantly.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe something as exuberant, you like it because it is lively, exciting, and full of energy and life. [approval] ⇒ This is bold and exuberant cooking.exuberantly graded adverb ⇒ ...exuberantly decorated.
