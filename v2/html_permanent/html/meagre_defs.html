meagre

or US meager (ˈmiːɡə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. deficient in amount, quality, or extent2. thin or emaciated3. lacking in richness or strength

Derived Forms

meagrely (ˈmeagrely) or US meagerly (ˈmeagerly) adverbmeagreness (ˈmeagreness) or US meagerness (ˈmeagerness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French maigre, from Latin macer lean, poor

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use meagergraded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an amount or quantity of something as meagre, you are critical of it because it is very small or not enough. [disapproval] ⇒ The bank's staff were already angered by a meagre 3.1% pay rise. ⇒ Their food supply is meager.
