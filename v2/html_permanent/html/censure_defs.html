censure

(ˈsɛnʃə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. severe disapproval; harsh criticismverb2. to criticize (someone or something) severely; condemn
Derived forms
censurer (ˈcensurer) noun
Word origin
C14: from Latin cēnsūra, from cēnsēre to consider, assess

Word forms: censures , censuring , censured
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you censure someone for something that they have done, you tell them that you strongly disapprove of it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The ethics committee may take a decision to admonish him or to censure him. [V n + for] ⇒ I would not presume to censure Osborne for hating his mother.Censure is also a noun. ⇒ It is a controversial policy which has attracted international censure.
