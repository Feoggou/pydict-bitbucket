stint1

(stɪnt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to be frugal or miserly towards (someone) with (something)2. archaic to stop or check (something)noun3. an allotted or fixed amount of work4. a limitation or check5. obsolete a pause or stoppage

Derived Forms

stinter (ˈstinter) noun

Word Origin

Old English styntan to blunt; related to Old Norse stytta to cut short; see stunt1

stint2

(stɪnt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
nounany of various small sandpipers of the chiefly northern genus Calidris (or Erolia), such as C. minuta (little stint)

Word Origin

Old English; related to Middle High German stinz small salmon, Swedish dialect stinta teenager; see stunt1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [oft adj N]A stint is a period of time which you spend doing a particular job or activity or working in a particular place. ⇒ He is returning to this country after a five-year stint in Hong Kong.
