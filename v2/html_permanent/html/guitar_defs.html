guitar

(ɡɪˈtɑː )
Definitions
nounmusic a plucked stringed instrument originating in Spain, usually having six strings, a flat sounding board with a circular sound hole in the centre, a flat back, and a fretted fingerboard. Range: more than three octaves upwards from E on the first leger line below the bass staff. See also electric guitar, bass guitar, Hawaiian guitar
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

guitarist (guiˈtarist) nounguitar-like (guiˈtar-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Spanish guitarra, from Arabic qītār, from Greek kithara cithara
