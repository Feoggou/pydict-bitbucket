Synonyms of involved

involved

adjective

  1. complicated, complex, intricate, hard, difficult, confused, confusing, sophisticated, elaborate, tangled, bewildering, jumbled, entangled, tortuous, Byzantine, convoluted, knotty, unfathomable, labyrinthine
    • The operation can be quite involved, requiring special procedures.
    OPPOSITES:  easy, simple, straightforward, elementary, simplified, uncomplicated, unsophisticated, easy-peasy (slang)
  2. implicated, taking part, caught up, mixed up, embroiled, entangled, enmeshed, ensnared
    • She became involved with the group soon after the Chernobyl disaster.
  • See involved in something

involve

verb

  1. entail, mean, demand, require, call for, occasion, result in, imply, give rise to, encompass, necessitate, take, require, presuppose
    • Running a kitchen involves a great deal of discipline and speed.
  2. include, contain, take in, embrace, cover, incorporate, draw in, comprise, number among
    • The cover-up involved people at the very highest level.
  3. implicate, tangle, mix up, embroil, link, entangle, incriminate, mire, stitch up (slang), enmesh, inculpate (formal), compromise, complicate, snarl up
    • I seem to have involved myself in something I don't understand.
  4. concern, draw in, associate, connect, bear on
    • He started involving me in the more confidential aspects of the job.
  5. preoccupy, hold, commit, bind, engage, grip, absorb, wrap up, rivet, engross
