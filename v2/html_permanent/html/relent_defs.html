relent

(rɪˈlɛnt )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to change one's mind about some decided course, esp a harsh one; become more mild or amenable2. (of the pace or intensity of something) to slacken3. (of the weather) to become more mild
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from re- + Latin lentāre to bend, from lentus flexible, tenacious

Definitions
1. verbIf you relent, you allow someone to do something that you had previously refused to allow them to do. [V] ⇒ Finally his mother relented and gave permission for her youngest son to marry.2. verbIf bad weather relents, it improves. [V] ⇒ If the weather relents, the game will be finished today.
