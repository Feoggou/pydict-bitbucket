picking

(ˈpɪkɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of picking someone or something ⇒ You can do the picking this time.2. the act of gathering fruit or vegetables from the plant ⇒ The grape picking starts tomorrow., ⇒ Premature picking and over-refrigeration are not the only devices supermarkets employ to create the impression of true freshness, while simultaneously stretching shelf life to its limits., ⇒ But persistent picking of flowers exhausts the plant and prevents the setting of seeds.3. the act of undoing a lock using something other than a key

Nobody else in this building is to know about it,' he said, picking up the file. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSThe mare was restless, no doubt picking up his apprehension.
