satyr

(ˈsætə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Greek mythology one of a class of sylvan deities, represented as goatlike men who drank and danced in the train of Dionysus and chased the nymphs2. a man who has strong sexual desires3. a man who has satyriasis4. any of various butterflies of the genus Satyrus and related genera, having dark wings often marked with eyespots: family Satyridae

Derived Forms

satyric (səˈtɪrɪk ) or satyrical (saˈtyrical) adjectivesatyr-ˌlike (ˈsatyr-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin satyrus, from Greek saturos
