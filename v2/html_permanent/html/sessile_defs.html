sessile

(ˈsɛsaɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of flowers or leaves) having no stalk; growing directly from the stem2. (of animals such as the barnacle) permanently attached to a substratum

Derived Forms

sessility (sɛˈsɪlɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C18: from Latin sēssilis concerning sitting, from sedēre to sit
Example sentences containing 'sessile'
