infuse

(ɪnˈfjuːz )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive; often foll by into) to instil or inculcate2. (transitive; foll by with) to inspire; emotionally charge3. to soak or be soaked in order to extract flavour or other properties4. rare (foll by into) to pour
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin infundere to pour into

infuse

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfjuːz ) for learners of English
Word forms: infuses , infusing , infused
Definitions
1. verbTo infuse a quality into someone or something, or to infuse them with a quality, means to fill them with it. [formal] [be V-ed + with] ⇒ Many of the girls seemed to be infused with excitement on seeing the snow. [V n + into] ⇒ A union would infuse unnecessary conflict into the company's employee relations. [Also V n + with]2. verbIf a quality infuses something, every part of that thing has that quality. [literary] [V n] ⇒ A strange spirit infused the place.3. ergative verbIf you infuse things such as tea leaves or herbs, or allow them to infuse, you put them in hot water for some time so that the water absorbs their flavour. You can also infuse things in other liquids. [V n] ⇒ ...teas made by infusing the roots of herbs. [V n in n] ⇒ Herbalists infuse the flowers in oil. [V] ⇒ Leave the tea to infuse.
