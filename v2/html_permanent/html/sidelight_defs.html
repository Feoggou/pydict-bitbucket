sidelight

(ˈsaɪdˌlaɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. light coming from the side2. a side window3. either of the two navigational running lights used by vessels at night, a red light on the port and a green on the starboard4. British either of two small lights on the front of a motor vehicle, used to indicate the presence of the vehicle at night rather than to assist the driver5. additional or incidental information

Word forms: sidelights
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe sidelights on a vehicle are the small lights at the front that help other drivers to notice the vehicle and to judge its width. [British]regional note:   in AM, usually use parking lights2. countable nounThe sidelights on a vehicle are lights on its sides. [US]3. countable nounA sidelight on a particular situation is a piece of information about that situation which is interesting but which is not particularly important. [+ on] ⇒ The book is full of amusing sidelights on his family background.
