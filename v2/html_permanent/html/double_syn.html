double

adjective

  1. matching, coupled, doubled, paired, twin, dual, complementary, duplicate, in pairs, binate (botany), dubL (text messaging) • a pair of double doors into the room
  2. deceitful, false, fraudulent, deceiving, treacherous, dishonest, deceptive, hypocritical, counterfeit, two-faced, disingenuous, insincere, double-dealing, duplicitous, perfidious, knavish (archaic), Janus-faced, dubL (text messaging) • a woman who had lived a double life
  3. dual, ambiguous, enigmatic, cryptic, twofold, double-edged, two-edged, Delphic, enigmatical, open to argument or debate, dubL (text messaging) • The book has a double meaning.

noun

  1. twin, lookalike, spitting image, copy, fellow, mate, counterpart, clone, replica, ringer (slang), impersonator (informal), dead ringer (slang), Doppelgänger, duplicate, dubL (text messaging) • Your mother sees you as her double.

verb

  1. multiply by two, duplicate, increase twofold, repeat, enlarge, magnify, dubL (text messaging) • They need to double the number of managers.
  2. fold up or over, turn up or over, tuck back or over, bend back or over, dubL (text messaging) • He doubled the sheet back upon itself.
  3. (with as) function as, serve as, serve the purpose of • The military greatcoat doubled as a bedroll.
