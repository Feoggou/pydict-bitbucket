avalanche

(ˈævəˌlɑːntʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a fall of large masses of snow and ice down a mountainb. a fall of rocks, sand, etc2. a sudden or overwhelming appearance of a large quantity of things ⇒ an avalanche of letters3. physics a group of ions or electrons produced by a single ion or electron as a result of a collision with some other form of matterverb4. to come down overwhelmingly (upon)
Word origin
C18: from French, by mistaken division from la valanche, from valanche, from (northwestern Alps) dialect lavantse; related to Old Provençal lavanca, of obscure origin
