want1

(wɒnt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to feel a need or longing for ⇒ I want a new hat2. (when tr, may take a clause as object or an infinitive) to wish, need, or desire (something or to do something) ⇒ he wants to go home3. (intransitive; usually used with a negative and often foll by for) to be lacking or deficient (in something necessary or desirable) ⇒ the child wants for nothing4. (transitive) to feel the absence of ⇒ lying on the ground makes me want my bed5. (transitive) to fall short by (a specified amount)6. (transitive) mainly British to have need of or require (doing or being something) ⇒ your shoes want cleaning7. (intransitive) to be destitute8. (tr; often passive) to seek or request the presence of ⇒ you're wanted upstairs9. (intransitive) to be absent10. (tr; takes an infinitive) informal should or ought (to do something) ⇒ you don't want to go out so late11. See want in12. See want outnoun13. the act or an instance of wanting14. anything that is needed, desired, or lacked ⇒ to supply someone's wants15. a lack, shortage, or absence ⇒ for want of common sense16. the state of being in need; destitution ⇒ the state should help those in want17. a sense of lack; craving

Word Origin

C12 (vb, in the sense: it is lacking), C13 (n): from Old Norse vanta to be deficient; related to Old English wanian to wane

(wɒnt )

Definitions
1. verb [no cont, no passive]If you want something, you feel a desire or a need for it. [V n] ⇒ I want a drink. [V n] ⇒ Ian knows exactly what he wants in life. [V to-inf] ⇒ People wanted to know who this talented designer was. [V n to-inf] ⇒ They began to want their father to be the same as other daddies. [V n v-ing] ⇒ They didn't want people staring at them as they sat on the lawn, so they put up high walls. [V n -ed] ⇒ He wanted his power recognised. [V n n] ⇒ I want my car this colour. [V n adj/prep] ⇒ And remember, we want him alive.2. verb [no cont, no passive]You can say that you want to say something to indicate that you are about to say it. [V to-inf] ⇒ I want to say how really delighted I am that you're having a baby. [V to-inf] ⇒ Look, I wanted to apologize for today. I think I was a little hard on you.3. verb [no cont, no passive]You use want in questions as a way of making an offer or inviting someone to do something. [V n] ⇒ Do you want another cup of coffee? [V to-inf] ⇒ Do you want to leave your bike here?4. verb [no cont, no passive]If you say to someone that you want something, or ask them if they want to do it, you are firmly telling them what you want or what you want them to do. [V n] ⇒ I want an explanation from you, Jeremy. [V n to-inf] ⇒ If you have a problem with that, I want you to tell me right now. [V to-inf] ⇒ Do you want to tell me what all this is about? [V n adv/prep] ⇒ I want my money back!5. verb [no cont, no passive]If you say that something wants doing, you think that it needs to be done. [mainly British, informal] [V -ing] ⇒ The windows wanted cleaning. [V v-ing] ⇒ Her hair wants cutting.6. verb [no cont, no passive]If you tell someone that they want to do a particular thing, you are advising them to do it. [informal] [V to-inf] ⇒ You want to be very careful not to have a man like Crevecoeur for an enemy. [V to-inf] ⇒ You want to look where you're going, mate.7. verb [usu passive]If someone is wanted by the police, the police are searching for them because they are thought to have committed a crime. [be V-ed] ⇒ They were wanted by the police. [be V-ed] ⇒ He has killed many in his time, and is wanted in at least three countries. [be V-ed + for] ⇒ He was wanted for the murder of a magistrate.wanted graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n] ⇒ He is one of the most wanted criminals in Europe.8. verbIf you want someone, you have a great desire to have sex with them. [V n] ⇒ Come on, darling. I want you.9. verbIf a child is wanted, its mother or another person loves it and is willing to look after it. [be V-ed] ⇒ Children should be wanted and planned. [V n] ⇒ I want this baby very much, because it certainly will be the last.10. verb [no cont]If someone wants you in a particular place or role, they desire you to be in that place or role. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ Albie wants you in his office. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ They didn't want her as attorney general. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ This is my territory. I want you out of here.11. singular nounA want of something is a lack of it. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ ...a want of manners and charm. ⇒ The men were daily becoming weaker from want of rest.12. uncount nounWant is the state of being extremely poor. [formal] ⇒ He said they were fighting for freedom of speech, freedom of worship, and freedom from want.13. plural noun [usu with poss]Your wants are the things that you want. ⇒ She couldn't lift a spoon without a servant anticipating her wants and getting it for her. [+ of] ⇒ Supermarkets often claim that they are responding to the wants of consumers by providing packaged foods.14. →  for want of15. →  if you want16. →  I don't want to/without wanting to17. →  what do you want?► →  want out
