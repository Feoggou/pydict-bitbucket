Pict

(pɪkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna member of any of the peoples who lived in Britain north of the Forth and Clyde in the first to the fourth centuries ad: later applied chiefly to the inhabitants of NE Scotland. Throughout Roman times the Picts carried out border raids

Word Origin

Old English Peohtas; later forms from Late Latin Pictī painted men, from pingere to paint
Example sentences containing 'Pict'
The naked girl, stained with gore like an ancient Pict with woad, kneeling by him, cradling his bleeding head. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEAD
