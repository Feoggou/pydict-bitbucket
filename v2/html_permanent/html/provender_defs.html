provender

(ˈprɒvɪndə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any dry feed or fodder for domestic livestock2. food in general

Word Origin

C14: from Old French provendre, from Late Latin praebenda grant, from Latin praebēre to proffer; influenced also by Latin prōvidēre to look after
Example sentences containing 'provender'
She surveyed her purchases anxiously, then bought all the herbs to go with all this provender. Davis, John Gordon SEIZE THE RECKLESS WIND`And you need not turn up your nose at the provender , Master Gimli,' said Merry. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSIndeed we owe you thanks for your assistance and the good provender was most welcomed. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
