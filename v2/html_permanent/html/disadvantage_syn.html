disadvantage

noun

  1. drawback, trouble, burden, weakness, handicap, liability, minus (informal), flaw, hardship, nuisance, snag, inconvenience, downside, impediment, hindrance, privation, weak point, fly in the ointment (informal) • They suffer the disadvantage of having been political exiles. OPPOSITES:  benefit, advantage, merit, convenience
  2. harm, loss, damage, injury, hurt, prejudice, detriment, disservice • An attempt to prevent an election would be to their disadvantage. OPPOSITES:  help, benefit, aid, profit, gain, advantage, convenience

verb

  1. handicap, limit, restrict, hamstring, hamper, hold back, hinder, retard, impede, hobble, place at a disadvantage • Competition could reduce liquidity and disadvantage some investors.
  • See at a disadvantage
