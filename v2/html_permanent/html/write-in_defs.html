write in

Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to insert in (a document, form, etc) in writing2. (adverb) to write a letter to a company, institution, etc3. (adverb) USa. to vote for (a person not on a ballot) by writing in his nameb. to cast (a vote) for such a person by writing in his namenoun write-in US4. the act of voting for a person by writing his or her name on a ballot5. a candidate or vote that has been written in6. (as modifier) ⇒ a write-in campaign
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

write-in

(ˈraɪtˌɪn)
Definitions
noun US politics1. a vote cast for an unlisted candidate by writing his or her name on a ballot paper ⇒ When Republican write-ins were included, Johnson's margin of victory was only 330 votes., ⇒ The state does not permit write-in votes.2. an unlisted candidate whose name someone writes on a ballot paper ⇒ Four years ago, he was a write-in in New Hampshire.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you write in to an organization, you send them a letter. [V P] ⇒ What's the point in writing in when you only print half the letter anyway? [V P n] ⇒ I think I'll write in Pat Wilson. [V n P] ⇒ I'm going to write him in on my ballot next year.3. → See also write-in →  write
