din1

(dɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a loud discordant confused noiseverbWord forms: dins, dinning or dinned2. (transitive; usually foll by into) to instil (into a person) by constant repetition3. (transitive) to subject to a din4. (intransitive) to make a din
Word origin
Old English dynn; compare Old Norse dynr, Old High German tuni

(dɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a formerly used logarithmic expression of the speed of a photographic film, plate, etc, given as –10log10E, where E is the exposure of a point 0.1 density units above the fog level; high-speed films have high numbers. Compare ISO rating2. a system of standard plugs, sockets, and cables formerly used for interconnecting domestic audio and video equipment
Word origin
C20: from German D(eutsche) I(ndustrie) N(orm) German Industry Standard
