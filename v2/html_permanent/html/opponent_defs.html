opponent

(əˈpəʊnənt )
Definitions
noun1. a person who opposes another in a contest, battle, etc2. anatomy an opponent muscleadjective3. opposite, as in position4. anatomy (of a muscle) bringing two parts into opposition5. opposing; contrary
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

opponency (opˈponency) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin oppōnere to oppose, from ob- against + pōnere to place

opponent for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(əpoʊnənt )
Word forms: opponents
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu with poss]A politician's opponents are other politicians who belong to a different party or who have different aims or policies. [+ in] ⇒ ...Mr Kennedy's opponent in the leadership contest. ⇒ He described the detention without trial of political opponents as a cowardly act.2. countable noun [usu poss N]In a sporting contest, your opponent is the person who is playing against you. ⇒ Norris twice knocked down his opponent in the early rounds of the fight. ⇒ He's the best opponent I've come across this season, a great player.3. countable nounThe opponents of an idea or policy do not agree with it and do not want it to be carried out. [+ of] ⇒ ...opponents of the spread of nuclear weapons. ⇒ He became an outspoken opponent of the old Soviet system.
