incongruous

(ɪnˈkɒŋɡrʊəs ) or incongruent
Definitions
adjective1. (when postpositive, foll by with or to) incompatible with (what is suitable); inappropriate2. containing disparate or discordant elements or parts
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incongruously (inˈcongruously) or incongruently (inˈcongruently) adverbincongruousness (inˈcongruousness) or incongruence (inˈcongruence) noun

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is incongruous seems strange when considered together with other aspects of a situation. [formal] ⇒ She was small and fragile and looked incongruous in an army uniform. ⇒ The Indian temple is an incongruous sight in the Welsh border country.incongruously graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ ...buildings perched incongruously in a high green valley.
