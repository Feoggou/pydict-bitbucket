commute

(kəˈmjuːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to travel some distance regularly between one's home and one's place of work2. (transitive) to substitute; exchange3. (transitive) law to reduce (a sentence) to one less severe4. to pay (an annuity) at one time, esp with a discount, instead of in instalments5. (transitive) to transform; change ⇒ to commute base metal into gold6. (intransitive) to act as or be a substitute7. (intransitive) to make a substitution; changenoun8. a journey made by commuting
Derived forms
commutable (comˈmutable) adjectivecomˌmutability (comˌmutaˈbility) or commutableness (comˈmutableness) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin commutāre to replace, from com- mutually + mutāre to change

[V + between] ⇒ McLaren began commuting between Paris and London. [V] ⇒ He's going to commute.commuter Word forms: commuters countable noun ⇒ The number of commuters to London has dropped by 100,000. ⇒ ...a commuter train.2. countable nounA commute is the journey that you make when you commute. [mainly US] ⇒ The average Los Angeles commute is over 60 miles a day.3. verb [usu passive]If a death sentence or prison sentence is commuted to a less serious punishment, it is changed to that punishment. [be V-ed + to] ⇒ His death sentence was commuted to life imprisonment. [be V-ed] ⇒ Prison sentences have been commuted.
