emetic

(ɪˈmɛtɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. causing vomitingnoun2. an emetic agent or drug
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

emetically (eˈmetically) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin ēmeticus, from Greek emetikos, from emein to vomit

emetic

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmɛtɪk) in the Pharmaceutical Industry
Word forms: (regular plural) emetics
Definitions
noun(Pharmaceutical: Drugs) An emetic is a substance that is given to someone in order to make them vomit.
SIMILAR WORDS: 
vomitive, vomitory ⇒ A direct emetic irritates the stomach and causes vomiting. ⇒ Berry poisoning can sometimes be avoided if a strong emetic is administered immediately to induce vomiting. ⇒ An emetic is a substance that is given to someone in order to make them vomit.
