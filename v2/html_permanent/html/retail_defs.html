retail

(ˈriːteɪl )
Definitions
noun1. the sale of goods individually or in small quantities to consumers. Compare wholesale (sense 1)adjective2. of, relating to, or engaged in such selling ⇒ retail pricesadverb3. in small amounts or at a retail priceverb4. to sell or be sold in small quantities to consumers5. (rɪˈteɪl) (transitive) to relate (gossip, scandal, etc) in detail, esp persistently
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

retailer (ˈretailer) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French retaillier to cut off, from re- + taillier to cut; see tailor

retail

Powered by COBUILD
(riːteɪl ) for learners of English
Word forms: retails , retailing , retailed
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The pronunciation is (riːteɪl) for meanings [sense 1] to [sense 3], and (riːteɪl) for meaning [sense 4].1. uncount noun [usu N n]Retail is the activity of selling goods direct to the public, usually in small quantities. Compare wholesale. [business] ⇒ ...retail stores. ⇒ Retail sales grew just 3.8 percent last year.2. adverb [ADV after v]If something is sold retail, it is sold in ordinary shops direct to the public. [business]3. verbIf an item in a shop retails at or for a particular price, it is on sale at that price. [business] [V + at/for] ⇒ It originally retailed at £23.50.4. verbIf someone retails a story or event, they tell it to someone else, often in detail and in an exciting way. [literary] recount [V n] ⇒ Mr Hastings gleefully retailed the story to Mr Anderson over lunch.5. → See also retailing
