stronghold

(ˈstrɒŋˌhəʊld )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a defensible place; fortress2. a major centre or area of predominance

Word Origin

C15: from strong + hold1 (in the archaic sense: a fortified place)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf you say that a place or region is a stronghold of a particular attitude or belief, you mean that most people there share this attitude or belief. ⇒ The western-most part of north Wales is a stronghold of Welsh-speakers. ⇒ The seat was a stronghold of the Labour party.2. countable noun [with poss]If you say that a place is a stronghold of a particular type of animal, you mean that more of that type of animal live there than in other places. ⇒ Shetland is the last stronghold of otters in the British Isles.
