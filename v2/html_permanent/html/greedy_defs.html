greedy

(ˈɡriːdɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: greedier or greediest1. excessively desirous of food or wealth, esp in large amounts; voracious2. (postpositive; foll by for) eager (for) ⇒ a man greedy for success
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

greedily (ˈgreedily) adverbgreediness (ˈgreediness) noun

Word Origin

Old English grǣdig; related to Old Norse grāthugr, Gothic grēdags hungry, Old High German grātac

greedy

Powered by COBUILD
(griːdi ) for learners of English

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as greedy, you mean that they want to have more of something such as food or money than is necessary or fair. ⇒ He attacked greedy bosses for awarding themselves big rises. ⇒ She is greedy and selfish.greedily graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Livy ate the pasties greedily and with huge enjoyment.
