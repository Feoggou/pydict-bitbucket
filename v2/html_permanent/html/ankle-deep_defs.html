ankle-deep

(ˌæŋkəlˈdiːp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. tall or deep enough to cover the ankles ⇒ ankle-deep mud, ⇒ ankle-deep grass2. standing in water, mud, etc that is tall or deep enough to cover the ankles ⇒ He was ankle-deep in water.
Example sentences containing 'ankle-deep'
In places the ground was boggy, and several times I sank ankle-deep. Anita Anderson SUMMER OF SECRETSThey turned to see her standing before one of the massive doors, ankle-deep in the dust drift that lay before it. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSGimli strode forward undeterred, and found that the water was shallow, no more than ankle-deep at the edge. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGS
