lucre

(ˈluːkə )
Definitions
nounusually facetious money or wealth (esp in the phrase filthy lucre)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin lūcrum gain; related to Old English lēan reward, German Lohn wages

lucre

Powered by COBUILD
(luːkəʳ ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounPeople sometimes refer to money or profit as lucre, especially when they think that it has been obtained by dishonest means. [humorous, or old-fashioned, disapproval] ⇒ ...so they can feel less guilty about their piles of filthy lucre.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
