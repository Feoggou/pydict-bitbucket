persuasive

(pəˈsweɪsɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivehaving the power or ability to persuade; tending to persuade ⇒ a persuasive salesman. Also (less common): persuasory

Derived Forms

persuasively (perˈsuasively) adverbpersuasiveness (perˈsuasiveness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone or something that is persuasive is likely to persuade a person to believe or do a particular thing. ⇒ What do you think were some of the more persuasive arguments on the other side? ⇒ I can be very persuasive when I want to be. ⇒ Michael Grade's persuasive powers overcame their objections.persuasively graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...a trained lawyer who can present arguments persuasively.persuasiveness uncount noun ⇒ He has the personality and the persuasiveness to make you change your mind.
