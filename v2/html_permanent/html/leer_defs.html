leer

(lɪə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to give an oblique, sneering, or suggestive look or grinnoun2. such a look
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

leering (ˈleering) adjective, nounleeringly (ˈleeringly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: perhaps verbal use of obsolete leer cheek, from Old English hlēor

Definitions
verbIf someone leers at you, they smile in an unpleasant way, usually because they are sexually interested in you. [disapproval] [V prep/adv] ⇒ Men were standing around, swilling beer and occasionally leering at passing females.
