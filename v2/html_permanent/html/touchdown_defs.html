touchdown

(ˈtʌtʃˌdaʊn )
Definitions
noun1. the moment at which a landing aircraft or spacecraft comes into contact with the landing surface2. rugby the act of placing or touching the ball on the ground behind the goal line, as in scoring a try3. American football a scoring play worth six points, achieved by being in possession of the ball in the opposing team's end zone. Abbreviation: TD See also field goal (sense 2)verb touch down (intr, adverb)4. (of a space vehicle, aircraft, etc) to land5. rugby to place the ball behind the goal line, as when scoring a try6. informal to pause during a busy schedule in order to catch up, reorganize, or rest
