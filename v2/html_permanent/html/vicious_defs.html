vicious

(ˈvɪʃəs )
Definitions
adjective1. wicked or cruel; villainous ⇒ a vicious thug2. characterized by violence or ferocity ⇒ a vicious blow3. informal unpleasantly severe; harsh ⇒ a vicious wind4. characterized by malice ⇒ vicious lies5. (esp of dogs, horses, etc) ferocious or hostile; dangerous6. characterized by or leading to vice7. invalidated by defects; unsound ⇒ a vicious inference8. obsolete noxious or morbid ⇒ a vicious exhalation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

viciously (ˈviciously) adverbviciousness (ˈviciousness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French vicieus, from Latin vitiōsus full of faults, from vitium a defect

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA vicious person or a vicious blow is violent and cruel. ⇒ He was a cruel and vicious man. ⇒ He suffered a vicious attack by a gang of white youths. ⇒ The blow was so sudden and vicious that he dropped to his knees.viciously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ She had been viciously attacked with a hammer.viciousness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the intensity and viciousness of these attacks.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA vicious remark is cruel and intended to upset someone. ⇒ It is a deliberate, nasty and vicious attack on a young man's character. ⇒ ...her shrewish temperament and vicious tongue.viciously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'He deserved to die,' said Penelope viciously.
