tense1

(tɛns )
Definitions
adjective1. stretched or stressed tightly; taut or rigid2. under mental or emotional strain3. producing mental or emotional strain ⇒ a tense day4. (of a speech sound) pronounced with considerable muscular effort and having relatively precise accuracy of articulation and considerable duration ⇒ in English the vowel (iː ) in 'beam' is tense. Compare lax (sense 4)verb5. (often foll by up) to make or become tense
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tensely (ˈtensely) adverbtenseness (ˈtenseness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin tensus taut, from tendere to stretch

tense2

(tɛns )
Definitions
noungrammar a category of the verb or verbal inflections, such as present, past, and future, that expresses the temporal relations between what is reported in a sentence and the time of its utterance

tense

Powered by COBUILD
(tens ) for learners of English
Word forms: tenser , tensest , tenses , tensing , tensed
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA tense situation or period of time is one that makes people anxious, because they do not know what is going to happen next. ⇒ This gesture of goodwill did little to improve the tense atmosphere at the talks. ⇒ After three very tense weeks he phoned again. ⇒ There was a tense silence.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are tense, you are anxious and nervous and cannot relax. ⇒ Dart, who had at first been very tense, at last relaxed.tensely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ She waited tensely for the next bulletin. ⇒ 'Tony, I can explain everything,' she said tensely.tenseness uncount noun ⇒ McKay walked slowly toward this screen, feeling a growing tenseness.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf your body is tense, your muscles are tight and not relaxed. ⇒ She lay, eyes shut, body tense. ⇒ A bath can relax tense muscles.tenseness uncount noun ⇒ If you feel a tenseness around the eyes, relax your muscles.4. ergative verb & verbIf your muscles tense, if you tense, or if you tense your muscles, your muscles become tight and stiff, often because you are anxious or frightened. [V] ⇒ Newman's stomach muscles tensed. [V] ⇒ He tensed as the big West Indian gripped his shoulder. [V n] ⇒ Jane tensed her muscles to stop them from shaking.Tense up means the same as tense. [V P] ⇒ When we are under stress our bodies tend to tense up. [V P] ⇒ I tried not to tense up, or become obviously wary. [V P n] ⇒ Tense up the muscles in both of your legs. [Also V n P]5. countable nounThe tense of a verb group is its form, which usually shows whether you are referring to past, present, or future time. ⇒ It was as though Corinne was already dead: they were speaking of her in the past tense.► →  tense up
