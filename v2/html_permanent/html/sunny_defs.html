sunny

(ˈsʌnɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -nier or -niest1. full of or exposed to sunlight2. radiating good humour3. of or resembling the sun

Derived Forms

sunnily (ˈsunnily) adverbsunniness (ˈsunniness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveWhen it is sunny, the sun is shining brightly. ⇒ The weather was surprisingly warm and sunny. ⇒ There is a chance of sunny spells in the West.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSunny places are brightly lit by the sun. sunlit ⇒ Most roses like a sunny position in a fairly fertile soil. ⇒ ...a sunny terrace.3. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who has a sunny disposition is usually cheerful and happy. ⇒ He was a nice lad–bright and with a sunny disposition. ⇒ The staff wear big sunny smiles.4. →  sunny side up
