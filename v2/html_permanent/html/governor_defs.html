governor

(ˈɡʌvənə )
Definitions
noun1. a person who governs2. the ruler or chief magistrate of a colony, province, etc3. the representative of the Crown in a British colony4. British the senior administrator or head of a society, prison, etc5. the chief executive of any state in the US6. a device that controls the speed of an engine, esp by regulating the supply of fuel, etc, either to limit the maximum speed or to maintain a constant speed7. Also called: head grammara. a word in a phrase or clause that is the principal item and gives the function of the whole, as hat in the big red hatb. (as modifier) ⇒ a governor noun8. British informal a name or title of respect for a father, employer, etcgubernatorial

governor

(gʌvərnər) in Automotive Engineering
Word forms: (regular plural) governors
Definitions
noun(Automotive engineering: Vehicle components, Engine, transmission, and exhaust) A governor is a device which limits the maximum speed of an engine, especially a diesel engine.
COLLOCATIONS: 
speed ~ ⇒ A lot of pickup trucks have a governor on the speed limit. ⇒ An idling speed governor is often fitted to ensure that the idling rpm does not vary with changing engine loads. ⇒ The governor provides automatic control of engine speed at idle and regulates the engine at high speeds.
