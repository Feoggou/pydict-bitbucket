deform

(dɪˈfɔːm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to make or become misshapen or distorted2. (transitive) to mar the beauty of; disfigure3. to subject or be subjected to a stress that causes a change of dimensions
Derived forms
deformable (deˈformable) adjectivedeˌformability (deˌformaˈbility) noundeformer (deˈformer) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin dēformāre, from de- + forma shape, beauty

ergative verb & verbIf something deforms a person's body or something else, it causes it to have an unnatural shape. In technical English, you can also say that the second thing deforms. [V n] ⇒ Bad rheumatoid arthritis deforms limbs. [V] ⇒ ...the ability of a metal to deform to a new shape without cracking.deformed graded adjective & adjective malformed ⇒ He was born with a deformed right leg.deformation (diːfɔːʳmeɪʃən )Word forms: deformations variable noun ⇒ Changing stresses bring about more cracking and rock deformation.
