truce

(truːs )
Definitions
noun1. an agreement to stop fighting, esp temporarily2. temporary cessation of something unpleasant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from the plural of Old English treow trow; see true, trust

truce

Powered by COBUILD
(truːs ) for learners of English
Word forms: truces
Definitions
countable nounA truce is an agreement between two people or groups of people to stop fighting or quarrelling for a short time. [+ between] ⇒ The fighting of recent days has given way to an uneasy truce between the two sides. ⇒ Let's call a truce.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
