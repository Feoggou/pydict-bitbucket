multitude

(ˈmʌltɪˌtjuːd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large gathering of people2. See the multitude3. a large number4. the state or quality of being numerous

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin multitūdō

multitude

Powered by COBUILD
(mʌltɪtjuːd , US -tuːd )
Word forms: multitudes
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. quantifierA multitude of things or people is a very large number of them. [+ of] ⇒ There are a multitude of small quiet roads to cycle along. [+ of] ⇒ Addiction to drugs can bring a multitude of other problems.→  a multitude of sins2. countable nounYou can refer to a very large number of people as a multitude. [written] ⇒ ...surrounded by a noisy multitude. ⇒ ...the multitudes that throng around the Pope.3. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]You can refer to the great majority of people in a particular country or situation as the multitude or the multitudes. ⇒ The hideous truth was hidden from the multitude. ⇒ It is our task to convince the multitudes that we are pursuing a lawful hobby in a lawful way.
Example sentences containing 'multitude'
'Say,' Rae said suddenly, her memory jogged by the picture of a gathered multitude. Laurie R. King FOLLY
