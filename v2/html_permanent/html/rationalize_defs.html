rationalize

or rationalise (ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz )
Definitions
verb1. to justify (one's actions, esp discreditable actions, or beliefs) with plausible reasons, esp after the event2. psychology to indulge, often unchallenged, in excuses for or explanations of (behaviour about which one feels uncomfortable or guilty)3. to apply logic or reason to (something)4. to eliminate unnecessary equipment, personnel, or processes from (a group of businesses, factory, etc), in order to make it more efficient5. (transitive) mathematics to eliminate one or more radicals without changing the value of (an expression) or the roots of (an equation)

Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, also use rationalise1. verbIf you try to rationalize attitudes or actions that are difficult to accept, you think of reasons to justify or explain them. [V n] ⇒ He further rationalized his activity by convincing himself that he was actually promoting peace. [V n] ⇒ I poured my thoughts out on paper in an attempt to rationalize my feelings.rationalization (ræʃənəlaɪzeɪʃən )Word forms: rationalizations variable noun ⇒ ...this rationalization of his bedside grief.2. verb [usu passive]When a company, system, or industry is rationalized, it is made more efficient, usually by getting rid of staff and equipment that are not essential. [mainly British, business] [be V-ed] ⇒ The network of 366 local offices is being rationalised to leave the company with 150 to 200 larger branch offices.rationalization uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the rationalization of the textile industry.
