incompetent

(ɪnˈkɒmpɪtənt )
Definitions
adjective1. not possessing the necessary ability, skill, etc to do or carry out a task; incapable2. marked by lack of ability, skill, etc3. law not legally qualified ⇒ an incompetent witness4. (of rock strata, folds, etc) yielding readily to pressure so as to undergo structural deformationnoun5. an incompetent person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incompetence (inˈcompetence) or incompetency (inˈcompetency) nounincompetently (inˈcompetently) adverb

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as incompetent, you are criticizing them because they are unable to do their job or a task properly. [disapproval] ⇒ The court declared him incompetent to manage his financial affairs. ⇒ I was incompetent at playing the piano. ⇒ He wants the power to sack incompetent teachers.An incompetent is someone who is incompetent. ⇒ I'm surrounded by incompetents!
