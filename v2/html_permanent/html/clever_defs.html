clever

(ˈklɛvə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. displaying sharp intelligence or mental alertness2. adroit or dexterous, esp with the hands3. smart in a superficial way4. British informal sly; cunning5. (predicative; used with a negative) dialect healthy; fit
Derived forms
cleverish (ˈcleverish) adjectivecleverly (ˈcleverly) adverbcleverness (ˈcleverness) noun
Word origin
C13 cliver (in the sense: quick to seize, adroit), of uncertain origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is clever is intelligent and able to understand things easily or plan things well. ⇒ He's a very clever man. ⇒ My sister was always a lot cleverer than I was. ⇒ Her mother was clever at many things.cleverly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ She would cleverly pick up on what I said.cleverness uncount noun ⇒ Her cleverness seems to get in the way of her emotions.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A clever idea, book, or invention is extremely effective and shows the skill of the people involved. ⇒ ...a clever and gripping novel. ⇒ A colleague of mine in Milan devised the following very clever little experiment. ⇒ ...this clever new gadget.cleverly graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed] ⇒ ...a cleverly-designed swimsuit.3. →  too clever by half
