Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf you describe someone as middle-aged, you mean that they are neither young nor old. People between the ages of 40 and 60 are usually considered to be middle-aged. ⇒ His sisters are grown up and his parents are middle-aged. ⇒ ...middle-aged, married businessmen.The middle-aged are people who are middle-aged. ⇒ ...the perfect holiday for the middle-aged.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's activities or interests as middle-aged, you are critical of them because you think they are typical of a middle-aged person, for example by being conventional or old-fashioned. [disapproval] ⇒ Her novels are middle-aged and boring.
