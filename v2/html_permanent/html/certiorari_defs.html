certiorari

(ˌsɜːtɪɔːˈrɛəraɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounlaw an order of a superior court directing that a record of proceedings in a lower court be sent up for review. See also mandamus, prohibition
Word origin
C15: from legal Latin: to be informed
Example sentences containing 'certiorari'
Stare decisis, amicus curiae, writs of certiorari , all that stuff. Tapply, William G THE DUTCH BLUE ERROR
