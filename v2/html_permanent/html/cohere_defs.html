cohere

(kəʊˈhɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to hold or stick firmly together2. to be connected logically; be consistent3. physics to be held together by the action of molecular forces
Word origin
C16: from Latin cohaerēre from co- together + haerēre to cling, adhere

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf the different elements of a piece of writing, a piece of music, or a set of ideas cohere, they fit together well so that they form a united whole. [V] ⇒ The various elements of the novel fail to cohere. [V + with] ⇒ This coheres with Peel's championing of alternative music. [V] ⇒ The empire could not cohere as a legitimate whole.
