copy

noun

  1. reproduction, duplicate, photocopy, carbon copy, image, print, fax, representation, fake, replica, imitation, forgery, counterfeit, Xerox®, transcription, likeness, replication, facsimile, Photostat® • Always keep a copy of everything in your own files. OPPOSITES:  model, original, pattern, prototype, the real thing

verb

  1. reproduce, replicate, duplicate, photocopy, transcribe, counterfeit, Xerox®, Photostat® • She never participated in copying classified documents for anyone. OPPOSITES:  create, originate
  2. imitate, act like, emulate, behave like, follow, repeat, mirror, echo, parrot, ape, mimic, simulate, follow suit, follow the example of • We all tend to copy people we admire. • coquettish gestures which she had copied from actresses in soap operas
