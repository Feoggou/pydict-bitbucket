caul

(kɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun anatomy1. a portion of the amniotic sac sometimes covering a child's head at birth2. a large fold of peritoneum hanging from the stomach across the intestines; the large omentum
Word origin
C13: from Old French cale, back formation from calotte close-fitting cap, of Germanic origin
