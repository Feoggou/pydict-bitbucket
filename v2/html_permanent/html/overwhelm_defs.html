overwhelm

(ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to overpower the thoughts, emotions, or senses of2. to overcome with irresistible force3. to overcome, as with a profusion or concentration of something4. to cover over or bury completely5. to weigh or rest upon overpoweringly6. archaic to overturn

overwhelm for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊvəʳhwelm )
Word forms: overwhelms , overwhelming , overwhelmed
Definitions
1. verbIf you are overwhelmed by a feeling or event, it affects you very strongly, and you do not know how to deal with it. [be V-ed] ⇒ He was overwhelmed by a longing for times past. [V n] ⇒ The need to talk to someone, anyone, overwhelmed her.overwhelmed graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ] [+ by] ⇒ Sightseers may be a little overwhelmed by the crowds and noise.2. verbIf a group of people overwhelm a place or another group, they gain complete control or victory over them. [V n] ⇒ It was clear that one massive Allied offensive would overwhelm the weakened enemy.
