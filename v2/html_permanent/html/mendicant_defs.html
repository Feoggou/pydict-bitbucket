mendicant

(ˈmɛndɪkənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. begging2. (of a member of a religious order) dependent on alms for sustenance ⇒ mendicant friars3. characteristic of a beggarnoun4. a mendicant friar5. a less common word for beggar

Derived Forms

mendicancy (ˈmendicancy) or mendicity (mɛnˈdɪsɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin mendīcāre to beg, from mendīcus beggar, from mendus flaw
Example sentences containing 'mendicant'
It was something that a mendicant fakir might wear, a wandering beggar who told fortunes in the marketplace for a couple of crowns. Robert Silverberg LORD PRESTIMION

Similar Words
