tunnel

(ˈtʌnəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an underground passageway, esp one for trains or cars that passes under a mountain, river, or a congested urban area2. any passage or channel through or under something3. a dialect word for funnel4. obsolete the flue of a chimneyverb -nels, -nelling, -nelled or US -nels, -neling or -neled5. (transitive) to make or force (a way) through or under (something) ⇒ to tunnel a hole in the wall, ⇒ to tunnel the cliff6. (intr; foll by through, under, etc) to make or force a way (through or under something) ⇒ he tunnelled through the bracken

Derived Forms

tunneller (ˈtunneller) or US tunneler (ˈtunneler) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French tonel cask, from tonne tun, from Medieval Latin tonna barrel, of Celtic origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use tunneling, tunneled1. countable nounA tunnel is a long passage which has been made under the ground, usually through a hill or under the sea. [+ through] ⇒ ...two new railway tunnels through the Alps. ⇒ ...the motorway tunnels under the Hudson river.2. verbTo tunnel somewhere means to make a tunnel there. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The rebels tunnelled out of a maximum security jail. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The caterpillars tunnel into the fruit to grow and mature. [Also V]3. → see also wind tunnel4. → light at the end of the tunnel
