one on one

or one-on-one (wʌn ɒn wʌn)
Definitions
adverb1. one to one ⇒ Talking one-on-one with people is not his idea of fun., ⇒ I was left one on one with the Major., ⇒ therapists who work one-on-one with patientsadjective2. denoting a relationship or encounter in which someone is involved with only one other person ⇒ It would be a one-on-one interview., ⇒ a one-on-one therapy session, ⇒ I'm going to take him for some one-on-one coaching.
