harmless

(ˈhɑːmlɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. not causing any physical or mental damage or injury2. unlikely to annoy or worry people ⇒ a harmless sort of man
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

harmlessly (ˈharmlessly) adverbharmlessness (ˈharmlessness) noun

harmless

Powered by COBUILD
(hɑːʳmləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is harmless does not have any bad effects, especially on people's health. ⇒ Industry has been working at developing harmless substitutes for these gases. ⇒ This experiment was harmless to the animals.harmlessly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Another missile exploded harmlessly outside the town.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as harmless, you mean that they are not important and therefore unlikely to annoy other people or cause trouble. ⇒ He seemed harmless enough. ⇒ I would not want to deny them a harmless pleasure.harmlessly adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ It started harmlessly enough, with a statement from the Secretary of State for Social Security.
