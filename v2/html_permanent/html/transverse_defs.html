transverse

(trænzˈvɜːs )
Definitions
adjective1. crossing from side to side; athwart; crossways2. geometry denoting the axis that passes through the foci of a hyperbola3. (of a flute, etc) held almost at right angles to the player's mouth, so that the breath passes over a hole in the side to create a vibrating air column within the tube of the instrument4. astronomy another word for tangential (sense 2)noun5. a transverse piece or object
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

transversely (transˈversely) adverbtransverseness (transˈverseness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin transversus, from transvertere to turn across, from trans- + vertere to turn
