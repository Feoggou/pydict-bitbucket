prevaricate

(prɪˈværɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(intransitive) to speak or act falsely or evasively with intent to deceive

Derived Forms

preˌvarication (preˌvariˈcation) nounprevariˌcator (preˈvariˌcator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin praevāricārī to walk crookedly, from prae beyond + vāricare to straddle the legs; compare Latin vārus bent
