recourse

(rɪˈkɔːs )
Definitions
noun1. the act of resorting to a person, course of action, etc, in difficulty or danger (esp in the phrase have recourse to)2. a person, organization, or course of action that is turned to for help, protection, etc3. the right to demand payment, esp from the drawer or endorser of a bill of exchange or other negotiable instrument when the person accepting it fails to pay4. See without recourse
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French recours, from Late Latin recursus a running back, from re- + currere to run

(rɪkɔːʳs ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounIf you achieve something without recourse to a particular course of action, you succeed without carrying out that action. To have recourse to a particular course of action means to have to do that action in order to achieve something. [formal] [+ to] ⇒ It enabled its members to settle their differences without recourse to war. ⇒ The public believes its only recourse is to take to the streets.
