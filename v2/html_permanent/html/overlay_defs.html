overlay

Definitions
verb (ˌəʊvəˈleɪ ) -lays, -laying or -laid (transitive)1. to lay or place something over or upon (something else)2. (often foll by with) to cover, overspread, or conceal (with)3. (foll by with) to cover (a surface) with an applied decoration ⇒ ebony overlaid with silver4. to achieve the correct printing pressure all over (a forme or plate) by adding to the appropriate areas of the packingnoun (ˈəʊvəˌleɪ )5. something that is laid over something else; covering6. an applied decoration or layer, as of gold leaf7. a transparent sheet giving extra details to a map or diagram over which it is designed to be placed8. printing material, such as paper, used to overlay a forme or plate

overlay for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊvəʳleɪ )
Word forms: overlays , overlaying , overlaid
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (oʊvəʳleɪ). The noun is pronounced (oʊvəʳleɪ).1. verb [usu passive]If something is overlaid with something else, it is covered by it. [be V-ed + with] ⇒ The floor was overlaid with rugs of oriental design. [V-ed] ⇒ ...woollen cloth, overlaid with gold and silver embroidery.2. variable noun [usu with supp]You can use overlay to refer to a substance which covers the surface of something. ⇒ Silver overlay is bonded to the entire surface. ⇒ ...an overlay of snow on the tops of the iron fences.3. verbIf something is overlaid with a feeling or quality, that feeling or quality is the most noticeable one, but there may be deeper and more important ones involved. [written] [be V-ed + with] ⇒ The party had been overlaid with a certain nervousness. [V n] ⇒ ...a surge of feeling which at this moment overlaid all others.Overlay is also a noun. ⇒ There can be an emotional overlay to the frustration of solving real problems. ⇒ ...the thin overlay of success.
