fearful

(ˈfɪəfʊl )
Definitions
adjective1. having fear; afraid2. causing fear; frightening3. informal very unpleasant or annoying ⇒ a fearful cold

fearfulness (ˈfearfulness) noun

fearful

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪəʳfʊl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ that]If you are fearful of something, you are afraid of it. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Bankers were fearful of a world banking crisis. ⇒ I had often been very fearful, very angry, and very isolated.fearfully adverb [usu ADV after v, also ADV with cl] ⇒ 'What are you going to do to me?' Alex asked fearfully.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]You use fearful to emphasize how serious or bad a situation is. [formal, emphasis] ⇒ The region is in a fearful recession. ⇒ ...the fearful consequences which might flow from unilateral military moves.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Fearful is used to emphasize that something is very bad. [informal, old-fashioned, emphasis] ⇒ You gave me a fearful shock! ⇒ 'It sounds the most fearful hard work,' Sybil said later.fearfully adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ This is fearfully expensive compared with the last one I bought.
