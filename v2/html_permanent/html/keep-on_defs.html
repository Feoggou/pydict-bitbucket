keep on

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. to continue or persist in (doing something) ⇒ keep on running2. (transitive) to continue to wear3. (transitive) to continue to employ ⇒ the firm kept on only ten men4. (intransitive; foll by about) to persist in talking (about)5. (intransitive; foll by at) to nag (a person)

keep on for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. → See keep [sense 7]2. phrasal verbIf you keep someone on, you continue to employ them, for example after they are old enough to retire or after other employees have lost their jobs. [V n P] ⇒ Sometimes they keep you on a bit longer if there's no one quite ready to step into your shoes. [V n P] ⇒ A skeleton staff of 20 is being kept on. [Also V P n (not pron)] →  keep
