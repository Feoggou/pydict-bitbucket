literal

(ˈlɪtərəl )
Definitions
adjective1. in exact accordance with or limited to the primary or explicit meaning of a word or text2. word for word3. dull, factual, or prosaic4. consisting of, concerning, or indicated by letters5. true; actual6. mathematics containing or using coefficients and constants represented by letters: ax2 + b is a literal expression. Compare numerical (sense 3a)noun7. Also called: literal error a misprint or misspelling in a text
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

literalness (ˈliteralness) or literality (ˌlɪtəˈrælɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin litterālis concerning letters, from Latin littera letter

literal

Powered by COBUILD
(lɪtərəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]The literal sense of a word or phrase is its most basic sense. ⇒ In many cases, the people there are fighting, in a literal sense, for their homes.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A literal translation is one in which you translate each word of the original work rather than giving the meaning of each expression or sentence using words that sound natural. ⇒ A literal translation of the name Tapies is 'walls.'3. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use literal to describe someone who uses or understands words in a plain and simple way. ⇒ Dennis is a very literal person.4. adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as the literal truth or a literal fact, you are emphasizing that it is true. [emphasis] ⇒ He was saying no more than the literal truth.
