colloquy

(ˈkɒləkwɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -quies1. a formal conversation or conference2. a literary work in dialogue form3. an informal conference on religious or theological matters
Derived forms
colloquist (ˈcolloquist) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin colloquium from colloquī to talk with, from com- together + loquī to speak
Example sentences containing 'colloquy'
he asked, and I inferred from his tone the desire to be excused from further colloquy. Katherine Mosb THE SEASON OF LILLIAN DAWES

Similar Words

talk, conference, debate, discussion, conversation, dialogue, discourse, confabulation, korero (New Zealand)
