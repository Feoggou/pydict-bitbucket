smudge

(smʌdʒ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to smear, blur, or soil or cause to do so2. (transitive) mainly US and Canadian to fill (an area) with smoke in order to drive insects away or guard against frostnoun3. a smear or dirty mark4. a blurred form or area ⇒ that smudge in the distance is a quarry5. mainly US and Canadian a smoky fire for driving insects away or protecting fruit trees or plants from frost

Derived Forms

smudgeless (ˈsmudgeless) adjectivesmudgily (ˈsmudgily) or smudgedly (ˈsmudgedly) adverb

Word Origin

C15: of uncertain origin

smudge

Powered by COBUILD
(smʌdʒ )
Word forms: smudges , smudging , smudged
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA smudge is a dirty mark. ⇒ There was a dark smudge on his forehead. ⇒ ...smudges of blood.2. verbIf you smudge a substance such as ink, paint, or make-up that has been put on a surface, you make it less neat by touching or rubbing it. [V n] ⇒ Smudge the outline using a cotton-wool bud. [V-ed] ⇒ Her lipstick was smudged. [Also V]3. verbIf you smudge a surface, you make it dirty by touching it and leaving a substance on it. [V n] ⇒ She kissed me, careful not to smudge me with her fresh lipstick. [V-ed] ⇒ She stood there in the old coat and woollen cap, her face smudged with dirt.
