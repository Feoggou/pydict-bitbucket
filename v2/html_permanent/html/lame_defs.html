lame1

(leɪm )
Definitions
adjective1. disabled or crippled in the legs or feet2. painful or weak ⇒ a lame back3. weak; unconvincing ⇒ a lame excuse4. not effective or enthusiastic ⇒ a lame try5. US slang conventional or uninspiringverb6. (transitive) to make lame
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lamely (ˈlamely) adverblameness (ˈlameness) noun

Word Origin

Old English lama; related to Old Norse lami, German lahm

lame

Powered by COBUILD
(leɪm ) for learners of English
Word forms: lamer , lamest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is lame, they are unable to walk properly because of damage to one or both of their legs. [+ in] ⇒ He was aware that she was lame in one leg. ⇒ David had to pull out of the Championships when his horse went lame.The lame are people who are lame. This use could cause offence. ⇒ ... the wounded and the lame of the last war.lameness uncount noun ⇒ Persistent damage or inadequate healing may lead to chronic lameness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something, for example an excuse, argument, or remark, as lame, you mean that it is poor or weak. ⇒ He mumbled some lame excuse about having gone to sleep. ⇒ All our theories sound pretty lame.lamely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'Lovely house,' I said lamely.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
