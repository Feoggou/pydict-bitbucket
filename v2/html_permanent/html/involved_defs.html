involved

(ɪnˈvɒlvd )
Definitions
adjective1. complicated; difficult to comprehend ⇒ an involved literary style2. (usually postpositive) concerned or implicated ⇒ one of the men involved3. (postpositive; foll by with) euphemistic having sexual relations ⇒ she was involved with a number of men

involved

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnvɒlvd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are involved in a situation or activity, you are taking part in it or have a strong connection with it. [+ in/with] ⇒ If she were involved in business, she would make a strong chief executive. ⇒ She became involved with political causes after the Chernobyl nuclear disaster. ⇒ The Farmers' Club is an organisation for people involved in agriculture.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are involved in something, you give a lot of time, effort, or attention to it. [+ in] ⇒ The family were deeply involved in Jewish culture. ⇒ ...women who are so involved in their careers that they have no time for friendship.3. adjective [v-link ADJ]The things involved in something such as a job or system are the necessary parts or consequences of it. [+ in] ⇒ We believe the time and hard work involved in completing such an assignment are worthwhile. ⇒ He cannot reveal how much money is involved in the scheme. ⇒ Let's take a look at some of the figures involved.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a situation or activity is involved, it has a lot of different parts or aspects, often making it difficult to understand, explain, or do. ⇒ The operations can be quite involved, requiring many procedures.5. graded adjective & adjectiveIf one person is involved with another, especially someone they are not married to, they are having a sexual or romantic relationship. [+ with] ⇒ He became romantically involved with a married woman.
