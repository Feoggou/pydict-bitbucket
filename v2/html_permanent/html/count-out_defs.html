count out

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. informal to leave out; exclude ⇒ count me out!2. (of a boxing referee) to judge (a floored boxer) to have failed to recover within the specified time. See count1 (sense 16)3. to count (something) aloud

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you count out a sum of money, you count the notes or coins as you put them in a pile one by one. [V P n] ⇒ Mr. Rohmbauer counted out the money and put it in an envelope. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verb [usu imper]If you tell someone to count you out, you mean that you do not want to be included in an activity. [V n P] ⇒ If this is the standard to which I have to drop to gain membership, then count me out! →  count
