alpha

(ˈælfə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the first letter in the Greek alphabet (Α, α), a vowel transliterated as a2. British the highest grade or mark, as in an examination3. (modifier)a. involving or relating to helium-4 nuclei ⇒ an alpha particleb. relating to one of two or more allotropes or crystal structures of a solid ⇒ alpha ironc. relating to one of two or more isomeric forms of a chemical compound, esp one in which a group is attached to the carbon atom to which the principal group is attached4. (modifier) denoting the dominant person or animal in a group ⇒ the alpha male
Word origin
via Latin from Greek, of Phoenician origin; related to Hebrew āleph, literally: ox
