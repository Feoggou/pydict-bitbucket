expansive

(ɪkˈspænsɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. able or tending to expand or characterized by expansion2. wide; extensive3. friendly, open, or talkative ⇒ an expansive person4. grand or extravagant ⇒ an expansive way of life5. psychiatry lacking restraint in the expression of feelings, esp in having delusions of grandeur or being inclined to overvalue oneself or one's work
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

expansive

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪkspænsɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]If something is expansive, it covers or includes a large area or many things. [formal] ⇒ ...an expansive grassy play area. ⇒ They have played an expansive style of rugby.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are expansive, you talk a lot, or are friendly or generous, because you are feeling happy and relaxed. ⇒ He was becoming more expansive as he relaxed. ⇒ The premier was in expansive mood.expansively graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ 'I'm here to make them feel good,' he says expansively.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something such as a period of time or an economy as expansive, you mean that it is associated with growth or expansion. ⇒ An active and expansive market economy is a necessary condition for progress.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
