swivel

(ˈswɪvəl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a coupling device which allows an attached object to turn freely2. such a device made of two parts which turn independently, such as a compound link of a chain3. a. a pivot on which is mounted a gun that may be swung from side to side in a horizontal planeb. Also called: swivel gun the gun itselfverb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled4. to turn or swing on or as if on a pivot5. (transitive) to provide with, secure by, or support with a swivel

Derived Forms

swivel-ˌlike (ˈswivel-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old English swīfan to turn; see swift

(swɪvəl )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use swiveling, swiveled1. ergative verb & verbIf something swivels or if you swivel it, it turns around a central point so that it is facing in a different direction. [V n adv/prep] ⇒ She swivelled her chair round and stared out across the back lawn. [V] ⇒ His chairs can swivel, but they can't move up or down. [Also V n]2. verbIf you swivel in a particular direction, you turn suddenly in that direction. [V adv/prep] ⇒ He swivelled round to face Sarah.3. ergative verbIf your head or your eyes swivel in a particular direction or if you swivel them in a particular direction, you quickly look in that direction. [V n] ⇒ Roger swivelled his head to look at her. [V prep/adv] ⇒ His eyes swivelled from one girl to the other. [Also V]
