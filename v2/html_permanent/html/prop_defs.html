prop1

(prɒp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: props, propping or propped (when tr, often foll by up)1. (transitive) to support with a rigid object, such as a stick2. (transitive; usually also foll by against) to place or lean3. (transitive) to sustain or support4. (intransitive) Australian and New Zealand to stop suddenly or unexpectedlynoun5. something that gives rigid support, such as a stick6. a person or thing giving support, as of a moral or spiritual nature7. rugby either of the forwards at either end of the front row of a scrum

Word Origin

C15: related to Middle Dutch proppe vine prop; compare Old High German pfropfo shoot, German Pfropfen stopper

prop

Powered by COBUILD
(prɒp )
Word forms: props , propping , propped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you prop an object on or against something, you support it by putting something underneath it or by resting it somewhere. [V n + on/against] ⇒ He rocked back in the chair and propped his feet on the desk. [V n on/against n] ⇒ He propped his bike against the bus. [Also V n adv/prep]Prop up means the same as prop. [V n P prep] ⇒ Sam slouched back and propped his elbows up on the bench behind him. [V P n prep] ⇒ If you have difficulty sitting like this, prop up your back against a wall.2. countable nounA prop is a stick or other object that you use to support something.3. countable nounTo be a prop for a system, institution, or person means to be the main thing that keeps them strong or helps them survive. ⇒ The army is one of the main props of the government. ⇒ I had two props in my life; one was alcohol, the other work.4. countable noun [usu pl]The props in a play or film are all the objects or pieces of furniture that are used in it. ⇒ ...the backdrop and props for a stage show. ⇒ ...stage props.5. countable noun [usu sing]In rugby, a prop or prop forward is one of the two players who position themselves in the front row when a group called a scrum is formed.6. countable nounA prop is the same as a propeller. [informal]► →  prop up
