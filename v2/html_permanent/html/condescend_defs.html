condescend

(ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to act graciously towards another or others regarded as being on a lower level; behave patronizingly2. to do something that one regards as below one's dignity
Word origin
C14: from Church Latin condēscendere to stoop, condescend, from Latin dēscendere to descend

Powered by COBUILD

Word forms: condescends , condescending , condescended
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone condescends to do something, they agree to do it, but in a way which shows that they think they are better than other people and should not have to do it. [disapproval] [V to-inf] ⇒ When he condescended to speak, he contradicted himself three or four times in the space of half an hour.2. verbIf you say that someone condescends to other people, you are showing your disapproval of the fact that they behave in a way which shows that they think they are superior to other people. [disapproval] [V + to] ⇒ Don't condescend to me. [Also V]
