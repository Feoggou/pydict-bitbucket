mannered

(ˈmænəd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having idiosyncrasies or mannerisms; affected ⇒ mannered gestures2. of or having mannerisms of style, as in art or literature3. (in combination) having manners as specified ⇒ ill-mannered

mannered

Powered by COBUILD
(mænəʳd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone's behaviour or a work of art as mannered, you dislike it because it is elaborate or formal, and therefore seems false or artificial. [disapproval] ⇒ ...Naomi's mannered voice. ⇒ If you arrange your picture too systematically the results can look very mannered and artificial.2. graded adjective & adjectiveMannered behaviour is polite and observes social customs. ⇒ Its intention is to restore pride in the past and create a more mannered society. ⇒ They are always perfectly polite and beautifully mannered.
