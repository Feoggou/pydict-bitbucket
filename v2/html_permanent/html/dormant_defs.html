dormant

(ˈdɔːmənt ) or dormient (ˈdɔːmɪənt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. quiet and inactive, as during sleep2. latent or inoperative3. (of a volcano) neither extinct nor erupting4. biology alive but in a resting torpid condition with suspended growth and reduced metabolism5. (usually postpositive) heraldry (of a beast) in a sleeping position▷ Compare active, passive
Derived forms
dormancy (ˈdormancy) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French dormant, from dormir to sleep, from Latin dormīre

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveSomething that is dormant is not active, growing, or being used at the present time but is capable of becoming active later on. ⇒ ...when the long dormant volcano of Mount St Helens erupted in 1980. ⇒ The virus remains dormant in nerve tissue until activated. ⇒ The United Nations is resuming a diplomatic effort that has lain dormant for almost two decades.dormancy (dɔːʳmənsi ) uncount noun ⇒ During dormancy the plants must be kept very dry.
