justified

(ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd)
Definitions
adjective1. reasonable and acceptable ⇒ In my opinion, the decision was wholly justified.2. having good and valid reasons ⇒ He was quite justified in refusing., ⇒ He's absolutely justified in resigning. He was treated shamefully.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

justified for learners of English

(dʒʌstɪfaɪd )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a decision, action, or idea as justified, you think it is reasonable and acceptable. ⇒ In my opinion, the decision was wholly justified.2. adjectiveIf you think that someone is justified in doing something, you think that their reasons for doing it are good and valid. [+ in] ⇒ He's absolutely justified in resigning. He was treated shamefully.
