troop

(truːp )
Definitions
noun1. a large group or assembly; flock ⇒ a troop of children2. a subdivision of a cavalry squadron or artillery battery of about platoon size3. (plural) armed forces; soldiers4. a large group of Scouts comprising several patrols5. an archaic spelling of troupeverb6. (intransitive) to gather, move, or march in or as if in a crowd7. (transitive) military, mainly British to parade (the colour or flag) ceremonially ⇒ trooping the colour8. (transitive) British military slang (formerly) to report (a serviceman) for a breach of discipline9. (intransitive) an archaic word for consort (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from French troupe, from troupeau flock, of Germanic origin

troop

Powered by COBUILD
(truːp ) for learners of English
Word forms: troops , trooping , trooped
Definitions
1. plural nounTroops are soldiers, especially when they are in a large organized group doing a particular task. ⇒ The next phase of the operation will involve the deployment of more than 35,000 troops from a dozen countries. ⇒ There were reports of troop movements.2. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]A troop is a group of soldiers within a cavalry or armoured regiment. [+ of] ⇒ ...a troop of enemy cavalry trotting towards the Dutch right flank.3. countable noun [usu with supp]A troop of boy scouts is a local group of them that meets regularly. ⇒ ...a Scout troop.4. countable nounA troop of people or animals is a group of them. [+ of] ⇒ Amy was aware of the little troop of travellers watching the two of them. ⇒ Out of beams and cracks came troops of beetles, ants and spiders.5. verbIf people troop somewhere, they walk there in a group, often in a sad or tired way. [informal] [V adv/prep] ⇒ They all trooped back to the house for a rest. [V adv/prep] ⇒ The men trooped into work with resignation.
