flat-out

(ˈflætˌaʊt)
Definitions
adjective informal1. done at maximum speed ⇒ crossed the line in a flat-out sprint, ⇒ going at a flat-out gallop2. US downright; complete and utter ⇒ Her request met with a flat-out refusal., ⇒ That allegation is a flat-out lie.adverb3. US simply; completely and utterly ⇒ Even people who flat-out don't like fish enjoy these sandwiches., ⇒ They say the industry is flat-out lying about the effects of deregulation., ⇒ Some of the alleged plots were flat-out ridiculous., ⇒ I admit I've been flat-out unkind to the woman in the past.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

flat out

(flæt aʊt)
Definitions
adjective1. totally exhausted ⇒ When I finally got home I was flat out and had to lie down for an hour.adverb informal2. with the maximum speed or effort ⇒ We were going flat out and weren't gaining on them at all., ⇒ I run flat out for a hundred yards, then jog for another hundred., ⇒ Everyone is working flat out to try to catch those responsible., ⇒ Even if we work flat out for the next three weeks, we shan't be able to complete the job on time.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
