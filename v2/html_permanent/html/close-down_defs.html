close down

(kləʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to cease or cause to cease operations ⇒ the shop closed down2. (transitive) sport to mark or move towards (an opposing player) in order to prevent him or her running with the ball or making or receiving a passnoun close-down (ˈkləʊzˌdaʊn )3. a closure or stoppage of operations, esp in a factory4. British radio, television the end of a period of broadcasting, esp late at night

nounthe act of ceasing to operate; closure ⇒ He branded rumours that the oil company was considering the eventual close-down of the plant as 'utter nonsense'.
