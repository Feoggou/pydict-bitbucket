Alcibiades

(ˌælsɪˈbaɪəˌdiːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun 450–404 bc, Athenian statesman and general in the Peloponnesian War: brilliant, courageous, and unstable, he defected to the Spartans in 415, but returned and led the Athenian victories at Abydos (411) and Cyzicus (410)
Derived forms
ˌAlciˌbiadean (ˌAlciˌbiaˈdean) adjective
