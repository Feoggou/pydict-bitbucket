lung

(lʌŋ )
Definitions
noun1. either one of a pair of spongy saclike respiratory organs within the thorax of higher vertebrates, which oxygenate the blood and remove its carbon dioxide2. any similar or analogous organ in other vertebrates or in invertebrates3. See at the top of one's lungspneumonicpulmonarypulmonic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English lungen; related to Old High German lungun lung. Compare lights2
