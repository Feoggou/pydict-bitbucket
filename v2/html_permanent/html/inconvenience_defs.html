inconvenience

(ˌɪnkənˈviːnjəns , -ˈviːnɪəns)
Definitions
noun1. the state or quality of being inconvenient2. something inconvenient; a hindrance, trouble, or difficultyverb3. (transitive) to cause inconvenience to; trouble or harass
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

inconvenience

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkənviːniəns ) for learners of English
Word forms: inconveniences , inconveniencing , inconvenienced
Definitions
1. variable nounIf someone or something causes inconvenience, they cause problems or difficulties. ⇒ We apologize for any inconvenience caused during the repairs. ⇒ The practical inconveniences of long hair are negligible.2. verbIf someone inconveniences you, they cause problems or difficulties for you. [V n] ⇒ He promised to be quick so as not to inconvenience them any further.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
