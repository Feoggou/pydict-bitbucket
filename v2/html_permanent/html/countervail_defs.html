countervail

(ˌkaʊntəˈveɪl , ˈkaʊntəˌveɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (when intr, usually foll by against) to act or act against with equal power or force2. (transitive) to make up for; compensate; offset
Word origin
C14: from Old French contrevaloir, from Latin contrā valēre, from contrā against + valēre to be strong
