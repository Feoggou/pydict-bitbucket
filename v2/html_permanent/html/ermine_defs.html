ermine

(ˈɜːmɪn )
Definitions
noun plural -mines or -mine1. the stoat in northern regions, where it has a white winter coat with a black-tipped tail2. the fur of this animal3. one of the two principal furs used on heraldic shields, conventionally represented by a white field flecked with black ermine tails. Compare vair4. the dignity or office of a judge, noble, or king5. short for ermine moth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C12: from Old French hermine, from Medieval Latin Armenius (mūs) Armenian (mouse)
