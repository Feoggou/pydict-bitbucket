awake

(əˈweɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: awakes, awaking, awoke, awaked, awoken or awaked Also: awaken1. to emerge or rouse from sleep; wake2. to become or cause to become alert3. (usually foll by to) to become or make aware (of) ⇒ to awake to reality4. (transitive) to arouse (feelings, etc) or cause to remember (memories, etc)adjective (postpositive)5. not sleeping6. (sometimes foll by to) lively or alertSee note at wake1
Word origin
Old English awacian, awacan; see wake1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ after v]Someone who is awake is not sleeping. ⇒ I don't stay awake at night worrying about that. ⇒ Nightmares kept me awake all night.2. →  wide awake3. ergative verb & verbWhen you awake or when something awakes you, you wake up. [literary] [V] ⇒ At midnight he awoke and listened to the radio for a few minutes. [V prep] ⇒ I awoke to the sound of the wind in the trees. [V n] ⇒ The sound of many voices awoke her with a start. [Also V to-inf]
