garter

(ˈɡɑːtə )
Definitions
noun1. a band, usually of elastic, worn round the arm or leg to hold up a shirtsleeve, sock, or stocking2. US and Canadiana. an elastic strap attached to a belt or corset having a fastener at the end, for holding up women's stockingsb. a similar fastener attached to a garter belt worn by men in order to support socks. Also called (in Britain and certain other countries): suspender3. See have someone's guts for gartersverb4. (transitive) to fasten, support, or secure with or as if with a garter
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old Northern French gartier, from garet bend of the knee, probably of Celtic origin
