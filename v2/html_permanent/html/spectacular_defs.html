spectacular

(spɛkˈtækjʊlə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or resembling a spectacle; impressive, grand, or dramatic2. unusually marked or great ⇒ a spectacular increase in spendingnoun3. a lavishly produced performance

Derived Forms

spectacularly (specˈtacularly) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is spectacular is very impressive or dramatic. OPPOSITE: unspectacular ⇒ ...spectacular views of the Sugar Loaf Mountain. ⇒ They have revamped the business with spectacular success. ⇒ The results have been spectacular.spectacularly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj/adv] ⇒ My turnover increased spectacularly. ⇒ Many of her movies had been spectacularly successful.2. countable noun [usu n N]A spectacular is a show or performance which is very grand and impressive. ⇒ ...a television spectacular. ⇒ ...one of the world's great sporting spectaculars.
