mythical

(ˈmɪθɪkəl ) or mythic (ˈmɪθɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to myth2. imaginary or fictitious

Derived Forms

mythically (ˈmythically) adverb

mythical

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something or someone that is mythical exists only in myths and is therefore imaginary. mythic ⇒ ...the Hydra, the mythical beast that had seven or more heads. ⇒ ...traditional stories woven around a pantheon of gods and mythical figures.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as mythical, you mean that it is untrue or does not exist. ⇒ ...the American West, not the mythical, romanticized West of cowboys and gunslingers, but the real West. ⇒ ...trying to preserve a mythical sense of nationhood.
