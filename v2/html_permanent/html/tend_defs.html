tend1

(tɛnd )
Definitions
verb (when intr, usually foll by to or towards)1. (when tr, takes an infinitive) to have a general disposition (to do something); be inclined ⇒ children tend to prefer sweets to meat2. (intransitive) to have or be an influence (towards a specific result); be conducive ⇒ the party atmosphere tends to hilarity3. (intransitive) to go or move (in a particular direction) ⇒ to tend to the south
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French tendre, from Latin tendere to stretch

tend2

(tɛnd )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to care for ⇒ to tend wounded soldiers2. (when intr, often foll by to) to attend (to) ⇒ to tend to someone's needs3. (transitive) to handle or control ⇒ to tend a fire4. (intransitive; often foll by to) informal, mainly US and Canadian to pay attention
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: variant of attend

tend

Powered by COBUILD
(tend ) for learners of English
Word forms: tends , tending , tended
Definitions
1. verbIf something tends to happen, it usually happens or it often happens. [V to-inf] ⇒ A problem for manufacturers is that lighter cars tend to be noisy. [V to-inf] ⇒ In older age groups women predominate because men tend to die younger. [V to-inf] ⇒ They tend to buy cheap processed foods like canned chicken and macaroni.2. verbIf you tend towards a particular characteristic, you often display that characteristic. [V + towards] ⇒ Artistic and intellectual people tend towards left-wing views. [Also V + to]3. verbYou can say that you tend to think something when you want to give your opinion, but do not want it to seem too forceful or definite. [vagueness] [V to-inf] ⇒ I tend to think that members of parliament by and large do a good job.4. verbIf you tend someone or something, you do what is necessary to keep them in a good condition or to improve their condition. [formal] [V n] ⇒ For years he tended her in her painful illness. [V n] ⇒ He tends the flower beds and evergreens that he has planted in the driveway.5. verbIf you tend to someone or something, you pay attention to them and deal with their problems and needs. [V + to] ⇒ In our culture, girls are brought up to tend to the needs of others. [V to n] ⇒ She hurried away to pour more coffee and tend to the grill.
