overgrown

(ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊn)
Definitions
adjective1. covered in weeds and unwanted plants ⇒ I led Lee around the overgrown garden., ⇒ We hurried on until we reached a courtyard that was overgrown with weeds.2. See an overgrown child

overgrown for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊvəʳgroʊn )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a garden or other place is overgrown, it is covered with a lot of untidy plants because it has not been looked after. [+ with] ⇒ We hurried on until we reached a courtyard overgrown with weeds. ⇒ As the equipment was unpacked, I led Lee around the overgrown garden.2. adjective [ADJ n]If you describe an adult as an overgrown child, you mean that their behaviour and attitudes are like those of a child, and that you dislike this. [disapproval] ⇒ ...a bunch of overgrown kids. ⇒ Daphne Farlow, at forty-five, still looked and spoke like an overgrown schoolgirl.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
