barrister

(ˈbærɪstə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: barrister-at-law (in England) a lawyer who has been called to the bar and is qualified to plead in the higher courts. Compare solicitor. See also advocate, counsel2. (in Canada) a lawyer who pleads in court3. US a less common word for lawyer
Word origin
C16: from bar1
