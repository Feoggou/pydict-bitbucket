gorget

(ˈɡɔːdʒɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a collar-like piece of armour worn to protect the throat2. a part of a wimple worn by women to cover the throat and chest, esp in the 14th century3. a band of distinctive colour on the throat of an animal, esp a bird
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gorgeted (ˈgorgeted) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French, from gorge; see gorge
Example sentences containing 'gorget'
We loosened the gorget covering his throat and pulled back his mail. David Zindell THE LIGHTSTONE: BOOK ONE, PART TWO OF THE EA CYCLE
