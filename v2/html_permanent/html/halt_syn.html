Synonyms of halt 1

halt1

verb

  1. stop, draw up, pull up, break off, stand still, wait, rest, call it a day, belay (nautical)
    • They halted at a short distance from the house.
    OPPOSITES:  start, begin, continue, maintain, proceed, go ahead, resume, commence
  2. come to an end, stop, cease
    • The flow of assistance to refugees has virtually halted.
  3. hold back, end, check, block, arrest, stem, curb, terminate, obstruct, staunch, cut short, impede, bring to an end, stem the flow, nip in the bud
    • Striking workers halted production at the auto plant yesterday.
    OPPOSITES:  encourage, boost, aid, forward

noun

  1. stop, end, close, break, stand, arrest, pause, interruption, impasse, standstill, stoppage, termination
    • Air traffic has been brought to a halt.
    OPPOSITES:  start, beginning, continuation, resumption, commencement

halt2

adjective

  1. (archaic) lame, crippled, limping
    • the old, the halt, the lame and the blind

verb

  1. falter, stumble, limp, hobble, be defective
  2. be unsure, haver, pause, hesitate, waver, stammer, think twice, dither (mainly British), boggle, swither (Scottish, dialect)
