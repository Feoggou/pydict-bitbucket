regent

(ˈriːdʒənt )
Definitions
noun1. the ruler or administrator of a country during the minority, absence, or incapacity of its monarch2. (formerly) a senior teacher or administrator in any of certain universities3. US and Canadian a member of the governing board of certain schools and colleges4. rare any person who governs or rulesadjective5. (usually postpositive) acting or functioning as a regent ⇒ a queen regent6. rare governing, ruling, or controlling
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

regental (ˈregental) adjectiveregentship (ˈregentship) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin regēns ruling, from regere to rule
