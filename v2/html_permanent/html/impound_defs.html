impound

(ɪmˈpaʊnd )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to confine (stray animals, illegally parked cars, etc) in a pound2. a. to seize (chattels, etc) by legal rightb. to take possession of (a document, evidence, etc) and hold in legal custody3. to collect (water) in a reservoir or dam, as for irrigation4. to seize or appropriate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impoundable (imˈpoundable) adjectiveimpoundage (imˈpoundage) or impoundment (imˈpoundment) nounimpounder (imˈpounder) noun

Definitions
verbIf something is impounded by policemen, customs officers or other officials, they officially take possession of it because a law or rule has been broken. [be V-ed] ⇒ The ship was impounded under the terms of the U.N. trade embargo. [V n] ⇒ The police moved in, arrested him and impounded the cocaine.
