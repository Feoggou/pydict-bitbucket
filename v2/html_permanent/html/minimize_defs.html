minimize

or minimise (ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to reduce to or estimate at the least possible degree or amount ⇒ to minimize a risk2. to rank or treat at less than the true worth; belittle ⇒ to minimize someone's achievements

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use minimise1. verbIf you minimize a risk, problem, or unpleasant situation, you reduce it to the lowest possible level, or prevent it increasing beyond that level. [V n] ⇒ Concerned people want to minimize the risk of developing cancer. [be V-ed] ⇒ Many of these problems can be minimised by sensible planning.2. verbIf you minimize something, you make it seem smaller or less significant than it really is. [V n] ⇒ Some have minimized the importance of ideological factors. [V n] ⇒ At his trial, he tried to minimize his behavior.3. verbIf you minimize a window on a computer screen, you make it very small, because you do not want to use it. [V n] ⇒ Click the square icon again to minimize the window.
