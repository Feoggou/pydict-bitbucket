effigy

(ˈɛfɪdʒɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -gies1. a portrait of a person, esp as a monument or architectural decoration2. a crude representation of someone, used as a focus for contempt or ridicule and often hung up or burnt in public (often in the phrases burn or hang in effigy)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

effigial (ɪˈfɪdʒɪəl ) adjective

Word Origin

C18: from Latin effigiēs, from effingere to form, portray, from fingere to shape
