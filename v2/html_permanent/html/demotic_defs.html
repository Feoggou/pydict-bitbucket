demotic

(dɪˈmɒtɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to the common people; popular2. of or relating to a simplified form of hieroglyphics used in ancient Egypt by the ordinary literate class outside the priesthood. Compare hieraticnoun3. the demotic script of ancient Egypt
Derived forms
demotist (deˈmotist) or demoticist (deˈmoticist) noun
Word origin
C19: from Greek dēmotikos of the people, from dēmotēs a man of the people, commoner; see demos

Demotic

(dɪˈmɒtɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the spoken form of Modern Greek, now increasingly used in literature. Compare Katharevusaadjective2. denoting or relating to this
