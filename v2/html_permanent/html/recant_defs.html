recant

(rɪˈkænt )
Definitions
verbto repudiate or withdraw (a former belief or statement), esp formally in public
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recantation (ˌriːkænˈteɪʃən ) nounrecanter (reˈcanter) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin recantāre to sing again, from re- + cantāre to sing; see chant

Definitions
verbIf you recant, you say publicly that you no longer hold a set of beliefs that you had in the past. [formal] [V] ⇒ White House officials ordered Williams to recant. [V n] ⇒ ...a man who had refused after torture to recant his heresy.
