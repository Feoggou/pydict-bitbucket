headache

(ˈhɛdˌeɪk )
Definitions
noun1. pain in the head, caused by dilation of cerebral arteries, muscle contraction, insufficient oxygen in the cerebral blood, reaction to drugs, etc. Technical name: cephalalgia2. informal any cause of worry, difficulty, or annoyance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

headache

Powered by COBUILD
(hedeɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: headaches
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you have a headache, you have a pain in your head. ⇒ I have had a terrible headache for the last two days.2. countable nounIf you say that something is a headache, you mean that it causes you difficulty or worry. ⇒ The airline's biggest headache is the increase in the price of aviation fuel. ⇒ This is a real headache for us.
