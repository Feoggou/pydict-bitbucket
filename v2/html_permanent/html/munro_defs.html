Munro1

(mʌnˈrəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural Munrosmountaineering any separate mountain peak over 3000 feet high: originally used of Scotland only but now sometimes extended to other parts of the British Isles

Word Origin

C20: named after Hugh Thomas Munro (1856–1919), who published a list of these in 1891

Munro2

(mʌnˈrəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1.  Alice, original name Alice Laidlaw. born 1931, Canadian short-story writer; her books include Lives of Girls and Women (1971), The Moons of Jupiter (1982), and The Love of a Good Woman (1999); winner of the Booker international prize (2009) for a lifetime body of work; awarded the Nobel prize for literature (2013)2.  H(ector) H(ugh), pen name Saki. 1870–1916, Scottish author, born in Burma (now Myanmar), noted for his collections of satirical short stories, such as Reginald (1904) and Beasts and Superbeasts (1914)
