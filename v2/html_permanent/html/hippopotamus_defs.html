hippopotamus

(ˌhɪpəˈpɒtəməs )
Definitions
noun plural -muses or -mi (-ˌmaɪ)1. a very large massive gregarious artiodactyl mammal, Hippopotamus amphibius, living in or around the rivers of tropical Africa: family Hippopotamidae. It has short legs and a thick skin sparsely covered with hair2. See pigmy hippopotamus
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin, from Greek hippopotamos river horse, from hippos horse + potamos river
