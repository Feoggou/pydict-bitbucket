darn

Powered by COBUILD
(dɑːʳn )
Word forms: darns , darning , darned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you darn something knitted or made of cloth, you mend a hole in it by sewing stitches across the hole and then weaving stitches in and out of them. [V n] ⇒ Aunt Emilie darned old socks. [V-ed] ⇒ Mari refolded the darned pullover and socks.darning uncount noun ⇒ ...chores such as sewing and darning.2. countable nounA darn is a part of a piece of clothing that has been darned.3. adjective [ADJ n]People sometimes use darn or darned to emphasize what they are saying, often when they are annoyed. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ There's not a darn thing he can do about it. ⇒ You invariably leave your health club a darned sight less healthy than when you went in.Darn is also an adverb. ⇒ ...the desire to be free to do just as we darn well please. ⇒ We start working pretty darn early.4. →  darn it5. →  I'll be darned
