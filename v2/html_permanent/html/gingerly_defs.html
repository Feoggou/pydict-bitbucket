gingerly

(ˈdʒɪndʒəlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. in a cautious, reluctant, or timid manneradjective2. cautious, reluctant, or timid
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gingerliness (ˈgingerliness) noun

Word Origin

C16: perhaps from Old French gensor dainty, from gent of noble birth; see gentle

gingerly

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɪndʒəʳli ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v]If you do something gingerly, you do it in a careful manner, usually because you expect it to be dangerous, unpleasant, or painful. [written] ⇒ She was touching the dressing gingerly with both hands. ⇒ I drove gingerly past the security check points.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'gingerly'
Rae gingerly picked up the phone, careful not to do any damage to her wet nails. Melinda Metz REVELATIONS
