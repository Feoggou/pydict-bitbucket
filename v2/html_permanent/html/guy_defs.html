guy1

(ɡaɪ )
Definitions
noun1. informal a man or youth2. British a crude effigy of Guy Fawkes, usually made of old clothes stuffed with straw or rags, that is burnt on top of a bonfire on Guy Fawkes Day3. British a person in shabby or ludicrously odd clothes4. (plural) informal persons of either sexverb5. (transitive) to make fun of; ridicule
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: short for Guy Fawkes

guy2

(ɡaɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a rope, chain, wire, etc, for anchoring an object, such as a radio mast, in position or for steadying or guiding it while being hoisted or loweredverb2. (transitive) to anchor, steady, or guide with a guy or guys
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: probably from Low German; compare Dutch gei brail, geiblok pulley, Old French guie guide, from guier to guide

Definitions
1. countable nounA guy is a man. [informal] ⇒ I was working with a guy from Manchester.2. → See also wise guy3. countable noun & plural nounAmericans sometimes address a group of people, whether they are male or female, as guys or you guys. [informal] ⇒ Hi, guys. How are you doing? ⇒ Mom wants to know if you guys still have that two-person tent.4. countable nounA guy is the same as a guy rope.5. countable nounIn Britain, a guy is a model of a man that is made from old clothes filled with straw or paper. Guys are burned on bonfires as part of the celebrations for Guy Fawkes Night.
