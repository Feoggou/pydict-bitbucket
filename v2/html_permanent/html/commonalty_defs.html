commonalty

(ˈkɒmənəltɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the ordinary people as distinct from those with authority, rank, or title, esp when considered as a political and social unit or estate of the realm. Compare third estate2. the members of an incorporated society
Word origin
C13: from Old French comunalte, from comunal communal
