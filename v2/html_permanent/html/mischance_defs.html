mischance

(mɪsˈtʃɑːns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. bad luck2. a stroke of bad luck
Example sentences containing 'mischance'
By some mischance , the false bottom of the bag had become dislodged in the fall. Higgins, Jack CONFESSIONALSaying this as much to protect himself from guilt over any mischance , as from concern for Cousins. Smith, Mitchell STONE CITYIf you were on lease, instead of having been a sale outright through some mischance , you would long since have been replaced. Asimov, Isaac THE COMPLETE STORIES VOLUME 2Some learned to use the mischance for favors... few of us begrudged them the extra rations or trivial treats so dearly bought. Lois McMaster Bujold THE CURSE OF CHALION
