cartilage

(ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ , ˈkɑːtlɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna tough elastic tissue composing most of the embryonic skeleton of vertebrates. In the adults of higher vertebrates it is mostly converted into bone, remaining only on the articulating ends of bones, in the thorax, trachea, nose, and ears. Nontechnical name: gristle
Derived forms
cartilaginous (ˌkɑːtɪˈlædʒɪnəs ) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin cartilāgō
