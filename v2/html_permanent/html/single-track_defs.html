single track

(ˈsɪŋɡəl træk)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a single pair of lines so that trains can travel in only one direction at a time ⇒ The single track of the small branch line ran down there in the valley between the river and lower slopes of the high hills.2. a road or path that is only wide enough for one vehicle or cyclist ⇒ There are 6.2 miles of twisting single track between Arisaig and Kinsadel.

single-track

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a railway) having only a single pair of lines, so that trains can travel in only one direction at a time2. (of a road) only wide enough for one vehicle3. able to think about only one thing; one-track
