Word Frequency

mark time

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
a. to move the feet alternately as in marching but without advancingb. to act in a mechanical and routine wayc. to halt progress temporarily, while awaiting developments▷ See mark
Example sentences containing 'mark time'
Oh, let me come soon to that safe haven where I shall mark time forever. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEADI don't want to mark time amongst all that clutter of pipes down there longer than I have to. Jenkins, Geoffrey A DAYSTAR OF FEARThis time there would be no escape into another situation in which she seemed merely to mark time. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENGowain strummed a beat on his strings and a low chant rose to mark time with the swallows. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
