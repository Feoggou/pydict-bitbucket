practicable

(ˈpræktɪkəbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. capable of being done; feasible2. usableSee note at practical

Derived Forms

ˌpracticability (ˌpracticaˈbility) or practicableness (ˈpracticableness) nounpracticably (ˈpracticably) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from French praticable, from pratiquer to practise; see practical

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If a task, plan, or idea is practicable, people are able to carry it out. [formal] OPPOSITE: impracticable, unfeasible ⇒ It is not reasonably practicable to offer her the original job back. ⇒ Teachers can only be expected to do what is reasonable and practicable.practicability (præktɪkəbɪlɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Knotman and I discussed the practicability of the idea.
