faculty

(ˈfækəltɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. one of the inherent powers of the mind or body, such as reason, memory, sight, or hearing2. any ability or power, whether acquired or inherent3. a conferred power or right4. a. a department within a university or college devoted to a particular branch of knowledgeb. the staff of such a departmentc. mainly US and Canadian all the teaching staff at a university, college, school, etc5. all members of a learned profession6. archaic occupation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14 (in the sense: department of learning): from Latin facultās capability; related to Latin facilis easy

Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl, oft poss N]Your faculties are your physical and mental abilities. ⇒ He was drunk and not in control of his faculties. [+ of] ⇒ It is also a myth that the faculty of hearing is greatly increased in blind people.2. variable nounA faculty is a group of related departments in some universities, or the people who work in them. [British] [+ of] ⇒ ...the Faculty of Social and Political Sciences.3. variable noun [oft N n]A faculty is all the teaching staff of a university or college, or of one department. [US] ⇒ The faculty agreed on a change in the requirements. ⇒ How can faculty improve their teaching so as to encourage creativity? ⇒ ...eminent Stanford faculty members.
