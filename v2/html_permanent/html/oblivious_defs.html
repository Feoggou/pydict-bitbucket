oblivious

(əˈblɪvɪəs )
Definitions
adjective(foll by to or of) unaware or forgetful▶ USAGE It was formerly considered incorrect to use oblivious to mean unaware, but this use is now acceptable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

obliviously (obˈliviously) adverbobliviousness (obˈliviousness) noun

oblivious for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(əblɪviəs )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are oblivious to something or oblivious of it, you are not aware of it. [+ to/of] ⇒ She lay motionless where she was, oblivious to pain. ⇒ Llewelyn appeared oblivious of his surroundings.obliviously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Burke was asleep, sprawled obliviously against the window.obliviousness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ Her obliviousness of what was happening in Germany seems extraordinary.
