beaver1

(ˈbiːvə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large amphibious rodent, Castor fiber, of Europe, Asia, and North America: family Castoridae. It has soft brown fur, a broad flat hairless tail, and webbed hind feet, and constructs complex dams and houses (lodges) in rivers2. the fur of this animal3. See mountain beaver4. a tall hat of beaver fur or a fabric resembling it, worn, esp by men, during the 19th century5. a woollen napped cloth resembling beaver fur, formerly much used for overcoats, etc6. a greyish- or yellowish-brown colour7. obsolete a full beard8. a bearded man9. (modifier) having the colour of beaver or made of beaver fur or some similar material ⇒ a beaver lamb coat, ⇒ a beaver stoleverb10. (intransitive; usually foll by away) to work industriously or steadily
Word origin
Old English beofor; compare Old Norse biōrr, Old High German bibar, Latin fiber, Sanskrit babhrú red-brown
