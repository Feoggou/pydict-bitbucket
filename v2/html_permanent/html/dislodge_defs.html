Word forms: dislodges , dislodging , dislodged
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo dislodge something means to remove it from where it was fixed or held. [V n + from] ⇒ Rainfall had dislodged debris from the slopes of the volcano. [V-ed] ⇒ In the exertion Tompkins' hat had become dislodged. [Also V n]2. verbTo dislodge a person from a position or job means to remove them from it. [V n] ⇒ He may challenge the Prime Minister even if he decides he cannot dislodge her this time.
