urea

(ˈjʊərɪə )
Definitions
nouna white water-soluble crystalline compound with a saline taste and often an odour of ammonia, produced by protein metabolism and excreted in urine. A synthetic form is used as a fertilizer, animal feed, and in the manufacture of synthetic resins. Formula: CO(NH2)2. Also called: carbamide
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ureal (uˈreal) or ureic (uˈreic) adjective

Word Origin

C19: from New Latin, from French urée, from Greek ouron urine
