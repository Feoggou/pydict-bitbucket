steerage

(ˈstɪərɪdʒ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the cheapest accommodation on a passenger ship, originally the compartments containing the steering apparatus2. an instance or the practice of steering and the effect of this on a vessel or vehicle
Example sentences containing 'steerage'
If he kept the helm down hard, he might gain a little steerage from the anchor and avoid the Curate. Philip Marsden THE MAIN CAGES`Would do them more good if they were down with the kids in steerage.
