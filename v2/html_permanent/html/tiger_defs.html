tiger

(ˈtaɪɡə )
Definitions
noun1. a large feline mammal, Panthera tigris, of forests in most of Asia, having a tawny yellow coat with black stripes2. (not in technical use) any of various other animals, such as the jaguar, leopard, and thylacine3. a dynamic, forceful, or cruel person4. a. a country, esp in E Asia, that is achieving rapid economic growthb. (as modifier) ⇒ a tiger economy5. archaic a servant in livery, esp a page or groom6. short for tiger moth7. South Africa slang a ten-rand note8. See have a tiger by the tail
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tigerish (ˈtigerish) or tigrish (ˈtigrish) adjectivetigerishly (ˈtigerishly) or tigrishly (ˈtigrishly) adverbtigerishness (ˈtigerishness) or tigrishness (ˈtigrishness) nountiger-like (ˈtiger-like) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French tigre, from Latin tigris, from Greek, of Iranian origin
