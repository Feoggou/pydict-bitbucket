Dolby

(ˈdɒlbɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun trademark1. any of various specialized electronic circuits, esp those used for noise reduction in tape recorders by functioning as companders on high-frequency signals2. a system used in cinemas and television sets which provides surround sound
Word origin
named after Ray Dolby (1933–2013), its US inventor
