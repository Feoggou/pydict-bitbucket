Word Frequency

grapnel

(ˈɡræpnəl )
Definitions
noun1. a device with a multiple hook at one end and attached to a rope, which is thrown or hooked over a firm mooring to secure an object attached to the other end of the rope2. a light anchor for small boats
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French grapin a little hook, from grape a hook; see grape
