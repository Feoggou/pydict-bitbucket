complimentary

(ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. conveying, containing, or resembling a compliment2. expressing praise; flattering3. given free, esp as a courtesy or for publicity purposes
Derived forms
ˌcomplimentarily (ˌcompliˈmentarily) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are complimentary about something, you express admiration for it. ⇒ The staff have been very complimentary, and so have the customers. ⇒ We often get complimentary remarks regarding the cleanliness of our patio.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A complimentary seat, ticket, or book is given to you free. ⇒ He had complimentary tickets to take his wife to see the movie. ⇒ ...a complimentary copy of Dr Sherwood's recently published book.
