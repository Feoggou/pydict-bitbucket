tenement

(ˈtɛnəmənt )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: tenement building (now esp in Scotland) a large building divided into separate flats2. a dwelling place or residence, esp one intended for rent3. mainly British a room or flat for rent4. property law any form of permanent property, such as land, dwellings, offices, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tenemental (ˌtɛnəˈmɛntəl ) or tenementary (ˌteneˈmentary) adjectivetenemented (ˈteneˌmented) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin tenementum, from Latin tenēre to hold
