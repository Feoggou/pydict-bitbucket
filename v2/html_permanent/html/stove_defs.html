stove1

(stəʊv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. another word for cooker (sense 1)2. any heating apparatus, such as a kilnverb (transitive)3. to process (ceramics, metalwork, etc) by heating in a stove4. Scottish to stew (meat, vegetables, etc)

Word Origin

Old English stofa bathroom; related to Old High German stuba steam room, Greek tuphos smoke

stove

Powered by COBUILD
(stoʊv )
Word forms: stoves
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA stove is a piece of equipment which provides heat, either for cooking or for heating a room. ⇒ She put the kettle on the gas stove.2. countable nounA stove is the top of a cooker. [mainly US]
Example sentences containing 'stove'
A large ceramic stove which had once been used for sleeping on during cold winter nights occupied one corner. Low, Ona TO HIS JUST DESSERTSAdam had found some cups and instant coffee and had put a pot on the ancient stove to boil water. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERThe mechanic filled a kettle with water and lit the little paraffin stove. O'Connor, Joe DESPERADOESJeffrey Tolliver was standing by the stove , talking to her mother.
