constructive

(kənˈstrʌktɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. serving to build or improve; positive ⇒ constructive criticism2. law deduced by inference or construction; not expressed but inferred3. law having a deemed legal effect ⇒ constructive notice4. another word for structural
Derived forms
constructively (conˈstructively) adverbconstructiveness (conˈstructiveness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveA constructive discussion, comment, or approach is useful and helpful rather than negative and unhelpful. ⇒ She welcomes constructive criticism. ⇒ After their meeting, both men described the talks as frank, friendly and constructive. ⇒ The Prime Minister has promised that Israel will play a constructive role. ⇒ At least I'm doing something constructive.constructively graded adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ We are prepared to sit down and talk constructively with our European partners. ⇒ Use the time constructively.
