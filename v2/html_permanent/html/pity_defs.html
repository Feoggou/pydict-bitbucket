pity

(ˈpɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural pities1. sympathy or sorrow felt for the sufferings of another2. See have pity on3. something that causes regret or pity4. an unfortunate chance ⇒ what a pity you can't come5. See more's the pityverbWord forms: pities, pitying or pitied6. (transitive) to feel pity for

Derived Forms

pitying (ˈpitying) adjectivepityingly (ˈpityingly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old French pité, from Latin pietās duty

Powered by COBUILD
(pɪti )
Word forms: pities , pitying , pitied
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounIf you feel pity for someone, you feel very sorry for them. [+ for] ⇒ He felt a sudden tender pity for her. ⇒ She knew that she was an object of pity among her friends.2. → see also self-pity3. verbIf you pity someone, you feel very sorry for them. [V n] ⇒ I don't know whether to hate or pity him.4. singular nounIf you say that it is a pity that something is the case, you mean that you feel disappointment or regret about it. [feelings] ⇒ It is a great pity that all pupils in the city cannot have the same chances. ⇒ Pity you haven't got your car, isn't it. ⇒ It seemed a pity to let it all go to waste.5. uncount nounIf someone shows pity, they do not harm or punish someone they have power over. ⇒ One should avoid showing too much pity. ⇒ She saw no pity in their faces.6. →  more's the pity7. →  for pity's sake8. →  take pity on someone9. →  the pity is that/the pity of it is that
