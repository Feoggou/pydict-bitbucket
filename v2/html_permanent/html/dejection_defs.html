dejection

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪdʒekʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounDejection is a feeling of sadness that you get, for example, when you have just been disappointed by something. ⇒ There was a slight air of dejection about her.
Example sentences containing 'dejection'
She smiled tentatively at Caroline's compliment, but there was a slight air of dejection about her. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENSide by side they were sitting on a bench, the picture of dejection. Melville, Anne THE HARDIE INHERITANCEIt never occurred to him that her dejection might be a ruse to get his pity, nor was it. McCorquodale, Robin DANSVILLEShe sat up sleepily and in deep dejection dropped her chin wearily in her hands, thinking of the day's chore. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
