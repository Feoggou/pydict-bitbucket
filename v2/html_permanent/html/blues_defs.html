blues

(bluːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun the blues (sometimes functioning as singular)1. a feeling of depression or deep unhappiness2. a type of folk song devised by Black Americans at the beginning of the 20th century, usually employing a basic 12-bar chorus, the tonic, subdominant, and dominant chords, frequent minor intervals, and blue notes
Derived forms
bluesy (ˈbluesy) adjective
