unduly

(ʌnˈdjuːlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. immoderately; excessively2. in contradiction of moral or legal standards

unduly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌndjuːli , US -duːli )
Definitions
adverb [ADV with v] [ADV adj]If you say that something does not happen or is not done unduly, you mean that it does not happen or is not done to an excessive or unnecessary extent. ⇒ 'But you're not unduly worried about doing this report?'—'No.'. ⇒ This will achieve greater security without unduly burdening the consumers or the economy. ⇒ He appealed to firms not to increase their prices unduly.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
