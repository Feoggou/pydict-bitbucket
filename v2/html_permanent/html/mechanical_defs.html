mechanical

(mɪˈkænɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. made, performed, or operated by or as if by a machine or machinery ⇒ a mechanical process2. concerned with machines or machinery3. relating to or controlled or operated by physical forces4. of or concerned with mechanics5. (of a gesture, etc) automatic; lacking thought, feeling, etc6. philosophy accounting for phenomena by physically determining forces7. (of paper, such as newsprint) made from pulp that has been mechanically ground and contains impuritiesnoun8. printing another name for camera-ready copy9. archaic another word for mechanic (sense 2)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A mechanical device has parts that move when it is working, often using power from an engine or from electricity. ⇒ ...a small mechanical device that taps out the numbers. ⇒ ...the oldest working mechanical clock in the world. ⇒ Most mechanical devices require oil as a lubricant.mechanically (mɪkænɪkli ) adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The air was circulated mechanically.2. adjective [ADJ n]Mechanical means relating to machines and engines and the way they work. ⇒ ...mechanical engineering. ⇒ The company undertakes mechanical work on all types of cars. ⇒ The train had stopped due to a mechanical problem.mechanically adverb [ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ The car was mechanically sound, he decided.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person as mechanical, you mean they are naturally good at understanding how machines work. ⇒ He was a very mechanical person, who knew a lot about sound. ⇒ I'm not mechanical like my father; I have to follow the instructions.mechanically graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed] ⇒ I'm not mechanically minded.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's action as mechanical, you mean that they do it automatically, without thinking about it. ⇒ It is real prayer, and not mechanical repetition. ⇒ Many girls have a kind of mechanical attitude towards sex. ⇒ Her retort was mechanical.mechanically graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He nodded mechanically, his eyes fixed on the girl.
