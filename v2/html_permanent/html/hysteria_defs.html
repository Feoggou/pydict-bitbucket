hysteria

(hɪˈstɪərɪə )
Definitions
noun1. a mental disorder characterized by emotional outbursts, susceptibility to autosuggestion, and, often, symptoms such as paralysis that mimic the effects of physical disorders. See also conversion disorder2. any frenzied emotional state, esp of laughter or crying
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from New Latin, from Latin hystericus hysteric

hysteria

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪstɪəriə , US -ster- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounHysteria among a group of people is a state of uncontrolled excitement, anger, or panic. ⇒ No one could help getting carried away by the hysteria. ⇒ Several were hurt in the panic. 'It was mass hysteria,' said Rev John Borders.2. uncount nounA person who is suffering from hysteria is in a state of violent and disturbed emotion as a result of shock. [medicine] hysterics ⇒ By now, she was screaming, completely overcome with hysteria.
