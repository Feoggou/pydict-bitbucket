richly

(ˈrɪtʃlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. in a rich or elaborate manner ⇒ a richly decorated carving2. fully and appropriately ⇒ he was richly rewarded for his service

richly

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪtʃli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV -ed/adj]If something is richly coloured, flavoured, or perfumed, it has a pleasantly strong colour, flavour, or perfume. ⇒ ...an opulent display of richly coloured fabrics. ⇒ ...the richly flavoured, hearty foods of the region.2. graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV -ed/adj]If something is richly decorated, patterned, or furnished, it has a lot of elaborate and beautiful decoration, patterns, or furniture. ⇒ Coffee steamed in the richly decorated silver pot. ⇒ ...a richly illustrated introduction to the art of the Islamic world.3. graded adverb & adverb [ADV before v, ADV -ed]If you say that someone richly deserves an award, success, or victory, you approve of what they have done and feel very strongly that they deserve it. [feelings] ⇒ He achieved the success he so richly deserved. ⇒ ...a deeply affecting performance that won him a richly deserved Oscar.4. graded adverb [ADV -ed]If a person or place is richly endowed or supplied with something, they have a lot of it. ⇒ ...a boy richly endowed with courage. ⇒ The hands are richly supplied with nerve endings.5. graded adverb & adverb [ADV before v, ADV -ed]If you are richly rewarded for doing something, you get something very valuable or pleasant in return for doing it. ⇒ It is a difficult book to read, but it richly rewards the effort. ⇒ Their caution has been richly rewarded.
