humdrum

(ˈhʌmˌdrʌm )
Definitions
adjective1. ordinary; dullnoun2. a monotonous routine, task, or person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

humdrumness (ˈhumˌdrumness) noun

Word Origin

C16: rhyming compound, probably based on hum

humdrum

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌmdrʌm ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as humdrum, you mean that they are ordinary, dull, or boring. [disapproval] ⇒ ...her lawyer husband, trapped in a humdrum but well-paid job. ⇒ The new government seemed rather humdrum.
