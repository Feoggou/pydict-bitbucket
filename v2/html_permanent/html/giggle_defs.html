giggle

(ˈɡɪɡəl )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to laugh nervously or foolishlynoun2. such a laugh3. informal something or someone that provokes amusement4. See the giggles5. See for a giggle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

giggler (ˈgiggler) noungiggling (ˈgiggling) noun, adjectivegigglingly (ˈgigglingly) adverbgiggly (ˈgiggly) adjective

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

giggle

Powered by COBUILD
(gɪgəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: giggles , giggling , giggled
Definitions
1. verbIf someone giggles, they laugh in a childlike way, because they are amused, nervous, or embarrassed. [V] ⇒ Both girls began to giggle. [V with quote] ⇒ 'I beg your pardon?' she giggled. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a giggling little girl.Giggle is also a noun. ⇒ She gave a little giggle.2. plural nounIf you say that someone has the giggles, you mean they cannot stop giggling. ⇒ I was so nervous I got the giggles. ⇒ She had a fit of the giggles.3. singular nounIf you say that something is a giggle, you mean it is fun or is amusing. [mainly British, informal] ⇒ I might buy one for a friend's birthday as a giggle.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
