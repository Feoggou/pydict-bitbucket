holler

(ˈhɒlə ) informal
Definitions
verb1. to shout or yell (something)noun2. a shout; call
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

variant of C16 hollow, from holla, from French holà stop! (literally: ho there!)

holler

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒləʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hollers , hollering , hollered
Definitions
verbIf you holler, you shout loudly. [mainly US, informal] [V] ⇒ The audience whooped and hollered. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Watch out!' he hollered. [V at/for n] ⇒ In a minute he'll be hollering at me for coming in late. [V + for] ⇒ Cal hollered for help. [V for n to-inf] ⇒ Nick hollered for her to pick up her orders. [Also V n] [Also V + at]Holler is also a noun. ⇒ On the ship's deck, after the whoops and hollers, the butchering begins.Holler out means the same as holler. [V P n] ⇒ I hollered out the names. [V P with quote] ⇒ I heard him holler out, 'Somebody bombed the Church.' [Also V P]
