item

Definitions
noun (ˈaɪtəm )1. a thing or unit, esp included in a list or collection2. accounting an entry in an account3. a piece of information, detail, or note ⇒ a news item4. informal two people having a romantic or sexual relationshipverb (ˈaɪtəm )5. (transitive) an archaic word for itemizeadverb (ˈaɪtɛm )6. likewise; also
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14 (adv) from Latin: in like manner

item

Powered by COBUILD
(aɪtəm ) for learners of English
Word forms: items
Definitions
1. countable nounAn item is one of a collection or list of objects. ⇒ The most valuable item on show will be a Picasso drawing. ⇒ The menu includes the occasional off-beat item.2. → See also collector's item3. countable nounAn item is one of a list of things for someone to do, deal with, or talk about. ⇒ The other item on the agenda is the tour.4. countable nounAn item is a report or article in a newspaper or magazine, or on television or radio. ⇒ There was an item in the paper about him.5. singular nounIf you say that two people are an item, you mean that they are having a romantic or sexual relationship. [informal] ⇒ She and Gino were an item.
