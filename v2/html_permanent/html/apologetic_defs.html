apologetic

(əˌpɒləˈdʒɛtɪk ) or apologetical (əˌpɒləˈdʒɛtɪkəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. expressing or anxious to make apology; contrite2. protecting or defending in speech or writing
Derived forms
aˌpologetically (aˌpoloˈgetically) adverb

(əpɒlədʒetɪk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are apologetic, you show or say that you are sorry for causing trouble for someone, for hurting them, or for disappointing them. ⇒ The hospital staff were very apologetic but that couldn't really compensate. ⇒ They were almost apologetic about the improvements they'd made. ⇒ 'I don't follow football,' she said with an apologetic smile.apologetically (əpɒlədʒetɪkli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'It's of no great literary merit,' he said, almost apologetically.
