notwithstanding

(ˌnɒtwɪθˈstændɪŋ , -wɪð-)
Definitions
preposition1. (often immediately postpositive) in spite of; despiteconjunction (subordinating)2. despite the fact that; althoughsentence connector3. in spite of that; nevertheless
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: not + withstanding, from Old English withstandan, on the model of Medieval Latin non obstante, Old French non obstant

notwithstanding for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒtwɪðstændɪŋ )
Definitions
prepositionIf something is true notwithstanding something else, it is true in spite of that other thing. [formal] ⇒ He despised William Pitt, notwithstanding the similar views they both held.Notwithstanding is also an adverb. ⇒ His relations with colleagues, differences of opinion notwithstanding, were unfailingly friendly.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
