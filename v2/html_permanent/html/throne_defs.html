throne

(θrəʊn )
Definitions
noun1. the ceremonial seat occupied by a monarch, bishop, etc on occasions of state2. the power, duties, or rank ascribed to a royal person3. a person holding royal rank4. (plural; often capital) the third of the nine orders into which the angels are traditionally divided in medieval angelologyverb5. to place or be placed on a throne
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

throneless (ˈthroneless) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French trone, from Latin thronus, from Greek thronos throne
