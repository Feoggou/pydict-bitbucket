masterful

(ˈmɑːstəfʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having or showing mastery2. fond of playing the master; imperious3. masterly▶ USAGE The use of masterful to mean masterly as in a masterful performance, although common, is considered incorrect by many people

Derived Forms

masterfully (ˈmasterfully) adverbmasterfulness (ˈmasterfulness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a man as masterful, you approve of him because he behaves in a way which shows that he is in control of a situation and can tell other people what to do. [approval] ⇒ Big, successful moves need bold, masterful managers.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour or actions as masterful, you mean that they show great skill. ⇒ ...a masterful performance of boxing and punching skills.
