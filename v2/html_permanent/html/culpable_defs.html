Definitions

adjectivedeserving censure; blameworthy
Derived forms
ˌculpability (ˌculpaˈbility) or culpableness (ˈculpableness) nounculpably (ˈculpably) adverb
Word origin
C14: from Old French coupable, from Latin culpābilis, from culpāre to blame, from culpa fault

Definitions

graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or their conduct is culpable, they are responsible for something wrong or bad that has happened. [formal] ⇒ Their decision to do nothing makes them culpable. ⇒ ...manslaughter resulting from culpable negligence.culpability (kʌlpəbɪlɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ He added there was clear culpability on the part of the government.
