dither

(ˈdɪðə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. mainly British to be uncertain or indecisive2. mainly US to be in an agitated state3. to tremble, as with coldnoun4. mainly British a state of indecision5. a state of agitation
Derived forms
ditherer (ˈditherer) noundithery (ˈdithery) adjective
Word origin
C17: variant of C14 (northern English dialect) didder, of uncertain origin

(dɪðəʳ )
Word forms: dithers , dithering , dithered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen someone dithers, they hesitate because they are unable to make a quick decision about something. [V + over] ⇒ We're still dithering over whether to marry. [V + about] ⇒ If you have been dithering about buying shares, now could be the time to do it. [Also V]

But you go straight back home and don't dither on the way," Janice said Brent-Dyer, Elinor ADRIENNE AND THE CHALET SCHOOLI dither , but there's still no word from the twilight zone outside, so I don't dither long.
