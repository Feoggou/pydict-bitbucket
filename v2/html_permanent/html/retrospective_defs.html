retrospective

(ˌrɛtrəʊˈspɛktɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. looking or directed backwards, esp in time; characterized by retrospection2. applying to the past; retroactivenoun3. an exhibition of an artist's life's work or a representative selection of it
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

retrospective

Powered by COBUILD
(retrəspektɪv ) for learners of English
Word forms: retrospectives
Definitions
1. countable nounA retrospective is an exhibition or showing of work done by an artist over many years, rather than his or her most recent work. ⇒ ...a retrospective of the films of Judy Garland. ⇒ They honoured him with a retrospective exhibition in 1987.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]Retrospective feelings or opinions concern things that happened in the past. ⇒ Afterwards, retrospective fear of the responsibility would make her feel almost faint.retrospectively adverb [ADV with v] in retrospect ⇒ Retrospectively, it seems as if they probably were negligent. ⇒ To ascribe opinions retrospectively is of course very dangerous.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Retrospective laws or legal actions take effect from a date before the date when they are officially approved. retroactive ⇒ Bankers are quick to condemn retrospective tax legislation.retrospectively adverb [ADV with v] retroactively ⇒ ...a decree which retrospectively changes the electoral law under which last year's national elections were held.
