cerulean

(sɪˈruːlɪən ), cerule (ˈsiːruːl) or ceruleous (sɪˈruːlɪəs)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna. a deep blue colour; azureb. (as adjective) ⇒ a cerulean sea
Word origin
C17: from Latin caeruleus, probably from caelum sky

`And to Leigh," he said, gazing at me with his cerulean eyes burning brightly. Andrews, Virginia WEB OF DREAMSSpat out by the Hyde Park railings beneath the thinning cerulean.
