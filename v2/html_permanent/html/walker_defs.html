walker

(ˈwɔːkə )
Definitions
noun1. a person who walks2. Also called: baby walker a tubular frame on wheels or castors to support a baby learning to walk3. a similar support for walking, often with rubber feet, for use by disabled or infirm people4. a woman's escort at a social event ⇒ let me introduce my walker for tonight
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Walker

(ˈwɔːkə )
Definitions
noun1.  Alice (Malsenior). born 1944, US writer: her works include In Love and Trouble: Stories of Black Women (1973) and the novels Meridian (1976), The Color Purple (1982), and Possessing the Secret of Joy (1992)2.  Sir John. born 1952, New Zealand middle-distance runner, the first athlete to run one hundred sub-four-minute miles; won gold in the 1500 metres at the 1976 Olympics
