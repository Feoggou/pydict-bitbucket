imaginable

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmædʒɪnəbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveYou use imaginable after a superlative such as 'best' or 'worst' to emphasize that something is extreme in some way. [emphasis] ⇒ ...their imprisonment under some of the most horrible circumstances imaginable. ⇒ He could not disguise that he had had the worst imaginable day for any minister.2. adjective [ADJ n, n ADJ]You use imaginable after a word like 'every' or 'all' to emphasize that you are talking about all the possible examples of something. You use imaginable after 'no' to emphasize that something does not have the quality mentioned. [emphasis] ⇒ ...all of the other imaginable ramifications of this technology. ⇒ Parents encourage every activity imaginable. ⇒ ...a place of no imaginable strategic value.
