stalwart

(ˈstɔːlwət )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. strong and sturdy; robust2. solid, dependable, and courageous ⇒ stalwart citizens3. resolute and firmnoun4. a stalwart person, esp a supporter

Derived Forms

stalwartly (ˈstalwartly) adverbstalwartness (ˈstalwartness) noun

Word Origin

Old English stǣlwirthe serviceable, from stǣl, shortened from stathol support + wierthe worth1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA stalwart is a loyal worker or supporter of an organization, especially a political party. ⇒ His free-trade policies aroused suspicion among Tory stalwarts. ⇒ Moving to Germany, he became a stalwart of the revered Kurt Edelhagen Orchestra.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A stalwart supporter or worker is loyal, steady, and completely reliable. ⇒ ...a stalwart supporter of the colonial government. ⇒ The stalwart volunteers marched in this morning ready to go to work.3. adjective [ADJ n]A stalwart man is strong. [literary, or old-fashioned] ⇒ I knew I was never in any danger with my stalwart bodyguard around me.
