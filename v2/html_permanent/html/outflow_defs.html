outflow

(ˈaʊtˌfləʊ )
Definitions
noun1. anything that flows out, such as liquid, money, ideas, etc2. the amount that flows out3. the act or process of flowing out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

outflow for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊtfloʊ )
Word forms: outflows
Definitions
countable nounWhen there is an outflow of money or people, a large amount of money or people move from one place to another. OPPOSITE: inflow [+ of] ⇒ There was a net outflow of about £650m in short-term capital. [+ of] ⇒ ...an increasing outflow of refugees.
