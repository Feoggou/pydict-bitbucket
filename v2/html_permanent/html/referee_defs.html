referee

(ˌrɛfəˈriː )
Definitions
noun1. a person to whom reference is made, esp for an opinion, information, or a decision2. the umpire or judge in any of various sports, esp football and boxing, responsible for ensuring fair play according to the rules3. a person who is willing to testify to the character or capabilities of someone4. law See Official Refereeverb -ees, -eeing or -eed5. to act as a referee (in); preside (over)

referee

Powered by COBUILD
(refəriː ) for learners of English
Word forms: referees , refereeing , refereed
Definitions
1. countable nounThe referee is the official who controls a sports event such as a football game or a boxing match.2. verbWhen someone referees a sports event or contest, they act as referee. [be V-ed] ⇒ The match will be refereed by Derek Bevan from Wales. [V] ⇒ Vautrot has refereed in two World Cups.3. countable nounA referee is a person who gives you a reference, for example when you are applying for a job. [mainly British]regional note:   in AM, use reference
