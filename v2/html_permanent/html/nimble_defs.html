nimble

(ˈnɪmbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. agile, quick, and neat in movement ⇒ nimble fingers2. alert; acute ⇒ a nimble intellect
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

nimbleness (ˈnimbleness) nounnimbly (ˈnimbly) adverb

Word Origin

Old English nǣmel quick to grasp, and numol quick at seizing, both from niman to take

nimble for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪmbəl )
Word forms: nimbler , nimblest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is nimble is able to move their fingers, hands, or legs quickly and easily. ⇒ Everything had been stitched by Molly's nimble fingers. [+ on] ⇒ Val, who was light and nimble on her feet, learnt to dance the tango.nimbly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Sabrina jumped nimbly out of the van. ⇒ Uncle George quickly descended the ladder and nimbly stepped aboard.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone has a nimble mind, you mean they are clever and can think very quickly. alert ⇒ A nimble mind backed by a degree in economics gave him a firm grasp of financial matters. ⇒ Elderly people are told that if they want to keep their minds nimble, they must use them.
