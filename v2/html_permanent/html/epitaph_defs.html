epitaph

(ˈɛpɪˌtɑːf , -ˌtæf)
Definitions
noun1. a commemorative inscription on a tombstone or monument2. a speech or written passage composed in commemoration of a dead person3. a final judgment on a person or thing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

epitaphic (ˌɛpɪˈtæfɪk ) adjectiveepitaphist (ˈepiˌtaphist) noun

Word Origin

C14: via Latin from Greek epitaphion, from epitaphios over a tomb, from epi- + taphos tomb
