awry

(əˈraɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb, adjective (postpositive)1. with a slant or twist to one side; askew2. away from the appropriate or right course; amiss
Word origin
C14 on wry; see a-2, wry

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If something goes awry, it does not happen in the way it was planned. ⇒ She was in a fury over a plan that had gone awry.2. graded adjective [v-link ADJ]If something is awry, it is not in its normal or proper position. [written] askew ⇒ His dark hair was all awry.
