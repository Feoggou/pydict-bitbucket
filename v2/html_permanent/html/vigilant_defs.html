vigilant

(ˈvɪdʒɪlənt )
Definitions
adjectivekeenly alert to or heedful of trouble or danger, as while others are sleeping or unsuspicious
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vigilantly (ˈvigilantly) adverbvigilantness (ˈvigilantness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin vigilāns keeping awake, from vigilāre to be watchful; see vigil

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is vigilant gives careful attention to a particular problem or situation and concentrates on noticing any danger or trouble that there might be. ⇒ He warned the public to be vigilant and report anything suspicious. ⇒ All but one of these letter bombs had been intercepted by vigilant post office staff.vigilance uncount noun ⇒ Drugs are a problem that requires constant vigilance.
