ludicrous

(ˈluːdɪkrəs )
Definitions
adjectiveabsurd or incongruous to the point of provoking ridicule or laughter
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ludicrously (ˈludicrously) adverbludicrousness (ˈludicrousness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin lūdicrus done in sport, from lūdus game; related to lūdere to play

ludicrous

Powered by COBUILD
(luːdɪkrəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as ludicrous, you are emphasizing that you think it is foolish, unreasonable, or unsuitable. [emphasis] ⇒ It was ludicrous to suggest that the visit could be kept secret. ⇒ It's a completely ludicrous idea.ludicrously graded adverb & adverb ⇒ By Western standards the prices are ludicrously low.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
