provost

(ˈprɒvəst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an appointed person who superintends or presides2. the head of certain university colleges or schools3. (in Scotland) the chairman and civic head of certain district councils or (formerly) of a burgh council. Compare convener (sense 2)4. Church of England the senior dignitary of one of the more recent cathedral foundations5. Roman Catholic Churcha. the head of a cathedral chapter in England and some other countriesb. (formerly) the member of a monastic community second in authority under the abbot6. (in medieval times) an overseer, steward, or bailiff in a manor7. obsolete a prison warder8. (prəˈvəʊ) British and Canadian military a military policeman

Word Origin

Old English profost head of a chapter, reinforced in Middle English by Anglo-Norman French provost, from Medieval Latin prōpositus, synonym of Latin praepositus chief, head
