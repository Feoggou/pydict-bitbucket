shilling

(ˈʃɪlɪŋ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a former British and Australian silver or cupronickel coin worth one twentieth of a pound: not minted in Britain since 1970. Abbreviation: s, sh2. the standard monetary unit of Kenya, Somalia, Tanzania, and Uganda: divided into 100 cents3. an old monetary unit of the US varying in value in different states4. (in combination) Scottish an indication of the strength and character of a beer, referring to the price after duty that was formerly paid per barrel ⇒ sixty-shilling. Symbol: /-

Word Origin

Old English scilling; related to Old Norse skillingr, Gothic skilliggs, Old High German skilling
