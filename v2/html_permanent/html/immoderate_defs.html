immoderate

(ɪˈmɒdərɪt , ɪˈmɒdrɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking in moderation; excessive ⇒ immoderate demands2. obsolete venial; intemperate ⇒ immoderate habits
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

immoderately (imˈmoderately) adverbimmoderation (imˌmoderˈation) or immoderateness (imˈmoderateness) noun

(ɪmɒdərət ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as immoderate, you disapprove of it because it is too extreme. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ He launched an immoderate tirade on Turner.
