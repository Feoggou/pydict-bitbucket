tied

(taɪd )
Definitions
adjective British1. (of a public house, retail shop, etc) obliged to sell only the beer, products, etc, of a particular producer ⇒ a tied house, ⇒ tied outlet2. (of a house or cottage) rented out to the tenant for as long as he or she is employed by the owner3. (of a loan) made by one nation to another on condition that the money is spent on goods or services provided by the lending nation
