snick

(snɪk )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small cut; notch2. a knot in thread, etc3. cricketa. a glancing blow off the edge of the batb. the ball so hitverb (transitive)4. to cut a small corner or notch in (material, etc)5. cricket to hit (the ball) with a snick

Word Origin

C18: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse snikka to whittle, Swedish snicka

As he spoke, Annie thought she heard a noise from behind her, a quiet snick like the lifting of the latch on the door.
