loaf1

(ləʊf )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural loaves (ləʊvz )1. a shaped mass of baked bread2. any shaped or moulded mass of food, such as cooked meat3. slang the head; sense ⇒ use your loaf!
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hlāf; related to Old High German hleib bread, Old Norse hleifr, Latin libum cake

loaf2

(ləʊf )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to loiter or lounge around in an idle way2. (transitive; foll by away) to spend (time) idly ⇒ he loafed away his life
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: perhaps back formation from loafer

loaf

Powered by COBUILD
(loʊf ) for learners of English
Word forms: loaves , loafs, loafing, loafed
Definitions
language note:   The plural form of the noun is loaves. Loafs is the third person singular present tense of the verb.1. countable nounA loaf of bread is bread which has been shaped and baked in one piece. It is usually large enough for more than one person and can be cut into slices. [+ of] ⇒ ...a loaf of crusty bread. ⇒ ...freshly baked loaves.2. verbIf you loaf, you stand or wait in a place, not doing anything interesting or useful. hang around [V] ⇒ Soldiers loafed at street corners.Loaf around means the same as loaf. [V P] ⇒ We had been at Cambridge together, she studying medicine and me loafing around.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
