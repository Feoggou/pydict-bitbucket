civilization

or civilisation (ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a human society that has highly developed material and spiritual resources and a complex cultural, political, and legal organization; an advanced state in social development2. the peoples or nations collectively who have achieved such a state3. the total culture and way of life of a particular people, nation, region, or period ⇒ classical civilization4. the process of bringing or achieving civilization5. intellectual, cultural, and moral refinement6. cities or populated areas, as contrasted with sparsely inhabited areas, deserts, etc

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use civilisation1. variable nounA civilization is a human society with its own social organization and culture. ⇒ The ancient civilizations of Central and Latin America were founded upon corn. ⇒ It seemed to him that western civilization was in grave economic and cultural danger.2. uncount nounCivilization is the state of having an advanced level of social organization and a comfortable way of life. ⇒ ...our advanced state of civilisation.3. uncount nounYou can refer to a place where you can enjoy the comforts that you consider to be necessary as civilization. ⇒ ...when I returned to civilization.
