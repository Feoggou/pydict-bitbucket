unpack

(ʌnˈpæk )
Definitions
verb1. to remove the packed contents of (a case, trunk, etc)2. (transitive) to take (something) out of a packed container3. (transitive) to remove a pack from; unload ⇒ to unpack a mule4. (transitive) to explain (a question, issue, etc) by analysing its component parts

unpack for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnpæk )
Word forms: unpacks , unpacking , unpacked
Definitions
1. verbWhen you unpack a suitcase, box, or similar container, or you unpack the things inside it, you take the things out of the container. [V n] ⇒ He unpacked his bag. [V n] ⇒ Our guide unpacked a picnic of ham sandwiches and offered us tea. [Also V]2. verbIf you unpack an idea or problem, you analyse it and consider it in detail. [V n] ⇒ A lot of ground has been covered in unpacking the issues central to achieving this market-led strategic change.
