gloaming

(ˈɡləʊmɪŋ ) or gloam (ɡləʊm)
Definitions
nounpoetic twilight or dusk
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English glōmung, from glōm; related to Old Norse glāmr moon
Example sentences containing 'gloaming'
In front of him lights sprang out in the gloaming ; dogs barked; feet came running. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSOnly a glimpse, as through a tall window, could be seen of the great valley in the gloaming below. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSIn the gloaming of the studio wings, Barry Twist and I watched him go. Tony Parsons MAN AND WIFEA little light entered the room from the gloaming outside the window. Neil Gaiman AMERICAN GODS

Similar Words

twilight, dusk, nightfall, half-light, eventide (archaic)
