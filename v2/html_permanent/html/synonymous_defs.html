synonymous

(sɪˈnɒnɪməs )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (often foll by with) being a synonym (of)2. (postpositive; foll by with) closely associated (with) or suggestive (of) ⇒ his name was synonymous with greed

Derived Forms

synonymously (synˈonymously) adverbsynonymousness (synˈonymousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you say that one thing is synonymous with another, you mean that the two things are very closely associated with each other so that one suggests the other or one cannot exist without the other. [+ with] ⇒ Paris has always been synonymous with elegance, luxury and style. ⇒ Going grey is not necessarily synonymous with growing old. ⇒ In politics, power and popularity are not synonymous.
