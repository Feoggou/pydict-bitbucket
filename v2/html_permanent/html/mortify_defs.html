mortify

(ˈmɔːtɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied1. (transitive) to humiliate or cause to feel shame2. (transitive) Christianity to subdue and bring under control by self-denial, disciplinary exercises, etc3. (intransitive) to undergo tissue death or become gangrenous

Derived Forms

mortiˌfier (ˈmortiˌfier) nounmortiˌfying (ˈmortiˌfying) adjectivemortiˌfyingly (ˈmortiˌfyingly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Church Latin mortificāre to put to death, from Latin mors death + facere to do
