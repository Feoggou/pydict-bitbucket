catechism

(ˈkætɪˌkɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. instruction by a series of questions and answers, esp a book containing such instruction on the religious doctrine of a Christian Church2. rigorous and persistent questioning, as in a test or interview
Derived forms
ˌcatechismal (ˌcateˈchismal) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin catēchismus, ultimately from Greek katēkhizein to catechize

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu sing]In a Catholic, Episcopal, or Orthodox Church, the catechism is a series of questions and answers about religious beliefs, which has to be learned by people before they can become full members of that Church.
