entirely

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntaɪəʳli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV adj] [ADV with v]Entirely means completely and not just partly. ⇒ ...an entirely new approach. ⇒ Fraud is an entirely different matter. ⇒ Their price depended almost entirely on their scarcity. ⇒ I failed in my career as a writer of fiction entirely because of deficiencies in the education system.2. adverb [ADV with v]Entirely is also used to emphasize what you are saying. [emphasis] ⇒ I agree entirely. ⇒ Oh, the whole episode was entirely his fault.3. adverb [not ADV, ADV group]People sometimes use the expression not entirely to reduce the force of a strong statement, especially a critical one. [vagueness] ⇒ They are not entirely happy with his criticism of the president. ⇒ We shall see that this is not entirely true. ⇒ She claimed the unemployment figures were not entirely unexpected. ⇒ This government is not entirely free of suspicion.
