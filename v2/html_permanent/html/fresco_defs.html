fresco

(ˈfrɛskəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -coes or -cos1. a very durable method of wall-painting using watercolours on wet plaster or, less properly, dry plaster (fresco secco), with a less durable result2. a painting done in this way
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Italian: fresh plaster, coolness, from fresco (adj) fresh, cool, of Germanic origin
