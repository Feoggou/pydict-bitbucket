jewel

(ˈdʒuːəl )
Definitions
noun1. a precious or semiprecious stone; gem2. a person or thing resembling a jewel in preciousness, brilliance, etc3. a gemstone, often synthetically produced, used as a bearing in a watch4. a piece of jewellery5. an ornamental glass boss, sometimes faceted, used in stained glasswork6. See jewel in the crownverb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled7. (transitive) to fit or decorate with a jewel or jewels
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jewelled (ˈjewelled) or US jeweled (ˈjeweled) adjectivejewel-like (ˈjewel-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French jouel, perhaps from jeu game, from Latin jocus

jewel for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒuːəl )
Word forms: jewels
Definitions
1. countable nounA jewel is a precious stone used to decorate valuable things that you wear, such as rings or necklaces. ⇒ ...a golden box containing precious jewels.2. → See also crown jewel3. countable nounIf you describe something or someone as a jewel, you mean that they are better, more beautiful, or more special than other similar things or than other people. [+ of] ⇒ Walk down Castle Street and admire our little jewel of a cathedral. ⇒ Alan, you're a jewel.4. →  jewel in sb's crown
