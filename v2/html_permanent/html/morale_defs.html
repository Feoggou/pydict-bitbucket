morale

(mɒˈrɑːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe degree of mental or moral confidence of a person or group; spirit of optimism

Word Origin

C18: morals, from French, n. use of moral (adj)

morale

Powered by COBUILD
(mərɑːl , -ræl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [oft with poss]Morale is the amount of confidence and cheerfulness that a group of people have. ⇒ Many pilots are suffering from low morale. ⇒ They hope to boost the morale of their troops.
Example sentences containing 'morale'
Women have very decided views on paradise, thinking it bad for morale. Gash, Jonathan THE TARTAN RINGERSIt had been Spaghetti House without negotiation, just a breakdown of morale. Kippax, Frank THE SCARI shall join her there after Dublin to boost the morale of the security forces. Forbes, Bryan A SONG AT TWILIGHTNews of the Nimrod jailbreak had had a stunning effect on Arab morale. Patrick Robinson BARRACUDA 945
