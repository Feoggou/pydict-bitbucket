moat

(məʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a wide water-filled ditch surrounding a fortified place, such as a castleverb2. (transitive) to surround with or as if with a moat

Word Origin

C14: from Old French motte mound

moat

Powered by COBUILD
(moʊt )
Word forms: moats
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA moat is a deep, wide channel dug round a place such as a castle and filled with water, in order to protect the place from attack.

The Countess gathered up her skirts and tripped along the bridge over the moat.
