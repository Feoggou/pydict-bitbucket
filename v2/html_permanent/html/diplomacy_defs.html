diplomacy

(dɪˈpləʊməsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cies1. the conduct of the relations of one state with another by peaceful means2. skill in the management of international relations3. tact, skill, or cunning in dealing with people
Word origin
C18: from French diplomatie, from diplomatique diplomatic

diplomacy

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪploʊməsi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounDiplomacy is the activity or profession of managing relations between the governments of different countries. ⇒ Today's Security Council resolution will be a significant success for American diplomacy. ⇒ The talks have now gone into a stage of quiet diplomacy.2. → see also shuttle diplomacy3. uncount nounDiplomacy is the skill of being careful to say or do things which will not offend people. ⇒ He stormed off in a fury, and it took all Minnelli's powers of diplomacy to get him to return.
