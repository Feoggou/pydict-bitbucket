amalgam

(əˈmælɡəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an alloy of mercury with another metal, esp with silver ⇒ dental amalgam2. a rare white metallic mineral that consists of silver and mercury and occurs in deposits of silver and cinnabar3. a blend or combination
Word origin
C15: from Medieval Latin amalgama, of obscure origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounSomething that is an amalgam of two or more things is a mixture of them. 2. uncount noun [oft N n]Amalgam is a mixture of mercury and another metal, usually silver, that is used in dentistry to make fillings. ⇒ ...amalgam fillings.
