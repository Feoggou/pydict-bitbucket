waterline

(ˈwɔːtəˌlaɪn)
Definitions
noun1. a line marked at the level around a vessel's hull to which the vessel will be immersed when afloat2. a line marking the level reached by a body of water
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]The waterline is a line, either real or imaginary, on the side of a ship representing the level the water reaches when the ship is at sea. ⇒ Ray painted below the waterline with a special anti-rust paint.
