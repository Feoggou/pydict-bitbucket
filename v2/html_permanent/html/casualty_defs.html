casualty

(ˈkæʒjʊəltɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. a serviceman or servicewoman who is killed, wounded, captured, or missing as a result of enemy action2. a person who is injured or killed in an accident3. a hospital department in which victims of accidents, violence, etc, are treated4. anything that is lost, damaged, or destroyed as the result of an accident, etc

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA casualty is a person who is injured or killed in a war or in an accident. ⇒ Troops fired on demonstrators near the Royal Palace causing many casualties.2. countable nounA casualty of a particular event or situation is a person or a thing that has suffered badly as a result of that event or situation. [+ of] ⇒ Fiat has been one of the greatest casualties of the recession.3. uncount nounCasualty is the part of a hospital where people who have severe injuries or sudden illnesses are taken for emergency treatment.
