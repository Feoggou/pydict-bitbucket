ornament

Definitions
noun (ˈɔːnəmənt )1. anything that enhances the appearance of a person or thing2. decorations collectively ⇒ she was totally without ornament3. a small decorative object4. something regarded as a source of pride or beauty5. music any of several decorations, such as the trill, mordent, etc, occurring chiefly as improvised embellishments in baroque musicverb (ˈɔːnəˌmɛnt ) (transitive)6. to decorate with or as if with ornaments7. to serve as an ornament to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ornamentation (ˌornamenˈtation) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin ornāmentum, from ornāre to adorn

ornament for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ɔːʳnəmənt )
Word forms: ornaments
Definitions
1. countable nounAn ornament is an attractive object that you display in your home or in your garden. ⇒ ...a shelf containing a few photographs and ornaments. ⇒ ...Christmas tree ornaments.2. countable noun [usu with supp]Pieces of jewellery are sometimes referred to as ornaments. [formal] ⇒ I guessed he was the chief because he wore more gold ornaments than the others.3. uncount nounDecorations and patterns on a building or a piece of furniture can be referred to as ornament. [formal] ⇒ ...walls of glass overlaid with ornament.
