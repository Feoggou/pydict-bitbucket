matins

or mattins (ˈmætɪnz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (functioning as singular or plural)1. a. mainly Roman Catholic Church the first of the seven canonical hours of prayer, originally observed at night but now often recited with lauds at daybreakb. the service of morning prayer in the Church of England2. literary a morning song, esp of birds

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, ultimately from Latin mātūtīnus of the morning, from Mātūta goddess of dawn
