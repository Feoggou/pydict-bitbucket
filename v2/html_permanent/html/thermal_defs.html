thermal

(ˈθɜːməl )
Definitions
adjective1. Also: thermic (ˈθɜːmɪk ) of, relating to, caused by, or generating heat or increased temperature2. hot or warm ⇒ thermal baths, ⇒ thermal spring3. (of garments or fabrics) specially designed so as to have exceptional heat-retaining propertiesnoun4. meteorology a column of rising air caused by local unequal heating of the land surface, and used by gliders and birds to gain height5. (plural) thermal garments, esp underclothes

Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Thermal means relating to or caused by heat or by changes in temperature. ⇒ ...thermal power stations. ⇒ ...financial assistance with repair, thermal insulation and improvements to homes through Government grants.2. adjective [ADJ n]Thermal streams or baths contain water which is naturally hot or warm. ⇒ Volcanic activity has created thermal springs and boiling mud pools.3. adjective [ADJ n]Thermal clothes are specially designed to keep you warm in cold weather. ⇒ My feet were like blocks of ice despite the thermal socks. ⇒ I put on my thermal leggings, long socks and the rest of my clothes.Thermals are thermal clothes. ⇒ Have you got your thermals on?4. countable nounA thermal is a movement of rising warm air. ⇒ Birds use thermals to lift them through the air.
