ballad

(ˈbæləd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a narrative song with a recurrent refrain2. a narrative poem in short stanzas of popular origin, originally sung to a repeated tune3. a slow sentimental song, esp a pop song
Word origin
C15: from Old French balade, from Old Provençal balada song accompanying a dance, from balar to dance, from Late Latin ballāre; see ball2

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA ballad is a long song or poem which tells a story in simple language. ⇒ ...an eighteenth century ballad about some lost children called the Babes in the Wood.2. countable nounA ballad is a slow, romantic, popular song. ⇒ 'You Don't Know Paris' is one of the most beautiful ballads that he ever wrote.
