military

(ˈmɪlɪtərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to the armed forces (esp the army), warlike matters, etc2. of, characteristic of, or about soldiersnoun plural -taries or -tary3. See the military

Derived Forms

militarily (ˈmilitarily) adverb

Word Origin

C16: via French from Latin mīlitāris, from mīles soldier

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Military means relating to the armed forces of a country. ⇒ Military action may become necessary. ⇒ The president is sending in almost 20,000 military personnel to help with the relief efforts. ⇒ ...last year's military coup.militarily (mɪlteərɪli ) adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ They remain unwilling to intervene militarily in what could be an unending war. ⇒ While that option would incur fewer casualties, it would not be militarily effective.2. adjective [ADJ n]Military means relating to or belonging to the army, rather than to the navy or the air force. ⇒ The attack has caused severe damage to American naval and military forces.3. countable noun [with sing or pl verb] [usu sing]The military are the armed forces of a country, especially officers of high rank. ⇒ The bombing has been far more widespread than the military will admit. ⇒ Did you serve in the military?4. graded adjective & adjectiveMilitary means well-organized, controlled, or neat, in a way that is typical of a soldier. ⇒ Your working day will need to be organized with military precision. ⇒ He has a military bearing, never failing to carry himself erect.
