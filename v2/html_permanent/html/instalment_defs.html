instalment

or US installment (ɪnˈstɔːlmənt )
Definitions
noun1. one of the portions, usually equal, into which a debt is divided for payment at specified intervals over a fixed period2. a portion of something that is issued, broadcast, or published in parts, such as a serial in a magazine3. a variant of installation (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from obsolete estallment, probably from Old French estaler to fix, hence to agree rate of payment, from estal something fixed, place, from Old High German stal stall1
