gloat

(ɡləʊt )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive; often foll by over) to dwell (on) with malevolent smugness or exultationnoun2. the act of gloating
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gloater (ˈgloater) noun

Word Origin

C16: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse glotta to grin, Middle High German glotzen to stare

gloat

Powered by COBUILD
(gloʊt ) for learners of English
Word forms: gloats , gloating , gloated
Definitions
verbIf someone is gloating, they are showing pleasure at their own success or at other people's failure in an arrogant and unpleasant way. [disapproval] [V + over] ⇒ Anti-abortionists are gloating over the court's decision. [V + about] ⇒ This is nothing to gloat about. [Also V]
