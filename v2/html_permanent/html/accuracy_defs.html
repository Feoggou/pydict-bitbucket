accuracy

(ˈækjʊrəsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cies1. faithful measurement or representation of the truth; correctness; precision2. physics, chemistry the degree of agreement between a measured or computed value of a physical quantity and the standard or accepted value for that quantity

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe accuracy of information or measurements is their quality of being true or correct, even in small details. [+ of] ⇒ We cannot guarantee the accuracy of these figures.2. uncount noun [oft with N]If someone or something performs a task, for example hitting a target, with accuracy, they do it in an exact way without making a mistake. ⇒ ...weapons that could fire with accuracy at targets 3,000 yards away. ⇒ Every bank pays close attention to the speed and accuracy of its staff.

accuracy (accuracies) n. (ˈækjʊrəsɪ). 1. the quality of being near to the true value. 2. (mathematics) the number of significant figures given in a number. 3. the quality or state of being correct or precise; the ability to do something skillfully without making mistakes; •correctness, precision, exactness, factuality, faithfulness, truthfulness, •truth.
