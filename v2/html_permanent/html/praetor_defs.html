praetor

or pretor (ˈpriːtə , -tɔː)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun(in ancient Rome) any of several senior magistrates ranking just below the consuls

Derived Forms

praetorial (praeˈtorial) or pretorial (preˈtorial) adjectivepraetorship (ˈpraetorship) or pretorship (ˈpretorship) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin: one who leads the way, probably from praeīre, from prae- before + īre to go
