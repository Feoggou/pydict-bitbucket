lair1

(lɛə )
Definitions
noun1. the resting place of a wild animal2. informal a place of seclusion or hiding3. an enclosure or shed for farm animals4. Scottish the ground for a grave in a cemeteryverb5. (intransitive) (esp of a wild animal) to retreat to or rest in a lair6. (transitive) to drive or place (an animal) in a lair
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English leger; related to lie2 and Old High German leger bed

lair

Powered by COBUILD
(leəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: lairs
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu with poss]A lair is a place where a wild animal lives, usually a place which is underground or well-hidden. ⇒ ...a fox's lair.2. countable noun [usu with poss]Someone's lair is the particular room or hiding place that they go to, especially when they want to get away from other people. [informal] ⇒ Green recounts how he once went to see Bremner in his lair. ⇒ The village was once a pirates' lair.
