derogate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt )1. (intransitive; foll by from) to cause to seem inferior or be in disrepute; detract2. (intransitive; foll by from) to deviate in standard or quality; degenerate3. (transitive) to cause to seem inferior, etc; disparage4. (transitive) to curtail the application of (a law or regulation)adjective (ˈdɛrəɡɪt , -ˌɡeɪt)5. archaic debased or degraded
Derived forms
derogately (ˈderogately) adverbˌderogation (ˌderoˈgation) nounderogative (dɪˈrɒɡətɪv ) adjectivederogatively (deˈrogatively) adverb
Word origin
C15: from Latin dērogāre to repeal some part of a law, modify it, from de- + rogāre to ask, propose a law

Similar Words

detract, compromise, diminish, run down, lessen, disparage, depreciate, cheapen, devaluate
