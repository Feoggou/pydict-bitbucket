prickly

(ˈprɪklɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -lier or -liest1. having or covered with prickles2. stinging or tingling3. bad-tempered or irritable4. full of difficulties; knotty ⇒ a prickly problem

Derived Forms

prickliness (ˈprickliness) noun

prickly

Powered by COBUILD
(prɪkəli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is prickly feels rough and uncomfortable, as if it has a lot of prickles. ⇒ The bunk mattress was hard, the blankets prickly and slightly damp. ⇒ The grass was prickly and cold.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is prickly loses their temper or gets upset very easily. ⇒ You know how prickly she is.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA prickly issue or subject is one that is rather complicated and difficult to discuss or resolve. ⇒ The issue is likely to prove a prickly one.
Example sentences containing 'prickly'
Having done so amounted to self-flagellation with a very prickly scourge. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
