capitulation

(kəˌpɪtjʊˈleɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of capitulating2. a document containing terms of surrender3. a statement summarizing the main divisions of a subject
Derived forms
capitulatory (caˈpitulatory) adjective
Example sentences containing 'capitulation'
It wasn't that she'd expected instant capitulation from Radecki. Val McDermid THE LAST TEMPTATIONHe got killed in the first German advance, towards Lille, before the capitulation. Malcolm, John THE GWEN JOHN SCULPTUREThough the pain he felt mounted with each successive blow the scene around him offered no sign of capitulation. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOWTim held up his hands, a gesture of patience and capitulation. Gregg Hurwitz THE KILL CLAUSE
