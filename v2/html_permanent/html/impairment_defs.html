(ɪmpɛərmənt) in Insurance

Definitions
noun(Insurance: Medical insurance) An impairment is a condition in which a part of a person's mind or body is damaged or is not working properly. ⇒ A person with a disability is defined as a person who has a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. ⇒ Enhanced annuities are likely to be weighted towards exposure to a handful of impairments, particularly cardiovascular disease and conditions related to smoking. ⇒ An impairment is a condition in which a part of a person's mind or body is damaged or is not working properly. ▶  mental impairment, physical impairment
