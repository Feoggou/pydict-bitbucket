averse

(əˈvɜːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (postpositive; usually foll by to) opposed, disinclined, or loath2. (of leaves, flowers, etc) turned away from the main stem. Compare adverse (sense 4)
Derived forms
aˈversely adverbaˈverseness noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin āversus, from āvertere to turn from, from vertere to turn

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu with neg]If you say that you are not averse to something, you mean that you quite like it or quite want to do it. [formal] [+ to] ⇒ He's not averse to publicity, of the right kind.

Mark had only said `find her ', but Alice was not averse to doing a bit of investigating on her own and Lyndsey intrigued her.
