whisk

(wɪsk )
Definitions
verb1. (tr; often foll by away or off) to brush, sweep, or wipe off lightly2. (transitive) to move, carry, etc, with a light or rapid sweeping motion ⇒ the taxi whisked us to the airport3. (intransitive) to move, go, etc, quickly and nimbly ⇒ to whisk downstairs for a drink4. (transitive) to whip (eggs, cream, etc) to a frothnoun5. the act of whisking6. a light rapid sweeping movement or stroke7. a utensil, often incorporating a coil of wires, for whipping eggs, etc8. a small brush or broom9. a small bunch or bundle, as of grass, straw, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse visk wisp; related to Middle Dutch wisch, Old High German wisc

whisk for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪsk )
Word forms: whisks , whisking , whisked
Definitions
1. verbIf you whisk someone or something somewhere, you take them or move them there quickly. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He whisked her across the dance floor. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ I was whisked away in a police car.2. verbIf you whisk something such as eggs or cream, you stir it very fast, often with an electric device, so that it becomes full of small bubbles. [V n] ⇒ Just before serving, whisk the cream. [V-ed] ⇒ Gently fold in the whisked egg whites. [V n with together] ⇒ In a separate bowl, whisk together the remaining sugar and the yolks.3. countable nounA whisk is a kitchen tool used for whisking eggs or cream. ⇒ Using a whisk, mix the yolks and sugar to a smooth paste. ⇒ ...an electric whisk.
