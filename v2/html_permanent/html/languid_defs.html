languid

(ˈlæŋɡwɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. without energy or spirit2. without interest or enthusiasm3. sluggish; inactive
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

languidly (ˈlanguidly) adverblanguidness (ˈlanguidness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin languidus, from languēre to languish

languid

Powered by COBUILD
(læŋgwɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as languid, you mean that they show little energy or interest and are very slow and casual in their movements. [literary] ⇒ To his delight a familiar, tall, languid figure lowered itself down the steps of a club. ⇒ Time spent at Jumby Bay can be as energetic or as languid as you wish.languidly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ We sat about languidly after dinner. ⇒ A tanned blonde in a bikini swims languidly in the clear swimming pool.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
