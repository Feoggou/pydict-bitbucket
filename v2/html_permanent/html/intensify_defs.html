intensify

(ɪnˈtɛnsɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
verb -fies, -fying or -fied1. to make or become intense or more intense2. (transitive) to increase the density of (a photographic film or plate)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intensification (inˌtensifiˈcation) noun

intensify

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntensɪfaɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: intensifies , intensifying , intensified
Definitions
ergative verb & verbIf you intensify something or if it intensifies, it becomes greater in strength, amount, or degree. [V n] ⇒ Britain is intensifying its efforts to secure the release of the hostages. [V] ⇒ The conflict is almost bound to intensify. [V-ed] ⇒ Groups of refugees are on the move following intensified fighting in the region.intensification (ɪntensɪfɪkeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ The country was on the verge of collapse because of the intensification of violent rebel attacks.
