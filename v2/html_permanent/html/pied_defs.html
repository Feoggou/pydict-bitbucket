pied

(paɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivehaving markings of two or more colours

Word Origin

C14: from pie2; an allusion to the magpie's black-and-white colouring
Example sentences containing 'pied'
When there was money she was often in London in the small pied à terre they had in Knightsbridge. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENAs she vanished over the top of the wall, the privy door flew open and Godfrey burst out wearing full armour, cap à pied. Fidelis Morgan THE RIVAL QUEENS: A COUNTESS ASHBY DE LA ZOUCHE MYSTERY
