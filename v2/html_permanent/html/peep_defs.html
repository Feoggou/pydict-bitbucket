peep1

(piːp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to look furtively or secretly, as through a small aperture or from a hidden place2. to appear partially or briefly ⇒ the sun peeped through the cloudsnoun3. a quick or furtive look4. the first appearance ⇒ the peep of dawn

Word Origin

C15: variant of peek

peep2

(piːp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (esp of young birds) to utter shrill small noises2. to speak in a thin shrill voicenoun3. a peeping sound4. US any of various small sandpipers of the genus Calidris (or Erolia) and related genera, such as the pectoral sandpiper

Word Origin

C15: of imitative origin

peep

Powered by COBUILD
(piːp )
Word forms: peeps , peeping , peeped
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you peep, or peep at something, you have a quick look at it, often secretly and quietly. [V + at] ⇒ Children came to peep at him round the doorway. [V] ⇒ Now and then she peeped to see if he was noticing her.Peep is also a noun. [+ at] ⇒ 'Fourteen minutes,' Chris said, taking a peep at his watch.2. verbIf something peeps out from behind or under something, a small part of it is visible or becomes visible. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Purple and yellow flowers peeped up between rocks. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Here and there a face peeped out from the shop doorway.3. →  hear a peep
