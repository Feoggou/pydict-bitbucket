presentiment

(prɪˈzɛntɪmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna sense of something about to happen; premonition

Word Origin

C18: from obsolete French, from pressentir to sense beforehand; see pre-, sentiment

presentiment

Powered by COBUILD
(prɪzentɪmənt )
Word forms: presentiments
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu N that]A presentiment is a feeling that a particular event, for example someone's death, will soon take place. [formal] ⇒ I had a presentiment that he represented a danger to me. [+ of] ⇒ He had a presentiment of disaster.
