Watt

(wɒt )
Definitions
noun James. 1736–1819, Scottish engineer and inventor. His fundamental improvements to the steam engine led to the widespread use of steam power in industry
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

watt

(wɒt )
Definitions
nounthe derived SI unit of power, equal to 1 joule per second; the power dissipated by a current of 1 ampere flowing across a potential difference of 1 volt. 1 watt is equivalent to 1.341 × 10–3 horsepower. Symbol: W
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: named after James Watt

watt in Electrical Engineering

Powered by COBUILD
(wɒt) or W
Word forms: (regular plural) watts
Definitions
noun(Electrical engineering: General) A watt is a unit of power, equal to a power rate of one joule of work per second of time. ⇒ A 100 watt amplifier would have to be reduced to 10 in order for an audio perception of a sound level to be cut in half. ⇒ The average person in the US consumes 60 barrels of oil per year and on average this is 10,000 watts of power consumption. ⇒ A watt is a unit of power, equal to a power rate of one joule of work per second of time.Word originsThis word is named for James Watt (1736-1819), the Scottish engineer.
