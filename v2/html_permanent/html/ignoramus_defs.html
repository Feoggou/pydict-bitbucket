ignoramus

(ˌɪɡnəˈreɪməs )
Definitions
noun plural -musesan ignorant person; fool
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from legal Latin, literally: we have no knowledge of, from Latin ignōrāre to be ignorant of; see ignore; modern usage originated from the use of Ignoramus as the name of an unlettered lawyer in a play by G. Ruggle, 17th-century English dramatist

Definitions
countable nounIf you describe someone as an ignoramus, you are being critical of them because they do not have the knowledge you think they ought to have. [formal, disapproval] dunce
