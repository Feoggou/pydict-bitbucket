shingles

(ˈʃɪŋɡəlz )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun(functioning as singular) an acute viral disease affecting the ganglia of certain nerves, characterized by inflammation, pain, and skin eruptions along the course of the affected nerve. Technical names: herpes zoster, zoster

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin cingulum girdle, rendering Greek zōnē zone
