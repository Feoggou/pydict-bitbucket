solitude

(ˈsɒlɪˌtjuːd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state of being solitary or secluded2. poetic a solitary place

Derived Forms

ˌsolitudinous (ˌsoliˈtudinous) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin sōlitūdō, from sōlus alone, sole1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounSolitude is the state of being alone, especially when this is peaceful and pleasant. ⇒ He enjoyed his moments of solitude before the pressures of the day began. ⇒ Imagine long golden beaches where you can wander in solitude.
Example sentences containing 'solitude'
For long, long years of solitude , fear, hunger, despair, you stayed in my system.
