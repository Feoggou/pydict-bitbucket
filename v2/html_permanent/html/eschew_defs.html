eschew

(ɪsˈtʃuː )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to keep clear of or abstain from (something disliked, injurious, etc); shun; avoid
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

eschewal (esˈchewal) nouneschewer (esˈchewer) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French eschiver, of Germanic origin; compare Old High German skiuhan to frighten away; see shy1, skew

Definitions
verbIf you eschew something, you deliberately avoid doing it or becoming involved in it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Although he appeared to enjoy a jet-setting life, he eschewed publicity and avoided nightclubs.
