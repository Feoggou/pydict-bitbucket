abject

(ˈæbdʒɛkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. utterly wretched or hopeless2. miserable; forlorn; dejected3. indicating humiliation; submissive ⇒ an abject apology4. contemptible; despicable; servile ⇒ an abject liar
Derived forms
abˈjection nounˈabjectly adverbˈabjectness noun
Word origin
C14: (in the sense: rejected, cast out): from Latin abjectus thrown or cast away, from abjicere, from ab- away + jacere to throw

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use abject to emphasize that a situation or quality is extremely bad. [emphasis] ⇒ Both of them died in abject poverty. ⇒ This scheme was an abject failure.abjectly graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Both have failed abjectly.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe someone as abject, you think they have no courage or respect for themselves. [literary] ⇒ He sounded abject and eager to please. ⇒ He looked back at the abject, silent girl and repeated his question.
