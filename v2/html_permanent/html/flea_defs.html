flea

(fliː )
Definitions
noun1. any small wingless parasitic blood-sucking insect of the order Siphonaptera, living on the skin of mammals and birds and noted for its power of leaping2. any of various invertebrates that resemble fleas, such as the water flea and flea beetle3. See flea in one's ear
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flēah; related to Old Norse flō, Old High German flōh
