rocket1

(ˈrɒkɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a self-propelling device, esp a cylinder containing a mixture of solid explosives, used as a firework, distress signal, line carrier, etc2. a. any vehicle propelled by a rocket engine, esp one used to carry a warhead, spacecraft, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ rocket propulsion, ⇒ rocket launcher3. British and New Zealand informal a severe reprimand (esp in the phrase get a rocket)verb -ets, -eting or -eted4. (transitive) to propel (a missile, spacecraft, etc) by means of a rocket5. (intr; foll by off, away, etc) to move off at high speed6. (intransitive) to rise rapidly ⇒ he rocketed to the top
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Old French roquette, from Italian rochetto a little distaff, from rocca distaff, of Germanic origin

rocket2

(ˈrɒkɪt )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: arugula a Mediterranean plant, Eruca sativa, having yellowish-white flowers and leaves used as a salad: family Brassicaceae (crucifers)2. any of several plants of the related genus Sisymbrium, esp S. irio (London rocket), which grow on waste ground and have pale yellow flowers3. See yellow rocket4. See sea rocket5. See dame's rocket▷ See also dyer's rocket, wall rocket
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from French roquette, from Italian rochetta, from Latin ērūca a caterpillar, hairy plant

rocket

Powered by COBUILD
(rɒkɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: rockets , rocketing , rocketed
Definitions
1. countable nounA rocket is a space vehicle that is shaped like a long tube.2. countable noun [oft N n]A rocket is a missile containing explosive that is powered by gas. ⇒ There has been a renewed rocket attack on the capital.3. countable nounA rocket is a firework that quickly goes high into the air and then explodes.4. verbIf things such as prices or social problems rocket, they increase very quickly and suddenly. [journalism] [V] ⇒ Fresh food is so scarce that prices have rocketed. [V-ing] ⇒ The nation has experienced four years of rocketing crime.5. verbIf something such as a vehicle rockets somewhere, it moves there very quickly. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A train rocketed by, shaking the walls of the row houses. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Dublin has rocketed up the charts to become one of Europe's most popular tourist destinations for city breaks.
