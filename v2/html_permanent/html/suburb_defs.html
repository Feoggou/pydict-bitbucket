suburb

(ˈsʌbɜːb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna residential district situated on the outskirts of a city or town

Derived Forms

suburbed (ˈsuburbed) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin suburbium, from sub- close to + urbs a city

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA suburb of a city or large town is a smaller area which is part of the city or large town but is outside its centre. [+ of] ⇒ Anna was born in 1923 in Ardwick, a suburb of Manchester. ⇒ ...the north London suburbs of Harrow, Barnet and Enfield.2. plural noun [oft in the N]If you live in the suburbs, you live in an area of houses outside the centre of a large town or city. ⇒ His family lived in the suburbs. ⇒ ...Bombay's suburbs.
