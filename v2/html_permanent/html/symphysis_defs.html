symphysis

(ˈsɪmfɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. anatomy, botany a growing together of parts or structures, such as two bony surfaces joined by an intermediate layer of fibrous cartilage2. a line marking this growing together3. pathology an abnormal adhesion of two or more parts or structures
Derived forms
symphysial or symphyseal (sɪmˈfɪzɪəl ) adjectivesymphystic (sɪmˈfɪstɪk ) or symphytic (symˈphytic) adjective
Word origin
C16: via New Latin from Greek sumphusis, from sumphuein, from syn- + phuein to grow
