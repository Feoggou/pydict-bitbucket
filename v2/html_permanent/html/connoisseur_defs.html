connoisseur

(ˌkɒnɪˈsɜː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna person with special knowledge or appreciation of a field, esp in the arts
Derived forms
ˌconnoisseurship (ˌconnoisˈseurship) noun
Word origin
C18: from French, from Old French conoiseor, from connoistre to know, from Latin cognōscere

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [oft n N]A connoisseur is someone who knows a lot about the arts, food, drink, or some other subject. ⇒ Sarah tells me you're something of an art connoisseur. [+ of] ⇒ ...connoisseurs of good food.
Example sentences containing 'connoisseur'
I'm a connoisseur , I know about these things and I'd've said late thirties. Isabel Wolff RESCUING ROSEI love collecting paintings but I'd hardly call myself a connoisseur. MacNeill, Alistair ALASTAIR MCLEAN'S 'NIGHT WATCH'For a connoisseur of his highly developed, distinctly exotic tastes, she was the perfect English rose. Stephanie Laurens ON A WILD NIGHT
