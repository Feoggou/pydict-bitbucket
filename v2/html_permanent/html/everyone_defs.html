everyone

(ˈɛvrɪˌwʌn , -wən)
Definitions
pronounevery person; everybody▶ USAGE Everyone and everybody are interchangeable, as are no one and nobody, and someone and somebody. Care should be taken to distinguish between everyone as a single word and every one as two words, the latter form correctly being used to refer to each individual person or thing in a particular group: every one of them is wrong
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

everyone

Powered by COBUILD
(evriwʌn ) or everybody for learners of English
Definitions
1. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use everyone or everybody to refer to all the people in a particular group. ⇒ Everyone in the street was shocked when they heard the news. ⇒ When everyone else goes home around 5 p.m. Lynn is still hard at work. ⇒ Not everyone thinks that the government is being particularly generous.2. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use everyone or everybody to refer to all people. ⇒ Everyone feels like a failure at times. ⇒ Everyone needs some free time for rest and relaxation. ⇒ You can't keep everybody happy.
