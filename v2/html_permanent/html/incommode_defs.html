incommode

(ˌɪnkəˈməʊd )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to bother, disturb, or inconvenience
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin incommodāre to be troublesome, from incommodus inconvenient, from in-1 + commodus convenient; see commode

Similar Words

inconvenience, trouble, upset, bother, embarrass, disturb, annoy, put out, hassle (informal), hinder, impede, vex, irk, be a trouble to, get in your hair (informal), give (someone) bother or trouble, put (someone) to trouble
