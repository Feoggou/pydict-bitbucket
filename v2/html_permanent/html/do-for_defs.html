do for

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (preposition) informal1. (transitive) to convict of a crime or offence ⇒ they did him for manslaughter2. (intransitive) to cause the ruin, death, or defeat of ⇒ the last punch did for him3. (intransitive) to do housework for4. See do well for oneself

phrasal verb [usu passive]If you say that you are done for, you mean that you are in a terrible and hopeless situation. [informal] [be V-ed P] ⇒ We need his help or we're done for, dead and gone, lost. →  do
