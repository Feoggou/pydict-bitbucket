coarse

(kɔːs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. rough in texture, structure, etc; not fine ⇒ coarse sand2. lacking refinement or taste; indelicate; vulgar ⇒ coarse jokes3. of inferior quality; not pure or choice4. (of a metal) not refined5. (of a screw) having widely spaced threads
Derived forms
coarsely (ˈcoarsely) adverbcoarseness (ˈcoarseness) noun
Word origin
C14: of unknown origin

Powered by COBUILD
(kɔːʳs )
Word forms: coarser , coarsest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveCoarse things have a rough texture because they consist of thick threads or large pieces. ⇒ ...a jacket made of very coarse cloth. ⇒ ...a beach of coarse sand.coarsely adverb ⇒ ...coarsely-ground black pepper.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as coarse, you mean that he or she talks and behaves in a rude and offensive way. [disapproval] ⇒ The soldiers did not bother to moderate their coarse humour in her presence.coarsely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The women laughed coarsely at some vulgar joke.coarseness uncount noun ⇒ The coarseness of her cursing amazed the workmen.
