alabaster

(ˈæləˌbɑːstə , -ˌbæstə)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fine-grained usually white, opaque, or translucent variety of gypsum used for statues, vases, etc2. a variety of hard semitranslucent calcite, often banded like marbleadjective3. of or resembling alabaster
Derived forms
ˌalaˈbastrine adjective
Word origin
C14: from Old French alabastre, from Latin alabaster, from Greek alabastros

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [usu N n]Alabaster is a white stone that is used for making statues, vases, and ornaments. ⇒ ...four carved alabaster figures of maidens.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]If you say that someone has alabaster skin, you mean that their skin is very beautiful because it is so white and smooth. [literary] ⇒ She wore a fine chain about her alabaster neck.
