at a pinch

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseIf you say that something is possible at a pinch, or in American English if you say that something is possible in a pinch, you mean that it would be possible if it was necessary, but it might not be very comfortable or convenient. ⇒ Six people, and more at a pinch, could be seated comfortably at the table. →  pinch
Example sentences containing 'at a pinch'
You can learn all that there is to know about their ways in a month, and yet after a hundred years they can still surprise you at a pinch. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSWe shall have to share pipes, as good friends must at a pinch. J.R.R. Tolkien THE LORD OF THE RINGSThere was a man in the hospital in Murnau who could at a pinch be Bergmann. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCE
