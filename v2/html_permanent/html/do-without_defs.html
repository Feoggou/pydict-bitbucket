do without

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intr, preposition)1. to forgo; manage without ⇒ I can't do without cigarettes2. not to require (uncalled-for comments or advice) ⇒ we can do without your criticisms thank you

do without

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you do without something you need, want, or usually have, you are able to survive, continue, or succeed although you do not have it. [V P n] ⇒ We can't do without the help of your organisation. [V P] ⇒ We've had a bit more money and that, and the baby doesn't do without.2. phrasal verbIf you say that you could do without something, you mean that you would prefer not to have it or it is of no benefit to you. [informal] [V P n] ⇒ He could do without her rhetorical questions at five o'clock in the morning. [V P n] ⇒ ...those who love France but can do without the natives. [V P n] ⇒ Like all teenagers there's one thing she'd rather do without–spots. →  do
