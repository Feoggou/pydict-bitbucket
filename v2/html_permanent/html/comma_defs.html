comma

(ˈkɒmə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the punctuation mark(,) indicating a slight pause in the spoken sentence and used where there is a listing of items or to separate a nonrestrictive clause or phrase from a main clause2. music a minute interval3. short for comma butterfly
Word origin
C16: from Latin, from Greek komma clause, from koptein to cut
