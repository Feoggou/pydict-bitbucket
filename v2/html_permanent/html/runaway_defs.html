runaway

(ˈrʌnəˌweɪ)
Definitions
adjective1. denoting a vehicle or animal that is moving forward quickly, its driver or rider having lost control of it ⇒ a runaway horse, ⇒ The runaway car crashed into a bench.2. denoting a situation in which something increases or develops very quickly and cannot be controlled ⇒ in an era of runaway inflation, ⇒ Our Grand Sale in June was a runaway success., ⇒ His last book was a runaway best-seller.noun3. someone, esp a child, who leaves home without telling anyone or without permission ⇒ a teenage runaway
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

runaway

Powered by COBUILD
(rʌnəweɪ ) for learners of English
Word forms: runaways
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]You use runaway to describe a situation in which something increases or develops very quickly and cannot be controlled. ⇒ Our Grand Sale in June was a runaway success. ⇒ ...a runaway best-seller. ⇒ ...in an era of runaway inflation.2. countable noun [oft N n]A runaway is someone, especially a child, who leaves home without telling anyone or without permission. ⇒ ...a teenage runaway. ⇒ ...a runaway slave.3. adjective [ADJ n]A runaway vehicle or animal is moving forward quickly, and its driver or rider has lost control of it. ⇒ The runaway car careered into a bench, hitting an elderly couple. ⇒ The narrative pulls you along like a runaway train. ⇒ ...a runaway horse.
