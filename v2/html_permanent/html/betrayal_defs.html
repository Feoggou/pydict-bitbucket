(bɪtreɪəl )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounA betrayal is an action which betrays someone or something, or the fact of being betrayed. [+ of] ⇒ She felt that what she had done was a betrayal of Patrick. ⇒ He acknowledged the sense of betrayal by civil rights leaders.
Example sentences containing 'betrayal'
To see Leo against her wishes would be the sort of betrayal he would condemn in anyone else. St. James, Ian FINAL RESORTThe father, I think, sees any such union outside the family as something of a personal betrayal of himself. Heller, Keith MAN'S LOVING FAMILY
