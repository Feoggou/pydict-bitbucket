moth

(mɒθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounany of numerous insects of the order Lepidoptera that typically have stout bodies with antennae of various shapes (but not clubbed), including large brightly coloured species, such as hawk moths, and small inconspicuous types, such as the clothes moths. Compare butterfly (sense 1)

Word Origin

Old English moththe; compare Middle Dutch motte, Old Norse motti
