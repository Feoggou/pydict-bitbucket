meal1

(miːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. any of the regular occasions, such as breakfast, lunch, dinner, etc, when food is served and eatenb. (in combination) ⇒ mealtime ▶ Related adjective: prandial2. the food served and eaten3. See make a meal of

Word Origin

Old English mǣl measure, set time, meal; related to Old High German māl mealtime

meal2

(miːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the edible part of a grain or pulse (excluding wheat) ground to a coarse powder, used chiefly as animal food2. Scottish oatmeal3. mainly US maize flour

Derived Forms

meal-less (ˈmeal-less) adjective

Word Origin

Old English melu; compare Dutch meel, Old High German melo, Old Norse mjöl

meal

(miːl )
Word forms: meals
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA meal is an occasion when people sit down and eat, usually at a regular time. ⇒ She sat next to him throughout the meal. ⇒ It's rare that I have an evening meal with my children.2. countable nounA meal is the food you eat during a meal. ⇒ The waiter offered him red wine or white wine with his meal. ⇒ Fresh fish makes a delicious meal.3. uncount noun [usu n N]Meal is a rough powder made of crushed grain. It is used to make flour or animal food.4. → see also bone meal5. →  make a meal of sth6. →  a square meal
