mixed up

(mɪkst ʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. If you are mixed up, you are confused, often because of emotional or social problems. ⇒ I don't know what I was thinking at the time. I was really mixed up., ⇒ John says he feels happy, but still a bit mixed up, about the contact with his natural mother., ⇒ I'm mixed up. I don't know what I want., ⇒ I think he's a rather mixed up kid.2. See to get mixed up3. if you get things mixed up, you confuse one with another ⇒ My sense of time was mixed up, I didn't know how long I'd been out of the house., ⇒ She gets some words mixed up.4. See to be mixed up in sth5. See to be mixed up with sb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are mixed up, you are confused, often because of emotional or social problems. ⇒ I think he's a rather mixed up kid. ⇒ I get mixed up about times and places.2. adjectiveTo be mixed up in something bad, or with someone you disapprove of, means to be involved in it or with them. ⇒ How could David be mixed up in a murder? ⇒ Why did I ever get mixed up with you?
