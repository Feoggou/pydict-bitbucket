conciliate

(kənˈsɪlɪˌeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to overcome the hostility of; placate; win over2. to win or gain (favour, regard, etc), esp by making friendly overtures3. archaic to make compatible; reconcile
Derived forms
conciliable (conˈciliable) adjectiveconciliˌator (conˈciliˌator) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin conciliāre to bring together, from concilium council
