obtuse

(əbˈtjuːs )
Definitions
adjective1. mentally slow or emotionally insensitive2. mathematicsa. (of an angle) lying between 90° and 180°b. (of a triangle) having one interior angle greater than 90°3. not sharp or pointed4. indistinctly felt, heard, etc; dull ⇒ obtuse pain5. (of a leaf or similar flat part) having a rounded or blunt tip
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obtusely (obˈtusely) adverbobtuseness (obˈtuseness) or obtusity (obˈtusity) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin obtūsus dulled, past participle of obtundere to beat down; see obtund

obtuse for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(əbtjuːs , US -tuːs )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is obtuse has difficulty understanding things, or makes no effort to understand them. [formal] ⇒ I've really been very obtuse and stupid. ⇒ I'm a limited and obtuse clergyman while you're the expert.obtuseness uncount noun ⇒ Naivety bordering on obtuseness helped sustain his faith.2. adjectiveAn obtuse angle is between 90° and 180°. Compare acute angle. [technical]
