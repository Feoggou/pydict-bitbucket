come up with

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you come up with a plan or idea, you think of it and suggest it. [V P P n] ⇒ Several of the members have come up with suggestions of their own. [V P P n] ⇒ 30 years ago, scientists came up with the theory that protons and neutrons are composed of three smaller particles.2. phrasal verbIf you come up with a sum of money, you manage to produce it when it is needed. [V P P n] ⇒ If Warren can come up with the $15 million, we'll go to London. →  come
