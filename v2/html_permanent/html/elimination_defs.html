elimination

(ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of eliminating or the state of being eliminated2. logic (qualified by the name of an operation) a syntactic rule specifying the conditions under which a formula or statement containing the specified operation may permit the derivation of others that do not contain it ⇒ conjunction-elimination, ⇒ universal elimination3. chemistry a type of chemical reaction involving the loss of a simple molecule, such as water or carbon dioxide
