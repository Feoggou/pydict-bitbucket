bout

(baʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a period of time spent doing something, such as drinkingb. a period of illness2. a contest or fight, esp a boxing or wrestling match
Word origin
C16: variant of obsolete bought turn; related to German Bucht bight; see about

bout

Powered by COBUILD
(baʊt )
Word forms: bouts
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf you have a bout of an illness or of an unpleasant feeling, you have it for a short period. [+ of] ⇒ He was recovering from a severe bout of flu. [+ of] ⇒ I was suffering with a bout of nerves.2. countable nounA bout of something that is unpleasant is a short time during which it occurs a great deal. [+ of] ⇒ The latest bout of violence has claimed twenty-four lives. [+ of] ⇒ A half-hour daily walk can be more beneficial than one hard bout of exercise a week.3. countable nounA bout is a boxing or wrestling match. ⇒ This will be his eighth title bout in 19 months.4. Some writers use 'bout or bout to represent about when the first syllable is not pronounced. ⇒ How 'bout some coffee? ⇒ I just felt the need to write bout it, I guess.
