sprain

(spreɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to injure (a joint) by a sudden twisting or wrenching of its ligamentsnoun2. the resulting injury to such a joint, characterized by swelling and temporary disability

Word Origin

C17: of uncertain origin

sprain

Powered by COBUILD
(spreɪn )
Word forms: sprains , spraining , sprained
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you sprain a joint such as your ankle or wrist, you accidentally damage it by twisting it or bending it violently. [V n] ⇒ He fell and sprained his ankle.sprained adjective [usu ADJ n] ⇒ ...a badly sprained ankle. ⇒ His wrist was sprained.2. countable nounA sprain is the injury caused by spraining a joint.
