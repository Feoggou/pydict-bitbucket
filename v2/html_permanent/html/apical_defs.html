apical

(ˈæpɪkəl , ˈeɪ-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of, at, or being the apex2. of or denoting a consonant articulated with the tip of the tongue, such as (t) or (d)
Derived forms
ˈapically adverb
Word origin
C19: from New Latin apicālis, from Latin: apex
Example sentences containing 'apical'
It had six true legs---tiny, pointed extensions, each one ending in an apical hook. Gregg Andrew Hurwitz MINUTES TO BURN
