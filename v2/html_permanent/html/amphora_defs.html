amphora

(ˈæmfərə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -phorae (-fəˌriː) or -phorasan ancient Greek or Roman two-handled narrow-necked jar for oil, wine, etc
Word origin
C17: from Latin, from Greek amphoreus, from amphi- + phoreus bearer, from pherein to bear
Example sentences containing 'amphora'
