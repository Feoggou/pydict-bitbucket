on the face of it for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
phraseYou say on the face of it when you are describing how something seems when it is first considered, in order to suggest that people's opinion may change when they know or think more about the subject. ⇒ On the face of it that seems to make sense. But the figures don't add up. ⇒ It is, on the face of it, difficult to see how the West could radically change its position. →  face
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
