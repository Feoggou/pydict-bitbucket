gang1

(ɡæŋ )
Definitions
noun1. a group of people who associate together or act as an organized body, esp for criminal or illegal purposes2. an organized group of workmen3. a herd of buffaloes or elks or a pack of wild dogs4. New Zealand a group of shearers who travel to different shearing sheds, shearing, classing, and baling wool5. a. a series of similar tools arranged to work simultaneously in parallelb. (as modifier) ⇒ a gang sawverb6. to form into, become part of, or act as a gang7. (transitive) electronics to mount (two or more components, such as variable capacitors) on the same shaft, permitting adjustment by a single control
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

gang

Powered by COBUILD
(gæŋ ) for learners of English
Word forms: gangs , ganging , ganged
Definitions
1. countable nounA gang is a group of people, especially young people, who go around together and often deliberately cause trouble. ⇒ During the fight with a rival gang he lashed out with his flick knife. ⇒ Gang members were behind a lot of the violence. [+ of] ⇒ He was attacked by a gang of youths.2. countable nounA gang is a group of criminals who work together to commit crimes. ⇒ Police were hunting for a gang who had allegedly stolen fifty-five cars. ⇒ ...an underworld gang. [+ of] ⇒ ...a gang of masked robbers.3. singular nounThe gang is a group of friends who frequently meet. [informal] ⇒ Come on over, we've got lots of the old gang here.4. countable nounA gang is a group of workers who do physical work together. [+ of] ⇒ ...a gang of labourers.► →  gang up
