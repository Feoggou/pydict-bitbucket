alienation

(ˌeɪljəˈneɪʃən , ˌeɪlɪə-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a turning away; estrangement2. the state of being an outsider or the feeling of being isolated, as from society3. psychiatry a state in which a person's feelings are inhibited so that eventually both the self and the external world seem unreal4. lawa. the transfer of property, as by conveyance or will, into the ownership of anotherb. the right of an owner to dispose of his property
Example sentences containing 'alienation'
Livy felt her fatigue slip from her, her sense of alienation from the world disappeared. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMENTo wait well is to truly express your lack of alienation from what is actual. Nicola Barker BEHINDLINGS"What I mean is that your feelings of alienation might be more pronounced right now and that you should take that into consideration. S.M. Stirling T2: INFILTRATOR
