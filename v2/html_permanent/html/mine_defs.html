mine1

(maɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
pronoun1. something or someone belonging to or associated with me ⇒ mine is best2. See of minedeterminer3. (preceding a vowel) an archaic word for my1 ⇒ mine eyes, ⇒ mine host

Word Origin

Old English mīn; compare Old High German, Old Norse mīn, Dutch mijn

mine2

(maɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a system of excavations made for the extraction of minerals, esp coal, ores, or precious stones2. any deposit of ore or minerals3. a lucrative source or abundant supply ⇒ she was a mine of information4. a device containing an explosive designed to destroy ships, vehicles, or personnel, usually laid beneath the ground or in water5. a tunnel or sap dug to undermine a fortification6. a groove or tunnel made by certain insects, esp in a leafverb7. to dig into (the earth) for (minerals)8. to make (a hole, tunnel, etc) by digging or boring9. to place explosive mines in position below the surface of (the sea or land)10. to undermine (a fortification) by digging mines or saps11. another word for undermine

Derived Forms

minable (ˈminable) or mineable (ˈmineable) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, probably of Celtic origin; compare Irish mein, Welsh mwyn ore, mine

mine - pronoun use

Powered by COBUILD
(maɪn )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
possessive pronoun & pronounMine is the first person singular possessive pronoun. A speaker or writer uses mine to refer to something that belongs or relates to himself or herself. ⇒ Her right hand is inches from mine. ⇒ That wasn't his fault, it was mine. ⇒ I'm looking for a friend of mine who lives here.

mine - noun and verb uses

Powered by COBUILD
(maɪn )
Word forms: mines , mining , mined
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft n N]A mine is a place where deep holes and tunnels are dug under the ground in order to obtain a mineral such as coal, diamonds, or gold. ⇒ ...coal mines.2. verb [usu passive]When a mineral such as coal, diamonds, or gold is mined, it is obtained from the ground by digging deep holes and tunnels. [be V-ed] ⇒ The pit is being shut down because it no longer has enough coal that can be mined economically. [V-ed] ⇒ ...the finest gems, mined from all corners of the world.3. countable nounA mine is a bomb which is hidden in the ground or in water and which explodes when people or things touch it.4. verbIf an area of land or water is mined, mines are placed there which will explode when people or things touch them. [be V-ed] ⇒ The approaches to the garrison have been heavily mined. [Also V n]5. →  mine of information6. → see also mining
