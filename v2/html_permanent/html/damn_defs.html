damn

(dæm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
exclamation1. slang an exclamation of annoyance (often in exclamatory phrases such as damn it! damn you! etc)2. informal an exclamation of surprise or pleasure (esp in the exclamatory phrase damn me!)adjective3. (prenominal) slang deserving damnation; detestableadverb, adjective (prenominal)4. slang (intensifier) ⇒ damn fool, ⇒ a damn good pianistadverb5. See damn allverb (mainly tr)6. to condemn as bad, worthless, etc7. to curse8. to condemn to eternal damnation9. (often passive) to doom to ruin; cause to fail ⇒ the venture was damned from the start10. (also intr) to prove (someone) guilty ⇒ damning evidence11. to swear (at) using the word damn12. See as near as damn it13. See damn with faint praisenoun14. slang something of negligible value; jot (esp in the phrase not worth a damn)15. See not give a damn
Word origin
C13: from Old French dampner, from Latin damnāre to injure, condemn, from damnum loss, injury, penalty

(dæm )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. exclamationDamn, damn it, and dammit are used by some people to express anger or impatience. [informal, rude, feelings] ⇒ Don't be flippant, damn it! This is serious.2. adjective [ADJ n]Damn is used by some people to emphasize what they are saying. [informal, rude, emphasis] ⇒ There's not a damn thing you can do about it now.Damn is also an adverb. ⇒ As it turned out, I was damn right. ⇒ Let's have a damn good party.3. verbIf you say that a person or a news report damns something such as a policy or action, you mean that they are very critical of it. [V n] ⇒ ...a sensational book in which she damns the ultra-right party. [V n] ⇒ ...a report damning the chocolate advertising people for targeting women in their campaigns.4. → see also damned, damning5. →  not give a damn6. →  damn near7. →  near as damn it
