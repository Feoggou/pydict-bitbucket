accomplice

(əˈkɒmplɪs , əˈkʌm-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna person who helps another in committing a crime
Word origin
C15: from a complice, interpreted as one word. See complice

Word forms: accomplices
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [oft poss N]Someone's accomplice is a person who helps them to commit a crime. ⇒ The gunman escaped on a motorcycle being ridden by an accomplice. ⇒ His accomplice was arrested after a high-speed police chase.
