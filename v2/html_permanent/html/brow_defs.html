brow

(braʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the part of the face from the eyes to the hairline; forehead2. short for eyebrow3. the expression of the face; countenance ⇒ a troubled brow4. the top of a mine shaft; pithead5. the jutting top of a hill, etc6. Northern England dialect a steep slope on a road
Word origin
Old English brū; related to Old Norse brūn eyebrow, Lithuanian bruvis, Greek ophrus, Sanskrit bhrūs

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu poss N]Your brow is your forehead. ⇒ He wiped his brow with the back of his hand. ⇒ She wrinkled her brow inquisitively.2. → to knit your brow3. countable noun [usu pl]Your brows are your eyebrows. ⇒ He had thick brown hair and shaggy brows.4. countable nounThe brow of a hill is the top part of it. [+ of] ⇒ He was on the look-out just below the brow of the hill.
