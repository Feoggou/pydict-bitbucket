jargon1

(ˈdʒɑːɡən )
Definitions
noun1. specialized language concerned with a particular subject, culture, or profession2. language characterized by pretentious syntax, vocabulary, or meaning3. gibberish4. another word for pidginverb5. (intransitive) to use or speak in jargon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, perhaps of imitative origin; see gargle

jargon2

(ˈdʒɑːɡɒn ) or jargoon (dʒɑːˈɡuːn )
Definitions
nounmineralogy rare a golden yellow, smoky, or colourless variety of zircon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from French, from Italian giargone, ultimately from Persian zargūn of the golden colour; see zircon

jargon for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɑːʳgən )
Definitions
uncount nounYou use jargon to refer to words and expressions that are used in special or technical ways by particular groups of people, often making the language difficult to understand. ⇒ The manual is full of the jargon and slang of self-improvement courses. ⇒ ...the reading habits of 600,000 C2 males (marketing jargon for skilled manual workers).
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
