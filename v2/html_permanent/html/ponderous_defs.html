ponderous

(ˈpɒndərəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of great weight; heavy; huge2. (esp of movement) lacking ease or lightness; awkward, lumbering, or graceless3. dull or laborious ⇒ a ponderous oration

Derived Forms

ponderously (ˈponderously) adverbponderousness (ˈponderousness) or ponderosity (ˌpɒndəˈrɒsɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin ponderōsus of great weight, from pondus weight

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectivePonderous writing or speech is very serious, uses more words than necessary, and is rather dull. [disapproval] ⇒ He had a dense, ponderous style.ponderously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...the rather ponderously titled 'Recommendation for National Reconciliation and Salvation'.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA movement or action that is ponderous is very slow or clumsy. [written] ⇒ His steps were heavy and ponderous.ponderously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Wilson shifted ponderously in his chair.
