incorporeal

(ˌɪnkɔːˈpɔːrɪəl )
Definitions
adjective1. without material form, body, or substance2. spiritual or metaphysical3. law having no material existence but existing by reason of its annexation of something material, such as an easement, touchline, copyright, etc ⇒ an incorporeal hereditament
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incorporeally (ˌincorˈporeally) adverbincorporeity (ɪnˌkɔːpəˈriːɪtɪ ) or incorporeality (ˌincorporeˈality) noun
