enchant

(ɪnˈtʃɑːnt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to cast a spell on; bewitch2. to delight or captivate utterly; fascinate; charm
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

enchanter (enˈchanter) nounenchantress (enˈchantress) feminine noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French enchanter, from Latin incantāre to chant a spell, from cantāre to chant, from canere to sing

Definitions
1. verbIf you are enchanted by someone or something, they cause you to have feelings of great delight or pleasure. [be V-ed] ⇒ Dena was enchanted by the house. [V n] ⇒ She enchanted you as she has so many others.enchanted graded adjective & adjective [+ with] ⇒ Don't expect young children to be as enchanted with the scenery as you are.2. verbIn fairy stories and legends, to enchant someone or something means to put a magic spell on them. [V n] ⇒ King Arthur hid his treasures here and Merlin enchanted the cave so that nobody should ever find them. [V-ed] ⇒ ...Celtic stories of cauldrons and enchanted vessels.
