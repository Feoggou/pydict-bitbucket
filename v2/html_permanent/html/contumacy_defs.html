contumacy

(ˈkɒntjʊməsɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cies1. obstinate and wilful rebelliousness or resistance to authority; insubordination; disobedience2. the wilful refusal of a person to appear before a court or to comply with a court order
Word origin
C14: from Latin contumācia, from contumāx obstinate; related to tumēre to swell, be proud
Example sentences containing 'contumacy'
`Archie, I have suffered your contumacy for longer than I care to think about. Goldsborough, Robert MURDER IN E MINOR

Similar Words

obstinacy, contempt, disobedience, intransigence, delinquency, stubbornness, insubordination, rebelliousness, recalcitrance, haughtiness, pig-headedness, refractoriness, perverseness, contrariety
