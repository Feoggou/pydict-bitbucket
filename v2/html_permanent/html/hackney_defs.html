hackney

(ˈhæknɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a compact breed of harness horse with a high-stepping trot2. a. a coach or carriage that is for hireb. (as modifier) ⇒ a hackney carriage3. a popular term for hack2 (sense 1)verb4. (tr; usually passive) to make commonplace and banal by too frequent use
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hackneyism (ˈhackneyism) noun

Word Origin

C14: probably after Hackney, where horses were formerly raised; sense 4 meaning derives from the allusion to a weakened hired horse

Hackney

(ˈhæknɪ )
Definitions
nouna borough of NE Greater London: formed in 1965 from the former boroughs of Shoreditch, Stoke Newington, and Hackney; nearby are Hackney Marshes, the largest recreation ground in London. Pop: 208 400 (2003 est). Area: 19 sq km (8 sq miles)
