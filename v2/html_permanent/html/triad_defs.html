triad

(ˈtraɪæd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a group of three; trio2. chemistry an atom, element, group, or ion that has a valency of three3. music a three-note chord consisting of a note and the third and fifth above it4. an aphoristic literary form used in medieval Welsh and Irish literature5. the US strategic nuclear force, consisting of intercontinental ballistic missiles, submarine-launched ballistic missiles, and bombers

Derived Forms

triadic (triˈadic) adjectivetriadism (ˈtriadism) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin trias, from Greek; related to Greek treis three
