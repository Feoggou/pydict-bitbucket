tidy

(ˈtaɪdɪ )
Definitions
adjective -dier or -diest1. characterized by or indicating neatness and order2. informal considerable ⇒ a tidy sum of money3. See bang tidyverb -dies, -dying or -died4. (when intr, usually foll by up) to put (things) in order; neatennoun plural -dies5. a. a small container in which odds and ends are keptb. See sink tidy6. mainly US and Canadian an ornamental protective covering for the back or arms of a chair
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tidily (ˈtidily) adverbtidiness (ˈtidiness) noun

Word Origin

C13 (in the sense: timely, seasonable, excellent): from tide1 + -y1; related to Dutch tijdig timely
