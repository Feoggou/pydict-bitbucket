die-hard

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who resists change or who holds onto an untenable position or outdated attitude2. (modifier) obstinately resistant to change
Derived forms
die-ˌhardism (ˈdie-ˌhardism) noun

die hard

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseIf you say that habits or attitudes die hard, you mean that they take a very long time to disappear or change, so that it may not be possible to get rid of them completely. ⇒ Old habits die hard. ⇒ Such prejudices die hard. →  die
Example sentences containing 'die hard'
There was a lot I wanted to say, but the habits of a lifetime die hard and I waited for her to lead the way in. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFMaybe it wouldn't have mattered in a place like this, but when you live in the city old habits die hard so I went back upstairs. Stuart Harrison BETTER THAN THISOld habits die hard... He heard the sound of an approaching vehicle; moments later an army jeep appeared at the end of the street. MacNeill, Alastair THE DEVIL'S DOOR
Example sentences containing 'die-hard'
They're starting to sound like die-hard socialists, not freedom fighters. Terrence Chen SONS OF HEAVEN
