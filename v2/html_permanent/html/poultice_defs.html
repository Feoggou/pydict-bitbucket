poultice

(ˈpəʊltɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: cataplasm medicine a local moist and often heated application for the skin consisting of substances such as kaolin, linseed, or mustard, used to improve the circulation, treat inflamed areas, etc2. Australian slang a large sum of money, esp a debt

Word Origin

C16: from earlier pultes, from Latin puls a thick porridge

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA poultice is a piece of cloth with a soft, often hot, substance such as clay or a mixture of herbs on it. It is put over a painful or swollen part of someone's body in order to reduce the pain or swelling.
