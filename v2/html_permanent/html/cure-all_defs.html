Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA cure-all is something that is believed, usually wrongly, to be able to solve all the problems someone or something has, or to cure a wide range of illnesses. [+ for] ⇒ He said the introduction of market discipline to the economy was not a magic cure-all for its problems. ⇒ Broussais was the first physician to use leeches as a cure-all.
