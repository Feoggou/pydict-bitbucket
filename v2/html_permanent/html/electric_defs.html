electric

(ɪˈlɛktrɪk )
Definitions
adjective1. of, derived from, produced by, producing, transmitting, or powered by electricity ⇒ electric current, ⇒ an electric cord, ⇒ an electric blanket, ⇒ an electric fence, ⇒ an electric fire2. (of a musical instrument) amplified electronically ⇒ an electric guitar, ⇒ an electric mandolin3. very tense or exciting; emotionally charged ⇒ an electric atmospherenoun4. informal an electric train, car, etc5. British informal electricity or electrical power6. (plural) an electric circuit or electric appliancesSee note at electronic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from New Latin electricus amber-like (because friction causes amber to become charged), from Latin ēlectrum amber, from Greek ēlektron, of obscure origin

electric

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪlektrɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]An electric device or machine works by means of electricity, rather than using some other source of power. ⇒ ...her electric guitar.2. adjective [ADJ n]An electric current, voltage, or charge is one that is produced by electricity.3. adjective [ADJ n]Electric plugs, sockets, or power lines are designed to carry electricity.4. adjective [ADJ n]Electric is used to refer to the supply of electricity. [informal] ⇒ An average electric bill might go up $2 or $3 per month.5. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe the atmosphere of a place or event as electric, you mean that people are in a state of great excitement. charged ⇒ The mood in the hall was electric.
