straight

adjective

  1. direct, unswerving, undeviating • Keep the boat in a straight line. OPPOSITES:  winding, indirect, roundabout, zigzag, circuitous
  2. level, even, right, square, true, smooth, in line, aligned, horizontal • There wasn't a single straight wall in the building. OPPOSITES:  bent, crooked, uneven, twisted, curved, askew, skewwhiff (British, informal)
  3. frank, plain, straightforward, blunt, outright, honest, downright, candid, forthright, bold, point-blank, upfront (informal), unqualified • a straight answer to a straight question OPPOSITES:  vague, indirect, ambiguous, evasive, cryptic, equivocal
  4. successive, consecutive, continuous, through, running, solid, sustained, uninterrupted, nonstop, unrelieved • They'd won twelve straight games before they lost. OPPOSITES:  broken, interrupted, discontinuous, non-consecutive
  5. straightforward, clear, simple, routine, clear-cut, uncomplicated • It's a straight choice between low-paid jobs and no jobs.
  6. (slang) conventional, conservative, orthodox, traditional, square (informal), bourgeois, Pooterish • Dorothy was described as a very straight woman. OPPOSITES:  fashionable, trendy (British, informal), voguish, cool
  7. honest, just, fair, decent, reliable, respectable, upright, honourable, equitable, law-abiding, trustworthy, above board, fair and square • You need to be straight with them to gain their respect. OPPOSITES:  bent (slang), crooked (informal), dishonest, unlawful, shady (informal), dishonourable
  8. (informal) heterosexual • His sexual orientation was a lot more gay than straight.
  9. undiluted, pure, neat, unadulterated, unmixed • a large straight whisky, with ice
  10. upright, erect, vertical, plumb, perpendicular • Is this picture straight?
  11. in order, organized, arranged, sorted out, neat, tidy, orderly, shipshape, put to rights • We need to get the house straight again before they come home. OPPOSITES:  disorderly, messy, untidy, in disarray, disorganized, confused
  12. accurate, fair, reliable, authentic, honest, trustworthy

adverb

  1. upright, erect, vertical, tall • Stand straight and stretch the left hand to the right foot.
  2. directly, precisely, exactly, as the crow flies, unswervingly, by the shortest route, in a beeline • Straight ahead were the low cabins of the motel.
  3. immediately, directly, promptly, instantly, at once, straightaway, without delay, without hesitation, forthwith, unhesitatingly, before you could say Jack Robinson (informal) • As always, we went straight to the experts for advice.
  4. frankly, honestly, point-blank, candidly, pulling no punches (informal), in plain English, with no holds barred • I told him straight that I had been looking for another job.
  • See go straight
