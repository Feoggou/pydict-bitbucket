embattled

(ɪmˈbætəld )
Definitions
adjective1. prepared for or engaged in conflict, controversy, or battle2. heraldry having an indented edge resembling battlements

embattled

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmbætəld ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a person, group, or organization as embattled, you mean that they are having a lot of problems or difficulties. ⇒ The embattled president also denied recent claims that he was being held hostage by his own soldiers.2. adjective [ADJ n]An embattled area is one that is involved in the fighting in a war, especially one that is surrounded by enemy forces. ⇒ Both sides say they want to try to reach a political settlement in the embattled north and east of the island.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
