verb

  2. let, lease, sublet, sublease
    • She rented rooms to university students.

noun

  1. hire, rental, lease, tariff, fee, payment
    • She worked to pay the rent.

rent2

noun

  1. tear, split, rip, slash, slit, gash, perforation, hole
    • a small rent in the silk
  2. opening, break, hole, crack, breach, flaw, chink
    • welling up from a rent in the ground
  3. division, break, split, breach, faction, rift, rupture, cleavage, discord, schism, disunity, dissension

rend

verb

    • pain that rends the heart
