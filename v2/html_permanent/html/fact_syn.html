Synonyms of fact

fact

noun

  1. truth, reality, gospel (truth), certainty, verity, actuality, naked truth
    • How much was fact and how much fancy no one knew.
    OPPOSITES:  lie, fiction, invention, delusion, yarn (informal), fable, fabrication, falsehood, untruth, tall story
  2. detail, point, feature, particular, item, specific, circumstance
    • The lorries always left in the dead of night when there were few witnesses around to record the fact.
  3. (criminal law) event, happening, act, performance, incident, deed, occurrence, fait accompli (French)
    • He was sure the gun was planted after the fact.

plural noun

  1. information, details, data, the score (informal), gen (British, informal), info (informal), the whole story, ins and outs, the lowdown (informal)
    • There is so much information you can find the facts for yourself.

QUOTATION:
In this life we want nothing but facts, sir; nothing but factsCharles Dickens – Hard Times
