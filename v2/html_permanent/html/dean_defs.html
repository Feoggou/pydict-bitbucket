dean

(diːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the chief administrative official of a college or university faculty2. (at Oxford and Cambridge universities) a college fellow with responsibility for undergraduate discipline3. mainly Church of England the head of a chapter of canons and administrator of a cathedral or collegiate church4. Roman Catholic Church the cardinal bishop senior by consecration and head of the college of cardinals. See also rural dean ▶ Related adjective: decanal
Derived forms
deanˌship (ˈdeanˌship) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French deien, from Late Latin decānus one set over ten persons, from Latin decem ten

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA dean is an important official at a university or college. [+ of] ⇒ She was Dean of the Science faculty at Sophia University.2. countable nounA dean is a priest who is the main administrator of a large church. [+ of] ⇒ ...Alan Webster, former Dean of St Paul's.3. countable noun [usu with supp]The dean of a group is the most important member of that group. [mainly US] doyen, doyenne ⇒ ...Aaron Copland, who was known as the dean of American composers.
