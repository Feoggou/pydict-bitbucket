region

(ˈriːdʒən )
Definitions
noun1. any large, indefinite, and continuous part of a surface or space2. an area considered as a unit for geographical, functional, social, or cultural reasons3. an administrative division of a country ⇒ Tuscany is one of the regions of the Italian Republic4. a realm or sphere of activity or interest5. range, area, or scope ⇒ in what region is the price likely to be?6. a division or part of the boday ⇒ the lumbar region7. (in Scotland from 1975 until 1996) any of the nine territorial divisions into which the mainland of Scotland was divided for purposes of local government; replaced in 1996 by council areas. See also islands council
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin regiō, from regere to govern

Definitions
1. countable nounA region is a large area of land that is different from other areas of land, for example because it is one of the different parts of a country with its own customs and characteristics, or because it has a particular geographical feature. ⇒ ...Barcelona, capital of the autonomous region of Catalonia. ⇒ ...a remote mountain region.2. plural nounThe regions are the parts of a country that are not the capital city and its surrounding area. [British] provinces ⇒ ...London and the regions. ⇒ Tax incentives would be used to attract firms to the regions, away from the South-East.3. countable nounYou can refer to a part of your body as a region. ⇒ ...the pelvic region. ⇒ ...the frontal region of the brain.4. →  in the region of
