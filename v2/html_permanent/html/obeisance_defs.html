obeisance

(əʊˈbeɪsəns , əʊˈbiː-)
Definitions
noun1. an attitude of deference or homage2. a gesture expressing obeisance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obeisant (oˈbeisant) adjectiveobeisantly (oˈbeisantly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French obéissant, present participle of obéir to obey

obeisance for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊbeɪsəns )
Word forms: obeisances
Definitions
1. uncount nounObeisance to someone or something is great respect shown for them. [formal] [+ to] ⇒ While he was still young and strong all paid obeisance to him.2. variable nounAn obeisance is a physical gesture, especially a bow, that you make in order to show your respect for someone or something. [formal] ⇒ One by one they came forward, mumbled grudging words of welcome, made awkward obeisances.
