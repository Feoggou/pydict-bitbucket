viscous

(ˈvɪskəs ) or viscose
Definitions
adjective1. (of liquids) thick and sticky; viscid2. having or involving viscosity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

viscously (ˈviscously) adverbviscousness (ˈviscousness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin viscōsus; see viscose

viscous

Powered by COBUILD
(vɪskəs) in Chemical Engineering
Definitions
adjective(Chemical Engineering: General) A viscous fluid is a fluid which does not flow easily. ⇒ Cold fluid may become viscous or thick enough to prevent it from being drawn into the pump. ⇒ In oil and syrup, the particles move sluggishly; such liquids are termed viscous. ⇒ A viscous fluid is a fluid which does not flow easily. ▶  viscosity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
