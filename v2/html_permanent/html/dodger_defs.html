dodger

(ˈdɒdʒə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who evades or shirks2. a shifty dishonest person3. a canvas shelter, mounted on a ship's bridge or over the companionway of a sailing yacht to protect the helmsman from bad weather4. archaic, US and Australian a handbill5. Australian informal food, esp bread

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu n N]A dodger is someone who avoids doing a duty or paying a charge, for example paying taxes or for train travel. ⇒ ...tax dodgers who hide their interest earnings. ⇒ ...a crackdown on fare dodgers.2. → see also draft dodger
Example sentences containing 'dodger'
Of course, her boyfriend, who had just been demobbed from the Royal Marines, said he was a war dodger. Leasor, James TANK OF SERPENTSFor years I had known Murdoch as the Vietnam draft dodger , and for the life of me I couldn't understand why he had denied his army service.
