tatters

(ˈtætəz)
Definitions
plural noun1. torn or ragged pieces, esp of material ⇒ A man, naked but for some tatters of cloth, was sitting hunched up beside the entrance., ⇒ The blustering gusts whipped the banners to tatters.2. See in tatters

tatters

Powered by COBUILD
(tætəʳz ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. plural noun [usu in N]Clothes that are in tatters are badly torn in several places, so that pieces can easily come off. rags ⇒ His jersey was left in tatters.2. plural noun [usu in N]If you say that something such as a plan or a person's state of mind is in tatters, you are emphasizing that it is weak, has suffered a lot of damage, and is likely to fail completely. [emphasis] ⇒ The economy is in tatters.
