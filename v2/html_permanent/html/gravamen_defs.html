gravamen

(ɡrəˈveɪmɛn )
Definitions
noun plural -vamina (-ˈvæmɪnə)1. law that part of an accusation weighing most heavily against an accused2. law the substance or material grounds of a complaint3. a rare word for grievance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin: trouble, from Latin gravāre to load, from gravis heavy; see grave2
