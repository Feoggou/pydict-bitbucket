interim

(ˈɪntərɪm )
Definitions
adjective1. (prenominal) temporary, provisional, or intervening ⇒ interim measures to deal with the emergencynoun2. See the interimadverb3. rare meantime
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin: meanwhile

Interim

(ˈɪntərɪm )
Definitions
nounany of three provisional arrangements made during the Reformation by the German emperor and Diet to regulate religious differences between Roman Catholics and Protestants
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

interim

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntərɪm ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Interim is used to describe something that is intended to be used until something permanent is done or established. ⇒ She was sworn in as head of an interim government in March. ⇒ ...an interim report.2. →  in the interim
