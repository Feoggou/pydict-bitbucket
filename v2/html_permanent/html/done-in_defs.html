Thanks for your comment, Stewart. That sense of the word is covered under 'do up'. The use of 'done up' as an equivalent of 'done in' is probably becoming less common, but it is possible to find numerous examples, such as this from a Patrick O'Brian novel: "I am going to turn in until the end of the watch. You might be well advised to do the same: you look quite done up."
