rubbish

(ˈrʌbɪʃ )
Definitions
noun1. worthless, useless, or unwanted matter2. discarded or waste matter; refuse3. foolish words or speech; nonsenseverb4. (transitive) informal to criticize; attack verbally
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14 robys, of uncertain origin

rubbish

Powered by COBUILD
(rʌbɪʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: rubbishes , rubbishing , rubbished
Definitions
1. uncount nounRubbish consists of unwanted things or waste material such as used paper, empty tins and bottles, and waste food. [mainly British] ⇒ ...unwanted household rubbish. ⇒ They had piled most of their rubbish into yellow skips.regional note:   in AM, usually use garbage, trash2. uncount nounIf you think that something is of very poor quality, you can say that it is rubbish. [British, informal] ⇒ He described her book as absolute rubbish.3. uncount nounIf you think that an idea or a statement is foolish or wrong, you can say that it is rubbish. [mainly British, informal] ⇒ He's talking rubbish. ⇒ These reports are total and utter rubbish.4. adjective [v-link ADJ]If you think that someone is not very good at something, you can say that they are rubbish at it. [British, informal] [+ at] ⇒ He was rubbish at his job. ⇒ I tried playing golf, but I was rubbish.5. verbIf you rubbish a person, their ideas or their work, you say they are of little value. [British, informal] [V n] ⇒ Five whole pages of script were devoted to rubbishing her political opponents. [V n] ⇒ Officials have simply rubbished all positive ideas.regional note:   in AM, use trash
