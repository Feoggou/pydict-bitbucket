subpoena

(səbˈpiːnə , səˈpiːnə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a writ issued by a court of justice requiring a person to appear before the court at a specified timeverb -nas, -naing or -naed2. (transitive) to serve with a subpoena

Word Origin

C15: from Latin: under penalty

Word forms: subpoenas , subpoenaing , subpoenaed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA subpoena is a legal document telling someone that they must attend a court of law and give evidence as a witness. ⇒ He has been served with a subpoena to answer the charges in court.2. verbIf someone subpoenas a person, they give them a legal document telling them to attend a court of law and give evidence. If someone subpoenas a piece of evidence, the evidence must be produced in a court of law. [V n] ⇒ Select committees have the power to subpoena witnesses. [V n] ⇒ The investigation will rely on existing powers to subpoena documents.
