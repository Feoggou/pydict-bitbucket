suffice

(səˈfaɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to be adequate or satisfactory for (something)2. See suffice it to say that
Derived forms
sufficer (sufˈficer) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French suffire, from Latin sufficere from sub- below + facere to make

Powered by COBUILD
(səfaɪs )
Word forms: suffices , sufficing , sufficed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verb [no cont]If you say that something will suffice, you mean it will be enough to achieve a purpose or to fulfil a need. [formal] [V] ⇒ A cover letter should never exceed one page; often a far shorter letter will suffice. [Also V to-inf]2. →  suffice it to say
Example sentences containing 'suffice'
The tried and true method of pointing out to them the budgetary savings implied might suffice. Gagman, Maurice DOUBTFUL MOTIVESAs it was a health farm, I was rather naively hoping that acting healthy would suffice. Anita Anderson SUMMER OF SECRETSThey had agreed that guarded speech would suffice for security in the urgency of the moment. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARHe wondered which death was his, or if the lightning of her fingers would suffice , and burn him to a crisp. C.J. Cherryh HAMMERFALL
