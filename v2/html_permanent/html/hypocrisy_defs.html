hypocrisy

(hɪˈpɒkrəsɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -sies1. the practice of professing standards, beliefs, etc, contrary to one's real character or actual behaviour, esp the pretence of virtue and piety2. an act or instance of this
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

hypocrisy

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪpɒkrɪsi ) for learners of English
Word forms: hypocrisies
Definitions
variable nounIf you accuse someone of hypocrisy, you mean that they pretend to have qualities, beliefs, or feelings that they do not really have. [disapproval] ⇒ He accused newspapers of hypocrisy in their treatment of the story. ⇒ You'll have little patience with the hypocrisy and double standards you encounter. ⇒ ...the hypocrisies of middle-class provincial life.

hypocrisy (hypocrisies) n. (hɪˈpɒkrəsɪ). 1. insincerity by virtue of pretending to have qualities or beliefs that you do not really have. 2. an expression of agreement that is not supported by real conviction. 3. a way of behaving in which you pretend to have better moral principles than you actually do; dissimulation, false virtue, cant, posturing, affectation, speciousness, empty talk, lip service رِيَاء؛ رِئَاء؛ مُدَاجَاة؛ نِفَاق؛ الرياء: التظاهر الكاذب بالفضيلة والدين
