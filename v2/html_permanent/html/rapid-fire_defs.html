rapid fire

Definitions
noun1. a fast rate of gunfireadjective rapid-fire2. a. firing shots rapidlyb. denoting medium-calibre mounted guns designed for rapid fire3. done, delivered, or occurring in rapid succession

rapid-fire

(ˈræpɪdˌfaɪə)
Definitions
adjective1. military firing shots rapidly or designed for rapid fire ⇒ In the back of the truck was a 12.7 millimeter rapid-fire machine gun.2. involving the rapid delivery or exchange of spoken language ⇒ Yul listened to their sophisticated, rapid-fire conversation.3. mainly US done or occurring very fast ⇒ the rapid-fire buying and selling of stocks, ⇒ The company made a rapid-fire series of settlements with 25 States., ⇒ China's rapid-fire industrialization
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

rapid-fire

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]A rapid-fire gun is one that shoots a lot of bullets very quickly, one after the other. ⇒ In the back of the truck was a 12.7 millimeter rapid-fire machine gun.2. adjective [ADJ n]A rapid-fire conversation or speech is one in which people talk or reply very quickly. ⇒ Yul listened to their sophisticated, rapid-fire conversation. ⇒ ...arguing a point in rapid-fire Spanish.3. adjective [ADJ n]A rapid-fire economic activity or development is one that takes place very quickly. [mainly US, journalism] ⇒ ...the rapid-fire buying and selling of stocks. ⇒ The company made a rapid-fire series of settlements with 25 States. ⇒ ...Japan's rapid-fire industrialization.
