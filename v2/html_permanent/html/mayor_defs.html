mayor

(mɛə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounthe chairman and civic head of a municipal corporation in many countries. Scottish equivalent: provost

Derived Forms

mayoral (ˈmayoral) adjectivemayorˌship (ˈmayorˌship) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French maire, from Latin maior greater. See major

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounThe mayor of a town or city is the person who has been elected to represent it for a fixed period of time or, in some places, to run its government.
