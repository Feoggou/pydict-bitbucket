him

(hɪm , unstressed ɪm )
Definitions
pronoun (objective)1. refers to a male person or animal ⇒ they needed him, ⇒ she baked him a cake, ⇒ not him again!2. mainly US a dialect word for himself ⇒ he ought to find him a wifeSee note at me1
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English him, dative of hē he1

him

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪm ) for learners of English
Definitions
language note:   Him is a third person singular pronoun. Him is used as the object of a verb or a preposition.1. singular pronoun & pronounYou use him to refer to a man, boy, or male animal. ⇒ John's aunt died suddenly and left him a surprisingly large sum. ⇒ Is Sam there? Let me talk to him. ⇒ On his arrival, Elaine met him at the bus station. ⇒ My brother had a lovely dog. I looked after him for about a week.2. singular pronoun & pronounIn written English, him is sometimes used to refer to a person without saying whether that person is a man or a woman. Some people dislike this use and prefer to use 'him or her' or 'them'. ⇒ If the child sees the word 'hear', we should show him that this is the base word in 'hearing' and 'hears'.3. singular pronoun [v PRON, prep PRON]In some religions, Him is used to refer to God. ⇒ God will help you if you turn to Him in humility and trust.
