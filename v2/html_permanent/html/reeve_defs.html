reeve1

(riːv )
Definitions
noun1. English history the local representative of the king in a shire (under the ealdorman) until the early 11th century. Compare sheriff2. (in medieval England) a manorial steward who supervised the daily affairs of the manor: often a villein elected by his fellows3. Canadian government (in certain provinces) a president of a local council, esp in a rural area4. (formerly) a minor local official in any of several parts of England and the US
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English gerēva; related to Old High German ruova number, array
