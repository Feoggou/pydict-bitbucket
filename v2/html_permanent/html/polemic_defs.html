polemic

(pəˈlɛmɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective also: polemical (pəˈlɛmɪkəl)1. of or involving dispute or controversynoun2. an argument or controversy, esp over a doctrine, belief, etc3. a person engaged in such an argument or controversy

Derived Forms

polemically (poˈlemically) adverbpolemicist (pəˈlɛmɪsɪst ) or polemist (ˈpɒlɪmɪst ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin polemicus, from Greek polemikos relating to war, from polemos war

polemic

Powered by COBUILD
(pəlemɪk )
Word forms: polemics
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA polemic is a very strong written or spoken attack on, or defence of, a particular belief or opinion. ⇒ ...a polemic against the danger of secret societies. ⇒ The book is both a history and a passionate polemic for tolerance.2. uncount nounPolemics is the skill or practice of arguing very strongly for or against a belief or opinion. ⇒ He enjoys polemics, persuasion, and controversy.
Example sentences containing 'polemic'
Neither the polemic nor the violent bloodshed would, in the end, have much effect on the Helen Pearces. Thomas, Rosie THE WHITE DOVEWhatever he had hoped to learn from Rhodes tonight was shrouded now in a haze of fuzzy polemic.
