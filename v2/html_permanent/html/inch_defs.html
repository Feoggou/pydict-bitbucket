inch1

(ɪntʃ )
Definitions
noun1. a unit of length equal to one twelfth of a foot or 0.0254 metre2. meteorologya. an amount of precipitation that would cover a surface with water one inch deep ⇒ five inches of rain fell in Januaryb. a unit of pressure equal to a mercury column one inch high in a barometer3. a very small distance, degree, or amount4. See every inch5. See inch by inch6. See within an inch ofverb7. to move or be moved very slowly or in very small steps ⇒ the car inched forward8. (transitive; foll by out) to defeat (someone) by a very small margin
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English ynce, from Latin uncia twelfth part; see ounce1

inch

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: inches , inching , inched
Definitions
1. countable noun [num N]An inch is an imperial unit of length, approximately equal to 2.54 centimetres. There are twelve inches in a foot. ⇒ ...a candy tin 6 inches high and 8 inches in diameter. ⇒ ...18 inches below the surface.2. ergative verb & verbTo inch somewhere or to inch something somewhere means to move there very slowly and carefully, or to make something do this. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...a climber inching up a vertical wall of rock. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ He inched the van forward. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ An ambulance inched its way through the crowd.3. →  every inch4. →  every inch5. →  inch by inch
