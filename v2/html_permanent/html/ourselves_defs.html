ourselves

(aʊəˈsɛlvz )
Definitions
pronoun1. a. the reflexive form of we or usb. (intensifier) ⇒ we ourselves will finish it2. (preceded by a copula) our usual selves ⇒ we are ourselves when we're together3. not standard used instead of we or us in compound noun phrases ⇒ other people and ourselves

ourselves for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(aʊəʳselvz )
Definitions
language note:   Ourselves is the first person plural reflexive pronoun.1. reflexive pronoun & pronounYou use ourselves to refer to yourself and one or more other people as a group. ⇒ We sat round the fire to keep ourselves warm. ⇒ It was the first time we admitted to ourselves that we were tired.2. reflexive pronoun & pronounA speaker or writer sometimes uses ourselves to refer to people in general. Ourselves is used as the object of a verb or preposition when the subject refers to the same people. ⇒ We all know that when we exert ourselves our heart rate increases.3. emphatic reflexive pronoun & pronounYou use ourselves to emphasize a first person plural subject. In more formal English, ourselves is sometimes used instead of 'us' as the object of a verb or preposition, for emphasis. [emphasis] ⇒ Others are feeling just the way we ourselves would feel in the same situation. ⇒ The people who will suffer won't be people like ourselves.4. emphatic reflexive pronoun & pronounIf you say something such as 'We did it ourselves', you are indicating that something was done by you and a particular group of other people, rather than anyone else. ⇒ We villagers built that ourselves, we had no help from anyone.
