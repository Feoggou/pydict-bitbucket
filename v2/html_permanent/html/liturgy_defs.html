liturgy

(ˈlɪtədʒɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -gies1. the forms of public services officially prescribed by a Church2. Also called: Divine Liturgy (often capital) mainly Eastern Churches the Eucharistic celebration3. a particular order or form of public service laid down by a Church
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: via Medieval Latin, from Greek leitourgia, from leitourgos minister, from leit- people + ergon work
