unseemly

(ʌnˈsiːmlɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. not in good style or taste; unbecoming2. obsolete unattractiveadverb3. rare in an unseemly manner

unseemly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnsiːmli )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone's behaviour is unseemly, you disapprove of it because it is not polite or not suitable for a particular situation or occasion. [literary, disapproval] [+ for] ⇒ It would be unseemly for judges to receive pay increases when others are having to tighten their belts. ⇒ ...unseemly drinking, brawling and gambling.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
