wash out

Definitions
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) to wash (the inside of something) so as to remove (dirt)2. Also: wash off to remove or be removed by washing ⇒ grass stains don't wash out easily3. (transitive) to cancel or abandon (a sporting event)noun washout4. informala. a total failure or disasterb. an incompetent person5. a sporting or social event that is cancelled due to rain6. geologya. erosion of the earth's surface by the action of running waterb. a narrow channel produced by this erosion7. aeronautics a decrease in the angle of attack of an aircraft wing towards the wing tip

wash-out

or washout (ˈwɒʃaʊt)
Definitions
noun1. informal a complete failure ⇒ The party was a complete wash-out.2. informal an incompetent person ⇒ Why do so many women complain that their mates are romantic washouts?3. a sporting or social event that is cancelled due to rain4. geology erosion of the earth's surface by the action of running water5. geology a narrow channel produced by erosion6. aeronautics a decrease in the angle of attack of an aircraft wing towards the wing tip
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

wash out for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you wash out a container, you wash the inside of it. [V P n] ⇒ It was my job to wash out the fish tank.2. phrasal verbIf dye or dirt washes out, it can be removed by washing. [V P] ⇒ With permanent tints, the result won't wash out.3. phrasal verbIf rain washes out a sports game or other event, it spoils it or prevents it from continuing. [V P n] ⇒ Rain washed out five of the last seven games.4. → See also washed-out, washout →  wash
