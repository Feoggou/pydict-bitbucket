incorruptible

(ˌɪnkəˈrʌptəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. incapable of being corrupted; honest; just2. not subject to decay or decomposition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incorruptibility (ˌincorˌruptiˈbility) or incorruptibleness (ˌincorˈruptibleness) nounincorruptibly (ˌincorˈruptibly) adverb

Definitions
adjectiveIf you describe someone as incorruptible, you approve of the fact that they cannot be persuaded or paid to do things that they should not do. [approval] ⇒ He was a sound businessman, totally reliable and incorruptible.
