stand in

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intr, adverb; usually foll by for) to act as a substitute2. See stand someone in good steadnoun stand-in3. a. a person or thing that serves as a substituteb. (as modifier) ⇒ a stand-in teacher4. a person who substitutes for an actor during intervals of waiting or in dangerous stunts

stand-in

(ˈstændɪn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna substitute for somebody in their absence ⇒ She couldn't get to the meeting so she sent me as her stand-in., ⇒ He was a stand-in for my regular doctor.

stand in

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you stand in for someone, you take their place or do their job, because they are ill or away. deputize [V P + for] ⇒ I had to stand in for her on Tuesday when she didn't show up. [V P] ⇒ ...the acting president, who's standing in while she's out of the country.2. → see also stand-in →  stand

stand-in

Word forms: stand-ins
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA stand-in is a person who takes someone else's place or does someone else's job for a while, for example because the other person is ill or away. ⇒ He was a stand-in for my regular doctor.
