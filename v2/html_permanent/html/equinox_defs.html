equinox

(ˈiːkwɪˌnɒks , ˈɛkwɪˌnɒks )
Definitions
noun1. either of the two occasions, six months apart, when day and night are of equal length. See vernal equinox, autumnal equinox2. another name for equinoctial point
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin equinoxium, changed from Latin aequinoctium, from aequi- equi- + nox night
