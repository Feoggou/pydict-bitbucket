nondescript

(ˈnɒndɪˌskrɪpt )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking distinct or individual characteristics; having no outstanding featuresnoun2. a nondescript person or thing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from non- + Latin dēscriptus, past participle of dēscribere to copy, describe

nondescript for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɒndɪskrɪpt )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something or someone as nondescript, you mean that their appearance is rather dull, and not at all interesting or attractive. ⇒ Europa House is one of those hundreds of nondescript buildings along the Bath Road. ⇒ ...a nondescript woman of uncertain age. ⇒ Her clothes told me nothing: they were about as nondescript and lacking in chic as it was possible to be.
