gad1

(ɡæd )
Definitions
verbWord forms: gads, gadding or gadded1. (intr; often foll by about or around) to go out in search of pleasure, esp in an aimless manner; gallivantnoun2. carefree adventure (esp in the phrase on or upon the gad)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gadder (ˈgadder) noun

Word Origin

C15: back formation from obsolete gadling companion, from Old English, from gæd fellowship; related to Old High German gatuling

gad2

(ɡæd )
Definitions
noun1. mining a short chisel-like instrument for breaking rock or coal from the face2. a goad for driving cattle3. a western US word for spur (sense 1)verbWord forms: gads, gadding or gadded4. (transitive) mining to break up or loosen with a gad
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norse gaddr spike; related to Old High German gart, Gothic gazds spike

Gad1

(ɡæd )
Definitions
noun, exclamationan archaic euphemism for God ⇒ by Gad!
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Gad2

(ɡæd )
Definitions
noun Old Testament1. a. Jacob's sixth son, whose mother was Zilpah, Leah's maidb. the Israelite tribe descended from himc. the territory of this tribe, lying to the east of the Jordan and extending southwards from the Sea of Galilee2. a prophet and admonisher of David (I Samuel 22; II Samuel 24)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
