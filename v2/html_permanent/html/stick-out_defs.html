Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to project or cause to project2. (transitive) informal to endure (something disagreeable) (esp in the phrase stick it out)3. See stick out a mile4. See stick out for

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you stick out part of your body, you extend it away from your body. [V P n] ⇒ She made a face and stuck out her tongue at him. [V n P] ⇒ He stuck his hand out and he said, 'Good evening.'2. → to stick your neck out3. phrasal verbIf something sticks out, it is very noticeable because it is unusual. [V P] ⇒ The things that stuck out were his cockiness and his four-letter words. [V P + from] ⇒ What had Cutter done to make him stick out from the crowd?4. → to stick out a mile5. → to stick out like a sore thumb6. →  stick it out →  stick
