preventive

(prɪˈvɛntɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. tending or intended to prevent or hinder2. medicinea. tending to prevent disease; prophylacticb. of or relating to the branch of medicine concerned with prolonging life and preventing disease3. (in Britain) of, relating to, or belonging to the customs and excise service or the coastguardnoun4. something that serves to prevent or hinder5. medicine any drug or agent that tends to prevent or protect against disease6. another name for contraceptive

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]Preventive actions are intended to help prevent things such as disease or crime. ⇒ Too much is spent on expensive curative medicine and too little on preventive medicine. ⇒ People accused the ministry of failing to take adequate preventive measures.
Example sentences containing 'preventive'
Suicide had the potential for being a contagious disease when the proper preventive measures weren't taken. Barbara Delinsky SUDDENLY
