abstain

(əbˈsteɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; usually foll by from)1. to choose to refrain ⇒ he abstained from alcohol2. to refrain from voting, esp in a committee, legislature, etc
Derived forms
abˈstainer noun
Word origin
C14: via Old French from Latin abstinēre, from abs- ab-1 + tenēre to hold, keep

Powered by COBUILD
(æbsteɪn )
Word forms: abstains , abstaining , abstained
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you abstain from something, usually something you want to do, you deliberately do not do it. [formal] [V + from] ⇒ Abstain from sex or use condoms. [V] ⇒ Do you drink alcohol, smoke, or abstain?2. verbIf you abstain during a vote, you do not use your vote. [V] ⇒ Three Conservative MPs abstained in the vote.
