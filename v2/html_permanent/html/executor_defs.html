executor

(ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtə )
Definitions
noun1. law a person appointed by a testator to carry out the wishes expressed in his or her will2. a person who executes
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

executorial (exˌecuˈtorial) adjectiveexecutorship (exˈecutorˌship) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Anglo-French executour, from Latin execūtor, from ex-1 + sequi follow
