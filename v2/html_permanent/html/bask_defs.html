bask

(bɑːsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive; usually foll by in)1. to lie in or be exposed to pleasant warmth, esp that of the sun2. to flourish or feel secure under some benevolent influence or favourable condition
Word origin
C14: from Old Norse bathask to bathe

Word forms: basks , basking , basked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you bask in the sunshine, you lie somewhere sunny and enjoy the heat. [V + in] ⇒ All through the hot, still days of their holiday Amy basked in the sun. [V] ⇒ Crocodiles bask on the small sandy beaches.2. verbIf you bask in someone's approval, favour, or admiration, you greatly enjoy their positive reaction towards you. [V + in] ⇒ He has spent a month basking in the adulation of the fans back in Jamaica. [V in n] ⇒ Livy smiled and basked in Rachel's approval.
