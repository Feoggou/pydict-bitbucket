bawl

(bɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to utter long loud cries, as from pain or frustration; wail2. to shout loudly, as in angernoun3. a loud shout or cry
Derived forms
bawler (ˈbawler) nounbawling (ˈbawling) noun
Word origin
C15: probably from Icelandic baula to low; related to Medieval Latin baulāre to bark, Swedish böla to low; all of imitative origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you bawl, you shout in a very loud voice, for example because you are angry or you want people to hear you. [V + at] ⇒ When I came back to the hotel Laura and Peter were shouting and bawling at each other. [V with quote] ⇒ Then a voice bawled: 'Lay off! I'll kill you, you little rascal!'. [V n] ⇒ He tried to direct the video like a fashion show, bawling instructions to the girls.Bawl out means the same as bawl. yell out [V P with quote] ⇒ Someone in the audience bawled out 'Not him again!' [Also V P n, V n P, V P + to]2. verbIf you say that a child is bawling, you are annoyed because it is crying loudly. [V] ⇒ One of the toddlers was bawling, and the other had a runny nose. [V-ing] ⇒ ...a bawling baby. [Also V with quote]► →  bawl out
