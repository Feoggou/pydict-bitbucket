plush

(plʌʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a fabric with a cut pile that is longer and softer than velvetb. (as modifier) ⇒ a plush chairadjective2. Also: plushy informal lavishly appointed; rich; costly

Derived Forms

plushly (ˈplushly) adverbplushness (ˈplushness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from French pluche, from Old French peluchier to pluck, ultimately from Latin pilus a hair, pile3

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something as plush, you mean that it is very smart, comfortable, or expensive. ⇒ ...a plush, four-storey, Georgian house in Mayfair. ⇒ ...one of the plushest posts in U.S. diplomacy.2. uncount nounPlush is a thick soft material like velvet, used especially for carpets and to cover furniture. ⇒ All the seats were in red plush.
