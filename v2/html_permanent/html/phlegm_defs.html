phlegm

(flɛm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the viscid mucus secreted by the walls of the respiratory tract2. archaic one of the four bodily humours3. apathy; stolidity; indifference4. self-possession; imperturbability; coolness

Derived Forms

phlegmy (ˈphlegmy) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French fleume, from Late Latin phlegma, from Greek: inflammation, from phlegein to burn
