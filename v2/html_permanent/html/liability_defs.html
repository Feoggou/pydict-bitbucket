liability

(ˌlaɪəˈbɪlɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the state of being liable2. a financial obligation3. a hindrance or disadvantage4. likelihood or probability

liability

Powered by COBUILD
(laɪəbɪlɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: liabilities
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]If you say that someone or something is a liability, you mean that they cause a lot of problems or embarrassment. ⇒ As the president's prestige continues to fall, they're clearly beginning to consider him a liability. ⇒ ...what was once a vote catching policy, is now a political liability.2. countable noun [usu pl]A company's or organization's liabilities are the sums of money which it owes. [business, or law] ⇒ The company had assets of $138 million and liabilities of $120.5 million.3. → See also liable
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
