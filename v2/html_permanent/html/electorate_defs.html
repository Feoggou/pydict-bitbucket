electorate

(ɪˈlɛktərɪt )
Definitions
noun1. the body of all qualified voters2. the rank, position, or territory of an elector of the Holy Roman Empire3. Australian and New Zealand the area represented by a Member of Parliament4. Australian and New Zealand the voters in a constituency
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

electorate

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪlektərət ) for learners of English
Word forms: electorates
Definitions
countable noun [with sing or pl verb]The electorate of a country or area is all the people in it who have the right to vote in an election. ⇒ He has the backing of almost a quarter of the electorate. ⇒ ...the Maltese electorate.
