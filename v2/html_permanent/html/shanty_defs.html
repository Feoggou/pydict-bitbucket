shanty1

(ˈʃæntɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. a ramshackle hut; crude dwelling2. Australian and New Zealand a public house, esp an unlicensed one3. (formerly, in Canada)a. a log bunkhouse at a lumber campb. the camp itself
Word origin
C19: from Canadian French chantier cabin built in a lumber camp, from Old French gantier gantry

shanty2

, shantey (ˈʃæntɪ ) chanty or chantey (ˈʃæntɪ, ˈtʃæn-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
USnoun plural -ties or -teysa song originally sung by sailors, esp a rhythmic one forming an accompaniment to work
Word origin
C19: from French chanter to sing; see chant

shanty

Powered by COBUILD
(ʃænti )
Word forms: shanties
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA shanty is a small rough hut which poor people live in, built from tin, cardboard, or other materials that are not very strong.2. countable nounA shanty is a song which sailors used to sing while they were doing work on a ship. ⇒ ...one of my father's favourite sea-shanties.
