exemption

(ɛgˈzɛmpʃən ; ɪgˈzɛmpʃən ) in American English
Definitions
noun1.  an exempting or being exempted; freedom or release from a liability, obligation, etc.; immunity2. a.  the exempting from an individual's taxable income of a specified sum for the taxpayer and each dependentb.  the sum specifiedc.  any such dependentSYNONYMY NOTE: exemption implies release from some obligation or legal requirement, esp. where others are not so released [exemption from the military draft]; immunity implies freedom from or protection against something disagreeable or menacing to which all or many are liable [immunity from a penalty, disease, taxes]; impunity specifically implies escape or freedom from punishment [to commit a crime with impunity]
