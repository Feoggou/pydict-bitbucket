poker1

(ˈpəʊkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a metal rod, usually with a handle, for stirring a fire2. a person or thing that pokes

poker2

(ˈpəʊkə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna card game of bluff and skill in which bets are made on the hands dealt, the highest-ranking hand (containing the most valuable combinations of sequences and sets of cards) winning the pool

Word Origin

C19: probably from French poque similar card game

poker

Powered by COBUILD
(poʊkəʳ )
Word forms: pokers
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounPoker is a card game that people usually play in order to win money. ⇒ Lon and I play in the same weekly poker game.2. countable nounA poker is a metal bar which you use to move coal or wood in a fire in order to make it burn better. ⇒ Mrs Malone took up the poker and stirred at the little fire burning beside her.
Example sentences containing 'poker'
Ben Walker, the chief of police before Jeffrey, played poker with Eddie Linton every Friday night. Karin Slaughter BLINDSIGHTED
