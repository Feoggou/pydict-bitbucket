flattered

(ˈflætəd)
Definitions
adjectivepleased and gratified ⇒ I was genuinely very flattered., ⇒ Miles was flattered by all the attention he was receiving., ⇒ She was flattered by Roberto's long letter., ⇒ She didn't know whether to feel flattered or indignant., ⇒ I felt very flattered to have been asked to join the board., ⇒ I was flattered that he remembered me., ⇒ I feel flattered that they should be so supportive., ⇒ We're flattered and honoured to receive this award.

flattered

Powered by COBUILD
(flætəʳd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ, ADJ that/to-inf]If you are flattered by something that has happened, you are pleased about it because it makes you feel important or special. [+ by] ⇒ She was flattered by Roberto's long letter. ⇒ I am flattered that they should be so supportive. ⇒ She didn't know whether to feel flattered or indignant.2. graded adjective [v-link ADJ, oft ADJ that/to-inf]People sometimes use flattered when they are expressing thanks in formal situations. ⇒ We're flattered and honoured to receive this Doris Day Award.
