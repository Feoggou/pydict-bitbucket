ample

(ˈæmpəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. more than sufficient; abundant ⇒ an ample helping2. large in size, extent, or amount ⇒ of ample proportions
Derived forms
ampleness (ˈampleness) noun
Word origin
C15: from Old French, from Latin amplus spacious

Word forms: ampler , amplest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If there is an ample amount of something, there is enough of it and usually some extra. ⇒ There'll be ample opportunity to relax, swim and soak up some sun. ⇒ The design of the ground floor created ample space for a good-sized kitchen.amply graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ They have been amply rewarded with huge salaries.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe someone's figure as ample, you mean that they are large in a pleasant or attractive way. [written] ⇒ ...a young mother with a baby resting against her ample bosom.
