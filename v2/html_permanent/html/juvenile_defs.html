juvenile

(ˈdʒuːvɪˌnaɪl )
Definitions
adjective1. young, youthful, or immature2. suitable or designed for young people ⇒ juvenile pastimes3. (of animals or plants) not yet fully mature4. of or denoting young birds that have developed their first plumage of adult feathers5. geology occurring at the earth's surface for the first time; new ⇒ juvenile water, ⇒ juvenile gasesnoun6. a juvenile person, animal, or plant7. an actor who performs youthful roles8. a book intended for young readers
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

juvenilely (ˈjuveˌnilely) adverbjuvenileness (ˈjuveˌnileness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin juvenīlis youthful, from juvenis young

juvenile for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒuːvənaɪl )
Word forms: juveniles
Definitions
1. countable nounA juvenile is a child or young person who is not yet old enough to be regarded as an adult. [formal] ⇒ The number of juveniles in the general population has fallen by a fifth in the past 10 years.2. adjective [ADJ n]Juvenile activity or behaviour involves young people who are not yet adults. ⇒ Juvenile crime is increasing at a terrifying rate. ⇒ ...a scheme to lock up persistent juvenile offenders.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as juvenile, you are critical of it because you think that it is silly or childish. [disapproval] ⇒ He's a typical male, as he gets older he becomes more juvenile.4. countable nounYoung animals are sometimes referred to as juveniles.
