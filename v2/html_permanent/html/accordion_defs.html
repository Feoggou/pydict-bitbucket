accordion

(əˈkɔːdɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a portable box-shaped instrument of the reed organ family, consisting of metallic reeds that are made to vibrate by air from a set of bellows controlled by the player's hands. Notes are produced by means of studlike keys2. short for piano accordion
Derived forms
acˈcordionist noun
Word origin
C19: from German Akkordion, from Akkord harmony, chord

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounAn accordion is a musical instrument in the shape of a fairly large box which you hold in your hands. You play the accordion by pressing keys or buttons on either side while moving the two sides together and apart. Accordions are used especially to play traditional popular music.
