(slaɪmi )
Word forms: slimier , slimiest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSlimy substances are thick, wet, and unpleasant. Slimy objects are covered in a slimy substance. ⇒ His feet slipped in the slimy mud. ⇒ Her hand touched something cold and slimy.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as slimy, you dislike them because they are friendly and pleasant in an insincere way. [British, informal, disapproval] ⇒ I've worked hard for what I have and I don't want it taken away by some slimy business partner.
