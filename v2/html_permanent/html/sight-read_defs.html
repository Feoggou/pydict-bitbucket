sight-read

(ˈsaɪtˌriːd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb -reads, -reading or -read (-ˌrɛd)to sing or play (music in a printed or written form) without previous preparation

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The form sight-read is used in the present tense, where it is pronounced (saɪt riːd ), and is the past tense and past participle, pronounced (saɪt red ).verbSomeone who can sight-read can play or sing music from a printed sheet the first time they see it, without practising it beforehand. [V] ⇒ Symphony musicians cannot necessarily sight-read. [Also V n]
