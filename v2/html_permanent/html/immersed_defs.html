immersed

(ɪˈmɜːst )
Definitions
adjective1. sunk or submerged2. (of plants) growing completely submerged in water3. (of a plant or animal organ) embedded in another organ or part4. involved deeply; engrossed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'immersed'
Standing close to, they were immersed in each other's breath. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSWe sometimes sit in the kitchen feeding our faces while Mum's in the living-room immersed in a book. Mosco, Maisie OUT OF THE ASHESI can't picture it --- if I try to I can only see him suffocating, immersed in her voluminous dollops of flesh. Jane Asher LOSING ITThe two men drank in silence, each immersed in the ritual, savoring the confluence of two extreme tastes in their mouths. Lustbader, Eric FLOATING CITY
