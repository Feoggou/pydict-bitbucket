rife

(raɪf )
Definitions
adjective (postpositive)1. of widespread occurrence; prevalent or current ⇒ rumour was rife in the village2. very plentiful; abundant3. (foll by with) abounding (in) ⇒ a land rife with poverty
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rifely (ˈrifely) adverbrifeness (ˈrifeness) noun

Word Origin

Old English rīfe; related to Old Norse rīfr generous, Middle Dutch rīve

rife

Powered by COBUILD
(raɪf ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you say that something, usually something bad, is rife in a place or that the place is rife with it, you mean that it is very common. ⇒ Speculation is rife that he will be sacked. ⇒ Bribery and corruption were rife in the industry. [+ with] ⇒ Hollywood soon became rife with rumors.
