strangely

Powered by COBUILD
(streɪndʒli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverbYou use strangely to emphasize that what you are saying is surprising. [emphasis] ⇒ Strangely, the race didn't start until 8.15pm. ⇒ No, strangely enough, this is not the case.2. → see also strange
Example sentences containing 'strangely'
The news was terribly sad, but the urge for action was strangely exciting. Jenny Colgan LOOKING FOR ANDREW MCCARTHYDavid's profile was heavily shadowed, giving his eyes a strangely hooded appearance. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERLike Tarja, he had never faced a battle on such a scale, and the aftermath left him strangely unsettled. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEP
