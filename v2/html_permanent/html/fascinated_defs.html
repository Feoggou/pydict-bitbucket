Definitions
adjectiveattracted and delighted ⇒ She caught her breath; one brief glance confirmed she was watching, fascinated more than horrified., ⇒ I sat on the stairs and watched, fascinated., ⇒ I ended the call and smiled sweetly at my fascinated audience., ⇒ Lionel listened in fascinated dread., ⇒ As a child, I had watched him do this with fascinated admiration., ⇒ Paula watched her with fascinated curiosity., ⇒ She was fascinated by 'Coco' Chanel., ⇒ I was fascinated by his story., ⇒ Dali was fascinated with Adolf Hitler., ⇒ I'm fascinated with the retro look.

fascinated

Powered by COBUILD
(fæsɪneɪtɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are fascinated by something, you find it very interesting and attractive, and your thoughts tend to concentrate on it. ⇒ I sat on the stairs and watched, fascinated. [+ by] ⇒ A new generation of scientists became fascinated by dinosaurs. [Also + with]
