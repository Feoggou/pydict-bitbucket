felt2

(fɛlt )
Definitions
noun1. a. a matted fabric of wool, hair, etc, made by working the fibres together under pressure or by heat or chemical actionb. (as modifier) ⇒ a felt hat2. any material, such as asbestos, made by a similar process of mattingverb3. (transitive) to make into or cover with felt4. (intransitive) to become matted
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English; related to Old Saxon filt, Old High German filz felt, Latin pellere to beat, Greek pelas close; see anvil, filter
