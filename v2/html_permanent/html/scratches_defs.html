scratches

(ˈskrætʃɪz )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun(functioning as singular) a disease of horses characterized by dermatitis in the region of the fetlock. Also called: cracked heels, mud fever

Word Origin

C16: so called because it makes the pastern appear to be scratched
Example sentences containing 'scratches'
It was doubly ridiculous, in that the Prince still bore the deep scratches from his good friend's hunting cat. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThe snaky patterns round the rim seemed little more than scratches , worn thin with the scrubbing of centuries. Amanda Hemingway THE GREENSTONE GRAIL: THE SANGREAL TRILOGY ONE
