despise

(dɪˈspaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to look down on with contempt; scorn ⇒ he despises flattery
Derived forms
despiser (deˈspiser) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French despire, from Latin dēspicere to look down, from de- + specere to look

Word forms: despises , despising , despised
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you despise something or someone, you dislike them and have a very low opinion of them. [V n] ⇒ I can never, ever forgive him. I despise him. [V n] ⇒ She secretly despises his work. [V pron-refl for n/-ing] ⇒ How I despised myself for my cowardice!
