entail

(ɪnˈteɪl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to bring about or impose by necessity; have as a necessary consequence ⇒ this task entails careful thought2. property law to restrict (the descent of an estate) to a designated line of heirs3. logic to have as a necessary consequencenoun4. property lawa. the restriction imposed by entailing an estateb. an estate that has been entailed
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

entailer (enˈtailer) noun

Word Origin

C14: entaillen, from en-1 + taille limitation, tail2

entail

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnteɪl ) for learners of English
Word forms: entails , entailing , entailed
Definitions
verbIf one thing entails another, it involves it or causes it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Such a decision would entail a huge political risk. [V n] ⇒ The changed outlook entails higher economic growth than was previously assumed. [V -ing] ⇒ The job of a choreologist entails teaching dancers the technique and performance of dance movements. [V n v-ing] ⇒ I'll never accept parole because that entails me accepting guilt.
