convey

(kənˈveɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to take, carry, or transport from one place to another2. to communicate (a message, information, etc)3. (of a channel, path, etc) to conduct, transmit, or transfer4. law to transmit or transfer (the title to property)5. archaic to steal
Derived forms
conveyable (conˈveyable) adjective
Word origin
C13: from Old French conveier, from Medieval Latin conviāre to escort, from Latin com- with + via way

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo convey information or feelings means to cause them to be known or understood by someone. [V n] ⇒ When I returned home, I tried to convey the wonder of this machine to my husband. [V n] ⇒ In every one of her pictures she conveys a sense of immediacy. [V n] ⇒ He also conveyed his views and the views of the bureaucracy.2. verbTo convey someone or something to a place means to carry or transport them there. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The railway company extended a branch line to Brightlingsea to convey fish direct to Billingsgate.
