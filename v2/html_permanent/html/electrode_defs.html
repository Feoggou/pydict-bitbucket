electrode

(ɪˈlɛktrəʊd )
Definitions
noun1. a conductor through which an electric current enters or leaves an electrolyte, an electric arc, or an electronic valve or tube2. an element in a semiconducting device that emits, collects, or controls the movement of electrons or holes
