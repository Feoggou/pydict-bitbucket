fully

(ˈfʊlɪ )
Definitions
adverb1. to the greatest degree or extent; totally; entirely2. amply; sufficiently; adequately ⇒ they were fully fed3. at least ⇒ it was fully an hour before she came

fully

Powered by COBUILD
(fʊli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v]Fully means to the greatest degree or extent possible. ⇒ She was fully aware of my thoughts. ⇒ I don't fully agree with that.2. adverb [ADV with v]You use fully to say that a process is completely finished. ⇒ He had still not fully recovered.3. graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v]If you describe, answer, or deal with something fully, you leave out nothing that should be mentioned or dealt with. ⇒ Fiers promised to testify fully and truthfully. ⇒ Major elements of these debates are discussed more fully later in this book.4. adverbFully is used to emphasize how great an amount is. [written, emphasis] ⇒ Fully 30% of the poor could not even afford access to illegal shanties.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
