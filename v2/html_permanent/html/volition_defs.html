volition

(vəˈlɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of exercising the will ⇒ of one's own volition2. the faculty or capability of conscious choice, decision, and intention; the will3. the resulting choice or resolution4. philosophy an act of will as distinguished from the physical movement it intends to bring about
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

volitional (voˈlitional) or volitionary (voˈlitionary) adjectivevolitionally (voˈlitionally) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin volitiō, from Latin vol- as in volō I will, present stem of velle to wish
