saturate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (ˈsætʃəˌreɪt )1. to fill, soak, or imbue totally2. to make (a chemical compound, vapour, solution, magnetic material, etc) saturated or (of a compound, vapour, etc) to become saturated3. (transitive) military to bomb or shell heavilyadjective (ˈsætʃərɪt , -ˌreɪt)4. a less common word for saturated

Derived Forms

ˌsaturater (ˌsatuˈrater) or ˌsaturator (ˌsatuˈrator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin saturāre, from satur sated, from satis enough

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf people or things saturate a place or object, they fill it completely so that no more can be added. [V n] ⇒ In the last days before the vote, both sides are saturating the airwaves. [be V-ed + with] ⇒ As the market was saturated with goods and the economy became more balanced, inflation went down.saturated adjective ⇒ As the domestic market becomes saturated, firms begin to export the product.2. verb [usu passive]If someone or something is saturated, they become extremely wet. [be V-ed] ⇒ If the filter has been saturated with motor oil, it should be discarded and replaced. [Also V n]saturated adjective ⇒ His work clothes, having become saturated with oil, had to be cleaned.
