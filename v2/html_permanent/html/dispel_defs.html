dispel

(dɪˈspɛl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -pels, -pelling or -pelled(transitive) to disperse or drive away
Derived forms
dispeller (disˈpeller) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin dispellere, from dis-1 + pellere to drive

verbTo dispel an idea or feeling that people have means to stop them having it. banish [V n] ⇒ The President is attempting to dispel the notion that he has neglected the economy.
