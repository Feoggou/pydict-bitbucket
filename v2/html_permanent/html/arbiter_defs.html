arbiter

(ˈɑːbɪtə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person empowered to judge in a dispute; referee; arbitrator2. a person having complete control of something
Word origin
C15: from Latin, of obscure origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn arbiter is a person or institution that judges and settles a quarrel between two other people or groups. [formal] ⇒ He was the ultimate arbiter on both theological and political matters. ⇒ ...the court's role as arbiter in the law-making process.2. countable nounAn arbiter of taste or style is someone who has a lot of influence in deciding what is fashionable or socially desirable. [formal] ⇒ Sequins have often aroused the scorn of arbiters of taste.
