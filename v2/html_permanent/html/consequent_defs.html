consequent

(ˈkɒnsɪkwənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. following as an effect or result2. following as a logical conclusion or by rational argument3. (of a river) flowing in the direction of the original slope of the land or dip of the stratanoun4. something that follows something else, esp as a result5. logic the resultant clause in a conditional sentence6. See affirming the consequent7. an obsolete term for denominator (sense 1)See note at consequential
Word origin
C15: from Latin consequēns following closely, from consequī to pursue

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n, oft n ADJ upon/on n]Consequent means happening as a direct result of an event or situation. [formal] consequential ⇒ The warming of the Earth and the consequent climatic changes affect us all. ⇒ The changes in social work consequent upon reorganization have been considerable.
