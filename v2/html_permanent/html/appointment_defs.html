appointment

(əˈpɔɪntmənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an arrangement to meet a person or be at a place at a certain time2. the act of placing in a job or position3. the person who receives such a job or position4. the job or position to which such a person is appointed5. (usually plural) a fixture or fitting6. property law nomination to an interest in property under a deed or will

(əpɔɪntmənt )
Word forms: appointments
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [usu with poss]The appointment of a person to a particular job is the choice of that person to do it. ⇒ His appointment to the Cabinet would please the right-wing. ⇒ ...his appointment as foreign minister in 1985.2. countable nounAn appointment is a job or position of responsibility. ⇒ Mr Fay is to take up an appointment as a researcher with the Royal Society.3. countable noun [N to-inf]If you have an appointment with someone, you have arranged to see them at a particular time, usually in connection with their work or for a serious purpose. [+ with] ⇒ She has an appointment with her accountant. ⇒ I made an appointment to see a specialist. ⇒ ...a dental appointment.4. →  by appointment
