scarce

(skɛəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. rarely encountered2. insufficient to meet the demand3. See make oneself scarceadverb4. archaic or literary scarcely

Derived Forms

scarceness (ˈscarceness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old Norman French scars, from Vulgar Latin excarpsus (unattested) plucked out, from Latin excerpere to select; see excerpt

(skeəʳs )
Word forms: scarcer , scarcest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If something is scarce, there is not enough of it. ⇒ Food was scarce and expensive. ⇒ Jobs are becoming increasingly scarce. ⇒ ...the allocation of scarce resources.2. →  make oneself scarce
Example sentences containing 'scarce'
He had speculated shrewdly a few years before when nickel became a scarce commodity and had made big money. Wood, Ted FOOLS GOLDLuckily those of his staff who hadn't made themselves scarce understood very little of it. Penn, John UNTO THE GRAVEEverything was so scarce , but she managed to find a scrawny chicken, some vegetables. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEI do not ask him how it is going, and I make myself scarce. Michael Gruber TROPIC OF NIGHT
