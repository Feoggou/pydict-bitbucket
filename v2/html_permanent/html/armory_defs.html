armory

(ˈɑrməri )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural ˈarmories1.  Obsolete armor or armorial bearings2.  a storehouse for weapons; arsenal3.  an aggregate of resources, etc.4.  Us a building housing the drill hall and offices of a National Guard unit5.  Us a place where firearms are made6.  heraldry
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.
Word origin
altered, by assoc. with armor < OFr armoierie, science of heraldry < armoier, to blazon coats of arms < arme, arm2

regional note:   in BRIT, use armoury1. countable nounA country's armory is all the weapons and military equipment that it has. ⇒ Nuclear weapons will play a less prominent part in NATO's armory in the future.2. countable nounAn armory is a place where weapons, bombs, and other military equipment are stored. ⇒ ...a failed attempt to steal weapons from an armory.3. countable nounIn the United States, an armory is a building used by the National Guard or Army Reserve for meetings and training. ⇒ The National Guard says an armory in Fairmont has opened to shelter stranded motorists.
