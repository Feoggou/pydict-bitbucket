intense

(ɪnˈtɛns )
Definitions
adjective1. of extreme force, strength, degree, or amount ⇒ intense heat2. characterized by deep or forceful feelings ⇒ an intense person▶ USAGE Intense is sometimes wrongly used where intensive is meant: the land is under intensive (not intense) cultivation. Intensely is sometimes wrongly used where intently is meant: he listened intently (not intensely)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intensely (inˈtensely) adverbintenseness (inˈtenseness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin intensus stretched, from intendere to stretch out; see intend

intense

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntens ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIntense is used to describe something that is very great or extreme in strength or degree. ⇒ He was sweating from the intense heat. ⇒ Suddenly the room filled with intense light. ⇒ Stevens's murder was the result of a deep-seated and intense hatred. ⇒ His threats become more intense, agitated, and frequent.intensely graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The fast-food business is intensely competitive.intensity (ɪntensɪti )Word forms: intensities variable noun [usu with poss] ⇒ The attack was anticipated but its intensity came as a shock.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe an activity as intense, you mean that it is very serious and concentrated, and often involves doing a great deal in a short time. ⇒ The battle for third place was intense. ⇒ The military on both sides are involved in intense activity.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe the way someone looks at you as intense, you mean that they look at you very directly and seem to know what you are thinking or feeling. ⇒ I felt so self-conscious under Luke's mother's intense gaze. ⇒ He gazed at me with those intense blue eyes.intensely graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He sipped his drink, staring intensely at me.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person as intense, you mean that they appear to concentrate very hard on everything that they do, and they feel and show their emotions in a very extreme way. ⇒ I know he's an intense player, but he does enjoy what he's doing. ⇒ She is taller than I imagined, more adult, more intense.intensity uncount noun ⇒ His intensity and the ferocity of his feelings alarmed me.
