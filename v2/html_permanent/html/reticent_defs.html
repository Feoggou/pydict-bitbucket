reticent

(ˈrɛtɪsənt )
Definitions
adjectivenot open or communicative; not saying all that one knows; taciturn; reserved
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reticence (ˈreticence) nounreticently (ˈreticently) adverb

Word Origin

C19: from Latin reticēre to keep silent, from re- + tacēre to be silent

reticent

Powered by COBUILD
(retɪsənt ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is reticent does not tell people about things. [+ about/on] ⇒ She is so reticent about her achievements. ⇒ Mrs. Smith, normally a reticent woman, took it upon herself to write to the President.reticence uncount noun ⇒ Pearl didn't mind his reticence; in fact she liked it.
