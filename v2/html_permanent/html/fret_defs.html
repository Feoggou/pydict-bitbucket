fret1

(frɛt )
Definitions
verbWord forms: frets, fretting or fretted1. to distress or be distressed; worry2. to rub or wear away3. to irritate or be irritated; feel or give annoyance or vexation4. to eat away or be eaten away by chemical action; corrode5. (intransitive) (of a road surface) to become loose so that potholes develop; scab6. to agitate (water) or (of water) to be agitated7. (transitive) to make by wearing away; erodenoun8. a state of irritation or anxiety9. the result of fretting; corrosion10. a hole or channel caused by fretting
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English fretan to eat; related to Old High German frezzan, Gothic fraitan, Latin peredere

fret2

(frɛt )
Definitions
noun1. a repetitive geometrical figure, esp one used as an ornamental border2. such a pattern made in relief and with numerous small openings; fretwork3. heraldry a charge on a shield consisting of a mascle crossed by a saltireverbWord forms: frets, fretting or fretted4. (transitive) to ornament with fret or fretwork
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fretless (ˈfretless) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French frete interlaced design used on a shield, probably of Germanic origin

fret3

(frɛt )
Definitions
nounany of several small metal bars set across the fingerboard of a musical instrument of the lute, guitar, or viol family at various points along its length so as to produce the desired notes when the strings are stopped by the fingers
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fretless (ˈfretless) adjective

Word Origin

C16: of unknown origin
