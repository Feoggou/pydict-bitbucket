anatomy

(əˈnætəmɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -mies1. the science concerned with the physical structure of animals and plants2. the physical structure of an animal or plant or any of its parts3. a book or treatise on this subject4. dissection of an animal or plant5. any detailed analysis ⇒ the anatomy of a crime6. informal the human body
Word origin
C14: from Latin anatomia, from Greek anatomē, from anatemnein to cut up, from ana- + temnein to cut

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAnatomy is the study of the structure of the bodies of people or animals.2. countable noun [usu poss N]You can refer to your body as your anatomy. [humorous] ⇒ The ball hit him in the most sensitive part of his anatomy.3. countable noun [oft with poss]An animal's anatomy is the structure of its body. ⇒ He had worked extensively on the anatomy of living animals.4. singular noun [usu the N of n]The anatomy of a subject or an idea is an examination or investigation of it. ⇒ This was a troubling essay on the anatomy of nationhood.
