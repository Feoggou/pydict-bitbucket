squeak

(skwiːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a short shrill cry or high-pitched sound2. informal an escape (esp in the phrases narrow squeak, near squeak)verb3. to make or cause to make a squeak4. (intr; usually foll by through or by) to pass with only a narrow margin ⇒ to squeak through an examination5. (intransitive) informal to confess information about oneself or another6. (transitive) to utter with a squeak
Derived forms
squeaker (ˈsqueaker) nounsqueaky (ˈsqueaky) adjectivesqueakily (ˈsqueakily) adverbsqueakiness (ˈsqueakiness) noun
Word origin
C17: probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish skväka to croak

squeak

Powered by COBUILD
(skwiːk )
Word forms: squeaks , squeaking , squeaked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something or someone squeaks, they make a short, high-pitched sound. [V] ⇒ My boots squeaked a little as I walked. [V adj] ⇒ The door squeaked open. [V + with] ⇒ She squeaked with delight.Squeak is also a noun. ⇒ He gave an outraged squeak.2. verbTo squeak through or squeak by means to only just manage to get accepted, get included in something, or win something. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The President's economic package squeaked through the House of Representatives by 219 votes to 213. [V prep/adv] ⇒ In spite of a dismal record at school, she narrowly squeaked into design school.3. → see also bubble and squeak
