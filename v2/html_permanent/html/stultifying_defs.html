stultifying

(ˈstʌltɪˌfaɪɪŋ)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. making useless, futile, or ineffectual, esp by routine ⇒ A rigid routine can be stultifying and boring.2. causing to appear absurd or inconsistent
Example sentences containing 'stultifying'
`Perhaps you'd find the city less stultifying ," he challenged. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSShe can feel the stultifying terror caught from Mama's hand, the abject stillness of Papa. Sara MacDonald SEA MUSICAlthough it was getting dark the stultifying heat of earlier still remained. various & introduction by Deirdre Chapman A ROOMFUL OF BIRDS - SCOTTISH SHORT STORIES 1990
