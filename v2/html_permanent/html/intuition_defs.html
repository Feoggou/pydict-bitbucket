intuition

(ˌɪntjʊˈɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. knowledge or belief obtained neither by reason nor by perception2. instinctive knowledge or belief3. a hunch or unjustified belief4. philosophy immediate knowledge of a proposition or object such as Kant's account of our knowledge of sensible objects5. the supposed faculty or process by which we obtain any of these
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

intuitional (ˌintuˈitional) adjectiveintuitionally (ˌintuˈitionally) adverb

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin intuitiō a contemplation, from Latin intuērī to gaze upon, from tuērī to look at
