grimy

Powered by COBUILD
(graɪmi ) for learners of English
Word forms: grimier , grimiest
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is grimy is very dirty. ⇒ ...a grimy industrial city.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'grimy'
Having just woken up, he looked old and tired, his face grey, his working clothes grimy and wrinkled. Cassidy, Anne IN REAL LIFEHe stood up shakily, sniffing loudly, wiping his grimy , stubbled face with the sleeve of his loose, filthy shirt. Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVENShe picked up the battered jug which lay between the bodies; its contents had stained the grimy sheets. Grace, C.L A SHRINE OF MURDERSRichard discreetly flicked a pound coin back through the air toward the man in the doorway, who caught it in one grimy hand. Neil Gaiman NEVERWHERE
