twirl

(twɜːl )
Definitions
verb1. to move or cause to move around rapidly and repeatedly in a circle2. (transitive) to twist, wind, or twiddle, often idly ⇒ she twirled her hair around her finger3. (intr; often foll by around or about) to turn suddenly to face another way ⇒ she twirled around angrily to face himnoun4. an act of rotating or being rotated; whirl or twist5. something wound around or twirled; coil6. a written flourish or squiggle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

twirler (ˈtwirler) noun

Word Origin

C16: perhaps a blend of twist + whirl

twirl

Powered by COBUILD
(twɜːʳl ) for learners of English
Word forms: twirls , twirling , twirled
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf you twirl something or if it twirls, it turns around and around with a smooth, fairly fast movement. [V n] ⇒ Bonnie twirled her empty glass in her fingers. [V prep/adv] ⇒ All around me leaves twirl to the ground. [Also V]2. verbIf you twirl, you turn around and around quickly, for example when you are dancing. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Several hundred people twirl around the ballroom dance floor.3. verbIf you twirl something such as your hair, you twist it around your finger. [V n] ⇒ Sarah lifted her hand and started twirling a strand of hair.
