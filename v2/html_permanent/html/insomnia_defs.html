insomnia

(ɪnˈsɒmnɪə )
Definitions
nounchronic inability to fall asleep or to enjoy uninterrupted sleep ▶ Related adjective: agrypnotic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

insomnious (inˈsomnious) adjective

Word Origin

C18: from Latin, from insomnis sleepless, from somnus sleep

insomnia n. (uncount noun). (ɪnˈsɒmnɪə). 1. an inability to sleep; chronic sleeplessness. 2. chronic inability to fall asleep or to enjoy uninterrupted sleep. 3. insomnia is a sleep disorder; sleeplessness, wakefulness, restlessness, inability to sleep. Antonyms: hypersomnia أرَق؛ سُهَاد؛ سُهْد
