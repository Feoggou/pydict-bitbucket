parole

(pəˈrəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. the freeing of a prisoner before his or her sentence has expired, on the condition that he or she is of good behaviourb. the duration of such conditional release2. a promise given by a prisoner, as to be of good behaviour if granted liberty or partial liberty3. a variant spelling of parol4. US military a password5. linguistics language as manifested in the individual speech acts of particular speakers. Compare langue, performance (sense 7), competence (sense 5)6. See on paroleverb (transitive)7. to place (a person) on parole

Derived Forms

parolable (paˈrolable) adjectiveparolee (pəˌrəʊˈliː ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Old French, from the phrase parole d'honneur word of honour; parole from Late Latin parabola speech

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounIf a prisoner is given parole, he or she is released before the official end of their prison sentence and has to promise to behave well. ⇒ Although sentenced to life, he will become eligible for parole after serving 10 years.→  on parole2. verb [usu passive]If a prisoner is paroled, he or she is given parole. [be V-ed] ⇒ He faces at most 12 years in prison and could be paroled after eight years.
