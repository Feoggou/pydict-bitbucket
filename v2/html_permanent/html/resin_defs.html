resin

(ˈrɛzɪn )
Definitions
noun1. any of a group of solid or semisolid amorphous compounds that are obtained directly from certain plants as exudations. They are used in medicine and in varnishes2. any of a large number of synthetic, usually organic, materials that have a polymeric structure, esp such a substance in a raw state before it is moulded or treated with plasticizer, stabilizer, filler, etc. Compare plastic (sense 1)verb3. (transitive) to treat or coat with resin
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

resinous (ˈresinous) adjectiveresinously (ˈresinously) adverbresinousness (ˈresinousness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French resine, from Latin rēsīna, from Greek rhētinē resin from a pine
