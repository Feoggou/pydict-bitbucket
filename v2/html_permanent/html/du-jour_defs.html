du jour

(duː ˈʒɔː , French dy ʒur)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(postpositive) informal currently very fashionable or popular ⇒ the young writer du jour
Word origin
C20: from French, literally: of the day (as used on restaurant menus of items that change daily)
Example sentences containing 'du jour'
She wanted to devour this man and all he was interested in was the plat du jour. Pacter, Trudi YELLOW BIRDThat's the cri du jour " "Lucidiots," Will said again, most entertained. Clive Barker SACRAMENTLabane was making nuclear power the issue du jour on his inaugural program. S.M. Stirling T2©: RISING STORM
