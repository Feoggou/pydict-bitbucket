book in

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. to reserve a room for (oneself or someone else) at a hotel2. mainly British to record something in a book or register, esp one's arrival at a hotel

book in

Powered by COBUILD
or book into
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phrasal verbWhen you book into a hotel or when you book in, you officially state that you have arrived to stay there, usually by signing your name in a register. [British] [V P n] ⇒ He was happy to book into the Royal Pavilion Hotel. [V n P n] ⇒ Today Mahoney booked himself into one of the best hotels in Sydney. [V P] ⇒ The three men stayed at two hotels in Nottingham, booking in at one the day before the crime. [Also V n P]regional note:   in AM, use check in, check into →  book
