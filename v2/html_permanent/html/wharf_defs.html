wharf

(wɔːf )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural wharves (wɔːvz ) or wharfs1. a platform of timber, stone, concrete, etc, built parallel to the waterfront at a harbour or navigable river for the docking, loading, and unloading of ships2. See the wharves3. an obsolete word for shore1verb (transitive)4. to moor or dock at a wharf5. to provide or equip with a wharf or wharves6. to store or unload on a wharf
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hwearf heap; related to Old Saxon hwarf, Old High German hwarb a turn, Old Norse hvarf circle
