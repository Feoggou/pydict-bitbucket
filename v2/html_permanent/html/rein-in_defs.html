rein in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbTo rein in something means to control it. [V P n] ⇒ His administration's economic policy would focus on reining in inflation. [V n P] ⇒ Mary spoiled both her children, then tried too late to rein them in.2. phrasal verbIf you rein in a horse, you stop it or cause it to go more slowly by pulling its reins. [V P] ⇒ The horsemen reined in and shouted at the men behind to turn back. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ Mrs Glick reined in the horse and stopped at the crossroads. [Also V n P] →  rein
