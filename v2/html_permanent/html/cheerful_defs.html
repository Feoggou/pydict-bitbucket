cheerful

(ˈtʃɪəfʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having a happy disposition; in good spirits2. pleasantly bright; gladdening ⇒ a cheerful room3. hearty; ungrudging; enthusiastic ⇒ cheerful help
Derived forms
cheerfully (ˈcheerfully) adverbcheerfulness (ˈcheerfulness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is cheerful is happy and shows this in their behaviour. ⇒ They are both very cheerful in spite of their colds. [+ about] ⇒ Jack sounded quite cheerful about the idea.cheerfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'We've come with good news,' Pat said cheerfully. ⇒ She greeted him cheerfully.cheerfulness uncount noun [oft adj N] ⇒ I remember this extraordinary man with particular affection for his unfailing cheerfulness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is cheerful is pleasant and makes you feel happy. ⇒ The nursery is bright and cheerful, with plenty of toys.3. graded adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe someone's attitude as cheerful, you mean they are not worried about something, and you think that they should be. optimistic ⇒ There is little evidence to support many of Mr Will's cheerful assumptions.cheerfully adverb [ADV before v] ⇒ ...cheerfully ignoring medical advice which could have prolonged his life.
