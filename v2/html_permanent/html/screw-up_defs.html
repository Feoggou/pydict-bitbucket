screw up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to twist out of shape or distort2. to summon up or call upon ⇒ to screw up one's courage3. (also intr) informal to mishandle or make a mess (of)4. (often passive) informal to cause to become very anxious, confused, or nervous ⇒ he is really screwed up about his examsnoun screw-up5. slang something mishandled or done badly

screw-up

(ˈskruːˌʌp)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun informal1. something mishandled or done badly ⇒ Someone is going to pay for this screw-up.2. a person who habitually fails or mishandles things ⇒ Bruce was a total screw-up.

screw up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbIf you screw up your eyes or your face, you tighten your eye or face muscles, for example because you are in pain or because the light is too bright. [V P n] ⇒ She had screwed up her eyes, as if she found the sunshine too bright. [V n P] ⇒ Close your eyes and screw them up tight. [V P] ⇒ His face screwed up in agony.2. phrasal verbIf you screw up a piece of paper, you squeeze it tightly so that it becomes very creased and no longer flat, usually when you are throwing it away. [British] [V n P] ⇒ He would start writing to his family and would screw the letter up in frustration. [V P n] ⇒ He screwed up his first three efforts after only a line or two.regional note:   in AM, use crush3. phrasal verbTo screw something up, or to screw up, means to cause something to fail or be spoiled. [informal] [V P n] ⇒ You can't open the window because it screws up the air conditioning. [V n P] ⇒ Get out. Haven't you screwed things up enough already! [V P] ⇒ Somebody had screwed up; they weren't there. →  screw
