Anabaptist

(ˌænəˈbæptɪst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a member of any of various 16th-century Protestant movements that rejected infant baptism, insisted that adults be rebaptized, and sought to establish Christian communism2. a member of a later Protestant sect holding the same doctrines, esp with regard to baptismadjective3. of or relating to these movements or sects or their doctrines
Derived forms
ˌAnaˈbaptism noun
Word origin
C16: from Ecclesiastical Latin anabaptista, from anabaptīzāre to baptize again, from Late Greek anabaptizein; see ana-, baptize
