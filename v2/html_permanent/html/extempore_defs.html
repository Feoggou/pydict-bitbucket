extempore

(ɪkˈstɛmpərɪ )
Definitions
adverb, adjectivewithout planning or preparation; impromptu
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin ex tempore instantaneously, from ex-1 out of + tempus time
Example sentences containing 'extempore'
There would be those dreadful extempore prayers and insincere eulogies from a minister who never knew my sister. Secombe, Fred GOODBYE CURATE

Similar Words
