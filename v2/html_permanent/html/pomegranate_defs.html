pomegranate

(ˈpɒmɪˌɡrænɪt , ˈpɒmˌɡrænɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an Asian shrub or small tree, Punica granatum, cultivated in semitropical regions for its edible fruit: family Punicaceae2. the many-chambered globular fruit of this tree, which has tough reddish rind, juicy red pulp, and many seeds

Word Origin

C14: from Old French pome grenate, from Latin pōmum apple + grenate, from Latin grānātum, from grānātus full of seeds
