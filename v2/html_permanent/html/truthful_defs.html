truthful

(ˈtruːθfʊl )
Definitions
adjective1. telling or expressing the truth; honest or candid2. realistic ⇒ a truthful portrayal of the king
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

truthfully (ˈtruthfully) adverbtruthfulness (ˈtruthfulness) noun

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf a person or their comments are truthful, they are honest and do not tell any lies. OPPOSITE: untruthful ⇒ Most religions teach you to be truthful. [+ about] ⇒ We've all learnt to be fairly truthful about our personal lives. ⇒ She could not give him a truthful answer.truthfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I answered all their questions truthfully.truthfulness uncount noun ⇒ I can say, with absolute truthfulness, that I did not injure her.
