botch

(bɒtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive; often foll by up)1. to spoil through clumsiness or ineptitude2. to repair badly or clumsilynoun3. Also called: botch-up a badly done piece of work or repair (esp in the phrase make a botch of (something))
Derived forms
botcher (ˈbotcher) noun
Word origin
C14: of unknown origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you botch something that you are doing, you do it badly or clumsily. [informal] [V n] ⇒ It is a silly idea and he has botched it. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a botched job.Botch up means the same as botch. [V P n] ⇒ I hate having builders botch up repairs on my house. [V n P] ⇒ Hemingway complained that Nichols had 'botched everything up'.2. countable noun [usu sing]If you make a botch of something that you are doing, you botch it. [informal] ⇒ I rather made a botch of that whole thing.
