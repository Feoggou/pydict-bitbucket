defenceless

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪfensləs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use defenselessgraded adjective & adjectiveIf someone or something is defenceless, they are weak and unable to defend themselves properly. ⇒ ...a savage attack on a defenceless young girl. ⇒ ...the kind of leader who would leave the country isolated and defenceless.
Example sentences containing 'defenceless'
He took off his spectacles, and Annie met his defenceless gaze. Penelope Fitzgerald HUMAN VOICESBut, asleep, he was round-faced and defenceless again, his flimsy shields all gone. Tony Parsons MAN AND WIFEIt looked frail and defenceless , almost as if it were frozen in position, its outline etched by foam. Lyall, Francis A DEATH IN TIME
