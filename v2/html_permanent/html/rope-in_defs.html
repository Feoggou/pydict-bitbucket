rope in

Definitions
verb (tr, adverb)1. British to persuade to take part in some activity2. US and Canadian to trick or entice into some activity

rope in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
phrasal verb [usu passive]If you say that you were roped in to do a particular task, you mean that someone persuaded you to help them do that task. [mainly British, informal] [be V-ed P + for] ⇒ Visitors were roped in for potato picking and harvesting. [get V-ed P to-inf] ⇒ I got roped in to help with the timekeeping. →  rope
