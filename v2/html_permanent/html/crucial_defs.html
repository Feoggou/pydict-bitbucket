crucial

(ˈkruːʃəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. involving a final or supremely important decision or event; decisive; critical2. informal very important3. slang very good
Derived forms
crucially (ˈcrucially) adverb
Word origin
C18: from French, from Latin crux cross

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as crucial, you mean it is extremely important. ⇒ He had administrators under him but took the crucial decisions himself. ⇒ ...the most crucial election campaign for years. [+ to] ⇒ Improved consumer confidence is crucial to an economic recovery.crucially graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Chewing properly is crucially important. ⇒ Crucially, though, it failed to secure the backing of the banks.
