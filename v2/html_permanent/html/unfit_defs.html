unfit

(ʌnˈfɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; often foll by for) unqualified, incapable, or incompetent ⇒ unfit for military service2. (postpositive; often foll by for) unsuitable or inappropriate ⇒ the ground was unfit for football3. in poor physical condition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unfitness (unˈfitness) noun

unfit for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnfɪt )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are unfit, your body is not in good condition because you have not been taking regular exercise. out of shape ⇒ Many children are so unfit they are unable to do even basic exercises.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ, ADJ to-inf]If someone is unfit for something, he or she is unable to do it because of injury or illness. [+ for] ⇒ He had a third examination and was declared unfit for duty. ⇒ Mr Abel's doctor has said he is unfit to travel.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ to-inf]If you say that someone or something is unfit for a particular purpose or job, you are criticizing them because they are not good enough for that purpose or job. [disapproval] [+ for] ⇒ Existing houses are becoming totally unfit for human habitation. ⇒ They were utterly unfit to govern America. ⇒ She is an unfit mother.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
