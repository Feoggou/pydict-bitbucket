Lent

(lɛnt )
Definitions
noun1. Christianity the period of forty weekdays lasting from Ash Wednesday to Holy Saturday, observed as a time of penance and fasting commemorating Jesus' fasting in the wilderness2. (modifier) falling within or associated with the season before Easter ⇒ Lent observance3. (plural) (at Cambridge University) Lent term boat races
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English lencten, lengten spring, literally: lengthening (of hours of daylight)
