exclaim

(ɪkˈskleɪm )
Definitions
verbto cry out or speak suddenly or excitedly, as from surprise, delight, horror, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exclaimer (exˈclaimer) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin exclāmāre, from clāmāre to shout

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪkskleɪm ) for learners of English
Word forms: exclaims , exclaiming , exclaimed
Definitions
verbWriters sometimes use exclaim to show that someone is speaking suddenly, loudly, or emphatically, often because they are excited, shocked, or angry. [V with quote] ⇒ 'He went back to the lab,' Iris exclaimed impatiently. [V that] ⇒ He exclaims that it must be a typing error.
