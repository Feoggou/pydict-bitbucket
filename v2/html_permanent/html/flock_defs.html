flock1

(flɒk )
Definitions
noun (sometimes functioning as plural)1. a group of animals of one kind, esp sheep or birds2. a large number of people; crowd3. a body of Christians regarded as the pastoral charge of a priest, a bishop, the pope, etc4. rare a band of people; groupverb (intransitive)5. to gather together or move in a flock6. to go in large numbers ⇒ people flocked to the church
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English flocc; related to Old Norse flokkr crowd, Middle Low German vlocke

flock2

(flɒk )
Definitions
noun1. a tuft, as of wool, hair, cotton, etc2. a. waste from fabrics such as cotton, wool, or other cloth used for stuffing mattresses, upholstered chairs, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ flock mattress3. very small tufts of wool applied to fabrics, wallpaper, etc, to give a raised pattern4. another word for flocculeverb5. (transitive) to fill, cover, or ornament with flock
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flocky (ˈflocky) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French floc, from Latin floccus; probably related to Old High German floccho down, Norwegian flugsa snowflake

flock

Powered by COBUILD
(flɒk ) for learners of English
Word forms: flocks , flocking , flocked
Definitions
1. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]A flock of birds, sheep, or goats is a group of them. [+ of] ⇒ They kept a small flock of sheep. ⇒ They are gregarious birds and feed in flocks.2. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]You can refer to a group of people or things as a flock of them to emphasize that there are a lot of them. [emphasis] [+ of] ⇒ These cases all attracted flocks of famous writers. [+ of] ⇒ ...his flock of advisers.3. verbIf people flock to a particular place or event, a very large number of them go there, usually because it is pleasant or interesting. [V + to] ⇒ The public have flocked to the show. [V to-inf] ⇒ The criticisms will not stop people flocking to see the film. [V prep/adv] ⇒ His greatest wish must be that huge crowds flock into the beautiful park.4. countable noun [oft poss N]A clergyman's flock is the group of Christians who come to his church or live in the area that he has responsibility for. [old-fashioned] congregation
