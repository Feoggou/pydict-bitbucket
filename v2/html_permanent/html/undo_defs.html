undo

(ʌnˈduː )
Definitions
verb -does, -doing, -did or -done (mainly tr)1. (also intr) to untie, unwrap, or open or become untied, unwrapped, etc2. to reverse the effects of3. to cause the downfall of4. obsolete to explain or solve

undoer (unˈdoer) noun

undo for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnduː )
Word forms: undoes , undoing , undid , undone
Definitions
1. verbIf you undo something that is closed, tied, or held together, or if you undo the thing holding it, you loosen or remove the thing holding it. [V n] ⇒ I managed secretly to undo a corner of the parcel. [V n] ⇒ I undid the bottom two buttons of my yellow and grey shirt. [V-ed] ⇒ Some clamps that had held the device together came undone.2. verbTo undo something that has been done means to reverse its effect. [V n] ⇒ A heavy-handed approach from the police could undo that good impression. [V n] ⇒ She knew it would be difficult to undo the damage that had been done. [V n] ⇒ If Michael won, he would undo everything I have fought for.3. verb [usu passive]If a person, organization, or plan is undone by something, that thing causes their failure. [be V-ed by n] ⇒ They were undone by a goal from John Barnes. [be V-ed by n] ⇒ Macbeth is the story of a Scottish soldier who becomes king but is undone by his own ambition.4. → See also undoing, undone
