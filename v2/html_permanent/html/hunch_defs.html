hunch

(hʌntʃ )
Definitions
noun1. an intuitive guess or feeling2. another word for hump3. a lump or large pieceverb4. to bend or draw (oneself or a part of the body) up or together5. (intransitive; usually foll by up) to sit in a hunched position
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: of unknown origin

hunch

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌntʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hunches , hunching , hunched
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you have a hunch about something, you are sure that it is correct or true, even though you do not have any proof. [informal] ⇒ I had a hunch that Susan and I would work well together. ⇒ Then Mr. Kamenar, acting on a hunch, ran a computer check at the Federal Election Commission.2. verbIf you hunch forward, you raise your shoulders, put your head down, and lean forwards, often because you are cold, ill, or unhappy. [V adv/prep] ⇒ He got out his map of Yorkshire and hunched over it to read the small print.3. verbIf you hunch your shoulders, you raise them and lean forwards slightly. [V n] ⇒ Wes hunched his shoulders and leaned forward on the edge of the counter.
