plod

(plɒd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: plods, plodding or plodded1. to make (one's way) or walk along (a path, road, etc) with heavy usually slow steps2. (intransitive) to work slowly and perseveringlynoun3. the act of plodding4. the sound of slow heavy steps5. British slang a policeman

Derived Forms

plodding (ˈplodding) adjectiveploddingly (ˈploddingly) adverbploddingness (ˈploddingness) noun

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone plods, they walk slowly and heavily. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Crowds of French and British families plodded around in yellow plastic macs.2. verbIf you say that someone plods on or plods along with a job, you mean that the job is taking a long time. [V adv] ⇒ He is plodding on with negotiations. [V adv] ⇒ Aircraft production continued to plod along at an agonizingly slow pace.plodding graded adjective [usu ADJ n] ⇒ The plot unfolds at a plodding pace.
