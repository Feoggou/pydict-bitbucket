Synonyms of limp 1

limp1

verb

  1. hobble, stagger, stumble, shuffle, halt (archaic), hop, falter, shamble, totter, dodder, hirple (Scottish)
    • He limped off with a leg injury.

noun

  1. lameness, hobble, hirple (Scottish)
    • A stiff knee forced her to walk with a limp.

limp2

adjective

  1. floppy, soft, relaxed, loose, flexible, slack, lax, drooping, flabby, limber, pliable, flaccid
    • The residue can leave the hair limp and dull looking.
    OPPOSITES:  hard, firm, solid, stiff, tense, rigid, taut, unyielding
  2. weak, tired, exhausted, worn out, spent, debilitated, lethargic, enervated
    • He carried her limp body into the room and laid her on the bed.
    OPPOSITES:  strong, tough, powerful, hardy, robust, sturdy
