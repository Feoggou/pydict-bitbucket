Bacchus

(ˈbækəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun(in ancient Greece and Rome) a god of wine and giver of ecstasy, identified with Dionysus
Word origin
C15: from Latin, from Greek Bakkhos; related to Latin bāca small round fruit, berry
Example sentences containing 'Bacchus'
