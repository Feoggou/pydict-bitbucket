vet

Powered by COBUILD
(vet ) for learners of English
Word forms: vets , vetting , vetted
Definitions
1. countable nounA vet is someone who is qualified to treat sick or injured animals. [mainly British]regional note:   in AM, usually use veterinarian2. countable nounA vet is someone who has served in the armed forces of their country, especially during a war. [US, informal] ⇒ The New England Shelter in Boston will serve Christmas dinner for 200 vets. ⇒ All three are Vietnam vets.3. verbIf something is vetted, it is checked carefully to make sure that it is acceptable to people in authority. [mainly British] [be V-ed] ⇒ He can find no trace of a rule requiring research to be vetted before publication. [be V-ed] ⇒ All objects are vetted by a distinguished panel of experts. [V n] ⇒ He had not been allowed to read any book until his mother had vetted it.4. verb [usu passive]If someone is vetted, they are investigated fully before being given a particular job, role, or position, especially one which involves military or political secrets. [British] [be V-ed] ⇒ She was secretly vetted before she ever undertook any work for me. [be V-ed] ⇒ People who marry into the Royal Family will have to be vetted much more carefully in future.vetting uncount noun ⇒ The government is to make major changes to the procedure for carrying out security vetting.
