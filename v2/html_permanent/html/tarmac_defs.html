tarmac

(ˈtɑːmæk )
Definitions
noun1. a paving material that consists of crushed stone rolled and bound with a mixture of tar and bitumen, esp as formerly used for a road, airport runway, etc. Full name: tarmacadam (ˌtɑːməˈkædəm ) See also macadam2. See the tarmacverb -macs, -macking or -macked3. (transitive) (usually not capital) to apply tarmac to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: an abbreviation of tarmacadm, from tar1 + macadam

tarmac

Powered by COBUILD
(tɑːʳmæk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounTarmac is a material used for making road surfaces, consisting of crushed stones mixed with tar. [British, trademark] ⇒ ...a strip of tarmac. ⇒ ...tarmac paths.regional note:   in AM, usually use blacktop2. singular nounThe tarmac is an area with a surface made of tarmac, especially the area from which planes take off at an airport. ⇒ Standing on the tarmac were two American planes.
