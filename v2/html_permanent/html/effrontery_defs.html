effrontery

(ɪˈfrʌntərɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -iesshameless or insolent boldness; impudent presumption; audacity; temerity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from French effronterie, from Old French esfront barefaced, shameless, from Late Latin effrons, literally: putting forth one's forehead; see front

Definitions
uncount nounEffrontery is behaviour that is bold, rude, or disrespectful. [formal, disapproval] [+ of] ⇒ One could only gasp at the sheer effrontery of the man.
