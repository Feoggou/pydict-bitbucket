Julian1

(ˈdʒuːljən , -lɪən)
Definitions
noun known as Julian the Apostate; Latin name Flavius Claudius Julianus. 331–363 ad, Roman emperor (361–363), who attempted to revive paganism in the Roman empire while remaining tolerant to Christians and Jews

Julian2

(ˈdʒuːljən , -lɪən)
Definitions
adjective1. of or relating to Julius Caesar2. denoting or relating to the Julian calendar
