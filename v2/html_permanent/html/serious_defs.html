serious

(ˈsɪərɪəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. grave in nature or disposition; thoughtful ⇒ a serious person2. marked by deep feeling; in earnest; sincere ⇒ is he serious or joking?3. concerned with important matters ⇒ a serious conversation4. requiring effort or concentration ⇒ a serious book5. giving rise to fear or anxiety; critical ⇒ a serious illness6. informal worthy of regard because of substantial quantity or quality ⇒ serious money, ⇒ serious wine7. informal extreme or remarkable ⇒ a serious haircut

Derived Forms

seriousness (ˈseriousness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin sēriōsus, from Latin sērius; probably related to Old English swǣr gloomy, Gothic swers esteemed

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSerious problems or situations are very bad and cause people to be worried or afraid. ⇒ Crime is an increasingly serious problem in Russian society. ⇒ The government still face very serious difficulties. ⇒ Doctors said his condition was serious but stable.seriously graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj/adv, ADV with v] ⇒ If this ban was to come in it would seriously damage my business. ⇒ They are not thought to be seriously hurt.seriousness uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the seriousness of the crisis.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSerious matters are important and deserve careful and thoughtful consideration. ⇒ I regard this as a serious matter. ⇒ Don't laugh boy. This is serious. ⇒ ...the serious business of running the country.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]When important matters are dealt with in a serious way, they are given careful and thoughtful consideration. ⇒ My parents never really faced up to my drug use in any serious way. ⇒ It was a question which deserved serious consideration. ⇒ ...serious discussions.seriously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The management will have to think seriously about their positions.4. adjective [ADJ n]Serious music or literature requires concentration to understand or appreciate it. ⇒ ...serious classical music. ⇒ There is no point reviewing a blockbuster as you might review a serious novel.5. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is serious about something, they are sincere about what they are saying, doing, or intending to do. [+ about] ⇒ You really are serious about this, aren't you? ⇒ I hope you're not serious.seriously adverb [ADV adj/adv, ADV with v] ⇒ Are you seriously jealous of Erica?seriousness uncount noun ⇒ In all seriousness, there is nothing else I can do. ⇒ They had shown a commitment and seriousness of purpose. [Also + of]6. graded adjective & adjectiveSerious people are thoughtful and quiet, and do not laugh very often. ⇒ He's quite a serious person. ⇒ She looked at me with big, serious eyes.seriously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ They spoke to me very seriously but politely.7. adjective [ADJ n]Serious money is a very large amount of money. [informal] ⇒ He started earning serious money only in the sixties.seriously adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ What's it like to be seriously rich at 15?
