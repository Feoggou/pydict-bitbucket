jejune

(dʒɪˈdʒuːn )
Definitions
adjective1. simple; naive; unsophisticated2. insipid; dull; dry3. lacking nourishment; insubstantial or barren
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jejunely (jeˈjunely) adverbjejuneness (jeˈjuneness) or jejunity (jeˈjunity) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin jējūnus hungry, empty

jejune for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɪdʒuːn )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something or someone as jejune, you are criticizing them for being very simple and unsophisticated. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ They were of great service in correcting my jejune generalizations.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something or someone as jejune, you mean they are dull and boring. [old-fashioned] ⇒ We knew we were in for a pretty long, jejune evening.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
