invitation

(ˌɪnvɪˈteɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. a. the act of inviting, such as an offer of entertainment or hospitalityb. (as modifier) ⇒ an invitation dance, ⇒ an invitation race2. the act of enticing or attracting; allurement

invitation

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnvɪteɪʃən ) for learners of English
Word forms: invitations
Definitions
1. countable noun [oft N to-inf]An invitation is a written or spoken request to come to an event such as a party, a meal, or a meeting. [+ to] ⇒ ...an invitation to lunch. ⇒ The Syrians have not yet accepted an invitation to attend. [+ of] ⇒ He's understood to be there at the personal invitation of President Daniel Arap Moi.2. countable nounAn invitation is the card or paper on which an invitation is written or printed. invite ⇒ Hundreds of invitations are being sent out this week. ⇒ ...gold embossed invitation cards.3. singular nounIf you believe that someone's action is likely to have a particular result, especially a bad one, you can refer to the action as an invitation to that result. ⇒ ...a war that most liberal Democrats regarded as an invitation to disaster. [+ to] ⇒ Don't leave your shopping on the back seat of your car–it's an open invitation to a thief.
