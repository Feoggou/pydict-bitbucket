Hipparchus1

(hɪˈpɑːkəs )
Definitions
noun1.  2nd century bc, Greek astronomer. He discovered the precession of the equinoxes, calculated the length of the solar year, and developed trigonometry2.  died 514 bc, tyrant of Athens (527–514)
