pore1

(pɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. (foll by over) to make a close intent examination or study (of a book, map, etc) ⇒ he pored over the documents for several hours2. (foll by over, on, or upon) to think deeply (about) ⇒ he pored on the question of their future3. (foll by over, on, or upon) rare to look earnestly or intently (at); gaze fixedly (upon)See note at pour

Word Origin

C13 pouren; perhaps related to peer2

pore2

(pɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. anatomy, zoology any small opening in the skin or outer surface of an animal2. botany any small aperture, esp that of a stoma through which water vapour and gases pass3. any other small hole, such as a space in a rock, soil, etc

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin porus, from Greek poros passage, pore

pore

Powered by COBUILD
(pɔːʳ )
Word forms: pores , poring , pored
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu pl]Your pores are the tiny holes in your skin. ⇒ The size of your pores is determined by the amount of oil they produce.2. countable noun [usu pl]The pores of a plant are the tiny holes on its surface. ⇒ A plant's lungs are the microscopic pores in its leaves.3. verbIf you pore over or through information, you look at it and study it very carefully. [V + over/through] ⇒ We spent hours poring over travel brochures. [V over/through n] ⇒ It will take several more months to pore through the volumes of documents.4. →  every pore
Example sentences containing 'pore'
No matter how much he thrashed and flailed his limbs, they kept at it so that he was soon bleeding from every pore. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEHang on a minute," Todd objected, sweat bursting from every pore in his body. St. James, Ian FINAL RESORTKane brought up the rear of our company, and he seemed able to sense danger through every pore of his skin.
