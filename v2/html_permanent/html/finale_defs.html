finale

(fɪˈnɑːlɪ )
Definitions
noun1. the concluding part of any performance or presentation2. the closing section or movement of a musical composition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Italian, n use of adj finale, from Latin fīnālis final

finale

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪnɑːli , -næli) for learners of English
Word forms: finales
Definitions
1. countable nounThe finale of a show, piece of music, or series of shows is the last part of it or the last one of them, especially when this is exciting or impressive. ⇒ ... the finale of Shostakovich's Fifth Symphony. ⇒ Tonight's light show is the grand finale of a month-long series of events.2. countable noun [usu sing, oft N to n]If you say that an event provides a particular kind of finale to something, you mean that it provides it with a particular kind of ending. conclusion ⇒ ...the dramatic finale to America's seven-year hostage crisis. ⇒ It was a sad finale to an otherwise spectacular career.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
