tertiary

(ˈtɜːʃərɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. third in degree, order, etc2. (of education) taking place after secondary school, such as at university, college, etc3. (of an industry) involving services as opposed to extraction or manufacture, such as transport, finance, etc. Compare primary (sense 8b), secondary (sense 7)4. Roman Catholic Church of or relating to a Third Order5. chemistrya. (of an organic compound) having a functional group attached to a carbon atom that is attached to three other groupsb. (of an amine) having three organic groups attached to a nitrogen atomc. (of a salt) derived from a tribasic acid by replacement of all its acidic hydrogen atoms with metal atoms or electropositive groups6. Also called: tertial ornithology rare of, relating to, or designating any of the small flight feathers attached to the part of the humerus nearest to the bodynoun plural -tiaries7. Also called: tertial ornithology rare any of the tertiary feathers8. Roman Catholic Church a member of a Third Order
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin tertiārius containing one third, from tertius third

Tertiary

(ˈtɜːʃərɪ )
Definitions
adjective1. of, denoting, or formed in the first period of the Cenozoic era, which lasted for 63 million years, during which mammals became dominantnoun2. See the Tertiary

Definitions
1. adjectiveTertiary means third in order, third in importance, or at a third stage of development. [formal] ⇒ He must have come to know those philosophers through secondary or tertiary sources. ⇒ ...the complementary tertiary colours, russet and olive green.2. adjective [ADJ n]Tertiary education is education at university or college level. [British] ⇒ ...institutions of tertiary education. ⇒ ...Selby Tertiary College.regional note:   in AM, use higher education
