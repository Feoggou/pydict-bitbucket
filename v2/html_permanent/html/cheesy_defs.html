cheesy

(ˈtʃiːzɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: cheesier or cheesiest1. like cheese in flavour, smell, or consistency2. informal (of a smile) broad but possibly insincere ⇒ a big cheesy grin3. informal banal or trite; in poor taste
Derived forms
cheesiness (ˈcheesiness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(tʃiːzi )
Word forms: cheesier , cheesiest
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Cheesy food is food that tastes or smells of cheese. ⇒ ...cheesy biscuits. ⇒ The sauce was too runny and not cheesy enough.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as cheesy, you mean that it is cheap, unpleasant, or insincere. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ ...a cheesy Baghdad hotel. ⇒ The King's Road was getting increasingly cheesy. ⇒ Politicians persist in imagining that 'the people' warm to their cheesy slogans.
