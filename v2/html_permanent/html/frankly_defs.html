frankly

Powered by COBUILD
(fræŋkli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj/-ed]You use frankly when you are expressing an opinion or feeling to emphasize that you mean what you are saying, especially when the person you are speaking to may not like it. [emphasis] ⇒ 'You don't give a damn about my feelings, do you.'—'Quite frankly, I don't.'. ⇒ Frankly, Thomas, this question of your loan is beginning to worry me. ⇒ I was frankly astonished at the degree to which different singers can affect the interpretation of a song.2. → See also frank
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
