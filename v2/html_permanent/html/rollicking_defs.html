rollicking

(ˈrɒlɪkɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. boisterously carefree and swaggeringnoun2. British informal a very severe telling-off; dressing-down
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C20: for sense 2: from rollick (vb) (in former sense: to be angry, make a fuss); perhaps influenced by bollocking

rollicking

Powered by COBUILD
(rɒlɪkɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]A rollicking occasion is cheerful and usually noisy. A rollicking book or film is entertaining and enjoyable, and not very serious. ⇒ Tony Benn's diaries are a rollicking read.Rollicking is also an adverb. ⇒ I'm having a rollicking good time.2. singular nounIf you give someone a rollicking, you tell them off in a very angry way. [British, informal] ⇒ 'The boss gave us a rollicking,' said McGoldrick.
