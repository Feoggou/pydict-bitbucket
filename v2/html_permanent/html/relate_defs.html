relate

(rɪˈleɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to tell or narrate (a story, information, etc)2. (often foll by to) to establish association (between two or more things) or (of something) to have relation or reference (to something else)3. (intransitive; often foll by to) to form a sympathetic or significant relationship (with other people, things, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

relatable (reˈlatable) adjectiverelater (reˈlater) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin relātus brought back, from referre to carry back, from re- + ferre to bear; see refer

Definitions
1. verbIf something relates to a particular subject, it concerns that subject. [V + to] ⇒ Other recommendations relate to the details of how such data is stored. [V to n] ⇒ It does not matter whether the problem you have relates to food, drink, smoking or just living. [V to n] ⇒ I had papers relating to the children which my wife and I had to sign.2. ergative reciprocal verb & verbThe way that two things relate, or the way that one thing relates to another, is the sort of connection that exists between them. [pl-n V] ⇒ More studies will be required before we know what the functions of these genes are and whether they relate to each other. [V + to] ⇒ Cornell University offers a course that investigates how language relates to particular cultural codes. [V n + to] ⇒ Many Christians today feel the need to relate their experience to that of the Hindu, the Buddhist and the Muslim. [V n] ⇒ ...a paper called 'Language and Freedom' in which Chomsky tries to relate his linguistic and political views. [V] ⇒ At the end, we have a sense of names, dates, and events but no sense of how they relate.3. verbIf you can relate to someone, you can understand how they feel or behave so that you are able to communicate with them or deal with them easily. [V + to] ⇒ He is unable to relate to other people. [V to n] ⇒ I think it is important for children to have brothers and sisters they can relate to. [V] ⇒ When people are cut off from contact with others, they lose all ability to relate.4. verbIf you relate a story, you tell it. [formal] [V n + to] ⇒ There were officials to whom he could relate the whole story. [V n] ⇒ She related her tale of living rough.
