haughty

(ˈhɔːtɪ )
Definitions
adjective -tier or -tiest1. having or showing arrogance2. archaic noble or exalted
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

haughtily (ˈhaughtily) adverbhaughtiness (ˈhaughtiness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Old French haut, literally: lofty, from Latin altus high

haughty

Powered by COBUILD
(hɔːti ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You use haughty to describe someone's behaviour or appearance when you disapprove of the fact that they seem to be very proud and to think that they are better than other people. [disapproval] ⇒ He spoke in a haughty tone.haughtily (hɔːtɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ Toni looked at him rather haughtily.
