hinder1

(ˈhɪndə )
Definitions
verb1. to be or get in the way of (someone or something); hamper2. (transitive) to prevent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hinderer (ˈhinderer) nounhindering (ˈhindering) adjective, noun

Word Origin

Old English hindrian; related to Old Norse hindra, Old High German hintarōn

hinder2

(ˈhaɪndə )
Definitions
adjective(prenominal) situated at or further towards the back or rear; posterior ⇒ the hinder parts
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English; related to Old Norse hindri latter, Gothic hindar beyond, Old High German hintar behind

hinder

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪndəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hinders , hindering , hindered
Definitions
1. verbIf something hinders you, it makes it more difficult for you to do something or make progress. [V n] ⇒ Does the fact that your players are part-timers help or hinder you? [V n] ⇒ Further investigation was hindered by the loss of all documentation on the case.2. verbIf something hinders your movement, it makes it difficult for you to move forward or move around. [V n] ⇒ A thigh injury increasingly hindered her mobility. [V n] ⇒ Landslides and bad weather are continuing to hinder the arrival of relief supplies to the area.
