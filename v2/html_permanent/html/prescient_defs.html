Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something was prescient, you mean that they were able to know or predict what was going to happen in the future. [formal] ⇒ ...'Bob Roberts', an eerily prescient comedy about a populist multimillionaire political candidate.prescience uncount noun ⇒ Over the years he's demonstrated a certain prescience in foreign affairs.
Example sentences containing 'prescient'
Perhaps in her way she'd been more prescient than any of us -- me, Marianne or her lover. Hugo Wilcken THE EXECUTIONThe children never win, not against the adults, he had warned Irena -- had he glanced with prescient suspicion at Nikitin, even then? Thomas, Craig THE LAST RAVEN
