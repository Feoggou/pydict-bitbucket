fatten

(ˈfætən )
Definitions
verb1. to grow or cause to grow fat or fatter2. (transitive) to cause (an animal or fowl) to become fat by feeding it3. (transitive) to make fuller or richer4. (transitive) to enrich (soil) by adding fertilizing agents

fatten

Powered by COBUILD
(fætən ) for learners of English
Word forms: fattens , fattening , fattened
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf an animal is fattened, or if it fattens, it becomes fatter as a result of eating more. [be V-ed] ⇒ The cattle are being fattened for slaughter. [V] ⇒ The creature continued to grow and fatten. [Also V n]2. verbIf you say that someone is fattening something such as a business or its profits, you mean that they are increasing the value of the business or its profits, in a way that you disapprove of. [business, disapproval] [V n] ⇒ They have kept the price of sugar artificially high and so fattened the company's profits.Fatten up means the same as fatten. [V P n] ⇒ The Government is making the taxpayer pay to fatten up a public sector business for private sale. [Also V n P]► →  fatten up
