whizz

or whiz (wɪz )
Definitions
verbWord forms: whizzes, whizzing or whizzed1. to make or cause to make a loud humming or buzzing sound2. to move or cause to move with such a sound3. (intransitive) informal to move or go rapidlynoun4. a loud humming or buzzing sound5. informal a person who is extremely skilful at some activity6. a slang word for amphetamine7. See take a whizz
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: of imitative origin

Definitions
1. verbIf something whizzes somewhere, it moves there very fast. [informal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ Stewart felt a bottle whizz past his head. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A car whizzed past.2. countable noun [oft a N at/with/on n]If you are a whizz at something, you are very good at it. [informal] ⇒ Simon's a whizz at card games.
