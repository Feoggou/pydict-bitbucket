headlong

(ˈhɛdˌlɒŋ )
Definitions
adverb, adjective1. with the head foremost; headfirst2. with great hasteadjective3. archaic (of slopes, etc) very steep; precipitous

headlong

Powered by COBUILD
(hedlɒŋ , US -lɔːŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV after v]If you move headlong in a particular direction, you move there very quickly. ⇒ He ran headlong for the open door.Headlong is also an adjective. ⇒ The army was in headlong flight.2. adverb [ADV after v]If you fall or move headlong, you fall or move with your head furthest forward. head first ⇒ She missed her footing and fell headlong down the stairs.3. adverb [ADV after v]If you rush headlong into something, you do it quickly without thinking carefully about it. ⇒ Do not leap headlong into decisions. ⇒ The country, they say, will inevitably now plunge headlong into decadence.Headlong is also an adjective. ⇒ ...the headlong rush to independence.
