stupor

(ˈstjuːpə )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a state of unconsciousness2. mental dullness; torpor

Derived Forms

stuporous (ˈstuporous) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin, from stupēre to be aghast

Word forms: stupors
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [usu sing, oft in/into a N]Someone who is in a stupor is almost unconscious and is unable to act or think normally, especially as a result of drink or drugs. ⇒ He fell back onto the sofa in a drunken stupor. ⇒ He was drinking himself into a stupor every night.
