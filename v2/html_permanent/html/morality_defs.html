morality

(məˈrælɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the quality of being moral2. conformity, or degree of conformity, to conventional standards of moral conduct3. a system of moral principles4. an instruction or lesson in morals5. short for morality play

Word forms: moralities
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounMorality is the belief that some behaviour is right and acceptable and that other behaviour is wrong. ⇒ ...standards of morality and justice in society. ⇒ ...an effort to preserve traditional morality.2. countable nounA morality is a system of principles and values concerning people's behaviour, which is generally accepted by a society or by a particular group of people. ⇒ ...a morality that is sexist. ⇒ ...communities and their shared moralities.3. uncount nounThe morality of something is how right or acceptable it is. [+ of] ⇒ ...the arguments about the morality of blood sports.
