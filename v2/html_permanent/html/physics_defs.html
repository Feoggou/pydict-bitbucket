physics

(ˈfɪzɪks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (functioning as singular)1. the branch of science concerned with the properties of matter and energy and the relationships between them. It is based on mathematics and traditionally includes mechanics, optics, electricity and magnetism, acoustics, and heat. Modern physics, based on quantum theory, includes atomic, nuclear, particle, and solid-state studies. It can also embrace applied fields such as geophysics and meteorology2. physical properties of behaviour ⇒ the physics of the electron3. archaic natural science or natural philosophy

Word Origin

C16: from Latin physica, translation of Greek ta phusika natural things, from phusis nature
