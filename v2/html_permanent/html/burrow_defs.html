burrow

(ˈbʌrəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a hole or tunnel dug in the ground by a rabbit, fox, or other small animal, for habitation or shelter2. a small snug place affording shelter or retreatverb3. to dig (a burrow) in, through, or under (ground)4. (intransitive; often foll by through) to move through by or as by digging ⇒ to burrow through the forest5. (intransitive) to hide or live in a burrow6. (intransitive) to delve deeply ⇒ he burrowed into his pockets7. to hide (oneself)
Derived forms
burrower (ˈburrower) noun
Word origin
C13: probably a variant of borough

(bʌroʊ , US bɜː- )
Word forms: burrows , burrowing , burrowed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA burrow is a tunnel or hole in the ground that is dug by an animal such as a rabbit.2. verbIf an animal burrows into the ground or into a surface, it moves through it by making a tunnel or hole. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The larvae burrow into cracks in the floor.3. verbIf you burrow in a container or pile of things, you search there for something using your hands. [V prep/adv] ⇒ He burrowed into the pile of charts feverishly. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...the enthusiasm with which he burrowed through old records in search of facts.4. verbIf you burrow into something, you move underneath it or press against it, usually in order to feel warmer or safer. [V prep/adv] ⇒ She turned her face away from him, burrowing into her heap of covers.
