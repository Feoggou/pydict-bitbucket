dispose

(dɪˈspəʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive; foll by of)a. to deal with or settleb. to give, sell, or transfer to anotherc. to throw out or awayd. to consume, esp hurriedlye. to kill2. to arrange or settle (matters) by placing into correct or final condition ⇒ man proposes, God disposes3. (transitive) to make willing or receptive4. (transitive) to adjust or place in a certain order or position5. (transitive; often foll by to) to accustom or conditionnoun6. an obsolete word for disposal, disposition
Derived forms
disposer (disˈposer) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French disposer, from Latin dispōnere to set in different places, arrange, from dis-1 + pōnere to place
