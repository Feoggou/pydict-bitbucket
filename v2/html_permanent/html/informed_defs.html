informed

(ɪnˈfɔːmd )
Definitions
adjective1. having much knowledge or education; learned or cultured2. based on information ⇒ an informed judgment

informed

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfɔːʳmd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is informed knows about a subject or what is happening in the world. ⇒ Informed people know the company is shaky. ⇒ ...the importance of keeping the public properly informed.2. → See also well-informed3. adjective [ADJ n]When journalists talk about informed sources, they mean people who are likely to give correct information because of their private or special knowledge. ⇒ According to informed sources, those taken into custody include at least one major-general.4. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]An informed guess or decision is one that likely to be good, because it is based on definite knowledge or information. ⇒ An informed guess at his personal wealth was $1.25 billion. ⇒ We are able to make more informed choices about how we use drugs.5. → See also inform
