drained

(dreɪnd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. worn out; exhausted ⇒ United left the pitch looking stunned and drained., ⇒ I was emotionally drained, hemmed in by my divorce lawyers., ⇒ He could rest only when he was too drained of energy to fret further.2. See drained of colour
Example sentences containing 'drained'
She threw her cigarette out of the window and put a hand through his hair while she drained the glass of wine. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSHis face was ashen, even the high color of his recent exertions on the squash court drained from it. Mark Mills AMAGANSETTHe almost gave up then; determination drained out of him like blood. Jon Cleary YESTERDAY'S SHADOW
