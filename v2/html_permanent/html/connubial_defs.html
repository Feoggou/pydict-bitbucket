connubial

(kəˈnjuːbɪəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveof or relating to marriage; conjugal ⇒ connubial bliss
Derived forms
conˌnubiality (conˌnubiˈality) nounconnubially (conˈnubially) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin cōnūbiālis from cōnūbium marriage, from com- together + nūbere to marry
Example sentences containing 'connubial'
He did not move or speak even when I joined him in the connubial bed, so I concluded I was in disfavor. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY

Similar Words

marital, married, wedded, nuptial, matrimonial, conjugal
