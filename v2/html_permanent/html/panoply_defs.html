panoply

(ˈpænəplɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -plies1. a complete or magnificent array2. the entire equipment of a warrior

Derived Forms

panoplied (ˈpanoplied) adjective

Word Origin

C17: via French from Greek panoplia complete armour, from pan- + hopla armour, pl of hoplon tool

singular nounA panoply of things is a wide range of them, especially one that is considered impressive. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ He was attended, as are all heads of state, by a full panoply of experts. ⇒ ...the marvellous panoply of exhibitions laid on this year.
