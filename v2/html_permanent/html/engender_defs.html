engender

(ɪnˈdʒɛndə )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to bring about or give rise to; produce or cause2. to be born or cause to be born; bring or come into being
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

engenderer (enˈgenderer) nounengenderment (enˈgenderment) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French engendrer, from Latin ingenerāre, from generāre to beget

Definitions
verbIf someone or something engenders a particular feeling, atmosphere, or situation, they cause it to occur. [formal] [V n] ⇒ It helps engender a sense of common humanity. [V n] ⇒ Mr Bowles could engender delight in students and musicians alike.
