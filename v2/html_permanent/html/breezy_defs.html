breezy

(ˈbriːzɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: breezier or breeziest1. fresh; windy ⇒ a breezy afternoon2. casual or carefree; lively; light-hearted ⇒ her breezy nature3. lacking substance; light ⇒ a breezy conversation
Derived forms
breezily (ˈbreezily) adverbbreeziness (ˈbreeziness) noun

Powered by COBUILD
(briːzi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as breezy, you mean that they behave in a casual, cheerful, and confident manner. ⇒ ...his bright and breezy personality. ⇒ Mona tried to sound breezy.breezily (briːzɪli ) graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'Hi,' he said breezily.2. graded adjectiveIf you describe something as breezy, especially music or clothing, you mean that it is bright, lively, and cheerful. [journalism] ⇒ This album is bright, breezy and playful.3. graded adjective & adjectiveWhen the weather is breezy, there is a fairly strong but pleasant wind blowing. ⇒ The day was breezy and warm.
