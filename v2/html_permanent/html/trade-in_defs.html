trade-in

Definitions
noun1. a. a used article given in part payment for the purchase of a new articleb. a transaction involving such part paymentc. the valuation put on the article traded ind. (as modifier) ⇒ a trade-in dealerverb trade in2. (tr, adverb) to give (a used article) as part payment for the purchase of a new article

Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you trade in an old car or appliance, you give it to the person you are buying a new one from so that you pay less. [V n P] ⇒ He had a Rolls-Royce, and he traded it in for two matching silver Range Rovers. [V P n] ⇒ Richard refused to trade in his old Canon cameras.2. → See also trade-in →  trade

trade-in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Word forms: trade-ins
Definitions
countable noun [oft N n]A trade-in is an arrangement in which someone buys something such as a new car or washing machine at a reduced price by giving their old one, as well as money, in payment. part exchange ⇒ ...the trade-in value of the car.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
