worldly-wise

(ˌwɜːldlɪˈwaɪz)
Definitions
adjectiveunderstanding the world and therefore difficult to shock or deceive ⇒ She's very worldly-wise for her age.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

worldly-wise for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as worldly-wise, you mean they are experienced and know about the practical or social aspects of life, and are not easily shocked or impressed.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

She sighed and put on one of her worldly-wise expressions - the experienced woman advising the innocent.
