trenchant

(ˈtrɛntʃənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. keen or incisive ⇒ trenchant criticism2. vigorous and effective ⇒ a trenchant foreign policy3. distinctly defined ⇒ a trenchant outline4. archaic or poetic sharp ⇒ a trenchant sword

Derived Forms

trenchancy (ˈtrenchancy) nountrenchantly (ˈtrenchantly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French trenchant cutting, from trenchier to cut; see trench

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveYou can use trenchant to describe something such as a criticism or comment that is very clear, effective, and forceful. [formal] ⇒ He was shattered and bewildered by this trenchant criticism. ⇒ His comment was trenchant and perceptive.
