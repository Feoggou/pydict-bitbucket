glabrous

(ˈɡleɪbrəs ) or glabrate (ˈɡleɪbreɪt , -brɪt)
Definitions
adjectivebiology without hair or a similar growth; smooth ⇒ a glabrous stem
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

glabrousness (ˈglabrousness) noun

Word Origin

C17 glabrous, from Latin glaber
Example sentences containing 'glabrous'
A line of shells, white and pink and glabrous in the bright sunlight, marked the line of the high tide. Erskine, Barbara MIDNIGHT IS A LONELY PLACEInstead, they take up those weapons in the male armoury more befitting of their glabrous state: candour, bluntness, directness, scepticism.
