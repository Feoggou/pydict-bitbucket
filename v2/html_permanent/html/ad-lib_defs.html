ad-lib

(ædˈlɪb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -libs, -libbing or -libbed1. to improvise and deliver without preparation (a speech, musical performance, etc)adjective (ad lib when predicative)2. improvised; impromptuadverb ad lib3. without restraint; freely4. music short for ad libitumnoun5. an improvised performance, often humorous
Derived forms
ad-ˈlibber noun
Word origin
C18: short for Latin ad libitum, literally: according to pleasure

ad-lib

Powered by COBUILD
also ad lib
Word forms: ad-libs , ad-libbing , ad-libbed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you ad-lib something in a play or a speech, you say something which has not been planned or written beforehand. [V n] ⇒ He began comically ad-libbing a script. [V n] ⇒ He's good at ad-libbing his way out of trouble. [V] ⇒ He is rather disjointed when he ad-libs. [V-ed] ⇒ ...ad-libbed phrases.2. countable nounAn ad-lib is something which is said without having been planned or written beforehand. ⇒ Every time I fluffed a line Lenny got me out of trouble with a brilliant ad-lib.Ad lib is also an adverb. ⇒ I spoke from the pulpit ad lib.
