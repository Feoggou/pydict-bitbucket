deride

(dɪˈraɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to speak of or treat with contempt, mockery, or ridicule; scoff or jeer at
Derived forms
derider (deˈrider) nounderidingly (deˈridingly) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Latin dērīdēre to laugh to scorn, from de- + rīdēre to laugh, smile

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you deride someone or something, you say that they are stupid or have no value. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Opposition MPs derided the Government's response to the crisis. [V n] ⇒ This theory is widely derided by conventional scientists.
