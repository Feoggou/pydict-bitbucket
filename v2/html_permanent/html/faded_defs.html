faded

(ˈfædɪd)
Definitions
adjectiveless colourful than previously ⇒ a cheap Italian restaurant with faded photographs of Sicily on the walls, ⇒ a pair of faded jeans, ⇒ a girl in a faded dress, ⇒ a faded blue/green/black
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'faded'
Sally looked away, as the music ended and the moment faded. Stuart Harrison BETTER THAN THISThe odour of damp wool cloaks that had permeated the Hall faded into nothing. Jennifer Fallon TREASON KEEPA long, narrow box on the table with a faded gold name on the back contained a string of pearls. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERS
