transfix

(trænsˈfɪks )
Definitions
verb -fixes, -fixing, -fixed or -fixt (transitive)1. to render motionless, esp with horror or shock2. to impale or fix with a sharp weapon or other device3. medicine to cut through (a limb or other organ), as in amputation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

transfixion (trænsˈfɪkʃən ) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin transfīgere to pierce through, from trans- + fīgere to thrust in
