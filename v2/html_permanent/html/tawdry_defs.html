tawdry

(ˈtɔːdrɪ )
Definitions
adjective -drier or -driestcheap, showy, and of poor quality ⇒ tawdry jewellery
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tawdrily (ˈtawdrily) adverbtawdriness (ˈtawdriness) noun

Word Origin

C16 tawdry lace, shortened and altered from Seynt Audries lace, finery sold at the fair of St Audrey (Etheldrida), 7th-century queen of Northumbria and patron saint of Ely, Cambridgeshire

tawdry

Powered by COBUILD
(tɔːdri ) for learners of English
Word forms: tawdrier , tawdriest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something such as clothes or decorations as tawdry, you mean that they are cheap and show a lack of taste. ⇒ ...tawdry jewellery.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe something such as a story or an event as tawdry, you mean that it is unpleasant or immoral. ⇒ ...the yawning gulf between her fantasies and the tawdry reality. ⇒ ...the tawdry business of day-to-day bartering and bargaining.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
