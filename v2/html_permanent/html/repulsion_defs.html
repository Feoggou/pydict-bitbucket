repulsion

(rɪˈpʌlʃən )
Definitions
noun1. a feeling of disgust or aversion2. physics a force tending to separate two objects, such as the force between two like electric charges or magnetic poles
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

repulsion

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪpʌlʃən ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounRepulsion is an extremely strong feeling of disgust. ⇒ She gave a dramatic shudder of repulsion.2. uncount nounRepulsion is a force that pushes two things apart, such as the force that there is in magnets. OPPOSITE: attraction ⇒ ...the electric repulsion between charged electrons.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
