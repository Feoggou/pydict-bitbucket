predatory

(ˈprɛdətərɪ , -trɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. zoology another word for predacious (sense 1)2. of, involving, or characterized by plundering, robbing, etc

Derived Forms

predatorily (ˈpredatorily) adverbpredatoriness (ˈpredatoriness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin praedātōrius rapacious, from praedārī to pillage, from praeda booty

predatory

Powered by COBUILD
(predətri , US -tɔːri )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Predatory animals live by killing other animals for food. ⇒ ...predatory birds like the eagle.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Predatory people or organizations are eager to gain something out of someone else's weakness or suffering. ⇒ People who run small businesses are frightened by the predatory behaviour of the banks.
