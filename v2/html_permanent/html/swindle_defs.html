swindle

(ˈswɪndəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to cheat (someone) of money, etc; defraud2. (transitive) to obtain (money, etc) by fraudnoun3. a fraudulent scheme or transaction

Derived Forms

swindler (ˈswindler) noun

Word Origin

C18: back formation from German Schwindler, from schwindeln, from Old High German swintilōn, frequentative of swintan to disappear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone swindles a person or an organization, they deceive them in order to get something valuable from them, especially money. [V n + out of] ⇒ A City businessman swindled investors out of millions of pounds. [V n out of n] ⇒ An oil executive swindled £250,000 out of his firm.Swindle is also a noun. ⇒ He fled to Switzerland rather than face trial for a tax swindle.swindler Word forms: swindlers countable noun ⇒ Swindlers have cheated investors out of £12 million.
