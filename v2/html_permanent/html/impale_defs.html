impale

or empale (ɪmˈpeɪl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. (often foll by on, upon, or with) to pierce with a sharp instrument ⇒ they impaled his severed head on a spear2. archaic to enclose with pales or fencing; fence in3. heraldry to charge (a shield) with two coats of arms placed side by side
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impalement (imˈpalement) or empalement (emˈpalement) nounimpaler (imˈpaler) or empaler (emˈpaler) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin impālāre, from Latin im- (in) + pālus pale2

verbTo impale something on a pointed object means to cause the point to go into it or through it. [V n + on] ⇒ Researchers observed one bird impale a rodent on a cactus. [V n on n] ⇒ Lenny swayed for a moment, then dropped to the ground, impaling himself on his switchblade. [V n on n, V n + with] ⇒ The boy died after being impaled on railings when he slipped while playing. [Also V n]
