tedium

(ˈtiːdɪəm )
Definitions
nounthe state of being bored or the quality of being boring; monotony
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin taedium, from taedēre to weary

tedium

Powered by COBUILD
(tiːdiəm ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounIf you talk about the tedium of a job, task, or situation, you think it is boring and rather frustrating. [+ of] ⇒ She began to wonder whether she wouldn't go mad with the tedium of the job.
