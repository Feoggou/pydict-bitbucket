clamber

(ˈklæmbə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (usually foll by up, over, etc) to climb (something) awkwardly, esp by using both hands and feetnoun2. a climb performed in this manner
Derived forms
clamberer (ˈclamberer) noun
Word origin
C15: probably a variant of climb

clamber

Powered by COBUILD
(klæmbəʳ )
Word forms: clambers , clambering , clambered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you clamber somewhere, you climb there with difficulty, usually using your hands as well as your feet. [V prep/adv] ⇒ They clambered up the stone walls of a steeply terraced olive grove. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Clambering over sackfuls of lemons, Boris tried to find a way out.
