diffidence

(ˈdɪfədəns )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun lack of confidence in oneself, marked by hesitation in asserting oneself; shyness
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.
Word origin
ME < L diffidentia < diffidens, prp. of diffidere, to distrust < dis-, not + fidere, to trust: see faith
Example sentences containing 'diffidence'
For once his seeming diffidence was genuine, but quite unnecessary. Penn, John UNTO THE GRAVEHe tapped on the door, opened it and entered with a certain diffidence. Lewis, Roy A TROUT IN THE MILK"Oh, you know, Chifoilisk," Shevek said in a low voice, with diffidence. Ursula K. Le Guin THE DISPOSSESSED
