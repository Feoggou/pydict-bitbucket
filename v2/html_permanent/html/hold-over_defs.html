hold over

Definitions
verb (tr, mainly adverb)1. to defer consideration of or action on2. to postpone for a further period3. to prolong (a note, chord, etc) from one bar to the next4. (preposition) to intimidate (a person) with (a threat)noun holdover US and Canadian informal5. an elected official who continues in office after his term has expired6. a performer or performance continuing beyond the original engagement

hold over

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you hold something over someone, you use it in order to threaten them or make them do what you want. [V n P n] ⇒ Did Laurie know something, and hold it over Felicity?2. phrasal verbIf something is held over, it does not happen or it is not dealt with until a future date. [be V-ed P] ⇒ Further voting might be held over until tomorrow. [V n P] ⇒ We would have held the story over until the next day. [Also V P n] →  hold
