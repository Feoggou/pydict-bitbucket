schedule

(ˈʃɛdjuːl , esp US ˈskɛdʒʊəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a plan of procedure for a project, allotting the work to be done and the time for it2. a list of items ⇒ a schedule of fixed prices3. a list of times, esp of arrivals and departures; timetable4. a list of tasks to be performed, esp within a set period5. law a list or inventory, usually supplementary to a contract, will, etc6. See on scheduleverb (transitive)7. to make a schedule of or place in a schedule8. to plan to occur at a certain time

Derived Forms

schedular (ˈschedular) adjective

Word Origin

C14: earlier cedule, sedule via Old French from Late Latin schedula small piece of paper, from Latin scheda sheet of paper

(ʃedjuːl , US skedʒuːl )
Word forms: schedules , scheduling , scheduled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA schedule is a plan that gives a list of events or tasks and the times at which each one should happen or be done. ⇒ He has been forced to adjust his schedule. ⇒ We both have such hectic schedules.2. uncount nounYou can use schedule to refer to the time or way something is planned to be done. For example, if something is completed on schedule, it is completed at the time planned. ⇒ The jet arrived in Johannesburg two minutes ahead of schedule. ⇒ Everything went according to schedule. ⇒ It will be completed several weeks behind schedule.3. verb [usu passive]If something is scheduled to happen at a particular time, arrangements are made for it to happen at that time. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ The space shuttle had been scheduled to blast off at 04:38. [be V-ed + for] ⇒ A presidential election was scheduled for last December. [be V-ed] ⇒ No new talks are scheduled.4. countable nounA schedule is a written list of things, for example a list of prices, details, or conditions. 5. countable nounA schedule is a list of all the times when trains, boats, buses, or aircraft are supposed to arrive at or leave a particular place. [mainly US] ⇒ ...a bus schedule.regional note:   in BRIT, usually use timetable6. countable nounIn a school or college, a schedule is a diagram that shows the times in the week at which particular subjects are taught. [US]regional note:   in BRIT, usually use timetable
