thoughtful

(ˈθɔːtfʊl )
Definitions
adjective1. considerate in the treatment of other people2. showing careful thought3. pensive; reflective
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are thoughtful, you are quiet and serious because you are thinking about something. ⇒ Nancy, who had been thoughtful for some time, suddenly spoke. ⇒ He was looking very thoughtful. ⇒ She had a thoughtful expression on her face.thoughtfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Daniel nodded thoughtfully.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as thoughtful, you approve of them because they remember what other people want, need, or feel, and try not to upset them. [approval] ⇒ ...a thoughtful and caring man. [+ of] ⇒ Thank you. That's very thoughtful of you. ⇒ It was a very kind and thoughtful gesture.thoughtfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...the bottle of wine he had thoughtfully purchased for the celebrations.thoughtfulness uncount noun ⇒ I can't tell you how much I appreciate your thoughtfulness.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something such as a book, film, or speech as thoughtful, you mean that it is serious and well thought out. ⇒ ...a thoughtful and scholarly book.thoughtfully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...these thoughtfully designed machines.
