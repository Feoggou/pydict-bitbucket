ague

(ˈeɪɡjuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fever with successive stages of fever and chills esp when caused by malaria2. a fit of shivering
Derived forms
aguish (ˈaguish) adjectiveaguishly (ˈaguishly) adverb
Word origin
C14: from Old French (fievre) ague acute fever; see acute
Example sentences containing 'ague'
I shivered suddenly, and then shook as with an ague and spent the rest of the night sitting up before the. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANHer sobbing was spasmodic, out of control, but he was more alarmed by her violent trembling, like one with the ague. Penman, Sharon HERE BE DRAGONSFor him, it had been collapse and fever, till his back half healed; then dysentery; then an ague. Lois McMaster Bujold THE CURSE OF CHALION
