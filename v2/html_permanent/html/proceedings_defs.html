proceedings

(prəˈsiːdɪŋz)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. the minutes of the meetings of a club, society, etc ⇒ The Department of Transport is to publish the conference proceedings., ⇒ The proceedings were published in the newspapers., ⇒ The conference proceedings will be published in the autumn., ⇒ It was published in the Society's proceedings., ⇒ Proceedings of the Historical Society2. law legal action; litigation ⇒ criminal proceedings against the former prime minister, ⇒ The company has entered bankruptcy proceedings., ⇒ No extradition proceedings had taken place., ⇒ Making a complaint will not prejudice your right to takelegal proceedings., ⇒ Her husband threatened to institute divorce proceedings., ⇒ The Council has brought proceedings to stop the store from opening on Sundays., ⇒ The man is facing criminal proceedings for kidnapping his daughter., ⇒ Disciplinary proceedings are to be taken against two senior police officers.3. formal the events of an occasion, meeting, etc ⇒ The proceedings of the inquiry will take place in private., ⇒ He viewed the proceedings with doubt and alarm., ⇒ Millions of people watched the proceedings on television., ⇒ The proceedings begin at seven o'clock., ⇒ the proceedings of Parliament, ⇒ The female protesters chained themselves to railings outside official buildings in London, as well as trying to disrupt the proceedings of Parliament.▶ USAGE The abbreviation for proceedings is proc.
