cheese1

(tʃiːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the curd of milk separated from the whey and variously prepared as a food2. a mass or complete cake of this substance3. any of various substances of similar consistency, etc ⇒ lemon cheese4. See big cheese5. See as alike as chalk and cheese
Word origin
Old English cēse, from Latin cāseus cheese; related to Old Saxon kāsi

(tʃiːz )

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounCheese is a solid food made from milk. It is usually white or yellow. ⇒ ...bread and cheese. ⇒ ...cheese sauce. ⇒ He cut the mould off a piece of cheese. ⇒ ...delicious French cheeses.2. → see also cottage cheese, cream cheese, goat cheese, macaroni cheese3. →  big cheese4. →  say cheese5. → as different as chalk and cheese
