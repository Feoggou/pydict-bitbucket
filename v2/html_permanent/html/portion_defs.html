portion

(ˈpɔːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a part of a whole; fraction2. a part allotted or belonging to a person or group3. an amount of food served to one person; helping4. lawa. a share of property, esp one coming to a child from the estate of his parentsb. archaic the property given by a woman to her husband at marriage; dowry5. a person's lot or destinyverb (transitive)6. to divide up; share out7. to give a share to (a person); assign or allocate8. law to give a dowry or portion to (a person); endow

Derived Forms

portionless (ˈportionless) adjective

Word Origin

C13: via Old French from Latin portiō portion, allocation; related to pars part

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA portion of something is a part of it. [+ of] ⇒ Damage was confined to a small portion of the castle. [+ of] ⇒ I have spent a fairly considerable portion of my life here. [+ of] ⇒ I had learnt a portion of the Koran. ⇒ Insurance can represent a significant portion of the total price of a holiday.2. countable nounA portion is the amount of food that is given to one person at a meal. ⇒ Desserts can be substituted by a portion of fresh fruit. ⇒ The portions were generous. ⇒ ...fish and chips at about £2.70 a portion.
