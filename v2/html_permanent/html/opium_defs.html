opium

(ˈəʊpɪəm )
Definitions
noun1. the dried juice extracted from the unripe seed capsules of the opium poppy that contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine: used in medicine as an analgesic2. something having a tranquillizing or stupefying effect
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Latin: poppy juice, from Greek opion, diminutive of opos juice of a plant
