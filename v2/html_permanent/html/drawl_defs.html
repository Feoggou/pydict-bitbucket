drawl

(drɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to speak or utter (words) slowly, esp prolonging the vowel soundsnoun2. the way of speech of someone who drawls
Derived forms
drawler (ˈdrawler) noundrawling (ˈdrawling) adjectivedrawly (ˈdrawly) adjective
Word origin
C16: probably frequentative of draw

Word forms: drawls , drawling , drawled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone drawls, they speak slowly and not very clearly, with long vowel sounds. [V with quote] ⇒ 'I guess you guys don't mind if I smoke?' he drawled. [V] ⇒ He has a deep voice and he drawls slightly.Drawl is also a noun. ⇒ ...Jack's southern drawl.
Example sentences containing 'drawl'
`Oh, hadn't you noticed," said Yoller, reverting to his usual, casual drawl.
