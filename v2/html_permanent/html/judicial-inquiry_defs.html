judicial inquiry

or judicial enquiry (dʒuːˈdɪʃəl ɪnˈkwaɪərɪ)
Definitions
nounlaw a formal legal investigation conducted into a matter of public concern by a judge, appointed by the government ⇒ But only now after a full-scale judicial inquiry, headed by a former High Court judge, Sir William Macpherson, has the truth emerged., ⇒ Lord Denning was appointed to conduct a judicial inquiry., ⇒ an independent judicial inquiry, ⇒ a judicial inquiry begins today into the deaths of two hundred people
