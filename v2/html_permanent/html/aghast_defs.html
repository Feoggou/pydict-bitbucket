aghast

(əˈɡɑːst )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(postpositive) overcome with amazement or horror
Word origin
C13: agast, from Old English gæstan to frighten. The spelling with gh is on the model of ghastly

graded adjective & adjective [ADJ after v, v-link ADJ, ADJ n]If you are aghast, you are filled with horror and surprise. [formal] horrified ⇒ She watched aghast as his life flowed away. ⇒ His colleagues were aghast at the sackings. ⇒ Tania stared at him aghast, unable to speak. [Also + at]
