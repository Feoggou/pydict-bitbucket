accommodate

(əˈkɒməˌdeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to supply or provide, esp with lodging or board and lodging2. (transitive) to oblige or do a favour for3. to adjust or become adjusted; adapt4. (transitive) to bring into harmony; reconcile5. (transitive) to allow room for; contain6. (transitive) to lend money to, esp on a temporary basis until a formal loan has been arranged
Derived forms
acˈcommoˌdative adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin accommodāre to make fit, from ad- to + commodus having the proper measure

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verb [no cont]If a building or space can accommodate someone or something, it has enough room for them. [V n] ⇒ The school in Poldown was not big enough to accommodate all the children. [V n] ⇒ The CD-ROMS will accommodate the works of all English poets from 600 to 1900.2. verbTo accommodate someone means to provide them with a place to live or stay. [V n] ⇒ ...a hotel built to accommodate guests for the wedding of King Alfonso. [be V-ed prep/adv] ⇒ Students are accommodated in homes nearby.3. verbIf something is planned or changed to accommodate a particular situation, it is planned or changed so that it takes this situation into account. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The roads are built to accommodate gradual temperature changes. [V n] ⇒ The way that American history is taught may change in order to accommodate some more of those cultures.4. verbIf you do something to accommodate someone, you do it with the main purpose of pleasing or satisfying them. [V n] ⇒ He has never put an arm around his wife to accommodate photographers. [V n] ⇒ He's already altered several of the proposals in his economic plan to accommodate demands of special interests.5. verbIf you accommodate to something new, you change your behaviour or ideas so that you are able to deal with it. [formal] adapt [V to n] ⇒ Some animal and plant species cannot accommodate to the rapidly changing conditions. [V pron-refl to n] ⇒ She walked slowly to accommodate herself to his pace.
