attain

(əˈteɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to achieve or accomplish (a task, goal, aim, etc)2. (transitive) to reach or arrive at in space or time ⇒ to attain old age3. (intransitive; often foll by to) to arrive (at) with effort or exertion ⇒ to attain to glory
Derived forms
attainable (atˈtainable) adjectiveatˌtainability (atˌtainaˈbility) or attainableness (atˈtainableness) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French ateindre, from Latin attingere to reach, from tangere to touch

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you attain something, you gain it or achieve it, often after a lot of effort. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Jim is halfway to attaining his pilot's licence.2. verbIf you attain a particular state or condition, you may reach it as a result of natural development or work hard to attain this state. [V n] ⇒ ...attaining a state of calmness and confidence.
