predict

(prɪˈdɪkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(tr; may take a clause as object) to state or make a declaration about in advance, esp on a reasoned basis; foretell

Derived Forms

predictable (preˈdictable) adjectivepreˌdictability (preˌdictaˈbility) or predictableness (preˈdictableness) nounpredictably (preˈdictably) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin praedīcere to mention beforehand, from prae before + dīcere to say

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you predict an event, you say that it will happen. [V n] ⇒ The latest opinion polls are predicting a very close contest. [V that] ⇒ He predicted that my hair would grow back 'in no time'. [V wh] ⇒ It's hard to predict how a jury will react. [V with quote] ⇒ 'The war will continue another two or three years,' he predicted.
