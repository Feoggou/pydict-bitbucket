recollect

(ˌrɛkəˈlɛkt )
Definitions
verb(when tr, often takes a clause as object) to recall from memory; remember
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recollective (ˌrecolˈlective) adjectiverecollectively (ˌrecolˈlectively) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin recolligere to gather again, from re- + colligere to collect1

recollect

Powered by COBUILD
(rekəlekt ) for learners of English
Word forms: recollects , recollecting , recollected
Definitions
verbIf you recollect something, you remember it. [V n] ⇒ Ramona spoke with warmth when she recollected the doctor who used to be at the county hospital. [V that] ⇒ His efforts, the Duke recollected many years later, were distinctly half-hearted. [Also V]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
