methyl isobutyl ketone

(ˌaɪsəʊˈbjuːtaɪl , -tɪl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna colourless insoluble liquid ketone used as a solvent for organic compounds, esp nitrocellulose; 4-methylpentan-2-one. Formula: (CH3)2CHCH2COCH3. Also called: hexone

Definitions
COBUILD Key Words for the Pharmaceutical Industry
noun(Pharmaceutical: Excipients) Methyl isobutyl ketone is a volatile liquid used as an alcohol denaturant (= a substance added so that humans cannot consume it). ⇒ Methyl isobutyl ketone is mainly used as a denaturant for rubbing alcohol. ⇒ Methyl isobutyl ketone is frequently added to ethanol as a denaturant, making the ethanol unsafe to drink. ⇒ Methyl isobutyl ketone is a volatile liquid used as an alcohol denaturant.
