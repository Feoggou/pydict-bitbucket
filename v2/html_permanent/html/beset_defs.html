beset

(bɪˈsɛt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -sets, -setting or -set (transitive)1. (esp of dangers, temptations, or difficulties) to trouble or harass constantly2. to surround or attack from all sides3. archaic to cover with, esp with jewels
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The form beset is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle.verbIf someone or something is beset by problems or fears, they have many problems or fears which affect them severely. [be V-ed + by/with] ⇒ The country is beset by severe economic problems. [be V-ed by/with n] ⇒ The discussions were beset with difficulties. [V n] ⇒ ...the problems now besetting the country.
