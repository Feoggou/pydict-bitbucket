or nextdoor (ˌnekstˈdɔː)

Definitions
language note:   The adjective is also spelled next-door.1. adverb [ADV after v, be ADV, oft n ADV]If a room or building is next door, it is the next one to the right or left. ⇒ I went next door to the bathroom. ⇒ She was next door at the time. ⇒ ...the old lady who lived next door. ⇒ The flat next door was empty.Next door is also an adjective. ⇒ She wandered back into the next door room. ⇒ The wires trailed through other parts of the HQ into a next door building.If a room or building is next door to another one, it is the next one to the left or right. ⇒ The kitchen is right next door to the dining room.2. adverb [n ADV]The people next door are the people who live in the house or flat to the right or left of yours. ⇒ The neighbours thought the family next door had moved.Next door is also an adjective. ⇒ Our next door neighbour knocked on the door to say that our car had been stolen.3. →  the boy next door/the girl next door
