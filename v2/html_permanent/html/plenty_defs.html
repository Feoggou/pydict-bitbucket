plenty

(ˈplɛntɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. (often foll by of) a great number, amount, or quantity; lots ⇒ plenty of time, ⇒ there are plenty of cars on display here2. generous or ample supplies of wealth, produce, or resources ⇒ the age of plenty3. See in plentydeterminer4. a. very many; ample ⇒ plenty of people believe in ghostsb. (as pronoun) ⇒ there's plenty more, ⇒ that's plenty, thanksadverb5. not standard, mainly US (intensifier) ⇒ he was plenty mad6. informal more than adequately; abundantly ⇒ the water's plenty hot enough

Word Origin

C13: from Old French plenté, from Late Latin plēnitās fullness, from Latin plēnus full

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. quantifierIf there is plenty of something, there is a large amount of it. If there are plenty of things, there are many of them. Plenty is used especially to indicate that there is enough of something, or more than you need. lots [+ of] ⇒ There was still plenty of time to take Jill out for pizza. [+ of] ⇒ Most businesses face plenty of competition. ⇒ Taking plenty of exercise can be of great benefit. [+ of] ⇒ Are there plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables in your diet?Plenty is also a pronoun. lots ⇒ I don't believe in long interviews. Fifteen minutes is plenty. ⇒ She's got plenty to do these days.2. uncount nounPlenty is a situation in which people have a lot to eat or a lot of money to live on. [formal] ⇒ You are all fortunate to be growing up in a time of peace and plenty. ⇒ ...an area that has become a symbol of despair in the midst of America's plenty.3. adverb [ADV adj/adv]You use plenty in front of adjectives or adverbs to emphasize the degree of the quality they are describing. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ The water looked plenty deep. ⇒ The compartment is plenty big enough. ⇒ Keep in mind you're going to be fighting lots of men who hit plenty hard.4. →  in plenty
