singe

(sɪndʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: singes, singeing or singed1. to burn or be burnt superficially; scorch ⇒ to singe one's clothes2. (transitive) to burn the ends of (hair, etc)3. (transitive) to expose (a carcass) to flame to remove bristles or hairnoun4. a superficial burn

Word Origin

Old English sengan; related to Middle High German sengen to singe, Dutch sengel spark, Norwegian sengla to smell of burning, Swedish sjängla to singe, Icelandic sāngr

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
ergative verb & verbIf you singe something or if it singes, it burns very slightly and changes colour but does not catch fire. [V n] ⇒ The electric fire had begun to singe the bottoms of his trousers. [V-ed] ⇒ Her hair was singed and her anorak was burnt. [V] ⇒ Toast the dried chillies in a hot pan until they start to singe.
