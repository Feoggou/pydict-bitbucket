chronology

(krəˈnɒlədʒɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -gies1. the determination of the proper sequence of past events2. the arrangement of dates, events, etc, in order of occurrence3. a table or list of events arranged in order of occurrence
Derived forms
chronologist (chroˈnologist) or chronologer (chroˈnologer) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe chronology of a series of past events is the times at which they happened in the order in which they happened. [+ of] ⇒ She gave him a factual account of the chronology of her brief liaison.2. countable nounA chronology is an account or record of the times and the order in which a series of past events took place. [+ of] ⇒ The second part of Duffy's book is a detailed chronology of the Reformation.
