flipper

(ˈflɪpə )
Definitions
noun1. the flat broad limb of seals, whales, penguins, and other aquatic animals, specialized for swimming2. Also called: fin (often plural) either of a pair of rubber paddle-like devices worn on the feet as an aid in swimming, esp underwater3. cricket a ball bowled with backspin imparted by the action of the bowler's wrist

flipper

Powered by COBUILD
(flɪpəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: flippers
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]Flippers are flat pieces of rubber that you can wear on your feet to help you swim more quickly, especially underwater.2. countable noun [usu pl]The flippers of an animal that lives in water, for example a seal or a penguin, are the two or four flat limbs which it uses for swimming.
