chimney

(ˈtʃɪmnɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a vertical structure of brick, masonry, or steel that carries smoke or steam away from a fire, engine, etc2. another name for flue1 (sense 1)3. short for chimney stack4. an open-ended glass tube fitting around the flame of an oil or gas lamp in order to exclude draughts5. British a fireplace, esp an old and large one6. geologya. a cylindrical body of an ore, which is usually oriented verticallyb. the vent of a volcano7. mountaineering a vertical fissure large enough for a person's body to enter8. anything resembling a chimney in shape or function
Word origin
C14: from Old French cheminée, from Late Latin camīnāta, from Latin camīnus furnace, from Greek kaminos fireplace, oven
