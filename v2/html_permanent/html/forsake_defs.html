forsake

(fəˈseɪk )
Definitions
verb -sakes, -saking, -sook (-ˈsʊk) or -saken (-ˈseɪkən) (transitive)1. to abandon2. to give up (something valued or enjoyed)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

forsaker (forˈsaker) noun

Word Origin

Old English forsacan

forsake

Powered by COBUILD
(fəʳseɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: forsakes , forsaking , forsook (fəʳsʊk ), forsaken
Definitions
1. verbIf you forsake someone, you leave them when you should have stayed, or you stop helping them or looking after them. [literary, disapproval] [V n] ⇒ I still love him and I would never forsake him. [V n] ⇒ ...children who've been forsaken by individual teachers. [V-ed] ⇒ I don't want him to feel forsaken and unhappy.2. verbIf you forsake something, you stop doing it, using it, or having it. [literary] [V n] ⇒ He doubted their claim to have forsaken military solutions to the civil war. [V n] ⇒ But that didn't make her forsake her ideals. [V n for n] ⇒ She forsook her notebook for new technology.3. verbIf you forsake a place or a thing, you leave it or go away from it. [literary] [V n] ⇒ At 53 he has no plans to forsake the hills.
