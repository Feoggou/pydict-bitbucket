Pluto2

(ˈpluːtəʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the second-largest dwarf planet in the solar system, located in the Kuiper belt; discovered in 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh (1906–97); classified as a planet until 2006, when it was reclassified; it has five known satellites. Period of revolution around the sun: 248 years; period of axial rotation: 6.5 days; mass: 0.00218 times that of earth; diameter: 2300 km; Pluto has an elliptical orbit, its closest point to the sun is 29.7 AU and its farthest point away is 49.7 AU verb (transitive)2. to reduce (something) in status or importance 3. to put an end to (something)

Word Origin

Latin, from Greek Ploutōn, literally: the rich one
