polygonpolygon | meaning of polygon in Collins Dictionariespolygon meaning, definition, what is polygon: a closed plane figure bounded by three or more straight sides that meet in pairs in the same number of vertices, and do not intersect other than at these vertices. The sum of the interior angles is (n–2) × 180° for n sides; the sum of the exterior angles is 360°. A regular polygon has all its sides and angles equal. polygon

(ˈpɒlɪˌɡɒn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna closed plane figure bounded by three or more straight sides that meet in pairs in the same number of vertices, and do not intersect other than at these vertices. The sum of the interior angles is (n–2) × 180° for n sides; the sum of the exterior angles is 360°. A regular polygon has all its sides and angles equal. Specific polygons are named according to the number of sides, such as triangle, pentagon, etc

Derived Forms

polygonal (pəˈlɪɡənəl ) adjectivepolygonally (poˈlygonally) adverb

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek polugōnon figure with many angles
