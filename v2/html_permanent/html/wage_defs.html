wage

(weɪdʒ )
Definitions
noun1. a. (often plural) payment in return for work or services, esp that made to workmen on a daily, hourly, weekly, or piece-work basis. Compare salaryb. (as modifier) ⇒ wage freeze2. (plural) economics the portion of the national income accruing to labour as earned income, as contrasted with the unearned income accruing to capital in the form of rent, interest, and dividends3. (often plural) recompense, return, or yield4. an obsolete word for pledgeverb (transitive)5. to engage in6. obsolete to pledge or wager7. archaic another word for hire (sense 1), hire (sense 2)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wageless (ˈwageless) adjectivewagelessness (ˈwagelessness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old Northern French wagier to pledge, from wage, of Germanic origin; compare Old English weddian to pledge, wed

wage for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weɪdʒ )
Word forms: wages , waging , waged
Definitions
1. countable nounSomeone's wages are the amount of money that is regularly paid to them for the work that they do. ⇒ His wages have gone up. ⇒ This may end efforts to set a minimum wage well above the poverty line.2. verbIf a person, group, or country wages a campaign or a war, they start it and continue it over a period of time. [V n] ⇒ ...the three factions that had been waging a civil war. [V n] ⇒ They waged a price war.usage note:   When used as a noun, pay is a general word which you can use to refer to the money you get from your employer for doing your job. Manual workers are paid wages, or a wage. The plural is more common than the singular, especially when you are talking about the actual cash that someone receives. Every week he handed all his wages in cash to his wife. Wages are usually paid, and quoted, as a weekly sum. ...a starting wage of five dollars an hour. Professional people and office workers receive a salary, which is paid monthly. However, when talking about someone's salary, you usually give the annual figure. I'm paid a salary of £15,000 a year. Your income consists of all the money you receive from all sources, including your pay.
