ax1

(æks ) or axe1 (æks )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural ˈaxes1.  a tool for chopping trees and splitting wood: it has a long wooden handle and a metal head with a blade usually on only one side2.  any similar tool or weapon, as a battle-ax, headsman's ax, etc.3.  Us, Slang a musical instrument verb transitiveWord forms: axed or ˈaxing4.  to trim, split, etc. with an ax5.  to cut off, remove, get rid of, etc. get the ax1 Us, Informal 1.  to be executed by beheading2.  to be discharged from one's job have an ax to grind1 Us to have an object of one's own to gain or promote

ax

Powered by COBUILD
(æks )
Word forms: axes, axing, axed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced American English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, sometimes in AM, use axe1. countable nounAn ax is a tool used for cutting wood. It consists of a heavy metal blade that is sharp at one edge and attached by its other edge to the end of a long handle.2. transitive verb [usu passive]If someone's job or something such as a public service or a television program is axed, it is ended suddenly and without discussion. ⇒ Community projects are being axed by hard-pressed social services departments.
