faun

(fɔːn )
Definitions
noun(in Roman legend) a rural deity represented as a man with a goat's ears, horns, tail, and hind legs
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

faunlike (ˈfaunˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: back formation from Faunes (plural), from Latin Faunus
