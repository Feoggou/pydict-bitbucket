compassion

(kəmˈpæʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna feeling of distress and pity for the suffering or misfortune of another, often including the desire to alleviate it
Word origin
C14: from Old French, from Late Latin compassiō fellow feeling, from compatī to suffer with, from Latin com- with + patī to bear, suffer

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounCompassion is a feeling of pity, sympathy, and understanding for someone who is suffering. [+ from] ⇒ Elderly people need time and compassion from their physicians.
