succulent

(ˈsʌkjʊlənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. abundant in juices; juicy2. (of plants) having thick fleshy leaves or stems3. informal stimulating interest, desire, etcnoun4. a plant that is able to exist in arid or salty conditions by using water stored in its fleshy tissues

Derived Forms

succulence (ˈsucculence) or succulency (ˈsucculency) nounsucculently (ˈsucculently) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin succulentus, from sūcus juice

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSucculent food, especially meat or vegetables, is juicy and good to eat. [approval] ⇒ Cook pieces of succulent chicken with ample garlic and a little sherry. ⇒ ...succulent early vegetables.2. countable nounSucculents or succulent plants are types of plants which have thick, fleshy leaves. ⇒ His potted succulents were looking parched.
