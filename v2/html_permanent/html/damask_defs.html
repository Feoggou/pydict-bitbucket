damask

(ˈdæməsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a reversible fabric, usually silk or linen, with a pattern woven into it. It is used for table linen, curtains, etcb. table linen made from thisc. (as modifier) ⇒ a damask tablecloth2. short for Damascus steel3. the wavy markings on such steel4. a. the greyish-pink colour of the damask roseb. (as adjective) ⇒ damask wallpaperverb5. (transitive) another word for damascene (sense 1)
Word origin
C14: from Medieval Latin damascus, from Damascus, where this fabric was originally made
