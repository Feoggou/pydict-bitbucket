gambit

(ˈɡæmbɪt )
Definitions
noun1. chess an opening move in which a chessman, usually a pawn, is sacrificed to secure an advantageous position2. an opening comment, manoeuvre, etc, intended to secure an advantage or promote a point of view
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from French, from Italian gambetto a tripping up, from gamba leg

gambit

Powered by COBUILD
(gæmbɪt ) for learners of English
Word forms: gambits
Definitions
1. countable nounA gambit is an action or set of actions, which you carry out in order to try to gain an advantage in a situation or game. ⇒ He sees the proposal as more of a diplomatic gambit than a serious defense proposal. ⇒ Campaign strategists are calling the plan a clever political gambit.2. countable nounA gambit is a remark which you make to someone in order to start or continue a conversation with them. ⇒ His favourite opening gambit is: 'You are so beautiful, will you be my next wife?'. ⇒ Bernard made no response to Tom's conversational gambits.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
