leading1

(ˈliːdɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. guiding, directing, or influencing2. (prenominal) principal or primary3. in the first position ⇒ the leading car in the procession4. mathematics (of a coefficient) associated with the term of highest degree in a polynomial containing one variable ⇒ in 5x2 + 2x + 3, 5 is the leading coefficient
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

leadingly (ˈleadingly) adverb

leading2

(ˈlɛdɪŋ )
Definitions
nounprinting the spacing between lines of photocomposed or digitized type. Also called: interlinear spacing

leading

Powered by COBUILD
(liːdɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]The leading person or thing in a particular area is the one which is most important or successful. ⇒ ...a leading member of Bristol's Sikh community. ⇒ Britain's future as a leading industrial nation depends on investment.2. adjective [ADJ n]The leading role in a play or film is the main role. A leading lady or man is an actor who plays this role.3. adjective [ADJ n]The leading group, vehicle, or person in a race or procession is the one that is at the front.
