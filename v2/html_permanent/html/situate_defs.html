situate

(ˈsɪtjʊˌeɪt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (tr; often passive) to allot a site to; place; locateadjective2. (now used esp in legal contexts) situated; located

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin situāre to position, from Latin situs a site

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you situate something such as an idea or fact in a particular context, you relate it to that context, especially in order to understand it better. [formal] [V n adv/prep] ⇒ How do we situate Christianity in the context of modern physics and psychology?

It was here that Mandralisca had persuaded the Five Lords to situate their capital. Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
