screech

Powered by COBUILD
(skriːtʃ )
Word forms: screeches , screeching , screeched
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf a vehicle screeches somewhere or if its tyres screech, its tyres make an unpleasant high-pitched noise on the road. [V prep/adv] ⇒ A black Mercedes screeched to a halt beside the helicopter. [V] ⇒ The car wheels screeched as they curved and bounced over the rough broken ground.2. verbWhen you screech something, you shout it in a loud, unpleasant, high-pitched voice. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Get me some water, Jeremy!' I screeched. [V + at] ⇒ ...a player who screeches at you on the field. [Also V, V n]Screech is also a noun. ⇒ The figure gave a screech.3. verbWhen a bird, animal, or thing screeches, it makes a loud, unpleasant, high-pitched noise. [V + at] ⇒ A macaw screeched at him from its perch. [V] ⇒ ...tropical birds screeching in an aviary. [V] ⇒ The air-conditioning screeched all the time. [V-ing] ⇒ Bronka is somewhat infamous for his screeching electric guitar work.Screech is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ He heard the screech of brakes.
