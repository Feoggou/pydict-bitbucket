reparation

(ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən )
Definitions
noun1. the act or process of making amends ⇒ an injury admitting of no reparation2. (usually plural) compensation exacted as an indemnity from a defeated nation by the victors: esp the compensation demanded of Germany by the Treaty of Versailles after World War I3. the act or process of repairing or state of having been repaired
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reparative (rɪˈpærətɪv ) or reparatory (reˈparatory) adjective

Word Origin

C14 reparacioun, ultimately from Latin reparāre to repair1
