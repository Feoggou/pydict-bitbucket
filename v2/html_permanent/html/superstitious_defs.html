superstitious

(ˌsuːpəˈstɪʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. disposed to believe in superstition2. of or relating to superstition

Derived Forms

ˌsuperstitiously (ˌsuperˈstitiously) adverbˌsuperstitiousness (ˌsuperˈstitiousness) noun

superstitious

Powered by COBUILD
(suːpəʳstɪʃəs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]People who are superstitious believe in things that are not real or possible, for example magic. ⇒ Jean was extremely superstitious and believed the colour green brought bad luck.2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Superstitious fears or beliefs are irrational and not based on fact. ⇒ A wave of superstitious fear spread among the townspeople. ⇒ In the countryside as a young doctor she encountered ancient superstitious beliefs and prejudices.
Example sentences containing 'superstitious'
I had promised my mother in my soul that I would never be superstitious. Ursula K. Le Guin THE BIRTHDAY OF THE WORLD
