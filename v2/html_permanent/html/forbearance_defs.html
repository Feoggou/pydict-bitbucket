forbearance

(fɔːˈbɛərəns )
Definitions
noun1. the act of forbearing2. self-control; patience3. law abstention from or postponement of the enforcement of a legal right, esp by a creditor allowing his debtor time to pay
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

forbearance

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːʳbeərəns ) for learners of English
Definitions
uncount nounIf you say that someone has shown forbearance, you admire them for behaving in a calm and sensible way about something that they have a right to be very upset or angry about. [formal, approval] ⇒ All the Greenpeace people behaved with impressive forbearance and dignity.
