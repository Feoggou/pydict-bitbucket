(plɑːstəʳd , plæs-)
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveIf something is plastered to a surface, it is sticking to the surface. [+ to] ⇒ His hair was plastered down to his scalp by the rain.2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If something or someone is plastered with a sticky substance, they are covered with it. [+ with/in] ⇒ My hands, boots and trousers were plastered with mud.3. adjectiveIf a story or photograph is plastered all over the front page of a newspaper, it is given a lot of space on the page and made very noticeable. ⇒ His picture was plastered all over the newspapers on the weekend.4. adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone gets plastered, they get very drunk. [informal] sloshed ⇒ With gin at 9p a tot, getting plastered is cheap and easy.5. adjectiveIf someone's broken arm or leg is plastered, it has a hard cover of plaster of Paris around it to protect the broken bone while it is mending. ⇒ She was sitting in a hospital bed, her plastered leg up in the air.
