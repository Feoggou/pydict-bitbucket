versus

(ˈvɜːsəs )
Definitions
preposition1. (esp in a competition or lawsuit) against; in opposition to. Abbreviation: v or (esp US) vs2. as opposed to; in contrast with
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Latin: turned (in the direction of), opposite, from vertere to turn

versus

Powered by COBUILD
(vɜːʳsəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. prepositionYou use versus to indicate that two figures, ideas, or choices are opposed. as opposed to ⇒ Only 18.8% of the class of 1982 had some kind of diploma four years after high school, versus 45% of the class of 1972. ⇒ ...bottle-feeding versus breastfeeding.2. prepositionVersus is used to indicate that two teams or people are competing against each other in a sporting event. ⇒ Italy versus Japan is turning out to be a surprisingly well matched competition. ⇒ ...Greg Norman and Michael Campbell versus Retief Goosen and David Frost.
