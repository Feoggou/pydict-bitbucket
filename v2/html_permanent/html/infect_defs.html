infect

(ɪnˈfɛkt )
Definitions
verb (mainly tr)1. to cause infection in; contaminate (an organism, wound, etc) with pathogenic microorganisms2. (also intr) to affect or become affected with a communicable disease3. to taint, pollute, or contaminate4. to affect, esp adversely, as if by contagion5. computing to affect with a computer virus6. mainly international law to taint with crime or illegality; expose to penalty or subject to forfeitureadjective7. archaic contaminated or polluted with or as if with a disease; infected
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

infector (inˈfector) or infecter (inˈfecter) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin inficere to dip into, stain, from facere to make

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfekt ) for learners of English
Word forms: infects , infecting , infected
Definitions
1. verbTo infect people, animals, or plants means to cause them to have a disease or illness. [V n] ⇒ A single mosquito can infect a large number of people. [V-ed] ⇒ ...objects used by an infected person. [V-ed] ⇒ ...people infected with HIV. [Also V n + with]infection (ɪnfekʃən ) uncount noun ⇒ ...plants that are resistant to infection.2. verbTo infect a substance or area means to cause it to contain harmful germs or bacteria. [V n] ⇒ The birds infect the milk. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a virus which is spread mainly by infected blood.3. verbWhen people, places, or things are infected by a feeling or influence, it spreads to them. [be V-ed + by] ⇒ For an instant I was infected by her fear. [V n + with] ⇒ He thought they might infect others with their bourgeois ideas. [V n] ⇒ His urge for revenge would never infect her.4. verbIf a virus infects a computer, it affects the computer by damaging or destroying programs. [computing] [V n] ⇒ This virus infected thousands of computers within days.
