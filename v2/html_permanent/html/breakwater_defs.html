breakwater

(ˈbreɪkˌwɔːtə ) or breakwall (ˈbreɪkˌwɔːl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: mole a massive wall built out into the sea to protect a shore or harbour from the force of waves2. another name for groyne

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA breakwater is a wooden or stone wall that extends from the shore into the sea and is built in order to protect a harbour or beach from the force of the waves.
