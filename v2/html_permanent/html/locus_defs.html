locus

(ˈləʊkəs )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural loci (ˈləʊsaɪ )1. (in many legal phrases) a place or area, esp the place where something occurred2. mathematics a set of points whose location satisfies or is determined by one or more specified conditions ⇒ the locus of points equidistant from a given point is a circle3. genetics the position of a particular gene on a chromosome
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: Latin

Definitions
countable noun [usu sing]The locus of something is the place where it happens or the most important area or point with which it is associated. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Barcelona is the locus of Spanish industry. ⇒ Thereafter, the military remained the locus of real power.
