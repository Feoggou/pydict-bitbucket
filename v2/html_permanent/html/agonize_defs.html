agonize

or agonise (ˈæɡəˌnaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to suffer or cause to suffer agony2. (intransitive) to make a desperate effort; struggle; strive
Derived forms
ˈagoˌnizingly or ˈagoˌnisingly adverb
Word origin
C16: via Medieval Latin from Greek agōnizesthai to contend for a prize, from agōn agon

Powered by COBUILD
(ægənaɪz )
Word forms: agonizes , agonizing , agonized
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, also use agoniseverbIf you agonize over something, you feel very anxious about it and spend a long time thinking about it. [V + over/about] ⇒ Perhaps he was agonizing over the moral issues involved. [V-ing] ⇒ She only made the decision to apply for training after years of agonizing. [Also V]
Example sentences containing 'agonize'
Nick, he used to agonize for two days before getting up the nerve to call you on the phone and invite you over for dinner. James Grippando A KING'S RANSOM
