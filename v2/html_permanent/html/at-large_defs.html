at large

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
a. (esp of a dangerous criminal or wild animal) free; not confinedb. roaming freely, as in a foreign countryc. as a whole; in generald. in full detail; exhaustivelye. See ambassador-at-large See ambassador (sense 4)▷ See large

at large

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseYou use at large to indicate that you are talking in a general way about most of the people mentioned. ⇒ I think the chances of getting reforms accepted by the community at large remain extremely remote. ⇒ Amongst the population at large the support for the present regime is virtually zero. →  large

at large

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
phraseIf you say that a dangerous person, thing, or animal is at large, you mean that they have not been captured or made safe. ⇒ The man who tried to have her killed is still at large. →  large
