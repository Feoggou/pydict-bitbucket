fell1

(fɛl )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to cut or knock down ⇒ to fell a tree, ⇒ to fell an opponent2. needlework to fold under and sew flat (the edges of a seam)noun3. US and Canadian the timber felled in one season4. a seam finished by felling
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fellable (ˈfellable) adjective

Word Origin

Old English fellan; related to Old Norse fella, Old High German fellen; see fall

fell

Powered by COBUILD
(fel ) for learners of English
Word forms: fells , felling , felled
Definitions
1. Fell is the past tense of fall.2. verb [usu passive]If trees are felled, they are cut down. [be V-ed] ⇒ Badly infected trees should be felled and burned.3. verbIf you fell someone, you knock them down, for example in a fight. [V n] ⇒ ...a blow on the forehead which felled him to the ground.4. → in one fell swoop
