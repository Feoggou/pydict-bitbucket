thirst

(θɜːst )
Definitions
noun1. a craving to drink, accompanied by a feeling of dryness in the mouth and throat2. an eager longing, craving, or yearning ⇒ a thirst for knowledgeverb3. (intransitive) to feel a thirst ⇒ to thirst for a drink, ⇒ to thirst after righteousness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English thyrstan, from thurst thirst; related to Old Norse thyrsta to thirst, Old High German dursten to thirst, Latin torrēre to parch

thirst

Powered by COBUILD
(θɜːʳst ) for learners of English
Word forms: thirsts , thirsting , thirsted
Definitions
1. variable nounThirst is the feeling that you need to drink something. ⇒ Coca is well-known for reducing hunger, thirst and fatigue. ⇒ Instead of tea or coffee, drink water to quench your thirst. ⇒ I had such a thirst.2. uncount nounThirst is the condition of not having enough to drink. ⇒ They died of thirst on the voyage.3. singular nounA thirst for something is a very strong desire for that thing. [+ for] ⇒ Children show a real thirst for learning. ⇒ ...their ever-growing thirst for cash.4. verbIf you say that someone thirsts for something, you mean that they have a strong desire for it. [literary] [V + for/after] ⇒ We all thirst for the same things.
