lance

(lɑːns )
Definitions
noun1. a long weapon with a pointed head used by horsemen to unhorse or injure an opponent2. a similar weapon used for hunting, whaling, etc3. surgery another name for lancet4. the sand lance. See sand eelverb (transitive)5. to pierce (an abscess or boil) with a lancet to drain off pus6. to pierce with or as if with a lance
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13 launce, from Old French lance, from Latin lancea

lance

Powered by COBUILD
(lɑːns , læns) for learners of English
Word forms: lances , lancing , lanced
Definitions
1. verb [usu passive]If a boil on someone's body is lanced, a small cut is made in it so that the liquid inside comes out. [medicine] [have n V-ed] ⇒ It is a painful experience having the boil lanced. [Also be V-ed]2. countable nounA lance is a long spear used in former times by soldiers on horseback. ⇒ ...the clang of lances striking armour.
