invent

(ɪnˈvɛnt )
Definitions
verb1. to create or devise (new ideas, machines, etc)2. to make up (falsehoods); fabricate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inventible (inˈventible) or inventable (inˈventable) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin invenīre to find, come upon, from in-2 + venīre to come

invent

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnvent ) for learners of English
Word forms: invents , inventing , invented
Definitions
1. verbIf you invent something such as a machine or process, you are the first person to think of it or make it. [V n] ⇒ He invented the first electric clock. [V n] ⇒ Writing had not been invented as yet.2. verbIf you invent a story or excuse, you try to make other people believe that it is true when in fact it is not. [V n] ⇒ I stood still, trying to invent a plausible excuse.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
