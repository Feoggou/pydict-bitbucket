jail

or gaol (dʒeɪl )
Definitions
noun1. a place for the confinement of persons convicted and sentenced to imprisonment or of persons awaiting trial to whom bail is not granted2. See get out of jailverb3. (transitive) to confine in prison
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jailless (ˈjailless) or gaolless (ˈgaolless) adjectivejail-like (ˈjail-like) or gaol-like (ˈgaol-like) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French jaiole cage, from Vulgar Latin caveola (unattested), from Latin cavea enclosure; see cage: the two spellings derive from the forms of the word that developed in two different areas of France, and the spelling gaol represents a pronunciation in use until the 17th century
