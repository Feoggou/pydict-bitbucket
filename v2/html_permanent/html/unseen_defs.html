unseen

(ʌnˈsiːn )
Definitions
adjective1. not observed or perceived; invisible2. (of passages of writing) not previously seen or preparednoun3. mainly British a passage, not previously seen, that is presented to students for translation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unseen

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnsiːn ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf you describe something as unseen, you mean that it has not been seen for a long time. ⇒ ...a spectacular ballroom, unseen by the public for over 30 years. ⇒ We print a selection of previously unseen photos from the Spanish rider's early years.2. adjective [ADJ n, ADJ after v]You can use unseen to describe things which people cannot see. ⇒ For me, a performance is in front of a microphone, over the radio, to an unseen audience. ⇒ There was barely time for the two boys to escape unseen.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
