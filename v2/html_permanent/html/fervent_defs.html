fervent

(ˈfɜːvənt ) or fervid (ˈfɜːvɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. intensely passionate; ardent ⇒ a fervent desire to change society2. archaic or poetic boiling, burning, or glowing ⇒ fervent heat
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fervently (ˈfervently) or fervidly (ˈfervidly) adverbferventness (ˈferventness) or fervidness (ˈfervidness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin fervēre to boil, glow

Powered by COBUILD

Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A fervent person has or shows strong feelings about something, and is very sincere and enthusiastic about it. ⇒ ...a fervent admirer of Morisot's work. ⇒ ...the fervent hope that matters will be settled promptly.fervently graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ Their claims will be fervently denied.
