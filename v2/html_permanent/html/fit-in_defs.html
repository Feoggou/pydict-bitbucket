fit in

Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to give a place or time to ⇒ if my schedule allows it, I'll fit you in2. (intr, adverb) to belong or conform, esp after adjustment ⇒ he didn't fit in with their plans

fit in

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you manage to fit a person or task in, you manage to find time to deal with them. squeeze in [V n P] ⇒ We work long hours both outside and inside the home and we rush around trying to fit everything in. [V P n] ⇒ I find that I just can't fit in regular domestic work.2. phrasal verbIf you fit in as part of a group, you seem to belong there because you are similar to the other people in it. [V P] ⇒ She was great with the children and fitted in beautifully.3. phrasal verbIf you say that someone or something fits in, you understand how they form part of a particular situation or system. [V P] ⇒ He knew where I fitted in and what he had to do to get the best out of me. [V P + with] ⇒ This fits in with what you've told me. →  fit
