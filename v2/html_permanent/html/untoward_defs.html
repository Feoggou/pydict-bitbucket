untoward

(ˌʌntəˈwɔːd , ʌnˈtəʊəd )
Definitions
adjective1. characterized by misfortune, disaster, or annoyance2. not auspicious; adverse; unfavourable3. unseemly or improper4. out of the ordinary; out of the way5. archaic refractory; perverse6. obsolete awkward, ungainly, or uncouth
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

untowardness (ˌuntoˈwardness) noun

untoward for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌntəwɔːʳd , US -tɔːʳd )
Definitions
adjective [ADJ n]If you say that something untoward happens, you mean that something happens that is unexpected and causes difficulties. [formal] ⇒ The surveyor's report didn't highlight anything untoward. ⇒ Tampering with a single enzyme can lead to untoward effects elsewhere.
