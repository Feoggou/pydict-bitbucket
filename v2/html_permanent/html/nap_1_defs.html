nap1

(næp )
Definitions
verbWord forms: naps, napping or napped (intransitive)1. to sleep for a short while; doze2. to be unaware or inattentive; be off guard (esp in the phrase catch someone napping)noun3. a short light sleep; doze
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hnappian; related to Middle High German napfen

nap2

(næp )
Definitions
noun1. a. the raised fibres of velvet or similar clothb. the direction in which these fibres lie when smoothed down2. any similar downy coating3. Australian informal blankets, beddingverbWord forms: naps, napping or napped4. (transitive) to raise the nap of (cloth, esp velvet) by brushing or similar treatment
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: probably from Middle Dutch noppe; related to Old English hnoppian to pluck

nap3

(næp )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: napoleon a card game similar to whist, usually played for stakes2. a call in this card game, undertaking to win all five tricks3. horse racing a tipster's choice for an almost certain winner4. See go nap5. See not to go nap on6. See nap handverbWord forms: naps, napping or napped7. (transitive) horse racing to name (a horse) as likely to win a race
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: short for napoleon, the original name of the card game

nap for learners of English

(næp )
Word forms: naps , napping , napped
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you have a nap, you have a short sleep, usually during the day. ⇒ Use your lunch hour to have a nap in your chair. ⇒ I might take a little nap.2. verbIf you nap, you sleep for a short period of time, usually during the day. [V] ⇒ An elderly person may nap during the day and then sleep only five hours a night.3. singular nounThe nap of a carpet or of a cloth such as velvet is the top layer of short threads, which usually lie smoothly in one direction.4. →  catch sb napping
