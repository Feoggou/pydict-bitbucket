(ˈɪnsələnt )
Definitions
adjectiveoffensive, impudent, or disrespectful

Derived Forms

insolence (ˈinsolence) nouninsolently (ˈinsolently) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin insolens, from in-1 + solēre to be accustomed

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is being insolent, you mean they are being rude to someone they ought to be respectful to. ⇒ ...her insolent stare. ⇒ The officer stamped his boot. 'Don't be insolent with me, mademoiselle.'insolence uncount noun ⇒ Pupils could be excluded from school for insolence.
