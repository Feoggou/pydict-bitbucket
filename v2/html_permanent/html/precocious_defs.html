precocious

(prɪˈkəʊʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. ahead in development, such as the mental development of a child2. botany (of plants, fruit, etc) flowering or ripening early

Derived Forms

precociously (preˈcociously) adverbprecociousness (preˈcociousness) or precocity (prɪˈkɒsɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin praecox early maturing, from prae early + coquere to ripen

graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A precocious child is very clever, mature, or good at something, often in a way that you usually only expect to find in an adult. ⇒ Margaret was always a precocious child. ⇒ She burst on to the world tennis scene as a precocious 14-year old. ⇒ Despite her precocious talent for music and art, she failed both subjects at school.precociously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj, oft ADV with v] ⇒ He was a precociously bright school boy.
