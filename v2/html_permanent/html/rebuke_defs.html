rebuke

(rɪˈbjuːk )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to scold or reprimand (someone)noun2. a reprimand or scolding
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rebukable (reˈbukable) adjectiverebuker (reˈbuker) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norman French rebuker, from re- + Old French buchier to hack down, from busche log, of Germanic origin

rebuke

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪbjuːk ) for learners of English
Word forms: rebukes , rebuking , rebuked
Definitions
verbIf you rebuke someone, you speak severely to them because they have said or done something that you do not approve of. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The president rebuked the House and Senate for not passing those bills within 100 days.Rebuke is also a noun. ⇒ U.N. member countries delivered a strong rebuke to both countries for persisting with nuclear testing programs. ⇒ 'Silly little boy' was his favourite expression of rebuke to his pupils.
