taxi

(ˈtæksɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural taxis or taxies1. Also called: cab, taxicab a car, usually fitted with a taximeter, that may be hired, along with its driver, to carry passengers to any specified destinationverbWord forms: taxies, taxiing, taxying or taxied2. to cause (an aircraft) to move along the ground under its own power, esp before takeoff and after landing, or (of an aircraft) to move along the ground in this way3. (intransitive) to travel in a taxi

Word Origin

C20: shortened from taximeter cab

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft by N]A taxi is a car driven by a person whose job is to take people where they want to go in return for money. ⇒ The taxi drew up in front of the Riviera Club. ⇒ He set off by taxi.2. ergative verb & verbWhen an aircraft taxis along the ground, or when a pilot taxis a plane somewhere, it moves slowly along the ground. [V prep/adv] ⇒ She gave permission to the plane to taxi into position and hold for takeoff. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ The pilot taxied the plane to the end of the runway. [Also V, V n]
