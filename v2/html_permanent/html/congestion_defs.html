congestion

(kənˈdʒɛstʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state of being overcrowded, esp with traffic or people2. the state of being overloaded or clogged with blood3. the state of being blocked with mucus

congestion

Powered by COBUILD
(kəndʒestʃən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft adj N]If there is congestion in a place, the place is extremely crowded and blocked with traffic or people. ⇒ The problems of traffic congestion will not disappear in a hurry. ⇒ Energy consumption, congestion and pollution have increased.2. uncount noun [oft adj N]Congestion in a part of the body is a medical condition in which the part becomes blocked. [formal] ⇒ ...nasal congestion.
Example sentences containing 'congestion'
Outside, the congestion caused by the delivery van had escalated. Mark Burnell CHAMELEONFinally, the congestion was cleared, and Frannie drove them down the pier into the village itself. Clive Barker SACRAMENTNone had bothered to try to have a car pick them up, the congestion was too great. Gaskin, Catherine THE AMBASSADOR'S WOMEN
