baby

(ˈbeɪbɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -bies1. a. a newborn or recently born child; infantb. (as modifier) ⇒ baby food2. an unborn child; fetus3. the youngest or smallest of a family or group4. a. a newborn or recently born animalb. (as modifier) ⇒ baby rabbits5. usually derogatory an immature person6. slang a young woman or sweetheart: often used as a term of address expressing affection7. a project of personal concern8. See be left holding the baby9. See throw the baby out with the bath wateradjective10. (prenominal) comparatively small of its type ⇒ a baby carverb -bies, -bying or -bied (transitive)11. to treat with love and attention12. to treat (someone) like a baby; pamper or overprotect
Derived forms
babyhood (ˈbabyhood) nounbabyish (ˈbabyish) adjective
Word origin
C14: probably childish reduplication; compare mama, papa1

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA baby is a very young child, especially one that cannot yet walk or talk. ⇒ She used to take care of me when I was a baby. ⇒ My wife has just had a baby. ⇒ Claire had to dress her baby sister.2. countable noun [usu N n]A baby animal is a very young animal. ⇒ ...a baby elephant. ⇒ ...baby birds.3. adjective [ADJ n]Baby vegetables are vegetables picked when they are very small. ⇒ Serve with baby new potatoes.4. countable noun [usu sing]Some people use baby as an affectionate way of addressing someone, especially a young woman, or referring to them. [informal] ⇒ You have to wake up now, baby. ⇒ He was confused, poor baby.5. →  to throw the baby out with the bath water6. →  be left holding the baby
