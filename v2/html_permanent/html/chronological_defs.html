chronological

(ˌkrɒnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl , ˌkrəʊ-) or chronologic (ˌkrɒnəˈlɒdʒɪk , ˌkrəʊ-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (esp of a sequence of events) arranged in order of occurrence2. relating to or in accordance with chronology
Derived forms
ˌchronologically (ˌchronoˈlogically) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]If things are described or shown in chronological order, they are described or shown in the order in which they happened. ⇒ I have arranged these stories in chronological order.chronologically adverb [ADV after v, ADV -ed/adj] ⇒ The exhibition is organised chronologically.2. adjective [ADJ n]If you refer to someone's chronological age, you are referring to the number of years they have lived, in contrast to their mental age or the stage they have reached in their physical or emotional development. [formal]
