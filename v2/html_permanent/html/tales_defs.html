Word Frequency

tales

(ˈteɪliːz )
Definitions
noun law1. (functioning as plural) a group of persons summoned from among those present in court or from bystanders to fill vacancies on a jury panel2. (functioning as singular) the writ summoning such jurors
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

talesman (ˈtalesman) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Medieval Latin phrase tālēs dē circumstantibus such men from among the bystanders, from Latin tālis such
