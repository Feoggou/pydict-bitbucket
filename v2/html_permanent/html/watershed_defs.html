watershed

(ˈwɔːtəˌʃɛd )
Definitions
noun1. the dividing line between two adjacent river systems, such as a ridge2. an important period or factor that serves as a dividing line

watershed for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɔːtəʳʃed )
Word forms: watersheds
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]If something such as an event is a watershed in the history or development of something, it is very important because it represents the beginning of a new stage in it. [+ in] ⇒ The election of Mary Robinson in 1990 was a watershed in Irish politics. ⇒ Tonight could prove to be a watershed for the international career of Barnes.2. countable nounThe watershed is a time before which television broadcasters have agreed not to show programmes unsuitable for children, for example programmes that contain scenes of sex or violence. [British] ⇒ Bad language before the watershed is widely resented. ⇒ The advert should only be shown after the 9pm watershed.3. countable nounA watershed is an area of high ground which divides two or more river systems, so that all streams on one side flow into one river and those on the other side flow into a different river. [technical]
