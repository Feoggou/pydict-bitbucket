placid

(ˈplæsɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivehaving a calm appearance or nature

Derived Forms

placidity (pləˈsɪdɪtɪ ) or placidness (ˈplacidness) nounplacidly (ˈplacidly) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin placidus peaceful; related to placēre to please

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA placid person or animal is calm and does not easily become excited, angry, or upset. ⇒ She was a placid child who rarely cried. ⇒ Marcus remained placid in the face of her outburst.placidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ 'No matter, we will pay the difference,' Helena said placidly. ⇒ The cow in the nearby field was still chewing placidly on its cud.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A placid place, area of water, or life is calm and peaceful. ⇒ ...the placid waters of Lake Erie. ⇒ He had been leading a placid life for the past eight years.
