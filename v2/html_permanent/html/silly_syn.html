silly

adjective

  1. stupid, ridiculous, absurd, daft, inane, childish, immature, senseless, frivolous, preposterous, giddy, goofy (informal), idiotic, dozy (British, informal), fatuous, witless, puerile, brainless, asinine, dumb-ass (slang), cockamamie (slang, mainly US), dopy (slang) • That's a silly thing to say. OPPOSITES:  smart, clever, intelligent, serious, aware, bright, acute, reasonable, mature, profound, sane, perceptive
  2. foolish, stupid, unwise, inappropriate, rash, irresponsible, reckless, foolhardy, idiotic, thoughtless, imprudent, inadvisable • Don't go doing anything silly, now, will you? OPPOSITES:  wise, sensible, prudent, thoughtful, well-thought-out
  3. senseless, stupid, numb, into oblivion, groggy, stupefied, into a stupor • Right now the poor old devil's drinking himself silly.
  4. (old-fashioned) dazed, stunned, muzzy, groggy (informal), in a daze, stupefied, benumbed

noun

  1. (informal) fool, idiot, twit (informal), goose (informal), clot (British, informal), wally (slang), prat (slang), plonker (slang), duffer (informal), simpleton, ignoramus, nitwit (informal), ninny, silly-billy (informal), dweeb (US, slang), putz (US, slang), eejit (Scottish & & Irish), thicko (British, slang), doofus (slang, mainly US), nerd or nurd (slang), dorba or dorb (Australian, slang), bogan (Australian, slang) • Come on, silly, we'll miss all the fun.
