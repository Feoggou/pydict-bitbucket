nip1

(nɪp )
Definitions
verbWord forms: nips, nipping or nipped (mainly tr)1. to catch or tightly compress, as between a finger and the thumb; pinch2. (often foll by off) to remove by clipping, biting, etc3. (when intr, often foll by at) to give a small sharp bite (to) ⇒ the dog nipped at his heels4. (esp of the cold) to affect with a stinging sensation5. to harm through cold ⇒ the frost nipped the young plants6. to check or destroy the growth of (esp in the phrase nip in the bud)7. slang to steal8. (intr; foll by along, up, out, etc) British informal to hurry; dart9. slang, mainly US and Canadian to snatchnoun10. the act of nipping; a pinch, snip, etc11. a. a frosty or chilly qualityb. severe frost or cold ⇒ the first nip of winter12. a small piece or quantity ⇒ he went out for a nip of fresh air13. a sharp flavour or tang14. archaic a taunting remark15. See nip and tuck16. See put the nips in
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse hnippa to prod

nip2

(nɪp )
Definitions
noun1. a small drink of spirits; dram2. mainly British a measure of spirits usually equal to one sixth of a gillverbWord forms: nips, nipping or nipped3. to drink (spirits), esp habitually in small amounts
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

nip for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪp )
Word forms: nips , nipping , nipped
Definitions
1. verb [no passive]If you nip somewhere, usually somewhere nearby, you go there quickly or for a short time. [British, informal] [V adv/prep] ⇒ Should I nip out and get some groceries? [V adv/prep] ⇒ Wayne is always nipping down to the corner shop for him. [V adv/prep] ⇒ Beasley told me he'd seen you, so I nipped straight home.2. verbIf an animal or person nips you, they bite you lightly or squeeze a piece of your skin between their finger and thumb. [V n] ⇒ I have known cases where dogs have nipped babies. [V n] ⇒ He nipped Billy's cheek with two rough fingers. [Also V at n, V]Nip is also a noun. ⇒ Some ants can give you a nasty nip.3. countable nounA nip is a small amount of a strong alcoholic drink. tot [+ from] ⇒ She had a habit of taking an occasional nip from a flask of cognac.4. → to nip something in the bud
