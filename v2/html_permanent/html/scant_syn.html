scant

adjective

  1. inadequate, insufficient, meagre, sparse, little, limited, bare, minimal, deficient, barely sufficient • There is scant evidence of strong economic growth to come. OPPOSITES:  full, sufficient, generous, adequate, satisfactory, ample, abundant, plentiful
  2. small, limited, inadequate, insufficient, meagre, negligible, measly, scanty, inconsiderable • The hole was a scant 0.23 inches in diameter.

'scant' in Other Languages

  • British English: scant ADJECTIVE
    You use scant to indicate that there is very little of something or not as much of something as there should be.The police payed scant attention to the theft from her car.
