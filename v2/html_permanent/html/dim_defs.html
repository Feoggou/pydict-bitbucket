dim

(dɪm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: dimmer or dimmest1. badly illuminated ⇒ a dim room2. not clearly seen; indistinct; faint ⇒ a dim shape3. having weak or indistinct vision ⇒ eyes dim with tears4. lacking in understanding; mentally dull5. not clear in the mind; obscure ⇒ a dim memory6. lacking in brilliance, brightness, or lustre ⇒ a dim colour7. tending to be unfavourable; gloomy or disapproving (esp in the phrase take a dim view)verbWord forms: dims, dimming or dimmed8. to become or cause to become dim9. (transitive) to cause to seem less bright, as by comparison10. US and Canadian (transitive) to switch (car headlights) from the main to the lower beam. Also called (in Britain and certain other countries): dip
Derived forms
dimly (ˈdimly) adverbdimness (ˈdimness) noun
Word origin
Old English dimm; related to Old Norse dimmr gloomy, dark

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveDim light is not bright. ⇒ She stood waiting, in the dim light. ⇒ Below decks, the lights were dim.dimly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV -ed] ⇒ Two lamps burned dimly. ⇒ He followed her into a dimly lit kitchen.dimness uncount noun ⇒ ...the dimness of an early September evening.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA dim place is rather dark because there is not much light in it. ⇒ The room was dim and cool and quiet.dimness uncount noun ⇒ I squinted to adjust my eyes to the dimness.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA dim figure or object is not very easy to see, either because it is in shadow or darkness, or because it is far away. ⇒ Pete's torch picked out the dim figures of Bob and Chang.dimly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ The shoreline could be dimly seen.4. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you have a dim memory or understanding of something, it is difficult to remember or is unclear in your mind. ⇒ It seems that the '60s era of social activism is all but a dim memory.dimly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ Christina dimly recalled the procedure. ⇒ I was dimly aware that dozens of curious people were looking at us.5. graded adjective & adjectiveIf the future of something is dim, you have no reason to feel hopeful or positive about it. ⇒ The prospects for a peaceful solution are dim.6. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as dim, you think that they are stupid. [informal]7. ergative verb & verbIf you dim a light or if it dims, it becomes less bright. [V n] ⇒ Dim the lighting–it is unpleasant to lie with a bright light shining in your eyes. [V] ⇒ The houselights dimmed.8. verbIf you are driving a car and dim the headlights, you operate a switch that makes them shine downwards, so that they do not shine directly into the eyes of other drivers. [US] [V n] ⇒ Dim your lights behind that car.regional note:   in BRIT, use dip9. ergative verb & verbIf your future, hopes, or emotions dim or if something dims them, they become less good or less strong. [V] ⇒ Their economic prospects have dimmed. [V n] ⇒ Forty eight years of marriage have not dimmed the passion between Bill and Helen.10. ergative verbIf your eyes dim or are dimmed by something, they become weaker or unable to see clearly. [V] ⇒ Her eyes dimmed with sorrow. [be V-ed] ⇒ The twinkle in his eyes was dimmed by tears.11. ergative verb & verbIf your memories dim or if something dims them, they become less clear in your mind. [V] ⇒ Their memory of what happened has dimmed. [V n] ⇒ The intervening years had dimmed his memory.12. → to take a dim view
