raccoon

or racoon (rəˈkuːn )
Definitions
noun plural -coons or -coon1. any omnivorous mammal of the genus Procyon, esp P. lotor (North American raccoon), inhabiting forests of North and Central America and the Caribbean: family Procyonidae, order Carnivora (carnivores). Raccoons have a pointed muzzle, long tail, and greyish-black fur with black bands around the tail and across the face2. the fur of the North American raccoon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Algonquian ärähkun, from ärähkuněm he scratches with his hands
