sanctum

(ˈsæŋktəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tums or -ta (-tə)1. a sacred or holy place2. a room or place of total privacy or inviolability

Word Origin

C16: from Latin, from sanctus holy

Powered by COBUILD
(sæŋtəm )
Word forms: sanctums
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu sing]If you refer to someone's inner sanctum, you mean a room which is private and sometimes secret, where they can be quiet and alone. ⇒ His bedroom's his inner sanctum.2. countable nounA sanctum is the holiest place inside a holy building such as a temple or mosque.
