incomplete

(ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt )
Definitions
adjective1. not complete or finished2. not completely developed; imperfect3. logica. (of a formal theory) not so constructed that the addition of a non-theorem to the axioms renders it inconsistentb. (of an expression) not having a reference of its own but requiring completion by another expression
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incompletely (ˌincomˈpletely) adverbincompleteness (ˌincomˈpleteness) or incompletion (ˌincomˈpletion) noun

incomplete

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnkəmpliːt ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is incomplete is not yet finished, or does not have all the parts or details that it needs. ⇒ The clearing of rubbish and drains is still incomplete. ⇒ European political union would be incomplete without a defence element. ⇒ Some offices had incomplete information on spending.
