alarum

(əˈlærəm , -ˈlɑːr-, -ˈlɛər-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. archaic an alarm, esp a call to arms2. (used as a stage direction, esp in Elizabethan drama) a loud disturbance or conflict (esp in the phrase alarums and excursions)
Word origin
C15: variant of alarm
Example sentences containing 'alarum'
As the dwarven party passed by, the pair went tense, ready to sound an alarum , no doubt, at the least sign of trouble.
