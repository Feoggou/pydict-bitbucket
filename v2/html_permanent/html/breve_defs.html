breve

(briːv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an accent, (˘), placed over a vowel to indicate that it is of short duration or is pronounced in a specified way2. music a note, now rarely used, equivalent in time value to two semibreves3. Roman Catholic Church a less common word for brief (sense 7)
Word origin
C13: from Medieval Latin breve, from Latin brevis short; see brief
