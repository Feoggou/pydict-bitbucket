redundancy

(rɪˈdʌndənsɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -cies1. a. the state or condition of being redundant or superfluous, esp superfluous in one's jobb. (as modifier) ⇒ a redundancy payment2. Also (less commonly): redundance excessive proliferation or profusion, esp of superfluity3. duplication of components in electronic or mechanical equipment so that operations can continue following failure of a part4. repetition of information or inclusion of additional information to reduce errors in telecommunication transmissions and computer processing

redundancy

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪdʌndənsi ) for learners of English
Word forms: redundancies
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]When there are redundancies, an organization tells some of its employees to leave because their jobs are no longer necessary or because the organization can no longer afford to pay them. [British, business] ⇒ The ministry has said it hopes to avoid compulsory redundancies.regional note:   in AM, use dismissals, layoffs2. uncount nounRedundancy means being made redundant. [business] ⇒ Thousands of bank employees are facing redundancy as their employers cut costs. ⇒ The company has had to make redundancy payments of £472 million.
