constitutive

(ˈkɒnstɪˌtjuːtɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. having power to enact, appoint, or establish2. chemistry (of a physical property) determined by the arrangement of atoms in a molecule rather than by their nature3. biochemistry (of an enzyme) formed continuously, irrespective of the cell's needs4. another word for constituent (sense 1)
Derived forms
constiˌtutively (ˈconstiˌtutively) adverb
