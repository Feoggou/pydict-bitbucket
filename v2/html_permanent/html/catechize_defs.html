catechize

or catechise (ˈkætɪˌkaɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to teach or examine by means of questions and answers2. to give oral instruction in Christianity, esp by using a catechism3. to put questions to (someone)
Derived forms
catechist (ˈcatechist), cateˌchizer (ˈcateˌchizer) or cateˌchiser (ˈcateˌchiser) nounˌcatechistic (ˌcateˈchistic) or ˌcatechistical (ˌcateˈchistical) adjectiveˌcatechistically (ˌcateˈchistically) adverbˌcatechization (ˌcatechiˈzation) or ˌcatechisation (ˌcatechiˈsation) noun
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin catēchizāre, from Greek katēkhizein, from katēkhein to instruct orally, literally: to shout down, from kata- down + ēkhein to sound

Similar Words

question, examine, drill, grill (informal), interrogate, cross-examine
