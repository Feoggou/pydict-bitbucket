solace

(ˈsɒlɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. comfort in misery, disappointment, etc2. something that gives comfort or consolationverb (transitive)3. to give comfort or cheer to (a person) in time of sorrow, distress, etc4. to alleviate (sorrow, misery, etc)

Derived Forms

solacer (ˈsolacer) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French solas, from Latin sōlātium comfort, from sōlārī to console

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounSolace is a feeling of comfort that makes you feel less sad. [formal] ⇒ I found solace in writing when my father died three years ago. ⇒ Henry was inclined to seek solace in drink.2. singular nounIf something is a solace to you, it makes you feel less sad. [formal] comfort ⇒ She found the companionship of Marcia a solace.
