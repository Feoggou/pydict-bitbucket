arraign

(əˈreɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to bring (a prisoner) before a court to answer an indictment2. to call to account; complain about; accuse
Derived forms
arˈraigner nounarˈraignment noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French araisnier to speak, accuse, from a-2 + raisnier, from Vulgar Latin ratiōnāre (unattested) to talk, argue, from Latin ratiō a reasoning

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verb [usu passive]If someone is arraigned on a particular charge, they are brought before a court of law to answer that charge. [law] [be V-ed] ⇒ She was arraigned today on charges of assault and kidnapping. [be V-ed for n/v-ing] ⇒ He was arraigned for criminally abetting a traitor.
