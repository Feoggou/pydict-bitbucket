(əˈbiːdɪənt )
Definitions
adjectiveobeying or willing to obey
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obediently (oˈbediently) adverb

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, from Latin oboediens, present participle of oboedīre to obey

obedient for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(oʊbiːdiənt )
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveA person or animal who is obedient does what they are told to do. OPPOSITE: disobedient ⇒ He was very respectful at home and obedient to his parents. ⇒ What a sweet, obedient little girl she was in the sixth grade.obedience uncount noun [+ to] ⇒ ...unquestioning obedience to the law. ⇒ They command the respect and obedience of the armed forces.obediently graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He was looking obediently at Keith, waiting for orders.
