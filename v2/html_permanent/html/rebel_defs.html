rebel

Definitions
verb (rɪˈbɛl ) -bels, -belling or -belled (intransitive; often foll by against)1. to resist or rise up against a government or other authority, esp by force of arms2. to dissent from an accepted moral code or convention of behaviour, dress, etc3. to show repugnance (towards)noun (ˈrɛbəl )4. a. a person who rebelsb. (as modifier) ⇒ a rebel soldier, ⇒ a rebel leader5. a person who dissents from some accepted moral code or convention of behaviour, dress, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

Word Origin

C13: from Old French rebelle, from Latin rebellis insurgent, from re- + bellum war

Word forms: rebels, rebelling , rebelled
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The noun is pronounced (rebəl ). The verb is pronounced (rɪbel ).1. countable noun [usu pl]Rebels are people who are fighting against their own country's army in order to change the political system there. ⇒ ...fighting between rebels and government forces. ⇒ ...rebel forces in Liberia.2. countable nounPoliticians who oppose some of their own party's policies can be referred to as rebels. ⇒ The rebels want another 1% cut in interest rates. ⇒ ...rebel MPs.3. verbIf politicians rebel against one of their own party's policies, they show that they oppose it. [V + against] ⇒ More than forty Conservative MPs rebelled against the government and voted against the bill. [V + over] ⇒ ...MPs planning to rebel over the proposed welfare cuts.4. countable nounYou can say that someone is a rebel if you think that they behave differently from other people and have rejected the values of society or of their parents. ⇒ She had been a rebel at school.5. verbWhen someone rebels, they start to behave differently from other people and reject the values of society or of their parents. [V] ⇒ The child who rebels is unlikely to be overlooked. [V + against] ⇒ I was very young and rebelling against everything.
