ladle

(ˈleɪdəl )
Definitions
noun1. a long-handled spoon having a deep bowl for serving or transferring liquids ⇒ a soup ladle2. a large bucket-shaped container for transferring molten metalverb3. (transitive) to lift or serve out with or as if with a ladle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ladleful (ˈladleˌful) noun

Word Origin

Old English hlædel, from hladan to draw out

ladle

Powered by COBUILD
(leɪdəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: ladles , ladling , ladled
Definitions
1. countable nounA ladle is a large, round, deep spoon with a long handle, used for serving soup, stew, or sauce.2. verbIf you ladle food such as soup or stew, you serve it, especially with a ladle. [V n prep] ⇒ Barry held the bowls while Liz ladled soup into them. [V n with adv] ⇒ Mrs King went to the big black stove and ladled out steaming soup. [Also V n]► →  ladle out
