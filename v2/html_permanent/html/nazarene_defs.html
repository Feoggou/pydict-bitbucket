Nazarene

(ˌnæzəˈriːn , ˈnæz-)
Definitions
noun1. an early name for a Christian (Acts 24:5) or (when preceded by the) for Jesus Christ2. a member of one of several groups of Jewish-Christians found principally in Syria3. a member of an association of German artists called the Nazarenes or Brotherhood of St Luke, including Friedrich Overbeck (1789–1869) and Peter von Cornelius (1783–1867), founded (1809) in Vienna to revive German religious art after the examples of the Middle Ages and early Renaissanceadjective4. of or relating to Nazareth or the Nazarenes
