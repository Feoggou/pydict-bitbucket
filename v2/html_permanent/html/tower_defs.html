tower

(ˈtaʊə )
Definitions
noun1. a tall, usually square or circular structure, sometimes part of a larger building and usually built for a specific purpose ⇒ a church tower, ⇒ a control tower2. a place of defence or retreat3. a mobile structure used in medieval warfare to attack a castle, etc4. See tower of strengthverb5. (intransitive) to be or rise like a tower; loom
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C12: from Old French tur, from Latin turris, from Greek

tower

Powered by COBUILD
(taʊəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: towers , towering , towered
Definitions
1. countable nounA tower is a tall, narrow building, that either stands alone or forms part of another building such as a church or castle. ⇒ ...an eleventh century castle with 120-foot high towers. ⇒ ...the Leaning Tower of Pisa.2. verbSomeone or something that towers over surrounding people or things is a lot taller than they are. [V + over/above] ⇒ He stood up and towered over her. [V over/above n] ⇒ At school, a girl may tower over most boys her age. [V over/above n] ⇒ The icebergs towered above them.3. countable nounA tower is a tall structure that is used for sending radio or television signals. ⇒ Troops are still in control of the television and radio tower.4. countable nounA tower is the same as a tower block. ⇒ ...his design for a new office tower in Frankfurt.5. countable nounA tower is a tall box that contains the main parts of a computer, such as the hard disk and the drives. [computing]6. → See also clock tower, control tower, ivory tower7. →  tower of strength
