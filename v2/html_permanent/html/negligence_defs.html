negligence

(ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns )
Definitions
noun1. the state or quality of being negligent2. a negligent act3. law a civil wrong whereby a person or party is in breach of a legal duty of care to another which results in loss or injury to the claimant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

negligence in Insurance

Powered by COBUILD
(nɛglɪdʒəns)
Definitions
noun(Insurance: General) If someone is guilty of negligence, they have not taken enough care or have failed to to do something which they ought to have done. ⇒ Some errors may not be compensated at all, depending on whether the error was the result of negligence. ⇒ Professional indemnity insurance provides indemnity to professional individuals or firms against claims for financial loss for negligence due to failure to exercise the required degree of care. ⇒ If someone is guilty of negligence, they have not taken enough care or have failed to to do something which they ought to have done.
