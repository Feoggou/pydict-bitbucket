console1

(kənˈsəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbto serve as a source of comfort to (someone) in disappointment, loss, sadness, etc
Derived forms
consolable (conˈsolable) adjectiveconsoler (conˈsoler) nounconsolingly (conˈsolingly) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin consōlārī, from sōlārī to comfort; see solace

console2

(ˈkɒnsəʊl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an ornamental bracket, esp one used to support a wall fixture, bust, etc2. the part of an organ comprising the manuals, pedals, stops, etc3. a unit on which the controls of an electronic system are mounted4. same as games console5. a cabinet for a television, gramophone, etc, designed to stand on the floor6. See console table
Word origin
C18: from French, shortened from Old French consolateur one that provides support, hence, supporting bracket, from Latin consōlātor a comforter; see console1

console

Powered by COBUILD
Word forms: consoles, consoling , consoled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The verb is pronounced (kənsoʊl ). The noun is pronounced (kɒnsoʊl ).1. verbIf you console someone who is unhappy about something, you try to make them feel more cheerful. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Never mind, Ned,' he consoled me. [V n] ⇒ Often they cry, and I have to play the role of a mother, consoling them. [V pron-refl] ⇒ He will have to console himself by reading about the success of his compatriots. [V pron-refl + with] ⇒ I can console myself with the fact that I'm not alone. [V pron-refl that] ⇒ He consoled himself that Emmanuel looked like a nice boy, who could be a good playmate for his daughter. [Also V n with/for n, V n that] [Also V pron-refl + for]consoling graded adjective comforting ⇒ It is not a consoling thought to Germans to see that Americans have the same kind of problem, too.consolation (kɒnsəleɪʃən )Word forms: consolations variable noun [+ for] ⇒ The only consolation for the Scottish theatre community is that they look likely to get another chance. ⇒ He knew then he was right, but it was no consolation.2. countable nounA console is a panel with a number of switches or knobs that is used to operate a machine.
