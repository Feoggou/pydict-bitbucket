incidentally

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnsɪdentli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverbYou use incidentally to introduce a point which is not directly relevant to what you are saying, often a question or extra information that you have just thought of. by the way ⇒ 'I didn't ask you to come. Incidentally, why have you come?' ⇒ The tower, incidentally, dates from the twelfth century.2. adverb [ADV with v]If something occurs only incidentally, it is less important than another thing or is not a major part of it. ⇒ The letter mentioned my great-aunt and uncle only incidentally.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
