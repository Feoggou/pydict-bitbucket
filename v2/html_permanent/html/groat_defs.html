groat

(ɡrəʊt )
Definitions
nounan English silver coin worth four pennies, taken out of circulation in the 17th century
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Middle Dutch groot, from Middle Low German gros, from Medieval Latin (denarius) grossus thick (coin); see groschen
Example sentences containing 'groat'
I mean, this pen's rare because I made it myself from hawthorn, not another like it in the world, but it's still not worth a bent groat. Gash, Jonathan THE TARTAN RINGERS
