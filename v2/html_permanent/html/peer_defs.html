peer1

(pɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a member of a nobility; nobleman2. a person who holds any of the five grades of the British nobility: duke, marquess, earl, viscount, and baron. See also life peer3. a. a person who is an equal in social standing, rank, age, etcb. (as modifier) ⇒ peer pressure4. archaic a companion; mate

Word Origin

C14 (in sense 3): from Old French per, from Latin pār equal

peer2

(pɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (intransitive)1. to look intently with or as if with difficulty ⇒ to peer into the distance2. to appear partially or dimly ⇒ the sun peered through the fog

Word Origin

C16: from Flemish pieren to look with narrowed eyes

peer

Powered by COBUILD
(pɪəʳ )
Word forms: peers , peering , peered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you peer at something, you look at it very hard, usually because it is difficult to see clearly. [V prep] ⇒ I had been peering at a computer print-out that made no sense at all. [V prep] ⇒ He watched the Customs official peer into the driver's window.2. countable nounIn Britain, a peer is a member of the nobility who has or had the right to vote in the House of Lords. ⇒ Lord Swan was made a life peer in 1981.3. countable noun [usu pl]Your peers are the people who are the same age as you or who have the same status as you. ⇒ ...children who are much cleverer than their peers. ⇒ His engaging personality made him popular with his peers.
