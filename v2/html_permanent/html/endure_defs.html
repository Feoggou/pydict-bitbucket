endure

(ɪnˈdjʊə )
Definitions
verb1. to undergo (hardship, strain, privation, etc) without yielding; bear2. (transitive) to permit or tolerate3. (intransitive) to last or continue to exist
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

endurable (enˈdurable) adjectiveendurability (enˌduraˈbility) or endurableness (enˈdurableness) nounendurably (enˈdurably) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Old French endurer, from Latin indūrāre to harden, from dūrus hard

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndjʊəʳ , US -dʊr ) for learners of English
Word forms: endures , enduring , endured
Definitions
1. verbIf you endure a painful or difficult situation, you experience it and do not avoid it or give up, usually because you cannot. undergo [V n] ⇒ The company endured heavy financial losses. [V n] ⇒ ...unbearable pain, which they had to endure in solitude because not even the doctors could get near them.2. verbIf something endures, it continues to exist without any loss in quality or importance. [V] ⇒ Somehow the language endures and continues to survive.enduring graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n] ⇒ ...the start of an enduring friendship.
