Synonyms of temper

temper

noun

  1. irritability, anger, irascibility, passion, resentment, irritation, annoyance, petulance, surliness, ill humour, peevishness, hot-headedness
    • I hope he can control his temper.
    OPPOSITES:  goodwill, contentment, good humour
  2. frame of mind, character, nature, attitude, mind, mood, constitution, humour, vein, temperament, tenor, disposition
    • He's known for his placid temper.
  3. rage, fury, bad mood, passion, paddy (British, informal), wax (informal, mainly British), tantrum, bate (British, slang), fit of pique, foulie (Australian, slang), hissy fit (informal), strop (British, informal)
    • She was still in a temper when I arrived.
  4. self-control, composure, cool (slang), calm, good humour, tranquillity, coolness, calmness, equanimity
    • I've never seen him lose his temper.
    OPPOSITES:  anger, fury, wrath, irritation, indignation, agitation, pique, bad mood, excitability, vexation, grumpiness, irascibility, foul humour, foulie (Australian, slang)

verb

  1. moderate, restrain, tone down, calm, soften, soothe, lessen, allay, mitigate, abate, assuage, mollify, soft-pedal (informal), palliate, admix
    • He had to learn to temper his enthusiasm.
    OPPOSITES:  intensify, arouse, heighten, excite, stir, provoke, aggravate
  2. strengthen, harden, toughen, anneal
    • a new way of tempering glass
    OPPOSITE:  soften
