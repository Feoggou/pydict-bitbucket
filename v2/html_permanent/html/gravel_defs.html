gravel

(ˈɡrævəl )
Definitions
noun1. an unconsolidated mixture of rock fragments that is coarser than sand2. geology a mixture of rock fragments with diameters in the range 4–76 mm3. pathology small rough calculi in the kidneys or bladderverb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled (transitive)4. to cover with gravel5. to confound or confuse6. US informal to annoy or disturb
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gravelish (ˈgravelish) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French gravele, diminutive of grave gravel, perhaps of Celtic origin
