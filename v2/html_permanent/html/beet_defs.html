beet

(biːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any chenopodiaceous plant of the genus Beta, esp the Eurasian species B. vulgaris, widely cultivated in such varieties as the sugar beet, mangelwurzel, beetroot, and spinach beet. See also chard2. the leaves of any of several varieties of this plant, which are cooked and eaten as a vegetable3. See red beet
Word origin
Old English bēte, from Latin bēta

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounBeet is a crop with a thick round root. It is often used to feed animals, especially cows. ⇒ ...fields of sweet corn and beet.2. → see also sugar beet3. variable noun [usu pl]Beets are dark red roots that are eaten as a vegetable. They are often preserved in vinegar. [US]regional note:   in BRIT, use beetroot
