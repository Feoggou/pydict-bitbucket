strangeness

(ˈstreɪndʒnɪs )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state or quality of being strange2. physics a property of certain elementary particles, characterized by a quantum number (strangeness number) conserved in strong and electromagnetic but not in weak interactions. It is associated with the presence of strange quarks
Example sentences containing 'strangeness'
There were no ice intrusions here, either, adding to her feeling of strangeness. Garth Nix LIRAEL: DAUGHTER OF THE CLAYRAlways, life was a reaching out into diversity and strangeness , into new forms of organization. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODShe had her hands clamped to the wound, but she could not entirely conceal its strangeness. Clive Barker SACRAMENTCarpenter, despite himself, felt moved by the grandeur and the strangeness of the scene.
