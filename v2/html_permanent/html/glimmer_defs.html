glimmer

(ˈɡlɪmə )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (of a light, candle, etc) to glow faintly or flickeringly2. to be indicated faintly ⇒ hope glimmered in his facenoun3. a glow or twinkle of light4. a faint indication
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: compare Middle High German glimmern, Swedish glimra, Danish glimre

glimmer

Powered by COBUILD
(glɪməʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: glimmers , glimmering , glimmered
Definitions
1. verbIf something glimmers, it produces or reflects a faint, gentle, often unsteady light. [V] ⇒ The moon glimmered faintly through the mists. [V-ing] ⇒ ...the glimmering ocean.2. countable nounA glimmer is a faint, gentle, often unsteady light. [+ of] ⇒ In the east there is the slightest glimmer of light.3. countable nounA glimmer of something is a faint sign of it. gleam [+ of] ⇒ Despite an occasional glimmer of hope, this campaign has not produced any results. [+ of] ⇒ He is celebrating his first glimmer of success.
