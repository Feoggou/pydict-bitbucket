variant

(ˈvɛərɪənt )
Definitions
adjective1. liable to or displaying variation2. differing from a standard or type ⇒ a variant spelling3. obsolete not constant; ficklenoun4. something that differs from a standard or type5. statistics another word for variate (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin variāns, from variāre to diversify, from varius various

(veəriənt ) for learners of English
Word forms: variants
Definitions
countable nounA variant of a particular thing is something that has a different form to that thing, although it is related to it. [+ of] ⇒ The quagga was a strikingly beautiful variant of the zebra. ⇒ An intriguing variant to pipelines would be to carry the water using the existing system of canals. ⇒ There are so many variant spellings of his name.
