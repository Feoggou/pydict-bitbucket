harvest

(ˈhɑːvɪst )
Definitions
noun1. the gathering of a ripened crop2. the crop itself or the yield from it in a single growing season3. the season for gathering crops4. the product of an effort, action, etc ⇒ a harvest of loveverb5. to gather or reap (a ripened crop) from (the place where it has been growing)6. (transitive) to receive or reap (benefits, consequences, etc)7. (transitive) mainly US to remove (an organ) from the body for transplantation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

harvesting (ˈharvesting) nounharvestless (ˈharvestless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English hærfest; related to Old Norse harfr harrow, Old High German herbist autumn, Latin carpere to pluck, Greek karpos fruit, Sanskrit krpāna shears

Powered by COBUILD
(hɑːʳvɪst ) for learners of English
Word forms: harvests , harvesting , harvested
Definitions
1. singular nounThe harvest is the gathering of a crop. ⇒ There were about 300 million tons of grain in the fields at the start of the harvest.2. countable nounA harvest is the crop that is gathered in. ⇒ ...a bumper potato harvest. ⇒ Millions of people are threatened with starvation as a result of drought and poor harvests.3. verbWhen you harvest a crop, you gather it in. [V n] ⇒ Many farmers are refusing to harvest the cane. [V-ed] ⇒ ...freshly harvested beetroot.harvesting uncount noun ⇒ ...war is hampering harvesting and the distribution of food aid.4. verbIf you harvest a large number of things, you collect them, often by making great efforts. [literary] collect [V n] ⇒ In his new career as a restaurateur he has blossomed and harvested many awards.5. →  reap the harvest
