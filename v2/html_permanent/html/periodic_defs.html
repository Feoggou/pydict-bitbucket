periodic

(ˌpɪərɪˈɒdɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. happening or recurring at intervals; intermittent2. of, relating to, or resembling a period3. having or occurring in repeated periods or cycles

Derived Forms

ˌperiodically (ˌperiˈodically) adverbperiodicity (ˌpɪərɪəˈdɪsɪtɪ ) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]Periodic events or situations happen occasionally, at fairly regular intervals. ⇒ Periodic checks are taken to ensure that high standards are maintained. ⇒ ...periodic bouts of illness.
Example sentences containing 'periodic'
It's the only place I need to know to appease my periodic cravings for pasta. Tapply, William G THE DUTCH BLUE ERRORAn overworked police force made periodic raids to enforce compliance. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARDecade after decade, perhaps century after century, they had made periodic attempts to return, only to have many of their number perish. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANThe stable compounds are made of constituents that have a regular, periodic motion relative to one another. Ursula K. Le Guin THE DISPOSSESSED
