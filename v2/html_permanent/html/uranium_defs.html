uranium

(jʊˈreɪnɪəm )
Definitions
nouna radioactive silvery-white metallic element of the actinide series. It occurs in several minerals including pitchblende, carnotite, and autunite and is used chiefly as a source of nuclear energy by fission of the radioisotope uranium-235. Symbol: U; atomic no: 92; atomic wt: 238.0289; half-life of most stable isotope, 238U: 451 × 109 years; valency: 2-6; relative density: 18.95 (approx.); melting pt: 1135°C; boiling pt: 4134°C
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from New Latin, from Uranus2; from the fact that the element was discovered soon after the planet
