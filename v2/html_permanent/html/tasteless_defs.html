tasteless

(ˈteɪstlɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. lacking in flavour; insipid2. lacking social or aesthetic taste3. rare unable to taste

Derived Forms

tastelessly (ˈtastelessly) adverbtastelessness (ˈtastelessness) noun

tasteless

Powered by COBUILD
(teɪstləs )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something such as furniture, clothing, or the way that a house is decorated as tasteless, you consider it to be vulgar and unattractive. ⇒ ...a flat crammed with spectacularly tasteless objets d'art.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something such as a remark or joke as tasteless, you mean that it is offensive. vulgar ⇒ I think that is the most vulgar and tasteless remark I ever heard in my life.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe food or drink as tasteless, you mean that it has very little or no flavour. ⇒ The fish was mushy and tasteless.
Example sentences containing 'tasteless'
