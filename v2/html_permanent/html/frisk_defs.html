frisk

(frɪsk )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to leap, move about, or act in a playful manner; frolic2. (transitive) (esp of animals) to whisk or wave briskly ⇒ the dog frisked its tail3. (transitive)a. to search (someone) by feeling for concealed weapons, etcb. to rob by searching in this waynoun4. a playful antic or movement; frolic5. the act or an instance of frisking a person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frisker (ˈfrisker) nounfriskingly (ˈfriskingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Old French frisque, of Germanic origin; related to Old High German frisc lively, fresh
