wisp

(wɪsp )
Definitions
noun1. a thin, light, delicate, or fibrous piece or strand, such as a streak of smoke or a lock of hair2. a small bundle, as of hay or straw3. anything slender and delicate ⇒ a wisp of a girl4. a mere suggestion or hint5. a flock of birds, esp snipeverb6. (intransitive; often foll by away) to move or act like a wisp7. (transitive) mainly British dialect to twist into a wisp8. (transitive) mainly British to groom (a horse) with a wisp of straw, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wisplike (ˈwispˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: variant of wips, of obscure origin; compare wipe

Definitions
1. countable nounA wisp of hair is a small, thin, untidy bunch of it. [+ of] ⇒ She smoothed away a wisp of hair from her eyes.2. countable nounA wisp of something such as smoke or cloud is an amount of it in a long thin shape. [+ of] ⇒ A thin wisp of smoke straggled up through the trees. ⇒ ...an occasional wisp of white cloud.
