pub

(pʌb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Formal name: public house mainly British a building with a bar and one or more public rooms licensed for the sale and consumption of alcoholic drink, often also providing light meals2. Australian and New Zealand a hotelverbWord forms: pubs, pubbing or pubbed3. (intransitive) informal to visit a pub or pubs (esp in the phrase go pubbing)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA pub is a building where people can have drinks, especially alcoholic drinks, and talk to their friends. Many pubs also serve food. [mainly British] ⇒ He was in the pub until closing time. ⇒ Richard used to run a pub. → bar
