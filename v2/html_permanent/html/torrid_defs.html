torrid

(ˈtɒrɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. so hot and dry as to parch or scorch2. arid or parched3. highly charged emotionally ⇒ a torrid love scene
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

torridity (torˈridity) or torridness (ˈtorridness) nountorridly (ˈtorridly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin torridus, from torrēre to scorch

Powered by COBUILD
(tɒrɪd , US tɔːrɪd ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Torrid weather is extremely hot and dry. [literary] ⇒ ...the torrid heat of a Spanish summer.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A torrid relationship or incident involves very strong emotions connected with love and sex. ⇒ She began a torrid love affair with a theatrical designer. ⇒ ...torrid bedroom scenes.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If someone or something has a torrid time, they experience a lot of difficulties. [British, journalism] ⇒ Seles, the victim of a death threat earlier this week, has had a torrid time during the Championships. ⇒ The minister suffered yet another torrid day of criticism.
