acquaint

(əˈkweɪnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. (foll by with or of) to make (a person) familiar or conversant (with); inform (of)2. (foll by with) mainly US to introduce (to); bring into contact (with)
Word origin
C13: via Old French and Medieval Latin from Latin accognitus, from accognōscere to know perfectly, from ad- (intensive) + cognōscere to know

Powered by COBUILD
(əkweɪnt )
Word forms: acquaints , acquainting , acquainted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you acquaint someone with something, you tell them about it so that they know it. If you acquaint yourself with something, you learn about it. [formal] [V n + with] ⇒ ...efforts to acquaint the public with their rights under the new law. [V pron-refl + with] ⇒ I want to acquaint myself with your strengths and weaknesses.2. → see also acquainted
Example sentences containing 'acquaint'
I know your account is supported by your father, and he has always been most understanding, but I would hesitate to acquaint him with this. Leasor, James TANK OF SERPENTSBut I just wanted to run this entire sequence right through, to acquaint you with the full picture and all of its ramifications. Patrick Robinson NIMITZ CLASS
