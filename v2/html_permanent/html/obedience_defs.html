obedience

(əˈbiːdɪəns )
Definitions
noun1. the condition or quality of being obedient2. the act or an instance of obeying; dutiful or submissive behaviour3. the authority vested in a Church or similar body4. the collective group of persons submitting to this authority. See also passive obedience
