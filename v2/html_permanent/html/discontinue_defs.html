discontinue

(ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -ues, -uing or -ued1. to come or bring to an end; interrupt or be interrupted; stop2. (transitive) law to terminate or abandon (an action, suit, etc)
Derived forms

Word forms: discontinues , discontinuing , discontinued
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you discontinue something that you have been doing regularly, you stop doing it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Do not discontinue the treatment without consulting your doctor. [Also V -ing]2. verb [usu passive]If a product is discontinued, the manufacturer stops making it. [be V-ed] ⇒ The Leica M2 was discontinued in 1967. [V-ed] ⇒ They have a huge selection of discontinued cookers.
