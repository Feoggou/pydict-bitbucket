to go

Powered by COBUILD

Definitions
phraseIf you say that there are a particular number of things to go, you mean that they still remain to be dealt with. ⇒ I still had another five operations to go. →  go
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

to go

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
phraseIf you say that there is a certain amount of time to go, you mean that there is that amount of time left before something happens or ends. ⇒ There is a week to go until the elections. →  go
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

to go

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
phraseIf you are in a café or restaurant and ask for an item of food to go, you mean that you want to take it away with you and not eat it there. [mainly US] ⇒ Large fries to go.regional note:   in BRIT, use to take out, to take away →  go
