hoot1

(huːt )
Definitions
noun1. the mournful wavering cry of some owls2. a similar sound, such as that of a train whistle3. a jeer of derision4. informal an amusing person or thing ⇒ the weekend was a hoot5. See not give a hootverb6. (often foll by at) to jeer or yell (something) contemptuously (at someone)7. (transitive) to drive (political speakers, actors on stage, etc) off or away by hooting8. (intransitive) to make a hoot9. (intransitive) British to blow a horn
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13 hoten, of imitative origin

hoot

Powered by COBUILD
(huːt ) for learners of English
Word forms: hoots , hooting , hooted
Definitions
1. ergative verb & verbIf you hoot the horn on a vehicle or if it hoots, it makes a loud noise on one note. [mainly British] [V n] ⇒ I never hoot my horn when I pick a girl up for a date. [V] ⇒ Somewhere in the distance a siren hooted. [V + at] ⇒ I can be very rude to motorists who hoot at me. [V] ⇒ It felt good to drive down the middle of the road, hooting at every junction.Hoot is also a noun. ⇒ Mortlake strode on, ignoring the car, in spite of a further warning hoot.regional note:   in AM, usually use honk, toot2. verbIf you hoot, you make a loud high-pitched noise when you are laughing or showing disapproval. [V] ⇒ The protesters chanted, blew whistles and hooted at the name of Governor Pete Wilson. [V with n] ⇒ Bev hooted with laughter.Hoot is also a noun. ⇒ His confession was greeted with derisive hoots. ⇒ This time she burst into hoots of laughter.3. verbWhen an owl hoots, it makes a sound like a long 'oo'. [V] ⇒ Out in the garden an owl hooted suddenly.4. singular noun [a N]If you say that someone or something is a hoot, you think they are very amusing. [informal] ⇒ Michael Fish is my favourite. He's a hoot, a real character.5. →  do not give a hoot
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
