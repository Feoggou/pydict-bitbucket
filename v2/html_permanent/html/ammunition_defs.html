ammunition

(ˌæmjʊˈnɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any projectiles, such as bullets, rockets, etc, that can be discharged from a weapon2. bombs, missiles, chemicals, biological agents, nuclear materials, etc, capable of use as weapons3. any means of defence or attack, as in an argument
Word origin
C17: from obsolete French amunition, by mistaken division from earlier la munition; see munition

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAmmunition is bullets and rockets that are made to be fired from guns. ⇒ He had only seven rounds of ammunition for the revolver.2. uncount nounYou can describe information that you can use against someone in an argument or discussion as ammunition. ⇒ The improved trade figures have given the government fresh ammunition.
