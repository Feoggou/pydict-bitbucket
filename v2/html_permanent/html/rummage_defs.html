rummage

(ˈrʌmɪdʒ )
Definitions
verb1. (when intr, often foll by through) to search (through) while looking for something, often causing disorder or confusionnoun2. an act of rummaging3. a jumble of articles4. obsolete confusion or bustle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

rummager (ˈrummager) noun

Word Origin

C14 (in the sense: to pack a cargo): from Old French arrumage, from arrumer to stow in a ship's hold, probably of Germanic origin

rummage

Powered by COBUILD
(rʌmɪdʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: rummages , rummaging , rummaged
Definitions
1. verbIf you rummage through something, you search for something you want by moving things around in a careless or hurried way. [V prep] ⇒ They rummage through piles of second-hand clothes for something that fits. [V prep] ⇒ Marianne went to rummage in the refrigerator. [Also V]Rummage is also a noun. ⇒ A brief rummage will provide several pairs of gloves. ⇒ ...a rummage through his wardrobe for some tennis whites.Rummage about and rummage around mean the same as rummage. [V P] ⇒ I opened the fridge and rummaged about. [V P n] ⇒ He rummaged around the post room and found the document.2. uncount nounRummage is old or unwanted things that people give away to charities. [US] ⇒ ...loads of pitiful rummage.regional note:   in BRIT, use jumble
