nova

(ˈnəʊvə )
Definitions
noun plural -vae (-viː) or -vasa variable star that undergoes a cataclysmic eruption, observed as a sudden large increase in brightness with a subsequent decline over months or years; it is a close binary system with one component a white dwarf. Compare supernova
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: New Latin nova (stella) new (star), from Latin novus new
