agave

(əˈɡeɪvɪ , ˈæɡeɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounany plant of the genus Agave, native to tropical America, with tall flower stalks rising from a massive, often armed, rosette of thick fleshy leaves: family Agavaceae. Some species are the source of fibres such as sisal or of alcoholic beverages such as pulque and tequila. See also century plant
Word origin
C18: New Latin, from Greek agauē, feminine of agauos illustrious, probably alluding to the height of the plant
