beauty

(ˈbjuːtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the combination of all the qualities of a person or thing that delight the senses and please the mind2. a very attractive and well-formed girl or woman3. informal an outstanding example of its kind ⇒ the horse is a beauty4. informal an advantageous feature ⇒ one beauty of the job is the short hours5. informal, old-fashioned a light-hearted and affectionate term of address ⇒ hello, my old beauty!exclamation6. (New Zealand English ˈbjuːdɪ) an expression of approval or agreement. Also (Scot, Austral, and NZ): you beauty
Word origin
C13: from Old French biauté, from biau beautiful; see beau

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounBeauty is the state or quality of being beautiful. ⇒ ...an area of outstanding natural beauty. ⇒ Everyone admired her elegance and her beauty.2. countable nounA beauty is a beautiful woman. [journalism] belle ⇒ She is known as a great beauty.3. countable nounYou can say that something is a beauty when you think it is very good. [informal] peach ⇒ The pass was a real beauty, but the shot was poor.4. countable noun [usu pl]The beauties of something are its attractive qualities or features. [literary] glory ⇒ He was beginning to enjoy the beauties of nature.5. adjective [ADJ n]Beauty is used to describe people, products, and activities that are concerned with making women look beautiful. ⇒ Additional beauty treatments can be booked in advance.6. countable nounIf you say that a particular feature is the beauty of something, you mean that this feature is what makes the thing so good. ⇒ There would be no effect on animals–that's the beauty of such water-based materials.
