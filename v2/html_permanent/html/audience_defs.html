audience

(ˈɔːdɪəns )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a group of spectators or listeners, esp at a public event such as a concert or play2. the people reached by a book, film, or radio or television programme3. the devotees or followers of a public entertainer, lecturer, etc; regular public4. an opportunity to put one's point of view, such as a formal interview with a monarch or head of state
Word origin
C14: from Old French, from Latin audientia a hearing, from audīre to hear

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]The audience at a play, concert, film, or public meeting is the group of people watching or listening to it. ⇒ The entire audience broke into loud applause. ⇒ He was speaking to an audience of students at the Institute for International Affairs.2. countable noun [with sing or pl verb]The audience for a television or radio programme consists of all the people who watch or listen to it. ⇒ The concert will be relayed to a worldwide television audience estimated at one thousand million.3. → see also studio audience4. countable noun [with sing or pl verb, usu sing]The audience of a writer or artist is the people who read their books or look at their work. ⇒ Merle's writings reached a wide audience during his lifetime. ⇒ She began to find a receptive audience for her work.5. countable noun [usu sing]If you have an audience with someone important, you have a formal meeting with them. [+ with] ⇒ The Prime Minister will seek an audience with the Queen later this morning.
