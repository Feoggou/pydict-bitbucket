bellow

(ˈbɛləʊ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to make a loud deep raucous cry like that of a bull; roar2. to shout (something) unrestrainedly, as in anger or pain; bawlnoun3. the characteristic noise of a bull4. a loud deep sound, as of pain or anger
Derived forms
bellower (ˈbellower) noun
Word origin
C14: probably from Old English bylgan; related to bellan to bell2

bellow

Powered by COBUILD
(beloʊ )
Word forms: bellows , bellowing , bellowed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone bellows, they shout angrily in a loud, deep voice. [V with quote] ⇒ 'I didn't ask to be born!' she bellowed. [V + at] ⇒ She prayed she wouldn't come in and find them there, bellowing at each other. [V n prep] ⇒ He bellowed information into the mouthpiece of his portable telephone. [Also V]Bellow is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ I was distraught and let out a bellow of tearful rage.2. verbWhen a large animal such as a bull or an elephant bellows, it makes a loud and deep noise. [V] ⇒ A heifer bellowed in her stall.3. countable noun [oft a pair of N]A bellows is or bellows are a device used for blowing air into a fire in order to make it burn more fiercely. ⇒ ...two stone forges, each equipped with bellows.
