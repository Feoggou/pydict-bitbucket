lonely

(ˈləʊnlɪ )
Definitions
adjective -lier or -liest1. unhappy as a result of being without the companionship of others ⇒ a lonely man2. causing or resulting from the state of being alone ⇒ a lonely existence3. isolated, unfrequented, or desolate4. without companions; solitary
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

loneliness (ˈloneliness) noun

lonely

Powered by COBUILD
(loʊnli ) for learners of English
Word forms: lonelier , loneliest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is lonely is unhappy because they are alone or do not have anyone they can talk to. ⇒ ...lonely people who just want to talk. ⇒ I feel lonelier in the middle of London than I do on my boat in the middle of nowhere.The lonely are people who are lonely. ⇒ He looks for the lonely, the lost, the unloved.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA lonely situation or period of time is one in which you feel unhappy because you are alone or do not have anyone to talk to. ⇒ I desperately needed something to occupy me during those long, lonely nights. ⇒ ...her lonely childhood.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA lonely place is one where very few people come. ⇒ It felt like the loneliest place in the world. ⇒ ...dark, lonely streets.
