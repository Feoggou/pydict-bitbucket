confessional

(kənˈfɛʃənəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of, like, or suited to a confessionnoun2. Christianity, mainly Roman Catholic Church a small stall, usually enclosed and divided by a screen or curtain, where a priest hears confessions3. a book of penitential prayers

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA confessional is the small room in a church where Christians, especially Roman Catholics, go to confess their sins.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA confessional speech or writing contains confessions. ⇒ The convictions rest solely on disputed witness and confessional statements. ⇒ Their first album was painfully frank to the point of being confessional.3. countable nounA confessional is a statement or meeting in which people confess things. ⇒ In regular group confessionals, the worst performers have to explain themselves to their peers.
