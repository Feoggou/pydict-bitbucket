degrade

(dɪˈɡreɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to reduce in worth, character, etc; disgrace; dishonour2. (diːˈɡreɪd) (transitive) to reduce in rank, status, or degree; remove from office; demote3. (transitive) to reduce in strength, quality, intensity, etc4. to reduce or be reduced by erosion or down-cutting, as a land surface or bed of a river. Compare aggrade5. chemistry to decompose or be decomposed into atoms or smaller molecules
Derived forms
degrader (deˈgrader) noun
Word origin
C14: from Late Latin dēgradāre, from Latin de- + gradus rank, degree

[formal] [V n] ⇒ ...the ability to meet human needs indefinitely without degrading the environment.3. ergative verb & verbIn science, if a substance degrades or if something degrades it, it changes chemically and decays or separates into different substances. [technical] [V] ⇒ This substance degrades rapidly in the soil. [V n] ⇒ ...the ability of these enzymes to degrade cellulose.
