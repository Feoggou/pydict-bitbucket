Fenian

(ˈfiːnɪən )
Definitions
noun1. (formerly) a member of an Irish revolutionary organization founded in the US in the 19th century to fight for an independent Ireland2. Irish mythology one of the Fianna3. derogatory, offensive an Irish Catholic or a person of Irish Catholic descentadjective4. of or relating to the Fenians
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

Fenianism (ˈFenianism) noun

Word Origin

C19: from Irish Gaelic fēinne, plural of fian band of warriors
