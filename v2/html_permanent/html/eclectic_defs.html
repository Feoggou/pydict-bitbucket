eclectic

(ɪˈklɛktɪk , ɛˈklɛk-)
Definitions
adjective1. (in art, philosophy, etc) selecting what seems best from various styles, doctrines, ideas, methods, etc2. composed of elements drawn from a variety of sources, styles, etcnoun3. a person who favours an eclectic approach, esp in art or philosophy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

eclectically (ecˈlectically) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Greek eklektikos, from eklegein to select, from legein to gather
