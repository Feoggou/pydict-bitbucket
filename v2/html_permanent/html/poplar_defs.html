poplar

(ˈpɒplə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any tree of the salicaceous genus Populus, of N temperate regions, having triangular leaves, flowers borne in catkins, and light soft wood. See also aspen, balsam poplar, Lombardy poplar, white poplar2. any of various trees resembling the true poplars, such as the tulip tree3. the wood of any of these trees

Word Origin

C14: from Old French poplier, from pouple, from Latin pōpulus
