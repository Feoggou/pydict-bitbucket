(ˈkɑːnɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounextensive slaughter, esp of human beings in battle
Word origin
C16: from French, from Italian carnaggio, from Medieval Latin carnāticum, from Latin carō flesh

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounCarnage is the violent killing of large numbers of people, especially in a war. [literary] ⇒ ...his strategy for stopping the carnage in Kosovo. ⇒ ...the carnage of motorway accidents.
