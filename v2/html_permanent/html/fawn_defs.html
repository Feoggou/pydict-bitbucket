fawn1

(fɔːn )
Definitions
noun1. a young deer of either sex aged under one year2. a. a light greyish-brown colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ a fawn raincoat3. See in fawnverb4. (of deer) to bear (young)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fawnlike (ˈfawnˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Old French faon, from Latin fētus offspring; see fetus

fawn2

(fɔːn )
Definitions
verb (intr; often foll by on or upon)1. to seek attention and admiration (from) by cringing and flattering2. (of animals, esp dogs) to try to please by a show of extreme friendliness and fondness (towards)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fawner (ˈfawner) nounfawning (ˈfawning) adjectivefawningly (ˈfawningly) adverbfawningness (ˈfawningness) noun

Word Origin

Old English fægnian to be glad, from fægen glad; see fain

fawn

Powered by COBUILD
(fɔːn ) for learners of English
Word forms: fawns , fawning , fawned
Definitions
1. colourFawn is a pale yellowish-brown colour. ⇒ ...a light fawn coat.2. countable nounA fawn is a very young deer. ⇒ The fawn ran to the top of the ridge.3. verbIf you say that someone fawns over a powerful or rich person, you disapprove of them because they flatter that person and like to be with him or her. [disapproval] [V + over/on/around] ⇒ People fawn over you when you're famous. [V-ing] ⇒ Nauseatingly fawning journalism that's all it is.
