opacity

(əʊˈpæsɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the state or quality of being opaque2. the degree to which something is opaque3. an opaque object or substance4. obscurity of meaning; unintelligibility5. physics, photography the ratio of the intensity of light incident on a medium, such as a photographic film, to that transmitted through the medium6. logic, philosophy the property of being an opaque context

Definitions
1. uncount nounOpacity is the quality of being difficult to see through. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ Opacity of the eye lens can be induced by deficiency of certain vitamins. ⇒ He insisted that the mineral content of the water determined the opacity.2. uncount nounIf you refer to something's opacity, you mean that it is difficult to understand. [formal] ⇒ Its dramatic nuances were often generalised to the point of opacity.
