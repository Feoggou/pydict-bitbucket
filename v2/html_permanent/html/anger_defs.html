anger

(ˈæŋɡə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a feeling of great annoyance or antagonism as the result of some real or supposed grievance; rage; wrathverb2. (transitive) to make angry; enrage
Word origin
C12: from Old Norse angr grief; related to Old English enge, Old High German engi narrow, Latin angere to strangle

Word forms: angers , angering , angered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAnger is the strong emotion that you feel when you think that someone has behaved in an unfair, cruel, or unacceptable way. ⇒ He cried with anger and frustration. [+ at] ⇒ Ellen felt both despair and anger at her mother.2. verbIf something angers you, it makes you feel angry. [V n] ⇒ The decision to allow more offshore oil drilling angered some Californians.
