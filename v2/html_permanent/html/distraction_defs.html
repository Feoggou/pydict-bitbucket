distraction

(dɪˈstrækʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act or an instance of distracting or the state of being distracted2. something that serves as a diversion or entertainment3. an interruption; obstacle to concentration4. mental turmoil or madness

(dɪstrækʃən )
Word forms: distractions
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA distraction is something that turns your attention away from something you want to concentrate on. ⇒ I feel this is getting to be a distraction from what I really want to do. ⇒ Total concentration is required with no distractions.2. countable nounA distraction is an activity which is intended to entertain and amuse you. ⇒ ...every conceivable distraction from show jumping to bouncy castles. ⇒ Their national distraction is going to the disco.3. →  drive someone to distraction
