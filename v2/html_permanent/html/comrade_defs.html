comrade

(ˈkɒmreɪd , -rɪd)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an associate or companion2. a fellow member of a political party, esp a fellow Communist or socialist
Derived forms
comradely (ˈcomradely) adjectivecomradeliness (ˈcomradeliness) nouncomradeˌship (ˈcomradeˌship) or comradery (ˈkɒmrædərɪ) noun
Word origin
C16: from French camarade, from Spanish camarada group of soldiers sharing a billet, from cámara room, from Latin; see camera, chamber

Word forms: comrades
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [usu poss N]Your comrades are your friends, especially friends that you share a difficult or dangerous situation with. [literary] ⇒ Unlike so many of his comrades he survived the war.2. title noun & vocative noun & countable nounSocialists or communists sometimes call each other comrade, especially in meetings. ⇒ The Party's authority, comrades, will be put to a serious test.
