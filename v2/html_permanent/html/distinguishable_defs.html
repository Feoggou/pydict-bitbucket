distinguishable

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪstɪŋgwɪʃəbəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If something is distinguishable from other things, it has a quality or feature which makes it possible for you to recognize it and see that it is different. [+ from] ⇒ ...features that make their products distinguishable from those of their rivals. ⇒ This is vintage port, and it is distinguishable by its deep red colour.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If something is distinguishable, you can see or hear it in conditions when it is difficult to see or hear anything. ⇒ He put his ear to the floor and heard angry shouts, but no words were distinguishable. ⇒ It was getting light and shapes were more distinguishable.
Example sentences containing 'distinguishable'
They had obviously changed name tags again, unaware that they were now distinguishable - at least for the remainder of the evening. Babson, Marian WEEKEND FOR MURDEROnce the almost daily image alterations settled a bit, they would be distinguishable. Anita Anderson SUMMER OF SECRETS
