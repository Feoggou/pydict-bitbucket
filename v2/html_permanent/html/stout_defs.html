stout

(staʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. solidly built or corpulent2. (prenominal) resolute or valiant ⇒ stout fellow3. strong, substantial, and robust4. See a stout heartnoun5. strong porter highly flavoured with malt

Derived Forms

stoutish (ˈstoutish) adjectivestoutly (ˈstoutly) adverbstoutness (ˈstoutness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French estout bold, of Germanic origin; related to Middle High German stolz proud, Middle Dutch stolt brave

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA stout person is rather fat. ⇒ He was a tall, stout man with gray hair.2. graded adjective & adjectiveStout shoes, branches, or other objects are thick and strong. ⇒ I hope you've both got stout shoes. ⇒ The old man picked up a stout stick that lay by his feet. ⇒ ...a stout oak door.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you use stout to describe someone's actions, attitudes, or beliefs, you approve of them because they are strong and determined. [approval] ⇒ He produced a stout defence of the car business. ⇒ The invasion was held up by unexpectedly stout resistance.stoutly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ She stoutly defended her husband during the trial. ⇒ ...stoutly anti-imperialist nations.4. mass nounStout is a strong dark-coloured beer. [British] ⇒ ...Guinness and other Irish stouts.
