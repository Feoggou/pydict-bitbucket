mastic

(ˈmæstɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: mastix an aromatic resin obtained from the mastic tree and used as an astringent and to make varnishes and lacquers2. See mastic tree3. any of several sticky putty-like substances used as a filler, adhesive, or seal in wood, plaster, or masonry4. a liquor flavoured with mastic gum

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Late Latin mastichum, from Latin, from Greek mastikhē resin used as chewing gum; from mastikhan to grind the teeth
