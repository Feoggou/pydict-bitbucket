throb

(θrɒb )
Definitions
verbWord forms: throbs, throbbing or throbbed (intransitive)1. to pulsate or beat repeatedly, esp with increased force ⇒ to throb with pain2. (of engines, drums, etc) to have a strong rhythmic vibration or beatnoun3. the act or an instance of throbbing, esp a rapid pulsation as of the heart ⇒ a throb of pleasure
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

throbbing (ˈthrobbing) adjectivethrobbingly (ˈthrobbingly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: perhaps of imitative origin

throb

Powered by COBUILD
(θrɒb ) for learners of English
Word forms: throbs , throbbing , throbbed
Definitions
1. verbIf part of your body throbs, you feel a series of strong and usually painful beats there. [V] ⇒ His head throbbed. [V with n] ⇒ Presently George's ankle began to throb with pain. [V-ing] ⇒ ...the throbbing tooth whose pain had woken her.Throb is also a noun. ⇒ The bruise on his stomach ached with a steady throb.2. verbIf something throbs, it vibrates and makes a steady noise. [literary] [V] ⇒ The engines throbbed. [V] ⇒ The music throbbed hypnotically. [V with n] ⇒ The gardens blazed with colour and throbbed with birdsong.Throb is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ Jake's head jerked up at the throb of the engine.
