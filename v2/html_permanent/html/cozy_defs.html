Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: ˈcozier or ˈcoziest1.  warm and comfortable; snug nounWord forms: plural ˈcozies2.  a knitted or padded cover placed over a teapot to keep the contents hot cozy up to Us Informal to try to ingratiate oneself, or make friends, with play it cozy Us Slang to act cautiously so as to avoid risk

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced American English Dictionary
regional note:   in BRIT, use cosy1. adjectiveA house or room that is cozy is comfortable and warm. ⇒ Downstairs there's a breakfast room and guests can relax in the cozy bar.2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are cozy, you are comfortable and warm. ⇒ They like to make sure their guests are comfortable and cozy.3. adjectiveYou use cozy to describe activities that are pleasant and friendly, and involve people who know each other well. ⇒ ...a cozy chat between friends.4. countable nounA cozy or a tea cozy is a soft knitted or fabric cover which you put over a teapot in order to keep the tea hot. ⇒ ...unusual miniature tea sets, elegant tea accessories, colorful cozies. ⇒ ...a whimsical tea cozy printed with a bright scene of the Tower of London.
