Word Frequency

let loose

Definitions
a. to set freeb. informal to make (a sound or remark) suddenly ⇒ he let loose a hollow laughc. informal to discharge (rounds) from a gun or guns ⇒ they let loose a couple of rounds of ammunition▷ See let
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'let loose'
Up the steps he plodded, and at every fourth or fifth step, he let loose a resounding belch. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODThe Bishop was a tall, thin, white-haired intellectual who had been cloistered in a college until he had been let loose into the diocese. Secombe, Fred GOODBYE CURATEWe were put back in the deserted glass corridors, let loose and ignored. Dexter Petley WHITE LIES
