foment

(fəˈmɛnt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to encourage or instigate (trouble, discord, etc); stir up2. medicine to apply heat and moisture to (a part of the body) to relieve pain and inflammation▶ USAGE Both foment and ferment can be used to talk about stirring up trouble: he was accused of fomenting/fermenting unrest. Only ferment can be used intransitively or as a noun: his anger continued to ferment (not foment); rural areas were unaffected by the ferment in the cities
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fomentation (ˌfəʊmɛnˈteɪʃən ) nounfomenter (foˈmenter) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin fōmentāre, from Latin fōmentum a poultice, ultimately from fovēre to foster
