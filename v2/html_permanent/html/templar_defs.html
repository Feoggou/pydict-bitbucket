Templar

(ˈtɛmplə )
Definitions
noun1. a member of a military religious order (Knights of the Temple of Solomon) founded by Crusaders in Jerusalem around 1118 to defend the Holy Sepulchre and Christian pilgrims; suppressed in 13122. (sometimes not capital) British a lawyer, esp a barrister, who lives or has chambers in the Inner or Middle Temple in London
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Medieval Latin templārius of the temple, from Latin templum temple1; first applied to the knightly order because their house was near the site of the Temple of Solomon
