delay

(dɪˈleɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to put off to a later time; defer2. (transitive) to slow up, hinder, or cause to be late; detain3. (intransitive) to be irresolute or put off doing something; procrastinate4. (intransitive) to linger; dawdlenoun5. the act or an instance of delaying or being delayed6. the interval between one event and another; lull; interlude
Derived forms
delayer (deˈlayer) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French delaier, from des- off + laier, variant of laissier to leave, from Latin laxāre to loosen, from laxus slack, lax

Word forms: delays , delaying , delayed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you delay doing something, you do not do it immediately or at the planned or expected time, but you leave it until later. [V n] ⇒ For sentimental reasons I wanted to delay my departure until June. [V n/-ing] ⇒ They had delayed having children, for the usual reason, to establish their careers. [V] ⇒ So don't delay, write in now for your chance of a free gift. → cancel2. verbTo delay someone or something means to make them late or to slow them down. [V n] ⇒ Can you delay him in some way? [V n] ⇒ Various set-backs and problems delayed production. [V n] ⇒ The passengers were delayed for an hour.3. verbIf you delay, you deliberately take longer than necessary to do something. [V] ⇒ If he delayed any longer, the sun would be up.4. variable nounIf there is a delay, something does not happen until later than planned or expected. ⇒ They claimed that such a delay wouldn't hurt anyone. ⇒ Although the tests have caused some delay, flights should be back to normal this morning.5. uncount nounDelay is a failure to do something immediately or in the required or usual time. ⇒ There is no time left for delay. ⇒ We'll send you a quote without delay.
