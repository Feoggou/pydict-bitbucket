successive

(səkˈsɛsɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. following another without interruption2. of or involving succession ⇒ a successive process

Derived Forms

successively (sucˈcessively) adverbsuccessiveness (sucˈcessiveness) noun

successive

Powered by COBUILD
(səksesɪv )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveSuccessive means happening or existing one after another without a break. ⇒ Jackson was the winner for a second successive year. ⇒ Britain was suffering from the failure of successive governments to co-ordinate a national transport policy.successively adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj-compar] ⇒ He successively won the British, European and World championships. ⇒ ...the successively higher levels of unemployment we have endured during the past three recessions.
Example sentences containing 'successive'
As each successive baby had been born, she herself had taken on its rearing. Spellman, Cathy Cash PAINT THE WIND
