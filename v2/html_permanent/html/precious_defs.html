precious

(ˈprɛʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. beloved; dear; cherished2. very costly or valuable3. held in high esteem, esp in moral or spiritual matters4. very fastidious or affected, as in speech, manners, etc5. informal worthless ⇒ you and your precious ideas!adverb6. informal (intensifier) ⇒ there's precious little left

Derived Forms

preciously (ˈpreciously) adverbpreciousness (ˈpreciousness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French precios, from Latin pretiōsus valuable, from pretium price, value

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something such as a resource is precious, you mean that it is valuable and should not be wasted or used badly. ⇒ After four months in foreign parts, every hour at home was precious. ⇒ A family break allows you to spend precious time together. ⇒ Water is becoming an increasingly precious resource.2. graded adjective & adjectivePrecious objects and materials are worth a lot of money because they are rare. ⇒ ...jewellery and precious objects belonging to her mother.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is precious to you, you regard it as important and do not want to lose it. [+ to] ⇒ Her family's support is particularly precious to Josie. ⇒ Mary left her most precious possession–a small bookcase–to her niece.4. adjective [ADJ n]People sometimes use precious to emphasize their dislike for things which other people think are important. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ You don't care about anything but yourself and your precious face.5. graded adjectiveIf you describe someone as precious, you mean that they behave in a formal and unnatural way. [disapproval] affected ⇒ Actors, he decided, were too precious and neurotic.6. →  precious little/precious few
