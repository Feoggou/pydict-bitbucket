puerile

(ˈpjʊəraɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. exhibiting silliness; immature; trivial2. of or characteristic of a child

Derived Forms

puerilely (ˈpuerilely) adverbpuerility (pjʊəˈrɪlɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin puerīlis childish, from puer a boy

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as puerile, you mean that they are silly and childish. [disapproval] ⇒ Concert organisers branded the group's actions as puerile. ⇒ The story is simple, even puerile. ⇒ ...puerile, schoolboy humour.
Example sentences containing 'puerile'
He's what the tabloids call a playboy: which translated means thick, lazy and puerile.
