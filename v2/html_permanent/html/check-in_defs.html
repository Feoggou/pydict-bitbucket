check in

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (intransitive) to record one's arrival, as at a hotel or for work; sign in or report2. (transitive) to register the arrival of (passengers, etc)noun check-in3. a. the formal registration of arrival, as at an airport or a hotelb. (as modifier) ⇒ check-in time4. the place where one registers arrival at an airport, etc

(ˈtʃɛkɪn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the process of registration before boarding a flight ⇒ You missed the check-in. You will just have to fly tomorrow.modifier2. for or at check-in ⇒ a check-in queue, ⇒ check-in staff, ⇒ the check-in counter, ⇒ our check-in policy

check in

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbWhen you check in or check into a hotel or clinic, or if someone checks you in, you arrive and go through the necessary procedures before you stay there. [V P] ⇒ I'll ring the hotel. I'll tell them we'll check in tomorrow. [V P n] ⇒ He has checked into an alcohol treatment centre. [V n P] ⇒ Check us in at the hotel and wait for my call. [Also V n P n]2. phrasal verbWhen you check in at an airport, you arrive and show your ticket before going on a flight. [V P] ⇒ He had checked in at Amsterdam's Schiphol airport for a flight to Manchester.3. → see also check-in, check [sense 7] →  check
