steak

(steɪk )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. See beefsteak2. any of various cuts of beef of varying quality, used for braising, stewing, etc3. a thick slice of pork, veal, etc, or of a large fish, esp cod or salmon4. minced meat prepared in the same way as steak ⇒ hamburger steak

Word Origin

C15: from Old Norse steik roast; related to steikja to roast on a spit; see stick1

Word forms: steaks
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA steak is a large flat piece of beef without much fat on it. You cook it by grilling or frying it.2. → see also rump steak, T-bone steak3. uncount nounSteak is beef that is used for making stews. It is often cut into cubes to be sold. ⇒ ...steak and kidney pie.4. → see also stewing steak5. countable noun [usu n N]A fish steak is a large piece of fish that contains few bones. ⇒ ...fresh salmon steaks.
