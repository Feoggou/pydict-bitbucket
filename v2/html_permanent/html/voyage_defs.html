voyage

(ˈvɔɪɪdʒ )
Definitions
noun1. a journey, travel, or passage, esp one to a distant land or by sea or air2. obsolete an ambitious projectverb3. to travel over or traverse (something) ⇒ we will voyage to Africa
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

voyager (ˈvoyager) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French veiage, from Latin viāticum provision for travelling, from viāticus concerning a journey, from via a way

voyage

Powered by COBUILD
(vɔɪɪdʒ ) for learners of English
Word forms: voyages , voyaging , voyaged
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]A voyage is a long journey on a ship or in a spacecraft. ⇒ He aims to follow Columbus's voyage to the West Indies. ⇒ ...the first space shuttle voyage to be devoted entirely to astronomy. → journey2. verbTo voyage to a place means to travel there, especially by sea. [formal] [V prep/adv] ⇒ The Greenpeace flagship is voyaging through the Arctic cold of the Barents Sea.voyager Word forms: voyagers countable noun [+ to] ⇒ ...fifteenth-century voyagers to the lands now called America and the Caribbean.voyaging uncount noun ⇒ Our boat would not have been appropriate for ocean voyaging.
