disturb

(dɪˈstɜːb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to intrude on; interrupt2. to destroy or interrupt the quietness or peace of3. to disarrange; muddle4. (often passive) to upset or agitate; trouble ⇒ I am disturbed at your bad news5. to inconvenience; put out ⇒ don't disturb yourself on my account
Derived forms
disturber (disˈturber) noun
Word origin
C13: from Latin disturbāre, from dis-1 + turbāre to confuse

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you disturb someone, you interrupt what they are doing and upset them. [V n] ⇒ I hope I'm not disturbing you. [V n] ⇒ Find a quiet, warm, comfortable room where you won't be disturbed.2. verbIf something disturbs you, it makes you feel upset or worried. [V n] ⇒ I dream about him, dreams so vivid that they disturb me for days. [V n] ⇒ He had been disturbed by the news of the attack on Hector Coyne.3. verbIf something is disturbed, its position or shape is changed. [be V-ed] ⇒ He'd placed his notes in the brown envelope. They hadn't been disturbed. [V n] ⇒ She patted Mona, taking care not to disturb her costume.4. verbIf something disturbs a situation or atmosphere, it spoils it or causes trouble. [V n] ⇒ Neither Baker nor Levy seemed eager to disturb the cordial atmosphere by discussing more sensitive issues. [V n] ⇒ What could possibly disturb such tranquility?5. →  to disturb the peace
