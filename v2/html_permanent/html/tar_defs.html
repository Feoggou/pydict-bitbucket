tar1

(tɑː )
Definitions
noun1. any of various dark viscid substances obtained by the destructive distillation of organic matter such as coal, wood, or peat2. another name for coal tarverbWord forms: tars, tarring or tarred (transitive)3. to coat with tar4. See tar and feather5. See tarred with the same brush
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tarry (ˈtarry) adjectivetarriness (ˈtarriness) noun

Word Origin

Old English teoru; related to Old Frisian tera, Old Norse tjara, Middle Low German tere tar, Gothic triu tree

tar

Powered by COBUILD
(tɑːʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: tars , tarring , tarred
Definitions
1. uncount nounTar is a thick black sticky substance that is used especially for making roads. ⇒ The oil has hardened to tar. ⇒ They drove across the river to New Hampshire on a hot tar road.2. uncount nounTar is one of the poisonous substances contained in tobacco.3. →  tarred with the same brush4. → See also tarred
