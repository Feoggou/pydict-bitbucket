rectifier

(ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪə )
Definitions
noun1. an electronic device, such as a semiconductor diode or valve, that converts an alternating current to a direct current by suppression or inversion of alternate half-cycles2. chemistry an apparatus for condensing a hot vapour to a liquid in distillation; condenser3. a thing or person that rectifies

Definitions
noun(Chemical Engineering: Distillation) A rectifier is the top part of a distillation column (= a tall vessel for distillation), in which hot vapor condenses. ⇒ The rectifier is the upper section of a distillation column and is where the higher concentration of lighter molecules are located. ⇒ In the crude distillation unit, the distillation column has two sections; the rectifier is at the top above the flash zone. ⇒ A rectifier is the top part of a distillation column, in which hot vapor condenses.
