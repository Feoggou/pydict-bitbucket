recapitulate

(ˌriːkəˈpɪtjʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb1. to restate the main points of (an argument, speech, etc); summarize2. (transitive) (of an animal) to repeat (stages of its evolutionary development) during the embryonic stages of its life3. to repeat at some point during a piece of music (material used earlier in the same work)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recapitulative (ˌrecaˈpitulative) or recapitulatory (ˌrecaˈpitulatory) adjective

Word Origin

C16: from Late Latin recapitulāre, literally: to put back under headings; see capitulate

Word forms: recapitulates , recapitulating , recapitulated
Definitions
verbYou can say that you are going to recapitulate the main points of an explanation, argument, or description when you want to draw attention to the fact that you are going to repeat the most important points as a summary. [V n] ⇒ Let's just recapitulate the essential points. [V] ⇒ It will shortly be put up for sale under the terms already communicated to you, which, to recapitulate, call for a very minimum of publicity.recapitulation (riːkəpɪtʃʊleɪʃən ) singular noun [+ of] ⇒ Chapter 9 provides a recapitulation of the material already presented.
