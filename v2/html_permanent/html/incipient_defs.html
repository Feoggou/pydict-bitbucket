incipient

(ɪnˈsɪpɪənt )
Definitions
adjectivejust starting to be or happen; beginning
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

incipience (inˈcipience) or incipiency (inˈcipiency) nounincipiently (inˈcipiently) adverb

Word Origin

C17: from Latin incipiēns, from incipere to begin, take in hand, from in-2 + capere to take

Definitions
adjective [ADJ n]An incipient situation or quality is one that is starting to happen or develop. [formal] ⇒ ...an incipient economic recovery. ⇒ There were signs of incipient panic.
