immolate

(ˈɪməʊˌleɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to kill or offer as a sacrifice, esp by fire2. literary to sacrifice (something highly valued)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

immolation (ˌimmoˈlation) nounimmolator (ˈimmoˌlator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin immolāre to sprinkle an offering with sacrificial meal, sacrifice, from im- (in) + mola spelt grain; see mill1
Example sentences containing 'immolate'
The marks met and sparked on the man's chest, became leaping golden flames, and a second later exploded to immolate the entire body. Garth Nix ABHORSEN

sacrifice, kill
