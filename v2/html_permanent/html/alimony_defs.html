alimony

(ˈælɪmənɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounlaw (formerly) an allowance paid under a court order by one spouse to another when they are separated but not divorced. See also maintenance
Word origin
C17: from Latin alimōnia sustenance, from alere to nourish

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounAlimony is money that a court of law orders someone to pay regularly to their former wife or husband after they have got divorced. Compare palimony. ⇒ A great deal of Jeff's money went in alimony to his three former wives.
