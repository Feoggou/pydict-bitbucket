Definitions

adjective1. liquefied; melted ⇒ molten lead2. made by having been melted ⇒ molten castsverb3. the past participle of melt

molten

Powered by COBUILD
(moʊltən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]Molten rock, metal, or glass has been heated to a very high temperature and has become a hot, thick liquid. ⇒ The molten metal is poured into the mould.
Example sentences containing 'molten'
For two pins May would have poured molten liver over that fluffy white head of hers. Richard Francis PROSPECT HILLPlates had moved inside Anne, chasms had opened up and this boiling rage of molten rock was seething to the surface. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSThe hurt of all these peoples and things, and everything, flowed into him like a river of molten stone. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODShards of blue burning within a silver that was smoky, molten. Elizabeth Lowell REMEMBER SUMMER
