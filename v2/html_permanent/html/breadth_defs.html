breadth

(brɛdθ , brɛtθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the linear extent or measurement of something from side to side; width2. a piece of fabric having a standard or definite width3. distance, extent, size, or dimension4. openness and lack of restriction, esp of viewpoint or interest; liberality
Word origin
C16: from obsolete brēde (from Old English brǣdu, from brād broad) + -th1; related to Gothic braidei, Old High German breitī

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounThe breadth of something is the distance between its two sides. [+ of] ⇒ The breadth of the whole camp was 400 paces.2. uncount nounThe breadth of something is its quality of consisting of or involving many different things. [+ of] ⇒ Older people have a tremendous breadth of experience. [+ of] ⇒ His breadth of knowledge filled me with admiration.3. →  the length and breadth of4. → see also hair's breadth
