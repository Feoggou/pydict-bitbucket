arrogate

(ˈærəˌɡeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to claim or appropriate for oneself presumptuously or without justification2. (transitive) to attribute or assign to another without justification
Derived forms
ˌarroˈgation nounarrogative (əˈrɒɡətɪv ) adjectiveˈarroˌgator noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin arrogāre, from rogāre to ask

(ærəgeɪt )
Word forms: arrogates , arrogating , arrogated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone arrogates to themselves something such as a responsibility or privilege, they claim or take it even though they have no right to do so. [formal, disapproval] [V + to] ⇒ The assembly arrogated to itself the right to make changes. [V n + to] ⇒ He arrogated the privilege to himself alone.
