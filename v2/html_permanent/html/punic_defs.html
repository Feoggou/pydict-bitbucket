Punic

(ˈpjuːnɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to ancient Carthage or the Carthaginians2. characteristic of the treachery of the Carthaginiansnoun3. the language of the ancient Carthaginians; a late form of Phoenician

Word Origin

C15: from Latin Pūnicus, variant of Poenicus Carthaginian, from Greek Phoinix
Example sentences containing 'Punic'
It was what we call in intelligence jargon a `honey trap", a ploy that must be at least as old as the Punic Wars. Collins, Larry BLACK EAGLES
