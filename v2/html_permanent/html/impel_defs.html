impel

(ɪmˈpɛl )
Definitions
verb -pels, -pelling or -pelled (transitive)1. to urge or force (a person) to an action; constrain or motivate2. to push, drive, or force into motion
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impellent (imˈpellent) noun, adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Latin impellere to push against, drive forward, from im- (in) + pellere to drive, push, strike
