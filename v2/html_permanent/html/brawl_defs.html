brawl1

(brɔːl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a loud disagreement or fight2. US slang an uproarious partyverb (intransitive)3. to quarrel or fight noisily; squabble4. (esp of water) to flow noisily
Derived forms
brawler (ˈbrawler) nounbrawling (ˈbrawling) noun, adjective
Word origin
C14: probably related to Dutch brallen to boast, behave aggressively

brawl

Powered by COBUILD
(brɔːl )
Word forms: brawls , brawling , brawled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA brawl is a rough or violent fight. punch-up ⇒ He had been in a drunken street brawl.2. verbIf someone brawls, they fight in a very rough or violent way. [V + with] ⇒ He was suspended for a year from University after brawling with police over a speeding ticket. [V] ⇒ Two gangs of youths brawled on the dance floor of the ferry.brawling uncount noun ⇒ The brawling between the England fans and locals last night went on for several hours.
Example sentences containing 'brawl'
I learned many years later that my father died in a brawl with a drunken patron in his shop. Robert Silverberg KING OF DREAMS
