(ˈbeɪsɪks)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. the fundamental principles or elements of something ⇒ supplies of basics such as bread and milk, ⇒ items that are the basics of a stylish wardrobe, ⇒ For a year I learnt the basics of journalism., ⇒ They will concentrate on teaching the basics of reading, writing, and arithmetic., ⇒ He did not master the basics of reading until he was eight.2. See to get back to basics

(beɪsɪks )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. plural nounThe basics of something are its simplest, most important elements, ideas, or principles, in contrast to more complicated or detailed ones. [+ of] ⇒ They will concentrate on teaching the basics of reading, writing and arithmetic. ⇒ A strong community cannot be built until the basics are in place. ⇒ Let's get down to basics and stop horsing around.2. plural nounBasics are things such as simple food, clothes, or equipment that people need in order to live or to deal with a particular situation. essentials ⇒ ...supplies of basics such as bread and milk. [+ of] ⇒ ...items that are the basics of a stylish wardrobe.3. →  back to basics
