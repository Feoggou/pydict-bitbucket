Definitions
adverb [with neg] [ADV adj/adv, ADV n]Half is sometimes used in negative statements, with a positive meaning, to emphasize a particular fact or quality. For example, if you say 'he isn't half lucky', you mean that he is very lucky. [British, informal, emphasis] ⇒ You don't half sound confident. ⇒ I didn't half get into trouble. ⇒ She eventually decided the acting profession wasn't half bad. ⇒ My kick wasn't half a bad effort for an old man. ⇒ 'There'd been a tremendous amount of poverty around and presumably this made some impact then.'—'Oh not half.' →  half
