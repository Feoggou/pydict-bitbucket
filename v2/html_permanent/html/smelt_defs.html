smelt1

(smɛlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to extract (a metal) from (an ore) by heating

Word Origin

C15: from Middle Low German, Middle Dutch smelten; related to Old High German smelzan to melt

Definitions

nounWord forms: plural smelt or smeltsany marine or freshwater salmonoid food fish of the family Osmeridae, such as Osmerus eperlanus of Europe, having a long silvery body and occurring in temperate and cold northern waters

Word Origin

Old English smylt; related to Dutch, Danish smelt, Norwegian smelta, German Schmelz

Word forms: smelts , smelting , smelted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. Smelt is a past tense and past participle of smell. [mainly British]2. verbTo smelt a substance containing metal means to process it by heating it until it melts, so that the metal is extracted and changed chemically. [V n] ⇒ Darby was looking for a way to improve iron when he hit upon the idea of smelting it with coke instead of charcoal.
