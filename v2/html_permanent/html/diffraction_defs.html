diffraction

(dɪˈfrækʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. physics a deviation in the direction of a wave at the edge of an obstacle in its path2. any phenomenon caused by diffraction and interference of light, such as the formation of light and dark fringes by the passage of light through a small aperture3. deflection of sound waves caused by an obstacle or by nonhomogeneity of a medium
Word origin
C17: from New Latin diffractiō a breaking to pieces, from Latin diffringere to shatter, from dis- apart + frangere to break
