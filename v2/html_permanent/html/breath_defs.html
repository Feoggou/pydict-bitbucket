breath

(brɛθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the intake and expulsion of air during respiration2. the air inhaled or exhaled during respiration3. a single respiration or inhalation of air, etc4. the vapour, heat, or odour of exhaled air ⇒ his breath on the window melted the frost5. a slight gust of air6. a short pause or rest ⇒ take a breath for five minutes7. a brief time ⇒ it was done in a breath8. a suggestion or slight evidence; suspicion ⇒ a breath of scandal9. a whisper or soft sound10. life, energy, or vitality ⇒ the breath of new industry11. phonetics the passage of air through the completely open glottis without vibration of the vocal cords, as in exhaling or pronouncing fricatives such as (f) or (h) or stops such as (p) or (k). Compare voice (sense 12)12. See a breath of fresh air13. See catch one's breath14. See hold one's breath15. See in the same breath16. See out of breath17. See save one's breath18. See take one's breath away19. See under one's breath
Word origin
Old English brǣth; related to brǣdan to burn, Old High German brādam heat, breath

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable noun [oft poss N]Your breath is the air that you let out through your mouth when you breathe. If someone has bad breath, their breath smells unpleasant. ⇒ I could smell the whisky on his breath. ⇒ Smoking causes bad breath.2. variable nounWhen you take a breath, you breathe in once. ⇒ He took a deep breath, and began to climb the stairs. ⇒ Gasping for breath, she leaned against the door. ⇒ He spoke for one and a half hours and barely paused for breath.3. singular noun [usu with brd-neg, usu N of n]If you say that there is not a breath of wind or air, you are emphasizing that there is no wind and the air is very still. [written, emphasis] ⇒ Not even a breath of wind stirred the pine branches.4. singular noun [N of n]A breath of something, is a small amount of it. [written] ⇒ It was left to Martina to add a breath of common sense to the proceedings.5. →  for a breath of fresh air/for a breath of air6. →  be a breath of fresh air7. →  get one's breath back8. →  catch one's breath9. →  to catch your breath10. →  to draw breath11. →  to hold your breath12. →  hold one's breath13. →  not hold one's breath14. →  last breath15. →  be out of breath16. →  in the same breath17. →  short of breath18. →  take one's breath away19. →  under one's breath20. →  to waste your breath21. → with bated breath22. → to fight for breath
