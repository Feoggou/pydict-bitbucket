contentious

(kənˈtɛnʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. tending to argue or quarrel2. causing or characterized by dispute; controversial3. law relating to a cause or legal business that is contested, esp a probate matter
Derived forms
contentiously (conˈtentiously) adverbcontentiousness (conˈtentiousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA contentious issue causes a lot of disagreement or arguments. [formal] ⇒ Sanctions are expected to be among the most contentious issues. ⇒ ...a country where land prices are politically contentious.contentiously graded adverb ⇒ This time he was contentiously omitted from the team.2. graded adjectiveA contentious person seems to like arguing and disagreeing with other people. [formal] argumentative ⇒ Rodney was a cheerful, elegant and gregarious if rather contentious man.
