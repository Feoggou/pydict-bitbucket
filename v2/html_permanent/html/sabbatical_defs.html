(səˈbætɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective also: Sabbatic1. of, relating to, or appropriate to the Sabbath as a day of rest and religious observancenoun2. short for sabbatical year

sabbatical

(səˈbætɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. denoting a period of leave granted to university staff, teachers, etc, esp approximately every seventh year ⇒ a sabbatical year, ⇒ sabbatical leave2. denoting a post that renders the holder eligible for such leavenoun3. any sabbatical period

Word Origin

C16: from Greek sabbatikos; see Sabbath

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable noun [oft on N]A sabbatical is a period of time during which someone such as a university teacher can leave their ordinary work and travel or study. ⇒ He took a year's sabbatical from the Foreign Office. ⇒ He's been on sabbatical writing a novel.
