torrent

(ˈtɒrənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a fast, voluminous, or violent stream of water or other liquid2. an overwhelming flow of thoughts, words, sound, etc3. computing a file that controls the transfer of data in a BitTorrent system. See BitTorrentadjective4. rare like or relating to a torrent

Word Origin

C17: from French, from Latin torrēns (noun), from torrēns (adjective) burning, from torrēre to burn

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA torrent is a lot of water falling or flowing rapidly or violently. [+ of] ⇒ Torrents of water gushed into the reservoir. ⇒ The rain came down in torrents, and we could see nothing. ⇒ The trip involved crossing a raging torrent.2. countable nounA torrent of abuse or questions is a lot of abuse or questions directed continuously at someone. [+ of] ⇒ He turned round and directed a torrent of abuse at me. ⇒ ...a £45,000 offer which prompted a torrent of criticism in the media.
