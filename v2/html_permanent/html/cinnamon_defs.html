cinnamon

(ˈsɪnəmən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a tropical Asian lauraceous tree, Cinnamomum zeylanicum, having aromatic yellowish-brown bark2. the spice obtained from the bark of this tree, used for flavouring food and drink3. See Saigon cinnamon4. any of several similar or related trees or their bark. See cassia (sense 2)5. a. a light yellowish brownb. (as modifier) ⇒ a cinnamon coat
Derived forms
cinnamic (cinˈnamic) or cinnamonic (ˌsɪnəˈmɒnɪk ) adjective
Word origin
C15: from Old French cinnamome, via Latin and Greek, from Hebrew qinnamown
