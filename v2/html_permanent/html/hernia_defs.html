hernia

(ˈhɜːnɪə )
Definitions
noun plural -nias or -niae (-nɪˌiː)the projection of an organ or part through the lining of the cavity in which it is normally situated, esp the protrusion of intestine through the front wall of the abdominal cavity. It is caused by muscular strain, injury, etc. Also called: rupture
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hernial (ˈhernial) adjectiveherniated (ˈherniˌated) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Latin
