marjoram

(ˈmɑːdʒərəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Also called: sweet marjoram an aromatic Mediterranean plant, Origanum (or Marjorana) hortensis, with small pale purple flowers and sweet-scented leaves, used for seasoning food and in salads: family Lamiaceae (labiates)2. Also called: wild marjoram, pot marjoram, origan a similar and related European plant, Origanum vulgare. See also oregano, origanum

Word Origin

C14: via Old French majorane, from Medieval Latin marjorana
