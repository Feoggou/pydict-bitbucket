stitch up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to join or mend by means of stitches or sutures2. slanga. to incriminate (someone) on a false charge by manufacturing evidenceb. to betray, cheat, or defraud3. slang to prearrange (something) in a clandestine mannernoun stitch-up4. slang a matter that has been prearranged clandestinely

stitch up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbTo stitch someone up means to trick them so that they are put in a difficult or unpleasant situation, especially one where they are blamed for something they have not done. [British, informal] [V n P] ⇒ He claimed that a police officer had threatened to stitch him up and send him to prison. [Also V P n]2. phrasal verbTo stitch up an agreement, especially a complicated agreement between several people, means to arrange it. [mainly British, informal] [V P n] ⇒ Shiraz has stitched up major deals all over the world to boost sales.3. → see stitch [sense 5] →  stitch
