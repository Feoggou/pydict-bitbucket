exquisite

(ɪkˈskwɪzɪt , ˈɛkskwɪzɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. possessing qualities of unusual delicacy and fine craftsmanship ⇒ jewels in an exquisite setting2. extremely beautiful and pleasing ⇒ an exquisite face3. outstanding or excellent ⇒ an exquisite victory4. sensitive; discriminating ⇒ exquisite taste5. fastidious and refined6. intense or sharp in feeling ⇒ exquisite pleasure, ⇒ exquisite painnoun7. obsolete a dandy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

exquisitely (exˈquisitely) adverbexquisiteness (exˈquisiteness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin exquīsītus excellent, from exquīrere to search out, from quaerere to seek

exquisite

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪkskwɪzɪt , ekskwɪzɪt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is exquisite is extremely beautiful or pleasant, especially in a delicate way. ⇒ The Indians brought in exquisite beadwork to sell. ⇒ Mr Zhang's photography is exquisite. ⇒ ...her exquisite manners.exquisitely graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ ...exquisitely crafted dolls' houses. ⇒ ...an exquisitely beautiful young woman.2. adjective [ADJ n]Exquisite is used to emphasize that a feeling or quality is very great or intense. [literary, emphasis] ⇒ The words issuing from her lips gave exquisite pleasure as they flowed over him. ⇒ She peeled it with exquisite care.
