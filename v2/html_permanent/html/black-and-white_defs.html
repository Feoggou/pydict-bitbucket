black and white

(blæk ənd waɪt) or black-and-white
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of an image) not in colour ⇒ a black-and-white photo of the two of us together, ⇒ old black and white film footage2. only transmitting images in black and white ⇒ They watched the ceremony on a flickering black-and-white television set.3. in which it is possible to draw clear boundaries or make absolute judgements ⇒ But this isn't a simple black and white affair, Marianne.noun4. See to be in black and white5. See in black and white6. See to see something in black and white▶ USAGE The abbreviation for black and white is B &amp; W or B/W

black-and-white

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a photograph, picture, sketch, etc, in black, white, and shades of grey rather than in colourb. (as modifier) ⇒ black-and-white film2. the neutral tones of black, white, and intermediate shades of grey. Compare colour (sense 2)3. See in black and white

black and white

Powered by COBUILD
also black-and-white
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. colourIn a black and white photograph or film, everything is shown in black, white, and grey. ⇒ ...a black-and-white photo of the two of us together. ⇒ ...old black and white film footage. ⇒ The pictures were in black and white.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A black and white television set shows only black-and-white pictures.3. adjectiveA black and white issue or situation is one which involves issues which seem simple and therefore easy to make decisions about. ⇒ But this isn't a simple black and white affair, Marianne. ⇒ She saw things in black and white.4. →  be in black and white
