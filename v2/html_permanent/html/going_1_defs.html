going

(ˈɡəʊɪŋ )
Definitions
noun1. a departure or farewell2. the condition of a surface such as a road or field with regard to walking, riding, etc ⇒ muddy going3. informal speed, progress, etc ⇒ we made good going on the tripadjective4. thriving (esp in the phrase a going concern)5. current or accepted, as from past negotiations or commercial operation ⇒ the going rate for electricians, ⇒ the going value of the firm6. (postpositive) available ⇒ the best going7. See going, going, gone!

going

Powered by COBUILD
(goʊɪŋ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. →  be going to2. →  be going to3. uncount noun [adj N]You use the going to talk about how easy or difficult it is to do something. You can also say that something is, for example, hard going or tough going. ⇒ He has her support to fall back on when the going gets tough. ⇒ Though the talks had been hard going at the start, they had become more friendly.4. uncount nounIn horse racing and horse riding, when you talk about the going, you are talking about the condition of the surface the horses are running on. ⇒ The going was soft; some horses found it hard work.5. adjective [ADJ n]The going rate or the going salary is the usual amount of money that you expect to pay or receive for something. ⇒ She says that's the going rate for a house this big. ⇒ That's about half the going price on world oil markets.6. → See also go17. →  have sth/a lot going for you8. →  get going9. →  while the going is good10. →  keep going11. →  keep going12. →  good/not bad going13. →  to be going on with14. →  going on15. → See also comings and goings16. → going concern
