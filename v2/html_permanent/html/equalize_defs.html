equalize

or equalise (ˈiːkwəˌlaɪz )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to make equal or uniform; regularize2. (intransitive) (in sports) to reach the same score as one's opponent or opponents
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

equalization (ˌequaliˈzation) or equalisation (ˌequaliˈsation) noun

Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, also use equalise1. verbTo equalize a situation means to give everyone the same rights or opportunities, for example in education, wealth, or social status. [V n] ⇒ Women in Indonesia have secured modern divorce laws that equalize the rights of husbands and wives. [V n] ⇒ Such measures are needed to equalize wage rates between countries.equalization (iːkwəlaɪzeɪʃən ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the equalization of parenting responsibilities between men and women.2. verbIn sports such as football, if a player equalizes, he or she scores a goal that makes the scores of the two teams equal. [British] [V] ⇒ Keegan equalized with only 16 minutes remaining. [V n] ⇒ They showed little sign of equalising the Portsmouth striker's glorious 55th-minute shot.
