apathy

(ˈæpəθɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. absence of interest in or enthusiasm for things generally considered interesting or moving2. absence of emotion
Word origin
C17: from Latin, from Greek apatheia, from apathēs without feeling, from a-1 + pathos feeling

apathy

Powered by COBUILD
(æpəθi )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounYou can use apathy to talk about someone's state of mind if you are criticizing them because they do not seem to be interested in or enthusiastic about anything. [disapproval] ⇒ They told me about isolation and public apathy. ⇒ ...the political apathy and emotional uncertainty of young Americans.
Example sentences containing 'apathy'
This is not, in my opinion, an unhealthy development: I should be far more worried if she were sunk in apathy. West, Charles STAGE FRIGHTNellie Jean was a regular visitor but not even she could rouse Roderick from his apathy. Fraser, Christine Marion NOBLE BEGINNNINGSNow, apathy had overtaken anger and her life was in the doldrums.
