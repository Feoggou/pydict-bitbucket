plausible

(ˈplɔːzəbəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. apparently reasonable, valid, truthful, etc ⇒ a plausible excuse2. apparently trustworthy or believable ⇒ a plausible speaker

Derived Forms

ˌplausibility (ˌplausiˈbility) or plausibleness (ˈplausibleness) nounplausibly (ˈplausibly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin plausibilis worthy of applause, from plaudere to applaud

plausible

Powered by COBUILD
(plɔːzɪbəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveAn explanation or statement that is plausible seems likely to be true or valid. OPPOSITE: implausible ⇒ A more plausible explanation would seem to be that people are fed up with the Conservative government. ⇒ That explanation seems entirely plausible to me.plausibly (plɔːzɪbli ) graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Having bluffed his way in without paying, he could not plausibly demand his money back.plausibility (plɔːzɪbɪlɪti ) uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the plausibility of the theory.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is plausible, you mean that they seem to be telling the truth and to be sincere and honest. ⇒ He was so plausible that he conned everybody.
