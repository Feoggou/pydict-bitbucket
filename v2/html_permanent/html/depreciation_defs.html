depreciation

(dɪˌpriːʃɪˈeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. accountinga. the reduction in value of a fixed asset due to use, obsolescence, etcb. the amount deducted from gross profit to allow for such reduction in value2. accounting a modified amount permitted for purposes of tax deduction3. the act or an instance of depreciating or belittling; disparagement4. a decrease in the exchange value of currency against gold or other currencies brought about by excess supply of that currency under conditions of fluctuating exchange rates. Compare devaluation (sense 1)
