valet

(ˈvælɪt , ˈvæleɪ )
Definitions
noun1. a manservant who acts as personal attendant to his employer, looking after his clothing, serving his meals, etc. French name: valet de chambre2. a manservant who attends to the requirements of patrons in a hotel, passengers on board ship, etc; stewardverb -ets, -eting or -eted3. to act as a valet for (a person)4. (transitive) to clean the bodywork and interior of (a car) as a professional service
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old French vaslet page, from Medieval Latin vassus servant; see vassal
