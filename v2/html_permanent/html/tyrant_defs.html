tyrant

(ˈtaɪrənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person who governs oppressively, unjustly, and arbitrarily; despot2. any person who exercises authority in a tyrannical manner3. anything that exercises tyrannical influence4. (esp in ancient Greece) a ruler whose authority lacked the sanction of law or custom; usurper

Word Origin

C13: from Old French tyrant, from Latin tyrannus, from Greek turannos
