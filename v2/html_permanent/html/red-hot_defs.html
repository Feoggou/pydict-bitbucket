red-hot

Definitions
adjective1. (esp of metal) heated to the temperature at which it glows red ⇒ iron is red-hot at about 500°C2. extremely hot ⇒ the stove is red-hot, so don't touch it3. keen, excited, or eager; enthusiastic4. furious; violent ⇒ red-hot anger5. very recent or topical ⇒ red-hot information6. Australian slang extreme, unreasonable, or unfair ⇒ the charges are red-hot

red-hot

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Red-hot metal or rock has been heated to such a high temperature that it has turned red. ⇒ ...red-hot iron.2. adjectiveA red-hot object is too hot to be touched safely. ⇒ In the main rooms red-hot radiators were left exposed.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Red-hot is used to describe a person or thing that is very popular, especially someone who is very good at what they do or something that is new and exciting. [journalism] ⇒ Some traders are already stacking the red-hot book on their shelves. ⇒ ...red-hot guitarist David Grissom.4. adjective [usu ADJ n]If someone or something is described as red-hot, they show a lot of passion, enthusiasm, or excitement. [informal] ⇒ ...a red-hot sex-life. ⇒ ...a red-hot fury she had never realized she possessed.5. adjective [ADJ n]The red-hot favourite in a race or contest is a person or team that is strongly expected to win. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ His team are red-hot favourites.
