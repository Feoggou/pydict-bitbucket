theatrical

(θɪˈætrɪkəl ) or theatric (θɪˈætrɪk)
Definitions
adjective1. of or relating to the theatre or dramatic performances2. exaggerated and affected in manner or behaviour; histrionic
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

theatricality (theˌatriˈcality) or theatricalness (theˈatricalness) nountheatrically (theˈatrically) adverb

theatrical

Powered by COBUILD
(θiætrɪkəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: theatricals
Definitions
1. adjective [ADJ n]Theatrical means relating to the theatre. ⇒ These are the prizes given for the most outstanding British theatrical performances of the year. ⇒ ...major theatrical productions.theatrically (θiætrɪkli ) adverb ⇒ Shaffer's great gift lies in his ability to animate ideas theatrically.2. graded adjective & adjectiveTheatrical behaviour is exaggerated and unnatural, and intended to create an effect. ⇒ In a theatrical gesture Glass clamped his hand over his eyes.theatricality (θiætrɪkælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ There was no theatricality in her long silence.theatrically adverb ⇒ He looked theatrically at his watch.3. graded adjective & adjectiveTheatrical can be used to describe something that is grand and dramatic, as if it is part of a performance in a theatre. ⇒ There was a theatrical air about the whole scene which had a great appeal for me. ⇒ The clothes were very showy, very theatrical.theatricality uncount noun [usu N of n] ⇒ I'd hate to miss out on the theatricality of a wedding. ⇒ ...the architectural theatricality of Venice.theatrically graded adverb & adverb ⇒ ...a white hotel theatrically set along a ridge.4. plural nounTheatricals are performances of plays and other entertainments, especially when they are done by actors who act as a hobby rather than a profession. [old-fashioned] dramatics ⇒ I always was good at amateur theatricals.
