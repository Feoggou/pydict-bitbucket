metaphysics

(ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (functioning as singular)1. the branch of philosophy that deals with first principles, esp of being and knowing2. the philosophical study of the nature of reality, concerned with such questions as the existence of God, the external world, etc3. See descriptive metaphysics4. (popularly) abstract or subtle discussion or reasoning

Derived Forms

metaphysician (ˌmɛtəfɪˈzɪʃən ) or metaphysicist (ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪsɪst ) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin, from Greek ta meta ta phusika the things after the physics, from the arrangement of the subjects treated in the works of Aristotle
