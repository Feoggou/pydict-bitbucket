Edessa

(ɪˈdɛsə )
Definitions
noun1. an ancient city on the N edge of the Syrian plateau, founded as a Macedonian colony by Seleucus I: a centre of early Christianity. Modern name: Urfa2. a market town in Greece: ancient capital of Macedonia. Pop (municipality): 25 729 (2001). Ancient name: Aegae (ˈiːɡiː ). Modern Greek name: Édhessa
