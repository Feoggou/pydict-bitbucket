tenon

(ˈtɛnən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the projecting end of a piece of wood formed to fit into a corresponding mortise in another pieceverb (transitive)2. to form a tenon on (a piece of wood)3. to join with a tenon and mortise

Derived Forms

tenoner (ˈtenoner) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French, from tenir to hold, from Latin tenēre
