Synonyms of engaged

engaged

adjective

  1. occupied, working, involved, committed, employed, busy, absorbed, tied up, preoccupied, engrossed
    • the various projects he was engaged on
  2. betrothed, promised, pledged, affianced, promised in marriage
    • He was engaged to Miss Julia Boardman.
    OPPOSITES:  free, available, unattached, uncommitted, fancy-free
  3. in use, busy, tied up, unavailable
    • We tried to phone you back but the line was engaged.
    OPPOSITES:  free, available

engage

verb

  1. participate in, join in, take part in, undertake, practise, embark on, enter into, become involved in, set about, partake of
    • They continue to engage in terrorist activities.
  2. captivate, win, draw, catch, arrest, fix, attract, capture, charm, attach, fascinate, enchant, allure, enamour
    • He engaged us with tales of his adventures.
  3. occupy, involve, draw, busy, grip, absorb, tie up, preoccupy, immerse, engross
    • He tried to engage me in conversation.
  4. employ, commission, appoint, take on, hire, retain, recruit, enlist, enrol, put on the payroll
    • We have been able to engage some staff.
    OPPOSITES:  remove, dismiss, sack (informal), fire (informal), axe (informal), discharge, oust, lay off, give notice to, give someone his or her P45 (informal)
  5. book, reserve, secure, hire, rent, charter, lease, prearrange
    • He managed to engage a room for the night.
  6. interlock, join, interact, mesh, interconnect, dovetail
    • Press the lever until you hear the catch engage.
  7. set going, apply, trigger, activate, switch on, energize, bring into operation
    • Show me how to engage the four-wheel drive.
  8. (military) begin battle with, attack, take on, encounter, combat, fall on, battle with, meet, fight with, assail, face off (slang), wage war on, join battle with, give battle to, come to close quarters with
    • They could engage the enemy beyond the range of the torpedoes.
  9. promise, agree, contract, commit, guarantee, bind, pledge, undertake, vow, oblige, covenant, vouch, obligate, betroth (archaic), affiance
