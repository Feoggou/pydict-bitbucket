lubricate

(ˈluːbrɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to cover or treat with an oily or greasy substance so as to lessen friction2. (transitive) to make greasy, slippery, or smooth3. (intransitive) to act as a lubricant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lubrication (ˌlubriˈcation) nounlubricational (ˌlubriˈcational) adjectivelubricative (ˈlubriˌcative) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin lūbricāre, from lūbricus slippery

(lubrɪkeɪt) in the Oil and Gas Industry
Word forms: (present) lubricates, (past) lubricated, (perfect) lubricated, (progressive) lubricating
Definitions
verb(Extractive engineering: Field development, Operations) To lubricate something is to apply a greasy or oily substance to it in order to reduce friction and its effect on moving parts. ⇒ The usual method of reducing friction has been to use a slippery fluid such as oil or grease to lubricate the parts. ⇒ The mud cools the rapidly rotating bit and lubricates the drill pipe as it turns in the well bore. ⇒ To lubricate something is to apply a greasy or oily substance to it in order to reduce friction and its effect on moving parts.
