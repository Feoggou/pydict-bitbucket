proclivity

(prəˈklɪvɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tiesa tendency or inclination

Word Origin

C16: from Latin prōclīvitās, from prōclīvis steep, from pro-1 + clīvus a slope

(prəklɪvɪti , US proʊ- )
Word forms: proclivities
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA proclivity is a tendency to behave in a particular way or to like a particular thing, often a bad way or thing. [formal] ⇒ He was indulging his own sexual proclivities. ⇒ ...a proclivity to daydream.
Example sentences containing 'proclivity'
She should know things inside out, and besides," Daddy said, `she seems to have a proclivity toward mechanical things. Andrews, Virginia WEB OF DREAMSHis English was no longer revered; now even the public-school English teacher seemed ashamed of his proclivity for the foreign language. José Latour OUTCAST
