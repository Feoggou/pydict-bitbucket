puritanical

(ˌpjʊərɪˈtænɪkəl ) or less commonly puritanic
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. usually derogatory strict in moral or religious outlook, esp in shunning sensual pleasures2. (sometimes capital) of or relating to a puritan or the Puritans

Derived Forms

ˌpuritanically (ˌpuriˈtanically) adverbˌpuritanicalness (ˌpuriˈtanicalness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as puritanical, you mean that they have very strict moral principles, and often try to make other people behave in a more moral way. [disapproval] ⇒ ...puritanical fathers. ⇒ He has a puritanical attitude towards sex.
Example sentences containing 'puritanical'
He was frightfully puritanical at the time as regards deviance of any kind. Moore, Margaret FORESTS OF THE NIGHTLaura said Paul was looking at it from a puritanical point of view. Martin, Joy THE IMAGE OF LAURAAlso, it was much more flamboyant than the flat-dayers ' bland figure eight, which appeared rather puritanical by comparison. Magnus Mills THE SCHEME FOR FULL EMPLOYMENTBoth my parents possessed strong puritanical streaks and so the life we led was hard without ever being uncomfortable. Mark Burnell THE RHYTHM SECTION
