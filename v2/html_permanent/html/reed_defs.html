reed

(riːd )
Definitions
noun1. any of various widely distributed tall grasses of the genus Phragmites, esp P. communis, that grow in swamps and shallow water and have jointed hollow stalks2. the stalk, or stalks collectively, of any of these plants, esp as used for thatching3. musica. a thin piece of cane or metal inserted into the tubes of certain wind instruments, which sets in vibration the air column inside the tubeb. a wind instrument or organ pipe that sounds by means of a reed4. one of the several vertical parallel wires on a loom that may be moved upwards to separate the warp threads5. a small semicircular architectural moulding. See also reeding6. an ancient Hebrew unit of length equal to six cubits7. an archaic word for arrow8. See broken reedverb (transitive)9. to fashion into or supply with reeds or reeding10. to thatch using reeds
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hreod; related to Old Saxon hriod, Old High German hriot

reed

Powered by COBUILD
(riːd ) for learners of English
Word forms: reeds
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]Reeds are tall plants that grow in large groups in shallow water or on ground that is always wet and soft. They have strong, hollow stems that can be used for making things such as mats or baskets.2. countable nounA reed is a small piece of cane or metal inserted into the mouthpiece of a woodwind instrument. The reed vibrates when you blow through it and makes a sound.
