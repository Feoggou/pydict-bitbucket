fall for

Definitions
verb (intr, preposition)1. to become infatuated with (a person)2. to allow oneself to be deceived by (a lie, trick, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

fall for

Powered by COBUILD
for learners of English
Definitions
1. phrasal verbIf you fall for someone, you are strongly attracted to them and start loving them. [V P n] ⇒ He was fantastically handsome–I just fell for him right away.2. phrasal verbIf you fall for a lie or trick, you believe it or are deceived by it. [V P n] ⇒ It was just a line to get you out here, and you fell for it! [V P n] ⇒ I told him I would think about it and asked for his telephone number. He didn't fall for that one. →  fall
