dally

(ˈdælɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -lies, -lying or -lied (intransitive)1. to waste time idly; dawdle2. (usually foll by with) to deal frivolously or lightly with; trifle; toy ⇒ to dally with someone's affections
Derived forms
dallier (ˈdallier) noun
Word origin
C14: from Anglo-French dalier to gossip, of uncertain origin

dally

Powered by COBUILD
(dæli )
Word forms: dallies , dallying , dallied
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you dally, you act or move very slowly, wasting time. [old-fashioned] [V] ⇒ The bureaucrats dallied too long. [V + over] ⇒ He did not dally over the choice of a partner. [Also V + with]2. verbIf someone dallies with you, they have a romantic, but not serious, relationship with you. [old-fashioned] [V + with] ⇒ In the past he dallied with actresses and lady novelists.
