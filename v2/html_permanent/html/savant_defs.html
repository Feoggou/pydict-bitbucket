savant

(ˈsævənt , French savɑ̃)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna man of great learning; sage

Derived Forms

savante (ˈsavante) feminine noun

Word Origin

C18: from French, from savoir to know, from Latin sapere to be wise; see sapient

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA savant is a person of great learning or natural ability. [formal] ⇒ The opinion of savants on the composition of the lunar surface.2. countable nounYou can refer to someone as an idiot savant if they seem to be less intelligent than normal people but are unusually good at doing one particular thing. ⇒ ...an idiot savant, an autistic with a gift for numbers.
