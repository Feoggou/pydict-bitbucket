signify

(ˈsɪɡnɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied (when tr, may take a clause as object)1. (transitive) to indicate, show, or suggest2. (transitive) to imply or portend ⇒ the clouds signified the coming storm3. (transitive) to stand as a symbol, sign, etc (for)4. (intransitive) informal to be significant or important

Derived Forms

signiˌfiable (ˈsigniˌfiable) adjectivesigniˌfier (ˈsigniˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French signifier, from Latin significāre, from signum a sign, mark + facere to make

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf an event, a sign, or a symbol signifies something, it is a sign of that thing or represents that thing. [V n] ⇒ The contrasting approaches to Europe signified a sharp difference between the major parties. [V that] ⇒ The symbol displayed outside a restaurant signifies there's excellent cuisine inside.2. verbIf you signify something, you make a sign or gesture in order to communicate a particular meaning. [V n] ⇒ Two jurors signified their dissent. [V that] ⇒ The U.N. flag was raised at the airport yesterday to signify that control had passed into its hands.
