crisis

(ˈkraɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-siːz)1. a crucial stage or turning point in the course of something, esp in a sequence of events or a disease2. an unstable period, esp one of extreme trouble or danger in politics, economics, etc3. pathology a sudden change, for better or worse, in the course of a disease. Also (archaic): crise
Word origin
C15: from Latin: decision, from Greek krisis, from krinein to decide
