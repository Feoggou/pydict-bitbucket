(ˈkəʊdʒənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectivecompelling belief or assent; forcefully convincing
Derived forms
cogency (ˈcogency) or cogence (ˈcogence) nouncogently (ˈcogently) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin cōgent-, cōgēns, driving together, from cōgere, from co- together + agere to drive
