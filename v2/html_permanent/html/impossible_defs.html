impossible

(ɪmˈpɒsəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. incapable of being done, undertaken, or experienced2. incapable of occurring or happening3. absurd or inconceivable; unreasonable ⇒ it's impossible to think of him as a bishop4. informal intolerable; outrageous ⇒ those children are impossible

impossible

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmpɒsɪbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ to-inf]Something that is impossible cannot be done or cannot happen. ⇒ It was impossible for anyone to get in because no one knew the password. ⇒ He thinks the tax is impossible to administer. ⇒ You shouldn't promise what's impossible. ⇒ Keller is good at describing music–an almost impossible task to do well.The impossible is something which is impossible. ⇒ They were expected to do the impossible. ⇒ No one can achieve the impossible.impossibly adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ Mathematical physics is an almost impossibly difficult subject.impossibility (ɪmpɒsɪbɪlɪti )Word forms: impossibilities variable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the impossibility of knowing absolute truth.2. adjective [ADJ n]An impossible situation or an impossible position is one that is very difficult to deal with. ⇒ The Government was now in an almost impossible position.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as impossible, you are annoyed that their bad behaviour or strong views make them difficult to deal with. [disapproval] ⇒ The woman is impossible, thought Frannie.
