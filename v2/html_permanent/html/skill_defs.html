skill

(skɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. special ability in a task, sport, etc, esp ability acquired by training2. something, esp a trade or technique, requiring special training or manual proficiency3. obsolete understanding

Derived Forms

skill-less (ˈskill-less) or skilless (ˈskilless) adjective

Word Origin

C12: from Old Norse skil distinction; related to Middle Low German schēle, Middle Dutch geschil difference

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA skill is a type of work or activity which requires special training and knowledge. ⇒ Most of us will know someone who is always learning new skills, or studying new fields.2. uncount nounSkill is the knowledge and ability that enables you to do something well. [+ of] ⇒ The cut of a diamond depends on the skill of its craftsman.
