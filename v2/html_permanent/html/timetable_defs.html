timetable

(ˈtaɪmˌteɪbəl )
Definitions
noun1. a list or table of events arranged according to the time when they take place; scheduleverb2. (transitive) to include in or arrange according to a timetable3. (intransitive) to draw up a timetable

timetable

Powered by COBUILD
(taɪmteɪbəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: timetables , timetabling , timetabled
Definitions
1. countable nounA timetable is a plan of the times when particular events are to take place. ⇒ The timetable was hopelessly optimistic. ⇒ Don't you realize we're working to a timetable? We have to have results. [+ for] ⇒ The two countries are to try to agree a timetable for formal talks.2. countable nounIn a school or college, a timetable is a list that shows the times in the week at which particular subjects are taught. You can also refer to the range of subjects that a student learns or the classes that a teacher teaches as their timetable. [British] ⇒ Options are offered subject to staff availability and the constraints of the timetable. ⇒ Members of the union will continue to teach their full timetables.regional note:   in AM, usually use class schedule3. countable nounA timetable is a list of the times when trains, boats, buses, or aeroplanes are supposed to arrive at or leave from a particular place. [mainly British] ⇒ For a local bus timetable, contact Dyfed County Council.regional note:   in AM, usually use schedule4. verb [usu passive]If something is timetabled, it is scheduled to happen or do something at a particular time. [mainly British] [be V-ed] ⇒ On both days, three very different trains will be timetabled. [be V-ed to-inf] ⇒ Opie is timetabled to work a four-day week.timetabling uncount noun ⇒ Timetabling is a nightmare for all schools.
