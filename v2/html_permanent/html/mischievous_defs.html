mischievous

(ˈmɪstʃɪvəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. inclined to acts of mischief2. teasing; slightly malicious ⇒ a mischievous grin3. causing or intended to cause harm ⇒ a mischievous plot

Derived Forms

mischievously (ˈmischievously) adverbmischievousness (ˈmischievousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA mischievous person likes to have fun by playing harmless tricks on people or doing things they are not supposed to do. ⇒ She rocks back and forth on her chair like a mischievous child. ⇒ He's a little mischievous.mischievously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ Kathryn winked mischievously.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA mischievous act or suggestion is intended to cause trouble. ⇒ ...a mischievous campaign by the press to divide the ANC. ⇒ The Foreign Office dismissed the story as mischievous and false.mischievously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ That does not require 'massive' military intervention, as some have mischievously claimed.
