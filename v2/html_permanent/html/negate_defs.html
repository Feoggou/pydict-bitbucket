negate

(nɪˈɡeɪt )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to make ineffective or void; nullify; invalidate2. to deny or contradict
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Latin negāre, from neg-, variant of nec not + aio I say

negate for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(nɪgeɪt )
Word forms: negates , negating , negated
Definitions
1. verbIf one thing negates another, it causes that other thing to lose the effect or value that it had. [formal] nullify [V n] ⇒ These weaknesses negated his otherwise progressive attitude towards the staff.2. verbIf someone negates something, they say that it does not exist. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He warned that to negate the results of elections would only make things worse.
