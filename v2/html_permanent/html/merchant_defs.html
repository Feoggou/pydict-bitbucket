merchant

(ˈmɜːtʃənt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a person engaged in the purchase and sale of commodities for profit, esp on international markets; trader2. mainly US and Canadian a person engaged in retail trade3. (esp in historical contexts) any trader4. derogatory a person dealing or involved in something undesirable ⇒ a gossip merchant5. (modifier)a. of the merchant navy ⇒ a merchant sailorb. of or concerned with trade ⇒ a merchant shipverb6. (transitive) to conduct trade in; deal in

Derived Forms

merchant-ˌlike (ˈmerchant-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French, probably from Vulgar Latin mercātāre (unattested), from Latin mercārī to trade, from merx goods, wares
