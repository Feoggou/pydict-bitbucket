Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounan act or instance of moving or happening more quickly ⇒ Google will not run five times faster if our research is implemented, but we do expect a 30 percent speed-up., ⇒ The president has ordered a speed-up of aid for the unemployed.

speed up

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbWhen something speeds up or when you speed it up, it moves or travels faster. [V P] ⇒ You notice that your breathing has speeded up a bit. [V P n] ⇒ He pushed a lever that speeded up the car. [Also V n P]2. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbWhen a process or activity speeds up or when something speeds it up, it happens at a faster rate. [V P] ⇒ Job losses are speeding up. [V P n] ⇒ I had already taken steps to speed up a solution to the problem. [V n P] ⇒ I kept praying that the DJ would speed the music up. →  speed
