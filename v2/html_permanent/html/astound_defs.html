astound

(əˈstaʊnd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(transitive) to overwhelm with amazement and wonder; bewilder
Word origin
C17: from astoned amazed, from Old French estoné, from estoner to astonish

(əstaʊnd )
Word forms: astounds , astounding , astounded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something astounds you, you are very surprised by it. [V n] ⇒ He used to astound his friends with feats of physical endurance. [Also V]
Example sentences containing 'astound'
Come the final days, according to her admirers, she would astound all with an even greater performance, from even more exquisite pipes. Ian St James Awards AT THE STROKE OF TWELVEThe mercy of your fellow men will astound you brisk and wary sighted people -- they will help you at the kerb, cut your meat for you. Fay Weldon THE PRESIDENT'S CHILDThe fact proved his thesis that she and she alone possessed the ability to honestly astound him ---and, yes, make him laugh. Stephanie Laurens THE PROMISE IN A KISS
