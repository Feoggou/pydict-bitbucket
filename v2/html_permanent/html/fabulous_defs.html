fabulous

(ˈfæbjʊləs )
Definitions
adjective1. almost unbelievable; astounding; legendary ⇒ fabulous wealth2. informal extremely good ⇒ a fabulous time at the party3. of, relating to, or based upon fable ⇒ a fabulous beast
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fabulously (ˈfabulously) adverbfabulousness (ˈfabulousness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin fābulōsus celebrated in fable, from fābula fable

fabulous

Powered by COBUILD
(fæbjʊləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as fabulous, you are emphasizing that you like it a lot or think that it is very good. [informal, emphasis] ⇒ This is a fabulous album. It's fresh, varied, fun. ⇒ The scenery and weather were fabulous.2. graded adjective [usu ADJ n]If you talk about someone's fabulous beauty, for example, or their fabulous success, you are emphasizing that they are extremely beautiful or extremely successful. [emphasis] ⇒ Despite his fabulous wealth, he is very much a man of the people.fabulously graded adverb [ADV adj/adv] ⇒ ...their fabulously rich parents.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
