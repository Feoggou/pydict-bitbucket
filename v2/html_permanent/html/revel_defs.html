revel

(ˈrɛvəl )
Definitions
verb -els, -elling, -elled or US -els, -eling or -eled (intransitive)1. (foll by in) to take pleasure or wallow ⇒ to revel in success2. to take part in noisy festivities; make merrynoun3. (often plural) an occasion of noisy merrymaking4. a less common word for revelry
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reveller (ˈreveller) or US reveler (ˈreveler) nounrevelment (ˈrevelment) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French reveler to be merry, noisy, from Latin rebellāre to revolt, rebel

(revəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: revels , revelling , revelled
Definitions
regional note:   in AM, use reveling, reveled1. verbIf you revel in a situation or experience, you enjoy it very much. [V + in] ⇒ Revelling in her freedom, she took a hotel room and stayed for several days. [V in n] ⇒ Cats positively revel in heat, whether natural or man-made.2. countable noun [usu pl]Revels are noisy celebrations. [literary] ⇒ The revels often last until dawn.
