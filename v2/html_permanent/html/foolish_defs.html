foolish

(ˈfuːlɪʃ )
Definitions
adjective1. unwise; silly2. resulting from folly or stupidity3. ridiculous or absurd; not worthy of consideration4. weak-minded; simple5. an archaic word for insignificant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

foolishly (ˈfoolishly) adverbfoolishness (ˈfoolishness) noun

foolish

Powered by COBUILD
(fuːlɪʃ ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone's behaviour or action is foolish, it is not sensible and shows a lack of good judgment. ⇒ It would be foolish to raise hopes unnecessarily. ⇒ It is foolish to risk skin cancer.foolishly adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ He admitted that he had acted foolishly.foolishness uncount noun ⇒ They don't accept any foolishness when it comes to spending money.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you look or feel foolish, you look or feel so silly or ridiculous that people are likely to laugh at you. ⇒ I just stood there feeling foolish and watching him. ⇒ I didn't want him to look foolish and be laughed at.foolishly graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ He saw me standing there, grinning foolishly at him.
