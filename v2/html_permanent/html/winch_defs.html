winch1

(wɪntʃ )
Definitions
noun1. a windlass driven by a hand- or power-operated crank2. a hand- or power-operated crank by which a machine is drivenverb3. (tr; often foll by up or in) to pull (in a rope) or lift (a weight) using a winch

winch for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪntʃ )
Word forms: winches , winching , winched
Definitions
1. countable nounA winch is a machine which is used to lift heavy objects or people who need to be rescued. It consists of a drum around which a rope or chain is wound.2. verbIf you winch an object or person somewhere, you lift or lower them using a winch. [V n with adv/prep] ⇒ He would attach a cable around the chassis of the car and winch it up on to the canal bank. [V n with adv/prep] ⇒ The crew members were winched to safety by helicopters.
