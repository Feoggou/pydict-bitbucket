Lethe

(ˈliːθɪ )
Definitions
noun1. Greek mythology a river in Hades that caused forgetfulness in those who drank its waters2. forgetfulness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

Lethean (lɪˈθiːən ) adjective

Word Origin

C16: via Latin from Greek, from lēthē oblivion
Example sentences containing 'Lethe'
He clicked on the morphine dispenser but this time he didn't feel the trickle of Lethe , as they'd begun to call it. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERS
