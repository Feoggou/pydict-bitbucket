reprehensible

(ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnsəbəl )
Definitions
adjectiveopen to criticism or rebuke; blameworthy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reprehensibility (ˌrepreˌhensiˈbility) or reprehensibleness (ˌrepreˈhensibleness) nounreprehensibly (ˌrepreˈhensibly) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Late Latin reprehensibilis, from Latin reprehendere to hold back, reprove

reprehensible

Powered by COBUILD
(reprɪhensɪbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you think that a type of behaviour or an idea is very bad and morally wrong, you can say that it is reprehensible. [formal] ⇒ Mr Cramer said the violence by anti-government protestors was reprehensible. ⇒ ...behaving in the most reprehensible manner.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
