sheet1

(ʃiːt )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a large rectangular piece of cotton, linen, etc, generally one of a pair used as inner bedclothes2. a. a thin piece of a substance such as paper, glass, or metal, usually rectangular in formb. (as modifier) ⇒ sheet iron3. a broad continuous surface; expanse or stretch ⇒ a sheet of rain4. a newspaper, esp a tabloid5. a piece of printed paper to be folded into a section for a book6. a page of stamps, usually of one denomination and already perforated7. any thin tabular mass of rock covering a large areaverb8. (transitive) to provide with, cover, or wrap in a sheet9. (intransitive) (of rain, snow, etc) to fall heavily

Word Origin

Old English sciete; related to sceat corner, lap, Old Norse skaut, Old High German scōz lap

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA sheet is a large rectangular piece of cotton or other cloth that you sleep on or cover yourself with in a bed. ⇒ Once a week, a maid changes the sheets. ⇒ ...the luxury of silk sheets.2. countable nounA sheet of paper is a rectangular piece of paper. [+ of] ⇒ ...a sheet of newspaper. ⇒ I was able to fit it all on one sheet.3. countable noun [usu n N]You can use sheet to refer to a piece of paper which gives information about something. [+ on] ⇒ ...information sheets on each country in the world.4. countable nounA sheet of glass, metal, or wood is a large, flat, thin piece of it. [+ of] ⇒ ...a cracked sheet of glass. [+ of] ⇒ Overhead, cranes were lifting giant sheets of steel. ⇒ Vinyl can be laid in sheet or tile form.5. countable nounA sheet of something is a thin wide layer of it over the surface of something else. [+ of] ⇒ ...a sheet of ice. [+ of] ⇒ ...a blue-grey sheet of dust.6. countable noun [usu N of n]A sheet of fire or water is a fast-moving mass of it that is difficult to see through. ⇒ The streets were now in one fierce sheet of flame. ⇒ Sheets of rain slanted across the road.7. countable noun [oft n N]In sailing, a sheet is a line or rope used for controlling the position of a sail on a boat.8. → see also balance sheet, broadsheet, dust sheet, fact sheet, groundsheet, news-sheet, scoresheet, spreadsheet, worksheet9. → as white as a sheet
