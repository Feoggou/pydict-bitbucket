Wells2

(wɛlz )
Definitions
noun1.  Henry. 1805–78, US businessman, who founded (1852) with William Fargo the express mail service Wells, Fargo and Company2.  H(erbert) G(eorge). 1866–1946, British writer. His science-fiction stories include The Time Machine (1895), War of the Worlds (1898), and The Shape of Things to Come (1933). His novels on contemporary social questions, such as Kipps (1905), Tono-Bungay (1909), and Ann Veronica (1909), affected the opinions of his day. His nonfiction works include The Outline of History (1920)
