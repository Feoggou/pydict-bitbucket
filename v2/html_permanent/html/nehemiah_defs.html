Nehemiah

(ˌniːɪˈmaɪə )
Definitions
noun Old Testament1. a Jewish official at the court of Artaxerxes, king of Persia, who in 444 bc became a leader in the rebuilding of Jerusalem after the Babylonian captivity2. the book recounting the acts of Nehemiah
