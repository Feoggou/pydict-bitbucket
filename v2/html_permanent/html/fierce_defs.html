fierce

(fɪəs )
Definitions
adjective1. having a violent and unrestrained nature; savage ⇒ a fierce dog2. wild or turbulent in force, action, or intensity ⇒ a fierce storm3. vehement, intense, or strong ⇒ fierce competition4. informal very disagreeable or unpleasant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fiercely (ˈfiercely) adverbfierceness (ˈfierceness) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French fiers, from Latin ferus

Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA fierce animal or person is very aggressive or angry. ⇒ They look like the teeth of some fierce animal.fiercely graded adverb & adverb ⇒ 'I don't know,' she said fiercely.2. graded adjective & adjectiveFierce feelings or actions are very intense or enthusiastic, or involve great activity. ⇒ Competition has been fierce to win a stake in Skoda. ⇒ The town was captured after a fierce battle with rebels at the weekend. ⇒ He inspires fierce loyalty in his friends.fiercely graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ He has always been ambitious and fiercely competitive.3. graded adjective & adjectiveFierce conditions are very intense, great, or strong. ⇒ The climbers were trapped by a fierce storm which went on for days.fiercely graded adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ As I arrived a lorry had just been set on fire and was burning fiercely.
