yew

(juː )
Definitions
noun1. any coniferous tree of the genus Taxus, of the Old World and North America, esp T. baccata, having flattened needle-like leaves, fine-grained elastic wood, and solitary seeds with a red waxy aril resembling berries: family Taxaceae2. the wood of any of these trees, used to make bows for archery3. archery a bow made of yew
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English īw; related to Old High German īwa, Old Norse ӯr yew, Latin ūva grape, Russian iva willow
