spearhead

(ˈspɪəˌhɛd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the pointed head of a spear2. the leading force in a military attack3. any person or thing that leads or initiates an attack, a campaign, etcverb4. (transitive) to lead or initiate (an attack, a campaign, etc)

Word forms: spearheads , spearheading , spearheaded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf someone spearheads a campaign or an attack, they lead it. [journalism] [V n] ⇒ ...Esther Rantzen, who is spearheading a national campaign against bullying. [V n] ⇒ Helicopters can to some extent take the place of tanks by spearheading the airborne attack.2. countable noun [usu sing, usu with supp]The spearhead of a campaign is the person or group that leads it. [journalism] ⇒ The marines went ashore as a spearhead this morning to capture key targets.
