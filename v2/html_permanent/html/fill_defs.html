fill

(fɪl )
Definitions
verb (mainly tr; often foll by up)1. (also intr) to make or become full ⇒ to fill up a bottle, ⇒ the bath fills in two minutes2. to occupy the whole of ⇒ the party filled two floors of the house3. to plug (a gap, crevice, cavity, etc)4. to meet (a requirement or need) satisfactorily5. to cover (a page or blank space) with writing, drawing, etc6. to hold and perform the duties of (an office or position)7. to appoint or elect an occupant to (an office or position)8. building to build up (ground) with fill9. (also intr) to swell or cause to swell with wind, as in manoeuvring the sails of a sailing vessel10. to increase the bulk of by adding an inferior substance11. poker to complete (a full house, etc) by drawing the cards needed12. mainly US and Canadian to put together the necessary materials for (a prescription or order)13. See fill the billnoun14. material such as gravel, stones, etc, used to bring an area of ground up to a required level15. See one's fill
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English fyllan; related to Old Frisian fella, Old Norse fylla, Gothic fulljan, Old High German fullen; see full1, fulfil
