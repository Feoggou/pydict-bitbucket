swine

(swaɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Word forms: plural swines a coarse or contemptible person2. Word forms: plural swine another name for a pig

Derived Forms

swineˌlike (ˈswineˌlike) adjectiveswinish (ˈswinish) adjectiveswinishly (ˈswinishly) adverbswinishness (ˈswinishness) noun

Word Origin

Old English swīn; related to Old Norse svīn, Gothic swein, Latin suīnus relating to swine

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
language note:   The form swines is used as the plural for meaning [sense 1]; swine is used as both the singular and plural for meaning [sense 2].1. countable nounIf you call someone a swine, you dislike them or think that they are a bad person, usually because they have behaved unpleasantly towards you. [informal, disapproval]2. countable nounA swine is a pig. [technical, old-fashioned] ⇒ ...imports of live swine from Canada. ⇒ ...pigs with swine fever.3. → to cast pearls before swine
