scot-free

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adverb, adjective(predicative) without harm, loss, or penalty

Word Origin

C16: see scot and lot

scot-free

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV after v]If you say that someone got away scot-free, you are emphasizing that they escaped punishment for something that you believe they should have been punished for. [emphasis] ⇒ Others who were guilty were being allowed to get off scot-free.
Example sentences containing 'scot-free'
Better to let the criminal go scot-free , she felt, than get involved in acts of moral denouncing. Salley Vickers INSTANCES OF THE NUMBER 3Everything about Glanville brought old angers to the surface, a deep resentment that to all intents he had got off scot-free. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEStill it seems so wrong they got off scot-free after doing something like that. Richard Wright 52449_CLARA
