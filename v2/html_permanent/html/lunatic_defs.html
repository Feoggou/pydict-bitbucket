lunatic

(ˈluːnətɪk )
Definitions
adjective also (rarely): lunatical (luːˈnætɪkəl )1. an archaic word for insane2. foolish; eccentric; crazynoun3. a person who is insane
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lunatically (luˈnatically) adverb

Word Origin

C13 (adj) via Old French from Late Latin lūnāticus crazy, moonstruck, from Latin lūna moon

lunatic

Powered by COBUILD
(luːnətɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: lunatics
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you describe someone as a lunatic, you think they behave in a dangerous, stupid, or annoying way. [informal, disapproval] ⇒ Her son thinks she's an absolute raving lunatic.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour or ideas as lunatic, you think they are very foolish and possibly dangerous. [disapproval] ⇒ ...the operation of the market taken to lunatic extremes. ⇒ ...a country spurned until now by all except the more lunatic of journalists and adventurers.3. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]If you describe a place or situation as lunatic, you mean that it is confused and seems out of control. ⇒ He pleads for sanity in a lunatic world.4. countable nounPeople who were mentally ill used to be called lunatics. [old-fashioned, offensive]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
