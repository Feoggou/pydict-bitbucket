juror

(ˈdʒʊərə )
Definitions
noun1. a member of a jury2. a person whose name is included on a panel from which a jury is selected3. a person who takes an oath
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Anglo-French jurour, from Old French jurer to take an oath, from Latin jūrāre
