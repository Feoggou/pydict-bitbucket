cinnabar

(ˈsɪnəˌbɑː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a bright red or brownish-red mineral form of mercuric sulphide (mercury(II) sulphide), found close to areas of volcanic activity and hot springs. It is the main commercial source of mercury. Formula: HgS. Crystal structure: hexagonal2. the red form of mercuric sulphide (mercury(II) sulphide), esp when used as a pigment3. a bright red to reddish-orange; vermilion4. a large red-and-black European moth, Callimorpha jacobaeae: family Arctiidae (tiger moths, etc)
Word origin
C15: from Old French cenobre, from Latin cinnābaris, from Greek kinnabari, of Oriental origin
