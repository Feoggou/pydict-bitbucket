(ˈlaʊzɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: lousier or lousiest1. slang very mean or unpleasant ⇒ a lousy thing to do2. slang inferior or bad ⇒ this is a lousy film3. infested with lice4. (foll by with) slanga. provided with an excessive amount (of) ⇒ he's lousy with moneyb. full of or teeming with

Powered by COBUILD
(laʊzi ) for learners of English
Word forms: lousier , lousiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as lousy, you mean that it is of very bad quality or that you do not like it. [informal] ⇒ He blamed Fiona for a lousy weekend. ⇒ At Billy's Cafe, the menu is limited and the food is lousy. [+ to-inf] ⇒ It's lousy to be the new kid.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as lousy, you mean that they are very bad at something they do. [informal] ⇒ I was a lousy secretary. [+ at] ⇒ There can be no argument about how lousy he is at public relations.3. adjectiveIf you describe the number or amount of something as lousy, you mean it is smaller than you think it should be. [informal] ⇒ The pay is lousy.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you feel lousy, you feel very ill. [informal] ⇒ I wasn't actually sick but I felt lousy.
