sine1

(saɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun (of an angle)a. a trigonometric function that in a right-angled triangle is the ratio of the length of the opposite side to that of the hypotenuseb. a function that in a circle centred at the origin of a Cartesian coordinate system is the ratio of the ordinate of a point on the circumference to the radius of the circle Abbreviation: sin

Word Origin

C16: from Latin sinus a bend; in New Latin, sinus was mistaken as a translation of Arabic jiba sine (from Sanskrit jīva, literally: bowstring) because of confusion with Arabic jaib curve
