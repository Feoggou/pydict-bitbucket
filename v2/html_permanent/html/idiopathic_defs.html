idiopathic

(ˌɪdiˌoʊˈpæθɪk ; ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk ) in American English
Definitions
adjective designating or of a disease whose cause is unknown or uncertain
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Derived Forms

idiopathy (ˌidiˈopathy) (ˌɪdiˈɑpəθi ) nounWord forms: plural ˌidiˈopathies

Word Origin

< Gr idiopatheia, feeling for oneself alone (see idio- & -pathy) + -ic
