Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is awful, you dislike that person or thing or you think that they are not very good. ⇒ We met and I thought he was awful. ⇒ I couldn't stand London! Bloody awful place. ⇒ ...an awful smell of paint. ⇒ Even if the weather's awful there's lots to do. ⇒ Jeans look awful on me.awfulness uncount noun ⇒ The programme's awfulness has ensured it is talked about.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is awful, you mean that it is extremely unpleasant, shocking, or bad. ⇒ Her injuries were massive. It was awful. ⇒ Some of their offences are so awful they would chill the blood.3. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you look or feel awful, you look or feel ill. ⇒ I hardly slept at all and felt pretty awful. ⇒ I looked awful and felt quite shaky.4. adjective [ADJ n]You can use awful with noun groups that refer to an amount in order to emphasize how large that amount is. [emphasis] ⇒ I've got an awful lot of work to do.awfully graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV adj/adv] ⇒ The caramel looks awfully good. ⇒ 'I'm awfully sorry,' she told him regretfully. ⇒ Would you mind awfully waiting a bit, I'll be back right away.5. adverb [ADV adj]You can use awful with adjectives that describe a quality in order to emphasize that particular quality. [US, informal, emphasis] ⇒ Gosh, you're awful pretty. ⇒ You know, 10 years sounds like an awful long time.
