bedridden

(ˈbɛdˌrɪdən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveconfined to bed because of illness, esp for a long or indefinite period
Word origin
Old English bedreda, from bedd bed + -rida rider, from rīdan to ride

bedridden

Powered by COBUILD
(bedrɪdən )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveSomeone who is bedridden is so ill or has such a severe disability that they cannot get out of bed. ⇒ He had to spend two years bedridden with an injury. ⇒ ...bedridden patients.
Example sentences containing 'bedridden'
My sister suffers from a chronically weak heart; she's upstairs in bed now, being mostly bedridden. Malcolm, John WHISTLER IN THE DARKCadmus, as I'm sure you know, is now bedridden , and I'm afraid he's very weak. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARThe doctor had explained that the elevation was temporary, but that John would be bedridden for a time. Barbara Delinsky TOGETHER ALONE
