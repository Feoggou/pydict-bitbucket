diverting

Powered by COBUILD
(daɪvɜːʳtɪŋ , US dɪ- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as diverting, you mean that it is amusing or entertaining. [old-fashioned] ⇒ It was a witty and diverting programme.
Example sentences containing 'diverting'
he asked, diverting her, fluttering his hand over the food, not interested himself, gulping at the wine. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSIt was on the main road and had been cordoned off by the police, who were diverting traffic down a side-street near it. Ferrars, Elizabeth THY BROTHER DEATHGo and tell Sergeant Wardle to put signs out diverting traffic round the village. Hilton, John Buxton PASSION IN THE PEAKPuzzles amuse me, and one involving a horse might prove more diverting than most. Julian May IRONCROWN MOON: PART TWO OF THE BOREAL MOON TALE
