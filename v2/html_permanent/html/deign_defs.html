deign

(deɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to think it fit or worthy of oneself (to do something); condescend ⇒ he will not deign to speak to us2. (transitive) archaic to vouchsafe ⇒ he deigned no reply
Word origin
C13: from Old French deignier, from Latin dignārī to consider worthy, from dignus worthy

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you say that someone deigned to do something, you are expressing your disapproval of the fact that they did it unwillingly, because they thought they were too important to do it. [formal, disapproval] [V to-inf] ⇒ At last, Harper deigned to speak. [V to-inf] ⇒ Weatherby didn't deign to reply.
