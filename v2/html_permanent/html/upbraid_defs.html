upbraid

(ʌpˈbreɪd )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to reprove or reproach angrily2. to find fault with
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

upbraider (upˈbraider) nounupbraiding (upˈbraiding) nounupbraidingly (upˈbraidingly) adverb

Word Origin

Old English upbregdan; related to Danish bebreide; see up, braid

upbraid for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌpbreɪd )
Word forms: upbraids , upbraiding , upbraided
Definitions
verbIf you upbraid someone, you tell them that they have done something wrong and criticize them for doing it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ His mother summoned him, upbraided him, wept and prayed. [V n + for] ⇒ His wife set about upbraiding him for neglecting the children.
