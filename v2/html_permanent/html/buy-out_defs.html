buy out

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (tr, adverb)1. to purchase the ownership, controlling interest, shares, etc, of (a company, etc)2. to gain the release of (a person) from the armed forces by payment of money3. to pay (a person) once and for all to give up (property, interest, etc)noun buyout4. the purchase of a company, esp by its former management or staff. See also leveraged buyout, management buyout

buy out

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you buy someone out, you buy their share of something such as a company or piece of property that you previously owned together. [V P n] ⇒ The bank had to pay to buy out most of the 200 former partners. [V n P] ⇒ He bought his brother out for $17 million.2. → see also buyout3. phrasal verbIf you buy someone out of the armed forces or another organization, you pay a sum of money so that they can leave before the end of the period they agreed to stay for. [V n P] ⇒ Carling eventually bought himself out of the army. →  buy
