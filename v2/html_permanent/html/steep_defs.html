steep1

(stiːp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. a. having or being a slope or gradient approaching the perpendicularb. (as noun) ⇒ the steep2. informal (of a fee, price, demand, etc) unduly high; unreasonable (esp in the phrase that's a bit steep)3. informal excessively demanding or ambitious ⇒ a steep task4. British informal (of a statement) extreme or far-fetched5. obsolete elevated

Derived Forms

steeply (ˈsteeply) adverbsteepness (ˈsteepness) noun

Word Origin

Old English steap; related to Old Frisian stāp, Old High German stouf cliff, Old Norse staup

steep2

(stiːp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to soak or be soaked in a liquid in order to soften, cleanse, extract an element, etc2. (tr; usually passive) to saturate; imbue ⇒ steeped in ideologynoun3. an instance or the process of steeping or the condition of being steeped4. a liquid or solution used for the purpose of steeping something

Derived Forms

steeper (ˈsteeper) noun

Word Origin

Old English stēpan; related to steap vessel, cup, Old High German stouf, Old Norse staup, Middle Dutch stōp

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA steep slope rises at a very sharp angle and is difficult to go up. ⇒ San Francisco is built on 40 hills and some are very steep. ⇒ ...a narrow, steep-sided valley.steeply graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ The road climbs steeply, with good views of Orvieto through the trees. ⇒ ...steeply terraced valleys. ⇒ ...houses with steeply sloping roofs.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA steep increase or decrease in something is a very big increase or decrease. ⇒ Consumers are rebelling at steep price increases.steeply graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Unemployment is rising steeply.3. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you say that the price of something is steep, you mean that it is expensive. [informal] ⇒ The annual premium can be a little steep, but will be well worth it if your dog is injured.4. verbTo steep food in a liquid means to put the food in the liquid for some time so that the food gets flavour from the liquid. [V n] ⇒ It's a drink made by steeping pineapple rind in water. [V-ed] ⇒ ...green beans steeped in olive oil.
