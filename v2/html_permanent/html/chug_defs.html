chug

(tʃʌɡ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a short dull sound, esp one that is rapidly repeated, such as that made by an engineverbWord forms: chugs, chugging or chugged2. (intransitive) (of an engine, etc) to operate while making such sounds3. (transitive) slang to drink (a liquid) quickly or greedily
Word origin
C19: of imitative origin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen a vehicle chugs somewhere, it goes there slowly, noisily and with difficulty. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The train chugs down the track.Chug is also a noun. ⇒ In the distance I could hear the chug of farm machinery.
