stemmed

(stɛmd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. a. having a stemb. (in combination) ⇒ a thin-stemmed plant, ⇒ a long-stemmed glass2. having had the stem or stems removed
Example sentences containing 'stemmed'
He had aged since they last met, but the changes in his appearance stemmed more from good living than from the advance of the years. Forbes, Bryan A SONG AT TWILIGHTPerhaps some of his aversion stemmed from uneasiness about his present assignment. Leasor, James TANK OF SERPENTSClyde's request stemmed from either a headache or a sudden phobia, he wasn't sure which. Gregg Andrew Hurwitz DO NO HARM
