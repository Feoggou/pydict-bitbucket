autopsy

(ˈɔːtəpsɪ , ɔːˈtɒp-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -sies1. Also called: necropsy, postmortem examination dissection and examination of a dead body to determine the cause of death2. an eyewitness observation3. any critical analysis
Word origin
C17: from New Latin autopsia, from Greek: seeing with one's own eyes, from auto- + opsis sight

countable nounAn autopsy is an examination of a dead body by a doctor who cuts it open in order to try to discover the cause of death. ⇒ The autopsy report gave the cause of death as poisoning.
