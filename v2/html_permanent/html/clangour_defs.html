clangour

or US clangor (ˈklæŋɡə , ˈklæŋə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a loud resonant often-repeated noise2. an uproarverb3. (intransitive) to make or produce a loud resonant noise
Derived forms
clangorous (ˈclangorous) adjectiveclangorously (ˈclangorously) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Latin clangor a noise, from clangere to clang
Example sentences containing 'clangour'
Someone tripped the fire alarm, which added its deafening clangour to the tumult. Pritchard, John NIGHT SISTERS
