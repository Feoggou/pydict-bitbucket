mandarin

(ˈmændərɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in the Chinese Empire) a member of any of the nine senior grades of the bureaucracy, entered by examinations2. a high-ranking official whose powers are extensive and thought to be outside political control3. a person of standing and influence, as in literary or intellectual circles4. a. a small citrus tree, Citrus nobilis, cultivated for its edible fruitb. the fruit of this tree, resembling the tangerine

Derived Forms

mandarinate (ˈmandarinate) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Portuguese mandarim, via Malay menteri from Sanskrit mantrin counsellor, from mantra counsel
