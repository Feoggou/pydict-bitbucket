hamper1

(ˈhæmpə )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to prevent the progress or free movement ofnoun2. nautical gear aboard a vessel that, though essential, is often in the way
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hamperedness (ˈhamperedness) nounhamperer (ˈhamperer) noun

Word Origin

C14: of obscure origin; perhaps related to Old English hamm enclosure, hemm hem1

hamper2

(ˈhæmpə )
Definitions
noun1. a large basket, usually with a cover2. British such a basket and its contents, usually food3. US a laundry basket

hamper

Powered by COBUILD
(hæmpəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hampers , hampering , hampered
Definitions
1. verbIf someone or something hampers you, they make it difficult for you to do what you are trying to do. [V n] ⇒ The bad weather hampered rescue operations. [V n] ⇒ I was hampered by a lack of information.2. countable nounA hamper is a basket containing food of various kinds that is given to people as a present. ⇒ ...a luxury food hamper.3. countable nounA hamper is a large basket with a lid, used especially for carrying food in. ⇒ ...a picnic hamper.
