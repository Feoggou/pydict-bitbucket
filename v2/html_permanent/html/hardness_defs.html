hardness

(ˈhɑːdnɪs )
Definitions
noun1. the quality or condition of being hard2. one of several measures of resistance to indentation, deformation, or abrasion. See Mohs scale, Brinell hardness number3. the quality of water that causes it to impair the lathering of soap: caused by the presence of certain calcium salts. Temporary hardness can be removed by boiling whereas permanent hardness cannot
