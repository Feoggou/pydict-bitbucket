Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as forceful, you approve of them because they express their opinions and wishes in a strong, emphatic, and confident way. [approval] ⇒ He was a man of forceful character, with considerable insight and diplomatic skills.forcefully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Mrs. Dambar was talking very rapidly and somewhat forcefully.forcefulness uncount noun ⇒ She had inherited her father's forcefulness.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is forceful has a very powerful effect and causes you to think or feel something very strongly. ⇒ It made a very forceful impression on me. ⇒ For most people a heart attack is a forceful reminder that they are mortal.forcefully graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Daytime television tended to remind her too forcefully of her own situation.3. graded adjective & adjectiveA forceful point or argument in a discussion is one that is good, valid, and convincing.
