detour

(ˈdiːtʊə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a deviation from a direct, usually shorter route or course of actionverb2. to deviate or cause to deviate from a direct route or course of action
Word origin
C18: from French détour, from Old French destorner to divert, turn away, from des- de- + torner to turn

detour

Powered by COBUILD
(diːtʊəʳ )
Word forms: detours , detouring, detoured
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounIf you make a detour on a journey, you go by a route which is not the shortest way, because you want to avoid something such as a traffic jam, or because there is something you want to do on the way. ⇒ He did not take the direct route to his home, but made a detour around the outskirts of the city.2. countable nounA detour is a special route for traffic to follow when the normal route is blocked, for example because it is being repaired. [US]regional note:   in BRIT, use diversion3. verbIf you detour, you make a detour. [V] ⇒ On the way back, Jarvis detoured to check the time of services at the church. [Also V prep]
