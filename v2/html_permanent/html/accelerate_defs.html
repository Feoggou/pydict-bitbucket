accelerate

(ækˈsɛləˌreɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to go, occur, or cause to go or occur more quickly; speed up2. (transitive) to cause to happen sooner than expected3. (transitive) to increase the velocity of (a body, reaction, etc); cause acceleration
Derived forms
acˈcelerable adjectiveacˈcelerative or acˈceleratory adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin accelerātus, from accelerāre to go faster, from ad- (intensive) + celerāre to hasten, from celer swift

Word forms: accelerates , accelerating , accelerated
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbIf the process or rate of something accelerates or if something accelerates it, it gets faster and faster. [V] ⇒ Growth will accelerate to 2.9 per cent next year. [V n] ⇒ The government is to accelerate its privatisation programme.2. verbWhen a moving vehicle accelerates, it goes faster and faster. [V] ⇒ Suddenly the car accelerated. [V prep/adv] ⇒ She accelerated away from the kerb.
