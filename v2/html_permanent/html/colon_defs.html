colon1

(ˈkəʊlən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. plural -lons the punctuation mark :, usually preceding an explanation or an example of what has gone before, a list, or an extended quotation2. plural -lons this mark used for certain other purposes, such as expressions of time, as in 2:45 p.m., or when a ratio is given in figures, as in 5:33. plural -la (-lə) (in classical prosody) a part of a rhythmic period with two to six feet and one principal accent or ictus
Word origin
C16: from Latin, from Greek kōlon limb, hence part of a strophe, clause of a sentence

colón - Spanish

(kəʊˈləʊn , koˈlon)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -lons or -lones (Spanish -ˈlones)1. the standard monetary unit of Costa Rica, divided into 100 céntimos2. the former standard monetary unit of El Salvador, divided into 100 centavos; replaced by the US dollar in 2001
Word origin
C19: American Spanish, from Spanish, after Cristóbal Colón Christopher Columbus
