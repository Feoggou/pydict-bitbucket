appropriate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (əˈprəʊprɪɪt )1. right or suitable; fitting2. rare particular; own ⇒ they had their appropriate methodsverb (əˈprəʊprɪˌeɪt ) (transitive)3. to take for one's own use, esp illegally or without permission4. to put aside (funds, etc) for a particular purpose or person
Derived forms
appropriable (apˈpropriable) adjectiveappropriately (apˈpropriately) adverbappropriateness (apˈpropriateness) nounappropriative (apˈpropriative) adjectiveappropriˌator (apˈpropriˌator) noun
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin appropriāre to make one's own, from Latin proprius one's own; see proper

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
pronunciation note:   The adjective is pronounced (əproʊpriət ). The verb is pronounced (əproʊprieɪt ).1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is appropriate is suitable or acceptable for a particular situation. ⇒ It is appropriate that Irish names dominate the list. ⇒ Dress neatly and attractively in an outfit appropriate to the job. ⇒ The teacher can then take appropriate action.appropriately graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Dress appropriately and ask intelligent questions. ⇒ It's entitled, appropriately enough, 'Art for the Nation'.appropriateness uncount noun [oft N of n] ⇒ He wonders about the appropriateness of each move he makes.2. verbIf someone appropriates something which does not belong to them, they take it, usually without the right to do so. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Several other newspapers have appropriated the idea. [V n] ⇒ The land was simply appropriated by the rulers.3. verbIf a government or organization appropriates an amount of money for a particular purpose, it reserves it for that purpose. [formal] earmark [V n] ⇒ Senator Lugar is skeptical that Congress will appropriate more funding for this purpose.
