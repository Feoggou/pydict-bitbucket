perplex

(pəˈplɛks )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to puzzle; bewilder; confuse2. to complicate ⇒ to perplex an issue

Word Origin

C15: from obsolete perplex (adj) intricate, from Latin perplexus entangled, from per- (thoroughly) + plectere to entwine

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf something perplexes you, it confuses and worries you because you do not understand it or because it causes you difficulty. [V n] ⇒ It perplexed him because he was tackling it the wrong way.
