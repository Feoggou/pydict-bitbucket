scrupulous

(ˈskruːpjʊləs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. characterized by careful observation of what is morally right2. very careful or precise

Derived Forms

scrupulously (ˈscrupulously) adverbscrupulousness (ˈscrupulousness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Latin scrūpulōsus punctilious

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Someone who is scrupulous takes great care to do what is fair, honest, or morally right. [approval] ⇒ You're being very scrupulous, but to what end? [+ about] ⇒ I have been scrupulous about telling them the dangers. [+ in] ⇒ The Board is scrupulous in its consideration of all applications for licences.scrupulously graded adverb ⇒ He is scrupulously fair, and popular with his staff. ⇒ Namibia has scrupulously upheld political pluralism.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Scrupulous means thorough, exact, and careful about details. ⇒ Both readers commend Knutson for his scrupulous attention to detail.scrupulously graded adverb ⇒ The streets and parks were scrupulously clean. ⇒ Hillsden scrupulously avoided any topic likely to arouse suspicion as to his motives.
