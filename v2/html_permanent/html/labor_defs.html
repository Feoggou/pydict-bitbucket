labor

(ˈleɪbər ) in American English
Definitions
noun1.  physical or mental exertion; work; toil2.  a specific task; piece of work3. a.  all wage-earning workers as a group. see also capital1. or managementb.  all manual workers whose work is characterized largely by physical exertion4.  labor unions collectively5.  the work accomplished by or the role in production of all workers, esp. workers for wages6.  [L-] Labor Party7.  Medicine the process or period of childbirth; parturition; esp., the muscular contractions of giving birth verb intransitive8.  to work; toil9.  to work hard; exert oneself to get or do something; strive10. a.  to move slowly and with difficulty ⇒ the car labored up the hillb.  to pitch and roll heavily ⇒ the ship labored in the rough sea11.  to be afflicted or burdened with a liability or limitation (with under) ⇒ to labor under a delusion12.  to undergo, and suffer the pains of, childbirth verb transitive13.  to spend too much time and effort on; develop in too great detail ⇒ to labor a point
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

OFr < L, labor, orig., hardship, pain, prob. < base of labi, to slip, totter: see lap1

(leɪbər ) for learners of American English
Word forms: labors, laboring, labored
Definitions
regional note:   in BRIT, use labour1. uncount noun [also N in pl]Labor is very hard work, usually physical work. ⇒ ...the labor of hauling the rocks away.2. intransitive verbSomeone who labors works hard using their hands. ⇒ ...he will be laboring 14 hundred meters below ground.3. transitive verb/intransitive verbIf you labor to do something, you do it with difficulty. ⇒ Scientists labored for months to unravel the mysteries of Neptune and still remain baffled. ⇒ We're laboring under an unfair disadvantage.4. uncount nounLabor is used to refer to the workers of a country or industry, considered as a group. ⇒ We have a problem of skilled labor. ⇒ Employers want cheap labor and consumers want cheap houses.5. uncount nounThe work done by a group of workers or by a particular worker is referred to as their labor. ⇒ He exhibits a profound humility in the low rates he pays himself for his labor.6. uncount nounLabor is the last stage of pregnancy, in which the baby is gradually pushed out of the womb by the mother. ⇒ Her labor had lasted ten hours before the doctor arranged a Cesarean section.
