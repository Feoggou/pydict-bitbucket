commiserate

(kəˈmɪzəˌreɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(when intr, usually foll by with) to feel or express sympathy or compassion (for)
Derived forms
commiserable (comˈmiserable) adjectivecomˌmiseration (comˌmiseˈration) nouncommiserative (comˈmiserative) adjectivecommiseratively (comˈmiseratively) adverbcommiserˌator (comˈmiserˌator) noun
Word origin
C17: from Latin commiserārī, from com- together + miserārī to bewail, pity, from miser wretched

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you commiserate with someone, you show them pity or sympathy when something unpleasant has happened to them. [V + with] ⇒ When I lost, he commiserated with me.commiseration (kəmɪzəreɪʃən )Word forms: commiserations uncount noun [also N in pl] ⇒ After half an hour's commiseration, we turned to more practical matters. ⇒ We have sent the team our commiserations.
