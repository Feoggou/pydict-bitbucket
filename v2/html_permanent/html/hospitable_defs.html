hospitable

(ˈhɒspɪtəbəl , hɒˈspɪt-)
Definitions
adjective1. welcoming to guests or strangers2. fond of entertaining3. receptive ⇒ hospitable to new ideas
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hospitableness (ˈhospitableness) nounhospitably (ˈhospitably) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin hospitāre to receive as a guest, from Latin hospes guest, host1

hospitable

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒspɪtəbəl , hɒspɪt-) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveA hospitable person is friendly, generous, and welcoming to guests or people they have just met. OPPOSITE: inhospitable ⇒ The locals are hospitable and welcoming. ⇒ He was very hospitable to me when I came to New York.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA hospitable climate or environment is one that encourages the existence or development of particular people or things. OPPOSITE: inhospitable, hostile ⇒ Even in summer this place did not look exactly hospitable. ⇒ ...hospitable political environments.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
