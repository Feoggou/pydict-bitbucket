cruciform

(ˈkruːsɪˌfɔːm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. shaped like a crossnoun2. a geometric curve, shaped like a cross, that has four similar branches asymptotic to two mutually perpendicular pairs of lines. Equation: x2y2 – a2x2 – a2y2 = 0, where x = y = ± a are the four lines
Derived forms
cruciˌformly (ˈcruciˌformly) adverb
Word origin
C17: from Latin crux cross + -form
