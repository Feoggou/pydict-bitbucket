filial

(ˈfɪljəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of, resembling, or suitable to a son or daughter ⇒ filial affection2. genetics designating any of the generations following the parental generation; F1 indicates the first filial generation, F2 the second, etc. Abbreviation: F
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

filially (ˈfilially) adverbfilialness (ˈfilialness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin fīliālis, from Latin fīlius son

filial

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪliəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
adjective [ADJ n]You can use filial to describe the duties, feelings, or relationships which exist between a son or daughter and his or her parents. [formal] ⇒ His father would accuse him of neglecting his filial duties. ⇒ Right now I'm in need of a little filial affection.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
