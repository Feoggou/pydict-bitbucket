ochre

or US ocher (ˈəʊkə )
Definitions
noun1. any of various natural earths containing ferric oxide, silica, and alumina: used as yellow or red pigments2. a. a moderate yellow-orange to orange colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ an ochre dressverb3. (transitive) to colour with ochre
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ochreous (ˈəʊkrɪəs , ˈəʊkərəs ), ochrous (ˈəʊkrəs ), ochry (ˈəʊkərɪ , ˈəʊkrɪ ) or US ocherous (ˈocherous) or ochery (ˈochery) adjectiveochroid (ˈəʊkrɔɪd ) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French ocre, from Latin ōchra, from Greek ōkhra, from ōkhros pale yellow
