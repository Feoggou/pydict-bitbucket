sudden

(ˈsʌdən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. occurring or performed quickly and without warning2. marked by haste; abrupt3. rare rash; precipitatenoun4. archaic an abrupt occurrence or the occasion of such an occurrence (in the phrase on a sudden)5. See all of a suddenadverb6. mainly poetic without warning; suddenly

Derived Forms

suddenness (ˈsuddenness) noun

Word Origin

C13: via French from Late Latin subitāneus, from Latin subitus unexpected, from subīre to happen unexpectedly, from sub- secretly + īre to go
