entertain

(ˌɛntəˈteɪn )
Definitions
verb1. to provide amusement for (a person or audience)2. to show hospitality to (guests)3. (transitive) to hold in the mind ⇒ to entertain an idea
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Old French entretenir, from entre- mutually + tenir to hold, from Latin tenēre

entertain

Powered by COBUILD
(entəʳteɪn ) for learners of English
Word forms: entertains , entertaining , entertained
Definitions
1. verbIf a performer, performance, or activity entertains you, it amuses you, interests you, or gives you pleasure. [V n] ⇒ ...games and ideas to entertain children. [be V-ed] ⇒ They were entertained by top singers, dancers and celebrities. [V] ⇒ Children's television not only entertains but also teaches.entertaining graded adjective & adjective ⇒ To generate new money the sport needs to be more entertaining. ⇒ This is a surprisingly entertaining film. ⇒ Miro is the most inventive and entertaining of surrealist painters.2. verbIf you entertain people, you provide food and drink for them, for example when you have invited them to your house. [V n] ⇒ I don't like to entertain guests anymore. [V n] ⇒ You weren't allowed to entertain men in your rooms even with a chaperone. [V] ⇒ The Monroes continued to entertain extravagantly.entertaining uncount noun ⇒ ...a cosy area for entertaining and relaxing.3. verbIf you entertain an idea or suggestion, you allow yourself to consider it as possible or as worth thinking about seriously. [formal] [V n] ⇒ I feel how foolish I am to entertain doubts. [V n] ⇒ I wouldn't entertain the idea of such an unsociable job.
