mammoth

(ˈmæməθ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any large extinct elephant of the Pleistocene genus Mammuthus (or Elephas), such as M. primigenius (woolly mammoth), having a hairy coat and long curved tusksadjective2. of gigantic size or importance

Word Origin

C18: from Russian mamot, from Tatar mamont, perhaps from mamma earth, because of a belief that the animal made burrows

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]You can use mammoth to emphasize that a task or change is very large and needs a lot of effort to achieve. [emphasis] ⇒ ...the mammoth task of relocating the library. ⇒ You can only undertake mammoth changes if the finances are there. ⇒ This mammoth undertaking was completed in 18 months.2. countable nounA mammoth was an animal like an elephant, with very long tusks and long hair, that lived a long time ago but no longer exists.
