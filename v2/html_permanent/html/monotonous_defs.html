monotonous

(məˈnɒtənəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. dull and tedious, esp because of repetition2. unvarying in pitch or cadence

Derived Forms

monotonously (moˈnotonously) adverbmonotonousness (moˈnotonousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is monotonous is very boring because it has a regular, repeated pattern which never changes. ⇒ It's monotonous work, like most factory jobs. ⇒ The food may get a bit monotonous, but there'll be enough of it.monotonously graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The rain dripped monotonously from the trees.
