role-play

or role play (ˈrəʊlpleɪ)
Definitions
nounthe act of imitating the role of a different person, for example as a training exercise or in language learning ⇒ Group members have to communicate with each other through role-play., ⇒ Ask a friend to give you some practice, through role-play, on these areas., ⇒ Divide into groups and use role-play to practise making your request.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

role play

Powered by COBUILD
also role-play for learners of English
Word forms: role plays , role playing , role played
Definitions
1. variable nounRole play is the act of imitating the character and behaviour of someone who is different from yourself, for example as a training exercise. ⇒ Group members have to communicate with each other through role-play.2. verbIf people role play, they do a role play. [V n] ⇒ Rehearse and role-play the interview with a friend beforehand. [Also V]role playing uncount noun ⇒ We did a lot of role playing.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
