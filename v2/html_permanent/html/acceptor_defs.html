acceptor

(əkˈsɛptə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. business the person or organization on which a draft or bill of exchange is drawn after liability has been accepted, usually by signature2. Also called: acceptor impurity electronics an impurity, such as gallium, added to a semiconductor material to increase its p-type conductivity by increasing the number of holes in the semiconductor. Compare donor (sense 5)3. electronics a circuit tuned to accept a particular frequency4. chemistry the atom or group that accepts electrons in a coordinate bond
