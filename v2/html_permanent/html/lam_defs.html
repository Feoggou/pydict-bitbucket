lam1

(læm )
Definitions
verbWord forms: lams, lamming or lammed slang1. (transitive) to thrash or beat2. (intr; usually foll by into or out) to make a sweeping stroke or blow
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Scandinavian; related to Old Norse lemja

lam2 - US and Canadian slang

(læm )
Definitions
noun1. a sudden flight or escape, esp to avoid arrest2. See on the lamverbWord forms: lams, lamming or lammed3. (intransitive) to escape or flee
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: perhaps from lam1 (hence, to be off)
