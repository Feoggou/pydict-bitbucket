swain

(sweɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun archaic or poetic1. a male lover or admirer2. a country youth

Derived Forms

swainish (ˈswainish) adjective

Word Origin

Old English swān swineherd; related to Old High German swein, Old Norse sveinn boy; see swine
