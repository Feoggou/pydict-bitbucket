parch

(pɑːtʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to deprive or be deprived of water; dry up ⇒ the sun parches the fields2. (tr; usually passive) to make very thirsty ⇒ I was parched after the run3. (transitive) to roast (corn, etc) lightly

Word Origin

C14: of obscure origin

Similar Words

dry up, burn, sear, blister, wither, evaporate, scorch, shrivel, dehydrate, desiccate, make thirsty
