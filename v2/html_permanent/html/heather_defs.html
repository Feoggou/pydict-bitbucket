heather

(ˈhɛðə )
Definitions
noun1. Also called: ling, heath a low-growing evergreen Eurasian ericaceous shrub, Calluna vulgaris, that grows in dense masses on open ground and has clusters of small bell-shaped typically pinkish-purple flowers2. any of certain similar plants3. a purplish-red to pinkish-purple colouradjective4. of a heather colour5. of or relating to interwoven yarns of mixed colours ⇒ heather mixture
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

heathered (ˈheathered) adjectiveheathery (ˈheathery) adjective

Word Origin

C14: originally Scottish and Northern English, probably from heath
