filch

(fɪltʃ )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to steal or take surreptitiously in small amounts; pilfer
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

filcher (ˈfilcher) noun

Word Origin

C16 filchen to steal, attack, perhaps from Old English gefylce band of men

filch

Powered by COBUILD
(fɪltʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: filches , filching , filched
Definitions
verbIf you say that someone filches something, you mean they steal it, especially when you do not consider this to be a very serious crime. [informal] [V n] ⇒ I filched some notes from his wallet.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
Example sentences containing 'filch'
To drive her, distract her, so she didn't have time to filch said reins from his grasp. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
