craven

(ˈkreɪvən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. cowardly; mean-spiritednoun2. a coward
Derived forms
cravenly (ˈcravenly) adverbcravenness (ˈcravenness) noun
Word origin
C13 cravant, probably from Old French crevant bursting, from crever to burst, die, from Latin crepāre to burst, crack

Definitions

graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is craven is very cowardly. [written, disapproval] ⇒ They condemned the deal as a craven surrender. ⇒ ...his craven obedience to his employers.
