cruelty

(ˈkruːəltɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. deliberate infliction of pain or suffering2. the quality or characteristic of being cruel3. a cruel action4. law conduct that causes danger to life or limb or a threat to bodily or mental health, on proof of which a decree of divorce may be granted

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounCruelty is behaviour that deliberately causes pain or distress to people or animals. [+ to] ⇒ Britain had laws against cruelty to animals but none to protect children. [+ of] ⇒ He had been unable to escape the cruelties of war.
