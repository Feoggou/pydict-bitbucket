quagmire

(ˈkwæɡˌmaɪə , ˈkwɒɡ-)
Definitions
noun1. a soft wet area of land that gives way under the feet; bog2. an awkward, complex, or embarrassing situation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from quag + mire

quagmire

Powered by COBUILD
(kwægmaɪəʳ ) for learners of English
Word forms: quagmires
Definitions
1. countable nounA quagmire is a difficult, complicated, or unpleasant situation which is not easy to avoid or escape from. [+ of] ⇒ His people had fallen further and further into a quagmire of confusion. ⇒ We have no intention of being drawn into a political quagmire.2. countable noun [usu sing]A quagmire is a soft, wet area of land which your feet sink into if you try to walk across it. ⇒ Rain had turned the grass into a quagmire.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
