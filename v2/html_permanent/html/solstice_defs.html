solstice

(ˈsɒlstɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. either the shortest day of the year (winter solstice) or the longest day of the year (summer solstice)2. either of the two points on the ecliptic at which the sun is overhead at the tropic of Cancer or Capricorn at the summer and winter solstices
Derived forms
solstitial (sɒlˈstɪʃəl ) adjectivesolstitially (solˈstitially) adverb
Word origin
C13: via Old French from Latin sōlstitium, literally: the (apparent) standing still of the sun, from sōl sun + sistere to stand still
