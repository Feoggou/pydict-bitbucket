bribe

(braɪb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to promise, offer, or give something, usually money, to (a person) to procure services or gain influence, esp illegallynoun2. a reward, such as money or favour, given or offered for this purpose3. any persuasion or lure4. a length of flawed or damaged cloth removed from the main piece
Derived forms
bribable (ˈbribable) or bribeable (ˈbribeable) adjectivebriber (ˈbriber) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French briber to beg, of obscure origin

bribe

(braɪb )
Word forms: bribes , bribing , bribed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA bribe is a sum of money or something valuable that one person offers or gives to another in order to persuade him or her to do something. ⇒ He was being investigated for receiving bribes.2. verbIf one person bribes another, they give them a bribe. [V n] ⇒ He was accused of bribing a senior bank official. [V n to-inf] ⇒ The government bribed the workers to be quiet.
