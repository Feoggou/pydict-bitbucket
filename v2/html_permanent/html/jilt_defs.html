jilt

(dʒɪlt )
Definitions
verb1. (transitive) to leave or reject (a lover), esp without previous warning ⇒ she was jilted at the altarnoun2. a woman who jilts a lover
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jilter (ˈjilter) noun

Word Origin

C17: from dialect jillet flighty girl, diminutive of proper name Gill

jilt for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒɪlt )
Word forms: jilts , jilting , jilted
Definitions
verbIf someone is jilted, the person they are having a romantic relationship with suddenly ends the relationship in a surprising and upsetting way. [informal] [be V-ed] ⇒ She was jilted by her first fiancé. [V n] ⇒ Driven to distraction, he murdered the woman who jilted him. [V-ed] ⇒ Police want to interview her jilted lover. [Also V n]
