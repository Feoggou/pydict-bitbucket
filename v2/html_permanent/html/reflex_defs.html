reflex

Definitions
noun (ˈriːflɛks )1. a. an immediate involuntary response, esp one that is innate, such as coughing or removal of the hand from a hot surface, evoked by a given stimulusb. (as modifier) ⇒ a reflex action. See also reflex arc2. a. a mechanical response to a particular situation, involving no conscious decisionb. (as modifier) ⇒ a reflex response3. a reflection; an image produced by or as if by reflection4. a speech element derived from a corresponding form in an earlier state of the language ⇒ "sorrow" is a reflex of Middle English "sorwe"adjective (ˈriːflɛks )5. mathematics (of an angle) between 180° and 360°6. (prenominal) turned, reflected, or bent backwardsverb (rɪˈflɛks )7. (transitive) to bend, turn, or reflect backwards
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

reflexible (reˈflexible) adjectivereflexibility (reˌflexiˈbility) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin reflexus bent back, from reflectere to reflect

(riːfleks ) for learners of English
Word forms: reflexes
Definitions
1. countable nounA reflex or a reflex action is something that you do automatically and without thinking, as a habit or as a reaction to something. ⇒ Walsh fumbled in his pocket, a reflex from his smoking days. ⇒ I turned to look inside the house in a reflex action.2. countable nounA reflex or a reflex action is a normal, uncontrollable reaction of your body to something that you feel, see, or experience. ⇒ ...tests for reflexes, like tapping the knee or the heel with a rubber hammer. ⇒ ...the stress hormone adrenaline, released by reflex action from the adrenal glands.3. plural nounYour reflexes are your ability to react quickly with your body when something unexpected happens, for example when you are involved in sport or when you are driving a car. reactions ⇒ It takes great skill, cool nerves and the reflexes of an athlete.
