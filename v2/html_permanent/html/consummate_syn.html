consummate

adjective

  1. skilled, perfect, supreme, polished, superb, practised, accomplished, tasty (British, informal), matchless • He acted the part with consummate skill.
  2. complete, total, supreme, extreme, ultimate, absolute, utter, conspicuous, unqualified, deep-dyed (usually derogatory) • He was a consummate liar and exaggerator.

verb

  1. complete, finish, achieve, conclude, perform, perfect, carry out, crown, fulfil, end, accomplish, effectuate, put the tin lid on • No one has yet been able to consummate a deal. OPPOSITES:  start, begin, initiate, conceive, originate, commence, get under way, inaugurate
