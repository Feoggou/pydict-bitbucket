dwarf

(dwɔːf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural dwarfs or dwarves (dwɔːvz )1. an abnormally undersized person2. a. an animal or plant much below the average height for the speciesb. (as modifier) ⇒ a dwarf tree3. (in folklore) a small ugly manlike creature, often possessing magical powers4. astronomy short for dwarf starverb5. to become or cause to become comparatively small in size, importance, etc6. (transitive) to stunt the growth of
Derived forms
dwarfish (ˈdwarfish) adjectivedwarfishly (ˈdwarfishly) adverbdwarfishness (ˈdwarfishness) noun
Word origin
Old English dweorg; related to Old Norse dvergr, Old High German twerc

Powered by COBUILD
(dwɔːʳf )
Word forms: dwarfs , dwarves , dwarfs , dwarfing , dwarfed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf one person or thing is dwarfed by another, the second is so much bigger than the first that it makes them look very small. [be V-ed] ⇒ His figure is dwarfed by the huge red McDonald's sign. [V n] ⇒ The U.S. air travel market dwarfs that of Britain.2. countable noun [with supp]Dwarf is used to describe a particular kind of star which is quite small and not very bright. ⇒ ...a white dwarf star. ⇒ ...a red dwarf.3. adjective [ADJ n]Dwarf is used to describe varieties or species of plants and animals which are much smaller than the usual size for their kind. ⇒ ...dwarf shrubs.4. countable nounIn children's stories, a dwarf is an imaginary creature that is like a small man. Dwarfs often have magical powers.5. countable nounIn former times, people who were much smaller than normal were called dwarfs. [offensive, old-fashioned]
