confirmation

(ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of confirming2. something that confirms; verification3. a rite in several Christian churches that confirms a baptized person in his or her faith and admits him or her to full participation in the church4. (in the philosophy of science) the relationship between an observation and the theory which it supposedly renders more probable. Compare hypothetico-deductive
Example sentences containing 'confirmation'
Cloutier's story and photos merely brought you confirmation. Gagman, Maurice DOUBTFUL MOTIVESShe nibbled her lower lip and looked at her sister, but merely gained confirmation that something was very, very wrong. Ashford, Jeffrey A QUESTION OF PRINCIPLEThe final and verbal confirmation of all my documentary proof. Robert Wilson THE COMPANY OF STRANGERSBut they still required, occasionally, some personal confirmation of their prominent positions in the grand scheme of things. Patrick Robinson NIMITZ CLASS
