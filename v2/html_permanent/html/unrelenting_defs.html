unrelenting

(ˌʌnrɪˈlɛntɪŋ )
Definitions
adjective1. refusing to relent or take pity; relentless; merciless2. not diminishing in determination, speed, effort, force, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unrelentingly (ˌunreˈlentingly) adverbunrelentingness (ˌunreˈlentingness) noun

unrelenting for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnrɪlentɪŋ )
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf you describe someone's behaviour as unrelenting, you mean that they are continuing to do something in a very determined way, often without caring whether they hurt or embarrass other people. ⇒ She established her authority with unrelenting thoroughness. ⇒ In the face of severe opposition and unrelenting criticism, the task seemed overwhelming.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something unpleasant as unrelenting, you mean that it continues without stopping. ceaseless ⇒ ...an unrelenting downpour of rain. ⇒ ...the unrelenting hardship of everyday life in medieval Europe.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
