malicious

(məˈlɪʃəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. characterized by malice2. motivated by wrongful, vicious, or mischievous purposes

Derived Forms

maliciously (maˈliciously) adverbmaliciousness (maˈliciousness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone's words or actions as malicious, you mean that they are intended to harm people or their reputation, or cause them embarrassment and upset. ⇒ That might merely have been malicious gossip. ⇒ She described the charges as malicious.maliciously graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v, oft ADV adj] ⇒ 'Oh, I stopped in at the club for a drink,' she said maliciously. ⇒ ...his maliciously accurate imitation of Hubert de Burgh.
