bollard

(ˈbɒlɑːd , ˈbɒləd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a strong wooden or metal post mounted on a wharf, quay, etc, used for securing mooring lines2. British a small post or marker placed on a kerb or traffic island to make it conspicuous to motorists3. mountaineering an outcrop of rock or pillar of ice that may be used to belay a rope
Word origin
C14: perhaps from bole1 + -ard
