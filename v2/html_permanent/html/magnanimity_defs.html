magnanimity

(ˌmæɡnəˈnɪmɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tiesgenerosity

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin magnanimitās, from magnus great + animus soul

magnanimity

Powered by COBUILD
(mægnənɪmɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounMagnanimity is kindness and generosity towards someone, especially after defeating them or being treated badly by them. [formal] ⇒ We will have to show magnanimity in victory. ⇒ The father of one victim spoke with remarkable magnanimity.
Example sentences containing 'magnanimity'
He was all magnanimity and grandness, and he cleared his throat and delivered a great sermon that boomed like thunder through the nave. Zindell, David THE BROKEN GODHe returned to the shop wreathed in magnanimity and blinding smiles. Daisy Waugh TEN STEPS TO HAPPINESSWhich suggested she should treat his current vacillation with some degree of magnanimity. Stephanie Laurens ON A WICKED DAWN
