Cistercian

(sɪˈstɜːʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna. Also called: White Monk a member of a Christian order of monks and nuns founded in 1098, which follows an especially strict form of the Benedictine ruleb. (as modifier) ⇒ a Cistercian monk
Word origin
C17: from French Cistercien, from Medieval Latin Cisterciānus, from Cistercium (modern Cîteaux), original home of the order
Example sentences containing 'Cistercian'
