kirtle

(ˈkɜːtəl )
Definitions
noun archaic1. a woman's skirt or dress2. a man's coat
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English cyrtel, probably from cyrtan to shorten, ultimately from Latin curtus cut short
Example sentences containing 'kirtle'
She sat at the high table, her fine wool kirtle and tunic as blue as her eyes. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFThe gravy dripped on to the wooden table and on to my lap, the hot grease sinking into the blue cloth of my kirtle. Harris, Elizabeth TIME OF THE WOLFShe felt warm in her underdress of woollen kirtle and green cord-bound gown. Grace, C.L A SHRINE OF MURDERSIn his search he tossed her torn kirtle on the bed without regard for the damage he had done. Kathleen E. Woodiwiss THE WOLF AND THE DOVE
