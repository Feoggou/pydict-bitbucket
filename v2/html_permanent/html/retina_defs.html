retina

(ˈrɛtɪnə )
Definitions
noun plural -nas or -nae (-ˌniː)the light-sensitive membrane forming the inner lining of the posterior wall of the eyeball, composed largely of a specialized terminal expansion of the optic nerve. Images focused here by the lens of the eye are transmitted to the brain as nerve impulses
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

retinal (ˈretinal) adjective

Word Origin

C14: from Medieval Latin, perhaps from Latin rēte net
