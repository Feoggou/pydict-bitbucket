casting

(ˈkɑːstɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an object or figure that has been cast, esp in metal from a mould2. the process of transferring molten steel to a mould3. the choosing of actors for a production4. hunting the act of directing a pack of hounds over ground where their quarry may recently have passed so that they can quest for, discover, or recapture its scent5. zoology another word for cast (sense 28), pellet (sense 4)

noun(Mechanical engineering: Manufacturing and assembly) Casting is a process in which a material such as metal or plastic in liquid form is poured into a mold and allowed to become hard, in order to make parts or products. ⇒ Casting is the most efficient way of making complex metal shapes. ⇒ Intricately shaped components that are difficult to produce by other methods can be produced by casting. ⇒ Casting is a process in which a material such as metal or plastic in liquid form is poured into a mold and allowed to become hard, in order to make parts or products.
