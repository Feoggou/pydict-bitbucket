vicegerent

(ˌvaɪsˈdʒɛrənt )
Definitions
noun1. a person appointed to exercise all or some of the authority of another, esp the administrative powers of a ruler; deputy2. Roman Catholic Church the Pope or any other representative of God or Christ on earth, such as a bishopadjective3. invested with or characterized by delegated authority
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vicegeral (ˌviceˈgeral) adjectivevicegerency (ˌviceˈgerency) noun

Word Origin

C16: from New Latin vicegerēns, from vice3 + Latin gerere to manage
