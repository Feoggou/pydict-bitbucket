dregs

(drɛɡz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. solid particles that tend to settle at the bottom of some liquids, such as wine or coffee2. residue or remains3. British slang a despicable person
Word origin
C14 dreg, from Old Norse dregg; compare Icelandic dreggjar dregs, Latin fracēs oil dregs

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. plural nounThe dregs of a liquid are the last drops left at the bottom of a container, together with any solid bits that have sunk to the bottom. [disapproval] ⇒ Colum drained the dregs from his cup.2. plural nounIf you talk about the dregs of society or of a community, you mean the people in it who you consider to be the most worthless and bad. [disapproval] ⇒ He sees dissidents as the dregs of society.
