strengthening

(ˈstrɛŋθənɪŋ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the reinforcing (of something) ⇒ The strengthening of the banking system will attract foreign investment., ⇒ One of its major achievements of was the strengthening of democracy in the poorer nations of the West., ⇒ From the point of view of productivity and competitiveness, the progressive strengthening of the role of non-executive directors is strongly desirable., ⇒ It was hoped the visit would lead to a strengthening of relations between the two countries., ⇒ The net result was to bring about the very strengthening of the ties between the two countries that he most feared., ⇒ This would speed up the strengthening of EU powers.2. the consolidation (of something) ⇒ I think the strengthening of the dollar has encouraged some foreign money to flow back into our markets., ⇒ They reported a strengthening of sales in the shops in November., ⇒ Moreover, every strengthening of our affections toward God will weaken the world's grip.adjective3. intended or helping to improve and make something stronger ⇒ stretching and strengthening exercises, ⇒ She thought the stretching and strengthening exercises might help his ailing knees., ⇒ It has a strengthening effect on the immune system.4. economics becoming more valuable or more productive ⇒ a strengthening economy, ⇒ the inability of American firms to compete with foreign firms because of the strengthening dollar at that time5. (of winds) becoming stronger ⇒ The forecast was for strengthening winds., ⇒ There was a short sharp shower followed by a strengthening breeze.
