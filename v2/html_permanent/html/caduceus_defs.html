caduceus

(kəˈdjuːsɪəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -cei (-sɪˌaɪ)1. classical mythology a staff entwined with two serpents and bearing a pair of wings at the top, carried by Hermes (Mercury) as messenger of the gods2. an insignia resembling this staff used as an emblem of the medical profession. Compare staff of Aesculapius
Word origin
C16: from Latin, from Doric Greek karukeion, from karux herald
