depose

(dɪˈpəʊz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to remove from an office or position, esp one of power or rank2. law to testify or give (evidence, etc) on oath, esp when taken down in writing; make a deposition
Derived forms
deposable (deˈposable) adjectivedeposer (deˈposer) noun
Word origin
C13: from Old French deposer to put away, put down, from Late Latin dēpōnere to depose from office, from Latin: to put aside; see depone
