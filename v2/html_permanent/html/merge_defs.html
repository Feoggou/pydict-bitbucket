merge

(mɜːdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to meet and join or cause to meet and join2. to blend or cause to blend; fuse

Derived Forms

mergence (ˈmergence) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin mergere to plunge

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative reciprocal verb & verbIf one thing merges with another, or is merged with another, they combine or come together to make one whole thing. You can also say that two things merge, or are merged. [V with n] ⇒ My life merged with his. [V + with] ⇒ Bank of America merged with a rival bank. [V] ⇒ The rivers merge just north of a vital irrigation system. [V + into] ⇒ The two countries merged into one. [V n] ⇒ He sees sense in merging the two agencies while both are new. [V n + with] ⇒ Then he showed me how to merge the graphic with text on the same screen. [Also V n + into]2. verbIf one sound, colour, or object merges into another, the first changes so gradually into the second that you do not notice the change. [V + into] ⇒ Like a chameleon, he could merge unobtrusively into the background. [V + with] ⇒ His features merged with the darkness. [V] ⇒ Night and day begin to merge.
