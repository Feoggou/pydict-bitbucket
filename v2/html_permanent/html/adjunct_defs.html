adjunct

(ˈædʒʌŋkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. something incidental or not essential that is added to something else2. a person who is subordinate to another3. grammara. part of a sentence other than the subject or the predicateb. (in systemic grammar) part of a sentence other than the subject, predicator, object, or complement; usually a prepositional or adverbial groupc. part of a sentence that may be omitted without making the sentence ungrammatical; a modifier4. logic another name for accident (sense 4)adjective5. added or connected in a secondary or subordinate position; auxiliary
Derived forms
adjunctive (əˈdʒʌŋktɪv ) adjectiveadjunctively (əˈdʒʌŋktɪvlɪ) adverbˈadjunctly adverb
Word origin
C16: from Latin adjunctus, past participle of adjungere to adjoin

(ædʒʌŋkt )
Word forms: adjuncts
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounSomething that is an adjunct to something larger or more important is connected with it or helps to perform the same task. [+ to] ⇒ Physical therapy is an important adjunct to drug treatments. ⇒ The Party was certainly not an official adjunct of the police department. [Also + of]2. countable nounIn grammar, an adjunct is a word or group of words which indicates the circumstances of an action, event, or situation. An adjunct is usually a prepositional phrase or an adverb group.
