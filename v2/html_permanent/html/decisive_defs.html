decisive

(dɪˈsaɪsɪv ) or decisory (dɪˈsaɪsərɪ)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. influential; conclusive ⇒ a decisive argument2. characterized by the ability to make decisions, esp quickly; resolute
Derived forms
decisively (deˈcisively) adverbdecisiveness (deˈcisiveness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf a fact, action, or event is decisive, it makes it certain that there will be a particular result. ⇒ ...his decisive victory in the presidential elections. ⇒ The election campaign has now entered its final, decisive phase. ⇒ The meeting between Molotov, Bidault and Bevin was decisive.decisively adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ The plan was decisively rejected by Congress three weeks ago.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf someone is decisive, they have or show an ability to make quick decisions in a difficult or complicated situation. ⇒ He should give way to a younger, more decisive leader.decisively graded adverb & adverb ⇒ 'I'll call for you at half ten,' she said decisively.decisiveness uncount noun ⇒ His supporters admire his decisiveness.
