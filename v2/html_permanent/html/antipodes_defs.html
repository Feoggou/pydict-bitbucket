antipodes

(ænˈtɪpəˌdiːz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. either or both of two points, places, or regions that are situated diametrically opposite to one another on the earth's surface, esp the country or region opposite one's own2. the people who live there3. See the antipodes4. (sometimes functioning as singular) the exact or direct opposite
Derived forms
antipodean (ænˌtɪpəˈdiːən ) adjective, noun
Word origin
C16: via Late Latin from Greek, plural of antipous having the feet opposite, from anti- + pous foot
