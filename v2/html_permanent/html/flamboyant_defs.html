flamboyant

(flæmˈbɔɪənt )
Definitions
adjective1. elaborate or extravagant; florid; showy2. rich or brilliant in colour; resplendent3. exuberant or ostentatious4. of, denoting, or relating to the French Gothic style of architecture characterized by flamelike tracery and elaborate carvingnoun5. Also called: flamboyante another name for royal poinciana
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

flamboyance (flamˈboyance) or flamboyancy (flamˈboyancy) nounflamboyantly (flamˈboyantly) adverb

Word Origin

C19: from French: flaming, from flamboyer to flame

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is flamboyant, you mean that they are very noticeable, stylish, and exciting. ⇒ Freddie Mercury was a flamboyant star of the British hard rock scene. ⇒ ...his lightning speed and flamboyant, aggressive style of play. ⇒ He wears flamboyant clothes more suited to a rock star than a literary figure.flamboyance uncount noun ⇒ Campese was his usual mixture of flamboyance and flair.flamboyantly graded adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ She dressed flamboyantly so that everyone would remember her.
