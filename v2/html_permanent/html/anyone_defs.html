anyone

(ˈɛnɪˌwʌn , -wən)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
pronoun1. any person; anybody2. (used with a negative or a question) a person of any importance ⇒ is he anyone in this town?3. (often preceded by just) any person at random; no matter who

anyone

Powered by COBUILD
(eniwʌn ) or anybody
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anyone or anybody in statements with negative meaning to indicate in a general way that nobody is present or involved in an action. ⇒ I won't tell anyone I saw you here. ⇒ You needn't talk to anyone if you don't want to. ⇒ He was far too scared to tell anybody. ⇒ Presidents are not any different from anybody else; they're human beings.2. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anyone or anybody in questions and conditional clauses to ask or talk about whether someone is present or doing something. ⇒ Why would anyone want that job? ⇒ How can anyone look sad at an occasion like this? ⇒ If anyone deserves to be happy, you do.3. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anyone or anybody before words which indicate the kind of person you are talking about. ⇒ I always had been the person who achieved things before anyone else at my age. ⇒ It's not a job for anyone who is slow with numbers. ⇒ Anybody interested in pop culture at all should buy 'Pure Cult'.4. indefinite pronoun & pronounYou use anyone or anybody to refer to a person when you are emphasizing that it could be any person out of a very large number of people. [emphasis] ⇒ Anyone could be doing what I'm doing. ⇒ Al Smith could make anybody laugh.5. →  anyone who is anyone/anybody who is anybody
