slay

(sleɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: slays, slaying, slew or slain (transitive)1. archaic or literary to kill, esp violently2. slang to impress (someone) sexually3. obsolete to strike

Derived Forms

slayer (ˈslayer) noun

Word Origin

Old English slēan; related to Old Norse slā, Gothic, Old High German slahan to strike, Old Irish slacaim I beat
