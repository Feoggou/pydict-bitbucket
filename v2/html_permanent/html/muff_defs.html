muff1

(mʌf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an open-ended cylinder of fur or cloth into which the hands are placed for warmth2. the tuft on either side of the head of certain fowls3. British taboo, slang the female genitals

Word Origin

C16: probably from Dutch mof, ultimately from French mouffle muffle1

muff2

(mʌf )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to perform (an action) awkwardly2. (transitive) to bungle (a shot, catch, etc) in a gamenoun3. any unskilful play in a game, esp a dropped catch4. any clumsy or bungled action5. a bungler

Word Origin

C19: of uncertain origin

muff

Powered by COBUILD
(mʌf )
Word forms: muffs , muffing , muffed
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you muff something, you do it badly or you make a mistake while you are doing it, so that it is not successful. [informal] [V n] ⇒ He muffed his opening speech. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a muffed opportunity.2. countable nounA muff is a piece of fur or thick cloth shaped like a short hollow cylinder. You wear a muff on your hands to keep them warm in cold weather.3. plural nounMuffs are the same as earmuffs.
