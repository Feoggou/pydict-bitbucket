mannerism

(ˈmænəˌrɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a distinctive and individual gesture or trait; idiosyncrasy2. (often capital) a principally Italian movement in art and architecture between the High Renaissance and Baroque periods (1520–1600) that sought to represent an ideal of beauty rather than natural images of it, using characteristic distortion and exaggeration of human proportions, perspective, etc3. adherence to a distinctive or affected manner, esp in art or literature

Derived Forms

mannerist (ˈmannerist) nounˌmanneristic (ˌmannerˈistic) or ˌmanneristical (ˌmannerˈistical) adjectiveˌmanneristically (ˌmannerˈistically) adverb
