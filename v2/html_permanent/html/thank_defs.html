thank

(θæŋk )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to convey feelings of gratitude to2. to hold responsible ⇒ he has his creditors to thank for his bankruptcy3. used in exclamations of relief ⇒ thank goodness, ⇒ thank God4. See I'll thank you to
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English thancian; related to Old Frisian thankia, Old Norse thakka, Old Saxon, Old High German thancōn

(θæŋk ) for learners of English
Word forms: thanks , thanking , thanked
Definitions
1. conventionYou use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to express your gratitude when someone does something for you or gives you what you want. [formulae] ⇒ Thank you very much for your call. [+ for] ⇒ Thanks for the information. ⇒ Oh thank you so much! They're so pretty! ⇒ Thanks a lot, Suzie. You've been great.2. conventionYou use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to politely accept or refuse something that has just been offered to you. [formulae] ⇒ 'You'd like a cup as well, would you, Mr Secombe?'—'Thank you, Jane, I'd love one.' ⇒ 'Would you like a cigarette?'—'No thank you.' ⇒ 'A whisky?'—'I'd better not, thanks.'3. conventionYou use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to politely acknowledge what someone has said to you, especially when they have answered your question or said something nice to you. [formulae] ⇒ The policeman smiled at her. 'Pretty dog.'—'Oh well, thank you.'. ⇒ 'His eyes were glassy?'—'And dilated. They were watery.'—'Thank you.' ⇒ 'It's great to see you.'—'Thanks. Same to you.'4. conventionYou use thank you or thank you very much in order to say firmly that you do not want someone's help or to tell them that you do not like the way that they are behaving towards you. [emphasis] ⇒ I can stir my own tea, thank you. ⇒ We know where we can get it, thank you very much.5. verbWhen you thank someone for something, you express your gratitude to them for it. [V n + for] ⇒ I thanked them for their long and loyal service. [V n] ⇒ When the decision was read out Mrs Gardner thanked the judges.6. plural nounWhen you express your thanks to someone, you express your gratitude to them for something. ⇒ They accepted their certificates with words of thanks.7. → See also thankyou8. →  give thanks9. →  thank God10. →  have sb to thank11. →  thanks to12. →  no thanks to13. → to thank your lucky stars
