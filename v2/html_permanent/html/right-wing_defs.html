right wing

Definitions
noun1. (often capitals) the conservative faction of an assembly, party, etc2. the part of an army or field of battle on the right from the point of view of one facing the enemy3. a. the right-hand side of the field of play from the point of view of a team facing its opponent's goalb. a player positioned in this area in any of various gamesc. the position occupied by such a playeradjective right-wing4. of, belonging to, or relating to the right wing

Definitions
language note:   The spelling right wing is also used for meaning [sense 2].1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A right-wing person or group has conservative or capitalist views. ⇒ ...a right-wing government. ⇒ Liberals say the paper is too right-wing.2. singular nounThe right wing of a political party consists of the members who have the most conservative or the most capitalist views. [+ of] ⇒ ...the right wing of the Conservative Party. ⇒ Why did the right wing have so much power within the Kremlin?
