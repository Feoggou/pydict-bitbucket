egg1

(ɛɡ )
Definitions
noun1. the oval or round reproductive body laid by the females of birds, reptiles, fishes, insects, and some other animals, consisting of a developing embryo, its food store, and sometimes jelly or albumen, all surrounded by an outer shell or membrane2. Also called: egg cell any female gamete; ovum3. the egg of the domestic hen used as food4. something resembling an egg, esp in shape or in being in an early stage of development5. See bad egg6. See good egg7. See lay an egg8. See put all one's eggs in one basket9. See teach one's grandmother to suck eggs10. See with egg on one's faceverb (transitive)11. to dip (food) in beaten egg before cooking12. informal to throw eggs at
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old Norse egg; related to Old English ǣg, Old High German ei

egg

Powered by COBUILD
(eg ) for learners of English
Word forms: eggs , egging , egged
Definitions
1. countable nounAn egg is an oval object that is produced by a female bird and which contains a baby bird. Other animals such as reptiles and fish also lay eggs. ⇒ ...a baby bird hatching from its egg. ⇒ ...ant eggs.2. variable nounIn Western countries, eggs often means hen's eggs, eaten as food. ⇒ Break the eggs into a shallow bowl and beat them lightly. ⇒ ...bacon and eggs.3. countable nounEgg is used to refer to an object in the shape of a hen's egg. ⇒ ...a chocolate egg.4. countable nounAn egg is a cell that is produced in the bodies of female animals and humans. If it is fertilized by a sperm, a baby develops from it. ⇒ It only takes one sperm to fertilize an egg.5. → See also Easter egg, nest egg, Scotch egg6. →  to put all your eggs in one basket7. →  to have egg on your face8. → a chicken and egg situation9. → the goose that lays the golden egg► →  egg on
