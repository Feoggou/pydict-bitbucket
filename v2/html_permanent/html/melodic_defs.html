melodic

(mɪˈlɒdɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to melody2. of or relating to a part in a piece of music3. tuneful or melodious

Derived Forms

melodically (meˈlodically) adverb

melodic

Powered by COBUILD
(mɪlɒdɪk )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Melodic means relating to melody. ⇒ ...Schubert's effortless gift for melodic invention.melodically (mɪlɒdɪkli ) adverb ⇒ ...the third of Tchaikovsky's ten operas, and melodically one of his richest scores.2. graded adjective & adjectiveMusic that is melodic has beautiful tunes in it. ⇒ Wonderfully melodic and tuneful, his songs have made me weep.melodically graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ The leader has also learned to play more melodically.
