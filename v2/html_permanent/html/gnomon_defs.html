gnomon

(ˈnəʊmɒn )
Definitions
noun1. the stationary arm that projects the shadow on a sundial2. a geometric figure remaining after a parallelogram has been removed from one corner of a larger parallelogram
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

gnomonic (gnoˈmonic) or gnomonical (gnoˈmonical) adjectivegnomonically (gnoˈmonically) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin, from Greek: interpreter, from gignōskein to know
Example sentences containing 'gnomon'
The minutely balanced works, the gnomon fitted to the perpendicular plane, would be far beyond their grasp. Fidelis Morgan THE RIVAL QUEENS: A COUNTESS ASHBY DE LA ZOUCHE MYSTERYIs like Ozmosees, to dream into other world - but you sometime solid there, real; gnomon never solid. Amanda Hemingway THE GREENSTONE GRAIL: THE SANGREAL TRILOGY ONE
