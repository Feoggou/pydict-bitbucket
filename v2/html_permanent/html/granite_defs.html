granite

(ˈɡrænɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a light-coloured coarse-grained acid plutonic igneous rock consisting of quartz, feldspars, and such ferromagnesian minerals as biotite or hornblende: widely used for building2. great hardness, endurance, or resolution3. another name for a stone (sense 9)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

granite-like (ˈgranite-ˌlike) adjectivegranitic (ɡrəˈnɪtɪk ) or granitoid (ˈgranitˌoid) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Italian granito grained, from granire to grain, from grano grain, from Latin grānum
