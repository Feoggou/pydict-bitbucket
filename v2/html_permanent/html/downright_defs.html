downright

(ˈdaʊnˌraɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. frank or straightforward; blunt ⇒ downright speech2. archaic directed or pointing straight downadverb, adjective (prenominal)3. (intensifier) ⇒ a downright certainty, ⇒ downright rude
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV adj]You use downright to emphasize unpleasant or bad qualities or behaviour. [emphasis] ⇒ ...ideas that would have been downright dangerous if put into practice. ⇒ She was often downright rude to him.Downright is also an adjective. out-and-out ⇒ ...suspicion and downright hostility. ⇒ ...downright bad manners.
