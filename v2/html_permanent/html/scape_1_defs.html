scape1

(skeɪp )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a leafless stalk in plants that arises from a rosette of leaves and bears one or more flowers2. zoology a stalklike part, such as the first segment of an insect's antenna

Derived Forms

scapose (ˈscapose) adjective

Word Origin

C17: from Latin scāpus stem, from (Doric) Greek skapos; see shaft
