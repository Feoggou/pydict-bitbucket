whimper

(ˈwɪmpə )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to cry, sob, or whine softly or intermittently2. to complain or say (something) in a whining plaintive waynoun3. a soft plaintive whine
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

whimperer (ˈwhimperer) nounwhimpering (ˈwhimpering) nounwhimperingly (ˈwhimperingly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from dialect whimp, of imitative origin

whimper for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(hwɪmpəʳ )
Word forms: whimpers , whimpering , whimpered
Definitions
1. verbIf someone whimpers, they make quiet unhappy or frightened sounds, as if they are about to start crying. [V] ⇒ She lay at the bottom of the stairs, whimpering in pain. [V-ing] ⇒ He made another pathetic whimpering sound.Whimper is also a noun. ⇒ David's crying subsided to a whimper.2. verbIf someone whimpers something, they say it in an unhappy or frightened way. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Let me go,' she whimpered. 'You're hurting me.'. [V n] ⇒ She whimpered something inaudible.3. →  not with a bang but a whimper
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
