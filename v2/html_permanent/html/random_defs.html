random

(ˈrændəm )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking any definite plan or prearranged order; haphazard ⇒ a random selection2. statisticsa. having a value which cannot be determined but only described probabilistically ⇒ a random variableb. chosen without regard to any characteristics of the individual members of the population so that each has an equal chance of being selected ⇒ random sampling3. informal (of a person) unknown ⇒ some random guy waiting for a busnoun4. See at random
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

randomly (ˈrandomly) adverbrandomness (ˈrandomness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French randon, from randir to gallop, of Germanic origin; compare Old High German rinnan to run

(rændəm ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A random sample or method is one in which all the people or things involved have an equal chance of being chosen. ⇒ The survey used a random sample of two thousand people across England and Wales. ⇒ The competitors will be subject to random drug testing.randomly adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...interviews with a randomly selected sample of thirty girls aged between 13 and 18.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe events as random, you mean that they do not seem to follow a definite plan or pattern. ⇒ ...random violence against innocent victims. ⇒ Children's words and actions are often fairly random. ⇒ ...random variations of the wind.randomly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ ...drinks and magazines left scattered randomly around.randomness uncount noun ⇒ Isn't this a demand to control the randomness of life?3. →  at random4. →  at random
