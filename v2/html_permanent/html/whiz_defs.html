whiz in American English

(hwɪz ; wɪz ) or whizz (hwɪz ; wɪz )
Definitions
verb intransitiveWord forms: whizzed or ˈwhizzing1.  to move swiftly with or as with a buzzing or hissing sound ⇒ the bus whizzed by him2.  to make the buzzing or hissing sound of something moving swiftly through the air3.  Slang to urinate verb transitive4.  to cause to whiz, esp. by rotating rapidly noun5.  a whizzing sound or movement6.  Usa.  Slang a person who is very quick, adroit, or skilled at something; expert ⇒ a whiz at footballb.  Slang, Obsolete something strikingly excellent, attractive, etc. ⇒ a whiz of a car7.  Slang the act of urinatingoften in the phrase take a whiz
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

echoic; (sense 6) see wiz

whiz for learners of American English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪz ) also whizz
Word forms: whizzes, whizzing, whizzed
Definitions
1. intransitive verbIf something whizzes somewhere, it moves there very fast. [informal] ⇒ They heard bullets continue to whiz over their heads.2. countable nounIf you are a whiz at something, you are very good at it. [informal] ⇒ Simon's a whiz at card games.
