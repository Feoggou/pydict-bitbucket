coagulate

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (kəʊˈæɡjʊˌleɪt )1. to cause (a fluid, such as blood) to change into a soft semisolid mass or (of such a fluid) to change into such a mass; clot; curdle2. chemistry to separate or cause to separate into distinct constituent phasesnoun (kəʊˈæɡjʊlɪt , -ˌleɪt)3. the solid or semisolid substance produced by coagulation
Derived forms
coagulable (coˈagulable) adjectivecoˌagulability (coˌagulaˈbility) nouncoˌagulation (coˌaguˈlation) nouncoagulative (kəʊˈæɡjʊlətɪv ) or coagulatory (ˌkəʊæɡjʊˈleɪtərɪ) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Latin coāgulāre to make (a liquid) curdle, from coāgulum rennet, from cōgere to drive together
