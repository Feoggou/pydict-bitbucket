raven1

(ˈreɪvən )
Definitions
noun1. a large passerine bird, Corvus corax, having a large straight bill, long wedge-shaped tail, and black plumage: family Corvidae (crows). It has a hoarse croaking cry2. a. a shiny black colourb. (as adjective) ⇒ raven hair
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English hrǣfn; related to Old High German hraban, Old Norse hrafn

raven2

(ˈrævən )
Definitions
verb1. to seize or seek (plunder, prey, etc)2. to eat (something) voraciously or greedily; be ravenous in eating
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ravener (ˈravener) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French raviner to attack impetuously; see ravenous
