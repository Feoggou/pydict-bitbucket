indicative

(ɪnˈdɪkətɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. (usually postpositive; foll by of) serving as a sign; suggestive ⇒ indicative of trouble ahead2. grammar denoting a mood of verbs used chiefly to make statements. Compare subjunctive (sense 1)noun3. grammara. the indicative moodb. a verb in the indicative mood
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

indicatively (inˈdicatively) adverb

indicative

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪndɪkətɪv ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If one thing is indicative of another, it suggests what the other thing is likely to be. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ The result was indicative of a strong retail market. ⇒ Often physical appearance is indicative of how a person feels.2. singular nounIn grammar, a clause that is in the indicative, or in the indicative mood, has a subject followed by a verb group. Examples are 'I'm hungry' and 'She was followed'. Clauses of this kind are typically used to make statements.
