leach1

(liːtʃ )
Definitions
verb1. to remove or be removed from a substance by a percolating liquid2. to lose or cause to lose soluble substances by the action of a percolating liquid3. another word for percolate (sense 1), percolate (sense 2)noun4. the act or process of leaching5. a substance that is leached or the constituents removed by leaching6. a porous vessel for leaching
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

leacher (ˈleacher) noun

Word Origin

C17: variant of obsolete letch to wet, perhaps from Old English leccan to water; related to leak
