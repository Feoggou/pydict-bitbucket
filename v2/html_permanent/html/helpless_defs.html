helpless

(ˈhɛlplɪs )
Definitions
adjective1. unable to manage independently2. made powerless or weak ⇒ they were helpless from so much giggling3. without help
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

helplessly (ˈhelplessly) adverbhelplessness (ˈhelplessness) noun

helpless

Powered by COBUILD
(helpləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [oft ADJ to-inf]If you are helpless, you do not have the strength or power to do anything useful or to control or protect yourself. ⇒ Parents often feel helpless, knowing that all the cuddles in the world won't stop the tears. ⇒ Children are dying, and I am helpless to do anything. ⇒ Once aboard we were soon helpless with laughter at the absurdity of it. ⇒ They are not merely helpless victims.helplessly graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ Their son watched helplessly as they vanished beneath the waves.helplessness uncount noun ⇒ I remember my feelings of helplessness.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
