tirade

(taɪˈreɪd )
Definitions
noun1. a long angry speech or denunciation2. prosody rare a speech or passage dealing with a single theme
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from French, literally: a pulling, from Italian tirata, from tirare to pull, of uncertain origin

Definitions
countable nounA tirade is a long angry speech in which someone criticizes a person or thing. [+ against] ⇒ She launched into a tirade against the policies that ruined her business. ⇒ He too has met a tirade of abuse.
