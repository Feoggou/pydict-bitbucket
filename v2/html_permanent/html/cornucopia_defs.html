cornucopia

(ˌkɔːnjʊˈkəʊpɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. Greek mythology the horn of Amalthea, the goat that suckled Zeus2. a representation of such a horn in painting, sculpture, etc, overflowing with fruit, vegetables, etc; horn of plenty3. a great abundance; overflowing supply4. a horn-shaped container
Derived forms
ˌcornucopian (ˌcornuˈcopian) adjective
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin, from Latin cornū cōpiae horn of plenty
