disk

(dɪsk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a variant spelling (esp US and Canadian) of disc2. Also called: magnetic disk, hard disk computing a direct-access storage device consisting of a stack of plates coated with a magnetic layer, the whole assembly rotating rapidly as a single unit. Each surface has a read-write head that can move radially to read or write data on concentric tracks. Compare drum1 (sense 9). See also floppy disk
