implead

(ɪmˈpliːd )
Definitions
verb (transitive) law rare1. a. to sue or prosecuteb. to bring an action against2. to accuse
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

impleadable (imˈpleadable) adjectiveimpleader (imˈpleader) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Anglo-French empleder; see im-, plead
