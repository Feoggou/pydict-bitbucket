petrify

(ˈpɛtrɪˌfaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -fies, -fying or -fied1. (tr; often passive) to convert (organic material, esp plant material) into a fossilized form by impregnation with dissolved minerals so that the original appearance is preserved2. to make or become dull, unresponsive, insensitive, etc; deaden3. (tr; often passive) to stun or daze with horror, fear, etc

Derived Forms

petriˌfier (ˈpetriˌfier) noun

Word Origin

C16: from French pétrifier, ultimately from Greek petra stone, rock

[V n] ⇒ Prison petrifies me and I don't want to go there.petrifying adjective ⇒ I found the climb absolutely petrifying.2. ergative verbIf something such as a society or institution petrifies, or if something petrifies it, it stops changing and developing. [formal] [V n] ⇒ ...the fear that a political deadlock may petrify economic initiatives. [Also V]
