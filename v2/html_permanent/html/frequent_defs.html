frequent

Definitions
adjective (ˈfriːkwənt )1. recurring at short intervals2. constant or habitualverb (frɪˈkwɛnt )3. (transitive) to visit repeatedly or habitually
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frequentable (freˈquentable) adjectivefrequenter (freˈquenter) nounfrequently (ˈfrequently) adverbfrequentness (ˈfrequentness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin frequēns numerous; perhaps related to Latin farcīre to stuff

Word forms: frequents , frequenting , frequented
Definitions
pronunciation note:   The adjective is pronounced (friːkwənt ). The verb is pronounced (frɪkwent ).1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf something is frequent, it happens often. ⇒ Bordeaux is on the main Paris-Madrid line so there are frequent trains. ⇒ He is a frequent visitor to the house.frequently graded adverb & adverb [usu ADV with v] ⇒ Iron and folic acid supplements are frequently given to pregnant women.2. verbIf someone frequents a particular place, they regularly go there. [formal] [V n] ⇒ I hear he frequents the Cajun restaurant in Hampstead.
