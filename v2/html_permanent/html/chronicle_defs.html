chronicle

(ˈkrɒnɪkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a record or register of events in chronological orderverb2. (transitive) to record in or as if in a chronicle
Derived forms
chronicler (ˈchronicler) noun
Word origin
C14: from Anglo-French cronicle, via Latin chronica (pl), from Greek khronika annals, from khronikos relating to time; see chronic

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo chronicle a series of events means to write about them or show them in broadcasts in the order in which they happened. [V n] ⇒ The series chronicles the everyday adventures of two eternal bachelors. [Also V wh]chronicler Word forms: chroniclers countable noun [+ of] ⇒ ...the chronicler of the English civil war.2. countable nounA chronicle is an account or record of a series of events. [+ of] ⇒ ...this vast chronicle of Napoleonic times.3. countable nounChronicle is sometimes used as part of the name of a newspaper. ⇒ ...the San Francisco Chronicle.
