or so for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
Definitions
phraseYou use or so when you are giving an approximate amount. [vagueness] ⇒ Though rates are heading down, they still offer real returns of 8% or so. ⇒ Matt got me a room there for a week or so when I first came here. ⇒ The driver usually spends four hours or so helping to load and prepare his lorry. →  so
