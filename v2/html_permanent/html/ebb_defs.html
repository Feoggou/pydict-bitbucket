ebb

(ɛb )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. (of tide water) to flow back or recede. Compare flow (sense 9)2. to fall away or declinenoun3. a. the flowing back of the tide from high to low water or the period in which this takes placeb. (as modifier) ⇒ the ebb tide. Compare flood (sense 3)4. See at a low ebb
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English ebba; related to Old Norse efja river bend, Gothic ibuks moving backwards, Old High German ippihōn to roll backwards, Middle Dutch ebbe ebb

ebb

Powered by COBUILD
(eb ) for learners of English
Word forms: ebbs , ebbing , ebbed
Definitions
1. verbWhen the tide or the sea ebbs, its level gradually falls. [V] ⇒ When the tide ebbs it's a rock pool inhabited by crustaceans.2. countable nounThe ebb or the ebb tide is one of the regular periods, usually two per day, when the sea gradually falls to a lower level as the tide moves away from the land. ⇒ ...the spring ebb tide. ⇒ We decided to leave on the ebb at six o'clock next morning.3. verbIf someone's life, support, or feeling ebbs, it becomes weaker and gradually disappears. [formal] [V] ⇒ ...as a man's physical strength ebbs. [V] ⇒ Were there occasions when enthusiasm ebbed?Ebb away means the same as ebb. [V P] ⇒ His little girl's life ebbed away. [V P] ⇒ Their popular support is ebbing away.4. →  at a/o's low(est) ebb5. →  ebb and flow
