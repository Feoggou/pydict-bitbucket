gust

(ɡʌst )
Definitions
noun1. a sudden blast of wind2. a sudden rush of smoke, sound, etc3. an outburst of emotionverb (intransitive)4. to blow in gusts ⇒ the wind was gusting to more than 50 mph
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Old Norse gustr; related to gjōsa to gush; see geyser

gust

Powered by COBUILD
(gʌst ) for learners of English
Word forms: gusts , gusting , gusted
Definitions
1. countable nounA gust is a short, strong, sudden rush of wind. [+ of] ⇒ A gust of wind drove down the valley. ⇒ A hurricane-force gust blew off part of a church tower.2. verbWhen the wind gusts, it blows with short, strong, sudden rushes. [V] ⇒ The wind gusted again. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The wind gusted up to 164 miles an hour.3. countable nounIf you feel a gust of emotion, you feel the emotion suddenly and intensely. [+ of] ⇒ ...a small gust of pleasure.
