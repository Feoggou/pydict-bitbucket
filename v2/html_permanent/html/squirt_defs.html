squirt

(skwɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to force (a liquid) or (of a liquid) to be forced out of a narrow opening2. (transitive) to cover or spatter with liquid so ejectednoun3. a jet or amount of liquid so ejected4. the act or an instance of squirting5. an instrument used for squirting6. informala. a person regarded as insignificant or contemptibleb. a short person

Derived Forms

squirter (ˈsquirter) noun

Word Origin

C15: of imitative origin

(skwɜːʳt )
Word forms: squirts , squirting , squirted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. ergative verb & verbIf you squirt a liquid somewhere or if it squirts somewhere, the liquid comes out of a narrow opening in a thin fast stream. [V n prep/adv] ⇒ Norman cut open his pie and squirted tomato sauce into it. [V prep/adv] ⇒ The water squirted from its throat. [be V-ed adv/prep] ⇒ The liquid is squirted out in powerful jets.Squirt is also a noun. [+ of] ⇒ It just needs a little squirt of oil.2. verbIf you squirt something with a liquid, you squirt the liquid at it. [V n + with] ⇒ I squirted him with water. [V n with n] ⇒ Its linings were simply squirted with oil.
