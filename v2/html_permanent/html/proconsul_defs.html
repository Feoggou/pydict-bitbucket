proconsul

(prəʊˈkɒnsəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an administrator or governor of a colony, occupied territory, or other dependency2. (in ancient Rome) the governor of a senatorial province

Derived Forms

proconsular (prəʊˈkɒnsjʊlə ) adjectiveproconsulate (proˈconsulate) or proconsulship (proˈconsulship) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Latin, from prō consule (someone acting) for the consul. See pro-2, consul
