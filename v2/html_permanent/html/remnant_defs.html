remnant

(ˈrɛmnənt )
Definitions
noun1. (often plural) a part left over after use, processing, etc2. a surviving trace or vestige, as of a former era ⇒ a remnant of imperialism3. a piece of material from the end of a roll, sold at a lower priceadjective4. remaining; left over
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French remenant remaining, from remanoir to remain

remnant

Powered by COBUILD
(remnənt ) for learners of English
Word forms: remnants
Definitions
1. countable nounThe remnants of something are small parts of it that are left over when the main part has disappeared or been destroyed. ⇒ After twenty-four hours of fighting, the remnants of the force were fleeing. [+ of] ⇒ Beneath the present church were remnants of Roman flooring. ⇒ Obedience by women towards men is a remnant of religious teaching.2. countable nounA remnant is a small piece of cloth that is left over when most of the cloth has been sold. Shops usually sell remnants cheaply.
