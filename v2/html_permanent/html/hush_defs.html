hush1

(hʌʃ )
Definitions
verb1. to make or become silent; quieten2. to soothe or be soothednoun3. stillness; silence4. an act of hushingexclamation5. a plea or demand for silence
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

hushed adjective

Word Origin

C16: probably from earlier husht quiet!, the -t being thought to indicate a past participle

hush2 - mining, Northern England

(hʌʃ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to run water over the ground to erode (surface soil), revealing the underlying strata and any valuable minerals present2. to wash (an ore) by removing particles of earth with rushing waternoun3. a gush of water, esp when artificially produced
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: of imitative origin

hush

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌʃ ) for learners of English
Word forms: hushes , hushing , hushed
Definitions
1. conventionYou say 'Hush!' to someone when you are asking or telling them to be quiet. ⇒ Hush, my love, it's all right.2. ergative verb & verbIf you hush someone or if they hush, they stop speaking or making a noise. [V n] ⇒ She tried to hush her noisy father. [V] ⇒ I had to box Max's ears to get him to hush.3. singular nounYou say there is a hush in a place when everything is quiet and peaceful, or suddenly becomes quiet. ⇒ A hush fell over the crowd and I knew something terrible had happened.► →  hush up
