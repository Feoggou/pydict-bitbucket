Fabian

(ˈfeɪbɪən )
Definitions
adjective1. of, relating to, or resembling the delaying tactics of the Roman general Quintus Fabius Maximus; cautious; circumspectnoun2. a member of or sympathizer with the Fabian Society
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C19: from Latin Fabiānus of Fabius
Example sentences containing 'Fabian'
There were limits to my daring in defiance of Hexton custom - I was a Fabian rather than a revolutionary by temperament. Barnard, Robert THE DISPOSAL OF THE LIVING

Similar Words

cautious, delaying, circumspect, procrastinating, attritional, cunctative or cunctatory (rare)
