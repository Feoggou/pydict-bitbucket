lately

Powered by COBUILD
(leɪtli ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adverb [ADV with v]You use lately to describe events in the recent past, or situations that started a short time ago. ⇒ Dad's health hasn't been too good lately. ⇒ Lord Tomas had lately been appointed Chairman of the Centre for Policy Studies. ⇒ 'Have you talked to her lately?' ⇒ Optimism about the U.S. economy has been a rare commodity lately.2. adverbYou can use lately to refer to the job a person has been doing until recently. [formal] ⇒ ...Timothy Jean Geoffrey Pratt, lately deputy treasury solicitor.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
