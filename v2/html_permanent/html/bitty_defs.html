bitty

(ˈbɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective -tier or -tiest1. lacking unity; disjointed2. containing bits, sediment, etc
Derived forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that something is bitty, you mean that it seems to be formed from a lot of different parts which you think do not fit together or go together well. [British, informal] ⇒ The programme was bitty and pointless.2. adjective [ADJ n]If you describe someone or something as a little bitty person or thing, you are emphasizing that they are very small. [US, informal, emphasis] ⇒ She's just a little bitty wisp of a girl.
