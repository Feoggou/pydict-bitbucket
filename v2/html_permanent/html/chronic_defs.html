chronic

(ˈkrɒnɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. continuing for a long time; constantly recurring2. (of a disease) developing slowly, or of long duration. Compare acute (sense 7)3. inveterate; habitual ⇒ a chronic smoker4. informala. very bad ⇒ the play was chronicb. very serious ⇒ he left her in a chronic condition
Derived forms
chronically (ˈchronically) adverbchronicity (krɒˈnɪsɪtɪ ) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin chronicus relating to time, from Greek khronikos, from khronos time

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]A chronic illness or disability lasts for a very long time. Compare acute. ⇒ ...chronic back pain.chronically (krɒnɪkli ) adverb [ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ Most of them were chronically ill.2. adjective [ADJ n]You can describe someone's bad habits or behaviour as chronic when they have behaved like that for a long time and do not seem to be able to stop themselves. habitual, inveterate ⇒ Anyone who does not believe that smoking is an addiction has never been a chronic smoker. ⇒ ...a chronic worrier.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]A chronic situation or problem is very severe and unpleasant. ⇒ One cause of the artist's suicide seems to have been chronic poverty. ⇒ There is a chronic shortage of patrol cars in this police district.chronically adverb [ADV adj/-ed] ⇒ Research and technology are said to be chronically underfunded.
