icy

(ˈaɪsɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: icier or iciest1. made of, covered with, or containing ice2. resembling ice3. freezing or very cold4. cold or reserved in manner; aloof

icy

Powered by COBUILD
(aɪsi ) for learners of English
Word forms: icier , iciest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as icy or icy cold, you mean that it is extremely cold. ⇒ An icy wind blew hard across the open spaces. ⇒ His shoes and clothing were wet through and icy cold.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn icy road has ice on it.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person or their behaviour as icy, you mean that they are not affectionate or friendly, and they show their dislike or anger in a quiet, controlled way. [disapproval] ⇒ His response was icy.icily graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v, ADV adj] ⇒ 'Have you finished?' he asked icily. ⇒ The prison official is icily polite and bureaucratic.
