slapdash

(ˈslæpˌdæʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. in a careless, hasty, or haphazard manneradjective2. careless, hasty, or haphazardnoun3. slapdash activity or work4. another name for roughcast (sense 1)
Word origin
C17: from slap + dash1

slapdash

Powered by COBUILD
(slæpdæʃ ) also slap-dash
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as slapdash, you mean that they do things carelessly without much thinking or planning. [disapproval] ⇒ ...a slapdash student. ⇒ Malcolm's work methods appear amazingly slapdash.
Example sentences containing 'slapdash'
Professor Eberhardt is an important man and we will stand for no slapdash revolutionary nonsense here. Appiganesi, Lisa DREAMS OF INNOCENCEThere were no clues in Johnny's bedroom; Diane had not seriously thought there would be, but tonight was not one for slapdash procedures. Trenhalle, John A MEANS TO EVILShe knew that even in the most slapdash manner she could not tackle the high entertaining rooms. Melville, Anne THE HARDIE INHERITANCEBut if our design is slapdash and our carving is hasty, why, the proportions will be inelegant and the balance untrue. Robert Silverberg VALENTINE PONTIFEX
