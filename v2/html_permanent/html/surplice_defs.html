surplice

(ˈsɜːplɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna loose wide-sleeved liturgical vestment of linen, reaching to the knees, worn over the cassock by clergymen, choristers, and acolytes

Derived Forms

surpliced (ˈsurpliced) adjective

Word Origin

C13: via Anglo-French from Old French sourpelis, from Medieval Latin superpellīcium, from super- + pellīcium coat made of skins, from Latin pellis a skin
