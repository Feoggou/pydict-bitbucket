infinite

(ˈɪnfɪnɪt )
Definitions
adjective1. a. having no limits or boundaries in time, space, extent, or magnitudeb. (as noun; preceded by the) ⇒ the infinite2. extremely or immeasurably great or numerous ⇒ infinite wealth3. all-embracing, absolute, or total ⇒ God's infinite wisdom4. mathematicsa. having an unlimited number of digits, factors, terms, members, etc ⇒ an infinite seriesb. (of a set) able to be put in a one-to-one correspondence with part of itselfc. (of an integral) having infinity as one or both limits of integration. Compare finite (sense 2)

infinite

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfɪnɪt ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjectiveIf you describe something as infinite, you are emphasizing that it is extremely great in amount or degree. [emphasis] ⇒ ...an infinite variety of landscapes. ⇒ With infinite care, John shifted position. ⇒ The choice is infinite.infinitely adverb [ADV adj/adv] ⇒ His design was infinitely better than anything I could have done.2. adjectiveSomething that is infinite has no limit, end, or edge. boundless ⇒ ...an infinite number of atoms. ⇒ Obviously, no company has infinite resources. ⇒ ...God's infinite mercy.infinitely adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ A centimeter can be infinitely divided into smaller units.The infinite is something which is infinite. ⇒ ...pondering on the infinite.
