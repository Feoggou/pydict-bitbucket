Synonyms of power

power

noun

  1. control, authority, influence, command, sovereignty, sway, dominance, domination, supremacy, mastery, dominion, ascendancy, mana (New Zealand), bottom
    • women who have reached positions of great power and influence
  2. ability, capacity, faculty, property, potential, capability, competence, competency
    • He was so drunk that he had lost the power of speech.
    OPPOSITES:  inability, incompetence, incapacity, incapability
  3. authority, right, licence, privilege, warrant, prerogative, authorization
    • The Prime Minister has the power to dismiss senior ministers.
  4. strength, might, energy, weight, muscle, vigour, potency, welly (slang), brawn, hard power
    • He had no power in his left arm.
    OPPOSITES:  weakness, impotence, feebleness, listlessness, enervation
  5. forcefulness, force, strength, punch (informal), intensity, potency, eloquence, persuasiveness, cogency, powerfulness
    • the power of his rhetoric
