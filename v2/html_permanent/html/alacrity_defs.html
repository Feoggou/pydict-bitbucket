alacrity

(əlækrɪti )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount noun [usu with N]If you do something with alacrity, you do it quickly and eagerly. [formal] ⇒ As you can imagine, I accepted with alacrity.
Example sentences containing 'alacrity'
The dim room was now deserted except for the two of them and a short fat bartender in shirt-sleeves, who served up a drink with alacrity. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEI was offered employment with the Queen's Guard and accepted it with alacrity. Robin Hobb THE GOLDEN FOOL: BOOK TWO OF THE TAWNY MANAnd she was temperamental --- like all girls could be --- and very demanding: questions popping up, with relentless alacrity. Nicola Barker BEHINDLINGS"I've been thinking," he said, with an alacrity that made her blink. S.M. Stirling T2: INFILTRATOR
