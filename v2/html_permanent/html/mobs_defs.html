mobs

(mɒbz ) informal
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
plural noun1. (usually foll by of) great numbers or quantities; lots ⇒ mobs of peopleadverb2. Australian and New Zealand a great deal ⇒ mobs better
Example sentences containing 'mobs'
The good-natured, slow-moving crowds of early evening had been replaced by mobs of noisy drunks. Tony Parsons MAN AND WIFEWhen the request came in, two chief constables stated that otherwise their men would have to withdraw and leave the streets to the mobs. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEYou fear even your own mobs , who perhaps might become infected by ours. Baxter, Stephen ANTI-ICE
