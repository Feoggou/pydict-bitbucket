impurity

(ɪmˈpjʊərɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the quality of being impure2. an impure thing, constituent, or element ⇒ impurities in the water3. electronics a small quantity of an element added to a pure semiconductor crystal to control its electrical conductivity. See also acceptor (sense 2), donor (sense 5)

impurity

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪmpjʊərɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: impurities
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu pl]Impurities are substances that are present in small quantities in another substance and make it dirty or of an unacceptable quality. ⇒ The air in the factory is filtered to remove impurities.2. uncount nounImpurity is the state of being no longer pure, especially sexually pure.
