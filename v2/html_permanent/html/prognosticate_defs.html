prognosticate

(prɒɡˈnɒstɪˌkeɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. to foretell (future events) according to present signs or indications; prophesy2. (transitive) to foreshadow or portend

Derived Forms

progˌnostication (progˌnostiˈcation) nounprognosticative (progˈnosticative) adjectiveprognostiˌcator (progˈnostiˌcator) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Medieval Latin prognōsticāre to predict
Example sentences containing 'prognosticate'
`My associate, Berkli, said he wanted you to prognosticate for us. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELS
