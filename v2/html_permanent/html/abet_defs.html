abet

Powered by COBUILD
(əbet )
Word forms: abets , abetting , abetted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf one person abets another, they help or encourage them to do something criminal or wrong. Abet is often used in the legal expression 'aid and abet'. [law, formal] [V n] ⇒ His wife was sentenced to seven years imprisonment for aiding and abetting him. [V n] ⇒ We shall strike hard, without flinching, at terrorists and those who abet them.2. verbTo abet something, especially something bad or undesirable, means to make it possible. [journalism, or formal, disapproval] [V n] ⇒ The media have also abetted the feeling of unreality.
