unrelieved

(ˌʌnrɪˈliːvd)
Definitions
adjectivevery severe and not replaced by anything better, even for a short time ⇒ The sun baked down on the concrete, unrelieved by any breeze., ⇒ the unrelieved monotony of days of work and rest alternating without any change throughout one's entire working life, ⇒ The story of Bhopal is one of unrelieved human misery., ⇒ Survey results had formed a picture of unrelieved gloom.

Definitions
adjectiveIf you describe something unpleasant as unrelieved, you mean that it is very severe and is not replaced by anything better, even for a short time. ⇒ ...unrelieved misery. [+ by] ⇒ The sun baked down on the concrete, unrelieved by any breeze.
