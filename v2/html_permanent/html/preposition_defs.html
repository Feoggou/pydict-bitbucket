preposition

(ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna word or group of words used before a noun or pronoun to relate it grammatically or semantically to some other constituent of a sentence. Abbreviation: prep▶ USAGE The practice of ending a sentence with a preposition (Venice is a place I should like to go to) was formerly regarded as incorrect, but is now acceptable and is the preferred form in many contexts

Derived Forms

ˌprepositional (ˌprepoˈsitional) adjectiveˌprepositionally (ˌprepoˈsitionally) adverb

Word Origin

C14: from Latin praepositiō a putting before, from pōnere to place
