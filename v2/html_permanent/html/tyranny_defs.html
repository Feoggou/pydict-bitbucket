tyranny

(ˈtɪrənɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -nies1. a. government by a tyrant or tyrants; despotismb. similarly oppressive and unjust government by more than one person2. arbitrary, unreasonable, or despotic behaviour or use of authority ⇒ the teacher's tyranny3. any harsh discipline or oppression ⇒ the tyranny of the clock4. a political unit ruled by a tyrant5. (esp in ancient Greece) government by a usurper6. a tyrannical act

Derived Forms

tyrannous (ˈtyrannous) adjectivetyrannously (ˈtyrannously) adverbtyrannousness (ˈtyrannousness) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French tyrannie, from Medieval Latin tyrannia, from Latin tyrannus tyrant

tyranny

Powered by COBUILD
(tɪrəni )
Word forms: tyrannies
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA tyranny is a cruel, harsh, and unfair government in which a person or small group of people have power over everyone else. ⇒ He described these regimes as tyrannies and dictatorships. ⇒ Self-expression and individuality are the greatest weapons against tyranny.2. uncount nounIf you describe someone's behaviour and treatment of others that they have authority over as tyranny, you mean that they are severe with them or unfair to them. ⇒ I'm the sole victim of Mother's tyranny.3. countable nounYou can describe something that you have to use or have as a tyranny if you think it is undesirable or unpleasant. [+ of] ⇒ The telephone is one of the great tyrannies of modern life.
