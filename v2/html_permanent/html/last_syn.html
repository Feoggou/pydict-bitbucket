Synonyms of last 1

last1

adjective

  1. previous, preceding, most recent
    • I got married last July.
  2. most recent, latest, previous
    • Much has changed since my last visit.
  3. hindmost, furthest, final, at the end, remotest, furthest behind, most distant, rearmost, aftermost
    • She said it was the very last house on the road.
    OPPOSITES:  first, foremost
  4. final, closing, concluding, ultimate, utmost
    • the last three pages of the chapter
    OPPOSITES:  first, opening, earliest, initial, introductory
  5. least likely, most unlikely, most unsuitable, least suitable
    • He would be the last person to do such a thing

adverb

  1. in or at the end, after, behind, in the rear, bringing up the rear
    • I testified last.

noun

  1. end, ending, close, finish, conclusion, completion, finale, termination
    • a thriller with plenty of twists to keep you guessing to the last

QUOTATION:
Many that are first shall be last; and the last shall be firstBible: St. Mark
  • See at last
  • See the last word

usage note:   Since last can mean either after all others or most recent, it is better to avoid using this word where ambiguity might arise, as in her last novel. Final or latest should be used as alternatives in such contexts to avoid any possible confusion.

last2

verb

  1. continue, keep, remain, survive, wear, carry on, endure, hold on, persist, keep on, hold out, abide
    • You only need a very small amount, so the tube lasts for ages.
    OPPOSITES:  end, stop, die, fail, fade, cease, depart, expire, terminate
  • See last out
  • See last something out
