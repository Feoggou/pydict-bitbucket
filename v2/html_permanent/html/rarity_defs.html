Definitions

rarity

Powered by COBUILD
(reərɪti ) for learners of English
Word forms: rarities
Definitions
1. countable noun [usu sing]If someone or something is a rarity, they are interesting or valuable because they are so unusual. [journalism] ⇒ Motorized wheelchairs are a rarity here. ⇒ He was a rarity among Wall Street lawyers. ⇒ Sontag has always been that rarity, a glamorous intellectual. ⇒ Other rarities include an interview with Presley.2. uncount noun [oft with poss]The rarity of something is the fact that it is very uncommon. ⇒ This indicates the rarity of such attacks. ⇒ It was a real prize due to its rarity and good condition. ⇒ 'Its rarity value is high,' Gibson announced gravely.
