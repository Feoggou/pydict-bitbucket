celerity

(sɪˈlɛrɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounrapidity; swiftness; speed
Word origin
C15: from Old French celerite, from Latin celeritās, from celer swift
Example sentences containing 'celerity'
He took his time about it: celerity had no part in the due processes of the law. Aird, Catherine A DEAD LIBERTYFrom the celerity with which the headwaiter approached her I decided she must be very rich or very distinguished. Elizabeth Peters LION IN THE VALLEY

Similar Words

speed, expedition, dispatch, velocity, haste, rapidity, quickness, swiftness, promptness, fleetness
