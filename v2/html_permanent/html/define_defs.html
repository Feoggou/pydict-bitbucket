define

(dɪˈfaɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to state precisely the meaning of (words, terms, etc)2. to describe the nature, properties, or essential qualities of3. to determine the boundary or extent of4. (often passive) to delineate the form or outline of ⇒ the shape of the tree was clearly defined by the light behind it5. to fix with precision; specify
Derived forms
definable (deˈfinable) adjectivedeˌfinability (deˌfinaˈbility) noundefinably (deˈfinably) adverbdefiner (deˈfiner) noun
Word origin
C14: from Old French definer to determine, from Latin dēfīnīre to set bounds to, from fīnīre to finish

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you define something, you show, describe, or state clearly what it is and what its limits are, or what it is like. [V wh] ⇒ We were unable to define what exactly was wrong with him. [V n] ⇒ He was asked to define his concept of cool.defined graded adjective & adjective [usu adv ADJ] ⇒ ...a party with a clearly defined programme and strict rules of membership.2. verbIf you define a word or expression, you explain its meaning, for example in a dictionary. [V n] ⇒ When people are asked 'What is intelligence?' they tend to reply: 'I don't know how to define it, but I can certainly recognize it when I see it.'. [V n + as] ⇒ Collins English Dictionary defines a workaholic as 'a person obsessively addicted to work'.
