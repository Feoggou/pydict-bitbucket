inhaler

(ɪnˈheɪlə )
Definitions
noun1. a device for breathing in therapeutic vapours through the nose or mouth, esp one for relieving nasal congestion or asthma2. a person who inhales

inhaler

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnheɪlər) in the Pharmaceutical Industry
Word forms: (regular plural) inhalers
Definitions
noun(Pharmaceutical: Devices) An inhaler is a device for the administration of an inhalant. It is put in the mouth and sends a particular amount of a drug into the lungs. ⇒ Inhalers use propellants to spray the drug out so the user can breathe the drug into their lungs. ⇒ Patients prescribed inhalant medication must be taught how to use an inhaler so that the medication reaches the lungs. ⇒ An inhaler is a device for the administration of an inhalant. It is put in the mouth and sends a particular amount of a drug into the lungs. ▶  dry-powder inhaler
