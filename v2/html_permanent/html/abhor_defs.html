abhor

(əbˈhɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -hors, -horring or -horred(transitive) to detest vehemently; find repugnant; reject
Derived forms
abhorrer (abˈhorrer) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin abhorrēre to shudder at, shrink from, from ab- away from + horrēre to bristle, shudder

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you abhor something, you hate it very much, especially for moral reasons. [formal] [V n] ⇒ He was a man who abhorred violence and was deeply committed to reconciliation. [V n] ⇒ If nature abhors a vacuum, journalists abhor a transition, when there is little news to cover.
