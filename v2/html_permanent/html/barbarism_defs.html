barbarism

(ˈbɑːbəˌrɪzəm )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a brutal, coarse, or ignorant act2. the condition of being backward, coarse, or ignorant3. a substandard or erroneously constructed or derived word or expression; solecism4. any act or object that offends against accepted taste
Word origin
C16: from Latin barbarismus error of speech, from Greek barbarismos, from barbaros barbarous

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
uncount nounIf you refer to someone's behaviour as barbarism, you strongly disapprove of it because you think that it is extremely cruel or uncivilized. [disapproval] ⇒ We do not ask for the death penalty: barbarism must not be met with barbarism.
Example sentences containing 'barbarism'
