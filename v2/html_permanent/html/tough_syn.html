Synonyms of tough

tough

adjective

  1. strong, determined, aggressive, high-powered, feisty (informal, mainly US & & Canadian), hard-nosed (informal), self-confident, unyielding, carnivorous (informal), hard as nails, two-fisted, self-assertive, badass (slang, mainly US)
    • She is tough and ambitious.
    OPPOSITES:  soft, weak, delicate
  2. hardy, strong, seasoned, fit, strapping, hardened, vigorous, sturdy, stout, stalwart, resilient, brawny, hard as nails
    • He's small, but he's tough, and I expect him to do well in the match.
  3. violent, rough, vicious, ruthless, pugnacious, hard-bitten, ruffianly, two-fisted, badass (slang, mainly US)
    • He shot three people, earning his reputation as a tough guy.
  4. rough, wild, criminal, violent, lawless, badass (slang, mainly US)
    • She doesn't seem to be cut out for this tough neighbourhood.
    OPPOSITES:  soft, gentle, tender, humane, civilized
  5. harsh, hard, awful, grim, unpleasant, bleak, austere, spartan, straitened
    • She had a pretty tough childhood.
  6. strict, severe, stern, hard, firm, exacting, adamant, resolute, draconian, intractable, inflexible, merciless, unforgiving, unyielding, unbending
    • He announced tough measures to limit the money supply.
    OPPOSITES:  easy, soft, flexible, mild, accommodating, benign, humane, compassionate, indulgent, lenient, merciful
  7. hard, difficult, exhausting, troublesome, uphill, strenuous, arduous, thorny, laborious, irksome
    • Whoever wins the election is going to have a tough job.
  8. difficult, hard, puzzling, baffling, troublesome, perplexing, thorny, knotty
    • It was a tough decision but I think we made the right one.
    OPPOSITES:  easy, easy-peasy (slang)
  9. resilient, hard, resistant, durable, strong, firm, solid, stiff, rigid, rugged, sturdy, inflexible, cohesive, tenacious, leathery, hard-wearing, robust
    • tough leather boots and trousers
    OPPOSITES:  delicate, soft, weak, tender, flexible, fragile, flimsy
  10. chewy, fibrous, leathery, stringy, sinewy, gristly
    • The steak was tough and the peas were like bullets.

noun

  1. ruffian, heavy (slang), rough (informal), bully, thug, hooligan, brute, rowdy, bravo, bully boy, bruiser (informal), roughneck (slang), boot boy, tsotsi (South Africa)
    • Three burly toughs elbowed their way to the front.
