anchorite

(ˈæŋkəˌraɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna person who lives in seclusion, esp a religious recluse; hermit
Derived forms
ˈanchoress feminine noun
Word origin
C15: from Medieval Latin anchorīta, from Late Latin anachōrēta, from Greek anakhōrētēs, from anakhōrein to retire, withdraw, from khōra a space
Example sentences containing 'anchorite'
That didn't mean I began to live like an anchorite ; far from it: I suppose I will always be true to my nature as a bon vivant. Adair, Tom (Intro) THREE KINDS OF KISSING - SCOTTISH SHORT STORIESAfter all, she wasn't an anchorite - or was it an eremite - with her thoughts as her raison d'être.
