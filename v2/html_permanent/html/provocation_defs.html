provocation

(ˌprɒvəˈkeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the act of provoking or inciting2. something that causes indignation, anger, etc3. English criminal law words or conduct that incite a person to attack another

provocation

(prɒvəkeɪʃən )
Word forms: provocations
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounIf you describe a person's action as provocation or a provocation, you mean that it is a reason for someone else to react angrily, violently, or emotionally. ⇒ He denies murder on the grounds of provocation. ⇒ The soldiers fired without provocation. ⇒ The Deputy Commander has condemned this weekend's protest as deliberate provocation.
