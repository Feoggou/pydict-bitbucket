avert

(əˈvɜːt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to turn away or aside ⇒ to avert one's gaze2. to ward off; prevent from occurring ⇒ to avert danger
Derived forms
aˈvertible or aˈvertable adjective
Word origin
C15: from Old French avertir, from Latin āvertere; see averse

avert

Powered by COBUILD
(əvɜːʳt )
Word forms: averts , averting , averted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you avert something unpleasant, you prevent it from happening. [V n] ⇒ Talks with the teachers' union over the weekend have averted a strike. ⇒ A fresh tragedy was narrowly averted yesterday.2. verbIf you avert your eyes or gaze from someone or something, you look away from them. [V n] ⇒ He avoids any eye contact, quickly averting his gaze when anyone approaches. [V-ed] ⇒ He kept his eyes averted. [Also V n from n]
Example sentences containing 'avert'
To avert my face, avoid catching his eye; hoping to be past before he'd even noticed me. Pritchard, John NIGHT SISTERSFor, historically, the British way has been to give a little to avert bloodier discontent. Baxter, Stephen ANTI-ICEYet she could identify no action, nothing she could do to avert the impending doom. Stephanie Laurens DEVIL'S BRIDE
