arguable

(ɑːʳgjuəbəl )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that it is arguable that something is true, you believe that it can be supported by evidence and that many people would agree with it. [formal] ⇒ It is arguable that this was not as grave a handicap as it might appear. ⇒ The judges said there was at least a good arguable case of negligence to answer.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]An idea, point, or comment that is arguable is not obviously true or correct and should be questioned. [formal] ⇒ It is arguable whether he ever had much control over the real economic power. ⇒ He said that her remarks disgusted every practising Christian. That claim is arguable.
