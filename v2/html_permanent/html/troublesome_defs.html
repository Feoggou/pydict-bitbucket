troublesome

(ˈtrʌbəlsəm )
Definitions
adjective1. causing a great deal of trouble; worrying, upsetting, or annoying2. characterized by violence; turbulent
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

troublesome

Powered by COBUILD
(trʌbəlsəm ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveYou use troublesome to describe something or someone that causes annoying problems or difficulties. ⇒ He needed surgery to cure a troublesome back injury. ⇒ Parents may find that a troublesome teenager becomes unmanageable.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA troublesome situation or issue is full of complicated problems or difficulties. ⇒ The economy has become a troublesome issue for the Conservative Party.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
