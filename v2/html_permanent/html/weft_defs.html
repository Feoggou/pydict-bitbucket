weft

(wɛft )
Definitions
nounthe yarn woven across the width of the fabric through the lengthwise warp yarn. Also called: filling, woof
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English, related to Old Norse veptr; see weave

weft for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weft )
Definitions
singular nounIn weaving, the weft of a piece of cloth is the threads which are passed sideways across the other threads. Compare warp.
