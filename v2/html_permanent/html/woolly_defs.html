woolly

or sometimes US wooly (ˈwʊlɪ )
Definitions
adjectiveWord forms: woollier, woolliestWord forms: sometimes US woolier or wooliest1. consisting of, resembling, or having the nature of wool2. covered or clothed in wool or something resembling it3. lacking clarity or substance ⇒ woolly thinking4. botany covered with long soft whitish hairs ⇒ woolly stems5. US recalling the rough and lawless period of the early West of America (esp in the phrase wild and woolly)nounWord forms: plural woollies or sometimes US woolies6. (often plural) a garment, such as a sweater, made of wool or something similar7. Western US and Australian (usually plural) an informal word for sheep
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

woolly for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wʊli )
Word forms: woollies
Definitions
regional note:   in AM, also use wooly1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is woolly is made of wool or looks like wool. ⇒ She wore this woolly hat with pompoms.2. countable nounA woolly is a woollen piece of clothing, especially a sweater. [British, informal]3. plural nounYou can refer to underwear as woollies, especially when it is knitted from wool, and has long legs or long sleeves. [US]4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe a person or their ideas as woolly, you are criticizing them for being confused or vague. [disapproval] ⇒ ...a weak and woolly Government. ⇒ It is no good setting vague and woolly goals–you will not know whether or not you have really achieved them.
