malt

(mɔːlt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. cereal grain, such as barley, that is kiln-dried after it has germinated by soaking in water2. See malt liquor3. short for malt whiskyverb4. to make into or become malt5. to make (something, esp liquor) with malt

Word Origin

Old English mealt; related to Dutch mout, Old Norse malt; see also melt

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounMalt is a substance made from grain that has been soaked in water and then dried in a hot oven. Malt is used in the production of whisky, beer, and other alcoholic drinks. ⇒ German beer has traditionally been made from just four ingredients–hops, malt, yeast and water.2. mass nounMalt is the same as malt whisky.3. countable nounA malt is a drink made from malted milk and sometimes other flavourings. [US]
