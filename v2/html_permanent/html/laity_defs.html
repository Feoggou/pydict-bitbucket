laity

(ˈleɪɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun1. laymen, as distinguished from clergymen2. all people not of a specific occupation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from lay3

laity

Powered by COBUILD
(leɪɪti ) for learners of English
Definitions
singular noun [with sing or pl verb]The laity are all the people involved in the work of a church who are not clergymen, monks, or nuns. ⇒ The Church and the laity were increasingly active in charity work. ⇒ Clergy and laity alike are divided in their views.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
