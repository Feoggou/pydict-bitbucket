idiot

(ˈɪdɪət )
Definitions
noun1. old-fashioned, offensive a person with severe learning difficulties2. a foolish or senseless person
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C13: from Latin idiōta ignorant person, from Greek idiōtēs private person, one who lacks professional knowledge, ignoramus; see idio-

idiot

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪdiət ) for learners of English
Word forms: idiots
Definitions
1. countable nounIf you call someone an idiot, you are showing that you think they are very stupid or have done something very stupid. [disapproval] ⇒ I knew I'd been an idiot to stay there. ⇒ You're an idiot!2. adjective [ADJ n]Idiot means stupid. ⇒ ...a bunch of idiot journalists waiting to ask me stupid questions.3. countable nounIn the past, people who had something wrong with their brains that made them seem less intelligent, or different from other people, were sometimes called idiots. [old-fashioned] ⇒ ...the village idiot.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
