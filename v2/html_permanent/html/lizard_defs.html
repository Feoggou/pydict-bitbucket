lizard

(ˈlɪzəd )
Definitions
noun1. any reptile of the suborder Lacertilia (or Sauria), esp those of the family Lacertidae (Old World lizards), typically having an elongated body, four limbs, and a long tail: includes the geckos, iguanas, chameleons, monitors, and slow worms ▶ Related adjectives: lacertilian, saurian2. a. leather made from the skin of such an animalb. (as modifier) ⇒ a lizard handbag
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin lacerta
