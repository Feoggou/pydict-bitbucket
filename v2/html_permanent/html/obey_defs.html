obey

(əˈbeɪ )
Definitions
verb1. to carry out (instructions or orders); comply with (demands)2. to behave or act in accordance with (one's feelings, whims, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

obeyer (oˈbeyer) noun

Word Origin

C13: from Old French obéir, from Latin oboedīre, from ob- to, towards + audīre to hear
