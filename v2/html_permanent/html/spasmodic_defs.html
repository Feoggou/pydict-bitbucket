spasmodic

(spæzˈmɒdɪk ) or rarely spasmodical
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. taking place in sudden brief spells2. of or characterized by spasms

Derived Forms

spasmodically (spasˈmodically) adverb

Word Origin

C17: New Latin, from Greek spasmos a spasm

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is spasmodic happens suddenly, for short periods of time, and at irregular intervals. ⇒ He managed to stifle the spasmodic sobs of panic rising in his throat. ⇒ My husband's work was so spasmodic.spasmodically (spæzmɒdɪkli ) graded adverb ⇒ The tremor occurred in Bucharest, where buildings trembled spasmodically for forty-five seconds or so.
