devoid

(dɪˈvɔɪd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective(postpositive; foll by of) destitute or void (of); free (from)
Word origin
C15: originally past participle of devoid (vb) to remove, from Old French devoidier, from de- de- + voider to void

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is devoid of a quality or thing, you are emphasizing that they have none of it. [formal, emphasis] [+ of] ⇒ I have never looked on a face that was so devoid of feeling. ⇒ The skies are virtually devoid of birdlife.
Example sentences containing 'devoid'
Pale at the best of times, she was now completely devoid of colour. Martin, Joy THE IMAGE OF LAURAThere is something innocent about Lucy that is touching: an openness, devoid of self-consciousness. Sara MacDonald SEA MUSICEven more than Hillsden's, the rooms were characterless, devoid of personal possessions -- only a temporary halt for a transient. Forbes, Bryan THE ENDLESS GAMEA formal sitting room, devoid of furnishings, lay below the balcony. Raymond E. Feist RISE OF A MERCHANT PRINCE: BOOK TWO OF THE SERPENTWAR SAGA
