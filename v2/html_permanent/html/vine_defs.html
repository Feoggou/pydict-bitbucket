vine

(vaɪn )
Definitions
noun1. any of various plants, esp the grapevine, having long flexible stems that creep along the ground or climb by clinging to a support by means of tendrils, leafstalks, etc2. the stem of such a plant
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

vined adjectivevineless (ˈvineless) adjectivevinelike (ˈvineˌlike) adjectiveviny (ˈviny) adjective

Word Origin

C13: from Old French vine, from Latin vīnea vineyard, from vīneus belonging to wine, from vīnum wine
