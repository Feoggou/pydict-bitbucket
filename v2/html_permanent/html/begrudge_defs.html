begrudge

(bɪˈɡrʌdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to give, admit, or allow unwillingly or with a bad grace2. to envy (someone) the possession of (something)
Derived forms
begrudgingly (beˈgrudgingly) adverb

begrudge

Powered by COBUILD
(bɪgrʌdʒ )
Word forms: begrudges , begrudging , begrudged
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you do not begrudge someone something, you do not feel angry, upset, or jealous that they have got it. [V n n] ⇒ I certainly don't begrudge him the Nobel Prize.2. verbIf you do not begrudge something such as time or money, you do not mind giving it up. [V n] ⇒ I do not begrudge the money I have lost.
