dialysis

(daɪˈælɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. the separation of small molecules from large molecules and colloids in a solution by the selective diffusion of the small molecules through a semipermeable membrane2. medicine See haemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis
Derived forms
dialytic (ˌdaɪəˈlɪtɪk ) adjectiveˌdialytically (ˌdiaˈlytically) adverb
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin: a separation, from Greek dialusis a dissolution, from dialuein to tear apart, dissolve, from luein to loosen
