thaw

(θɔː )
Definitions
verb1. to melt or cause to melt from a solid frozen state ⇒ the snow thawed2. to become or cause to become unfrozen; defrost3. (intransitive) to be the case that the ice or snow is melting ⇒ it's thawing fast4. (intransitive) to become more sociable, relaxed, or friendlynoun5. the act or process of thawing6. a spell of relatively warm weather, causing snow or ice to melt7. an increase in relaxation or friendliness
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

thawer (ˈthawer) nounthawless (ˈthawless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English thawian; related to Old High German douwen to thaw, Old Norse theyja to thaw, Latin tabēre to waste away

(θɔː ) for learners of English
Word forms: thaws , thawing , thawed
Definitions
1. verbWhen ice, snow, or something else that is frozen thaws, it melts. [V] ⇒ It's so cold the snow doesn't get a chance to thaw. [V] ⇒ The ground has thawed.2. countable nounA thaw is a period of warmer weather when snow and ice melt, usually at the end of winter. ⇒ We slogged through the mud of an early spring thaw.3. ergative verb & verbWhen you thaw frozen food or when it thaws, you leave it in a place where it can reach room temperature so that it is ready for use. defrost [V n] ⇒ Always thaw pastry thoroughly. [V] ⇒ The food in the freezer had thawed during a power cut.Thaw out means the same as thaw. [V n P] ⇒ Thaw it out completely before reheating in a saucepan. [V P n] ⇒ I remember to thaw out the chicken before I leave home. [Also V P]4. ergative verb & verbIf something thaws relations between people or if relations thaw, they become friendly again after a period of being unfriendly. [V n] ⇒ At least this second meeting had helped to thaw the atmosphere. [V] ⇒ It took up to Christmas for political relations to thaw.Thaw is also a noun. [+ in] ⇒ His visit is one of the most striking results of the thaw in relations between East and West.► →  thaw out
