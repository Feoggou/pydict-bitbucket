genuflect

(ˈdʒɛnjʊˌflɛkt )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to act in a servile or deferential manner2. Roman Catholic Church to bend one or both knees as a sign of reverence, esp when passing before the Blessed Sacrament
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

genuflection (ˌgenuˈflection) or especially British genuflexion (ˌgenuˈflexion) noungenuflector (ˈgenuˌflector) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Medieval Latin genūflectere, from Latin genu knee + flectere to bend

genuflect

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒenjʊflekt ) for learners of English
Word forms: genuflects , genuflecting , genuflected
Definitions
1. verbIf you genuflect, you bend one or both knees and bow, especially in church, as a sign of respect. [formal] [V] ⇒ He genuflected in front of the altar.2. verbYou can say that someone is genuflecting to something when they are giving it a great deal of attention and respect, especially if you think it does not deserve this. [mainly journalism, disapproval] [V + to] ⇒ They refrained from genuflecting to the laws of political economy. [Also V prep]
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
