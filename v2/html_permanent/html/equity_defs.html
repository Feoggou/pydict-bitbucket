equity

(ˈɛkwɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the quality of being impartial or reasonable; fairness2. an impartial or fair act, decision, etc3. law a system of jurisprudence founded on principles of natural justice and fair conduct. It supplements the common law and mitigates its inflexibility, as by providing a remedy where none exists at law4. law an equitable right or claim ⇒ equity of redemption5. the interest of ordinary shareholders in a company6. the market value of a debtor's property in excess of all debts to which it is liable
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French equite, from Latin aequitās, from aequus level, equal
