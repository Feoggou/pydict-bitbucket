Powered by COBUILD
(ɪntərækt ) for learners of English
Word forms: interacts , interacting , interacted
Definitions
1. verbWhen people interact with each other or interact, they communicate as they work or spend time together. [V] ⇒ While the other children interacted and played together, Ted ignored them. [V + with] ⇒ ...rhymes and songs to help parents interact with their babies.interaction (ɪntərækʃən )Word forms: interactions variable noun ⇒ This can sometimes lead to somewhat superficial interactions with other people. [+ among] ⇒ ...our experience of informal social interaction among adults.2. verbWhen people interact with computers, or when computers interact with other machines, information or instructions are exchanged. [V + with] ⇒ Millions of people want new, simplified ways of interacting with a computer. [V] ⇒ There will be a true global village in which telephones, computers and televisions interact.interaction Word forms: interactions variable noun ⇒ ...experts on human-computer interaction.3. verbWhen one thing interacts with another or two things interact, the two things affect each other's behaviour or condition. [V] ⇒ You have to understand how cells interact. [V + with] ⇒ Atoms within the fluid interact with the minerals that form the grains.interaction variable noun [+ between] ⇒ ...the interaction between physical and emotional illness.
