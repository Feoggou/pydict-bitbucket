breakout

(ˈbreɪkˌaʊt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an escape, esp from prison or confinement ⇒ Five crocs were at large in Darwin's rural region following a mass breakout from a crocodile farm., ⇒ A prisoner escaped one day after he was recaptured following a previous breakout., ⇒ High Point prison had the highest number of breakouts of any jail in Britain., ⇒ a mass breakout by more than 50 prisoners who overwhelmed their guards2. a great success, esp following relatively mediocre or disappointing performances ⇒ She is hoping for a breakout with this latest movie.3. sport a sudden attack, esp from a defensive position, in football, hockey, etc. another name for breakawaymodifier4. constituting or causing a sudden big success ⇒ She maintains that the role of Marianne in Sense and Sensibility was her breakout role., ⇒ Who will be the breakout stars of the World Cup 2012?
