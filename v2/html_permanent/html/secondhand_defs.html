secondhand

(ˈsɛkəndˈhænd )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
adjective1.  not direct from the original source; not original2.  used or worn previously by another; not new3.  of or dealing in merchandise that is not new adverb4.  not firsthand; not directly

secondhand

Powered by COBUILD
(sɛkəndhænd ) also second-hand
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced American English Dictionary
1. adjectiveSecondhand things are not new and have been owned by someone else. ⇒ They could afford a secondhand car, she thought.2. adjective [ADJ n]A secondhand store sells secondhand goods. ⇒ ...lovingly restored old pieces bought from a secondhand store.3. adjectiveSecondhand stories, information, or opinions are those you learn about from other people rather than directly or from your own experience. ⇒ He urged the committee to discount any secondhand knowledge or hearsay.4. → at second hand
Example sentences containing 'secondhand'
For some time, Jenny had made do with secondhand tack for her horse, a sorrel gelding named Kiddo. J.A. Jance DEVIL'S CLAW
