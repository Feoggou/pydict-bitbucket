(peɪnfʊli )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adverbYou use painfully to emphasize a quality or situation that is undesirable. [emphasis] ⇒ Things are moving painfully slowly. ⇒ ...a painfully shy young man. ⇒ I am painfully aware that staff have a heavy work schedule.2. → see also painful
Example sentences containing 'painfully'
His shirt was damp with blood, and his shoulder throbbed painfully. Stewart, Michael COMPULSIONA movement caught Dennison's eye and he was still turning when a stone caught him painfully on the shoulder. MacNeill, Alastair THE DEVIL'S DOORMy heart was thumping painfully , the emotions I was feeling clashing painfully with the knot of anger in my gut. Matthews, Lew A CONVICTION OF GUILT
