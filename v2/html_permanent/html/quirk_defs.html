quirk

(kwɜːk )
Definitions
noun1. an individual peculiarity of character; mannerism or foible2. an unexpected twist or turn ⇒ a quirk of fate3. a continuous groove in an architectural moulding4. a flourish, as in handwriting
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

quirky (ˈquirky) adjectivequirkily (ˈquirkily) adverbquirkiness (ˈquirkiness) noun

Word Origin

C16: of unknown origin

quirk

Powered by COBUILD
(kwɜːʳk ) for learners of English
Word forms: quirks
Definitions
1. countable noun [adj N]A quirk is something unusual or interesting that happens by chance. [+ of] ⇒ By a tantalising quirk of fate, the pair have been drawn to meet in the first round of the championship. ⇒ The spate of storms and hurricanes in recent years could be a statistical quirk.2. countable nounA quirk is a habit or aspect of a person's character which is odd or unusual. ⇒ Brown was fascinated by people's quirks and foibles.
