conduit

(ˈkɒndɪt , -djʊɪt)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a pipe or channel for carrying a fluid2. a rigid tube or duct for carrying and protecting electrical wires or cables3. an agency or means of access, communication, etc4. botany a water-transporting element in a plant; a xylem vessel or a tracheid5. a rare word for fountain
Word origin
C14: from Old French, from Medieval Latin conductus channel, aqueduct, from Latin condūcere to lead, conduce
