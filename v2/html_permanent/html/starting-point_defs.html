starting point

(ˈstɑːtɪŋ pɔɪnt)

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the place from which you start ⇒ This makes a good starting point for a tour around the island., ⇒ The newly built airport acts as a starting point for safaris into the game parks and reserves of northern Tanzania., ⇒ They had already walked a couple of miles from their starting point.2. a starting point for a discussion or process can be used to begin it or act as a basis for it ⇒ I think that's actually quite a good starting point., ⇒ A pair of pretty, faded pink antique curtains was the starting point for the room's colour scheme., ⇒ These proposals represent a realistic starting point for negotiation.

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounSomething that is a starting point for a discussion or process can be used to begin it or act as a basis for it. [+ for] ⇒ These proposals represent a realistic starting point for negotiation. ⇒ A pair of pretty, faded pink antique curtains was the starting point for the room's colour scheme.2. countable nounWhen you make a journey, your starting point is the place from which you start. ⇒ The newly built airport acts as a starting point for safaris into the game parks and reserves of northern Tanzania. ⇒ They had already walked a couple of miles or more from their starting point.
