adduct

(əˈdʌkt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. (of a muscle) to draw or pull (a leg, arm, etc) towards the median axis of the body. Compare abduct (sense 2)noun2. chemistry a compound formed by direct combination of two or more different compounds or elements
Derived forms
adˈduction noun
Word origin
C19: from Latin addūcere; see adduce
