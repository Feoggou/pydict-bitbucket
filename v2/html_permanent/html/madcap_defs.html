madcap

(ˈmædˌkæp )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. impulsive, reckless, or livelynoun2. an impulsive, reckless, or lively person

Word Origin

C16: from mad + cap (in the figurative sense: head)

graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]A madcap plan or scheme is very foolish and not likely to succeed. [informal] ⇒ The politicians simply flitted from one madcap scheme to another. ⇒ His fast-paced novels are full of bizarre situations and madcap antics.
