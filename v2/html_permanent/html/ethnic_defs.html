ethnic

(ˈɛθnɪk ) or ethnical
Definitions
adjective1. relating to or characteristic of a human group having racial, religious, linguistic, and certain other traits in common2. relating to the classification of mankind into groups, esp on the basis of racial characteristics3. denoting or deriving from the cultural traditions of a group of people ⇒ the ethnic dances of Slovakia4. characteristic of another culture ⇒ the ethnic look, ⇒ ethnic foodnoun5. mainly US and Australian a member of an ethnic group, esp a minority group▶ USAGE Referring to a person as an ethnic is broadly acceptable in the US, Australia, and Canada, but could well cause offence in the UK and elsewhere
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

ethnically (ˈethnically) adverbethnicity (ɛθˈnɪsɪtɪ ) noun

Word Origin

C14 (in the senses: heathen, Gentile): from Late Latin ethnicus, from Greek ethnikos, from ethnos race

ethnic

Powered by COBUILD
(eθnɪk ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Ethnic means connected with or relating to different racial or cultural groups of people. ⇒ ...a survey of Britain's ethnic minorities. ⇒ ...ethnic tensions.ethnically (eθnɪkli ) adverb [usu ADV -ed/adj] ⇒ ...a predominantly young, ethnically mixed audience.2. adjective [ADJ n]You can use ethnic to describe people who belong to a particular racial or cultural group but who, usually, do not live in the country where most members of that group live. ⇒ There are still several million ethnic Germans in Russia.ethnically adverb [ADV adj] ⇒ ...a large ethnically Albanian population.3. graded adjective & adjectiveEthnic clothing, music, or food is characteristic of the traditions of a particular ethnic group, and different from what is usually found in modern Western culture. ⇒ ...the original flavours of ethnic dishes. ⇒ ...a magnificent range of ethnic fabrics.
