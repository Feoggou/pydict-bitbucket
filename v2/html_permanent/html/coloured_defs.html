Coloured - US

or Colored (ˈkʌləd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural Coloureds, ColouredWord forms: US Coloreds or Colored1. old-fashioned, offensive an individual who is not a White person, esp a Black person2. South Africaa. a person of mixed ethnic parentage or descentb. a person of mixed ethnic descent speaking English or Afrikaans as their mother tongueadjective3. old-fashioned, offensive designating or relating to a Coloured person or Coloured people▶ USAGE The use of Coloured to refer to a person of mixed ethnic origin is likely to cause offence and should be avoided

coloured

Powered by COBUILD
(kʌləʳd )
Word forms: coloureds
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
regional note:   in AM, use colored1. adjectiveSomething that is coloured a particular colour is that colour. ⇒ The illustration shows a cluster of five roses coloured apricot-orange. ⇒ ...a cheap gold-coloured bracelet.2. adjectiveSomething that is coloured is a particular colour or combination of colours, rather than being just white, black, or the colour that it is naturally. ⇒ You can often choose between plain white or coloured and patterned scarves. ⇒ ...brightly-coloured silks laid out on market stalls.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]A coloured person belongs to a race of people with dark skins. [offensive, old-fashioned]4. countable nounPeople whose skin is dark are sometimes referred to as coloureds. [offensive, old-fashioned]
