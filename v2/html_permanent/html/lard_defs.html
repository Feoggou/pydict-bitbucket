lard

(lɑːd )
Definitions
noun1. the rendered fat from a pig, esp from the abdomen, used in cooking2. informal excess fat on a person's bodyverb (transitive)3. to prepare (lean meat, poultry, etc) by inserting small strips of bacon or fat before cooking4. to cover or smear (foods) with lard5. to add extra material to (speech or writing); embellish
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

lardlike (ˈlardˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C15: via Old French from Latin lāridum bacon fat

lard

Powered by COBUILD
(lɑːʳd ) for learners of English
Word forms: lards , larding , larded
Definitions
1. uncount nounLard is soft white fat obtained from pigs. It is used in cooking. ⇒ ...lard or beef fat.2. verbIf speech or writing is larded with particular types of words, it contains a lot of them or too many of them. [be V-ed with n] ⇒ Official speeches in recent days have been larded with promises of democracy. [V-ed] ⇒ ...a long phone call, larded with 'darlings' and a sickening amount of baby-talk. [Also V n with n]
