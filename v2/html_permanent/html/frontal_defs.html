frontal

(ˈfrʌntəl )
Definitions
adjective1. of, at, or in the front2. of or relating to the forehead ⇒ frontal artery3. of or relating to the anterior part of a body or organ4. meteorology of, relating to, or resulting from a front or its passage ⇒ frontal rainfallnoun5. a decorative hanging for the front of an altar6. See frontal lobe, frontal bone7. another name for frontlet (sense 1)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

frontally (ˈfrontally) adverb

Word Origin

C14 (in the sense: adornment for forehead, altar cloth): via Old French frontel, from Latin frontālia (pl) ornament worn on forehead, frontellum altar cloth, both from frōns forehead, front

Definitions
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Frontal means relating to or involving the front of something, for example the front of an army, a vehicle, or the brain. [formal] ⇒ Military leaders are not expecting a frontal assault by the rebels. ⇒ He pioneered the surgical technique called frontal lobotomy.2. adjective [usu ADJ n]A frontal attack or challenge criticizes or threatens something in a very strong, direct way. head-on ⇒ He launches a frontal attack on working-class organizations.3. → See also full-frontal
