surety

(ˈʃʊətɪ , ˈʃʊərɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. a person who assumes legal responsibility for the fulfilment of another's debt or obligation and himself becomes liable if the other defaults2. security given against loss or damage or as a guarantee that an obligation will be met3. obsolete the quality or condition of being sure4. obsolete a means of assurance or safety5. See stand surety

Derived Forms

suretyˌship (ˈsuretyˌship) noun

Word Origin

C14: from Old French seurte, from Latin sēcūritās security

COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounA surety is money or something valuable which you give to someone to show that you will do what you have promised. ⇒ The insurance company will take warehouse stocks or treasury bonds as surety. ⇒ Bristol Crown Court granted conditional bail with a surety of £2,500.
