Synonyms of slip 1

slip1

verb

  1. fall, trip (over), slide, skid, lose your balance, miss or lose your footing
    • Be careful not to slip.
  2. slide, fall, drop, slither
    • The hammer slipped out of her grasp.
  3. sneak, creep, steal, slope, sidle, move stealthily, ghost, insinuate yourself
    • She slipped downstairs and out of the house.
  4. drop, sink, plunge, slump, tumble, nosedive
    • The club has slipped to the bottom of division four.
  5. decline, fall, deteriorate, drop, worsen, wane, degenerate
    • There is a general public belief that standards have slipped.
  6. pass, elapse, roll by, wear on, tick by, glide
    • Time slipped by in silence.

noun

  1. mistake, failure, error, blunder, lapse, omission, boob (British, slang), oversight, slip-up (informal), indiscretion, bloomer (British, informal), faux pas, slip of the tongue, imprudence, barry or Barry Crocker (Australian, slang)
    • There must be no slips.
  • See give someone the slip
  • See let something slip
  • See slip away
  • See slip something off
  • See slip something on
  • See slip up

slip2

noun

  1. strip, piece, scrap, sliver, sheet
    • little slips of paper
  2. cutting, shoot, runner, sprout, sprig, offshoot, scion
  • See a slip of a
