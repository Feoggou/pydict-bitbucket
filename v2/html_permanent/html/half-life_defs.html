half-life

Definitions
noun1. the time taken for half of the atoms in a radioactive material to undergo decay. Symbol: τ2. the time required for half of a quantity of radioactive material absorbed by a living tissue or organism to be naturally eliminated (biological half-life) or removed by both elimination and decay (effective half-life)
