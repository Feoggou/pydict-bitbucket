anesthesia

(ˌænɛsˈθiʒə ; ˌænɪsˈθiʒiə ; ˌænɪsˈθiziə ; ˌ ænɪsˈθiziə )
Definitions
Webster’s New World College Dictionary
noun1.  a partial or total loss of the sense of pain, temperature, touch, etc., produced by disease2.  Us a loss of sensation induced by an anesthetic, hypnosis, or acupuncture and limited to a specific area (local anesthesia) or involving a loss of consciousness (general anesthesia)
