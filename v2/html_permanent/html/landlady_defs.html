landlady

(ˈlændˌleɪdɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -dies1. a woman who owns and leases property2. a landlord's wife3. a woman who owns or runs a lodging house, pub, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

landlady

Powered by COBUILD
(lændleɪdi ) for learners of English
Word forms: landladies
Definitions
1. countable nounSomeone's landlady is the woman who allows them to live or work in a building which she owns, in return for rent. ⇒ We had been made homeless by our landlady.2. countable nounThe landlady of a pub is the woman who owns or runs it, or the wife of the person who owns or runs it. [British] [+ of] ⇒ ...Bet, the landlady of the Rovers Return.3. countable nounA landlady is the woman who owns or runs a boarding house or inn. [US, also British, old-fashioned]
