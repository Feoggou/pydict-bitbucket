comply

(kəmˈplaɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -plies, -plying or -plied (intransitive)1. (usually foll by with) to act in accordance with rules, wishes, etc; be obedient (to)2. obsolete to be obedient or complaisant
Derived forms
complier (comˈplier) noun
Word origin
C17: from Italian complire, from Spanish cumplir to complete; see compliment

Word forms: complies , complying , complied
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone or something complies with an order or set of rules, they are in accordance with what is required or expected. [V + with] ⇒ The commander said that the army would comply with the ceasefire. [V with n] ⇒ Some beaches had failed to comply with European directives on bathing water. [V] ⇒ There are calls for his resignation, but there is no sign yet that he will comply. [Also V to n]
