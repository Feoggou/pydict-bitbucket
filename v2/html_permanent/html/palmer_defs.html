palmer

(ˈpɑːmə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (in Medieval Europe) a pilgrim bearing a palm branch as a sign of his visit to the Holy Land2. (in Medieval Europe) an itinerant monk3. (in Medieval Europe) any pilgrim4. any of various artificial angling flies characterized by hackles around the length of the body

Word Origin

C13: from Old French palmier, from Medieval Latin palmārius, from Latin palma palm
