tapestry

(ˈtæpɪstrɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -tries1. a heavy ornamental fabric, often in the form of a picture, used for wall hangings, furnishings, etc, and made by weaving coloured threads into a fixed warp2. another word for needlepoint3. a colourful and complicated situation ⇒ the rich tapestry of London life
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

tapestried (ˈtapestried) adjectivetapestry-like (ˈtapestry-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French tapisserie carpeting, from Old French tapiz carpet; see tapis

(tæpɪstri ) for learners of English
Word forms: tapestries
Definitions
1. variable nounA tapestry is a large piece of heavy cloth with a picture sewn on it using coloured threads. ⇒ The seats of the chairs had been recovered in tapestry. ⇒ He sat on a tapestry cushion next to the hearth. ⇒ On the opposite wall is a large tapestry.2. countable nounYou can refer to something as a tapestry when it is made up of many varied types of people or things. [literary] [+ of] ⇒ Hedgerows and meadows are thick with a tapestry of wild flowers. ⇒ ...Chicago's political tapestry.
