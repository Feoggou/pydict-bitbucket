propel

(prəˈpɛl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb -pels, -pelling or -pelled(transitive) to impel, drive, or cause to move forwards

Word Origin

C15: from Latin prōpellere to drive onwards, from pro-1 + pellere to drive

propel

Powered by COBUILD
(prəpel )
Word forms: propels , propelling , propelled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbTo propel something in a particular direction means to cause it to move in that direction. [V n prep] ⇒ The tiny rocket is attached to the spacecraft and is designed to propel it toward Mars.-propelled combines with nouns to form adjectives which indicate how something, especially a weapon, is propelled. ⇒ ...rocket-propelled grenades. ⇒ ...the first jet-propelled aeroplane.2. verbIf something propels you into a particular activity, it causes you to do it. [V n prep] ⇒ It was a shooting star that propelled me into astronomy in the first place. [be V-ed] ⇒ He is propelled by both guilt and the need to avenge his father.
