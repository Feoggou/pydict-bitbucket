crossing

(ˈkrɒsɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the place where one thing crosses another2. a place, often shown by markings, lights, or poles, where a street, railway, etc, may be crossed3. the intersection of the nave and transept in a church4. the act or instance of travelling across something, esp the sea5. the act or process of crossbreeding

Word forms: crossings
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA crossing is a journey by boat or ship to a place on the other side of a sea, river, or lake. ⇒ He made the crossing from Cape Town to Sydney in just over twenty-six days. ⇒ The vessel docked in Swansea after a ten-hour crossing.2. countable nounA crossing is a place where two roads, paths, or lines cross.3. countable nounA crossing is the same as a pedestrian crossing. [British] ⇒ A car hit her on a crossing.regional note:   in AM, use crosswalk4. → see also pelican crossing, zebra crossing5. countable nounA crossing is the same as a grade crossing or a level crossing.
