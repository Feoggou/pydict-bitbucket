full up

or full-up (fʊl ʌp)
Definitions
adjective1. filled to capacity; unable to hold any more (things, people, etc) ⇒ The prisons are all full up.2. unable to eat any more (due to already eating a lot, etc) ⇒ I'm full up.
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Definitions
1. adjective [v-link ADJ]Something that is full up has no space left for any more people or things. ⇒ The prisons are all full up.2. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are full up you have eaten or drunk so much that you do not want to eat or drink anything else. [informal]
