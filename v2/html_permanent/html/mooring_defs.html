mooring

(ˈmʊərɪŋ , ˈmɔː-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a place for mooring a vessel2. a permanent anchor, dropped in the water and equipped with a floating buoy, to which vessels can moor

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA mooring is a place where a boat can be tied so that it cannot move away, or the object it is tied to. ⇒ Free moorings will be available. ⇒ ...mooring fees.2. plural nounMoorings are the ropes, chains, and other objects used to moor a boat. ⇒ He cut the engine and grabbed the mooring lines. ⇒ Emergency workers fear that the burning ship could slip its moorings.
