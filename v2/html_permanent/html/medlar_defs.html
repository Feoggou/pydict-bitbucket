medlar

(ˈmɛdlə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a small Eurasian rosaceous tree, Mespilus germanica2. the fruit of this tree, which resembles the crab apple and is not edible until it has begun to decay3. any of several other rosaceous trees or their fruits

Word Origin

C14: from Old French medlier, from Latin mespilum medlar fruit, from Greek mespilon
Example sentences containing 'medlar'
If a peach were at its best when it was as rotten as a medlar , one would soon find a dark, wrinkled peach good-looking. O'Brian, Patrick TESTIMONIESFred notices that the twisted branches of the medlar look as if they might be dying. Sara MacDonald SEA MUSIC
