loathe

(ləʊð )
Definitions
verb(transitive) to feel strong hatred or disgust for
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

loather (ˈloather) noun

Word Origin

Old English lāthiān, from loath

loathe

Powered by COBUILD
(loʊð ) for learners of English
Word forms: loathes , loathing , loathed
Definitions
verbIf you loathe something or someone, you dislike them very much. [V n] ⇒ The two men loathe each other. [V v-ing] ⇒ She loathed being the child of impoverished labourers.
