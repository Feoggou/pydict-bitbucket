wicket

(ˈwɪkɪt )
Definitions
noun1. a small door or gate, esp one that is near to or part of a larger one2. US a small window or opening in a door, esp one fitted with a grating or glass pane, used as a means of communication in a ticket office, bank, etc3. a small sluicegate, esp one in a canal lock gate or by a water wheel4. US a croquet hoop5. a. cricket either of two constructions, placed 22 yards apart, consisting of three pointed stumps stuck parallel in the ground with two wooden bails resting on top, at which the batsman standsb. the strip of ground between thesec. a batsman's turn at batting or the period during which two batsmen bat ⇒ a third-wicket partnershipd. the act or instance of a batsman being got out ⇒ the bowler took six wickets6. See keep wicket7. See on a sticky wicket
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C18: from Old Northern French wiket; related to Old Norse vikja to move

wicket for learners of English

(wɪkɪt )
Word forms: wickets
Definitions
1. countable nounIn cricket, a wicket is a set of three upright sticks with two small sticks on top of them at which the ball is bowled. There are two wickets on a cricket pitch.2. countable nounIn cricket, a wicket is the area of grass in between the two wickets on the pitch.3. countable nounIn cricket, when a wicket falls or is taken, a batsman is out.
