accurate

(ˈækjərɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. faithfully representing or describing the truth2. showing a negligible or permissible deviation from a standard ⇒ an accurate ruler3. without error; precise; meticulous4. mathematicsa. (to n significant digits) representing the first n digits of the given number starting with the first nonzero digit, but approximating to the nearest digit in the final position ⇒ since π = 3.14159…, the approximation 3.1416 is accurate to 5 significant digits.b. (to n decimal places) giving the first n digits after the decimal point without further approximation ⇒ π = 3.1415 is in this sense accurate to 4 decimal places
Derived forms
ˈaccurately adverbˈaccurateness noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin accūrātus, past participle of accūrāre to perform with care, from cūra care

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveAccurate information, measurements, and statistics are correct to a very detailed level. An accurate instrument is able to give you information of this kind. ⇒ Police have stressed that this is the most accurate description of the killer to date. ⇒ This may provide a quick and accurate way of monitoring the amount of carbon dioxide in the air. ⇒ Quartz timepieces are very accurate, to a minute or two per year.accurately graded adverb & adverb ⇒ The test can accurately predict what a bigger explosion would do.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn accurate statement or account gives a true or fair judgment of something. ⇒ Joseph Stalin gave an accurate assessment of the utility of nuclear weapons. ⇒ They were accurate in their prediction that he would change her life drastically.accurately graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] OPPOSITE: inaccurately ⇒ What many people mean by the word 'power' could be more accurately described as 'control'.3. graded adjective & adjectiveYou can use accurate to describe the results of someone's actions when they do or copy something correctly or exactly. ⇒ Marks were given for accurate spelling and punctuation. ⇒ ...his maliciously accurate imitation of Hubert de Burgh.4. graded adjective & adjectiveAn accurate weapon or throw reaches the exact point or target that it was intended to reach. You can also describe a person as accurate if they fire a weapon or throw something in this way. ⇒ The rifle was extremely accurate. ⇒ The pilots, however, were not as accurate as they should be.accurately graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] OPPOSITE: inaccurately ⇒ ...the technology to aim bombs accurately from aircraft.
