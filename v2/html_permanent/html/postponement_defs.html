postponement

Powered by COBUILD
(poʊspoʊnmənt )
Word forms: postponements
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
variable nounThe postponement of an event is the act of delaying it happening or arranging for it to take place at a later time than originally planned. ⇒ The postponement was due to a dispute over where the talks should be held. [Also + of]
Example sentences containing 'postponement'
Whatever it was it meant the end or at least the postponement of promised joy. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEADBut truly, I suppose you're right enough about getting that postponement. Kerr, Katharine A TIME OF WARBut she guessed -- again rightly -- that the attempt to involve them was the reason for postponement of her own trial. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WAR
