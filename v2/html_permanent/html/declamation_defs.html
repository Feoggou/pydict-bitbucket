declamation

(ˌdɛkləˈmeɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a rhetorical or emotional speech, made esp in order to protest or condemn; tirade2. a speech, verse, etc, that is or can be spoken3. the act or art of declaiming4. music the artistry or technique involved in singing recitative passages
Example sentences containing 'declamation'
At dawn he rose, mounted his horse, and left the village without a declamation , without a stated quest. Tepper, Sheri S. A PLAGUE OF ANGELSHis face gone blood-red with whiskey and the words tumbling out in ecstatic declamation , tossing in the lop of his own talk. Annie Proulx THE SHIPPING NEWS
