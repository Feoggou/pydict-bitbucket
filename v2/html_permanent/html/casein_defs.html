casein

(ˈkeɪsɪɪn , -siːn)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna phosphoprotein, precipitated from milk by the action of rennin, forming the basis of cheese: used in the manufacture of plastics and adhesives. Also called (US): paracasein
Word origin
C19: from Latin cāseus cheese + -in
Example sentences containing 'casein'
The one painting I did take note of was a little scene of Tachnadray, done with skill in, of all things, milk casein paint. Gash, Jonathan THE TARTAN RINGERS
