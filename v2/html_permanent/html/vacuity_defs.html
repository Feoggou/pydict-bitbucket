vacuity

(væˈkjuːɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. the state or quality of being vacuous; emptiness2. an empty space or void; vacuum3. a lack or absence of something specified ⇒ a vacuity of wind4. lack of normal intelligence or awareness; vacancy ⇒ his stare gave an impression of complete vacuity5. something, such as a statement, saying, etc, that is inane or pointless6. (in customs terminology) the difference in volume between the actual contents of a container and its full capacity
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C16: from Latin vacuitās empty space, from vacuus empty

Definitions
uncount noun [usu with poss]If you refer to the vacuity of something or someone, you are critical of them because they lack intelligent thought or ideas. [formal, disapproval] ⇒ His vacuity was a handicap in these debates. ⇒ ...a campaign notable for its intellectual vacuity and personal nastiness.
