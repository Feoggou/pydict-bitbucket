throat

(θrəʊt )
Definitions
noun1. a. that part of the alimentary and respiratory tracts extending from the back of the mouth (nasopharynx) to just below the larynxb. the front part of the neck2. something resembling a throat, esp in shape or function ⇒ the throat of a chimney3. botany the gaping part of a tubular corolla or perianth4. informal a sore throat5. See cut one's throat6. See have by the throat7. See jump down someone's throat8. See ram something down someone's throat9. See stick in one's throatgulargutturaljugularlaryngeal
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English throtu; related to Old High German drozza throat, Old Norse throti swelling

Definitions
1. countable noun [oft poss N]Your throat is the back of your mouth and the top part of the tubes that go down into your stomach and your lungs. ⇒ She had a sore throat. ⇒ As she stared at him she felt her throat go dry.2. countable noun [oft poss N]Your throat is the front part of your neck. ⇒ His striped tie was loosened at his throat.3. →  to clear your throat4. →  to ram something down someone's throat5. →  at each other's throats6. →  to stick in your throat7. → a lump in your throat
