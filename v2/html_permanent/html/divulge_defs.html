divulge

(daɪˈvʌldʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb(tr; may take a clause as object) to make known (something private or secret); disclose
Derived forms
divulgence (diˈvulgence) or divulgement (diˈvulgement) noundivulger (diˈvulger) noun
Word origin
C15: from Latin dīvulgāre, from di-2 + vulgāre to spread among the people, from vulgus the common people

(daɪvʌldʒ , US dɪ- )
Word forms: divulges , divulging , divulged
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you divulge a piece of secret or private information, you tell it to someone. [formal] [V n] ⇒ Officials refuse to divulge details of the negotiations. [V n] ⇒ He was charged with divulging state secrets. [V wh] ⇒ I do not want to divulge where the village is. [Also V n + to, V that]
