antiquity

(ænˈtɪkwɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ties1. the quality of being ancient or very old ⇒ a vase of great antiquity2. the far distant past, esp the time preceding the Middle Ages in Europe3. the people of ancient times collectively; the ancients

(æntɪkwɪti )
Word forms: antiquities
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounAntiquity is the distant past, especially the time of the ancient Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans. ⇒ ...famous monuments of classical antiquity. ⇒ The town was famous in antiquity for its white bulls.2. countable noun [usu pl]Antiquities are things such as buildings, statues, or coins that were made in ancient times and have survived to the present day. ⇒ ...collectors of Roman antiquities.3. uncount nounThe antiquity of something is its great age. ⇒ ...a town of great antiquity. ⇒ It indicates the antiquity of the tradition.
