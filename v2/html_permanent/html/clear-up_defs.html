clear up

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (adverb)1. (transitive) to explain or solve (a mystery, misunderstanding, etc)2. to put (a place or thing that is disordered) in order3. (intransitive) (of the weather) to become brighternoun clear-up (ˈklɪərˌʌp )4. the act or an instance of clearing up

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbWhen you clear up or clear a place up, you tidy things and put them away. [V P] ⇒ After breakfast they played while I cleared up. [V P n] ⇒ I cleared up my room. [Also V n P]2. phrasal verbTo clear up a problem, misunderstanding, or mystery means to settle it or find a satisfactory explanation for it. [V P n (not pron)] ⇒ There should be someone to whom you can turn for any advice or to clear up any problems. [be V-ed P] ⇒ During dinner the confusion was cleared up: they had mistaken me for Kenny. [Also V n P, V P n]3. → see also clear-up4. ergative phrasal verb & phrasal verbTo clear up a medical problem, infection, or disease means to cure it or get rid of it. If a medical problem clears up, it goes away. [V P n] ⇒ Antibiotics should be used to clear up the infection. [V P] ⇒ Acne often clears up after the first three months of pregnancy. [Also V n P]5. phrasal verbWhen the weather clears up, it stops raining or being cloudy. brighten up [V P] ⇒ It all depends on the weather clearing up. →  clear

clear-up

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [ADJ n]The clear-up rate for a crime or in an area is the percentage of criminals caught by the police, compared to the total number of crimes reported. [British] ⇒ The clear-up rate for murders remains high. ⇒ The Metropolitan Police say clear-up figures were improved.
