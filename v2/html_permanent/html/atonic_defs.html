atonic

(eɪˈtɒnɪk , æ-)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of a syllable, word, etc) carrying no stress; unaccented2. pathology lacking body or muscle tonenoun3. an unaccented or unstressed syllable, word, etc, such as for in food for thought
Derived forms
atonicity (ˌætəˈnɪsɪtɪ , ˌeɪtəʊ-) noun
Word origin
C18: from Latin atonicus, from Greek atonos lacking tone; see atony
