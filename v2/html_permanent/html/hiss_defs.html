hiss

(hɪs )
Definitions
noun1. a voiceless fricative sound like that of a prolonged s2. such a sound uttered as an exclamation of derision, contempt, etc, esp by an audience or crowd3. electronics receiver noise with a continuous spectrum, caused by thermal agitation, shot noise, etcexclamation4. an exclamation of derision or disapprovalverb5. (intransitive) to produce or utter a hiss6. (transitive) to express with a hiss, usually to indicate derision or anger7. (transitive) to show derision or anger towards (a speaker, performer, etc) by hissing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

hiss

Powered by COBUILD
(hɪs ) for learners of English
Word forms: hisses , hissing , hissed
Definitions
1. verbTo hiss means to make a sound like a long 's'. [V prep] ⇒ The tires of Lenny's bike hissed over the wet pavement as he slowed down. [V] ⇒ My cat hissed when I stepped on its tail. [V-ing] ⇒ Caporelli made a small hissing sound of irritation.Hiss is also a noun. ⇒ ...the hiss of water running into the burnt pan. ⇒ The CD banished for ever all the hisses and crackles that had plagued disc recordings until then.hissing uncount noun ⇒ ...a silence broken only by a steady hissing from above my head.2. verbIf you hiss something, you say it forcefully in a whisper. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Now, quiet,' my mother hissed. [V + at/to] ⇒ 'Stay here,' I hissed at her.3. verbIf people hiss at someone such as a performer or a person making a speech, they express their disapproval or dislike of that person by making long loud 's' sounds. [V] ⇒ One had to listen hard to catch the words of the President's speech as the delegates booed and hissed. [V + at] ⇒ Some local residents whistled and hissed at them as they entered. [Also V n]Hiss is also a noun. ⇒ After a moment the barracking began. First came hisses, then shouts.
