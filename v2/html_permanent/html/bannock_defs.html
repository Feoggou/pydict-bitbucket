bannock

(ˈbænək )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna round flat unsweetened cake originating in Scotland, made from oatmeal or barley and baked on a griddle
Word origin
Old English bannuc; of Celtic origin; compare Gaelic bannach, Cornish banna a drop, bit; perhaps related to Latin pānicium, from pānis bread
Example sentences containing 'bannock'
We had a meal -- bacon, eggs, some tinned beans, a sort of bannock of flour and honey, more mugs of tea. Innes, Hammond HIGH STAND`Look, Beth, I'll give you this if you play wi "me at break and I'll give you a buttered bannock. Fraser, Christine Marion NOBLE BEGINNNINGS
