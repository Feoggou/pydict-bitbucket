virago

(vɪˈrɑːɡəʊ )
Definitions
noun plural -goes or -gos1. a loud, violent, and ill-tempered woman; scold; shrew2. archaic a strong, brave, or warlike woman; amazon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

viraginous (vɪˈrædʒɪnəs ) adjectivevirago-like (viˈrago-ˌlike) adjective

Word Origin

Old English, from Latin: a manlike maiden, from vir a man
Example sentences containing 'virago'
It was a large, gaunt woman speaking, a virago with a grim, ravaged face. Townsend, Eileen IN LOVE AND WARThe sight of a clergyman on her doorstep transformed her in an instant from a virago into a quietly spoken model of respectability. Secombe, Fred GOODBYE CURATEThere---sitting in the boat---was the storm-maker, the virago herself, a scarf of dark silk draped over her head. Clive Barker GALILEEMy niece Caterina, a true virago , is being held in one of the Borgia castles and her territories have been conquered as well. Mario Puzo THE FAMILY

Similar Words

harridan, fury, shrew, vixen, scold, battle-axe (informal), termagant (rare), Xanthippe, ballbreaker (slang)
