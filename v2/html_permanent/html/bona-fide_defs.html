bona fide

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective (ˈbəʊnə ˈfaɪdɪ )1. real or genuine ⇒ a bona fide manuscript2. undertaken in good faith ⇒ a bona fide agreementnoun (ˈbɔːnə fɑɪd)3. Irish informal a public house licensed to remain open after normal hours to serve bona fide travellers
Word origin
C16: from Latin

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjective [usu ADJ n]If something or someone is bona fide, they are genuine or real. [formal] ⇒ We are happy to donate to bona fide charitable causes. ⇒ We simply cannot believe that a bona fide seller would conduct business on this basis.
