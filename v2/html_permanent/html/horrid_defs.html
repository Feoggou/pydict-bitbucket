horrid

(ˈhɒrɪd )
Definitions
adjective1. disagreeable; unpleasant ⇒ a horrid meal2. repulsive or frightening3. informal unkind
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

horridly (ˈhorridly) adverbhorridness (ˈhorridness) noun

Word Origin

C16 (in the sense: bristling, shaggy): from Latin horridus prickly, rough, from horrēre to bristle

horrid

Powered by COBUILD
(hɒrɪd , US hɔːr- ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as horrid, you mean that it is very unpleasant indeed. [informal] ⇒ What a horrid smell! ⇒ The winter was horrid.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as horrid, you mean that they behave in a very unpleasant way towards other people. [informal] [+ to] ⇒ I must have been a horrid little girl. ⇒ I love both my parents, but they're horrid to each other.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
