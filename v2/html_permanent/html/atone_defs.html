atone

(əˈtəʊn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive; foll by for) to make amends or reparation (for a crime, sin, etc)2. (transitive) to expiate ⇒ to atone a guilt with repentance3. obsolete to be in or bring into agreement
Derived forms
aˈtonable or aˈtoneable adjectiveaˈtoner noun
Word origin
C16: back formation from atonement

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you atone for something that you have done, you do something to show that you are sorry you did it. [formal] [V + for] ⇒ He felt he had atoned for what he had done to his son. [V by -ing] ⇒ He atoned by apologizing. [V] ⇒ I'll never live long enough to atone.
