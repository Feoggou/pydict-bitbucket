Orpheus

(ˈɔːfɪəs , -fjuːs)
Definitions
nounGreek mythology a poet and lyre-player credited with the authorship of the poems forming the basis of Orphism. He married Eurydice and sought her in Hades after her death. He failed to win her back and was killed by a band of bacchantes
