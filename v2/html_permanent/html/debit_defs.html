debit

(ˈdɛbɪt ) accounting
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. acknowledgment of a sum owing by entry on the left side of an accountb. the left side of an accountc. an entry on this sided. the total of such entriese. (as modifier) ⇒ a debit balance. Compare credit (sense 10)verb -bits, -biting or -bited2. (transitive)a. to record (an item) as a debit in an accountb. to charge (a person or his or her account) with a debt. Compare credit (sense 17)
Word origin
C15: from Latin dēbitum debt
