arrange

verb

  1. plan, agree, prepare, determine, schedule, organize, construct, devise, contrive, fix up, jack up (New Zealand, informal) • She arranged an appointment for Friday afternoon.
  2. put in order, group, form, order, sort, class, position, range, file, rank, line up, organize, set out, sequence, exhibit, sort out (informal), array, classify, tidy, marshal, align, categorize, systematize, dispose, jack up (New Zealand, informal) • He started to arrange the books in piles. OPPOSITES:  disturb, scatter, mess up, disorganize, disarrange, muss (US & & Canadian)
  3. adapt, score, orchestrate, harmonize, instrument • The songs were arranged by a well-known pianist.
  4. agree, determine, settle, compromise, adjust, come to terms
