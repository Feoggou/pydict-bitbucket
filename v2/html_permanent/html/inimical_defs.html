inimical

(ɪˈnɪmɪkəl )
Definitions
adjective1. adverse or unfavourable2. not friendly; hostile
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

inimically (inˈimically) adverbinimicalness (inˈimicalness) or inimicality (inˌimiˈcality) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Late Latin inimīcālis, from inimīcus, from in-1 + amīcus friendly; see enemy

Definitions
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Conditions that are inimical to something make it difficult for that thing to exist or do well. [formal] ⇒ ...a false morality that is inimical to human happiness. [+ to] ⇒ ...goals inimical to Western interests.
