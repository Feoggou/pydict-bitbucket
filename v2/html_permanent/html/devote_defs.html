devote

(dɪˈvəʊt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to apply or dedicate (oneself, time, money, etc) to some pursuit, cause, etc2. obsolete to curse or doom
Derived forms
devotement (deˈvotement) noun
Word origin
C16: from Latin dēvōtus devoted, solemnly promised, from dēvovēre to vow; see de-, vow

devote

Powered by COBUILD
(dɪvoʊt )
Word forms: devotes , devoting , devoted
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you devote yourself, your time, or your energy to something, you spend all or most of your time or energy on it. [V n + to] ⇒ He decided to devote the rest of his life to scientific investigation. [V n + to] ⇒ Considerable resources have been devoted to proving him a liar. [V pron-refl + to] ⇒ She gladly gave up her part-time job to devote herself entirely to her art.2. verbIf you devote a particular proportion of a piece of writing or a speech to a particular subject, you deal with the subject in that amount of space or time. [V n + to] ⇒ He devoted a major section of his massive report to an analysis of U.S. aircraft design. [V n to n] ⇒ Page upon page is devoted to the chain of events leading to the Prime Minister's resignation.
