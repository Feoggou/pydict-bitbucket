infusion

(ɪnˈfjuːʒən )
Definitions
noun1. the act of infusing2. something infused3. an extract obtained by soaking4. medicine introduction of a liquid, such as a saline solution, into a vein or the subcutaneous tissues of the body
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

infusion

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfjuːʒən ) for learners of English
Word forms: infusions
Definitions
1. variable nounIf there is an infusion of one thing into another, the first thing is added to the other thing and makes it stronger or better. [formal] [+ of] ⇒ He brought a tremendous infusion of hope to the people. ⇒ ...a series of capital infusions.2. countable noun [usu with supp, also by N]An infusion is a liquid made by leaving herbs in hot water until the flavour is strong. ⇒ ...a herbal infusion. ⇒ This remedy contains infusions of five flowers.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

infusion

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪnfyuʒən) in the Pharmaceutical Industry
Word forms: (regular plural) infusions
Definitions
noun(Pharmaceutical: Administration) Infusion, or an infusion, is the introduction of a liquid, such as a saline solution, into a vein or the subcutaneous tissues of the body. ⇒ Intravenous infusion is used to introduce the solution into the body. ⇒ During an infusion, monitor the rate at which the solution is entering the veins. ⇒ Infusion is the introduction of a liquid, such as a saline solution, into a vein or the subcutaneous tissues of the body.
