being

(ˈbiːɪŋ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the state or fact of existing; existence2. essential nature; self ⇒ she put her whole being into the part3. something that exists or is thought to exist, esp something that cannot be assigned to any category ⇒ a being from outer space4. a person; human being5. (in the philosophy of Aristotle) actuality. Compare becoming (sense 3)

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. Being is the present participle of be1.2. link verbBeing is used in non-finite clauses where you are giving the reason for something. [V n] ⇒ It being a Sunday, the old men had the day off. [V n] ⇒ Little boys, being what they are, might decide to play on it. [V adj] ⇒ Of course, being young, I did not worry. [Also V prep]3. countable nounYou can refer to any real or imaginary creature as a being. ⇒ People expect a horse to perform like a car, with no thought for its feelings as a living being. ⇒ ...beings from outer space.4. → see also human being5. uncount nounBeing is existence. Something that is in being or comes into being exists. ⇒ Abraham Maslow described psychology as 'the science of being.' ⇒ The Kingdom of Italy formally came into being on 17 March 1861. ⇒ ...the complex process by which the novel is brought into being.6. →  being as7. → see also well-being8. → other things being equal9. → for the time being
