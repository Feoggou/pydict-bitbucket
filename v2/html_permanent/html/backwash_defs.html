backwash

(ˈbækˌwɒʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a sucking movement of water, such as that of retreating waves. Compare swash (sense 4)2. water washed backwards by the motion of oars or other propelling devices3. the backward flow of air set up by an aircraft's engines4. a condition resulting from a previous event; repercussionverb5. (transitive) to remove oil from (combed wool)
