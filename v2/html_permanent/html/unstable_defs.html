unstable

(ʌnˈsteɪbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking stability, fixity, or firmness2. disposed to temperamental, emotional, or psychological variability3. (of a chemical compound) readily decomposing4. physicsa. (of an elementary particle) having a very short lifetimeb. spontaneously decomposing by nuclear decay; radioactive ⇒ an unstable nuclide5. electronics (of an electrical circuit, mechanical body, etc) having a tendency to self-oscillation
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

unstableness (unˈstableness) noununstably (unˈstably) adverb

unstable for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnsteɪbəl )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveYou can describe something as unstable if it is likely to change suddenly, especially if this creates difficulty or danger. ⇒ After the fall of Pitt in 1801 there was a decade of unstable government. ⇒ The situation is unstable and potentially dangerous.2. graded adjective & adjectiveUnstable objects are likely to move or fall. ⇒ Both clay and sandstone are unstable rock formations.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf people are unstable, their emotions and behaviour keep changing because their minds are disturbed or upset. ⇒ He was emotionally unstable. ⇒ Coleridge was also a highly unstable person.
