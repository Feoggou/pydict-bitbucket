fractious

(ˈfrækʃəs )
Definitions
adjective1. irritable2. unruly▶ USAGE Fractious is sometimes wrongly used where factious is meant: this factious (not fractious) dispute has split the party still further
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

fractiously (ˈfractiously) adverbfractiousness (ˈfractiousness) noun

Word Origin

C18: from (obsolete) fraction discord + -ous

Powered by COBUILD
(frækʃəs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as fractious, you disapprove of them because they become upset or angry very quickly about small unimportant things. [disapproval] ⇒ ...fractious national movements. ⇒ Nancy was in a fractious mood. ⇒ The children were predictably fractious.
