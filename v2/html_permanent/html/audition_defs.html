audition

(ɔːˈdɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a test at which a performer or musician is asked to demonstrate his ability for a particular role, etc2. the act, sense, or power of hearingverb3. to judge by means of or be tested in an audition
Word origin
C16: from Latin audītiō a hearing, from audīre to hear

audition

Powered by COBUILD
(ɔːdɪʃən )
Word forms: auditions , auditioning , auditioned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounAn audition is a short performance given by an actor, dancer, or musician so that a director or conductor can decide if they are good enough to be in a play, film, or orchestra.2. ergative verb & verbIf you audition or if someone auditions you, you do an audition. [V for n] ⇒ I was auditioning for the part of a jealous girlfriend. [V + for] ⇒ They're auditioning for new members of the cast for 'Miss Saigon' today. [V] ⇒ I heard your record and I want you to come and audition. [Also V n, V n + for]
