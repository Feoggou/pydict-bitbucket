ail

(eɪl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (transitive) to trouble; afflict2. (intransitive) to feel unwell
Word origin
Old English eglan to trouble, from egle troublesome, painful, related to Gothic agls shameful

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something ails a group or area of activity, it is a problem or source of trouble for that group or for people involved in that activity. [V n] ⇒ A full-scale debate is under way on what ails the industry.2. verbIf something ails someone, they are ill. [old-fashioned] [V n] ⇒ 'What ails you?' he asked.
