simulated

(ˈsɪmjʊˌleɪtɪd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (of fur, leather, pearls, etc) being an imitation of the genuine article, usually made from cheaper material2. (of actions, qualities, emotions, etc) imitated; feigned
Example sentences containing 'simulated'
I know it, I know it," Jack said, with only partly simulated gloom. Aldiss, Brian SOMEWHERE EAST OF LIFEThe nurse shook her head with simulated compassion: `I'm really sorry, but I'm afraid smoking's not allowed here. Hugo Wilcken THE EXECUTION
