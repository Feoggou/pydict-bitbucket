celebrate

verb

  1. rejoice, party, enjoy yourself, carouse, live it up (informal), whoop it up (informal), make merry, paint the town red (informal), go on a spree, large it (British, slang), put the flags out, roister, kill the fatted calf • I was in a mood to celebrate.
  2. commemorate, honour, observe, toast, drink to, keep • Tom celebrated his birthday two days ago.
  3. perform, observe, preside over, officiate at, solemnize, reverence • Pope John Paul celebrated mass today in a city in central Poland.
  4. praise, honour, commend (informal), glorify, publicize, exalt, laud, extol, big up (slang, mainly Caribbean), eulogize, crack up • a festival to celebrate the life and work of this great composer
