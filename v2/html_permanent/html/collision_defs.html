collision

(kəˈlɪʒən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a violent impact of moving objects; crash2. the conflict of opposed ideas, wishes, attitudes, etc ⇒ a collision of interests3. physics an event in which two or more bodies or particles come together with a resulting change of direction and, normally, energy
Word origin
C15: from Late Latin collīsiō from Latin collīdere to collide

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounA collision occurs when a moving object crashes into something. [+ with] ⇒ They were on their way to the Shropshire Union Canal when their van was involved in a collision with a car. [+ between] ⇒ I saw a head-on collision between two aeroplanes.2. countable nounA collision of cultures or ideas occurs when two very different cultures or people meet and conflict. ⇒ It's the collision of disparate ideas that alters one's perspective. [+ of] ⇒ The play represents the collision of three generations. [Also + between/with]
