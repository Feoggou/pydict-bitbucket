raffle

(ˈræfəl )
Definitions
noun1. a. a lottery in which the prizes are goods rather than moneyb. (as modifier) ⇒ a raffle ticketverb2. (transitive; often foll by off) to dispose of (goods) in a raffle
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

raffler (ˈraffler) noun

Word Origin

C14 (a dice game): from Old French, of obscure origin

raffle

Powered by COBUILD
(ræfəl ) for learners of English
Word forms: raffles , raffling , raffled
Definitions
1. countable nounA raffle is a competition in which you buy tickets with numbers on them. Afterwards some numbers are chosen, and if your ticket has one of these numbers on it, you win a prize. ⇒ Any more raffle tickets? Twenty-five pence each or five for a pound.2. verbIf someone raffles something, they give it as a prize in a raffle. [V n] ⇒ During each show we will be raffling a fabulous prize.
