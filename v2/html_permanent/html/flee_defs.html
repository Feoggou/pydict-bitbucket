flee1

(fliː )
Definitions
verbWord forms: flees, fleeing or fled1. to run away from (a place, danger, etc); fly ⇒ to flee the country2. (intransitive) to run or move quickly; rush; speed ⇒ she fled to the door

Word Origin

flee

Powered by COBUILD
(fliː ) for learners of English
Word forms: flees , fleeing , fled
Definitions
verb [no passive]If you flee from something or someone, or flee a person or thing, you escape from them. [written] [V] ⇒ He slammed the bedroom door behind him and fled. [V prep/adv] ⇒ He fled to Costa Rica to avoid military service. [V prep/adv] ⇒ ...refugees who have fled from wars, famine and persecution. [V n] ⇒ ...refugees fleeing persecution or torture. [V n] ⇒ Thousands have been compelled to flee the country in makeshift boats.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
