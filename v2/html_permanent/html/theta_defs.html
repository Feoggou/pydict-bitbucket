theta

(ˈθiːtə )
Definitions
noun1. the eighth letter of the Greek alphabet (Θ, θ), a consonant, transliterated as th2. the lower-case form of this letter used in phonetic transcription to represent the voiceless dental fricative th as in thick, both. Compare edh
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C17: from Greek, of Semitic origin; compare Hebrew tēth
