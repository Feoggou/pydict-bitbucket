comprise

(kəmˈpraɪz )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to include; contain2. to constitute the whole of; consist of ⇒ her singing comprised the entertainment▶ USAGE The use of of after comprise should be avoided: the library comprises (not comprises of) 500 000 books and manuscripts
Derived forms
comprisable (comˈprisable) adjectivecomprisal (comˈprisal) noun
Word origin

Word forms: comprises , comprising , comprised
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you say that something comprises or is comprised of a number of things or people, you mean it has them as its parts or members. [formal] [V n] ⇒ The special cabinet committee comprises Mr Brown, Mr Mandelson, and Mr Straw. [V n] ⇒ The exhibition comprises 50 oils and watercolours. [be V-ed + of] ⇒ The task force is comprised of congressional leaders, cabinet heads and administration officials. [V-ed] ⇒ A crowd comprised of the wives and children of scientists staged a demonstration.2. verbThe things or people that comprise something are the parts or members that form it. [formal] [V n] ⇒ ...the multitude of ideas, ambitions and regrets that comprises the culture of Russia today. [V n] ⇒ Women comprise 44% of hospital medical staff.
