Definitions
phraseIf someone, especially in a court, is told something in mitigation, they are told something that makes a crime or fault easier to understand and excuse. [formal] ⇒ Kieran Coonan QC told the judge in mitigation that the offences had been at the lower end of the scale. ⇒ In mitigation, it should be pointed out that there wasn't much incentive to make world-beating wine. →  mitigation
