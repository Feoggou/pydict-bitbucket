estrange

(ɪˈstreɪndʒ )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. (usually passive; often foll by from) to separate and live apart from (one's spouse) ⇒ he is estranged from his wife2. (usually passive; often foll by from) to antagonize or lose the affection of (someone previously friendly); alienate
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

estrangement (esˈtrangement) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old French estranger, from Late Latin extrāneāre to treat as a stranger, from Latin extrāneus foreign; see strange

Similar Words
