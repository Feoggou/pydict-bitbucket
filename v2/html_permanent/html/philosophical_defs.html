philosophical

(ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkəl ) or philosophic
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of or relating to philosophy or philosophers2. reasonable, wise, or learned3. calm and stoical, esp in the face of difficulties or disappointments4. (formerly) of or relating to science or natural philosophy

Derived Forms

ˌphilosophically (ˌphiloˈsophically) adverbˌphilosophicalness (ˌphiloˈsophicalness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectivePhilosophical means concerned with or relating to philosophy. ⇒ He was not accustomed to political or philosophical discussions.philosophically (fɪləsɒfɪkli ) adverb [ADV with v] [ADV adj] ⇒ Wiggins says he's not a coward, but that he's philosophically opposed to war.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is philosophical does not get upset when disappointing or disturbing things happen. [approval] [+ about] ⇒ Lewis has grown philosophical about life.philosophically graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ She says philosophically: 'It could have been far worse.'
