elk

(ɛlk )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural elks or elk1. a large deer, Alces alces, of N Europe and Asia, having large flattened palmate antlers: also occurs in North America, where it is called a moose2. See American elk3. a stout pliable waterproof leather made from calfskin or horsehide
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English eolh; related to Old Norse elgr, Old High German elaho, Latin alcēs, Greek alkē, elaphos deer

Powered by COBUILD
(elk ) for learners of English
Word forms: elks , elk
Definitions
variable nounAn elk is a type of large deer. Elks have big, flat horns called antlers and are found in Northern Europe, Asia, and North America. Some British speakers use elk to refer to the European and Asian varieties of this animal, and moose to refer to the North American variety.
