Zulu

(ˈzuːlʊ , -luː)
Definitions
noun1. plural -lus or -lu a member of a tall Negroid people of SE Africa, living chiefly in South Africa, who became dominant during the 19th century due to a warrior-clan system organized by the powerful leader, Shaka2. the language of this people, belonging to the Bantu group of the Niger-Congo family, closely related to Swazi and Xhosa3. communications a code word for the letter z
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

from Zulu amaZulu people of the sky
