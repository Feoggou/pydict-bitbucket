uncivil

(ʌnˈsɪvəl )
Definitions
adjective1. lacking civility or good manners2. an obsolete word for uncivilized
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

uncivility (ˌʌnsɪˈvɪlɪtɪ ) or uncivilness (unˈcivilness) noununcivilly (unˈcivilly) adverb
Example sentences containing 'uncivil'
He'd been short and uncivil with her, but it was difficult enough coping with his own problems at the moment, let alone hers. Dobbs, Michael WALL GAMESTechnically, they were not supposed to drink on duty, but he felt it was uncivil to be in a bar and not buy any of its chief commodity. Bringle, Mary DEATH OF AN UNKNOWN MANWalking down the town's narrow central street, they created their own, uncivil procession. Kalpakian, Laura GRACED LAND
