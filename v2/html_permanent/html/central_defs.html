central

(ˈsɛntrəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. in, at, of, from, containing, or forming the centre of something ⇒ the central street in a city, ⇒ the central material of a golf ball2. main, principal, or chief; most important ⇒ the central cause of a problem3. a. of or relating to the central nervous systemb. of or relating to the centrum of a vertebra4. of, relating to, or denoting a vowel articulated with the tongue held in an intermediate position halfway between the positions for back and front vowels, as for the a of English soda5. (of a force) directed from or towards a point6. informal (immediately postpositive) used to describe a place where a specified thing, quality, etc is to be found in abundance ⇒ nostalgia central

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjectiveSomething that is central is in the middle of a place or area. ⇒ ...Central America's Caribbean coast. ⇒ The disruption has now spread and is affecting a large part of central Liberia. ⇒ ...a rich woman living in central London.centrally adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ The main cabin has its full-sized double bed centrally placed with plenty of room around it.2. graded adjective & adjectiveA place that is central is easy to reach because it is in the centre of a city, town, or particular area. ⇒ ...a central location in the capital.centrally graded adverb & adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ ...this centrally located hotel, situated on the banks of the Marne Canal.3. adjective [ADJ n]A central group or organization makes all the important decisions that are followed throughout a larger organization or a country. ⇒ There is a lack of trust towards the central government in Rome. ⇒ ...the central committee of the Cuban communist party.centrally adverb [ADV -ed, ADV after v] ⇒ This is a centrally planned economy.4. graded adjective & adjectiveThe central person or thing in a particular situation is the most important one. [+ to] ⇒ Black dance music has been central to mainstream pop since the early '60s. ⇒ ...a central part of their culture.centrality uncount noun [+ of] ⇒ The centrality of the German economy to the welfare of Europe must be recognised.centrally graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ In her memoirs Naomi is quick to acknowledge that her grandmother was centrally important in her venture as a writer.
