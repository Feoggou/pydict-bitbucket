proverb

(ˈprɒvɜːb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a short, memorable, and often highly condensed saying embodying, esp with bold imagery, some commonplace fact or experience2. a person or thing exemplary in respect of a characteristic ⇒ Antarctica is a proverb for extreme cold3. ecclesiastical a wise saying or admonition providing guidanceverb (transitive)4. to utter or describe (something) in the form of a proverb5. to make (something) a proverb

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Latin prōverbium, from verbum word
