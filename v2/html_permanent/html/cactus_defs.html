cactus

(ˈkæktəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -tuses or -ti (-taɪ)1. any spiny succulent plant of the family Cactaceae of the arid regions of America. Cactuses have swollen tough stems, leaves reduced to spines or scales, and often large brightly coloured flowers2. See cactus dahlia
Derived forms
cactaceous (kækˈteɪʃəs ) adjective
Word origin
C17: from Latin: prickly plant, from Greek kaktos cardoon
