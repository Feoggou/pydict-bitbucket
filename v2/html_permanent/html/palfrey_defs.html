palfrey

(ˈpɔːlfrɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounarchaic a light saddle horse, esp ridden by women

Word Origin

C12: from Old French palefrei, from Medieval Latin palafredus, from Late Latin paraverēdus, from Greek para beside + Latin verēdus light fleet horse, of Celtic origin

She paused uncertainly before the stall of his chestnut palfrey , and then moved towards the far door, out into the stable yard.
