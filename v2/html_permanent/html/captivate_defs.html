captivate

(ˈkæptɪˌveɪt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to hold the attention of by fascinating; enchant2. an obsolete word for capture
Derived forms
captiˌvatingly (ˈcaptiˌvatingly) adverbˌcaptivation (ˌcaptiˈvation) nouncaptiˌvator (ˈcaptiˌvator) noun
Word origin
C16: from Late Latin captivāre, from captīvus captive

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf you are captivated by someone or something, you find them fascinating and attractive. [be V-ed + by] ⇒ I was captivated by her brilliant mind. [V n] ⇒ For 40 years she has captivated the world with her radiant looks.
Example sentences containing 'captivate'
Skillfully fanning her interest, teasing, tantalizing, he set himself to captivate with simple kisses laced with potent temptation. Stephanie Laurens ALL ABOUT LOVE
