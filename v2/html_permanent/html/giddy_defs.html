giddy

(ˈɡɪdɪ )
Definitions
adjective -dier or -diest1. affected with a reeling sensation and feeling as if about to fall; dizzy2. causing or tending to cause vertigo3. impulsive; scatterbrained4. See my giddy auntverb -dies, -dying or -died5. to make or become giddy
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

giddily (ˈgiddily) adverbgiddiness (ˈgiddiness) noun

Word Origin

Old English gydig mad, frenzied, possessed by God; related to God

giddy

Powered by COBUILD
(gɪdi ) for learners of English
Word forms: giddier , giddiest
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you feel giddy, you feel unsteady and think that you are about to fall over, usually because you are not well. ⇒ He felt giddy and light-headed.giddiness uncount noun ⇒ A wave of giddiness swept over her.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you feel giddy with delight or excitement, you feel so happy or excited that you find it hard to think or act normally. ⇒ Anthony was giddy with self-satisfaction. ⇒ Being there gave me a giddy pleasure.giddiness uncount noun ⇒ There's almost a giddiness surrounding the talks in Houston.
