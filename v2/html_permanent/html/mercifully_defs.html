mercifully

(ˈmɜːsɪfʊlɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. in a way that shows mercy; compassionately ⇒ mercifully put down2. (sentence modifier) fortunately; one is relieved to say that ⇒ mercifully, all went well

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV adj] [ADV with v]You can use mercifully to show that you are glad that something good has happened, or that something bad has not happened or has stopped. [feelings] ⇒ Mercifully, a friend came to the rescue. ⇒ Bolivia has been mercifully free of large-scale, drug-related violence.
