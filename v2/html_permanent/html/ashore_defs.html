ashore

(əˈʃɔː )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adverb1. towards or onto land from the water ⇒ we swam ashoreadjective, adverb (postpositive)2. on land, having come from the water ⇒ a day ashore before sailing

ashore

(əʃɔːʳ )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adverb [ADV after v, be ADV]Someone or something that comes ashore comes from the sea onto the shore. ⇒ Oil has come ashore on a ten mile stretch to the east of Plymouth. ⇒ Once ashore, the vessel was thoroughly inspected.
Example sentences containing 'ashore'
Rope and barrels, timber and paint, flour and tea, all washed ashore , made life easier for the islanders. Lunnon-Wood, Mike LET NOT THE DEEPThe tide swept most of the boxes ashore along the beach near York Watergate.
