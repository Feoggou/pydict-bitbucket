quail1

(kweɪl )
Definitions
nounWord forms: plural quails or quail1. any small Old World gallinaceous game bird of the genus Coturnix and related genera, having a rounded body and small tail: family Phasianidae (pheasants)2. any of various similar and related American birds, such as the bobwhite
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French quaille, from Medieval Latin quaccula, probably of imitative origin

quail2

(kweɪl )
Definitions
verb(intransitive) to shrink back with fear; cower
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: perhaps from Old French quailler, from Latin coāgulāre to curdle

quail

Powered by COBUILD
(kweɪl ) for learners of English
Word forms: quails , quail , quails , quailing , quailed
Definitions
1. countable nounA quail is a type of small bird which is often shot and eaten. ⇒ I've shot hundreds of quail with that gun.Quail is the meat of this bird eaten as food. ⇒ They dined off salmon, quail, and fruit.2. verbIf someone or something makes you quail, they make you feel very afraid, often so that you hesitate. [literary] [V] ⇒ The very words make many of us quail. [V + at] ⇒ He told Naomi she was becoming just like Maya. Naomi quailed at the thought.
