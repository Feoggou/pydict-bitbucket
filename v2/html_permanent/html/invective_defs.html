invective

(ɪnˈvɛktɪv )
Definitions
noun1. vehement accusation or denunciation, esp of a bitterly abusive or sarcastic kindadjective2. characterized by or using abusive language, bitter sarcasm, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

invectively (inˈvectively) adverbinvectiveness (inˈvectiveness) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin invectīvus reproachful, scolding, from Latin invectus carried in; see inveigh

uncount nounInvective is rude and unpleasant things that people shout at people they hate or are angry with. [formal] ⇒ A woman had hurled racist invective at the family. ⇒ Crowley maintained a stream of invective and abuse against Waite.
