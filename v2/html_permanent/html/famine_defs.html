famine

(ˈfæmɪn )
Definitions
noun1. a severe shortage of food, as through crop failure or overpopulation2. acute shortage of anything3. violent hunger
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: from Old French, via Vulgar Latin, from Latin famēs hunger

variable nounFamine is a situation in which large numbers of people have little or no food, and many of them die. ⇒ Thousands of refugees are trapped by war, drought and famine. ⇒ The civil war is obstructing distribution of famine relief by aid agencies.
