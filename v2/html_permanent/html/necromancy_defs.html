necromancy

(ˈnɛkrəʊˌmænsɪ )
Definitions
noun1. the art or practice of supposedly conjuring up the dead, esp in order to obtain from them knowledge of the future2. black magic; sorcery
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

necromancer (ˈnecroˌmancer) nounnecromantic (ˌnecroˈmantic) adjective

Word Origin

C13: (as in sense 1) ultimately from Greek nekromanteia, from nekros corpse; (as in sense 2) from Medieval Latin nigromantia, from Latin niger black, which replaced necro- through folk etymology
