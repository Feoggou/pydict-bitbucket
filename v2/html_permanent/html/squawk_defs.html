squawk

(skwɔːk )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a loud raucous cry; screech2. informal a loud complaint or protestverb3. to utter a squawk or with a squawk4. (intransitive) informal to complain loudly

Derived Forms

squawker (ˈsquawker) noun

Word Origin

C19: of imitative origin

Powered by COBUILD
(skwɔːk )
Word forms: squawks , squawking , squawked
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbWhen a bird squawks, it makes a loud harsh noise. [V] ⇒ I threw pebbles at the hens, and that made them jump and squawk.Squawk is also a noun. ⇒ A mallard suddenly took wing, rising steeply into the air with an angry squawk.2. verbIf a person squawks, they complain loudly, often in a high-pitched, harsh tone. [informal] [V that] ⇒ Mr Arbor squawked that the deal was a double-cross. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Wait for me!' Melanie squawked. 'I'm not staying here alone.' [Also V]Squawk is also a noun. ⇒ She gave a loud squawk when the water was poured on her.
