courage

(ˈkʌrɪdʒ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the power or quality of dealing with or facing danger, fear, pain, etc2. See the courage of one's convictions3. See take one's courage in both hands4. obsolete mind; disposition; spirit
Word origin
C13: from Old French corage, from cuer heart, from Latin cor

courage

Powered by COBUILD
(kʌrɪdʒ , US kɜːr- )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounCourage is the quality shown by someone who decides to do something difficult or dangerous, even though they may be afraid. ⇒ General Lewis Mackenzie has impressed everyone with his authority and personal courage. ⇒ They do not have the courage to apologise for their actions.2. → see also Dutch courage3. →  the courage of your convictions4. → to pluck up the courage
