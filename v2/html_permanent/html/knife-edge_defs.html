knife edge

Definitions
noun1. the sharp cutting edge of a knife2. any sharp edge3. a sharp-edged wedge of hard material on which the beam of a balance pivots or about which a pendulum is suspended4. a critical point in the development of a situation, process of making a decision, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

knife-edge

(ˈnaɪfˌɛdʒ) or knife-edged
Definitions
modifier1. tense and exciting because it is unclear what is going to happen next ⇒ a knife-edge battle for the loyalties of voters, ⇒ a knife-edge final set tie-break, ⇒ a knife-edge thriller about a computer hacker2. sharp-edged ⇒ South-East Ridge is a spectacular approach with good views along a knife-edge approach., ⇒ trousers with knife-edge creases
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

knife-edge for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
also knife edge
Definitions
1. →  on a knife-edge2. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]You can use knife-edge to refer to something that is very exciting or tense because you do not know what is going to happen next. [mainly British] ⇒ Tonight's knife-edge vote could be uncomfortably close.
