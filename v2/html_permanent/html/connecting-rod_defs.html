connecting rod

Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a rod or bar for transmitting motion, esp one that connects a rotating part to a reciprocating part2. such a rod that connects the piston to the crankshaft in an internal-combustion engine or reciprocating pump. See also big end, little end3. a similar rod that connects the crosshead of a steam engine to the crank

noun(Automotive engineering: Vehicle components, Engine, transmission, and exhaust) A connecting rod is a rod that connects the crank to the piston in an engine. ⇒ The connecting rods connect the piston to the crankshaft. ⇒ The hot, expanding gases of combustion push on the piston, which moves a connecting rod, which turns a crankshaft. ⇒ The connecting rod is attached to a crankshaft, which changes the piston's up-and-down motion into a circular motion that makes the wheels go around.Related wordsThe following also connect parts of vehicles:beam axleexhaust manifoldtrack rod
