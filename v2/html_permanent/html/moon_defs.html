moon

(muːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. (sometimes capital) the natural satellite of the earth. Diameter: 3476 km; mass: 7.35 × 1022 kg; mean distance from earth: 384 400 km; periods of rotation and revolution: 27.32 days ▶ Related adjective: lunar2. the face of the moon as it is seen during its revolution around the earth, esp at one of its phases ⇒ new moon, ⇒ full moon3. any natural satellite of a planet4. moonlight; moonshine5. something resembling a moon6. a month, esp a lunar one7. See once in a blue moon8. See over the moon9. See reach for the moonverb10. (when tr, often foll by away; when intr, often foll by around) to be idle in a listless way, as if in love, or to idle (time) away11. (intransitive) slang to expose one's buttocks to passers-by

Derived Forms

moonless (ˈmoonless) adjective

Word Origin

Old English mōna; compare Old Frisian mōna, Old High German māno

Moon1

(muːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nouna system of embossed alphabetical signs for blind readers, the fourteen basic characters of which can, by rotation, mimic most of the letters of the Roman alphabet, thereby making learning easier for those who learned to read before going blind. Compare Braille1

moon

Powered by COBUILD
(muːn )
Word forms: moons , mooning, mooned
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. singular noun [oft full/new N]The moon is the object that you can often see in the sky at night. It goes round the Earth once every four weeks, and as it does so its appearance changes from a circle to part of a circle. ⇒ ...the first man on the moon. ⇒ There will be no moon. ⇒ ...the light of a full moon.2. → see also new moon3. countable noun [usu poss N]A moon is an object similar to a small planet that travels around a planet. ⇒ ...Neptune's large moon.4. verb [usu cont]If you are mooning around, you are spending time doing nothing in particular, for example because you feel unhappy or lazy, or are worried about something. mope [V adv/prep] ⇒ Lettie was mooning around all morning, doing nothing. [V adv/prep] ⇒ My working days were spent mooning round his department, trying to sneak a chance encounter.5. verbIf you moon at someone, you turn your back to them and show them your bare bottom. [British, informal]6. →  blue moon7. →  over the moon
