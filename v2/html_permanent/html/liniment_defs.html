liniment

(ˈlɪnɪmənt )
Definitions
nouna medicated liquid, usually containing alcohol, camphor, and an oil, applied to the skin to relieve pain, stiffness, etc
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C15: from Late Latin linīmentum, from linere to smear, anoint

Definitions
noun (count) (noncount)(Pharmaceutical: Dosage forms) Liniment is a liquid that you rub into the skin in order to reduce pain or relieve stiffness. ⇒ Liniments can be rubbed into the skin to relieve aches from arthritis and stiffness in muscles. ⇒ A liniment is usually in the form of a thin liquid applied to the skin. ⇒ Liniment is a liquid that you rub into the skin in order to reduce pain or relieve stiffness.
