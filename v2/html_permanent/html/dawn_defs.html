dawn

(dɔːn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. daybreak; sunrise ▶ Related adjective: auroral2. the sky when light first appears in the morning3. the beginning of somethingverb (intransitive)4. to begin to grow light after the night5. to begin to develop, appear, or expand6. (usually foll by on or upon) to begin to become apparent (to)
Derived forms
dawnˌlike (ˈdawnˌlike) adjective
Word origin
Old English dagian to dawn; see day

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounDawn is the time of day when light first appears in the sky, just before the sun rises. sunrise, daybreak ⇒ Nancy woke at dawn.2. singular nounThe dawn of a period of time or a situation is the beginning of it. [literary] ⇒ We can only guess what went through the mind of a man who could look back to the dawn of powered flight. ⇒ ...the dawn of the radio age.3. verbIf something is dawning, it is beginning to develop or come into existence. [written] [V] ⇒ Throughout Europe a new railway age, that of the high-speed train, has dawned. [V] ⇒ A new era seemed to be about to dawn for the coach and his young team. [V-ing] ⇒ Now there is a dawning realisation that drastic action is necessary.dawning singular noun ⇒ ...the dawning of the space age. ⇒ Tettlinger uncovered his eyes in the first dawning of hope.4. verbWhen you say that a particular day dawned, you mean it arrived or began, usually when it became light. [written] [V] ⇒ When the great day dawned, the first concern was the weather. [V adj] ⇒ The next day dawned sombre and gloomy.5. → at the crack of dawn► →  dawn on
