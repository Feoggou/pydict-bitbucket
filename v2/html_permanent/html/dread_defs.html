dread

(drɛd )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verb (transitive)1. to anticipate with apprehension or terror2. to fear greatly3. archaic to be in awe ofnoun4. great fear; horror5. an object of terror6. slang a Rastafarian7. archaic deep reverenceadjective8. literary awesome; awe-inspiring
Word origin
Old English ondrǣdan; related to Old Saxon antdrādan, Old High German intrātan

Word forms: dreads , dreading , dreaded
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf you dread something which may happen, you feel very anxious and unhappy about it because you think it will be unpleasant or upsetting. [V n/v-ing] ⇒ I'm dreading Christmas this year. [V n/-ing] ⇒ I dreaded coming back, to be honest. [V n -ing] ⇒ I suffer badly from cold sores and dread them appearing on my wedding day. [V that] ⇒ I'd been dreading that the birth would take a long time. [Also V to-inf]2. uncount nounDread is a feeling of great anxiety and fear about something that may happen. ⇒ She thought with dread of the cold winters to come.3. adjective [usu ADJ n]Dread means terrible and greatly feared. [literary] ⇒ ...a more effective national policy to combat this dread disease.4. adjective [ADJ n]You can use dread to describe something that you, or a particular group of people, find annoying or undesirable. [informal, feelings] dreaded ⇒ ...the dread phrase 'politically correct'.5. → see also dreaded6. →  dread to think
