sprig

(sprɪɡ )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a shoot, twig, or sprout of a tree, shrub, etc; spray2. an ornamental device resembling a spray of leaves or flowers3. a small wire nail without a head4. informal, rare a youth5. informal, rare a person considered as the descendant of an established family, social class, etc6. New Zealand another name for stud1 (sense 7)verbWord forms: sprigs, sprigging or sprigged (transitive)7. to fasten or secure with sprigs8. to ornament (fabric, wallpaper, etc) with a design of sprigs9. to make sprays from (twigs and branches)

Derived Forms

sprigger (ˈsprigger) nounspriggy (ˈspriggy) adjective

Word Origin

C15: probably of Germanic origin; compare Low German sprick, Swedish sprygg
