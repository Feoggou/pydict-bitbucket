esteem

(ɪˈstiːm )
Definitions
verb (transitive)1. to have great respect or high regard for ⇒ to esteem a colleague2. formal to judge or consider; deem ⇒ to esteem an idea impropernoun3. high regard or respect; good opinion4. archaic judgment; opinion
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

esteemed (esˈteemed) adjective

Word Origin

C15: from Old French estimer, from Latin aestimāre estimate

esteem

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪstiːm ) for learners of English
Word forms: esteems , esteeming , esteemed
Definitions
1. uncount nounEsteem is the admiration and respect that you feel towards another person. [formal] ⇒ He is held in high esteem by colleagues in the construction industry. ⇒ Their public esteem has never been lower. ⇒ He said he retained immense regard and esteem for the prime minister.2. verbIf you esteem someone or something, you respect or admire them. [formal] [V n] ⇒ I greatly esteem your message in the midst of our hard struggle.3. → See also self-esteem
