scientific

(ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ) or scientifical (ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪkəl)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. (prenominal) of, relating to, derived from, or used in science ⇒ scientific equipment2. (prenominal) occupied in science ⇒ scientific manpower3. conforming with the principles or methods used in science ⇒ a scientific approach

Derived Forms

ˌscientifically (ˌscienˈtifically) adverb

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Scientific is used to describe things that relate to science or to a particular science. ⇒ Scientific research is widely claimed to be the source of the high standard of living in the U.S.. ⇒ ...the use of animals in scientific experiments. ⇒ ...scientific instruments.scientifically (saɪəntɪfɪkli ) adverb ⇒ ...scientifically advanced countries.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you do something in a scientific way, you do it carefully and thoroughly, using experiments or tests. ⇒ It's not a scientific way to test their opinions. ⇒ ...the scientific study of capitalist development.scientifically graded adverb & adverb systematically ⇒ Efforts are being made to research it scientifically.
