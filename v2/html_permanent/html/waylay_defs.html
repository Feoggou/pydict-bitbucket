waylay

(weɪˈleɪ )
Definitions
verb -lays, -laying or -laid (transitive)1. to lie in wait for and attack2. to await and intercept unexpectedly
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

waylayer (wayˈlayer) noun

Word Origin

C16: from way + lay1

waylay for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(weɪleɪ , US -leɪ )
Word forms: waylays , waylaying , waylaid
Definitions
verbIf someone waylays you, they stop you when you are going somewhere, for example in order to talk to you, to steal something from you, or to attack you. [V n] ⇒ She was forever waylaying him in odd holes and corners of the hotel. [V n] ⇒ The trucks are being waylaid by bandits. ⇒ I'm sorry, Nick, I got waylaid.
