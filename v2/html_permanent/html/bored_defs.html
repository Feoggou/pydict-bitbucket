(bɔːʳd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]If you are bored, you feel tired and impatient because you have lost interest in something or because you have nothing to do. [+ with] ⇒ I am getting very bored with this entire business.
Example sentences containing 'bored'
He'd lifted his gun because he needed the distraction and he was bored because there weren't going to be any ducks anyway. Stuart Harrison LOST SUMMERThere was chronic danger of something falling out of the sky and braining you if you got so bored you forgot to walk fast. Anita Anderson SOMEBODYOccasionally, she tapped a key on her word processor and gave the screen a bored stare before returning to her conversation. Val McDermid KICK BACK
