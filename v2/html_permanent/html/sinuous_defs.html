sinuous

(ˈsɪnjʊəs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. full of turns or curves; intricate2. devious; not straightforward3. supple; lithe

Derived Forms

sinuously (ˈsinuously) adverbsinuousness (ˈsinuousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin sinuōsus winding, from sinus a curve

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is sinuous moves with smooth twists and turns. [literary] ⇒ ...the silent, sinuous approach of a snake through the long grass. ⇒ He has a distinctively sinuous way of walking.2. graded adjective [usu ADJ n]Something that is sinuous has many smooth turns and curves. [literary] ⇒ I drove along sinuous mountain roads.
