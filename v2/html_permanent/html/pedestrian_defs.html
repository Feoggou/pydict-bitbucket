pedestrian

(pɪˈdɛstrɪən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. a person travelling on foot; walkerb. (as modifier) ⇒ a pedestrian precinctadjective2. dull; commonplace ⇒ a pedestrian style of writing

Word Origin

C18: from Latin pedester, from pēs foot

pedestrian

Powered by COBUILD
(pɪdestriən )
Word forms: pedestrians
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft N n]A pedestrian is a person who is walking, especially in a town or city, rather than travelling in a vehicle. ⇒ In Los Angeles a pedestrian is a rare spectacle. ⇒ More than a third of all pedestrian injuries are to children.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as pedestrian, you mean that it is ordinary and not at all interesting. [disapproval] ⇒ His style is so pedestrian that the book becomes a real bore. ⇒ I drove home contemplating my own more pedestrian lifestyle.
