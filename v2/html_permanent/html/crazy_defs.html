Definitions
Collins English Dictionary

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone or something as crazy, you think they are very foolish or strange. [informal, disapproval] mad ⇒ People thought they were all crazy to try to make money from manufacturing. ⇒ That's why he's got so caught up with this crazy idea about Mr. Trancas. ⇒ ...that crazy, mixed-up world out there.crazily adverb [ADV after v, ADV adj] ⇒ The teenagers shook their long, black hair and gesticulated crazily. ⇒ Our policies are crazily extravagant and very destructive.craziness uncount noun ⇒ We had to have a sense of humour because of the craziness of it all.2. graded adjective & adjectiveSomeone who is crazy is insane. [informal] ⇒ If I sat home and worried about all this stuff, I'd go crazy. ⇒ He strides around the room beaming like a crazy man.Crazy is also a noun. ⇒ Outside, mumbling, was one of New York's ever-present crazies.3. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are crazy about something, you are very enthusiastic about it. If you are not crazy about something, you do not like it. [informal] [+ about] ⇒ He's still crazy about both his work and his hobbies. ⇒ I'm also not crazy about the initial terms of the deal.Crazy is also a combining form. ⇒ Every football-crazy schoolboy in Europe dreams of one day being involved in the championships.4. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you are crazy about someone, you are deeply in love with them. [informal] [+ about] ⇒ None of that matters, because we're crazy about each other.5. graded adjective & adjective [v-link ADJ]If something or someone makes you crazy or drives you crazy, they make you extremely annoyed or upset. [informal] ⇒ This sitting around is driving me crazy. ⇒ When Jock woke up and found you gone he went crazy.6. →  like crazy
