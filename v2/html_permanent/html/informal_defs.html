informal

(ɪnˈfɔːməl )
Definitions
adjective1. not of a formal, official, or stiffly conventional nature ⇒ an informal luncheon2. appropriate to everyday life or use ⇒ informal clothes3. denoting or characterized by idiom, vocabulary, etc, appropriate to everyday conversational language rather than to formal written language4. denoting a second-person pronoun in some languages used when the addressee is regarded as a friend or social inferior ⇒ In French the pronoun "tu" is informal, while "vous" is formal

(ɪnfɔːʳməl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ]Informal speech or behaviour is relaxed and friendly rather than serious, very correct, or official. ⇒ She is refreshingly informal. ⇒ His friend was less good-looking, but a lot more informal and relaxed.informally graded adverb & adverb [ADV after v] ⇒ She was always there at half past eight, chatting informally to the children.informality (ɪnfɔːʳmælɪti ) uncount noun ⇒ He was overwhelmed by their friendly informality.2. graded adjective & adjectiveAn informal situation is one which is relaxed and friendly and not very serious or official. ⇒ I would like it to be an informal occasion. ⇒ The house has an informal atmosphere.informality uncount noun ⇒ Eleanor enjoyed the relative informality of island life.3. graded adjective & adjectiveInformal clothes are casual and suitable for wearing when you are relaxing, but not on formal occasions. ⇒ Most of the time Jenny needs informal clothes. ⇒ For lunch, dress is informal.informally graded adverb & adverb ⇒ Everyone dressed informally in shorts or jeans.4. adjective [usu ADJ n]You use informal to describe something that is done unofficially or casually without planning. ⇒ The two leaders will retire to Camp David for informal discussions. ⇒ ...an informal meeting of E.U. ministers. ⇒ We had an informal party at a hotel, and people just flooded in.informally adverb ⇒ He began informally to handle Ted's tax affairs for him.
