caption

(ˈkæpʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a title, brief explanation, or comment accompanying an illustration; legend2. a heading, title, or headline of a chapter, article, etc3. graphic material, usually containing lettering, used in television presentation4. another name for subtitle (sense 2)5. the formal heading of a legal document stating when, where, and on what authority it was taken or madeverb6. to provide with a caption or captions
Word origin
C14 (meaning: seizure, an arrest; later, heading of a legal document): from Latin captiō a seizing, from capere to take

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounA caption is the words printed underneath a picture or cartoon which explain what it is about. ⇒ On the back of the photo is written the simple caption, 'Mrs. Monroe'.2. verbWhen someone captions a picture or cartoon, they put a caption under it. [V n quote] ⇒ The Sun had captioned a picture of Princess Diana 'Princess of Veils'. [V-ed] ⇒ The photograph is captioned 'People Power'. [V-ed] ⇒ The book is well written, properly illustrated and excellently captioned.
