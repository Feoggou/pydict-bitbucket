reckless

(ˈrɛklɪs )
Definitions
adjectivehaving or showing no regard for danger or consequences; heedless; rash ⇒ a reckless driver, ⇒ a reckless attempt
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

recklessly (ˈrecklessly) adverbrecklessness (ˈrecklessness) noun

Word Origin

Old English recceleās (see reck, -less); related to Middle Dutch roekeloos, Old High German ruahhalōs

reckless

Powered by COBUILD
(rekləs ) for learners of English
Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone is reckless, you mean that they act in a way which shows that they do not care about danger or the effect their behaviour will have on other people. ⇒ She loved to ride; on horseback, she was reckless and utterly without fear. ⇒ He is charged with causing death by reckless driving.recklessly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v, ADV adj] ⇒ He was leaning recklessly out of the unshuttered window. ⇒ A congressional report charges that federal safety laws were recklessly violated.recklessness uncount noun ⇒ He felt a surge of recklessness. ⇒ The headstrong recklessness of youth may be fine in some areas, but not behind the wheel of a car.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
