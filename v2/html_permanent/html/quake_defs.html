quake

(kweɪk )
Definitions
verb (intransitive)1. to shake or tremble with or as with fear2. to convulse or quiver, as from instabilitynoun3. the act or an instance of quaking4. informal short for earthquake
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English cwacian; related to Old English cweccan to shake, Old Irish bocaim, German wackeln

quake

Powered by COBUILD
(kweɪk ) for learners of English
Word forms: quakes , quaking , quaked
Definitions
1. countable nounA quake is the same as an earthquake. ⇒ The quake destroyed mud buildings in many remote villages.2. verbIf you quake, you shake, usually because you are very afraid. [V + with] ⇒ I just stood there quaking with fear. [V] ⇒ Her shoulders quaked.3. →  quake in one's boots/shoes
