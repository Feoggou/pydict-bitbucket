squabble

(ˈskwɒbəl )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
verb1. (intransitive) to quarrel over a small matternoun2. a petty quarrel

Derived Forms

squabbler (ˈsquabbler) noun

Word Origin

C17: probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Swedish dialect sqvabbel to quarrel

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbWhen people squabble, they quarrel about something that is not really important. [V] ⇒ Mother is devoted to Dad although they squabble all the time. [pl-n V over/about n] ⇒ The children were squabbling over the remote-control gadget for the television. [V + with] ⇒ My four-year-old squabbles with his friends.squabbling uncount noun ⇒ In recent months its government has been paralysed by political squabbling.
