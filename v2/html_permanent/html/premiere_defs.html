premiere

(ˈprɛmɪˌɛə , ˈprɛmɪə )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. the first public performance of a film, play, opera, etc2. the leading lady in a theatre companyverb3. to give or be the first public performance of

Word Origin

C19: from French, feminine of premier first

premiere

Powered by COBUILD
(premieəʳ , US prɪmjer )
Word forms: premieres , premiering , premiered
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable nounThe premiere of a new play or film is the first public performance of it. ⇒ A new Czech film has had its premiere at the Karlovy Vary film festival. ⇒ ...a royal film premiere.2. ergative verb & verbWhen a film or show premieres or is premiered, it is shown to an audience for the first time. [V] ⇒ The documentary premiered at the Jerusalem Film Festival. [be V-ed] ⇒ The opera is due to be premiered by ENO next year.
