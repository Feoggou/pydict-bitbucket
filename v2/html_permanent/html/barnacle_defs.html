barnacle

(ˈbɑːnəkəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any of various marine crustaceans of the subclass Cirripedia that, as adults, live attached to rocks, ship bottoms, etc. They have feathery food-catching cirri protruding from a hard shell. See acorn barnacle, goose barnacle2. a person or thing that is difficult to get rid of
Derived forms
barnacled (ˈbarnacled) adjective
Word origin
C16: related to Late Latin bernicla, of obscure origin
