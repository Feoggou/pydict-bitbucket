apoplexy

(ˈæpəˌplɛksɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounsudden loss of consciousness, often followed by paralysis, caused by rupture or occlusion of a blood vessel in the brain
Word origin
C14: from Old French apoplexie, from Late Latin apoplēxia, from Greek: from apoplēssein to cripple by a stroke, from plēssein to strike
