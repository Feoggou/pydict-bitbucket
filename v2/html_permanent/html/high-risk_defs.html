high-risk

Powered by COBUILD
(haɪ rɪsk) in Insurance
Definitions
adjective(Insurance: General) A high-risk customer, risk, occupation, or property is particularly likely to be exposed to a danger. ⇒ Certain types of occupations, which are more physically intensive or expose the person to hazardous work environments, are considered high-risk type occupations. ⇒ This policy is a way of providing automobile insurance to persons considered to be high-risk drivers who cannot obtain protection in the voluntary markets. ⇒ A high-risk customer, risk, occupation, or property is particularly likely to be exposed to a danger. ▶  riskRelated wordsA customer may be high-risk because of adverse selection, the tendency of those in dangerous jobs or with high-risk lifestyles to want to take out life insurance. These customers may have a high level of occupational hazard.The opposite of high-risk is low-risk.
