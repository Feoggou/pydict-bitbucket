mighty

(ˈmaɪtɪ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjectiveWord forms: mightier or mightiest1. a. having or indicating might; powerful or strongb. (as collective noun; preceded by the) ⇒ the mighty2. very large; vast3. very great in extent, importance, etcadverb4. informal, mainly US and Canadian (intensifier) ⇒ he was mighty tired

Derived Forms

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Mighty is used to describe something that is very large or powerful. [literary] ⇒ There was a flash and a mighty bang. ⇒ ...a land marked with vast lakes and mighty rivers.2. graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj/adv]Mighty is used in front of adjectives and adverbs to emphasize the quality that they are describing. [mainly US, informal, emphasis] ⇒ It's something you'll be mighty proud of. ⇒ Being paper money, it rotted away mighty fast.3. → see also high and mighty
