drizzle

(ˈdrɪzəl )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. very light rain, specifically consisting of droplets less than 0.5 mm in diameterverb2. (intransitive) to rain lightly3. (transitive) to moisten with tiny droplets
Derived forms
drizzly (ˈdrizzly) adjective
Word origin
Old English drēosan to fall; related to Old Saxon driosan, Gothic driusan, Norwegian drjōsa

drizzle

Powered by COBUILD
(drɪzəl )
Word forms: drizzles , drizzling , drizzled
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount noun [oft a N]Drizzle is light rain falling in fine drops. ⇒ The drizzle had now stopped and the sun was breaking through.2. verbIf it is drizzling, it is raining very lightly. [V] ⇒ Clouds had come down and it was starting to drizzle. [V-ing] ⇒ I walked home in the drizzling rain.3. verbIf you drizzle a liquid over food or drizzle food with a liquid, you pour a small quantity of the liquid all over the food. [V n over n] ⇒ Drizzle the remaining dressing over the duck and salad. [V n with n] ⇒ Drizzle them with warmed extra virgin olive oil. [Also V n with over]
