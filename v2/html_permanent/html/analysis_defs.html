analysis

(əˈnælɪsɪs )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun plural -ses (-ˌsiːz)1. the division of a physical or abstract whole into its constituent parts to examine or determine their relationship or value. Compare synthesis (sense 1)2. a statement of the results of this3. short for psychoanalysis4. chemistrya. the decomposition of a substance into its elements, radicals, or other constituents in order to determine the kinds of constituents present (qualitative analysis) or the amount of each constituent (quantitative analysis)b. the result obtained by such a determination5. linguistics the use of word order together with word function to express syntactic relations in a language, as opposed to the use of inflections. Compare synthesis (sense 4)6. mathematics the branch of mathematics principally concerned with the properties of functions, largely arising out of calculus7. philosophy (in the writings of Kant) the separation of a concept from another that contains it. Compare synthesis (sense 6a)8. See in the last analysis
Word origin
C16: from New Latin, from Greek analusis, literally: a dissolving, from analuein, from ana- + luein to loosen

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. variable nounAnalysis is the process of considering something carefully or using statistical methods in order to understand it or explain it. ⇒ Her criteria defy analysis. [+ of] ⇒ We did an analysis of the way that government money has been spent in the past.2. variable nounAnalysis is the scientific process of examining something in order to find out what it consists of. ⇒ They collect blood samples for analysis at a national laboratory. ⇒ Jacobsen based his conclusion on an analysis of the decay of samarium-147 into neodymium-143.3. countable nounAn analysis is an explanation or description that results from considering something carefully. [+ of] ⇒ ...coming up after the newscast, an analysis of President Bush's domestic policy.4. →  in the final analysis/in the last analysis
