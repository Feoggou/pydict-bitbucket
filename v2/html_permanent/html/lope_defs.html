lope

(ləʊp )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) (of a person) to move or run with a long swinging stride2. (intransitive) (of four-legged animals) to run with a regular bounding movement3. to cause (a horse) to canter with a long easy stride or (of a horse) to canter in this mannernoun4. a long steady gait or stride
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

loper (ˈloper) noun

Word Origin

C15: from Old Norse hlaupa to leap; compare Middle Dutch lopen to run

(loʊp ) for learners of English
Word forms: lopes , loping , loped
Definitions
verbIf a person or animal lopes somewhere, they run in an easy and relaxed way, taking long steps. [V prep/adv] ⇒ He was loping across the sand toward Nancy. [V prep/adv] ⇒ Matty saw him go loping off, running low. [Also V]loping adjective [ADJ n] ⇒ She turned and walked away with long, loping steps.
