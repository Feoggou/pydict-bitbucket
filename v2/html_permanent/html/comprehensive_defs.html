comprehensive

(ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. of broad scope or content; including all or much2. (of a car insurance policy) providing protection against most risks, including third-party liability, fire, theft, and damage3. having the ability to understand4. of, relating to, or being a comprehensive schoolnoun5. short for comprehensive school
Derived forms
ˌcomprehensively (ˌcompreˈhensively) adverbˌcomprehensiveness (ˌcompreˈhensiveness) noun

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjectiveSomething that is comprehensive includes everything that is needed or relevant. ⇒ The Rough Guide to Nepal is a comprehensive guide to the region.2. countable nounIn Britain, a comprehensive is a state school in which children of all abilities are taught together. ⇒ ...Birmingham's inner-city comprehensives. ⇒ She taught French at Cheam Comprehensive in South London.Comprehensive is also an adjective. ⇒ He left comprehensive school at the age of 16. ⇒ ...Rushcliffe Comprehensive School.
