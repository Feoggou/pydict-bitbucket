blab

(blæb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
verbWord forms: blabs, blabbing or blabbed1. to divulge (secrets) indiscreetly2. (intransitive) to chatter thoughtlessly; prattlenoun3. a less common word for blabber (sense 1), blabber (sense 2)
Derived forms
blabbing (ˈblabbing) noun, adjective
Word origin
C14: of Germanic origin; compare Old High German blabbizōn, Icelandic blabbra

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
verbIf someone blabs about something secret, they tell people about it. [informal] [V + about] ⇒ Her mistake was to blab about their affair. [V + to] ⇒ No blabbing to your mates! [V n prep] ⇒ She'll blab it all over the school. [Also V]
