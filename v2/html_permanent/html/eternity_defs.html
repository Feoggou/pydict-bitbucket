eternity

(ɪˈtɜːnɪtɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -ties1. endless or infinite time2. the quality, state, or condition of being eternal3. (usually plural) any of the aspects of life and thought that are considered to be timeless, esp timeless and true4. theology the condition of timeless existence, believed by some to characterize the afterlife5. a seemingly endless period of time ⇒ an eternity of waiting
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

eternity

Powered by COBUILD
(ɪtɜːʳnɪti ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. uncount nounEternity is time without an end or a state of existence outside time, especially the state which some people believe they will pass into after they have died. ⇒ I have always found the thought of eternity terrifying. ⇒ ...laying him to rest for all eternity.2. singular nounIf you say that a situation lasted for an eternity, you mean that it seemed to last an extremely long time, usually because it was boring or unpleasant. ⇒ The war continued for an eternity. ⇒ The ringing went on for what seemed an eternity, and then someone answered.
COBUILD Advanced English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers
