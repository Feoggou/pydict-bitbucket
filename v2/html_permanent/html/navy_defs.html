navy

(ˈneɪvɪ )
Definitions
noun plural -vies1. the warships and auxiliary vessels of a nation or ruler2. See the navy3. Also: navy blue dark blue4. archaic or literary a fleet of ships5. (as modifier) ⇒ a navy custom
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

C14: via Old French from Vulgar Latin nāvia (unattested) ship, from Latin nāvis ship

navy for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(neɪvi )
Word forms: navies
Definitions
1. countable nounA country's navy consists of the people it employs to fight at sea, and the ships they use. ⇒ The government announced an order for three Type 23 frigates for the Royal Navy yesterday. ⇒ Her own son was also in the Navy. ⇒ ...a United States navy ship.2. colourSomething that is navy or navy-blue is very dark blue. ⇒ I mostly wore black or navy trousers. ⇒ ...a navy-blue blazer.
