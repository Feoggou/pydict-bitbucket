wince1

(wɪns )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive) to start slightly, as with sudden pain; flinchnoun2. the act of wincing
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

wincer (ˈwincer) noun

Word Origin

C18 (earlier (C13) meaning: to kick): via Old French wencier, guenchir to avoid, from Germanic; compare Old Saxon wenkian, Old High German wenken

wince for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(wɪns )
Word forms: winces , wincing , winced
Definitions
verbIf you wince, the muscles of your face tighten suddenly because you have felt a pain or because you have just seen, heard, or remembered something unpleasant. [V] ⇒ Every time he put any weight on his left leg he winced in pain. [V] ⇒ He winced at the thought of dining with Camilla. [V with quote] ⇒ 'Shh!' Sunny winced.Wince is also a noun. ⇒ He suppressed a wince as motion renewed the pain.
