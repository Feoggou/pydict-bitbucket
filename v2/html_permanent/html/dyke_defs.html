dyke1

or dike (daɪk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. an embankment constructed to prevent flooding, keep out the sea, etc2. a ditch or watercourse3. a bank made of earth excavated for and placed alongside a ditch4. Scottish a wall, esp a dry-stone wall5. a barrier or obstruction6. a vertical or near-vertical wall-like body of igneous rock intruded into cracks in older rock7. Australian and New Zealand informala. a lavatoryb. (as modifier) ⇒ a dyke rollverb8. (transitive) to protect, enclose, or drain (land) with a dyke
Word origin
C13: modification of Old English dic ditch; compare Old Norse dīki ditch
