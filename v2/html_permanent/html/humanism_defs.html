humanism

(ˈhjuːməˌnɪzəm )
Definitions
noun1. the denial of any power or moral value superior to that of humanity; the rejection of religion in favour of a belief in the advancement of humanity by its own efforts2. a philosophical position that stresses the autonomy of human reason in contradistinction to the authority of the Church3. (often capital) a cultural movement of the Renaissance, based on classical studies4. interest in the welfare of people
