coalition

(ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a. an alliance or union between groups, factions, or parties, esp for some temporary and specific reasonb. (as modifier) ⇒ a coalition government2. a fusion or merging into one body or mass
Derived forms
ˌcoalitional (ˌcoaˈlitional) adjectiveˌcoalitionist (ˌcoaˈlitionist) or ˌcoalitioner (ˌcoaˈlitioner) noun
Word origin
C17: from Medieval Latin coalitiō, from Latin coalēscere to coalesce

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. countable noun [oft N n]A coalition is a government consisting of people from two or more political parties. ⇒ Since June the country has had a coalition government. ⇒ It took five months for the coalition to agree on and publish a medium-term economic programme.2. countable nounA coalition is a group consisting of people from different political or social groups who are co-operating to achieve a particular aim. [+ of] ⇒ He had been opposed by a coalition of about 50 civil rights, women's and Latino organizations.
