critique

(krɪˈtiːk )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a critical essay or commentary, esp on artistic work2. the act or art of criticizing
Word origin
C17: from French, from Greek kritikē, from kritikos able to discern

critique

Powered by COBUILD
(krɪtiːk )
Word forms: critiques
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
countable nounA critique is a written examination and judgment of a situation or of a person's work or ideas. [formal] evaluation [+ of] ⇒ She had brought a book, a feminist critique of Victorian lady novelists. ⇒ ...the Marxist critique of capitalism.
Example sentences containing 'critique'
She was wrestling with a critique on Van Gogh when her mother came in. Stewart, Michael GRACEOh yes, said Alison or Alice -- and Ronnie sorely resented the critique of his referee-whistle solo on `Urban Police Patrol '. David Cavanagh MUSIC FOR BOYSThe first is what we call ideological critique , the work of analyzing the internal contradictions of ideologies. Alex Shakar THE SAVAGE GIRL
