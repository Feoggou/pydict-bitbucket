earn

(ɜːn )
Definitions
verb1. to gain or be paid (money or other payment) in return for work or service2. (transitive) to acquire, merit, or deserve through behaviour or action ⇒ he has earned a name for duplicity3. (transitive) (of securities, investments, etc) to gain (interest, return, profit, etc)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English earnian; related to Old High German arnēn to reap, Old Saxon asna salary, tithe

Powered by COBUILD
(ɜːʳn ) for learners of English
Word forms: earns , earning , earned
Definitions
1. verbIf you earn money, you receive money in return for work that you do. [V n] ⇒ Charlie was earning eight pounds, I was earning five. [V n] ⇒ What a lovely way to earn a living. [V n] ⇒ The dancers can earn anything between £80 and £130 for each session. [V] ⇒ She was always out earning.2. verbIf something earns money, it produces money as profit or interest. [V n] ⇒ ...a current account which earns little or no interest. [V n] ⇒ We buy everything abroad with the money earned from oil imports.3. verbIf you earn something such as praise, you get it because you deserve it. [V n] ⇒ Companies must earn a reputation for honesty. [V n n] ⇒ I think that's earned him very high admiration.4. → to earn a crust
