slush

(slʌʃ )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. any watery muddy substance, esp melting snow2. informal sloppily sentimental language3. nautical waste fat from the galley of a shipverb4. (intransitive; often foll by along) to make one's way through or as if through slush5. (intransitive) to make a slushing sound

Word Origin

C17: related to Danish slus sleet, Norwegian slusk slops; see sludge, slosh

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. uncount nounSlush is snow that has begun to melt and is therefore very wet and dirty. ⇒ Becker's eyes were as cold and grey as the slush on the pavements outside.2. uncount nounIf you describe a love story as slush, you mean that you dislike it because it is too sentimental and cannot be taken seriously. [disapproval]
