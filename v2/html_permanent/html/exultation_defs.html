exultation

(ˌɛgzəlˈteɪʃən ; ˌ ɛksəlˈteɪʃən ) in American English
Definitions
noun the act of exulting; rejoicing; jubilation; triumph: also exˈultance (ɛgˈzʌltəns ; ɪgˈzʌltəns ), exˈultancy (ɛgˈzʌltənsi ; ɪgˈzʌltənsi )
Webster’s New World College Dictionary, Fourth Edition, compiled by the editors of Webster’s New World Dictionaries. Copyright © 2010 by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. Published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt. All rights reserved.

Word Origin

ME exultacion < L exultatio, exsultatio < pp. of exsultare
Example sentences containing 'exultation'
The greater the hope, the nearer we aspire to exultation , the more dismal and the deeper the fall. Fay Weldon THE PRESIDENT'S CHILDI said, sounding disappointed to conceal the surge of exultation I was feeling. Anthony Masters CASCADES - THE DAY OF THE DEADHe poured out both glasses, his blue eyes bright with exultation. Telushkin, Josef THE UNORTHODOX MURDER OF RABBI MOSSI was not going to thank him, or shout with exultation as I wanted to. Siddons, Anne Rivers DOWNTOWN
