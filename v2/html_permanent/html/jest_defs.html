jest

(dʒɛst )
Definitions
noun1. something done or said for amusement; joke2. a frivolous mood or attitude; playfulness; fun ⇒ to act in jest3. a jeer or taunt4. an object of derision; laughing stock; buttverb5. to act or speak in an amusing, teasing, or frivolous way; joke6. to make fun of (a person or thing); scoff or mock
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

jestful (ˈjestful) adjectivejesting (ˈjesting) adjective, nounjestingly (ˈjestingly) adverb

Word Origin

C13: variant of gest

jest for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(dʒest )
Word forms: jests , jesting , jested
Definitions
1. countable nounA jest is something that you say that is intended to be amusing. [formal] ⇒ It was a jest rather than a reproach. ⇒ The men talk as cheerfully as ever; jests are bandied about freely.→  in jest2. verbIf you jest, you tell jokes or say amusing things. [formal] [V + with] ⇒ He enjoyed drinking and jesting with his cronies. [V with quote] ⇒ A newspaper half jested: Had alcohol not already killed him, this would surely drive him to drink.
