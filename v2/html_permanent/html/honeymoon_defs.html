honeymoon

(ˈhʌnɪˌmuːn )
Definitions
noun1. a. a holiday taken by a newly married coupleb. (as modifier) ⇒ a honeymoon cottage2. a holiday considered to resemble a honeymoon ⇒ a second honeymoon3. the early, usually calm period of a relationship, such as a political or business oneverb4. (intransitive) to take a honeymoon
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

honeymooner (ˈhoneyˌmooner) noun

Word Origin

C16: traditionally explained as an allusion to the feelings of married couples as changing with the phases of the moon

honeymoon

Powered by COBUILD
(hʌnimuːn ) for learners of English
Word forms: honeymoons , honeymooning , honeymooned
Definitions
1. countable nounA honeymoon is a holiday taken by a man and a woman who have just got married.2. verbWhen a recently-married couple honeymoon somewhere, they go there on their honeymoon. [V] ⇒ They honeymooned in Venice. [V-ing] ⇒ ...honeymooning Japanese tourists.3. countable nounYou can use honeymoon to refer to a period of time after the start of a new job or new government when everyone is pleased with the person or people concerned and is nice to them. ⇒ Austrians voted for E.U. membership by a two-to-one majority in a June 1994 referendum, but the honeymoon is definitely over. ⇒ Brett is enjoying a honeymoon period with both press and public.
