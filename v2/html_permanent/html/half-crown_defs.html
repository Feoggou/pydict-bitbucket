half-crown

Definitions
nouna British silver or cupronickel coin worth two shillings and sixpence (now equivalent to 121⁄2p), taken out of circulation in 1970. Also called: half-a-crown

Comment by roger_crunden on 27 Aug 2016
A commonly used expression when I was young (1940s and 60s) 'I'm going on the half crown side' meaning I'm going on the other side. I can't find any reference to this today. There may have been other connotations perhaps meaning going on the more expensive side or better side.
