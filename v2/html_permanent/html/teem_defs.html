teem1

(tiːm )
Definitions
verb1. (intransitive; usually foll by with) to be prolific or abundant (in); abound (in)2. obsolete to bring forth (young)
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Word Origin

Old English tēman to produce offspring; related to West Saxon tīeman; see team

teem2

(tiːm )
Definitions
verb1. (intr; often foll by down or with rain) to pour in torrents ⇒ it's teeming down2. (transitive) to pour or empty out
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

teemer (ˈteemer) noun

Word Origin

C15 temen to empty, from Old Norse tœma; related to Old English tōm, Old High German zuomīg empty

teem

Powered by COBUILD
(tiːm ) for learners of English
Word forms: teems , teeming , teemed
Definitions
verb [usu cont]If you say that a place is teeming with people or animals, you mean that it is crowded and the people and animals are moving around a lot. [V + with] ⇒ For most of the year, the area is teeming with tourists.
