avant-garde

(ˌævɒŋˈɡɑːd , French avɑ̃ɡard)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. those artists, writers, musicians, etc, whose techniques and ideas are markedly experimental or in advance of those generally acceptedadjective2. of such artists, etc, their ideas, or techniques3. radical; daring
Derived forms
ˌavant-ˈgardism nounˌavant-ˈgardist noun
Word origin
from French: vanguard

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]Avant-garde art, music, theatre, and literature is very modern and experimental. ⇒ ...avant-garde concert music.Avant-garde is also a noun. ⇒ He was an enthusiast for the avant-garde.2. singular noun [the N]You can refer to the artists, writers, and musicians who introduce new and very modern ideas as the avant-garde. ⇒ In Paris he made friends among the avant-garde.
