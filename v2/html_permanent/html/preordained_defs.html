preordained

Powered by COBUILD
(priːɔːʳdeɪnd )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
adjectiveIf you say that something is preordained, you mean you believe it to be happening in the way that has been decided by a power such as God or fate. [formal] ⇒ ...the belief that our actions are the unfolding of a preordained destiny.
Example sentences containing 'preordained'
His mouth was dry with apprehension, even though the choice was preordained. Erskine, Barbara MIDNIGHT IS A LONELY PLACE"'So your whingeing about your life being laid out in advance is preordained , is it? West, Charles STAGE FRIGHTIt had been one of Fletcher's last acts to choose her to go to the Mission; he'd even indicated that it had somehow been preordained. Clive Barker THE GREAT AND SECRET SHOW
