cherub

(ˈtʃɛrəb )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
nounWord forms: plural cherubs or cherubim (ˈtʃɛrəbɪm , -ʊbɪm)1. theology a member of the second order of angels, whose distinctive gift is knowledge, often represented as a winged child or winged head of a child2. an innocent or sweet child
Derived forms
cherubic (tʃəˈruːbɪk ) or cherubical (cheˈrubical) adjectivecherubically (cheˈrubically) adverb
Word origin
Old English, from Hebrew kěrūbh
