masculine

(ˈmæskjʊlɪn )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. possessing qualities or characteristics considered typical of or appropriate to a man; manly2. unwomanly3. grammara. denoting a gender of nouns, occurring in many inflected languages, that includes all kinds of referents as well as some male animate referentsb. (as noun) ⇒ German "Weg" is a masculine

Derived Forms

masculinely (ˈmasculinely) adverbˌmasculinity (ˌmascuˈlinity) or rare masculineness (ˈmasculineness) noun

Word Origin

C14: via French from Latin masculīnus, from masculus male, from mās a male

masculine

(mæskjʊlɪn )
Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. adjective [usu ADJ n]Masculine qualities and things relate to or are considered typical of men, in contrast to women. ⇒ ...masculine characteristics like a husky voice and facial hair. ⇒ ...masculine pride.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you say that someone or something is masculine, you mean that they have qualities such as strength or confidence which are considered typical of men. ⇒ ...her aggressive, masculine image. ⇒ The Duke's study was very masculine, with deep red wall-covering and dark oak shelving.3. adjectiveIn some languages, a masculine noun, pronoun, or adjective has a different form from a feminine or neuter one, or behaves in a different way.
