Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. phrasal verbIf you point out an object or place, you make people look at it or show them where it is. [V n P] ⇒ They kept standing up to take pictures and point things out to each other. [V P n] ⇒ They'd already driven along the wharf so that she could point out her father's boat.2. phrasal verbIf you point out a fact or mistake, you tell someone about it or draw their attention to it. [V P that] ⇒ Critics point out that the prince, on his income, should be paying tax. [V P that] ⇒ I should point out that these estimates cover just the hospital expenditures. [V P n] ⇒ We all too easily point out our mothers' failings. [Also V n P] →  point
