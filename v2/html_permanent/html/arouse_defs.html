Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. verbIf something arouses a particular reaction or attitude in people, it causes them to have that reaction or attitude. [V n] ⇒ We left in the daytime so as not to arouse suspicion. [V n] ⇒ ...the deep public anger you have aroused.2. verbIf something arouses a particular feeling or instinct that exists in someone, it causes them to experience that feeling or instinct strongly. [V n] ⇒ There is nothing like a long walk to arouse the appetite. [V n] ⇒ He aroused her mothering instincts. [Also V n in n]3. verb [usu passive]If you are aroused by something, it makes you feel sexually excited. [be V-ed] ⇒ Some men are aroused when their partner says erotic words to them.aroused graded adjective & adjective [usu v-link ADJ] ⇒ Some men feel that they get most sexually aroused in the morning.arousing graded adjective & adjective ⇒ Being stroked by a partner is usually more arousing than stroking yourself.4. verbIf something arouses you, it makes you feel angry. [V n] ⇒ He apologized, saying this subject always aroused him.5. verbIf something arouses you from sleep, it wakes you up. [written] awaken [V n] ⇒ About two o'clock, we were aroused from our sleep by a knocking at the door.
