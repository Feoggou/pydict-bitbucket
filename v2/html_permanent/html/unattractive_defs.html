unattractive

(ˌʌnəˈtræktɪv )
Definitions
adjective1. not appealing to the senses or mind through beauty, form, character, etc2. not arousing interest ⇒ an unattractive proposition
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

unattractive for learners of English

Powered by COBUILD
(ʌnətræktɪv )
Definitions
1. graded adjective & adjectiveUnattractive people and things are unpleasant in appearance. ⇒ I'm 27, have a nice flat, a good job and I'm not unattractive. ⇒ ...an unattractive shade of orange. ⇒ ...an unattractive and uninteresting city.2. graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe something as unattractive, you mean that people do not like it and do not want to be involved with it. unappealing [+ to] ⇒ The market is still unattractive to many insurers. ⇒ It is not an unattractive option to make programmes for other companies.
