blue gum

or bluegum (ˈbluːˌɡʌm)
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. a tall fast-growing widely cultivated Australian myrtaceous tree, Eucalyptus globulus, having aromatic leaves containing a medicinal oil, bark that peels off in shreds, and hard timber. The juvenile leaves are bluish in colour2. any of several other eucalyptus trees▷ See also red gum (sense 1)
