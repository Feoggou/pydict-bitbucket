gangway

(ˈɡæŋˌweɪ )
Definitions
noun1. an opening in a ship's side to take a gangplank2. another word for gangplank3. British an aisle between rows of seats4. Also called: logway mainly US a ramp for logs leading into a sawmill5. a main passage in a mine6. temporary planks over mud or earth, as on a building sitesentence substitute7. clear a path!

Definitions
1. countable noun [usu the N in sing]A gangway is the same as a gangplank.2. countable nounThe gangway is the passage between rows of seats, for example in a theatre or aircraft, for people to walk along. [British] ⇒ A man in the gangway suddenly stood up to reach for something in the overhead locker.
