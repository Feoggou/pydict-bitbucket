splendid

(ˈsplɛndɪd )

Definitions

Collins English Dictionary
adjective1. brilliant or fine, esp in appearance2. characterized by magnificence; imposing3. glorious or illustrious ⇒ a splendid reputation4. brightly gleaming; radiant ⇒ her splendid face, ⇒ splendid colours5. very good or satisfactory ⇒ a splendid time

Derived Forms

splendidly (ˈsplendidly) adverbsplendidness (ˈsplendidness) noun

Word Origin

C17: from Latin splendidus, from splendēre to shine

Definitions
COBUILD Advanced British English Dictionary
1. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you say that something is splendid, you mean that it is very good. ⇒ The book includes a wealth of splendid photographs. ⇒ Our house has got a splendid view across to the Cotswolds. ⇒ I found him to be splendid company during the hour of our acquaintance.splendidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ I have heard him tell people that we get along splendidly.2. graded adjective & adjective [usu ADJ n]If you describe a building or work of art as splendid, you mean that it is beautiful, impressive, and extremely well made. ⇒ ...a splendid Victorian mansion.splendidly graded adverb & adverb [ADV adj, ADV with v] ⇒ The young women are splendidly dressed, some in floor-length ball gowns. ⇒ Its historic buildings are being slowly and splendidly renovated. ⇒ ...this splendidly readable and robust autobiography.3. exclamationYou can say 'splendid' in a conversation to indicate that you approve of a particular situation or something that someone has said. [feelings] excellent ⇒ 'I was thinking I might do a lemon cream sauce and baked potatoes.' 'Splendid!' Midge applauded.
