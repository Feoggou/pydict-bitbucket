plaint

(pleɪnt )
Definitions
Collins English Dictionary
noun1. archaic a complaint or lamentation2. law a statement in writing of grounds of complaint made to a court of law and asking for redress of the grievance

Word Origin

C13: from Old French plainte, from Latin planctus lamentation, from plangere to beat
