imperious

(ɪmˈpɪərɪəs )
Definitions
adjective1. domineering; arrogant; overbearing2. rare urgent; imperative
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

imperiously (imˈperiously) adverbimperiousness (imˈperiousness) noun

Word Origin

C16: from Latin imperiōsus from imperium command, power

Definitions
graded adjective & adjectiveIf you describe someone as imperious, you mean that they have a proud manner and expect to be obeyed. [written] ⇒ Her attitude is imperious at times. ⇒ From across the desk she gave him a witheringly imperious look.imperiously graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ Imperiously she beckoned me out of the room.
