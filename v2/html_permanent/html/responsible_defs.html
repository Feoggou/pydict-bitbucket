responsible

(rɪˈspɒnsəbəl )
Definitions
adjective1. (postpositive; usually foll by for) having control or authority (over)2. (postpositive; foll by to) being accountable for one's actions and decisions (to) ⇒ to be responsible to one's commanding officer3. (of a position, duty, etc) involving decision and accountability4. (often foll by for) being the agent or cause (of some action) ⇒ to be responsible for a mistake5. able to take rational decisions without supervision; accountable for one's own actions ⇒ a responsible adult6. able to meet financial obligations; of sound credit
Collins English Dictionary. Copyright © Harper Collins Publishers

Derived Forms

responsibleness (reˈsponsibleness) nounresponsibly (reˈsponsibly) adverb

Word Origin

C16: from Latin rēsponsus, from rēspondēre to respond

responsible

Powered by COBUILD
(rɪspɒnsɪbəl ) for learners of English
Definitions
1. adjective [v-link ADJ]If someone or something is responsible for a particular event or situation, they are the cause of it or they can be blamed for it. [+ for] ⇒ He still felt responsible for her death. ⇒ I want you to do everything you can to find out who's responsible.2. adjective [v-link ADJ]If you are responsible for something, it is your job or duty to deal with it and make decisions relating to it. [+ for] ⇒ ...the minister responsible for the environment. ⇒ The man responsible for finding the volunteers is Dr. Charles Weber.3. adjectiveIf you are responsible to a person or group, they have authority over you and you have to report to them about what you do. [+ to] ⇒ I'm responsible to my board of directors. ⇒ The government will be responsible to the President alone.4. graded adjective & adjectiveResponsible people behave properly and sensibly, without needing to be supervised. ⇒ He's a very responsible sort of person. ⇒ He feels that the media should be more responsible in what they report.responsibly graded adverb & adverb [ADV with v] ⇒ He urged everyone to act responsibly.5. graded adjective & adjective [ADJ n]Responsible jobs involve making important decisions or carrying out important tasks. ⇒ I work in a government office. It's a responsible position, I suppose, but not very exciting. ⇒ They have been demoted to less responsible jobs.
