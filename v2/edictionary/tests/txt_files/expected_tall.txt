DEFINTIONS
tall
[Very Common]

origin
C14 (in the sense: big, comely, valiant); related to Old English getæl prompt, Old High German gizal quick, Gothic untals foolish

derived forms
(noun) tallness

adjective
o) of more than average height
o) 
     *) (postpositive) having a specified height
         e.g. a woman five feet tall
     *) (in combination)
         e.g. a twenty-foot-tall partition
o) (informal) exaggerated or incredible
    e.g. a tall story
o) (informal) difficult to accomplish
    e.g. a tall order
o) (i) an archaic word for excellent


TRANSLATIONS
tall
adjective
Someone or something that is tall is above average height.
    e.g. He was very tall.



EXAMPLES
o) Smoking torches threw tall shadows on the canvas walls, which made Mikel's eyes water.
o) Mikel was giddy with relief as the tall knight in the lead lifted his faceplate.
o) A tall male figure stood in the garage door, silhouetted against the bright light outside.
