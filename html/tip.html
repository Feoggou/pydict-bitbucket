Definitions of tip

tip (tɪp )

Definitions

noun

  1. the pointed, tapering, or rounded end or top of something long and slim
  2. something attached to the end, as a cap, ferrule, etc.
  3. a top or apex, as of a mountain

transitive verb

Word forms:  tipped,  ˈtipping
  1. to make a tip on
  2. to cover the tip or tips of (with something)
  3. to serve as the tip of
  4. (US) to remove the stems from (berries, etc.)
  • See tip in
  • See tip of the iceberg

Derived Forms

ˈtipless adjective

Word Origin

ME tippe, akin to MLowG tip, point, top, Ger zipf- in zipfel, an end, tip, prob. < IE base * dumb-, tail > Avestan duma-, tail

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= end, extremity, head, peak, pinnacle, point, summit, top
= cap, crown, finish, surmount, top

tip (tɪp )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  tipped,  ˈtipping
  1. to strike lightly and sharply; tap
  2. to give a small present of money to (a waiter, porter, etc.) for some service
  3. (informal)
    1. to give secret information to in an attempt to be helpful (often with off)
    2. to reveal or divulge (a secret, plot, etc.)
  4. (US, baseball)
    1. to hit (the ball) a glancing blow
    2. to glance off (the bat, glove, etc.)
  5. (sport) to deflect or tap (a ball, puck, etc.) in a particular direction, esp. into a goal

intransitive verb

  1. to give a tip or tips

noun

  1. a light, sharp blow; tap see also foul tip
  2. a piece of secret information given confidentially in an attempt to be helpful   ⇒ a tip on the race
  3. a suggestion, hint, warning, etc.
  4. a small sum of money, often a percentage of the total billed, given to a waiter, porter, etc. for services; gratuity
  5. (sport) a deflection or tap of the ball, puck, etc., esp. one that scores a goal
  • See tip one's hand

Word Origin

akin ? to tip1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= gratuity, gift
= hint, clue, pointer, suggestion, warning
= reward, remunerate
= advise, caution, forewarn, suggest, warn

tip (tɪp )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  tipped,  ˈtipping
  1. to overturn or upset (often with over)
  2. to cause to tilt or slant
  3. to raise slightly or touch the brim of (one's hat) in salutation

intransitive verb

  1. to tilt or slant
  2. to overturn or topple (often with over)

noun

  1. a tipping or being tipped; tilt; slant
  2. (British) a place for dumping rubbish, etc.; dump
  • See tip the scales
  • See tip the scales at

Word Origin

ME tipen < ?

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= tilt, incline, lean, list, slant
= dump, empty, pour out, unload
= dump, refuse heap, rubbish heap
