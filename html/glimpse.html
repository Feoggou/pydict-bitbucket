glimpse (glɪmps )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  glimpsed,  ˈglimpsing
  1. to catch a brief, quick view of, as in passing; perceive momentarily and incompletely

intransitive verb

  1. to look quickly; glance (at)

noun

  1. (archaic) a brief, sudden shining; flash
  2. a faint, fleeting appearance; slight trace
  3. a brief, quick view

Word Origin

ME glimsen (with unhistoric -p-) < base of OE glæm (see gleam), akin to MHG glimsen, MDu glinsen

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= look, glance, peek, peep, sight, sighting
= catch sight of, espy, sight, spot, spy, view
