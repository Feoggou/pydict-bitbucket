gloom (glum )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to be or look morose, displeased, or dejected
  2. to be, become, or appear dark, dim, or dismal

transitive verb

  1. to make dark, dismal, dejected, etc.

noun

  1. darkness; dimness; obscurity
  2. a dark or dim place
  3. deep sadness or hopelessness

Word Origin

< ME gloum(b)en, to look morose, prob. < Scand, as in Norw dial. glome, to stare somberly, akin to EFris glumen, to peer secretly (< IE * ĝhlu- < base * ghêl- > gleam, , glow): meaning infl. by OE glom, twilight

Synonyms
