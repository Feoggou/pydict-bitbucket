Definitions of rouse

rouse (raʊz )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  roused,  ˈrousing
  1. to cause (game) to rise from cover, come out of a lair, etc.; stir up to flight or attack
  2. to stir up, as to anger or action; excite
  3. to cause to come out of a state of sleep, repose, unconsciousness, etc.; wake
  4. (nautical) to pull with force, esp. by hand; haul

intransitive verb

  1. to rise from cover, etc. (said of game)
  2. to come out of a state of sleep, repose, etc.; wake
  3. to become active

noun

  1. the act of rousing
  2. a violent stir
stir

Derived Forms

ˈrouser noun

Word Origin

LME rowsen: orig. technical term in hawking & hunting, hence prob. < Anglo-Fr or OFr

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= wake up, awaken, call, rise, wake
= excite, agitate, anger, animate, incite, inflame, move, provoke, stimulate, stir

rouse (raʊz )

Definitions

noun

(archaic)
  1. a drink of liquor
  2. a carousal

Word Origin

aphetic for carouse (from mistaking drink carouse as drink a rouse)
