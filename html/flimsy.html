flimsy (ˈflɪmzi )

Definitions

adjective

Word forms:  ˈflimsier,  ˈflimsiest
  1. thin and easily broken or damaged; poorly made and fragile; frail
  2. ill-conceived and inadequate; ineffectual   ⇒ a flimsy excuse

noun

Word forms:   plural ˈflimsies
(mainly British)
  1. a sheet of thin paper, as used for carbon copies
  2. written or typed copy on such paper

Derived Forms

ˈflimsily adverb
ˈflimsiness noun

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= fragile, delicate, frail, insubstantial, makeshift, rickety, shaky
= thin, gauzy, gossamer, light, sheer, transparent
= unconvincing, feeble, implausible, inadequate, lousy (slang), pathetic, poor, unsatisfactory, weak
