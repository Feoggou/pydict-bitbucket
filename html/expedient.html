expedient (ɛkˈspidiənt ;  ɪkˈspidiənt )

Definitions

adjective

  1. useful for effecting a desired result; suited to the circumstances or the occasion; advantageous; convenient
  2. based on or offering what is of use or advantage rather than what is right or just; guided by self-interest; politic

noun

  1. an expedient thing; means to an end
  2. a device used in an emergency; makeshift; resource
ˈreˌsource

Derived Forms

exˈpediently adverb

Word Origin

ME < OFr < L expediens, prp. of expedire: see expedite

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= means, contrivance, device, makeshift, measure, method, resort, scheme, stopgap
= advantageous, appropriate, beneficial, convenient, effective, helpful, opportune, practical, suitable, useful, win-win (informal)
