Definitions of fold

fold (foʊld )

Definitions

transitive verb

    1. to bend or press (something) so that one part is over another; double up on itself   ⇒ to fold a sheet
    2. to make more compact by so doubling a number of times
  2. to draw together and intertwine   ⇒ to fold the arms
  3. to draw (wings) close to the body
  4. to clasp in the arms; embrace
  5. to wrap up; envelop

intransitive verb

  1. to be or become folded
  2. (US, informal) to fail (; specif.,)
    1. to be forced to close, as a business, play, etc.
    2. to succumb, as to exhaustion; collapse
  3. (US, poker) to withdraw from the betting on a hand, specif. by turning over one's exposed cards

noun

  1. a folded part or layer
  2. a mark made by folding
  3. a hollow or crease produced by folded parts or layers
  4. (British) a hollow; small valley
  5. (geology) a rock layer folded by pressure
  • See fold in

Word Origin

ME folden < OE faldan (WS fealdan), akin to Ger falten < IE * pel-to < base * pel-, to fold > simple, , triple

Synonyms

= crease, bend, furrow, overlap, pleat, wrinkle

fold (foʊld )

Definitions

noun

  1. a pen in which to keep sheep
  2. sheep kept together; flock of sheep
  3. a group or organization with common interests, aims, faith, etc., as a church

transitive verb

  1. to keep or confine in a pen

Word Origin

ME < OE fald, akin to Du vaalt, enclosed place, Dan fold, sheep pen

-fold (foʊld )

Definitions

  1. (forming adjectives) having (a specified number of) parts   ⇒ a tenfold division
  2. (forming adjectives and adverbs) (a specified number of) times as many, as much, as large   ⇒ to profit tenfold

Word Origin

ME -fold, -fald < OE -feald: see fold1
