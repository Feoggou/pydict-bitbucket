Definitions of haze

haze (heɪz )

Definitions

noun

  1. a dispersion of fine sand, smoke, dust, etc. in the air that reduces visibility
  2. a slight confusion or vagueness of mind

intransitive verb, transitive verb

Word forms:  hazed,  ˈhazing
  1. to make or become hazy (often with over)
mist

Word Origin

prob. back-form. < hazy

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= mist, cloud, fog, obscurity, vapor

haze (heɪz )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  hazed,  ˈhazing
  1. (nautical) to punish or harass by forcing to do hard, unnecessary work
  2. (US) to initiate or discipline (fellow students) by forcing to do ridiculous, humiliating, or painful things
  3. (US & Western US) to drive (horses or cattle) while on horseback

Word Origin

< ? OFr haser, to irritate, annoy
