snake (sneɪk )

Definitions

noun

  1. any of a limbless suborder (Serpentes, order Squamata) of reptiles with an elongated, scaly body, lidless eyes, and a tapering tail: some species have a poisonous bite
  2. a treacherous or deceitful person
  3. a plumber's tool consisting of a long, flexible rod of spiraled wire for removing obstructions from pipes, etc.

intransitive verb

Word forms:  snaked,  ˈsnaking
  1. to move, curve, twist, or turn like a snake

transitive verb

  1. to clear obstructions from (a pipe, drain, etc.) by means of a snake (sense 3)
  2. (US, informal) to drag or pull, esp. lengthwise and with force
  3. (informal) to pull quickly

Derived Forms

ˈsnakeˌlike adjective

Word Origin

ME < OE snaca, akin to ON snakr, MLowG snake: for IE base see snail
