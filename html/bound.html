Definitions of bound

bound (baʊnd )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to move with a leap or series of leaps
  2. to spring back from a surface after striking it, as a ball; bounce; rebound

transitive verb

  1. to cause to bound or bounce

noun

  1. a jump; leap
  2. a springing back from a surface after striking it; bounce
skip

Word Origin

MFr bondir < OFr, to leap, make a noise, orig., to echo back < LL bombitare, to buzz, hum < L bombus, a humming: see bomb

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= leap, bob, bounce, gambol, hurdle, jump, skip, spring, vault

bound (baʊnd )

Definitions

transitive verb, intransitive verb

  1. (pt. & pp. of) bind

adjective

  1. confined by or as by binding; tied
  2. closely connected or related
  3. certain; sure; destined   ⇒ bound to lose
  4. under compulsion; obliged   ⇒ legally bound to accept
  5. constipated
  6. provided with a binding or attached cover, as a book
  7. (informal) having one's mind made up; resolved   ⇒ a team bound on winning
  8. (linguistics) designating a form, or morpheme, that never occurs alone as an independent word   ⇒ in “singing,” -ing is a bound form, but sing is not
    see also free (sense 26)
ˈlimit
  • See bound up in

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= tied, cased, fastened, fixed, pinioned, secured, tied up
= certain, destined, doomed, fated, sure
= obliged, beholden, committed, compelled, constrained, duty-bound, forced, pledged, required

bound (baʊnd )

Definitions

noun

  1. a boundary; limit
  2. ([pl.]) an area near, alongside, or enclosed by a boundary

transitive verb

  1. to provide with bounds; limit; confine
  2. to be a limit or boundary to
  3. (US) to name the boundaries of (a state, etc.)

intransitive verb

  1. to have a boundary (on another country, etc.)
  • See out of bounds

Word Origin

ME bounde < OFr bunne, bodne < ML bodina, butina, boundary, boundary marker

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= limit, confine, demarcate, encircle, enclose, hem in, restrain, restrict, surround

bound (baʊnd )

Definitions

adjective

  1. ready to go or going; headed (often with for)   ⇒ bound for home
  2. (archaic) ready; prepared

Word Origin

ME boun, ready (+ -d, prob. by assoc. with bound2) < ON buinn, pp. of bua, to prepare: see , bondage

bind (baɪnd )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  bound,  ˈbinding
  1. to tie together; make fast or tight, as with a rope or band
  2. to hold or restrain as if tied or tied down   ⇒ bound by convention
  3. to gird or encircle with a belt, girdle, etc.; wrap or fasten around
  4. to bandage (often with up)
  5. to make stick together; make cohere
  6. to tighten the bowels of; constipate
  7. to strengthen, secure, or ornament the edges of by a band, as of tape
  8. to fasten together the printed pages of (a book) and enclose them within a protective cover
  9. to secure or make firm (a bargain, contract, etc.)
  10. to obligate by duty, love, etc.
  11. to compel, as by oath, legal restraint, or contract
  12. to make an apprentice of; indenture (often with out or over)
  13. to unite or hold, as by a feeling of loyalty or love

intransitive verb

  1. to do the act of binding
  2. to be or become tight, hard, or stiff
  3. to be constricting or restricting
  4. to stick together
  5. to be obligatory or binding in force

noun

  1. anything that binds
  2. (US, informal) a difficult or restrictive situation; jam   ⇒ to be in a bind
  3. (music) tie (sense 19)
tie
  • See bind over

Word Origin

ME binden < OE bindan < IE base * bhendh- > band1, , bend1, Sans badhnti, (he) binds, Goth bindan

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= secure, fasten, hitch, lash, stick, strap, tie, wrap
= oblige, compel, constrain, engage, force, necessitate, require
= nuisance, bore, drag (informal), pain in the neck (informal)
= difficulty, dilemma, quandary, spot (informal)

-bound (baʊnd )

Definitions

  1. going or headed toward   ⇒ southbound, championship- bound
  2. confined by or to   ⇒ snowbound, housebound

Word Origin

(sense 1) see bound4; (sense 2) see , bound2

Example Sentences Including 'bound'

She'd flown to Inverness and taken a train to Lairg, from where she'd caught a bus bound for Durness.
Mark Burnell THE RHYTHM SECTION (2002)
