Definitions of glance

glance (glæns ;  glɑns )

Definitions

intransitive verb

Word forms:  glanced,  ˈglancing
  1. to strike a surface obliquely and go off at an angle (usually with off)
  2. to make an indirect or passing reference (with over, at, etc.)
  3. to flash or gleam
  4. to look suddenly and briefly; take a quick look

transitive verb

  1. to cause (an object) to strike a surface at an angle and be deflected

noun

  1. a glancing off; deflected impact
  2. a flash or gleam
  3. a quick look
flash

Word Origin

ME glansen, glenchen, prob. a blend < OFr glacier, to slip (see glacis) + guenchir, to elude < Frank * wenkjan, to totter; akin to OE wancol, unstable

Synonyms

= look, glimpse, peek, peep, scan, view
= gleam, flash, glimmer, glint, glisten, glitter, reflect, shimmer, shine, twinkle
= look, dekko (slang), glimpse, peek, peep, view

glance (glæns )

Definitions

noun

  1. any of various ores with a metallic luster: now applied to only a few metallic ores, as silver glance ( argentite) and lead glance ( galena)

Word Origin

Ger glanz, lit., luster: for IE base see glass
