stitch (stɪtʃ )

Definitions

noun

    1. a single complete in-and-out movement of the threaded needle in sewing
    2. suture (sense 4), suture (sense 4c)
  2. a single loop of yarn worked off a needle in knitting, crocheting, etc.
  3. the piece of thread worked in, or a loop, knot, etc. made, by stitching
  4. a particular kind of stitch or style of stitching
  5. a sudden, sharp pain in the side or back
  6. a bit or piece; specif., an article of clothing   ⇒ wearing not a stitch

intransitive verb

  1. to make stitches; sew

transitive verb

  1. to fasten, join, repair, adorn, or operate upon with or as with stitches; sew
  2. to fasten or unite (cartons, booklets, etc.) with staples
  • See in stitches

Derived Forms

ˈstitcher noun

Word Origin

ME stiche < OE stice, a puncture, stab: for IE base see stick
