bosom (ˈbʊzəm ;  (occas.) ˈbuzəm )

Definitions

noun

  1. the human breast; specif., a woman's breasts
  2. a thing thought of as like this   ⇒ the bosom of the sea
  3. the breast regarded as the source of feelings or the seat of inmost thoughts
  4. the enclosing space formed by the breast and arms in embracing
  5. the inside; midst   ⇒ in the bosom of one's family
    1. the part of a dress, shirt, etc. that covers the breast
    2. the space inside this part

transitive verb

  1. to embrace; cherish
  2. to conceal in the bosom; hide

adjective

  1. cherished; intimate   ⇒ a bosom companion
breast

Word Origin

ME < OE bosm; prob. < IE base * bhou-, *bhū-, to grow, swell

Synonyms

= breast, bust, chest
= intimate, boon, cherished, close, confidential, dear, very dear
