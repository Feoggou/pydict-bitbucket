spite (spaɪt )

Definitions

noun

    1. a mean or evil feeling toward another, characterized by the inclination to hurt, humiliate, annoy, frustrate, etc.; ill will; malice
    2. an instance of this; a grudge
  2. (obsolete) something annoying or irritating

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈspited,  ˈspiting
  1. to behave in a spiteful manner toward; vent one's spite upon by hurting, annoying, frustrating, etc.
  • See in spite of

Word Origin

ME, aphetic < despite: see despite

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= malice, animosity, hatred, ill will, malevolence, spitefulness, spleen, venom
= hurt, annoy, harm, injure, vex
