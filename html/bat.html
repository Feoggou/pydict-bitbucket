Definitions of bat

bat (bæt )

Definitions

noun

  1. any stout club, stick, or cudgel
  2. a club used to strike the ball in baseball and cricket
  3. a ping-pong paddle, squash racket, etc.
  4. (US) a turn at batting, as in baseball see also at-bat
  5. (British) a batsman at cricket
  6. ([usually pl.]) cotton batting, esp. of an inferior quality; batt
  7. the whip used by a jockey
  8. (informal) a blow or hit
  9. (US, slang) a drinking bout; spree
  10. (British, informal) fast pace; speed
  11. (ceramics) a plaster disk on which the clay is based in modeling

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈbatted,  ˈbatting
  1. to strike with or as with a bat
  2. to have a batting average of see also batting average

intransitive verb

  1. to use a bat   ⇒ to bat left-handed
  2. to take a turn at batting   ⇒ to bat third in the lineup
  • See at bat
  • See bat around
  • See bat out
  • See go to bat for
  • See (right) off the bat

Word Origin

ME < OE batt, cudgel (prob. < Welsh bat < IE base * bhat-, to strike) & < OFr batte, pestle < battre, batter1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= hit, bang, smack, strike, swat, thump, wallop (informal), whack

bat (bæt )

Definitions

noun

  1. any of an order (Chiroptera) of furry, nocturnal flying mammals having membranous wings and navigating by echolocation; chiropter: various bats feed on insects, nectar, fruit, flesh, or blood
  • See blind as a bat
  • See have bats in the belfry

Word Origin

altered < ME bakke < Scand, as in OSwed backa

bat (bæt )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈbatted,  ˈbatting
  1. (informal) to wink; blink; flutter
  • See not bat an eye

Word Origin

ME baten, to flap (wings) < OFr battre, batter1
