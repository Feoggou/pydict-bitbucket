bog (bɑg ;  bɔg )

Definitions

noun

  1. wet, spongy ground, characterized by decaying mosses that form peat; a small marsh or swamp

transitive verb, intransitive verb

Word forms:  bogged,  ˈbogging
  1. to sink or become stuck in or as in a bog; mire (often with down)

Derived Forms

ˈboggy adjective

Word Origin

< Gael & Ir bog, soft, moist (> Ir bogach, a bog) < IE * bhugh- < base * bheugh-, to bend > bow1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= marsh, fen, mire, morass, quagmire, slough, swamp, wetlands
