Definitions of scaffolding

scaffolding (ˈskæfəldəldɪŋ ;  ˈskæfˌoʊldəldɪŋ )

Definitions

noun

  1. the poles, planks, etc. that form a scaffold
  2. a scaffold or system of scaffolds

scaffold (ˈskæfəld ;  ˈskæfˌoʊld )

Definitions

noun

  1. a temporary wooden or metal framework for supporting workmen and materials during the erecting, repairing, or painting of a building, etc.
  2. a raised platform on which criminals are executed, as by hanging
  3. a temporary wooden stage or platform, as that on which medieval plays were presented
  4. any raised framework

transitive verb

  1. to furnish or support with, or put on, a scaffold

Word Origin

ME scafald < OFr escafalt < es- (L ex-, out) + VL * catafalicum: see catafalque
