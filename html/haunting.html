Definitions of haunting

haunting (ˈhɔntɪŋ )

Definitions

adjective

  1. often recurring to the mind; not easily forgotten   ⇒ a haunting tune

Derived Forms

ˈhauntingly adverb

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= poignant, evocative, nostalgic, persistent, unforgettable

haunt (hɔnt ;  hɑnt ;  (for 6, usually) hænt )

Definitions

transitive verb

  1. to visit (a place) often or continually; frequent
  2. to seek the company or companionship of; run after
  3. to appear or recur repeatedly to, often to the point of obsession   ⇒ memories haunted her
  4. to be associated with; fill the atmosphere of; pervade   ⇒ memories of former gaiety haunt the house

noun

    1. a place often visited   ⇒ to make the library one's haunt
    2. a lair or feeding place of animals
  2. (dialect) a ghost

Word Origin

ME haunten < OFr hanter, to frequent < Gmc * haimetan (akin to OE hamettan, to domicile) < * haim, home

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= plague, obsess, possess, prey on, recur, stay with, torment, trouble, weigh on
= meeting place, hangout (informal), rendezvous, stamping ground
