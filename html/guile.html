guile (gaɪl )

Definitions

noun

  1. slyness and cunning in dealing with others

Word Origin

ME gile < OFr guile < Frank * wigila, guile, akin to OE wigle: see wile

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= cunning, artifice, cleverness, craft, deceit, slyness, trickery, wiliness

Example Sentences Including 'guile'

He knew that absolutely, sensed the truth in her untutored response, read it in her lack of guile.
Stephanie Laurens A RAKE'S VOW (2001)

Comments

Comment by LSDexitOzAmericaDotOrg on 23 Feb 2013

The late Dr. Robert A. Cook of 'Walk with the King' Ministry referred to 'guile' in three different lessons as being 'manipulative.' Reading between the lines, 'guile' associates with 'wile' and 'wile' comes from 'witch.' 'Manipulative' hits more on target when 'guile' is dealing with sorcery, and very possibly has been deliberately diverted to better deviate from that context. Consider this insight where 'guile' is being used in the Holy Bible.
