Definitions of railing

railing (ˈreɪlɪŋ )

Definitions

noun

  1. materials for rails
  2. rails collectively
  3. a fence or balustrade made of rails and posts

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= fence, balustrade, barrier, paling, rails

rail (reɪl )

Definitions

noun

  1. a bar of wood, metal, etc. placed horizontally between upright posts to serve as a barrier or support
  2. a fence or railing; specif., the fence surrounding the infield of a racetrack
  3. any of a series of parallel metal bars laid upon crossties or in the ground to make a track for railroad cars, streetcars, etc.
  4. a railroad or railway as a means of transportation   ⇒ to travel by rail
  5. a horizontal piece of wood separating the panels in doors or wainscoting
  6. the rim of a billiard table
  7. (nautical) a narrow, wooden or metal piece forming the top of a ship's bulwarks

transitive verb

  1. to supply with rails or a railing; fence

adjective

  1. of or pertaining to a railway or railroad
  • See (go) off the rails
  • See ride on a rail

Word Origin

ME raile < OFr reille < L regula, rule
