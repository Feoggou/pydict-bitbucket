Definitions of rake

rake (reɪk )

Definitions

noun

  1. any of various long-handled tools with teeth or prongs at one end, used for gathering loose grass, hay, leaves, etc., or for smoothing broken ground
  2. any of various similar toothed devices   ⇒ oyster rake

transitive verb

Word forms:  raked,  ˈraking
    1. to gather or scrape together with or as with a rake
    2. to make (a lawn, etc.) tidy with a rake
  2. to gather with great care
  3. to scratch or smooth with a rake, as in leveling broken ground
  4. to cover (a fire) with ashes
  5. to scratch or scrape
  6. to search through minutely; scour
  7. to direct gunfire along (a line of troops, the deck of a ship, etc.) (often figurative)
  8. to look over rapidly and searchingly

intransitive verb

  1. to use a rake
  2. to search as if with a rake
  3. to scrape or sweep (with over, across, etc.)
  • See rake in
  • See rake up

Word Origin

ME < OE raca; akin to ON reka, spade, Ger rechen, a rake < IE base * reĝ-, to direct, put in order > right

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= gather, collect, remove
= search, comb, scour, scrutinize

rake (reɪk )

Definitions

intransitive verb

Word forms:  raked,  ˈraking
  1. to be slightly inclined; slant, as a ship's masts, etc.

transitive verb

  1. to cause to slant or incline

noun

  1. a slanting or inclination
    1. away from the perpendicular   ⇒ the rake of a mast
    2. away from the horizontal   ⇒ the rake of a stage
  2. the angle made by the edge of a cutting tool and a plane perpendicular to the surface that is being worked on

Word Origin

< ? or akin to Swed raka, to project, akin to OE hrægan, to project < IE base * krek-, * krok-, to project

rake (reɪk )

Definitions

intransitive verb

Word forms:  raked,  ˈraking
  1. to fly after game (said of a hawk)
  2. to run after game with the nose to the track instead of in the wind (said of a hunting dog)

Word Origin

ME raken < OE racian, to speed forward: for IE base see rake1
