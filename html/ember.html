Definitions of ember

ember (ˈɛmbər )

Definitions

noun

  1. a glowing piece of coal, wood, etc. from a fire; esp., such a piece smoldering among ashes
  2. ([pl.]) the smoldering remains of a fire

Word Origin

ME eymere & (with intrusive -b) eymbre < OE æmerge (& ON eimyrja) < æm- (akin to ON eimr, steam) + -yrge (akin to ON ysja, fire) < IE base * eus-, to burn > L urere, to burn

ember (ˈɛmbər )

Definitions

adjective

  1. ([often E-]) designating or of three days (Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday) set aside for prayer and fasting in a specified week of each of the four seasons of the year in various Christian churches

Word Origin

ME (SE dial.) embyr-, as in embyr-dayes < OE ymbren, lit., a coming around < ymbryne, circuit, revolution < ymb, round (akin to ambi-) + ryne, a running (< base of , run)

Example Sentences Including 'ember'

He stomped out the ember on the carpet where it had fallen.
Patrick Ness THE CRASH OF HENNINGTON (2003)
