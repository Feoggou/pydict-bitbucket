Definitions of herd

herd (hɜrd )

Definitions

noun

  1. a number of cattle, sheep, or other animals feeding, living, or being driven together
    1. any large group suggestive of this; crowd; company
    2. the common people; masses (a contemptuous term)

transitive verb, intransitive verb

  1. to gather together or move as a herd, group, crowd, etc.
group

Word Origin

ME < OE heord, akin to Ger herde < IE base * kerdho-, a row, group > Sans árdha, a herd, troop

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= multitude, collection, crowd, drove, flock, horde, mass, mob, swarm, throng
= congregate, assemble, collect, flock, gather, huddle, muster, rally

herd (hɜrd )

Definitions

noun

  1. a herdsman (now chiefly in combination)   ⇒ cowherd, shepherd

transitive verb, intransitive verb

  1. to tend or drive as a herdsman
  • See ride herd on

Derived Forms

ˈherder noun

Word Origin

ME herde < OE hierde (akin to Ger hirt) < same base as herd1
