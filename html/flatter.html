Definitions of flatter

flatter (ˈflætər )

Definitions

transitive verb

  1. to praise too much, untruly, or insincerely, as in order to win favor
  2. to try to please, or ingratiate oneself with, by praise and attention
  3. to make seem better or more attractive than is so   ⇒ his portrait flatters him
  4. to make feel pleased or honored; gratify the vanity of   ⇒ it's flattering to be remembered
  5. to please or gratify (the eye, ear, senses, etc.)
  6. to encourage, esp. falsely

intransitive verb

  1. to use flattery
  • See flatter oneself

Derived Forms

ˈflatterer noun
ˈflatteringly adverb

Word Origin

ME flateren < OFr flater, to smooth, caress with flat hand < Frank * flat, akin to OHG flaz, flat1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= praise, brown-nose (slang), butter up, compliment, pander to, soft-soap (informal), sweet-talk (informal), wheedle
= suit, become, do something for, enhance, set off, show to advantage

flat (flæt )

Definitions

adjective

Word forms:  ˈflatter,  ˈflattest
  1. having a smooth, level surface; having little or no depression or elevation
    1. lying extended at full length
    2. spread out smooth and level
  3. touching at as many points as possible   ⇒ with his back flat against the wall
    1. having little depth or thickness; broad, even, and thin
    2. having a flat heel or no heel   ⇒ flat shoes
  5. designating or having an almost straight or level trajectory or flight
  6. absolute; positive   ⇒ a flat denial
  7. not variable; fixed   ⇒ a flat rate, a flat tax
    1. without much business activity   ⇒ a flat market
  9. having little or no sparkle or taste; insipid   ⇒ a flat drink
  10. having little or no interest; monotonous; dull
  11. not clear or full; blurred   ⇒ a flat sound
  12. (US) emptied of air   ⇒ a flat tire
  13. (US, informal) completely without money; penniless
  14. without gloss   ⇒ flat paint
  15. (art)
    1. lacking relief, depth, or perspective
    2. uniform in tint or shade
  16. (grammar)
    1. not having the sign to (said of an infinitive: Ex.: go in “make it go”)
    2. not having an inflectional ending (said esp. of certain adverbs: Ex.: he drove fast)
  17. (music)
    1. lower in pitch by a half step   ⇒ D-flat (D57394)
    2. out of tune by being below the true or proper pitch
  18. (phonetics) designating the vowel a when it represents the sound (a) as in had or hat, articulated with the tongue in a relatively level position
  19. (photography) lacking in contrast

adverb

Word forms:  ˈflatter,  ˈflattest
  1. in a flat manner; flatly (in various senses)
  2. in a prone or supine position
    1. exactly; precisely   ⇒ to run a race in ten seconds flat
    2. bluntly; abruptly   ⇒ she left him flat
  4. (US, finance) with no interest
  5. (music) below the true or proper pitch

noun

  1. a flat surface or part   ⇒ the flat of the hand, of a sword, etc.
  2. ([often pl.]) an expanse of level land
  3. a low-lying marsh
  4. a shallow; shoal
  5. any of various flat things (; specif.,)
    1. a shallow box or container, as for growing seedlings
    2. (US) flatcar
    3. a piece of theatrical scenery on a flat frame
    4. (US) a deflated tire
    5. ([pl.]) women's flat-heeled shoes or slippers
  6. (American football) the area flanking either end of the offensive line
  7. (music)
    1. a note or tone one half step below another
    2. the sign () indicating such a note

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈflatted,  ˈflatting
  1. (music) to make flat; lower a half step

intransitive verb

  1. to sing or play below the true or proper pitch
inˈsipid, ˈlevel
  • See fall flat
  • See flat out

ˈflatness noun
