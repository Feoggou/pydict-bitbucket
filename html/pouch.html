pouch (paʊtʃ )

Definitions

noun

  1. a small bag or sack for carrying something, as in one's pocket   ⇒ a leather tobacco pouch
  2. (US) a mailbag; specif., one whose opening can be locked, as for sending diplomatic dispatches
  3. anything shaped like a pouch
  4. (Scottish) a pocket (in clothing)
  5. (archaic) a purse
  6. (anatomy) any pouchlike cavity or part
  7. (zoology)
    1. marsupium (sense 1)
    2. a baglike part, as of a pelican's bill or a gopher's cheeks, used to carry food

transitive verb

  1. to put in a pouch
  2. to make into a pouch; make pouchy
  3. to swallow (said of fish and certain birds)

intransitive verb

  1. to form a pouch or pouchlike cavity

Word Origin

ME pouche < MFr poche, var. of poque: see poke2
