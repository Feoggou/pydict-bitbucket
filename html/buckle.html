Definitions of buckle

buckle (ˈbʌkəl )

Definitions

noun

  1. a clasp on one end of a strap or belt for fastening the other end in place
  2. a clasplike ornament, as for shoes
  3. a clasp on a strap that fits into a matching part on a post, another strap, etc. thereby securing the strap

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈbuckled,  ˈbuckling
  1. to fasten or join with a buckle

intransitive verb

  1. to be fastened or joined by a buckle
  2. (obsolete) to engage in a struggle; grapple
  • See buckle down

Word Origin

ME bokel, a buckle, boss of a shield < OFr bocle < LL bucula, beaver, shield < L buccula, cheek strap of a helmet, dim. of bucca, cheek: see buccal

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= fastener, catch, clasp, clip, hasp
= fasten, clasp, close, hook, secure

buckle (ˈbʌkəl )

Definitions

transitive verb, intransitive verb

Word forms:  ˈbuckled,  ˈbuckling
  1. to bend, warp, or crumple, as under pressure or in intense heat

noun

  1. a distortion caused by buckling; bend, bulge, kink, etc.
  • See buckle under

Word Origin

ME bokelen, to arch the body < bokel, buckle; prob. infl. by OFr bocler, to bulge < bocle: see buckle1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= distort, bend, bulge, cave in, collapse, contort, crumple, fold, twist, warp
