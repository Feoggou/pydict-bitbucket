mild (maɪld )

Definitions

adjective

    1. gentle or kind in disposition, action, or effect; not severe, harsh, bitter, etc.
    2. not extreme in any way; moderate; temperate   ⇒ a mild winter
  2. having a soft, pleasant taste or flavor; not strong, sour, bitter, biting, or sharp (said of tobacco, cheese, etc.)
  3. designating steel that is tough but malleable and contains only a small percentage of carbon
soft

Derived Forms

ˈmildness noun

Word Origin

ME milde < OE, akin to Ger mild < IE * meldh- < base * mel-, to crush, rub fine, tender > melt, , mill1
