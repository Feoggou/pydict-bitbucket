muster (ˈmʌstər )

Definitions

transitive verb

  1. to assemble or summon (troops, etc.), as for inspection, roll call, or service
  2. to put through a roll call
  3. to gather together and display; collect; summon (often with up)   ⇒ to muster up strength
  4. to have in number; amount to

intransitive verb

  1. to come together or gather; specif., to assemble as for inspection or roll call

noun

  1. a gathering together or assembling, as of troops for inspection
    1. the persons or things assembled; assemblage
    2. the sum of these
  3. the roll, or list, of persons in a military or naval unit (also ) muster roll
ˈgather

  • See pass muster

Word Origin

ME mousteren < OFr moustrer, to exhibit, show < ML mustrare < L monstrare, to show < monstrum: see monster
