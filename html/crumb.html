crumb (krʌm )

Definitions

noun

  1. a very small piece broken off something; small particle or bit, esp. of bread, cake, etc.
  2. any bit or scrap   ⇒ crumbs of knowledge
  3. the soft part of bread within the crust
  4. (US, slang) a worthless, disgusting, or despicable person (also ) ˈcrumˈbum

transitive verb

  1. (rare) to crumble
  2. to clear of crumbs   ⇒ to crumb a table
  3. (cookery) to cover or thicken with crumbs

Word Origin

ME crome < OE cruma, lit., scraping from bread crust, akin to Ger krume < IE * gr-eu, to scratch, scrape (> Ger krauen, to scratch) < base * ger-, to turn, twist

Synonyms
