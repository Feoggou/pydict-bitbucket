feeble (ˈfibəl )

Definitions

adjective

Word forms:  ˈfeebler,  ˈfeeblest
  1. weak; not strong (; specif.,)
    1. infirm   ⇒ a feeble old man
    2. without force or effectiveness   ⇒ a feeble light, a feeble attempt
    3. easily broken; frail   ⇒ a feeble barrier
weak

Derived Forms

ˈfeebleness noun
ˈfeebly adverb

Word Origin

ME feble < OFr faible, feble < L flebilis, to be wept over < flere, to weep < IE base * bhlē-, to howl > bleat, , blare

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= weak, debilitated, doddering, effete, frail, infirm, puny, sickly, weedy (informal)
= unconvincing, flimsy, inadequate, insufficient, lame, lousy (slang), paltry, pathetic, poor, tame, thin
