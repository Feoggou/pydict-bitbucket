fume (fjum )

Definitions

noun

  1. ([often pl.]) a gas, smoke, or vapor, esp. if offensive or suffocating
  2. ([sometimes pl.]) anything imaginary or without substance
  3. ([pl.], rare) anything that weakens clear thinking
  4. (rare) an outburst of anger, annoyance, etc.
  5. (chemistry) a number of solid or liquid particles within a gas

intransitive verb

Word forms:  fumed,  ˈfuming
  1. to give off gas, smoke, or vapor
  2. to rise up or pass off in fumes
  3. to show, or give way to, anger, annoyance, etc.

transitive verb

  1. to expose to fumes
  2. to give off as fumes

Derived Forms

ˈfumingly adverb

Word Origin

ME < OFr fum < L fumus < IE base * dheu-, to blow, smoke, be turbid > dull

Synonyms
