novelty (ˈnɑvəlti )

Definitions

noun

Word forms:   plural ˈnovelties
  1. the quality of being novel; newness; freshness
  2. something new, fresh, or unusual; change; innovation
  3. a small, often cheap, cleverly made article, usually for play or adornment (usually used in pl.)

adjective

  1. having characteristics that are new, unusual, atypical, etc.   ⇒ a novelty tune

Word Origin

ME novelte < OFr noveleté < LL novellitas

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= newness, freshness, innovation, oddity, originality, strangeness, surprise, unfamiliarity, uniqueness
= gimmick, curiosity, gadget
= knick-knack, bauble, memento, souvenir, trifle, trinket
