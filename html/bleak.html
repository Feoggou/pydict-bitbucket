Definitions of bleak

bleak (blik )

Definitions

adjective

  1. exposed to wind and cold; unsheltered; treeless; bare
  2. cold and cutting; harsh
  3. not cheerful; gloomy; dreary
  4. not promising or hopeful   ⇒ a bleak future
  5. (obsolete) pale; wan

Derived Forms

ˌbleakly adverb
ˈbleakness noun

Word Origin

ME bleik < ON bleikr, pale: see bleach

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= exposed, bare, barren, desolate, unsheltered, weather-beaten, windswept
= dismal, cheerless, depressing, discouraging, dreary, gloomy, grim, hopeless, joyless, somber

bleak (blik )

Definitions

noun

Word forms:   plural bleak, bleaks
  1. any of a genus (Alburnus) of small, slender European carp with silvery scales, an extract of which is used in making artificial pearls

Word Origin

< ON bleikja < bleikr: see bleak1
