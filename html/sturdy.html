Definitions of sturdy

sturdy (ˈstɜrdi )

Definitions

adjective

Word forms:  ˈsturdier,  ˈsturdiest
  1. that will not yield or compromise; firm; resolute   ⇒ sturdy defiance
  2. physically strong; vigorous; hardy
  3. strongly built or constructed

Derived Forms

ˈsturdily adverb
ˈsturdiness noun

Word Origin

ME, defiant, refractory, hardy < OFr estourdi, stunned, reckless (basic sense “hard to influence or control”) < VL * exturdire, to be dizzy (? from too much chattering) < L ex-, intens. + turdus, a thrush1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= robust, athletic, brawny, hardy, lusty, muscular, powerful
= well-built, durable, solid, substantial, well-made
