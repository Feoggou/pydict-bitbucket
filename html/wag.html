Definitions of wag

wag (wæg )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  wagged,  ˈwagging
    1. to cause (something fastened or held at one end) to move rapidly and repeatedly back and forth, from side to side, or up and down   ⇒ the dog wagged his tail
    2. to shake (a finger) or nod (the head), as in summoning, reproving, etc.
  2. to move (the tongue) in talking, esp. in idle or malicious gossip

intransitive verb

  1. to move rapidly and repeatedly back and forth, from side to side, or up and down: said as of a part of the body
  2. to keep moving in talk, esp. in idle or malicious gossip (said of the tongue)
  3. to walk or move with a swaying motion; waddle

noun

  1. the act or an instance of wagging

Derived Forms

ˈwagger noun

Word Origin

ME waggen, prob. < ON vaga, to move back and forth, rock, akin to OE wagian, to shake, totter < IE base * weĝh-, to move > L vehere, to carry

wag (wæg )

Definitions

noun

  1. a comical or humorous person; joker; wit

Word Origin

prob. short for obs. waghalter, a gallows bird, applied to a joker, rogue (< ? wag1 + , halter1)
