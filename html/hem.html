Definitions of hem

hem (hɛm )

Definitions

noun

  1. the border on a garment or piece of cloth, usually made by folding the edge and sewing it down
  2. any border, edge, or margin

transitive verb

Word forms:  hemmed,  ˈhemming
  1. to fold back the edge of and sew down; put a hem or hems on
  • See hem in

Word Origin

ME < OE, akin to MLowG ham, enclosed piece of land < IE base * kem-, to compress, impede > hamper1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= edge, border, fringe, margin, trimming

hem (hɛm: )

Definitions

(conventionalized pronun.)

exclamation, noun

  1. (used to suggest) the sound made in clearing the throat

intransitive verb

Word forms:  hemmed,  ˈhemming
  1. to make this sound, as in trying to get attention or in showing doubt
  • See hem and haw

hem- (him ;  hɛm )

Definitions

  1. hemo- (used before a vowel)
