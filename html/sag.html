Definitions of sag

sag (sæg )

Definitions

intransitive verb

Word forms:  sagged,  ˈsagging
  1. to sink, bend, or curve, esp. in the middle, from weight or pressure
  2. to hang down unevenly or loosely
  3. to lose firmness, strength, or intensity; weaken through weariness, age, etc.; droop   ⇒ sagging spirits
  4. to decline in price, value, sales, etc.
  5. (nautical) to drift   ⇒ to sag to leeward

transitive verb

  1. to cause to sag

noun

  1. the act or an instance of sagging
  2. the degree or amount of sagging
  3. (US) a place of sagging; sunken or depressed place

Word Origin

ME saggen, prob. < Scand, akin to Swed sacka, Norw dial. sakka, sagga < IE base * sengw-, to sink

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= sink, bag, dip, droop, fall, give way, hang loosely, slump
= tire, droop, flag, wane, weaken, wilt
