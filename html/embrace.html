Definitions of embrace

embrace (ɛmˈbreɪs ;  ɪmˈbreɪs )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  emˈbraced,  emˈbracing
  1. to clasp in the arms, usually as an expression of affection or desire; hug
  2. to accept readily; avail oneself of   ⇒ to embrace an opportunity
  3. to take up or adopt, esp. eagerly or seriously   ⇒ to embrace a new profession
  4. to encircle; surround; enclose   ⇒ an isle embraced by the sea
  5. to include; contain   ⇒ biology embraces botany and zoology
  6. to take in mentally; perceive   ⇒ his glance embraced the scene

intransitive verb

  1. to clasp or hug each other in the arms

noun

  1. an embracing; hug
inˈclude

Derived Forms

emˈbraceable adjective
emˈbracement noun
emˈbracer noun

Word Origin

ME embracen < OFr embracier < VL * imbrachiare < L im-, in + brachium, an arm: see brace1

Synonyms

= include, comprehend, comprise, contain, cover, encompass, involve, take in
= hug, clasp, clinch (slang), cuddle, squeeze
