Definitions of rattle

rattle (ˈrætəl )

Definitions

intransitive verb

Word forms:  ˈrattled,  ˈrattling
  1. to make a series of sharp, short sounds in quick succession
  2. to go or move with such sounds   ⇒ a wagon rattling over the stones
  3. to talk rapidly and incessantly; chatter (often with on)

transitive verb

  1. to cause to rattle   ⇒ to rattle the handle of a door
  2. to utter or perform rapidly
  3. (US) to confuse or upset; disconcert   ⇒ to rattle a speaker with catcalls

noun

  1. a quick succession of sharp, short sounds
  2. a rattling noise made by air passing through the mucus of a partly closed throat see also death rattle
  3. a noisy uproar; loud chatter
  4. (US)
    1. a series of horny rings at the end of a rattlesnake's tail, used to produce a rattling sound
    2. any of these
  5. a device, as a baby's toy or a percussion instrument, made to rattle when shaken
emˈbarrass
  • See rattle around in

Word Origin

ME ratelen, prob. of WGmc echoic orig., akin to Ger rasseln

Synonyms

= clatter, bang, jangle

= fluster, disconcert, disturb, faze, perturb, shake, upset
