profuse (proʊˈfjus ;  prəˈfjus )

Definitions

adjective

  1. giving or pouring forth freely; generous, often to excess (usually with in)   ⇒ profuse in her apologies
  2. given or poured forth freely and abundantly
ˈplentiful
profuse implies a pouring or giving forth freely, often to the point of excess [profuse thanks ]; lavish implies an unstinted, generous, sometimes unreasonably liberal, giving [lavish attentions ]; extravagant always suggests unreasonably excessive, wasteful spending or giving [extravagant living ]; prodigal implies such reckless extravagance as to suggest eventual impoverishment [the prodigal heirs to a fortune ]; luxuriant suggests production in great and rich abundance [luxuriant foliage ]; lush1 implies such great luxuriance as to seem excessive [a lush jungle ]
limited, scant, spare

Derived Forms

proˈfusely adverb
proˈfuseness noun

Word Origin

ME < L profusus, pp. of profundere, to pour out < pro-, forth + fundere, to pour: see pro-2 & , found3

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= plentiful, abundant, ample, bountiful, copious, luxuriant, overflowing, prolific
