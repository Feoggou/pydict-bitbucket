writhe (raɪð )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  writhed,  ˈwrithing
  1. to cause to twist or turn; contort

intransitive verb

  1. to make twisting or turning movements; contort the body, as in agony; squirm
  2. to suffer great emotional distress, as from embarrassment or revulsion

noun

  1. a writhing movement; contortion

Derived Forms

ˈwrither noun

Word Origin

ME writhen < OE writhan, to twist, wind about, akin to ON rītha < IE base * wer-, to bend, twist > wreath, , wry

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= squirm, jerk, struggle, thrash, thresh, toss, twist, wiggle, wriggle
