hulk (hʌlk )

Definitions

noun

    1. (archaic) any ship
    2. a big, unwieldy ship
    1. the hull of an old, dismantled ship
    2. such a ship used for storage in a port or, earlier, as a prison
    3. a deserted wreck or ruins
  3. a big, clumsy person or thing

intransitive verb

  1. to rise bulkily (usually with up)
  2. (dialect) to slouch or lounge about in a heavy, clumsy manner

Word Origin

ME < OE hulc < ML hulcus < Gr holkas, towed vessel < IE * solkos, a pull, something dragged < base * selk-, to pull > Gr hēlkein, to pull, OE sulh, a plow

Synonyms
