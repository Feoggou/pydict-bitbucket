Definitions of foreboding

foreboding (fɔrˈboʊdɪŋ ;  ˈfɔrˌboʊdɪŋ )

Definitions

noun

  1. a prediction, portent, or presentiment, esp. of something bad or harmful

adjective

  1. characterized by foreboding
ˈominous

Derived Forms

foreˈbodingly adverb

Word Origin

OE forebodung

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= dread, anxiety, apprehension, apprehensiveness, chill, fear, misgiving, premonition, presentiment

forebode (fɔrˈboʊd )

Definitions

transitive verb, intransitive verb

Word forms:  foreˈboded,  foreˈboding
  1. to indicate beforehand; portend; foretell; predict (esp. something bad or harmful)
  2. to have a presentiment of (something bad or harmful)

Derived Forms

foreˈboder noun

Word Origin

OE forebodian: see fore- & , bideverb:intransitive

Example Sentences Including 'foreboding'

Perhaps he wasn't the only one with a powerful sense of foreboding.
J.A. Jance KISS OF THE BEES (2001)
