wry (raɪ )

Definitions

transitive verb, intransitive verb

Word forms:  wried,  ˈwrying
  1. to writhe or twist

adjective

Word forms:  ˈwrier,  ˈwryer,  ˈwriest,  ˈwryest
  1. (archaic) turned or bent to one side; twisted
  2. made by twisting or distorting the features   ⇒ a wry face
  3. (rare) distorted, as in meaning
  4. dry, ironic, sardonic, etc.   ⇒ wry humor

Derived Forms

ˈwryly adverb
ˈwryness noun

Word Origin

ME wrien, to twist, bend < OE wrigian, to turn, twist, akin to OFris wrigia, to bend, stoop < IE * wreik- (> L rica, head veil) < base * wer-, to turn, bend

Synonyms

= ironic, droll, dry, mocking, sarcastic, sardonic
= contorted, crooked, twisted, uneven
