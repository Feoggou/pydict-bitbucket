Definitions of loom

loom (lum )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to appear, take shape, or come in sight indistinctly as through a mist, esp. in a large, portentous, or threatening form (often with up [the peak loomed up before us ]: also used figuratively [the specter of war loomed ahead ])

noun

  1. a looming appearance, as of a ship in the fog

Word Origin

earlier lome, loam < ?

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= appear, bulk, emerge, hover, impend, menace, take shape, threaten

loom (lum )

Definitions

noun

  1. a machine for weaving thread or yarn into cloth
  2. the art of weaving (usually with the)
  3. the part of an oar or paddle between the handle and the blade

transitive verb

  1. to weave on a loom

Word Origin

ME lome < OE (ge)loma, tool, utensil; (sense 3) ON hlumr
