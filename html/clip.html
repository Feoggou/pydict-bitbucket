Definitions of clip

clip (klɪp )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  clipped,  ˈclipping
  1. to cut or cut off with shears or scissors
  2. to cut (an item) out of (a newspaper, magazine, etc.)
    1. to cut short
    2. to shorten by omitting syllables, letters, etc.
  4. to cut the hair of
  5. to cut off the edge of (coins)
  6. (informal) to hit or punch with a quick, sharp blow
  7. (US, slang) to cheat or swindle, esp. by overcharging

intransitive verb

  1. to clip something
  2. to move rapidly

noun

  1. an act or instance of clipping
  2. a thing clipped; specif., the amount of wool clipped from sheep at one time or in one season
  3. a rapid pace
  4. (cinema, television) a sequence of film, videotape, etc., as a short selection taken from a longer feature or a brief piece inserted in a newscast
  5. (informal) a quick, sharp blow
  6. clipped form
  • See at a clip

Word Origin

ME clippen < ON klippa

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= trim, crop, curtail, cut, pare, prune, shear, shorten, snip
= smack, clout (informal), cuff, knock, punch, strike, thump, wallop (informal), whack

clip (klɪp )

Definitions

intransitive verb, transitive verb

Word forms:  clipped,  ˈclipping
  1. to fasten with a clip or clips
  2. (mainly dialect) to hug; embrace closely
  3. (US, American football) to block (an opponent who is not carrying the ball) from behind: an illegal act

noun

  1. any of various devices that clip or fasten things together, grip or hold something, etc.
  2. cartridge clip
  3. (obsolete) an embrace
  4. (US, American football) an act of clipping

Word Origin

ME clippen < OE clyppan, to embrace < IE * gleb- < base * gel-: see climb

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= attach, fasten, fix, hold, pin, staple
