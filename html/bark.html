Definitions of bark

bark (bɑrk )

Definitions

noun

  1. the outside covering of the stems and roots of trees and woody plants
  2. some kinds of this matter used in tanning, dyeing, etc.
  3. cinchona

transitive verb

  1. to treat with a bark infusion, as in leather tanning
  2. to take the bark off; specif., to girdle (a tree)
  3. (informal) to scrape some skin off   ⇒ to bark one's shin
skin

Word Origin

ME < ON bǫrkr, akin to MLowG borke

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= covering, casing, cortex (anatomy) (botany), crust, husk, rind, skin

bark (bɑrk )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to make the characteristic sharp, abrupt cry of a dog
  2. to make a sound like this   ⇒ the engine barked
  3. to speak or shout sharply; snap
  4. (informal) to cough
  5. (US, slang) to advertise a show, sale, etc. by shouting about it in public

transitive verb

  1. to say or advertise with a bark or shout

noun

  1. the sharp, abrupt sound made by a dog
  2. any sharp, abrupt sound or utterance like this
  • See bark up the wrong tree

Word Origin

ME berken < OE beorcan, akin to ON berkja, of echoic orig.

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= yap, bay, growl, howl, snarl, woof, yelp

bark (bɑrk )

Definitions

noun

  1. (old-fashioned, poetic) any boat, esp. a small sailing boat
  2. a sailing vessel with its two forward masts square-rigged and its rear mast rigged fore and aft

Word Origin

ME barke, prob. via Port or ML barca (> OFr barque) < LL, small boat: see barge
