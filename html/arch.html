Definitions of arch

arch (ɑrtʃ )

Definitions

noun

  1. a curved structure, as of masonry, that supports the weight of material over an open space, as in a bridge, doorway, etc.
  2. any similar structure, as a monument
  3. the form of an arch
  4. anything shaped like an arch; specif., an archlike anatomical part   ⇒ the dental arch, arch of the foot, etc.

transitive verb

  1. to provide with an arch or arches
  2. to cause to take the form of an arch; curve or bend
  3. to span as an arch

intransitive verb

  1. to form an arch

Word Origin

ME < OFr arche < ML arca < L arcus, arch: see arc

Synonyms

= curve, arc, bend, bow, hump, semicircle

arch (ɑrtʃ )

Definitions

adjective

  1. main; chief; principal   ⇒ the arch villain
  2. clever; crafty
  3. gaily mischievous; pert   ⇒ an arch look

Word Origin

< arch-; with changed meaning because of use in archknave, archrogue

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= playful, frolicsome, mischievous, pert, roguish, saucy, sly, waggish

arch- (ɑrtʃ ;  (in `archangel' & its derivatives) ɑrk )

Definitions

  1. main, chief, principal (often used in forming titles of rank)   ⇒ archangel, archenemy; archduke

Word Origin

ME arche- < OE arce- < L archi-, arch- < Gr archos, first, ruler < archein, begin, rule
