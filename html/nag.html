Definitions of nag

nag (næg )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  nagged,  ˈnagging
  1. to annoy by continual scolding, faultfinding, complaining, urging, etc.
  2. to keep troubling, worrying, etc.   ⇒ nagged by a thought

intransitive verb

  1. to urge, scold, find fault, etc. constantly
  2. to cause continual discomfort, pain, etc.   ⇒ a nagging toothache

noun

  1. a person, esp. a woman, who nags (also ) ˈnagger

Derived Forms

ˈnaggingly adverb
ˈnaggy adjective
(ˈnaggier,  ˈnaggiest)

Word Origin

< Scand (as in Swed nagga, obs. Dan nagge, to nibble, gnaw, nag) < ON gnaga: for IE base see gnaw: for sense development see , fret1

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= scold, annoy, badger, harass, hassle (informal), henpeck, irritate, pester, plague, upbraid, worry
= scold, harpy, shrew, tartar, virago

nag (næg )

Definitions

noun

  1. (archaic) a small saddle horse; pony
  2. a horse that is worn-out, old, etc.
  3. (slang) a racehorse, esp. an inferior one

Word Origin

ME nagge, akin to obs. Du negghe < ?

Synonyms
