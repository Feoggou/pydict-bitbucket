rampart (ˈræmˌpɑrt ;  ˈræmpərt )

Definitions

noun

  1. an embankment of earth, usually surmounted by a parapet, encircling a castle, fort, etc., for defense against attack
  2. any defense or bulwark

transitive verb

  1. to protect as with a rampart

Word Origin

Fr rempart < remparer, to fortify a place < re-, again + emparer < Prov amparer, to fortify < L ante, before + parare, to prepare

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= defense, bastion, bulwark, fence, fortification, wall
