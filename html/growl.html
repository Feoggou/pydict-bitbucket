growl (graʊl )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to make a low, rumbling, menacing sound in the throat, as a dog does
  2. to complain in an angry or surly manner
  3. to rumble, as thunder, cannons, etc.

transitive verb

  1. to express by growling

noun

  1. the act or sound of growling
  2. (US, jazz) a low, rough, husky, often muted sound produced on a trumpet, trombone, etc.

Derived Forms

ˈgrowlingly adverb

Word Origin

ME groulen, to rumble, prob. of echoic orig.
