Definitions of pop

pop (pɑp )

Definitions

noun

  1. a sudden short, light explosive sound
  2. a shot as with a revolver or rifle
  3. any carbonated, nonalcoholic beverage (from the sound produced when the cork or cap is removed from the bottle)
  4. a frozen confection consisting of ice cream or flavored ice on the end of a small stick
  5. (US, baseball) a ball hit high in the air, usually not beyond the infield
  6. (US, slang)
    1. an attempt; try
    2. each; apiece (used with a)   ⇒ we paid $5 a pop to get in

intransitive verb

Word forms:  popped,  ˈpopping
  1. to make, or burst with, a short, light, explosive sound
  2. to move, go, come, etc. suddenly and quickly, and usually unexpectedly   ⇒ to pop into a room
  3. to open wide suddenly, or protrude, as with amazement (said of the eyes)
  4. to shoot a pistol, etc.
  5. (US, baseball) to hit the ball high in the air, usually not beyond the infield (often with out or up)
  6. (slang) to offer to pay (with for)   ⇒ I'll pop for lunch

transitive verb

  1. (US) to cause to pop, as corn by roasting
    1. to fire (a pistol, etc.)
    2. to shoot
  3. to put suddenly, quickly, or unexpectedly   ⇒ to pop one's head in the door, to pop a question at someone
  4. (US, slang) to swallow (a pill, capsule, etc.)
  5. (US, baseball) to hit (the ball) high in the air, usually not beyond the infield

adverb

  1. with or like a pop
  • See pop off
  • See pop the question

Word Origin

ME poppe: echoic

Definitions

adjective

    1. designating or of music popular with the general public   ⇒ a pop singer, a pop album
    2. pops1
  2. intended for the popular taste, esp. as exploited commercially   ⇒ pop culture, pop psychology
  3. (US) designating or of a realistic art style, esp. in painting and sculpture, using techniques and popular subjects adapted from commercial art and the mass media, such as comic strips, posters, etc.   ⇒ a pop artist
