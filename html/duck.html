Definitions of duck

duck (dʌk )

Definitions

transitive verb, intransitive verb

  1. to plunge or dip under water for a moment
  2. to lower, turn, or bend (the head, body, etc.) suddenly, as in avoiding a blow or in hiding
  3. (US, informal) to avoid or evade   ⇒ the candidate ducked the issue
  4. (slang) to move (in or out) quickly

noun

  1. the act of ducking

Derived Forms

ˈducker noun

Word Origin

ME douken < OE * ducan, to plunge, dive, akin to OHG tūhan (Ger tauchen), MLowG dūken, Du duiken, to dive

Synonyms

duck (dʌk )

Definitions

noun

  1. Word forms:   plural ducks, duck
    . any of a large number of relatively small waterfowl with a flat bill, short neck and legs, and webbed feet
  2. a female duck see also drake1
  3. the flesh of a duck as food
  4. (mainly British, informal) a darling; dear
  5. (US, slang) a person, esp. one qualified as being “odd,” “harmless,” “funny,” etc.
  • See like water off a duck's back

Word Origin

ME doke < OE duce, lit., diver, ducker < base of * ducan, to plunge, dive (see duck1); replaces OE ened (akin to Ger ente), common Gmc word for the bird
