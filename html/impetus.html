impetus (ˈɪmpətəs )

Definitions

noun

Word forms:   plural ˈimpetuses
  1. the force with which a body moves against resistance, resulting from its mass and the velocity at which it is set in motion
  2. anything that stimulates activity; driving force or motive; incentive; impulse

Word Origin

L < impetere, to rush upon < in-, in + petere, to rush at: see feather

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= incentive, catalyst, goad, impulse, motivation, push, spur, stimulus
= force, energy, momentum, power
