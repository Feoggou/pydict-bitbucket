Definitions of bay

bay (beɪ )

Definitions

noun

    1. a part of a sea or lake that cuts into the shoreline; wide inlet: usually smaller than a gulf
    2. (international law) a small gulf with an opening to the sea of less than 24 nautical miles and a strictly defined minimum area: used to determine territorial waters
  2. (US) any level land area making an indentation, as into a woods, range of hills, etc.

Word Origin

ME bai < OFr baie < ML baia, prob. < Iberian

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= inlet, bight, cove, gulf, natural harbor, sound

bay (beɪ )

Definitions

noun

    1. an opening or alcove marked off by pillars, columns, etc.
    2. a recess in a wall, as for a window
    3. bay window
  2. a part of a building projecting from the main part; wing
  3. a compartment or space (; specif.,)
    1. a bin in a barn, for storing hay or grain
    2. a compartment in an aircraft or spacecraft   ⇒ bomb bay, cargo bay
    3. in a service station, the area for one car
  4. sick bay

Word Origin

ME bai < OFr baée < baer, bayer, to gape, yawn < VL batare, to gape

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= recess, alcove, compartment, niche, nook, opening

bay (beɪ )

Definitions

intransitive verb

  1. to bark or howl in long, deep tones

transitive verb

  1. to bark at; howl at
  2. to chase with yelps and barks
  3. to bring to or hold at bay
  4. to utter in long, deep tones

noun

  1. the sound of baying
  2. the situation of or as of a hunted animal forced to turn and fight
  • See at bay
  • See bring to bay

Word Origin

ME baien, abaien < OFr baiier, abaiier < IE base * bai-, echoic of howling

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= howl, bark, clamor, cry, growl, yelp

bay (beɪ )

Definitions

noun

  1. laurel (sense 1)
  2. ([pl.])
    1. a wreath of bay leaves, a classical token of honor given to poets and conquerors
    2. honor; fame
  3. any of various trees or shrubs of various families, as rosebay or bayberry

Word Origin

ME bai < OFr baie < L baca, berry

bay (beɪ )

Definitions

adjective

  1. reddish-brown (said esp. of horses)

noun

  1. a horse or, sometimes, some other animal of this color
  2. reddish brown

Word Origin

ME bai < OFr baie < L badius
