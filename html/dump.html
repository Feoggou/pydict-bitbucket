Definitions of dump

dump (dʌmp )

Definitions

transitive verb

  1. to throw down or out roughly; empty out or unload as in a heap or mass
    1. to throw away (garbage, rubbish, etc.), esp. in a place set apart for the purpose
    2. to get rid of in an abrupt, rough, or careless manner
  3. to sell (a commodity) in a large quantity at a low price, esp. in a foreign market at a price below that of the domestic market
  4. (US)
    1. to transfer (data in a computer memory) to another section of storage or to another storage medium
    2. to print out (data in a computer memory)
  5. (US, American football) to throw (a short pass) into the flat

intransitive verb

  1. to fall in a heap or mass
  2. to unload rubbish
  3. to dump commodities
  4. (US, slang) to defecate (somewhat vulgar)

noun

  1. a rubbish pile
  2. (US) a place for dumping rubbish, etc.
  3. (US) a copy, esp. a printout, of data stored in a computer
  4. (military history) a temporary storage center in the field, as for ammunition, food, or clothing
  5. (US, slang) a bowel movement: somewhat vulgar (mainly in the phrase take a dump)
  6. (US, informal) a place that is unpleasant, ugly, run-down, etc.
  • See dump on

Word Origin

ME dompen, to plunge, throw down; prob. < ON base akin to Dan dumpe, Swed dompa: for IE base see deep
