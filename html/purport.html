purport (pɜrˈpɔrt ;  (also, and for n. always,) ˈpɜrˌpɔrt )

Definitions

transitive verb

  1. to profess or claim as its meaning
  2. to give the appearance, often falsely, of being, intending, etc.

noun

  1. meaning; tenor; sense; drift
  2. intention; object

Derived Forms

purˈported adjective
purˈportedly adverb

Word Origin

Anglo-Fr purporter < OFr porporter < por- (< L pro: see pro1), forth + porter, to bear < L portare: see , port3

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= claim, allege, assert, profess
= significance, drift, gist, idea, implication, import, meaning

Translations for 'purport'

  • American English: purport If you say that someone or something purports to do or be a particular thing, you mean that they claim to do or be that thing, although you may not always believe that claim. purported, purporting, purports...a book that purports to tell the whole truth.
