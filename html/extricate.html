extricate (ˈɛkstrɪˌkeɪt )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  ˈextriˌcated,  ˈextriˌcating
  1. to set free; release or disentangle (from a net, difficulty, etc.)

Derived Forms

ˌextricaˈbility noun
ˈextricable  (ˈɛkstrəkəbəl )  adjective
ˌextriˈcation noun

Word Origin

< L extricatus, pp. of extricare, to disentangle < ex-, out + tricae, vexations: see trick

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= free, disengage, disentangle, get out, release, remove, rescue, wriggle out of
