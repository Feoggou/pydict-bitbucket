soothe (suð )

Definitions

transitive verb

Word forms:  soothed,  ˈsoothing
  1. to make calm or composed, as by gentle treatment, flattery, etc.; appease; mollify
  2. to allay or relieve (pain, an ache, etc.); assuage

intransitive verb

  1. to have a soothing effect
ˈcomfort

Derived Forms

ˈsoother noun
ˈsoothing adjective
ˈsoothingly adverb

Word Origin

ME sothen < OE sothian, to bear witness to, prove true < soth: see sooth

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= calm, allay, appease, de-stress, hush, lull, mollify, pacify, quiet, still
= relieve, alleviate, assuage, ease
