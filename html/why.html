why (hwaɪ ;  waɪ )

Definitions

adverb

  1. for what reason, cause, or purpose? with what motive? (used in direct, indirect, and implied questions)   ⇒ why did he go? he told her why he went

conjunction

  1. because of which; on account of which   ⇒ he knows of no reason why you shouldn't go
  2. the reason for which   ⇒ do you know why he went? this is why he went

noun

Word forms:   plural whys
  1. the reason, cause, motive, purpose, etc.   ⇒ never mind the why and wherefore

exclamation

  1. used to express surprise, impatience, indignation, etc.
  2. used as an expletive, to preface a remark
  • See why not

Word Origin

ME hwi < OE, instrumental case of hwæt, what
