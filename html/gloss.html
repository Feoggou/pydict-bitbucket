Definitions of gloss

gloss (glɔs ;  glɑs )

Definitions

noun

  1. the brightness or luster of a smooth, polished surface; sheen
  2. a deceptively smooth or pleasant outward appearance, as in manners or speech
  3. lip gloss

transitive verb

  1. to give a polished, shiny surface to; make lustrous
  2. to smooth over or cover up (an error, inadequacy, fault, etc.); make appear right by specious argument or by minimizing (often with over)

intransitive verb

  1. to become shiny

Derived Forms

ˈglosser noun

Word Origin

prob. < Scand, as in Norw dial. glosa, to gleam: for IE base see glass

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= shine, brightness, gleam, luster, patina, polish, sheen, veneer

gloss (glɔs ;  glɑs )

Definitions

noun

  1. words of explanation or translation inserted between the lines of a text
    1. a note of comment or explanation accompanying a text, as in a footnote or margin
    2. a collection of such notes
  3. a false or misleading interpretation

transitive verb

  1. to furnish (a text) with glosses
  2. to interpret falsely

intransitive verb

  1. to annotate a text

Derived Forms

ˈglosser noun

Word Origin

ME glose < OFr or ML glosa, for L glossa, foreign or strange word needing explanation < Gr glōssa, orig., tongue, language < * glōchia, pointed object < glochis, point < IE base * glogh-, thorn, point > OSlav gloge, thorn

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= comment, annotation, commentary, elucidation, explanation, footnote, interpretation, note, translation
= interpret, annotate, comment, elucidate, explain, translate
