poignant (ˈpɔɪnjənt ; , also) ˈpɔɪnənt )

Definitions

adjective

    1. sharp or pungent to the smell or, formerly, the taste
    2. keenly affecting the other senses   ⇒ poignant beauty
    1. sharply painful to the feelings; piercing
    2. evoking pity, compassion, etc.; emotionally touching or moving
  3. sharp, biting, penetrating, pointed, etc.   ⇒ poignant wit
ˈmoving, ˈpaˌthos

Derived Forms

ˈpoignancy noun
ˈpoignantly adverb

Word Origin

ME poynant < MFr poignant, prp. of poindre < L pungere, to prick: see point

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= moving, bitter, distressing, heart-rending, intense, painful, pathetic, sad, touching
