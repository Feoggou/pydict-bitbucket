quaint (kweɪnt )

Definitions

adjective

  1. (obsolete) clever or skilled
  2. (rare) wrought with skill; ingenious
  3. unusual or old-fashioned in a pleasing way
  4. singular; unusual; curious
  5. fanciful; whimsical
strange

Derived Forms

ˈquaintly adverb
ˈquaintness noun

Word Origin

ME cointe < OFr < L cognitus, known: see cognition

Synonyms

View thesaurus entry
= unusual, bizarre, curious, droll, eccentric, fanciful, odd, old-fashioned, peculiar, queer, rum (British) (slang), singular, strange
= old-fashioned, antiquated, old-world, picturesque
